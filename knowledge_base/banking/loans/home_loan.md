# Home Loan (गृह ऋण / होम लोन)

## Overview

### English
A home loan is a secured loan provided by financial institutions (banks, housing finance companies) to purchase, construct, renovate, or extend a residential property. The property itself acts as security/collateral until the loan is fully repaid.

### Hindi
होम लोन (गृह ऋण) एक सुरक्षित लोन (secured loan) है जो बैंकों या हाउसिंग फाइनेंस कंपनियों द्वारा घर खरीदने, बनाने, मरम्मत करने या बढ़ाने के लिए दिया जाता है। जब तक लोन की पूरी रकम वापस नहीं चुका दी जाती, तब तक संपत्ति (property) बैंक के पास गिरवी (security/collateral) के रूप में रहती है।

## Quick Summary
A home loan is a long-term financial commitment, typically ranging from 5 to 30 years. Financial institutions evaluate the applicant's repayment capacity based on income, employment stability, age, and credit profile (CIBIL score). Loans are disbursed either in stages (for construction) or in a lump sum (for ready-to-move-in properties). 

Interest on home loans can be floating (linked to external benchmarks like RBI's Repo Rate) or fixed. RBI regulations protect borrowers by prohibiting prepayment penalties on floating-rate loans for individuals. Regular repayment is done through Equated Monthly Instalments (EMIs), which consist of both principal and interest components.

---

## Definition
A Home Loan is a secured credit facility extended by a lender (Bank or Housing Finance Company) to a borrower to finance the purchase of land, construction of a house, buying of a pre-owned property, or renovation/extension of an existing home. The lender holds the title deeds of the property as security until the entire loan outstanding, including interest and other charges, is fully settled.

---

## Why It Matters
For most Indian households, buying a house is the largest lifetime financial investment. Understanding home loans is critical because:
- **Capital Access:** It enables citizens to own a home early in life without having to save the entire purchase amount upfront.
- **Tax Savings:** Borrowers can save significant amounts on income tax under Section 80C (for principal repayment) and Section 24b (for interest paid) of the Income Tax Act.
- **Financial Planning:** Knowing how interest rates, EMIs, and tenures work prevents borrowers from falling into debt traps or facing foreclosure.
- **Legal Protection:** Lenders perform thorough legal and technical checks on the property before approving the loan, reducing the buyer's risk of purchasing a disputed property.

## Eligibility
General eligibility parameters across Indian banks:
- **Age:** 18 to 70 years (must be fully repaid before or by retirement age).
- **Employment Status:** Salaried individuals (working in MNCs, public sector, or reputed private firms) or Self-Employed individuals (doctors, CAs, business owners, traders) with stable income.
- **Minimum Income:** Typically ₹15,000 to ₹25,000 per month (varies by city and lender).
- **Credit Score:** A CIBIL score of 750 or above is preferred to get lower interest rates.
- **Co-Applicant:** Adding a co-applicant (spouse, parents, children) with an active income source improves loan eligibility.

---

## Required Documents
- **Identity & Address Proof (KYC):** PAN Card (mandatory), Aadhaar Card, Passport, Voter ID, or Driving License.
- **Income Proof for Salaried:**
  - Salary slips for the last 3 months.
  - Form 16 and Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last 2 years.
  - Bank statements for the last 6 months showing salary credit.
- **Income Proof for Self-Employed:**
  - ITR with computation of income for the last 2 to 3 years.
  - Balance Sheet and Profit & Loss Statement certified by a CA.
  - Business bank account statements for the last 6 to 12 months.
  - Business registration certificate (GST, Shop Act, etc.).
- **Property Documents:**
  - Allotment letter, builder-buyer agreement, or sale deed.
  - No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the builder/society.
  - Title deeds showing clear chain of ownership.
  - Approved building plan and layout map.
  - Property tax receipts.

## Features
- **Loan Tenure:** Flexible repayment tenures up to 30 years.
- **Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio:** Banks finance up to 75% to 90% of the property value depending on the loan size (as per RBI guidelines).
- **Interest Options:** Choice between Floating Rates (which change with market benchmarks) and Fixed Rates (which remain constant for a specified period).
- **EMI Facility:** Repayment structured through monthly instalments combining principal and interest.

---

## Benefits
- **Tax Deductions:** Under Section 24b, deduction up to ₹2 Lakhs per year on interest paid. Under Section 80C, deduction up to ₹1.5 Lakhs on principal repayment.
- **Property Safe-Keeping:** Banks perform extensive due diligence on property titles, minimizing the risk of fraud.
- **Balance Transfer Option:** Borrowers can transfer their outstanding loan to another lender offering a lower interest rate.
- **No Prepayment Charges:** Individual borrowers face zero penalty for prepaying or foreclosing floating-rate loans.

---

## Risks
- **Interest Rate Risk:** Floating interest rates can increase due to inflation or RBI Repo Rate hikes, leading to longer tenures or higher EMIs.
- **Default & Foreclosure Risk:** Non-payment of EMIs can lead to a drop in CIBIL score and, ultimately, the bank seizing and auctioning the property under the SARFAESI Act.
- **Technical/Construction Delay Risk:** If a project is delayed by the builder, the borrower still has to pay the EMI or Pre-EMI, causing financial strain.
- **Cyber & Agent Fraud:** Falling prey to unauthorized agents charging high upfront commissions, or sharing sensitive KYC/banking credentials on unverified online portals.

---

## Charges & Fees
Common charges (subject to bank-specific structures and RBI regulations):
- **Processing Fee:** Upfront charge for loan processing (typically 0.25% to 1% of the loan amount, or a flat fee).
- **Prepayment/Foreclosure Charges:** Nil for floating-rate loans for individuals (as mandated by RBI). May apply to fixed-rate loans or non-individual borrowers.
- **Technical & Legal Valuation Charges:** Fee paid to bank-empanelled lawyers and engineers to inspect the property.
- **Stamp Duty & Registration Charges:** Paid to the state government for property registration and creating a mortgage (Memorandum of Entry).
- **MODT Charges (Memorandum of Deposit of Title Deed):** Government fee for registering the mortgage.
- **Late Payment Penalty:** Charges levied on delayed EMI payments (usually 1% to 2% per month on the overdue amount).

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **No Prepayment Penalty:** RBI circular prohibits banks and NBFCs from charging foreclosure or prepayment penalties on all floating-rate home loans borrowed by individuals.
- **External Benchmark Linked Lending Rate (EBLR):** Since October 1, 2019, all retail loans (including home loans) must be linked to an external benchmark, primarily the RBI Repo Rate, ensuring transparent and immediate transmission of interest rate changes.
- **LTV Caps:** RBI mandates maximum loan limits based on property value:
  - Up to ₹30 Lakhs: LTV up to 90%
  - Above ₹30 Lakhs to ₹75 Lakhs: LTV up to 80%
  - Above ₹75 Lakhs: LTV up to 75%
- **CERSAI Registration:** Banks must register security interests (mortgages) with the Central Registry of Securitisation Asset Reconstruction and Security Interest of India to prevent multiple loans on the same property.

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Apply and Get a Home Loan:
1. **Compare Lenders:** Research and compare interest rates, processing fees, and customer reviews.
2. **Apply online/offline:** Submit the application form with KYC and income documents.
3. **Obtain Sanction Letter:** The bank issues a conditional offer letter after assessing your eligibility.
4. **Legal & Technical Clearance:** Provide property documents for verification.
5. **Execute Mortgage:** Sign the loan agreement, submit original property papers, and register the mortgage (MODT).
6. **Disbursement:** The bank releases the funds to the seller/builder.

### How to Close a Home Loan:
1. **Obtain Foreclosure Statement:** Ask the bank for the final outstanding amount.
2. **Pay Outstanding Amount:** Settle the amount via cheque, demand draft, or bank transfer.
3. **Collect Original Deeds:** Within 30 days of closure (as per RBI rules), collect all submitted original property papers.
4. **Obtain NOC/NDC:** Get the No Objection Certificate and No Dues Certificate.
5. **Remove Lien/Charge:** Visit the registrar's office to remove the bank's mortgage lien from the property record.
6. **Verify CIBIL Update:** Ensure the bank updates the status as "Closed" in the credit bureau records within 30 days.

---

## Safety Tips
- **Verify Lenders:** Only apply with RBI-regulated Commercial Banks or registered Housing Finance Companies (HFCs).
- **Original Document Handling:** Always collect a detailed, signed receipt of the original documents submitted to the bank.
- **Avoid Cash Transactions:** Never pay processing fees or agent commissions in cash. All fees should be paid via official bank instruments (cheques/online transfers) favoring the bank.
- **Read the Fine Print:** Carefully read the Most Important Terms and Conditions (MITC) document before signing.
- **Protect Credentials:** Never share your online banking passwords, OTPs, or credit card details with agents promising easy loan approvals.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Borrowing Too Much:** Choosing an EMI that exceeds 40-50% of your take-home monthly income.
- **Ignoring CIBIL Score:** Applying for a loan with a poor credit score, leading to loan rejection or high interest rates.
- **Skipping Legal Check:** Relying solely on the builder's word or ignoring an independent legal check of the property papers.
- **Choosing the Wrong Interest Option:** Opting for fixed-rate loans without realizing they are often priced much higher than floating-rate loans.
- **Not Insuring the Loan:** Failing to buy a term insurance or home loan insurance plan to protect family members from liability in case of an untoward event.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is a Home Loan?** It is a secured loan taken to buy, build, or renovate a residential property.
2. **Who regulates home loans in India?** The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) regulates commercial banks and NBFCs/HFCs.
3. **What is CIBIL score and why is it important for a home loan?** CIBIL score is a 3-digit number representing credit history. A higher score (750+) helps in getting lower interest rates and faster approvals.
4. **Can I apply for a home loan jointly?** Yes, you can add a co-applicant (spouse, parent, co-owner) to increase income eligibility.
5. **What is the maximum tenure of a home loan?** Typically up to 30 years, subject to the retirement age of the borrower.
6. **What is Floating Interest Rate?** An interest rate linked to a market benchmark (like Repo Rate) that changes during the loan tenure.
7. **What is Fixed Interest Rate?** An interest rate that remains constant throughout the loan tenure (or a specific fixed term).
8. **What is Repo Rate linked lending rate (RLLR)?** It is an external benchmark rate linked directly to the RBI's repo rate.
9. **Can a bank charge penalty for prepaying a floating-rate home loan?** No, as per RBI guidelines, banks cannot charge prepayment or foreclosure penalties on floating-rate home loans for individual borrowers.
10. **What is LTV in home loans?** Loan-to-Value ratio. It is the percentage of the property value that the bank can fund.
11. **What is CERSAI?** A central registry that keeps records of all mortgaged properties in India to prevent frauds.
12. **Can I get tax benefits on home loans?** Yes, under Section 24b (up to ₹2 Lakhs on interest) and Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 Lakhs on principal) of the Income Tax Act.
13. **What are processing fees?** A one-time charge by the bank to process the loan application.
14. **What happens if I delay an EMI payment?** The bank charges a late fee, your CIBIL score drops, and repeated defaults can lead to property seizure under the SARFAESI Act.
15. **What is MODT?** Memorandum of Deposit of Title Deed. It is a legal charge registered with the sub-registrar acknowledging that property deeds are deposited with the bank.
16. **Can I transfer my home loan to another bank?** Yes, through a home loan balance transfer, if you find a bank offering lower rates.
17. **What is Pre-EMI?** The interest payable on the disbursed loan amount before full disbursement and start of regular EMIs.
18. **What is a Sanction Letter?** A formal document issued by the bank stating that your loan is approved, along with applicable terms.
19. **What happens to the original documents after full repayment?** The bank must return all original title deeds and issue a No Dues Certificate within 30 days.
20. **Can I get a home loan to purchase agricultural land?** No, home loans are only for residential properties. You must apply for agricultural land loans separately.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Home loan interest rates are fixed forever. **Fact:** Most home loans are on floating rates, which change based on RBI policies.
2. **Myth:** Banks charge a high fee for prepaying loans. **Fact:** RBI has banned prepayment penalties on floating-rate loans for individuals.
3. **Myth:** The property is automatically insured when taking a home loan. **Fact:** Property insurance is a separate policy; borrowers must purchase it to protect the structure.
4. **Myth:** Co-owners do not need to be co-borrowers. **Fact:** All co-owners of a property must join as co-applicants in the loan.
5. **Myth:** Banks fund 100% of the property cost. **Fact:** Banks only fund up to 75%-90% of the property value; the rest is paid by the borrower as a down payment.
6. **Myth:** A bad CIBIL score means I can never get a home loan. **Fact:** You can get loans from NBFCs/HFCs at a higher interest rate, but you should try to improve your score first.
7. **Myth:** I can claim tax benefit on any home loan from friends/relatives. **Fact:** You can claim tax benefit on interest paid to relatives, but principal repayment tax benefit (Section 80C) is only available for loans from banks/HFCs.
8. **Myth:** Lenders own the property during the loan tenure. **Fact:** You own the property, but the lender holds the title deeds as security.
9. **Myth:** Once a loan is sanctioned, the bank must disburse it. **Fact:** Sanction is conditional. Disbursement depends on satisfactory legal and technical verification of the property.
10. **Myth:** Banks cannot increase the loan tenure without consent. **Fact:** In floating-rate loans, banks often increase the tenure automatically when interest rates rise to keep the EMI constant.
