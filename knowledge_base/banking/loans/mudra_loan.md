# Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) - MUDRA Loan

## Overview

**English**: The Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) is a flagship scheme of the Government of India launched to "fund the unfunded." It provides loans up to ₹10 lakh to non-corporate, non-farm small/micro enterprises. These loans are classified under MUDRA loans given by Commercial Banks, RRBs, Small Finance Banks, MFIs, and NBFCs.

**Hindi Unicode**: प्रधानमंत्री मुद्रा योजना (PMMY) भारत सरकार की एक प्रमुख योजना है जिसे "फंड द अनफंडेड" (गैर-वित्तपोषित को वित्तपोषित करने) के उद्देश्य से शुरू किया गया है। यह गैर-कॉर्पोरेट, गैर-कृषि लघु/सूक्ष्म उद्यमों को ₹10 लाख तक का ऋण प्रदान करती है। वाणिज्यिक बैंकों, क्षेत्रीय ग्रामीण बैंकों, लघु वित्त बैंकों, एमएफआई और एनबीएफसी द्वारा दिए गए इन ऋणों को मुद्रा ऋण के रूप में वर्गीकृत किया गया है।

## Quick Summary
- **Scheme Name**: Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY)
- **Launch Year**: 2015
- **Maximum Loan Amount**: ₹10 Lakhs (Now increased to ₹20 Lakhs for entrepreneurs who have successfully repaid previous Tarun loans, as per recent budget announcements, but standard guidelines up to 10 Lakhs still widely apply).
- **Collateral Required**: Nil (Collateral-free)
- **Categories**: Shishu (up to ₹50,000), Kishore (₹50,001 to ₹5 lakh), Tarun (₹5,00,001 to ₹10 lakh).
- **Target Audience**: Micro-enterprises, small businesses, vendors, shopkeepers, artisans.

## Definition
MUDRA (Micro Units Development & Refinance Agency Ltd.) is an NBFC supporting the development of the micro-enterprise sector in India. MUDRA provides refinance support to Banks / MFIs / NBFCs for lending to micro units having loan requirements up to 10 lakh. A MUDRA loan is the actual credit facility extended by these lending institutions to the end borrower under the PMMY scheme.

## Why It Matters
MUDRA loans are crucial for financial inclusion and grassroots economic development. They empower small business owners who lack collateral or formal credit history to access the banking system, thereby fostering entrepreneurship, generating employment, and boosting local economies. It brings informal sector enterprises into the formal financial ecosystem.

The flow starts from the government providing corpus and policy. MUDRA refinances the lenders. Lenders assess the borrower's application under one of the three categories and disburse the collateral-free loan.

## Eligibility
- **Entity Type**: Individuals, Proprietorships, Partnerships, Private Limited Companies, Public Companies, or any other legal entity.
- **Business Type**: Income-generating activities in manufacturing, trading, or services sectors. (Includes allied agriculture activities like poultry, dairy, beekeeping, but excludes pure crop loans).
- **Age**: Minimum 18 years, up to 65 years.
- **Credit Track Record**: The applicant should not be a defaulter to any bank or financial institution.
- **Skill/Experience**: Applicant must possess the necessary skills, experience, or knowledge to undertake the proposed business activity.
- **Collateral**: No collateral or third-party guarantee is required.

## Required Documents
- **Proof of Identity**: Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, PAN Card, Passport, Driving License.
- **Proof of Residence**: Utility bill (electricity, water, telephone), property tax receipt, Aadhaar Card, Passport.
- **Proof of Business Identity**: Business registration certificates, licenses, Udyam Registration, GST Registration (if applicable), lease agreement.
- **Financial Documents**: Bank statement of the last 6 months. For Kishore and Tarun loans, audited balance sheets of the last 2 years, income tax returns, and projected balance sheets for 1 year (or loan duration) may be required.
- **Application Form**: Duly filled PMMY application form (different forms for Shishu, and Kishore/Tarun).
- **Photos**: Two recent passport-size photographs of the applicant.
- **Caste Certificate**: If applicable (SC/ST/OBC).

## Features & Benefits
1. **Zero Collateral Requirement**: The most significant feature of a MUDRA loan is that it is strictly collateral-free. Borrowers do not need to pledge personal or business assets, making it accessible to those who have zero asset backing.
2. **Flexible Repayment Tenure**: Repayment periods generally range from 3 to 5 years, depending on the bank's internal policy and the cash flow of the business. It may include a moratorium period of up to 6 months based on the income generation cycle.
3. **MUDRA Card Integration**: Borrowers are often issued a RuPay-based MUDRA card, acting as a working capital credit limit. It allows multiple drawals and repayments in a cost-effective manner and can be used at ATMs or POS terminals.
4. **Three Tier Product Structure**:
   - *Shishu*: Promotes starting a new business (up to ₹50,000).
   - *Kishore*: Supports expanding an existing small business (₹50,001 to ₹5 Lakh).
   - *Tarun*: Helps established businesses grow further (₹5 Lakh to ₹10 Lakh).
5. **Coverage under CGTMSE/CGFMU**: These loans are backed by the Credit Guarantee Fund for Micro Units (CGFMU), minimizing the risk for lending institutions and encouraging them to lend more aggressively to micro-enterprises.

## Risks
- **Financial Risk**: Non-repayment can lead to severe damage to the borrower's CIBIL score. Even though it's collateral-free, the lender can initiate legal proceedings to recover the dues, which might lead to financial distress.
- **Technical Risk**: Cyber frauds where scammers pose as MUDRA loan agents demanding upfront processing fees online. Borrowers might fall prey to phishing links promising instant loan approvals.
- **Legal Risk**: Diversion of funds (using the business loan for personal expenses like weddings or medical emergencies) violates the loan agreement and can result in immediate loan recall and legal action by the bank.
- **Cyber Risk**: Sharing OTPs or MUDRA card details can result in unauthorized withdrawals from the working capital limit.

## Charges & Fees
- **Processing Fee**:
  - For Shishu Loans (up to ₹50,000): Nil (Banks are strictly prohibited from charging processing fees).
  - For Kishore and Tarun Loans: Generally 0.50% to 1.00% of the loan amount + GST (Varies by bank/NBFC).
- **Interest Rate**: There is no fixed interest rate; it is governed by the RBI and the internal policies of the lending institution, typically linked to MCLR/RLLR. It usually ranges from 8.5% to 12% p.a.
- **Pre-payment Penalty**: Mostly Nil, but some NBFCs might charge a nominal fee if closed within a year.
- **Documentation Charges**: Actual stamp duty and nominal documentation charges as per state laws.

## RBI / Government Rules
- **No Refusal Policy for Shishu**: Banks are mandated by the DFS (Department of Financial Services) to not arbitrarily reject Shishu loan applications if the applicant meets basic viability criteria.
- **CGFMU Guarantee**: Banks must cover the MUDRA loans under the CGFMU (Credit Guarantee Fund for Micro Units) operated by NCGTC, ensuring risk mitigation.
- **Monitoring**: RBI actively monitors MUDRA loan disbursals and NPA (Non-Performing Asset) levels across banks. Banks have specific quarterly and annual targets for MUDRA disbursements.
- **Priority Sector Lending (PSL)**: Loans extended under PMMY qualify for priority sector lending classification for banks, aiding them in meeting their regulatory targets.

## Step-by-Step Process
1. **Business Plan Preparation**: The applicant must have a clear business plan showing the requirement of funds and projected income.
2. **Selecting the Lender**: Choose a Commercial Bank, RRB, or NBFC that offers MUDRA loans.
3. **Application Submission**: Fill out the correct application form (Shishu or Kishore/Tarun). Submit it along with identity, address, and business proofs.
4. **Bank Appraisal**: The bank manager or field officer visits the business site (or proposed site) to assess viability.
5. **Sanction**: Upon satisfactory appraisal, the loan is sanctioned, and an agreement is signed.
6. **Disbursement**: The loan amount is disbursed, often a part as a term loan (for machinery/assets) and a part as a working capital limit via a MUDRA RuPay card.
7. **Repayment**: Start paying EMIs or managing the working capital interest as per the agreed schedule.

## Safety Tips
- **No Upfront Fees**: The government or banks NEVER ask for upfront cash or digital payments for "MUDRA loan approval." Beware of fake agents.
- **Secure MUDRA Card**: Treat your MUDRA RuPay card like any other debit/credit card. Never share the PIN, OTP, or CVV.
- **Official Channels**: Apply directly at the bank branch or through the official Udyamitra portal (udyamimitra.in) or JanSamarth portal.
- **Beware of Fake Apps**: Do not download unverified apps from the Play Store claiming to offer instant MUDRA loans.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Applying for Personal Use**: Seeking a MUDRA loan to buy a personal car or fund a family wedding. MUDRA is strictly for income-generating business activities.
2. **Lack of Proper Accounting**: Not maintaining proper bank transactions or ledgers for an existing business, leading to rejection under Kishore or Tarun categories.
3. **Ignoring Credit History**: Assuming that since it's a government scheme, CIBIL score doesn't matter. Banks will check credit history; defaults will lead to rejection.
4. **Unrealistic Projections**: Submitting inflated projected balance sheets that don't match the ground reality of the business scale.
5. **Falling for Fraudsters**: Paying "commissions" to middlemen who promise guaranteed MUDRA loan sanctions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Who is eligible for a MUDRA loan?**
Any Indian citizen with a business plan for a non-farm sector income-generating activity such as manufacturing, processing, trading, or service sector, and whose credit need is less than ₹10 lakh, can apply.

2. **Is there any subsidy in PMMY?**
There is no capital subsidy built into the MUDRA loan scheme itself. However, if your proposal is linked to some other government scheme (like PMEGP), the subsidy of that scheme may be routed through the MUDRA loan.

3. **Do I need to provide collateral?**
No, all MUDRA loans are collateral-free. You do not need to mortgage any property or provide third-party guarantees.

4. **What is a MUDRA card?**
A MUDRA Card is a RuPay debit card issued against the working capital portion of the loan. It allows you to withdraw cash from ATMs or make purchases at POS machines for your business needs.

5. **Can women entrepreneurs apply for MUDRA loans?**
Yes, women entrepreneurs are highly encouraged to apply. Banks often offer slightly concessional interest rates (usually 0.25% lower) for women applicants.

6. **Are agriculture activities covered?**
Direct agriculture (crop cultivation) is not covered. However, allied agriculture activities like dairy, poultry, beekeeping, and pisciculture are eligible for MUDRA loans.

7. **How much processing fee is charged?**
There is no processing fee for Shishu loans (up to ₹50,000). For Kishore and Tarun loans, a nominal fee (0.50% to 1%) may be charged depending on the bank's policy.

8. **What is the interest rate?**
The interest rate is not fixed by the government. It depends on the bank's MCLR/RLLR and the applicant's risk profile, usually ranging from 8.5% to 12% p.a.

9. **Can a start-up get a Tarun loan?**
Typically, start-ups are given Shishu loans. Tarun loans (₹5L - ₹10L) require proof of an established business, track record, and higher turnover, which start-ups usually lack.

10. **How long does the approval take?**
For Shishu loans, it can take 7 to 10 days. For Kishore and Tarun, it may take 2 to 4 weeks due to deeper financial appraisal and field inspections.

11. **Do I need a GST number for a Shishu loan?**
No, a GST number is not mandatory for a Shishu loan, especially for small vendors. A simple Udyam Aadhaar or local municipal license is often sufficient.

12. **Can I apply online?**
Yes, you can apply online through the JanSamarth portal (jansamarth.in) or the Udyamimitra portal, which routes your application to the chosen bank.

13. **What if I default on a MUDRA loan?**
The bank will report the default to CIBIL, ruining your credit score. They will also initiate standard recovery procedures, including legal action, and claim the guarantee from CGFMU.

14. **Is Udyam Registration mandatory?**
While not strictly mandatory for Shishu, having an Udyam Registration makes the process smoother and is highly recommended. It is usually required for Kishore and Tarun loans.

15. **Can professionals like doctors or CAs get MUDRA loans?**
Yes, professionals starting their own practice or clinic can apply for a MUDRA loan under the service sector category.

16. **I already have a personal loan. Can I get a MUDRA loan?**
Yes, as long as your EMI repayment track record is good and you have the repayment capacity for the new loan based on your business income projections.

17. **Can NBFCs issue MUDRA loans?**
Yes, many registered NBFCs and Microfinance Institutions (MFIs) are authorized to disburse MUDRA loans, sometimes offering faster processing but slightly higher interest rates.

18. **What is the maximum repayment period?**
The repayment period is generally 3 to 5 years. It can include a moratorium period of up to 6 months during which only interest needs to be paid.

19. **Will my business assets be hypothecated?**
Yes. While there is no collateral, the assets created out of the bank loan (like machinery purchased or stock acquired) will be hypothecated to the bank as primary security.

20. **Can I convert my existing business loan into a MUDRA loan?**
If your existing loan fits the criteria (under ₹10 lakh, micro-enterprise), the bank may classify it as a MUDRA loan at the backend for their target fulfillment, but you cannot independently "convert" it just to get lower rates.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth**: MUDRA loan means the government is giving free money.
   **Fact**: It is a loan that must be repaid with interest. It is not a grant or free money.
2. **Myth**: There is a huge subsidy on MUDRA loans.
   **Fact**: PMMY does not have an inbuilt capital subsidy. Only specific linked schemes might offer subsidies.
3. **Myth**: You don't need to pay back MUDRA loans.
   **Fact**: Defaulting will ruin your CIBIL score and result in legal recovery actions by the bank.
4. **Myth**: Only manufacturing businesses can apply.
   **Fact**: Service, trading, and allied agriculture sectors are fully eligible.
5. **Myth**: You need political connections to get a MUDRA loan.
   **Fact**: Loans are sanctioned purely on the viability of the business plan and the applicant's creditworthiness.
6. **Myth**: The interest rate is 0%.
   **Fact**: Interest is charged by the bank as per RBI guidelines, usually between 8.5% and 12%.
7. **Myth**: I can get a ₹10 Lakh loan immediately for a new business.
   **Fact**: New businesses without a track record are generally given Shishu loans (up to ₹50,000). Tarun loans are for established units.
8. **Myth**: MUDRA loans can be used to buy a house.
   **Fact**: Funds can strictly be used only for business purposes, not personal consumption.
9. **Myth**: Shishu loan requires GST and 2 years of ITR.
   **Fact**: Shishu loan documentation is highly simplified and does not require GST or ITR for street vendors or micro units.
10. **Myth**: If the bank rejects my application, I cannot apply again.
    **Fact**: You can apply again if you rectify the shortcomings in your business plan or credit profile.
