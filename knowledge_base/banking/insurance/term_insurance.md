# Term Insurance

## Overview
**English:** Term insurance is a pure protection life insurance policy that provides financial coverage to the policyholder's family for a specific period or 'term'. If the insured person dies during the policy term, the death benefit is paid to the nominee.
**Hindi:** टर्म इंश्योरेंस एक शुद्ध सुरक्षा जीवन बीमा पॉलिसी है जो एक विशिष्ट अवधि या 'टर्म' के लिए पॉलिसीधारक के परिवार को वित्तीय कवरेज प्रदान करती है। यदि बीमित व्यक्ति की पॉलिसी अवधि के दौरान मृत्यु हो जाती है, तो नामांकित व्यक्ति को मृत्यु लाभ का भुगतान किया जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Term insurance ek pure protection life insurance policy hai jo policyholder ki family ko ek specific time (term) ke liye financial coverage deti hai. Agar insured person ki death policy term ke dauran hoti hai, toh nominee ko death benefit milta hai.

## Quick Summary
Term insurance is the simplest and most affordable form of life insurance. It focuses solely on providing a death benefit without any investment component. It ensures that dependents are financially protected and can maintain their lifestyle or pay off debts in case of the policyholder's untimely demise.

## Definition
Term insurance is a type of life insurance policy that guarantees payment of a stated death benefit if the covered person dies during a specified term. Unlike other life insurance products, it does not build cash value. Its primary purpose is to offer maximum life coverage at a relatively low premium cost.

## Why It Matters
Term insurance is critical for financial planning. It acts as an income replacement tool for families that depend on a primary earner. In the absence of the breadwinner, it helps settle outstanding loans, fund children's education, and cover daily living expenses, thereby preventing the family from falling into a financial crisis.

## Eligibility
- **Age:** Minimum entry age is typically 18 years. Maximum entry age is usually 65 years.
- **Income:** Must have a regular source of income to justify the sum assured (usually capped at 15-20 times the annual income).
- **Health:** Applicants must undergo medical tests or declare health conditions. Pre-existing conditions may affect eligibility or premium rates.
- **Nationality:** Resident Indians, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), and Persons of Indian Origin (PIOs) are eligible under FEMA regulations.

## Required Documents
- **Identity Proof:** Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Passport, or Voter ID.
- **Address Proof:** Utility bills, Bank statement, Passport, or Aadhaar Card.
- **Income Proof:** Last 3 months' salary slips, Form 16, Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last 2-3 years, or Bank statements.
- **Age Proof:** Birth Certificate, School Leaving Certificate, PAN Card.
- **Medical Reports:** Depending on age and sum assured, standard medical check-up reports as mandated by the insurer.
- **Photographs:** Recent passport-sized photographs.

## Features & Benefits
1. **High Sum Assured at Low Premium:** Since term plans do not have an investment component, the entire premium goes towards covering the mortality risk, allowing individuals to buy high coverage (e.g., ₹1 Crore) for a minimal monthly premium.
2. **Financial Security for Dependents:** Guarantees that the family's financial needs, such as daily expenses, education, and marriage of children, are taken care of in the policyholder's absence.
3. **Debt Protection:** Helps the family repay large liabilities like home loans, car loans, or personal loans without losing their assets or standard of living.
4. **Tax Benefits:** Premiums paid are eligible for tax deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, and the death benefit received is entirely tax-free under Section 10(10D).
5. **Add-on Riders:** Policyholders can enhance their coverage by adding riders such as Accidental Death Benefit, Critical Illness Cover, and Waiver of Premium for a small extra cost.

## Risks
- **Financial Risk:** If the policy lapses due to non-payment of premiums, the life cover ceases immediately, and no refund is provided (except in Return of Premium plans).
- **Technical Risk:** Discrepancies in online application forms or failure of digital payment gateways can lead to delays in policy issuance or unintentional lapse.
- **Legal Risk:** Non-disclosure or hiding of material facts (like pre-existing diseases, smoking habits) can result in the rejection of a death claim, leaving the family stranded.
- **Cyber Risk:** Sharing sensitive financial and health data online carries the risk of data breaches or phishing attacks if not done through secure, official channels.

## Charges & Fees
- **Premium:** The primary cost of the policy, varying by age, gender, lifestyle, and term.
- **Rider Charges:** Additional premium charged for supplementary benefits like critical illness or accidental death riders.
- **Late Payment Penalty:** Interest or penalty fees levied if premiums are paid after the grace period (usually 15-30 days).
- **Medical Test Fees:** Often borne by the insurer, but in some specific high-risk cases or policy reinstatements, the applicant may need to pay.
- **GST:** Goods and Services Tax at 18% is applicable on the premium amount.

## IRDAI / Government Rules
- **Claim Settlement Guarantee:** Under IRDAI regulations, if a policy has been in force for three continuous years, the insurer cannot reject a claim on the grounds of misrepresentation or non-disclosure (Section 45 of the Insurance Act, 1938).
- **Free Look Period:** Customers have a right to review the policy terms for 15 days (30 days for electronic policies) and cancel it for a refund if unsatisfied.
- **Grace Period:** Insurers must provide a grace period of 30 days for yearly/half-yearly/quarterly premium payments and 15 days for monthly payments.
- **E-Insurance:** IRDAI mandates digitisation of insurance policies through standard e-insurance accounts (eIA).

## Step-by-Step Process
1. **Requirement Analysis:** Assess the financial needs, liabilities, and income to determine the adequate sum assured and policy term.
2. **Comparison:** Compare policies from different insurers based on premium, claim settlement ratio, and rider options.
3. **Application:** Fill out the proposal form accurately, either online or through an agent, providing personal, occupational, and health details.
4. **Documentation & Payment:** Submit the required KYC and income documents and pay the initial premium.
5. **Medical Examination:** Undergo a medical check-up if required by the insurer based on age and sum assured.
6. **Underwriting:** The insurer evaluates the risk and may accept the proposal at standard rates, increase the premium (load), or reject it.
7. **Policy Issuance:** Upon acceptance, the policy document is issued and sent to the customer.

## Safety Tips
- **Complete Disclosure:** Always disclose complete and accurate information about your health, lifestyle (especially smoking/drinking habits), and family medical history.
- **Direct Payment:** Pay premiums directly through the insurer's official website or verified payment links to avoid fraud by intermediaries.
- **Secure Documents:** Keep your policy document safe and ensure your nominee knows its location and the claim process.
- **Beware of Phishing:** Do not share OTPs, PINs, or policy details with unverified callers claiming to be from the insurance company or IRDAI.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Inadequate Cover:** Buying a term plan with a sum assured that is too low to cover the family's future needs and inflation.
2. **Hiding Information:** Concealing medical history or smoking habits to get lower premiums, which ultimately leads to claim rejection.
3. **Short Policy Term:** Choosing a term that ends before retirement or before major financial responsibilities are fulfilled.
4. **Ignoring Riders:** Not opting for critical illness or waiver of premium riders, missing out on comprehensive protection against severe health issues.
5. **Delaying Purchase:** Waiting too long to buy term insurance, resulting in significantly higher premiums as age increases.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What is term insurance?**
Term insurance is a basic life insurance policy that offers a death benefit to the nominee if the policyholder passes away during the specific policy period. It has no maturity value unless you choose a 'Return of Premium' variant. It is designed solely for pure risk coverage. It is the cheapest form of life insurance.

**2. Can I buy multiple term insurance policies?**
Yes, you can buy multiple term insurance policies from different companies. However, you must disclose the details of existing policies when applying for a new one. The total sum assured across all policies should be commensurate with your income capacity. This helps in diversifying risk across multiple insurers.

**3. What happens if I survive the policy term?**
In a standard term insurance plan, there is no maturity benefit, meaning you don't get anything back if you survive the term. The premiums paid cover the risk of death during that period. If you want your premiums back, you can opt for a Term Plan with Return of Premium (TROP), which costs slightly more.

**4. What is a rider in term insurance?**
A rider is an add-on benefit attached to the base term policy for an extra premium. Common riders include accidental death benefit, critical illness cover, and waiver of premium. They provide extra financial protection against specific risks not covered by the standard death benefit. You should evaluate your needs before adding riders.

**5. How much sum assured should I choose?**
A general rule of thumb is to choose a sum assured that is at least 15 to 20 times your current annual income. You should also factor in outstanding loans, future expenses like children's education, and inflation. Proper calculation ensures your family won't face financial hardship.

**6. Does term insurance cover accidental death?**
Yes, standard term insurance covers death due to an accident, illness, or natural causes. However, if you add an 'Accidental Death Benefit' rider, the nominee gets an additional sum along with the base sum assured in case of accidental death. This provides enhanced coverage for unforeseen tragedies.

**7. Is suicide covered in term insurance?**
Suicide is generally covered in term insurance policies, but there is a waiting period. Usually, if the policyholder commits suicide within 12 months from the policy commencement or revival date, the insurer will only refund 80% of the premiums paid or the surrender value, and no death benefit is given. After 12 months, the full death benefit is payable.

**8. Do smokers have to pay higher premiums?**
Yes, smokers and tobacco users have a higher mortality risk, so insurance companies charge them higher premiums compared to non-smokers. It is crucial to honestly declare your smoking habits; failing to do so is considered fraud and will lead to claim rejection. Even occasional smoking must be declared.

**9. Can NRIs buy term insurance in India?**
Yes, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and Persons of Indian Origin (PIOs) can buy term insurance in India subject to FEMA regulations and the insurer's underwriting guidelines. They may need to undergo medical tests in their country of residence or while visiting India. The premiums can be paid via NRE/NRO accounts.

**10. What is a 'Return of Premium' term plan (TROP)?**
A TROP is a variant of term insurance where, if the policyholder survives the policy term, the insurer returns all the premiums paid (excluding taxes and rider premiums). The premiums for TROP are significantly higher than a standard term plan. It appeals to people who want guaranteed returns if they don't claim the death benefit.

**11. What is the claim settlement ratio?**
Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR) is the percentage of death claims approved and settled by the insurer out of the total claims received in a financial year. A high CSR (consistently above 95%) indicates the company's reliability in settling claims. However, it should not be the sole factor; you should also check claim settlement turnaround time.

**12. Is the death benefit taxable?**
No, the death benefit received by the nominee is completely tax-free under Section 10(10D) of the Income Tax Act, subject to certain conditions. This ensures that the family receives the full amount intended for their financial security without any tax deductions.

**13. What is the grace period for premium payment?**
Insurance companies offer a grace period if you miss your premium due date. It is usually 30 days for yearly, half-yearly, and quarterly payment frequencies, and 15 days for monthly payments. During this period, the life cover remains active, and you can pay the premium without losing the policy benefits.

**14. What happens if I stop paying premiums?**
If you stop paying premiums and the grace period expires, your policy will lapse. All benefits will cease immediately, and the life cover ends. You will not receive any refund of the premiums already paid, which means you lose the financial protection for your family.

**15. Can a lapsed term policy be revived?**
Yes, most insurers allow you to revive a lapsed policy within a specific period, typically 2 to 5 years from the date of the first unpaid premium. Revival requires payment of all outstanding premiums along with interest/penalty, and you may need to submit a fresh declaration of good health or undergo new medical tests.

**16. What is the Married Women's Property (MWP) Act in term insurance?**
If a term policy is bought under the MWP Act, 1874, the death benefit becomes a trust for the sole benefit of the wife and/or children. It ensures that the payout cannot be claimed by creditors or relatives, providing a protective ring around the family's financial security. It is highly recommended for businessmen with loans.

**17. Do I need to undergo a medical test?**
While some low sum assured policies or young applicants might be exempt, undergoing a medical test is generally required for high-value term plans. The test helps the insurer accurately assess your health risk. It is better to have a medical test as it reduces the chances of claim rejection later based on pre-existing undisclosed conditions.

**18. At what age should I buy term insurance?**
The ideal time to buy term insurance is as soon as you start earning and have dependents. Buying it in your 20s or early 30s ensures that you lock in a very low premium for the rest of your life. Delaying the purchase leads to exponentially higher premiums due to age and potential health issues.

**19. How does the insurer pay the death benefit?**
The nominee can choose how to receive the death benefit. The most common method is a single lump-sum payment. Some policies offer options like staggered monthly payments, or a combination of a lump sum and monthly income, which is useful if the nominee lacks financial management skills to handle a large corpus.

**20. Can I change my nominee later?**
Yes, you can change your nominee at any time during the policy term. You simply need to fill out a nomination change form and submit it to the insurance company along with the nominee's KYC details. It is important to keep the nomination updated, especially after life events like marriage or childbirth.

## Common Myths vs Facts

**Myth 1:** Term insurance is a waste of money if I don't die.
**Fact:** Term insurance is not an investment; it is pure risk protection. The premium you pay guarantees peace of mind and financial security for your family. Think of it like car insurance; you don't consider the premium wasted just because you didn't have an accident.

**Myth 2:** I am single, so I don't need term insurance.
**Fact:** Even if you are single, you might have financial liabilities like an education loan, or aging parents who depend on you. Term insurance protects your co-signers or dependents from bearing your debt burden if something happens to you.

**Myth 3:** My company provides group life insurance, so I don't need a personal term plan.
**Fact:** Corporate life cover is tied to your employment. If you change jobs, get laid off, or retire, the cover ceases. A personal term plan provides uninterrupted coverage regardless of your employment status.

**Myth 4:** Older people cannot buy term insurance.
**Fact:** While it's cheaper to buy when young, many insurers offer term plans up to the age of 60 or 65. The premiums will be higher, and medical tests are mandatory, but you can still secure coverage for your family.

**Myth 5:** The insurance company will definitely reject the claim to save money.
**Fact:** Insurers are tightly regulated by IRDAI. If you have been honest in your application, disclosed all medical facts, and paid premiums on time, the claim will be settled. Section 45 prevents insurers from rejecting claims after 3 years for non-disclosure.

**Myth 6:** Smokers cannot get term insurance.
**Fact:** Smokers can definitely buy term insurance, but they will be charged a higher premium due to the increased health risk. It is vital to declare smoking habits accurately to ensure the policy is valid.

**Myth 7:** Buying online is risky; agents are safer.
**Fact:** Buying online directly from the insurer's website is safe, convenient, and often cheaper because there are no intermediary commissions. Online platforms provide transparent information and secure payment gateways.

**Myth 8:** Women don't need term insurance.
**Fact:** Women, whether working professionals or homemakers, contribute significantly to the family's financial and functional stability. Term insurance is essential for women to ensure their dependents are protected in their absence.

**Myth 9:** I should always choose the policy with the lowest premium.
**Fact:** While premium cost is important, you should also evaluate the insurer's Claim Settlement Ratio, customer service, rider options, and policy features. The cheapest policy might not always offer the best terms or support.

**Myth 10:** Term insurance covers all types of deaths from day one.
**Fact:** While accidental and natural deaths are covered from day one, suicide is typically not covered for the first 12 months. Any death caused by illegal activities or specific exclusions mentioned in the policy may also be denied.
