# Vehicle Insurance (Motor Insurance)

## Overview
**English:** Vehicle Insurance, also known as Motor Insurance, is a type of general insurance policy that protects the vehicle owner against financial losses arising from accidental damage, theft, or natural calamities involving their vehicle, as well as third-party liabilities.
**Hindi:** वाहन बीमा (मोटर बीमा) एक प्रकार की सामान्य बीमा पॉलिसी है जो वाहन मालिक को उनके वाहन से जुड़े दुर्घटना के नुकसान, चोरी, या प्राकृतिक आपदाओं के साथ-साथ तीसरे पक्ष की देनदारियों से उत्पन्न होने वाले वित्तीय नुकसान से बचाती है।
**Hinglish:** Vehicle Insurance ya Motor Insurance ek aisi policy hai jo vehicle owner ko accident, chori, ya natural disasters se hone wale financial loss se bachati hai, aur isme third-party liabilities bhi cover hoti hain.

## Quick Summary
Vehicle insurance is a mandatory requirement under Indian law (Motor Vehicles Act) for any vehicle plying on public roads. It provides financial coverage for damages to your own vehicle (own damage) and legal liabilities towards third-party injuries, death, or property damage. There are two main types: Third-Party Liability (mandatory) and Comprehensive Cover (optional but recommended).

## Definition
Motor insurance is a legal contract between the vehicle owner and the insurance company, wherein the insurer agrees to pay for specific financial losses associated with the insured vehicle in exchange for a premium. This covers cars, two-wheelers, and commercial vehicles. It safeguards the policyholder from out-of-pocket expenses resulting from road accidents, theft, fire, or natural calamities like floods and earthquakes, alongside fulfilling the legal mandate of third-party coverage.

## Why It Matters
Having a valid vehicle insurance policy is crucial for multiple reasons. Firstly, it is a legal necessity; driving without valid third-party insurance is a punishable offense attracting hefty fines and possible imprisonment. Secondly, road accidents are unpredictable, and repair costs for vehicles have skyrocketed. An adequate policy prevents financial ruin by covering these expensive repair bills. Thirdly, in the unfortunate event of causing injury or death to a third party, the compensation amounts can be astronomically high; the insurance policy shields the vehicle owner from such debilitating financial liabilities. Finally, it provides peace of mind to the driver, allowing them to commute without the constant fear of accidental financial burdens.

## Eligibility
- **Age:** The policyholder must be at least 18 years old.
- **Valid Driving License:** Must possess a valid driving license appropriate for the vehicle category.
- **Vehicle Ownership:** The vehicle must be registered in the name of the policyholder.
- **Valid Registration Certificate (RC):** The vehicle must have a temporary or permanent RC issued by the RTO.
- **Fitness Certificate:** For commercial vehicles, a valid fitness certificate is mandatory.
- **Not Applicable:** Minors cannot directly purchase vehicle insurance in their name unless a guardian acts on their behalf. Individuals without a driving license can own a vehicle and buy insurance but cannot drive it on public roads.

## Features & Benefits
1. **Third-Party Liability Coverage:** This is the most fundamental feature, legally mandated by the government. It covers the legal liability arising due to an accident caused by the insured vehicle that results in injury, death, or property damage to a third party. The compensation for injury or death is decided by the Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal (MACT) and is theoretically unlimited, whereas third-party property damage is usually capped (e.g., up to ₹7.5 lakhs for private cars). This feature ensures that the policyholder is protected from potentially bankrupting legal liabilities.
2. **Own Damage (OD) Cover:** This feature protects the insured vehicle against damages caused by natural calamities (earthquakes, floods, typhoons, hurricanes, lightning) and man-made disasters (riots, strikes, malicious acts, terrorist activities). It also covers damages from road accidents, fire, and theft. The compensation is based on the Insured Declared Value (IDV) of the vehicle, which represents the current market value minus standard depreciation.
3. **No Claim Bonus (NCB):** NCB is a reward given by the insurance company to the policyholder for not making any claims during the preceding policy year. It acts as a progressive discount on the premium payable for the own damage cover during renewal. The NCB discount starts at 20% for the first claim-free year and can go up to a maximum of 50% for five consecutive claim-free years, significantly reducing the long-term cost of insurance for safe drivers.
4. **Cashless Garage Network:** Most prominent insurance providers have tie-ups with an extensive network of authorized garages and repair centers across the country. If the insured vehicle meets with an accident and is taken to one of these network garages, the policyholder does not need to pay the entire repair bill upfront. The insurance company settles the approved claim amount directly with the garage, and the policyholder only pays the mandatory deductibles and depreciation costs, drastically easing the immediate financial burden.
5. **Add-on Covers (Riders):** Comprehensive policies allow the addition of specialized riders for enhanced protection at an extra premium. Popular add-ons include Zero Depreciation Cover (pays full claim without deducting depreciation on parts), Engine Protect Cover (covers engine damage due to water ingression or oil leakage), Return to Invoice Cover (pays the original invoice price in case of total loss or theft), and Roadside Assistance (provides 24x7 towing, battery jumpstart, and flat tire support).

## Risks
- **Financial Risks:** Relying solely on a third-party policy exposes the owner to heavy financial losses if their own vehicle is severely damaged or stolen. Furthermore, under-declaring the Insured Declared Value (IDV) to save on premium can lead to insufficient compensation during a total loss claim. Additionally, claim rejections due to policy violations (e.g., drunk driving, driving without a license) can result in massive, unexpected out-of-pocket expenses.
- **Technical Risks:** Modern vehicles are equipped with complex electronic components, sensors, and computer systems. Standard insurance might not fully cover electrical or mechanical breakdowns unless specific add-ons are purchased. Furthermore, delays in digital claim processing or failure of cashless systems due to server downtimes at the garage or insurer's end can cause significant inconvenience and delay in repairs.
- **Legal Risks:** Driving with an expired insurance policy is a strict violation of the Motor Vehicles Act, leading to heavy challans, vehicle impoundment, or imprisonment. In the event of a severe accident causing death or grievous injury to a third party while uninsured, the vehicle owner will face severe criminal charges and will be personally liable for compensating the victims, which can run into crores of rupees and result in personal bankruptcy.
- **Cyber Risks:** With the digitization of insurance issuance, policyholders face the risk of purchasing fake policies through fraudulent websites or unauthorized agents. Sharing sensitive vehicle data, KYC details, and payment information on unsecured portals or via tele-callers can lead to identity theft and financial fraud. It is critical to verify the authenticity of the policy through the VAHAN portal or the insurer's official channels.

## Charges & Fees
- **Base Premium:** The core cost of the insurance policy. For third-party cover, this is fixed annually by the IRDAI based on the vehicle's engine capacity (cc). For comprehensive cover, it depends on the vehicle's IDV, make, model, age, and geographical zone.
- **Add-on Premium:** Additional cost for selecting optional riders like Zero Depreciation, Engine Protect, Consumables Cover, or Return to Invoice.
- **Goods and Services Tax (GST):** A mandatory 18% GST is levied on the total premium amount by the government of India.
- **Compulsory Deductible:** A fixed amount the policyholder must bear during every claim (e.g., ₹1000 for standard private cars up to 1500cc, ₹2000 for higher CC cars). This is mandated by IRDAI to avoid trivial claims and reduce administrative overhead.
- **Voluntary Deductible:** An optional higher deductible chosen by the policyholder to reduce the overall premium. If chosen, this amount is deducted from the claim settlement in addition to the compulsory deductible.
- **Depreciation Deductions:** In standard comprehensive policies, the cost of parts replaced during repair is subject to depreciation based on age and material (e.g., 50% for plastic/rubber/nylon, 30% for fiberglass). This is deducted from the final claim amount unless a Zero Depreciation add-on is active.
- **Endorsement Charges:** Minor administrative fees for making changes to the policy document mid-term (e.g., address change, adding an LPG/CNG kit, correcting engine/chassis numbers).
- **Processing Fees:** Typically negligible or zero for direct online purchases on insurer websites, but some third-party aggregators might include a small convenience fee.

## RBI / Government Rules
- **Motor Vehicles Act, 1988:** Section 146 of the Act mandates that every vehicle operating in public spaces must possess at least a valid Third-Party Liability insurance policy to protect the interests of innocent victims.
- **Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019:** Substantially increased the penalties for driving without insurance. The fine for the first offense is ₹2,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 months, and for subsequent offenses, it is ₹4,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 months.
- **IRDAI Regulations:** The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) governs all insurance policies. They fix the premium rates for Third-Party insurance annually. IRDAI also mandates the issuance of a 3-year third-party policy for new private cars and a 5-year third-party policy for new two-wheelers at the time of purchase.
- **Personal Accident (PA) Cover Mandate:** IRDAI mandates a compulsory Personal Accident cover of ₹15 Lakhs for the registered owner-driver of the vehicle. If the owner has multiple vehicles, one PA cover is sufficient. If they already have a separate standalone PA cover of ₹15 lakhs, they can opt out of this mandate by providing proof.
- **MACT Guidelines:** The Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal (MACT) handles compensation claims for third-party injuries and fatalities, following strict judicial guidelines to ensure fair and timely compensation to the victims or their families.

## Step-by-Step Process
**Buying a New Policy:**
1. Visit the official website of the chosen insurance provider or an authorized, IRDAI-registered aggregator platform.
2. Enter the vehicle registration number. For brand new unregistered vehicles, enter the exact make, model, variant, and RTO location.
3. Select the preferred type of policy: Third-Party only or Comprehensive.
4. If choosing Comprehensive, review and adjust the Insured Declared Value (IDV) and select required add-ons based on your needs.
5. Provide accurate owner details, KYC information, and previous policy details (if renewing).
6. Review the final premium quote and make the payment securely online via UPI, Net Banking, or Credit/Debit Card.
7. The policy document (PDF) is instantly emailed to your registered address and can be downloaded immediately.

**Filing a Claim (Cashless):**
1. Inform the insurance company immediately after the incident via their toll-free number, email, or mobile app. Do this before moving the vehicle if possible.
2. File an FIR at the nearest police station if the incident involves theft, third-party injury, death, or severe malicious damage.
3. Take or tow the vehicle to an authorized network garage designated by the insurer.
4. Submit the required documents (duly filled Claim form, RC copy, DL copy, Policy copy, FIR) to the garage manager or the visiting surveyor.
5. The appointed surveyor inspects the vehicle, assesses the damage, and approves the repair estimate.
6. The garage completes the necessary repairs.
7. Pay your share of the liability (compulsory deductibles, any opted voluntary deductibles, depreciation on parts, non-covered consumables) and take delivery. The insurer settles the remaining approved amount directly with the garage.

## Safety Tips
- **Digital Authenticity:** Always purchase insurance directly from official insurer websites or IRDAI-registered brokers. Verify the active policy status on the mParivahan app or the IIB (Insurance Information Bureau) portal to ensure you haven't been sold a fake document.
- **Do Not Share OTPs:** Insurance companies will never ask for banking OTPs, CVV numbers, or UPI PINs to process a claim or renewal. Beware of fraudulent tele-callers claiming to offer incredibly cheap renewals.
- **Immediate Intimation:** Always inform the insurer about an accident or theft within 24-48 hours. Delayed intimation without a valid reason is a primary cause for claim rejection.
- **Accurate Information:** Never falsify information regarding previous claims, driver age, vehicle usage (commercial vs private), or vehicle modifications. Misrepresentation will inevitably void the policy during a rigorous claim investigation.
- **Keep Copies:** Always keep a digital copy of your insurance policy, RC, and DL in Digilocker or mParivahan. Carry physical copies in the vehicle if traveling to areas with poor internet connectivity or if specifically required by local authorities.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Ignoring the IDV:** Many policyholders artificially lower the Insured Declared Value (IDV) during renewal just to reduce the premium amount by a few hundred rupees. In the tragic case of total loss or theft, they receive a payout significantly lower than the actual market value of the vehicle, leading to massive financial loss.
2. **Not Disclosing Modifications:** Installing high-end stereos, alloy wheels, or a CNG kit without officially endorsing it in the policy document. If these undeclared accessories cause damage or are damaged in an accident, the claim will be outright rejected as it alters the risk profile of the vehicle.
3. **Losing Out on NCB for Minor Issues:** Claiming for very minor scratches, broken mirrors, or small dents where the actual repair cost is less than the accumulated No Claim Bonus (NCB) discount. Making even a ₹2000 claim resets a hard-earned 50% NCB to zero, increasing the premium drastically for the subsequent year.
4. **Delay in Renewal:** Letting the policy expire and driving uninsured even for a single day. If the policy lapses, the vehicle needs to be physically re-inspected, and if it remains expired beyond 90 days, the accumulated NCB (which could be worth thousands) is completely and permanently lost.
5. **Not Understanding 'Zero Dep':** Assuming that a standard comprehensive policy covers absolutely everything without deductions. Standard policies deduct heavy depreciation for plastic (50%), rubber, and glass parts. Failing to buy a 'Zero Depreciation' add-on results in the owner paying a surprisingly large chunk of the repair bill from their own pocket.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Is third-party insurance compulsory for old vehicles?**
Yes, third-party insurance is absolutely mandatory for all vehicles plying on public roads, regardless of their age. The Motor Vehicles Act requires this to ensure that victims of road accidents receive compensation. Even a 20-year-old car must have valid third-party coverage.

**2. What is Insured Declared Value (IDV)?**
IDV is the maximum sum assured fixed by the insurer that is provided on the theft or total loss of a vehicle. In simple terms, it is the current market value of your vehicle for insurance purposes. It is calculated by adjusting the manufacturer's listed selling price with standard depreciation rates mandated by the Indian Motor Tariff.

**3. Will my claim be rejected if I was driving without a license?**
Absolutely. Driving without a valid and effective driving license is a strict violation of the law and the fundamental insurance contract. Any claim arising from an accident where the driver lacked a valid license will be completely repudiated by the insurance company.

**4. What happens to my insurance if I sell my car?**
When you sell your car, you must transfer the insurance policy to the new owner's name within 14 days of the RC transfer. Alternatively, you can cancel the policy entirely and retain the No Claim Bonus (NCB) certificate, which can be applied as a discount to a new vehicle purchased in your name.

**5. Can I claim insurance for an accident caused by a drunk driver?**
No, driving under the influence of alcohol or intoxicating drugs is illegal and constitutes a fundamental breach of policy conditions. Insurance companies will unequivocally reject any own-damage claims arising from such incidents, and you could face criminal charges.

**6. What is a Zero Depreciation cover?**
Standard insurance policies deduct depreciation on replaced parts during an accident repair, meaning you pay a significant portion of the cost. A Zero Depreciation (or Bumper-to-Bumper) add-on waives this deduction entirely, and the insurer pays the full cost of replacement parts, subject only to compulsory deductibles.

**7. Does car insurance cover engine damage due to flooding?**
A standard comprehensive policy covers the external damage to the car due to floods but specifically excludes engine damage if you try to start a submerged car, causing a hydrostatic lock. To cover internal engine damage caused by water ingression, you specifically need an 'Engine Protect' add-on.

**8. What is the mandatory Personal Accident (PA) cover?**
IRDAI mandates a compulsory PA cover of ₹15 Lakhs for the registered owner-driver of the vehicle. This provides substantial compensation to the owner or their nominees in case of accidental death or permanent total disability while driving, traveling in, or mounting/dismounting the insured vehicle.

**9. Can I transfer my No Claim Bonus (NCB) to a new car?**
Yes, NCB belongs to you, the policyholder, not the vehicle. If you sell your old car and buy a new one, you can easily transfer the accumulated NCB to the new car's policy, provided you obtain an NCB retention certificate from the insurer.

**10. Why is the premium for commercial vehicles higher?**
Commercial vehicles (taxis, trucks) are driven more extensively, carry heavy loads or multiple passengers, and are on the road for longer hours. This statistically increases their exposure to accidents and risks significantly, prompting insurers to charge much higher premiums compared to private vehicles.

**11. Is damage by rats or rodents covered?**
Usually, standard comprehensive policies cover damage caused by fire, theft, accidents, and natural calamities. However, damage to wiring or internal parts caused by rodents is often considered wear and tear or consequential damage and is typically not covered unless explicitly stated in your specific policy wording.

**12. Can I cancel my vehicle insurance policy mid-term?**
You can cancel your policy mid-term, but you must provide proof that the vehicle is insured elsewhere (a new active policy) or proof that the RC has been canceled by the RTO (in case the vehicle is scrapped). Insurers do not allow cancellation of the mandatory third-party cover without proof of alternative coverage.

**13. What is a deductible in motor insurance?**
A deductible is the initial amount of the claim that the policyholder must pay out of pocket before the insurance company steps in to pay the rest. There is a compulsory deductible set by IRDAI (e.g., ₹1000) and an optional voluntary deductible that you can choose to lower your upfront premium.

**14. Are accessories covered under standard insurance?**
Factory-fitted accessories are generally covered by default. However, after-market electrical or non-electrical accessories (like high-end stereos, alloy wheels, custom lights) are not covered. You must explicitly declare them and pay an extra premium to get them insured.

**15. Does insurance cover tire bursts or punctures?**
Ordinary tire punctures or tire bursts due to normal wear and tear are not covered. However, if the tires are damaged as a direct result of a major accident that also damages the vehicle, they may be covered, often at 50% depreciation unless a specific 'Tire Protect' add-on is taken.

**16. What is Return to Invoice (RTI) cover?**
In case of total loss (like a severe crash) or theft, standard insurance only pays the depreciated IDV. Return to Invoice is a powerful add-on that bridges the gap between the IDV and the original invoice price (including road tax and registration), ensuring you can afford to buy a similar new car.

**17. How is the premium for Third-Party insurance decided?**
The premium rates for Third-Party liability cover are fixed centrally by the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI). These rates are revised annually based on the vehicle's engine capacity (cc) and historical claim data analyzed across the entire country.

**18. What happens if I lose my physical insurance document?**
Losing the physical copy is not a major issue today. You can simply download a digital copy (PDF) from the insurer's website, request it via email, or access it through the Digilocker or mParivahan mobile applications, which are legally valid during police checks.

**19. Can someone else drive my car and be covered by insurance?**
Yes, if a friend or family member is driving your car with your explicit permission and holds a valid driving license, the insurance will cover damages in an accident. However, commercial use of a private vehicle by anyone will instantly void the coverage.

**20. What is a surveyor in motor insurance?**
An insurance surveyor is an independent, specialized professional licensed by IRDAI who visits the garage after an accident is reported. They inspect the damaged vehicle, verify the cause of the accident, assess the required repairs, and approve the final estimate for the insurance company to settle.

## Common Myths vs Facts

**Myth 1:** Red cars cost more to insure than other colors because they are driven faster.
**Fact:** Insurance companies in India do not factor in the color of the vehicle whatsoever when calculating premiums. Premiums are strictly based on the make, model, engine capacity, IDV, location, and the owner's claim history.

**Myth 2:** Comprehensive insurance covers absolutely every single type of damage.
**Fact:** Even comprehensive policies have standard exclusions like normal wear and tear, mechanical breakdowns, driving under the influence, and consequential damages. You need specific add-ons for things like engine protection or zero depreciation.

**Myth 3:** If someone borrows my car and crashes it, their insurance will cover the accident.
**Fact:** Motor insurance in India is tied primarily to the vehicle, not just the driver. If a friend crashes your car, a claim must be filed under your vehicle's insurance policy, which will subsequently impact your No Claim Bonus (NCB).

**Myth 4:** I lose my No Claim Bonus (NCB) if I change my insurance company.
**Fact:** NCB is a reward that belongs to you, the policyholder. You can easily transfer your accumulated NCB when switching to a completely different insurance provider at the time of renewal by providing the renewal notice or an NCB certificate.

**Myth 5:** Third-party insurance covers injuries to the driver of the insured vehicle.
**Fact:** Third-party insurance strictly covers legal liabilities toward other people or property injured/damaged by your vehicle. It does not cover your injuries or damages to your own vehicle. You need a Personal Accident cover for the driver.

**Myth 6:** Claiming for a broken mirror is a smart way to get value out of the policy.
**Fact:** The cost of replacing a mirror might be significantly less than the NCB discount you will lose upon renewal next year. It is usually financially wiser to pay for small repairs out of pocket and save the NCB for major accidents.

**Myth 7:** Older cars don't need comprehensive insurance because their value is low.
**Fact:** While older cars have lower IDVs, comprehensive insurance still rigorously protects against theft, fire, and natural disasters, and covers significant repair costs from major accidents, making it highly valuable regardless of the car's age.

**Myth 8:** After-market accessories are automatically covered in my comprehensive policy.
**Fact:** After-market accessories like expensive stereos, CNG kits, or custom wheels must be explicitly declared and endorsed in the policy by paying an extra premium, otherwise they are entirely excluded from coverage.

**Myth 9:** I have a grace period of 30 days to drive legally if my policy expires.
**Fact:** There is absolutely zero grace period for driving. If your policy expires at midnight, driving the next morning is illegal. While insurers give a 90-day grace period to renew and retain the NCB, the vehicle is strictly uninsured and cannot be driven during that gap.

**Myth 10:** The insurer will provide a free replacement car while mine is being repaired.
**Fact:** Standard motor insurance does not offer a replacement vehicle. You only get this benefit if you have explicitly purchased a specialized 'Daily Allowance', 'Conveyance Benefit' or 'Downtime Protection' add-on cover.

**Agent (Hinglish):** Sir, kyunki policy expire hue 90 din se zyada ho chuke hain, aapka jama kiya hua No Claim Bonus (NCB) lapse ho gaya hai. Renewal ke liye ab car ki physical inspection (survey) karni padegi. Inspection clear hone ke baad hi aapko nayi policy issue ki jayegi, jiska premium thoda zyada ho sakta hai.

**Example 9: CNG Kit Endorsement**
**Customer (Hindi):** मैंने अपनी कार में बाहर से एक अच्छी CNG किट लगवाई है। क्या मुझे अब इंश्योरेंस कंपनी को बताना होगा?
**Agent (Hindi):** जी हाँ, यह बहुत ही महत्वपूर्ण है! आपको तुरंत आरटीओ (RTO) से अपनी आरसी में सीएनजी किट को अपडेट करवाना होगा, और फिर हमें सूचित करके पॉलिसी में इसका एंडोर्समेंट (Endorsement) करवाना होगा। इसके लिए थोड़ा अतिरिक्त प्रीमियम लगेगा। अगर आप ऐसा नहीं करते हैं और कोई दुर्घटना या आग लगती है, तो आपका क्लेम पूरी तरह से रिजेक्ट हो जाएगा।

**Example 10: FIR Requirement**
**Customer (English):** My car was severely scratched in the mall parking lot. Do I need to file an FIR to claim insurance?
**Agent (English):** For minor damages like scratches or dents where no third party is injured and the culprit is unknown, an FIR is usually not required. You can directly file a claim and take it to the garage. However, if the car is stolen, heavily vandalized, or involved in a serious accident injuring someone, filing an FIR is absolutely mandatory.

**Example 11: Return to Invoice**
**Customer (Hinglish):** Agar meri bilkul nayi car agle mahine chori ho jaye toh kya mujhe poore paise milenge?
**Agent (Hinglish):** Agar aapke paas normal standard policy hai, toh aapko car ki current depreciated value (IDV) milegi jo aapke invoice price se kam hoti hai. Par agar aapne 'Return to Invoice' (RTI) add-on liya hai, toh company aapko car ka poora on-road price (invoice amount + RTO taxes) wapas karegi jisse aap nayi car le sakein.

**Example 12: Changing Insurers**
**Customer (Hindi):** क्या मैं रिन्यूअल के समय अपनी बीमा कंपनी बदल सकता हूँ? मेरी पुरानी कंपनी की क्लेम सर्विस बिल्कुल अच्छी नहीं है।
**Agent (Hindi):** बिल्कुल सर! आप रिन्यूअल के समय किसी भी अन्य आईआरडीएआई (IRDAI) रजिस्टर्ड कंपनी में अपनी पॉलिसी बड़ी आसानी से पोर्ट कर सकते हैं। बस अपनी पुरानी पॉलिसी की कॉपी दें। आपका जमा किया हुआ नो क्लेम बोनस (NCB) भी बिना किसी परेशानी के नई कंपनी में सुरक्षित ट्रांसफर हो जाएगा।

**Example 13: Voluntary Deductible**
**Customer (English):** The premium for my luxury car is very high. How can I reduce my comprehensive insurance premium?
**Agent (English):** One effective way is to opt for a 'Voluntary Deductible'. This means you willingly agree to pay a higher fixed amount from your pocket during a claim (e.g., ₹10,000 instead of the standard ₹2,000). By taking on more upfront risk, the insurance company offers a substantial discount on your annual premium.

**Example 14: Driving Someone Else's Car**
**Customer (Hinglish):** Main apne dost ki car chala raha tha aur ek chhota accident ho gaya. Kya uska insurance meri galti cover karega?
**Agent (Hinglish):** Haan, motor insurance car par hota hai, driver par nahi. Kyunki aapke paas valid driving license tha aur aap unki permission se car chala rahe the, claim unki policy ke under hi process hoga. Lekin dhyan rahe, is accident ka direct asar unke aane wale No Claim Bonus (NCB) par padega.

**Example 15: Selling the car**
**Customer (Hindi):** मैंने अपनी पुरानी कार अभी बेच दी है। क्या मुझे अब अपना इंश्योरेंस कैंसिल करना होगा?
**Agent (Hindi):** सर, आपके पास दो विकल्प हैं। या तो पॉलिसी को नए मालिक के नाम पर ट्रांसफर कर सकते हैं (जिसके लिए उन्हें एक्स्ट्रा फीस देनी होगी), या फिर आप पॉलिसी कैंसिल कर सकते हैं (यदि नए मालिक ने अपना नया बीमा करवा लिया है)। अगर आप कैंसिल करते हैं, तो आप अपना NCB रिटेंशन लेटर ले सकते हैं, जिसे आप अपनी अगली नई कार के प्रीमियम को कम करने के लिए आसानी से इस्तेमाल कर सकते हैं।

## Government Services
- **mParivahan App & VAHAN Portal:** An incredible digital initiative by the Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH). Users can instantly verify the active insurance status, fitness validity, and exact details of any vehicle across India simply by entering the registration number.
- **DigiLocker:** Approved by the Government of India, DigiLocker allows all citizens to seamlessly store digital copies of their motor insurance policies, driving licenses, and RCs. Under the IT Act, these digital documents are legally recognized on par with physical documents during routine police or RTO checks.
- **Insurance Information Bureau (IIB):** A central repository mandated by IRDAI that rigorously tracks vehicle insurance history, accident records, and claim data centrally to prevent fraud and deeply assist law enforcement in tracing hit-and-run vehicles.
