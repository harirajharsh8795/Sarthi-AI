# Dormant Account (निष्क्रिय खाता)

## Overview

### English
A dormant account (also known as an inoperative account) is a bank account that has not seen any customer-induced transactions (deposits, withdrawals, transfers) for a continuous period of over two years. Banks flag these accounts to prevent fraud and require fresh KYC for reactivation.

### Hindi
निष्क्रिय खाता (Dormant Account / Inoperative Account) वह बैंक खाता है जिसमें लगातार दो वर्षों से अधिक समय से ग्राहक द्वारा कोई लेन-देन (जमा, निकासी, ट्रांसफर) नहीं किया गया है। बैंक धोखाधड़ी रोकने के लिए इन खातों को ब्लॉक कर देते हैं और इन्हें फिर से शुरू करने के लिए नए केवाईसी (KYC) की मांग करते हैं।

### Hinglish

## Quick Summary
When a bank account remains unused for a long time, it passes through two stages: "Inactive" and then "Dormant". If there are no customer-induced transactions for 12 months, the account is classified as Inactive. If the inactivity continues for more than 24 months, it is classified as Dormant or Inoperative. Once an account becomes dormant, digital banking services, debit card usage, ATM transactions, and chequebook clearance are suspended for security reasons.

However, interest on savings accounts continues to credit regularly even if the account is dormant. As per RBI rules, banks cannot charge fees to reactivate a dormant account. To reactivate it, the customer must submit fresh KYC documents (identity and address proof) at the branch and perform a transaction (such as a deposit or withdrawal).

---

## Definition
A Dormant Account is an inoperative financial ledger account at a bank where no customer-induced debit or credit transactions have occurred for a period exceeding 24 months, triggering regulatory restrictions on withdrawals and digital transactions to safeguard the account from unauthorized access.

---

## Why It Matters
Understanding dormant account rules is important because:
- **Fraud Protection:** Idle accounts are prime targets for cyber criminals and internal banking frauds; keeping them dormant limits unauthorized withdrawals.
- **Unclaimed Deposits:** Millions of rupees lie unclaimed in inoperative accounts in India. Knowing how to claim these funds helps heirs and depositors recover their money.
- **No Penalty Rights:** Customers should know that banks are prohibited from charging activation fees or minimum balance penalties on dormant accounts.

---

## How It Works
The classification and reactivation timeline:

Account has active transactions
↓
No customer transaction for 12 months → Flagged as "Inactive"
(Alert sent to customer; basic services still work)
↓
No customer transaction for 24 months → Flagged as "Dormant"
(Internet banking, ATM, and cheque clearing are blocked)
↓
Bank continues to credit savings interest regularly
↓
Customer submits fresh KYC documents at the home branch
↓
Bank reactivates the account (Zero charges)
↓
Customer performs a deposit/withdrawal transaction to keep it active

---

## Eligibility
- **Applicable Accounts:** Regular savings accounts, current accounts, and salary accounts.
- **Exemptions:** Accounts opened for government schemes (like PMJDY) are subject to special relaxed rules, but can still be flagged if completely unused.
- **System-generated transactions:** Automated interest credits or dividend deposits do not count as customer-induced transactions.

---

## Required Documents
To reactivate a dormant account, the customer must submit:
- **Reactivation Request Letter:** Written application to the branch manager.
- **Fresh KYC Documents (Identity & Address):** Self-attested copies of Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, or Passport.
- **Address Verification Proof:** Utility bill (Electricity/Gas) if the current address differs from the database.
- **Photographs:** Recent passport-sized color photographs.

---

## Features
- **Security Freeze:** Suspends outbound transactions to protect funds.
- **Interest Protection:** Savings accounts continue to accrue interest at prevailing rates.
- **Nominee Claims:** Nominees can claim funds from dormant/inoperative accounts of deceased holders following the claim process.

---

## Benefits
- **Zero Cost Reactivation:** Reactivation is entirely free of charge.
- **Secures Hard-Earned Money:** Temporary block prevents leakage of funds through duplicate cards or forged signatures.

---

## Risks
- **Inconvenience:** Transaction failures during emergencies if the customer tries to use a dormant debit card.
- **Unclaimed Fund Transfer:** If an account remains inoperative for 10 years or more, the funds are transferred to the RBI's Depositor Education and Awareness (DEA) Fund. However, the customer can still claim it back.

---

## Charges & Fees
- **Activation Fees:** Nil (RBI strictly prohibits charging customers for reactivating dormant accounts).
- **Minimum Balance Penalty:** Banks cannot levy penalties for non-maintenance of minimum balance on dormant or inoperative accounts.

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **No Penalties:** RBI Master Circular mandates that banks cannot charge for the activation of inoperative accounts.
- **Interest Payout:** Banks must credit interest on savings accounts regularly, regardless of the active or dormant status of the account.
- **DEA Fund Transfer:** Accounts with no transaction for 10 years are classified as "Unclaimed Deposits," and the balance is transferred to the RBI's Depositor Education and Awareness (DEA) Fund. The customer can search and claim this money via the RBI's **UDGAM** portal.

---

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Reactivate a Dormant Account:
1. **Locate the Branch:** Visit your home branch where the account was originally opened.
2. **Submit KYC Application:** Ask for the KYC update / Dormant Account Reactivation form.
3. **Fill Nomination & Details:** Fill the form and add/verify nominee details.
4. **Attach Documents:** Attach photocopies of your Aadhaar Card and PAN Card.
5. **In-Person Verification (IPV):** Present your original Aadhaar and PAN for verification by the bank official.
6. **Account Activation:** The bank will update your KYC and reactivate the account within 24 to 48 hours.
7. **Perform Transaction:** Perform a customer-induced transaction (deposit a small amount or withdraw cash) immediately to ensure it remains active.

---

## Safety Tips
- **Make a Small Transaction Yearly:** Perform at least one minor transaction (like a ₹10 UPI transfer or deposit) once a year to prevent the account from slipping into inactive status.
- **Update Contact Details:** Ensure your registered mobile number and email ID are active so you receive notifications before the account turns dormant.
- **Avoid Shared Devices for KYC:** Never click on links in SMS/Emails claiming to reactivate your account online (phishing); reactivation always requires official KYC verification.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Assuming Interest Stops:** Believing that interest will not accumulate on money in a dormant account.
- **Paying Reactivation Fees:** Paying cash to agents or bank staff who claim there is a fee for activating dormant accounts.
- **Ignoring Bank SMS Alerts:** Ignoring bank warnings sent via SMS/Email prior to the account turning inactive or dormant.
- **Forgetting to Settle Standing Instructions:** Forgetting that recurring bills linked to the account will fail once the account is flagged as dormant.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is a dormant account?** A bank account with no customer-induced transactions for more than 2 years.
2. **What is an inactive account?** A bank account with no customer-induced transactions for more than 12 months.
3. **What is a customer-induced transaction?** Any financial or non-financial transaction initiated by the customer, like cash deposit, ATM withdrawal, UPI transfer, or net banking login.
4. **Do automatic interest credits keep the account active?** No, automatic interest credits or bank fee debits are bank-induced transactions and do not prevent dormancy.
5. **Does the bank charge fees to reactivate a dormant account?** No, RBI rules prohibit banks from charging fees for reactivating dormant accounts.
6. **Can I use my debit card if my account is dormant?** No, debit cards and ATM withdrawals are blocked for safety.
7. **Will I lose my money if my account becomes dormant?** No, your money remains safe in the account.
8. **What is the UDGAM portal?** An RBI portal where citizens can search for their unclaimed deposits across multiple banks.
9. **What is the DEA Fund?** The Depositor Education and Awareness Fund of the RBI, where money from accounts unused for 10+ years is transferred.
10. **Can I claim my money back after it is transferred to the DEA Fund?** Yes, you can claim the full amount along with interest by submitting KYC to your bank.
11. **Do banks charge penalty for low balance on dormant accounts?** No, banks cannot levy penalties for not maintaining a minimum balance on dormant accounts.
12. **Can I transfer money into a dormant account?** Yes, inbound credits (like deposits or transfers) might be allowed, but you cannot withdraw or transfer money out until reactivated.
13. **How do I know if my account is dormant?** The bank will send notifications. You can also check by trying to check your balance online or visiting the branch.
14. **Can I reactivate a dormant account online?** Most banks require physical KYC verification at the branch, but some offer Video KYC options online.
15. **Does a salary account become dormant?** Yes, if salary credits stop and no other transaction is made for 2 years, it turns dormant.
16. **Can I write a check from a dormant account?** No, cheques issued from a dormant account will be dishonored by the bank.
17. **What is inoperative account?** It is another term used by the RBI for a dormant account.
18. **Can a joint account become dormant?** Yes, if none of the joint holders perform any transaction for 2 years.
19. **How much time does reactivation take?** Typically 24 to 48 hours after submitting KYC documents.
20. **Can a nominee reactivate a dormant account?** No, only the account holder can reactivate it. A nominee can only claim the funds after the holder's death.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** The bank owns the money in a dormant account. **Fact:** The money always belongs to the depositor or their legal heirs.
2. **Myth:** Banks stop paying interest on dormant savings accounts. **Fact:** Banks must credit interest regularly at savings account rates.
3. **Myth:** Reactivating a dormant account requires opening a new account. **Fact:** The same account number is restored after updating KYC.
4. **Myth:** Banks can charge a heavy penalty to reactivate the account. **Fact:** RBI guidelines strictly prohibit banks from charging any fee for reactivation.
5. **Myth:** If my money is sent to the DEA Fund, it is lost forever. **Fact:** You can claim it back from the bank at any time by proving ownership and submitting KYC.
6. **Myth:** Net banking login counts as a transaction to prevent dormancy. **Fact:** Simply logging in may not suffice; you should perform a transaction like a fund transfer or request a statement.
7. **Myth:** Checking account balance at an ATM prevents dormancy. **Fact:** Balance inquiry does not qualify as a customer-induced financial transaction for this purpose in all banks; a financial transaction (deposit/withdrawal) is safest.
8. **Myth:** Banks turn accounts dormant without notice. **Fact:** Banks are required to contact customers via SMS/Email at least 3 months before the account becomes inoperative.
9. **Myth:** Standing instructions (like SIPs) keep the account active. **Fact:** Yes, customer-induced standing instructions that result in transaction debit keep the account active.
10. **Myth:** I must go to the main head office of the bank to reactivate it. **Fact:** You can submit reactivation documents at any branch of your bank, though the home branch processes it faster.

    **Assistant:** Reactivate hone ke turant baad aapko ek chota transaction (jaise ₹10-20 ka deposit ya withdrawal) karna chahiye taaki system me wo active status me rahe.
