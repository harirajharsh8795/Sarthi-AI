# Current Account (चालू खाता)

## Overview

### English
A current account is a type of demand deposit account opened by businesses, companies, merchants, and entrepreneurs who require high-frequency financial transactions. Unlike savings accounts, it has no transaction limits and does not pay interest.

### Hindi
चालू खाता (Current Account) एक प्रकार का डिमांड डिपॉजिट खाता है जो व्यवसायों, कंपनियों, व्यापारियों और उद्यमियों द्वारा खोला जाता है जिन्हें बार-बार वित्तीय लेन-देन (high-frequency transactions) की आवश्यकता होती है। बचत खाते के विपरीत, इसमें लेन-देन की कोई सीमा नहीं होती है और इस पर कोई ब्याज नहीं मिलता है।

### Hinglish

## Quick Summary
A current account is tailored to handle the fluid operational needs of businesses. It allows unlimited deposits and withdrawals on a daily basis. Banks do not offer interest on the balance held in current accounts (as prohibited by RBI guidelines). Instead, banks offer features like Overdraft (OD), Cash Credit, bulk remittances, and specialized corporate payment gateways to facilitate trade.

To maintain a current account, banks mandate a higher Monthly Average Balance (MAB) compared to savings accounts. Failure to maintain this balance results in penalties. Opening a current account requires solid proof of business registration, tax documentation (GST/PAN), and adherence to RBI credit discipline guidelines to prevent multiple accounts by borrowers.

---

## Definition
A Current Account is a commercial bank account designed for business entities to execute unlimited financial receipts and payments. It acts as a liquid deposit facility that allows the account holder to withdraw funds on demand without prior notice, facilitating daily trade and commerce.

---

## Why It Matters
For any business operating in India, a current account is vital because:
- **No Transaction Cap:** Allows unlimited payments to suppliers and receipts from customers daily.
- **Legal Compliance:** Ensures business transactions are separated from personal finances, which is mandatory for tax audits, GST compliance, and partnership rules.
- **Access to Overdraft:** Businesses can borrow money temporarily beyond their account balance to manage short-term cash flow gaps.
- **Corporate Image:** Enhances credibility by allowing clients to pay in the name of the business entity (e.g., "M/S Traders") rather than an individual's personal name.

---

## How It Works
The standard current account operational cycle:

Business deposits cash or receives customer payments (UPI, NEFT, RTGS)
↓
Bank maintains zero-interest ledger balance
↓
Business makes unlimited payments to suppliers via cheques/online modes
↓
If balance is low, business utilizes pre-approved Overdraft facility
↓
Bank charges interest on the utilized Overdraft amount daily
↓
Business maintains the Monthly Average Balance (MAB) to avoid fees
↓
Periodic statement is sent to the business CA for accounting and tax audits

## Eligibility
- **Entities:** Proprietorship firms, Partnership firms, Private and Public Limited Companies, LLPs, Trusts, Societies, Associations, and Sole Proprietors.
- **Individuals:** Individual business owners, professionals (doctors, CAs, lawyers) with valid business registrations.
- **RBI Restrictions:** Businesses that have cash credit (CC) or overdraft (OD) facilities with any bank must open their current account primarily with the lending bank to ensure credit discipline.

---

## Required Documents
- **Identity & Address Proof of Promoters:** Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Passport.
- **Proprietorship Firm Proofs (Any Two):**
  - GST Registration Certificate.
  - Shop and Establishment License.
  - License issued by Registering Authority (like ICAI, Medical Council).
  - Utility bills (Electricity/Water) in the name of the firm.
- **Partnership Firm:** Partnership Deed, Partnership Registration Certificate, and PAN of the firm.
- **Companies:** Certificate of Incorporation, Memorandum & Articles of Association (MOA & AOA), Board Resolution, and PAN of the company.
- **Common requirement:** PAN of the business entity is mandatory for opening a current account.

---

## Features
- **Zero Interest:** No interest is paid on credit balances (as per RBI regulations).
- **Overdraft Facility:** Borrowing limit allowed against deposits, property, or business turnover.
- **Cheque Facility:** Issuance of specialized business cheques and multicity cheque facilities.
- **Bulk Payments:** Access to corporate internet banking for bulk salary payouts, vendor transfers, and tax payments.

---

## Benefits
- **Liquidity Management:** Instant access to funds for daily purchases.
- **Trade Facilitation:** Support for Bank Guarantees (BG) and Letters of Credit (LC) for national and international trade.
- **Payment Collection:** Direct integration with POS terminals, payment gateways, and QR codes.

---

## Risks
- **Higher Minimum Balance:** Requires keeping significant cash idle to avoid penalties.
- **Overdraft Overuse Risk:** Accumulating high debt due to excessive utilization of the overdraft limit, leading to interest expenses.
- **Cyber Fraud Risk:** High-value corporate transactions are targets for phishing, business email compromise (BEC), and corporate credential thefts.

---

## Charges & Fees
Typical charges (subject to bank-specific structures and RBI regulations):
- **Monthly Average Balance (MAB) Penalty:** Fees charged if average balance falls below target (ranges from ₹5,000 to ₹1,00,000 depending on the bank and account variant).
- **Cash Deposit Charges:** Banks allow free cash deposits up to a limit per month; deposits beyond that attract charges (e.g., ₹3 to ₹5 per ₹1000).
- **Cheque Bounce Fee:** Penalties for dishonored outbound or inbound cheques.
- **Online Transaction Fees:** Minimal fees for bulk NEFT, RTGS, or IMPS transactions.

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **No Interest Payouts:** RBI strictly prohibits banks from paying interest on current account credit balances.
- **Credit Discipline Circular (2020/2021):** RBI mandates that no bank can open a current account for customers who have availed cash credit (CC) or overdraft (OD) facilities from other banks, unless they fall under specific exposure thresholds (under ₹5 Crore, ₹5 to ₹50 Crore, etc.) to prevent diversion of funds.
- **GST Linking:** Current account transactions are mapped against the GST returns of the business during tax assessments.

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Open a Current Account:
1. **Choose Bank & Variant:** Select a bank and a current account variant (Silver, Gold, Platinum) matching your expected monthly cash transaction volume.
2. **Compile Business Proofs:** Gather your GST registration, trade licenses, PAN card, and identity proofs.
3. **Fill Application Form:** Fill the corporate/current account opening form.
4. **Obtain Board Resolution/Partnership Consent:** For companies and partnerships, get the signed consent from partners or directors.
5. **KYC Verification:** Present original documents to the bank's relationship manager.
6. **Deposit Initial Funds:** Deposit the initial required minimum balance.
7. **Activate Corporate Banking:** Set up corporate internet banking with multiple transaction authorizers for security.

---

## Safety Tips
- **Dual Authorization:** Enable multi-level approval (maker-checker system) for corporate online transactions to prevent single-point fraud.
- **Link GST Correctly:** Ensure your current account is linked with your GST portal to avoid tax mismatches.
- **Regular Audit:** Review account transaction logs weekly to spot unauthorized payments or duplicate debits.
- **Physical Check Security:** Keep chequebooks locked and never sign blank cheques.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Using Personal Account for Business:** Continuing to use a personal savings account for business transactions, which violates RBI rules and leads to tax scrutiny.
- **Ignoring Credit Facility Restrictions:** Opening a current account with a new bank without disclosing your active cash credit (CC) facility at another bank, leading to account freezing.
- **Not Monitoring MAB:** Keeping low balances in current accounts with high MAB requirements, leading to heavy monthly penalties.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is a current account?** A business bank account meant for high-volume daily transactions with no limits on transaction numbers.
2. **Can an individual open a current account?** Yes, sole proprietors and professionals can open current accounts in their business name.
3. **Why is interest not paid on current accounts?** RBI guidelines prohibit banks from paying interest on current accounts to maintain banking liquidity structures.
4. **What is the minimum balance for a current account?** It varies, usually starting from ₹5,000 in semi-urban areas to ₹20,000 or more in metros.
5. **What is an overdraft facility?** A feature allowing you to withdraw money beyond your actual account balance up to a pre-approved limit.
6. **What is the difference between savings and current accounts?** Savings account is for personal savings and earns interest; current account is for business transactions, has higher transaction capacity, and pays no interest.
7. **Is a GST certificate mandatory to open a current account?** Yes, for businesses, GST or equivalent registration proofs are required under KYC rules.
8. **Can I convert my savings account to a current account?** No, you must open a new current account and close the savings account if needed.
9. **What are cash deposit limits in current accounts?** Banks provide a free cash deposit limit per month; transactions above this limit attract fees.
10. **Can I get a debit card with a current account?** Yes, banks issue business debit cards with higher daily withdrawal and spending limits.
11. **What is the RBI credit discipline circular?** A rule preventing borrowers from opening current accounts in multiple banks to hide cash flows.
12. **What is a proprietorship account?** A current account opened in the name of a business owned by a single individual.
13. **Can I open a current account with zero balance?** Yes, some banks offer zero-balance current accounts for startups for a initial promotional period.
14. **What is a partnership current account?** An account opened for a business run by two or more partners.
15. **What is the transaction limit in current accounts?** There is no limit on transaction counts, though limit on cash handling may attract fees.
16. **Are current account deposits covered by DICGC?** Yes, current account deposits are insured up to ₹5 Lakhs per customer per bank.
17. **What is a board resolution?** A formal document signed by company directors authorizing the opening of a bank account.
18. **Can a payments bank open current accounts?** Yes, payments banks can open current accounts for small merchants and proprietorships.
19. **What is Cash Credit (CC)?** A loan facility where businesses withdraw funds up to a limit based on their inventory and receivables.
20. **Who is a nominee in a current account?** Nominees are allowed only for sole proprietorship current accounts, not for partnerships or corporate accounts.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Current accounts are only for big companies. **Fact:** Any small shopkeeper, vendor, CA, or freelancer can open a current account.
2. **Myth:** Overdraft is free. **Fact:** You pay interest on the exact amount and number of days you utilize the overdraft facility.
3. **Myth:** Current accounts have unlimited free cash deposits. **Fact:** While transaction counts are unlimited, physical cash deposit limits are capped, and excess deposits attract fees.
4. **Myth:** You can hide cash transactions using a current account. **Fact:** Current accounts are linked to PAN and GST, making all transactions visible to tax authorities.
5. **Myth:** You can open a current account anywhere regardless of loans. **Fact:** RBI rules prohibit opening current accounts in banks other than your lending bank.
6. **Myth:** Any partner can close a partnership account. **Fact:** Account closure usually requires consent and signatures of all partners.
7. **Myth:** If you maintain the minimum balance, all services are free. **Fact:** Service charges still apply for transactions like outstation cheques, SMS alerts, and cash deposits.
8. **Myth:** Payments banks cannot open current accounts. **Fact:** Payments banks are allowed to open current accounts for small merchants.
9. **Myth:** Business accounts cannot receive international transfers. **Fact:** Current accounts can receive international trade payments, subject to FEMA rules.
10. **Myth:** Current accounts cannot be frozen. **Fact:** Like any account, current accounts can be frozen for tax non-compliance, KYC default, or suspicious transactions.

## Conversation Examples
1. **User:** Bhaiya, shop ke naam par account kholna hai, kaun sa sahi rahega?
   **Assistant:** Aapko Current Account (चालू खाता) kholna chahiye. Ye business transactions ke liye hota hai aur isme transaction ki koi limit nahi hoti.
2. **User:** Kya current account me interest milta hai?
   **Assistant:** Nahi, RBI ke niyam ke mutabik current account me koi interest nahi milta kyunki ye business transactions ke liye humesha liquid rehta hai.
3. **User:** Minimum balance kitna maintain karna hoga?
   **Assistant:** Ye bank par aur account variant par depend karta hai. Aamtaur par ye ₹5,000 se lekar ₹20,000 ya usse zyada bhi ho sakta hai.
4. **User:** Overdraft limit kya hoti hai?
   **Assistant:** Overdraft ek facility hai jisme aap bank balance khatam hone ke baad bhi ek limit tak paise nikal sakte hain. Is par aapko daily basis par interest dena hota hai jitna paisa aap use karte hain.
5. **User:** Mere pass GST certificate nahi hai, kya account khulega?
   **Assistant:** Agar GST mandatory nahi hai toh aap dusre business proofs jaise Shop Act licence, MSME registration ya Trade licence dekar account khol sakte hain.
6. **User:** Main ek savings account ko business ke liye use kar raha hu, kya dikkat hai?
   **Assistant:** Savings account ko business ke liye use karne par Income Tax Department ki taraf se scrutiny ho sakti hai aur RBI rules ke tehat bank account freeze bhi ho sakta hai.
7. **User:** RBI ka current account rule kya hai?
   **Assistant:** RBI ke rule ke mutabik agar aapne kisi bank se cash credit (CC) ya overdraft (OD) facility li hui hai, toh aapko apna current account usi bank me rakhna hoga.
8. **User:** Kya current account se international payment kar sakte hain?
   **Assistant:** Haan, business trade ke liye current account se foreign trade payments kiye ja sakte hain, lekin isme FEMA aur RBI ki trade guidelines follow karni hoti hain.
9. **User:** Company account chalane ke liye signature kiske lagenge?
   **Assistant:** Board resolution me jin directors ya officials ko sign karne ke liye authorize kiya gaya hoga, wahi transaction kar sakte hain.
10. **User:** Maker-Checker system kya hai?
    **Assistant:** Ye ek safety system hai jisme ek vyakti payment request create karta hai (maker) aur doosra vyakti use approve karta hai (checker). Isse fraud nahi hota.
11. **User:** Current account me cash jama karne ka koi charge lagta hai kya?
    **Assistant:** Haan, har bank ek limit tak free cash deposit deta hai (jaise ₹2 Lakh per month), uske baad deposit karne par nominal charge lagta hai.
12. **User:** Partnership firm ka account kaise close karein?
    **Assistant:** Partnership firm ka account close karne ke liye sabhi partners ke signed authorization letter aur signature lagte hain.
13. **User:** Payments bank current account de sakte hain?
    **Assistant:** Haan, payments banks small merchants aur local shopkeepers ke liye current account open kar sakte hain.
14. **User:** Kya current account balance safe hai?
    **Assistant:** Haan, DICGC ke rules ke mutabik bank fail hone par current account deposit par bhi ₹5 Lakh tak ki safety insurance guaranteed hai.
15. **User:** Mujhe daily limit badhani hai, kya karein?
    **Assistant:** Aap apne bank se contact karke higher current account variant choose kar sakte hain ya transaction limits badhane ke liye manual request de sakte hain.
