## Quick Summary
The Saarthi AI Banking Knowledge Base is designed to improve financial literacy among Indian citizens by simplifying banking operations, central bank rules, digital payment procedures, and fraud prevention methods. However, financial markets, interest parameters, banking fees, and RBI policies are highly dynamic and subject to frequent updates. Thus, the information presented in these documents might not always reflect the most real-time changes of individual banking institutions.

Therefore, users must treat this corpus as a learning tool rather than an absolute regulatory or operational authority. For any active financial decision—such as applying for a loan, investing in a scheme, filing a formal legal complaint, or registering a dispute—citizens must consult their respective bank branch managers, certified financial planners, or check the official, updated notifications on the RBI and government portal websites.

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Verify Banking Information:
1. **Read Article:** Study the topic in the Saarthi AI database to understand the basic mechanism.
2. **Visit Official Site:** Go to the official website of the target bank (e.g., sbi.co.in) or the Reserve Bank of India (rbi.org.in).
3. **Download Interest Sheet:** Check the latest Interest Rate and Service Charges PDF, usually updated monthly.
4. **Consult Branch Executive:** Visit the nearest branch to confirm if any local or promotional rates apply to your profile.
5. **Finalize Deal:** Ensure the final loan agreement or deposit receipt matches the rates verified in step 3 and 4.

## Safety Tips
- **Verify URL Security:** Only check bank details on websites starting with `https://` and displaying a lock icon in the browser address bar.
- **Ignore Unofficial Promoters:** No bank executive or Saarthi AI representative will contact you asking to process loans based on this offline database.
- **Never Pay Cash to Verify:** Official verification of bank rules or downloading RBI circulars is entirely free.

## Common Mistakes
- **Assuming Rates are Identical:** Believing all banks offer the exact same interest rates on deposits or loans.
- **Neglecting to Read Agreements:** Signing bank agreements assuming they exactly match the generic templates in this database.
- **Assuming Subsidies are Guaranteed:** Thinking that qualifying for a government scheme (like PMAY) is guaranteed, without checking the specific bank's evaluation criteria.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** This database contains binding legal commitments from banks. **Fact:** This database is only a guide; legal contracts are signed directly with the bank.
2. **Myth:** If I follow the step-by-step process here, my bank cannot reject my application. **Fact:** Lenders have internal credit check parameters and retain the right to reject applications.
3. **Myth:** Savings interest rates are the same everywhere. **Fact:** Interest rates vary significantly between public, private, and cooperative banks.
4. **Myth:** If a rate is listed here, it is valid today. **Fact:** Rates change regularly; you must check with the bank for the current day's rates.
5. **Myth:** The RBI forces all banks to have the same processing fees. **Fact:** Banks are free to set their own processing fees within general fair practice limits.
6. **Myth:** This disclaimer protects me from bank frauds. **Fact:** This disclaimer outlines limits of liability; you must actively follow safety tips to prevent fraud.
7. **Myth:** Saarthi AI is affiliated with the RBI. **Fact:** Saarthi AI is an independent software tool designed for literacy and is not affiliated with the RBI.
8. **Myth:** Legal actions can be initiated using this text. **Fact:** Only official acts, circulars, and signed agreements are admissible in consumer courts.
9. **Myth:** All government schemes mentioned here are active. **Fact:** Some schemes may have ceased or been modified; check the respective ministry portal.
10. **Myth:** Bank managers must agree with the contents of this app. **Fact:** Bank managers are bound by their bank's official circulars and policies, not external apps.
