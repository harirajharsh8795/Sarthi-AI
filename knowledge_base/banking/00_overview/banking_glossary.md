## Quick Summary
The financial sector employs a specific vocabulary to describe transactions, monetary policies, risks, and regulatory frameworks. Words like "Liquidity", "NPA", "Collateral", "Amortization", and "Spread" frequently appear in bank statements, loan offers, and news reports. Without a clear understanding of these terms, individuals can easily make erroneous financial decisions, sign unfavorable contracts, or misunderstand interest computations.

This glossary serves as a comprehensive reference guide to simplify those definitions. By bridging the gap between professional financial terminology and common languages (English, Hindi, and Hinglish), it empowers users to navigate banks, NBFCs, and digital payment networks confidently and safely.

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Decode Your Bank Agreement:
1. **Locate Key Terms:** Find terms like "Rate of Interest", "Tenure", "Processing Fees", and "Prepayment Charges" in your agreement.
2. **Consult Glossary:** Look up these terms in this glossary or bank's Key Fact Statement.
3. **Compare Options:** Verify if the bank's terms match the standard definitions defined by the RBI.
4. **Clarify with Branch:** If a term is unclear, ask the branch manager to explain it in writing before signing.

---

## Safety Tips
- **Beware of "Zero Interest" Claims:** Always check the processing fee and hidden charges, which often offset the zero-interest claim.
- **Clarify "Pre-approved" Loans:** Verify if a pre-approved loan has processing fees and requires formal documentation, as it is often just a marketing term.
- **Never Share CVV/PIN:** No glossary term or banking protocol requires sharing your CVV or PIN with bank staff or online representatives.

## Common Mistakes
- **Confusing Floating and Fixed Rates:** Assuming fixed rates stay the same forever, whereas some contracts have a clause allowing revision after a few years.
- **Equating Nominee with Owner:** Assuming the nominee becomes the sole owner of the funds after the depositor's death.
- **Ignoring "Default" Definition:** Not knowing that missing even one EMI is classified as a default in credit records.
- **Underestimating "Processing Fees":** Focusing only on interest rates and ignoring high upfront processing or administrative fees.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is an Asset in banking?** It is anything owned by the bank that has value, such as loans given to customers.
2. **What is a Liability in banking?** It is what the bank owes to others, primarily the deposits made by customers.
3. **What is a Non-Performing Asset (NPA)?** A loan where the interest or principal payment remains overdue for more than 90 days.
4. **What is Repo Rate?** The rate at which the RBI lends money to commercial banks.
5. **What is Reverse Repo Rate?** The rate at which the RBI borrows money from commercial banks.
6. **What is Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)?** The percentage of deposits that banks must keep with the RBI in cash.
7. **What is Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR)?** The percentage of deposits that banks must invest in government securities.
8. **What is CIBIL Score?** A 3-digit score showing credit history; higher is better.
9. **What is an EMI?** Equated Monthly Instalment, the fixed monthly payment made by a borrower to the bank.
10. **What is a Moratorium?** A temporary suspension of EMI payments allowed by banks under special circumstances.
11. **What is Collateral?** An asset (like property or gold) pledged by a borrower to secure a loan.
12. **What is Amortization?** The process of spreading out a loan into a series of periodic payments (EMIs) over time.
13. **What is a Lien?** A legal right of the bank to keep possession of a customer's property or funds until a debt is paid.
14. **What is KYC?** Know Your Customer; standard identity and address verification.
15. **What is Net Interest Margin (NIM)?** The difference between interest earned from loans and interest paid to depositors.
16. **What is a No Dues Certificate (NDC)?** A document issued by the bank confirming that a loan has been fully paid off.
17. **What is Foreclosure?** Closing a loan before its tenure by paying off the entire outstanding amount.
18. **What is a Demand Draft (DD)?** A secure payment instrument issued by a bank directing another branch to pay a sum to the payee.
19. **What is a Joint Account?** A bank account opened and operated by two or more individuals.
20. **What is DICGC?** Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation, which insures deposits up to ₹5 Lakhs.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** NPA means the bank has lost all the money. **Fact:** NPA is a classification of delayed payment; banks can still recover the money through legal processes or security auctions.
2. **Myth:** "Liquidity" refers to the cash in the bank's ATM only. **Fact:** Liquidity refers to the bank's total ability to meet its immediate financial obligations using cash or easily convertible assets.
3. **Myth:** Pre-EMI is the same as regular EMI. **Fact:** Pre-EMI is only the interest component paid during construction; it does not reduce the principal loan amount.
4. **Myth:** "Default" means you go to jail immediately. **Fact:** Default is a civil matter; banks follow a legal recovery process before taking extreme actions.
5. **Myth:** Repo rate cuts automatically reduce fixed deposit rates immediately. **Fact:** Banks adjust deposit rates based on their overall asset-liability management, not just repo rate cuts.
6. **Myth:** "Solvency" is the same as "Liquidity". **Fact:** Solvency is the ability to pay long-term debts, while liquidity is the ability to meet short-term obligations.
7. **Myth:** "Unsecured loan" means the borrower does not have to repay it. **Fact:** It only means the bank does not hold collateral; the borrower is still legally obligated to repay.
8. **Myth:** Bank "spread" is the interest rate on a savings account. **Fact:** Spread is the difference between interest charged on loans and interest paid on deposits.
9. **Myth:** "MODT" is a processing fee. **Fact:** MODT is a government stamp duty charge for registering the mortgage of title deeds.
10. **Myth:** KYC needs to be done only once in a lifetime. **Fact:** KYC must be updated periodically based on the customer's risk profile (low, medium, high).

## Conversation Examples
1. **User:** NPA kya hota hai bhaiya?
   **Assistant:** NPA ka matlab hai Non-Performing Asset. Jab koi vyakti bank se loan leta hai aur 90 din tak uski EMI ya interest nahi chukata, toh bank us loan account ko NPA ghoshit kar deta hai.
2. **User:** Bank manager bol raha hai ki collateral chahiye. Ye kya hai?
   **Assistant:** Collateral wo property, gold ya fixed deposit hota hai jise aap loan secure karne ke liye bank ke paas girvi rakhte hain. Agar aap loan nahi chuka paate, toh bank ise bechkar apna paisa recover karta hai.
3. **User:** Repo Rate badhne se mere loan par kya asar padega?
   **Assistant:** Repo rate badhne se bankon ke liye RBI se paisa lena mehanga ho jata hai. Is wajah se aapke loan ki interest rate aur EMI ya tenure badh sakti hai.
4. **User:** CRR aur SLR me kya farq hai?
   **Assistant:** CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio) cash ke roop me RBI ke paas rakhna hota hai, jabki SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio) banks ko government securities ya gold me invest karke khud ke paas rakhna hota hai.
5. **User:** Moratorium kya hota hai? Kya isme interest maaf hota hai?
   **Assistant:** Moratorium ek temporary period hota hai jisme aapko EMI dene se chhoot milti hai (jaise lockdown ya flood ke waqt). Isme interest maaf nahi hota, balki wo aapke loan balance me judta jata hai.
6. **User:** No Dues Certificate kab lena chahiye?
   **Assistant:** Jab aapka loan poora chuka diya jaye, tab aapko bank se No Dues Certificate (NDC) zaroor lena chahiye. Ye saboot hai ki aap par bank ka koi karz nahi hai.
7. **User:** Amortization table kya hota hai?
   **Assistant:** Ye ek schedule hota hai jo dikhata hai ki aapke har mahine ki EMI me kitna paisa interest me ja raha hai aur kitna principal amount ko kam kar raha hai.
8. **User:** Foreclosure aur prepayment me kya farq hai?
   **Assistant:** Foreclosure ka matlab hai pura loan ek sath chukakar band karna. Prepayment ka matlab hai loan chalte waqt beech-beech me kuch bada amount chukana taaki principal kam ho sake.
9. **User:** Spread kya hota hai?
   **Assistant:** Bank jis rate par loan dete hain (jaise 10%) aur jis rate par deposit par interest dete hain (jaise 4%), un dono ke farq ko Spread bolte hain. Ye bank ki kamai ka hissa hota.
10. **User:** Liquidity crunch ka kya matlab hai?
    **Assistant:** Jab bank ya market me immediate cash flow ki kami ho jati hai, toh use liquidity crunch kehte hain. Isse aam logon ko loan milna mushkil ho sakta hai.
11. **User:** Credit appraisal kya hota hai?
    **Assistant:** Loan dene se pehle bank jo aapki income, credit history aur repayment capacity ki checking karta hai, use credit appraisal kehte hain.
12. **User:** Demand Draft bank cheque se alag kaise hai?
    **Assistant:** Cheque bounce ho sakta hai agar account me paise na hon, par Demand Draft (DD) kabhi bounce nahi hota kyunki iske paise bank pehle hi jama karwa leta hai.
13. **User:** Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR) badhne se kya hoga?
    **Assistant:** SLR badhne se bankon ko government bonds me zyada paisa lagana padta hai, jisse unke paas aam logon ko loan dene ke liye kam paisa bachta hai.
14. **User:** EBLR kya hai?
    **Assistant:** EBLR (External Benchmark Linked Lending Rate) retail loans ka wo interest rate hai jo seedhe RBI ke Repo rate jaise external standards se juda hota hai, jisse rates transparent rehte hain.
15. **User:** Base Rate aur MCLR kya the?
    **Assistant:** Ye purane interest rate benchmarks the jisse banks loan rates tay karte the. Ab inki jagah EBLR ne le li hai, jo zyada transparent hai.
