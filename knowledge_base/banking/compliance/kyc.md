# Know Your Customer (KYC) Guidelines and Procedures

## Overview

**English:** Know Your Customer (KYC) is a mandatory compliance process enforced by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) that requires banks and financial institutions to verify the identity and address of their customers. This ensures that the financial system is not misused for money laundering, terrorism financing, or fraudulent activities. It acts as the foundational step for any customer onboarding and continuous financial engagement.

## Quick Summary
- **Purpose:** To verify customer identity, assess risk, and prevent financial crimes.
- **Mandatory for:** All individuals and entities engaging in financial transactions, opening accounts, or availing credit.
- **Types of KYC:** Physical KYC, e-KYC (Aadhaar-based OTP or Biometric), Video KYC (V-CIP), and C-KYC (Centralized KYC).
- **Update Frequency (Re-KYC):** Every 2 years for High-Risk, 8 years for Medium-Risk, and 10 years for Low-Risk customers.
- **Regulator:** Reserve Bank of India (RBI) under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA).

---

## Definition
KYC, or Know Your Customer, refers to the rigorous process of verifying the identity of the clients and assessing their suitability, along with the potential risks of illegal intentions towards the business relationship. In banking, KYC forms the core of Customer Due Diligence (CDD), ensuring institutions know exactly who is using their services, where their money comes from, and whether their financial behavior aligns with their declared profile.

---

## Why It Matters
KYC is not merely a bureaucratic hurdle; it is the primary defense mechanism of the global and domestic financial ecosystem. It matters because:
1. **Fraud Prevention:** By verifying identities with government-backed documents, banks can prevent identity theft and impersonation.
2. **Anti-Money Laundering (AML):** It ensures that black money or ill-gotten wealth is not legitimized through the formal banking sector.
3. **Combating Financing of Terrorism (CFT):** Restricts the flow of funds to extremist groups and illegal organizations.
4. **Regulatory Compliance:** Failure to adhere to KYC norms leads to severe penalties from the RBI and potential license cancellation for the institution.
5. **Customer Trust and Security:** A robust KYC framework assures customers that their institution is safe, secure, and resilient against systemic risks.

## Eligibility
KYC is a universal requirement for anyone attempting to interact with the formal financial system in India. 
- **Resident Individuals:** Must provide proof of identity and address (e.g., Aadhaar, PAN).
- **Non-Resident Indians (NRIs):** Must provide passport copies, foreign address proofs, and visa details.
- **Minors:** KYC of the natural or legal guardian is required. Minors above 10 operating self-accounts must also provide their KYC.
- **Companies/Corporates:** Certificate of Incorporation, Memorandum of Association (MoA), Articles of Association (AoA), Board Resolution, and KYC of Directors/Beneficial Owners.
- **Trusts & Societies:** Trust deed, registration certificate, and KYC of trustees.
- **Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs):** Require enhanced due diligence and senior management approval.

---

## Required Documents
To complete the KYC process, customers must submit Officially Valid Documents (OVDs). 
**Proof of Identity (PoI) and Proof of Address (PoA) (Any one of the following 6 OVDs):**
1. **Passport:** Must be valid and not expired. Serves as both PoI and PoA.
2. **Driving License:** Must be valid. Serves as both PoI and PoA.
3. **Voter's Identity Card:** Issued by the Election Commission of India. Serves as both PoI and PoA.
4. **PAN Card:** Mandatory for all financial transactions above ₹50,000. Serves ONLY as Proof of Identity.
5. **Aadhaar Card:** Can be authenticated offline (XML) or online (OTP/Biometric). Serves as both PoI and PoA.
6. **NREGA Job Card:** Duly signed by an officer of the State Government.
7. **Letter issued by the National Population Register (NPR):** Containing details of name and address.

*Note: If the updated address is not present in the OVD, utility bills (electricity, water, gas) not older than two months can be accepted temporarily, but an updated OVD must be submitted within 3 months.*

## Features & Benefits
1. **Centralized KYC (C-KYC):** Customers undergo KYC only once across the entire financial sector. The CERSAI registry issues a 14-digit CKYC number, which can be quoted to open accounts with any other bank, mutual fund, or insurance company without resubmitting documents.
2. **Video Customer Identification Process (V-CIP):** A paperless, contactless, and fully digital onboarding process. Customers can complete their full KYC from the comfort of their homes via a secure video call with a bank official, utilizing geo-tagging and facial recognition.
3. **Tiered e-KYC System:** OTP-based Aadhaar e-KYC allows customers to open limited-functionality accounts instantly (e.g., maximum balance of ₹1 Lakh). Full biometric authentication or V-CIP upgrades this to a normal account, providing seamless digital access.
4. **Enhanced Financial Security:** By establishing a solid paper trail and digital footprint, KYC protects customers against unauthorized transactions and identity theft, creating a safer banking environment.
5. **Dynamic Risk Profiling:** The KYC system automatically categorizes customers into Low, Medium, or High risk based on their transaction patterns and background, allowing banks to tailor monitoring processes efficiently.

## Risks

**Financial Risks:**
Failure to complete KYC or submitting forged documents can lead to account freezing, freezing of funds, and inability to perform any financial transactions. If a customer is flagged for suspicious activities due to mismatched KYC data, their funds might be locked under AML protocols indefinitely.

**Technical Risks:**
During digital or Video KYC, poor internet connectivity, system downtimes at UIDAI (Aadhaar) servers, or browser incompatibilities can cause the application process to fail midway, requiring the customer to restart the procedure or visit a physical branch. 

**Legal Risks:**
Providing false information, using another person's documents, or acting as a money mule constitutes a criminal offense under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA). This can result in severe legal action, imprisonment, and heavy fines.

**Cyber Risks:**
Phishing scams often masquerade as "KYC Update" requests. Fraudsters send malicious links via SMS or WhatsApp, prompting customers to enter their credentials or download screen-sharing apps, leading to complete bank account drain. True KYC is strictly done via official banking apps or portals.

## Charges & Fees
- **KYC Registration / Onboarding:** Free of charge (Banks cannot charge for updating or collecting KYC documents).
- **C-KYC Registration:** Free for the customer. The financial institution absorbs the nominal cost of uploading data to the CERSAI registry.
- **e-KYC / Video KYC:** Completely free of charge for the customer.
- **Penalty for Non-Compliance:** While banks do not levy a specific monetary "fee" for not doing KYC, the account will be placed under a debit freeze (no withdrawals allowed) or closed entirely if KYC remains pending despite repeated notices.

## RBI / Government Rules
1. **Master Direction – KYC Direction, 2016:** RBI mandates all regulated entities to formulate a KYC policy incorporating Customer Acceptance Policy, Customer Identification Procedures, Monitoring of Transactions, and Risk Management.
2. **Periodic Updation of KYC (Re-KYC):** RBI mandates updating KYC periodically. High-risk customers every 2 years, Medium-risk every 8 years, and Low-risk every 10 years. If there is no change in status, a self-declaration via non-face-to-face channels (email, net banking, ATM) is sufficient.
3. **PAN-Aadhaar Linking:** The Government of India mandates linking PAN with Aadhaar. Failure to do so makes the PAN inoperative, restricting the customer from executing high-value transactions and opening new accounts.
4. **Aadhaar Masking:** Under UIDAI guidelines, banks collecting physical copies of Aadhaar cards must ensure that the first 8 digits of the Aadhaar number are masked/blacked out for security purposes.

## Step-by-Step Process

**Process for Video KYC (V-CIP):**
1. **Initiate Application:** The customer visits the bank's official website or app, fills in basic details, and enters PAN and Aadhaar numbers.
2. **Aadhaar Authentication:** An OTP is sent to the mobile number linked to Aadhaar. The customer inputs this OTP to fetch details via UIDAI.
3. **Agent Connection:** The customer is connected to a bank agent via a secure video link. 
4. **Location & Consent:** The customer grants camera, microphone, and location access. The system captures the live geolocation to ensure the customer is physically present in India.
5. **Document Capture:** The agent prompts the customer to show their original physical PAN card to the camera, which is captured via OCR.
6. **Liveness Check & Photo:** A live photograph of the customer is captured. Facial matching software compares this photo with the Aadhaar database photo.
7. **Verification & Activation:** The agent confirms the details, and the account is upgraded to a full KYC account within hours.

**Process for Physical Re-KYC (Address Change):**
1. **Visit Branch:** Customer visits the home branch or any nearest branch.
2. **Submit Form:** Fill out the Re-KYC form indicating the new address.
3. **Provide OVD:** Submit a self-attested photocopy of the new Proof of Address (e.g., Voter ID, Passport).
4. **Original Verification:** Show the original document to the bank official for 'Original Seen and Verified' (OSV) stamping.
5. **Processing:** The bank updates the core banking system and CKYC registry within 2-3 working days.

## Safety Tips
1. **Never Click Unverified Links:** Do not click on SMS or WhatsApp links claiming your account will be blocked without immediate KYC verification. 
2. **Avoid Screen Sharing Apps:** Never download apps like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or QuickSupport for KYC processes. Bank officials will never ask you to install these.
3. **Use Official Channels:** Always use the bank's official mobile banking app, verified internet banking portal, or visit a physical branch for KYC updates.
4. **Do Not Share OTPs:** OTPs are for your authorization only. Do not share Aadhaar OTPs or Bank OTPs with anyone claiming to be a KYC executive over a phone call.
5. **Mask Aadhaar:** When providing physical copies of your Aadhaar card for KYC, always mask the first 8 digits of your Aadhaar number to prevent misuse.

---

## Common Mistakes
1. **Ignoring Re-KYC Notices:** Customers often ignore SMS and email reminders to update their KYC, leading to sudden account freezes and immense inconvenience when they urgently need funds.
2. **Providing Unacceptable Documents:** Submitting private rent agreements or unverified affidavits as Proof of Address. Only specific state-registered documents or OVDs are accepted.
3. **Mismatch in Names:** Having a different name spelling on the PAN card versus the Aadhaar card. This causes API failures during digital onboarding and delays account opening.
4. **Using Expired Documents:** Submitting an expired passport or a driving license that has passed its validity date for KYC purposes.
5. **Not Updating Mobile Numbers:** Failing to link the current active mobile number with Aadhaar. e-KYC and Video KYC cannot proceed without Aadhaar OTP authentication.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What happens if I do not update my KYC?**
If you fail to update your KYC despite repeated reminders, the bank is legally obligated to freeze your account. Initially, a partial freeze (debit freeze) is applied where you cannot withdraw funds. Eventually, the account may be completely blocked or closed.

**2. Can I do my KYC completely online?**
Yes. You can complete your KYC entirely online using the Video Customer Identification Process (V-CIP) or Aadhaar OTP-based e-KYC. Video KYC provides you with a full-fledged account without any transaction limits.

**3. Is PAN mandatory for KYC?**
Yes, PAN (Permanent Account Number) or Form 60 is mandatory for establishing a bank account and conducting financial transactions. For transactions exceeding ₹50,000, PAN is strictly required.

**4. What is CKYC and how is it different from normal KYC?**
Central KYC (CKYC) is a centralized repository of KYC records maintained by CERSAI. Once your KYC is submitted to the CKYC registry, you are assigned a 14-digit CKYC number. You can use this number to open accounts with other financial institutions without resubmitting documents.

**5. Can I use a rent agreement as an address proof for KYC?**
A standard unregistered rent agreement is not considered an Officially Valid Document (OVD). However, a registered lease or rent agreement is acceptable by some institutions under specific conditional guidelines, usually accompanied by a utility bill.

**6. My Aadhaar address is old. Can I still open an account?**
Yes. You can open an account using your Aadhaar for identity, and submit a self-declaration of your current address, provided you submit an OVD with the updated address within a grace period (usually 3 months) to make it permanent.

**7. Do minors need to complete KYC?**
Yes, if a minor is opening a self-operated account (usually allowed above age 10), their KYC is required. For accounts operated by guardians, the guardian's KYC must be completed thoroughly.

**8. Is Video KYC safe?**
Absolutely. Video KYC is conducted over encrypted, highly secure banking channels. The connection is peer-to-peer between the bank and customer, and geo-tagging ensures security. The RBI mandates stringent cybersecurity protocols for V-CIP.

**9. Can NRIs perform Video KYC?**
As per current RBI guidelines, Video KYC requires the customer to be physically present within the geographical boundaries of India at the time of the video call, verified via live geo-tagging. Therefore, NRIs abroad cannot use V-CIP.

**10. What is a 'High Risk' customer in KYC?**
High-risk customers include Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs), non-face-to-face customers with missing information, high net worth individuals with unexplained wealth sources, and entities operating in sectors prone to money laundering. They require KYC updates every 2 years.

**11. Does KYC expire?**
KYC doesn't "expire," but it becomes outdated. RBI mandates Re-KYC periodically to ensure the bank has your latest address, contact details, and financial profile. 

**12. Can I use my digital Aadhaar (e-Aadhaar) for physical KYC?**
Yes, a printed copy of your e-Aadhaar or downloading it via XML offline KYC is perfectly valid, provided the QR code is legible and the details can be authenticated.

**13. Why did the bank ask for my income proof during KYC?**
While basic KYC only requires Identity and Address proof, enhanced due diligence for credit cards, loans, or high-value accounts requires income proof to ascertain your repayment capacity and source of funds.

**14. I lost my CKYC number, how can I find it?**
You do not need to memorize it. Any bank or financial institution can retrieve your CKYC number by searching the CERSAI database using your PAN card number and Date of Birth.

**15. Is it mandatory to submit a physical photograph now?**
For physical branch KYC, yes, passport-size photographs are required. However, for e-KYC and Video KYC, a live photo is captured digitally, eliminating the need for physical photographs.

**16. Are there any fees for Video KYC?**
No, there are absolutely no fees charged by any bank or financial institution for conducting a Video KYC. It is a free service provided for customer convenience.

**17. What should I do if my name in PAN and Aadhaar do not match?**
You must get the demographic details corrected in either the Income Tax portal (PAN) or UIDAI (Aadhaar) so that they match exactly. Until then, digital KYC platforms may reject your application due to a mismatch.

**18. Can I submit an electricity bill as my KYC address proof?**
Utility bills (electricity, water, post-paid phone, gas) not older than two months are considered 'Deemed OVDs' for temporary address proof, but you will eventually need to provide a standard OVD like Aadhaar, Voter ID, or Passport.

**19. What is OTP-based e-KYC?**
OTP-based e-KYC validates your Aadhaar details via an OTP sent to your registered mobile number. Accounts opened this way are "restricted" accounts with limits (e.g., max balance ₹1 Lakh) until a full physical or Video KYC is completed.

**20. Can a joint account be opened using Video KYC?**
Currently, most banks only support opening individual single-holding accounts via Video KYC. For joint accounts, physical KYC or a different onboarding workflow is usually required.

## Common Myths vs Facts

**Myth 1:** KYC is only required when opening a new bank account.
**Fact:** KYC is an ongoing process. RBI mandates periodic Re-KYC updates depending on your risk profile, even if your account is decades old.

**Myth 2:** An unregistered rent agreement is a valid address proof for KYC.
**Fact:** Unregistered rent agreements are generally not accepted as Officially Valid Documents (OVDs) under strict RBI guidelines.

**Myth 3:** Banks charge a penalty fee if I don't update my KYC.
**Fact:** Banks do not charge monetary penalties for pending KYC, but they will freeze your account transactions until the compliance is met.

**Myth 4:** Video KYC can be done from anywhere in the world.
**Fact:** Video KYC requires the customer to be physically present in India, which is verified dynamically through live GPS tracking during the call.

**Myth 5:** SMS links are a safe way to update my KYC.
**Fact:** Banks never send SMS links asking you to update KYC online. These are almost always phishing scams designed to steal your credentials.

**Myth 6:** I have to visit the branch to update my KYC if my address hasn't changed.
**Fact:** If there is no change in your KYC details, you can submit a self-declaration via NetBanking, Mobile App, or registered email without visiting the branch.

**Myth 7:** PAN card can be used as an address proof.
**Fact:** PAN card only serves as a Proof of Identity. It does not contain an address and cannot be used as a Proof of Address.

**Myth 8:** If I have done KYC for my mutual funds, I don't need it for my bank account.
**Fact:** While CKYC allows sharing of records, if your bank account requires a higher level of due diligence or your CKYC is outdated, the bank will ask for fresh documents.

**Myth 9:** Minors don't need KYC.
**Fact:** Minors require KYC. If the account is operated by a guardian, the guardian's KYC is needed. If operated by the minor, their KYC is necessary.

**Myth 10:** Video KYC is less secure than branch KYC.
**Fact:** Video KYC uses advanced facial recognition, OCR, geo-tagging, and liveness checks, making it highly secure and often less prone to human error than physical verification.

## Government Services
- **DigiLocker Integration:** Customers can directly fetch their Aadhaar, PAN, and Driving License securely via DigiLocker APIs during the digital KYC journey. This eliminates the need for physical document uploads and speeds up verification.
- **UIDAI e-KYC (Aadhaar):** Forms the backbone of biometric and OTP-based instant customer onboarding.
- **CERSAI (Central Registry of Securitisation Asset Reconstruction and Security Interest of India):** Maintains the Central KYC (CKYC) records registry for the entire Indian financial sector.
- **Income Tax Department (NSDL/UTIITSL):** Used for real-time verification of PAN card status and PAN-Aadhaar linkage status.
