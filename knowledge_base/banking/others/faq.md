To resolve complex issues or avail services detailed in this FAQ, customers typically need:

2. **Proof of Address (POA):** Utility bills (electricity, water, gas), Aadhaar Card, Passport, or registered rent agreement.
3. **Financial Proof:** Income tax returns, recent salary slips, or bank statements (usually for loans or credit cards).
4. **Recent Passport Size Photographs.**
5. **Duly filled Service Request Forms (SRF):** For specific banking requests like address change or signature update.

## Charges & Fees
While reading the FAQ is free, the services discussed incur charges:
- **Account Maintenance Charges:** Minimum Average Balance (MAB) non-maintenance fees ranging from ₹100 to ₹600 per month depending on the bank and account type.
- **Card Annual Fees:** Ranging from ₹150 for basic debit cards to ₹10,000+ for premium credit cards.
- **Transaction Fees:** NEFT/RTGS charges (often free online, but chargeable at branches), IMPS charges (₹5 to ₹15), and ATM withdrawal fees beyond free limits (₹21 per transaction).
- **Service Request Fees:** Cheque book issuance (beyond free leaves), duplicate statement printing, and signature verification often attract nominal fees of ₹50 to ₹100 plus GST.

## RBI / Government Rules
- **Charter of Customer Rights:** The RBI mandates that all banks provide transparent, fair, and accessible grievance redressal mechanisms, which this FAQ supports.
- **Zero Liability Policy:** According to RBI, a customer has zero liability for unauthorized electronic banking transactions if reported within 3 working days.
- **KYC Master Direction:** The RBI enforces strict Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and KYC norms, requiring periodic updates of customer information, which is a frequent subject of customer queries.
- **Banking Ombudsman Scheme:** If a customer's grievance is not resolved within 30 days by the bank, the RBI provides an appellate authority (Ombudsman) for free resolution.

## Step-by-Step Process
**General Grievance Redressal Process:**
1. **Level 1 (Branch/Call Center):** The customer contacts the standard helpline, uses the chatbot, or visits the home branch to raise the issue. A unique Ticket/Complaint ID is generated.
2. **Level 2 (Nodal Officer):** If the issue remains unresolved after 7-10 days, the customer escalates the ticket to the bank's designated Nodal Officer via email or written letter.
3. **Level 3 (Principal Nodal Officer):** Further escalation within the bank if Level 2 fails, usually requiring a waiting period of up to 30 days from the initial complaint.
4. **Level 4 (Banking Ombudsman):** If the bank rejects the complaint or fails to respond within 30 days, the customer files a complaint on the RBI CMS (Complaint Management System) portal.

## Safety Tips
- **Never Share Credentials:** Bank officials will never ask for your PIN, OTP, CVV, or Internet Banking passwords. Never share these over phone, email, or SMS.
- **Use Official Channels:** Always access the FAQ and support through the bank's official app or verified website (look for HTTPS and the padlock icon).
- **Beware of Search Engine Scams:** Do not call customer care numbers found randomly on Google search results; they are often manipulated by scammers. Use the number on the back of your card.
- **Regular Monitoring:** Frequently check your bank statements and SMS alerts for any unauthorized transactions and report them immediately.

## Common Mistakes
1. **Ignoring SMS Alerts:** Customers often ignore routine SMS alerts, missing critical notifications about unauthorized transactions or policy changes until it is too late.
2. **Using Simple Passwords:** Setting easily guessable passwords (like '123456' or 'password') or sharing a common PIN across multiple cards and devices.
3. **Delaying Complaint Registration:** Waiting weeks to report a failed ATM withdrawal or unauthorized charge, which voids the RBI's Zero Liability protection.
4. **Clicking Unverified Links:** Clicking on links in SMS or emails claiming "Your account is blocked, update KYC here," leading to phishing websites.
5. **Assuming All Digital Transactions are Free:** Failing to check the fee schedule for IMPS, cross-currency markups, or third-party ATM withdrawals, leading to unexpected deductions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **How do I open a new savings account?**
To open a new savings account, you can either visit the nearest branch with your Aadhaar and PAN card or apply online through the bank's official website or app using Video KYC. The online process is paperless and typically activates your account within a few hours.

2. **What is Minimum Average Balance (MAB)?**
MAB is the minimum average amount you must maintain in your account over a specific period (usually a month or quarter). If the balance falls below this limit, the bank will levy a non-maintenance penalty as per its fee schedule.

3. **How can I update my registered mobile number?**
For security reasons, changing your registered mobile number usually requires a visit to the home branch or a bank ATM. At the ATM, you can use your debit card and PIN to authenticate the change, while at the branch, you must submit a written request.

4. **What should I do if I lose my debit card?**
Immediately block your card to prevent unauthorized use. You can block it via Internet Banking, the mobile app, by sending a specific SMS format, or by calling the toll-free customer care number. Once blocked, you can request a replacement card.

5. **Why was my transaction declined but money deducted?**
This is a common issue caused by network timeouts between the merchant, payment gateway, and the bank. The deducted amount is kept in a suspense account and is automatically reversed to your account within 3 to 5 working days as per RBI guidelines.

6. **How do I register for Internet Banking?**
You can register for Internet Banking online by visiting the bank's official website. You will need your Customer ID, account number, and an active debit card (with PIN) to authenticate and set up your login and transaction passwords.

7. **What is the difference between NEFT, RTGS, and IMPS?**
NEFT settles transactions in half-hourly batches, RTGS is for high-value transactions (minimum ₹2 Lakh) settled in real-time, and IMPS is an instant, 24/7 fund transfer service for smaller amounts. All three are regulated by the RBI.

8. **How can I get my bank statement?**
You can view and download your bank statement for free through Internet Banking or the mobile app. You can also request it via SMS, email, or by visiting the branch (which may incur a physical printing charge).

9. **What is a KYC update and why is it required?**
KYC (Know Your Customer) is a mandatory RBI requirement to prevent identity theft, fraud, and money laundering. Banks require periodic updates to ensure your identity and address records are current and accurate.

10. **How do I apply for a cheque book?**
You can request a new cheque book through the mobile app, internet banking, at an ATM, or by submitting a slip at the branch. The cheque book will be mailed to your registered address within 7-10 working days.

11. **Can I change my home branch?**
Yes, you can transfer your account from one branch to another. This can often be done online via Internet Banking under the 'Service Requests' section or by submitting a branch transfer form at your current or prospective branch.

12. **What is a CIBIL score?**
A CIBIL score is a three-digit number ranging from 300 to 900 that represents your creditworthiness based on your past credit history. A higher score (typically above 750) increases your chances of getting loan approvals and better interest rates.

13. **How do I dispute an unauthorized transaction?**
Call customer care immediately to block the card/account and register a formal complaint. By doing this within 3 days, you ensure maximum protection under RBI's limited liability guidelines. Note down the complaint reference number.

14. **What is a UPI PIN?**
A UPI PIN (Personal Identification Number) is a 4 or 6-digit passcode you create during UPI registration. It is used to authorize all UPI transactions. Never share this PIN; it is only meant for sending money, not receiving it.

15. **How can I close my bank account?**
To close an account, you generally must visit your home branch in person, submit an account closure form, surrender any unused cheques and debit cards, and ensure your account balance is zero (or request the remaining balance via DD/transfer).

16. **Why is my account frozen or marked inactive?**
Accounts are marked inactive or dormant if there have been no customer-induced transactions for 12 to 24 months. Accounts may be frozen by regulatory order or if KYC documents have expired and not been updated despite reminders.

17. **How do I reactivate a dormant account?**
To reactivate a dormant account, you must visit your branch and submit a written request along with fresh KYC documents (Aadhaar, PAN). After verification, you will need to perform a small deposit or withdrawal transaction.

18. **What are the charges for ATM withdrawals?**
Most banks offer 3 to 5 free ATM withdrawals per month at their own and other banks' ATMs. Beyond this limit, a charge of up to ₹21 plus GST is levied per financial transaction, and lesser for non-financial queries.

19. **How do I update my nominee details?**
Nominee details can be updated easily through Internet Banking or the mobile app in the 'Profile' or 'Services' section. Alternatively, you can fill out the DA1, DA2, or DA3 forms at your branch to add, cancel, or change a nominee.

20. **Is it safe to use public Wi-Fi for banking?**
No, it is highly unsafe. Public Wi-Fi networks are often unencrypted, making it easy for hackers to intercept your login credentials or session data. Always use a secure, private connection or cellular data for financial transactions.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** A bank can ask for my UPI PIN to reverse a failed transaction.
   **Fact:** A bank will NEVER ask for your UPI PIN. The PIN is strictly for you to authorize outgoing payments. Reversals happen automatically.
2. **Myth:** Keeping zero balance will negatively affect my credit score (CIBIL).
   **Fact:** Savings account balances do not impact your CIBIL score. CIBIL is only affected by credit cards and loans. However, the bank may levy MAB penalties.
3. **Myth:** I must visit my home branch for every service request.
   **Fact:** Over 90% of service requests, including card blocking, nominee updates, and statement generation, can be done via mobile or internet banking.
4. **Myth:** Digital transactions are completely free of cost.
   **Fact:** While UPI is currently free, other digital transactions like IMPS, payment gateway charges for credit cards, and cross-currency markups often have associated fees.
5. **Myth:** If my money is stolen digitally, the bank will always refund it.
   **Fact:** The bank will only refund it if the fault lies with the bank's security or if you report unauthorized transactions within the stipulated 3-day RBI window.
6. **Myth:** Closing an old, unused credit card will immediately boost my credit score.
   **Fact:** Closing an old credit card reduces your total credit history length and available credit limit, which can sometimes temporarily lower your score.
7. **Myth:** The RBI controls the interest rates for my savings account.
   **Fact:** RBI deregulated savings account interest rates in 2011. Individual banks are free to set their own interest rates based on their policies.
8. **Myth:** I cannot open a bank account without a PAN card.
   **Fact:** You can open a basic savings account without a PAN card by submitting Form 60, though your transaction limits will be strictly capped.
9. **Myth:** A dormant account is permanently closed.
   **Fact:** A dormant account is only temporarily restricted for security reasons. It can be fully reactivated by submitting KYC documents at the branch.
10. **Myth:** Only the account holder can deposit money into the account.
    **Fact:** Anyone can deposit cash or transfer money into your account provided they have your correct account number and IFSC code.

## Government Services
Modern banking in India is deeply integrated with government digital initiatives to provide seamless services:
- **Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY):** Offers zero-balance accounts to unbanked individuals, providing them with a RuPay debit card and access to overdraft facilities.
- **DigiLocker Integration:** Customers can directly fetch their Aadhaar, PAN, and driving licenses from DigiLocker during the Video KYC process for hassle-free account opening.
- **Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT):** Subsidies (like LPG), pensions, and scholarships are routed directly into the customer's Aadhaar-linked bank account via the APBS (Aadhaar Payment Bridge System).
- **Atal Pension Yojana & PMJJBY/PMSBY:** Customers can easily enroll in these government-backed micro-pension and micro-insurance schemes directly through their banking app.
