# Card Fraud (कार्ड धोखाधड़ी)

## Overview

### English
Card fraud is a type of identity theft or financial crime where unauthorized transactions are made using a debit or credit card, card number, or account details. It includes physical card theft, skimming, cloning, and online Card-Not-Present (CNP) fraud.

### Hindi
कार्ड धोखाधड़ी (Card Fraud) एक प्रकार की पहचान चोरी या वित्तीय अपराध है जहां डेबिट या क्रेडिट कार्ड, कार्ड नंबर या खाते के विवरण का उपयोग करके अनधिकृत लेनदेन (unauthorized transactions) किए जाते हैं। इसमें भौतिक कार्ड की चोरी, स्किमिंग, क्लोनिंग और ऑनलाइन कार्ड-नॉट-प्रेजेंट (CNP) धोखाधड़ी शामिल है।

## Quick Summary
Card fraud is a growing concern with the rise of digital payments. Fraudsters use various methods to steal card information. "Skimming" involves installing small, hidden card-reading devices over ATM slots or POS machines to copy the magnetic strip details. "Cloning" is the process of writing this copied data onto blank plastic cards to create a duplicate.

In the online space, "Card-Not-Present" (CNP) fraud occurs when criminals use stolen card details (number, expiry date, CVV) to make purchases on websites, often intercepting the OTP through phishing or SIM swap. RBI has implemented robust security standards, including mandatory multi-factor authentication (OTP) for online transactions in India, and zero-liability guidelines to protect victims of banking fraud.

---

## Definition
Card Fraud is the unauthorized acquisition and use of payment card credentials (debit, credit, or prepaid) to withdraw cash, purchase goods, or transfer funds illegally, breaching security protocols like PINs, CVVs, and OTPs.

---

## Why It Matters
Understanding card fraud is essential for every cardholder to:
- **Protect Financial Assets:** Enables customers to identify fraud indicators early, stopping criminals before they drain the bank account.
- **Ensure Quick Refund Claims:** Knowing the zero liability rules ensures that you report the fraud within the RBI-mandated 3-day window to qualify for a full reimbursement from the bank.
- **Adopt Secure Habits:** Helps users recognize compromised ATMs or phishing messages, keeping their card credentials secure.

---

## How It Works
The standard operational pathway of card skimming and cloning fraud:

Fraudster places a skimmer device over an ATM card reader slot
↓
Unsuspecting customer inserts card; skimmer copies magnetic strip data
↓
Hidden camera (or keypad overlay) records the customer entering the 4-digit PIN
↓
Fraudster retrieves the skimmer and camera, downloading the card details and PIN
↓
Fraudster writes the data onto a blank magnetic card (Cloning)
↓
Fraudster uses the cloned card and recorded PIN at another ATM to withdraw cash
↓
Real customer receives withdrawal SMS alerts and realizes the fraud

## Features
- **Skimming:** Physical data theft at ATMs/POS.
- **Phishing/Vishing:** Tricking customers into revealing card numbers, CVVs, and OTPs over fake calls or links.
- **Card-Not-Present (CNP) Fraud:** Online transactions using stolen card credentials without the physical card.
- **SIM Swap Fraud:** Disabling the customer's SIM to redirect bank transaction OTPs to the fraudster's phone.

---

## Benefits
- **Risk Awareness:** Knowing the methods of fraud helps in avoiding compromised terminals and websites.
- **RBI Protection Benefits:** Legal coverage under RBI's zero customer liability rules if reported on time.

---

## Risks
- **Financial Loss:** Irreversible loss of funds if the fraud is reported late (beyond 7 days) and is determined to be due to customer negligence.
- **Identity Theft:** Stolen card data being used to open fake merchant accounts or fund illegal activities.
- **Credit Score Damage:** Unsettled fraudulent charges on credit cards can lead to payment defaults and a drop in CIBIL score.

---

## Charges & Fees
- **Fraud Reporting Fees:** Nil (reporting fraud and disputing transactions is a free service provided by all banks).

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **Zero Customer Liability:** Under RBI circular (2017), the customer has zero liability if the fraud occurs due to bank negligence or third-party breaches where the customer reports the incident within 3 working days.
- **Limited Liability:** If the fraud is due to customer negligence (sharing OTP/PIN), the customer bears the loss until they report it. Once reported, any subsequent transactions are the bank's liability.
- **TDS and Chargeback Settlement:** Banks must credit (shadow reversal) the disputed amount into the customer's account within 10 working days of reporting, pending final investigation (up to 90 days).

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Report Card Fraud and Claim Refund:
1. **Block the Card:** Immediately block the card via SMS, mobile banking app, or customer care to stop further transactions.
2. **Collect Transaction Evidence:** Take screenshots of the transaction SMS alerts and download your bank statement.
3. **Contact the Bank:** Call your bank's 24/7 dedicated fraud reporting helpline within 3 working days to register a formal dispute. Get the Complaint Reference Number.
4. **File Police Complaint:** Visit the local police station or file a cybercrime complaint online at **cybercrime.gov.in** within 24 hours. Get a copy of the complaint receipt/acknowledgement.
5. **Submit Dispute Form:** Visit your bank branch and submit the official Transaction Dispute Form along with the police complaint copy and bank statement.
6. **Track Resolution:** The bank will temporarily credit (shadow credit) the disputed amount within 10 working days while they investigate. The investigation must be completed within 90 days.

## Safety Tips
- **Shield Keypad during entry:** Always cover the keypad with your hand when typing your PIN at ATMs and shops.
- **Never Share OTP/CVV:** No legitimate bank employee or payment portal will call you asking for your card's CVV or transaction OTP.
- **Avoid public Wi-Fi for Transactions:** Do not input card details on shopping websites while connected to public cyber cafe networks or free public Wi-Fi.
- **Manage app usage settings:** Disable international and online usage on your bank app when you do not need them.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Delaying Card Block:** Wasting hours trying to find a misplaced card instead of blocking it immediately when an unexpected transaction alert is received.
- **Sharing OTP to "Cancel" Transactions:** Falling for scams where the caller claims they need the OTP to "cancel" a transaction that just occurred. The OTP actually approves the transaction.
- **Not Filing a Police Complaint:** Believing that notifying the bank is sufficient. Banks require a formal police complaint copy to process insurance and chargeback claims.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is card fraud?** The unauthorized use of your credit or debit card details to make payments or withdraw cash.
2. **What is card skimming?** A physical fraud where criminals place a device over the ATM slot to copy the magnetic data of your card.
3. **What is card cloning?** Copying stolen card data onto a blank plastic card to create a duplicate card that works at ATMs.
4. **What is Card-Not-Present (CNP) fraud?** Fraudulent transactions done online where the physical card is not required, using only stolen card details.
5. **What should I do immediately after detecting card fraud?** Block the card instantly using the mobile banking app or by calling customer care.
6. **What is the RBI zero liability rule?** A rule protecting customers from loss if the fraud is reported to the bank within 3 working days of occurrence.
7. **How long do I have to report a card fraud?** You should report it immediately. To get zero liability benefit, report it within 3 working days.
8. **What is a shadow credit?** A temporary refund credit that the bank gives you within 10 working days of reporting a fraud while they investigate.
9. **Where can I file a cybercrime complaint?** Online at the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or at your local police station's cyber cell.
10. **Can I get my money back if I shared my OTP?** If you shared the OTP, it is classified as customer negligence. You may have to bear the loss until you report and block the card.
11. **How do fraudsters get my card details?** Through skimming devices at ATMs, phishing emails, fake websites, or data breaches at online shopping portals.
12. **What is the 3-digit number on the back of the card?** The CVV (Card Verification Value), which is a crucial security code for online transactions.
13. **Can a blocked card be used for fraud?** No, once blocked, the card is completely deactivated and cannot be used.
14. **Does the bank charge fees to report fraud?** No, reporting fraud is a free service.
15. **What is SIM swap fraud?** A scam where fraudsters block your active SIM card and get a duplicate issued to receive your bank OTPs.
16. **Why did I receive an OTP for a transaction I didn't make?** Someone is trying to use your card details online. Block your card immediately.
17. **Can a cloned card be used without a PIN?** For ATM cash withdrawals, a PIN is always required. For contactless POS payments under ₹5,000, tap-and-pay may work if enabled.
18. **How long does a bank take to resolve a fraud claim?** Investigation must be resolved within 90 days as per RBI rules.
19. **What is a chargeback?** A dispute process where the issuing bank demands the merchant's bank to return the funds due to a fraudulent transaction.
20. **Should I file a complaint for a small amount?** Yes, regardless of the amount, you must report it to prevent further fraud and block the card.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Banks will automatically refund all stolen money. **Fact:** Refund depends on when you report the fraud and whether you shared details like OTPs/PINs.
2. **Myth:** Changing the PIN removes the fraudster's access to my card number online. **Fact:** Online transactions do not require a PIN; they require the CVV and OTP. You must block the card permanently to stay safe.
3. **Myth:** Cyber cell complaints require paying a fee. **Fact:** Filing a complaint on the government's cybercrime portal is completely free.
4. **Myth:** Fraudsters can scan my card from miles away. **Fact:** Contactless cards can only be read from a distance of under 4 cm, making long-distance scanning impossible.
5. **Myth:** Bank employees need my OTP to reverse a transaction. **Fact:** Bank employees never need your OTP to reverse or credit funds.
6. **Myth:** If my card is in my pocket, it cannot be cloned. **Fact:** If your card was swiped at a compromised ATM or shop earlier, the data was already copied and can be cloned while the card is in your pocket.
7. **Myth:** RuPay cards are immune to fraud. **Fact:** All card networks (RuPay, Visa, Mastercard) are vulnerable if safety rules are ignored.
8. **Myth:** My CIBIL score will not be affected by credit card fraud. **Fact:** If you do not report and dispute the fraud, the unpaid amount is marked as a default, damaging your CIBIL score.
9. **Myth:** Shadow credit means the case is resolved. **Fact:** Shadow credit is temporary. If the bank's investigation proves customer negligence, they can withdraw the shadow credit.
10. **Myth:** Public Wi-Fi is safe if the site has a lock icon. **Fact:** Public Wi-Fi networks can be intercepted by hackers who can read your keystropes; avoid banking transactions on them.

1. **User:** Mere debit card se ₹20,000 kat gaye hain par card mere paas hai. Main kya karu?
   **Assistant:** Ye card cloning ya online fraud ho sakta hai. Sabse pehle mobile banking app se card block karein ya bank helpline par call karke card hotlist karwayein. Iske baad cybercrime.gov.in par report karein aur bank me dispute file karein.
2. **User:** Mujhe ek call aaya bank se ki card limit badhane ke liye OTP batao, kya bata du?
   **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Bank kabhi bhi limit badhane ke liye call par OTP nahi maangta. Ye fraud call hai, call cut karein aur card details share na karein.
3. **User:** Maine galti se OTP share kar diya aur paise kat gaye. Kya bank refund karega?
   **Assistant:** Agar aapne khud OTP share kiya hai, toh ye customer negligence mana jata hai. RBI rules ke tehat isme refund milna mushkil hota hai. Fir bhi card block karein aur bank me dispute file karke koshish karein.
4. **User:** RBI ka zero liability rule kya hai?
   **Assistant:** Agar fraud bank ki galti ya system breach ke kaaran hua hai, aur aapne transaction hone ke 3 din ke andar bank ko inform kar diya hai, toh aapki zero liability hoti hai aur bank ko poora paisa wapas karna hoga.
5. **User:** ATM me card slot ke upar ek chota box laga hai, kya ye normal hai?
   **Assistant:** Nahi, ye card skimmer ho sakta hai jo card data copy karta hai. Aise ATM me card na dalein aur bank manager ko ya security guard ko suchit karein.
6. **User:** Bank bol raha hai ki shadow credit de diya hai. Ye kya hota hai?
   **Assistant:** Shadow credit ek temporary refund hota hai jo bank fraud ki report milne ke 10 din ke andar aapke account me daal deta hai jab tak investigation poori nahi hoti (max 90 days).
7. **User:** Kya credit card fraud ke liye police FIR zaroori hai?
   **Assistant:** Haan, banks ko insurance claim process karne ke liye cyber police complaint ki copy chahiye hoti hai. Aap ise online cybercrime.gov.in par darj kar sakte hain.
8. **User:** Maine transaction dispute file kiya tha, kitne din me solve hoga?
   **Assistant:** RBI ke rules ke tehat bank ko transaction dispute ka final solution 90 din (3 mahine) ke andar dena hota hai.
9. **User:** Kya main block card se UPI payment kar sakta hu?
   **Assistant:** Haan, aamtaur par UPI payments chalti rehti hain kyuki wo bank account se linked hain, card se nahi. Par UPI PIN reset karne ke liye naya card active hona zaroori hai.
10. **User:** Mujhe kaise pata chalega ki meri complaint registered ho gayi hai?
    **Assistant:** Bank aapko sms ya email par ek unique 'Complaint Reference Number' bhejega. Ise sambhal kar rakhein.
11. **User:** SIM card block ho gaya hai aur bank se messages nahi aa rahe.
    **Assistant:** Ye SIM Swap fraud ho sakta hai. Fraudsters ne aapka number deactivate karwa kar duplicate SIM le li hogi. Turant mobile operator aur bank se contact karke accounts freeze karwayein.
12. **User:** Debit card cloning se kaise bachein?
    **Assistant:** ATM ka use karte waqt keypad dhak kar PIN dalein aur swipe machine par card khud swipe karein. Mobile app me usage limits kam rakhein.
13. **User:** Fraud transaction hone ke 10 din baad pata chala, kya poora refund milega?
    **Assistant:** Agar aap 4 se 7 din ke baad report karte hain, toh aapki liability bank ke rules ke tehat badh sakti hai (max ₹25,000 tak ka loss aapko jhelna pad sakta hai). 7 din ke baad report karne par refund bank ki discretion par hota hai.
14. **User:** Kya credit card disputes me interest lagta rahega jab tak case chal raha hai?
    **Assistant:** Bank aamtaur par disputed amount par interest aur charges ko freeze kar dete hain jab tak investigation chal rahi ho, par aapko iske liye bank se confirmation le lena chahiye.
15. **User:** Maine card block kar diya hai par naya card kab tak aayega?
    **Assistant:** Card block hone ke baad aap app se ya branch me naye replacement card ke liye apply kar sakte hain, jo 5 se 7 working days me deliver ho jayega.
