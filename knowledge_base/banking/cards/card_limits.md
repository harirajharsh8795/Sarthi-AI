# Card Limits (कार्ड लिमिट प्रबंधन)

## Overview

### English
Card limits are transaction controls set on debit and credit cards that restrict the maximum amount of money that can be spent or withdrawn daily. These limits can be customized by the customer for different payment channels (ATMs, POS terminals, online websites, and contactless payments) to enhance safety.

### Hindi

### Hinglish

RBI ke niyam ke tehat aap apne bank app se alag-alag payment methods (jaise ATM, POS machine, online shopping aur contactless Wi-Fi tap) ke liye ye limits badha ya ghata sakte hain.

## Quick Summary
Every debit and credit card comes with default transaction limits set by the issuing bank based on the card variant (Classic, Gold, Platinum). These limits define the maximum cash you can withdraw from ATMs in a day and the maximum POS/online purchases you can make. Since 2020, RBI regulations mandate that banks must provide customers the ability to modify these limits and switch off specific transaction channels (like international or online usage) at any time.

Using online banking or mobile apps, cardholders can manage their risk by setting lower daily limits. For instance, if you only withdraw up to ₹5,000 from ATMs, you can set your ATM limit to ₹5,000 instead of keeping the bank's default limit of ₹40,000. If the card is lost or stolen, fraudsters cannot withdraw more than the limit set by you.

---

## Definition
Card Limits are user-configurable or bank-mandated caps applied to a payment card's daily transaction volume, separated by transaction types (ATM cash withdrawal, domestic POS, domestic e-commerce, international usage, and contactless tap-and-pay).

---

## Why It Matters
Managing card limits is essential to:
- **Minimize Fraud Losses:** If a card is skimmed or compromised, the loss is capped at the user-defined limit instead of the entire account balance or credit line.
- **Enforce Budget Discipline:** Prevents impulse shopping on credit/debit cards by setting low daily online purchase limits.
- **Prevent Accidental Contactless Debits:** Setting low contactless tap-and-pay limits or turning it off entirely prevents unauthorized nearby scanning debits.
- **Comply with Safety Practices:** Lowering limits before travelling or keeping international channels switched off until needed is a standard cyber safety practice.

---

## How It Works
The limit verification process during a transaction:

Cardholder sets custom daily limits via Mobile App (e.g., ATM: ₹10,000, Online: ₹2,000)
↓
Cardholder attempts a transaction (e.g., online purchase of ₹5,000)
↓
Merchant sends authorization request to the issuing Bank
↓
Bank's system checks:
- Is the online channel enabled? (Yes)
- Is the amount (₹5,000) within the user's online limit? (No, limit is ₹2,000)
↓
Bank rejects transaction and sends SMS: "Transaction failed. Limit exceeded."
↓
No funds are debited; cardholder can increase limit in app to retry if needed

## Eligibility
- **Applicable Cards:** All retail debit and credit cards issued in India.
- **Account Status:** The account must be active, and mobile banking/net banking must be registered to modify limits online.

---

## Required Documents
- **For Online Modification:** No documents are needed. Only registered mobile OTP is required.
- **For Written Requests:** Written limit modification application letter submitted at the home branch along with KYC verification.

---

## Features
- **ATM Limit:** Restricts daily cash withdrawals.
- **POS Limit:** Restricts physical purchases at merchant stores using swipe machines.
- **E-Commerce Limit:** Restricts online shopping transactions.
- **Contactless (NFC) Limit:** Restricts PIN-less tap-and-pay transactions (capped at ₹5,000 per transaction by RBI).
- **International Limit:** Restricts transactions outside India or on foreign websites.

---

## Benefits
- **Risk Mitigation:** Shields the main bank account balance from major security threats.
- **Instant Control:** Limits can be increased or decreased in real-time, taking effect immediately.
- **Channel Specific Isolation:** You can keep domestic ATM withdrawals active while completely disabling international online usage.

---

## Risks
- **Transaction Blockage:** Forgetting that you set a low limit can cause legitimate emergency payments to fail until you log in and increase it.
- **Default Reset Risks:** Some banks may reset card limits to default values when a new replacement card is issued.

---

## Charges & Fees
- **Limit Modification Charges:** Nil (RBI mandates that banks cannot charge any fee for changing or managing transaction limits online).

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **RBI Mandate (2020):** RBI directed all banks to disable international and online usage by default on all newly issued cards. Customers must actively enable these features and set limits.
- **Mandatory User Controls:** Banks must provide round-the-clock limit modification facility via mobile app, net banking, IVR, or ATMs.
- **Contactless Limit:** RBI restricts PIN-less contactless transactions to a maximum of ₹5,000 per transaction.

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Modify Card Limits via Mobile App:
1. **Log in:** Open and log into your bank's mobile app.
2. **Go to Cards:** Navigate to the 'Cards' or 'Debit/Credit Card Services' tab.
3. **Select Manage Limits:** Click on 'Manage Card Limits' or 'Manage Usage'.
4. **Choose Channels:** You will see sliders or input boxes for different channels:
   - Domestic ATM
   - Domestic POS
   - Domestic E-commerce
   - Contactless (NFC)
   - International (ATM/POS/Online)
5. **Adjust Sliders:** Move the sliders or enter the amount to set your desired daily limits. You can also toggle switches to turn off unused channels entirely.
6. **Confirm with OTP:** Click save and enter the OTP sent to your registered mobile number to apply the changes.

---

## Safety Tips
- **Keep Limits Low by Default:** Keep your daily limits set to the lowest practical amount you need for routine transactions.
- **Switch Off International Usage:** Keep international transactions switched off unless you are actively travelling abroad or buying from a foreign website.
- **Disable NFC if Unused:** Turn off the contactless (NFC) feature if you do not use tap-and-pay, to prevent skimming risks.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Assuming Bank Defaults are Safe:** Leaving bank-defined maximum default limits active (e.g., keeping a ₹1 Lakh daily online limit active when you rarely spend more than ₹500 online).
- **Not Checking New Cards:** Forgetting that new replacement cards might have online transactions disabled by default, causing initial activation attempts to fail.
- **Ignoring Channel Settings:** Keeping e-commerce channels open on cards used solely for ATM cash withdrawals.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What are card limits?** Caps set on the maximum amount of money you can spend or withdraw daily using a bank card.
2. **How do I change my daily ATM withdrawal limit?** You can change it using your bank's mobile app, net banking, or by visiting the branch.
3. **Can I disable online shopping on my debit card?** Yes, you can turn off the "E-commerce" or "Online" transaction channel in your app card controls.
4. **What is the maximum limit for contactless payments?** Capped at ₹5,000 per transaction without a PIN, as per RBI guidelines.
5. **Is there any fee to modify card limits?** No, modifying card limits through online banking is completely free.
6. **What happens if I try to spend more than my set card limit?** The transaction will fail instantly with a "Limit Exceeded" error message, and no money will be deducted.
7. **Are new cards set to maximum limit by default?** No, as per RBI rules, new cards have online and international transactions disabled by default.
8. **Can I set different limits for international and domestic transactions?** Yes, you can set separate daily limits for domestic and international usage.
9. **How long does it take for a limit change to take effect?** The change happens instantly in real-time.
10. **Can I decrease my card limit to zero?** Yes, you can set limits to zero or switch off the channel completely to freeze card usage.
11. **Do card limits affect my CIBIL score?** No, limits on savings debit cards do not affect CIBIL. On credit cards, using too much of your limit (high utilization) can lower your score.
12. **Can a bank change my credit card limit without my permission?** No, banks require explicit consent from the holder to increase credit card limits.
13. **What is the default ATM limit for classic cards?** Usually between ₹20,000 and ₹40,000 per day.
14. **Why is my transaction failing online even though I have money?** The online (e-commerce) channel may be switched off or the transaction amount exceeds your set daily limit.
15. **Can I set limits on a minor's debit card?** Yes, parents can manage and set lower limits on minor debit cards.
16. **What is POS limit?** Point of Sale limit; the daily cap on physical card swipes or dip payments at shops/malls.
17. **Can I change limits via SMS banking?** Some banks allow basic limit changes via SMS codes, but mobile apps/net banking are recommended.
18. **Can I set a monthly spending limit?** Most cards only support daily limits, though some credit cards allow setting billing-cycle limits.
19. **What should I do if I need to make a one-time high-value transaction?** Log into the app, increase the limit, complete the transaction, and lower the limit back to normal.
20. **Who do I contact if I cannot modify limits on the app?** Contact your bank's helpline or visit the branch for technical assistance.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Once I set a low limit, I can never increase it again. **Fact:** You can increase or decrease your limits as many times as you want in real-time.
2. **Myth:** Banks charge a processing fee to modify card limits. **Fact:** RBI guidelines mandate that limit management features must be free.
3. **Myth:** High credit card limits mean I must spend more. **Fact:** High limits only represent borrowing capacity; you should keep your actual spends below 30% of the limit.
4. **Myth:** Contactless tap-and-pay transactions have unlimited value. **Fact:** The PIN-less limit is strictly capped at ₹5,000 per transaction in India.
5. **Myth:** Turning off international usage blocks payments on Indian sites. **Fact:** International channel only blocks payments to merchants registered outside India. Domestic payments will work normally.
6. **Myth:** Debit card limits are decided by the government. **Fact:** Minimum standards are regulated, but actual limits are set by the issuing bank based on card variants and modified by the customer.
7. **Myth:** If I set my limit to zero, my card is blocked permanently. **Fact:** Setting limits to zero is a temporary freeze; you can restore the limit and use the card whenever needed.
8. **Myth:** The limit on my add-on card is the same as the primary card. **Fact:** Primary cardholders can set custom, lower limits on add-on cards.
9. **Myth:** Card limits apply to UPI transactions. **Fact:** Card limits only apply to card transactions (ATM/POS/Online). UPI has its own daily transaction limits (usually ₹1 Lakh/day).
10. **Myth:** New debit cards are fully active for international use when received. **Fact:** RBI rules require international channels to be switched off on all new cards by default.

## Conversation Examples
1. **User:** Maine naya debit card liya hai par online shopping website par transaction decline ho raha hai. Balance kaafi hai.
   **Assistant:** RBI ke rules ke mutabik naye cards par online (e-commerce) transactions by default band hote hain. Aapko bank app me jaakar card settings me 'E-commerce' channel ko enable karna hoga aur limit set karni hogi.
2. **User:** Card limit change karne ka kya charge lagta hai?
   **Assistant:** Bank app ya net banking ke zariye daily card limits change karne par koi charge nahi lagta, ye bilkul free hai.
3. **User:** Wi-Fi card (contactless card) se bina PIN ke koi bhi paise nikal sakta hai, ise kaise rokein?
   **Assistant:** Aap bank app ke 'Manage Limits' section me jaakar 'Contactless' ya 'NFC' transaction ko completely switch off (deactivate) kar sakte hain, ya iski limit ₹500-1000 tak kar sakte hain.
4. **User:** ATM withdrawal limit aur POS limit me kya farq hai?
   **Assistant:** ATM limit cash nikalne ke liye hoti hai, jabki POS limit shop par card swipe/dip karke payment karne ke liye hoti hai. Aap dono ki daily limit alag-alag set kar sakte hain.
5. **User:** Mera credit card limit ₹2 Lakh hai, kya main ise kam kar sakta hu?
   **Assistant:** Haan, aap safety ke liye bank app me jaakar use temporarilty ₹50,000 ya apni pasand ki kisi limit par lock kar sakte hain. Isse card misuse hone par bhi bada nuksan nahi hoga.
6. **User:** Mujhe ek baar ₹50,000 ka online transaction karna hai par meri limit ₹10,000 set hai. Kya karein?
   **Assistant:** Aap mobile app par jaakar online limit ko badhakar ₹50,000 karein, transaction complete karein, aur uske baad safety ke liye limit wapas ₹10,000 par set kar dein. Ye instant apply hota hai.
7. **User:** Kya minor debit card ki limits parents change kar sakte hain?
   **Assistant:** Haan, minor account ke guardian apne net banking ya app se minor debit card ki daily spend limits control kar sakte hain.
8. **User:** ATM se daily kitna cash nikal sakte hain?
   **Assistant:** Ye aapke card type (Classic, Platinum) par depend karta hai. Classic cards par normal limit ₹20,000 to ₹40,000 daily hoti hai. Aap ise app se check aur change kar sakte hain.
9. **User:** Google Pay payments par card limits asar karti hain kya?
   **Assistant:** GPay (UPI) direct bank account se link hota hai, card se nahi. Isliye debit card ki limit change karne se UPI transactions par koi asar nahi padta.
10. **User:** Main videsh (foreign) ja raha hu, card ko kaise use karu?
    **Assistant:** Jaane se pehle bank app me jaakar 'International Usage' ko enable karein aur foreign ATM aur shopping ke liye daily limit set kar lein.
11. **User:** RBI ka 2020 ka card rule kya hai?
    **Assistant:** Is rule ke tehat sabhi naye cards par online, international aur contactless transactions disabled hote hain jab tak customer unhe khud active na kare, taaki card security badhe.
12. **User:** Limit exceed ka message aaya aur transaction fail ho gaya, kya bank penalty lega?
    **Assistant:** Nahi, limit exceed hone par transaction fail ho jata hai par is par koi penalty charge nahi lagta.
13. **User:** Net banking se card limit kaise change karein?
    **Assistant:** Net banking me login karein, 'Cards' -> 'Manage Cards' -> 'Modify Limits' par click karein. Custom limits select karke OTP se verify karein.
14. **User:** Card limit change karne ka OTP nahi aa raha, kya branch jana hoga?
    **Assistant:** Ek baar network aur registered number check karein. Agar phir bhi nahi aata, toh bank helpline par call karein ya branch jaakar application de sakte hain.
15. **User:** Over-limit charge kya hota hai?
    **Assistant:** Credit cards par agar aap pre-approved limit se zyada spend kar dete hain (bank ki system capability ke tehat), toh bank over-limit charge (jaise ₹500) laga sakta hai. Isse bachne ke liye limit strict rakhein.
