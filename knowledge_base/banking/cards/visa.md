# Visa Card (वीजा कार्ड)

## Overview

### English
Visa is a global payment technology network operated by Visa Inc., an American multinational corporation. It processes electronic retail transactions worldwide, issuing co-branded debit, credit, and prepaid cards through partner banks, featuring high global acceptance and specialized tier rewards.

## Quick Summary
Visa is one of the world's largest payment processing networks. It acts as an intermediary, facilitating transaction routing and settlement between the merchant's bank and the cardholder's issuing bank. Unlike banks, Visa does not issue cards directly to consumers or extend credit; it licenses its network technology to banks, which handle the account opening, lending, and interest rates.

Visa cards are highly valued by international travelers due to their seamless acceptance at millions of ATMs and merchant locations in over 200 countries. Visa categorizes its cards into progressive tiers (Classic, Gold, Platinum, Signature, and Infinite), with higher tiers offering benefits like luxury hotel discounts, concierge services, and comprehensive travel insurance.

---

## Definition
Visa is a multinational payment card network that license its electronic transaction-processing technology to financial institutions, routing authorization requests between merchants and issuers globally.

---

## Why It Matters
Understanding Visa cards is important for bank customers because:
- **Global Travel Ease:** Essential for booking international flights, hotels, and withdrawing local cash from ATMs abroad.
- **Tier-based Benefits:** Knowing the differences between tiers (e.g., Platinum vs. Signature) helps customers utilize free airport lounge visits, golf course access, and dining discounts.
- **Security Protocols:** Features Visa Secure (3D Secure technology) to prevent online e-commerce transaction frauds.

---

## How It Works
The global transaction routing cycle of a Visa card:

Cardholder swipes or taps Visa card at a POS terminal (in India or abroad)
↓
POS terminal sends transaction request to the merchant's acquiring bank
↓
Acquiring bank routes the transaction to Visa's global processing network (VisaNet)
↓
VisaNet routes the request to the cardholder's issuing Bank (in India or foreign country)
↓
Issuing Bank approves or declines based on balance and verification
↓
VisaNet transmits the decision back to the acquiring bank and POS terminal
↓
Transaction completes; funds are settled between the banks subsequently

## Eligibility
- **Savings/Current Account Holders:** Banks offer Visa debit cards to account holders based on minimum balance variants.
- **Credit Cards:** Offered to individuals who meet the bank's income criteria, employment stability, and CIBIL score requirements.
- **Fixed Deposit Backed:** Secured Visa credit cards can be issued against a bank Fixed Deposit (FD).

---

## Required Documents
- **Not Applicable:** For debit cards, an active bank account is required. For credit cards, standard income proofs (salary slips, ITR) and KYC (Aadhaar/PAN) must be submitted to the bank.

---

## Features
- **Global Acceptance:** Accepted at millions of merchants and ATMs worldwide.
- **Visa payWave:** Tap-and-pay contactless technology for fast transactions up to ₹5,000 without entering a PIN in India.
- **Visa Secure:** An extra layer of security (One-Time Password / 3D Secure) for online shopping.
- **Multiple Tiers:** Options ranging from basic Classic and Gold to premium Platinum, Signature, and Infinite.

---

## Benefits
- **Lounge Access:** Free access to select airport lounges globally (for Platinum, Signature, and Infinite variants).
- **Travel Insurance:** Complimentary lost baggage, flight delay, and medical emergency insurance during international travel on premium credit cards.
- **Concierge Services:** 24/7 personal assistance for booking travel, dining, and events (Signature/Infinite).

---

## Risks
- **Cross-Border Fee Risk:** High markup charges (usually 2% to 3.5%) when transactions are made in currencies other than Indian Rupees (INR).
- **No Native UPI Link:** Unlike RuPay, Visa credit cards cannot be linked to UPI apps for merchant payments in India (subject to future regulatory integrations).
- **Annual Maintenance Cost:** Premium Visa variants (like Signature or Infinite) often have higher annual fees compared to domestic cards.

---

## Charges & Fees
Typical charges vary by bank and are subject to RBI guidelines:
- **Annual Maintenance Charges:** Classic cards range from ₹150 to ₹250. Platinum and Signature cards range from ₹250 to ₹1,000+ per year.
- **Cross-border Markup Fee:** 2% to 3.5% fee on foreign exchange transactions.
- **Cash Withdrawal Fee (ATMs):** Standard ATM usage charges apply beyond free limits.
- **MDR (Merchant Discount Rate):** Merchants pay a processing fee (typically 1% to 2%) to banks to accept Visa cards, unlike RuPay debit which has zero MDR.

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **Data Localization Mandate:** RBI mandates that Visa must store all data relating to payment systems in servers located in India, though they are allowed to process transactions abroad provided the data is deleted there within 24 hours.
- **Card Choice Mandate (2024):** RBI directives require banks to give customers the choice to select their preferred card network (RuPay, Visa, or Mastercard) at the time of account opening or renewal.

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Select and Upgrade to a Visa Card:
1. **Check Eligibility:** Review your account balance or income to see which Visa tier (Platinum, Signature) you qualify for.
2. **Submit Request:** Log into your bank's app, go to Card Services, and select 'Upgrade Card' or submit a written form at the branch.
3. **Select Visa Network:** Choose the Visa option from the network choice list.
4. **Card Delivery:** Receive the card within 7 working days.
5. **Set PIN:** Visit the bank ATM or use the app to set your 4-digit PIN before the first use.

---

## Safety Tips
- **Enable Visa Secure:** Ensure your card is registered for 3D Secure verification to prevent unauthorized online transactions.
- **Monitor International Transaction Toggles:** Keep international transactions disabled on your bank app when you are not travelling, to prevent unauthorized foreign debits.
- **Check ATM Card Slot:** Look for loose parts or cameras near the keypad at ATMs to prevent card copying (skimming).

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Assuming Visa works on UPI Credit:** Trying to link a Visa credit card to Google Pay/PhonePe for QR code scanning (which is currently only supported for RuPay cards).
- **Ignoring Forex Markup Fees:** Using a standard Visa card for heavy shopping on international websites without realizing that a 3.5% currency conversion fee will be added to the bill.
- **Using Premium Cards without Claiming Benefits:** Paying high annual fees for a Visa Signature/Infinite card but neglecting to use the free airport lounge or golf privileges.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is a Visa card?** A payment card that runs on the global payment network operated by Visa Inc.
2. **Who issues Visa cards?** Banks and financial institutions issue Visa cards; Visa only provides the payment processing technology network.
3. **What is Visa payWave?** Visa's contactless tap-and-pay technology for payments under ₹5,000 without entering a PIN.
4. **Is Visa an Indian company?** No, Visa is an American multinational corporation headquartered in California, USA.
5. **What are the different tiers of Visa cards?** Classic, Gold, Platinum, Signature, and Infinite.
6. **Can I use my Visa card at any ATM?** Yes, it can be used at any ATM displaying the Visa logo globally.
7. **Is Visa credit card linkable to UPI?** No, as of now, only RuPay credit cards are linkable to UPI in India.
8. **What is the annual fee for a Visa debit card?** Usually between ₹150 and ₹500 plus GST, depending on the card tier and your bank.
9. **What is Visa Secure?** A security system that requires an OTP or password to approve online transactions.
10. **Can I get lounge access with a Visa Platinum card?** Yes, many banks offer complimentary domestic lounge access on Visa Platinum debit or credit cards.
11. **What is a cross-border markup fee?** A fee charged when you use your card in a foreign currency, typically 2% to 3.5% of the transaction amount.
12. **How is Visa different from Mastercard?** Both are global payment networks with similar features; acceptance rates and benefits are virtually identical, though specific merchant partnerships may vary.
13. **Can I choose Visa when opening a bank account?** Yes, under RBI guidelines, banks must offer customers a choice of card networks (RuPay, Visa, Mastercard).
14. **What is the maximum limit of a Visa card?** ATM withdrawal and purchase limits depend on your card tier (Classic has lower limits; Signature/Infinite have high limits).
15. **Does Visa offer zero liability for fraud?** Yes, Visa offers a Global Zero Liability policy for unauthorized card transactions if reported promptly.
16. **Why is my Visa card declined internationally?** You must enable "International Usage" in your bank app settings before using the card abroad.
17. **What is a Visa Infinite card?** The highest premium tier card offered by Visa, featuring high limits, lifestyle benefits, and dedicated concierge services.
18. **Can I use a Visa card to pay utility bills?** Yes, Visa cards are widely accepted on all bill payment portals.
19. **What is the chargeback process for Visa?** If you face fraud or do not receive goods, you can file a dispute (chargeback) with your bank to reverse the Visa transaction.
20. **Can I get a zero-balance account with a Visa card?** Some banks offer Visa cards with zero-balance accounts, but they may charge an annual card maintenance fee.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Visa cards are issued directly by Visa Inc. **Fact:** Visa does not issue cards or open accounts; cards are issued by banks using Visa's payment network technology.
2. **Myth:** Visa cards do not comply with Indian data laws. **Fact:** Visa complies fully with RBI's data localization rules, storing transaction data on servers in India.
3. **Myth:** All Visa cards have free international lounge access. **Fact:** Lounge access is only available on premium tiers (Platinum, Signature, Infinite) and depends on the issuing bank's terms.
4. **Myth:** Visa is accepted everywhere in India but RuPay is not. **Fact:** Both networks are accepted at virtually all ATMs and POS terminals in India.
5. **Myth:** Visa credit cards can be used for UPI payments. **Fact:** Visa credit cards cannot be linked to UPI for merchant QR payments in India at present.
6. **Myth:** Tap-and-pay on Visa has no safety limits. **Fact:** Yes, it is strictly capped at ₹5,000 per transaction by the RBI, above which a PIN is required.
7. **Myth:** Visa charges the customer a fee for every swipe. **Fact:** The merchant pays the processing fee (MDR); customer transactions are free unless a specific convenience fee applies.
8. **Myth:** Visa and Mastercard are the same company. **Fact:** They are separate, competing companies listed on the US stock exchange.
9. **Myth:** You can reset your Visa card PIN on the Visa website. **Fact:** PIN resets can only be done through your issuing bank's ATM, app, or website.
10. **Myth:** Visa cards cannot be blocked instantly. **Fact:** Visa cards can be blocked instantly in real-time through your bank's hotlisting service.

## Conversation Examples
1. **User:** Visa card aur RuPay card me kya farq hai?
   **Assistant:** Visa ek US-based global network hai jo duniyabhar me chalte hain, jabki RuPay ek Indian network hai jise NPCI ne banaya hai. RuPay credit card ko aap UPI se link kar sakte hain, par Visa card par ye facility abhi available nahi hai.
2. **User:** Main Dubai ja raha hu, kya mera Visa debit card wahan chalega?
   **Assistant:** Haan, Visa card internationally accept hota hai. Bas jaane se pehle bank app me jaakar 'International Transactions' aur 'ATM withdrawals' ko active kar lein aur limits set kar lein.
3. **User:** Visa Signature card ke kya benefits hain?
   **Assistant:** Visa Signature ek premium tier card hai jisme complimentary airport lounge access, 24/7 concierge service, hotel bookings par discounts aur free travel insurance jaise benefits milte hain.
4. **User:** Visa card par international transactions par kitna charge lagta hai?
   **Assistant:** International transaction karne par bank aapse 2% se 3.5% tak ka 'Forex Markup Fee' charge karte hain currency convert karne ke liye.
5. **User:** Kya main account kholte waqt bank se Visa card maang sakta hu?
   **Assistant:** Haan, RBI ke rules ke mutabik bank ko aapko card network choose karne ka option dena hoga. Aap application form me Visa select kar sakte hain.
6. **User:** payWave kya hai jo mere Visa card par likha hai?
   **Assistant:** payWave Visa ka contactless payment feature hai. Aap isse shop par swipe machine par bina PIN dale, bas card ko tap karke ₹5,000 tak ka payment kar sakte hain.
7. **User:** Visa credit card billing disputes kaise solve hote hain?
   **Assistant:** Agar aapke card par koi unauthorized transaction ho jaye, toh aap bank me chargeback dispute file kar sakte hain. Bank Visa network ke zariye transaction ki jaanch karke paise wapas dila sakta hai.
8. **User:** Verification code kya hota hai online shopping ke waqt?
   **Assistant:** Online shopping ke waqt secure checkout ke liye bank aapke phone par ek OTP bhejta hai jise Visa Secure code kehte hain, iske bina payment nahi hoti.
9. **User:** Visa Classic card par kitne benefits milte hain?
   **Assistant:** Visa Classic ek entry-level basic card hai. Isme transactions aur ATM cash withdrawal ki facility milti hai par lounge access ya premium discount benefits nahi hote.
10. **User:** Kya Visa credit card ka use unsafe hai?
    **Assistant:** Nahi, Visa card me chip and pin safety ke sath online 3D Secure verification hota hai. Ye tabhi unsafe hai agar aap apna PIN ya OTP kisi se share karein.
11. **User:** Visa card block kaise karein agar kho jaye?
    **Assistant:** Process same hai. Aap bank mobile app se, net banking se, ya bank ke dedicated customer care number par call karke card block karwa sakte hain.
12. **User:** Secured Visa card kaise milta hai?
    **Assistant:** Aap bank me jaakar Fixed Deposit (FD) kholte hain aur uske badle me bank aapko card limit dekar secured Visa credit card de deta hai.
13. **User:** Swipe machines par Visa acceptance charge kya hota hai?
    **Assistant:** Swipe machine par payments accept karne par bank dukandar se MDR (Merchant Discount Rate) charge karte hain. Customer ke liye ye free hota hai.
14. **User:** Kya main foreign websites par Visa card use kar sakta hu?
    **Assistant:** Haan, Visa cards global websites (jaise Netflix, Google, international stores) par payments ke liye accept hote hain, bas online international usage enable hona chahiye.
15. **User:** Mera Visa card swipe karne par read nahi ho raha.
    **Assistant:** Agar card ka chip ya magnetic strip ganda ya scratch ho gaya hai, toh swipe machine use read nahi kar payegi. Aapko bank se naya card request karna hoga.
