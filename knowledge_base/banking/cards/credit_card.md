# Credit Card (क्रेडिट कार्ड)

## Overview

### English
A credit card is a payment card issued by a financial institution that enables the holder to borrow funds up to a pre-approved credit limit to make purchases or withdraw cash. The borrowed amount must be repaid within a specified interest-free grace period to avoid high interest charges.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
A credit card functions as a short-term unsecured loan. Unlike a debit card, which deducts money instantly from your bank account, a credit card allows you to make purchases now and pay later. Lenders issue bills monthly, giving cardholders an interest-free grace period of 20 to 50 days. If the total outstanding bill is paid on or before the due date, no interest is charged.

However, if a cardholder only pays the "Minimum Amount Due" or rolls over the balance, banks apply high interest rates (ranging from 36% to 48% per year), which can lead to a debt trap. Additionally, cash withdrawals using credit cards (Cash Advance) attract high fees and immediate interest. Responsible usage improves your CIBIL score, while defaults can damage your credit rating.

---

## Definition
A Credit Card is a physical or virtual payment card issued by a lender that provides a revolving line of credit up to a customized limit, allowing cardholders to make online and offline transactions subject to monthly billing and repayment terms.

---

## Why It Matters
Understanding credit cards is crucial for financial well-being because:
- **Builds Credit Score:** Consistent, timely credit card payments are the fastest way to build a high credit score (CIBIL), making future home or business loans cheaper.
- **Interest-Free Funds:** Provides up to 45-50 days of interest-free credit, helping in managing short-term cash flows or emergency expenses.
- **Reward Privileges:** Offers benefits like cashbacks, reward points, air miles, and free airport lounge access.
- **Consumer Protection:** In case of merchant fraud, credit card transactions are easier to dispute and charge back compared to debit cards, protecting your cash.

---

## How It Works
The monthly billing and repayment lifecycle of a credit card:

Cardholder makes transactions throughout the billing cycle (e.g., 1st to 30th of the month)
↓
Bank generates the monthly statement bill on the Statement Date
↓
Statement shows: Total Outstanding, Minimum Amount Due, and Due Date
↓
Grace Period (typically 20 days) begins
↓
Cardholder repays before the Due Date:
- Option A: Pays Total Outstanding → Zero interest is charged
- Option B: Pays only Minimum Amount Due (usually 5%) → Interest applies on the balance from transaction dates
- Option C: Non-payment → Late fees + High interest + Drop in CIBIL score
↓
Credit limit is restored based on the payment received

## Eligibility
- **Age:** 18 to 70 years.
- **Income Stability:** Minimum monthly income requirement varies (usually starting from ₹15,000 to ₹25,000 for salaried, and higher for self-employed).
- **Credit Profile:** A healthy CIBIL score (750 or above) is preferred for card approval and higher credit limits.
- **FD-Backed Card Option:** Individuals with no income proof or low credit scores can get a secured credit card backed by a Fixed Deposit (FD) (usually getting a limit of 80-90% of the FD value).

---

## Required Documents
- **Identity & Address Proof (KYC):** Aadhaar Card, PAN Card (mandatory), Passport, or Voter ID.
- **Income Proof for Salaried:**
  - Salary slips for the last 2-3 months.
  - Form 16 or Income Tax Return (ITR).
  - Bank account statement showing salary credit.
- **Income Proof for Self-Employed:**
  - ITR for the last 1-2 years with computation of income.
  - CA-certified balance sheet and profit & loss statements.

## Features
- **Revolving Credit Limit:** The credit limit is restored as soon as payments are made, allowing continuous use.
- **Interest-free Period:** Grace period of 20 to 50 days on retail purchases.
- **EMI Conversion:** Option to convert high-value purchases (e.g., above ₹2,500) into monthly EMIs.
- **Cash Advance Limit:** A small portion of the credit limit allocated for cash withdrawals at ATMs (attracts high fees and immediate interest).

---

## Benefits
- **Emergency Funds:** Immediate backup for unexpected medical or travel expenses.
- **Purchase Rewards:** Savings through cashbacks and reward points on online/offline shopping.
- **Global Acceptance:** Accepted globally for international booking and travel.

---

## Risks
- **High Interest Debt Trap:** Interest rates are extremely high (3% to 4% per month, compounding), which can quickly escalate debt if bills are rolled over.
- **CIBIL Score Damage:** Missing payments or exceeding credit utilization limits (above 30%) lowers your credit score, making future loans hard to get.
- **Hidden Fees:** Charges like annual fees, over-limit fees, and cash advance charges if the card is not used carefully.

---

## Charges & Fees
Typical charges (subject to bank-specific tariffs and RBI guidelines):
- **Annual/Joining Fees:** Varies by card (from zero for lifetime free cards to ₹10,000+ for premium cards).
- **Finance Charges (Interest):** 36% to 48% per year (3% to 4% monthly) charged on the unpaid balance if the full bill is not paid.
- **Late Payment Fee:** Flat charge based on the outstanding amount (typically ₹100 to ₹1,300) if the minimum due is not paid by the due date.
- **Cash Advance Fee:** Charged for ATM withdrawals (usually 2.5% of the transaction or ₹500, whichever is higher, plus immediate interest).
- **Over-limit Fee:** Charged if spending exceeds the approved credit limit (usually 2.5% of the over-limit amount or ₹500).

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **Billing Cycle Transparency:** RBI mandates that banks must send credit card statements at least 14 days before the due date to give customers sufficient time to pay.
- **No Unsolicited Cards:** Banks are strictly prohibited from issuing credit cards or upgrading limits without the customer's explicit consent. Violations attract heavy penalties (double the billed amount as compensation).
- **Interest-free Period Waiver:** If a card has unpaid dues from the previous cycle, the interest-free period on new purchases is suspended until the balance is fully cleared.

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Calculate and Pay the Bill:
1. **Receive Statement:** Check your bill details on the statement date via email or app.
2. **Review Transactions:** Verify all listed transactions. If you find errors, report them immediately.
3. **Select Payment Option:**
   - **Recommended:** Select "Pay Total Outstanding" to avoid interest.
   - **Emergency Only:** Select "Pay Minimum Amount Due" (usually 5%) to avoid late fees, knowing that high interest will apply on the remaining balance.
4. **Make Payment:** Pay online via Net Banking, UPI, debit card, or auto-debit before the due date.

### How to Convert a Purchase into EMIs:
1. **Make Transaction:** Buy a product (e.g., laptop) using your credit card.
2. **Open App:** Go to the card transaction history on your bank's app within 3 to 7 days.
3. **Select Conversion:** Click on 'Convert to EMI' next to the transaction.
4. **Choose Tenure:** Select the repayment tenure (e.g., 3, 6, 9, or 12 months) and review the interest rate and processing fees.
5. **Confirm:** Submit the request. The amount is blocked from your limit and billed as EMIs monthly.

## Safety Tips
- **Never Pay Only Minimum Due Regularly:** Paying only the minimum due leads to compounding interest debt; always aim to pay the total outstanding.
- **Do not share OTP/CVV:** No bank representative will ask for your card's CVV or transaction OTP.
- **Set Spending Alerts:** Set limit alerts on your card app to notify you when spending crosses a certain threshold (e.g., 30% of your credit limit).
- **Disable International Usage:** Turn off international and contactless features when not travelling.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Withdrawing Cash from ATM:** Using a credit card at an ATM, which incurs high advance fees and high interest starting from day one (no grace period).
- **Maxing Out the Card:** Utilizing 90% to 100% of the credit limit, which signals credit hunger to bureaus and drops your CIBIL score.
- **Ignoring Due Dates:** Forgetting due dates, leading to late fees, high interest, and negative credit marks.
- **Applying for Too Many Cards:** Submitting multiple card applications in a short period, which leads to hard inquiries and lowers your credit score.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is a credit card?** A card that allows you to make purchases on credit up to a pre-approved limit, to be repaid later.
2. **How does a credit card differ from a debit card?** A debit card uses your own bank balance; a credit card uses money borrowed from the bank.
3. **What is the interest-free period?** A grace period of 20 to 50 days during which no interest is charged if the bill is paid in full.
4. **What is the credit limit?** The maximum amount of money you are allowed to borrow on the card at any given time.
5. **What is the Minimum Amount Due?** The minimum payment (usually 5% of outstanding) required by the due date to avoid late fees, though interest still applies on the rest.
6. **What is a cash advance?** Cash withdrawn from an ATM using a credit card. It has no interest-free period and carries high fees.
7. **Is CIBIL score important for a credit card?** Yes, a good CIBIL score (750+) is preferred to get card approval and higher limits.
8. **What is a lifetime free credit card?** A card that has no annual or joining maintenance fees.
9. **Can I convert my credit card bill into EMIs?** Yes, you can convert specific transactions or the total bill into monthly EMIs through the bank app.
10. **What is an billing cycle?** The monthly period during which card transactions are recorded, ending with the bill generation.
11. **What happens if I miss my credit card payment?** You face late payment fees, interest charges on the outstanding balance, and a drop in your CIBIL score.
12. **Can a bank issue a credit card without my consent?** No, RBI rules strictly ban the issuance of unsolicited credit cards.
13. **What is the interest rate on unpaid credit card bills?** Typically 3% to 4% per month (36% to 48% per year), which is extremely high.
14. **What is a secured credit card?** A card issued against a Fixed Deposit (FD) as collateral, ideal for beginners or those with low CIBIL scores.
15. **Can I use my credit card internationally?** Yes, if international usage is enabled in your card controls.
16. **What is a chargeback on a credit card?** A dispute filed by a cardholder to reverse a fraudulent or disputed transaction.
17. **What is a credit utilization ratio?** The percentage of your credit limit that you use. It is best to keep it below 30%.
18. **Can I transfer my credit card balance to another card?** Yes, some banks offer balance transfer facilities to move outstanding dues to a lower-interest card.
19. **What is the CVV number?** A 3-digit security code on the back of the card used for online transactions.
20. **Can I close my credit card at any time?** Yes, by paying off all outstanding dues and submitting a closure request to the bank.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Paying the Minimum Amount Due means no interest will be charged. **Fact:** Paying the minimum due only saves you from late fees; high interest is still charged on the remaining balance.
2. **Myth:** Having multiple credit cards destroys your CIBIL score. **Fact:** Having multiple cards does not hurt CIBIL as long as you pay all bills on time and keep total credit utilization below 30%.
3. **Myth:** Lifetime free cards have hidden monthly charges. **Fact:** Lifetime free cards have zero annual or joining fees, though interest and transaction-specific fees still apply if you don't pay on time.
4. **Myth:** You should never use credit cards. **Fact:** Responsible credit card usage is highly beneficial for building a credit history and gaining reward points.
5. **Myth:** Banks can increase my credit limit whenever they want. **Fact:** Banks must obtain explicit consent from the cardholder before increasing the credit limit.
6. **Myth:** ATM cash withdrawals on credit cards have a 45-day grace period. **Fact:** Cash advances have zero grace period; interest starts accumulating from the second the cash is withdrawn.
7. **Myth:** If my card is lost, I am liable for all fraudulent transactions. **Fact:** If reported to the bank within 3 working days, the customer has zero liability for unauthorized transactions.
8. **Myth:** Credit card interest rates are regulated and capped by the government. **Fact:** Credit card interest rates are determined by individual banks based on market risk and are not capped.
9. **Myth:** You cannot get a credit card if you are unemployed. **Fact:** You can get a secured credit card backed by a Fixed Deposit without proving income.
10. **Myth:** Closing a credit card always improves your CIBIL score. **Fact:** Closing a card reduces your total available credit limit, which can increase your credit utilization ratio and lower your score temporarily.

## Conversation Examples
1. **User:** Maine credit card ka minimum amount due pay kar diya hai, toh kya mujhe interest dena hoga?
   **Assistant:** Haan, minimum due pay karne se aap sirf late fees se bachte hain. Baki bache hue balance par bank transaction ki date se daily basis par interest charge karega, jo kaafi mehanga hota hai. Isliye total outstanding pay karna hi sabse behtar hai.
2. **User:** Credit card se ATM se cash nikalna kaisa hai?
   **Assistant:** Credit card se cash nikalna (Cash Advance) bilkul nahi karna chahiye. Is par interest-free period nahi milta, pehle din se hi interest lagna shuru ho jata hai aur ek heavy cash transaction fee bhi lagti hai.
3. **User:** Mera CIBIL score 650 hai, kya mujhe credit card mil sakta hai?
   **Assistant:** Aamtaur par banks 750 se upar CIBIL score ko achha mante hain. 650 par card milna mushkil hai. Lekin aap bank me Fixed Deposit (FD) karwa kar uske badle me 'Secured Credit Card' le sakte hain jisse aapka CIBIL score bhi sudhar jayega.
4. **User:** Lifetime free credit card ka kya matlab hai?
   **Assistant:** Iska matlab hai ki aapko card ke liye koi saalana (annual) ya joining fee nahi deni hogi. Lekin agar aap bill time par nahi chukate, toh interest aur late fees waisi hi lagenge jaise normal cards par lagte hain.
5. **User:** Meri marzi ke bina bank ne mera credit card limit badha diya hai. Kya ye sahi hai?
   **Assistant:** Nahi, RBI ke guidelines ke mutabik bank bina aapki permission ke credit limit nahi badha sakta. Aap iski shikayat bank manager se kar sakte hain aur unhe limit wapas kam karne ko keh sakte hain.
6. **User:** Credit card bill ko EMI me kaise convert karein?
   **Assistant:** Agar aapne koi badi kharidari ki hai (jaise ₹3,000 se upar), toh aap bill generate hone se pehle bank ki mobile app me transaction history me jaakar use 'Convert to EMI' option se installments me badal sakte hain. Is par processing fee aur interest lagta hai.
7. **User:** Card block karne ke liye kya karna hoga?
   **Assistant:** Aap turant mobile banking app par jaakar card block/hotlist kar sakte hain, ya bank ke customer care number par call karke card block karwa sakte hain.
8. **User:** CIBIL score sudharne ke liye credit card ka use kaise karein?
   **Assistant:** Har mahine card ka use karein par limit ka sirf 30% tak hi spend karein (Credit Utilization Ratio low rakhein) aur bill generate hone par total outstanding bill due date se pehle pay karein.
9. **User:** Credit card billing cycle kya hota hai?
   **Assistant:** Ye aamtaur par 30 din ka period hota hai jisme aapke saare kharche note hote hain. Cycle khatam hone par bill generate hota hai aur uske baad aapko payment karne ke liye lagbhag 20 din ka grace period milta hai.
10. **User:** Kya main apna credit card close kar sakta hu?
    **Assistant:** Haan, aapko apne saare outstanding dues (baki bill) pay karne honge. Uske baad bank app se ya customer care ko call karke card close karne ki request de sakte hain.
11. **User:** Card statement me koi anjan charge dikh raha hai, kya karein?
    **Assistant:** Turant bank ko contact karein aur us transaction ko dispute karein. Bank iski jaanch karega aur agar wo charge galat hai toh use reverse kar dega.
12. **User:** Credit card limit aur cash limit me kya farq hai?
    **Assistant:** Credit limit wo total limit hai jo aap online/offline shopping ke liye use kar sakte hain. Cash limit iska ek chota part hota hai jo aap ATM se cash nikalne ke liye use kar sakte hain, jo ki avoid karna chahiye.
13. **User:** Kya credit card ka use unsafe hai?
    **Assistant:** Nahi, agar aap card details (CVV, OTP) kisi se share nahi karte aur card limits ko app ke zariye manage karte hain, toh credit card transactions safe hain.
14. **User:** Secured credit card kaise kaam karta hai?
    **Assistant:** Aap bank me ek Fixed Deposit (jaise ₹20,000) kholte hain, aur bank us FD ke 80% to 90% ke barabar ki limit ka credit card de deta hai. Agar aap payment nahi chukate, toh bank FD se recover kar leta hai.
15. **User:** Unsolicited credit card milne par kya karein?
    **Assistant:** Agar bank ne bina aapki permission ke card bheja hai aur active kar diya hai, toh aap RBI Ombudsman me complaint kar sakte hain. Bank ko iske liye harjana (penalty) dena hoga.
