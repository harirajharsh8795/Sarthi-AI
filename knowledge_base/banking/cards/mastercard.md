# Mastercard (मास्टरकार्ड)

## Overview

### English
Mastercard is a global payment processing technology network operated by Mastercard Incorporated, an American multinational financial services corporation. It facilitates electronic transactions globally, partnering with banks to issue co-branded debit, credit, and prepaid cards with global acceptance.

## Quick Summary
Mastercard operates one of the world's fastest payment processing networks, routing transactions across millions of merchants and ATMs in more than 210 countries. Mastercard does not issue cards directly to users; instead, it licenses its transaction routing platform to licensed banks, which manage the customer relationship, credit limits, interest rates, and banking fees.

In India, Mastercard is widely used for both domestic and international transactions. Mastercard categorizes its products into progressive tiers (Standard, Gold, Platinum, World, and World Elite), designed to offer varying levels of benefits, including domestic and international airport lounge access, dining privileges, and e-commerce buyer protection.

---

## Definition
Mastercard is a multinational financial services technology network that licenses its electronic retail transaction processing platform to financial institutions, routing authorization requests globally.

---

## Why It Matters
Understanding Mastercard is important for bank customers because:
- **Global Usability:** Provides high international acceptance, making overseas ATMs and foreign online merchants accessible.
- **Data Compliance History:** Teaches customers the importance of local data laws (Mastercard was briefly restricted by RBI in 2021 for non-compliance and restored in 2022 after complying with local data storage laws).
- **Lounge and Travel Protection:** Offers premium tier benefits like airport lounge vouchers and travel insurance, which customers must actively claim.

---

## How It Works
The electronic transaction routing cycle of a Mastercard card:

Cardholder swipes or taps Mastercard at a POS machine (in India or abroad)
↓
POS terminal sends transaction request to the merchant's acquiring bank
↓
Acquiring bank routes the transaction to Mastercard's processing network (Mastercard Network)
↓
Mastercard routes the request to the cardholder's issuing Bank in India
↓
Issuing Bank checks balance/limit, validates security PIN, and approves
↓
Mastercard routes approval back to the merchant's POS terminal
↓
Transaction completes; funds are settled between the banks within standard clearance cycles

## Eligibility
- **Savings/Current Account Holders:** Banks offer Mastercard debit cards to account holders based on minimum balance variants.
- **Credit Cards:** Offered to individuals who meet the bank's income criteria, employment stability, and CIBIL score requirements.
- **Fixed Deposit Backed:** Secured Mastercard credit cards can be issued against a bank Fixed Deposit (FD).

---

## Required Documents
- **Not Applicable:** For debit cards, an active bank account is required. For credit cards, standard income proofs (salary slips, ITR) and KYC (Aadhaar/PAN) must be submitted to the bank.

---

## Features
- **Mastercard Identity Check:** Multi-factor authentication (OTP) for secure online transactions.
- **Mastercard Contactless:** Tap-and-pay contactless technology for PIN-less payments up to ₹5,000 in India.
- **NCMC Integration:** Available NCMC wallet on select debit cards for public transport payments.
- **Varying Tiers:** Standard, Gold, Platinum, World, and World Elite variants.

---

## Benefits
- **World Elite Privileges:** Luxury travel offers, global hotel benefits, and dedicated concierge desk services.
- **Lounge Access:** Free access to premium domestic and international airport lounges (for Platinum, World, and World Elite variants).
- **E-commerce Protection:** Insurance cover on premium cards protecting against purchase damage or online delivery loss.

---

## Risks
- **Forex Markup Fee:** Charges of 2% to 3.5% apply on foreign currency transactions.
- **No Native UPI Link:** Unlike RuPay, Mastercard credit cards cannot be linked to UPI apps for merchant payments in India at present.
- **Annual Maintenance Cost:** Premium Mastercard variants (like World or World Elite) often have higher annual fees compared to domestic cards.

---

## Charges & Fees
Typical charges vary by bank and are subject to RBI guidelines:
- **Annual Maintenance Charges:** Classic cards range from ₹150 to ₹250. Platinum and World cards range from ₹250 to ₹1,000+ per year.
- **Cross-border Markup Fee:** 2% to 3.5% fee on foreign exchange transactions.
- **Cash Withdrawal Fee (ATMs):** Standard ATM usage charges apply beyond free limits.
- **MDR (Merchant Discount Rate):** Merchants pay a processing fee (typically 1% to 2%) to banks to accept Mastercard cards.

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **Data Localization Compliance:** Mastercard complies fully with RBI's data localization rules, storing transaction data on servers in India.
- **Card Choice Mandate (2024):** RBI directives require banks to give customers the choice to select their preferred card network (RuPay, Visa, or Mastercard) at the time of account opening or renewal.

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Select and Activate a Mastercard:
1. **Choose Network:** Request your bank for a Mastercard at the time of opening an account or renewing a card.
2. **Receive Kit:** Collect the card welcome kit sent by the bank.
3. **Generate PIN:** Go to the bank's ATM, select Green PIN/PIN Generation, enter the OTP sent to your registered mobile number, and set your 4-digit code.
4. **Configure Channels:** Log into your mobile banking app, navigate to Card Controls, and enable domestic e-commerce or POS usage as needed.

---

## Safety Tips
- **Keep Card Controls Locked:** Disable international and online usage in the app when you are not using them.
- **Do not share OTPs:** Keep transaction OTPs and your card's 3-digit CVV number confidential.
- **Check for Skimmers:** Verify that the ATM card slot is secure and does not look tampered with before inserting the card.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Assuming Mastercard works on UPI Credit:** Trying to link a Mastercard credit card to Google Pay/PhonePe for QR code scanning (which is currently only supported for RuPay cards).
- **Ignoring Forex Markup Fees:** Using a standard Mastercard card for heavy shopping on international websites without realizing that a 3.5% currency conversion fee will be added to the bill.
- **Using Premium Cards without Claiming Benefits:** Paying high annual fees for a Mastercard World card but neglecting to use the free airport lounge or golf privileges.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is a Mastercard?** A payment card that runs on the global payment network operated by Mastercard Incorporated.
2. **Who issues Mastercard cards?** Banks and financial institutions issue Mastercard cards; Mastercard only provides the payment processing technology network.
3. **What is Mastercard Contactless?** Mastercard's contactless tap-and-pay technology for payments under ₹5,000 without entering a PIN.
4. **Is Mastercard an Indian company?** No, Mastercard is an American multinational corporation headquartered in New York, USA.
5. **What are the different tiers of Mastercard cards?** Standard, Gold, Platinum, World, and World Elite.
6. **Can I use my Mastercard at any ATM?** Yes, it can be used at any ATM displaying the Mastercard logo globally.
7. **Is Mastercard credit card linkable to UPI?** No, as of now, only RuPay credit cards are linkable to UPI in India.
8. **What is the annual fee for a Mastercard debit card?** Usually between ₹150 and ₹500 plus GST, depending on the card tier and your bank.
9. **What is Mastercard Identity Check?** A security system that requires an OTP or password to approve online transactions.
10. **Can I get lounge access with a Mastercard Platinum card?** Yes, many banks offer complimentary domestic lounge access on Mastercard Platinum debit or credit cards.
11. **What is a cross-border markup fee?** A fee charged when you use your card in a foreign currency, typically 2% to 3.5% of the transaction amount.
12. **How is Mastercard different from Visa?** Both are global payment networks with similar features; acceptance rates and benefits are virtually identical.
13. **Can I choose Mastercard when opening a bank account?** Yes, under RBI guidelines, banks must offer customers a choice of card networks (RuPay, Visa, Mastercard).
14. **What is the maximum limit of a Mastercard card?** ATM withdrawal and purchase limits depend on your card tier.
15. **Does Mastercard offer zero liability for fraud?** Yes, Mastercard offers a Global Zero Liability policy for unauthorized card transactions if reported promptly.
16. **Why is my Mastercard declined internationally?** You must enable "International Usage" in your bank app settings before using the card abroad.
17. **What is a Mastercard World Elite card?** The highest premium tier card offered by Mastercard, featuring high limits, lifestyle benefits, and dedicated concierge services.
18. **Can I use a Mastercard to pay utility bills?** Yes, Mastercard is widely accepted on all bill payment portals.
19. **What is the chargeback process for Mastercard?** If you face fraud or do not receive goods, you can file a dispute (chargeback) with your bank to reverse the transaction.
20. **Can I get a zero-balance account with a Mastercard?** Yes, some banks offer Mastercard cards with zero-balance accounts, but they may charge an annual card maintenance fee.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Mastercard is a bank. **Fact:** Mastercard does not issue cards or open accounts; cards are issued by banks using Mastercard's payment network technology.
2. **Myth:** Mastercard is currently banned in India. **Fact:** The RBI ban was lifted in June 2022 after Mastercard complied with data localization guidelines. It is fully operational now.
3. **Myth:** All Mastercard cards have free international lounge access. **Fact:** Lounge access is only available on premium tiers (Platinum, World, World Elite) and depends on the issuing bank's terms.
4. **Myth:** Mastercard is accepted everywhere in India but RuPay is not. **Fact:** Both networks are accepted at virtually all ATMs and POS terminals in India.
5. **Myth:** Mastercard credit cards can be used for UPI payments. **Fact:** Mastercard credit cards cannot be linked to UPI for merchant QR payments in India at present.
6. **Myth:** Tap-and-pay on Mastercard has no safety limits. **Fact:** Yes, it is strictly capped at ₹5,000 per transaction by the RBI, above which a PIN is required.
7. **Myth:** Mastercard charges the customer a fee for every swipe. **Fact:** The merchant pays the processing fee (MDR); customer transactions are free unless a specific convenience fee applies.
8. **Myth:** Visa and Mastercard are the same company. **Fact:** They are separate, competing companies.
9. **Myth:** You can reset your Mastercard card PIN on the Mastercard website. **Fact:** PIN resets can only be done through your issuing bank's ATM, app, or website.
10. **Myth:** Mastercard cards cannot be blocked instantly. **Fact:** Mastercard cards can be blocked instantly in real-time through your bank's hotlisting service.

1. **User:** Mastercard aur RuPay card me kya farq hai?
   **Assistant:** Mastercard ek US-based global network hai jo duniyabhar me chalte hain, jabki RuPay ek Indian network hai jise NPCI ne banaya hai. RuPay credit card ko aap UPI se link kar sakte hain, par Mastercard card par ye facility abhi available nahi hai.

3. **User:** Mastercard World card ke kya benefits hain?
   **Assistant:** Mastercard World ek premium tier card hai jisme complimentary airport lounge access, 24/7 concierge service, hotel bookings par discounts aur free travel insurance jaise benefits milte hain.
4. **User:** Mastercard card par international transactions par kitna charge lagta hai?
   **Assistant:** International transaction karne par bank aapse 2% se 3.5% tak ka 'Forex Markup Fee' charge karte hain currency convert karne ke liye.
5. **User:** Kya main account kholte waqt bank se Mastercard card maang sakta hu?
   **Assistant:** Haan, RBI ke rules ke mutabik bank ko aapko card network choose karne ka option dena hoga. Aap application form me Mastercard select kar sakte hain.
6. **User:** Contactless payment kya hai jo mere Mastercard card par likha hai?
   **Assistant:** Aap isse shop par swipe machine par bina PIN dale, bas card ko tap karke ₹5,000 tak ka payment kar sakte hain.
7. **User:** Mastercard credit card billing disputes kaise solve hote hain?
   **Assistant:** Agar aapke card par koi unauthorized transaction ho jaye, toh aap bank me chargeback dispute file kar sakte hain. Bank Mastercard network ke zariye transaction ki jaanch karke paise wapas dila sakta hai.
8. **User:** Verification code kya hota hai online shopping ke waqt?
   **Assistant:** Online shopping ke waqt secure checkout ke liye bank aapke phone par ek OTP bhejta hai jise Mastercard Identity Check code kehte hain, iske bina payment nahi hoti.
9. **User:** Mastercard Classic card par kitne benefits milte hain?
   **Assistant:** Mastercard Classic ek entry-level basic card hai. Isme transactions aur ATM cash withdrawal ki facility milti hai par lounge access ya premium discount benefits nahi hote.
10. **User:** Kya Mastercard credit card ka use unsafe hai?
    **Assistant:** Nahi, Mastercard card me chip and pin safety ke sath online Secure verification hota hai. Ye tabhi unsafe hai agar aap apna PIN ya OTP kisi se share karein.
11. **User:** Mastercard card block kaise karein agar kho jaye?
    **Assistant:** Process same hai. Aap bank mobile app se, net banking se, ya bank ke dedicated customer care number par call karke card block karwa sakte hain.
12. **User:** Secured Mastercard card kaise milta hai?
    **Assistant:** Aap bank me jaakar Fixed Deposit (FD) kholte hain aur uske badle me bank aapko card limit dekar secured Mastercard credit card de deta hai.
13. **User:** Swipe machines par Mastercard acceptance charge kya hota hai?
    **Assistant:** Swipe machine par payments accept karne par bank dukandar se MDR (Merchant Discount Rate) charge karte hain. Customer ke liye ye free hota hai.
14. **User:** Kya main foreign websites par Mastercard card use kar sakta hu?
    **Assistant:** Haan, Mastercard cards global websites (jaise Netflix, Google, international stores) par payments ke liye accept hote hain, bas online international usage enable hona chahiye.
15. **User:** Mera Mastercard card swipe karne par read nahi ho raha.
    **Assistant:** Agar card ka chip ya magnetic strip ganda ya scratch ho gaya hai, toh swipe machine use read nahi kar payegi. Aapko bank se naya card request karna hoga.
