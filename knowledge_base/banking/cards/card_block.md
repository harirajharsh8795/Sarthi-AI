# Card Block (कार्ड ब्लॉक करना / हॉटलिस्टिंग)

## Overview

### English
Card blocking (also called hotlisting) is a critical security procedure that deactivates a lost, stolen, or compromised debit or credit card. It immediately stops all further transactions (ATM, online, and POS) to prevent unauthorized access and protect account funds.

### Hindi
कार्ड ब्लॉक करना (Card Block / हॉटलिस्टिंग) एक महत्वपूर्ण सुरक्षा प्रक्रिया है जो खोए हुए, चोरी हुए या संदिग्ध डेबिट या क्रेडिट कार्ड को निष्क्रिय कर देती है। यह खाते की सुरक्षा करने और अनधिकृत लेन-देन को रोकने के लिए तुरंत सभी एटीएम, ऑनलाइन और पीओएस (POS) भुगतानों को रोक देती है।

### Hinglish
Card block karna (jise hotlisting bhi kehte hain) ek bahut zaroori security process hai jo aapke khoye hue, chori hue ya fraud transactions wale debit ya credit card ko block (deactivate) kar deta hai. Isse card se hone wale saare payments, ATM withdrawals aur online shopping turant ruk jate hain.

## Quick Summary
Blocking a bank card is the primary action a customer must take in case of card theft, loss, or detection of unauthorized transaction alerts. Under RBI guidelines, banks must offer multiple channels for instant card blocking, available 24/7. These channels include sending an SMS from the registered mobile number, blocking via mobile banking apps or net banking portals, calling toll-free helplines, or physically visiting the branch.

Many modern banks also offer a "Temporary Block" or "Switch Off" feature, which allows customers to temporarily pause transactions (useful if the card is misplaced at home) and unblock it later. A "Permanent Block" (Hotlisting), however, is irreversible. Once permanently blocked, the card cannot be used again, and the bank must issue a new card to the customer.

---

## Definition
Card Blocking is the immediate, system-wide deactivation of a debit or credit card in the bank's database, neutralizing the card's magnetic strip, chip, and CVV to reject any incoming authorization requests.

---

## Why It Matters
Understanding card blocking is crucial because:
- **Protects Life Savings:** A compromised debit card can lead to the entire savings account being emptied; blocking the card stops the financial loss immediately.
- **Enforces Zero Liability:** As per RBI rules, to claim zero liability for fraudulent transactions, the customer must report the fraud and block the card within 3 days of the incident.
- **Prevents Misuse of Lost Assets:** Lost cards can be used by third parties for contactless payments (tap-and-pay under ₹5,000) that do not require PIN entry.

---

## How It Works
The operational flow for blocking a card:

Loss of card or fraudulent SMS alert detected
↓
Customer chooses blocking channel (SMS, App, Call, or Branch)
↓
Customer inputs card details or registered identity proofs
↓
Bank verification system matches the details
↓
System flags the card status as "Hotlisted" or "Blocked" in real-time
↓
All subsequent authorization requests from networks (RuPay/Visa/Mastercard) are rejected
↓
Bank sends confirmation SMS containing Block Reference Number
↓
New replacement card request is registered (optional)

---

## Eligibility
- **Applicable Cards:** All active debit cards, credit cards, prepaid cards, and forex cards issued by RBI-regulated banks and NBFCs.
- **Who can block:** The primary cardholder, joint account holders, or authorized family members (in case of emergencies via phone call after identity verification).

---

## Required Documents
- **Not Applicable for Online/SMS Blocking:** No physical documents are required. You only need:
  - Registered mobile number for SMS/OTP.
  - Net Banking user ID and password.
- **For Branch Visits:** Account number, identity proof (Aadhaar or PAN Card), and a written card block application letter.

---

## Features
- **Permanent Block (Hotlisting):** Irreversible block. A new card must be issued.
- **Temporary Block/Freeze:** Reversible block via mobile app; card can be unblocked (switched on) by the customer at any time.
- **Specific Channel Control:** Option to block only specific transaction channels (e.g., block international transactions while keeping domestic ATM withdrawals active).

---

## Benefits
- **Instant Security:** Real-time deactivation prevents immediate losses.
- **Proof of Action:** The bank issues a unique block reference number which acts as legal proof of reporting in case of disputes.
- **Convenient Access:** 24/7 accessibility through toll-free helpline numbers and online banking apps.

---

## Risks
- **Delayed Blocking Risk:** If blocking is delayed by even a few minutes after receiving a fraud alert, significant funds can be withdrawn.
- **Transaction Bounces:** Active standing instructions (utility bills, OTT subscriptions) linked to a blocked credit/debit card will fail.
- **Fake Customer Care Numbers:** Calling fake numbers found on Google search instead of official bank helplines can lead to further fraud.

---

## Charges & Fees
- **Card Blocking Charges:** Nil (RBI guidelines prohibit banks from charging any fee for blocking a card).
- **Replacement Card Fee:** Varies by bank and card variant (typically ₹150 to ₹300 plus GST for issuing a new replacement card).

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **Toll-Free Helpline Requirement:** RBI mandates that all banks must provide a dedicated 24/7 toll-free helpline number and automated IVR options for card blocking.
- **Immediate Acknowledgment:** Banks must send an instant SMS or email confirmation with a reference number to the customer acknowledging the block.
- **Liability Freeze:** Once the card is blocked, the customer has zero liability for any subsequent unauthorized transactions that occur on that card.

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Block Card via Mobile App:
1. **Log in:** Open and log into your bank's mobile banking application.
2. **Navigate to Cards:** Go to the 'Cards', 'Card Services', or 'Manage Cards' section.
3. **Select Card:** Select the specific debit or credit card you want to block.
4. **Choose Block Option:** Click on 'Block Card' or 'Hotlist Card'.
5. **Select Type:** Choose "Permanent Block" (if card is lost/stolen) or "Temporary Block" (if card is misplaced).
6. **Confirm with OTP:** Confirm the action by entering the OTP sent to your registered mobile number.
7. **Save Reference:** Save the transaction confirmation message and block reference number.

### How to Block Card via SMS:
1. **Compose Message:** Open the messaging app on your registered mobile number.
2. **Type Code:** Type the bank's specific SMS block code (e.g., "BLOCK CARD <last 4 digits of card number>"). *Verify the exact format on your bank's official website.*
3. **Send:** Send the SMS to the bank's designated number (e.g., 567676).
4. **Verify SMS Receipt:** Look for the incoming confirmation SMS stating that your card has been successfully hotlisted.

---

## Safety Tips
- **Save Official Helpline Numbers:** Save your bank's official toll-free card blocking number in your phone contacts list for emergency access.
- **Never Use Google Search blindly for Helplines:** Fraudsters often upload fake customer care numbers on search engines. Only trust the number printed on your bank statement or the bank's official website.
- **Unlink Cards on App Before Blocking:** If possible, temporarily set transaction limits to zero in the app before initiating a permanent block.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Assuming Card is Blocked without SMS Confirmation:** Believing the card is blocked after a call disconnects, without waiting for the official confirmation SMS containing the reference number.
- **Delaying Block to Search for Card:** Wasting critical hours searching for a misplaced card instead of temporarily switching it off via the banking app.
- **Sharing OTP during Blocking Call:** Sharing transaction OTPs with callers who claim they need it to block your card. The bank does not require transaction OTPs to block a card.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What does hotlisting a card mean?** It is the process of permanently blocking a card in the bank's system to prevent transactions.
2. **Can I unblock a permanently blocked card?** No, a permanent block is irreversible. You must apply for a new card.
3. **Is there any charge to block a card?** No, banks cannot charge any fee for blocking a card.
4. **Can I block my card temporarily?** Yes, most bank apps offer a "Switch On/Off" or "Temporary Block" feature.
5. **What happens to my account balance when I block my debit card?** Your balance remains completely safe and untouched. Only the card is deactivated; bank account transactions (transfers, deposits) continue normally.
6. **Can I use UPI after blocking my debit card?** Yes, UPI transactions (like Google Pay, PhonePe) usually continue to work because they are linked to the account, not the card. However, you cannot reset your UPI PIN without an active debit card.
7. **What is a block reference number?** A unique number sent by the bank confirming the date and time your card was blocked. It is crucial for resolving liability disputes.
8. **Can I block my card if I don't know my card number?** Yes, by calling customer care, verifying your identity (account number, name, DOB), the executive can locate and block the card.
9. **How do I block a credit card?** You can block it using the bank's app, net banking, or by calling the dedicated credit card helpline.
10. **What is the zero liability rule?** A rule where customers are not held responsible for fraudulent transactions if they report the loss and block the card within 3 working days.
11. **Do I have to visit the branch to block my card?** No, online apps, SMS, and phone calls are faster and recommended for instant blocking.
12. **Can I block a joint account card?** Yes, any joint holder can request the block of cards linked to the account.
13. **How long does it take to block a card?** The block is processed in real-time and happens instantly.
14. **What should I do if I find a card I blocked permanently?** Cut it into pieces and discard it. It is useless once hotlisted.
15. **Will my auto-debits stop if I block my card?** Yes, any recurring payments linked directly to the card number will fail. Dues linked directly to the account number (like NACH) will continue.
16. **Why did the bank block my card without my request?** Banks may block cards if they detect suspicious transactions or if KYC updates are pending.
17. **Can I block my card through an ATM?** Some banks offer option to block cards at their own ATMs.
18. **Can I block my card from abroad?** Yes, by logging into net banking or calling the bank's international helpline.
19. **How much does a replacement card cost?** Typically ₹150 to ₹300 depending on the bank.
20. **Who is responsible for card security?** The cardholder is responsible for keeping PINs safe; once reported lost, the liability shifts to the bank.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** If I block my debit card, my bank account is closed. **Fact:** Only the card is deactivated. The savings or current account remains active.
2. **Myth:** Temporarily switching off a card cancels all subscriptions. **Fact:** It only pauses card debits. Subscriptions are not cancelled; transactions will simply fail until you switch the card back on.
3. **Myth:** I need to report to the police before blocking the card. **Fact:** You must block the card first to stop the fraud. Filing a police report/FIR is a subsequent step for claiming insurance or disputing liability.
4. **Myth:** Banks charge money to block a card in emergencies. **Fact:** Blocking is a free service mandated by the RBI.
5. **Myth:** A blocked card can still receive tap-and-pay transactions. **Fact:** No transaction of any kind can be processed on a hotlisted card.
6. **Myth:** The bank can unblock a card if I find it after permanent blocking. **Fact:** Once marked as hotlisted, the card is permanently dead; banks cannot reactivate it due to security system architectures.
7. **Myth:** If I delete the banking app, my card is blocked. **Fact:** Deleting the app does not affect the card; the card remains active.
8. **Myth:** I can block my card by replying to the fraud SMS. **Fact:** Never reply to fraud SMS alerts. Always use the official SMS codes or banking channels to block.
9. **Myth:** Only the branch manager can block a card. **Fact:** Any bank representative at customer care, or automated IVR, can block it instantly.
10. **Myth:** Standard BSBDA (Jan Dhan) cards cannot be blocked via SMS. **Fact:** SMS blocking is available for all card types across Scheduled Commercial Banks.

   **Assistant:** Haan, UPI transactions chalte rahenge kyunki wo bank account se linked hote hain, card se nahi. Par naya UPI PIN generate karne ke liye aapko active card ki zaroorat padegi.
9. **User:** ATM card swipe slot me fast gaya hai, kya block karna hoga?
   **Assistant:** Agar card ATM se bahar nahi aa raha, toh safety ke liye use instantly block kar dein aur bank branch ko suchit karein.

    **Assistant:** Card block 24/7 kabhi bhi kiya ja sakta hai. Iski koi limit nahi hoti.

    **Assistant:** Haan, credit card block karne ka process bhi same hai. Aap app se ya credit card customer care helpline par call karke block karwa sakte hain.
13. **User:** Kya transaction limit zero karne se card block ho jata hai?
    **Assistant:** Limit zero karne se payments ruk jayengi, par ye permanent block nahi hai. Complete safety ke liye hotlisting zaroori hai.
14. **User:** NRI card abroad se block kaise karein?
    **Assistant:** NRI customers net banking portal ke zariye ya bank ki international toll-free NRI helpline par call karke card block karwa sakte hain.
15. **User:** Block reference number kya kaam aata hai?
    **Assistant:** Agar card block karne ke baad bhi koi transaction ho jata hai, toh block reference number ye proof hai ki aapne report kar diya tha, jisse aapki koi liability nahi banti.
