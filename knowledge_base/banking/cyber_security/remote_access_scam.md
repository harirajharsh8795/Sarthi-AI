# Remote Access Scam (रिमोट एक्सेस स्क्रीन-शेयरing घोटाला)

## Overview

### English
A remote access scam is a cyber fraud method where attackers trick victims into downloading screen-sharing applications (like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or RustDesk) on their phones or computers. This allows the fraudsters to view the victim's screen in real-time, intercept transactional OTPs, and steal bank credentials.

### Hindi
रिमोट एक्सेस घोटाला (Remote Access Scam / स्क्रीन-शेयरिंग धोखाधड़ी) एक साइबर धोखाधड़ी पद्धति है जिसमें हमलावर पीड़ितों को उनके फोन या कंप्यूटर पर स्क्रीन-शेयरिंग एप्लिकेशन (जैसे AnyDesk, TeamViewer, या RustDesk) डाउनलोड करने के लिए बहकाते हैं। यह जालसाजों को वास्तविक समय में पीड़ित की स्क्रीन देखने, ट्रांजैक्शन ओटीपी (OTP) को देखने और बैंक विवरण चोरी करने की अनुमति देता है।

### Hinglish
Remote access scam (ya screen sharing fraud) ek aisa fraud hota hai jisme thag log call par bank executive ya helpline support bankar baat karte hain aur aapko apne phone ya computer par screen sharing apps (jaise AnyDesk, TeamViewer, RustDesk) download karne ko kehte hain. In apps ko download karne ke baad unhe aapki mobile screen ka poora control mil jata hai, jisse wo aapke screen par aane wale OTP aur net banking passwords ko asani se dekhkar chura lete hain.

## Quick Summary
Remote access scams target the trust of digital banking users. Scammers pretend to help the user resolve a technical issue, update their KYC, or process a refund. They instruct the victim to search for and install a remote desktop control application from the official Google Play Store or Apple App Store.

Once the app is installed, the victim is asked to share the unique 9-digit or 10-digit "Access Code" displayed on the app. The moment the victim provides this code and clicks "Allow Connection," the fraudster gains complete control over the device. The fraudster asks the victim to log into their net banking or make a minor ₹1 payment. By watching the screen, the fraudster notes down the credentials, intercepts the OTP, and performs unauthorized transfers while keeping the victim's screen active.

---

## Definition
A Remote Access Scam is a social engineering attack that exploits legitimate desktop sharing and remote support software to bypass host-based authentication protocols, enabling attackers to execute unauthorized financial transactions via screen mirror replication.

---

## Why It Matters
Being aware of remote access scams is vital because:
- **Bypasses Security Apps:** Traditional bank apps cannot detect this fraud because the transaction is executed from the victim's own physical device and IP address.
- **Accidental Loss of Control:** Giving screen access means criminals can read your chats, view personal photos, and open other connected apps (UPI, email, notes).
- **Fast Money Siphoning:** Within minutes of connection, scammers can drain multiple linked accounts, activate credit cards, and transfer funds.

---

## How It Works
The standard operational sequence of an AnyDesk/TeamViewer remote access scam:

Customer complains about a pending refund on a public forum or calls a fake helpline number
↓
Fraudster contacts customer: "I am from the bank technical support. I will refund your money."
↓
Fraudster tells customer: "Install the verification support app (e.g., AnyDesk) from Play Store."
↓
Customer installs the app and receives a 9-digit connection code
↓
Fraudster asks: "Please read out the 9-digit code for remote verification."
↓
Customer shares code and clicks 'Allow' or 'Accept' warning prompts
↓
Fraudster can now see customer's phone screen in real-time on their laptop
↓
Fraudster instructs: "Open your bank app/UPI and enter your login PIN to verify refund."
↓
Customer types PIN (fraudster records it visually)
↓
Fraudster initiates fund transfers from their side; OTP pops up on customer's screen
↓
Fraudster reads the OTP directly from the screen share and completes the transaction

## Features
- **Legitimate App Exploitation:** Using certified, safe remote-support software for illegal hacking.
- **Visual Screen Interception:** Recording passwords and PINs as they are typed.
- **Permission Bypassing:** Tricking users into tapping "Allow" on security warnings.
- **Remote Keyboard/Mouse Control:** Allowing the fraudster to type or click items on the victim's screen.

---

## Benefits of Knowledge
- **App Block Awareness:** Understanding that banks never ask you to install third-party screen-sharing apps to verify details.
- **Urgent Action:** Knowing how to turn off internet or uninstall screen-sharing apps instantly if control is compromised.

---

## Risks
- **Total Asset Loss:** Complete drain of linked bank accounts and credit limits.
- **Persistent Spyware:** If the app is not uninstalled, scammers can reconnect later when the phone is idle.
- **Sensitive Data Theft:** Exposure of private gallery photos, emails, Aadhaar details, and contact lists.

---

## Charges & Fees
- **Fraud Reporting:** Nil.

## RBI / Government Rules
- **No Remote Access Mandate:** RBI guidelines explicitly prohibit banks from asking customers to download any third-party software or apps to resolve transaction issues.
- **Zero Liability Protection Rules:** If a customer voluntarily shares a remote control code and clicks "Allow", it is classified as customer sharing (negligence), making refund recovery dependent on the speed of reporting to 1930 Cyber Helpline.
- **Bank App Blocking Security:** RBI encourages banks to build screens blocks in their banking apps that automatically turn the app screen black if a screen-sharing app is running in the background.

## Step-by-Step Process

### What to do if you realize you are connected to a scammer:
1. **Turn off Internet:** Instantly swipe down and turn off Mobile Data and Wi-Fi, or put the phone on Airplane Mode. This cuts the remote connection immediately.
2. **Uninstall the App:** Locate the remote access app (AnyDesk, TeamViewer, RustDesk, etc.) and click 'Uninstall' or 'Delete' immediately.
3. **Change All Passwords:** Change your net banking passwords, UPI PINs, and email passwords immediately from a different safe device.
4. **Notify the Bank:** Call your bank helpline to freeze your accounts and block your linked cards.
5. **Report to Cyber Cell:** Dial **1930** or log onto **cybercrime.gov.in** to report the fraudster's phone number.

---

## Safety Tips
- **Never Download on Instruction:** Never download any app (especially AnyDesk, QuickSupport, TeamViewer) based on directions from a caller.
- **Ignore Warning Prompts:** If you open these apps and see a red security warning screen alert, do not ignore it. It is there to warn you about scams.
- **Do not share Connection IDs:** Treat the 9-digit app code like an OTP or password; never share it with anyone.
- **Check Bank App Blackout:** If your banking app screen goes black, check if any screen recording or sharing app is running and close it first.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Allowing Permissions Blindly:** Clicking "Accept" or "Allow Control" on pop-up boxes without reading the warning text.
- **Leaving the App Active:** Forgetting to uninstall the app after the call ends, allowing fraudsters to access the device later.
- **Typing PINs while screen sharing is active:** Opening banking apps and typing passwords while a remote participant is connected.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is a remote access scam?** A scam where criminals trick you into installing screen-sharing apps to steal your banking details.
2. **Name some common remote access apps.** AnyDesk, TeamViewer, RustDesk, UltraViewer, AirDroid, QuickSupport.
3. **Are AnyDesk and TeamViewer illegal apps?** No, they are legitimate remote technical support tools used by IT companies. Scammers only exploit them.
4. **How do scammers connect to my phone?** By asking you to read out the unique 9-digit code displayed on the app and requesting you to tap "Allow."
5. **Can scammers see my screen if I just download the app?** No, they cannot see your screen unless you share the connection code and click "Allow."
6. **Can scammers see my OTP?** Yes, because screen sharing mirrors everything on your screen, including SMS notifications containing OTPs.
7. **What happens if I turn off my mobile data?** The remote connection breaks instantly.
8. **Why does my banking app screen turn black when using AnyDesk?** It is a security feature built by banks to prevent screen-sharing apps from capturing your passwords.
9. **Can scammers control my phone mouse/screen?** Yes, if you grant "Control" permission on the app.
10. **Do banks ask you to install AnyDesk for KYC?** No, banks never ask you to install third-party screen-sharing apps.
11. **Can I get my money back if lost in this scam?** Yes, if you report it immediately to the Cyber Cell helpline **1930** to freeze the transactions.
12. **How do I block access permanently?** By uninstalling the screen-sharing app from your phone.
13. **Can scammers access my photos and files?** Yes, they have access to view everything on your phone as long as the screen share is active.
14. **What is a connection ID code?** A unique number assigned to your device by the remote app to allow other users to connect.
15. **What is the Cyber Cell helpline number?** 1930.
16. **Why do scammers call pretending to be from telecom (JIO/Airtel)?** They claim your SIM is block or KYC is pending to trick you into downloading screen-sharing apps.
17. **Can a factory reset remove remote access spyware?** Yes, a factory reset will delete all apps and remove any unauthorized screen-sharing software.
18. **Can scammers use remote access to read my WhatsApp chats?** Yes, they can see whatever app you open on your screen.
19. **What is a shadow reversal?** A temporary credit given by banks during fraud investigations.
20. **How do I report a fake helpline number on Google?** You can report the search result listing to Google Search support.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Just downloading AnyDesk hacks your phone. **Fact:** Simply downloading the app is safe. The hack only happens if you share the 9-digit code and approve the incoming connection.
2. **Myth:** Screen sharing apps can read my thoughts. **Fact:** Scammers can only see what is physically visible on the screen. They cannot steal details unless you open the bank app and type them.
3. **Myth:** Net banking passwords are automatically protected from screen sharing. **Fact:** Unless the banking app has a blackout security screen active, scammers can visually record the characters you type.
4. **Myth:** Bank employees need remote access to process refunds. **Fact:** Bank back-end teams process refunds internally; they never need to connect to your phone.
5. **Myth:** Deleting the app icon from home screen uninstalls it. **Fact:** You must go to settings -> Apps and uninstall it completely to ensure it is deleted.
6. **Myth:** Turning off the screen stops the connection. **Fact:** If the app is running in the background, scammers can reconnect when you unlock the screen. Uninstall the app to stay safe.
7. **Myth:** Government portals are safe from remote access scams. **Fact:** Scammers use remote access to hack into government portals (like EPF or Income Tax) using your active sessions.
8. **Myth:** These scams only happen on computers. **Fact:** Mobile screen-sharing fraud is now highly common due to the popularity of mobile banking.
9. **Myth:** The connection code changes every second. **Fact:** The connection ID is permanent for your device, although connection permissions must be granted each time.
10. **Myth:** Sharing screen with family is also dangerous. **Fact:** Sharing screen with trusted family members is safe, but never share it with unknown callers claiming to be officials.

1. **User:** Mujhe BSNL se call aaya ki mera SIM KYC block hone wala hai. Unhone mujhe AnyDesk install karne ko bola, kya main karu?
   **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Ye ek remote access scam hai. Telecom companies ya banks kabhi bhi AnyDesk download karne ko nahi bolte. Agar aapne code share kiya, toh wo aapka bank account khaali kar denge.
2. **User:** Maine customer support ko call kiya gas booking refund ke liye. Unhone bola 'Support App' download karo. Maine 9-digit code de diya.
   **Assistant:** Aapka phone abhi scammer ke control me hai. Turant phone ka mobile data/Wi-Fi band karein (Airplane mode lagayein) aur us support app ko instantly uninstall (delete) karein.
3. **User:** AnyDesk link karne ke baad mere screen par warning dikh rahi hai.
   **Assistant:** AnyDesk aur TeamViewer me built-in security warning hoti hai ki 'Apna code kisi ko na batayein'. Is warning ko ignore na karein, ye scammers se bachane ke liye hi hai.
4. **User:** Maine screen share chalu ki thi aur bank app khola. Phir screen black ho gayi. Kya phone hack ho gaya?
   **Assistant:** Ghabraiye nahi, ye bank app ka security feature hai. Jab koi screen-sharing app background me chalu hoti hai, toh bank security data leak rokne ke liye screen ko automatic black kar deta hai.
5. **User:** AnyDesk connect karne par mere account se ₹50,000 debit ho gaye. Kya paise wapas milenge?
   **Assistant:** Turant card block karein aur 1930 Cyber Fraud Helpline par call karke transaction report karein. Agar aap turant (1-2 ghante me) report karte hain, toh Cyber Cell beneficiary account freeze karke paisa wapas dila sakta hai.
6. **User:** Kya WhatsApp call par screen share karna safe hai?
   **Assistant:** WhatsApp par naya screen share button hota hai. Agar aap kisi anjan call par hain, toh wahan screen share na karein warna aapka OTP aur personal messages scammer ke paas chale jayenge.
7. **User:** Maine remote access app uninstall kar diya hai, kya ab mera phone safe hai?
   **Assistant:** App delete hone ke baad connection break ho jata hai aur phone safe ho jata hai. Ek baar safety ke liye phone ko restart karein aur security scan run karein.
8. **User:** Scammer bol raha hai ki wo verify karne ke liye humara computer chala raha hai.
   **Assistant:** Ye bilkul fraud hai. Government ya banks verify karne ke liye remote control nahi chalate. Computer shutdown karein aur connection block karein.
9. **User:** Remote access code share karna kiske barabar hai?
   **Assistant:** Remote access code share karna apna phone physically chori karne wale ke haath me dene ke barabar hai. Isliye ise hamesha secret rakhein.
10. **User:** Net banking password leak ho gaya screen sharing me, kya badlu?
    **Assistant:** Haan, computer format ya scan karne ke baad net banking portal par jaakar apna password turant badal dein.
11. **User:** Kya mobile banking apps me bio-metric login ise rok sakta hai?
    **Assistant:** Biometric login (fingerprint) remote user bypass nahi kar sakta par agar unke paas screen control hai toh wo OTP aur password padh kar dusre device par login kar sakte hain.
12. **User:** Mere pass bank manager ki call aayi thi help desktop application link karne ke liye.
    **Assistant:** Fake calls se savdhan rahein. Bank staff aapse app download karne ko nahi kahenge.
13. **User:** Scammer ne bola connection block nahi hoga agar hum app use karenge.
    **Assistant:** App uninstall hone par scammers ke paas connection active rakhne ka koi tarika nahi rehta.
14. **User:** Kya digital signature remote login me use ho sakte hain?
    **Assistant:** Digital signature security hardware tokens (USB) ke zariye hote hain jo physical touch mangte hain, par software credentials compromise ho sakte hain.
15. **User:** Remote control app check karne ke liye bank me jana padega?
    **Assistant:** Nahi, aap phone ke 'Settings' -> 'Apps' list me jaakar check kar sakte hain ki AnyDesk ya TeamViewer jaisa koi app chalu toh nahi hai.
