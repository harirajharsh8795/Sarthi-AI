# Vishing (विशिंग / वॉयस फ़िशिंग / फोन कॉल घोटाला)

## Overview

### English
Vishing (short for Voice Phishing) is a phone-based social engineering attack where fraudsters call victims pretending to be bank managers, credit card representatives, or government officials. They use psychological pressure or lucrative offers to manipulate the victim into sharing passwords, PINs, CVVs, and OTPs.

### Hindi
विशिंग (Vishing / वॉयस फ़िशिंग) एक फोन-आधारित सोशल इंजीनियरिंग हमला है जिसमें जालसाज बैंक मैनेजर, क्रेडिट कार्ड प्रतिनिधि या सरकारी अधिकारी होने का नाटक करते हुए पीड़ितों को कॉल करते हैं। वे पीड़ित को पासवर्ड, पिन, सीवीवी (CVV) और ओटीपी (OTP) साझा करने के लिए हेरफेर करने के लिए मनोवैज्ञानिक दबाव या आकर्षक ऑफर्स का उपयोग करते हैं।

## Quick Summary
Vishing is phishing conducted over voice calls. Scammers use internet-calling technology (VoIP) to spoof official caller IDs, making the call appear as if it is coming from the bank's head office. The caller typically adopts a highly professional tone, addressing the victim by name to establish credibility.

The most common vishing scripts in India claim that the customer's debit card has been blocked due to incomplete KYC, or that they are eligible for a free credit card upgrade with no annual fees. To "unblock" the card or "approve" the upgrade, the caller requests the card number and CVV, followed by the transaction OTP sent to the victim's phone. Under RBI rules, banks never ask for sensitive credentials like OTPs or PINs over the phone.

---

## Definition
Vishing is an interactive voice-based social engineering attack that utilizes telephony systems (PSTN or VoIP) to establish a false trust relationship with a target, manipulating them into disclosing authentication credentials or executing financial transfers.

---

## Why It Matters
Understanding vishing is crucial because:
- **Direct Voice Manipulation:** Speaking to a polite, professional human makes victims bypass normal security rules they would otherwise follow online.
- **Urgency Tactics:** Attackers use threats of immediate account suspension to force decisions without giving the victim time to think or consult others.
- **Exploits Caller ID Spoofing:** Criminals can show the bank's actual helpline number on your screen using software, making it hard to detect by sight alone.

---

## How It Works
The standard operational sequence of a credit card limit upgrade vishing scam:

Scammer spoof bank's number and calls customer: "I am speaking from the credit card upgrade department."
↓
Scammer says: "You are eligible to upgrade your card limit to ₹5 Lakhs for free. I need to verify your card status."
↓
Scammer asks for the 16-digit card number and the 3-digit CVV on the back of the card
↓
Trusting the professional tone, the customer reads out the card details
↓
Scammer initiates an online transfer of ₹1 Lakh on a shopping portal using the card details
↓
Bank sends a real transaction OTP to the customer's phone
↓
Scammer says: "I have triggered the upgrade system. Please read out the 6-digit confirmation code received on your phone."
↓
Customer reads out the OTP without reading the SMS text warning (which says "OTP for transaction of ₹1 Lakh")
↓
Scammer enters the OTP, completes the transaction, and disconnects the call

  - Caller's exact phone number and date/time of call.
  - The name and bank department claimed by the caller.
  - Bank transaction statement showing details of debited funds.
  - Cyber Crime complaint printout.

## Features
- **Caller ID Spoofing:** Displaying fake numbers (like bank helpline numbers) on caller ID screens.
- **Interactive Voice Response (IVR) Spoofing:** Asking users to type their PIN on the dialpad to bypass speaking to a human, which still captures the keys.
- **Social Engineering Scripts:** Highly researched templates designed to manipulate fear or greed.

---

## Benefits of Vishing Awareness
- **Immediate Skeptical Reaction:** Knowing that banks never ask for OTPs or PINs helps you refuse and hang up immediately.
- **Secure Verification:** Understanding that you must verify alerts only by calling the official number printed on the back of your physical card.

---

## Risks
- **Account Takeover:** Loss of control over net banking logins.
- **Severe Financial Loss:** Rapid siphoning of savings or credit limits via online wallets.
- **Secondary Scam Targeting:** Once victimized, details are sold to other scam networks as a soft target.

---

## Charges & Fees
- **Fraud Dispute Filing:** Nil.

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **Mandatory Safe Call Warning:** RBI mandates that banks must periodically send SMS and email alerts stating that they never call to ask for OTPs, PINs, CVV, or passwords.
- **Customer Negligence Liability:** Under RBI customer protection rules, if a customer shares their OTP over a phone call, it is classified as customer negligence. The customer is liable for the loss until they report the incident and block the card. Once blocked, subsequent losses are borne by the bank.

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Handle a Suspicious Banking Call:
1. **Listen for Red Flags:** If the caller asks for your card details, CVV, OTP, net banking password, or asks you to download any app, it is a scam.
2. **Refuse and Hang Up:** Do not argue. Simply say: "I will contact my branch directly," and disconnect the call immediately.
3. **Verify Official Contacts:** Look at the back of your debit/credit card. Find the official toll-free customer care number listed there.
4. **Call the Official Number:** Call that number and ask if they made any calls regarding your account.
5. **Block Card (If details were shared):** If you already shared details, block the card immediately using your mobile app or during the call to the official helpline.
6. **Report:** Report the caller's number on **cybercrime.gov.in** or call **1930**.

---

## Safety Tips
- **The "No Phone Sharing" Rule:** Never share your OTP, PIN, password, or CVV on a phone call, even if the caller claims to be the bank manager or police officer.
- **Ignore Caller ID Names:** Do not trust names displayed on Truecaller or phone caller ID, as scammers can easily register names like "SBI Head Office" or "HDFC KYC support."
- **Read OTP Messages carefully:** Always read the SMS text accompanying an OTP. If it says "OTP for transaction," do not read it out.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Assuming Bank Helplines Call You:** Believing that since the call came from a number looking like the bank's helpline, it must be genuine. Remember, incoming call IDs can be spoofed.
- **Sharing OTP to "Verify Identity":** Sharing the OTP thinking it is a login code to prove you are the real account holder.
- **Sharing PINs on IVR Keypad:** Believing that entering your PIN on the phone keypad during a call is safe. Keypad tones (DTMF) can be recorded by scammers to steal the PIN.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is vishing?** Phishing conducted over phone calls to steal sensitive bank information.
2. **How does vishing differ from standard phishing?** Vishing uses voice calls; phishing uses emails or websites.
3. **Do banks call to update KYC details?** Banks may call to remind you to visit the branch, but they never ask for card numbers, CVVs, or OTPs over the phone.
4. **What is caller ID spoofing?** A technology that allows scammers to display a fake number (like the bank's actual helpline) on your phone screen.
5. **Can scammers see the PIN I type on my dialpad?** Yes, the tones produced when you press keys (DTMF) can be translated into numbers by the scammer's software.
6. **Is it safe to share my birth date or address on call?** While not a banking credential, scammers use these details to build trust or bypass security questions on official bank calls. Avoid sharing them.
7. **What is the cyber cell helpline number?** 1930.
8. **What if I shared my CVV but not the OTP?** Your card is still at risk. Scammers can use it on international websites that do not require OTP verification. Block the card immediately.
9. **Can the bank reverse a transaction if I was tricked on a call?** Banks cannot reverse real-time transfers instantly. You must call 1930 within the Golden Hour to freeze the funds.
10. **Why did the caller address me by my full name?** Scammers buy leaked databases containing customer names, addresses, and bank names to sound authentic.
11. **Do credit card companies call to waive annual fees?** Scammers use "annual fee waiver" or "reward point expiry" as baits to steal card details.
12. **Can I report vishing calls on Truecaller?** Yes, mark the number as spam on Truecaller to warn other users.
13. **What is the penalty for phone banking fraud in India?** Imprisonment and heavy fines under Section 66D of the IT Act.
14. **Why did the bank block my card after I reported a fake call?** For security, to prevent any transactions if the card credentials were leaked during the call.
15. **What is the full form of CVV?** Card Verification Value.
16. **Are zero-balance accounts targeted by fake calls?** Yes, scammers target all active bank accounts.
17. **What is a vishing script?** A pre-written dialog guide used by scammers to handle customer objections and sound professional.
18. **Can I record vishing calls?** Yes, call recordings serve as good electronic evidence for cyber police investigations.
19. **What should I do if a caller threatens me with arrest?** Hang up immediately. Police do not arrest or trial citizens over phone calls (Digital Arrest).
20. **Is there any charge to complain about fake calls?** No, it is a free service.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Banks need your OTP to reverse a transaction failure. **Fact:** Banks process refunds internally; they never require OTPs to credit money back to your account.
2. **Myth:** If Truecaller shows a green tick mark, the call is 100% verified. **Fact:** Scammers can bypass basic Truecaller verification using fake business profiles. Stay alert.
3. **Myth:** entering PIN on dialpad during call is safe. **Fact:** Keypad tones are easily read by scammers; never enter PINs during active phone calls.
4. **Myth:** Only old people fall for voice scams. **Fact:** Young professionals are also targeted successfully using credit card limit upgrades or tax refund baits.
5. **Myth:** Police can block the scammer's phone instantly. **Fact:** It requires a formal complaint registration on the cyber portal to trigger telecom and IMEI blocking procedures.
6. **Myth:** The bank manager needs my card number to check my balance. **Fact:** Bank employees can access your account details using your account number inside their secure internal network. They do not need card details.
7. **Myth:** If I block the number, they cannot call again. **Fact:** Scammers use internet calling software that generates a new phone number for every call.
8. **Myth:** Vishing is only done in English. **Fact:** Scammers speak fluently in Hindi, Hinglish, and regional languages (Tamil, Telugu, Bengali) to sound local.
9. **Myth:** I won a lottery because the bank call said so. **Fact:** Banks do not conduct lottery contests or call customers with cash prizes.
10. **Myth:** If I hang up, the bank will charge me a penalty for disrespect. **Fact:** You have the absolute right to hang up on any call. Banks do not penalize customers for hanging up.

## Conversation Examples
1. **User:** Mujhe HDFC Bank se phone aaya ki aapka debit card block ho gaya hai, unblock karne ke liye OTP bataiye. Kya bata du?
   **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Ye ek vishing fraud call hai. Bank kabhi bhi card unblock karne ke liye phone par OTP nahi maangta. Call cut karein aur us number ko block karein.
2. **User:** Caller bol raha hai ki wo bank ka manager bol raha hai aur unke paas meri saari details hain.
   **Assistant:** Scammers leaked database se aapka naam aur account bank name jaan lete hain taaki wo asli lag sakein. Unhe manager na manein, unhe koi CVV ya OTP na batayein aur phone rakh dein.
3. **User:** Scammer ne bola ki card upgrade ke liye card ke peeche likha 3-digit number (CVV) chahiye. Kya main de du?
   **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. CVV ek secret number hota hai jo kisi ke sath share nahi kiya jata. CVV dene par wo bina aapke swipe karein online card use kar sakte hain.
4. **User:** Phone call par bank help ke naam par PIN enter karne ko bole toh kya karein?
   **Assistant:** Phone call par dialpad se PIN enter karne ko kahein toh call cut karein. Dialpad ki keys (tones) se scammers PIN chura lete hain.
5. **User:** Maine call par galti se details share kar di hain aur ab darr lag raha hai.
   **Assistant:** Turant mobile app open karke apna debit/credit card block karein ya net banking password badal dein. Agar koi transaction ho gaya hai toh 1930 helpline par call karein.
6. **User:** Truecaller par 'HDFC Bank Customer Care' dikh raha hai par number normal mobile ka hai (+91-9xxxxxxxxx).
   **Assistant:** Truecaller par koi bhi apna naam bank ke naam se save kar sakta hai. Bank customer care numbers aamtaur par toll-free (1800-xxx) ya specific short numbers hote hain. Is call par trust na karein.
7. **User:** Kya main suspicious call ko record kar sakta hu?
   **Assistant:** Haan, call recording safe hai aur iska use cyber police me complaint darj karte waqt fraudster ke khilaf evidence ke roop me kiya ja sakta hai.
8. **User:** Caller ne mujhe loan verify karne ke liye bola.
   **Assistant:** Pre-approved loans ke alerts official banking app me check karein, phone call par direct data share na karein.
9. **User:** Kya police vishing calls ke call coordinates trace karti hai?
    **Assistant:** Haan, cyber cell department telecom service provider logs ki madad se scammers ke tower location aur IMEI devices track karte hain.
10. **User:** Mujhse kisi ne processing fee mangi call par card active karne ke liye.
    **Assistant:** Card activation free hota hai. Kisi ko call par koi processing fee na dein, ye fraud hai.
11. **User:** Vishing complaint ke liye cyber cell office jana compulsory hai?
    **Assistant:** Pehli complaint aap online cybercrime.gov.in par register kar sakte hain, jo direct regional cyber cell ko forward ho jati hai.
12. **User:** Scammer bol raha hai ki main verification link par click karu call chalu rakhte hue.
    **Assistant:** Call chalu rakh kar link click karne se wo aapse OTP maangenge aur aap darr me share kar denge. Call cut karein aur link delete karein.
13. **User:** Kya bank emails aur calls me updates verify karte hain?
    **Assistant:** Bank alerts hamesha information purpose ke liye hote hain, verification details physical branch me ya video KYC ke secure system se hi hoti hain.
14. **User:** Mera card balance safe hai agar maine credit card details share ki hain?
    **Assistant:** Debit card detail share karne par savings balance aur credit card share karne par card limit dono risk me hoti hain. Turant block request karein.
15. **User:** Phone call spam report kaise karein?
    **Assistant:** Aap us number ko spam list me mark karein aur TRAI 1909 ya DND service par call block complain register karwayein.
