# Digital Arrest (डिजिटल अरेस्ट घोटाला)

## Overview

### English
Digital Arrest is a modern cybercrime scam where fraudsters impersonate law enforcement officers (from CBI, Police, Customs, ED, or Telecom Departments) and place victims under fake "digital custody" via video calls. They threaten the victim with arrest for alleged crimes (like drug parcels or money laundering) and extort large sums of money.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
The "Digital Arrest" scam is a highly sophisticated social engineering attack. It usually begins with a phone call claiming that a parcel sent in the victim's name has been intercepted containing drugs, or that their mobile number/Aadhaar is linked to illegal money laundering activities. To create panic, the caller transfers the call to a "senior police officer" or "CBI agent."

The victim is then forced to join a video call (typically Skype or Zoom) where the fraudsters wear official-looking uniforms and operate from rooms decorated like police stations. They command the victim to stay on camera constantly, forbidding them from talking to family members or leaving their room. Under immense psychological pressure, the victim is forced to transfer their savings to "government verification accounts" to prove their innocence, which the fraudsters instantly withdraw.

---

## Definition
Digital Arrest is a form of online extortion and cyber impersonation wherein criminals falsely represent themselves as statutory law enforcement authorities to place a victim under involuntary surveillance via real-time video link, using coercion to extract immediate financial transfers under the guise of bail or verification deposits.

---

## Why It Matters
Awareness of digital arrest scams is extremely critical because:
- **High Psychological Abuse:** Victims are subjected to extreme fear, isolation, and mental torture for hours or even days, leading to massive financial and emotional trauma.
- **Prey on Innocent Citizens:** Fraudsters target professionals, senior citizens, and homemakers who are unfamiliar with legal procedures and easily panicked by threats from police or government agencies.
- **Illegal Financial Loss:** Victims are coerced into transferring their entire life savings (often lakhs or crores of rupees) through RTGS or IMPS under pressure.

---

## How It Works
The step-by-step workflow of a Digital Arrest scam:

Victim receives call from "FedEx/Customs/Telecom" claiming Aadhaar/parcel is linked to drugs
↓
Caller creates urgency and transfers call to "Police/CBI" on Skype/Zoom
↓
Fraudster in fake uniform shows fake ID cards, warrants, and a simulated police station setup
↓
Fraudster places victim under "Digital Arrest," ordering them to keep video on 24/7
↓
Victim is forbidden from contacting family, friends, or local police under threat of jail
↓
Fraudster claims: "Verify your bank funds. Transfer them to our clearance account."
↓
Terrified victim executes RTGS/IMPS transfer to the fraudster's mule account
↓
Once money is transferred, fraudsters disconnect call, delete accounts, and disappear

## Eligibility
- **Targets:** Anyone possessing an active mobile number, bank account, and internet access, especially senior citizens and retired individuals.

---

## Required Documents
- **Not Applicable:** For reporting digital arrest fraud, the victim should document:
  - Skype ID or phone number used by the fraudsters.
  - Transaction receipts/bank statements of the transfers made.
  - Screen captures of fake warrants or IDs sent by the scammers.
  - Complaint copy filed on cybercrime.gov.in.

---

## Features
- **Authority Impersonation:** Pretending to be officers from CBI, Mumbai/Delhi Police, Customs, ED, or TRAI.
- **Simulated Environments:** Creating realistic backgrounds resembling police offices or courtrooms during video calls.
- **Digital Custody:** Coercing victims to remain visible on camera continuously to prevent them from seeking help.
- **Fake Legal Documents:** Showing forged arrest warrants, official stamps, and supreme court directives on WhatsApp.

---

## Benefits of Knowledge
- **Fear Neutralization:** Knowing that Indian law does not recognize "Digital Arrest" helps citizens disconnect the call immediately.
- **Quick Action:** Prompts victims to call the Cyber Crime Helpline 1930 immediately to freeze transferred funds in time.

---

## Risks
- **Total Asset Loss:** Complete depletion of bank accounts and fixed deposits.
- **Severe Mental Trauma:** Victims suffer extreme stress and anxiety due to fake threats of imprisonment and public shame.
- **Data Compromise:** Fraudsters may force victims to share sensitive bank login credentials and Aadhaar details.

---

## Charges & Fees
- **Fraud Dispute Filing:** Nil (free service).

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **No Digital Arrest Provision:** The Government of India, MHA, and RBI have clarified that **no police department, court, or investigative agency has the authority to arrest or keep any citizen in custody via phone or video call.**
- **Physical Summon Mandate:** Police and enforcement agencies must issue physical summons or notices under section 41A of CrPC (or equivalent laws) for investigation; phone/video calls are not legal methods of arrest.
- **Immediate Account Freezing:** RBI-regulated banks must act instantly on notifications from cybercrime portals to freeze the beneficiary accounts of such frauds.

## Step-by-Step Process

### What to do if you receive a Digital Arrest Call:
1. **Remain Calm:** Do not panic when the caller mentions drugs, money laundering, or police arrest.
2. **Understand the Law:** Remind yourself: **Police never arrest anyone via video calls.**
3. **Disconnect the Call:** Hang up immediately. Do not join any Skype, Zoom, or video link sent to you.
4. **Do Not Share Details:** Do not share your bank account numbers, passwords, Aadhaar, PAN, or family details.
5. **Verify Independently:** If they claim to be from a specific police station, search for the official number of that police station on the state government website and call them to verify.
6. **Report Immediately:** Call the Cyber Crime Helpline **1930** or visit **cybercrime.gov.in** to report the number.

---

## Safety Tips
- **Block Unknown Video Requests:** Never accept video calls from unknown numbers on WhatsApp, Skype, or Zoom.
- **Discuss with Family:** If anyone threatens you with legal action online, immediately tell your family members or a trusted neighbor. Do not suffer in isolation.
- **Do Not Transfer Money to "Verify":** Government agencies will never ask you to transfer money to a private bank account to "verify your funds."

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Obeying the "Silence" Order:** Keeping the scam secret from family members and local authorities out of fear.
- **Believing Uniforms and Backgrounds:** Trusting the caller just because they are wearing a police uniform or showing a badge on camera. Uniforms and ID cards are easily faked.
- **Executing RTGS transfers:** Hurrying to the bank or using mobile banking to transfer money under pressure, thinking it is a temporary safety deposit.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is a digital arrest?** A fake cyber scam where criminals pretend to be police/CBI on video call to lock you in your home digitally and steal money.
2. **Can the police arrest me via Skype or Zoom?** No, police and law agencies never execute arrests or custody via video calls.
3. **What is a customs drug parcel scam?** A scam where caller claims a courier (like FedEx) containing illegal drugs has been caught in your name.
4. **Why do fraudsters ask me to keep my video call on?** To isolate you and prevent you from calling your family, bank, or real police for help.
5. **Do CBI officers wear uniforms on Skype?** Real CBI officers do not conduct investigations or verify bank balances via Skype calls.
6. **What should I do if I transferred money to scammers?** Call the national cybercrime helpline **1930** immediately to freeze the transaction before they withdraw it.
7. **Are there fake arrest warrants in this scam?** Yes, fraudsters send forged arrest warrants with fake logos of the Supreme Court, CBI, or Police on WhatsApp.
8. **Can TRAI block my number for illegal advertisements?** TRAI does not call individual subscribers to block numbers or threaten arrest. A telecom provider will only send official SMS.
9. **How do scammers get my Aadhaar number?** From online leaks, fake websites, or data breaches.
10. **Where do I report digital arrest calls?** On the cybercrime portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or by dialing 1930.
11. **Do government departments ask for verification deposits?** No, no government agency asks you to deposit personal cash to prove your innocence.
12. **Can they legally block me from leaving my house?** No, criminals are only using psychological threats; they have no physical presence or legal authority over you.
13. **Who is targeted by digital arrest scams?** Primarily retired officials, senior citizens, teachers, and professionals.
14. **What is section 41A notice?** A legal notice issued by police in India asking a person to appear at the station for questioning. It is always delivered physically.
15. **How did scammers know my name and address?** They compile data from leaked directories and online databases to sound convincing.
16. **What is Skype/Zoom overlay?** A trick where scammers use pre-recorded videos or fake green screens of police stations to deceive you.
17. **Can banks help retrieve money lost in digital arrest?** Yes, if reported within the golden hour (first 2-3 hours) through 1930, banks can block the mule accounts and hold the cash.
18. **Why do scammers use RTGS or IMPS?** Because these methods transfer large amounts of cash instantly, which can be withdrawn quickly.
19. **What is a cyber crime cell?** A specialized police unit in India that investigates online frauds and cybercrimes.
20. **Should I tell my bank about the digital arrest call?** Yes, keep your bank informed so they can monitor your account for suspicious activity.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** The CBI conducts online trials via Skype calls. **Fact:** No Indian court or agency holds online trials or custody hearings on Skype.
2. **Myth:** If they have a police uniform and a warrant copy, it must be real. **Fact:** Uniforms are easily purchased, and warrant copies are created on simple document editors with fake stamps.
3. **Myth:** Disconnecting a police call will lead to instant physical arrest. **Fact:** Hanging up on a suspicious video call is safe. Real police will issue a physical summons first.
4. **Myth:** Banks cannot freeze my money once transferred. **Fact:** If reported instantly to 1930, the cyber cell can issue commands to banks to freeze the money in the scammer's account.
5. **Myth:** Government accounts are used for verification deposits. **Fact:** Scammers provide private accounts (mule accounts) belonging to innocent people, not government treasury accounts.
6. **Myth:** Digital arrest is a new legal law in India. **Fact:** There is no such law or concept as "Digital Arrest" under the Indian Penal Code or IT Act.
7. **Myth:** Scammers cannot trace you if you hang up. **Fact:** Scammers only have your mobile number; they cannot track your physical location in real-time.
8. **Myth:** Only greedy people get scammed. **Fact:** Scammers exploit fear and legal ignorance; even highly educated people fall victim due to panic.
9. **Myth:** Sharing Aadhaar number means you are compromised. **Fact:** Aadhaar alone cannot be used to withdraw money without biometrics or OTP, but keep it secure.
10. **Myth:** You must wait for your bank branch to open to report fraud. **Fact:** You must call 1930 immediately; it is active 24/7.

   **Assistant:** Ghabraiye nahi. Wo warrant bilkul nakli hai. Government ya courts kabhi bhi WhatsApp par arrest warrant nahi bhejte. Aap is number ko block karein aur cybercrime.gov.in par report karein.
3. **User:** Unhone mujhe bola ki apna camera chalu rakho aur kisi se baat mat karo warna cbi team ghar aa jayegi.
   **Assistant:** Ye aapko darakar akela karne ki koshish hai taaki aap kisi se sach na bata sakein. Camera turant band karein aur call cut karke apne family members ko sach batayein. Koi team nahi aayegi.
4. **User:** Maine darr me aakar kal ₹5 Lakh transfer kar diye clearance ke liye, kya wo wapas milenge?
   **Assistant:** Aap bina ek minute gawaye cyber crime helpline number 1930 par call karke apni transaction details register karwayein. Agar paisa scammer ke account me abhi bhi hai, toh use block kiya ja sakta hai.
5. **User:** Scammer bol raha hai ki wo mere bank account ki security check karne ke liye temporary deposit mang raha hai.
   **Assistant:** Government ka aisa koi rule nahi hai jo bank verification ke liye paise mangta ho. Ye fraud ka tarika hai, kisi ko paise na bhejein.
6. **User:** Skype par call karne wala shakhs police uniform me tha aur piche station ka board tha.
   **Assistant:** Fraudsters nakli uniforms aur fake background templates ka use karte hain taaki wo asli police dikhein. Inki baaton me na aayein.
7. **User:** Kya TRAI sach me call karke number block karne ki dhamki deta hai?
   **Assistant:** Nahi, TRAI kabhi bhi subscribers ko number block karne ke liye direct call ya dhamki nahi deta. Ye scammer ki chaal hoti hai.
8. **User:** Maine call cut kar diya par wo dusre number se call kar rahe hain, kya karu?
   **Assistant:** Apne phone me unknown callers ko block karein ya phone settings me 'Silence Unknown Callers' active karein. Kuch samay ke liye call receive na karein.
9. **User:** Cyber Crime Portal par report kaise darj karein?
   **Assistant:** cybercrime.gov.in website par jayein, 'Report Women/Child Related Crime' ya 'Report Other Cyber Crime' select karein, incident details, phone numbers aur bank details bhar kar submit karein.
10. **User:** Kya is fraud ke liye local police station me jana zaroori hai?
    **Assistant:** 1930 helpline par report karne ke baad aap local police station ke Cyber Cell me jaakar written shikayat darj karwa sakte hain jo ki safe rehne ke liye zaroori hai.
11. **User:** Mujhe unka Skype ID yaad nahi hai complaint ke liye.
    **Assistant:** Aap Skype app me jaakar chat history se unka username aur screen name nikal sakte hain aur complaint me de sakte hain.
12. **User:** Fraudsters ne mere Aadhaar card ka misuse kiya hai, iska kaise pata lagayein?
    **Assistant:** Aap UIDAI ki official website par jaakar apne Aadhaar ka authentication history check kar sakte hain ki wahan koi unauthorized biometrics ya otp use toh nahi hua.
13. **User:** Kya bank is fraud me compensation deta hai?
    **Assistant:** Agar customer ne khud darr me aakar paise transfer kiye hain (authorized transaction), toh bank compensation nahi deta. Isliye paise bachaane ke liye 1930 par quick action lena zaroori hai.
14. **User:** Scammer ne bola ki ye secrecy case hai aur kisi ko bataya toh desh droh (treason) lag jayega.
    **Assistant:** Desh droh ya legal actions ka darr dikhana inka sabse bada hathiyar hai. Ye fake hai, aap bina dare apne family aur police ko suchit karein.
15. **User:** Mera beta dar gaya hai aur keh raha hai ki unhone uske phone par police verification link bheja hai.
    **Assistant:** Link par click na karein. Usme spy apps ya remote desktop control apps ho sakte hain jo aapka phone hack kar lenge. App delete karein aur browser history clear karein.
