# Reporting Fraud (साइबर धोखाधड़ी की रिपोर्ट करना)

## Overview

### English
Reporting fraud is the formal process of notifying your bank and law enforcement agencies about unauthorized financial transactions or cybercrimes. Quick reporting within the "Golden Hour" (first 2-3 hours) is critical to freezing the stolen funds in the scammer's account and recovering the lost money.

### Hindi
धोखाधड़ी की रिपोर्ट करना (Reporting Fraud / साइबर शिकायत) आपके बैंक और कानून प्रवर्तन एजेंसियों को अनधिकृत वित्तीय लेनदेन या साइबर अपराधों के बारे में सूचित करने की औपचारिक प्रक्रिया है। "गोल्डन ऑवर" (पहले 2-3 घंटे) के भीतर त्वरित रिपोर्टिंग चोरों के खाते में चोरी के पैसे को ब्लॉक करने और उसे वापस पाने के लिए महत्वपूर्ण है।

### Hinglish
Reporting fraud ka matlab hota hai apne bank aur cyber police ko kisi bhi unauthorized (bina marzi ke) transaction ya cybercrime ke baare me inform karna. Fraud hone ke turant baad, pehle 2-3 ghante (jise "Golden Hour" kehte hain) ke andar report karne se chori ka paisa scammers ke accounts me freeze hone aur wapas milne ke chances sabse zyada hote hain.

## Quick Summary
When a customer falls victim to online banking fraud, UPI scams, card cloning, or phishing, fast action is required. The Indian Government, in coordination with the Ministry of Home Affairs, operates the National Cyber Crime Helpline **1930** (formerly 155260) and the online portal **cybercrime.gov.in**.

Under RBI's customer protection guidelines, reporting timeline dictates your liability. If you report an unauthorized electronic transaction within three working days, your liability is zero, meaning the bank must refund the full amount. If reported within 4-7 days, your liability is capped (maximum loss ranges from ₹5,000 to ₹25,000 depending on account type), and beyond 7 days, the refund depends on bank policies.

---

## Definition
Reporting Fraud is the immediate execution of statutory notifications to the card-issuing bank and the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal, logging transaction metadata (IDs, timestamps, accounts) to trigger inter-bank freezing mechanisms and limit consumer financial liability.

---

## Why It Matters
Promptly reporting cyber fraud is crucial because:
- **Freezes Scammer's Account:** The 1930 network connects major banks in real-time. A report can freeze the stolen cash before it is withdrawn from ATMs or moved to other mule accounts.
- **Enforces Legal Liability Claims:** Delays in reporting shift the financial liability onto the customer. Reporting within 3 days legally binds the bank to process a refund.
- **Secures Banking Ecosystem:** Logging scammers' numbers and bank accounts helps authorities blacklist these accounts, preventing fraud against other citizens.

---

## How It Works
The inter-bank communication workflow when a fraud is reported via 1930:

Victim notices money is debited and calls **1930** within the Golden Hour
↓
Cyber Cell operator verifies identity and enters transaction details (Transaction ID, Date, Bank, Amount)
↓
Information is uploaded to the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (NCRP)
↓
The NCRP system instantly sends alert to the victim's Bank (Bank A) and the beneficiary Bank (Bank B)
↓
Bank B checks the scammer's account and freezes the disputed amount immediately
↓
If the scammer already moved funds to Bank C, the system forwards the freeze alert to Bank C
↓
Cyber Cell instructs victim to submit written dispute form to Bank A and file online FIR to claim refund

## Eligibility
- **Applicable Incidents:** All digital financial frauds in India, including UPI fraud, card cloning, phishing, vishing, net banking hacks, and digital arrest extortions.
- **Who can report:** The account holder, immediate family members, or anyone with details of the fraud transaction.

---

## Required Documents
To file a complete report, keep these details ready:
- **Transaction Details:** Bank Account Number, Transaction ID (UTR number), Date, Time, and exact Amount debited.
- **Evidence Screenshots:** Screenshots of the debit SMS alert, WhatsApp chat with the scammer, or phishing email headers.
- **ID Proof:** Copy of Aadhaar Card or PAN Card (needed for bank disputes and police FIR).

---

## Features
- **Dial 1930 Helpline:** 24/7 national voice support linking cyber cells and banks.
- **Cybercrime Portal:** Online web portal (cybercrime.gov.in) to file complaints and track status.
- **Shadow Credit:** Bank's mandate to credit the disputed amount temporarily to the victim's account within 10 days of reporting.

---

## Benefits
- **Financial Protection:** Limits or completely waives customer liability under RBI rules.
- **Trackable Resolution:** Every complaint gets a unique acknowledgement number for tracking investigations.
- **Community Safety:** Blacklists scammer's device IMEI numbers, SIMs, and bank accounts.

---

## Risks of Delayed Reporting
- **Permanent Fund Loss:** Once cash is withdrawn by fraudsters from ATMs, recovery becomes extremely difficult.
- **Claim Rejections:** Banks reject dispute claims if reported after 7 days without valid reasons (like hospitalization).
- **Secondary Fraud Calls:** Scammers calling again pretending to be "recovery agents" to steal more money.

---

## Charges & Fees
- **Fraud Reporting & Complaint Filing:** Nil (Government services and bank disputes are completely free).

---

## RBI / Government Rules
- **RBI Circular on Customer Protection (2017):**
  - **Within 3 days reporting:** Zero customer liability for unauthorized third-party transactions.
  - **4 to 7 days reporting:** Customer liability is limited to a maximum of ₹5,000 (basic savings/PMJDY) or up to ₹25,000 (credit cards/current accounts).
  - **Beyond 7 days:** Subject to the bank's board-approved security policies.
- **Shadow Credit Mandate:** Banks must credit (reversal) the disputed amount within 10 working days of reporting, pending final resolution (which must be closed in 90 days).

## Step-by-Step Process

### How to Report Financial Fraud Step-by-Step:
1. **Call 1930 Immediately:** Dial 1930 from your phone. Follow the IVR instructions to connect to a cyber cell officer.
2. **Provide Details:** Provide your name, account number, caller ID of the scammer, UTR transaction number, amount, and beneficiary bank name if known.
3. **Save SMS Link:** You will receive a confirmation SMS containing a login ID and a link to the cybercrime portal.
4. **Log in and Complete Portal Form:** Visit **cybercrime.gov.in** within 24 hours. Log in using the ID received and upload screenshot evidence, chats, and ID copies to complete the online FIR/complaint.
5. **Request Bank Block:** Contact your bank's official support desk to freeze your compromised cards and account.
6. **Submit Written Dispute:** Visit your bank branch and submit a physical Transaction Dispute Form along with the cyber complaint acknowledgement copy. Keep a stamped copy of the receipt for your records.

---

## Safety Tips
- **The Golden Hour Rule:** Treat the first 2 hours after a fraud as critical. Do not wait for the next morning or office hours to call 1930.
- **Verify URLs:** Always check that you are logging into `cybercrime.gov.in` and not a fake portal. Look for the government ".gov.in" extension.
- **Avoid Private Recovery Agencies:** Never pay private online entities claiming they can hack and recover your stolen funds. They are scammers.

---

## Common Mistakes
- **Failing to note down Transaction IDs:** Calling 1930 without having the bank SMS or transaction ID ready, causing delays in entering details.
- **Not Informing the Bank:** Assuming that calling 1930 is enough. You must notify the bank separately to protect your account and start the refund process.
- **Deleting Evidence:** Deleting WhatsApp chats, call logs, or SMS histories in panic. Keep all communication logs intact as evidence for the cyber cell.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is the first thing I should do after banking fraud?** Call the national cybercrime helpline **1930** immediately and block your bank account/card.
2. **What is 1930?** A toll-free helpline number operated by the Ministry of Home Affairs to assist victims of online financial fraud.
3. **What is the "Golden Hour" in cyber fraud?** The first 2 hours after the transaction occurs, during which freezing funds is most successful.
4. **What is the official government cybercrime website?** cybercrime.gov.in
5. **How much refund do I get if I report within 3 days?** You are eligible for a 100% refund (zero customer liability) under RBI rules, provided you didn't share your PIN/OTP.
6. **What if the fraud was due to my own mistake (sharing OTP)?** You bear the loss until you report it to the bank. Any transaction occurring after you block the card is the bank's liability.
7. **What is shadow credit?** A temporary credit refund given by the bank within 10 days of your complaint while they investigate the case.
8. **How long does the bank take to resolve a fraud dispute?** RBI guidelines state the case must be resolved within 90 days.
9. **Can I file a cyber complaint if the amount is very small (like ₹500)?** Yes, all frauds should be reported to help police track scam networks.
10. **Do I need a lawyer to file a cyber complaint?** No, you can file it yourself online or call 1930.
11. **What is a UTR number?** Unique Transaction Reference number; a 12-digit number found in your transaction SMS or bank statement.
12. **Can I track my cyber complaint status online?** Yes, by logging onto cybercrime.gov.in using the acknowledgment number sent to you.
13. **Is 1930 helpline active on Sundays?** Yes, the 1930 helpline is active 24/7, including holidays and weekends.
14. **What happens to the scammer's bank account after I report?** The bank freezes the account, stopping all withdrawals and transfers.
15. **What if my money was transferred to multiple accounts?** The cyber cell system tracks the money trail and sends freeze orders to all subsequent beneficiary banks.
16. **Can I file a complaint on behalf of my parents?** Yes, you can file it for them using their bank and identity details.
17. **Is a physical police FIR required for bank refunds?** Yes, banks usually require a copy of the police complaint or NCRP online acknowledgement.
18. **Why is the cybercrime site asking for my Aadhaar?** To verify the complainant's identity and prevent fake reports.
19. **What is a mule account?** A bank account used by scammers to receive and quickly move stolen money.
20. **Can I complain against the bank if they refuse to register my fraud dispute?** Yes, you can raise a complaint with the RBI Ombudsman at cms.rbi.org.in.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Cyber police cannot recover money once it is sent. **Fact:** If reported during the Golden Hour, the money can be frozen in the scammer's account and returned to you.
2. **Myth:** 1930 is only for credit card frauds. **Fact:** You can report all digital frauds, including UPI, Net Banking, and wallet scams.
3. **Myth:** If you delete the scammer's number, police can still trace it. **Fact:** Police need the exact phone number and chat logs; do not delete any communication evidence.
4. **Myth:** Banks will refund the money instantly. **Fact:** Banks credit a temporary shadow credit within 10 days, but final settlement takes up to 90 days of investigation.
5. **Myth:** Scammers can unfreeze their accounts easily. **Fact:** Once frozen by the police/NCRP, accounts can only be unfrozen after a formal clearance certificate is issued by the cyber cell.
6. **Myth:** You must pay a fee to cyber cells to recover money. **Fact:** Any agency or website asking for fees to recover cyber fraud money is fake. Government services are 100% free.
7. **Myth:** If the transaction was done via UPI, the bank is not responsible. **Fact:** UPI transactions are banking transactions; they are covered under the same RBI customer protection rules.
8. **Myth:** I can report the fraud next week. **Fact:** Delaying report increases your liability and reduces the chances of recovering the money.
9. **Myth:** The police will raid the scammer's house immediately. **Fact:** The first step is freezing the funds digitally; physical raids and arrests are part of subsequent investigations.
10. **Myth:** I don't need to report if I changed my bank password. **Fact:** Changing password protects your remaining balance, but reporting is necessary to recover the stolen money.

## Conversation Examples
1. **User:** Mere account se ₹50,000 kat gaye hain abhi 10 minute pehle. Mujhe kya karna chahiye?
   **Assistant:** Sabse pehle, turant dial karein **1930** par. Ye cyber crime helpline hai. Apne bank account, transaction ID (UTR) aur amount ki details operator ko bataiye taaki wo scammer ke account ko freeze karwa sakein. Iske baad card/account block karwayein.
2. **User:** 1930 par call lagne par kya pucha jata hai?
   **Assistant:** Operator aapse aapka naam, mobile number, bank account details, fraud transaction ka time, date, amount aur message me aaya UTR/Transaction ID puchega. Ye details pehle se ek paper par likh kar rakhein.
3. **User:** Kya mujhe online cyber portal par bhi alag se register karna hoga?
   **Assistant:** Haan, 1930 par call karne ke baad aapke paas ek SMS aayega jisme login details hongi. cybercrime.gov.in par jaakar un details se login karein aur transaction slip aur WhatsApp chats ki screenshots upload karke complaint complete karein.
4. **User:** Maine transaction dispute file kiya tha. Bank bol raha hai ki verification chal rahi hai. Refund kab tak aayega?
   **Assistant:** RBI ke rules ke mutabik bank ko complaint milne ke 10 working days ke andar temporary shadow credit dena hota hai. Poora investigation max 90 days me complete hoga.
5. **User:** Golden Hour kya hota hai?
   **Assistant:** Fraud hone ke pehle 2 ghante ko Golden Hour kehte hain. Is time me scammers paisa nikal nahi pate, isliye agar aap is dauran 1930 par call karte hain toh paise freeze hone aur wapas milne ke chances 90% se zyada hote hain.
6. **User:** Maine bank ko call kiya par unhone bola cyber police me jao, kya bank mana kar sakta hai?
   **Assistant:** Bank dispute complaint lene se mana nahi kar sakta. Aap cyber complaint ki copy bank me submit karein. Agar bank fir bhi mana kare, toh aap RBI Ombudsman portal (cms.rbi.org.in) par bank ki shikayat kar sakte hain.
7. **User:** Kya WhatsApp chat ka screenshot legal proof mana jata hai?
   **Assistant:** Haan, cyber cell aur court me WhatsApp chats, SMS alerts aur call logs ke screenshots ko electronic evidence (Section 65B of Evidence Act) ke tehat valid proof mana jata hai.
8. **User:** Meri liability kab zero hoti hai?
   **Assistant:** Agar fraud bank ki technical fault se hua hai, ya bina aapki galti ke kisine fraud kiya aur aapne 3 working days ke andar bank ko bata diya, toh aapki koi liability nahi hoti, bank ko poora refund dena hoga.
9. **User:** Mera UPI transaction fail ho gaya tha par paise kat gaye, kya ye fraud hai?
   **Assistant:** Ye technical transaction failure ho sakta hai. Agar 24-48 hours me paise wapas nahi aate, toh bank app me UPI transaction history me jaakar raise dispute karein. Ye aamtaur par automatic refund ho jata hai.
10. **User:** Maine helpline 1930 par call kiya par number busy aa raha hai.
    **Assistant:** Network congestion ke kaaran busy ho sakta hai. Aap turant cybercrime.gov.in portal par jaakar online complaint submit kar sakte hain. Ye bhi same portal par hi register hoti hai.
11. **User:** Kya bank me written copy dena zaroori hai?
    **Assistant:** Haan, online portal par report karne ke baad bank branch me jaakar sign kiya hua Transaction Dispute Form jama karna zaroori hai taaki bank card/account transactions study kar sake.
12. **User:** Fraud transaction credit card par hua hai, kya rules alag hain?
    **Assistant:** Credit card par bhi zero liability aur limited liability ke rules same hain. Dispute file hone ke baad bank investigation chalne tak card par interest aur charges ko freeze kar deta hai.
13. **User:** Mule account kya hota hai?
    **Assistant:** Scammers chori ke paise mangaane ke liye kisi third person ka bank account use karte hain taaki wo khud police ki pakad se bach sakein. Inhe mule accounts kehte hain.
14. **User:** Cyber Crime Portal par registered complaint ko withdraw kaise karein?
    **Assistant:** Agar paisa wapas mil jata hai ya koi galatfahmi door ho jati hai, toh aap portal par login karke request cancel ya clarification upload kar sakte hain.
15. **User:** Fraud reporting helpline dial karne ke kya koi charges hain?
    **Assistant:** Nahi, 1930 ek toll-free helpline number hai aur ispar call karne ka koi charge nahi lagta.
