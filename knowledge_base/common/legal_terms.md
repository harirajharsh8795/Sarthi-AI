# Common Legal Terms & Terminology in India

## Overview
**English**: Navigating the Indian legal system requires a fundamental understanding of commonly used legal terms. This document provides a comprehensive guide to essential legal vocabulary, ensuring citizens can effectively interact with law enforcement, courts, and legal professionals.

## Detailed Framework / Content Structure

| Term | Domain | Definition (English) | Hindi Translation | Context of Usage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Affidavit** | Legal/Admin | A written statement confirmed by oath or affirmation, for use as evidence in court. | हलफनामा (Halafnama) | Used for name changes, address proofs, and court declarations. |
| **Bail** | Criminal Law | Temporary release of an accused person awaiting trial, often on condition that a sum of money is lodged to guarantee their appearance in court. | जमानत (Zamanat) | Applied when an individual is arrested for a bailable or non-bailable offense. |
| **FIR (First Information Report)** | Criminal Law | A document prepared by police when they receive information about the commission of a cognizable offense. | प्रथम सूचना रिपोर्ट (Pratham Suchna Report) | Filed immediately after a crime like theft or assault occurs. |
| **Power of Attorney (PoA)** | Civil/Property | Written authorization to represent or act on another's behalf in private affairs, business, or some other legal matter. | मुख्तारनामा (Mukhtarnama) | Used in property registration or when someone cannot be physically present. |
| **Subpoena/Summons** | Judicial | A writ ordering a person to attend a court. | सम्मन (Samman) | Sent by the court to witnesses or defendants requiring their presence. |
| **Injunction** | Civil Law | A judicial order that restrains a person from beginning or continuing an action threatening or invading the legal right of another. | निषेधाज्ञा (Nishedhagya / Stay Order) | Used to stop illegal construction or unauthorized property sale. |
| **Decree** | Judicial | An official order issued by a legal authority; the formal expression of an adjudication. | हुक्मनामा / डिक्री (Hukmnama) | Final verdict in a civil suit, like a divorce decree. |
| **Plaintiff** | Civil Law | A person who brings a case against another in a court of law. | वादी (Vaadi) | The person who initiates a civil lawsuit. |
| **Defendant** | Civil/Criminal | An individual, company, or institution sued or accused in a court of law. | प्रतिवादी / अभियुक्त (Prativadi) | The person defending themselves against a claim or charge. |
| **Habeas Corpus** | Constitutional | A writ requiring a person under arrest to be brought before a judge or into court, to secure the person's release unless lawful grounds are shown for their detention. | बंदी प्रत्यक्षीकरण (Bandi Pratyakshikaran) | Used in cases of illegal detention or missing persons taken by authorities. |

## Applicability
- **Who is covered**: All citizens of India, foreign nationals residing in India, corporate entities, and legal bodies interacting with the Indian judicial system.
- **Who is exempt**: No individual is entirely exempt from the application of legal terms if they are subjected to Indian jurisdiction. However, diplomats have diplomatic immunity under international law.
- **Special conditions**: Minors and individuals with unsound minds are represented by legal guardians (Next Friend) in civil and criminal proceedings.

## Rights of Citizens
**English**: Citizens have the right to demand court documents, FIR copies, and legal notices in a language they understand. They possess the fundamental right to legal aid and representation.
**Hindi**: नागरिकों को उन अदालती दस्तावेजों, एफआईआर प्रतियों और कानूनी नोटिसों को उस भाषा में मांगने का अधिकार है जिसे वे समझते हैं। उनके पास कानूनी सहायता और प्रतिनिधित्व का मौलिक अधिकार है।
**Hinglish**: Citizens ke paas right hai ki unhe court documents, FIR copies, aur legal notices unki samajh aane wali language mein mile. Unke paas legal aid aur representation ka fundamental right hota hai.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English**: Citizens must provide truthful information when signing an Affidavit (perjury is a crime). They must obey Summons/Subpoenas and respect court Injunctions unconditionally.
**Hindi**: हलफनामे पर हस्ताक्षर करते समय नागरिकों को सच्ची जानकारी प्रदान करनी चाहिए (झूठी गवाही देना एक अपराध है)। उन्हें सम्मन का पालन करना चाहिए और अदालत की निषेधाज्ञा का बिना शर्त सम्मान करना चाहिए।
**Hinglish**: Affidavit sign karte waqt citizens ko truthful information deni chahiye (perjury ek crime hai). Unhe Summons ko obey karna chahiye aur court Injunctions ki respect karni chahiye.

## Step-by-Step Procedure / Usage Guide
**English**: 
1. **Identify the Need**: Determine whether your issue is civil (e.g., property dispute) or criminal (e.g., theft).
2. **Draft the Document**: Use the correct legal term (e.g., draft a 'Plaint' for a civil suit, or file an 'FIR' for a crime).
3. **Execution**: Sign documents like Affidavits in front of an Oath Commissioner or Notary.
4. **Filing**: Submit the document to the appropriate authority (Police Station for FIRs, Registry for Civil Suits).
5. **Follow up**: Keep track of the 'Hearing' dates and 'Decree' or 'Order' issued by the court.

**Hindi**:
1. **आवश्यकता की पहचान करें**: निर्धारित करें कि आपकी समस्या दीवानी (जैसे संपत्ति विवाद) है या आपराधिक (जैसे चोरी)।
2. **दस्तावेज़ तैयार करें**: सही कानूनी शब्द का प्रयोग करें (जैसे दीवानी मुकदमे के लिए 'वादपत्र' तैयार करें, या अपराध के लिए 'एफआईआर' दर्ज करें)।
3. **निष्पादन**: ओथ कमिश्नर या नोटरी के सामने हलफनामे जैसे दस्तावेजों पर हस्ताक्षर करें।
4. **फाइलिंग**: उचित प्राधिकारी को दस्तावेज़ जमा करें।
5. **अनुवर्ती कार्रवाई**: अदालत द्वारा जारी 'सुनवाई' की तारीखों और 'डिक्री' पर नज़र रखें।

**Hinglish**:
1. **Need Identify karein**: Decide karein ki issue civil hai ya criminal.
2. **Document Draft karein**: Correct legal term use karein, jaise civil suit ke liye 'Plaint' aur crime ke liye 'FIR'.
3. **Execution**: Affidavit jaise documents Oath Commissioner ke saamne sign karein.
4. **Filing**: Sahi authority ke paas document submit karein.
5. **Follow up**: Court ki 'Hearing' dates aur 'Decree' ka track rakhein.

## Required Documents / Reference Tables
To formalize various legal procedures, standard documents are needed:
- **For Affidavits**: E-Stamp paper of requisite value (Rs. 10 to Rs. 100 depending on state), Valid ID Proof (Aadhaar, PAN).
- **For Power of Attorney**: Passport size photos, Identity proofs of Executant and Attorney, Two witnesses.
- **For FIR**: Written complaint detailing the incident, date, time, and location.
- **For Bail**: Bail bond application, Surety's ID, Property documents (if required as surety).

## Authorities / Helplines / Organizations
- **National Legal Services Authority (NALSA)**: Toll-Free Helpline 15100 (for free legal aid).
- **Police Helpline**: 112 (National Emergency Number).
- **Bar Council of India**: Regulatory body for legal professionals.
- **District Courts & High Courts**: Primary venues for civil and criminal adjudication.

## Common Errors & Misconceptions
- Using "Robbery" and "Theft" interchangeably (Robbery involves force or fear, Theft is secret removal).
- Thinking "Bail" means acquittal. Bail is just temporary release; the trial still continues.
- Confusing "Notary" with "Magistrate". A Notary only authenticates documents; a Magistrate has judicial powers.
- Assuming an "Agreement" is always a valid "Contract". A contract is an agreement enforceable by law.

## Safety & Verification Tips
**English**: Always verify the credentials of an advocate via the Bar Council roll number. Never sign a blank legal document or an Affidavit without reading it thoroughly. Keep certified copies of all court judgments.
**Hindi**: हमेशा बार काउंसिल रोल नंबर के माध्यम से वकील की साख को सत्यापित करें। कभी भी किसी खाली कानूनी दस्तावेज या हलफनामे पर उसे पूरी तरह पढ़े बिना हस्ताक्षर न करें। अदालत के सभी निर्णयों की प्रमाणित प्रतियां रखें।
**Hinglish**: Hamesha Bar Council roll number ke through advocate ke credentials verify karein. Kabhi bhi blank legal document ya Affidavit par bina padhe sign na karein. Sabhi court judgments ki certified copies apne paas rakhein.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What is the difference between an FIR and a Police Complaint?**
**English**: An FIR is registered only for cognizable offenses (serious crimes where police can arrest without a warrant). A police complaint can be for non-cognizable offenses as well, which requires a magistrate's permission for police to investigate.
**Hindi**: एफआईआर केवल संज्ञेय अपराधों के लिए दर्ज की जाती है। पुलिस शिकायत गैर-संज्ञेय अपराधों के लिए भी हो सकती है, जिसकी जांच के लिए मजिस्ट्रेट की अनुमति की आवश्यकता होती है।
**Hinglish**: FIR sirf cognizable offenses (serious crimes) ke liye register hoti hai. Police complaint non-cognizable offenses ke liye bhi ho sakti hai, jisme investigation ke liye magistrate ki permission chahiye.

**Q2: Can I get Bail for any crime?**
**English**: No. Offenses are categorized as bailable and non-bailable. In bailable offenses, bail is a right. In non-bailable offenses, bail is at the discretion of the judge based on the case's merits.
**Hindi**: नहीं। अपराधों को जमानती और गैर-जमानती के रूप में वर्गीकृत किया गया है। जमानती अपराधों में जमानत एक अधिकार है। गैर-जमानती में यह न्यायाधीश के विवेक पर निर्भर करता है।
**Hinglish**: Nahi. Offenses bailable aur non-bailable hote hain. Bailable mein bail right hai, jabki non-bailable mein bail judge ke discretion par depend karti hai.

**Q3: What does 'Pro Bono' mean?**
**English**: 'Pro Bono' refers to legal work undertaken voluntarily and without payment, as a public service. Many lawyers take pro bono cases to help marginalized sections of society.
**Hindi**: 'प्रो बोनो' का अर्थ है स्वेच्छा से और बिना भुगतान के जनसेवा के रूप में किया गया कानूनी कार्य।
**Hinglish**: 'Pro Bono' ka matlab hai legal work jo voluntarily bina payment ke as a public service kiya jaye.

**Q4: What is a 'Stay Order' or 'Injunction'?**
**English**: A stay order is a judicial directive that temporarily halts a legal proceeding or prevents an action (like stopping demolition). It maintains the status quo until the court hears the matter fully.
**Hindi**: स्टे ऑर्डर एक न्यायिक निर्देश है जो अस्थायी रूप से किसी कानूनी कार्यवाही या कार्रवाई को रोकता है। यह यथास्थिति बनाए रखता है।
**Hinglish**: Stay order ek judicial directive hai jo kisi legal proceeding ya action ko temporarily rokta hai, taaki status quo maintain rahe.

**Q5: What is an Affidavit and when is it required?**
**English**: An affidavit is a written, sworn statement of fact voluntarily made by an affiant under an oath. It is required for court filings, address updates, or declaring legal facts.
**Hindi**: हलफनामा शपथ के तहत स्वेच्छा से दिया गया तथ्य का एक लिखित बयान है। यह अदालत में फाइलिंग या पते के अपडेट के लिए आवश्यक है।
**Hinglish**: Affidavit ek written sworn statement hota hai jo oath ke under diya jata hai. Yeh court filings ya address change ke liye chahiye hota hai.

**Q6: What is a Subpoena?**
**English**: A subpoena is a formal document that orders a named individual to appear before a duly authorized body at a fixed time to give testimony. Ignoring it can lead to a penalty or arrest.
**Hindi**: सम्मन एक औपचारिक दस्तावेज है जो किसी व्यक्ति को गवाही देने के लिए निश्चित समय पर अदालत में पेश होने का आदेश देता है।
**Hinglish**: Subpoena ek formal document hai jo kisi person ko court mein testimony dene ke liye bulata hai. Ise ignore karne par arrest ho sakti hai.

**Q7: Who is a Plaintiff?**
**English**: A plaintiff is the person or entity that initiates a civil lawsuit against someone else (the defendant) in a court of law, seeking a legal remedy.
**Hindi**: वादी वह व्यक्ति या संस्था है जो किसी अन्य व्यक्ति (प्रतिवादी) के खिलाफ दीवानी मुकदमा शुरू करता है।
**Hinglish**: Plaintiff woh person ya entity hai jo court mein kisi ke khilaaf civil lawsuit initiate karta hai.

**Q8: What is Contempt of Court?**
**English**: Contempt of court is the offense of being disobedient to or disrespectful towards a court of law and its officers, which can result in a fine or imprisonment.
**Hindi**: अदालत की अवमानना अदालत और उसके अधिकारियों के प्रति अवज्ञाकारी या अपमानजनक होने का अपराध है।
**Hinglish**: Contempt of court ka matlab court ki disobey karna ya disrespect karna hai, jiske liye fine ya jail ho sakti hai.

**Q9: What does 'Habeas Corpus' literally mean?**
**English**: It is a Latin term meaning "you shall have the body". It is a writ used to bring a prisoner or detainee before the court to determine if their imprisonment or detention is lawful.
**Hindi**: यह एक लैटिन शब्द है जिसका अर्थ है "शरीर को प्रस्तुत करना"। इसका उपयोग किसी बंदी को अदालत में पेश करने के लिए किया जाता है।
**Hinglish**: Yeh Latin term hai jiska matlab hai "produce the body". Yeh illegal detention check karne ke liye court use karti hai.

**Q10: What is the difference between a Decree and an Order?**
**English**: A decree is a formal expression of an adjudication that conclusively determines the rights of the parties. An order is a formal expression of a decision of a civil court which is not a decree.
**Hindi**: एक डिक्री न्यायनिर्णयन की एक औपचारिक अभिव्यक्ति है जो अंतिम रूप से अधिकारों का निर्धारण करती है। आदेश अदालत का वह निर्णय है जो डिक्री नहीं है।
**Hinglish**: Decree final adjudication hoti hai jo rights determine karti hai. Order court ka decision hota hai par woh final decree nahi hota.

**Q11: Can a Power of Attorney be revoked?**
**English**: Yes, a standard Power of Attorney can be revoked by the principal at any time by issuing a Deed of Revocation, provided it is not an irrevocable Power of Attorney.
**Hindi**: हाँ, एक मानक पावर ऑफ अटॉर्नी को निरस्तीकरण विलेख जारी करके किसी भी समय रद्द किया जा सकता है।
**Hinglish**: Haan, standard Power of Attorney ko principal kabhi bhi Revocation Deed issue karke cancel kar sakta hai.

**Q12: What is an Anticipatory Bail?**
**English**: Anticipatory bail is a direction to release a person on bail, issued even before the person is arrested, when they apprehend arrest for a non-bailable offense.
**Hindi**: अग्रिम जमानत किसी व्यक्ति को गिरफ्तार होने से पहले जमानत पर रिहा करने का निर्देश है, जब उसे गिरफ्तारी की आशंका हो।
**Hinglish**: Anticipatory bail arrest se pehle li jati hai jab kisi person ko non-bailable offense mein arrest ka dar ho.

**Q13: What is Perjury?**
**English**: Perjury is the intentional act of swearing a false oath or falsifying an affirmation to tell the truth, whether spoken or in writing, concerning matters material to an official proceeding.
**Hindi**: झूठी गवाही (परजुरी) एक आधिकारिक कार्यवाही के संबंध में जानबूझकर झूठी शपथ लेने या सच बोलने की प्रतिज्ञा को झूठा साबित करने का कार्य है।
**Hinglish**: Perjury ka matlab hai court ya official proceeding mein jaan-boojh kar jhoothi gawahi dena ya jhootha affidavit file karna.

**Q14: What is a 'Cognizable Offense'?**
**English**: A cognizable offense is a serious crime (like murder, rape, kidnapping) where a police officer has the authority to make an arrest without a warrant and to start an investigation with or without the permission of a court.
**Hindi**: संज्ञेय अपराध एक गंभीर अपराध है जहां एक पुलिस अधिकारी को बिना वारंट के गिरफ्तार करने का अधिकार होता है।
**Hinglish**: Cognizable offense ek serious crime hota hai jisme police bina warrant ke arrest kar sakti hai aur investigation shuru kar sakti hai.

**Q15: What is the difference between 'Acquittal' and 'Conviction'?**
**English**: Acquittal is the legal finding that a criminal defendant is not guilty of the charges. Conviction is the formal declaration that someone is guilty of a criminal offense.
**Hindi**: बरी करना (Acquittal) यह निष्कर्ष है कि अभियुक्त दोषी नहीं है। दोषसिद्धि (Conviction) यह औपचारिक घोषणा है कि कोई व्यक्ति अपराधी है।
**Hinglish**: Acquittal ka matlab hai defendant ka nirdosh saabit hona (bari hona). Conviction ka matlab hai court dwara guilty (doshi) karaar diya jana.

**Q16: What is a PIL (Public Interest Litigation)?**
**English**: A PIL is a legal action initiated in a court of law for the enforcement of public interest or general rights, rather than individual rights. Any citizen can file it.
**Hindi**: जनहित याचिका (PIL) व्यक्तिगत अधिकारों के बजाय सार्वजनिक हित को लागू करने के लिए अदालत में शुरू की गई एक कानूनी कार्रवाई है।
**Hinglish**: PIL ek legal action hai jo public interest ke liye court mein file ki jati hai, ise koi bhi citizen file kar sakta hai.

**Q17: What does 'Alimony' mean?**
**English**: Alimony, or spousal support, is a legal obligation on a person to provide financial support to their spouse before or after marital separation or divorce.
**Hindi**: गुजारा भत्ता (एलिमोनी) विवाह विच्छेद या तलाक के बाद जीवनसाथी को वित्तीय सहायता प्रदान करने का कानूनी दायित्व है।
**Hinglish**: Alimony divorce ya separation ke baad spouse ko financial support dene ka legal obligation hota hai.

**Q18: What is 'Ex Parte' decision?**
**English**: An ex parte decision is one decided by a judge without requiring all of the parties to the controversy to be present, usually granted in urgent situations.
**Hindi**: एकतरफा (एक्स-पार्टे) निर्णय वह है जो किसी विवाद में सभी पक्षों के उपस्थित हुए बिना ही न्यायाधीश द्वारा तय किया जाता है।
**Hinglish**: Ex parte decision woh hota hai jisme judge ek side ki baat sunkar urgent situation mein faisla suna dete hain bina doosri party ki presence ke.

**Q19: What is an 'Amicus Curiae'?**
**English**: Amicus Curiae literally translates to "friend of the court". It refers to a person or organization, not a party to a case, who volunteers to offer information or expertise to assist the court.
**Hindi**: एमिकस क्यूरी का अर्थ है "अदालत का मित्र"। यह वह व्यक्ति है जो मामले में पक्षकार नहीं है, लेकिन अदालत की सहायता करता है।
**Hinglish**: Amicus Curiae ka matlab "friend of the court" hota hai. Yeh woh person ya organization hai jo court ki assist karta hai bina case ka party hue.

**Q20: What is a 'Charge Sheet'?**
**English**: A charge sheet is a final report prepared by law enforcement agencies after completing an investigation, laying out the specific charges, evidence, and list of witnesses against the accused.
**Hindi**: चार्ज शीट जांच पूरी होने के बाद तैयार की गई एक अंतिम रिपोर्ट है, जिसमें आरोपी के खिलाफ विशिष्ट आरोप और साक्ष्य होते हैं।
**Hinglish**: Charge sheet investigation puri hone ke baad police ki final report hoti hai, jisme accused ke khilaaf charges, evidence aur witnesses ki list hoti hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts

**Myth 1:** An FIR guarantees conviction.
**Fact:** An FIR is just the beginning of a police investigation, not a proof of guilt.

**Myth 2:** You cannot change your lawyer in the middle of a case.
**Fact:** You have the fundamental right to change your legal representation at any stage of the trial by taking a 'No Objection Certificate' (NOC) from your current lawyer.

**Myth 3:** Bail means the case is closed.
**Fact:** Bail simply means you are out of jail temporarily while the legal trial proceeds in court.

**Myth 4:** Oral agreements are never valid in court.
**Fact:** Oral agreements can be legally binding, though they are much harder to prove without corroborative evidence.

**Myth 5:** Police can arrest anyone for any crime without a warrant.
**Fact:** Police can only arrest without a warrant in 'cognizable' offenses. For 'non-cognizable' offenses, a Magistrate's warrant is required.

**Myth 6:** Affidavits can be written on plain paper.
**Fact:** Affidavits must generally be printed on non-judicial stamp paper of a specific denomination and duly notarized to have legal validity.

**Myth 7:** Public Interest Litigation (PIL) can be filed to solve personal disputes.
**Fact:** PILs can strictly only be filed for issues concerning the larger public interest, not private grievances.

**Myth 8:** You lose all your property if you lose a civil suit.
**Fact:** The court only awards damages or decrees specific performance as asked by the plaintiff. It does not indiscriminately strip property.

**Myth 9:** A 'Subpoena' is an arrest warrant.
**Fact:** A subpoena is merely a summons to appear in court as a witness or to produce documents. It is not an arrest order.

**Myth 10:** Ignorance of the law is a valid defense.
**Fact:** 'Ignorantia juris non excusat' is a fundamental legal principle meaning ignorance of the law excuses no one.

## Conversation Examples

**Conversation 1:**
*Citizen*: Mujhe court se ek Summons aaya hai, iska kya matlab hai?
*Assistant*: Summons ek official court order hai jisme aapko ek specific date aur time par court mein present hone ko kaha gaya hai, either as a witness ya as a party to the case.
*Citizen*: Agar main na jau toh?
*Assistant*: Agar aap bina valid reason ke Summons ignore karte hain, toh court aapke khilaaf arrest warrant issue kar sakti hai.

**Conversation 2:**
*Citizen*: What should I do if the police refuse to register my FIR?
*Assistant*: If a station house officer (SHO) refuses to register an FIR for a cognizable offense, you can send the complaint in writing by post to the Superintendent of Police (SP).
*Citizen*: What if the SP also doesn't act?
*Assistant*: You can file a private complaint directly before the Judicial Magistrate under Section 156(3) of the CrPC.

**Conversation 3:**
*Citizen*: Ek zameen ke liye mujhe Power of Attorney (PoA) banwani hai.
*Assistant*: Zameen ke transactions ke liye aapko 'Registered General Power of Attorney' banwani hogi, jisme Sub-Registrar office mein proper stamp duty pay karke registration karwana zaroori hai.
*Citizen*: Kya main isse baad mein cancel kar sakta hu?
*Assistant*: Haan, aap ek Revocation Deed register karke us PoA ko cancel kar sakte hain.

**Conversation 4:**
*Citizen*: Is Anticipatory Bail the same as Regular Bail?
*Assistant*: No, Anticipatory Bail is applied for BEFORE a person is arrested, when they fear they might be arrested. Regular Bail is applied for AFTER a person has already been arrested and is in custody.
*Citizen*: Which court grants Anticipatory Bail?
*Assistant*: Anticipatory Bail can only be granted by the Sessions Court or the High Court.

**Conversation 5:**
*Citizen*: Maine property dispute mein Stay Order liya hai, iska kya asar hoga?
*Assistant*: Stay Order (Injunction) ka matlab hai ki court ne current situation (status quo) ko maintain karne ka order diya hai. Jab tak final faisla nahi aata, property ko na becha ja sakta hai aur na hi waha koi construction ho sakti hai.

**Conversation 6:**
*Citizen*: My name is spelled wrong in my educational certificate. How to fix it?
*Assistant*: You need to swear an Affidavit on a stamp paper detailing the correct spelling, get it notarized, and publish the name correction in two local newspapers.
*Citizen*: Do I need a lawyer for the affidavit?
*Assistant*: No, you can draft it yourself or use standard templates available with any local notary.

**Conversation 7:**
*Citizen*: What does 'Pro Bono' representation mean?
*Assistant*: 'Pro Bono' means the lawyer will handle your case for free, as a public service, without charging any legal fees.
*Citizen*: Are there government bodies that provide this?
*Assistant*: Yes, the National Legal Services Authority (NALSA) provides free legal aid to eligible categories of citizens.

**Conversation 8:**
*Citizen*: Kya divorce ke baad Alimony dena zaroori hota hai?
*Assistant*: Alimony ya maintenance court decide karti hai based on the financial status, income, aur needs of both spouses. Agar ek spouse financially dependent hai, toh court alimony ka order de sakti hai.

**Conversation 9:**
*Citizen*: Ex-parte decree kya hoti hai?
*Assistant*: Agar court ki date par doosri party (defendant) lagataar notice ke baad bhi present nahi hoti, toh court sirf aapki baat sunkar faisla de sakti hai. Ise Ex-parte decree kehte hain.

**Conversation 10:**
*Citizen*: FIR mein 'Cognizable' aur 'Non-Cognizable' ka kya difference hai?
*Assistant*: Cognizable offenses serious crimes hote hain (jaise murder, theft) jisme police turant FIR likhti hai. Non-Cognizable offenses minor hote hain jisme police Magistrate ke order ke bina investigate nahi kar sakti.

**Conversation 11:**
*Citizen*: Who is an Amicus Curiae?
*Assistant*: An Amicus Curiae is an independent expert or lawyer appointed by the court to assist it in complex cases. They do not represent either the plaintiff or the defendant.

**Conversation 12:**
*Citizen*: Mera ek civil suit chal raha hai. Isme Decree ka kya matlab hai?
*Assistant*: Decree court ka final decision hota hai jo dono parties ke rights ko conclusively determine karta hai. Suit jeetne par court aapke favour mein Decree pass karti hai.

**Conversation 13:**
*Citizen*: What is Habeas Corpus?
*Assistant*: It is a constitutional writ. If someone is illegally detained by the police or any person, you can file a Habeas Corpus petition in the High Court or Supreme Court to demand that the detained person be brought before the court.

**Conversation 14:**
*Citizen*: Chargesheet kya hoti hai?
*Assistant*: Chargesheet police ki final investigation report hoti hai. Jab police ke paas accused ke khilaaf enough evidence mil jata hai, tab wo court mein Chargesheet file karte hain taaki trial shuru ho sake.

**Conversation 15:**
*Citizen*: Can a person who is acquitted be tried again for the exact same crime?
*Assistant*: No. Under the principle of 'Double Jeopardy', a person cannot be prosecuted and punished for the same offense more than once.
