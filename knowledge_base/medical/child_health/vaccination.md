# Bachho ka Teekakaran (Childhood Vaccination Schedule, Vaccines, and Care)

## Overview

### English
Vaccination is the most effective clinical method to protect children from life-threatening infectious diseases. In India, the National Immunisation Schedule (NIS) provides free vaccines to protect children against 12 vaccine-preventable diseases, including Tuberculosis, Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus, Polio, Measles, Rubella, severe childhood diarrhea (Rotavirus), and pneumonia. Achieving full immunisation protects the child individually and helps build herd immunity in the community. Managing mild post-vaccination reactions, such as fever or local swelling, is simple and should not delay subsequent doses.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
Childhood vaccination protects against major diseases. Key vaccines in the National Immunisation Schedule include BCG, OPV/IPV, Rotavirus, Pentavalent (DPT-HepB-Hib), PCV, and MR. Post-vaccination symptoms are typically mild, such as low-grade fever, local pain, redness, or fussiness. Diagnostics involve clinical immunization card checks. Management involves paracetamol for fever and a cold sponge for swelling. Prevention emphasizes completing all vaccines on schedule, including catch-up doses under Mission Indradhanush.

---

## Definition
* **Immunisation:** The process whereby a person is made immune or resistant to an infectious disease, typically by the administration of a vaccine.
* **National Immunisation Schedule (NIS):** The official government schedule detailing the specific vaccines, doses, routes of administration, and exact ages at which children in India should receive immunisations.
* **Mission Indradhanush:** An immunization campaign by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW) to identify and vaccinate partially vaccinated or unvaccinated children in high-risk, remote areas.

---

## Immunology / Background
Vaccines train the child's immune system:
1. **Antigen Recognition:** Vaccines contain weakened or killed parts of microbes (antigens) that do not cause disease but stimulate B-lymphocytes to produce specific **antibodies**.
2. **Immunological Memory:** The immune system creates memory cells. If the child is exposed to the real active virus or bacteria in the future, these memory cells instantly recognize the pathogen and release antibodies to destroy it before it can cause severe disease.

## Causes of Vaccine Preventable Diseases

### The 12 Targeted Diseases in India
* **Tuberculosis (TB):** Caused by *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* bacteria, causing lung and brain damage (BCG protects against severe childhood forms).
* **Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus (DPT):** Bacterial infections causing throat blockage, severe "whooping" cough, and lockjaw muscle spasms.
* **Polio:** Poliovirus infecting the nervous system, causing permanent paralysis of legs.
* **Hepatitis B:** Viral infection causing liver damage and chronic cirrhosis.
* **Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib):** Bacteria causing pediatric meningitis and pneumonia.
* **Rotavirus:** High-risk virus causing severe, dehydrating diarrhea.
* **Pneumococcus:** *Streptococcus pneumoniae* causing severe pneumonia and brain fever.
* **Measles & Rubella (MR):** Highly contagious viral infections causing severe rash, fever, blindness, and congenital birth defects.
* **Japanese Encephalitis (JE):** Mosquito-borne viral brain fever (targeted in endemic districts).

### Risk Factors for Missing Vaccines
* Lack of awareness among parents, fear of post-vaccination fever, migrating to different cities, or living in remote rural pockets without active healthcare sub-centers.

## Symptoms (Post-Vaccination Side Effects)

### English
* **Normal Mild Reactions:** Low-grade fever (99°F to 101°F); mild pain, redness, and swelling at the injection site (common after Pentavalent/DPT); mild fussiness or sleepiness; a small sore or blister forming at the site of BCG vaccination on the left shoulder (occurs 2-3 weeks after, which is a normal sign of successful vaccination).
* **Severe Rare Reactions (Anaphylaxis):** High fever ($>104^\circ$F); persistent, high-pitched crying lasting more than 3 hours; fits or convulsions (febrile seizures); swelling of the face or throat, hives, and difficulty breathing within 30 minutes of injection (extremely rare allergic reaction).

### Hindi
* **सामान्य हल्की प्रतिक्रियाएं:** हल्का बुखार (99°F से 101°F); इंजेक्शन वाली जगह पर हल्का दर्द, लाली और सूजन (पेंटावेलेंट/डीपीटी के बाद आम); बच्चे का चिड़चिड़ा होना; बाएं कंधे पर बीसीजी (BCG) का टीका लगने की जगह पर एक छोटा घाव या पपड़ी बनना (यह टीका सफल होने का सामान्य संकेत है और 2-3 सप्ताह बाद बनता है)।
* **गंभीर दुर्लभ प्रतिक्रियाएं:** तेज बुखार ($>104^\circ$F); लगातार 3 घंटे से अधिक समय तक बच्चे का रोना; तेज बुखार के साथ दौरे पड़ना; चेहरे या गले में सूजन, चकत्ते और सांस लेने में कठिनाई (अत्यंत दुर्लभ एलर्जी प्रतिक्रिया)।

### Hinglish
* **Normal Side Effects:** Halka bukhar (99°F se 101°F); sui (injection) lagne wali jagah par pain, redness aur sujan hona; bachhe ka thoda chidchida ho jana; left shoulder par BCG teeke ke 2-3 week baad ek chota ghaw/phunsiya banna (yeh normal hai aur iska scar ban jata hai jo teeka successful hone ka sign hai).
* **Severe Rare Side Effects:** Behad tez bukhar (>104°F); bachhe ka lagatar 3 ghante se tez rona; bukhar ke sath doure padna (seizures); injection lagne ke turant baad face par sujan, hives (laal thapde), aur saans lene me dikkat hona (allergic reaction).

## Diagnosis

### Immunisation Card Verification
* A pediatrician or auxiliary nurse midwife (ANM) checks the child’s Mother and Child Protection (MCP) card (Mamta card) to identify which vaccines have been given and which are overdue or missed.

### Evaluation of Vaccine Reaction
* Distinguishing between a normal, self-limiting vaccine reaction (local soreness, mild fever) and a severe Adverse Event Following Immunization (AEFI) requiring medical care.

## Treatment & Care of Post-Vaccination Reactions

### Local Site Care

#### English
* Apply a cold compress (a clean cloth dipped in cold water) gently to the swollen injection site to reduce pain. Do not rub the injection site. Do not apply hot compresses or massage the area. Do not apply any ointments or home remedies to the BCG sore.

#### Hindi
* दर्द और सूजन को कम करने के लिए सूजी हुई जगह पर ठंडे पानी में भीगा साफ कपड़ा (cold compress) धीरे-धीरे लगाएं। इंजेक्शन वाली जगह को रगड़ें या मालिश न करें। गर्म सिकाई न करें। बीसीजी (BCG) के घाव पर कोई मरहम या दवा न लगाएं।

#### Hinglish
* Injection lagne wali jagah par sujan aur pain kam karne ke liye saaf kapde ko thande paani me bhigo kar halki sikaai (cold compress) karein. Sui wali jagah ko hath se bilkul na ragdein ya massage karein. BCG wale ghaw par koi tel ya ointment na lagayein.

### Fever Management

#### English
* Give oral Paracetamol drops/syrup under pediatric dosage guidance if the child develops a fever or is in severe pain. Keep the child in comfortable, loose cotton clothes and offer plenty of breast milk or water.

#### Hindi
* यदि बच्चे को बुखार आता है या तेज दर्द होता है, तो बाल रोग विशेषज्ञ की सलाह पर पेरासिटामोल सिरप/ड्रॉप्स दें। बच्चे को ढीले सूती कपड़े पहनाएं और अधिक मात्रा में स्तनपान या पानी दें।

#### Hinglish
* Bukhar aane par doctor ki batayi hui pediatric Paracetamol drops/syrup dein. Bachhe ko tight kapde na pehnayein, cotton ke dheele kapde pehnayein aur breastfeeding/paani pilayein.

### Advanced AEFI Management

#### English
* **Adrenaline Injection:** Given immediately by clinical staff if a child develops severe allergic shock (anaphylaxis) within minutes of vaccination. (Every government immunisation session has an AEFI kit containing Adrenaline).
* **Intravenous (IV) Anticonvulsants:** Administered in a pediatric ward if the child experiences a febrile seizure due to high fever.

#### Hindi
* **एड्रेनालाईन इंजेक्शन (Adrenaline):** यदि टीकाकरण के तुरंत बाद गंभीर एलर्जी प्रतिक्रिया (anaphylaxis) होती है, तो डॉक्टर द्वारा दिया जाने वाला जीवन रक्षक इंजेक्शन।
* **दौरे की दवाएं (Anticonvulsants):** अस्पताल में दी जाने वाली दवाएं यदि तेज बुखार के कारण बच्चे को दौरे पड़ते हैं।

#### Hinglish
* **Adrenaline Injection:** Agar sui lagne ke turant baad severe allergic shock (anaphylaxis) ho, to hospital me life-saving Adrenaline injection diya jata hai.
* **Febrile Seizure treatment:** Tez bukhar ke doure padne par hospital me pediatric ward me control ki dawa di jati hai.

## National Immunisation Schedule (NIS) - India
*The following is the official schedule for infant immunisation in India:*
1. **At Birth:**
   * **BCG:** Injectable (left upper arm) - Protects against Tuberculosis.
   * **OPV (0 dose):** Oral drops - Protects against Polio.
   * **Hepatitis B (Birth dose):** Injectable (thigh) - Protects against Hep B.
2. **At 6 Weeks, 10 Weeks, and 14 Weeks:**
   * **Pentavalent (1, 2, 3):** Injectable (thigh) - Covers Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus, Hep B, and Hib.
   * **OPV (1, 2, 3):** Oral drops - Polio protection.
   * **Rotavirus Vaccine (1, 2, 3):** Oral drops - Severe diarrhea protection.
   * **Fractional IPV (1, 2):** Injectable (intradermal - given at 6 and 14 weeks) - Inactivated Polio protection.
   * **PCV (1, 2):** Injectable (thigh - given at 6 and 14 weeks) - Pneumococcal protection.
3. **At 9 Months:**
   * **MR (1st dose):** Injectable (right upper arm) - Measles and Rubella protection.
   * **PCV Booster:** Injectable (thigh) - Pneumococcal booster.
   * **Vitamin A (1st dose):** Oral solution - Blindness prevention (given every 6 months up to 5 years).
   * **JE (1st dose):** Injectable (left upper arm - in endemic districts).
4. **At 16 to 24 Months:**
   * **MR (2nd dose) & JE (2nd dose).**
   * **DPT Booster (1st dose) & OPV Booster.**
5. **At 5 to 6 Years:**
   * **DPT Booster (2nd dose).**
6. **At 10 Years & 16 Years:**
   * **Td Vaccine:** Tetanus and adult Diphtheria booster.

## Prevention of Vaccine Default (Missed Doses)

### English
* **Track Mamta Card:** Keep the MCP (Mother and Child Protection) card safe and note down the next due date written by the ANM.
* **Participate in Catch-Up Campaigns:** If your child has missed any doses, visit the nearest government immunization session immediately or during **Mission Indradhanush** catch-up rounds to complete the schedule.
* **Follow Vaccination Timelines:** Do not delay vaccines due to minor skin rashes, mild colds, or loose stools; vaccinations are safe to give during minor illnesses.

### Hindi
* **ममता कार्ड (MCP Card) संभाल कर रखें:** टीकाकरण कार्ड को सुरक्षित रखें और एएनएम (ANM) द्वारा लिखी गई अगली तारीख पर बच्चे को केंद्र ले जाएं।
* **कैच-अप अभियानों में भाग लें:** यदि बच्चे का कोई टीका छूट गया है, तो उसे तुरंत पूरा करने के लिए **मिशन इंद्रधनुष** के तहत टीके लगवाएं।
* **मामूली बीमारियों में देरी न करें:** हल्के सर्दी-जुकाम, खांसी या दस्त होने पर टीकाकरण में देरी न करें; इन स्थितियों में भी टीका लगवाना सुरक्षित है।

### Hinglish
* **Mamta Card (MCP) safe rakhein:** Teekakaran card ko sambhal kar rakhein aur agla teeka kab lagna hai, wo date check karte rahein.
* **Mission Indradhanush me catch-up karein:** Agar bachhe ka koi teeka chhoot gaya hai, to sarkari dispensary me jakar use turant poora karayein.
* **Halki bimari me sui na rokhein:** Halka sardi-khansi ya patla dast hone par teekakaran me deri na karein, in bimariyon me teeka lagwana bilkul safe hai.

## Possible Complications of Unvaccinated State
* **Permanent Paralysis:** Contracting wild poliovirus, leading to asymmetric flaccid paralysis of legs.
* **Congenital Rubella Syndrome:** A pregnant woman catching Rubella from an unvaccinated child, causing severe heart defects, cataracts, and deafness in the unborn baby.
* **Subacute Sclerosing Panencephalitis (SSPE):** A rare, fatal brain degeneration occurring years after a severe Measles infection in an unvaccinated child.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The child develops a high fever ($>104^\circ$F/40°C) that does not drop with paracetamol.**
* **The child experiences a fit/seizure, or becomes unconscious/floppy.**
* **The child shows signs of severe allergic shock: swelling of face, difficulty breathing, or severe rashes within hours of vaccination.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If the swelling or redness at the injection site continues to grow larger after 48 hours.
* If the child has a high fever that lasts more than 24-48 hours after vaccination.
* If you have lost your child’s immunisation card and need to create a catch-up schedule.

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **Universal Immunization Programme (UIP):** One of the largest public health programs in the world, providing 12 life-saving vaccines free of cost to all infants and pregnant women in India.
* **Mission Indradhanush (Intensified Mission Indradhanush - IMI):** Special catch-up campaigns targeting districts with low vaccination rates to ensure unvaccinated children are fully immunised.
* **U-WIN Portal:** A digital platform developed by the Indian government (modeled on Co-WIN) to register pregnant women and children, track vaccinations digitally, and send automated SMS reminders for next doses.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is the National Immunisation Schedule (NIS)?**
   The NIS is a government-recommended schedule of essential vaccinations given to children in India from birth to 16 years of age, provided free of cost at government health centers.

2. **Why is the BCG vaccine given on the left shoulder?**
   BCG is traditionally given in the left upper arm (intradermally) to maintain uniformity across all healthcare workers, making it easy to identify the vaccine scar later.

3. **My child has a mild cold and cough. Can they get vaccinated?**
   Yes. A mild cold, cough, low-grade fever, or mild loose stools are not contraindications. It is completely safe to vaccinate a child during mild illnesses.

4. **What should I do if my child missed a vaccine dose?**
   Do not restart the series. Visit a pediatrician or ANM immediately to get a "catch-up" dose. Completing the remaining doses is crucial for protection.

5. **Why does a blister form after the BCG vaccine?**
   A small nodule or blister forms at the BCG injection site 2-3 weeks after vaccination, which may rupture and form a shallow sore that heals with a permanent scar. This is a normal, healthy reaction.

6. **What is the Pentavalent vaccine?**
   The Pentavalent vaccine is a single injection that protects against five deadly diseases: Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus, Hepatitis B, and *Haemophilus influenzae* type b (Hib).

7. **How does the Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) differ from the Inactivated Polio Vaccine (IPV)?**
   OPV is given as oral drops and contains live-attenuated virus that protects the gut. IPV is an injection of killed virus given to provide strong blood immunity. Both are used together in India.

8. **What is Mission Indradhanush?**
   It is a government health mission aimed at immunizing all unvaccinated or partially vaccinated children and pregnant women in remote, high-risk, and underserved areas in India.

9. **Can the MR vaccine cause autism?**
    No. Extensive scientific research involving millions of children worldwide has proven that the Measles-Rubella (MR) vaccine is extremely safe and does not cause autism.

10. **Where can I get a new Mamta card if I lost the old one?**
    You can get a duplicate Mother and Child Protection (MCP) card from your local ASHA worker, ANM, or the nearest government primary health center (PHC).

11. **Why is Vitamin A given every 6 months to children?**
    Vitamin A is given orally to children from 9 months to 5 years (total of 9 doses) to prevent night blindness, corneal damage, and boost immunity against severe infections.

12. **What should I do if my child has a fever after vaccination?**
    Low-grade fever is a normal sign that the body is building immunity. Give paracetamol pediatric syrup as prescribed by your doctor and use a cool damp cloth on the forehead.

13. **Why should I not massage the injection site?**
    Massaging or rubbing the injection site can force the vaccine fluid into deeper muscle layers too quickly, causing increased pain, local inflammation, or sterile abscesses.

14. **What is the PCV vaccine schedule?**
    Under the UIP, the Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine (PCV) is given at 6 weeks, 14 weeks, and a booster dose is given at 9 months of age.

15. **Can my child get vaccinated at a generic Jan Aushadhi Kendra?**
    No. Jan Aushadhi Kendras sell generic medicines but do not administer vaccines. Vaccines are administered at Anganwadis, sub-centers, PHCs, government hospitals, and private clinics.

16. **What is the U-WIN platform?**
    U-WIN is the Indian government's digital registry for tracking and recording the vaccination status of pregnant women and children, sending automated SMS reminders for upcoming doses.

17. **What is Congenital Rubella Syndrome?**
    If a pregnant woman is infected with Rubella, it can cause severe birth defects in the fetus, including blindness, deafness, and heart damage. Vaccinating children prevents them from spreading it to pregnant women.

18. **Why does the DPT booster cause a large swelling?**
    The pertussis component in the DPT booster can cause a localized reaction, presenting as pain and swelling. This is normal and resolves in 2-3 days with cold compresses.

19. **What is AEFI?**
    AEFI stands for Adverse Event Following Immunization. It is any untoward medical occurrence that follows immunization, which is monitored closely by the government for safety.

20. **Can I delay vaccinations until my baby is older and stronger?**
    No. Delaying vaccines leaves the baby unprotected during their most vulnerable months, when diseases like whooping cough or rotavirus diarrhea can be fatal. Stick strictly to the schedule.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Vaccines make the child sick with fever, so they are dangerous.
  * **Fact:** A mild fever is a normal sign that the child's immune system is actively responding to the vaccine and building protection. It is harmless.
* **Myth:** Natural immunity from catching the disease is better than vaccine immunity.
  * **Fact:** Catching the disease (like polio or measles) can lead to permanent leg paralysis, brain damage, blindness, or death. Vaccines provide safe protection without the disease.
* **Myth:** You should massage the injection site with oil to reduce pain.
  * **Fact:** Massaging can damage tissues and irritate the area, increasing pain and risk of infection. Use a cool damp cloth gently instead.
* **Myth:** Mission Indradhanush vaccines are of lower quality than private hospital vaccines.
  * **Fact:** All government vaccines under the UIP and Mission Indradhanush are WHO-prequalified, highly tested, and of the exact same high quality as private vaccines.
* **Myth:** If you miss the exact date on the Mamta card, you have to restart all vaccines from the beginning.
  * **Fact:** You never need to restart the vaccine course. Simply visit the clinic and continue from where you left off.
* **Myth:** MMR vaccine causes autism in children.
  * **Fact:** This is a proven hoax. The original study making this claim was retracted due to falsified data. No link exists between vaccines and autism.
* **Myth:** Giving multiple vaccines at the same time (like Pentavalent + Rotavirus) overwhelms the baby's immune system.
  * **Fact:** A baby's immune system is strong enough to process thousands of antigens daily. Giving combined vaccines reduces the number of clinic visits and pricks.
* **Myth:** Breastfeeding is a natural vaccine, so vaccinated drops are not needed.
  * **Fact:** Breast milk boosts overall immunity but does not provide specific protection against diseases like polio, tetanus, or measles. Vaccines are essential.
* **Myth:** Polio has been eradicated from India, so OPV drops are no longer needed.
  * **Fact:** India is polio-free, but neighboring countries still report cases. Continued vaccination is essential to prevent the virus from returning.
* **Myth:** Girls do not need the Rubella vaccine.
  * **Fact:** Rubella vaccine is crucial for girls. It prevents them from catching Rubella when they grow up and become pregnant, protecting their unborn babies from severe birth defects.

* **Assistant:** Nahi, yeh bilkul normal reaction hai. BCG teeke ke 2-4 hafte baad wahan ek choti phunsni banna normal hai jo baad me sukh kar ek permanent scar (nishan) chhodti hai. Aap is par koi tel, powder, ya antiseptic na lagayein, na ise phodein. Yeh apne aap dry hokar heal ho jayegi.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Sui lagne ke 15 minutes baad mere bachhe ke chehre (face) par achanak sujan aa gayi hai aur use saans lene me takleef ho rahi hai.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Yeh sui lagne ke baad ki ek severe allergic reaction (Anaphylaxis) ho sakti hai, jo bohot rare hoti hai. Aap bina ek second waste kiye bachhe ko turant hospital ke emergency room me le jayein jahan use Adrenaline injection diya jayega.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** Mission Indradhanush kya hota hai aur isme teeke kaise lagte hain?
* **Assistant:** Mission Indradhanush government ka ek special campaign hai jisme un bachho ko dhoondh kar teeke lagaye jate hain jinke teeke kisi wajah se chhoot gaye hain ya jo door-daraaz ke gaon me rehte hain. Isme bhi sabhi teeke bilkul free lagaye jate hain.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** U-WIN app kya hai aur iska kya use hai?
* **Assistant:** U-WIN government ka ek digital portal hai (jaise Co-WIN tha) jisme bachho aur pregnant women ke teekakaran ka digital record rakha jata hai. Isme register karne par aapko aane wale teeke ke automatic SMS reminders aate hain aur aap digital certificate bhi download kar sakte hain.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Mere relative ko measles rubella (MR) teeka lagna hai par unhe lagatar halka sardi-khansi hai. Kya teeka lagwayein?
* **Assistant:** Haan, halki sardi-khansi ya halka dast hone par MR teeka lagwana bilkul safe hai. Teeke ko tab tak na rokein jab tak ki bachhe ko severe high bukhar na ho ya wo hospital me admit na ho.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** DPT booster teeka lagne ke baad bachhe ki jangh (thigh) par bohot badi sujan aur laal-pan ho gaya hai aur bachha chal nahi pa raha.
* **Assistant:** DPT teeke ke baad thigh par local sujan aur pain hona aam baat hai. Aap sui lagne wale area par saaf cotton kapde ko thande paani me bhigo kar sikaai karein (din me 3-4 baar). Bachhe ko paracetamol syrup dein. Yeh sujan 2-3 din me apne aap theek ho jayegi.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Kya polio vaccine ki drops pilane ke baad baby ko breastfeed karwa sakte hain?
* **Assistant:** Haan. Polio drops pilane ke turant baad breastfeed karwane me koi burai nahi hai. Breast milk polio drops ki effectiveness ko kam nahi karta.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** PCV vaccine kya hoti hai aur ye kis bimari se bachati hai?
* **Assistant:** PCV ka matlab Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine hai. Yeh bachhe ko Streptococcus pneumoniae bacteria se hone wale khatarnak Pneumonia (fefde ka infection) aur Meningitis (dimagi bukhar) se bachata hai.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the cost of pediatric paracetamol syrup at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?
* **Assistant:** Generic Pediatric Paracetamol drops or syrups are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, often costing only ₹10 to ₹15 per bottle, compared to ₹40+ in private pharmacies.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Congenital Rubella Syndrome kya hota hai aur Rubella vaccine kyu zaroori hai?
* **Assistant:** Agar pregnant woman ko rubella infection ho jaye, to baby severe birth defects (jaise blindness, deafness, heart problems) ke sath paida ho sakta hai jise Congenital Rubella Syndrome kehte hain. Bachho ko Rubella vaccine dekar hum pregnant women ko is infection se bachate hain.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** Kya private hospitals ke teeke sarkari hospitals se better hote hain?
* **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Yeh ek bada myth hai. Government dispensaries me milne wale teeke WHO approved aur highly tested hote hain. Dono jagah ke teeke same quality ke hote hain, bas government me yeh bilkul free hote hain.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Sui lagne ke baad bachhe ko achanak achanak fits (doure) pad rahe hain aur bukhar 104°F ho gaya hai.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Yeh high fever se hone wale "Febrile Seizure" (doure) ka case ho sakta hai. Aap bachhe ko turant karwat dilakar paas ke hospital ke pediatric emergency room me le jayein aur forehead par normal paani ki patti rakhein.
