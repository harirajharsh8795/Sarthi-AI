# Vaccines (Vaccines) ki Pehchan, UIP Schedule aur Immunity Guide (National Immunization and Vaccine Safety Guide)

## Overview

### English
Vaccines are biological preparations that provide **active acquired immunity** to a specific infectious disease. They work by introducing a weakened, killed, or synthetic antigen of a pathogen (virus or bacteria) into the body, training the immune system to produce antibodies and memory cells without causing the disease itself. Vaccines are classified into **Live-Attenuated** (e.g., BCG, MMR, OPV), **Inactivated** (e.g., IPV, Rabies), **Subunit/Recombinant** (e.g., Hepatitis B, HPV), and **mRNA** (e.g., COVID-19 mRNA vaccines). In India, the government’s **Universal Immunization Programme (UIP)** provides 12 life-saving vaccines completely free of cost to all children and pregnant women.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
Vaccines train the immune system for active immunity. Key classes include live-attenuated and inactivated vaccines. The national UIP schedule covers birth to 16 years. First aid handles mild local reactions with paracetamol and monitors for immediate anaphylaxis (allergic shock). Diagnostics check antibody titers. Management tracks immunization records.

---

## Definition
* **Active Acquired Immunity:** Protective immunity stimulated by introducing an antigen into the body, inducing antibody production and memory cell formation.
* **Live-Attenuated Vaccines:** Vaccines containing live virus/bacteria that have been weakened so they cannot cause disease in healthy people (e.g., BCG, Rotavirus, Varicella).
* **UIP (Universal Immunization Programme):** India’s national vaccination program, launched in 1985, targeting zero vaccine-preventable deaths.

## National Immunization Schedule (UIP India)

The standard government schedule for free pediatric vaccination in India:

| Age | Vaccines Administered | Disease Protection |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **At Birth** | BCG, Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV-0), Hepatitis B birth dose | TB, Polio, Hepatitis B |
| **6 Weeks** | Pentavalent-1, OPV-1, Rotavirus Vaccine (RVV-1), Fractional IPV (fIPV-1), PCV-1 | DPT + HepB + Hib, Polio, Diarrhea, Pneumonia |
| **10 Weeks** | Pentavalent-2, OPV-2, RVV-2 | DPT + HepB + Hib, Polio, Diarrhea |
| **14 Weeks** | Pentavalent-3, OPV-3, RVV-3, fIPV-2, PCV-2 | DPT + HepB + Hib, Polio, Diarrhea, Pneumonia |
| **9 - 12 Months** | MR-1 (Measles-Rubella), JE-1 (in endemic districts), PCV-Booster | Measles, Rubella, Brain Fever, Pneumonia |
| **16 - 24 Months**| MR-2, JE-2, DPT-Booster-1, OPV-Booster | Measles, Rubella, Diphtheria + Pertussis + Tetanus, Polio |
| **5 - 6 Years** | DPT-Booster-2 | Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus |
| **10 & 16 Years** | Td (Tetanus & adult Diphtheria) | Tetanus, Diphtheria |

## Symptoms of Vaccine Reactions & Side Effects

### English
* **Mild Local Reactions (Normal):** Mild pain, redness, and swelling directly at the injection site; low-grade fever ($99^\circ\text{F} - 100.5^\circ\text{F}$) lasting 24-48 hours; mild irritability or drowsiness in infants.
* **Severe Allergic Reaction (Anaphylaxis - Rare):** Swelling of the face, lips, or throat; difficulty breathing or wheezing; hives and pale skin; rapid heart rate and fainting.

### Hindi
* **सामान्य लक्षण (Normal reactions):** सुई लगने वाली जगह पर हल्का दर्द, लाली और सूजन होना; 1-2 दिन तक हल्का बुखार रहना; बच्चों का चिड़चिड़ा होना।
* **गंभीर एलर्जी (Anaphylaxis - अत्यंत दुर्लभ):** चेहरे और गले में सूजन आना; सांस लेने में कठिनाई होना; बेहोश होना।

### Hinglish
* **Normal Reactions (Halke side effects):** Injection lagne wali jagah par halka dard, redness (laali) aur sujan (swelling) hona; 24-48 ghante tak halka bukhar (low-grade fever) rehna; bachho ka chichida hona.
* **Anaphylaxis (Severe Allergy - behod rare):** Face, lips ya throat me sujan aana; saans lene me severe takleef hona; chitti rashes hona aur behosh hona.

## First Aid & Post-Vaccination Care

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Apply a cool, damp cloth** gently over the injection site to reduce local swelling and burning pain.
* **Give Paracetamol syrup/tablet** to manage post-vaccination fever and irritability. Calculate the pediatric dose based on body weight ($15\text{ mg/kg}$).
* **Wait at the clinic for 30 minutes** after receiving any vaccine. Almost all severe life-threatening allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) occur within 30 minutes of injection. Staying allows immediate clinical rescue.
* **Continue breastfeeding** infants normally after vaccination.

#### Hindi
* **ठंडे कपड़े से सेकें:** सुई वाली जगह पर ठंडे गीले कपड़े से हल्के से सेकें।
* **बुखार के लिए पैरासिटामोल दें:** बच्चे के वजन के अनुसार पैरासिटामोल (Paracetamol) सिरप दें।
* **टीकाकरण के बाद 30 मिनट तक अस्पताल में रुकें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Cold sponge karein:** Sui lagne wali jagah par sujan kam karne ke liye clean thande gile kapde se sponge karein.
* **Paracetamol syrup dein:** Fever aur dard kam karne ke liye doctor ki salah se bachhe ke weight ke according Paracetamol syrup dein.
* **30 minutes tak clinic me rukein:** Vaccine lagne ke **30 minutes tak hospital me hi wait karein**. Agar koi severe reaction (anaphylaxis) hona hai, to wo isi time me hoga aur doctor turant adrenaline de kar jaan bacha sakte hain.
* **Breastfeed jari rakhein.**

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT rub, massage, or squeeze the injection site.** Massaging forces the vaccine into local tissues too rapidly, worsening muscle inflammation and pain, and can cause a sterile abscess (lump).
* **Do NOT apply ice directly on the skin** (can cause frostbite); always wrap ice in a clean cloth.
* **Do NOT give Aspirin to children** for post-vaccination fever (due to Reye's syndrome risk). Use Paracetamol.

#### Hindi
* **टीकाकरण की जगह पर मालिश (massage) न करें:** रगड़ने से दर्द और मांसपेशियों की सूजन बढ़ सकती है।
* **सीधे बर्फ न रगड़ें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Sui wali jagah par maalish (massage) na karein:** Injection site ko press ya massage na karein, isse vaccine tezi se phail kar muscle me severe pain aur hard gath (abscess) bana sakti hai.
* **Direct ice na lagayein:** Baraf ko hamesha cloth me lapet kar hi lagayein.
* **Aspirin bilkul na dein:** Bukhar ke liye paracetamol hi dein.

## Diagnostic Evaluation of Vaccine Safety
* **Anaphylaxis Protocol:** If a patient collapses after vaccination, emergency doctors immediately assess airway patency, check blood pressure, and inject **Adrenaline (Epinephrine - 1:1000 intramuscularly)**.
* **Antibody Titer Tests:** e.g., Anti-HBs titer check to confirm if a healthcare worker has built active immunity after a 3-dose Hepatitis B vaccine series ($> 10\text{ mIU/mL}$ indicates protection).

3. **Anaphylaxis Rescue Antidote:** Vasopressor (e.g., Adrenaline / Epinephrine injection).

## Prevention of Vaccine-Preventable Diseases (VPDs)
* **Herd Immunity:** Achieving high vaccination rates ($> 90-95\%$) in a community protects unvaccinated vulnerable individuals (like newborns or cancer patients) by stopping the transmission of pathogens.
* **Immunization Record Maintenance:** Keep the national immunization card safe and track booster dates.
* **Pre-pregnancy Vaccination:** Women should complete MR (Rubella) and Td vaccines before planning pregnancy to prevent Congenital Rubella Syndrome.

---

## Possible Complications of Missed Vaccinations
* **Childhood Epidemics:** Outbreaks of Measles, Diphtheria (throat blockage), or Pertussis (whooping cough) leading to high pediatric mortality.
* **Congenital Rubella Syndrome:** Blindness, deafness, and heart defects in newborns if pregnant mothers contract rubella.
* **Polio Paralysis:** Permanent irreversible paralysis in children.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The child develops sudden difficulty breathing, swelling of the face/lips/throat, or collapses within minutes of vaccination (Anaphylactic shock).**
* **The child develops high fever ($> 104^\circ\text{F}$), extreme lethargy, or seizures/fits (encephalopathy).**
* **The child cries uncontrollably for more than 3 hours without stopping.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **Universal Immunization Programme (UIP):** A central government initiative providing free vaccines against 12 vaccine-preventable diseases to all children and pregnant mothers.
* **Mission Indradhanush:** A targeted immunization campaign to reach unvaccinated or partially vaccinated children in remote, rural, and urban slum areas.
* **AEFI (Adverse Event Following Immunization) Registry:** A national tracking network under the MoHFW that monitors and investigates any severe side effects after vaccination to ensure vaccine safety.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Offers generic syringes, needles, alcohol swabs, and paracetamol syrups at highly discounted rates.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a vaccine?**
   A vaccine is a biological preparation that trains the body's immune system to recognize and destroy specific pathogens (viruses/bacteria) without causing the disease.

2. **What is the Universal Immunization Programme (UIP)?**
   UIP is India’s national vaccination program that provides 12 life-saving vaccines completely free of charge to all children and pregnant women.

3. **Why do babies get a fever after vaccination?**
   A low-grade fever is a normal sign that the vaccine is working. It shows that the baby's immune system is actively building antibodies and memory cells.

4. **Why must I wait 30 minutes at the clinic after vaccination?**
   Severe, life-threatening allergic reactions (anaphylaxis) are very rare but occur within 30 minutes of injection. Staying at the clinic ensures immediate medical rescue with adrenaline.

5. **Why should I not massage the injection site?**
   Massaging forces the vaccine fluid to spread too fast in local tissues, causing severe muscle inflammation, pain, and can lead to a hard lump (sterile abscess).

6. **What is a live-attenuated vaccine?**
   A live vaccine contains a weakened version of the live virus or bacteria (e.g., BCG, Measles). It provides strong, long-lasting immunity but is avoided in immunocompromised patients.

7. **Where can I get free vaccines for my child in India?**
   All routine vaccines are provided completely free at all government PHCs, sub-centers, Anganwadis, and government civil hospitals.

8. **What is Mission Indradhanush?**
   It is a government catch-up immunization campaign designed to locate and vaccinate all children who missed their routine vaccines in remote or rural areas.

9. **Why is the Hepatitis B vaccine birth dose given within 24 hours?**
   The birth dose protects the newborn from contracting Hepatitis B from the mother during delivery, preventing chronic carrier states and liver cancer later in life.

10. **What is the difference between MR and MMR vaccines?**
    The government MR vaccine protects against Measles and Rubella. The private MMR vaccine protects against Measles, Mumps, and Rubella.

11. **Can I use ice on the vaccination site?**
    Yes, but do not rub ice directly on the skin. Wrap the ice in a clean, damp cloth and hold it gently against the area to reduce swelling and pain.

12. **Why do children need booster doses?**
    Immunity from some vaccines (like DPT) declines over time. Booster doses "remind" the immune system to maintain high levels of protective antibodies.

13. **What is AEFI?**
    AEFI (Adverse Event Following Immunization) is a government surveillance system that tracks, registers, and investigates any severe side effects after vaccination.

14. **What is Herd Immunity?**
    When a high percentage of the population ($>90\%$) is vaccinated, it stops the virus from spreading, protecting vulnerable individuals who cannot get vaccinated.

15. **What is the price of generic Paracetamol syrup at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Paracetamol pediatric syrup is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹8 to ₹12 per bottle.

16. **Why is the BCG vaccine given at birth?**
    The BCG vaccine protects infants against severe, life-threatening forms of tuberculosis, such as TB meningitis and miliary TB.

17. **Can a pregnant woman get vaccinated?**
    Yes. Pregnant women receive the Td (Tetanus & adult Diphtheria) vaccine to protect both the mother and the newborn baby from neonatal tetanus.

18. **Why is Aspirin contraindicated for post-vaccination fever in children?**
    Giving Aspirin to children during viral immune responses can trigger Reye’s Syndrome, a rare but fatal brain and liver swelling. Use Paracetamol only.

19. **What is the vaccine for Cervical Cancer?**
    The HPV (Human Papillomavirus) vaccine protects against the viruses that cause cervical cancer. It is recommended for girls aged 9 to 14 years.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover vaccine reaction ICU care?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for severe vaccine allergic reactions, anaphylactic shock ICU stays, and all rescue medications.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Vaccines cause autism in children.
  * **Fact:** This myth was based on a fraudulent 1998 study that has been completely debunked. Numerous global studies involving millions of children have proven that vaccines do not cause autism.
* **Myth:** Multiple vaccines given at once will overload a baby's immune system.
  * **Fact:** A baby's immune system deals with thousands of environmental germs daily. Combined vaccines (like Pentavalent) are safe and reduce the number of painful injections.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures the fever and local pain after a vaccine.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Use paracetamol syrup and cool water sponging to manage local vaccine symptoms.
* **Myth:** Vaccines contain dangerous microchips to track citizens.
  * **Fact:** This is a ridiculous conspiracy theory. Vaccines contain only viral/bacterial antigens, sterile water, lipids, and trace salts to stimulate natural immunity.
* **Myth:** If you maintain good hygiene and clean water, you do not need vaccines.
  * **Fact:** Hygiene prevents many infections, but highly contagious airborne diseases like Measles or Polio will still spread without vaccination.
* **Myth:** Natural immunity from getting the actual disease is always better than vaccine immunity.
  * **Fact:** Natural infection can cause permanent damage or death (e.g., polio paralysis, measles brain swelling). Vaccines build the same immunity safely without the disease.
* **Myth:** Pregnant women should never receive any vaccines.
  * **Fact:** Td and influenza vaccines are highly safe and recommended during pregnancy to protect both the mother and the unborn baby.
* **Myth:** Vaccines are a conspiracy by pharmaceutical companies and are completely ineffective.
  * **Fact:** Vaccines have eradicated Smallpox globally and eliminated Polio from India, saving millions of children's lives every year.
* **Myth:** You should massage the baby's thigh with hot mustard oil after vaccination.
  * **Fact:** Massaging can cause severe local inflammation, muscle damage, and sterile abscesses. Keep the area clean and apply cool damp cloth compresses only.
* **Myth:** The Polio vaccine causes infertility in children.
  * **Fact:** This is a false rumor. The polio vaccine is completely safe and has no effect on reproductive systems.
