# CT Scan (Computed Tomography) ki Pehchan, Prakriya aur Niyam (CT Scan Imaging and Contrast Safety)

## Overview

### English
A Computed Tomography (CT) scan is an advanced radiodiagnostic imaging technique that uses a rotating X-ray tube and computer processing to generate detailed, cross-sectional, 3D images (slices) of internal organs, bones, soft tissues, and blood vessels. It is highly effective for rapidly detecting internal bleeding (brain hemorrhage in stroke or trauma), fractures, pulmonary embolisms, and staging cancers. When a contrast dye (iodine-based contrast) is injected to highlight blood vessels, the patient must have normal kidney function (checked via creatinine level) to safely excrete the dye and avoid contrast-induced nephropathy.

### Hindi
सीटी स्कैन (CT Scan - Computed Tomography) एक उन्नत रेडियो-डायग्नोस्टिक इमेजिंग तकनीक है जो शरीर के आंतरिक अंगों, हड्डियों, कोमल ऊतकों (soft tissues) और रक्त वाहिकाओं के विस्तृत, 3D क्रॉस-सेक्शनल चित्र (slices) बनाने के लिए घूमने वाले एक्स-रे और कंप्यूटर का उपयोग करती है। यह स्ट्रोक या दुर्घटनाओं में आंतरिक रक्तस्राव (brain hemorrhage), ट्यूमर और कैंसर के विभिन्न चरणों (staging) का पता लगाने के लिए अत्यधिक प्रभावी है। यदि रक्त वाहिकाओं को स्पष्ट रूप से देखने के लिए कंट्रास्ट डाई (contrast dye) दी जाती है, तो मरीज के गुर्दे का स्वस्थ होना (क्रिएटिनिन जांच) आवश्यक है ताकि डाई सुरक्षित रूप से बाहर निकल सके।

## Quick Summary
A CT scan uses rotating X-rays to create detailed cross-sectional 3D body slices. It is vital for diagnosing trauma, stroke, and tumors. Key preparations require checking kidney function (Creatinine) before Contrast (CECT) scans. Symptoms of contrast allergies include itching and hives. Treatment for severe allergy involves adrenaline injections. Prevention focuses on limiting unnecessary scans due to radiation.

---

## Definition
* **Computed Tomography (CT):** An imaging method in which a computer coordinates a series of X-ray images taken from different angles to create cross-sectional views of internal organs and tissues.
* **CECT (Contrast-Enhanced CT):** A CT scan performed after injecting an iodine-based contrast medium intravenously, which highlights blood vessels, tumors, and inflammation.
* **Contrast-Induced Nephropathy (CIN):** A form of acute kidney injury caused by exposure to medical contrast media, primarily risking patients with pre-existing renal impairment.

---

## Clinical Relevance & CT Types
CT scan is a fast, life-saving diagnostic tool:
1. **Non-Contrast CT (NCCT):** Done without dye. Essential in acute head trauma or stroke to instantly differentiate between a bleed (white area on CT) and an ischemic clot, directing thrombolytic therapy.
2. **Contrast-Enhanced CT (CECT):** Requires iodine dye. Crucial for oncology (visualizing tumor borders, lymph nodes, and metastases) and abdominal pain (evaluating appendicitis, pancreatitis).
3. **CT Angiography (CTA):** Detailed imaging of blood vessels, used to diagnose coronary artery blocks, aortic aneurysms, or pulmonary embolisms.

---

## Contrast Scan Requirements (Safety Checklist)

Before getting a Contrast (CECT) scan, the patient must fulfill these safety criteria:
* **Serum Creatinine (KFT):** Must be checked within the last 48-72 hours. **If creatinine is $> 1.5$ mg/dL, contrast dye is avoided** to prevent acute kidney injury (Contrast-Induced Nephropathy).
* **Fasting:** The patient must fast (no food) for 4 to 6 hours before a contrast scan to prevent vomiting/aspiration during contrast injection.
* **Allergy History:** Inform the radiologist if the patient is allergic to iodine, seafood, or has asthma.
* **Metformin Drug Stop:** Patients taking Metformin for diabetes must stop the medicine for 48 hours *after* a contrast scan to avoid lactic acidosis.

## Symptoms of Contrast Dye Reactions (Side Effects)

### Mild Reactions (Common)

#### English
* A sudden warm flushing sensation throughout the body; a metallic taste in the mouth; mild nausea or urge to urinate. (These are normal and pass within a minute).

#### Hindi
* शरीर में अचानक गर्माहट महसूस होना; मुंह में धातु जैसा अजीब स्वाद आना; हल्की उल्टी जैसा महसूस होना। (यह सामान्य है और एक मिनट में ठीक हो जाता है)।

#### Hinglish
* Injection lagte hi body me achanak garmi (flush) mehsus hona; muh me metal jaisa taste aana; halki ulti (nausea) jaisa lagna. (Yeh normal hai aur 1 minute me chala jata hai).

### Moderate to Severe Allergic Reactions (Emergency)

#### English
* Severe itching, red hives (rash) on skin; swelling of the face, lips, or throat; wheezing, difficulty breathing, or throat tightness; sudden drop in blood pressure.

#### Hindi
* त्वचा पर गंभीर खुजली और लाल चकत्ते (hives); चेहरे, होंठों या गले में सूजन; सांस लेने में कठिनाई या गले में जकड़न (Anaphylaxis)।

#### Hinglish
* Skin par tez khujli aur laal thapde (rash) banna; chehre, lips ya gale me sujan aana; saans lene me takleef hona ya gale me ghutn lagna (Severe allergic reaction).

## Test Protocol & Radiation Safety
* **Procedure:** The patient lies flat on a motorized bed that slides into the doughnut-shaped CT scanner. The machine rotates, making a whirring sound. The patient must remain completely still and may be asked to hold their breath for a few seconds.
* **Radiation Awareness:** A single CT scan delivers a radiation dose equivalent to 100 to 200 standard chest X-rays. **Avoid repeated, unnecessary CT scans, especially in children and pregnant women**, due to cumulative cancer risks. Lead aprons are used to shield ovaries/testes when scanning other body areas.

## Medical Management of Contrast Reactions

### For Severe Allergic Reactions (Anaphylaxis)

#### English
* Administer an immediate intramuscular Adrenaline injection. Give intravenous fluids, corticosteroids (Hydrocortisone), and antihistamines in the radiology suite.

#### Hindi
* तुरंत मांसपेशियों में एड्रेनालाईन (Adrenaline) का जीवन रक्षक इंजेक्शन देना।

#### Hinglish
* Severe reaction hone par radiology lab me hi immediate Adrenaline injection lagaya jata hai aur IV steroids (Hydrocortisone) diye jaate hain.

### For Post-Scan Contrast Washout

#### English
* Encourage the patient to drink 2 to 3 liters of water over the next 24 hours to flush the iodine contrast dye out of their kidneys through urine.

#### Hindi
* गुर्दे से कंट्रास्ट डाई को बाहर निकालने के लिए अगले 24 घंटों में 2-3 लीटर पानी पिएं।

#### Hinglish
* Scan ke baad next 24 ghante me 2-3 liters paani piyein taaki kidneys ke raste contrast dye urine ke jariye bahar nikal sake.

## Diagnostics / Indicating Conditions
* **CT Head:** Acute stroke (hemorrhagic vs. ischemic), skull fractures, brain tumors, subdural hematoma.
* **CT Abdomen:** Acute pancreatitis, appendicitis, intestinal obstruction, kidney stones, liver cysts.
* **CT Chest (HRCT):** Interstitial lung disease (ILD), severe COVID-19 pneumonia, bronchiectasis.

---

## Prevention of Contrast Kidney Damage
* **Pre-hydration:** Hydrate the patient with intravenous normal saline before and after the contrast injection if they have mild kidney dysfunction.
* **Alternative Imaging:** Suggest MRI or Ultrasound instead of CT contrast if the patient has severe renal failure or is pregnant.
* **Verify Creatinine:** Never skip checking the serum creatinine report before a CECT scan.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Sudden face/throat swelling and severe difficulty breathing within hours of a contrast CT scan.**
* **Anuria (complete stop in urine production) or swelling of the legs/face within 24-48 hours after a contrast scan.**
* **Sudden severe headache, confusion, or weakness on one side of the body.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Programme for Prevention and Control of Non-Communicable Diseases (NPCDCS):** Installs advanced CT scan machines in government district hospitals, providing free scans for cancer and stroke patients.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless diagnostic packages for CECT abdomen, CT head emergency scans, and CT angiographies.
* **Free Diagnostics Services Initiative (NHM):** Provides completely free CT scan services to BPL cardholders at designated government medical colleges.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a CT scan?**
   A CT (Computed Tomography) scan uses rotating X-rays and computers to create detailed 3D cross-sectional images of the body's internal organs.

2. **What is the difference between NCCT and CECT scans?**
   NCCT is done without dye. CECT (Contrast-Enhanced CT) uses an injected iodine-based contrast dye to highlight blood vessels, tumors, and organs.

3. **Why do I need a creatinine blood test before a contrast CT scan?**
   The contrast dye is cleared by the kidneys. If kidney function is poor (high creatinine), the dye can cause severe kidney damage (Contrast-Induced Nephropathy).

4. **Is fasting required before a CT scan?**
   Fasting for 4-6 hours is required for contrast scans (CECT) to prevent nausea and vomiting. Non-contrast scans (NCCT) usually do not require fasting.

5. **How long does a CT scan take?**
   The actual scanning process is very fast, taking only 5 to 10 minutes. Contrast preparation can take an additional 30 minutes.

6. **What does the warm feeling during contrast injection mean?**
   When contrast dye is injected, it is normal to feel a sudden warm flushing sensation throughout the body and a metallic taste in the mouth. It fades in a minute.

7. **Are CT scans safe during pregnancy?**
   No. CT scans use ionizing radiation, which can harm the developing fetus. Pregnant women should avoid CT scans unless it is a life-saving emergency.

8. **Where can BPL cardholders get a free CT scan in India?**
   BPL cardholders can get free CT scans at designated government district civil hospitals and medical colleges under the National Health Mission (NHM).

9. **Why must Metformin be stopped after a contrast scan?**
   Metformin combined with contrast-induced kidney strain can cause a dangerous buildup of lactic acid in the blood (lactic acidosis). Stop it for 48 hours post-scan.

10. **How do I flush the contrast dye out of my body after the test?**
    Drink plenty of water (2 to 3 liters) over the 24 hours following the scan to help your kidneys flush the dye out through your urine.

11. **What is the difference between a CT scan and an MRI?**
    CT scan uses X-ray radiation and is very fast (good for bone fractures/acute bleeding). MRI uses magnetic fields and radio waves, taking longer but showing superior details of soft tissues.

12. **Can children get CT scans?**
    Yes, but children's tissues are more sensitive to radiation. Pediatric protocols with lower radiation doses ("kid-sized CT") must be used, and scans should only be done when essential.

13. **What is a CT Angiography (CTA)?**
    It is a specialized contrast CT scan that focuses on blood vessels to check for blocks, narrowing, aneurysms, or clots (such as in the heart or lungs).

14. **Why do I need to drink a large amount of water/liquid before an abdominal CT?**
    Drinking oral contrast liquid helps coat and highlight the stomach and intestines, making it easy to differentiate them from other abdominal organs.

15. **What is the radiation dose of a CT scan?**
    A single CT scan delivers a radiation dose equivalent to about 100 to 200 standard chest X-rays, depending on the body part scanned.

16. **Can I wear jewelry during a CT scan?**
    No. Metal jewelry, watches, zippers, and hairpins can block the X-ray beams and create white lines (artifacts) on the scan, ruining the images.

17. **What is Contrast-Induced Nephropathy (CIN)?**
    CIN is a temporary decline in kidney function caused by the toxic effect of contrast dye on renal tubule cells.

18. **Can a person with asthma get a contrast CT scan?**
    Asthma patients have a slightly higher risk of allergic reactions to contrast dye. Inform your radiologist, who may prescribe preventive antihistamines/steroids.

19. **What does a CT scan of the head show in a stroke?**
    It instantly shows if the stroke is hemorrhagic (active brain bleeding appears white) or ischemic (clot), helping the doctor decide if clot-busting drugs can be given.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover cardiac CT angiographies?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for cardiac CT angiographies and abdominal contrast scans at empaneled hospitals.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** CT scans and MRIs are the exact same test.
  * **Fact:** CT scans use X-ray radiation and are fast. MRIs use strong magnets, take much longer, and show different soft tissue details.
* **Myth:** Contrast dye will permanently damage anyone's kidneys.
  * **Fact:** Contrast dye is completely safe for people with normal kidney function (creatinine $<1.2$ mg/dL) who drink plenty of water after the test.
* **Myth:** You can have a contrast CT scan immediately after eating a heavy meal.
  * **Fact:** Having a full stomach raises the risk of vomiting and inhaling food into the lungs (aspiration) during contrast injection. Fast for 4-6 hours.
* **Myth:** Repeated CT scans are completely harmless.
  * **Fact:** CT scans use ionizing radiation. Multiple repeated scans over years increase the cumulative radiation dose, raising long-term cancer risks.
* **Myth:** If you are allergic to seafood, you cannot have a contrast CT scan.
  * **Fact:** While there is a minor cross-reactivity, a seafood allergy does not completely ban contrast scans. Pre-medication with antihistamines is used.
* **Myth:** The warm sensation during contrast injection means something went wrong.
  * **Fact:** The warm flush and metallic taste are normal side effects of the iodine dye entering your blood circulation.
* **Myth:** Lead aprons shield the whole body from CT scan radiation.
  * **Fact:** Lead aprons only shield areas they cover (like the pelvis). They do not stop radiation directed at the specific body part being scanned.
* **Myth:** CT scans take hours to complete.
  * **Fact:** The actual scan takes only a few minutes. You slide in and out of the scanner very quickly.
* **Myth:** A head CT scan can diagnose all types of mental illnesses.
  * **Fact:** CT scans show structural abnormalities like bleeding or tumors. They cannot diagnose functional mental illnesses like depression or schizophrenia.
* **Myth:** You must sleep during a CT scan.
  * **Fact:** You must remain awake and follow instructions to hold your breath or remain still. Sedation is only used for babies who cannot lie still.
