# LFT (Liver Function Test) ki Pehchan, Range aur Parikshan (Liver Function Panel and Bilirubin Guide)

## Overview

### English
A Liver Function Test (LFT), also known as a Hepatic Function Panel, is a group of clinical blood chemistries designed to assess the functional state of the liver and biliary tract. It measures enzymes released by damaged liver cells (SGPT/ALT, SGOT/AST, and Alkaline Phosphatase - ALP), proteins synthesized by the liver (Albumin, Total Protein), and Bilirubin (a yellow pigment from red blood cell breakdown). LFT is crucial for diagnosing jaundice, fatty liver disease, viral hepatitis (A, B, C, E), drug-induced liver injury, and liver cirrhosis. The test requires a venous blood draw, and 8-12 hours of fasting is recommended for accurate protein and bilirubin readings.

## Quick Summary
An LFT measures bilirubin, SGPT, SGOT, ALP, and proteins. Key diagnostic values define normal Bilirubin ($0.2 - 1.2$ mg/dL) and normal SGPT ($7 - 56$ U/L). Symptoms of liver disease include yellow skin/eyes (jaundice), dark urine, and abdominal pain. Diagnostics evaluate cell damage. Management involves quitting alcohol, low-fat diets, and treating underlying viral hepatitis.

---

## Definition
* **Liver Function Test (LFT):** A panel of blood tests checking bilirubin, liver enzymes (ALT, AST, ALP), and liver proteins (Albumin, Globulin) to screen for liver damage.
* **Bilirubin:** A yellowish pigment formed in the liver by the breakdown of hemoglobin from old red blood cells, excreted in bile. Elevated levels cause jaundice.
* **SGPT (Serum Glutamic Pyruvic Transaminase) / ALT:** An enzyme found primarily in the liver; its elevation is a highly specific marker of acute liver cell damage.

---

## Clinical Parameters & Enzymes
The LFT evaluates major liver cell markers:
1. **Serum Bilirubin (Total, Direct, Indirect):**
   * **Total Bilirubin:** Sum of direct and indirect. **Levels $> 2.0$ mg/dL cause visible yellowing of eyes/skin (clinical jaundice).**
   * **Direct (Conjugated):** Processed by the liver. Elevation indicates bile duct blockage (gallstones or pancreatic tumor).
   * **Indirect (Unconjugated):** Unprocessed. Elevation indicates hemolysis (rapid RBC destruction).
2. **Liver Enzymes (Cell Damage Markers):**
   * **SGPT / ALT:** Highly specific to the liver. Spikes up to thousands in acute viral hepatitis or paracetamol poisoning.
   * **SGOT / AST:** Found in liver, heart, and muscles. Elevated in liver damage, alcohol abuse, or heart attacks.
   * **Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP):** Found in bile ducts and bones. High levels indicate bile obstruction or bone disorders.
3. **Liver Synthesis Markers (Proteins):**
   * **Serum Albumin:** The main protein made by the liver. **Low albumin indicates chronic liver failure (cirrhosis)** or severe malnutrition.

## Normal Reference Ranges (Adults)

| Parameter | Normal Range | Clinical Significance of Elevation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Total Bilirubin** | $0.2 - 1.2$ mg/dL | Jaundice, hepatitis, gallstones, hemolysis |
| **Direct Bilirubin** | $0.0 - 0.3$ mg/dL | Biliary tract obstruction, gallstones |
| **SGPT / ALT** | $7 - 56$ U/L | Acute hepatitis, fatty liver, drug toxicity |
| **SGOT / AST** | $10 - 40$ U/L | Alcoholic liver damage, muscle injury |
| **Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP)**| $44 - 147$ U/L | Bile duct blockage, bone growth/metastasis |
| **Serum Albumin** | $3.5 - 5.0$ g/dL | Low level (<3.5) = Liver cirrhosis, edema |

### English
* Yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes (jaundice); dark, mustard-colored urine; pale, clay-colored stools; swelling and pain in the upper right side of the abdomen; swelling in the legs (edema) and abdomen (ascites); severe skin itching (pruritus).

## Test Preparation & Lab Protocol
* **Preparation:** 8 to 12 hours of fasting is recommended. Avoid drinking alcohol or taking unnecessary paracetamol/painkillers for 48 hours before the blood draw, as they can cause temporary enzyme spikes.
* **Procedure:** A venous blood sample is drawn into a gold-capped (gel barrier) or red-capped chemistry tube for laboratory serum analysis.

---

## Medical Management of Results

### For Severe Acute Hepatitis (SGPT/SGOT > 1000 U/L)

#### English
* Hospitalization for supportive care, intravenous fluids, and monitoring liver clotting function (PT/INR). Stop all non-essential oral drugs to prevent acute liver failure.

#### Hindi
* अस्पताल में भर्ती, आईवी (IV) तरल पदार्थ और यकृत थक्के (clotting) की निगरानी। गैर-जरूरी दवाएं बंद करें।

#### Hinglish
* Hospital me admit karein. Clotting function (PT/INR) check karein. Liver par load kam karne ke liye sabhi non-essential tablets/medicines turant band karein.

### For Chronic Liver Cirrhosis (Albumin < 2.5 g/dL with Ascites)

#### English
* Consult a gastroenterologist. Restrict daily salt intake, take diuretics (water pills) to reduce abdomen fluid, and manage portal hypertension.

#### Hindi
* नमक का सेवन पूरी तरह बंद करें। पेट का पानी (ascites) कम करने के लिए मूत्रवर्धक (diuretics) दवाएं लें।

#### Hinglish
* Diet me namak (salt) bilkul band karein. Abdomen me paani (ascites) kam karne ke liye doctor ki salah se diuretics tablets lein.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to hepatic disorders:*
1. **Hepatoprotective Supplements:** Support liver cell membrane stabilization (e.g., Ursodeoxycholic Acid - UDCA, Silymarin / Milk Thistle).
2. **Diuretics (Loop + Aldosterone Antagonists):** Excrete excess fluid in ascites (e.g., Spironolactone, Furosemide).
3. **Lactulose Syrup:** Flushes out ammonia via bowels to prevent hepatic coma/encephalopathy in cirrhosis patients.
4. **Antivirals:** Treat underlying chronic viral hepatitis infections (e.g., Tenofovir for Hep B, Sofosbuvir for Hep C).
5. **Vitamin K Injections:** Administered clinically to correct prolonged clotting times (high INR) caused by liver failure.

---

## Prevention of Liver Damage
* **Limit Alcohol Intake:** Avoid heavy alcohol consumption, which causes fatty liver and cirrhosis.
* **Get Vaccinated:** Get vaccinated against Hepatitis A and Hepatitis B.
* **Eat Low-Fat Diet:** Limit fried, oily food to prevent Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD).
* **Safe Medication:** Never take more than 4 grams of paracetamol daily, as it causes direct liver cell death (hepatotoxicity).

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Liver Disease
* **Hepatic Encephalopathy:** failing liver unable to clear ammonia, which travels to the brain, causing confusion, flapping tremors, coma, and death.
* **Esophageal Variceal Bleeding:** Portal hypertension causing swollen veins in the esophagus to burst, leading to life-threatening vomiting of blood.
* **Hepato-Cellular Carcinoma:** Chronic liver cirrhosis progressing to primary liver cancer.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient vomits large amounts of fresh blood or passes black, tarry stools (variceal bleeding emergency).**
* **The patient develops sudden confusion, slurred speech, or flapping tremors of hands (hepatic encephalopathy).**
* **Sudden severe abdominal pain with high fever in a patient with ascites (bacterial peritonitis).**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Viral Hepatitis Control Program (NVHCP):** Provides completely free screening, diagnosis, and antiviral drugs (Tenofovir, Sofosbuvir) for Hepatitis B and C at all government medical colleges and treatment centers.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless treatment packages for liver cirrhosis care, liver transplant surgery, and hepatic emergency hospitalizations.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Offers generic hepatoprotective drugs (UDCA), diuretics, and antivirals at generic stores at very low prices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a Liver Function Test (LFT)?**
   LFT is a group of blood tests assessing liver health by measuring enzymes (SGPT, SGOT, ALP), proteins (Albumin), and bilirubin levels.

2. **What does a high SGPT/ALT mean in my report?**
   SGPT is an enzyme found inside liver cells. When liver cells are damaged or inflamed (due to fatty liver, alcohol, or virus), SGPT leaks into the blood, raising its level.

3. **What is Bilirubin and how does it relate to jaundice?**
   Bilirubin is a yellow pigment from RBC breakdown. If the liver cannot filter it, bilirubin builds up in the blood. Levels $> 2.0$ mg/dL cause yellow eyes and skin (jaundice).

4. **Why is fasting recommended before an LFT test?**
   Fasting for 8-12 hours is recommended because eating food can temporarily alter direct bilirubin and liver protein levels, causing inaccurate test results.

5. **What is the difference between SGPT and SGOT?**
   SGPT (ALT) is found mainly in the liver, making it a specific marker for liver damage. SGOT (AST) is found in the liver, heart, and muscles, rising in liver or muscle injuries.

6. **What does low Albumin in an LFT indicate?**
   Albumin is a protein made by the liver. A low albumin level indicates chronic liver damage (like cirrhosis) where the liver has lost its ability to synthesize proteins.

7. **How does fatty liver disease affect LFT results?**
   Fatty liver disease causes mild, chronic liver cell inflammation, presenting as persistent mild elevations in SGPT and SGOT (usually $1.5$ to $3$ times the normal limit).

8. **Where can I get free treatment for Hepatitis B and C in India?**
   Under the government's National Viral Hepatitis Control Program (NVHCP), free diagnostic tests and life-saving antiviral medicines are provided at all government medical colleges.

9. **Why does liver cirrhosis cause fluid buildup in the abdomen (ascites)?**
   Cirrhosis causes scarring that blocks blood flow through the liver (portal hypertension), and low albumin levels fail to hold fluid in blood vessels, leaking water into the abdomen.

10. **Why is paracetamol dangerous for the liver in high doses?**
    The liver breaks down paracetamol. In overdose ($>4$g/day), a toxic byproduct (NAPQI) builds up, destroying liver cells and causing acute, fatal liver failure.

11. **What is Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) in LFT?**
    ALP is an enzyme related to the bile ducts and bones. High ALP with normal SGPT indicates a blockage in the bile duct (like a gallstone) or bone diseases.

12. **Can heavy alcohol drinking cause cirrhosis?**
    Yes. Long-term heavy alcohol intake causes fat buildup, followed by liver inflammation (hepatitis) and permanent scarring (cirrhosis), destroying liver function.

13. **Why do liver patients experience severe skin itching?**
    When bile flow is blocked, bile salts build up in the bloodstream and settle under the skin, irritating nerves and causing severe, chronic itching.

14. **What is Hepatic Encephalopathy?**
    It is a brain dysfunction that occurs when a damaged liver cannot filter toxic ammonia from the blood. The ammonia travels to the brain, causing confusion, tremors, and coma.

15. **Why does liver failure cause easy bruising or bleeding?**
    The liver synthesizes clotting factors (proteins that help blood clot). In liver failure, lack of these proteins leads to prolonged bleeding and easy bruising.

16. **What does a high SGOT/SGPT ratio ($>2.0$) indicate?**
    An AST/ALT (SGOT/SGPT) ratio greater than 2.0 is highly suggestive of alcoholic liver disease.

17. **Can a pregnant woman get high bilirubin?**
    Yes. Pregnancy can cause specific liver conditions like intrahepatic cholestasis, causing high bilirubin and severe itching, which requires medical monitoring.

18. **What is the normal range of total bilirubin?**
    The normal range of total bilirubin in blood is $0.2$ to $1.2$ mg/dL.

19. **What is the role of the NVHCP program?**
    NVHCP is a national health program providing free screening, diagnostic viral load tests, and highly effective generic antiviral treatments for Hepatitis B and C.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover liver transplant surgeries?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless surgery packages for liver transplants, donor surgeries, ICU stays, and post-operative immunosuppressive medications.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Drinking sugarcane juice can cure all types of jaundice instantly.
  * **Fact:** Sugarcane juice provides hydration and glucose, which supports the liver, but it cannot cure viral hepatitis (like Hep B/C) or gallstone blockage. Sufferers need medical care.
* **Myth:** Fatty liver is a harmless condition that does not require any action.
  * **Fact:** Non-alcoholic fatty liver (NAFLD) can progress to liver inflammation (NASH), permanent scarring (cirrhosis), and liver failure if left untreated with diet and weight loss.
* **Myth:** Branded liver tonics are essential to clean the liver of toxins.
  * **Fact:** Healthy livers clean themselves. Most over-the-counter liver tonics have no scientific backing. Avoid alcohol and eat a low-fat diet instead.
* **Myth:** Jaundice is only caused by eating unhygienic street food.
  * **Fact:** Hygienic food prevents Hepatitis A and E. However, jaundice can also be caused by alcohol damage, gallstones, medicines, or blood-borne Hepatitis B and C.
* **Myth:** If your LFT enzymes (SGPT/SGOT) are normal, you cannot have liver cirrhosis.
  * **Fact:** In advanced burn-out cirrhosis, liver cells are already replaced by scar tissue, so enzymes may not leak, showing normal SGPT/SGOT despite liver failure.
* **Myth:** Liver patients should completely avoid eating turmeric (haldi).
  * **Fact:** Normal amounts of turmeric in cooked food are safe. Large herbal doses should be avoided, but eliminating cooked spices is unnecessary.
* **Myth:** Liver failure is contagious and spreads through breathing.
  * **Fact:** Liver disease is not contagious. However, viral Hepatitis A/E spread through contaminated water, and B/C spread through blood transfusions or unprotected sex.
* **Myth:** Eating boiled food with zero oil is the only cure for liver disease.
  * **Fact:** Oily foods should be restricted in fatty liver, but a completely boiled diet is unnecessary. A balanced, low-fat, high-protein diet is recommended.
* **Myth:** Taking 8 tablets of paracetamol daily for body pain is completely safe for the liver.
  * **Fact:** Taking more than 4 grams (8 tablets of 500mg) of paracetamol in 24 hours can cause direct, fatal liver damage.
* **Myth:** Liver transplant is a highly dangerous surgery that never succeeds.
  * **Fact:** Liver transplantation has a success rate of over 85-90% and allows patients with end-stage liver failure to live healthy lives for decades.
