# ECG (Electrocardiogram) ki Pehchan, Range aur Parikshan (ECG Wave Analysis and Heart Rhythm Guide)

## Overview

### English
An Electrocardiogram (ECG or EKG) is a non-invasive diagnostic test that records the electrical activity of the heart over a period of time using electrodes placed on the patient's skin (chest and limbs). It generates a paper tracing of electrical waves—the P wave, QRS complex, and T wave—which reflect atrial depolarization, ventricular depolarization, and ventricular repolarization. An ECG is the gold standard for rapidly diagnosing myocardial infarction (heart attack, showing ST-segment elevation or depression), arrhythmias (abnormal heart rhythms), and heart blockages. The test is painless, takes less than 5 minutes, and requires no fasting.

## Quick Summary
An ECG records the heart's electrical waves (P, QRS, T). It is crucial for detecting heart attacks, arrhythmias, and conduction blocks. Key diagnostics identify Normal Sinus Rhythm at 60-100 bpm. Symptoms of cardiac issues include sudden chest pain, palpitations, and sweating. Diagnostics involve placing 12 leads on the chest and limbs. Management focuses on emergency aspirin and immediate angiography for ST-elevation heart attacks.

---

## Definition
* **Electrocardiogram (ECG):** A graphic recording of the electrical potentials generated by the depolarization and repolarization of the myocardium (heart muscle) during each cardiac cycle.
* **STEMI (ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction):** A severe type of heart attack where the coronary artery is completely blocked, showing a classic elevation of the ST segment on the ECG.
* **Sinus Rhythm:** The normal regular rhythm of the heart, where electrical impulses originate from the sinoatrial (SA) node in the right atrium.

---

## Clinical Relevance & Wave Analysis
The ECG records specific electrical vectors of the heart:
1. **P Wave:** Represents atrial depolarization (contraction of the upper heart chambers).
2. **QRS Complex:** Represents ventricular depolarization (contraction of the lower, main pumping chambers).
3. **T Wave:** Represents ventricular repolarization (relaxation of the ventricles).
4. **ST Segment:** The flat line between ventricular contraction and relaxation. **ST elevation indicates acute, active heart muscle death (Heart Attack) requiring immediate clot-busting or angioplasty.**
5. **PR Interval & QT Interval:** Measures conduction times. Prolonged intervals can indicate heart blocks or risk of sudden cardiac arrest.

---

## Normal vs. Abnormal ECG Readings

* **Normal Sinus Rhythm:** Heart rate between 60 and 100 beats per minute (bpm), with regular rhythm and a P wave preceding every QRS complex.
* **Sinus Bradycardia:** Regular heart rate $< 60$ bpm (normal in athletes, but can indicate SA node blockage in the elderly).
* **Sinus Tachycardia:** Regular heart rate $> 100$ bpm (caused by anxiety, fever, anemia, or exercise).
* **Atrial Fibrillation (AF):** Highly irregular, chaotic heart rhythm with absent P waves (high stroke risk).
* **ST-Segment Elevation:** Indicates active complete coronary artery blockage (STEMI Heart Attack).

## Test Preparation & Electrode Placement

### Preparation
* No fasting is required. Inform the technician if the patient has chest hair (which may need to be shaved for electrodes to stick) or is wearing jewelry.

### Electrode Placement (12-Lead ECG)
* **Limb Leads (4 electrodes):** Placed on both wrists and ankles to record electrical activity in the frontal plane (leads I, II, III, aVR, aVL, aVF).
* **Chest Leads (6 electrodes):** Placed at specific anatomical positions across the chest wall to record activity in the horizontal plane (leads V1 to V6).
* The patient must lie completely still and breathe normally; moving or shivering creates electrical noise (artifacts) on the graph.

## Medical Management of Results

### For Acute Heart Attack (ST Elevation)

#### English
* **Immediate Emergency Action:** Give the patient a chewable Aspirin (300mg) + Clopidogrel tablet immediately. Administer oxygen. Transport the patient instantly to a cath-lab equipped hospital for primary angioplasty (PCI) or clot-busting injection (thrombolysis).

#### Hindi
* **आपातकालीन कदम:** तुरंत चबाने वाली एस्पिरिन (Aspirin 300mg) देना। मरीज को तुरंत कैथ-लैब (cath-lab) वाले अस्पताल ले जाएं।

#### Hinglish
* **Immediate Emergency Step:** Patient ko turant chewable Aspirin (300mg) chabane ke liye dein. Oxygen lagayein aur bina ek second waste kiye nearest cath-lab hospital me shift karein primary angioplasty ke liye.

### For Severe Arrhythmia (Atrial Fibrillation / Heart Block)

#### English
* Consult a cardiologist. Management may require blood thinners (to prevent stroke), heart-rate-controlling drugs (Beta-blockers), or surgical pacemaker insertion for heart block.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to cardiac electrical disorders:*
1. **Antiplatelets (Emergency):** Prevent blood clots from growing larger during a heart attack (e.g., Aspirin, Clopidogrel, Ticagrelor).
2. **Beta-Blockers:** Slow down heart rate and reduce blood pressure (e.g., Metoprolol, Bisoprolol).
3. **Antiarrhythmics:** Restore normal heart rhythm in cases of tachycardia or atrial fibrillation (e.g., Amiodarone, Flecainide).
4. **Anticoagulants (Blood Thinners):** Prevent strokes in patients with atrial fibrillation (e.g., Warfarin, Apixaban, Dabigatran).
5. **Thrombolytics (Clot-Busters):** Injected in emergency to dissolve coronary clots when angioplasty is unavailable (e.g., Streptokinase, Tenecteplase).

## Prevention of Cardiac Rhythm Disorders
* **Adopt Healthy Diet:** Follow a low-sodium, low-cholesterol diet with fresh vegetables and fruits.
* **Quit Tobacco:** Stop smoking immediately, as nicotine triggers arrhythmias and plaque rupture.
* **Control BP and Sugar:** Hypertension and diabetes are leading causes of coronary artery disease and heart attacks.
* **Limit Caffeine and Alcohol:** Excessive caffeine or alcohol can trigger atrial fibrillation (holiday heart syndrome).

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated ECG Anomalies
* **Sudden Cardiac Death:** Chaotic ventricular rhythm (ventricular fibrillation) causing the heart to stop pumping instantly.
* **Ischemic Stroke:** Blood clot forming in the left atrium during atrial fibrillation and traveling to the brain.
* **Complete Heart Block:** Electrical signals failing to reach ventricles, leading to severe bradycardia, fainting, and organ damage.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Crushing chest pain lasting more than 15 minutes, radiating to left arm/neck, with cold sweating and nausea.**
* **Sudden fainting or collapse (syncope) in a patient with history of heart disease.**
* **Sudden shortness of breath, choking feeling, or coughing up pink frothy sputum (acute heart failure).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is an ECG test?**
   An Electrocardiogram (ECG) is a painless, 5-minute test that records the electrical signals of your heart to detect heart attacks, blocks, and irregular rhythms.

2. **What do the waves on an ECG show?**
   The P wave shows contraction of the upper heart chambers (atria). The QRS complex shows contraction of the main lower chambers (ventricles). The T wave shows ventricles relaxing.

3. **Does a normal ECG completely rule out a heart attack?**
   No. In the early stages of a heart attack, the ECG can be normal (especially in non-ST elevation heart attacks - NSTEMI). If chest pain persists, doctors check cardiac enzymes (Troponin-T) and repeat the ECG.

4. **Is fasting required before an ECG test?**
   No. Fasting is not required. You can eat, drink, and take your regular medications before having an ECG.

5. **What is Sinus Rhythm?**
   Sinus rhythm is the normal, regular heartbeat generated by the heart's natural pacemaker, the sinoatrial (SA) node, keeping the rate between 60 to 100 bpm.

6. **What does "ST elevation" mean in an ECG report?**
   ST elevation indicates that a major coronary artery is completely blocked, cutting off blood supply to heart muscle cells, representing an acute heart attack (STEMI) emergency.

7. **How is a 12-lead ECG performed?**
   A technician attaches 10 sticky electrodes: 4 on your wrists and ankles (limb leads) and 6 across your left chest wall (chest leads) to capture 12 different electrical views of your heart.

8. **Can anxiety cause an abnormal ECG?**
   Anxiety can cause a fast heart rate (sinus tachycardia) on the ECG, but it does not cause structural changes like ST elevation or heart blocks.

9. **What is Atrial Fibrillation (AF)?**
   Atrial Fibrillation is a common arrhythmia where the upper chambers of the heart beat chaotically and irregularly, showing a highly irregular QRS pattern on the ECG and rising the risk of blood clots.

10. **Where can I get a free ECG test in India?**
    An ECG test is performed completely free of cost for all patients at all government PHCs, community health community centers, and district civil hospitals.

11. **Why does the ECG technician shave chest hair?**
    Thick chest hair prevents the sticky electrodes from making proper contact with the skin, which creates electrical interference (artifacts) on the ECG report.

12. **What is a Pacemaker and when is it needed?**
    A pacemaker is a small battery-powered device surgically placed under the skin to send electrical impulses to the heart if the heart’s natural electrical system fails (heart block).

13. **Why do doctors give Aspirin during a suspected heart attack?**
    Aspirin acts as a blood thinner, preventing platelets from sticking together to block the artery further, helping preserve heart muscle until treatment.

14. **What is the difference between an ECG and an Echocardiogram (Echo)?**
    An ECG checks the heart's electrical wiring system. An Echocardiogram is an ultrasound that checks the heart's pumping muscles, valves, and physical chambers.

15. **Can high potassium levels affect the ECG?**
    Yes. High potassium (hyperkalemia) is dangerous and changes the ECG pattern, causing tall, peaked T waves, which can lead to cardiac arrest if untreated.

16. **What is a Holter Monitor?**
    A Holter monitor is a portable ECG device worn for 24 to 48 hours to record the heart's electrical activity continuously during normal daily activities, helping detect occasional arrhythmias.

17. **What is a Treadmill Test (TMT)?**
    TMT (Exercise ECG) is a test where an ECG is recorded while you walk on a treadmill. It checks how your heart performs under physical stress, revealing blocks not seen during rest.

18. **Why should I lie completely still during an ECG?**
    Moving, talking, or shivering causes muscle electrical activity to be picked up by the electrodes, creating wavy lines (artifacts) that make the ECG report hard to read.

19. **What does a prolonged QT interval mean?**
    A prolonged QT interval means the heart takes longer than normal to recharge between beats, which can trigger sudden, life-threatening irregular rhythms.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover angioplasty surgeries?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers completely cashless packages for coronary angiographies, emergency angioplasties (stent insertion), ICU stay, and medications.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** An ECG can tell if you will have a heart attack in the future.
  * **Fact:** An ECG only shows the heart's activity at that exact moment. A normal resting ECG cannot predict a future heart attack, which is why TMT or angiographies are done for prediction.
* **Myth:** You can feel the electrical current from the ECG machine.
  * **Fact:** The ECG machine only records the heart's natural electricity; it does not send any electricity into your body. The test is completely painless and safe.
* **Myth:** If you have chest pain but the ECG is normal, you are completely safe.
  * **Fact:** A normal ECG can happen during NSTEMI heart attacks or early chest pain. Doctors must check blood Troponin-I levels and repeat the ECG.
* **Myth:** ECG can only be done in big cardiac hospitals.
  * **Fact:** Small portable ECG machines are available at almost all local clinics, PHCs, and ambulances for instant checking.
* **Myth:** Shaving chest hair for ECG causes skin infections.
  * **Fact:** Dry shaving with a sterile disposable razor is safe and necessary to ensure proper electrode contact and clean graphs.
* **Myth:** Pacemakers must be charged with an external plug daily.
  * **Fact:** Pacemakers contain high-grade lithium batteries surgically sealed inside the device, which last for 7 to 15 years before needing replacement.
* **Myth:** You must stop taking all blood pressure medicines before an ECG.
  * **Fact:** Do not stop taking your daily medicines unless explicitly asked by your doctor. Inform the technician about the medicines you take.
* **Myth:** ECG is only for elderly people.
  * **Fact:** Anyone experiencing sudden chest pain, palpitations, or fainting, regardless of age, should get an ECG.
* **Myth:** ECG is highly expensive.
  * **Fact:** ECG is one of the cheapest medical tests, costing only ₹150 to ₹300 in local labs, and is free at government health centers.
* **Myth:** Eating a heavy meal before ECG changes the heart waves.
  * **Fact:** Eating does not alter the heart's electrical pathways. You can have an ECG anytime.
