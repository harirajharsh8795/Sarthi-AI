# Thyroid Rog (Hypo/Hyperthyroidism) ki Pehchan aur Upachar (Thyroid Care and Levothyroxine Guide)

## Overview

### English
Thyroid disorders are chronic endocrine conditions affecting the thyroid gland, a butterfly-shaped organ in the neck that produces hormones regulating body metabolism. The two primary disorders are Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid producing insufficient hormones, causing slowed metabolism, weight gain, and fatigue) and Hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid producing excess hormones, causing accelerated metabolism, rapid weight loss, and tremors). These are diagnosed using a Thyroid Profile blood test (TSH, Free T3, and Free T4). Management involves daily hormone replacement (Levothyroxine) for hypothyroidism, or anti-thyroid medications and radioactive iodine for hyperthyroidism.

## Quick Summary
Thyroid disorders involve abnormal hormone secretion by the thyroid gland. Hypothyroidism is characterized by low T3/T4 and high TSH, causing fatigue, weight gain, and cold intolerance. Hyperthyroidism is characterized by high T3/T4 and low TSH, causing weight loss, palpitations, and heat intolerance. Diagnostics include blood TSH, Free T3, and Free T4 tests. Treatment involves Levothyroxine for hypothyroidism and Carbimazole for hyperthyroidism. Prevention emphasizes regular testing.

---

## Definition
* **Hypothyroidism:** A clinical state resulting from underproduction of thyroid hormones (T3 and T4), leading to a generalized slowing of metabolic processes, marked by elevated Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH) in primary cases.
* **Hyperthyroidism (Thyrotoxicosis):** A clinical state resulting from excess circulating thyroid hormones, causing hypermetabolism, marked by suppressed or undetectable TSH levels.
* **Goiter:** An abnormal enlargement of the thyroid gland, presenting as a visible swelling at the base of the neck, commonly caused by iodine deficiency or autoimmune thyroiditis.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The pituitary gland controls the thyroid:
1. **Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Thyroid Axis:** The pituitary gland in the brain releases **TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)**, which tells the thyroid gland in the neck to produce T3 and T4.
2. **Feedback Loop:** If T3 and T4 levels drop (Hypothyroidism), the brain senses this and pumps out extra TSH to stimulate the thyroid, leading to a high TSH reading in blood tests. If T3/T4 are too high (Hyperthyroidism), TSH production is suppressed to near zero.

## Causes

### Causes of Hypothyroidism
* **Hashimoto's Thyroiditis:** An autoimmune disorder where the immune system mistakenly attacks thyroid tissue, slowly destroying its hormone-producing capacity.
* **Iodine Deficiency:** Lack of dietary iodine (essential for making T3 and T4 hormones, though reduced due to iodized salt programs).
* **Thyroidectomy:** Surgical removal of the thyroid gland (for tumors or goiter), causing permanent hypothyroidism.

### Causes of Hyperthyroidism
* **Graves' Disease:** An autoimmune disorder where antibodies stimulate the thyroid gland to overproduce hormones, often causing bulging eyes (exophthalmos).
* **Toxic Multinodular Goiter:** Enlarged thyroid with nodules that independently overproduce hormones.

### Risk Factors
* Female gender (8 times more common in women), family history of autoimmune diseases, and age over 60.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Hypothyroidism Symptoms (Slow Metabolism):**
   * Constant fatigue and weakness.
   * Unexplained weight gain despite eating less.
   * Severe sensitivity to cold weather (feeling cold easily).
   * Dry, rough skin and thinning hair.
   * Chronic constipation.
   * Irregular, heavy menstrual periods in women.
   * Muscle weakness and joint stiffness.
* **Hyperthyroidism Symptoms (Fast Metabolism):**
   * Rapid, unexplained weight loss despite eating more.
   * Heart palpitations or rapid heartbeat (tachycardia).
   * Hand tremors (shaking of fingers).
   * Severe sensitivity to hot weather and excessive sweating.
   * Frequent bowel movements or diarrhea.
   * Anxiety, irritability, and sleep problems.
   * Visible swelling in the front of the neck (Goiter).
* **Emergency Symptoms (Myxedema Coma):** Extreme hypothermia, low BP, slow heart rate, confusion, or unconsciousness in a patient with severe untreated hypothyroidism.
* **Emergency Symptoms (Thyroid Storm):** High fever ($>104^\circ$F), rapid heartbeat ($>140$ bpm), confusion, extreme agitation, or fits in a hyperthyroid patient.

## Diagnosis

### Thyroid Profile Blood Test (T3, T4, TSH)
* The primary diagnostic tool. Must interpret results carefully:
   * **Primary Hypothyroidism:** **High TSH ($> 4.5$ mIU/L) with Low Free T4.**
   * **Subclinical Hypothyroidism:** High TSH with normal Free T4 (often monitored without medicine).
   * **Hyperthyroidism:** **Low/suppressed TSH ($< 0.1$ mIU/L) with High Free T4 and T3.**

### Thyroid Ultrasound
* Imaging to check the size of the thyroid gland, evaluate goiter swelling, and look for solid or fluid-filled nodules.

### Radioactive Iodine Uptake (RAIU) Scan
* The patient swallows a tiny dose of radioactive iodine. A scanner checks how much iodine the thyroid absorbs. High absorption confirms Graves' hyperthyroidism.

## Treatment

### Hypothyroidism Management & Levothyroxine Use

#### English
* **Take Levothyroxine Daily:** Take the prescribed tablet daily in the morning on an empty stomach, at least 30-60 minutes before drinking tea, coffee, or eating breakfast.
* **Do Not Mix with Supplements:** Do not take calcium, iron, or antacid tablets within 4 hours of taking levothyroxine, as they block hormone absorption.
* **Regular Monitoring:** Recheck TSH levels every 6-8 weeks after starting or changing the dose.

* **खाली पेट दवा लें:** लेवोथायरोक्सिन (Levothyroxine) टैबलेट को रोजाना सुबह खाली पेट, चाय या नाश्ते से कम से कम 30-60 मिनट पहले लें।
* **कैल्शियम/लोहे के साथ न लें:** लेवोथायरोक्सिन लेने के 4 घंटे के भीतर कैल्शियम या आयरन सप्लीमेंट न लें।
* **नियमित जांच:** खुराक बदलने के बाद हर 6-8 सप्ताह में टीएसएच (TSH) की जांच कराएं।

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **Thyroid Hormone Replacements:** Synthetic T4 hormone used daily to treat hypothyroidism (e.g., Levothyroxine Sodium).
2. **Anti-Thyroid Drugs:** Block the thyroid gland from making T3 and T4 hormones in hyperthyroidism (e.g., Carbimazole, Methimazole, Propylthiouracil).
3. **Beta-Blockers:** Prescribed temporarily in hyperthyroidism to control rapid heart rate, shaking, and anxiety (e.g., Propranolol).
4. **Radioactive Iodine-131:** Oral capsule therapy that targets and destroys overactive thyroid cells.
5. **Steroid anti-inflammatories:** Prescribed clinically for severe subacute thyroiditis pain or Graves' eye disease.

## Prevention

### English
* **Use Iodized Salt:** Use certified iodized table salt in cooking to prevent goiter and thyroid deficiency (especially important in pregnancy).
* **Regular Screening:** Women planning pregnancy should get their TSH checked, as untreated hypothyroidism during pregnancy can cause developmental delays in the baby.
* **Avoid Self-Dosing:** Do not stop or alter your levothyroxine dose without checking TSH levels first.

### Hindi
* **आयोडीन युक्त नमक:** घेंघा (goiter) और थायराइड की कमी से बचने के लिए हमेशा आयोडीन युक्त नमक का उपयोग करें।
* **गर्भावस्था में जांच:** गर्भावस्था की योजना बनाने से पहले टीएसएच (TSH) की जांच अवश्य कराएं, क्योंकि थायराइड की कमी बच्चे के मानसिक विकास को प्रभावित कर सकती है।

## Possible Complications
* **Myxedema Coma:** A rare, life-threatening complication of long-term untreated hypothyroidism, triggered by cold or infection, presenting as hypothermia and coma.
* **Thyroid Storm:** A sudden, fatal surge in thyroid hormones in hyperthyroidism, causing heart failure and high fever.
* **Congenital Hypothyroidism:** A baby born with thyroid deficiency due to maternal iodine deficiency, leading to severe mental retardation (cretinism).

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **High fever ($>104^\circ$F), extreme agitation, confusion, and a racing heart rate ($>140$ bpm) in a hyperthyroid patient (Thyroid Storm).**
* **Extreme drowsiness, low body temperature, shallow breathing, and slow heart rate in a patient with history of hypothyroidism (Myxedema Coma).**
* **Sudden onset of severe chest pain and breathlessness.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you experience constant fatigue, weight gain, and skin dryness despite normal diet.
* If you have rapid weight loss, shaking hands, and feel your heart racing.
* If you notice a visible swelling in the front of your neck.

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Iodine Deficiency Disorders Control Programme (NIDDCP):** Mandates the sale of iodized salt in India and monitors iodine nutrition levels in the population.
* **PM Bhartiya Janaushadhi Pariyojana:** Offers generic Levothyroxine and Carbimazole tablets at generic stores at very low prices (₹20 to ₹35 per bottle of 100 tablets).
* **NPCDCS screening:** Focuses on detecting endocrine disorders like diabetes and thyroid issues in maternal clinics.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What are Thyroid disorders?**
   Thyroid disorders are conditions affecting the thyroid gland in the neck, causing it to produce too little hormone (Hypothyroidism) or too much hormone (Hyperthyroidism).

2. **Why must Levothyroxine be taken empty stomach?**
   Food, tea, coffee, and milk significantly reduce the absorption of levothyroxine in the stomach. Taking it empty stomach 1 hour before breakfast ensures maximum absorption.

3. **What is TSH?**
   TSH stands for Thyroid Stimulating Hormone. It is released by the brain's pituitary gland to tell the thyroid to make hormones. A high TSH indicates hypothyroidism.

4. **What is Graves' disease?**
   Graves' disease is an autoimmune disorder that is the leading cause of hyperthyroidism, where antibodies stimulate the thyroid to overproduce hormones and cause bulging eyes.

5. **Can thyroid disease cause hair loss?**
   Yes. Both severe hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism can cause hair to become thin, dry, and fall out. The hair usually grows back once hormone levels are normalized.

6. **What is Goiter?**
   Goiter is the visible swelling of the thyroid gland in the neck, commonly caused by iodine deficiency or autoimmune thyroid inflammation.

7. **Can hypothyroidism cause weight gain?**
   Yes. Lack of thyroid hormones slows down the body's basal metabolic rate, causing the body to burn fewer calories, leading to moderate weight gain (mostly fluid retention).

8. **Where can I buy cheap thyroid medicines in India?**
   Generic Levothyroxine (Thyroxine) tablets are available at government PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a highly subsidized price (around ₹25 for 100 tablets).

9. **What is Myxedema Coma?**
   Myxedema Coma is a rare, life-threatening emergency caused by severe, untreated hypothyroidism, presenting as very low body temperature, slow breathing, and coma.

10. **Why should pregnant women get their TSH checked?**
    The unborn baby relies on the mother's thyroid hormones for brain development during the first trimester. Untreated maternal hypothyroidism can cause cognitive delays.

11. **Why can't I take calcium tablets with my thyroid pill?**
    Calcium carbonate and iron supplements bind to levothyroxine in the gut, preventing its absorption. Keep a gap of at least 4 hours between these pills.

12. **Is thyroid disease curable?**
    Autoimmune hypothyroidism has no permanent cure, but it is easily managed by taking a daily hormone replacement pill for life. Some hyperthyroid cases can go into long-term remission.

13. **What is subclinical hypothyroidism?**
    It is a stage where the TSH level is mildly elevated, but T3 and T4 levels are still in the normal range. It often does not require medicine, only regular monitoring.

14. **Why does hyperthyroidism cause hand tremors?**
    Excess thyroid hormones over-stimulate the nervous system, increasing sensitivity to adrenaline, which causes rapid muscle twitching or shaking of the fingers.

15. **What is Radioactive Iodine therapy?**
    It is a treatment for hyperthyroidism where the patient drinks radioactive iodine. The overactive thyroid cells absorb the iodine and are destroyed, stopping hormone overproduction.

16. **Why does hypothyroidism cause chronic constipation?**
    Thyroid hormones regulate muscle contractions in the digestive tract. Low hormones slow down gut movements, leading to dry stools and constipation.

17. **What is the normal range of TSH?**
    For most healthy adults, the normal TSH range is 0.4 to 4.5 mIU/L. During pregnancy, the target TSH is usually lower (under 2.5 or 3.0 mIU/L).

18. **Can thyroid nodules be cancerous?**
    Most thyroid nodules (over 90-95%) are benign (non-cancerous). However, a doctor may recommend an ultrasound and FNAC biopsy to rule out cancer.

19. **What is the role of the NIDDCP program?**
    NIDDCP is a government initiative that monitors iodine levels and ensures all salt sold for human consumption in India is properly iodized to prevent goiter.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover thyroid surgery?**
    Yes, the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme covers cashless surgery costs (thyroidectomy) for large goiters, nodules, or thyroid cancer at empaneled hospitals.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** If you have thyroid disease, you must completely stop eating cabbage and cauliflower.
  * **Fact:** These vegetables contain goitrogens, but they only affect thyroid function if eaten raw in massive quantities daily. Cooking destroys goitrogens, making them safe.
* **Myth:** Only overweight people have thyroid issues.
  * **Fact:** Hypothyroidism causes weight gain, but hyperthyroidism causes rapid weight loss. Thin people can also have thyroid disorders.
* **Myth:** Branded thyroid pills are much better than generic ones.
  * **Fact:** Generic Levothyroxine from PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras contains the exact same hormone and is highly effective at a fraction of the price.
* **Myth:** You can stop taking your thyroid medicine once your TSH report comes back normal.
  * **Fact:** Your TSH is normal because the medicine is replacing the missing hormone. If you stop the pill, the hormone levels will drop again.
* **Myth:** Thyroid is a contagious disease.
  * **Fact:** Thyroid disorders are non-infectious, mostly autoimmune or nutritional conditions; they cannot spread from person to person.
* **Myth:** Iodine-rich salt is bad for high blood pressure patients, so they should eat non-iodized salt.
  * **Fact:** High BP patients must limit overall salt (sodium), but the salt they do eat should still be iodized to prevent thyroid deficiency.
* **Myth:** Eating coconut oil cures hypothyroidism permanently.
  * **Fact:** Coconut oil has no scientific backing to replace levothyroxine hormone. Always take prescribed medications.
* **Myth:** If you miss a morning dose, you should take double the dose the next day.
  * **Fact:** If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember that day. If it is already the next day, do not double the dose; consult your doctor.
* **Myth:** Hyperthyroidism is caused by overeating sugar.
  * **Fact:** Hyperthyroidism is an autoimmune or nodular disorder; it is not caused by diet or sugar.
* **Myth:** All thyroid swellings (goiters) require immediate surgery.
  * **Fact:** Most goiters are managed with iodine supplements or thyroid medicines. Surgery is only needed if the swelling blocks breathing or is cancerous.

* **Assistant:** NIDDCP ensures that all edible salt sold in Indian markets is iodized. This simple measure has successfully reduced goiter and cretinism (mental retardation in babies) cases across India.
