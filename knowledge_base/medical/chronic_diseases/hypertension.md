# Uccha Raktachap (Hypertension/High BP) ki Pehchan aur Upachar (Hypertension Care and Blood Pressure Guide)

## Overview

### English
Hypertension, commonly known as high blood pressure, is a chronic medical condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is persistently elevated. It is defined as a systolic blood pressure $\ge 140$ mmHg and/or a diastolic blood pressure $\ge 90$ mmHg. Known as a "silent killer," hypertension often presents with no symptoms for years while silently damaging blood vessels, leading to heart attacks, stroke, and kidney failure. Management requires strict sodium restriction (DASH diet), regular aerobic exercise, daily adherence to antihypertensive medications, and monitoring BP at home using a digital monitor.

## Quick Summary
Hypertension involves persistently elevated arterial blood pressure. The primary cause is essential (idiopathic) factors linked to genetics and lifestyle. Common symptoms (when present) include headaches (especially in the morning), dizziness, blurred vision, nosebleeds, and palpitations. Diagnostics include BP measurements over multiple days, ECG, and kidney function tests. Treatment involves anti-hypertensive drugs and a low-sodium diet. Prevention focuses on limiting salt, avoiding stress, and staying active.

---

## Definition
* **Hypertension:** A persistent elevation of systemic arterial blood pressure, defined clinically as a systolic blood pressure (SBP) of 140 mmHg or greater and/or a diastolic blood pressure (DBP) of 90 mmHg or greater, based on multiple readings.
* **Systolic vs. Diastolic:** Systolic pressure (top number) is the pressure in arteries when the heart beats/contracts. Diastolic pressure (bottom number) is the pressure when the heart rests between beats.
* **Hypertensive Crisis:** A severe, sudden spike in blood pressure ($\ge 180/120$ mmHg) that can cause acute organ damage (stroke, heart failure, aortic dissection).

---

## Anatomy / Background
High pressure damages the circulatory system:
1. **Arterial Stiffness:** Blood vessels have elastic muscular walls. Persistent high pressure causes these walls to thicken, lose elasticity (arteriosclerosis), and develop fatty plaques (atherosclerosis), narrowing the channels.
2. **Heart Strain:** The heart (left ventricle) must pump blood against this high pressure. Over time, the heart muscle thickens (hypertrophy) to compensate, eventually becoming stiff and weak, leading to heart failure.

## Causes

### Primary (Essential) Hypertension (~95% of cases)
* No single identifiable cause; develops gradually over years due to a combination of genetic inheritance (family history) and environmental/lifestyle triggers.

### Secondary Hypertension (~5% of cases)
* Caused by an underlying medical condition, including:
   * Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) or narrowing of renal arteries (renal artery stenosis).
   * Endocrine disorders like Conn's syndrome (excess aldosterone) or Pheochromocytoma (adrenaline-secreting tumor).
   * Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA).

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** High dietary sodium (salt) intake ($> 5$ grams/day), physical inactivity, obesity (excess body fat raises cardiac output), heavy chronic alcohol consumption, active smoking, and chronic mental stress.
* **Clinical:** Family history, advanced age, and diabetes.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** Often completely asymptomatic ("silent killer"); minor dull headaches in the morning; occasional dizziness or lightheadedness.
* **Common Symptoms (When BP is very high):** Persistent throbbing headache, especially at the back of the head in the morning; blurred vision or double vision; frequent nosebleeds (epistaxis); shortness of breath during light walking; heart palpitations (feeling your heartbeat).
* **Advanced Symptoms (Organ Damage):** Chest pain (angina); leg swelling (cor pulmonale); frothy urine (kidney filtration damage); transient ischemic attacks (temporary face dropping or arm weakness).
* **Emergency Symptoms (Hypertensive Crisis):** Severe chest pain with shortness of breath; sudden severe headache with confusion, vomiting, and fits; sudden loss of speech or paralysis on one side of the body (stroke).

## Diagnosis

### Blood Pressure Measurement Protocol
* To diagnose hypertension, a doctor takes readings on at least 2-3 separate clinic visits:
   * **Normal BP:** $< 120/80$ mmHg.
   * **Pre-hypertension (Elevated):** 120-129 / $< 80$ mmHg.
   * **Hypertension Stage 1:** 130-139 / 80-89 mmHg.
   * **Hypertension Stage 2:** $\ge 140/90$ mmHg.
* **Digital BP Monitoring:** Educating the patient to sit quietly for 5 minutes, keep their arm supported at heart level, and record BP at home.

### Electrocardiogram (ECG) & Echocardiogram (Echo)
* **ECG:** Done to check for left ventricular hypertrophy (enlarged heart muscle) or rhythm disturbances caused by chronic high BP.

### Kidney Function Tests (KFT) & Urinalysis
* High BP damages kidney filters. Checking blood urea and creatinine, and testing urine for protein leakage (microalbuminuria) is necessary.

## Treatment

### Dietary & Lifestyle Modifications

#### English
* **DASH Diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension):** Limit daily salt intake to less than 1 teaspoon ($< 5$ grams/day). Avoid processed pickles, papad, namkeen, salted butter, and soy sauce. Increase intake of potassium-rich foods (bananas, green leafy vegetables, coconut water) which helps lower BP.
* **Regular Exercise:** Perform 30 minutes of aerobic exercise (brisk walking, swimming, cycling) daily.
* **Stress Management:** Practice daily yoga, deep breathing exercises (pranayama), or meditation.

#### Hindi
* **DASH आहार (कम सोडियम):** दैनिक नमक का सेवन 1 चम्मच (5 ग्राम से कम) तक सीमित करें। अचार, पापड़, नमकीन, सॉस और चिप्स खाने से बचें। पोटैशियम से भरपूर खाद्य पदार्थ (केला, हरी पत्तेदार सब्जियां, नारियल पानी) लें।
* **दैनिक व्यायाम:** रोजाना 30 मिनट तेज टहलें या साइकिल चलाएं।
* **तनाव प्रबंधन:** ध्यान, योग या प्राणायाम करें।

#### Hinglish
* **DASH Diet (Salt kam karein):** Daily namak (salt) ka use 1 chammach (5 grams) se kam karein. Achar, papad, namkeen, chips, aur salted butter bilkul na khayein. Potassium rich food jaise kela, hari sabziyan, aur nariyal paani lein (yeh BP kam karta hai).
* **Daily exercise:** Rojana 30 minutes brisk walk, cycling, ya swimming karein.
* **Stress dur karein:** Chronic stress se BP badhta hai, isiliye daily meditation ya pranayama karein.

#### English
* Sit comfortably with feet flat on the floor, support the arm on a table at heart level, and place the digital cuff on the bare upper arm. Avoid drinking tea/coffee or smoking for 30 minutes before checking.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **ACE Inhibitors:** Relax blood vessels by blocking the production of a natural chemical that narrows them (e.g., Ramipril, Enalapril).
2. **ARBs (Angiotensin II Receptor Blockers):** Block the action of vessel-narrowing chemicals, commonly used as first-line agents (e.g., Telmisartan, Losartan).
3. **Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs):** Relax the muscles of blood vessels and slow down heart rate (e.g., Amlodipine).
4. **Diuretics (Water Pills):** Help the kidneys flush excess water and sodium out of the body, reducing blood volume and pressure (e.g., Chlorthalidone, Hydrochlorothiazide).
5. **Beta-Blockers:** Reduce the heart rate and the heart's workload (e.g., Metoprolol, Atenolol).

## Prevention

### English
* **Restict Salt Intake:** Limit daily salt in cooking. Do not keep a salt shaker on the dining table.
* **Maintain Healthy BMI:** Keep weight under control; losing weight lowers BP significantly.
* **Avoid Tobacco & Limit Alcohol:** Stop smoking bidis/cigarettes, as nicotine constricts arteries immediately.
* **Know Your Numbers:** Get your blood pressure checked at least once a year after reaching 30 years of age.

## Possible Complications
* **Brain Stroke (Paralysis):** A weakened blood vessel in the brain bursting due to high pressure (hemorrhagic stroke) or a blood clot forming in a narrowed artery (ischemic stroke).
* **Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack):** Blockage of coronary arteries damaged by high pressure, cutting off blood supply to heart muscles.
* **Hypertensive Nephropathy:** High pressure destroying the filtering nephrons of the kidneys, leading to chronic kidney failure.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Sudden, severe chest pain that radiates to the left arm, neck, or back, with cold sweat.**
* **Sudden loss of speech, difficulty speaking, or sudden numbness/weakness in one side of the face, arm, or leg (Stroke).**
* **A sudden spike in BP ($\ge 180/120$ mmHg) accompanied by severe headache, vomiting, or confusion.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If your home BP monitor shows systolic readings consistently above 135 mmHg or diastolic above 85 mmHg.
* If you experience frequent morning headaches, dizziness, or chest tightness.
* If you develop side effects from your BP medicines, such as dry cough (common with ACE inhibitors) or ankle swelling (common with CCBs).

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **Indian Hypertension Control Initiative (IHCI):** A large-scale government program aimed at promoting standardized BP measurement, ensuring free availability of anti-hypertensive drugs at primary health wellness centers, and tracking patient control rates.
* **NPCDCS Programme:** Conducts screening for non-communicable diseases, offering free BP checks and medication refills at sub-centers and PHCs.
* **PM Bhartiya Janaushadhi Pariyojana:** Offers generic anti-hypertensive tablets (Telmisartan, Amlodipine, Metoprolol) at generic stores at extremely low prices (₹10 to ₹15 per strip).

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Hypertension?**
   Hypertension is a chronic medical condition where the pressure of blood against the arterial walls is consistently elevated, defined clinically as $\ge 140/90$ mmHg.

2. **Why is high BP called a "silent killer"?**
   Because it rarely causes symptoms in its early stages. A person can live with high BP for years without knowing, while it slowly damages blood vessels, leading to heart attacks or strokes.

3. **What is the difference between Systolic and Diastolic blood pressure?**
   Systolic pressure (top number) is the pressure in the arteries when the heart contracts. Diastolic pressure (bottom number) is the pressure when the heart relaxes between beats.

4. **What is the DASH diet?**
   DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) is a low-sodium diet rich in potassium, calcium, and magnesium (fruits, vegetables, whole grains) proven to lower blood pressure.

5. **Why should I limit my salt intake if I have high BP?**
   Excess sodium causes the body to retain water, increasing the volume of blood in the blood vessels, which directly raises blood pressure.

6. **How does potassium help lower blood pressure?**
   Potassium helps relax blood vessel walls and stimulates the kidneys to flush out more sodium through urine, lowering blood pressure.

7. **How does a digital BP monitor work at home?**
   It uses oscillometric sensors inside an inflatable cuff to detect blood flow through the brachial artery, displaying systolic, diastolic, and pulse rates digitally.

8. **Can chronic stress cause hypertension?**
   Yes. Chronic stress triggers the release of hormones like adrenaline and cortisol, which raise heart rate and constrict blood vessels, causing blood pressure to rise.

9. **What are ACE inhibitors and ARBs?**
   These are blood pressure medications. ACE inhibitors block the production of a vessel-narrowing chemical, while ARBs (like Telmisartan) block its action on blood vessels, helping them dilate.

10. **Where can I get free BP medications in India?**
    Under the government’s IHCI program, free anti-hypertensive medications and regular BP checks are provided at all government PHCs, sub-centers, and district hospitals.

11. **Can high BP cause kidney damage?**
    Yes. High blood pressure damages the delicate capillaries in the kidneys (nephrons). Over time, this reduces the kidneys' ability to filter waste, leading to chronic kidney disease.

12. **Why does Amlodipine cause swollen ankles?**
    Amlodipine (a Calcium Channel Blocker) dilates small blood vessels in the legs. Gravity causes fluid to pool in the ankles and feet, causing mild swelling. This is a common side effect.

13. **Is high BP reversible without medications?**
    If caught early in the pre-hypertension stage, BP can often be brought back to normal solely through weight loss, reducing salt, regular exercise, and stress management.

14. **Why should I never stop taking my BP pills once my BP is normal?**
    Your BP is normal because the medicines are working. Stopping them suddenly can cause a rebound spike in blood pressure, increasing the risk of a heart attack or stroke.

15. **What is a Hypertensive Crisis?**
    It is a medical emergency where blood pressure spikes suddenly to $180/120$ mmHg or higher, which can cause acute damage to the brain, heart, or kidneys.

16. **Why does nicotine from smoking raise BP?**
    Nicotine stimulates the nervous system to release adrenaline, which immediately narrows blood vessels and increases heart rate, raising blood pressure.

17. **What is the normal range of blood pressure?**
    For a healthy adult, normal blood pressure is defined as below 120/80 mmHg.

18. **Can high BP cause an eye stroke?**
    Yes. High pressure can cause the delicate blood vessels in the retina to burst or block, leading to sudden, painless loss of vision (retinal vein occlusion).

19. **What is the role of the Indian Hypertension Control Initiative (IHCI)?**
    IHCI is a government health program that aims to control hypertension by training health workers, ensuring supply of free generic drugs, and tracking BP control rates in communities.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover stroke treatment costs?**
    Yes, the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme covers cashless treatment packages for stroke ICU admission, brain scans, thrombolytic therapy, and post-stroke rehabilitation.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** High BP only affects nervous, stressed, or angry people.
  * **Fact:** High BP is a physical condition that can affect calm, relaxed individuals. It is driven by genetics, weight, salt, and age, not just anger.
* **Myth:** If you feel fine, you do not need to take your BP medicine.
  * **Fact:** Hypertension is asymptomatic. Even if you feel healthy, uncontrolled high pressure is actively damaging your heart and kidneys. Take meds daily.
* **Myth:** Using rock salt (sendha namak) or pink salt does not raise blood pressure.
  * **Fact:** Rock salt, sea salt, and pink salt contain the exact same chemical—sodium chloride—and raise blood pressure just as much as table salt.
* **Myth:** Hypertension only affects older people.
  * **Fact:** Due to rising obesity, sedentary lifestyles, and junk food, many young adults in their 20s and 30s are being diagnosed with high BP.
* **Myth:** Drinking lemon juice cures high BP instantly during a spike.
  * **Fact:** Lemon juice has no direct, immediate effect on blood pressure. During a severe spike, take your prescribed medicine or seek emergency care.
* **Myth:** BP medications will make you weak and dependent on them for life.
  * **Fact:** BP pills are not addictive. They are essential daily health regulators that protect your heart and kidneys from failing.
* **Myth:** If your BP is normal on one check, you do not have hypertension.
  * **Fact:** BP changes throughout the day. A diagnosis of hypertension requires multiple elevated readings taken on separate days.
* **Myth:** Garlic pills are a complete replacement for BP tablets.
  * **Fact:** Garlic has a very minor, temporary blood pressure lowering effect. It cannot replace clinical antihypertensive drugs.
* **Myth:** Hypertension is contagious.
  * **Fact:** Hypertension is a non-infectious chronic metabolic and vascular condition.
* **Myth:** You can sweat out high blood pressure in a sauna or steam room.
  * **Fact:** The heat in saunas can cause blood vessels to dilate, causing a sudden dangerous drop in blood pressure or dehydration, which is risky for hypertensive patients.

* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment benefits are provided for diagnostic CT scans, emergency stroke ICU admission, thrombolytic therapy, and brain surgeries for eligible cardholders.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** High BP me Amlodipine tablet lene se pairon me sujan kyu aati hai?
* **Assistant:** Amlodipine blood vessels ko relax karti hai, jisse gravity ke karan pairon ke takhno (ankles) me fluid jama ho jata hai aur sujan (edema) aati hai. Yeh ek common side effect hai. Agar sujan badhe, to apne doctor se consult karein, wo dose change ya drug switch kar sakte hain.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** DASH diet me kya parhez rakhna chahiye?
* **Assistant:** DASH diet me sodium (salt) kam karein, packaged chips, papad, namkeen, achar, aur processed foods band karein. Fruits (jaise kela), hari sabziyan, aur low-fat dairy products badhayein jisme potassium aur magnesium ho.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Kya smoking bidi peene se BP turant badh jata hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, bidi/cigarette me majood nicotine blood me ja kar adrenal glands ko trigger karta hai, jisse adrenaline release hota hai. Yeh blood vessels ko tight aur narrow kar deta hai, jisse BP turant badh jata hai.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the toll-free health helpline for IHCI?
* **Assistant:** You can contact the National Health Portal helpline at 1800-180-1104 to get information about nearest government hospitals, free BP screening centers, and availability of generic medicines.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Mujhe achanak subah ke waqt sir me piche aur gardan me bohot tez dard hota hai. BP checkup karana chahiye kya?
* **Assistant:** Haan, subah uthte hi sir ke piche (back of head) aur gardan me tez dard hona High BP (hypertension) ka classic symptom ho sakta hai. Aap bina deri kiye urologist ya general doctor se consult karke apna BP check karwayein.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** Hypertensive Crisis kya hota hai aur iski range kya hai?
* **Assistant:** Hypertensive Crisis achanak BP ka $\ge 180/120$ mmHg se upar jana hai. Agar iske sath chest pain, saans phoolna, ya headache ho, to yeh organ damage kar sakta hai aur isme immediate hospital emergency treatment ki zarurat hoti hai.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Mere relative ka high BP hai aur achanak unke face ka ek side tedha ho gaya hai aur wo bol nahi pa rahe hain.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Face ka ek side tedha hona (face drooping) aur aawaz ladkharana/na bol pana (speech difficulty) Brain Stroke (paralysis) ka classic sign hai. Aap bina ek minute gawaye unhe immediate hospital ke emergency room me stroke center le jayein. Fast treatment se paralysis completely reverse ho sakta hai.
