# Dama Rog (Asthma) ki Pehchan, Bachao aur Upachar (Asthma Management and Inhaler Guide)

## Overview

### English
Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disease of the airways, characterized by bronchial hyperresponsiveness, airway obstruction, and mucus overproduction. It leads to recurrent episodes of wheezing, breathlessness, chest tightness, and coughing, particularly at night or early morning. These symptoms are triggered by environmental factors (pollen, dust, smoke), physical exertion, or respiratory infections. While asthma is a lifelong condition, it can be highly controlled using rescue and controller inhalers. Proper inhaler technique, often using a spacer device, is crucial for directing the medication into the lungs instead of the throat.

## Quick Summary
Asthma causes chronic airway inflammation and constriction. Triggers include dust mites, pollen, pet dander, cold air, and stress. Common symptoms include wheezing, dry cough, chest tightness, and shortness of breath. Diagnostics include spirometry (lung function tests) and peak flow monitoring. Treatment involves inhaled corticosteroids (controllers) and bronchodilators (relievers). Prevention focuses on avoiding triggers and using inhalers correctly.

---

## Definition
* **Bronchial Asthma:** A heterogeneous disease, usually characterized by chronic airway inflammation, defined by the history of respiratory symptoms that vary over time and in intensity, together with variable expiratory airflow limitation.
* **Asthma Attack (Exacerbation):** An acute worsening of shortness of breath, cough, wheezing, or chest tightness, requiring immediate rescue medication.
* **Spirometry:** A diagnostic test that measures the amount and/or speed of air that can be inhaled and exhaled, used to confirm airway obstruction and reversibility.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The respiratory tree consists of bronchi and bronchioles:
1. **Bronchospasm:** In asthma patients, the smooth muscles surrounding the bronchioles are hyperreactive. When exposed to triggers, these muscles contract violently (bronchospasm), narrowing the airway.
2. **Mucosal Edema & Mucus Plugs:** The inner lining of the airways swells (edema) and secretes thick, sticky mucus. This further blocks the narrow air passages, trapping air in the alveoli and causing difficulty in exhaling.

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Asthma
* **Immune Hyperreactivity:** An overreaction of T-helper 2 (Th2) cells and eosinophils to inhaled particles, producing immunoglobulin E (IgE) antibodies that trigger allergic inflammation.
* **Genetic Predisposition:** A family history of atopy (asthma, eczema, or allergic rhinitis/hay fever).

### Common Asthma Triggers
* **Allergens:** House dust mites, pollen grains, mold spores, and pet dander (animal hair/saliva).
* **Irritants:** Tobacco smoke, vehicle exhaust, strong chemical fumes, perfumes, and outdoor air pollution ($PM_{2.5}$).
* **Physical & Emotional:** Cold air, dry winter winds, intense exercise (exercise-induced bronchospasm), and strong emotional expressions (laughing or crying hard).

### Risk Factors
* Having other allergic conditions (atopic dermatitis, food allergies), obesity, and maternal smoking during pregnancy.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** Frequent dry coughing, especially at night; mild wheezing (whistling sound) during exhalation; feeling out of breath easily during light play or walking.
* **Common Symptoms:** Shortness of breath (dyspnea); high-pitched whistling sound (wheezing) when breathing out; chest tightness or pressure; dry, hacking cough that gets worse during colds or at night.
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Difficulty talking in complete sentences (needing to stop for breath); using neck and chest muscles to breathe (intercostal retractions); chest locked in an expanded state; peak flow meter readings dropping into the yellow/red zone.
* **Emergency Symptoms:** Bluish color of lips or fingernails (cyanosis); severe struggle to breathe, causing indrawing of the throat (tracheal tug); extreme confusion, drowsiness, or collapse during an attack (silent chest - airways too narrow to even make a wheezing sound).

## Diagnosis

### Spirometry & Bronchodilator Reversibility Test
* The gold standard test. The patient blows hard into a spirometer machine. If the FEV1/FVC ratio is low, it indicates obstruction. The test is repeated after giving a puff of salbutamol; an improvement in FEV1 of $\ge 12\%$ and $\ge 200$ mL confirms reversible asthma.

### Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF) Monitoring
* The patient blows into a small handheld device called a Peak Flow Meter daily. Variable readings (morning vs evening) or dropping values help monitor asthma control and warning signs.

### Fractional Exhaled Nitric Oxide (FeNO)
* Measures the level of nitric oxide gas in the breath, which is a marker for allergic airway inflammation (eosinophilic inflammation).

### Allergy Testing (Skin Prick Test)
* Done to identify specific environmental allergens (dust, pollen, mold) that trigger the patient's asthma.

## Treatment

### Inhaler Technique & Device Management

#### English
* **Always Use a Spacer:** For Metered Dose Inhalers (MDI), attach a spacer device. It holds the medicine mist so the patient can breathe it in slowly and deeply, ensuring it reaches the lungs instead of depositing in the throat (which causes oral thrush).
* **Rinse Mouth:** Always rinse the mouth with water and spit it out after using a steroid inhaler to prevent oral thrush (fungal infection) and hoarseness.

#### Hindi
* **हमेशा स्पेसर (Spacer) का उपयोग करें:** एमडीआई (MDI) इनहेलर के साथ एक स्पेसर डिवाइस जोड़ें। यह दवा के कोहरे को रोक कर रखता है जिससे मरीज उसे धीरे-धीरे और गहराई से सांस ले सकता है।
* **मुंह साफ करें (Rinse Mouth):** मुंह के छालों (थ्रश) को रोकने के लिए स्टेरॉयड इनहेलर का उपयोग करने के बाद हमेशा पानी से कुल्ला करें और थूक दें।

#### Hinglish
* **Hamesha Spacer use karein:** MDI Inhaler ke sath hamesha spacer pipe lagayein. Yeh dawa ko hold rakhta hai jisse aap dheere aur gehri saans lekar dawa sidha lungs me khinch sakte hain.
* **Kulla karein (Rinse):** Steroid inhaler use karne ke turant baad saaf paani se kulla (rinse) karke thuk dein, taaki muh me fungal infection (oral thrush) na ho.

### Home Care during Mild Wheezing

#### English
* Sit the patient upright (do not let them lie down, which makes breathing harder). Loosen tight clothing. Give 2-4 puffs of a rescue salbutamol inhaler immediately using a spacer, repeating every 20 minutes if needed.

### Medical Management

#### English
* **Controller Inhalers (Daily):** Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS) taken daily to prevent inflammation and prevent attacks, even when the patient feels completely fine.
* **Rescue Inhalers (As-Needed):** Quick-acting bronchodilators (e.g., Salbutamol) taken during a sudden attack to open up the airways quickly.

#### Hindi
* **कंट्रोलर इनहेलर (दैनिक):** इनहेल्ड कॉर्टिकोस्टेरॉइड्स (ICS) जो सूजन को रोकने और हमलों से बचने के लिए रोजाना लिए जाते हैं, तब भी जब मरीज पूरी तरह से ठीक महसूस कर रहा हो।
* **रेस्क्यू इनहेलर (जरूरत पड़ने पर):** तीव्र-क्रिया वाले ब्रोंकोडायलेटर्स (जैसे साल्बुटामोल) जो वायुमार्ग को तुरंत खोलने के लिए दौरे के दौरान लिए जाते हैं।

#### Hinglish

* **Rescue Inhalers (As-Needed):** Salbutamol jaise quick relief inhalers jo achanak saans phoolne par airways ko turant kholne ke liye liye jaate hain.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS - Controllers):** Reduce swelling and mucus in the airways (e.g., Fluticasone, Budesonide).
2. **Short-Acting Beta-Agonists (SABA - Rescues):** Relax airway muscles quickly to relieve spasms (e.g., Salbutamol, Levosalbutamol).
3. **Long-Acting Beta-Agonists (LABA):** Long-term bronchodilation, always combined with ICS (e.g., Formoterol, Salmeterol).
4. **Leukotriene Receptor Antagonists (LTRA):** Daily oral tablets to reduce allergic inflammation and prevent exercise-induced asthma (e.g., Montelukast).
5. **Systemic Corticosteroids:** Oral tablets used for a short course (3-5 days) to control a severe asthma flare-up (e.g., Prednisolone).

## Prevention

### English
* **Identify & Avoid Triggers:** Keep the house free of dust. Do not use heavy carpets. Wash bedsheets in hot water weekly to kill dust mites. Keep pets out of the bedroom.
* **Use Inhalers Daily:** Do not stop controller inhalers when symptoms improve; stopping them causes the inflammation to return, risking a severe attack.
* **Get Annual Flu Vaccine:** Take the annual influenza vaccine to prevent viral chest infections that trigger asthma attacks.
* **Exercise Safely:** If exercise triggers your asthma, take 2 puffs of your rescue inhaler 15 minutes before starting physical activity.

* **ट्रिगर्स की पहचान करें और उनसे बचें:** घर को धूल-मुक्त रखें। भारी कालीनों का उपयोग न करें। धूल के कणों (dust mites) को मारने के लिए साप्ताहिक रूप से चादरों को गर्म पानी में धोएं।

## Possible Complications
* **Status Asthmaticus:** An acute, severe asthma attack that does not respond to repeated rescue inhaler puffs, representing a life-threatening emergency.
* **Pneumothorax:** Air leaking from the lungs into the chest cavity due to high pressure in blocked airways, causing the lung to collapse.
* **Oral Candidiasis (Thrush):** Fungal infection in the mouth caused by steroid deposition due to poor inhaler rinsing technique.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient cannot speak even a few words without stopping to breathe.**
* **The patient’s lips, tongue, or fingernails turn blue or grey (Cyanosis).**
* **The rescue inhaler does not provide any relief, and chest indrawing is severe.**
* **The chest becomes quiet, and wheezing sounds disappear completely during breathing struggle (Silent Chest).**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If your child has a persistent dry cough at night or wakes up coughing.
* If you need to use your rescue salbutamol inhaler more than 2 times a week.
* If you need help checking your inhaler technique or want to create an Asthma Action Plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Bronchial Asthma?**
   Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disease of the airways that causes the saans ki naliyan (air passages) to swell, narrow, and produce excess mucus, causing breathing difficulty.

2. **What is the difference between a Rescue and a Controller inhaler?**
   Rescue inhalers (SABA/Salbutamol) work in minutes to open airways during an attack. Controller inhalers (ICS/Budesonide) reduce chronic inflammation and must be used daily to prevent attacks.

3. **Why should I always use a spacer with an inhaler?**
   A spacer holds the medicine mist so you can breathe it in slowly. This ensures the medicine reaches deep into the lungs instead of hitting the back of the throat, reducing throat irritation and thrush.

4. **Why is it important to rinse the mouth after using a steroid inhaler?**
   Steroid medicine left in the mouth can lower local immunity, leading to a fungal throat infection (oral thrush) and hoarseness of voice. Rinsing and spitting out water prevents this.

5. **Is asthma hereditary?**
   Yes, asthma has a strong genetic link. A child is more likely to develop asthma if their parents have asthma, allergic rhinitis (sneezing), or eczema.

6. **What is Spirometry?**
   Spirometry is a lung function test where you blow hard into a tube connected to a machine. It measures how much air you can breathe out and how fast, confirming airway blockages.

7. **Can children outgrow asthma?**
   Many children who have asthma symptoms in early childhood improve or become symptom-free as they grow older and their airways widen, though symptoms can return in adulthood.

8. **What is an asthma trigger?**
   An asthma trigger is any substance or condition (like dust mites, pollen, pet dander, cold air, cigarette smoke, or exercise) that causes the airways to react and swell, leading to symptoms.

9. **Can I exercise if I have asthma?**
    Yes. Regular exercise is healthy for the lungs. If physical activity triggers your asthma (exercise-induced asthma), take 2 puffs of your rescue inhaler 15 minutes before starting.

10. **Where can I get cheap inhalers in India?**
    You can buy generic Salbutamol and Budesonide inhalers at very low prices (₹50 to ₹90 per inhaler) at government Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Kendras.

11. **What is a Peak Flow Meter?**
    It is a small, handheld device that measures how fast you can blow air out of your lungs. Daily readings help track asthma control and warn you of an upcoming attack.

12. **Why does asthma worsen at night?**
    At night, the body’s natural cortisol levels (which suppress inflammation) drop, airways cool down, and lying flat makes it easier for mucus to pool, triggering cough and wheezing.

13. **Is an inhaler habit-forming or addictive?**
    No. Inhalers are not addictive. They deliver tiny, precise doses of medicine directly to the lungs. Using them daily is necessary to control the chronic disease, not due to addiction.

14. **What is Status Asthmaticus?**
    It is a severe, life-threatening asthma attack that does not respond to home rescue inhalers, requiring immediate emergency hospital care.

15. **Why are oral steroid tablets only given for a few days?**
    Oral steroid tablets (like Prednisolone) are highly effective at stopping severe inflammation but can cause side effects if taken long-term. Inhalers are safer for daily use.

16. **How do I clean my spacer device?**
    Wash the spacer once a week in warm water with a few drops of dish soap. Let it air dry completely on a clean towel. Do not wipe it with a cloth, which creates static electricity that traps medicine.

17. **Can passive smoking trigger a child’s asthma?**
    Yes. Secondhand tobacco smoke is a powerful airway irritant that damages the cilia in the child's lungs, triggering frequent and severe asthma attacks.

18. **What is the "Silent Chest" in an asthma emergency?**
    During a severe attack, if the airways become so narrow that almost no air can move in or out, the wheezing sound disappears. This "silent chest" is a sign of respiratory failure.

19. **What is the difference between Asthma and COPD?**
    Asthma is a reversible allergic airway inflammation common in children/young adults. COPD is permanent, progressive lung damage caused by long-term smoking in older adults.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover asthma hospital admission?**
    Yes, the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme covers cashless treatment expenses for hospital admissions due to severe asthma attacks or status asthmaticus.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Inhalers are a last resort used only when the disease is very severe.
  * **Fact:** Inhalers are the safest and most effective first-line treatment for all stages of asthma, delivering medicine directly to the lungs with minimal side effects.
* **Myth:** Using inhalers daily will make the child addicted to them.
  * **Fact:** Inhalers are not addictive. They are a daily preventive care method, similar to wearing glasses for poor vision.
* **Myth:** Asthma is a contagious disease that spreads by sharing food.
  * **Fact:** Asthma is a non-infectious, chronic inflammatory and genetic condition. You cannot catch asthma from someone else.
* **Myth:** Children with asthma should not play sports or run.
  * **Fact:** With proper controller inhalers, children with asthma can participate in all sports. Many Olympic athletes have asthma.
* **Myth:** Inhaler steroids cause weight gain and stunt child growth.
  * **Fact:** Inhaled steroids are delivered in micrograms directly to the lungs, with negligible absorption into the body. They do not affect growth or cause weight gain.
* **Myth:** Steaming with Vicks is a cure for asthma attacks.
  * **Fact:** Steaming with strong menthol can actually irritate hyperreactive airways and worsen bronchospasms. Use rescue inhalers instead.
* **Myth:** Asthma can be cured permanently by alternative medicines.
  * **Fact:** Asthma has no permanent cure. Alternative therapies claiming permanent cures often mix unlabelled steroid powders, which can cause severe side effects.
* **Myth:** If you don't hear wheezing, the asthma attack is not serious.
  * **Fact:** A complete lack of wheezing during a severe breathing struggle (silent chest) means the airways are dangerously blocked, representing a life-threatening emergency.
* **Myth:** Eating sour foods like curd or lemon causes asthma.
  * **Fact:** Sour foods do not cause asthma. However, if a patient has a specific food allergy, it can act as a trigger.
* **Myth:** Inhalers are difficult for toddlers to use.
  * **Fact:** Toddlers can easily use MDIs using a spacer attached to a soft silicone face mask that covers their nose and mouth.

## Conversation Examples

### Conversation 1
* **User:** Mere 8 saal ke bete ko dhool-mitti me khelne ke baad achanak saans phoolne lagti hai aur chest se seeti jaisi aawaz aati hai. Kya karein?
* **Assistant:** Bachhe ko dhool me khelne ke baad saans phoolna aur wheezing (seeti ki aawaz) hona Bronchial Asthma ka classic lakshan hai. Aap use seedha baithayein aur agar ghar par rescue salbutamol inhaler hai, to use spacer ke jariye 2 puffs turant dein. Checkup ke liye pediatrician ya pulmonologist se consult karke spirometry jaanch karayein.

### Conversation 2
* **User:** What is the right technique of using a Metered Dose Inhaler (MDI)?
* **Assistant:** Shake the inhaler, connect it to the spacer, exhale completely, place the spacer mouthpiece in your mouth, press the inhaler once, and breathe in slowly and deeply through your mouth. Hold your breath for 5-10 seconds, then rinse your mouth with water and spit it out.

### Conversation 3
* **User:** Doctor ne mere bete ko daily Budesonide inhaler lene ko bola hai, par wo ab bilkul normal hai. Kya hum dawa band kar dein?
* **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Budesonide ek Controller inhaler (steroid) hai jo lungs ke andar ki chronic sujan ko dabaye rakhta hai. Agar aap bachhe ke normal hone par dawa band kar denge, to sujan wapas badh jayegi aur use achanak severe attack ho sakta hai. Ise daily jaari rakhein.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** Inhaler ke baad kulla karna kyu zaroori hai?
* **Assistant:** Inhaler me steroid hota hai. Agar sui/mist mouth me reh jaye, to wahan local immunity kam hone se white patches ya chhale (oral thrush) ho sakte hain aur aawaz bhaari ho sakti hai. Inhaler ke baad paani se rinse karke thukne se isse bacha ja sakta hai.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Mere bete ko achanak achanak asthma attack aaya hai, wo ek word bhi nahi bol pa raha aur lips neele pad rahe hain.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Ek baar me bol na pana aur lips ka neela padna (cyanosis) oxygen ki severe kami aur respiratory failure ka sanket hai. Yeh ek medical emergency hai. Aap ghar par inhaler ka wait na karein, bachhe ko turant emergency hospital le jayein ya 108 call karein.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** peak flow meter kya hota hai aur ise kaise use karein?
* **Assistant:** Peak Flow Meter ek chota handheld device hai. Isme lambi saans lekar tezi se phoonk (blow) mari jati hai. Yeh batata hai ki lungs se hawa kitni tezi se nikal rahi hai. Iski daily reading badalne se asthma attack ka pehle hi pata chal jata hai.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Kya alternative medicines asthma ka permanent ilaaj kar sakti hain?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, medical science me asthma ka koi permanent cure nahi hai, ise sirf control kiya ja sakta hai. Alternative powders me aksar illegal steroids hote hain jo kidneys aur bones ko kharab kar sakte hain. Inhalers hi sabse safe ilaaj hain.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Does Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY cover asthma emergency treatment?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment benefits are provided for hospital admission due to severe asthma attacks, nebulization, pediatric ICU care, and oxygen therapy at empaneled hospitals.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Dama ke rogi ko ghar me kya parhez rakhna chahiye?
* **Assistant:** Ghar me dust accumulation se bachein. Room me heavy carpets aur curtains na lagayein taaki dust mites na grow ho ske. Bedsheets ko garm paani me dhoyein. Ghar ke andar smoking bilkul band karwayein aur pets ko bedroom se door rakhein.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Montelukast tablet kis kaam aati hai dama me?
* **Assistant:** Montelukast ek oral tablet hai (LTRA) jo allergic chemicals ko block karti hai. Ise rojana raat ko sone se pehle diya jata hai taaki airways ki allergy aur sujan kam ho sake. Yeh inhaler ke sath supportive dawa ki tarah kaam karti hai.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Inhaler lene se kya bachhe ki growth ruk jayegi?
* **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Inhaled steroid micrograms me hota hai jo direct lungs me jata hai. Yeh blood me absorb nahi hota, isiliye isse bachhe ki height ya growth par koi asar nahi padta. Dama uncontrolled rakhne se bachha kamzor ho sakta hai.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the price of generic Budesonide inhaler at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?
* **Assistant:** Generic Budesonide (200mcg) inhalers are available at government PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, often costing only ₹70 to ₹90, compared to ₹300+ for branded ones in private shops.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Steaming karne se kya asthma ka attack theek ho sakta hai?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, severe attack me steam kaam nahi karti. Ulti menthol steam airways ko aur irritate karke constriction badha sakti hai. Attack ke dauran sirf rescue salbutamol inhaler hi kaam karega.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** What is GINA in asthma guidelines?
* **Assistant:** GINA (Global Initiative for Asthma) ek international organization hai jo asthma ke diagnosis, management, aur safety guidelines ke standard rules banati hai. AIIMS aur Indian doctors GINA guidelines ko hi follow karte hain.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Dama ke attack me "Silent Chest" ka kya matlab hai?
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Jab attack ke dauran saans ki naliyan itni patli ho jayein ki stethoscope se wheezing (seeti) ki aawaz bhi na aaye, to use Silent Chest kehte hain. Yeh extreme emergency hai jisme bachha behosh ho sakta hai. Immediate hospital le jayein.
