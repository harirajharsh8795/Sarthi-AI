# Madhumeh (Diabetes Mellitus) ki Pehchan, Upachar aur Dekhbhal (Diabetes Care and Insulin Guide)

## Overview

### English
Diabetes Mellitus is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by persistent hyperglycemia (high blood sugar) resulting from defects in insulin secretion, insulin action, or both. The two primary types are Type 1 Diabetes (autoimmune destruction of insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas, requiring lifelong insulin) and Type 2 Diabetes (progressive insulin resistance and relative insulin deficiency, strongly linked to obesity and lifestyle). Over time, uncontrolled diabetes causes severe complications, damaging the eyes (retinopathy), kidneys (nephropathy), nerves (neuropathy), and blood vessels (heart attacks). Management requires lifestyle modifications, oral anti-diabetic drugs, or insulin therapy, alongside regular blood glucose monitoring.

## Quick Summary
Diabetes Mellitus involves persistent hyperglycemia due to insulin deficiency or resistance. Major types are Type 1 and Type 2. Key symptoms are the classic triad: frequent urination (polyuria), excessive thirst (polydipsia), and increased hunger (polyphagia), along with unexplained weight loss. Diagnostics include fasting blood sugar, postprandial sugar, and HbA1c tests. Treatment involves oral tablets, insulin therapy, diet, and exercise. Prevention focuses on weight control and avoiding sugar.

---

## Definition
* **Diabetes Mellitus (DM):** A group of metabolic diseases characterized by hyperglycemia resulting from defects in insulin secretion, insulin action, or both.
* **HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin):** A blood test that measures the average blood glucose levels over the past 2 to 3 months by checking the percentage of hemoglobin coated with sugar.
* **Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA):** A life-threatening emergency primarily in Type 1 diabetics, caused by a complete lack of insulin, leading to high blood sugar, fat breakdown, and buildup of toxic ketones in the blood.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The pancreas regulates blood glucose levels:
1. **Islets of Langerhans (Beta Cells):** Located in the pancreas, these cells produce the hormone **insulin** in response to rising blood sugar after meals. Insulin acts as a key to let glucose enter body cells for energy.
2. **Insulin Resistance:** In Type 2 Diabetes, the locks on cell walls are rusty (resistant). The pancreas makes insulin, but cells cannot use it. Glucose remains trapped in the blood, starving cells and damaging blood vessels.

## Causes

### Primary Causes by Diabetes Type
* **Autoimmune Destruction (Type 1):** The body's immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas.
* **Insulin Resistance (Type 2):** Cells become insensitive to insulin, driven by excess fat cells (visceral obesity), genetic inheritance, and physical inactivity.

### Secondary Causes
* Gestational Diabetes (high blood sugar developing during pregnancy).
* Pancreatic diseases (chronic pancreatitis or surgical removal of the pancreas).

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** Diet high in refined carbohydrates and sugars, sedentary lifestyle (no exercise), visceral obesity, and chronic mental stress.
* **Clinical:** Family history of diabetes, age over 45, and high blood pressure or cholesterol levels.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** Needing to urinate frequently, especially at night; feeling constantly thirsty; feeling unusually hungry even after eating; slow-healing cuts or bruises.
* **Common Symptoms (The Triad):**
   * **Polyuria:** Frequent urination.
   * **Polydipsia:** Excessive, unquenchable thirst.
   * **Polyphagia:** Increased hunger.
   * Unexplained rapid weight loss (especially in Type 1); dry, itchy skin; blurred vision; tingling or numbness in the hands or feet (diabetic neuropathy).
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Constant numbness or burning pain in feet (neuropathy); chronic non-healing foot ulcers; decreased vision; frothy urine (nephropathy/kidney damage).
* **Emergency Symptoms (Hypoglycemia):** Sudden cold sweat, shaking, rapid heartbeat, extreme hunger, confusion, dizziness, fits, or loss of consciousness (blood sugar $< 70$ mg/dL).
* **Emergency Symptoms (DKA/HHS):** Deep, rapid breathing (Kussmaul breathing) with a fruity breath odor; severe nausea and vomiting; extreme confusion or coma (blood sugar $> 250$ or $> 600$ mg/dL).

## Diagnosis

### Blood Glucose Panel
* **Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS):** Checked after 8 hours of fasting. **A level $\ge 126$ mg/dL diagnoses diabetes** (normal $< 100$ mg/dL).
* **Postprandial Blood Sugar (PPBS):** Checked 2 hours after a meal. **A level $\ge 200$ mg/dL diagnoses diabetes** (normal $< 140$ mg/dL).
* **Random Blood Sugar (RBS):** Checked anytime; a level $\ge 200$ mg/dL with classic symptoms diagnoses diabetes.

### HbA1c Test (Glycated Hemoglobin)
* The gold standard for long-term monitoring. **An HbA1c $\ge 6.5\%$ diagnoses diabetes** (Pre-diabetes: 5.7 - 6.4%, Normal: $< 5.7\%$). Target for diabetic patients is usually under 7%.

### Urine Test (Microalbuminuria)
* Checking urine for microalbumin (proteins) to detect early diabetic kidney damage.

## Treatment

### Lifestyle, Diet & Daily Glucometer Monitoring

#### English
* **Dietary Management:** Limit sugar, sweets, white rice, maida, and potatoes. Eat foods with a low glycemic index, such as whole grains, green leafy vegetables, oats, and salad.
* **Physical Activity:** Walk briskly for 30-45 minutes daily. Physical exercise helps muscle cells absorb glucose directly from the blood without needing insulin.
* **Glucometer Checks:** Test blood sugar at home using a portable glucometer, keeping a logbook for the doctor.

#### Hindi
* **आहार नियंत्रण:** चीनी, मिठाई, सफेद चावल, मैदा और आलू का सेवन बंद करें। चोकरयुक्त आटा, हरी सब्जियां, ओट्स, अंकुरित अनाज और सलाद लें।
* **व्यायाम:** रोजाना 30-45 मिनट तेज गति से टहलें। शारीरिक व्यायाम मांसपेशियों को बिना इंसुलिन के सीधे रक्त से ग्लूकोज सोखने में मदद करता है।
* **ग्लूकोमीटर का उपयोग:** घर पर नियमित ग्लूकोमीटर से शुगर की जांच करें।

* **Diet control karein:** Meetha, maida, aaloo aur white rice bilkul band karein. Low glycemic index food jaise chokardar aata, ragi, oats, hari sabziyan, aur salad khayein.

#### English
* Inspect feet daily for cuts, blisters, or redness. Wash feet daily in warm water and dry them completely, especially between the toes. Never walk barefoot (even inside the house) to prevent diabetic foot ulcers.

#### English

### Management of Low Sugar (Hypoglycemia) - Rule of 15

#### English
* If blood sugar drops below 70 mg/dL and the patient is conscious: Give 15 grams of fast-acting sugar (3 teaspoons of sugar in water, or a glass of fruit juice). Wait 15 minutes and recheck sugar. Repeat if still low. If unconscious, do not put fluids in the mouth; take the patient to the emergency room immediately for IV Dextrose.

#### Hindi
* लो-शुगर का इलाज (Rule of 15): यदि शुगर 70 mg/dL से कम हो और मरीज होश में हो: 15 ग्राम ग्लूकोज या 3 चम्मच चीनी पानी में घोलकर दें। 15 मिनट बाद दोबारा शुगर जांचें। यदि मरीज बेहोश हो, तो मुंह में कुछ न डालें; सीधे अस्पताल ले जाएं।

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **Biguanides (First-Line):** Reduce glucose production in the liver and improve insulin sensitivity (e.g., Metformin).
2. **Sulfonylureas:** Stimulate beta cells in the pancreas to release more insulin (e.g., Glimepiride, Gliclazide).
3. **DPP-4 Inhibitors & SGLT2 Inhibitors:** SGLT2 inhibitors filter excess glucose out of the body through urine (e.g., Empagliflozin, Sitagliptin).
4. **Insulin Therapy:** Replaces the hormone insulin. Available as rapid-acting, short-acting, intermediate-acting, or long-acting forms (e.g., Insulin Glargine, Human Regular Insulin).
5. **Glucagon Injection:** Emergency hormone injection used to treat severe hypoglycemia when the patient is unconscious.

## Prevention

### English
* **Weight Management:** Maintain a healthy weight; losing 5-7% of body weight can reduce Type 2 diabetes risk by 60%.
* **Avoid Refined Sugars:** Eliminate carbonated sodas, packaged juices, pastries, and sweets.
* **Exercise Daily:** Perform 150 minutes of moderate-intensity physical activity per week.
* **Routine Checks:** If over 35 years or overweight, get an annual fasting blood glucose or HbA1c test.

* **वजन नियंत्रित रखें:** शरीर के अतिरिक्त वजन को कम करें; केवल 5-7% वजन कम करने से टाइप 2 मधुमेह का खतरा 60% तक कम हो सकता है।
* **परिष्कृत शर्करा (Refined Sugar) से बचें:** कोल्ड ड्रिंक्स, पैक किए गए जूस, मिठाई और जंक फूड का सेवन बंद करें।
* **सक्रिय रहें:** सप्ताह में कम से कम 150 मिनट मध्यम तीव्रता वाला व्यायाम करें।

## Possible Complications
* **Diabetic Foot Ulceration:** Nerve damage (neuropathy) causing loss of pain sensation in feet, leading to unnoticed injuries that become severely infected, potentially requiring toe or leg amputation.
* **Diabetic Nephropathy:** Damage to kidney capillaries, leading to protein leakage (albuminuria) and progressive chronic kidney failure requiring dialysis.
* **Diabetic Retinopathy:** Damage to retinal blood vessels, causing micro-aneurysms, hemorrhages, and permanent blindness.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Unconscious, confused, or fitting patient with a blood sugar reading under 70 mg/dL (Severe Hypoglycemia).**
* **High blood sugar ($>250$ mg/dL) accompanied by continuous vomiting, deep rapid breathing, and abdominal pain (DKA emergency).**
* **A foot wound or blister that has turned black, foul-smelling, or swollen (diabetic gangrene).**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you have persistent high blood sugar readings ($>180$ mg/dL) despite taking your daily medicines.
* If you experience frequent episodes of low blood sugar (shaking, sweating) in a week (requires medication dose adjustment).
* If you notice any small cut, sore, or blister on your foot that is not healing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Diabetes Mellitus?**
   Diabetes Mellitus is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high blood glucose levels, caused by the body's inability to produce or properly use insulin.

2. **What is the difference between Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes?**
   Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune disease where the body destroys insulin-producing cells, requiring lifelong insulin. Type 2 diabetes is a lifestyle-linked disease where cells become resistant to insulin, managed with tablets and diet.

3. **What is the HbA1c test?**
   The HbA1c test measures the average blood sugar level over the past 2-3 months by checking the percentage of hemoglobin coated with glucose. A value $\ge 6.5\%$ confirms diabetes.

4. **What is Hypoglycemia and what is its treatment?**
   Hypoglycemia is low blood sugar ($< 70$ mg/dL), causing shaking, cold sweat, and confusion. Treat it immediately using the Rule of 15: eat 15g of sugar/glucose, wait 15 minutes, and recheck.

5. **Why do diabetics develop foot ulcers?**
   High blood sugar damages nerves (neuropathy), causing loss of pain sensation in the feet. Minor cuts or blisters go unnoticed, become infected, and turn into deep ulcers.

6. **What is Metformin?**
   Metformin is the first-line oral anti-diabetic tablet that lowers blood sugar by reducing glucose production in the liver and improving insulin sensitivity in muscles.

7. **How does exercise help lower blood sugar?**
   When you exercise, your muscles contract and can absorb glucose directly from the blood for energy without needing insulin, lowering blood sugar levels.

8. **Where can I buy cheap insulin in India?**
   You can buy generic insulin vials and cartridges at highly subsidized rates at government Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Kendras.

9. **What is Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)?**
   DKA is a life-threatening emergency caused by a complete lack of insulin, forcing the body to break down fat for energy, producing toxic acidic ketones that build up in the blood.

10. **Why do doctors recommend checking diabetic patients' eyes annually?**
    Uncontrolled blood sugar damages micro-vessels in the retina (Diabetic Retinopathy) silently, which can lead to permanent blindness if not detected and treated early.

11. **Can type 2 diabetes be reversed?**
    In early stages, type 2 diabetes can be put into remission (reversal) through significant weight loss, a low-carb diet, and regular exercise, bringing blood sugar back to normal without medications.

12. **Why should insulin never be frozen?**
    Freezing destroys the protein structure of insulin, making it completely ineffective. Store insulin in the refrigerator door or main compartment ($2^\circ\text{C}$ to $8^\circ\text{C}$), never in the freezer.

13. **What does a fasting blood sugar of 115 mg/dL indicate?**
    A fasting blood sugar between 100 and 125 mg/dL indicates Pre-diabetes, meaning you have a high risk of developing full diabetes unless you modify your diet and exercise.

14. **Why do diabetics urinate frequently?**
    When blood sugar levels exceed 180 mg/dL, the kidneys cannot absorb the excess glucose, which spills into the urine, pulling water along with it and causing frequent urination (polyuria).

15. **What is Gestational Diabetes?**
    It is a type of high blood sugar that develops during pregnancy due to placental hormones blocking insulin. It usually resolves after delivery but increases the mother's future risk of Type 2 diabetes.

16. **Why does diabetes cause kidney failure?**
    High glucose damages the delicate filtering units (glomeruli) in the kidneys over years, causing protein leakage (proteinuria) and progressing to chronic kidney failure.

17. **What is a glucometer?**
    A glucometer is a small, portable medical device used at home to check blood sugar levels instantly by analyzing a drop of blood from a finger prick.

18. **Can I stop taking diabetes pills once my sugar is normal?**
    No. Your sugar is normal because the pills are working. Stopping them will cause your blood sugar to rise again. Dose changes should only be done by a doctor.

19. **What is the normal range of blood sugar after eating?**
    In healthy, non-diabetic adults, blood sugar should be under 140 mg/dL two hours after a meal. In diabetics, the target postprandial level is usually under 180 mg/dL.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover diabetic dialysis costs?**
    Yes, the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme covers cashless hemodialysis sessions and treatments for diabetic kidney failure at empaneled dialysis centers.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Eating too much sugar directly causes type 1 diabetes.
  * **Fact:** Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune disease where the body destroys its own pancreas cells; it is not caused by eating sugar.
* **Myth:** Diabetics can eat unlimited amounts of honey or jaggery (gur) instead of white sugar.
  * **Fact:** Honey and jaggery contain glucose and fructose and raise blood sugar levels just as rapidly as white sugar. They must be restricted.
* **Myth:** Insulin injections are a last stage treatment; once started, you will die soon.
  * **Fact:** Insulin is a life-saving hormone. Starting insulin early controls sugar, protects organs (kidneys, heart), and helps you live a long, healthy life.
* **Myth:** Bitter foods like bitter gourd (karela) juice cure diabetes.
  * **Fact:** Karela contains substances that help lower blood sugar slightly, but it cannot cure diabetes. It should not replace prescribed medications.
* **Myth:** If you have diabetes, you must never walk barefoot on grass.
  * **Fact:** Diabetics should never walk barefoot *anywhere* (including grass, sand, or carpet) due to the risk of stepping on sharp objects that cause non-healing ulcers.
* **Myth:** Type 2 diabetes only affects overweight people.
  * **Fact:** While obesity is a major risk factor, thin people can also develop type 2 diabetes due to genetics, age, and visceral fat around organs (skinny fat).
* **Myth:** Skipping meals is a good way to lower blood sugar.
  * **Fact:** Skipping meals can cause dangerous blood sugar drops (hypoglycemia) or trigger the liver to release stored glucose, causing sugar spikes later.
* **Myth:** Diabetes is contagious and spreads through saliva.
  * **Fact:** Diabetes is a non-infectious metabolic disorder; it cannot spread by sharing food, kissing, or touching.
* **Myth:** If your blood sugar is high, you should drink hot water.
  * **Fact:** Hot water does not lower blood sugar. Hydration with normal water is good, but medication and diet are the only ways to reduce glucose.
* **Myth:** Special diabetic cookies and chocolates are completely safe to eat in unlimited amounts.
  * **Fact:** These products often contain hidden carbohydrates, fats, or artificial sweeteners that can still raise sugar levels or cause diarrhea.

## Conversation Examples

### Conversation 1
* **User:** Mere pitaji ki age 55 saal hai, unka random blood sugar 280 aaya hai aur unhe bohot pyaas lag rahi hai aur baar-baar peshab aa raha hai. Kya ye gathiya hai?
* **Assistant:** Random blood sugar 280 mg/dL aana aur baar-baar peshab (polyuria) aur pyaas (polydipsia) lagna Diabetes Mellitus (Sugar ki bimari) ka classic lakshan hai. Yeh gathiya (arthritis) nahi hai. Gathiya jodo ki sujan hoti hai. Aapko pitaji ko endocrinologist ya general physician ko dikhana chahiye aur fasting blood sugar aur HbA1c test karana chahiye.

### Conversation 2
* **User:** What is the rule of 15 for low blood sugar?
* **Assistant:** If blood sugar drops below 70 mg/dL and the patient is awake: Give 15g of fast sugar (e.g., 3 teaspoons of sugar in water or 1/2 cup of juice). Wait 15 minutes and check blood sugar again. If still below 70, repeat the steps. Once normal, eat a light snack (roti or biscuit) to stabilize it.

### Conversation 3
* **User:** Mere dada ji ko diabetes hai aur unke pair ke anguthe (toe) me ek chota cut lag gaya hai jisme se halka paani aa raha hai par unhe koi dard nahi hai.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Diabetes patients me nerve damage (neuropathy) ke karan dard mehsus nahi hota. Yeh painless cut "Diabetic Foot Ulcer" me badal sakta hai jo haddiyon tak infection phaila sakta hai. Aap unhe nange pair bilkul na chalne dein, ghaw ko saaf bandage se cover karein, aur turant doctor ya surgeon ko dikhayein.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** insulin pen aur vials ko kaise store karein? Kya freezer me rakhna safe hai?
* **Assistant:** Insulin vials aur pens ko fridge ke main compartment ya door me ($2^\circ\text{C}$ to $8^\circ\text{C}$) rakhna chahiye. Ise freezer me bilkul na rakhein. Freezer me jamne (freeze) se insulin protein kharab ho jata hai aur wo be-asar ho jata hai. Garm weather me direct dhoop se bhi bachayein.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Mere relative ko diabetes hai aur wo achanak behosh ho gaye hain, thanda pasina aa raha hai aur haath kaap rahe hain.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Yeh severe Hypoglycemia (sugar ka bohot kam hona) ka sanket hai. Patient behosh hai, isiliye unke muh me koi liquid ya chini paani na dalein, isse saans ki nali block ho sakti hai. Unhe turant hospital ke emergency room le jayein jahan doctor IV Dextrose (glucose drip) lagayenge.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** HbA1c test kya hota hai aur iski normal range kya hai?
* **Assistant:** HbA1c test pichle 3 mahine ke average blood sugar ko dikhata hai. Normal range 5.7% se kam hoti hai. 5.7% se 6.4% pre-diabetes (high risk) hota hai, aur 6.5% ya usse zyada aana diabetes ki diagnosis ko confirm karta hai.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Kya jaggery (gur) khana sugar patients ke liye safe hai?
* **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Gur (jaggery) aur honey (shahad) me bhi glucose aur fructose hota hai jo white sugar ki tarah hi blood sugar ko tezi se badhata hai. Sugar patients ko gur aur chini dono se parhez karna chahiye.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Does Ayushman Bharat card cover diabetic dialysis cost?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment packages cover hemodialysis sessions, nephrologist consultations, and treatments for diabetic kidney failure (nephropathy).

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Metformin tablet khane ke kya fayde hain?
* **Assistant:** Metformin type 2 sugar ki first-line dawa hai. Yeh liver me extra glucose banna kam karti hai, muscles me insulin sensitivity badhati hai jisse cells sugar asani se use kar sakein, aur yeh weight badhne se bhi rokti hai.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Diabetic retinopathy kya hota hai?
* **Assistant:** Uncontrolled blood sugar aankho ki retina (parde) ki micro-blood vessels ko damage kar deta hai jise Diabetic Retinopathy kehte hain. Isse parde me bleeding ho sakti hai jisse aankho ki roshni permanently ja sakti hai. Isiliye diabetics ko saal me ek bar eye checkup karana chahiye.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Kya walk karne se sach me sugar control hoti hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, daily 30-40 minutes brisk walk ya exercise karne se muscles ko energy ke liye glucose ki zarurat hoti hai. Muscles bina insulin ki madad ke blood se direct sugar ko consume karti hain, jisse natural tarike se sugar level kam hota hai.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the price of generic Metformin at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?
* **Assistant:** Generic Metformin (500mg) tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, often costing only ₹5 to ₹10 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹30 to ₹50 for branded versions.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Pregnancy ke dauran sugar badhna kya dangerous hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, pregnancy me sugar badhna (Gestational Diabetes) baby aur mother dono ke liye risky hai. Isse delivery me complication ho sakti hai aur baby ka weight abnormal badh sakta hai. Ise doctor ki salah se insulin injections se control kiya jata hai.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** What is the role of SGLT2 inhibitors in diabetes?
* **Assistant:** SGLT2 inhibitors (jaise Empagliflozin) ek advanced drug class hai jo kidneys ke jariye excess glucose (sugar) ko peshab (urine) ke raste body se bahar nikalti hain, jisse blood sugar kam hota hai aur heart par load kam hota hai.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Type 1 diabetes ke bachhe ko achanak vomiting shuru ho gayi hai aur uski saans se fruits jaisi smell aa rahi hai.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Yeh "Diabetic Ketoacidosis" (DKA) ka classic sign hai jo body me insulin ki bhari kami se hota hai. Yeh ek life-threatening emergency hai. Aap bina ek bhi minute waste kiye bachhe ko hospital emergency le jayein jahan IV fluids aur insulin drip shuru ki jayegi.
