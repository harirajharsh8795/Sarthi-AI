# Mirgi Rog (Epilepsy) ki Pehchan, Pratham Upachar aur Upachar (Seizures and Epilepsy Care)

## Overview

### English
Epilepsy is a chronic neurological disorder characterized by recurrent, unprovoked seizures, which are sudden episodes of abnormal, excessive electrical activity in the brain. Seizures can manifest as temporary confusion, staring spells, uncontrollable jerking movements of the limbs (convulsions), or loss of consciousness, depending on the brain region involved. Epilepsy is diagnosed using electroencephalography (EEG) and brain scans. While it has no permanent cure, seizures are highly controlled in over 70% of cases using daily anti-epileptic drugs (AEDs). Knowing seizure first aid—keeping the patient safe, karwat (sideways) positioning, and avoiding inserting objects in the mouth—is crucial.

## Quick Summary
Epilepsy involves recurrent seizures from brain electrical surges. Major types are generalized (tonic-clonic, absence) and focal. Common symptoms include muscle stiffness, rhythmic jerking, loss of bladder control, and temporary unconsciousness. Diagnostics include Electroencephalogram (EEG) tests and MRI brain scans. Treatment relies on daily anti-epileptic drugs (AEDs). Prevention centers on first aid, avoiding sleep deprivation, and managing triggers.

---

## Definition
* **Epilepsy:** A disorder of the brain characterized by an enduring predisposition to generate epileptic seizures and by the neurobiologic, cognitive, psychological, and social consequences of this condition (requires at least two unprovoked seizures).
* **Generalized Tonic-Clonic Seizure (Grand Mal):** A major seizure involving the entire brain, characterized by muscle stiffening (tonic phase) followed by rhythmic, violent jerking movements of the limbs (clonic phase) and loss of consciousness.
* **EEG (Electroencephalogram):** A diagnostic test that detects electrical activity in the brain using small metal discs (electrodes) attached to the scalp, displaying brain wave patterns.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The brain functions through electrochemical signals:
1. **Neuronal Hyperexcitation:** In a healthy brain, neurons fire electrical signals in an orderly manner. During a seizure, a group of neurons suddenly fires synchronized, rapid bursts of electricity, disrupting normal brain function.
2. **Focal vs. Generalized:** If the electrical surge is limited to one part of the brain, it is a focal seizure (causing localized twitching or altered smell). If it spreads to the entire brain, it is a generalized seizure, causing full body convulsions and loss of consciousness.

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Epilepsy
* **Brain Injury or Trauma:** Scar tissue formed in the brain after a head injury (accidents) disrupting local electrical pathways.
* **Brain Tumors or Stroke:** Damage to brain cells due to lack of blood flow (stroke) or growth of a tumor, triggering abnormal electrical firing.
* **Infections:** Infections of the brain and its protective linings, such as meningitis, encephalitis, or neurocysticercosis (tapeworm cysts in the brain, common in India).

### Secondary & Genetic Factors
* Inherited genetic mutations affecting the ion channels on neuronal membranes (channelopathies).
* Developmental brain abnormalities occurring during pregnancy (e.g., lack of oxygen during birth).

### Risk Factors & Triggers for Seizures
* **Triggers:** Sleep deprivation (most common trigger), high physical/mental stress, flashing lights (photosensitive epilepsy), high fever (especially in young children), and missing a daily dose of anti-epileptic medicine.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs (Aura):** A sudden, unusual feeling before a seizure, such as a strange smell, metallic taste in the mouth, or a feeling of "deja vu" (familiarity).
* **Common Symptoms (Tonic-Clonic):** Sudden cry or scream as airway muscles contract; falling to the ground; body becoming completely stiff (tonic phase); violent, rhythmic jerking of arms and legs (clonic phase); biting of the tongue; frothing from the mouth; loss of bladder or bowel control.
* **Absence Seizure Symptoms (Petit Mal):** Staring blankly into space for a few seconds; fluttering of eyelids; temporary loss of awareness (common in children, often mistaken for daydreaming).
* **Post-Seizure State (Postictal Phase):** Extreme fatigue, deep sleep, confusion, headache, and no memory of the seizure event after waking up.
* **Emergency Symptoms:** A seizure lasting more than 5 minutes; having multiple seizures in a row without waking up in between (Status Epilepticus - life-threatening).

## Diagnosis

### Electroencephalogram (EEG)
* The primary test. Electrodes are pasted on the scalp to record brain waves. Specific spikes or sharp waves on the EEG help locate abnormal electrical activity and classify the epilepsy type.

### Brain Imaging (MRI & CT Scan)
* **Brain MRI:** Detailed scans to look for structural causes of epilepsy, such as brain scars, cysts (neurocysticercosis), vascular abnormalities, or small tumors.
* **CT Scan:** Done in emergencies to rule out immediate bleeding or stroke.

## Seizure First Aid (Emergency Management)

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Ease the patient to the floor.** Place something soft (like a folded jacket) under their head.
* **Turn the patient gently onto one side (karwat position)** to keep their airway clear and let saliva drain out.
* **Clear the area** of sharp or hard objects to prevent injury.
* **Time the seizure** using a watch. Remain calm and stay with the patient until the seizure ends naturally.

#### Hindi
* **मरीज को धीरे से जमीन पर लेटाएं।** सिर के नीचे कोई मुलायम चीज (जैसे मुड़ा हुआ कोट) रखें।
* **मरीज को धीरे से एक करवट (side position) में लिटाएं** ताकि लार बाहर निकल सके और सांस की नली खुली रहे।
* **आसपास की नुकीली चीजों को हटा दें** ताकि चोट न लगे।
* **दौरे के समय को नोट करें।** शांत रहें और दौरा समाप्त होने तक मरीज के साथ रहें।

#### Hinglish
* **Patient ko dheere se zameen par leta dein.** Sir ke niche koi naram kapda ya bag rakhein.
* **Patient ko dheere se ek karwat (left/right side) dilayein** taaki saliva (laar) bahar nikal sake aur saans ki nali khuli rahe.
* **Aas-pass ki nukili ya hard cheezein hata dein** taaki chot na lage.
* **Daure ka time note karein.** Ghhabrayein nahi aur daura khatam hone tak patient ke sath rahein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT hold the patient down** or try to stop their movements.
* **Do NOT force anything into the patient's mouth** (spoon, fingers, or water). It can break teeth or block the airway.
* **Do NOT make them smell shoes, onions, or keys.** (This is a useless myth and delays first aid).
* **Do NOT offer water or food** until the patient is fully awake and conscious.

#### Hindi
* **मरीज को जबरदस्ती न पकड़ें** और न ही उनके झटकों को रोकने की कोशिश करें।
* **मरीज के मुंह में जबरदस्ती कुछ भी न डालें** (जैसे चम्मच, उंगली, या पानी)। इससे दांत टूट सकते हैं या सांस रुक सकती है।
* **जूता या प्याज न सुंघाएं।** (यह एक अंधविश्वास है जिससे प्राथमिक चिकित्सा में देरी होती है)।
* **होश में आने तक कुछ भी न खिलाएं-पिलाएं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Patient ke hath-pair zabardasti na pakdein** aur na hi unke jhatko ko rokne ki koshish karein.
* **Muh ke andar zabardasti kuch na dalein** (jaise chammach, ungli ya paani). Isse daant toot sakte hain ya saans block ho sakti hai.
* **Joota, chabhi ya pyaaj na sunghayein.** (Yeh bilkul bekar andhvishwas hai jisse first aid me deri hoti hai).

### Daily Medication Compliance

#### English

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **First-Line AEDs (Daily):** Broad-spectrum medications that reduce brain excitability (e.g., Sodium Valproate, Levetiracetam, Carbamazepine).
2. **Emergency Benzodiazepines:** Fast-acting injections or nasal sprays used to stop an active seizure (e.g., Midazolam nasal spray, IV Lorazepam).
3. **Newer Generation AEDs:** Used for refractory cases with fewer side effects (e.g., Lamotrigine, Lacosamide).
4. **Anthelmintics (for Cysts):** Used under steroid cover to treat neurocysticercosis tapeworm infections (e.g., Albendazole).
5. **Steroids:** Prescribed clinically to reduce brain swelling around active cysts.

## Prevention

### English
* **Ensure Sleep Hygiene:** Get 7-8 hours of continuous sleep daily, as sleep deprivation is the most common trigger.
* **Take Medications Daily:** Strictly follow the prescribed medication schedule.
* **Prevent Head Injuries:** Wear helmets during two-wheeler riding or sports to prevent brain trauma.
* **Cook Pork Thoroughly:** Cook pork and wash vegetables thoroughly to prevent tapeworm egg ingestion (neurocysticercosis).

## Possible Complications
* **Status Epilepticus:** A seizure lasting longer than 5 minutes, causing severe lack of oxygen to the brain and muscle breakdown, which can lead to kidney shutdown and death.
* **SUDEP (Sudden Unexpected Death in Epilepsy):** A rare, unexplained death in a patient with uncontrolled epilepsy, usually occurring during sleep.
* **Physical Injuries:** Fractures, shoulder dislocations, or drowning during a seizure episode.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **A seizure that lasts longer than 5 minutes (danger of Status Epilepticus).**
* **The patient has a second seizure immediately after the first, without waking up.**
* **The patient does not wake up or starts having difficulty breathing after the jerking stops.**
* **The seizure occurs in water (swimming pool or bath) or during pregnancy.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If a person experiences a seizure for the first time in their life.
* If the frequency of seizures increases despite taking daily anti-epileptic medicines.
* If a woman with epilepsy is planning a pregnancy (requires changing to safer medications to prevent birth defects).

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Mirgi (Epilepsy)?**
   Epilepsy is a chronic brain disorder characterized by recurrent, unprovoked seizures caused by sudden, abnormal electrical surges in the brain.

2. **What should I do first when someone has a seizure?**
   Ease the person to the floor, place a soft jacket under their head, clear sharp objects, turn them gently onto one side (karwat position), and time the seizure. Stay with them until it ends.

3. **Why is it dangerous to put a spoon or fingers in a seizing patient’s mouth?**
   Violent jaw spasms can cause the patient to bite your fingers or break their own teeth on the spoon. The broken teeth or spoon can block the airway, causing suffocation.

4. **Is epilepsy a mental illness or caused by spirits?**
   No. Epilepsy is a physical, neurological disorder of the brain's electrical system. It has nothing to do with mental illness or evil spirits.

5. **What is an EEG test?**
   An Electroencephalogram (EEG) is a painless test where small metal discs (electrodes) are placed on the scalp to record the brain's electrical activity, helping diagnose epilepsy.

6. **What is Status Epilepticus?**
   It is a medical emergency where a seizure lasts longer than 5 minutes, or multiple seizures occur without the patient waking up in between, requiring immediate hospital care.

7. **Can a woman with epilepsy have a healthy baby?**
   Yes. Over 90% of pregnant women with epilepsy have healthy babies. However, they must plan their pregnancy with a neurologist to adjust medicines to safer doses.

8. **Why does sleep deprivation trigger seizures?**
   Lack of sleep alters the chemical balance and increases excitability in brain cells, making it much easier for an abnormal electrical surge to happen.

9. **What is Neurocysticercosis?**
    It is a brain infection caused by tapeworm larvae (usually from eating food contaminated with tapeworm eggs). It is a leading cause of adult-onset seizures in India.

10. **Where can I get cheap anti-epileptic medicines in India?**
    You can buy generic anti-epileptic drugs like Sodium Valproate and Levetiracetam at generic prices (up to 80% cheaper) at government PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras.

11. **What is an "Aura" before a seizure?**
    An aura is an early warning sign felt just before a seizure starts, presenting as a strange taste, smell, stomach discomfort, or sudden intense fear.

12. **Why should anti-epileptic medicines never be stopped suddenly?**
    Stopping AEDs suddenly can cause the brain to react with continuous, non-stop severe seizures (Status Epilepticus), which can be fatal.

13. **Can flashing lights trigger seizures?**
    Yes. A small percentage of people have Photosensitive Epilepsy, where flashing lights (strobe lights, video games) can trigger seizures.

14. **What is the difference between a seizure and epilepsy?**
    A seizure is a single episode of abnormal brain electrical activity. Epilepsy is the chronic condition diagnosed when a person has two or more unprovoked seizures.

15. **Why do doctors recommend turning a seizing patient on their side?**
    Turning them on their side (recovery position) allows saliva or vomit to drain out of the mouth safely, preventing it from entering the lungs and choking them.

16. **Can a person swallow their tongue during a seizure?**
    No. It is physically impossible to swallow your tongue. The tongue can relax and block the throat, which is prevented by turning the patient on their side.

17. **What is a focal seizure?**
    A focal seizure starts in a localized area of the brain, causing symptoms like twitching of one hand, altered senses, or lip-smacking without losing consciousness.

18. **Why is swimming dangerous for people with epilepsy?**
    If a seizure occurs while swimming, the patient can silently drown within seconds. Swimming should only be done under close supervision.

19. **What is SUDEP?**
    SUDEP stands for Sudden Unexpected Death in Epilepsy. It is a rare, unexplained death in people with epilepsy, usually occurring during or after a seizure in sleep.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover epilepsy hospital admission?**
    Yes, the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme covers cashless treatment expenses for hospital admissions due to status epilepticus, head injuries, or brain cyst removal surgeries.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Make the patient smell a dirty shoe or onion to stop a mirgi attack.
  * **Fact:** Dirty shoes or onions have no effect on brain electrical activity. Seizures stop naturally in 1-3 minutes. This myth only delays proper first aid (karwat position).
* **Myth:** Hold the patient's limbs tightly to stop the shaking.
  * **Fact:** Trying to restrain the shaking can cause muscle tears, shoulder dislocation, or bone fractures. Let the shaking happen safely.
* **Myth:** Force a metal key or spoon into the mouth to prevent tongue biting.
  * **Fact:** Putting metal in the mouth can break teeth, which can block the airway. Do not put anything in the mouth.
* **Myth:** Epilepsy is caused by black magic or bad karma.
  * **Fact:** Epilepsy is a medical neurological disorder caused by brain injury, genetics, or infections, diagnosed by EEG and treated with medicines.
* **Myth:** People with epilepsy cannot study or work in normal jobs.
  * **Fact:** With regular medicine, most people with epilepsy are completely seizure-free and can study, work, and lead highly successful lives.
* **Myth:** Mirgi is contagious and spreads by touching saliva.
  * **Fact:** Epilepsy is not infectious; you cannot catch it from contact with a patient's saliva or touch.
* **Myth:** If you have one seizure, you have epilepsy for life.
  * **Fact:** A single seizure caused by high fever, low blood sugar, or drug reaction is not epilepsy. Epilepsy is diagnosed only after two or more unprovoked seizures.
* **Myth:** Women with epilepsy are sterile and cannot marry.
  * **Fact:** Women with epilepsy can marry and have healthy children under proper medical supervision.
* **Myth:** Steaming is a treatment for seizures.
  * **Fact:** Steaming does not stop brain electrical surges. Seizures require anti-epileptic drugs.
* **Myth:** You should give the patient water to drink during a seizure.
  * **Fact:** Giving water to an unconscious or seizing patient can cause the liquid to enter their lungs, leading to choking or aspiration pneumonia.

### Conversation 3
* **User:** Doctor ne mere bete ko "Sodium Valproate" tablet likhi hai rojana khane ko. Bachhe ko 1 mahine se daura nahi aaya hai. Kya hum dawa band kar dein?
* **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Sodium Valproate ek anti-epileptic tablet hai jo brain ki electrical activity ko normal rakhti hai. Agar aap dawa band kar denge, to brain me achanak waves badh jayengi aur bachhe ko non-stop daure (status epilepticus) aa sakte hain jo jaanleva hai. Dawa doctor ke bataye anusar regular jaari rakhein.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** Daura aane par "karwat" (recovery position) dilana kyu zaroori hai?
* **Assistant:** Daure ke dauran saliva (laar) ya vomit (ulti) nikal sakti hai. Agar patient peeth ke bal leta rahega, to ye liquid unke lungs me ja kar saans block kar sakta hai. Karwat (side position) me letane se liquid asani se bahar nikal jata hai aur saans chalti rehti hai.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Mere relative ko achanak achanak daura shuru hua hai, aur ab 7 minutes ho gaye hain par jhatke band nahi ho rahe aur wo behosh hain.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Daura 5 minutes se zyada lagatar chalna "Status Epilepticus" ki emergency hai. Yeh life-threatening hai aur brain me oxygen ki kami kar sakta hai. Aap bina ek second waste kiye unhe turant ambulance (108) bulakar nearest hospital ke emergency room me le jayein.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** Neurocysticercosis kya hota hai aur isse mirgi kaise hoti hai?
* **Assistant:** Neurocysticercosis tapeworm (pork worm) ke ando ke infection se hota hai jo gandi sabziyan khane ya adhpaka meat khane se brain me pahunch jate hain aur wahan cyst (ganth) bana lete hain. Yeh cyst brain me irritation karke mirgi ke daure trigger karti hai. Iska ilaaj Albendazole aur steroids se hota hai.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Kya mirgi ka daura aane par patient ke muh me chammach daalna chahiye?
* **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Daure ke dauran jaw muscles tight hoti hain. Chammach daalne se teeth toot sakte hain ya jaw damage ho sakta hai. Broken teeth throat me ja kar choking kar sakte hain. Muh me kuch na dalein, bas patient ko side me karwat dilayein.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Does Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY cover brain cyst and epilepsy treatments?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment packages cover diagnostic CT/MRI, urology/neurology consultations, hospital stay for status epilepticus, and neurosurgery for brain cyst removal.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Mirgi ke patient ko lifestyle me kya parhez rakhna chahiye?
* **Assistant:** Sabse main parhez sleep deprivation (neend ki kami) se bachna hai. Rojana 7-8 ghante ki deep neend lein. Flashing lights (strobe lights, video games) se bachein. Helmet ke bina bike na chalayein aur deep water ya aag ke paas akele na jayein.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Midazolam nasal spray kya hota hai aur ise kab use karte hain?
* **Assistant:** Midazolam nasal spray ek emergency medicine hai. Agar ghar par patient ko daura 2-3 minute se zyada lamba chale, to naak me iska ek spray kiya jata hai jo brain me ja kar active daure ko turant rok deta hai. Ise hamesha ghar par emergency ke liye rakhein.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Kya mirgi ke patients normal school ya college ja sakte hain?
* **Assistant:** Haan. Regular medicines khane par 70-80% patients bilkul normal rehte hain aur daure nahi aate. Wo normal school ja sakte hain, sports khel sakte hain, aur study kar sakte hain. Bas teachers ko unki condition aur first aid ke bare me bata kar rakhein.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the price of generic Sodium Valproate at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?
* **Assistant:** Generic Sodium Valproate (200mg/500mg) tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, often costing only ₹15 to ₹25 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹80+ for branded equivalents.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Kya mirgi bhag-daur ya tension lene se badh sakti hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, extreme mental stress aur tension body me stress hormones badhate hain jo brain cells ki excitability ko badha kar seizures trigger kar sakte hain. Stress relief ke liye meditation aur light exercise karein.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** What is the role of National Mental Health Programme (NMHP) in epilepsy?
* **Assistant:** The NMHP works to reduce the social stigma and discrimination faced by patients with epilepsy, educating communities that it is a treatable neurological disease and not a sign of mental instability or evil spirits.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Pregnancy plan karne se pehle mirgi ki medicine badalna kyu zaroori hai?
* **Assistant:** Kuch anti-epileptic medicines (jaise Valproate) unborn baby me birth defects (jaise neural tube defects) kar sakti hain. Isiliye pregnancy plan karne se pehle neurologist safer medicines (jaise Levetiracetam) prescribe karte hain aur folic acid supplements shuru karte hain.
