# Gland ka Cancer (Prostate Cancer) ki Pehchan aur Upachar (Prostate Cancer Symptoms, Diagnosis, and Care)

## Overview

### English
Prostate cancer is a primary malignancy that develops in the prostate gland, a small walnut-sized gland in men that produces seminal fluid. It is one of the most common cancers in aging men, typically growing very slowly and remaining confined to the gland for many years. However, some types are aggressive and spread early. Common symptoms include difficulty urinating, weak urine flow, frequent urination at night, or blood in the semen. Early detection through Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA) blood tests and Digital Rectal Exams (DRE) is key. Treatment includes active surveillance, surgery (prostatectomy), radiation, and hormone-blocking therapies.

## Quick Summary
Prostate cancer affects the prostate gland in aging men. The primary cause is genetic mutations driven by aging and family history. Common symptoms include weak urine flow, frequent nighttime urination, blood in the urine or semen, and lower back or pelvic pain. Diagnostics include Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA) blood tests, Digital Rectal Exams (DRE), transrectal ultrasound, and biopsies. Treatment involves surgery, radiation, and hormone therapies. Prevention focuses on a healthy diet and regular screening after age 50.

---

## Definition
* **Prostate Adenocarcinoma:** A malignant tumor arising from the glandular epithelial cells of the prostate, representing over 95% of all prostate cancers.
* **Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA):** A glycoprotein enzyme secreted by epithelial cells of the prostate gland; elevated levels in blood screen for prostate disorders, including cancer.
* **Radical Prostatectomy:** Surgical removal of the entire prostate gland, surrounding tissue, and seminal vesicles to cure localized prostate cancer.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The prostate gland surrounds the male urethra:
1. **Urethral Compression:** The prostate lies directly below the urinary bladder and wraps around the urethra (the tube carrying urine). As a tumor grows, it pinches the urethra, leading to urine blockage and urinary retention.
2. **Zones of the Prostate:** The prostate has a peripheral zone (where ~70-80% of cancers develop, accessible via rectal exam) and a transition zone (where benign prostate enlargement, or BPH, occurs).

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Prostate Cancer
* **Genetic Mutations:** Acquired mutations in genes regulating prostate cell growth and response to male hormones (androgens). Inherited mutations in cancer-susceptibility genes (like BRCA1, BRCA2, or Lynch syndrome genes) significantly multiply the risk.
* **Androgen Stimulation:** High levels of male sex hormones (testosterone and dihydrotestosterone - DHT) driving the division of mutated prostate cells.

### Secondary & Aging Factors
* Advanced age (the single strongest risk factor, with over 80% of cases diagnosed in men over 65).
* Family history of prostate, breast, or ovarian cancers (suggesting inherited genetic mutations).

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** A diet high in red meat and high-fat dairy products, obesity, and lack of exercise.
* **Environmental:** Exposure to heavy metals like cadmium or certain industrial chemical agents.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** Mild difficulty starting urination; a weak or slow urine flow; needing to urinate frequently, especially at night (nocturia).
* **Common Symptoms:** Frequent sudden urges to urinate; difficulty emptying the bladder completely; dribbling of urine at the end of urination; painful or burning sensation during urination or ejaculation; blood in the urine (hematuria) or semen.
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Persistent, deep bone pain in the lower back, hips, or ribs (indicates spread to bones); unexplained weight loss; leg weakness or numbness (due to tumor spreading to the spine and pressing on nerves).
* **Emergency Symptoms:** Sudden, complete inability to pass urine with severe pain (acute urinary retention); sudden loss of bowel or bladder control.

## Diagnosis

### Digital Rectal Examination (DRE) & PSA Screening
* **PSA Blood Test:** Screening test measuring PSA levels in blood. Levels above 4 ng/mL require further check, and values above 10 ng/mL strongly suggest cancer.
* **Digital Rectal Exam (DRE):** A doctor inserts a gloved, lubricated finger into the rectum to feel the back of the prostate gland, checking for hard nodules or asymmetry.

### Transrectal Ultrasound (TRUS) & Multiparametric MRI
* **TRUS:** A probe is inserted into the rectum to get detailed images of the prostate gland and guide a biopsy needle.
* **mpMRI:** High-resolution scans to locate cancer nodules within the prostate and check for spread into surrounding tissues.

### Prostate Biopsy (TRUS-Guided Biopsy)
* The gold-standard diagnostic test. A urologist extracts 10 to 12 tissue cores from different parts of the prostate using a spring-loaded needle under ultrasound guidance. The tissue is evaluated under a microscope to calculate the **Gleason Score** (grading the aggressiveness of the cancer).

## Treatment

### Lifestyle & Dietary Care

#### English
* Consume a heart-healthy diet rich in tomato-based foods (containing lycopene), soy products, and cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cabbage). Maintain a healthy weight and exercise regularly to lower testosterone spikes.

* टमाटर आधारित खाद्य पदार्थों (लाइकोपीन युक्त), सोया उत्पादों और हरी पत्तेदार सब्जियों (ब्रोकोली, गोभी) से भरपूर स्वस्थ आहार लें। वजन नियंत्रित रखें और नियमित व्यायाम करें।

### Medical Management

#### English
* **Active Surveillance:** Closely monitoring very low-risk, slow-growing cancers with regular PSA tests and DREs, delaying active treatment to avoid side effects.
* **Androgen Deprivation Therapy (ADT):** Using hormone injections or tablets to lower testosterone levels, starving the prostate cancer cells of the hormone they need to grow.

#### Hindi
* **सक्रिय निगरानी (Active Surveillance):** बहुत कम जोखिम वाले, धीमी गति से बढ़ने वाले कैंसर की नियमित पीएसए (PSA) परीक्षणों के साथ बारीकी से निगरानी करना।
* **हार्मोनल थेरेपी (ADT):** टेस्टोस्टेरोन के स्तर को कम करने के लिए इंजेक्शन या टैबलेट का उपयोग करना, जिससे प्रोस्टेट कैंसर कोशिकाओं की वृद्धि को रोका जा सके।

#### Hinglish
* **Active Surveillance:** Slow-growing prostate cancer me bina operation ke regular PSA test se monitor karte rehna taaki side effects se bacha sake.
* **Hormone Therapy (ADT):** Injection ya tablets se body me testosterone hormone ko block karna taaki cancer cells ki growth ruk sake.

### Surgical & Radiation Interventions

#### English
* **Radical Prostatectomy:** Surgical removal of the entire prostate gland, suitable for localized early-stage cancer.
* **Bilateral Orchiectomy:** Surgical removal of both testicles (the primary source of testosterone production) to treat advanced prostate cancer.
* **Radiation (External Beam & Brachytherapy):** Directing radiation beams at the prostate, or implanting tiny radioactive seeds directly inside the gland (brachytherapy).

#### Hindi
* **रेडिकल प्रोस्टेटेक्टॉमी (Prostatectomy):** पूरी प्रोस्टेट ग्रंथि को सर्जरी द्वारा हटाना (शुरुआती कैंसर के लिए उपयुक्त)।
* **द्विपक्षीय ऑर्किडेक्टॉमी (Orchiectomy):** उन्नत प्रोस्टेट कैंसर के इलाज के लिए दोनों अंडकोषों (testicles) को सर्जरी द्वारा हटाना।
* **ब्रेकीथेरेपी (Brachytherapy):** प्रोस्टेट ग्रंथि के भीतर सीधे छोटे रेडियोधर्मी बीजों (seeds) को प्रत्यारोपित करना।

#### Hinglish
* **Prostatectomy:** Early cancer me poori prostate gland ko surgically nikalna.
* **Bilateral Orchiectomy:** Advanced cancer me dono testicles (jo testosterone banate hain) ko surgically nikalna.
* **Brachytherapy:** Prostate gland ke andar radioactive seeds daal kar local radiation dena.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **LHRH Agonists / Antagonists (ADT Injections):** Shut down signals from the brain that tell the testicles to make testosterone (e.g., Leuprolide, Degarelix).
2. **Anti-Androgens:** Block testosterone from binding to receptors on prostate cancer cells (e.g., Bicalutamide, Enzalutamide).
3. **Chemotherapy Agents:** Used for hormone-resistant advanced prostate cancer (e.g., Docetaxel, Cabazitaxel).
4. **Alpha-Blockers:** Relax the neck of the bladder and prostate muscles to improve urine flow in patients with blockages (e.g., Tamsulosin).
5. **Bone-Targeted Drugs:** Strengthen bones and prevent fractures in patients whose cancer has spread to bones (e.g., Zoledronic Acid).

## Prevention

### English
* **Screening after Age 50:** Discuss with your doctor about getting a baseline PSA blood test and DRE once you reach 50 years of age.
* **Healthy Diet:** Reduce consumption of animal fats, red meat, and high-fat dairy. Increase intake of foods containing lycopene (cooked tomatoes).
* **Physical Activity:** Maintain a healthy weight and exercise for 30 minutes daily.
* **Stop Smoking:** Quit tobacco to reduce the risk of aggressive prostate cancers.

* **50 वर्ष की आयु के बाद स्क्रीनिंग:** 50 वर्ष की आयु प्राप्त करने पर पीएसए (PSA) रक्त परीक्षण और डिजिटल रेक्टल परीक्षा (DRE) कराने के बारे में डॉक्टर से चर्चा करें।

## Possible Complications
* **Urinary Incontinence:** Inability to control urine flow, leading to leaking, commonly occurring temporarily after prostatectomy surgery.
* **Erectile Dysfunction (Impotence):** Loss of ability to achieve or maintain an erection, caused by damage to the delicate nerves wrapping around the prostate during surgery or radiation.
* **Castration-Resistant Prostate Cancer (CRPC):** Advanced cancer that stops responding to hormone-blocking therapies, requiring chemotherapy.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Sudden, complete inability to pass urine accompanied by severe lower abdominal swelling and pain (Acute Retention).**
* **Sudden weakness or numbness in legs, or inability to walk (indicates spinal cord compression from bone metastasis).**
* **Sudden loss of control over bowel movements or urination.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you have persistent difficulty starting urination, a weak urine stream, or urinary dribbling.
* If you experience frequent nighttime urination (nocturia) that disrupts your sleep.
* If you notice blood in your urine or semen, or have persistent deep pain in your hips or lower back.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Prostate Cancer?**
   Prostate cancer is cancer that develops in the cells of the prostate gland, a small walnut-sized gland in men that wraps around the urethra and produces seminal fluid.

2. **What is the PSA test and what do the results mean?**
   Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA) is a protein measured in the blood. Normal levels are generally under 4 ng/mL. Elevated levels can suggest prostate cancer, BPH (benign enlargement), or infection.

3. **What is BPH and how is it different from prostate cancer?**
   BPH (Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia) is a non-cancerous enlargement of the prostate common in aging men. Prostate cancer is a malignant growth. Both cause similar urinary symptoms, but only cancer can spread.

4. **What is a Digital Rectal Exam (DRE)?**
   A DRE is a quick clinic examination where a doctor inserts a lubricated, gloved finger into the rectum to feel the back of the prostate for hard nodules or shape changes.

5. **What is the Gleason Score?**
   The Gleason Score is a grading system (ranging from 6 to 10) calculated during a prostate biopsy. A higher score indicates a more aggressive, fast-growing cancer.

6. **Why does prostate surgery cause urine leakage?**
   The prostate surrounds the urethra. During surgery to remove the prostate, the urinary sphincter muscle can be temporarily stretched or bruised, causing temporary leakage (incontinence).

7. **What is Androgen Deprivation Therapy (ADT)?**
   ADT is hormone therapy that lowers the levels of male hormones (androgens/testosterone) in the body, which starves prostate cancer cells of the hormone they need to grow.

8. **Why does prostate cancer spread to bones?**
   Prostate cancer cells can enter local veins and travel through the blood, commonly settling in the bones of the spine, pelvis, and ribs, causing severe bone pain.

9. **Can prostate cancer be cured completely?**
   Yes. If detected early in the localized stage (confined to the prostate gland), radical prostatectomy surgery or radiation therapy has a 100% cure rate.

10. **Where can I get a free PSA test in India?**
    Free baseline PSA tests and urology consultations are available at government medical colleges and district civil hospitals, covered cashless under Ayushman Bharat.

11. **What is Active Surveillance?**
    Active Surveillance is a management strategy for slow-growing, low-risk prostate cancers where active treatment is delayed, and the patient is closely monitored to avoid treatment side effects.

12. **What is Brachytherapy?**
    Brachytherapy is a type of radiation therapy where tiny radioactive seeds are implanted directly inside the prostate gland, delivering targeted radiation to destroy cancer cells locally.

13. **Why do testicles get removed to treat prostate cancer?**
    Bilateral orchiectomy (removing both testicles) is a quick surgical way to lower testosterone levels by 90-95%, which stops the growth of advanced prostate cancer.

14. **What is the difference between PSA and Free PSA?**
    PSA exists in two forms in blood: bound to proteins or free. A lower percentage of Free PSA (under 10-15%) increases the likelihood of prostate cancer.

15. **Does having BPH increase the risk of getting prostate cancer?**
    No. Having an enlarged prostate (BPH) does not increase your risk of developing prostate cancer. However, a man can have both BPH and prostate cancer at the same time.

16. **Why do doctors prescribe Tamsulosin for prostate issues?**
    Tamsulosin is an alpha-blocker. It relaxes the muscle cells in the bladder neck and prostate, widening the urethra to make it easier for patients to pass urine.

17. **What is Castration-Resistant Prostate Cancer (CRPC)?**
    CRPC is an advanced stage of prostate cancer that continues to grow and spread even when testosterone levels in the body are reduced to extremely low levels (castrate levels).

18. **Can Kegel exercises help after prostate surgery?**
    Yes. Kegel exercises strengthen the pelvic floor muscles that control urine flow. Doing them regularly after surgery significantly helps rebuild bladder control.

19. **What are the side effects of hormone therapy for prostate cancer?**
    Side effects of lowering testosterone include hot flashes, loss of muscle mass, weight gain, osteoporosis (weak bones), loss of libido, and erectile dysfunction.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover robotic prostate surgery?**
    Yes, the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme covers radical prostatectomy surgery packages, including laparoscopic urological procedures at empaneled hospitals.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Only men with symptoms like urinary blockage can have prostate cancer.
  * **Fact:** Early-stage prostate cancer usually has zero symptoms. Symptoms only appear when the tumor grows large enough to compress the urethra.
* **Myth:** High PSA levels always mean you have prostate cancer.
  * **Fact:** PSA can rise due to non-cancerous conditions like BPH, urinary tract infections (UTI), or recent cycling. Further biopsy is needed to confirm cancer.
* **Myth:** Prostate cancer only affects men over 80.
  * **Fact:** While more common in older men, men in their 50s and 60s can also develop aggressive prostate cancer, especially if they have a family history.
* **Myth:** Prostate cancer surgery always causes permanent impotence.
  * **Fact:** With modern nerve-sparing surgical techniques and medications, many men can recover erectile function over 6-12 months.
* **Myth:** All prostate cancers require immediate surgery or radiation.
  * **Fact:** Many low-risk prostate cancers grow so slowly that they never cause harm. These are safely managed with active surveillance, avoiding side effects.
* **Myth:** Drinking herbal juices can shrink an enlarged prostate.
  * **Fact:** Herbal drinks cannot shrink prostate tumors. Delaying medical care for unverified remedies can lead to complete urinary blockage or cancer spread.
* **Myth:** Prostate cancer is highly contagious and spreads to partners.
  * **Fact:** Cancer is an internal cellular genetic disease; it is not infectious.
* **Myth:** Vasectomy causes prostate cancer.
  * **Fact:** Multiple scientific studies have proven that having a vasectomy does not increase your risk of developing prostate cancer.
* **Myth:** Only testicles produce testosterone.
  * **Fact:** While testicles produce 90-95% of testosterone, the adrenal glands (located above the kidneys) produce the rest, which can also stimulate cancer cells.
* **Myth:** Prostate cancer cannot be cured if it spreads.
  * **Fact:** Even if prostate cancer has spread to bones, it can be successfully managed for many years using modern hormone therapies and chemotherapy, allowing a good quality of life.

Surgery ke baad shuruati kuch hafto me urine leak hone (incontinence) ki problem thodi aati hai kyunki sphincter muscles weak ho jati hain. Rojana pelvic floor exercises (Kegels) karne se bladder control 3-6 mahine me normal ho jata hai.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** LFT ya Kidney test ki tarah kya prostate cancer ka koi test hota hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, prostate gland ke liye main screening test PSA (Prostate-Specific Antigen) blood test hai. Agar PSA badha aata hai, to confirm diagnosis ke liye transrectal ultrasound (TRUS) and biopsy ki jati hai.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Mere relative ko achanak achanak severe pet dard ke sath peshab aana bilkul band ho gaya hai. Pet phool gaya hai.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Yeh "Acute Urinary Retention" (peshab ka achanak block hona) ka case hai jo prostate gland ke extreme pressure ke karan hota hai. Unhe bina ek minute waste kiye turant hospital ke emergency room me le jayein jahan catheter pipe daal kar peshab nikala jayega.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** Gleason Score kya hota hai aur prostate biopsy report me iska kya role hai?
* **Assistant:** Gleason Score biopsy ke tissues ko dekh kar banaya jata hai (6 se 10 scale). 6 score ka matlab slow cancer hai, jabki 8 se 10 score ka matlab aggressive aur tezi se phailne wala cancer hai. Sahi treatment plan chune me iska bohot important role hota hai.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** ADT hormone injection (Leuprolide) dene ke baad pitaji ko extreme pasina (hot flashes) aur kamzori lag rahi hai.
* **Assistant:** ADT injection body me testosterone level ko block karta hai, jisse body me "male menopause" jaise symptoms hote hain. Hot flashes (garam pasina) aur kamzori isi hormone ki kami ka normal side effect hai. Unhe calcium supplements dein, active rakhein, aur urologist ko iske bare me batayein.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Does Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY cover prostate cancer hormone therapy injections?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment packages cover diagnostic TRUS biopsy, radical prostatectomy surgery, bilateral orchiectomy, and hormonal injection therapies (like Leuprolide) at all empaneled hospitals.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Kya bilateral orchiectomy (dono testicles nikalna) se cancer theek ho jata hai?
* **Assistant:** Advanced stage prostate cancer me dono testicles ko surgically nikalne (orchiectomy) se body ka 95% testosterone banna turant band ho jata hai. Isse cancer cells ko growth hormone nahi milta aur advanced cancer control ho jata hai, jisse pain me aaram milta hai.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Prostate cancer ke patient ko lower back (kamar) me tez dard kyu rehta hai?
* **Assistant:** Agar prostate cancer local stage se badh kar phailne lagta hai, to yeh sabse pehle spine (kamar ki haddi) aur pelvis me metastasize (spread) hota hai. Kamar me tez aur deep dard isi bone metastasis ke karan ho sakta hai. Immediate doctor consult karein.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Kya prostate cancer se bachne ke liye tamatar khana safe hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, paka hua tamatar khana prostate health ke liye bohot accha hai kyunki isme "Lycopene" namak powerful antioxidant hota hai jo prostate cells ko genetic mutation se bachane me help karta hai.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the cost of prostate cancer drug Bicalutamide at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?
* **Assistant:** Generic Bicalutamide (50mg) tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, often costing only ₹30 to ₹50 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹300+ for branded versions.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Kya vasectomy (purush nasbandi) karwane se prostate cancer ka khatra badhta hai?
* **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Yeh ek galat myth hai. Scientific research me vasectomy aur prostate cancer ke beech koi bhi link nahi mila hai. Vasectomy bilkul safe family planning method hai.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** Active Surveillance kya hota hai aur kya ye dangerous hai?
* **Assistant:** Active Surveillance me slow-growing cancer ko bina operation ke close monitor kiya jata hai. Yeh dangerous nahi hai kyunki agar regular checks me cancer badhta dikhega, tabhi doctor operation ya radiation shuru karenge, tab tak side effects se bacha ja sakta hai.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Pitaji ko urologist ne Tamsulosin tablet likhi hai, ise raat ko khane ko kyu bola hai?
* **Assistant:** Tamsulosin prostate ki muscles ko relax karke peshab ki nali ko kholta hai. Isse shuruati days me achanak blood pressure low hone ya dizziness (chakkar) ka side effect ho sakta hai, isiliye ise raat ko sone se pehle khane ki salah di jati hai.
