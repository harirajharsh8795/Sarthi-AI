# Garbhashay ke Muh ka Cancer (Cervical Cancer) ki Pehchan aur Bachao (Cervical Cancer Screening, HPV, and Care)

## Overview

### English
Cervical cancer is a type of cancer that occurs in the cells of the cervix, the lower part of the uterus that connects to the vagina. It is almost exclusively caused by chronic infection with high-risk strains of the Human Papillomavirus (HPV), a common sexually transmitted virus. Cervical cancer is highly preventable through timely HPV vaccination in adolescent girls and regular screening using Pap smears or HPV DNA tests in adult women. When detected early, it is highly treatable through surgery (hysterectomy), chemotherapy, and radiation therapy.

## Quick Summary
Cervical cancer is caused by persistent high-risk HPV infection. Common symptoms include abnormal vaginal bleeding (between periods or after intercourse), foul-smelling vaginal discharge, and pelvic pain. Diagnostics include Pap smear screening, HPV DNA testing, colposcopy, and biopsies. Treatment involves surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation. Prevention focuses on the HPV vaccine for girls aged 9-14 and regular Pap smears for women over 30.

---

## Definition
* **Cervical Carcinoma:** Malignant neoplasm of the cervix uteri, arising from the squamous or glandular cells lining the transition zone of the cervix.
* **Human Papillomavirus (HPV):** A DNA virus from the papillomavirus family that infects human skin and mucosal membranes, with high-risk strains (HPV 16 and 18) linked to cervical cancers.
* **Pap Smear (Papanicolaou Test):** A screening procedure where cells are scraped from the cervix and examined under a microscope to detect pre-cancerous cellular changes (dysplasia).

---

## Anatomy / Background
The female reproductive tract includes:
1. **Cervix:** The narrow neck of the uterus that projects into the vagina. It has an ectocervix (lined by squamous cells) and an endocervix (lined by columnar cells).
2. **Transformation Zone:** The border area where squamous and columnar cells meet. Most cervical cancers start here, as this zone is highly susceptible to HPV integration.

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Cervical Cancer
* **Persistent High-Risk HPV Infection:** Integration of HPV DNA into the host genome, where viral oncogenes (E6 and E7) deactivate human tumor suppressor proteins (p53 and Rb), causing cell cycles to run uncontrolled. High-risk strains **HPV 16** and **HPV 18** cause over 70% of cases.

### Secondary & Co-Factors
* Early age of first sexual intercourse (cervical tissues are immature and highly vulnerable to viral infection).
* Having multiple sexual partners, increasing the risk of exposure to different HPV strains.

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** Active smoking (tobacco chemicals concentrate in cervical mucus, damaging cell DNA), poor personal hygiene, and lack of barrier protection (condoms) during intercourse.
* **Clinical:** Long-term use of oral contraceptive pills (more than 5 years) and immunocompromised state (e.g., HIV infection makes it difficult for the body to clear HPV).

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** Often completely asymptomatic in early stages; minor watery vaginal discharge; light spotting after sexual intercourse (post-coital bleeding).
* **Common Symptoms:** Abnormal vaginal bleeding between regular menstrual cycles; heavy, irregular menstrual bleeding; post-menopausal bleeding; persistent, foul-smelling or blood-tinged vaginal discharge; pain during sexual intercourse (dyspareunia).
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Persistent, dull pelvic pain or lower back pain; swelling in the legs (edema due to lymph blockage); difficulty or pain during urination/bowel movements; blood in urine (hematuria).
* **Emergency Symptoms:** Sudden, massive vaginal bleeding causing dizziness, rapid pulse, pale cold skin, or fainting (severe hemorrhagic shock).

## Diagnosis

### Pap Smear & HPV DNA Testing
* **Pap Smear:** A nurse or doctor scrapes cells from the cervix using a brush. The cells are sent to a lab to check for abnormal cells (dysplasia). Recommended every 3 years for women aged 21-65.
* **HPV DNA Test:** Molecular testing of cervical cells to detect the presence of high-risk HPV strains. Recommended every 5 years for women over 30.

### Colposcopy & Cervical Biopsy
* **Colposcopy:** If a Pap smear is abnormal, a doctor uses a magnifying instrument (colposcope) to look closely at the cervix, applying vinegar (acetic acid) which turns abnormal cells white.
* **Directed Biopsy:** Taking a small tissue sample from the white areas for microscopic analysis to confirm cervical cancer.

### Imaging for Staging
* **MRI & PET-CT:** Diagnostic scans to check the size of the tumor and see if it has spread to the pelvic lymph nodes, bladder, or rectum.

## Treatment

### Lifestyle & Preventive Care

#### English
* Maintain high genital hygiene. Ensure the use of condoms to prevent transmission of HPV and other STIs. Do not smoke, as nicotine weakens cervical local immunity.

#### Hindi
* जननांगों (genitals) की स्वच्छता का विशेष ध्यान रखें। एचपीवी (HPV) और अन्य यौन संचारित रोगों के संचरण को रोकने के लिए कंडोम का उपयोग सुनिश्चित करें। धूम्रपान न करें, क्योंकि निकोटीन गर्भाशय ग्रीवा की स्थानीय प्रतिरक्षा को कमजोर करता है।

### Surgical & Radiation Interventions

#### English
* **Cone Biopsy (Conization):** Removing a cone-shaped piece of tissue containing abnormal cells to treat pre-cancerous stages.
* **Hysterectomy:** Surgical removal of the uterus and cervix to treat early-stage cervical cancer.
* **Radiation (External Beam & Brachytherapy):** Directing radiation beams at the pelvis, or placing radioactive sources directly inside the vagina near the cervix (brachytherapy).

#### Hindi
* **कोन बायोप्सी (Conization):** पूर्व-कैंसर (pre-cancerous) चरणों के इलाज के लिए गर्भाशय ग्रीवा से असामान्य कोशिकाओं वाले ऊतक का एक शंक्वाकार टुकड़ा निकालना।
* **हिस्टेरेक्टॉमी (Hysterectomy):** शुरुआती स्तन/गर्भाशय ग्रीवा के कैंसर के इलाज के लिए गर्भाशय (uterus) और गर्भाशय ग्रीवा (cervix) को सर्जरी द्वारा हटाना।
* **ब्रेकीथेरेपी (Brachytherapy):** पेल्विस पर विकिरण केंद्रित करना, या गर्भाशय ग्रीवा के ठीक पास योनि के भीतर सीधे रेडियोधर्मी स्रोतों को रखना।

#### Hinglish
* **Cone Biopsy:** Pre-cancerous stage me cervix se abnormal tissue ka cone-shape piece surgically nikalna.
* **Hysterectomy:** Early cancer stage me bacchedani (uterus) aur cervix ko surgically nikalna.
* **Brachytherapy:** Vagina ke andar cervix ke thik paas radioactive source rakh kar direct radiation dena.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **HPV Vaccines:** Recombinant vaccines that prevent infection against primary high-risk HPV strains (e.g., Gardasil 9, Cervavac).
2. **Chemotherapy Alkylating Agents:** Destroy cancer cell DNA (e.g., Cisplatin, Carboplatin).
3. **Targeted Therapy (VEGF Inhibitors):** Block blood vessel growth to the tumor, starving it (e.g., Bevacizumab).
4. **Antifungals & Antibiotics:** Prescribed clinically to treat vaginal superinfections during radiation therapy.
5. **Hemostatics:** Medications used to reduce heavy uterine bleeding in advanced cervical cancer patients (e.g., Tranexamic Acid).

## Prevention

### English
* **HPV Vaccination:** Administer the HPV vaccine to girls aged 9 to 14 years (2 doses). Cervavac is India's first indigenous, affordable HPV vaccine.
* **Regular Screening:** All women over 30 should undergo a Pap smear test every 3 years or an HPV DNA test every 5 years.
* **Practice Safe Sex:** Consistently use condoms to reduce HPV transmission.
* **Avoid Smoking:** Quit tobacco to prevent carcinogens from concentrating in cervical fluids.

## Possible Complications
* **Hemorrhagic Shock:** Life-threatening blood loss from tumor invasion into major uterine arteries.
* **Renal Failure (Uremia):** Advanced tumor blocking the ureters (the tubes from kidneys to bladder), stopping urine flow and causing kidney shutdown.
* **Vaginal Fistulas:** Abnormal tunnels forming between the vagina and bladder or rectum due to tumor damage or radiation, causing urine/stool leakage.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Sudden, heavy vaginal bleeding that completely soaks multiple pads in an hour.**
* **Severe, sharp lower abdominal pain with inability to pass urine for over 24 hours.**
* **Sudden cold sweat, dizziness, rapid shallow breathing, or fainting.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you experience unusual vaginal bleeding between your periods or after sexual intercourse.
* If you notice vaginal bleeding after you have already gone through menopause.
* If you have persistent, foul-smelling or blood-tinged vaginal discharge.

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Programme for Prevention and Control of Non-Communicable Diseases (NPCDCS):** Conducts free cervical cancer screening (Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid - VIA) at government Health and Wellness Centers (HWCs).
* **Indigenous HPV Vaccine Initiative:** The Indian government is rolling out Cervavac (developed by Serum Institute of India) to be included in the national immunization schedule for schoolgirls.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless treatments for cervical cancer, including diagnostic colposcopy, biopsy, hysterectomy surgeries, chemoradiation, and brachytherapy packages.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Cervical Cancer?**
   Cervical cancer is cancer that starts in the cervix, the lower part of the uterus that connects to the vagina, almost always caused by persistent high-risk HPV infection.

2. **What is HPV and how does it cause cervical cancer?**
   Human Papillomavirus (HPV) is a common sexually transmitted virus. High-risk strains (HPV 16 and 18) integrate into the DNA of cervical cells, causing abnormal cell growth that can become cancer over 10-20 years.

3. **What is a Pap smear test?**
   A Pap smear is a screening test where cells are gently scraped from the cervix and checked under a microscope to detect pre-cancerous changes before they become cancer.

4. **Is cervical cancer contagious?**
   No, the cancer itself is not contagious. However, the HPV virus that causes it is highly contagious and spreads through sexual contact.

5. **At what age should girls get the HPV vaccine?**
   The HPV vaccine is recommended for girls aged 9 to 14 years, requiring 2 doses. It is most effective when given before the onset of sexual activity.

6. **What is Cervavac?**
   Cervavac is India's first indigenous quadrivalent HPV vaccine, manufactured by the Serum Institute of India, protecting against HPV strains 6, 11, 16, and 18 at an affordable price.

7. **How often should women get screened for cervical cancer?**
   Women aged 21-65 should get a Pap smear every 3 years. Women over 30 can switch to HPV DNA testing every 5 years or co-testing (Pap + HPV) every 5 years.

8. **What are the early symptoms of cervical cancer?**
   Early cervical cancer usually has no symptoms. The first signs may include watery vaginal discharge and light bleeding after sexual intercourse (post-coital bleeding).

9. **Can a woman get cervical cancer after a hysterectomy?**
   If a total hysterectomy was done (removing both uterus and cervix) for a benign condition, the risk is extremely low. However, if the cervix was left behind (partial hysterectomy), cancer can still occur.

10. **Where can I get free cervical cancer screening in India?**
    Free screening using VIA (Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid) is available for women over 30 at government community Health and Wellness Centers (HWCs) and district hospitals.

11. **Why does smoking increase the risk of cervical cancer?**
    Tobacco byproducts concentrate in the cervical mucus, damaging the DNA of cervical cells and weakening the local immune system's ability to fight off HPV infection.

12. **What is Brachytherapy?**
    Brachytherapy is a type of radiation therapy where a radioactive source is placed directly inside or very close to the tumor (in the vagina/cervix), delivering high-dose radiation directly to cancer cells.

13. **Is the HPV vaccine safe?**
    Yes. Extensive clinical trials and global monitoring of millions of doses show that the HPV vaccine is extremely safe, with minor side effects like mild pain at the injection site.

14. **Can condoms completely prevent HPV transmission?**
    Condoms reduce the risk of HPV transmission significantly, but they do not offer 100% protection because HPV can infect skin areas not covered by a condom.

15. **What does a Pap smear result of "LSIL" or "HSIL" mean?**
    LSIL (Low-grade Squamous Intraepithelial Lesion) suggests mild cell changes, usually cleared by the body. HSIL (High-grade) suggests severe pre-cancerous changes that require a colposcopy and treatment.

16. **Why does cervical cancer cause kidney failure in late stages?**
    Advanced cervical tumors can grow sideways and compress the ureters (the tubes carrying urine from kidneys to bladder), blocking urine flow and causing kidney failure (uremia).

17. **What is a colposcopy?**
    A colposcopy is a detailed examination of the cervix using a special magnifying lens (colposcope). The doctor applies acetic acid (vinegar) to make abnormal areas turn white for a biopsy.

18. **Can a woman get pregnant after cervical cancer treatment?**
    Standard treatments like hysterectomy or pelvic radiation destroy fertility. However, in very early stages, fertility-sparing surgeries (like trachelectomy) may preserve the uterus for pregnancy.

19. **What is the difference between cervical cancer and uterine (endometrial) cancer?**
    Cervical cancer starts at the neck of the uterus (cervix) and is linked to HPV. Uterine cancer starts in the inner lining of the uterus (endometrium) and is linked to hormonal imbalances.

20. **Can older women get the HPV vaccine?**
    Women up to age 26 can get catch-up vaccination. For women aged 27-45, the vaccine is less effective because they have likely already been exposed to HPV, but it can be discussed with a doctor.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Cervical cancer is a hereditary disease passed down from mothers.
  * **Fact:** Cervical cancer is not genetic. It is caused by chronic infection with sexually transmitted HPV virus strains.
* **Myth:** Only women who have multiple sexual partners get cervical cancer.
  * **Fact:** Even a woman with only one lifetime partner can get HPV and develop cervical cancer if her partner was exposed to the virus.
* **Myth:** The HPV vaccine causes infertility in girls.
  * **Fact:** This is a false rumor. Numerous international studies have proven that the HPV vaccine does not affect fertility and is completely safe.
* **Myth:** Pap smear is a highly painful surgery.
  * **Fact:** A Pap smear is a quick, non-surgical clinic procedure. It may cause a mild scraping sensation or discomfort for a few seconds, but it is not painful.
* **Myth:** If you have no vaginal pain, you cannot have cervical cancer.
  * **Fact:** Early cervical cancer is completely painless. Pain only occurs in advanced stages when the tumor spreads to nerves or bones.
* **Myth:** Cervical cancer cannot be cured.
  * **Fact:** If detected early in the pre-cancerous or early localized stage through Pap smears, cervical cancer has a cure rate of nearly 100%.
* **Myth:** Condoms provide 100% protection against HPV.
  * **Fact:** Condoms reduce the risk significantly, but HPV can infect surrounding skin not covered by a condom.
* **Myth:** The HPV vaccine is a cure for existing cervical cancer.
  * **Fact:** The vaccine only prevents future infection; it cannot cure an active HPV infection or treat established cervical cancer.
* **Myth:** Vaginal douching after sex washes away the HPV virus.
  * **Fact:** Douching does not wash away viruses; instead, it irritates the vaginal lining and pushes bacteria/viruses deeper, increasing infection risk.
* **Myth:** Bleeding after menopause is just normal menstrual resetting.
  * **Fact:** Any bleeding after menopause is abnormal and must be immediately investigated for uterine or cervical cancer.

* **Assistant:** Periods ke beech me bleeding aur post-coital spotting (sex ke baad blood aana) Cervical Cancer ke lakshan ho sakte hain. Aapko bina deri kiye gynecologist se consult karni chahiye aur ek Pap Smear test aur pelvis ultrasound karana chahiye.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** Pap Smear test kya hota hai aur iski report kitne din me aati hai?
* **Assistant:** Pap Smear test me gynecologist cervix se cells ka sample lekar lab me check karne bhejti hain taaki pre-cancerous cells ka pata lagaya sake. Iski report aamtaur par 3 se 5 din me aa jati hai.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Meri maa ko menopause huye 5 saal ho gaye hain, par kal unhe achanak bleeding shuru hui.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Menopause ke baad kisi bhi tarah ki bleeding hona bilkul abnormal hai aur yeh uterine ya cervical cancer ka shuruati lakshan ho sakta hai. Unhe bina ek bhi din gawaye gynecologist ke paas le jayein aur transvaginal ultrasound aur biopsy karayein.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** VIA screening kya hoti hai aur ye kaha hoti hai?
* **Assistant:** VIA (Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid) screening me doctor cervix par dilute vinegar lagate hain, jisse abnormal cells white dikhne lagti hain. Yeh screening government Health and Wellness Centers (HWCs) par BPL women ke liye free hoti hai.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Kya Pap smear me LSIL aane ka matlab confirm cancer hai?
* **Assistant:** Nahi. LSIL (Low-grade Squamous Intraepithelial Lesion) ka matlab pre-cancerous cells me halka badlaav hai. Yeh confirm cancer nahi hai aur aamtaur par body ka immune system ise khud theek kar deta hai. Pushti ke liye doctor colposcopy aur repeat pap smear ki salah dete hain.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Does Ayushman Bharat cover the cost of cervical cancer radiation therapy?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment benefits are provided for gynecological surgery (hysterectomy), chemotherapy, external beam radiation, and brachytherapy at all government and empaneled hospitals.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Breachytherapy (internal radiation) kaise di jati hai?
* **Assistant:** Brachytherapy me radiation dene wale tools (applicators) ko direct vagina ke andar cervix ke paas rakha jata hai. Isse tumor par high-dose radiation target hoti hai aur aas-pass ke organs (bladder/rectum) safe rehte hain.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Kya bacchedani nikalne (hysterectomy) ke baad mahilayein pregnant ho sakti hain?
* **Assistant:** Nahi. Hysterectomy me bacchedani (uterus) aur cervix ko surgically nikal diya jata hai, jiske baad pregnancy hona bilkul impossible ho jata hai. Agar patient young hai, to doctor fertility-sparing options discuss kar sakte hain.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Condom use karne se kya HPV transmission bilkul ruk jata hai?
* **Assistant:** Condom use karne se HPV ka risk kafi hadd tak kam ho jata hai, lekin 100% nahi rukta. Kyunki HPV skin-to-skin contact se phailta hai aur yoni ke aas-pass ki skin jo condom se cover nahi hoti, wahan se bhi virus transmit ho sakta hai.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the toll-free national cancer helpline number?
* **Assistant:** You can contact the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare toll-free national health helpline at 1800-180-1104 to get information about government cancer hospitals, free screening camps, and financial help.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Mujhe yoni se lagatar badboodar peela paani (discharge) aa raha hai. Kya ye normal hai?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, vagina se lagatar badboodar, peela, ya blood-mixed discharge aana vaginal infection ya cervical inflammation/cancer ka sign ho sakta hai. Aapko gynecologist se milkar vaginal swab aur examination karana chahiye.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** Cervical cancer me kidney kyu fail ho jati hai?
* **Assistant:** Advanced stage me cervical tumor fail kar ureters (kidneys se bladder tak jaane wali pipes) ko block kar deta hai. Isse urine kidney me block ho jata hai (hydronephrosis), jisse kidneys fail ho jati hain aur creatinine badh jata hai.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Vagina se achanak bohot heavy bleeding ho rahi hai aur chakkar aa raha hai. Kya karu?
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Yeh severe blood loss (hemorrhagic shock) ka sanket hai. Patient ko turant litayein, pairon ke niche takiya rakh kar unhe upar uthayein, aur bina ek minute gawaye 108 call karke emergency hospital le jayein.
