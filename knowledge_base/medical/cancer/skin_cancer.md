# Tvacha ka Cancer (Skin Cancer) ki Pehchan aur Upachar (Skin Cancer Symptoms, Diagnosis, and Care)

## Overview

### English
Skin cancer is the abnormal growth of skin cells, most commonly developing on skin exposed to the sun's ultraviolet (UV) radiation. The three major types are Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC, slow-growing and localized), Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC, which can spread), and Melanoma (the most aggressive and deadly form starting in pigment-producing melanocytes). Early detection is critical, guided by monitoring changes in moles using the ABCDE rule. Treatment options include surgical excision, Mohs micrographic surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, and immunotherapy. Protecting the skin with sunscreen (SPF 30+) is the primary method of prevention.

## Quick Summary
Skin cancer manifests primarily as Basal Cell, Squamous Cell, or Melanoma. The leading cause is sun UV radiation exposure damaging cell DNA. Common symptoms include a changing mole, a pearly bump, or a non-healing skin sore. Diagnostics include clinical dermoscopy, skin biopsies, and staging scans. Treatment involves surgical excision, Mohs surgery, and immunotherapy. Prevention focuses on sunscreen, sun protection, and skin checks.

---

## Definition
* **Melanoma:** A highly malignant tumor of melanocytes (pigment-producing cells), which can metastasize rapidly to other organs if not caught early.
* **Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC):** The most common skin cancer, arising from basal cells in the deepest layer of the epidermis, presenting as a pearly or waxy bump.
* **Mohs Micrographic Surgery:** A precise surgical technique where thin layers of cancer-containing skin are progressively removed and examined under a microscope until only cancer-free tissue remains.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The skin protects the body and contains pigment cells:
1. **Epidermis (Outer Layer):** Contains squamous cells on the surface, basal cells below them, and **melanocytes** scattered throughout. Melanocytes produce melanin, which gives skin its color and absorbs UV rays.
2. **UV DNA Damage:** UV radiation penetrates the epidermis, breaking the DNA bonds in these cells. If the body cannot repair this damage, the cells mutate and divide uncontrollably, forming skin cancer.

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Skin Cancer
* **Ultraviolet (UV) Radiation:** Exposure to UVA and UVB rays from sunlight. UV rays damage the cellular DNA of squamous, basal, or melanocyte cells, triggering oncogenic mutations.
* **Tanning Beds:** Use of artificial UV radiation sources for skin tanning, which delivers highly concentrated UV rays, multiplying melanoma risks.

### Secondary & Environmental Factors
* Exposure to chemical carcinogens, particularly arsenic (sometimes found in contaminated drinking water or industrial settings).
* Having fair skin, light hair, and blue/green eyes, which means less protective melanin in the skin.

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** Working outdoors in intense sunlight without sun protection (farmers, construction workers), and history of severe, blistering sunburns during childhood.
* **Clinical:** Inherited genetic conditions like Xeroderma Pigmentosum (where cells cannot repair any UV damage), having a large number of moles (more than 50), and a weakened immune system.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** A new, unusual spot or mole on the skin; an existing mole that changes size, shape, or color; a sore that bleeds, heals, and then bleeds again.
* **Common Symptoms (BCC & SCC):** A pearly or waxy bump on the face or neck; a flat, flesh-colored or brown scar-like lesion; a firm, red nodule; a flat lesion with a rough, scaly, or crusted surface (SCC).
* **Melanoma Symptoms (ABCDE Rule):**
   * **A - Asymmetry:** One half of the mole does not match the other half.
   * **B - Border:** The edges are irregular, ragged, notched, or blurred.
   * **C - Color:** The color is not uniform and has shades of black, brown, red, or blue.
   * **D - Diameter:** The spot is larger than 6 mm (size of a pencil eraser).
   * **E - Evolving:** The mole is changing in size, shape, color, or starts itching/bleeding.
* **Emergency Symptoms:** Rapidly spreading skin lesion with severe swelling, warmth, and dark black discoloration (aggressive melanoma or secondary tissue death).

## Diagnosis

### Dermatoscopy & Skin Mapping
* A dermatologist uses a handheld magnifying device (dermatoscope) to check the pigment patterns and vascular structures of suspicious moles.

### Skin Biopsy (Punch, Shave, or Excision Biopsy)
* The gold-standard diagnostic test. A doctor numbs the skin and removes the suspicious mole or lesion completely (excision) or takes a small core sample (punch) to examine under a microscope to confirm cancer.

### Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy (SLNB)
* For melanoma cases, a dye is injected to locate the first lymph node where cancer cells would spread. This node is removed and checked under a microscope to see if the cancer has spread.

## Treatment

### Lifestyle & Sun Protection

#### English
* Apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen (SPF 30+) daily 20 minutes before stepping out in the sun. Wear protective clothing (wide-brimmed hats, full sleeves, sunglasses). Avoid outdoor activities during peak sun hours (10 AM to 4 PM).

* धूप में बाहर निकलने से 20 मिनट पहले दैनिक रूप से ब्रॉड-स्पेक्ट्रम सनस्क्रीन (SPF 30+) लगाएं। सुरक्षात्मक कपड़े (चौड़ी रीम वाली टोपी, पूरी आस्तीन के कपड़े, धूप का चश्मा) पहनें। दोपहर की तेज धूप (सुबह 10 बजे से शाम 4 बजे तक) में बाहर जाने से बचें।

#### English

### Medical Management

#### English
* Topical chemotherapy creams (like 5-Fluorouracil) for early, superficial skin cancers. Targeted drug therapies (like BRAF inhibitors) for advanced melanomas. Immunotherapy to stimulate the immune system to target skin cancer cells.

#### Hindi
* शुरुआती, सतही त्वचा कैंसर के लिए स्थानीय कीमोथेरेपी क्रीम (जैसे 5-Fluorouracil)। उन्नत मेलानोमा के लिए लक्षित दवाएं (BRAF inhibitors)। त्वचा कैंसर कोशिकाओं को नष्ट करने के लिए इम्यूनोथेरेपी।

#### Hinglish
* Early, skin ki surface wale cancer me local chemotherapy creams (jaise 5-Fluorouracil) lagana. Advanced melanoma me targeted medicines (tablets like Vemurafenib) dena. Cancer cells ko marne ke liye immunotherapy injections dena.

### Surgical & Radiation Interventions

#### English
* **Surgical Excision:** Cutting out the cancerous tumor along with a safe margin of surrounding healthy skin under local anesthesia.
* **Mohs Micrographic Surgery:** Removing the tumor layer by layer and checking each layer immediately under a microscope, preserving the maximum amount of healthy skin (ideal for face, nose, and eyelids).
* **Cryosurgery:** Freezing and destroying early, superficial skin lesions using liquid nitrogen.

#### Hindi
* **सर्जिकल निष्कासन (Excision):** स्थानीय संज्ञाहरण (local anesthesia) के तहत कैंसरग्रस्त ट्यूमर को उसके आसपास की थोड़ी सी स्वस्थ त्वचा के साथ काटकर बाहर निकालना।
* **मोह्स सर्जरी (Mohs Surgery):** ट्यूमर को परत-दर-परत हटाना और तुरंत माइक्रोस्कोप के नीचे जांच करना, ताकि चेहरे पर स्वस्थ त्वचा को अधिकतम बचाया जा सके।
* **क्रायोसर्जरी (Cryosurgery):** तरल नाइट्रोजन (liquid nitrogen) का उपयोग करके शुरुआती, सतही घावों को जमाकर नष्ट करना।

#### Hinglish
* **Surgical Excision:** Local anesthesia dekar cancer tumor ko thode healthy tissue ke sath surgically nikalna.
* **Mohs Surgery:** Face, nose ya eyelids par se tumor ko layer-by-layer nikal kar turant microscope me check karna, isse healthy skin safe rehti hai.
* **Cryosurgery:** Liquid nitrogen ki madad se superficial (upari) skin lesion ko freeze karke destroy karna.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **Topical Chemotherapeutics:** Kill cancer cells in the top layers of skin when applied as a cream (e.g., 5-Fluorouracil cream, Imiquimod cream).
2. **BRAF/MEK Inhibitors (Targeted):** Block the mutated BRAF gene pathway found in many melanomas (e.g., Vemurafenib, Dabrafenib).
3. **Immunotherapy PD-1 Inhibitors:** Help T-cells recognize and destroy melanoma cells (e.g., Pembrolizumab, Nivolumab).
4. **CTLA-4 Inhibitors (Immunotherapy):** Stimulate immune response against advanced cancers (e.g., Ipilimumab).
5. **Local Antiseptics:** Ointments used post-surgery to prevent bacterial infections in open wounds.

## Prevention

### English
* **Use Broad-Spectrum Sunscreen:** Apply SPF 30+ daily, even on cloudy days, and reapply every 2 hours if swimming or sweating.
* **Avoid Peak Sun:** Stay in the shade between 10 AM and 4 PM when UV rays are strongest.
* **Wear Protective Gear:** Use wide-brimmed hats, UV-blocking sunglasses, and tightly woven clothing.
* **Avoid Tanning Beds:** Completely avoid artificial UV tanning booths.
* **Perform Self-Exams:** Monitor your skin monthly using the ABCDE rule.

## Possible Complications
* **Metastasis:** Melanoma spreading through blood and lymph vessels to the brain, lungs, liver, or bones, causing advanced organ failure.
* **Disfigurement:** Scarring or tissue loss on the face, nose, or ears after large surgical removal of skin tumors.
* **Recurrence:** Skin cancer returning in the same area after treatment, requiring more aggressive surgery.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **A mole that starts bleeding heavily or oozing pus, accompanied by rapid swelling and severe pain.**
* **Sudden severe confusion, fits, or loss of consciousness in a patient diagnosed with melanoma (suggests brain metastasis).**
* **High fever with chills and severe pain in a surgical wound.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you have a mole that is changing in size, shape, color, or is asymmetrical.
* If a mole starts itching, bleeding, or becomes painful.
* If you have a sore or scab on your skin that does not heal after 3-4 weeks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Melanoma?**
   Melanoma is the most aggressive and dangerous type of skin cancer, starting in the pigment-producing cells called melanocytes, which can spread quickly if not treated early.

2. **What is the ABCDE rule for checking moles?**
   It is a guide to identify warning signs of melanoma: A (Asymmetry), B (Irregular Border), C (Color variation), D (Diameter > 6mm), and E (Evolving or changing over time).

3. **What is the difference between BCC and SCC?**
   Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC) is slow-growing, rarely spreads, and looks like a pearly bump. Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) grows faster, can spread to lymph nodes, and looks like a firm red nodule or scaly patch.

4. **Why is UV radiation the main cause of skin cancer?**
   Ultra Violet (UV) rays from the sun penetrate skin cells and damage their DNA. If this damage accumulates, cells mutate and grow uncontrollably, leading to cancer.

5. **What is Mohs surgery?**
   Mohs surgery is a highly precise technique where a surgeon removes skin cancer layer by layer, checking each layer under a microscope immediately, preserving maximum healthy skin.

6. **Can people with dark skin get skin cancer?**
   Yes. While dark skin has more protective melanin, skin cancer can still develop, often in less sun-exposed areas like under the fingernails, on the palms, or soles of the feet.

7. **What is a skin biopsy?**
   A skin biopsy is a procedure where a doctor numbs the skin and removes a sample of a suspicious lesion or the entire mole to examine it under a microscope to confirm cancer.

8. **How does sunscreen prevent skin cancer?**
   Sunscreen contains chemical or physical barriers that absorb or reflect harmful UVA and UVB rays, preventing them from damaging the DNA of skin cells.

9. **What does a pearly bump on the face indicate?**
   A shiny, pearly or waxy bump on sun-exposed areas like the nose or face is a classic warning sign of Basal Cell Carcinoma and requires a dermatologist checkup.

10. **Where can BPL cardholders get free skin cancer surgery in India?**
    Eligible BPL families can receive cashless skin biopsy, Mohs surgery, or excision surgery under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme at empaneled hospitals.

11. **Why do tanning beds increase skin cancer risk?**
    Tanning beds emit high-intensity UV radiation that causes direct DNA damage to skin cells, significantly increasing the risk of melanoma, especially in young users.

12. **What is a Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy?**
    It is a test done in melanoma cases to find and remove the first lymph node where cancer is likely to spread, checking if the cancer has metastasized.

13. **Is skin cancer contagious?**
    No, skin cancer is not contagious. You cannot catch it by touching someone, sharing towels, or caring for a skin cancer patient.

14. **Why is chemotherapy cream used for skin cancer?**
    For very early, superficial cancers, applying a chemotherapy cream (like 5-Fluorouracil) directly to the skin kills cancer cells locally without needing surgery.

15. **Can a sunburn cause skin cancer years later?**
    Yes. Severe, blistering sunburns during childhood or youth double the risk of developing melanoma later in life because the DNA damage remains in the cells.

16. **What is a wide local excision?**
    It is a surgical procedure to remove a skin cancer tumor along with a safe margin of surrounding healthy skin to ensure no cancer cells are left behind.

17. **Can skin cancer spread to the brain?**
    Yes. Aggressive melanoma can metastasize through the blood and spread to other organs, most commonly the brain, lungs, and liver.

18. **What SPF sunscreen is recommended?**
    Dermatologists recommend using a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF (Sun Protection Factor) of 30 or higher daily.

19. **What is Cryosurgery?**
    Cryosurgery is a treatment that uses extreme cold (liquid nitrogen) to freeze and destroy early, pre-cancerous skin lesions.

20. **Can skin cancer be cured completely?**
    Yes. If detected early, skin cancers like BCC and SCC have a cure rate of over 95% to 99% with simple surgery. Even melanoma has a high cure rate if caught before spreading.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Skin cancer only affects people with very fair skin.
  * **Fact:** While more common in fair skin, anyone can get skin cancer, including people with dark skin, often on palms, soles, or under nails.
* **Myth:** You do not need sunscreen on cloudy or winter days.
  * **Fact:** Up to 80% of UV rays can penetrate clouds and reflect off concrete or water, causing skin damage even on cloudy days.
* **Myth:** If a mole is not painful, it is not cancer.
  * **Fact:** Early-stage skin cancers and melanomas are completely painless. Pain is rarely a symptom of early skin cancer.
* **Myth:** A base tan from a tanning bed protects you from sunburn and cancer.
  * **Fact:** A tan is a sign of skin injury. Tanning beds emit concentrated UV rays that damage DNA, increasing cancer risk.
* **Myth:** Natural oils like coconut oil can act as sunscreen.
  * **Fact:** Coconut oil offers an SPF of only 2-8, which is not enough to block harmful UV rays. Always use broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30+.
* **Myth:** Skin cancer is highly contagious.
  * **Fact:** Cancer is an internal cellular genetic disease; it is not infectious.
* **Myth:** If you remove a mole, the cancer will spread faster.
  * **Fact:** Removing a suspicious mole (excisional biopsy) is a safe and necessary medical step to diagnose and cure skin cancer.
* **Myth:** Only elderly people get skin cancer.
  * **Fact:** Melanoma is one of the most common cancers diagnosed in young adults under 30, especially young women due to sun exposure and tanning.
* **Myth:** Sunscreen chemicals cause more cancer than the sun.
  * **Fact:** Numerous scientific studies have proven that approved sunscreens are safe and highly effective at preventing skin cancer.
* **Myth:** Skin cancer only develops on sun-exposed areas.
  * **Fact:** Skin cancer can develop anywhere on the body, including covered areas like the buttocks, soles of feet, or under toenails.

### Conversation 1

* **Assistant:** Til ka size badhna aur uske kinare irregular (irregular borders) hona Melanoma (skin cancer) ka shuruati lakshan ho sakta hai (ABCDE rule). Aapko bina deri kiye kisi dermatologist ko ise dikhana chahiye aur skin biopsy karani chahiye. Jab tak checkup na ho, use touch ya scratching na karein.

### Conversation 2
* **User:** What is the cost of Mohs surgery for skin cancer in India?
* **Assistant:** In private clinics, Mohs surgery can cost between ₹40,000 to ₹80,000 depending on the complexity. However, it is performed free of cost and covered cashless under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme at government medical colleges.

### Conversation 3
* **User:** Meri nose par ek pearly chamakdar bump ho gaya hai jo dheere-dheere badh raha hai.
* **Assistant:** Nose ya face par pearly (moti jaisa) chamakdar bump Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC - common skin cancer) ka sign ho sakta hai. Yeh slow-growing hota hai par ise surgically nikalna zaroori hota hai taaki nose ke tissues kharab na hon. Aap dermatologist se checkup karayein.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** LFT test ki tarah kya skin cancer ka koi blood test hota hai?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, skin cancer ke liye koi specific screening blood test nahi hota. Iski diagnosis clinical dermoscopy (magnifying lens checkup) aur suspected lesion ki skin biopsy se hi ki jati hai.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Mere surgical excision wound ke paas achanak red lines aa gayi hain aur tez bukhar hai.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Surgery wale ghaw ke paas achanak redness phailna aur tez bukhar aana severe bacterial infection (cellulitis/sepsis) ka sign hai. Aap bina deri kiye turant hospital ke emergency room me dikhayein taaki IV antibiotics shuru ho sakein.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** ABCDE rule kya hota hai til check karne ke liye?
* **Assistant:** ABCDE rule se til check kiya jata hai: A (Asymmetry - dono side match na hona), B (Border - irregular kinare), C (Color - alag-alag rang), D (Diameter - 6mm se bada), aur E (Evolving - lagatar badlaav ya khujli).

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Kya dark skin wale logo ko sunscreen lagana zaroori hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, dark skin me melanin zyada hota hai jo thoda protection deta hai, par unhe bhi UV damage aur skin cancer ka risk hota hai (khaskar palms aur soles me). Isiliye sabhi ko SPF 30+ sunscreen daily lagana chahiye.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Does Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY cover skin biopsy and Mohs surgery?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment benefits are provided for diagnostic dermoscopy, punch/excision biopsy, radical excision, Mohs surgery, and reconstruction procedures.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Tanning beds use karne se cancer ka risk kyu badhta hai?
* **Assistant:** Tanning beds me high-intensity artificial UV rays use hoti hain jo direct skin cells ke DNA ko damage karti hain. Isse melanoma ka risk 75% tak badh jata hai, isiliye inka use bilkul na karein.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Skin cancer biopsy reports me "clean margins" ka kya matlab hai?
* **Assistant:** Clean margins का matlab hai ki surgery ke dauran nikale gaye tissue ke kinaro par koi cancer cells nahi mile hain, yaani ki tumor poori tarah se bahar nikal diya gaya hai aur phailne ka risk bohot kam hai.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Kya sunscreen lagane ke baad hum dhoop me jitna chahein reh sakte hain?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, sunscreen UV rays se protect karta hai par 100% block nahi karta. Sunscreen lagane ke baad bhi peak sun hours (10 AM se 4 PM) me direct dhoop me lambe samay tak rehne se bachein, aur har 2 ghante me ise reapply karein.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the cost of generic sunscreen creams at Jan Aushadhi stores?
* **Assistant:** PM Jan Aushadhi stores offer generic dermatological bases and skin protectants at highly subsidized rates, costing around ₹20 to ₹40 per tube compared to expensive branded sunscreens.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Mere relative ko melanoma hai aur wo achanak behosh ho gaye hain aur fits (doure) pad rahe hain.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Melanoma agar body me phailta hai to yeh brain me metastasize ho jata hai. Unhe turant emergency hospital le jayein jahan brain MRI aur emergency treatment shuru kiya ja sake.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** 5-Fluorouracil cream kya hoti hai aur ise kaise lagayein?
* **Assistant:** 5-Fluorouracil ek local chemotherapy cream hai jo early, superficial skin cancer me di jati hai. Ise doctor ke bataye gaye tareeke se glove pehan kar lagayein. Yeh skin par redness aur jalan kar sakti hai jo ki iske normal side effects hain.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Kya narial tel (coconut oil) ko sunscreen ki tarah use kar sakte hain?
* **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Coconut oil ka SPF sirf 2 se 8 hota hai jo dhoop se bachane ke liye bilkul naafi hai. Sunburn aur cancer se bachne ke liye hamesha SPF 30+ broad-spectrum sunscreen hi lagayein.
