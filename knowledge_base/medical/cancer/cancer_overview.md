# Cancer ki Samanya Jankari aur Bachao (General Cancer Awareness, Symptoms, and Prevention)

## Overview

### English
Cancer is a broad term for a large group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled division of abnormal cells that can invade and destroy surrounding tissues. It can originate in almost any organ or tissue of the body. Cancers are classified as either benign (non-cancerous, localized) or malignant (cancerous, capable of spreading to other organs via metastasis). The primary modes of cancer treatment include surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, immunotherapy, and targeted drug therapies. Early detection through screening (such as PAP smears, mammography, and oral screening) is key to successful treatment.

## Quick Summary
Cancer is defined by uncontrolled cellular mutations and potential metastasis. Primary types include carcinomas, sarcomas, leukemias, and lymphomas. Common early warning signs include unexplained weight loss, persistent fever, chronic cough, and changes in skin moles. Diagnostics include biopsies, CT/PET scans, and blood tumor markers. Treatments consist of surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation. Prevention emphasizes avoiding tobacco, alcohol, and processed meats, while getting vaccinated for HPV and Hepatitis B.

---

## Definition
* **Malignancy:** A term for diseases in which abnormal cells divide without control and can invade nearby tissues and spread through the blood and lymph systems.
* **Metastasis:** The spread of cancer cells from the place where they first formed to another part of the body.
* **Carcinogen:** Any substance, radionuclide, or radiation that is directly involved in causing cancer by damaging cellular DNA.

---

## Pathology / Background
Cancer begins with changes (mutations) in the DNA of a cell:
1. **Loss of Cell Cycle Control:** Normal cells grow, divide, and die in a controlled manner (apoptosis). Cancer cells bypass these regulatory checkpoints and divide indefinitely.
2. **Angiogenesis:** Cancer tumors stimulate the growth of new blood vessels to supply themselves with nutrients and oxygen.
3. **Primary Categories:**
   * **Carcinomas:** Start in the skin or tissues that line internal organs (e.g., lung, breast, colon cancers).
   * **Sarcomas:** Start in bone, cartilage, fat, muscle, or blood vessels.
   * **Leukemias & Lymphomas:** Start in blood-forming tissues (bone marrow) or immune glands.

---

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Cellular Mutations
* **DNA Damages:** Acquired mutations in proto-oncogenes (which stimulate cell growth) or tumor suppressor genes (which slow cell division) like the p53 gene.
* **Chemical Carcinogens:** Inhaling tobacco smoke (contains over 70 known carcinogens), chewing betel nut/tobacco, or consuming foods containing chemical preservatives (nitrosamines in processed meats).

### Secondary & Infectious Factors
* Chronic oncogenic viral infections:
   * Human Papillomavirus (HPV) causing cervical and throat cancers.
   * Hepatitis B and C viruses causing liver cancer.
   * *Helicobacter pylori* bacteria causing stomach cancer.

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** Obesity (causes chronic systemic inflammation), physical inactivity, heavy alcohol use, and a diet low in fruits and vegetables.
* **Environmental:** Exposure to outdoor air pollution (particulate matter), radon gas in homes, and ultraviolet (UV) radiation from sunlight.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** Unexplained weight loss of 5 kg or more without dieting; persistent mild fever at night; constant physical fatigue that doesn't improve with rest.
* **Common Symptoms:** Painless, growing lump in the breast, neck, or underarms; changes in a skin mole (asymmetry, bleeding, color changes); chronic cough or hoarseness lasting more than 3 weeks; persistent indigestion or difficulty swallowing.
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Severe, localized bone or abdominal pain; visible blood in urine or stools; yellowing of skin/eyes (jaundice); neurological signs (seizures, weakness) indicating spread to the brain.
* **Emergency Symptoms:** Sudden inability to breathe; severe, uncontrollable bleeding; sudden high fever with chills in a chemotherapy patient (neutropenic sepsis).

## Diagnosis

### Visual Screening & Clinical Exams
* Periodic checks of oral cavity for white patches (leukoplakia), palpation of breasts, and pelvic exams.

### Medical Imaging
* **X-ray & Ultrasound:** Diagnostic tests to look for fluid accumulation or tumors in lungs and abdominal organs.
* **CT & MRI:** Detailed cross-sectional scans to check tumor size and see if cancer has invaded nearby structures.
* **PET Scan (Positron Emission Tomography):** Injecting a tiny amount of radioactive sugar; cancer cells absorb sugar rapidly, making them light up on the scan to detect spread (metastasis).

### Histopathology (Biopsy)
* The gold-standard diagnostic test. A doctor removes a tissue sample from the tumor using a needle or surgery to confirm the cancer type, grade, and biological markers under a microscope.

### Blood Tumor Markers
* Proteins produced by cancer cells that can be measured in blood to monitor treatment (e.g., PSA for prostate cancer, CA-125 for ovarian cancer, CEA for colon cancer).

### Medical Management

#### English
* **Chemotherapy:** Using cytotoxic drugs to kill rapidly dividing cancer cells throughout the body.
* **Immunotherapy:** Medications that train the body's own immune system to recognize and destroy cancer cells (e.g., Pembrolizumab).
* **Targeted Therapy:** Drugs that target specific mutated proteins inside cancer cells to block their growth signals.

#### Hindi
* **कीमोथेरेपी (Chemotherapy):** पूरे शरीर में तेजी से विभाजित होने वाली कैंसर कोशिकाओं को नष्ट करने के लिए दवाओं का उपयोग करना।
* **इम्यूनोथेरेपी (Immunotherapy):** ऐसी दवाएं जो शरीर की स्वयं की प्रतिरक्षा प्रणाली को कैंसर कोशिकाओं को पहचानने और नष्ट करने के लिए प्रशिक्षित करती हैं।
* **लक्षित थेरेपी (Targeted Therapy):** ऐसी दवाएं जो कैंसर कोशिकाओं के भीतर विशिष्ट प्रोटीन को अवरुद्ध करती हैं ताकि उनकी वृद्धि रोकी जा सके।

#### Hinglish

* **Immunotherapy:** Body ki khud ki immunity ko cancer cells ke khilaf train karne wali advanced medicines dena.
* **Targeted Therapy:** Cancer cells ke growth signals ko block karne wali specific targeted medicines (tablets) dena.

#### English
* **Surgical Resection:** Physically cutting out the localized tumor along with a safe margin of healthy tissue.
* **Radiation Therapy:** Using high-energy X-ray beams directed at the tumor to damage cancer cell DNA and shrink the tumor.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **Cytotoxic Chemotherapeutics:** Stop cell division by binding to DNA or microtubule structures (e.g., Paclitaxel, Cisplatin, Doxorubicin).
2. **Hormonal Antagonists:** Starve hormone-dependent cancers of the hormones required for their growth (e.g., Tamoxifen for breast, Bicalutamide for prostate).
3. **Monoclonal Antibodies (Immunotherapy):** Bind to cancer cells to mark them for destruction by the immune system (e.g., Rituximab, Pembrolizumab).
4. **Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors (Targeted):** Block intracellular growth pathways inside mutated cancer cells (e.g., Imatinib, Erlotinib).
5. **Antiemetics:** Powerful medications used to prevent severe nausea and vomiting caused by chemotherapy (e.g., Ondansetron, Aprepitant).

## Possible Complications
* **Metastatic Dysfunction:** Cancer spreading to multiple organs, causing liver failure, respiratory failure, or brain swelling.
* **Cachexia:** Severe wasting of muscle and fat tissue caused by cancer altering the body's metabolism, leading to extreme weakness.
* **Surgical & Chemo Risks:** High susceptibility to severe infections, internal bleeding, or long-term organ damage (kidney/heart toxicity).

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Chemotherapy patient developing a fever of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher with shivering (Neutropenic Sepsis).**
* **Sudden severe difficulty breathing or chest pain.**
* **Uncontrolled bleeding from any body opening or sudden fits/seizures.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you have a cough that lasts more than 3 weeks or if you cough up blood.
* If you notice a painless lump in any part of your body (breast, neck, groin) that is slowly growing.
* If you experience rapid, unexplained weight loss of more than 5 kg within a few months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is cancer?**
   Cancer is a large group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and division of abnormal cells that can invade and destroy healthy body tissues.

2. **What is the difference between benign and malignant tumors?**
   Benign tumors are non-cancerous, slow-growing, and do not spread to other parts of the body. Malignant tumors are cancerous, grow rapidly, and can spread (metastasize) to other organs.

3. **What is metastasis?**
   Metastasis is the process where cancer cells break away from the primary tumor, travel through the blood or lymph system, and form new tumors in other organs.

4. **What is Chemotherapy and how does it work?**
   Chemotherapy is a drug treatment that uses powerful chemicals to target and destroy rapidly dividing cells, which is a key characteristic of cancer cells.

5. **Why does chemotherapy cause hair loss?**
   Chemotherapy drugs target all rapidly dividing cells in the body. Since hair follicle cells also divide rapidly, they are temporarily affected, causing hair loss.

6. **What is Radiation Therapy?**
   Radiation therapy uses high-energy rays (like X-rays or protons) directed at the tumor to damage the DNA of cancer cells, shrinking the tumor or destroying it.

* **What is a biopsy and why is it necessary?**
   A biopsy is a procedure where a doctor removes a small tissue sample from a suspected tumor to examine it under a microscope. It is the only way to confirm if a lump is cancerous.

8. **Can cancer be prevented?**
   Up to 30-50% of cancer cases can be prevented by avoiding tobacco, limiting alcohol, maintaining a healthy weight, eating fresh fruits/vegetables, and getting vaccinated for HPV/Hepatitis B.

9. **What is a PET scan?**
   A PET scan is an advanced imaging test that uses a radioactive sugar tracer to locate active cancer cells throughout the body, showing if cancer has spread (metastasized).

10. **Where can BPL families get free cancer treatment in India?**
    Eligible BPL families can receive cashless treatment up to ₹5 Lakh under Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY) or financial help up to ₹15 Lakh under the Rashtriya Arogya Nidhi (RAN) scheme.

11. **What is HPV and which cancers does it cause?**
    Human Papillomavirus (HPV) is a common virus that can cause genetic changes leading to cervical cancer in women, as well as throat and anal cancers in both men and women.

12. **What is the HPV vaccine and who should get it?**
    The HPV vaccine protects against the virus strains that cause cervical cancer. It is recommended for girls aged 9 to 14 years, ideally before they become sexually active.

13. **Why do cancer patients develop a high risk of infections?**
    Both blood cancers and chemotherapy destroy white blood cells (which fight infections), making the patient's immune system extremely weak.

14. **What is neutropenic fever?**
    Neutropenic fever is a medical emergency in a chemotherapy patient where their white blood cell count is very low and they develop a fever of 100.4°F or higher, requiring immediate hospital antibiotics.

15. **What are tumor markers?**
    Tumor markers are substances (usually proteins) found in the blood or urine that rise in the presence of certain cancers, used to monitor how well a treatment is working.

16. **Is cancer a contagious disease?**
    No, cancer is not contagious. You cannot catch cancer from another person by touching, kissing, sharing food, or caring for them.

17. **What is Immunotherapy?**
    Immunotherapy is an advanced cancer treatment that stimulates or trains the body's own immune system (T-cells) to recognize and destroy cancer cells.

18. **Can chronic stress cause cancer?**
    Stress does not directly cause cancer. However, chronic stress weakens the immune system and can lead to unhealthy habits like smoking or drinking, which increase cancer risk.

19. **What does cancer "staging" (Stage 1 to 4) mean?**
    Staging describes how large the tumor is and how far it has spread. Stage 1 is localized and early; Stage 4 (metastatic) means the cancer has spread to distant organs.

20. **Can cancer be cured completely?**
    Yes, if detected early, many cancers (like breast, cervical, skin, and childhood leukemias) can be cured completely with modern treatments.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Cancer is a contagious disease that spreads by touch.
  * **Fact:** Cancer is caused by internal genetic mutations; it is completely non-contagious.
* **Myth:** If you have cancer, you should stop eating sugar because it feeds cancer.
  * **Fact:** While all cells use glucose for energy, cutting sugar completely from your diet does not stop cancer cells from growing, nor does eating sugar directly cause cancer to grow.
* **Myth:** Traditional herbal juices can cure Stage 4 cancer.
  * **Fact:** There is no scientific evidence that herbal juices cure cancer. Delaying medical surgery or chemotherapy for unverified herbal remedies can be fatal.
* **Myth:** Biopsy causes cancer cells to spill and spread to other organs.
  * **Fact:** Biopsies are performed using safe, sterile techniques that do not cause cancer to spread. A biopsy is essential for planning correct treatment.
* **Myth:** Cancer always causes severe pain in its early stages.
  * **Fact:** Early-stage cancer is usually completely painless. Pain only occurs in later stages when the tumor presses on nerves or organs.
* **Myth:** Wearing underwire bras causes breast cancer.
  * **Fact:** There is no scientific link between bra types, underwires, or tight clothing and the development of breast cancer.
* **Myth:** Chemo drugs always kill the patient instead of curing them.
  * **Fact:** Chemotherapy is a lifesaving treatment. While it has side effects, modern supportive care medicines manage these side effects very well.
* **Myth:** Only people with a family history of cancer get it.
  * **Fact:** Only 5-10% of cancers are hereditary. The remaining 90% are caused by aging, environment, smoking, and lifestyle choices.
* **Myth:** Hair lost during chemotherapy will never grow back.
  * **Fact:** Hair loss is temporary. Once chemotherapy treatment is completed, hair grows back normally within a few months.
* **Myth:** Cell phones and Wi-Fi routers cause brain cancer.
  * **Fact:** Current scientific research shows no evidence that the non-ionizing radiofrequency radiation from cell phones or Wi-Fi causes cancer.

* **Assistant:** Bina kisi parhez ya exercise ke achanak weight kam hona (unexplained weight loss) aur chronic thakan rehna cancer ya kisi badi metabolic bimari ka shuruati lakshan ho sakta hai. Aapko bina deri kiye doctor se consult karna chahiye aur routine blood tests (CBC, sugar) aur basic screening karani chahiye.

### Conversation 2
* **User:** What is the help provided under the Rashtriya Arogya Nidhi (RAN) scheme for cancer?
* **Assistant:** Under the RAN scheme, the Indian government provides financial assistance up to ₹15 Lakh for the treatment of life-threatening cancers at super-specialty government hospitals for families living below the poverty line (BPL).

### Conversation 3
* **User:** Chemo chal rahe patient ko high fever (>101°F) ke sath thand lag rahi hai. Kya karein?
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Yeh "Neutropenic Fever" ka lakshan hai jo ek life-threatening medical emergency hai. Unhe gunguna paani ya paracetamol dekar subah ka wait na karein. Turant patient ko paas ke emergency hospital le jayein jahan IV antibiotics shuru ho sakein.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** Pap Smear test kyu kiya jata hai aur kis age me karana chahiye?
* **Assistant:** Pap Smear test cervical cancer (garbhashay ke muh ka cancer) ki screening ke liye kiya jata hai. 30 saal ki age ke baad har married ya sexually active woman ko har 3 se 5 saal me ek baar Pap Smear test zaroori karana chahiye taaki abnormal cells ka cancer banne se pehle hi pata chal sake.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Kya cancer test (Biopsy) karwane se cancer body me tezi se phail jata hai?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, yeh ek bilkul galat myth hai. Biopsy se cancer ke phailne ka koi risk nahi hota. Biopsy ek safe procedure hai jisse cancer ke type aur grade ka confirm pata chalta hai, jo sahi ilaaj planning ke liye zaroori hai.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** What is the National Cancer Grid (NCG) of India?
* **Assistant:** The National Cancer Grid (NCG) is a network of major cancer centers in India (linked with Tata Memorial Hospital) that aims to establish uniform standards of affordable, high-quality cancer care across the country.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Mere face par ek purana til (mole) achanak bada ho raha hai aur usme se khujli aur blood aa raha hai.
* **Assistant:** Til (mole) ka size badhna, border irregular hona, rang badalna, ya usme se blood/khujli aana Melanoma (skin cancer) ka lakshan ho sakta hai. Aapko bina deri kiye kisi dermatologist ya oncologist ko ise dikhana chahiye aur skin biopsy karani chahiye.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Does Ayushman Bharat card cover cancer chemotherapy drugs?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment benefits are provided for oncology surgeries, chemotherapy cycles (including critical drugs), and radiation therapy at all government and empaneled private hospitals.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Kya cervical cancer se bachne ke liye koi vaccine hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, HPV (Human Papillomavirus) vaccine cervical cancer se bachata hai. AIIMS aur WHO ke anusar, 9 se 14 saal ki girls ko iski 2 doses lagwani chahiye taaki unhe future me cervical cancer ka risk na ho.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Chemo ke dauran pet me ulcer (mucositis) se bachne ke liye kya karein?
* **Assistant:** Chemo ke dauran saliva kam hota hai jisse mouth ulcers (mucositis) hote hain. Isse bachne ke liye har baar khane ke baad saaf paani ya baking soda wale paani se kulla karein. Spicy aur dry khana band karein, aur soft/liquid diet lein.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Kya sugar khana bilkul band karne se cancer cells mar jate hain?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, sugar band karne se cancer cells nahi marte. Cancer cells normal cells ki tarah glucose use karte hain, lekin agar aap sugar band kar denge to body ki healthy cells bhi kamzor ho jayengi. Balanced diet lein, refined sugar kam karein, par khana bilkul band na karein.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the toll-free cancer helpline number in India?
* **Assistant:** You can contact the National Cancer Helpline or consult the National Health Portal helpline at 1800-180-1104 for guidance on cancer hospitals, treatments, and government financial aid schemes.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Mere gala pichle 1 mahine se kharab hai aur aawaz bilkul baith gayi hai. Kya karu?
* **Assistant:** 3 hafte se zyada aawaz ka baithna (hoarseness) ya gale me khich-khich rehna larynx (throat) cancer ya severe infection ka lakshan ho sakta hai. Aap kisi ENT specialist ya oncologist se consult karein aur laryngoscopy jaanch karayein.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** Tumor Lysis Syndrome (TLS) kya hota hai?
* **Assistant:** Jab chemotherapy se tezi se cancer cells marte hain, to unke andar ka potassium aur uric acid achanak blood me release ho jata hai. Yeh kidneys ko block kar sakta hai jise Tumor Lysis Syndrome kehte hain. Iske bachao ke liye chemo ke dauran heavy IV fluids diye jaate hain.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Chest X-ray me lung me shadow dikh raha hai aur khansi me blood aaya hai.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Chest X-ray me shadow aur khansi me blood aana (hemoptysis) lung cancer, tuberculosis, ya severe pneumonia ka sign ho sakta hai. Aap bina ek bhi din gawaye pulmonologist ya chest specialist se consult karein aur CECT Scan karayein.
