# Lungs ka Cancer (Fefde ka Cancer) ki Pehchan aur Upachar (Lung Cancer Symptoms, Diagnosis, and Care)

## Overview

### English
Lung cancer is a primary malignancy originating in the tissues of the lungs, primarily in the cells lining the air passages (bronchi). It is the leading cause of cancer-related deaths globally, strongly associated with tobacco smoking (cigarettes, bidis, hookah) and exposure to environmental air pollution. The two main types are Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC, representing ~85% of cases) and Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC, highly aggressive). Treating lung cancer requires surgical removal (lobectomy), radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted therapies, or immunotherapy. Early detection through low-dose CT scans in high-risk smokers is key to increasing survival rates.

## Quick Summary
Lung cancer occurs as Non-Small Cell or Small Cell types. Tobacco smoking is the leading cause, alongside exposure to indoor wood smoke and air pollution. Common symptoms include a persistent cough lasting over 3 weeks, coughing up blood, shortness of breath, wheezing, and chest pain. Diagnostics include chest X-rays, CECT scans, bronchoscopy, and needle biopsies. Treatment involves lobectomy surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy. Prevention focuses on avoiding tobacco.

---

## Definition
* **Bronchogenic Carcinoma:** A malignant tumor originating in the epithelial lining of the bronchi or bronchioles.
* **Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC):** An umbrella term for several types of lung cancers that behave similarly, including adenocarcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, and large cell carcinoma.
* **Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC):** An aggressive, fast-growing type of lung cancer almost exclusively seen in heavy smokers, which tends to metastasize early.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The lungs receive inhaled air through the bronchial tree:
1. **Bronchi & Bronchioles:** Covered by protective epithelial cells. Inhaled carcinogens damage these cells' DNA, causing squamous cell carcinoma or adenocarcinoma.
2. **Alveoli:** Microscopic air sacs. Some lung cancers (like adenocarcinomas) start in the outer tissues of the lungs near these alveoli, often presenting as a peripheral nodule that grows silently.

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Lung Cancer
* **Tobacco Carcinogen Inhalation:** Active smoking of cigarettes, bidis, or cigars. Tobacco smoke contains over 70 carcinogens (polyphenols, nitrosamines) that bind to and damage lung cell DNA.
* **Radon Gas Exposure:** Inhalation of radioactive radon gas, a naturally occurring decay product of uranium found in soil and rocks, which can accumulate in basements and homes.

### Secondary & Environmental Factors
* Long-term exposure to indoor biomass smoke from burning firewood or coal in unventilated rural kitchens.
* Occupational exposure to asbestos fibers, silica dust, arsenic, or chromium.

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** Passive smoking (secondhand smoke exposure), poor diet, and chronic exposure to high outdoor particulate matter ($PM_{2.5}$).
* **Clinical:** Pre-existing lung damage such as chronic pulmonary fibrosis or severe COPD.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** A mild, persistent dry cough that doesn't go away; slight shortness of breath during normal walking; minor chest discomfort.
* **Common Symptoms:** Coughing up blood or blood-tinged rust-colored sputum (hemoptysis); wheezing or a whistling sound during breathing; persistent chest pain that worsens during deep breathing or coughing; hoarseness of voice (due to tumor pressing on the laryngeal nerve).
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Rapid, unexplained weight loss; constant fatigue; severe shortness of breath at rest; persistent bone pain (indicates spread to bones); facial or neck swelling (due to tumor blocking the superior vena cava vein).
* **Emergency Symptoms:** Sudden, massive coughing up of bright red blood (hemorrhage); extreme difficulty breathing with choking; sudden collapse or fits (indicating spread to the brain).

## Diagnosis

### Medical History & Chest X-ray
* Visualizing the lungs using a standard Chest X-ray to look for shadows, nodules, fluid buildup (pleural effusion), or lung collapse.

### Contrast-Enhanced CT (CECT) Chest
* Detailed scanning of the chest cavity to locate the exact size, position, and vascularity of the lung tumor and check for enlarged lymph nodes in the mediastinum (center of chest).

### Bronchoscopy & Needle Biopsy
* **Bronchoscopy:** Inserting a thin, lighted tube with a camera down the throat into the airways to look directly at the tumor and scrape cells for analysis.
* **CT-Guided Biopsy:** Using a needle through the chest wall under CT guidance to extract a sample from tumors located near the outer edges of the lungs.

### Sputum Cytology
* Examining sputum (mucus) under a microscope to look for cancer cells shed from the lining of the airways.

## Treatment

### Lifestyle & Preventive Care

#### English
* Completely stop smoking bidi, cigarettes, and avoid places with passive smoke. Use air purifiers and wear protective masks (N95) in heavily polluted industrial zones. Perform gentle breathing exercises under clinical guidance.

#### English

### Surgical & Radiation Interventions

#### English
* **Lobectomy:** Surgical removal of an entire lobe of the lung, the most common surgery for early-stage NSCLC.
* **Pneumonectomy:** Surgical removal of the entire lung if the tumor is located near the center of the chest.
* **Stereotactic Body Radiotherapy (SBRT):** Directing highly focused, high-dose radiation beams at small tumors in patients who cannot undergo surgery.

#### Hindi
* **लोबेक्टॉमी (Lobectomy):** फेफड़े के एक पूरे हिस्से (lobe) को सर्जरी द्वारा निकालना, शुरुआती चरण के NSCLC के लिए सबसे आम सर्जरी।
* **न्यूमोनेक्टॉमी (Pneumonectomy):** यदि ट्यूमर छाती के केंद्र के पास स्थित है, तो पूरे फेफड़े को सर्जरी द्वारा हटाना।
* **एसबीआरटी (SBRT):** उन रोगियों में छोटे ट्यूमर पर अत्यधिक केंद्रित, उच्च खुराक वाली विकिरण बीम निर्देशित करना जो सर्जरी नहीं करा सकते।

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **Targeted EGFR Inhibitors:** Block EGFR protein signals that cause cancer cells to multiply (e.g., Gefitinib, Erlotinib, Osimertinib).
2. **Targeted ALK Inhibitors:** Block abnormal ALK gene signals in lung cancer cells (e.g., Crizotinib, Alectinib).
3. **Chemotherapy Platinum Duplets:** Standard chemotherapy drug combinations used for lung cancer (e.g., Cisplatin + Pemetrexed, Carboplatin + Paclitaxel).
4. **Immunotherapy PD-1/PD-L1 Inhibitors:** Help T-cells recognize and destroy lung cancer cells (e.g., Pembrolizumab, Nivolumab).
5. **Bronchodilators:** Relax airway muscles to relieve shortness of breath in lung cancer patients (e.g., Levosalbutamol).

## Prevention

### English
* **Say No to Tobacco:** Stop smoking bidi, cigarettes, and hookah. (quitting smoking reduces lung cancer risk by 50% within 10 years).
* **Avoid Biomass Smoke:** Switch from firewood/coal to clean LPG cooking gas under government schemes (PM Ujjwala Yojana).
* **Screen Radon Gas:** Ensure proper ventilation in homes built over soil rich in natural uranium.
* **Protect against Industrial Dust:** Use professional respirators if working in asbestos mining, glass manufacturing, or coal mines.

## Possible Complications
* **Pleural Effusion:** Accumulation of fluid in the chest cavity around the lungs, compressing the lung tissue and causing severe breathlessness.
* **Superior Vena Cava Syndrome:** The tumor pressing on the main vein carrying blood from the upper body to the heart, causing severe face, neck, and arm swelling.
* **Spinal Cord Compression:** Metastasis to the spine pressing on the spinal cord, causing leg paralysis and loss of bladder control.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Gasping for breath, or inability to talk in complete sentences due to breathlessness.**
* **Coughing up a cupful or more of bright red blood (severe hemoptysis).**
* **Sudden weakness/numbness in both legs or inability to pass urine after spine pain.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you have a cough that produces sputum and lasts more than 3 weeks (requires TB and cancer screening).
* If you notice blood stains in your sputum or cough up blood.
* If you are a heavy smoker over 50 years of age, to discuss getting a screening Low-Dose CT (LDCT) scan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Lung Cancer?**
   Lung cancer is a primary malignancy starting in the cells of the lungs, primarily in the bronchi or bronchioles, strongly linked to tobacco smoke.

2. **What is the difference between Small Cell and Non-Small Cell lung cancer?**
   Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC) is more common (~85% of cases) and grows slowly. Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC) is highly aggressive, grows rapidly, spreads early, and is almost always seen in heavy smokers.

3. **Can non-smokers get lung cancer?**
   Yes. While smoking is the leading cause, non-smokers can get lung cancer due to passive smoking, air pollution, indoor wood smoke, radon gas, or occupational exposure to asbestos.

4. **What is Bronchoscopy?**
   Bronchoscopy is a diagnostic test where a thin tube with a camera is passed down into the airways to look directly at the lung tumor and collect cells for a biopsy.

5. **Why does lung cancer cause coughing up blood?**
   As the tumor grows, it invades and ruptures the delicate blood vessels lining the airways, causing blood to leak into the mucus, which is coughed up (hemoptysis).

6. **What is a Lobectomy?**
   A lobectomy is the surgical removal of an entire lobe of the lung, which is the standard curative treatment for early-stage Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer.

7. **How does radon gas cause lung cancer?**
   Radon is a radioactive gas from natural uranium decay in soil. When inhaled, it releases alpha particles that damage the DNA of lung cells, causing cancer.

8. **Where can heavy smokers get screened for lung cancer?**
   Smokers aged 50-80 with a heavy smoking history should get an annual Low-Dose CT (LDCT) scan, which can detect early stage cancer before symptoms appear.

9. **What is $SpO_2$ and what is normal?**
   $SpO_2$ is blood oxygen saturation. Normal is 95-100%. In lung cancer patients, tumor blockages or fluid buildup can drop $SpO_2$ below 90%, requiring oxygen therapy.

10. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover targeted lung cancer therapy?**
    Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment benefits are provided for targeted drugs (like Gefitinib) and immunotherapy packages for eligible patients.

11. **Why is wood smoke in kitchens bad for the lungs?**
    Burning wood or cow dung in closed kitchens releases high levels of particulate matter and carbon monoxide, which damages lung cells similarly to cigarette smoke, causing rural lung cancer.

12. **What is a Pleural Effusion?**
    It is the abnormal accumulation of fluid in the space around the lungs (pleural cavity). It compresses the lungs, making it difficult for them to expand, causing severe breathlessness.

13. **Why does lung cancer cause hoarseness of voice?**
    A tumor in the left lung can grow and press on the recurrent laryngeal nerve, which controls the vocal cords, causing a hoarse, raspy voice.

14. **What is SBRT?**
    Stereotactic Body Radiotherapy (SBRT) is an advanced radiation technique that delivers highly focused, high-dose radiation beams directly to small lung tumors, used for patients who cannot undergo surgery.

15. **How long is the survival rate if lung cancer is detected early?**
    If detected early (Stage 1) before spreading, the 5-year survival rate for Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer is over 60% to 80% with surgery.

16. **Why do doctors check for EGFR and ALK mutations in lung cancer?**
    If these mutations are present, the patient can be treated with highly effective targeted drug tablets (like Osimertinib or Crizotinib) instead of standard chemotherapy.

17. **Can a person live normally with only one lung?**
    Yes. If one lung is removed (pneumonectomy), the remaining healthy lung can expand and perform enough gas exchange for a normal, light-activity life.

18. **Why does lung cancer spread to bones?**
    Lung cancer cells can enter the rich pulmonary blood vessels, allowing them to travel through the bloodstream and settle in bones (especially the spine and ribs).

19. **What is the difference between lung cancer and Tuberculosis (TB)?**
    Both cause chronic cough, fever, and weight loss. However, TB is a bacterial infection treated with antibiotics, while cancer is a malignant cellular growth diagnosed by biopsy.

20. **Can vaping or e-cigarettes cause lung cancer?**
    Vaping exposes lungs to chemicals, flavorings, and heavy metals that cause lung injury. Long-term studies are ongoing, but vaping is believed to increase cancer risk.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Only heavy cigarette smokers get lung cancer.
  * **Fact:** Non-smokers can get lung cancer due to passive smoking, air pollution, radon gas, and occupational dust (like asbestos).
* **Myth:** Coughing up blood always means you have Tuberculosis (TB).
  * **Fact:** While TB is common in India, coughing up blood is also a major warning sign of lung cancer and requires immediate screening.
* **Myth:** Inhalers are used to treat lung cancer.
  * **Fact:** Inhalers only temporarily relax airway muscles to relieve breathlessness; they cannot treat or cure the cancer tumor.
* **Myth:** If you have lung cancer, surgery will cause the cancer to spread to the other lung.
  * **Fact:** Surgery is the best cure for early-stage cancer. It removes the tumor completely and does not cause it to spread.
* **Myth:** Hookah is safe because the smoke is filtered through water.
  * **Fact:** Hookah water does not filter out carcinogens. One hour of hookah is equivalent to smoking multiple cigarettes, causing severe lung damage.
* **Myth:** Lung cancer is highly contagious and spreads through coughing.
  * **Fact:** Cancer is not infectious; you cannot catch lung cancer from someone coughing near you.
* **Myth:** If a chest X-ray is normal, you are 100% free of lung cancer.
  * **Fact:** Small or early-stage lung tumors can be hidden behind the heart or ribs on an X-ray. A CECT chest scan is required for detailed screening.
* **Myth:** Heavy bidi smokers can clean their lungs by drinking herbal syrups.
  * **Fact:** There is no syrup that can clean tar or repair DNA damage in the lungs. The only way to stop damage is to quit smoking completely.
* **Myth:** Lung cancer only affects elderly people.
  * **Fact:** While more common in older adults, younger adults can also develop lung cancer, especially adenocarcinomas in non-smoking women.
* **Myth:** Alternative herbal smoking mixtures are safe for the lungs.
  * **Fact:** Inhaling any kind of smoke (herbal or tobacco) releases carbon monoxide and tar, damaging lung cells.

* **Assistant:** Generic Gefitinib (250mg) tablets are available at government PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, often costing only ₹500 to ₹800 per pack, compared to ₹5,000+ for branded versions in private pharmacies.

### Conversation 3
* **User:** Meri dadi ko lung cancer hai aur unke gale aur face par achanak sujan aa gayi hai. Kya ye emergency hai?
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Gale aur face par achanak sujan aana "Superior Vena Cava Syndrome" ka lakshan ho sakta hai, jisme lung tumor gale ki main blood vessel ko block kar deta hai. Yeh ek medical emergency hai. Unhe turant hospital ke emergency room me le jayein.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** Low-Dose CT (LDCT) scan kya hota hai aur kyu kiya jata hai?
* **Assistant:** Low-Dose CT (LDCT) scan ek specialized chest scan hai jisme normal CT ke mukable bohot kam radiation hoti hai. Yeh heavy smokers me early-stage lung cancer nodules ko screen karne ke liye kiya jata hai jab unhe koi symptom nahi hote.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Chemo ke dauran pulse oximeter me oxygen level 89% dikha raha hai, kya karein?
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** $SpO_2$ level 89% aana oxygen ki severe kami ko dikhata hai. Patient ko turant oxygen support ki zarurat hai. Unhe aaram se baithayein (letayein nahi) aur bina deri kiye 108 helpline par call karke ambulance bulayein.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** Bronchoscopy test me kya kiya jata hai?
* **Assistant:** Bronchoscopy me doctor throat ke jariye saans ki nali me ek patli tube (camera ke sath) daalte hain. Isse saans ki nali ke andar tumor ko directly dekha jata hai aur wahan se tissue ka chota sample (biopsy) liya jata.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Kya lung cancer me ek side ka fefda (lung) surgically nikalna possible hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan. Agar tumor lung ke center me ho aur spread na hua ho, to surgery ke jariye us side ka poora lung nikal diya jata hai (Pneumonectomy). Bacha hua ek lung normal life ke liye gas exchange kar sakta hai.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Does Ayushman Bharat cover lung cancer lobectomy surgery cost?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment benefits are provided for lung cancer diagnostic CT scans, bronchoscopy, lobectomy/pneumonectomy surgeries, chemotherapy cycles, and radiation therapy.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Kya koyle ya lakdi ke chulhe ke dhuen se rural women me lung cancer ho sakta hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, band rasoi me lakdi ya koyle ke chulhe ka dhuaan lungs ke liye daily 10-15 cigarette peene jitna hi nuxandeh hota hai. Isse rural women me lung cancer aur COPD ka risk bohot badh jata hai. Unhe LPG gas use karni chahiye.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** My grandfather is 68 years old and has lung cancer. Should he get the pneumonia vaccine?
* **Assistant:** Yes. AIIMS and oncologists recommend that lung cancer patients, especially elderly ones, should receive the Pneumococcal vaccine to protect their weak lungs from severe secondary bacterial pneumonia.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Kya smoking chhodne se lung cancer ka risk kam ho jata hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, smoking chhodne ke 10 saal ke andar lung cancer hone ka risk lagbhag aadha ho jata hai, aur cardiovascular system bhi improve hota hai. Smoking jitni jaldi chhodein, utna behtar hai.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** Sputum Cytology test kya hota hai?
* **Assistant:** Sputum Cytology me balgam ke sample ko microscope ke neeche check kiya jata hai taaki saans ki nali se jhadne wali cancer cells ka pata lagaya sake. Yeh ek simple screening test hai.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Gym me running karne par saans phoolti hai, kya mujhe lung cancer hai?
* **Assistant:** Gym me bhagne par saans phoolna normal physical strain, low stamina, ya asthma/bronchitis ka sign ho sakta hai. Agar aapko chronic khansi aur blood spit nahi hai, to cancer ka risk bohot kam hai. Apni stamina badhayein aur exercise karein.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** What is the role of PM Ujjwala Yojana in lung health?
* **Assistant:** The PM Ujjwala Yojana provides free LPG gas connections to BPL families, replacing traditional wood-burning stoves, which reduces biomass smoke inhalation in rural kitchens, preventing lung cancer and COPD in women.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Lung cancer ke patient ko peeth (spine) me tez dard kyu hota hai?
* **Assistant:** Lung cancer cells blood ke jariye lungs se nikal kar spine ki haddiyon me phail sakte hain (bone metastasis). Yeh bone pain aur spinal cord compress kar sakta hai, jisse leg paralysis ho sakta hai. Immediate doctor consult karein.
