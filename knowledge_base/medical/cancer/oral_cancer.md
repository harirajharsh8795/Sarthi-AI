# Muh ka Cancer (Oral Cancer) ki Pehchan aur Upachar (Oral Cancer Symptoms, Diagnosis, and Care)

## Overview

### English
Oral cancer is a type of cancer that develops in any part of the oral cavity, including the lips, tongue, gums, inner lining of the cheeks (buccal mucosa), roof of the mouth (palate), and floor of the mouth. In India, oral cancer is highly prevalent, particularly among men, due to widespread tobacco chewing (gutkha, khaini, paan masala) and smoking. Most oral cancers are Squamous Cell Carcinomas, beginning in the flat cells lining the mouth. Early detection through regular dental checkups and identifying pre-cancerous lesions (like white patches - leukoplakia) is crucial for a complete cure, which involves surgery, radiation therapy, and chemotherapy.

## Quick Summary
Oral cancer forms in the mouth cavity. The main causes are chewing tobacco/gutkha, smoking, alcohol, and HPV infection. Common symptoms include a non-healing mouth ulcer lasting over 3 weeks, difficulty swallowing or chewing, white/red patches in the mouth, and voice changes. Diagnostics include clinical oral exams, toluidine blue staining, and punch biopsies. Treatment involves surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy. Prevention focuses on quitting tobacco and alcohol.

---

## Definition
* **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC):** A type of cancer that begins in the squamous epithelial cells lining the mucosal surfaces of the oral cavity.
* **Leukoplakia:** A clinical term for a persistent white patch or plaque in the mouth lining that cannot be rubbed off and has a risk of turning into cancer.
* **Erythroplakia:** A clinical term for a red, velvety patch in the mouth lining that has a very high risk of malignant transformation.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The oral cavity consists of structures lined by mucosal epithelium:
1. **Squamous Epithelium:** The flat, protective cells lining the cheeks and tongue. Chemical carcinogens from chewing gutkha dissolve in saliva and directly irritate these cells over years, causing DNA mutations.
2. **Lymphatic Drainage:** The oral cavity has a rich network of lymph nodes in the neck. If not treated early, oral cancer cells easily migrate (metastasize) to these cervical lymph nodes, making surgery more complex.

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Oral Cancer
* **Chewing Tobacco & Betel Nut:** Chewing gutkha, khaini, zarda, or betel quid (paan). These substances contain highly toxic chemical carcinogens (nitrosamines) and rough fibers that scratch the delicate mouth lining, forcing rapid cell replication that leads to DNA damage.
* **Alcohol & Tobacco Synergism:** Alcohol dissolves chemical carcinogens from tobacco smoke, allowing them to penetrate deep into mucosal cells, multiplying the cancer risk.

### Secondary & Infectious Factors
* Human Papillomavirus (HPV 16 strain) infection, especially in cancers of the tonsils and back of the throat (oropharyngeal cancer).
* Chronic trauma or irritation from sharp, broken teeth or ill-fitting dentures rubbing against the tongue or cheek.

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** Poor oral hygiene (allows bacteria to convert alcohol into carcinogenic acetaldehyde), nutritional deficiencies (lack of iron and vitamins A/C/E), and advanced age.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** A small, painless red or white patch on the gums or inner cheeks; a minor ulcer in the mouth that doesn't heal for 2 weeks; mild difficulty chewing hard foods.
* **Common Symptoms:** A painful mouth ulcer (chal) that does not heal after 3 weeks and bleeds easily; a lump or thickening in the cheek or neck; difficulty swallowing (dysphagia) or chewing; a feeling that something is caught in the throat; numbness of the tongue or lips.
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Severe, radiating ear pain on one side; loose teeth with bleeding gums; difficulty opening the mouth fully (trismus - lockjaw); foul-smelling breath (halitosis); rapid, unexplained weight loss.
* **Emergency Symptoms:** Severe swelling in the mouth or throat causing difficulty breathing or swallowing saliva; sudden massive bleeding from the mouth.

## Diagnosis

### Visual Oral Screening & Staining
* **Toluidine Blue Staining:** A liquid dye applied to the mouth lining; abnormal cells absorb the blue dye, highlighting areas that need a biopsy.
* **Clinical Examination:** A dentist checks all areas of the mouth and feels the neck for enlarged lymph nodes.

### Oral Biopsy (Punch & Incisional Biopsy)
* The gold-standard diagnostic test. Under local anesthesia, a doctor takes a small circular piece of tissue from the edge of the mouth ulcer using a punch tool, which is examined under a microscope to confirm cancer.

### Contrast-Enhanced CT (CECT) Face & Neck
* Detailed scans to check how deep the tumor has grown into the jawbone, tongue muscles, or lymph nodes in the neck.

## Treatment

### Lifestyle & Preventive Care

#### English
* Completely quit gutkha, khaini, paan masala, and bidi/cigarettes. Brush teeth twice daily and rinse with clean water after every meal. Avoid hot, spicy, or sharp foods.

#### Hindi
* गुटखा, खैनी, पान मसाला चबाना और बीड़ी/सिगरेट पीना पूरी तरह से बंद करें। दिन में दो बार ब्रश करें और हर भोजन के बाद साफ पानी से कुल्ला करें। अत्यधिक गर्म, मसालेदार या तीखे भोजन से बचें।

#### Hinglish
* Gutkha, khaini, paan masala chabana aur bidi/cigarette peena bilkul band karein. Din me do baar brush karein aur khane ke baad kulla karein. Teekha aur bohot garam khana na khayein.

### Home Care during Treatment

#### English
* Use a soft silicone toothbrush. Rinse your mouth every 2 hours with a saline-baking soda solution (1/2 tsp salt + 1/2 tsp baking soda in a glass of warm water) to soothe radiation burns and prevent thrush.

#### Hindi

#### Hinglish

### Medical Management

#### English
* Chemotherapy cycles to shrink the tumor before surgery or destroy leftover cells. Targeted drugs to block cell receptors. Nutritional supplements via a feeding tube (Ryle's tube) if swallowing is impossible.

#### Hindi

#### Hinglish
* Surgery se pehle tumor ka size chota karne ke liye chemotherapy dena. Khana nigalna impossible hone par naak ke raste feeding tube (Ryle's tube) se liquid nutrition dena.

### Surgical & Radiation Interventions

#### English
* **Glossectomy:** Surgical removal of a part of or the entire tongue.
* **Maxillectomy / Mandibulectomy:** Removing the affected jawbone (upper jaw/maxilla or lower jaw/mandible).
* **Neck Dissection:** Surgical removal of lymph nodes in the neck to prevent cancer spread.
* **Radiation Therapy:** High-energy beams targeted at the oral cavity to destroy cancer cells, often combined with surgery.

#### Hindi
* **ग्लोसेक्टॉमी (Glossectomy):** जीभ के एक हिस्से या पूरी जीभ को सर्जरी द्वारा हटाना।
* **मैंडिब्युलेक्टॉमी (Mandibulectomy):** प्रभावित जबड़े की हड्डी (निचला जबड़ा/mandible) को सर्जरी द्वारा हटाना।
* **नेक डिसेक्शन (Neck Dissection):** कैंसर के प्रसार को रोकने के लिए गर्दन की लसीका ग्रंथियों को सर्जरी द्वारा हटाना।
* **विकिरण चिकित्सा (Radiation Therapy):** कैंसर कोशिकाओं को नष्ट करने के लिए मुंह की गुहा पर उच्च-ऊर्जा किरणें केंद्रित करना।

#### Hinglish
* **Glossectomy:** Tongue (jeebh) ke cancerous part ko ya poori tongue ko surgically nikalna.
* **Mandibulectomy:** Cancer se kharab niche ke jabde ki haddi (jawbone) ko surgically nikalna.
* **Neck Dissection:** Gardan ke un lymph nodes ko nikalna jahan cancer phailne ka darr ho.
* **Radiation Therapy:** High-energy beams se muh ke andar bache huye cancer cells ko destroy karna.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **Chemotherapy Agents:** Bind to cell DNA to stop cancer replication (e.g., Cisplatin, 5-Fluorouracil, Docetaxel).
2. **Targeted Epidermal Growth Factor (EGFR) Inhibitors:** Block growth receptors on squamous cancer cells (e.g., Cetuximab).
3. **Mouthwashes & Pain Gels:** Form protective local layers to relieve severe pain from mouth ulcers (e.g., Benzydamine mouthwash, Lignocaine gel).
4. **Antifungals:** Used to treat oral thrush (fungal infection) that commonly develops during chemotherapy/radiation (e.g., Clotrimazole mouth paint, Nystatin suspension).
5. **Antibiotics:** Prescribed clinically to control bacterial infections in open mouth wounds.

## Prevention

### English
* **Stop Tobacco Chewing:** Completely quit gutkha, khaini, paan masala, and smoking. (The body can reverse pre-cancerous lesions if tobacco is stopped early).
* **Limit Alcohol Intake:** Do not consume alcohol, especially in combination with tobacco.
* **Correct Sharp Teeth:** Visit a dentist to smooth sharp tooth edges or adjust dentures that rub against your tongue or cheek.
* **Eat Fresh Produce:** Consume a diet rich in green vegetables, citrus fruits, and carrots containing beta-carotene.

## Possible Complications
* **Osteoradionecrosis:** Death of jawbone tissue due to high-dose radiation therapy, causing severe jaw pain, loose teeth, and exposed bone.
* **Trismus (Lockjaw):** Permanent stiffening of jaw muscles after surgery or radiation, preventing the patient from opening their mouth.
* **Severe Dysphagia:** Loss of ability to swallow, requiring long-term liquid diet via a stomach feeding tube (PEG).

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Severe swelling in the throat/mouth causing difficulty breathing or choking.**
* **Sudden, heavy, uncontrollable bleeding from the mouth.**
* **Inability to swallow even saliva, causing drooling.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you have a mouth ulcer (chala) that has not healed for over 3 weeks, even if it is not painful.
* If you notice a persistent white or red patch inside your mouth or on your tongue.
* If you experience difficulty opening your mouth fully or difficulty moving your tongue.

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Tobacco Control Programme (NTCP):** Promotes awareness about the health hazards of tobacco, establishes tobacco cessation centers, and enforces COTPA laws banning tobacco advertisements.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless hospitalization, diagnostic biopsy, CECT scans, glossectomy, mandibulectomy surgeries, neck dissections, reconstruction surgeries, and radiation therapy packages.
* **Dental Health Clinics (NDOHP):** Provides free dental checkups and screening for oral pre-cancerous lesions at government community health centers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Oral Cancer?**
   Oral cancer is cancer that develops in any part of the mouth cavity, including the lips, gums, cheeks, tongue, or palate, most commonly caused by chewing tobacco.

2. **What is Leukoplakia?**
   Leukoplakia is a persistent white patch or plaque in the mouth lining that cannot be rubbed off, which has a risk of turning into cancer if not treated.

3. **What is Erythroplakia?**
   Erythroplakia is a red, velvety patch in the mouth lining that has a very high risk of turning into cancer and requires immediate biopsy.

4. **Why is gutkha chewing the leading cause of oral cancer in India?**
   Gutkha contains chemical carcinogens (nitrosamines) and rough areca nut fibers. Chabane (chewing) scratches the mouth lining, letting carcinogens penetrate cells, causing cancer.

5. **Is a biopsy test necessary for mouth ulcers?**
   If a mouth ulcer does not heal after 3 weeks, a punch biopsy is absolutely necessary to rule out oral cancer. It is a quick and safe test.

6. **What is a Glossectomy?**
   A glossectomy is the surgical removal of a part of or the entire tongue to treat cancer. Reconstructive surgery using skin flaps is often done to help the patient speak and swallow.

7. **Can a sharp tooth cause mouth cancer?**
   A sharp tooth cannot cause cancer on its own. However, chronic irritation from a sharp tooth constantly cutting the tongue or cheek can lead to long-term inflammation, increasing cancer risk.

8. **What is Trismus?**
   Trismus (lockjaw) is a condition where a patient cannot open their mouth fully, commonly occurring due to scarring after jaw surgery or radiation therapy.

9. **Does alcohol increase the risk of oral cancer?**
   Yes. Alcohol dries the mouth lining, making it easier for chemical carcinogens (from tobacco or gutkha) to enter cells, multiplying the risk of cancer.

10. **Where can BPL families get free oral cancer screening in India?**
    Free screening for oral cancers is available at all government Primary Health Centers (PHCs) and Health Wellness Centers (HWCs) under the NPCDCS program.

11. **Why does radiation therapy cause dry mouth?**
    Radiation beams target the cancer but can also damage nearby salivary glands, reducing saliva production and causing chronic dry mouth (xerostomia).

12. **What is a Neck Dissection?**
    A neck dissection is a surgical procedure to remove lymph nodes in the neck to check if cancer has spread and to prevent further spread.

13. **Can oral cancer be cured completely?**
    Yes. If detected early in the pre-cancerous stage (leukoplakia) or Stage 1, oral cancer can be cured completely with surgery, with minimal facial changes.

14. **Why is a feeding tube (Ryle's tube) used in oral cancer?**
    If a patient has severe mouth pain or jaw stiffness post-surgery, they cannot chew or swallow. A feeding tube is placed through the nose to supply liquid food directly to the stomach.

15. **Does smoking bidis cause oral cancer?**
    Yes. Bidi smoke contains high levels of carbon monoxide and nicotine. The heat and chemicals damage oral mucosal cells, causing lip and mouth cancers.

16. **What is Osteoradionecrosis?**
    It is a complication of radiation therapy where blood supply to the jawbone is lost, causing bone tissue to die and become exposed inside the mouth.

17. **Can HPV cause oral cancer?**
    Yes. The Human Papillomavirus (specifically HPV 16) is associated with oropharyngeal cancer (back of the throat, tonsils), often spreading through oral sex.

18. **Why is baking soda mouthwash recommended during radiation?**
    Baking soda neutralizes acid in the mouth, reduces pain from radiation ulcers, and helps prevent fungal infections (oral thrush).

19. **What is reconstruction surgery in oral cancer?**
    Reconstruction surgery uses skin, muscle, or bone flaps (often taken from the arm, leg, or chest) to rebuild the jawbone or tongue removed during cancer surgery.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover oral cancer reconstruction?**
    Yes, the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme covers cashless expenses for cancer removal, neck dissection, and reconstructive plastic surgery.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Mouth ulcers are always caused by stomach heat and can be cured with vitamins.
  * **Fact:** Simple mouth ulcers heal in 1-2 weeks. An ulcer that does not heal after 3 weeks can be oral cancer, which cannot be cured by vitamins.
* **Myth:** Smoking bidi is safer than chewing gutkha.
  * **Fact:** Both are highly carcinogenic. Chewing gutkha leads to cheek/tongue cancer, while bidi causes lung, throat, and lip cancers.
* **Myth:** Biopsy makes oral cancer spread faster.
  * **Fact:** A biopsy does not spread cancer. It is the only scientific way to confirm cancer and plan the correct surgery.
* **Myth:** Applying lime (chuna) on a mouth ulcer cures it.
  * **Fact:** Applying lime causes chemical burns on the already damaged mucosa, worsening the ulcer and inflammation.
* **Myth:** Oral cancer only affects elderly tobacco users.
  * **Fact:** Due to rising gutkha use, many young adults in their 20s and 30s are being diagnosed with advanced oral cancer in India.
* **Myth:** Once a part of the tongue is removed, you can never speak again.
  * **Fact:** With modern reconstructive surgery using skin flaps and speech therapy, patients can speak and swallow reasonably well.
* **Myth:** Brushing daily completely prevents gutkha cancer.
  * **Fact:** Brushing cannot reverse DNA mutations caused by gutkha. The only way to prevent cancer is to quit tobacco completely.
* **Myth:** Paan masala without tobacco is completely safe.
  * **Fact:** Paan masala contains betel nut (supari), which is classified as a Group 1 carcinogen by the WHO and damages the mouth lining.
* **Myth:** Oral cancer is contagious and spreads through kissing.
  * **Fact:** Cancer is an internal cellular disease; it is not infectious.
* **Myth:** Radiation therapy burns are permanent and will not heal.
  * **Fact:** Radiation skin burns and mouth ulcers are temporary. They heal within 4-6 weeks after the radiation therapy is completed.

* **Assistant:** Tambaku na khane par bhi agar tongue ka chala (ulcer) 3 hafte se zyada na heal ho, to biopsy zaroori hai. Aisa sharp daant ke lagatar lagne se ya HPV virus ke karan ho sakta hai. Aap turant ENT specialist ya oral surgeon se consult karein.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** Mandibulectomy surgery kya hoti hai? Kya face badal jayega?
* **Assistant:** Mandibulectomy me cancer se kharab niche ke jabde ki haddi (jawbone) ko nikala jata hai. Face ka structure banaye rakhne ke liye surgery ke dauran pair ki fibula bone ya steel plate se jaw reconstruction kiya jata hai, jisse chehra pehle jaisa normal dikhne lagta hai.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Mere relative ko oral cancer surgery ke baad naak me pipe (feeding tube) dali hai. Kya ye permanent hai?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, yeh naak ki pipe (Ryle's tube) temporary hoti hai. Surgery ke baad muh ke wounds ko dry rakhne aur heal hone ke liye 1-2 hafte liquid food is pipe se diya jata hai. Jab patient muh se nigalna shuru kar dete hain, to ye pipe nikal di jati hai.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** Trismus (lockjaw) ko theek karne ke liye kya exercises karein?
* **Assistant:** Surgery ya radiation ke baad jaw stiffness (trismus) se bachne ke liye regular jaw exercises karein (jaise wooden spatulas/ice cream sticks ko teeth ke beech me rakh kar dheere-dheere jaw kholna). Ise rojana 3-4 baar karein.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Does chewing supari (areca nut) cause cancer without tobacco?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Supari (areca nut) is a proven Group 1 carcinogen. Even if you chew supari without tobacco, it scratches the mucosal lining and causes oral submucous fibrosis (OSMF), which can turn into cancer.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** What is the toll-free tobacco cessation helpline number in India?
* **Assistant:** You can call the government National Tobacco Cessation Helpline at 1800-11-2356 for free counseling, support, and advice to help you quit chewing gutkha/khaini or smoking.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Chemo aur radiation ke dauran gale me dry-mouth aur thukan ki kami kyu hoti hai?
* **Assistant:** Radiation beams cancer ke sath muh me majood salivary glands (lar banane wali glands) ko damage karti hain, jisse lar (saliva) banna kam ho jata hai aur mouth dry rehta hai. Iske liye saline water se rinse karein aur paani pite rahein.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** My dad has throat cancer and has difficulty breathing. Is this an emergency?
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Gale ke cancer me saans lene me takleef hona airway blockage (saans ki nali band hona) ka sign hai. Aap bina ek second waste kiye unhe turant emergency hospital le jayein jahan tracheostomy (gale me hole) karke saans bahal ki ja sakti hai.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Kya bidi pine se lips (honth) ka cancer ho sakta hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, bidi pine se lips ka direct contact garam nicotine aur chemicals se hota hai, jisse honth ki cells mutate ho jati hain aur lips cancer ka risk bohot badh jata hai.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** Sputum Cytology test ki tarah kya mouth cancer ke liye koi liquid test hota hai?
* **Assistant:** Mouth cancer me clinical oral examination ke bad direct punch biopsy ki jati hai. Sputum cytology lungs ke liye hoti hai. Mouth ke chalo ke liye brush cytology screen test hota hai par biopsy hi gold standard hai.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Mujhse teekha khana bilkul nahi khaya jata aur muh sirf do ungli khulta hai.
* **Assistant:** Yeh Oral Submucous Fibrosis (OSMF) ke lakshan hain, jo paan masala ya gutkha chabane se hota hai. Isme mouth ki inner skin hard ho jati hai aur elastic tissue damage ho jata hai. Tambaku turant band karein aur dentist se ilaaj shuru karayein.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** What is the price of generic cancer medication Cetuximab at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?
* **Assistant:** Generic targeted oncology monoclonal antibodies like Cetuximab are available under government programs at highly subsidized prices, costing significantly less than their private equivalents.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Mere relative ko muh se achanak bohot heavy blood nikal raha hai surgery ke baad.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Yeh severe postoperative bleeding (hemorrhage) hai. Unka face aage ki taraf jhukayein (taaki blood gale me na jaye) aur wound par ek saaf cotton cloth se press karke pressure banayein. Turant 108 call karke emergency hospital le jayein.
