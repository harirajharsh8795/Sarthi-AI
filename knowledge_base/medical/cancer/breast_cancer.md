# Stana Cancer (Breast Cancer) ki Pehchan aur Upachar (Breast Cancer Symptoms, Detection, and Care)

## Overview

### English
Breast cancer is the most common cancer diagnosed in women globally and in India, originating in the cells of the breast ducts or lobules. It occurs when cells in the breast grow uncontrollably, often forming a painless lump. While it primarily affects women, men can also develop breast cancer. Early detection through regular Breast Self-Exams (BSE), clinical exams, and screening Mammography significantly increases the chances of a complete cure. Treatment options include surgery (lumpectomy or mastectomy), chemotherapy, radiation therapy, and hormone or targeted therapies.

## Quick Summary
Breast cancer forms in breast ducts or lobules. Common symptoms include a hard, painless lump in the breast or armpit, changes in breast shape, skin dimpling (orange peel texture), and nipple discharge or retraction. Diagnostics include mammograms, breast ultrasounds, and needle biopsies. Medical treatment involves surgery, radiation, chemotherapy, and hormone therapies. Prevention focuses on self-exams, avoiding obesity, and limiting alcohol.

---

## Definition
* **Breast Carcinoma:** A malignant tumor that starts in the cells of the breast, classified mainly into Ductal Carcinoma in Situ (DCIS), Invasive Ductal Carcinoma (IDC), and Invasive Lobular Carcinoma (ILC).
* **Mammography:** A low-dose X-ray procedure specifically designed for detailed imaging of breast tissue to detect early calcium deposits or micro-lumps before they can be felt.
* **Mastectomy:** The surgical removal of one or both breasts, partially or completely, to treat or prevent breast cancer.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The breast consists of glandular tissue, fat, and supporting ligaments:
1. **Lobules:** Glands that produce milk.
2. **Ducts:** Small tubes that carry milk from the lobules to the nipple. Most breast cancers start in the lining of these ducts (Ductal Carcinoma).
3. **Lymph Nodes:** Small immune glands in the underarm (axilla). Breast cancer cells often spread first to these axillary lymph nodes before entering the bloodstream.

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Breast Cancer
* **Genetic Mutations:** Inherited mutations in specific tumor suppressor genes, particularly **BRCA1** and **BRCA2**. Women carrying these mutations have up to an 80% lifetime risk.
* **Estrogen Exposure:** Long-term exposure to high estrogen levels, caused by early menstruation (before age 12), late menopause (after age 55), or never having children.

### Secondary & Environmental Factors
* Receiving prior radiation therapy to the chest area during childhood/adolescence (e.g., for Hodgkin lymphoma).
* Long-term use of combined Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) during menopause.

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** Post-menopausal obesity (fat tissues produce estrogen), physical inactivity, alcohol consumption, and heavy active smoking.
* **Clinical:** Advanced age (most cases diagnosed after age 50) and a dense breast structure.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** A small, painless, hard lump deep inside the breast or underarm; minor changes in the size or shape of one breast.
* **Common Symptoms:** Pulling inward of the nipple (retraction); discharge from the nipple, especially if it is bloody or occurs only from one breast; skin changes on the breast, including redness, scaling, or dimpling (looking like an orange peel - *peau d'orange*).
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Constant, localized pain in the breast or armpit; ulceration of the skin over the breast; hard, swollen lymph nodes in the collarbone or neck area; bone pain (suggesting spread to bones).
* **Emergency Symptoms:** Severe swelling and redness over the entire breast accompanied by warmth and skin thicking without fever (Inflammatory Breast Cancer - rapid and aggressive).

## Diagnosis

### Clinical & Self-Breast Examination (BSE)
* **BSE:** Educating women to physically feel their breasts once a month (after menstruation) to check for new lumps or changes.
* **Clinical Exam:** Examination by a registered doctor to palpate suspicious lumps and assess lymph nodes.

### Mammography & Breast Ultrasound
* **Mammography:** Low-dose X-ray screening recommended annually for women over 40-45 years to detect microscopic calcium deposits or deep lumps.
* **Breast Ultrasound:** Used in younger women (under 40) who have dense breast tissue, or to check if a lump is solid or a fluid-filled cyst.

### Biopsy (FNAC & Core Needle Biopsy)
* **Core Needle Biopsy:** The gold standard test. A doctor uses a hollow needle to remove small cylinders of tissue from the breast lump to confirm cancer under a microscope and check for hormone receptors (**ER, PR, HER2** status).

## Treatment

### Lifestyle & Preventive Rehabilitation

#### English
* Maintain a healthy body mass index (BMI) through active exercise. Avoid self-medicating with estrogen creams. Participate in post-surgery shoulder mobility exercises to prevent lymphedema (arm swelling).

* नियमित व्यायाम के माध्यम से शरीर का स्वस्थ वजन (BMI) बनाए रखें। गोरेपन या मेनोपॉज के लिए बिना डॉक्टर की सलाह के एस्ट्रोजन युक्त क्रीम का उपयोग न करें। सर्जरी के बाद बांह की सूजन (lymphedema) को रोकने के लिए कंधे के मोबिलिटी व्यायाम करें।

### Home Care during Treatment

#### English
* Keep the skin over the radiated breast area clean and dry, avoiding friction from tight undergarments. Use gentle, non-scented moisturizers under oncology guidance.

### Medical Management

#### English
* Chemotherapy cycles before surgery (neoadjuvant) to shrink the tumor, or after surgery (adjuvant) to prevent relapse. Hormone-blocking tablets (like Tamoxifen) for ER/PR-positive cancers. Targeted therapy (like Trastuzumab) for HER2-positive cancers.

* ट्यूमर को सिकोड़ने के लिए सर्जरी से पहले (neoadjuvant) या सर्जरी के बाद (adjuvant) कीमोथेरेपी चक्र। एस्ट्रोजन रिसेप्टर-पॉजिटिव कैंसर के लिए हार्मोन-ब्लॉकिंग दवाएं (जैसे Tamoxifen)। HER2-पॉजिटिव कैंसर के लिए लक्षित दवाएं (जैसे Trastuzumab)।

#### Hinglish
* Tumor ka size chota karne ke liye surgery se pehle ya bad me chemotherapy cycles dena. Estrogen receptor positive cancer me hormone blocking tablets (jaise Tamoxifen) dena. HER2 positive cases me targeted therapy (jaise Trastuzumab) dena.

### Surgical Interventions

#### English
* **Lumpectomy (Breast-Conserving Surgery):** Removing only the cancer lump along with a clean margin of healthy surrounding tissue, preserving the breast structure.
* **Mastectomy:** Surgical removal of the entire breast tissue to prevent cancer spread.
* **Axillary Lymph Node Dissection:** Removing underarm lymph nodes to check and treat cancer spread.

#### Hindi
* **लम्पेक्टॉमी (स्तन-संरक्षण सर्जरी):** स्तन की मूल संरचना को बनाए रखते हुए केवल कैंसर की गांठ और उसके आसपास के थोड़े से स्वस्थ ऊतक को हटाना।
* **मास्टेक्टॉमी (Mastectomy):** कैंसर के प्रसार को रोकने के लिए पूरे स्तन के ऊतकों को सर्जरी द्वारा निकालना।
* **एक्सिलरी लिम्फ नोड विच्छेदन:** कैंसर के प्रसार की जांच और उपचार के लिए बगल की लसीका ग्रंथियों को हटाना।

#### Hinglish
* **Lumpectomy:** Breast ke size ko bina nuksan pahunche sirf cancer lump (gaanth) ko nikalna.
* **Mastectomy:** Cancer ko phailne se rokne ke liye pure breast tissue ko surgically nikalna.
* **Axillary Dissection:** Cancer check karne ke liye bagal (armpit) ke lymph nodes ko surgically nikalna.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **Hormone Blockers (SERMs):** Block estrogen receptors on breast cancer cells, preventing their growth (e.g., Tamoxifen).
2. **Aromatase Inhibitors (AIs):** Lower estrogen levels in post-menopausal women by blocking the aromatase enzyme (e.g., Anastrozole, Letrozole).
3. **HER2 Inhibitors:** Target and block the HER2 protein receptor that drives aggressive cancer cell division (e.g., Trastuzumab/Herceptin).
4. **Chemotherapy Alkylating Agents:** Destroy cancer cell DNA during replication (e.g., Cyclophosphamide, Paclitaxel).
5. **Lymphedema Management Drugs:** Diuretics or skin protectants used to manage post-surgical arm swelling.

## Prevention

### English
* **Monthly Self-Exams:** Perform a Breast Self-Exam (BSE) every month 3-5 days after your period ends.
* **Mammography Screening:** Schedule a mammogram every 1-2 years once you reach 45 years of age.
* **Avoid Post-Menopausal Weight Gain:** Reduce intake of trans-fats and sugars, as obesity increases circulating estrogen.
* **Promote Breastfeeding:** Breastfeed for at least 12 months if possible, which lowers lifetime breast cancer risk.
* **Limit Alcohol & Quit Smoking:** Avoid tobacco and alcohol to protect cellular DNA.

### Hindi
* **मासिक स्व-जांच:** मासिक धर्म समाप्त होने के 3-5 दिन बाद हर महीने स्व-स्तन परीक्षा (BSE) करें।
* **मैमोग्राफी स्क्रीनिंग:** 45 वर्ष की आयु के बाद हर 1-2 साल में मैमोग्राफी स्क्रीनिंग कराएं।

## Possible Complications
* **Lymphedema:** Painful, chronic swelling of the arm on the side of surgery, caused by the removal of axillary lymph nodes.
* **Metastasis:** Cancer cells spreading to the bones, liver, lungs, or brain, causing advanced organ failure.
* **Cardiac Toxicity:** Weakening of heart muscle cells (cardiomyopathy) as a side effect of certain targeted chemotherapy drugs (like Trastuzumab).

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Sudden, rapid swelling, heat, and redness over the entire breast (Inflammatory Cancer sign).**
* **Sudden severe shortness of breath or chest pain (chemotherapy blood clot/embolism risk).**
* **Sudden weakness, confusion, fits, or loss of consciousness.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you feel any new lump, thickening, or hard knot in your breast or armpit.
* If you notice your nipple pulling inward, or skin over the breast dimpling like an orange peel.
* If you experience spontaneous, bloody discharge from one of your nipples.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a Breast Self-Exam (BSE) and how often should it be done?**
   BSE is a monthly self-examination where a woman looks and feels for any lumps, thickness, or changes in her breasts, done 3-5 days after her period ends.

2. **Is every breast lump a sign of cancer?**
   No. Over 80% of breast lumps are benign (non-cancerous), such as fibroadenomas (simple cysts) or infections. However, every new lump must be evaluated by a doctor.

3. **What is a Mammogram?**
   A mammogram is a specialized low-dose X-ray of the breast used to detect micro-calcifications or lumps before they can be felt physically.

4. **Why is the BRCA gene test done?**
   The BRCA1 and BRCA2 gene test is done to check for inherited genetic mutations that significantly increase the risk of breast and ovarian cancers.

5. **What is the difference between Lumpectomy and Mastectomy?**
   A lumpectomy removes only the breast cancer lump and a small margin of healthy tissue. A mastectomy removes the entire breast tissue.

6. **What is Lymphedema and why does it happen?**
   Lymphedema is chronic swelling of the arm after breast cancer surgery. It happens when axillary lymph nodes are removed, blocking lymph fluid drainage from the arm.

7. **Do men get breast cancer?**
   Yes. Men have a small amount of breast tissue behind the nipple and can develop breast cancer, presenting usually as a hard lump behind the nipple.

8. **What does ER/PR-positive breast cancer mean?**
   It means the cancer cells have receptors for estrogen (ER) and progesterone (PR) hormones. These cancers grow in response to these hormones and are treated with hormone-blocking drugs.

9. **What is Trastuzumab (Herceptin)?**
   Trastuzumab is a targeted therapy drug used to treat HER2-positive breast cancers by locking onto and blocking the HER2 receptor protein.

10. **Where can I get free breast cancer screening in India?**
    Free clinical breast screening is available at government HWCs, and cashless mammography/biopsy tests are covered under Ayushman Bharat at empaneled hospitals.

11. **Why does obesity raise the risk of breast cancer after menopause?**
    After menopause, the ovaries stop producing estrogen. Instead, fat tissue becomes the main source of estrogen. Obesity increases estrogen levels, promoting breast cancer growth.

12. **Can breast implants cause cancer?**
    Standard implants do not cause breast cancer, though a rare type of immune system cancer (BIA-ALCL) has been linked to certain textured breast implants.

13. **Why does breastfeeding reduce breast cancer risk?**
    Breastfeeding delays ovulation and reduces the total number of menstrual cycles a woman has in her lifetime, lowering exposure to estrogen hormones.

14. **What is a Core Needle Biopsy?**
    It is a diagnostic procedure where a hollow needle is inserted into a breast lump to extract small cylinders of tissue for microscopic examination to confirm cancer.

15. **Does wearing tight bras or underwire bras cause breast cancer?**
    No. There is no scientific evidence linking bra types, tight clothing, or underwire bras to the development of breast cancer.

16. **What does "peau d'orange" mean in breast symptoms?**
    "Peau d'orange" means the skin of the breast looks pitted and dimpled like an orange peel, which is a key sign of inflammatory breast cancer blocking lymph vessels.

17. **Can hormone replacement therapy (HRT) cause breast cancer?**
    Yes, long-term use of combined estrogen-progestin HRT for menopausal symptoms increases the risk of breast cancer.

18. **Why is radiation therapy given after a lumpectomy?**
    Radiation therapy is given to destroy any microscopic cancer cells that might have been left behind in the remaining breast tissue, reducing the risk of cancer returning.

19. **What are aromatase inhibitors?**
    Aromatase inhibitors are medications that stop the body from producing estrogen after menopause, used to treat hormone-receptor-positive breast cancers.

20. **Can breast cancer spread to other organs?**
    Yes. Advanced breast cancer can metastasize (spread) through the blood or lymph system to other organs, most commonly the bones, lungs, liver, and brain.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Only women with a family history of breast cancer will get it.
  * **Fact:** Over 85% of women diagnosed with breast cancer have zero family history. It is mostly caused by lifestyle, aging, and environment.
* **Myth:** Deodorants and antiperspirants cause breast cancer.
  * **Fact:** Scientific studies show no link between the use of underarm deodorants or antiperspirants and breast cancer.
* **Myth:** A mammogram is highly painful and causes cancer due to radiation.
  * **Fact:** A mammogram may feel slightly uncomfortable due to compression, but it is not painful. The radiation dose is extremely low and does not cause cancer.
* **Myth:** Fine Needle Aspiration (FNAC) or biopsy makes cancer spread faster.
  * **Fact:** Biopsies are completely safe and do not cause cancer cells to spread. They are necessary to plan the correct treatment.
* **Myth:** If a breast lump is painless, it is not cancer.
  * **Fact:** Most breast cancers present as a completely painless lump. Pain is rarely a symptom of early-stage breast cancer.
* **Myth:** Breast cancer is highly contagious.
  * **Fact:** Cancer is not infectious; you cannot catch breast cancer from someone else by hugging, sharing food, or caregiving.
* **Myth:** Herbal teas and traditional poultices can dissolve breast lumps.
  * **Fact:** Herbal home remedies cannot dissolve cancerous lumps. Delaying medical surgery or chemotherapy to try unverified remedies can be fatal.
* **Myth:** Mastectomy (removing the breast) is the only cure for breast cancer.
  * **Fact:** For many early-stage cancers, a lumpectomy (removing only the lump) followed by radiation is just as effective as a mastectomy.
* **Myth:** Men cannot get breast cancer because they do not have breasts.
  * **Fact:** Men have a small amount of breast tissue behind their nipples and can develop breast cancer, although it is less common than in women.
* **Myth:** A blow or injury to the breast can cause breast cancer.
  * **Fact:** An injury does not cause cancer. However, an injury may prompt a woman to feel her breast, leading to the accidental discovery of a pre-existing lump.

* **Assistant:** ER/PR positive ka matlab hai ki cancer cells estrogen aur progesterone hormones ki madad se grow kar rahe hain. Iska ilaaj hormonal blocking tablets (jaise Tamoxifen ya Letrozole) se bohot acche se kiya jata hai, jo cancer ko wapas aane se rokti hain.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** Kya breast cancer ke surgery ke baad haath me sujan (lymphedema) hona normal hai?
* **Assistant:** Breast surgery me bagal ke lymph nodes nikalne se haath me sujan (lymphedema) ho sakti hai. Isse bachne ke liye haath par heavy weight na uthayein, blood pressure check na karayein, aur regular shoulder mobility exercises karein. Agar sujan badhe, to doctor se consult karein.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Underwire bra peenne se cancer hota hai kya?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, underwire bra ya tight kapde peenne se breast cancer ka koi link nahi hai. Yeh ek galat myth hai. Cancer cells genetic mutations, aging, aur lifestyle factors ke karan badhte hain.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** Breast self-exam (BSE) kaise aur kab karna chahiye?
* **Assistant:** Har mahine periods khatam hone ke 3-5 din baad mirror ke samne khade hokar dono breasts ka size, shape aur nipple retraction check karein. Fir letkar haath se circular motion me breast aur underarm me lumps feel karein.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Does Ayushman card cover the cost of breast cancer surgery?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment benefits are provided for mammography, core biopsy, lumpectomy, mastectomy, and chemotherapy cycles at all government and empaneled hospitals.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Meri behen ko breast cancer diagnosis ke liye core needle biopsy likhi hai. Kya isse cancer phail jayega?
* **Assistant:** Bilkul nahi. Core needle biopsy se cancer ke phailne ka koi risk nahi hota. Yeh ek bilkul safe procedure hai jo cancer ki confirming diagnosis aur hormone receptor status check karne ke liye zaroori hai.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Kya delivery ke baad breastfeed karwane se breast cancer ka risk sach me kam hota hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, research ke anusar jo mahilayein baby ko kam se kam 12 mahine tak breastfeed karwati hain, unme estrogen hormone ka exposure kam hota hai, jisse unme lifetime breast cancer ka risk kafi kam ho jata hai.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Radiation therapy ke dauran skin par kya care karein?
* **Assistant:** Radiation wale hisse par tight fitting innerwear na pehnein. Skin par saaf paani aur doctor ke bataye huye mild, fragrance-free cream hi lagayein. Dhoop ki direct sunlight se us hisse ko bachayein.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Nipple se achanak khoon (bloody discharge) aana kis cheez ka sign hai?
* **Assistant:** Nipple se bina dabaye achanak bloody discharge aana breast ducts ke andar tumor ya cancer (ductal carcinoma) ka sign ho sakta hai. Aapko bina ek bhi din waste kiye surgeon se consult karna chahiye.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the cost of Letrozole tablets at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?
* **Assistant:** Generic Letrozole (2.5mg) tablets used for hormone-positive breast cancer are available at Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, often costing only ₹15 to ₹25 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹150+ for branded ones.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Kya purushon (men) me bhi breast cancer ho sakta hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, purushon me bhi nipples ke piche breast tissues hote hain, isiliye unhe bhi breast cancer ho sakta hai. Agar nipple ke piche koi hard pain-less gaanth feel ho, to turant checkup karana chahiye.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** BRCA1 aur BRCA2 gene test kyu kiya jata hai?
* **Assistant:** Agar kisi ki family me maa, behen, ya mausi ko kam umar me breast ya ovarian cancer hua ho, to inherited genetic risk check karne ke liye BRCA gene test kiya jata hai. Isse cancer hone ke high risk ka pehle hi pata chal jata hai.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Breast ki skin ka santre ke chhilke jaisa (peau d'orange) hona kya dikhata hai?
* **Assistant:** Skin ka santre ke chhilke jaisa pitted aur dimpled ho jana "Inflammatory Breast Cancer" ka sanket ho sakta hai, jisme cancer cells lymph vessels ko block kar dete hain. Yeh ek aggressive cancer hai jisme turant treatment ki zarurat hoti hai.
