# Dizziness (Dizziness/Chakkar) ki Pehchan, Kaaran aur First Aid (Vertigo, Stroke FAST Screening and Fall Prevention)

## Overview

### English
Dizziness is an imprecise term used to describe various sensations, including lightheadedness, unsteadiness, feeling faint (presyncope), or a spinning sensation (**Vertigo**). Pathophysiologically, it is classified into:
1. **Peripheral Vertigo:** Caused by inner ear disorders (e.g., Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo [BPPV], Labyrinthitis).
2. **Central Vertigo / Stroke:** Caused by brainstem or cerebellar lesions (ischemic or hemorrhagic stroke).
3. **Cardiovascular/Systemic:** Orthostatic hypotension (BP drop), dehydration, or hypoglycemia (low blood sugar).
*First aid requires sitting or lying down immediately to prevent falls and performing the **FAST stroke screening** (Face, Arm, Speech, Time).*

## Quick Summary
Dizziness ranges from inner-ear vertigo to life-threatening strokes. Fall prevention requires sitting down immediately. FAST screening excludes brain lesions. Diagnostics check orthostatic blood pressure and head MRIs. Management corrects systemic causes.

---

## Definition
* **Vertigo:** A specific type of dizziness characterized by a false sensation of spinning or rotational movement of oneself or the environment.
* **Orthostatic Hypotension:** A sudden drop in blood pressure (usually $> 20\text{ mmHg}$ systolic) that occurs when standing up quickly from a sitting or lying position, causing temporary dizziness.
* **FAST Screening:** A rapid clinical assessment tool for stroke: **F**ace drooping, **A**rm weakness, **S**peech difficulty, **T**ime to call emergency services.

---

## Differential Diagnosis: Vertigo vs. Presyncope vs. Central Stroke

Clinicians must distinguish harmless inner-ear vertigo from systemic cardiac or stroke issues:

| Diagnostic Feature | Inner Ear Vertigo (BPPV/Labyrinthitis) | Presyncope (Dehydration / Low BP) | Central Stroke (Cerebellar Infarction) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sensation** | **Rotational spinning** ( room is turning ) | Lightheadedness, feeling about to faint | Loss of balance, unsteadiness |
| **Nausea/Vomiting** | Severe, triggered by head movement | Mild or absent | Persistent, severe |
| **Nystagmus (Eye movement)** | Present (horizontal/rotatory, fatiguable)| Absent | **Present (vertical, non-fatiguable)** |
| **Neurological Signs**| Absent | Tunnel vision, pale skin | **Face drooping, arm weakness, slurred speech** |
| **Triggers** | Turning head in bed, looking up | Standing up quickly, hot environment | Sudden onset, independent of movement |
| *Action* | **Epley Maneuver** / Betahistine | Rehydration, lie down with legs elevated | **Immediate 108 call for Brain MRI** |

## Symptoms & Danger Signs of Dizziness

### English
* **Normal Symptoms:** Transient unsteady feeling, mild wooziness, resolving with rest.
* **Neurological Danger Signs (Emergency):** **FAST positive: Face drooping on one side; arm weakness (one arm drifts down when raised); slurred or abnormal speech.**
* **Cardiovascular Danger Signs:** Chest pain, palpitations, or fainting (syncope) during dizziness; sudden double vision or inability to walk straight (ataxia).

### Hindi
* **सामान्य लक्षण:** थोड़ी देर के लिए सिर घूमना जो बैठने पर ठीक हो जाए।
* **स्ट्रोक के खतरे के संकेत (Emergency):** **चेहरे का एक तरफ लटकना (Face drooping); एक हाथ में कमजोरी महसूस होना; बोलने में हकलाहट होना;** आंखों के सामने अचानक दोहरा (double vision) दिखाई देना या चलने में संतुलन खोना।

### Hinglish
* **Normal Symptoms:** Achanak khade hone par 2-3 seconds ke liye sir ghumna aur thik ho jana.
* **Stroke ke Danger Signs (Emergency):** **FAST positive: Face ka ek side se deela padna (Face Drooping); ek haath uthane par kamjori ke karan niche girna (Arm weakness); bolne me ladkhadahat (Slurred speech).**
* **Other Warning Signs:** Chakkar ke sath chest me dard hona ya behosh (syncope) ho jana.

## Step-by-Step Fall Prevention & First Aid Protocol

If a patient is experiencing acute dizziness, execute this protective protocol:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Sit or lie down immediately.** If you feel dizzy, sit down on the floor or lie flat on a bed. Do not try to walk to another room. Lying down increases blood flow to the brain, resolving lightheadedness.
* **Elevate the legs.** If the patient feels faint, have them lie flat on their back and raise their legs **$12\text{ Inches}$** using pillows. This drains pooled venous blood back to the heart and brain.
* **Perform the FAST stroke check.** Ask the patient to smile (check for symmetry), raise both arms (check for drift), and speak a simple sentence. If any of these are failed, note the time and call **108**.
* **Rehydrate slowly.** If the dizziness is due to heat or dehydration, give a glass of water, coconut water, or ORS.
* **Maintain blood sugar.** If the patient is diabetic and skipped a meal, check their finger-stick glucose. If low, give 15g of fast-acting sugar (3 teaspoons of sugar dissolved in water or fruit juice).

#### Hindi
* **तुरंत बैठें या लेट जाएं:** यह दिमाग में रक्त संचार बढ़ाता है और गिरने से बचाता है।
* **पैरों को ऊपर उठाएं (Leg Elevation):** मरीज को सीधा लिटाकर पैरों के नीचे तकिया लगाएं।
* **FAST परीक्षण करें:** यदि चेहरा लटके या हाथ में कमजोरी हो, तो तुरंत आपातकालीन सहायता लें।
* **धीरे-धीरे पानी या ओआरएस (ORS) पिलाएं।**
* **शुगर चेक करें (Hypoglycemia):** शुगर कम होने पर चीनी-पानी पिलाएं।

#### Hinglish
* **Turant baith ya let jayein:** Chakkar aate hi bina kisi support ke khade rehne ki galti na karein. Fauran floor par baith jayein ya bed par flat let jayein. Letne se brain me blood supply badh jati hai aur chakkar normal ho jate hain.
* **Pairon ko upar uthayein (Leg Elevation):** Agar patient behosh hone lage, to unhe leta kar dono **legs ko 12 inches upar** lift karein (pillows rakh kar). Isse blood direct brain me flow karega.
* **FAST stroke check karein:** Patient ko smile karne ko bolein (check karein face tida to nahi hai), dono arms lift karne ko bolein aur baat karwayein. Agar koi asymmetry hai to time note karein aur immediate shift karein.
* **ORS ya Sugar pani dein:** Agar dehydration se hai to gungune paani me ORS ghol kar dein. Agar diabetic patient ka sugar drop hua hai to 3 chammach sugar paani me ghol kar pilayein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT try to stand up quickly.**
* **Do NOT drive a vehicle or operate machinery** if you have had dizziness.
* **Do NOT give fluids to an unconscious patient.**
* **Do NOT ignore a vertical spinning eye movement (nystagmus).**

#### Hindi
* **चक्कर आने पर अचानक उठकर खड़े न हों।**
* **वाहन न चलाएं।**
* **बेहोश मरीज को मुंह से कोई तरल पदार्थ न दें।**

* **Chakkar aane par driving na karein.**
* **Behosh patient ko paani na pilayein.**

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Orthostatic BP Measurement:** Blood pressure is measured while lying flat, and then repeated after standing up for 3 minutes to test for orthostatic drop.
* **Finger-stick Glucometer Test:** Rapidly measures blood glucose to rule out hypoglycemia.
* **Brain MRI (with stroke protocol):** Mandatory if central vertigo is suspected, to check for cerebellar infarction or bleed.

---

## Medical & Surgical Management
* **Canalith Repositioning (Epley Maneuver):** A physical maneuver performed by doctors to move calcium stones (otoconia) out of the inner ear canals, curing positional vertigo (BPPV) without medicines.
* **Thrombolytic Therapy:** Giving IV Alteplase (clot-buster) within the **4.5-hour golden window** if the dizziness is caused by an acute ischemic stroke.

## Prevention of Dizziness & Falls
* **Hydration Compliance:** Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily, especially in hot weather.
* **Slow Transitions:** Sit on the edge of the bed for 1 minute before standing up in the morning.
* **Fall-Proof Home:** Keep floors dry, remove loose carpets, and install grab bars in toilets for elderly patients with chronic dizziness.

---

## Possible Complications of Unmanaged Dizziness
* **Severe Head Injury / Hip Fracture:** Caused by sudden falls during a dizzy spell, which is highly dangerous in older adults.
* **Permanent Disability:** If a stroke causing dizziness is not treated within the 4.5-hour window.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient fails any part of the FAST stroke screening (Face, Arm, Speech, Time).**
* **Dizziness is accompanied by sudden chest pain, palpitations, or shortness of breath.**
* **The patient has a sudden loss of coordination, inability to speak, or double vision.**
* **The patient faints and does not regain consciousness within 1 minute.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is vertigo?**
   Vertigo is a specific type of dizziness where the patient feels a false sensation that they or their surroundings are spinning or rotating.

2. **Why does standing up quickly cause dizziness?**
   This is called **Orthostatic Hypotension**. When you stand up quickly, gravity pulls blood down to your legs, temporarily reducing blood flow to the brain before the blood vessels can constrict.

3. **What is the FAST test for stroke?**
   FAST stands for: **F**ace drooping, **A**rm weakness (one arm drifts down), **S**peech slurring, **T**ime to call 108. It is used to quickly detect a stroke.

4. **What is the Epley Maneuver?**
   The Epley Maneuver is a sequence of head movements performed by a doctor to relocate displaced calcium crystals in the inner ear, curing positional vertigo (BPPV) instantly without drugs.

5. **How does low blood sugar (hypoglycemia) cause giddiness?**
   The brain requires a constant supply of glucose to function. When blood sugar drops below $70\text{ mg/dL}$, it causes brain cells to starve, leading to dizziness, sweating, and confusion.

6. **What is the price of generic Betahistine at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
   Generic Betahistine (16mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹18 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹80-₹120 in private medical stores.

7. **How does inner ear inflammation cause dizziness?**
   The inner ear (labyrinth) controls your body's balance. Viral infections causing inflammation (labyrinthitis) send mismatched balance signals to the brain, triggering severe vertigo and vomiting.

8. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover stroke treatment?**
   Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for diagnostic brain CT/MRI scans, emergency clot-busting stroke injections (thrombolysis), and ICU hospitalizations.

9. **Why is a vertical eye movement (nystagmus) a warning sign?**
   Horizontal eye movement is common in harmless inner ear vertigo. **Vertical nystagmus** (eyes beating up and down) indicates a central brain stem or cerebellar lesion, requiring an immediate MRI to rule out stroke.

10. **Why should an elderly person sit on the bed edge before standing up?**
    Sitting on the bed edge for a minute allows the cardiovascular system to adapt to the change in posture, preventing a sudden drop in blood pressure and subsequent falls.

11. **What is Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo (BPPV)?**
    BPPV is the most common cause of vertigo. It occurs when tiny calcium crystals in the inner ear become loose and float into the semicircular canals, causing brief spinning spells when turning the head.

12. **Can severe dehydration cause chakkar?**
    Yes. Dehydration reduces overall blood volume, lowering blood pressure and decreasing oxygen delivery to the brain, especially when standing.

13. **What is the golden window for stroke treatment?**
    The golden window is **4.5 hours** from the start of symptoms. Injecting clot-dissolving medicines within this time can reverse stroke damage.

14. **Why is giving water to a fainting person dangerous?**
    An unconscious or drowsy person cannot swallow correctly. Giving water can cause the fluid to enter the windpipe (aspiration), leading to choking or severe lung infection.

15. **What is the difference between syncope and dizziness?**
    Dizziness is the feeling of being unsteady or spinning. Syncope is temporary loss of consciousness (fainting) due to a sudden drop in blood flow to the brain.

16. **How does high blood pressure cause dizziness?**
    Extremely high blood pressure (hypertensive crisis) increases pressure in the brain's blood vessels, causing headache, dizziness, and raising the risk of hemorrhagic stroke.

17. **What is a Cerebellar Stroke?**
    It is a stroke affecting the cerebellum, the balance center of the brain. It presents with severe vertigo, inability to walk, and slurred speech, without classic face or arm paralysis.

18. **What is the role of Tele-MANAS in managing dizziness?**
    If dizziness is a psychosomatic symptom of severe panic attacks or anxiety, Tele-MANAS (14416) provides free, 24/7 counseling support.

19. **How is orthostatic blood pressure tested?**
    The doctor measures blood pressure while the patient is lying down, then has the patient stand up and remeasures the BP at 1 and 3 minutes. A drop of $> 20\text{ mmHg}$ systolic is positive.

20. **Is generic Cinnarizine as effective as branded versions?**
    Yes. Generic Cinnarizine tablets contain the exact same active calcium channel blocker and suppress inner ear balance signals just as effectively, at an 80% lower cost.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** If you feel dizzy, you should stand up and walk around to get fresh air.
  * **Fact:** Walking during a dizzy spell increases your risk of falling and sustaining a head injury or fracture. Sit or lie down immediately.
* **Myth:** Chakkar is always caused by weakness and can be cured by eating heavy meals.
  * **Fact:** Giddiness can be caused by inner ear disorders, low BP, heart disease, or a stroke. Eating food will not cure these conditions.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures dizziness instantly by absorbing negative energy.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Recovering from dizziness requires sitting down and diagnosing the underlying cause.
* **Myth:** Vertigo is a brain tumor and is always untreatable.
  * **Fact:** Most vertigo is caused by BPPV, a harmless inner ear issue that is highly treatable with simple physical movements like the Epley Maneuver.
* **Myth:** If a stroke patient is dizzy, giving them hot tea immediately cures the stroke.
  * **Fact:** Drowsy stroke patients can choke on liquids. They require immediate transport to a hospital for clot-busting therapy.
* **Myth:** High BP never causes dizziness; it is only caused by low BP.
  * **Fact:** Both extremely low BP and extremely high BP can cause dizziness. Monitor blood pressure to confirm.
* **Myth:** Branded expensive vertigo tablets work faster than Jan Aushadhi generic versions.
  * **Fact:** Generic Betahistine tablets meet the same CDSCO standards and contain the exact same active medicine, working identically at a much lower cost.
* **Myth:** Fainting (syncope) is just a sign of seeking attention and does not need a doctor.
  * **Fact:** Fainting indicates a sudden drop in brain blood flow. It can be caused by heart arrhythmias or valve blocks, requiring medical checkups.
* **Myth:** All dizzy patients should be given aspirin immediately.
  * **Fact:** Aspirin is only for suspected heart attacks. If the dizziness is due to a bleeding stroke or ear issue, aspirin is not the primary treatment.
* **Myth:** Once a vertigo spell ends, you are permanently cured.
  * **Fact:** Positional vertigo (BPPV) can recur if the ear crystals shift again. Daily balance exercises help prevent recurrences.
