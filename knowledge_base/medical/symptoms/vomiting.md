# Vomiting (Vomiting/Ulti) ki Pehchan, Kaaran aur First Aid (Ondansetron, Projectile Vomiting and Dehydration Guide)

## Overview

### English
Vomiting, clinically termed **Emesis**, is the forceful oral expulsion of gastric contents, coordinated by the vomiting center in the medulla oblongata. Pathophysiologically, it is triggered by activation of the chemoreceptor trigger zone (CTZ) or vagal afferents. Key clinical categories include:
1. **Gastroenteritis / Food Poisoning:** Accompanied by diarrhea, resolving with oral rehydration and antiemetics (**Ondansetron**).
2. **Raised Intracranial Pressure (ICP):** Classic **projectile vomiting** (without warning nausea) accompanied by severe headache, indicating brain mass or hemorrhage.
3. **Bowel Obstruction:** Bilious (green) vomiting with severe abdominal pain.
*First aid requires side-lying recovery position to prevent choking (aspiration).*

### Hindi
उल्टी आना (Vomiting / उल्टी या एमिसिस), पेट की सामग्री का मुंह के रास्ते जबरन बाहर निकलना है। यह पाचन तंत्र की गड़बड़ी (gastroenteritis), भोजन विषाक्तता (food poisoning), मस्तिष्क में बढ़े दबाव (Raised ICP) या आंतों में रुकावट (Bowel Obstruction) का लक्षण हो सकता है। प्राथमिक उपचार में निर्जलीकरण (Dehydration) को रोकना, एंटीएमेटिक दवा (**Ondansetron**) देना और उल्टी के दौरान सांस की नली में रुकावट से बचने के लिए मरीज को करवट (side recovery position) दिलाकर लिटाना शामिल है। **उल्टी में खून आना (Hematemesis) या बिना जी मिचलाए अचानक दूर तक फेंकने वाली उल्टी (Projectile Vomiting) गंभीर खतरे के संकेत हैं।**

## Quick Summary
Emesis represents the neuro-mediated expulsion of gastric contents. Projectile vomiting without nausea signals raised ICP. Bilious vomiting suggests obstruction. First aid requires side-lying recovery positions and antiemetics. Diagnostics check serum electrolytes. Management corrects dehydration.

## Definition
* **Projectile Vomiting:** Forceful vomiting where stomach contents are ejected several feet away, typically occurring suddenly without preceding nausea, indicating raised intracranial pressure or pyloric stenosis in infants.
* **Aspiration Pneumonia:** A life-threatening lung infection caused by accidentally inhaling vomit or oral fluids into the lungs, common when an unconscious or convulsing patient vomits while lying flat on their back.
* **Hematemesis:** The vomiting of blood, which may look bright red or dark brown like "coffee grounds," indicating active bleeding in the upper gastrointestinal tract (stomach ulcer or esophageal tear).

---

## How to Tell the Difference: Food Poisoning vs. Raised ICP vs. Bowel Obstruction

It is clinically vital to identify the cause of vomiting to guide correct first aid:

| Diagnostic Feature | Food Poisoning / Gastroenteritis | Raised Brain Pressure (ICP) | Bowel Obstruction (Blockage) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nausea / Ghabrahat** | **Severe preceding nausea**, sweating | **Absent** (vomits suddenly without warning) | Variable, mild |
| **Vomiting Character**| Food particles, yellow bile | **Projectile** (shoots out forcefully) | **Green/bilious** (bile), later fecal-like |
| **Associated Symptoms**| **Watery diarrhea**, mild abdominal cramps | **Severe headache, double vision, drowsiness** | **Severe crampy abdominal pain, swollen stomach, no gas** |
| **Triggers** | Eating outside food, viral exposure | Head injury, high BP, stroke | Prior abdominal surgery, hernia |
| *First-Line Drug* | **ORS + Ondansetron** (Zofran) | **Urgent Head CT scan** (no oral drugs) | **Urgent laparotomy surgery** (NPO - nothing by mouth) |

## Symptoms & Danger Signs of Vomiting (Red Flags)

### English
* **Gastrointestinal Danger Signs:** Vomiting bright red blood or "coffee ground" material (Hematemesis); persistent green/yellow bilious vomiting accompanied by a swollen, painful stomach and inability to pass gas (Bowel Obstruction).
* **Neurological Danger Signs:** Projectile vomiting after a head injury; vomiting accompanied by severe headache, neck stiffness, confusion, or difficulty speaking.
* **Dehydration Signs:** Sunken eyes, dry tongue, no urine for $> 6$ hours, lethargy.

## Step-by-Step Emergency First Aid Protocol

If a patient is vomiting continuously, execute this protective protocol:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Place the patient in the recovery position.** If the patient is drowsy, confused, or having a seizure, have them lie on their side (left lateral recovery position). This keeps the airway open and allows vomit to drain out of the mouth, preventing fatal **Aspiration Pneumonia**.
* **Administer Ondansetron (4mg)** orally (fast-dissolving tablet) if the patient has simple gastroenteritis and can tolerate oral intake. Wait 15-20 minutes before offering liquids.
* **Rehydrate slowly.** Once vomiting has paused (after antiemetics), give small sips of WHO ORS (1-2 teaspoons every 1-2 minutes for children, or 100-200ml for adults). *Do not let them gulp large amounts of water, as this stretches the stomach wall and triggers immediate rebound vomiting.*
* **NPO for suspected obstruction.** If the patient has a swollen stomach, severe pain, and green vomiting, keep them strictly NPO (nothing by mouth) and call **108**.

#### Hindi
* **मरीज को करवट (left side recovery position) दिलाकर लिटाएं:** यदि मरीज अचेत हो, तो सीधा न लिटाएं (उल्टी फेफड़ों में जाने का खतरा)।
* **ओनडांसेट्रॉन (Ondansetron 4mg) दें:** उल्टी रोकने की सुरक्षित दवा है।
* **धीरे-धीरे ओआरएस (ORS) पिलाएं:** चम्मच से थोड़े-थोड़े अंतराल पर पिलाएं।
* **आंतों की रुकावट के संकेत होने पर मुंह से कुछ न दें (NPO)।**

#### Hinglish
* **Patient ko left side recovery position (karwat) me letayein:** Agar patient behosh, drowsy ya fits aa rahe hon, to unhe flat letaane ki galti na karein. Karwat dilakar letaane se ulti bahar nikal jati hai aur saans ki nali me nahi phasti.
* **Ondansetron (4mg) tablet dein:** Ulti rokkne ke liye **Ondansetron (Mouth dissolving tablet)** ko tongue par rakhein. Yeh safe aur fast-acting antiemetic hai.
* **ORS patla pilayein:** Goli ke 20 mins baad ORS ke chote-chote sips dein (chammach se 1-2 drops har minut me bachhon ko). Achanak 1 bada glass paani peene se stomach stretch hoga aur dobara ulti trigger ho jayegi.
* **Bowel blockage me NPO rakhein.**

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT force an actively vomiting patient to drink.** Wait for the stomach to settle.
* **Do NOT give any oral fluids to an unconscious patient.**
* **Do NOT suppress projectile vomiting after head injury** with over-the-counter antiemetics. It is a neurological symptom requiring an immediate brain CT scan, not a stomach syrup.

* **बेहोश मरीज को पानी न पिलाएं।**

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Serum Electrolytes (Sodium, Potassium, Chloride):** Mandatory check in persistent vomiting to diagnose electrolyte depletion (e.g., hypokalemic hypochloremic metabolic alkalosis).
* **Finger-stick Glucometer check:** Rules out Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA), which presents with persistent vomiting and rapid deep breathing.
* **Non-contrast Head CT Scan:** Mandatory if projectile vomiting, headache, or head injury history is present.

---

## Medical & Surgical Management
* **Intravenous Rehydration:** Rapid infusion of Normal Saline or Ringer's Lactate in the emergency ward to restore blood volume.
* **IV Antiemetics:** Injectable Ondansetron (4mg/2ml) or Metoclopramide (10mg/2ml) administered to stop severe vomiting.
* **Nasogastric (NG) Tube Decompression:** A tube is passed through the nose into the stomach to drain accumulated green fluids in bowel obstruction.

## Prevention of Vomiting Episodes
* **Avoid Food Poisoning:** Eat fresh, hot, hygienic food. Avoid street food left in the open.
* **Slow Rehydration:** Take small, frequent sips of water during gastroenteritis.
* **Vaccination:** Rotavirus vaccine prevents infant viral gastroenteritis.

---

## Possible Complications of Chronic Vomiting
* **Severe Metabolic Alkalosis:** Loss of hydrochloric acid from the stomach causes blood pH to rise.
* **Mallory-Weiss Tear:** Forceful vomiting tears the esophagus lining, causing bleeding.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient vomits bright red blood or coffee-ground material.**
* **The patient has projectile vomiting without nausea, or vomiting starts after a head injury.**
* **The patient has green/bilious vomiting, severe abdominal pain, and has not passed gas for 24 hours.**
* **The patient is lethargic, confused, or unresponsive.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is projectile vomiting?**
   Projectile vomiting is the forceful ejection of stomach contents several feet away, often occurring suddenly without preceding nausea, signaling raised brain pressure.

2. **Why is the recovery position important during vomiting?**
   Placing a drowsy or convulsing patient in the side recovery position allows vomit to drain out of the mouth safely, preventing it from entering the lungs (aspiration).

3. **What is Ondansetron?**
   Ondansetron is a safe, effective antiemetic medicine that blocks serotonin signals triggering the vomiting reflex, without causing drowsiness.

4. **Why does a bowel obstruction cause green vomiting?**
   A blockage in the intestines prevents fluid from moving down. Bile (a green digestive fluid made by the liver) backs up into the stomach, resulting in green, bilious vomiting.

5. **What is Hematemesis?**
   Hematemesis is the medical term for vomiting blood, indicating active bleeding in the stomach or food pipe, requiring immediate emergency care.

6. **What is the price of generic Ondansetron (4mg) at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
   Generic Ondansetron (4mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹15 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹40-₹60 in private pharmacies.

7. **How does low blood potassium occur after vomiting?**
   Vomiting expels gastric acid and fluids. The kidneys respond by excreting potassium, leading to **Hypokalemia**, which causes muscle weakness and cramps.

8. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover bowel blockage surgeries?**
   Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for diagnostic CT scans, exploratory laparotomy surgeries, nasogastric decompression, IV fluids, and ICU care.

9. **Why is drinking large cups of water bad immediately after vomiting?**
    Gulping large amounts of fluid stretches the irritated stomach wall, triggering a stretch-receptor reflex that causes immediate, painful rebound vomiting.

10. **What is a Mallory-Weiss tear?**
    It is a small tear in the lining of the food pipe (esophagus) caused by the extreme physical force of severe, repetitive vomiting, leading to blood streaks in vomit.

11. **What is a non-contrast head CT scan?**
    It is a quick brain scan done without dye. It is used in patients with projectile vomiting to check for brain swelling or internal bleeding.

12. **Can a diabetic patient vomit due to high sugar?**
    Yes. Extremely high blood sugar causes **Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)**, a medical emergency characterized by persistent vomiting, severe dehydration, and deep rapid breathing.

13. **What is the role of a nasogastric (NG) tube in green vomiting?**
    An NG tube is passed through the nose into the stomach to continuously drain trapped bile and fluids, relieving abdominal pressure and preventing aspiration.

14. **Why should Metoclopramide not be given to children without advice?**
    Metoclopramide can cross the blood-brain barrier in children, causing severe muscle spasms and neck twisting (extrapyramidal side effects). Use Ondansetron instead.

15. **What is the first aid for motion sickness vomiting?**
    Sit in the front seat of the vehicle, keep eyes on the horizon, ensure fresh air flow, and take a preventative antihistamine (Promethazine) 30 minutes before travel.

16. **Why is hand washing important in preventing food poisoning?**
    Bacteria like *Staphylococcus* are transferred from hands to food. Washing hands with soap blocks the contamination of food, preventing gastroenteritis.

17. **Can kidney failure cause nausea and vomiting?**
    Yes. When kidneys fail, toxic waste products (like urea) build up in the blood. This condition, called **Uremia**, triggers the brain's vomiting center.

18. **How does generic Domperidone work?**
    Domperidone is a prokinetic drug. It increases gut motility and speeds up stomach emptying, reducing nausea and bloating.

19. **What is the role of ASHA workers in managing childhood vomiting?**
    ASHA workers identify dehydrated children, distribute WHO ORS packets, and coordinate immediate transport to primary health centers for IV fluids.

20. **Is generic Ondansetron syrup safe for toddlers?**
    Yes. Generic Ondansetron syrup is highly safe and effective for controlling vomiting in toddlers when dosed correctly based on weight under medical advice.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** If you have severe vomiting, you must drink 2 big glasses of ice-cold water immediately to wash the stomach.
  * **Fact:** Gulping water stretches the stomach wall, triggering immediate rebound vomiting. Take only small, frequent sips of ORS after the stomach settles.
* **Myth:** Projectile vomiting after a head injury is just a sign of normal fear and can be cured by sleeping.
  * **Fact:** Projectile vomiting after head trauma is a sign of brain swelling or bleeding. It is a critical neurological emergency requiring an immediate CT scan.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures vomiting and nausea instantly.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Antiemetic medications (Ondansetron) or ORS are required to resolve vomiting and dehydration.
* **Myth:** Giving an unconscious person water helps them wake up faster after fainting.
  * **Fact:** Unconscious people cannot swallow. Giving them liquids will cause the fluid to enter their lungs, causing fatal choking or aspiration pneumonia.
* **Myth:** Green vomiting is just "excess bile" from skipping breakfast and is completely harmless.
  * **Fact:** Persistent green, bilious vomiting is a warning sign of a physical blockage in the intestines (bowel obstruction), requiring urgent surgery.
* **Myth:** You must take generic antibiotics (like Norflox-TZ) for every episode of vomiting.
  * **Fact:** Most vomiting is caused by viral gastroenteritis or toxins in food, where antibiotics are useless. Focus on ORS and Ondansetron.
* **Myth:** Senda namak (rock salt) water is a complete replacement for ORS in severe vomiting.
  * **Fact:** Rock salt water does not contain glucose and potassium. Glucose is required for the gut to absorb sodium. Only WHO ORS has the correct balance.
* **Myth:** Branded expensive anti-vomit syrups are safer than government generic versions.
  * **Fact:** Generic Ondansetron syrups are manufactured under strict CDSCO quality control and cure vomiting just as effectively, at an 80% lower cost.
* **Myth:** If a child is vomiting, you should stop breastfeeding them immediately.
  * **Fact:** Continue breastfeeding. Breast milk is easily digested, provides essential antibodies, and prevents dehydration during gastroenteritis.
* **Myth:** Vomiting blood is always caused by eating spicy foods.
  * **Fact:** Vomiting blood (hematemesis) indicates active bleeding from a stomach ulcer, esophageal tear, or liver disease. It requires immediate medical evaluation.
