# Constipation (Constipation/Kabz) ki Pehchan, Kaaran aur Upachar (Dietary Fiber, Laxatives and Obstruction Warnings)

## Overview

### English
Constipation is a common gastrointestinal symptom characterized by infrequent bowel movements (typically **fewer than 3 times per week**), difficulty or straining during defecation, and the passage of hard, lumpy, or dry stools. Pathophysiologically, it arises from slowed colonic transit time, allowing the large intestine to absorb excessive water from the stool. Causes include low dietary fiber, dehydration, lack of physical activity, and medications (e.g., iron supplements, opioids). **First aid relies on increasing soluble fiber (Psyllium husk / Isabgol) and water. However, severe abdominal pain, vomiting, and the inability to pass gas (flatus) indicate a life-threatening Bowel Obstruction emergency.**

## Quick Summary
Constipation is characterized by infrequent, hard stools. Causes include dehydration and low fiber. Bowel obstruction signs (vomiting, no flatus) are surgical emergencies. Diagnostics check abdominal X-rays. Management uses bulk-forming and osmotic laxatives.

---

## Definition
* **Psyllium Husk (Isabgol):** A natural bulk-forming laxative rich in soluble fiber that absorbs water in the gut, making stools soft and easy to pass.
* **Bowel Obstruction:** A mechanical or functional blockage of the small or large intestine, preventing the passage of food, fluids, and gas, requiring urgent surgical care.
* **Laxative Abuse:** The chronic, unprescribed use of stimulant laxatives (like Senna), which damages the colonic nerves, leading to "lazy bowel" and permanent dependency.

---

## Clinical Comparison of Laxatives

Different laxatives work via distinct pharmacological mechanisms:

| Laxative Class | Clinical Examples | Mechanism of Action | Safe for Long-term Use? | Target Patients |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bulk-Forming** | **Psyllium Husk (Isabgol)**, Methylcellulose | Absorbs water, swells the stool, stimulates natural contractions | **Yes (Safest)** | Chronic constipation, pregnancy |
| **Osmotic** | **Lactulose Syrup**, Polyethylene Glycol (PEG)| Draws water into the colon chemically, softening the stool | Yes (Under medical guidance) | Elderly, pediatric constipation |
| **Stool Softeners**| Liquid Paraffin (Cremaffin) | Lubricates the stool surface, reducing pain during passage | No (Blocks vitamin absorption) | Hemorrhoids (piles), post-surgery |
| **Stimulant** | Bisacodyl (Dulcolax), Senna | Irritates bowel lining, forcing immediate contractions | **No (Causes dependency)** | Acute relief only (max 3-5 days) |

## Symptoms of Constipation & Obstruction Danger Signs

### English
* **Normal Symptoms:** Fewer than 3 bowel movements per week; hard, lumpy, or dry stools; straining and feeling of incomplete evacuation; mild bloating.
* **Obstruction Danger Signs (Emergency):** **Severe, constant, or crampy abdominal pain; persistent vomiting (can be green/bilious); a completely distended (swollen) stomach; inability to pass both stool and gas (flatus).**

* **सामान्य लक्षण:** सप्ताह में ३ से कम बार मल आना; कड़ा और सूखा मल; पेट फूलना।
* **आंतों में रुकावट के आपातकालीन संकेत:** **पेट में असहनीय मरोड़ और दर्द; लगातार उल्टी आना; पेट का फूल जाना; मल और पेट की गैस (flatus) दोनों का बिल्कुल बंद हो जाना।**

### Hinglish
* **Normal Symptoms:** Week me 3 bar se kam pet saaf hona; stool ka dry aur hard hona; pet me halka bhari-pan (bloating).
* **Emergency Signs (Obstruction):** **Pet me extreme crampy pain (marod) hona; baar-bar ulti (vomiting) hona; pet ka achanak balloon ki tarah phool jana; aur piche se na to stool aur na hi gas (flatus) pass hona.**

## Step-by-Step Care & Dietary Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Increase dietary fiber slowly.** Eat whole grains (oats, brown rice), green vegetables (broccoli, spinach), and fruits (papaya, apples, prunes). Target **$25 - 30\text{ grams}$** of fiber daily.
* **Drink 3-4 liters of water daily.** Fiber requires water to work; if you take fiber supplements without drinking enough water, the fiber will clump and make constipation worse.
* **Use Isabgol (Psyllium Husk) correctly.** Mix 1-2 tablespoons of Isabgol in a full glass of warm water or milk, drink it immediately, and **follow it with a second full glass of water** at bedtime.
* **Respond to the urge immediately.** Do not delay bowel movements, as this allows the colon to absorb more water, hardening the stool.

#### Hindi
* **आहार में फाइबर (Fiber) बढ़ाएं:** हरी सब्जियां, फल और ओट्स खाएं।
* **प्रतिदिन ३-४ लीटर पानी पिएं:** पर्याप्त पानी के बिना फाइबर कब्ज को बढ़ा सकता है।
* **ईसबगोल को एक बड़े गिलास पानी में मिलाकर पिएं** और उसके बाद एक अतिरिक्त गिलास पानी अवश्य पिएं।
* **शारीरिक रूप से सक्रिय रहें (walking)।**

#### Hinglish
* **Diet me Fiber badhayein:** Rojana oats, chokar wali roti (whole wheat), green vegetables (palak) aur fruits (papita) khayein.
* **Daily 3-4 liters paani pिएं:** Fiber supplements tabhi kaam karte hain jab body me paryapt paani ho. Agar paani kam piyenge to fiber aanto me jam jayega aur kabz severe ho jayega.
* **Isabgol sahi tarike se lein:** 1-2 chammach Isabgol powder ko **1 bade glass gungune paani ya milk me mix karein aur turant pi lein, aur uske baad 1 glass saada paani aur piyein**.
* **Daily walking/physical activity karein.**

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT take stimulant laxatives (Bisacodyl/Senna) daily.** Limit their use to 3-5 days. Prolonged use destroys the myenteric plexus (nerves) of the colon, leading to a non-functioning "atonic colon" (melanosis coli) where the patient cannot pass stool at all without drugs.
* **Do NOT give mineral oil or liquid paraffin to infants or sleeping adults.** If accidentally inhaled into the lungs, it causes fatal lipid pneumonia.
* **Do NOT take laxatives if you suspect bowel obstruction.**
* **Do NOT strain forcefully.**

#### Hindi
* **बिसाकोडिल (Bisacodyl/Dulcolax) गोलियों का दैनिक सेवन न करें:** इससे आंतों की नसें मृत हो सकती हैं और दवा पर निर्भरता (dependency) बन सकती है।
* **कब्ज होने पर अधिक चाय या कॉफी न पिएं:** कैफीन शरीर को निर्जलित (dehydrate) करता है।

#### Hinglish
* **Dulcolax (Bisacodyl) daily na khayein:** Bisacodyl ya Senna tablets ko rojana khane se aanto ki nerves permanently damage ho jati hain, jisse aante natural contraction band kar deti hain. Ise **Laxative Dependency** (dawa ki aadat tharna) kehte hain. Ise maximum 3-5 din hi lein.
* **Bowel obstruction me laxatives na lein:** Agar aant me blockage ke warning signs (vomiting, no flatus) hon, to laxative tablet bilkul na khayein, isse aant phat (rupture) sakti hai.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Abdominal X-ray (Erect):** Mandatory test check for dilated bowel loops, air-fluid levels (confirming obstruction), or fecal impaction.
* **Thyroid Profile (TSH):** Evaluates for hypothyroidism, a major systemic cause of chronic constipation.
* **Colonoscopy:** Done in patients aged $> 45$ with chronic constipation and weight loss to rule out colorectal cancer.

---

## Medical & Surgical Management
* **Enema Administration:** Injecting liquid (glycerin or saline enema) into the rectum to soften hard impacted stool for immediate evacuation.
* **Emergency Laparotomy:** Urgent abdominal surgery performed to clear mechanical bowel obstruction (e.g., volvulus, intussusception, or tumor blockage) and remove dead gut tissue.

## Prevention of Chronic Constipation
* **Establish a Regular Bowel Routine:** Sit on the toilet at the same time every morning (gastrocolic reflex).
* **Use a Squatty Potty / Squatting Position:** Squatting straightens the puborectalis muscle, allowing the rectum to empty completely without straining.
* **Avoid Dehydration.**

---

## Possible Complications of Chronic Constipation
* **Hemorrhoids (Piles) & Anal Fissures:** Straining tears the anal lining, causing painful bleeding and cuts during stool passage.
* **Fecal Impaction:** Hard dry stool gets locked in the rectum, requiring manual evacuation by a doctor.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient has severe, constant, or crampy abdominal pain accompanied by persistent vomiting.**
* **The patient is completely unable to pass both stool and gas (flatus) for $> 48$ hours.**
* **The patient passes stools mixed with heavy dark blood or jelly-like mucus.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is constipation?**
   Constipation is defined as having fewer than 3 bowel movements per week, difficulty passing stools, or passing hard, dry stools.

2. **How does Isabgol (Psyllium Husk) work?**
   Isabgol is a bulk-forming laxative. It absorbs water in the gut, swelling into a gel that softens the stool and stimulates natural bowel movements.

3. **Why must I drink extra water when taking Isabgol?**
   Soluble fiber requires water to expand. If you take Isabgol without enough water, it can dry up and form a hard block in your intestines, worsening constipation.

4. **What is a bowel obstruction and what are its symptoms?**
   A bowel obstruction is a physical blockage in the intestines. Symptoms include severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, a swollen stomach, and the complete inability to pass gas or stool.

5. **Why is daily use of Dulcolax (Bisacodyl) dangerous?**
   Dulcolax is a stimulant laxative. Daily use damages the nerves of the colon, making it lazy. The colon loses its natural ability to contract, causing chronic dependency.

6. **What is the price of generic Lactulose syrup at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
   Generic Lactulose syrup is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹40 to ₹60 per bottle, compared to ₹150-₹220 for branded versions in private pharmacies.

7. **How does squatting help relieve constipation?**
   Squatting (or using a footstool under your feet on a western toilet) relaxes the puborectalis muscle, straightening the rectal canal for easier, strain-free stool passage.

8. **Can thyroid problems cause chronic constipation?**
   Yes. An underactive thyroid (hypothyroidism) slows down the body's metabolism, including the movement of the digestive tract, leading to chronic constipation.

9. **What is the difference between osmotic and stimulant laxatives?**
   Osmotic laxatives (Lactulose) draw water into the colon to soften stools naturally. Stimulant laxatives (Bisacodyl) irritate the intestinal wall to force contractions.

10. **Why should liquid paraffin be avoided in infants?**
    If infants choke or vomit liquid paraffin, the oil can enter their lungs, causing a severe and dangerous form of chemical pneumonia (lipid pneumonia).

11. **What is an erect abdominal X-ray?**
    It is an X-ray taken while standing. It is used to check for air-fluid levels or dilated bowel loops, which help diagnose a bowel obstruction.

12. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover emergency bowel surgery?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for diagnostic laparoscopy, emergency laparotomy for bowel obstruction, and ICU care.

13. **How does daily exercise help prevent kabz?**
    Physical activity stimulates the natural contractions of the muscles in the intestines (peristalsis), speeding up bowel transit time.

14. **What is an anal fissure?**
    An anal fissure is a small tear in the lining of the anus, usually caused by passing hard stools. It causes sharp pain and bright red bleeding during defecation.

15. **Can iron supplements cause constipation?**
    Yes. Oral iron tablets are a common cause of constipation and dark/black stools. Drink extra water and consult your doctor for stool-softening support.

16. **What is a fecal impaction?**
    It is a large mass of dry, hard stool that becomes stuck in the rectum and cannot be passed. It requires manual extraction by a healthcare professional.

17. **Why does chronic constipation lead to piles (hemorrhoids)?**
    Constant straining to pass hard stools increases pressure in the veins around the anus, causing them to swell, stretch, and bleed (piles).

18. **Is generic Polyethylene Glycol safe for children?**
    Yes. Polyethylene Glycol (PEG 3350) is an osmotic laxative that is highly safe and effective for treating pediatric constipation under pediatric guidance.

19. **What is the role of the NPCDCS program in colon health?**
    NPCDCS promotes lifestyle modifications and screens older adults experiencing chronic constipation for early signs of colorectal cancers.

20. **Is generic Isabgol as effective as branded versions?**

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** If you have constipation, you should take 3 tablets of Dulcolax daily to keep your stomach clean.
  * **Fact:** Taking Dulcolax daily damages the nerves in your colon, leading to permanent laxative dependency and a lazy bowel. Use bulk-forming Isabgol instead.
* **Myth:** Drinking 5 cups of black tea or coffee daily cures constipation.
  * **Fact:** Caffeine in tea and coffee acts as a diuretic, dehydrating your body. Dehydration draws water out of your colon, making stools harder and worsening constipation.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures constipation instantly by releasing energy blocks.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Bowel movement requires dietary fiber, water, and exercise.
* **Myth:** Senda namak (rock salt) mixed with warm water is the best daily cure for kabz.
  * **Fact:** Excessive salt intake can raise blood pressure and dehydrate the intestines. Use natural fiber like Isabgol or lactulose syrup under doctor advice.
* **Myth:** Bowel obstruction can be cleared by giving the patient a strong enema at home.
  * **Fact:** Giving an enema or laxatives during a complete bowel obstruction can raise pressure in the blocked gut, causing it to rupture, which is fatal.
* **Myth:** You must pass stool every single morning, otherwise toxins will build up in your body.
  * **Fact:** Normal bowel frequency ranges from three times a day to three times a week. Constipation is diagnosed only if frequency drops below 3 times a week or causes pain.
* **Myth:** Piles (hemorrhoids) are caused by eating hot foods like chicken or eggs.
  * **Fact:** Piles are caused by chronic straining due to hard stools and constipation. Eggs and chicken do not cause piles; lack of fiber and water does.
* **Myth:** Generic Lactulose syrup from Jan Aushadhi is diluted and takes longer to work.
  * **Fact:** Generic Lactulose is manufactured under strict quality standards and has the exact same osmotic potency as branded versions, at a much lower cost.
* **Myth:** Children never get constipation; it is only an adult problem.
  * **Fact:** Constipation is highly common in children due to low water intake, withholding stool at school, or milk-heavy diets lacking solid fiber.
* **Myth:** Chronic constipation can be permanently cured by wearing tight abdominal belts.
  * **Fact:** Belts have no effect on intestinal nerves or stool hydration. Focus on diet, hydration, and exercise.
