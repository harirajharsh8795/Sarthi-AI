# Headache (Headache/Sir Dard) ki Pehchan, Types aur Red Flags (Migraine, Meningitis and Thunderclap Warnings)

## Overview

### English
Headache, clinically termed **Cephalea**, is pain or discomfort in any region of the head or upper neck. Pathophysiologically, it is classified into:
1. **Primary Headaches:** (e.g., Tension Headache, Migraine, Cluster Headache) - benign but painful.
2. **Secondary Headaches:** (e.g., Meningitis, Brain Tumors, Subarachnoid Hemorrhage [SAH] from ruptured brain aneurysm).
*First aid requires identifying diagnostic "Red Flags" like a sudden-onset **"Thunderclap Headache"** (reaching peak severity in $< 1\text{ minute}$, indicating bleeding in the brain) and fever with neck stiffness (indicating meningitis), requiring immediate emergency transport and brain scans.*

### Hindi
सिरदर्द (Headache / सेफालिया या सिर दर्द), सिर या गर्दन के ऊपरी हिस्से में होने वाला दर्द है। यह दो श्रेणियों में आता है: **प्राथमिक (Primary)** जैसे माइग्रेन (Migraine) या तनाव जनित (Tension) सिरदर्द, और **द्वितियक (Secondary)** जो मस्तिष्क के भीतर किसी गंभीर संक्रमण (Meningitis) या रक्तस्राव (Brain Hemorrhage) का लक्षण हो सकता है। प्राथमिक उपचार में **खतरे के संकेतों (Red Flags)** की पहचान करना अत्यंत महत्वपूर्ण है, जैसे कि **अचानक होने वाला असहनीय सिरदर्द (Thunderclap Headache - ऐसा महसूस होना कि सिर फट गया है)** और बुखार के साथ गर्दन का अकड़ना। ये आपातकालीन स्थितियां हैं।

### Hinglish

1. **Tension Headache:** Poor sleep ya stress se dono taraf hone wala mild pain.
2. **Migraine:** Aadhe sir me (one side) achanak hone wala tez throb (teekha) pain jo ulti aur light se badhta hai.
3. **Emergency Secondary Headache:** Meningitis (infection) ya Ruptured Brain Aneurysm (nas phatna).
Isme **Red Flags** pehchanna compulsory hai, jaise **"Thunderclap Headache"** (1 minute ke andar zindagi ka sabse khatarnak dard hona). isme immediate **108** par call karein.

## Quick Summary
Cephalea distinguishes benign primary headaches from life-threatening secondary brain events. Red flags feature thunderclap headaches and neck stiffness. First aid requires resting in quiet, dark rooms. Diagnostics check head CTs and lumbar punctures. Management resolves the specific vascular or infectious cause.

## Definition
* **Migraine:** A chronic neurological disorder characterized by recurrent, moderate-to-severe throbbing headaches, typically unilateral (affecting one side) and accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light/sound.
* **Thunderclap Headache:** A sudden, excruciating headache that peaks within 60 seconds, classically described as the "worst headache of one's life," indicating subarachnoid hemorrhage.
* **Medication Overuse Headache (Rebound):** Chronic headaches caused by the frequent, daily use of headache relief drugs (like Paracetamol or triptans) for $> 10-15$ days a month.

## Differential Diagnosis of Common Headaches

Understanding the type of headache guides correct clinical treatment:

| Feature | Tension-Type Headache | Migraine Attack | Ruptured Brain Aneurysm (SAH) | Meningitis (Brain Infection) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pain Location** | Band-like pattern around the whole head | **One side of head** (unilateral, front/temple) | Entire head, sudden onset | Entire head, radiating to neck |
| **Pain Character** | Dull, squeezing, non-throbbing | **Pulsating, throbbing, beating** | **Explosive, "thunderclap" peak in $< 60\text{ sec}$** | Severe, constant, worse with movement |
| **Associated Signs** | Mild neck stiffness | **Nausea, vomiting, light/sound sensitivity (Aura)** | **Fainting, seizure, slurred speech, confusion** | **High fever, rigid neck stiffness, photophobia** |
| **Triggers** | Stress, dehydration, eye strain | Chocolate, stress, bright light, sleep deprivation | Coughing, lifting weight | Viral or bacterial exposure |
| *First-Line Action*| **Rehydration** + Paracetamol / Rest | Rest in dark room + **Sumatriptan / Naproxen**| **Immediate CT scan via 108 emergency** | **Urgent lumbar puncture + IV antibiotics** |

## Symptoms & Danger Signs of Headaches (Red Flags)

Clinicians use the mnemonic **SNOOP** to detect life-threatening headaches:

### English
* **S - Systemic Symptoms:** Headache accompanied by high fever, weight loss, or chills (Meningitis/Cancer).
* **N - Neurological Signs:** FAST positive (Face drooping, arm weakness, slurred speech); double vision; confusion.
* **O - Onset:** Ruptured brain aneurysm signs: **Sudden, explosive "Thunderclap" onset peaking within 1 minute.**
* **O - Older Age:** New-onset headache in a patient aged $> 50$ years (Giant Cell Arteritis).
* **P - Prior History:** Change in headache pattern, or headache worsening when lying down or coughing (raised brain pressure).

### Hindi
* **दिमाग के खतरे के लाल संकेत (Red Flags):**
  * **अचानक और तीव्र दर्द (Thunderclap):** ६० सेकंड में जीवन का सबसे भयंकर सिरदर्द होना (Brain hemorrhage)।
  * **गर्दन की अकड़न (Meningitis):** सिरदर्द के साथ तेज बुखार होना और गर्दन आगे न झुक पाना।
  * **स्ट्रोक के लक्षण:** चेहरे का टेढ़ा होना, लकवा मारना, बोलने में हकलाहट।

### Hinglish
* **Sir Dard ke Red Flags (Immediate Emergency):**
  * **Thunderclap Headaches:** 60 seconds ke andar sudden explosive pain hona (nas phatne ka sign).
  * **Meningitis Stiff Neck:** Sir dard ke sath tez bukhar aur gardan aage na jhukna.
  * **FAST positive:** Ek side ka chehra deela padna, bolne me ladkhadahat, ya double vision hona.

## First Aid & De-escalation Protocol (Migraine & Tension Headache)

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Rest in a quiet, dark room.** During a Migraine attack, sit or lie down in a silent room with the curtains drawn and lights off. Bright lights and noise stimulate irritated cranial nerves, worsening pain.
* **Apply a cool compress.** Place a cold washcloth or ice pack wrapped in a towel on your forehead or temples. Cold constricts dilated cerebral blood vessels, reducing pain.
* **Hydrate immediately.** Drink 2-3 glasses of water. Dehydration is the leading trigger of tension headaches, as it drops blood volume and alters brain tissue pressure.
* **Limit painkiller use.** Use Paracetamol or Ibuprofen only when necessary. Complete the prescribed Migraine preventative therapy (e.g., Propranolol or Amitriptyline) rather than relying on daily painkillers.

#### Hindi
* **शांत, अंधेरे कमरे में आराम करें:** तेज रोशनी और शोर माइग्रेन के दर्द को बढ़ाते हैं।
* **ठंडी सिकाई (Cool compress) करें:** माथे या कनपटी पर ठंडा कपड़ा रखें।
* **तुरंत २-३ गिलास पानी पिएं:** निर्जलीकरण (Dehydration) सिरदर्द का मुख्य कारण है।
* **दर्द निवारक दवाओं (painkillers) का सीमित उपयोग करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Quiet, dark room me rest karein:** Migraine attack ke dauran lights aur loud noise se cranial nerves irritate hoti hain aur dard double ho jata hai. Humesha chup-chaap andhere kamre me let jayein.
* **Cold compress (thandi patti) karein:** Forehead aur temples (kanpati) par thande paani ki patti ya towel me wrapped ice pack rakhein. Isse dilated vessels narrow hoti hain aur pain kam hota hai.
* **Immediate 2-3 glass paani piyein:** Tension headache ka main trigger hydration kam hona hai. Paani peene se brain pressure normal ho jata hai.
* **Painkillers limit me lein:** Paracetamol ya Combiflam ka daily use rokkdein. Doctor se migraine preventive dawa (Propranolol) likhwayein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT take painkillers daily.** Taking tablets (like Paracetamol, Ibuprofen, or Triptans) for $> 10-15$ days a month causes **Medication Overuse Headaches** (rebound pain), where the brain becomes hypersensitive to pain signals when the drug wears off.
* **Do NOT ignore a thunderclap headache** by sleeping it off. Ruptured brain aneurysms are fatal in 40% of cases if untreated.
* **Do NOT drink coffee or energy drinks** to cure a migraine, as caffeine withdrawal can trigger worse rebound attacks.

#### Hindi
* **दैनिक दर्द निवारक गोलियां (painkillers) न खाएं:** इससे रीबाउंड सिरदर्द (rebound headache) होता है।
* **अचानक उठे तीव्र सिरदर्द (thunderclap) को एसिडिटी समझकर टालें नहीं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Rojana pain killer goliya na khayein:** Month me 10-15 din se zyada Paracetamol ya Aspirin khane se brain me **Medication Overuse Headache** (rebound pain) shuru ho jata hai. Goli ka asar khatam hote hi sir dard fir se wapas aa jata hai.
* **Thunderclap headache ko normal samajh kar letne ki galti na karein:** Nas phatna 40% cases me fatal hota hai. immediately hospital bhagein.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Head CT Scan (Non-contrast):** Gold standard diagnostic check to immediately detect blood in the brain spaces (Subarachnoid Hemorrhage).
* **Lumbar Puncture (CSF Analysis):** Performed if CT is negative but Meningitis is suspected, to analyze cerebrospinal fluid pressure, protein, glucose, and white cells.
* **Brain MRI:** Best for detecting cerebellar strokes, brain tumors, or venous sinus thrombosis.

---

## Medical & Surgical Management
* **Abortive Migraine Therapy:** Triptans (e.g., Sumatriptan) are given to constrict cranial blood vessels during an active migraine attack.
* **Endovascular Coiling / Neurosurgical Clipping:** Surgical procedures performed by neurosurgeons to block a ruptured brain aneurysm and stop internal bleeding.
* **Antibiotic & Steroid Therapy:** Emergency IV Ceftriaxone + Dexamethasone started for bacterial meningitis.

## Prevention of Recurrent Headaches
* **Keep a Headache Diary:** Record triggers (lack of sleep, skipped meals, processed cheese, MSG, bright screens) to identify and avoid them.
* **Maintain Sleep Hygiene:** Wake up and go to bed at the same time daily.
* **Avoid Dehydration.**

---

## Possible Complications of Unmanaged Secondary Headaches
* **Permanent Brain Herniation & Death:** Caused by high pressure in the skull from brain infection or bleeding.
* **Brain Stroke & Paralysis.**

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **A sudden, explosive headache that peaks within 1 minute (Thunderclap Headache).**
* **The headache is accompanied by high fever, neck stiffness, and painful sensitivity to light.**
* **The patient has associated slurred speech, one-sided facial drooping, or arm weakness (FAST positive).**
* **The headache started after a head injury or is worsening continuously.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a thunderclap headache?**
   A thunderclap headache is a sudden, explosive headache that peaks in severity within 60 seconds, often indicating a ruptured brain aneurysm (SAH).

2. **Why does dehydration cause sir dard?**
   Dehydration reduces blood volume and blood pressure, which decreases oxygen delivery to the brain and causes temporary swelling of blood vessels, triggering pain.

3. **What is a Migraine?**
   A Migraine is a neurological condition causing recurrent, throbbing pain on one side of the head, often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and light sensitivity.

4. **Why is daily use of pain relief tablets dangerous?**
   Daily use ($> 10-15$ days a month) causes **Medication Overuse Headaches**. The brain becomes habituated to the drug and triggers rebound headaches as the medicine wears off.

5. **What is the price of generic Sumatriptan at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
   Generic Sumatriptan (50mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹15 to ₹25 per strip of 4 tablets, compared to ₹80-₹120 in private medical stores.

6. **What is the difference between CT and MRI of the brain?**
   A Head CT scan is faster (takes 5 minutes) and is best for detecting emergency bleeding (hemorrhage). A Brain MRI is slower but provides detailed images of tumors or strokes.

7. **How does a cold compress help in Migraine?**
   During a Migraine, brain blood vessels dilate (expand) and press on nerve endings. A cold compress constricts these vessels, reducing pain transmission.

8. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover brain aneurysm surgery?**
   Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for neurosurgical clipping, endovascular coiling, brain CT/MRI scans, and ICU hospitalization.

9. **What is Meningitis and how does it cause headache?**
   Meningitis is an infection of the membranes covering the brain. The infection causes inflammation and swelling, resulting in severe headache, high fever, and neck stiffness.

10. **Why should Migraine patients sleep in a dark room?**
    Migraine causes hypersensitivity to sensory inputs (photophobia/phonophobia). Light and sound stimulate the trigeminal nerve, worsening the headache.

11. **What is a Lumbar Puncture?**
    It is a diagnostic test where a needle is inserted into the lower back to collect cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) to check for meningitis or bleeding.

12. **Can high blood pressure cause sir dard?**
    Yes. A sudden, extreme rise in blood pressure (hypertensive crisis) causes pressure in the brain blood vessels, leading to a throbbing headache and requiring urgent care.

13. **What is the role of the government's 108 ambulance in stroke cases?**
    ASHA and 108 ambulance services provide rapid transport to stroke-ready hospitals that can perform emergency CT scans and thrombolysis.

14. **Why are ACE inhibitor BP tablets linked to headaches?**
    Some blood pressure medications dilate blood vessels. When starting these medicines, the dilation can cause mild, transient headaches.

15. **What is a sinus headache?**
    A sinus headache is caused by pressure buildup inside the facial sinuses due to infection. It presents as pain in the forehead, cheeks, or behind the eyes.

16. **Why does caffeine withdrawal cause headache?**
    Caffeine constricts brain blood vessels. When you stop drinking coffee, the vessels dilate suddenly, increasing blood flow and triggering a rebound headache.

17. **What is the first aid for a severe tension headache?**
    Drink 2 glasses of water, rest in a quiet space, gently massage your neck and shoulder muscles, and take a single dose of Paracetamol.

18. **Can a brain tumor cause headache?**
    Yes. A brain tumor causes headaches that are typically worse in the morning, worsen with coughing or bending down, and may be accompanied by vomiting.

19. **How does generic Propranolol help prevent Migraine?**
    Propranolol is a beta-blocker. Taken daily, it stabilizes blood vessels in the brain, preventing the sudden dilation and spasm that triggers Migraine attacks.

20. **Is generic Naproxen as effective as branded versions?**
    Yes. Generic Naproxen contains the exact same active non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) and reduces migraine inflammation just as effectively, at an 80% lower cost.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** If you have a severe headache, you should tie a tight cloth around your head and sleep it off.
  * **Fact:** Tying a cloth does not cure brain hemorrhage or meningitis. If you have danger signs like a sudden thunderclap headache, go to the emergency room immediately.
* **Myth:** Migraine is just a normal headache and is caused by gastric gas in the stomach.
  * **Fact:** Migraine is a neurological disorder involving blood vessel and nerve pathway changes in the brain. Gastric gas does not cause Migraine.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures headaches instantly by absorbing the negative brain energy.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Headache relief requires hydration, rest, or medical therapy.
* **Myth:** Taking 4 tablets of Saridon daily is safe for chronic sir dard.
  * **Fact:** Daily use of analgesics causes Medication Overuse Headaches and raises the risk of stomach ulcers, kidney damage, and liver toxicity.
* **Myth:** Triptan medicines (Sumatriptan) are daily preventative pills for Migraine.
  * **Fact:** Triptans are abortive medicines, taken only at the start of a migraine attack. Taking them daily causes rebound headaches and cardiovascular issues.
* **Myth:** Men never get Migraines; it is only a female emotional problem.
  * **Fact:** While more common in women due to hormonal cycles, men can also suffer from severe Migraines. It is a biological brain disorder, not an emotional one.
* **Myth:** Tepid water gargles cure severe brain infections (meningitis).
  * **Fact:** Gargles only soothe the throat. Meningitis is a brain membrane infection requiring emergency IV antibiotics in a hospital.
* **Myth:** Generic migraine medicines from Jan Aushadhi Kendras take twice as long to work.
  * **Fact:** Generic sumatriptan and naproxen contain the exact same active formulations and are absorbed at the same rate, at an 80% lower cost.
* **Myth:** A sudden, worst-ever headache is always caused by lack of sleep.
  * **Fact:** A sudden, explosive headache peaking in 1 minute is a Thunderclap Headache. It is a critical warning sign of a ruptured brain aneurysm.
* **Myth:** Sinus headaches are the most common type of chronic headaches.
  * **Fact:** Most self-diagnosed sinus headaches are actually Migraines, as both cause pain in the face, forehead, and nasal congestion.
