# Chest Pain (Chest Pain) ki Pehchan, Kaaran aur First Aid (Cardiac vs. Gastric Chest Pain Guide)

## Overview

### English
Chest Pain is discomfort or pain felt anywhere along the front of the body between the neck and upper abdomen. Pathophysiologically, chest pain requires immediate clinical sorting to rule out life-threatening emergencies:
1. **Cardiac Emergencies:** Acute Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack), Angina Pectoris, Aortic Dissection.
2. **Pulmonary Emergencies:** Pulmonary Embolism, Tension Pneumothorax.
3. **Non-Emergencies:** GERD (Gastric Acidity), Musculoskeletal pain.
*Cardiac pain typically presents as a crushing, heavy squeezing pressure radiating to the left arm, neck, or jaw, accompanied by heavy cold sweating and dyspnea. First aid for suspected cardiac pain is to chew Aspirin (325mg) immediately.*

## Quick Summary
Chest pain demands sorting cardiac pressure from gastric acidity. Cardiac indicators feature crushing pain radiating to the left arm/jaw with cold sweating. First aid requires chewing a 325mg aspirin and sublingual nitrates. Diagnostics check ECGs and troponin levels. Management stabilizes cardiac perfusion.

## Definition
* **Angina Pectoris:** Chest pain or discomfort caused when the heart muscle does not get enough oxygen-rich blood, typically triggered by physical exertion and relieved by rest or sublingual Nitroglycerin.
* **GERD (Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease):** Acid reflux from the stomach into the esophagus, causing a burning sensation behind the breastbone (heartburn).
* **Troponin-I:** A cardiac-specific protein released into the blood when heart muscle cells are damaged during a heart attack.

---

## How to Tell the Difference: Cardiac Pain vs. Gastric/Acidity Pain

It is clinically vital to distinguish cardiac ischemia from acid reflux:

| Diagnostic Feature | Cardiac Chest Pain (Heart Attack / Angina) | Gastric / Acidity Chest Pain (Acid Reflux) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Type of Sensation**| **Crushing, heavy weight, squeezing pressure** | **Burning sensation ( जलन ), sharp, hot feeling** |
| **Location** | Behind the breastbone, diffuse (cannot point with one finger) | Under the ribs, localized (can point with a finger) |
| **Radiation** | **Radiates to left arm, shoulder, neck, jaw, or back** | Does not spread, stays in the center or throat |
| **Trigger** | Physical exertion, walking fast, cold wind, heavy stress | Eating spicy foods, lying flat immediately after eating |
| **Relieving Factors** | **Rest or Nitroglycerin under the tongue** (within 5 mins) | Antacid gel (e.g., Digene) or drinking cold milk |
| **Sweating** | **Profuse cold sweating, pale skin, nausea** | Absent, no cold sweats |
| *Lab Diagnosis* | **Abnormal ECG / Positive Troponin-I** | Normal ECG / Normal Troponin-I |

## Symptoms & Danger Signs of Chest Pain

### English
* **Cardiac Danger Signs:** Crushing central chest pain lasting $> 10$ minutes, unaffected by movement or breathing; radiation of pain to the left arm, neck, shoulder, or jaw; shortness of breath (dyspnea) and dizziness; **profuse cold sweating and feeling of impending doom**.
* **Other Danger Signs:** Sudden tearing back pain (Aortic Dissection); sharp chest pain with coughing up blood (Pulmonary Embolism).

* **दिल के दौरे के खतरे के संकेत:** सीने में अत्यधिक भारीपन और निचोड़ने जैसा दर्द जो १० मिनट से अधिक रहे; दर्द का बाएं हाथ या जबड़े में फैलना; **ठंडा चिपचिपा पसीना आना**; सांस फूलना और चक्कर आना।
* **अन्य गंभीर लक्षण:** पीठ में अचानक चीरने जैसा तेज दर्द होना (Aortic Dissection)।

* **Aortic Dissection warning:** Back (peed) me sudden achanak teer se chirne jaisa sharp pain hona.

## Step-by-Step Emergency First Aid Protocol (Suspected Heart Attack)

If you suspect a patient is having a heart attack, execute this protocol immediately:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Call the National Ambulance Service (108)** immediately. Do not wait to see if the pain goes away.
* **Chew an Aspirin tablet (325mg)** immediately. Chewing (not swallowing) breaks down the drug fast, allowing it to enter the blood within 5 minutes and stop platelets from growing the block in the heart artery.
* **Sit the patient down in a semi-upright position** (resting against a wall with knees bent). This reduces the workload on the heart.
* **Administer Nitroglycerin (Sorbitrate 5mg)** under the tongue **ONLY IF** the patient has a known history of heart disease and it has been prescribed by their doctor. *Ensure their blood pressure is not low before giving it.*

#### Hindi
* **तुरंत १०८ एम्बुलेंस को कॉल करें।**
* **एस्पिरिन (Aspirin 325mg) की गोली चबाकर खिलाएं:** चबाने से दवा खून में ५ मिनट में मिलकर थक्के को बढ़ने से रोकती है।
* **मरीज को दीवार के सहारे अर्ध-बैठी (semi-sitting) स्थिति में बैठाएं।**
* **डॉक्टर द्वारा निर्देशित होने पर ही जीभ के नीचे सोर्बिट्रेट (Sorbitrate 5mg) रखें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Immediate 108 Emergency Ambulance call karein.**
* **Aspirin (325mg) ki goli chabane ko dein:** Patient ko bolyein ki wo Aspirin goli ko **chaba (chew) kar** nigle, paani se direct na nigle. Chabane se ye 5 mins me blood me absorb hokar heart ke block ko badhne se rokti hai aur life save karti hai.
* **Semi-sitting position me baithayein:** Patient ko floor par wall ke sahare knee modd kar baithayein taaki heart par load kam ho sake.
* **Sorbitrate (5mg) jeebh ke niche rakhein:** Agar patient heart ke hain aur unhe doctor ne Sorbitrate prescribed ki hai, to 1 goli jeebh ke niche (sublingual) rakhein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT give Nitroglycerin (Viagra interaction warning)** if the patient has taken **Sildenafil (Viagra)** or Tadalafil in the last 24-48 hours. Combining them causes a fatal collapse in blood pressure.
* **Do NOT let the patient walk, climb stairs, or drive themselves** to the hospital. Any physical exertion increases the heart's oxygen demand, rapidly expanding the dead muscle area (infarct).
* **Do NOT ignore the pain as "just acidity."** When in doubt, always treat it as a cardiac emergency until ruled out by an ECG.

#### Hindi
* **सोर्बिट्रेट (Sorbitrate) न दें यदि मरीज ने पिछले ४८ घंटों में वियाग्रा (Viagra) ली हो:** इससे बीपी अचानक शून्य होकर मौत हो सकती है।
* **मरीज को चलने, सीढ़ियां चढ़ने या खुद ड्राइव करने न दें।**
* **दर्द को "केवल एसिडिटी" समझकर टालें नहीं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Viagra ke sath Sorbitrate na dein:** Agar patient ne pichle 24-48 ghante me erection ki dawa **Viagra (Sildenafil)** khayi hai, to unhe Sorbitrate/Nitroglycerin bilkul na lein warna BP sudden zero drop ho jayega aur cardiac arrest ho jayega.
* **Patient ko stairs chadhne ya walk karne na dein:** Physical exertion se heart par pressure badhega aur damage double ho jayega. Stretch par leta kar hi shift karein.
* **Acidity samajh kar ignore na karein.**

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **ECG (Electrocardiogram):** Done within 10 minutes of arrival at the emergency room to check for ST-elevation (STEMI).
* **Serum Troponin-I:** Blood test checked at 0 and 3 hours; elevated levels confirm cardiac muscle necrosis.
* **Echocardiogram (ECHO):** Ultrasound of the heart to check for wall motion abnormalities.

---

## Medical & Surgical Management
* **Thrombolysis:** Giving clot-busting injections (e.g., Streptokinase, Tenecteplase) within the "golden hour" (first 1-2 hours) if catheterization is unavailable.
* **Primary Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI):** Emergency coronary angiography followed by ballooning and placing a metallic stent to open the blocked coronary artery.
* **CABG (Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting):** Open heart bypass surgery if multiple main arteries are blocked.

## Prevention of Cardiac Chest Pain
* **Control Risk Factors:** Keep blood pressure $< 130/80\text{ mmHg}$, HbA1c $< 7\%$, and LDL cholesterol $< 70\text{ mg/dL}$.
* **Quit Smoking & Tobacco:** Smoking is the leading cause of sudden vasospasm and heart attacks in youth.
* **Adopt Mediterranean Diet:** Eat fresh vegetables, nuts, and fish; limit trans fats (refined oils).

---

## Possible Complications of Unmanaged Cardiac Chest Pain
* **Cardiogenic Shock & Sudden Cardiac Death:** The heart fails completely due to massive muscle death.
* **Arrhythmias:** Fatal irregular heartbeats (VFib) that stop blood flow in minutes.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Central chest pain that lasts longer than 10 minutes and is accompanied by cold sweating and shortness of breath.**
* **The pain radiates to the left arm, neck, or jaw.**
* **The patient collapses, loses consciousness, or stops breathing (requires immediate CPR).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is the most common cause of sudden chest pain?**
   While chest pain can be caused by gastric acidity, muscle strain, or lung infections, the most critical causes are cardiac, such as a heart attack or angina.

2. **How do I differentiate between heart attack pain and acidity pain?**
   Heart attack pain is a crushing pressure radiating to the left arm/jaw, accompanied by cold sweating and shortness of breath. Acidity pain is a burning sensation under the ribs, usually relieved by drinking cold milk or antacids.

3. **Why should a patient chew Aspirin during a heart attack?**
   Chewing Aspirin allows the drug to be absorbed rapidly through the oral mucosa. It blocks platelets from forming new clots, preventing the arterial blockage from worsening.

4. **What is Sorbitrate (Nitroglycerin) and how is it taken?**
   Sorbitrate is a vasodilator. It is placed under the tongue (sublingual), where it dissolves and rapidly dilates the coronary arteries, reducing heart workload within 5 minutes.

5. **Why is combining Viagra with Nitroglycerin fatal?**
   Both drugs cause blood vessel dilation. Taking them together causes a sudden, catastrophic drop in blood pressure, leading to cardiac arrest.

6. **What is the "Golden Hour" in a heart attack?**
   The Golden Hour refers to the first **60 minutes** after symptom onset. Opening the blocked artery during this time (via angioplasty or thrombolysis) prevents permanent heart muscle damage.

7. **Where can I get a free emergency ECG test in India?**
   ECG tests are performed **completely free of cost** at all government primary health centers (PHCs) and district civil hospitals.

8. **What does a positive Troponin-I blood test mean?**
   Troponin-I is a protein found only in heart muscle cells. A positive test means these cells have died and leaked troponin into the blood, confirming a heart attack.

9. **What is the difference between Angina and a Heart Attack?**
   Angina is temporary chest pain caused by reduced blood flow during exertion, resolved by rest. A heart attack is a complete blockage causing permanent muscle death, unresolved by rest.

10. **Why does a heart attack cause cold sweating?**
    The sudden drop in heart output triggers a massive sympathetic nervous system fight-or-flight response, releasing adrenaline which causes cold, clammy sweating.

11. **What is the price of generic Aspirin (150mg) at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Aspirin (150mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹2 to ₹4 per strip of 14 tablets, compared to ₹10-₹15 in private medical stores.

12. **Can a panic attack cause chest pain?**
    Yes. Hyperventilation during a panic attack causes chest tightness, hyper-sensitivity, and rapid heartbeats, which can mimic a heart attack. An ECG is required to rule out cardiac issues.

13. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover angioplasty stent surgery?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for coronary angiographies, emergency angioplasty with drug-eluting stents, post-op ICU monitoring, and medicines.

14. **What is a Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG)?**
    CABG is open-heart surgery where blood vessels from the leg or chest are grafted bypass blocked coronary arteries, restoring normal blood flow to the heart.

15. **Why is smoking dangerous for heart health?**
    Nicotine constricts blood vessels, raises blood pressure, and damages arterial walls, causing rapid cholesterol plaque buildup and sudden heart attacks in young adults.

16. **How is an ECG test done?**
    An ECG is a painless test where electrodes are attached to the chest and limbs to record the heart's electrical activity on a graph paper.

17. **What is a Silent Heart Attack?**
    It is a heart attack that occurs without chest pain, common in diabetic patients and elderly individuals due to diabetic nerve damage (neuropathy) blocking pain signals.

18. **Can I use a pulse oximeter during chest pain?**
    Yes. A pulse oximeter monitors heart rate and oxygen saturation ($SpO_2$). If $SpO_2$ drops below 94% during chest pain, it indicates hypoxia and requires immediate oxygen.

19. **What is the role of the government's 108 service in chest pain emergencies?**
    Dialing 108 brings a free ambulance equipped with ECG machines, oxygen cylinders, and emergency drugs to start cardiac stabilization during transport.

20. **Is generic Atorvastatin as effective as branded versions?**
    Yes. Generic Atorvastatin contains the exact same active lipid-lowering compound and stabilizes arterial plaque just as effectively, at an 80% lower cost.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** If you have chest pain, you should drink hot water mixed with baking soda to cure the heart attack.
  * **Fact:** Baking soda has no effect on blocked coronary arteries. Doing this delays critical care during the golden hour, which can be fatal. Chew Aspirin and call 108.
* **Myth:** Chest pain on the left side is always a heart attack, and pain in the center is always gas.
  * **Fact:** Cardiac pain is diffuse and typically felt behind the breastbone in the center of the chest. Any chest pain must be checked by an ECG.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures chest pain instantly by absorbing negative energy.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Low heart blood flow requires vasodilators (nitrates) or urgent hospital angioplasty.
* **Myth:** Heart attacks only happen to old people, young adults with chest pain have gas.
  * **Fact:** Heart attacks are rising rapidly in young adults under 30 in India due to high stress, smoking, and poor lifestyle. Never ignore chest pain in youth.
* **Myth:** If you can walk and talk, you are not having a heart attack.
  * **Fact:** Many people remain conscious and can walk during a heart attack. However, walking increases oxygen demand, causing more heart muscle death. Rest completely.
* **Myth:** Taking 3 tablets of Sorbitrate together is safe for any chest pain.
  * **Fact:** Taking an overdose of Sorbitrate causes a severe, dangerous drop in blood pressure, leading to fainting and shock. Take only one prescribed tablet.
* **Myth:** Heart bypass surgery (CABG) is a permanent cure and you can smoke after it.
  * **Fact:** CABG bypasses the blocks, but if you continue smoking and eating unhealthy food, the new graft vessels will block again in a few years.
* **Myth:** Generic heart medicines from Jan Aushadhi clinics are weak.
  * **Fact:** Generic heart drugs meet the same national pharmacopoeia standards (IP) and are equally safe and effective, at an 80% lower cost.
* **Myth:** A silent heart attack is not dangerous because it does not cause pain.
  * **Fact:** A silent heart attack causes the same amount of heart muscle death and carries the same risk of cardiac arrest. It is highly dangerous.
* **Myth:** A heart attack and cardiac arrest are the exact same thing.
  * **Fact:** A heart attack is a plumbing problem (blocked blood vessel). A cardiac arrest is an electrical problem (heart stops beating). A heart attack can cause a cardiac arrest.
