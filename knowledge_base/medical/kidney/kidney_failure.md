# Kidney Failure (Gurde ki Kharabi) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Acute vs Chronic Kidney Disease and Dialysis Guide)

## Overview

### English
Kidney Failure, also referred to as Renal Failure, is a critical medical condition where the kidneys lose their ability to sufficiently filter metabolic waste products, excess fluids, and toxins from the blood. It is classified into two distinct types: **Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)** (a sudden, often reversible loss of kidney function over hours or days) and **Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)** (a slow, progressive, irreversible loss of function over months or years, leading to End-Stage Renal Disease [ESRD]). Diagnosis is based on elevated Serum Creatinine and Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN), and a reduced **Glomerular Filtration Rate (eGFR)**. Management requires dietary modification, blood pressure control, and Renal Replacement Therapy (hemodialysis, peritoneal dialysis, or kidney transplantation) for advanced stages.

## Quick Summary
Kidney failure is the loss of renal filtering capacity. It is classified as acute (reversible) or chronic (irreversible). Symptoms feature decreased urine output, swelling (edema) in legs/face, fatigue, and shortness of breath. Diagnostics measure elevated serum creatinine, BUN, and low eGFR. Management uses blood pressure control, fluid restriction, and dialysis.

---

## Definition
* **AKI (Acute Kidney Injury):** A rapid decrease in kidney function, defined by an increase in serum creatinine of $\ge 0.3\text{ mg/dL}$ within 48 hours or urine output $< 0.5\text{ mL/kg/h}$ for 6 hours.
* **CKD (Chronic Kidney Disease):** Abnormalities of kidney structure or function, present for $> 3$ months, with implications for health, clinically graded by eGFR ($< 60\text{ mL/min/1.73 m}^2$ indicates kidney damage).
* **Hemodialysis:** A clinical procedure where a machine filters waste and excess fluid from the patient's blood through an external dialyzer (artificial kidney).

---

## Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) Stages based on eGFR

CKD is divided into five stages based on the Glomerular Filtration Rate (eGFR):
1. **Stage 1 (eGFR $\ge 90$):** Kidney damage with normal or high kidney function.
2. **Stage 2 (eGFR $60 - 89$):** Mild decrease in kidney function.
3. **Stage 3 (eGFR $30 - 59$):** Moderate decrease in kidney function. (Requires close nephrologist monitoring).
4. **Stage 4 (eGFR $15 - 29$):** Severe decrease in kidney function. (Preparation for dialysis/transplant).
5. **Stage 5 (eGFR $< 15$ - End-Stage Renal Disease):** **Kidney Failure.** The kidneys can no longer sustain life. Immediate dialysis or transplant is mandatory.

## Symptoms of Kidney Failure

### English
* Decreased urine output (oliguria) or complete absence of urine (anuria); swelling in the legs, ankles, feet, or face (fluid retention/edema); shortness of breath (due to fluid building up in the lungs - pulmonary edema); persistent nausea, vomiting, loss of appetite, and a metallic taste in the mouth (uremia); severe fatigue, drowsiness, and confusion; itchy skin.

## First Aid & Home Care (For CKD/Dialysis Patients)

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Restrict daily fluid intake strictly** as advised by the nephrologist (usually limited to $800\text{ mL} - 1\text{ Liter}$ per day, including water, tea, and dal). Excess fluid leads to heart strain and lung congestion.
* **Follow a low-potassium, low-sodium, and low-protein diet.** Avoid high-potassium foods (bananas, mangoes, coconut water, citrus fruits, green leafy vegetables) to prevent cardiac arrest.
* **Monitor blood pressure daily.** High BP accelerates kidney damage.
* **Maintain vascular access (AV Fistula)** carefully if on hemodialysis. Keep it clean, avoid blood pressure checks or needle pricks on that arm, and check daily for a "thrill" (vibration indicating blood flow).

#### Hindi
* **तरल पदार्थ (Fluid) का सेवन सीमित करें:** डॉक्टर के निर्देशानुसार दिनभर में केवल 800ml - 1 लीटर पानी/चाय पिएं।
* **पोटेशियम और सोडियम कम खाएं:** केला, आम, नारियल पानी न लें, इससे दिल का दौरा (cardiac arrest) पड़ सकता है।
* **फिस्टुला (AV Fistula) की देखभाल करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Paani/Fluid intake limit karein:** Rojana pine ke paani aur baki liquids (chai, souch, dal) ki quantity strictly 800ml se 1 Liter tak hi rakhein. Zyada paani pine se lungs me paani bhar jayega aur saans rukne lagegi.
* **Low Potassium diet lein:** High-potassium wale foods (kela, aam, nariyal pani, nimbu paani, palak) bilkul na khayein. Blood me potassium level badhne se heart sudden block (cardiac arrest) ho sakta hai.
* **AV Fistula ka dhyan rakhein:** Jis haath me dialysis ke liye fistula bana ho, us haath par heavy weight na uthayein, us haath se BP check na karwayein, aur usme vibrate (thrill) mehsus karte rahein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT take over-the-counter painkillers** (NSAIDs like Ibuprofen, Diclofenac, Naproxen, or Meftal). These drugs are highly nephrotoxic and can completely shut down remaining kidney function. Use Paracetamol only for pain, under guidance.
* **Do NOT use salt substitutes** (low-sodium salts), as they are made of potassium chloride and can cause fatal hyperkalemia.
* **Do NOT skip dialysis sessions.** Missing even one session causes toxic waste and fluid to build up rapidly, which can be fatal.

#### Hindi
* **दर्दनिवारक (NSAIDs) बिल्कुल न लें:** इबुप्रोफेन या डाइक्लोफेनाक लेने से बचे हुए गुर्दे भी पूरी तरह नष्ट हो सकते हैं।
* **लो-सोडियम नमक (Salt substitute) न खाएं:** इनमें पोटेशियम क्लोराइड होता है, जो दिल के लिए खतरनाक है।

#### Hinglish
* **Painkillers (NSAIDs) bilkul na lein:** Ibuprofen, Diclofenac (voveran) ya Combiflam painkillers bilkul na khayein. Yeh medicines bachi-khuchi kidney ko bhi completely destroy kar deti hain. Dard ke liye paracetamol le sakte hain.
* **Low-sodium salt (Senda namak/kala namak) na lein:** Inme potassium chloride hota hai jo kidney patients ke liye zeher samaan hai.
* **Dialysis miss na karein:** Doctor dwara batayi gayi date par zaroori dialysis karwayein, ek bhi session miss karne se body me zeher aur paani bhar sakta hai.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Serum Creatinine & Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN):** Blood checks to measure waste levels. (Normal Creatinine: $0.6 - 1.2\text{ mg/dL}$).
* **Glomerular Filtration Rate (eGFR):** Calculated from creatinine, age, and gender to identify the CKD stage.
* **Urine Routine & Microscopy:** Checks for protein loss (Albuminuria) and red blood cells.
* **Kidney Ultrasound:** Evaluates kidney size. Shrinkage ($< 8.5\text{ cm}$) indicates chronic, irreversible damage (CKD).

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to kidney failure management:*
1. **Phosphate Binders:** Prevent phosphorus absorption from food (e.g., Calcium Acetate, Sevelamer, Lanthanum Carbonate).
2. **Erythropoietin-Stimulating Agents (ESAs):** Treat anemia caused by lack of kidney hormone (e.g., Erythropoietin injections, Darbepoetin).
3. **Potassium Binders:** Treat mild hyperkalemia (e.g., Sodium Polystyrene Sulfonate, Patiromer).
4. **Antihypertensives:** Protect kidneys (e.g., ARBs/ACE inhibitors like Telmisartan, Ramipril for early CKD). *Avoid in advanced AKI.*

## Prevention of Kidney Failure
* **Control Blood Sugar:** Diabetes is the leading cause of kidney failure (Diabetic Nephropathy). Keep $HbA1c < 7\%$.
* **Manage Hypertension:** Keep blood pressure $< 130/80\text{ mmHg}$ to prevent glomerular damage.
* **Avoid Nephrotoxic Drugs:** Limit use of painkillers and unverified herbal supplements.
* **Stay Hydrated:** Drink adequate water (for healthy kidneys; not for failed kidneys).

---

## Possible Complications of Kidney Failure
* **Hyperkalemia:** High potassium ($> 6.0\text{ mEq/L}$) stopping the heart from beating.
* **Pulmonary Edema:** Fluid accumulation in lungs causing suffocation.
* **Uremic Encephalopathy:** Brain toxicity from urea buildup, leading to seizures and coma.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Severe difficulty breathing, gasping for air, or chest pain (fluid in lungs).**
* **The patient becomes confused, drowsy, starts twitching, or has a seizure (uremic brain toxicity).**
* **Sudden irregular heartbeat or palpitations (sign of dangerously high potassium).**
* **Complete stoppage of urine for over 24 hours accompanied by severe face/leg swelling.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is kidney failure?**
   It is a condition where the kidneys lose their ability to filter metabolic waste products and excess fluid from the blood.

2. **What is the difference between AKI and CKD?**
   AKI is a sudden, temporary loss of kidney function that can be reversed. CKD is a slow, permanent loss of function that cannot be reversed.

3. **What is a normal Creatinine level?**
   Normal serum creatinine is $0.6$ to $1.2\text{ mg/dL}$. A rising level indicates declining kidney function.

4. **Why do kidney failure patients develop swelling (edema)?**
   Failed kidneys cannot excrete excess water and sodium. This fluid pools in tissues, causing swelling in the legs, feet, and face.

5. **What is Glomerular Filtration Rate (eGFR)?**
   eGFR is a calculated score representing how many milliliters of blood the kidneys filter per minute. Normal is $> 90$; below $15$ indicates kidney failure.

6. **Why is high potassium dangerous in kidney failure?**
   Failed kidneys cannot excrete potassium. High blood potassium (hyperkalemia) disrupts the heart's electrical signals, which can cause sudden cardiac arrest.

7. **What foods are high in potassium and must be avoided?**
   Avoid bananas, mangoes, citrus fruits, coconut water, potatoes, tomatoes, and spinach.

8. **Where can I get free dialysis in India?**
   Under the Pradhan Mantri National Dialysis Programme (PMNDP), BPL cardholders receive free hemodialysis at all government district hospitals.

9. **What is an AV Fistula?**
   An AV Fistula is a surgically created connection between an artery and a vein in the arm, used to provide reliable access for hemodialysis needles.

10. **Why do kidney patients develop anemia?**
    Healthy kidneys produce the hormone erythropoietin, which tells the bone marrow to make red blood cells. Failed kidneys stop producing this hormone, causing severe anemia.

11. **Can I use low-sodium salt (Senda namak) in kidney disease?**
    No. Salt substitutes contain potassium chloride instead of sodium chloride. Ingesting them can raise potassium to fatal levels. Use normal salt in restricted amounts.

12. **Why is fluid restriction crucial for dialysis patients?**
    Since failed kidneys cannot make urine, any water drunk builds up in the blood. This causes high blood pressure and can leak into the lungs (pulmonary edema), causing suffocation.

13. **What is uremia?**
    Uremia is the buildup of toxic nitrogenous waste products (urea, creatinine) in the blood, causing nausea, vomiting, metallic taste, and brain confusion.

14. **Is kidney transplant a permanent cure?**
    While it is the best treatment and allows a normal lifestyle, it requires taking lifelong immunosuppressant drugs to prevent the body from rejecting the new kidney.

15. **What is the price of generic sevelamer at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Sevelamer (400mg/800mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, costing only ₹150 to ₹250 per pack, compared to ₹1,000+ for branded versions.

16. **Why are NSAID painkillers like Diclofenac banned in kidney disease?**
    NSAIDs block prostaglandins that keep blood vessels in the kidneys dilated. Taking them narrows these vessels, cutting off blood flow and causing acute kidney failure.

17. **What is the difference between hemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis?**
    Hemodialysis filters blood outside the body using a machine at a clinic 3 times a week. Peritoneal dialysis uses a catheter in the abdomen to filter waste at home daily.

18. **How does diabetes damage the kidneys?**
    High blood sugar damages the tiny filtering units (glomeruli) in the kidneys over years, causing protein to leak into urine (Diabetic Nephropathy).

19. **What is the role of Erythropoietin injections?**
    Erythropoietin (EPO) injections are synthetic hormones given weekly to dialysis patients to stimulate red blood cell production and treat severe anemia.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover kidney transplant surgery?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for kidney transplant surgery, donor workup, ICU stays, and post-transplant immunosuppressants.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Drinking 5 liters of water daily will cure failed kidneys.
  * **Fact:** While water is good for healthy kidneys, drinking excess water when kidneys have already failed is highly dangerous, leading to fluid overload in the lungs and heart failure. Restrict fluids.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key or metal cures kidney failure.
  * **Fact:** Sucking on metals has no medical effect on kidney filters. It is a dangerous myth. Only dialysis or transplant can treat kidney failure.
* **Myth:** Once you start dialysis, you can never stop and will die soon.
  * **Fact:** Dialysis replaces kidney function and keeps the patient alive and active for years. For AKI, dialysis is temporary until the kidneys recover.
* **Myth:** Eating low-sodium salt (Senda namak) is completely safe for kidney patients.
  * **Fact:** Low-sodium salt is rich in potassium, which can cause sudden cardiac arrest in kidney patients. Avoid it completely.
* **Myth:** Kidney failure only happens to old people.
  * **Fact:** Glomerulonephritis, genetic diseases (polycystic kidney), or drug toxicities can cause kidney failure in children and young adults.
* **Myth:** If you are passing normal volumes of urine, your kidneys are 100% healthy.
  * **Fact:** In early stages of kidney failure, the kidneys can still excrete water (urine) but lose the ability to filter microscopic toxins (urea/creatinine). Check blood creatinine.
* **Myth:** Ayurvedic or herbal pills can cure Stage 5 CKD without dialysis.
  * **Fact:** There is no herbal cure for failed kidney filters. Many heavy-metal-based herbal preparations actually accelerate kidney destruction.
* **Myth:** Kidney transplant requires replacing the old kidneys.
  * **Fact:** In a transplant, the two non-functioning kidneys are usually left in place. The new donor kidney is placed in the lower abdomen (pelvis) and connected to blood vessels.
* **Myth:** Dialysis is extremely painful and causes permanent weakness.
  * **Fact:** While insertion of needles can cause mild discomfort, dialysis itself is not painful. Modern dialysis allows patients to work and travel normally.
* **Myth:** A high-protein diet is good for kidney failure patients before starting dialysis.
  * **Fact:** Before starting dialysis, a low-protein diet is advised to reduce the buildup of nitrogenous waste (urea). Once on regular dialysis, protein intake is increased to replace lost amino acids.
