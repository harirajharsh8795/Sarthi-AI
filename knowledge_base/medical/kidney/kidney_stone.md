# Kidney Stone (Pathri) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Renal Colic and Hydration Guide)

## Overview

### English
Kidney Stones, clinically termed **Renal Calculi** or **Nephrolithiasis**, are hard, crystalline deposits of minerals and acid salts that form inside the kidneys when urine becomes highly concentrated. The most common type is **Calcium Oxalate** stones. When a stone moves from the kidney into the narrow ureter, it blocks urine flow, triggering **Renal Colic**—a sudden, excruciating, sharp flank pain (side of the back) that radiates downward to the lower abdomen and groin. Diagnostics utilize non-contrast CT KUB (Kidney-Ureter-Bladder). Small stones ($< 5\text{ mm}$) usually pass naturally with high hydration and Medical Expulsive Therapy (Tamsulosin). Large stones require lithotripsy (ESWL) or minimally invasive surgical removal (URSL, PCNL).

## Quick Summary
Kidney stones are mineral deposits causing renal colic. Symptoms feature sharp, spasmodic flank pain radiating to the groin, vomiting, and hematuria (blood in urine). First aid uses intensive hydration (if no blockage) and NSAID pain relief. Diagnostics use NCCT KUB. Management utilizes medical expulsive therapy or surgical lithotripsy.

---

## Definition
* **Renal Colic:** The acute, severe, wave-like pain caused by the stretching of the ureter due to a stone obstruction.
* **Hematuria:** The presence of red blood cells in the urine, making it look pink, red, or smoky brown, caused by the stone scraping the ureteral wall.
* **MET (Medical Expulsive Therapy):** The clinical use of medications (like alpha-blockers) to relax ureteral muscles, helping stones pass spontaneously.

---

## Types of Kidney Stones & Dietary Causes

Stones form due to specific mineral excesses in the urine:
1. **Calcium Oxalate (75-80% of cases):** Caused by low water intake, high sodium, and eating oxalate-rich foods (spinach, chocolate, tea, nuts).
2. **Uric Acid Stones:** Caused by high-protein diets (non-veg), gout, or dehydration. These are invisible on normal X-rays (radiolucent).
3. **Struvite (Infection) Stones:** Caused by chronic urinary tract infections (UTIs) by urea-splitting bacteria, forming large "staghorn" stones filling the kidney.
4. **Cystine Stones:** A rare genetic disorder causing excess cystine amino acid leakage.

## First Aid & Renal Colic Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Increase fluid intake significantly (if able to tolerate).** Drink 3 to 4 liters of water daily to help flush small stones out of the urinary tract. *However, do not force water if the patient is vomiting continuously.*
* **Take NSAID painkillers** (like Diclofenac, Ibuprofen) under medical guidance. Unlike other pains, renal colic is caused by local inflammation and pressure; NSAIDs reduce ureteral swelling, providing superior pain relief compared to normal antispasmodics.
* **Strain urine** using a clean cloth or tea strainer to catch the stone when it passes, so it can be clinically analyzed.
* **Apply hot water fermentation** (heating pad) on the painful side of the back to relax muscles.

#### Hindi
* **अधिक पानी पिएं:** दिनभर में 3-4 लीटर पानी पिएं (अगर उल्टी न हो रही हो)।
* **दर्दनिवारक (NSAIDs) लें:** रीनल कोलिक दर्द के लिए डाइक्लोफेनाक सबसे प्रभावी है।
* **पेशाब को छानें:** पथरी बाहर निकलने पर उसे लैब जांच के लिए रखें।

#### Hinglish
* **Khub paani piyein:** Rojana 3 se 4 liters paani piyein taaki pressure se chhoti pathri nikal sake. Par agar continuous vomiting ho, to forced paani na piyein.
* **Diclofenac/NSAID painkillers lein:** Pathri ke dard (renal colic) ke liye **Diclofenac (voveran/dynapar)** ya Ibuprofen tablets sabse best kaam karti hain kyunki ye ureter ki sujan ko kam karti hain.
* **Peshab ko chanein:** Peshab karte waqt tea strainer ya kapde se chanein taaki agar pathri nikle to use lab analysis ke liye bacha sakein.
* **Garam paani ki sek karein:** Back side me heating pad se sek karne par muscle pain me aaram milta hai.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT take excess water if urine is completely blocked.** If the patient has not passed urine for hours, drinking excessive water will build up pressure in the kidney, worsening the pain and risking kidney damage (hydronephrosis).
* **Do NOT self-medicate with high-dose calcium pills.**
* **Do NOT delay surgery** if the stone is large and causing severe kidney swelling.

#### Hindi
* **पेशाब पूरी तरह बंद होने पर पानी न पिएं:** इससे किडनी में दबाव (hydronephrosis) बढ़ सकता है।
* **बिना जांच के देशी काढ़ा या दवाएं न लें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Peshab band hone par excess paani na piyein:** Agar patient ka peshab bilkul band ho gaya hai, to dher sara paani na piyein warna kidney me paani bhar jayega (hydronephrosis) jisse kidney block hokar damage ho sakti hai.
* **Bina doctor ke kaadha na piyein:** High doses me unverified desi jadibutiyan ya kaadha pine se acidity aur kidney damage ka risk rehta hai.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Non-Contrast CT KUB (NCCT KUB):** The absolute gold standard diagnostic scan. Detects all stone types, sizes, and locations with $99\%$ accuracy.
* **Ultrasound KUB:** A safe screening test. Shows kidney swelling (**Hydronephrosis**) and large stones, but can miss small stones in the middle ureter.
* **Urine Routine:** Checks for red blood cells (microscopic hematuria) and white blood cells (indicating infection).
* **KFT (Kidney Function Test):** Checks blood Creatinine to ensure both kidneys are filtering normally.

## Medical & Surgical Management

### Medical Expulsive Therapy (MET)

#### English
* For small stones ($< 10\text{ mm}$), doctors prescribe Alpha-Blockers like **Tamsulosin (0.4mg daily)**. It relaxes ureteral smooth muscles, facilitating spontaneous passage.

#### Hindi
* छोटी पथरी के लिए **टैमसुलोसिन (Tamsulosin)** दवा देना, जो मूत्रनली को चौड़ा करती है।

#### Hinglish
* Chhoti pathri (< 10mm) ko nikalne ke liye doctor **Tamsulosin (0.4mg daily)** tablet dete hain. Yeh nali ki muscles ko relax (dhila) karti hai jisse pathri bina dard ke nikal jati hai.

### Surgical Interventions (for large stones)

#### English
* **ESWL (Lithotripsy):** Shockwaves focus on the stone to break it into dust.
* **URSL (Laser):** A thin scope enters the ureter to dust the stone.
* **PCNL:** Minimally invasive keyhole surgery through the back for large staghorn stones.

#### Hindi
* **लिथोट्रिप्सी (ESWL):** शॉकवेव से पथरी तोड़ना।
* **पीसीएनएल (PCNL) / यूआरएसएल (URSL):** दूरबीन या लेजर विधि से पथरी निकालना।

#### Hinglish
* Badi pathri hone par: **ESWL** (shock waves se pathri todna), **URSL** (laser nali ke raste pathri todna), ya **PCNL** (back me chota hole karke kidney se badi pathri nikalna) surgery ki jati hai.

2. **NSAID Analgesics:** Relieve renal colic pain (e.g., Diclofenac injection/tablet, Ketorolac).
3. **Urinary Alkalinizers:** Dissolve uric acid stones (e.g., Potassium Citrate / Citric Acid oral solution).
4. **Antispasmodics:** Reduce smooth muscle spasm (e.g., Drotaverine).

## Prevention of Kidney Stones
* **Abundant Hydration:** Drink at least 3 liters of water daily. Urine should be light yellow or clear.
* **Reduce Dietary Oxalates:** Limit spinach, chocolate, nuts, soy, and strong tea.
* **Limit Sodium (Salt):** High salt forces calcium into urine, forming stones. Keep salt $< 5\text{ g/day}$.
* **Eat Citric Fruits:** Lemons, oranges, and limes contain citrate, which binds calcium in urine, preventing stone crystals.
* **Normal Calcium Intake:** Do not stop eating dairy (milk/curd). Dietary calcium binds oxalates in the stomach, preventing stone formation.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Stones
* **Hydronephrosis:** Swelling and pressure inside the kidney due to urine blockage, causing permanent kidney damage.
* **Pyelonephritis (Severe UTI):** Blocked urine becoming infected, leading to kidney pus and sepsis.
* **Ureteral Stricture:** Scarring of the ureteral wall.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient has high fever with chills/shivering, severe flank pain, and vomiting (indicates kidney infection/sepsis).**
* **The patient is unable to pass any urine for over 12-24 hours.**
* **The pain is completely uncontrolled despite taking oral painkillers.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a kidney stone?**
   A kidney stone is a hard deposit of minerals and salts (most commonly calcium oxalate) that crystallizes in the kidneys due to concentrated urine.

2. **What is "Renal Colic"?**
   Renal colic is the sudden, severe, sharp pain caused by a stone blocking the ureter, characteristically radiating from the back to the groin.

3. **Why do doctors check NCCT KUB for kidney stones?**
   Non-Contrast CT KUB is the gold standard diagnostic scan because it detects all types of stones (including uric acid stones which are invisible on normal X-rays).

4. **How does Tamsulosin help in passing kidney stones?**
   Tamsulosin is an alpha-blocker that relaxes the smooth muscles of the lower ureter, widening the tube and allowing small stones to pass out easily.

5. **Should I stop drinking milk if I have calcium stones?**
   No. Stopping dairy is a mistake. Normal dietary calcium binds oxalates in your stomach before they enter the kidneys, actually preventing stone formation.

6. **What is ESWL (Lithotripsy)?**
   ESWL (Extracorporeal Shock Wave Lithotripsy) is a non-invasive treatment where high-energy shock waves are focused on the stone to break it into tiny gravel.

7. **Where can BPL cardholders get free kidney stone surgery in India?**
   Eligible cardholders can receive completely free cashless PCNL, URSL, or laser surgeries at all government and empaneled private hospitals under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY card.

8. **Why does a kidney stone cause vomiting?**
   The kidneys and stomach share common nerve pathways (celiac plexus). Severe stretching of the ureter by a stone triggers reflex nausea and vomiting.

9. **What is a Double-J (DJ) Stent?**
   A DJ stent is a thin, flexible plastic tube placed in the ureter to keep it open, bypass blockages, and help stone fragments drain safely after surgery.

10. **Why does high salt intake cause kidney stones?**
    High sodium (salt) forces extra calcium out of the blood and into the urine, raising the concentration of calcium in the kidneys and promoting crystallization.

11. **Can beer dissolve kidney stones?**
    No. Drinking beer does not dissolve stones. While it increases urine flow, alcohol causes dehydration, which raises the risk of future stone formation. Stick to water.

12. **What is "Hydronephrosis"?**
    Hydronephrosis is the stretching and swelling of the kidney pelvis with trapped urine, caused by a stone blocking the ureter, which can lead to kidney failure.

13. **Which foods are high in oxalates and should be avoided?**
    Avoid spinach (palak), beetroot, chocolate, nuts (cashews, almonds), soy products, and strong black tea.

14. **How does lemon juice prevent kidney stones?**
    Lemons are rich in Citrate. Citrate binds to calcium in the urine, preventing calcium from combining with oxalate to form crystals.

15. **What is the price of generic Tamsulosin at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Tamsulosin (0.4mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹40 to ₹65 per strip, compared to ₹180-₹240 for branded versions.

16. **Why is a fever with a kidney stone a medical emergency?**
    Fever with chills indicates a blocked kidney that has developed a bacterial infection (pyelonephritis). This can cause life-threatening sepsis (pus in blood).

17. **What is PCNL surgery?**
    PCNL (Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy) is a keyhole surgery where a surgeon makes a small incision in the back to directly enter the kidney and remove large stones.

18. **Can uric acid stones be dissolved without surgery?**
    Yes. Uric acid stones can often be dissolved by alkalizing the urine (raising pH) using oral Potassium Citrate syrup, combined with high water intake.

19. **How much water should a kidney stone patient drink?**
    Drink at least 3 to 4 liters of water daily. A good indicator is urine color: it should be crystal clear or very light yellow.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover laser stone dusting (URSL)?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for laser lithotripsy, endourology procedures, DJ stenting, and all diagnostic CT scans.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Drinking beer is the best medical treatment to pass kidney stones.
  * **Fact:** Beer increases urine flow temporarily but causes dehydration and uric acid buildup, raising the risk of more stones. Drink water or lemon juice.
* **Myth:** Stop eating all calcium-rich foods (milk, curd) if you have calcium stones.
  * **Fact:** Restricting dietary calcium raises oxalate levels in your blood, increasing stone risk. Eat normal calcium and restrict oxalate-rich foods instead.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures the renal colic pain instantly.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys has no medical effect on ureter spasms or stones. Use NSAID painkillers like Diclofenac or seek urologist care.
* **Myth:** All kidney stones require immediate surgery.
  * **Fact:** Most stones smaller than 5mm pass naturally with high hydration and alpha-blockers. Only large or blocking stones need surgery.
* **Myth:** Kidney stones are caused by swallowing tomato or guava seeds.
  * **Fact:** Swallowed seeds pass out through stool, not urine. Kidney stones form from microscopic mineral salts in the kidneys, not whole seeds.
* **Myth:** Once a kidney stone is surgically removed, you will never get them again.
  * **Fact:** Kidney stone recurrence rate is 50% within 5-10 years if dietary habits (low water, high salt) are not corrected.
* **Myth:** A normal X-ray rules out all kidney stones completely.
  * **Fact:** Uric acid and cystine stones are "radiolucent" (invisible on normal X-rays). Only a CT scan or ultrasound can detect them.
* **Myth:** Drinking cranberry juice dissolves kidney stones.
  * **Fact:** Cranberry juice prevents UTIs but does not dissolve kidney stones. Use Potassium Citrate or lemon juice for stones.
* **Myth:** Kidney stones only occur in men.
  * **Fact:** While more common in men, women can develop kidney stones due to chronic UTIs (struvite stones) or metabolic disorders.
* **Myth:** Intestinal pain and kidney stone pain are the same.
  * **Fact:** Intestinal pain is diffuse and centered around the navel. Kidney stone pain (renal colic) is a sharp, severe wave-like flank pain radiating to the groin.
