# Suicide Prevention (Suicide Prevention) ki Pehchan, Warning Signs aur Safety Plan (Crisis Intervention and Tele-MANAS Guide)

## Overview

### English
Suicide Prevention involves a coordinated clinical, social, and environmental framework designed to identify, de-escalate, and support individuals experiencing acute suicidal ideation (thoughts of ending one's life). Suicidal behavior is frequently a severe complication of underlying psychiatric illnesses, particularly Major Depressive Disorder, Bipolar Disorder, Borderline Personality Disorder, or severe Substance Abuse. First aid requires recognizing verbal and behavioral warning signs, executing an immediate **Suicide Safety Plan** (including direct questioning, non-judgmental active listening, and complete removal of all lethal means), and immediately accessing emergency psychiatric resources like the national **Tele-MANAS helpline (14416)**.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
Suicide prevention targets reducing self-harm incidence. Key warning signs feature talking about death, giving away belongings, and withdrawing. First aid requires direct questioning, non-judgmental listening, removing all lethal means, and continuous supervision. Diagnostics assess risk severity. Management combines psychiatric crisis care with safety plans.

## Definition
* **Suicidal Ideation:** Having thoughts, ideas, or plans about committing suicide, which can range from passive thoughts (wishing to not wake up) to active planning.
* **Safety Plan:** A written, structured list of coping strategies and support resources designed to help individuals navigate a suicidal crisis safely.
* **Lethal Means Restriction:** The systematic removal or locking away of highly lethal items (e.g., medicines, pesticides, firearms) to prevent impulsive self-harm.

## Verbal and Behavioral Warning Signs of Suicide

It is critical to recognize these warning signs and take immediate clinical action:

| Warning Sign Category | Verbal Signs (What They Say) | Behavioral Signs (What They Do) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Direct Statements** | *"I want to kill myself,"* *"I wish I were dead"* | Researching methods online (e.g., searching drugs) |
| **Indirect Statements**| *"I won't be a burden much longer,"* *"Nothing matters"*| **Buying lethal items** (ropes, pesticides, pills) |
| **Hopelessness** | *"There is no way out,"* *"Things will never get better"*| **Giving away prized possessions**, writing a will |
| **Isolation** | *"I want to be left completely alone"* | Suddenly saying goodbye to friends and family |
| **Mood Changes** | Expressing deep emotional agony or pain | **Sudden calm or peace** after a long depression |
| *Significance of Calm* | *Indicates they have made the final decision to die.* | *High risk of immediate suicide attempt.* |

## The Suicide Safety Plan Protocol (First Aid for Active Crisis)

If a relative or friend exhibits warning signs or expresses suicidal thoughts, immediately execute this emergency protocol:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Ask the question directly.** Say clearly and calmly: *"Are you thinking about suicide?"* or *"Do you have a plan to end your life?"* Research proves this does not plant the idea; instead, it shows you care, reduces panic, and saves lives.
* **Remove all lethal means immediately.** Lock away and hide all sleeping pills, other medicines, pesticides, ropes, cords, knives, blades, and firearms. **Most suicides are impulsive; removing means buys time for the brain to calm down.**
* **Listen non-judgmentally (Active Listening).** Let them cry, shout, or speak. Do not judge, lecture, or say: *"Think about your family,"* or *"Why are you so weak?"* Just listen and say: *"I am here with you. You don't have to carry this pain alone. We will get help together."*
* **Keep them under 24/7 supervision.** Never leave the person alone, not even for a minute to use the bathroom.
* **Call Tele-MANAS (14416)** immediately.

#### Hindi
* **सीधे और साफ शब्दों में पूछें:** *"क्या तुम आत्महत्या के बारे में सोच रहे हो?"* यह पूछना जान बचाता है।
* **खतरनाक सामान तुरंत हटाएं:** सभी दवाएं, रस्सी, कीटनाशक और धारदार वस्तुएं ताले में बंद कर दें।
* **आलोचना किए बिना सुनें (Active listening):** उपदेश न दें, बस कहें: *"मैं तुम्हारे साथ हूं, हम मिलकर इसका समाधान निकालेंगे।"*
* **अकेला न छोड़ें:** चौबीसों घंटे मरीज पर नजर रखें।

#### Hinglish
* **Direct aur clear sawal puchein:** Unse poochyein—*"Kya aapko suicide (aatmahatya) ka khayal aa raha hai? Kya aapne apni jaan dene ka koi plan banaya hai?"* Direct poochne se patient ko lagta hai ki koi unki takleef samajh raha hai, isse darr kam hota hai.
* **Saare lethal means (khatarnak items) immediate hata dein:** Ghar me rakhi saari sleeping pills, baki medicines, pesticide (keedemarne ki dawa), rassi, chaaku aur blades ko lock kar dein. **Most suicide attempts sudden impulse me hote hain, agar means nahi milenge to jaan bach jayegi.**
* **Non-judgmental active listening karein:** Patient ko lecture na dein, ye na bolein—*"Tujhe kis chiz ka darr hai, himmat rakh!"* Unhe rone aur bolne dein. Bolein—*"Mai tumhare sath hu, hum is problem ko sath milkar solve karenge."*
* **24/7 supervision me rakhein:** Patient ko ek second ke liye bhi akela na chhodein (bathroom bhi akele na jaane dein).
* **Helpline 14416 par call karein.**

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT ignore or dismiss their threats.** Never say: *"You are just doing this for attention"* or *"You are talking like a coward."*
* **Do NOT promise to keep their suicidal thoughts a secret.** You must break confidentiality to get them professional help.
* **Do NOT challenge them.** Never say: *"Go ahead and do it then."*
* **Do NOT leave them alone.**

#### Hindi
* **धमकियों को नजरअंदाज न करें:** यह न कहें कि *"तुम सिर्फ नाटक कर रहे हो।"*
* **बात को गुप्त रखने का वादा न करें:** जान बचाने के लिए डॉक्टरों और परिवार को बताना जरूरी है।
* **सकारात्मक सोच (think positive) के लिए मजबूर न करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Threats ko ignore na karein:** Kabhi ye na bolein—*"Tu bas darrane ke liye bol raha hai,"* ya *"Tujhse kuch nahi hoga."*
* **Secret rakhne ka wada na karein:** Unki suicidal thoughts ko chhiupane ka wada na karein, unki jaan bachane ke liye family aur doctor ko batana mandatory hai.
* **Think Positive ka pressure na banayein:** Clinical depression chemicals ka imbalance hai, ye lecture se nahi, medicine se thik hoga.

## Diagnostic Evaluation of Suicide Risk
* **Psychiatric Risk Assessment:** Clinicians use tools like the **Columbia-Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS)** to classify suicide risk into Low, Moderate, or High (Active intent and plan).
* **Toxicology Screen & Blood Tests:** Done in emergency rooms if overdose is suspected.
* **Rule out medical causes:** Like hyperthyroidism or heavy drug intoxication.

---

## Medical & Crisis Management
* **Emergency Psychiatric Admission:** If the patient is at High Risk (has a plan and active intent), they must be admitted to a secure psychiatric ward for continuous observation (suicide watch).
* **Pharmacological Therapy:**
   * **Rapid Stabilization:** Lithium Carbonate has proven clinical anti-suicidal properties and is started in bipolar/depressive patients.
   * **Antidepressants (SSRIs):** Treat the underlying depression over weeks.
   * **Ketamine / Esketamine Infusion:** Used in tertiary psychiatric clinics for rapid reduction of acute suicidal thoughts within hours.

## Prevention of Suicidal Crises
* **Maintain the Safety Plan:** Keep the emergency contact numbers (Tele-MANAS, psychiatrist, trusted family member) written on a card.
* **Restricting Access to Pesticides:** Lock up agricultural pesticides (the leading cause of impulsive suicide in rural India).
* **Treat Mental Illness Early:** Seek clinical help at the first signs of depression or schizophrenia.
* **Responsible Media Reporting:** Media must avoid detailing suicide methods to prevent copycat suicides (the Werther effect).

---

## Possible Complications of Unprevented Suicidal Behavior
* **Death:** The ultimate tragedy.
* **Permanent Disability:** Severe brain damage from lack of oxygen (after hanging/drowning attempts), liver/kidney failure (after pesticide/drug overdoses), or permanent paralysis (after jumps).
* **Intense Family Trauma:** Post-traumatic stress and high suicide risk in surviving family members.

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient has swallowed a large amount of pills, drank chemical poisons, or is actively attempting self-harm.**
* **The patient states: *"I have decided to end my life today,"* and has secured a weapon or method.**
* **The patient collapses, loses consciousness, or has severe breathing difficulty.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **Tele-MANAS (Tele Mental Health Assistance and Networking Across States):** A 24/7 free, confidential central helpline (**14416** or **1800-891-4416**) providing immediate psychological counseling and suicide crisis de-escalation in 22 regional Indian languages.
* **Decriminalization of Suicide (Mental Healthcare Act, 2017):** Section 115 states that any person who attempts suicide shall be presumed to have severe stress and **shall not be prosecuted or punished under the IPC.** The government is legally required to provide rehabilitation and care.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless packages for emergency poisoning stomach washes, intensive care (ICU) admissions for self-harm injuries, and psychiatric ward care.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Provides generic antidepressants, mood stabilizers, and emergency medical supplies at 80% discounted rates.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is suicide prevention?**
   Suicide prevention refers to coordinated efforts to reduce suicide rates by providing mental health care, crisis counseling, and restricting access to lethal means.

2. **Why should I ask someone directly if they are thinking about suicide?**
   Asking directly does not put the idea in their head. It shows that you are willing to listen to their pain, which decreases their anxiety and risk of suicide.

3. **What is a Suicide Safety Plan?**
   A safety plan is a simple, written list of personal coping strategies, trusted contacts, and emergency helpline numbers that a person can use when having a crisis.

4. **Why is locking away medicines and pesticides important?**
   Most suicide attempts are highly impulsive. If a person cannot easily access a lethal method during a 10-minute wave of intense pain, the impulse will pass, saving their life.

5. **Where can I get immediate free help for suicidal thoughts in India?**
   You can call the government's free, 24/7 Tele-MANAS helpline at **14416** to speak directly with trained mental health counselors.

6. **What does a sudden "calmness" in a depressed person mean?**
   If a severely depressed person suddenly becomes very peaceful or happy, it is a major warning sign. It often means they have resolved their conflict by deciding to end their life.

7. **Is suicide illegal in India?**
   No. Under Section 115 of the Mental Healthcare Act 2017, a person who attempts suicide is presumed to be under extreme stress and is not punished. The government must provide them with care.

8. **Which medicine has proven anti-suicidal properties?**
   **Lithium Carbonate** is clinically proven to reduce the risk of suicide in patients with Bipolar Disorder and Major Depressive Disorder.

9. **What is the Columbia-Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS)?**
   It is a standard questionnaire used by psychiatrists to evaluate the severity of a patient's suicidal thoughts and determine if they require urgent hospital admission.

10. **Why should I not tell a suicidal person to "think of their family"?**
    Suicidal patients often suffer from a distortion called "perceived burdensomeness." They genuinely believe their family would be happier and better off if they were dead. Arguing this worsens their guilt.

11. **What is the Werther Effect?**
    It is a phenomenon where detailed media reporting of a suicide (especially of a celebrity) triggers a wave of copycat suicides in the community.

12. **Can Ketamine be used for suicide prevention?**
    Yes. In hospital settings, low-dose IV Ketamine or intranasal Esketamine infusions are used to rapidly shut down acute suicidal thoughts within hours in severe crises.

13. **Why should I never promise to keep a friend's suicidal thoughts a secret?**
    A suicidal crisis is a medical emergency. Keeping it secret prevents them from getting professional help, which can be fatal. Break confidentiality to save their life.

14. **What is the price of generic Lithium at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Lithium Carbonate (300mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹15 to ₹25 per strip of 10 tablets.

15. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover self-harm ICU admissions?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for ICU stays, poisoning treatments, mechanical ventilators, surgery for self-injury, and psychiatric therapy.

16. **Is suicidal behavior genetic?**
    A family history of suicide or depression raises the risk due to genetic factors and shared environments. However, treatment and support can prevent it.

17. **What is a "passive" suicidal thought?**
    A passive thought is wishing to die or not wake up (e.g., *"I wish I would get hit by a car"*), without actively making a plan. It still requires clinical attention.

18. **Why should I never leave a suicidal person alone?**
    The impulse to self-harm can strike suddenly. Having someone present acts as a physical barrier and provides immediate support, preventing action.

19. **What is the role of the Tele-MANAS program?**
    It is a national initiative providing 24/7 free mental health counseling, crisis de-escalation, and psychiatric referrals across all Indian states.

20. **Can therapy help prevent future suicidal crises?**
    Yes. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) teach patients healthy coping mechanisms, problem-solving, and emotional regulation.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** People who talk about suicide are just seeking attention and will never actually do it.
  * **Fact:** Almost everyone who attempts suicide gives verbal or behavioral warning signs beforehand. Never ignore suicide threats; treat every threat as a medical emergency.
* **Myth:** Asking someone *"Are you thinking of killing yourself?"* will give them the idea and cause suicide.
  * **Fact:** Asking directly reduces anxiety, shows you care, and allows the person to share their pain, which actively lowers suicide risk.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures suicidal thoughts by absorbing the negative brain energy.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless myth. Suicidal thoughts are driven by severe clinical depression or psychiatric crises requiring medical and psychological care.
* **Myth:** People who attempt suicide are selfish and do not care about their loved ones.
  * **Fact:** Suicidal patients suffer from extreme, unbearable mental agony. Their brain chemistry is distorted, making them believe they are a burden and that their family is better off without them.
* **Myth:** Suicide attempts should be punished by the police under Section 309 of the IPC.
  * **Fact:** Section 309 has been effectively bypassed by the Mental Healthcare Act 2017. Attempting suicide is now treated as a call for help and stress, not a crime.
* **Myth:** If someone has made up their mind to commit suicide, nothing can stop them.
  * **Fact:** Suicidal crises are usually short-term and highly impulsive. If you intervene, restrict access to lethal methods, and support them through the crisis, they can live a long life.
* **Myth:** Only crazy or weak people commit suicide.
  * **Fact:** Suicide can affect anyone, including successful, highly intelligent, and strong individuals who are overwhelmed by severe clinical depression or life trauma.
* **Myth:** Antidepressants cure suicidal thoughts instantly on the first day.
  * **Fact:** Antidepressants take 3-6 weeks to work. In fact, in young adults, they can temporarily raise agitation and suicidal thoughts in the first 2 weeks, requiring close monitoring.
* **Myth:** Senda namak (rock salt) stops suicidal impulses by balancing negative vibes.
  * **Fact:** Rock salt has no chemical effect on brain receptors or depression. Use proven medical care and call Tele-MANAS (14416).
* **Myth:** A person who survives a suicide attempt will never try it again.
  * **Fact:** A past suicide attempt is the single strongest risk factor for future suicide. They require continuous, long-term psychiatric follow-up.
