# Anxiety Disorders (Anxiety/Ghabrahat) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Management (Panic Attacks and CBT Guide)

## Overview

### English
Anxiety Disorders represent a group of mental health conditions characterized by excessive, persistent, and uncontrollable worry, fear, and dread that interfere with daily activities. Major types include **Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)**, **Panic Disorder** (characterized by sudden panic attacks), and **Social Anxiety Disorder**. During a **Panic Attack**, severe physical symptoms like rapid palpitations, chest tightness, and shortness of breath occur, frequently mimicking a heart attack. First-line clinical treatments involve **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)** and targeted pharmacotherapy (e.g., SSRIs).

## Quick Summary
Anxiety disorders feature persistent, excessive worry. Types include GAD and panic disorder. Symptoms feature panic attacks, palpitations, chest tightness, and fear of dying. First aid utilizes the 4-7-8 deep breathing technique. Diagnostics rule out cardiac causes. Management combines CBT therapy with SSRIs.

## Definition
* **Anxiety:** A normal emotional response to stress, which becomes a disorder when it is excessive, out of proportion to the situation, and lasts for more than 6 months.
* **Panic Attack:** A sudden, intense episode of fear triggering severe physical reactions when there is no real danger or apparent cause.
* **CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy):** A structured psychological therapy that helps patients identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors.

---

## Differentiating a Panic Attack from a Heart Attack

Because a panic attack replicates cardiac distress, it is crucial to recognize clinical differences:

| Symptom Feature | Panic Attack (Anxiety) | Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pain Character** | Sharp, localized, stabbing chest pain | **Crushing pressure**, squeezing chest pain |
| **Pain Radiation** | Stays in chest, does not spread | **Radiates to left arm, jaw, neck, or back** |
| **Onset** | Sudden, peak intensity within $10\text{ mins}$ | Gradual, builds over minutes, continuous |
| **Associated Signs** | Trembling, fear of dying, tingling hands | **Cold sweating (diaphoresis), nausea, vomiting** |
| **ECG / Trop-I Test** | Completely normal | **Abnormal ECG / Positive Troponin-I** |

## Symptoms of Anxiety & Panic Disorders

### English
* **Emotional:** Persistent, excessive worry about everyday things; restlessness, irritability, and feeling constantly "on edge"; dreading social situations or fear of losing control.
* **Physical:** Rapid heart rate (palpitations); tightness or pain in the chest; shortness of breath or choking sensation; cold sweats and shaking/trembling; tingling or numbness in hands/feet; chronic muscle tension, headaches, and insomnia.

* **शारीरिक लक्षण:** दिल की धड़कन तेज होना (palpitations); सीने में जकड़न और सांस लेने में कठिनाई; ठंडा पसीना आना और हाथ-पैर कांपना।

## First Aid for a Panic Attack (The 4-7-8 Breathing Technique)

### Immediate Crisis Intervention

#### English
1. **The 4-7-8 Deep Breathing Technique:**
   * **Step 1:** Breathe in quietly through your nose for **4 seconds**.
   * **Step 2:** Hold your breath completely for **7 seconds**.
   * **Step 3:** Exhale completely through your mouth, making a "whoosh" sound, for **8 seconds**.
   * *Repeat this cycle 4 times. Deep slow breathing stimulates the vagus nerve, lowering heart rate and stopping the fight-or-flight panic cycle.*
2. **Grounding Exercise (The 5-4-3-2-1 Method):** Focus the patient's mind back to reality by asking them to name:
   * **5** things they can see.
   * **4** things they can touch/feel.
   * **3** things they can hear.
   * **2** things they can smell.
   * **1** thing they can taste.
3. Reassure the patient: *"You are safe. This is just anxiety. It will pass in a few minutes. You are not having a heart attack."*

#### Hindi
1. **4-7-8 गहरी सांस लेने की तकनीक (4-7-8 Breathing):**
   * **4 सेकंड** तक नाक से धीरे-धीरे सांस लें।
   * **7 सेकंड** तक सांस को रोक कर रखें।
   * **8 सेकंड** तक मुंह से फुफकार मारते हुए सांस पूरी तरह बाहर छोड़ें।
2. **5-4-3-2-1 ग्राउंडिंग (Grounding method):** मरीज का ध्यान भटकाने के लिए आसपास की 5 चीजें देखने, 4 चीजें छूने, 3 आवाजें सुनने, 2 चीजें सूंघने और 1 चीज चखने को कहें।
3. आश्वस्त करें: *"आप सुरक्षित हैं, यह पैनिक अटैक है जो 10 मिनट में ठीक हो जाएगा।"*

#### Hinglish
1. **4-7-8 Deep Breathing Technique apply karein:**
   * **Step 1:** Nose (naak) se **4 seconds** tak slowly saans andar kheencheik.
   * **Step 2:** Saans ko **7 seconds** tak rokk kar (hold) rakhein.
   * **Step 3:** Mouth (muh) se **8 seconds** tak slowly saans ko bahar nikalein (whoosh sound ke sath).
   * *Is cycle ko 4-5 baar karein. Deep breathing vagus nerve ko activate karke heartbeat ko immediately slow karti hai.*
2. **5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Method use karein:** Patient ka dhyan darr se hatane ke liye unse boliye ki wo:
   * **5** chizein jo dekh sakte hain unka naam batayein.
   * **4** chizein jo touch kar sakte hain.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Electrocardiogram (ECG) & Troponin-I Test:** Crucial initial tests done in emergency clinics to rule out heart attack (myocardial infarction).
* **Thyroid Function Test (TFT):** Done to check for **Hyperthyroidism** (overactive thyroid), which can chemically mimic anxiety and panic attacks.
* **Clinical Interview:** Evaluated by a psychiatrist based on DSM-5 diagnostic criteria.

---

## Medical & Psychological Management
* **CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy):** The gold standard psychological treatment. Conducted by a clinical psychologist to help patients reframe catastrophic thoughts.
* **Pharmacological Therapy:**
   * **SSRIs:** Daily long-term therapy to stabilize serotonin levels (e.g., Escitalopram, Sertraline).
   * **Benzodiazepines:** Rapid-acting relief given only for short-term crisis management (e.g., Clonazepam, Alprazolam) under strict supervision due to addiction risks.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to anxiety management:*
1. **SSRIs (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors):** First-line long-term (e.g., Escitalopram, Sertraline).
2. **SNRIs (Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors):** e.g., Duloxetine.
3. **Benzodiazepines (Anxiolytics):** Quick relief but habit-forming (e.g., Clonazepam, Alprazolam).
4. **Beta-Blockers:** Control physical shaking and palpitations (e.g., Propranolol).

## Prevention of Anxiety & Panic Attacks
* **Regular Aerobic Exercise:** Reduces stress hormones and releases endorphins.
* **Avoid Caffeine & Stimulants:** High caffeine (tea, coffee, energy drinks) chemically triggers the heart rate, mimicking or starting a panic attack.
* **Sleep Hygiene:** Ensure 7-8 hours of sound sleep daily.
* **Limit Alcohol & Nicotine:** Avoid substances that increase rebound anxiety.

---

## Possible Complications of Chronic Anxiety
* **Clinical Depression:** Highly comorbid; untreated anxiety leads to depression.
* **Substance Abuse:** Self-medicating with alcohol or drug addiction.
* **Social Isolation:** Agoraphobia (fear of leaving home or entering crowded places).

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient expresses active thoughts of self-harm or suicide due to overwhelming panic/anxiety.**
* **A patient experiencing "anxiety" has chest pain that radiates to their left arm, jaw, neck, accompanied by heavy cold sweating (signs of a true heart attack).**
* **The patient collapses or becomes unresponsive.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is an anxiety disorder?**
   An anxiety disorder is a mental health condition where fear, worry, and tension are excessive, persist for months, and interfere with daily living.

2. **What is a panic attack?**
   A panic attack is a sudden, intense episode of fear that triggers severe physical reactions (palpitations, shortness of breath) when there is no real danger.

3. **How do I tell the difference between a panic attack and a heart attack?**
   Panic attack pain is sharp, peaks fast, and stays in the chest. Heart attack pain feels like crushing pressure, spreads to the left arm/jaw, and is accompanied by cold sweating and vomiting.

4. **What is the 4-7-8 breathing technique?**
   Breathe in through the nose for 4 seconds, hold for 7 seconds, and exhale through the mouth for 8 seconds. This calms the nervous system during panic.

5. **What is CBT?**
   Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a talking therapy that teaches you to identify and change negative, catastrophic thought patterns.

6. **Can thyroid issues cause anxiety?**
   Yes. An overactive thyroid gland (hyperthyroidism) releases excess hormones that speed up metabolism, causing symptoms like palpitations, sweating, and anxiety.

7. **Why are sleeping/anxiety pills like Alprazolam dangerous?**
   Benzodiazepines (Alprazolam, Clonazepam) are highly addictive. Taking them daily for more than a few weeks can cause dependency and severe withdrawal symptoms.

8. **Where can I get free mental health counseling in India?**
   You can call the government's 24/7 free Tele-MANAS helpline (**14416**) to speak directly with trained counselors and psychologists.

9. **Why does caffeine trigger anxiety?**
   Caffeine is a central nervous system stimulant. It increases heart rate and blood pressure, which can chemically trigger a panic attack in sensitive people.

10. **What is Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)?**
    GAD is a condition characterized by chronic, excessive, and uncontrollable worry about everyday life events, lasting for more than 6 months.

11. **How does Propranolol help in anxiety?**
    Propranolol is a beta-blocker. It does not treat the mental worry but blocks adrenaline, stopping physical symptoms like hand shaking, sweating, and rapid heartbeats.

12. **What is the price of generic Escitalopram at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Escitalopram (10mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹15 per strip, compared to ₹70-₹110 in private pharmacies.

13. **Why do my hands and feet tingle during a panic attack?**
    During panic, fast breathing (hyperventilation) lowers carbon dioxide levels in the blood, causing blood vessels to constrict and nerve pathways to tingle (numbness).

14. **What is the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding method?**
    It is a sensory exercise to stop panic: focus on 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, and 1 you taste.

15. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover psychiatric hospitalizations?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless treatment packages for severe psychiatric disorders, acute panic/suicidal crisis monitoring, and therapy.

16. **Is anxiety genetic?**
    Yes. A family history of anxiety disorders or depression raises the risk of developing anxiety. However, environment and stress also play major roles.

17. **What is Agoraphobia?**
    It is a type of anxiety disorder where a person fears and avoids places or situations (like crowds, open spaces, or traveling) that might cause panic or feeling trapped.

18. **Why are SSRIs preferred over Benzodiazepines for long-term anxiety?**
    SSRIs (like Escitalopram) are non-addictive and treat the underlying serotonin imbalance over months, while Benzodiazepines are habit-forming and only mask symptoms.

19. **How long does it take for anxiety medicines to start working?**
    Daily SSRIs typically take **3 to 6 weeks** to show full therapeutic effects on anxiety. Do not stop them early if you do not see immediate results.

20. **Can exercise help reduce anxiety?**
    Yes. Regular aerobic exercise (like brisk walking or swimming) releases endorphins (feel-good hormones), lowers stress hormones, and physically relaxes muscle tension.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Anxiety is just normal worry, and patients should just "stop thinking about it."
  * **Fact:** Anxiety disorders are real medical conditions involving brain chemical imbalances. A patient cannot simply choose to turn off the anxiety without therapy or medicine.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures the palpitations and panic immediately.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys has no medical effect. Deep breathing (4-7-8) stimulates the vagus nerve, which physically slows down the heart rate and panic.
* **Myth:** Taking daily anxiety pills will make you a "zombie" or drug addict forever.
  * **Fact:** Standard long-term treatments like SSRIs are completely non-addictive. When taken under medical guidance, they restore normal brain function without causing sluggishness.
* **Myth:** Senda namak (rock salt) cures anxiety by absorbing negative energy.
  * **Fact:** Rock salt has no chemical effect on brain neurotransmitters or anxiety. Use scientifically proven methods like CBT.
* **Myth:** If you have a panic attack, it means your heart is weak and you will die of a heart attack.
  * **Fact:** Panic attacks are caused by a hyper-active nervous system, not heart disease. A panic attack itself is harmless and will not damage your heart or kill you.
* **Myth:** Children never suffer from anxiety; it is only a disease of adults.
  * **Fact:** Children can develop anxiety disorders, which present as school refusal, stomach aches, temper tantrums, or separation anxiety.
* **Myth:** Drinking alcohol is a great way to calm your nerves and cure anxiety.
  * **Fact:** Alcohol provides temporary relief, but as it wears off, it causes chemical changes in the brain that make anxiety much worse (rebound anxiety).
* **Myth:** Herbal weight-loss teas are safe and help calm anxiety.
  * **Fact:** Many herbal teas contain high levels of caffeine or unregulated stimulants that can trigger severe palpitations and panic attacks.
* **Myth:** You should stop your anxiety medicines as soon as you feel calm.
  * **Fact:** Stopping SSRIs suddenly can cause a relapse of severe anxiety and withdrawal symptoms. Always taper off slowly under a psychiatrist's guidance.
* **Myth:** Anxiety only happens to mentally weak people who cannot handle life stress.
  * **Fact:** Anxiety can affect anyone, regardless of intelligence or mental strength. It is a biological condition linked to genetics and brain chemistry.
