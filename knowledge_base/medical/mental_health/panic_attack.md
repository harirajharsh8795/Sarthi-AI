# Panic Attack (Panic Attack) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Immediate Relief (Breathing and Grounding First Aid Guide)

## Overview

### English
A Panic Attack is a sudden, discrete episode of intense fear, terror, or discomfort that peaks within 10 minutes and triggers severe physical and cognitive reactions, even in the absence of real danger. It involves a massive activation of the **Sympathetic Nervous System (SNS)**, flooding the body with adrenaline. Key physical symptoms include crushing palpitations, chest pain, and shortness of breath, which closely mimic a life-threatening **Heart Attack**. First aid centers on stabilizing respiration via the **4-7-8 breathing technique**, sensory distraction using the **5-4-3-2-1 grounding method**, and seeking medical evaluation to rule out cardiac emergencies.

## Quick Summary
A panic attack is a sudden surge of intense fear. Symptoms feature chest tightness, palpitations, hyperventilation, fear of dying, and tingling hands. First aid relies on the 4-7-8 deep breathing method. Diagnostics rule out myocardial infarction with ECG. Management combines grounding with fast-acting benzodiazepines under supervision.

## Definition
* **Panic Attack:** A sudden episode of intense fear that triggers severe physical reactions when there is no real danger or apparent cause.
* **Hyperventilation:** Rapid or deep breathing caused by anxiety or panic, leading to excessive loss of carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) in the blood.
* **Grounding:** A therapeutic mental exercise designed to distract the brain from panic thoughts by focusing on immediate physical senses.

---

## How to Tell the Difference: Panic Attack vs. Heart Attack

It is clinically vital to distinguish panic distress from a cardiac event:

| Symptom | Panic Attack (Anxiety) | Heart Attack (Cardiac Event) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pain Type** | Sharp, stabbing, localized chest pain | **Crushing pressure**, heavy squeezing weight |
| **Radiation** | Stays in the chest area, does not spread | **Radiates to left arm, shoulder, jaw, or neck** |
| **Onset & Peak** | Sudden, peaks within $10\text{ mins}$, then fades | Gradual, continuous, worsens over time |
| **Breathing** | Rapid shallow breathing (hyperventilation) | Shortness of breath with **heavy cold sweating** |
| **Nausea** | Rarely present | Common, accompanied by vomiting/dizziness |
| *Lab Tests* | Normal ECG and Troponin-I levels | **Abnormal ECG / Positive Troponin-I** |

## Symptoms of a Panic Attack

### English
* **Physical:** Sudden rapid pounding heartbeat (palpitations); chest tightness, pressure, or sharp pain; shortness of breath, hyperventilation, or a feeling of choking; trembling, shaking, and cold sweats; numbness or tingling sensation in hands, fingers, or feet (due to $CO_2$ loss); dizziness, lightheadedness, or feeling faint.
* **Cognitive:** Intense fear of dying or losing control; feeling detached from reality or one's own body (depersonalization/derealization).

### Hindi
* **शारीरिक लक्षण:** दिल की धड़कन अत्यंत तेज होना (palpitations); सीने में जकड़न या दर्द; सांस फूलना या दम घुटना; हाथ-पैर कांपना और ठंडा पसीना; हाथ-पैरों में झनझनाहट (tingling) होना।
* **मानसिक लक्षण:** मौत का तीव्र डर होना; पागल हो जाने का डर।

### Hinglish
* **Physical Symptoms:** Dil ka achanak kafi tez aur jor se dhadakna (palpitations); chest me jakdan ya sharp pain; saans rukna ya suffocation feel hona; haath-pair kaapna aur thanda pasina aana; hyperventilation ke karan fingers aur feet me tingling (suyi chubhne jaisa) mehsus hona; chakkar aana.
* **Mental Symptoms:** Achanak lagna ki mai marne wala hu ya pagal ho jaunga (fear of dying).

## Immediate Relief Protocol (First Aid for Panic Attack)

If a patient is experiencing an active panic attack, execute this step-by-step rescue protocol:

### Step 1: Respiration Control (The 4-7-8 Breathing Technique)

#### English
* Panic makes the patient breathe rapidly (hyperventilate), which dumps carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) and causes dizziness and tingling hands. Slowing down breathing is the fastest way to stop panic.
  1. Breathe in quietly through your nose for **4 seconds**.
  2. Hold your breath completely for **7 seconds**.
  3. Exhale slowly through your mouth, making a "whoosh" sound, for **8 seconds**.
  4. *Repeat this 4 times. This stimulates the vagus nerve to slow down the heart rate.*

#### Hindi
* **4-7-8 गहरी सांस की तकनीक:**
  * **4 सेकंड** नाक से धीरे-धीरे सांस लें।
  * **7 सेकंड** सांस को अंदर रोक कर रखें (hold)।
  * **8 सेकंड** मुंह से धीरे-धीरे हवा बाहर छोड़ें।
  * *इसे 4-5 बार दोहराएं ताकि धड़कन सामान्य हो सके।*

#### Hinglish
* Hyperventilation (tez saans) rokkne ke liye **4-7-8 Breathing** karwayein:
  1. Naak se slowly **4 seconds** tak saans andar lein.
  2. Saans ko **7 seconds** tak andar hold kar ke rakhein.
  3. Muh se slowly **8 seconds** tak saans bahar nikalein.
  4. *Ise 4-5 baar karein. Deep breathing vagus nerve ko activate karke dil ki dhadkan ko normal karti hai.*

### Step 2: Mental Grounding (The 5-4-3-2-1 Technique)

#### English
* Ask the patient to name:
  * **5** things they can see in the room.
  * **4** things they can physically feel/touch (e.g., chair, clothes).
  * **3** things they can hear (e.g., traffic, fan).
  * **2** things they can smell.
  * **1** thing they can taste.
* *This forces the brain to process physical sensory data, pulling it away from the internal panic loop.*

#### Hindi
* **5-4-3-2-1 ग्राउंडिंग विधि:** मरीज का ध्यान भटकाने के लिए उन्हें आसपास की 5 चीजें देखने, 4 चीजें छूने, 3 आवाजें सुनने, 2 चीजें सूंघने और 1 चीज चखने को कहें।

#### Hinglish
* **5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Method:** Patient ka dhyan darr se hatane ke liye unse boliye ki wo:
  * **5** chizein jo dekh sakte hain unka naam batayein.
  * **4** chizein jo touch kar sakte hain.
  * **3** chizein jo sunn sakte hain.
  * **2** chizein jo smell kar sakte hain.
  * **1** chiz jo taste kar sakte hain.

### Step 3: Reassurance

#### English
* Say in a calm, firm, low voice: *"You are safe. This is a panic attack. It will pass in 5-10 minutes. You are not having a heart attack. I am right here with you."*

#### Hindi
* शांत स्वर में कहें: *"तुम सुरक्षित हो। यह सिर्फ पैनिक अटैक है जो कुछ मिनटों में ठीक हो जाएगा।"*

#### Hinglish
* Soft voice me reassure karein: *"Aap bilkul safe hain. Yeh sirf ek panic attack hai, 5-10 mins me aaram mil jayega. Aapko heart attack nahi aa raha hai."*

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **ECG & Serum Troponin-I:** Mandatory tests performed in emergency clinics to rule out an active heart attack.
* **Thyroid Profile (TFT):** Done to check for hyperthyroidism, which triggers frequent panic attacks.
* **Psychiatric Evaluation:** Checks for Panic Disorder (recurrent unexpected panic attacks).

---

## Medical & Psychological Management
* **CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy):** The most effective long-term therapy. Patients are taught to expose themselves to physical symptoms of panic (like hyperventilating) in a safe room to learn that these symptoms are harmless.
* **Pharmacological Therapy:**
   * **SSRIs (Daily):** Prevent attacks from starting (e.g., Escitalopram, Sertraline).
   * **Benzodiazepines (Emergency rescue only):** e.g., Clonazepam ($0.25 - 0.5\text{ mg}$ sublingually under tongue) provides immediate calm during an active attack but must not be taken daily due to addiction risk.

## Prevention of Panic Attacks
* **Avoid Caffeine & Energy Drinks:** Caffeine triggers heart rate, chemically initiating panic attacks.
* **Regular Exercise:** Burns off physical stress hormones.
* **Sleep Schedule:** Maintain 7-8 hours of sleep.
* **Mindfulness and Yoga:** Calms HPA axis activity.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Panic Attacks
* **Agoraphobia:** Severe fear of leaving the house or entering open spaces, leading to isolation.
* **Clinical Depression:** Triggered by the chronic fear of having another attack.
* **Substance Abuse:** Using alcohol to self-medicate the anxiety.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient experiences chest pain that radiates to their left arm, jaw, neck, accompanied by heavy cold sweating and vomiting (signs of a true heart attack).**
* **The patient expresses suicidal thoughts or attempts self-harm.**
* **The patient remains unresponsive or collapses.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a panic attack?**
   A panic attack is a sudden, intense episode of fear that triggers severe physical reactions (palpitations, chest pain) when there is no real danger.

2. **Why does a panic attack feel like a heart attack?**
   Panic triggers a massive adrenaline rush, which causes a rapid heart rate, chest tightness, and shortness of breath, mimicking cardiac distress.

3. **How do I tell the difference between a panic attack and a heart attack?**
   Panic attack pain is sharp, stays in the chest, and peaks in 10 minutes. Heart attack pain feels like crushing pressure, spreads to the left arm/jaw, and includes cold sweating and vomiting.

4. **Why do my fingers tingle during a panic attack?**
   Rapid breathing (hyperventilation) during panic causes you to exhale too much carbon dioxide ($CO_2$). Low blood $CO_2$ constricts blood vessels, causing tingling in your hands and face.

5. **What is the 4-7-8 breathing technique?**
   Inhale through the nose for 4 seconds, hold for 7 seconds, and exhale through the mouth for 8 seconds. This stimulates the vagus nerve to slow down your heart.

6. **What is the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding method?**
   It is a mental exercise to distract your brain: identify 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, and 1 you taste.

7. **Where can I call for free counseling during a panic attack?**
   You can call the government's free, 24/7 Tele-MANAS helpline at **14416** to speak with trained counselors immediately.

8. **Why are sleeping/anxiety pills like Alprazolam dangerous?**
   Benzodiazepines (Alprazolam, Clonazepam) are highly addictive. Taking them daily for more than 2-4 weeks can cause severe physical dependence and withdrawal.

9. **Can thyroid issues cause panic attacks?**
   Yes. An overactive thyroid gland (hyperthyroidism) releases excess hormones that speed up metabolism, causing symptoms like palpitations, sweating, and panic.

10. **What is Panic Disorder?**
    It is a type of anxiety disorder characterized by recurrent, unexpected panic attacks and constant worry about having future attacks.

11. **How does Propranolol help in panic attacks?**
    Propranolol is a beta-blocker. It does not treat the mental fear but blocks adrenaline, stopping physical symptoms like hand tremors, sweating, and rapid heartbeats.

12. **What is the price of generic Escitalopram (10mg) at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Escitalopram (10mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹15 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹70-₹110 in private pharmacies.

13. **Can caffeine trigger a panic attack?**
    Yes. Caffeine is a central nervous system stimulant. It raises heart rate and blood pressure, which can chemically trigger a panic attack in sensitive people.

14. **Why should I not argue with a panic patient?**
    Arguing or telling them "it's all in your head" increases their fear and panic. They need calm reassurance and breathing guidance.

15. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover panic attack emergencies?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for emergency admissions, cardiac diagnostic tests (ECG, Troponin), and psychiatric monitoring.

16. **Is a panic attack dangerous? Can it kill you?**
    No. While a panic attack feels terrifying, it is medically harmless and will not damage your heart or cause death.

17. **What is Agoraphobia?**
    It is a fear of being in places or situations where escape might be difficult or help might not be available during a panic attack, leading to avoiding crowds or leaving home.

18. **Why do doctors prescribe SSRIs for panic attacks?**
    SSRIs (like Escitalopram) are non-addictive and treat the underlying serotonin imbalance over months, preventing panic attacks from starting.

19. **How long does it take for a panic attack to end?**
    Most panic attacks reach their peak intensity within 10 minutes and resolve completely within 20 to 30 minutes.

20. **Can exercise help reduce panic attacks?**
    Yes. Regular aerobic exercise (like brisk walking or swimming) releases endorphins (feel-good hormones) and lowers overall stress hormones, raising the panic threshold.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Panic attacks are just attention-seeking drama, and patients should just "pull themselves together."
  * **Fact:** Panic attacks are real physiological events caused by sudden sympathetic nervous system overload and adrenaline floods. They require proper clinical support.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures the panic attack instantly.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys has no medical effect. Doing 4-7-8 breathing is the proven way to slow down the heart rate and calm the nervous system.
* **Myth:** If you have a panic attack, it means you have heart disease and will get a heart attack.
  * **Fact:** Panic attacks are driven by anxiety, not blocked coronary arteries. A panic attack itself does not cause heart damage.
* **Myth:** Senda namak (rock salt) stops panic attacks by absorbing negative energy.
  * **Fact:** Rock salt has no chemical effect on brain neurotransmitters or panic. Use grounding methods and consult a doctor.
* **Myth:** Taking daily anxiety pills is the only way to cure panic attacks.
  * **Fact:** Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), particularly interoceptive exposure, is highly effective and can cure panic without long-term medication.
* **Myth:** Breathing into a paper bag is the only way to treat hyperventilation.
  * **Fact:** While paper bags can help rebreathe $CO_2$, it carries risk if the patient is actually having an asthma attack or heart issue. Controlled 4-7-8 breathing is safer.
* **Myth:** Panic attacks only happen to women.
  * **Fact:** Panic attacks affect men and women of all ages. Men often hide the symptoms or self-medicate with alcohol, leading to under-diagnosis.
* **Myth:** A panic attack will go away faster if you run or walk briskly.
  * **Fact:** Running raises the heart rate, which can make the brain think the "danger" is real, worsening the panic. Sit down, rest, and breathe.
* **Myth:** You should stop your panic medicines as soon as the attacks stop.
  * **Fact:** Stopping medications suddenly can cause a severe relapse of panic. Always taper off slowly under a psychiatrist's guidance.
* **Myth:** Panic attacks are highly contagious and can spread to people nearby.
  * **Fact:** Panic attacks are not contagious. However, seeing someone panic can raise anxiety in others. Remain calm to help de-escalate the patient.
