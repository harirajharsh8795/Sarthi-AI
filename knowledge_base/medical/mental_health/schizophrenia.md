# Schizophrenia (Schizophrenia) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Care (Hallucinations, Delusions and Antipsychotics Guide)

## Overview

### English
Schizophrenia is a chronic, severe, and disabling neurodevelopmental and psychiatric disorder characterized by a disruption in thinking, emotions, perceptions, and behavior. It is pathologically classified by **Positive Symptoms** (delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech), **Negative Symptoms** (flat affect, lack of motivation, social withdrawal), and **Cognitive Symptoms** (poor executive functioning). Auditory hallucinations (hearing non-existent voices) and delusions of persecution (paranoia) are classic presentations. Treatment centers on long-term **Antipsychotic Medications** (such as Risperidone or Clozapine), family psychoeducation, and clinical rehabilitation.

### Hindi
स्किज़ोफ्रेनिया (Schizophrenia / स्किज़ोफ्रेनिया या गंभीर मतिभ्रम रोग), एक पुरानी, गंभीर और अक्षम करने वाली मानसिक बीमारी है। यह मरीज के विचारों, भावनाओं, धारणाओं (perceptions) और व्यवहार में गड़बड़ी पैदा करती है। इसके लक्षणों में **सकारात्मक लक्षण** (मतिभ्रम - आवाजें सुनाई देना [Hallucinations], और झूठे पक्के विश्वास - वहम [Delusions]), **नकारात्मक लक्षण** (भावनाओं का खत्म होना, सामाजिक अलगाव), और सोचने-समझने की क्षमता में कमी शामिल हैं। इसका इलाज लंबे समय तक चलने वाली एंटीसाइकोटिक (Antipsychotic) दवाओं से होता है।

## Quick Summary
Schizophrenia features positive (hallucinations, delusions) and negative (flat affect, social withdrawal) symptoms. Diagnostics involve detailed clinical evaluation. The primary safety intervention is verbal de-escalation of paranoia. Management relies on atypical antipsychotics.

---

## Definition
* **Delusion:** A fixed, false belief held with absolute conviction despite clear evidence to the contrary (e.g., believing the police is tracking them through the fan).
* **Hallucination:** A sensory perception in the absence of any external stimulus; most commonly **auditory** (hearing running commentary or commanding voices).
* **Antipsychotics:** A class of psychotropic medications that block dopamine D2 receptors in the brain to reduce positive psychotic symptoms.

---

## Symptoms of Schizophrenia (Positive vs. Negative)

Schizophrenia presents with two distinct sets of symptoms:

| Symptom Category | Positive Symptoms (Added Behaviors) | Negative Symptoms (Lost Behaviors) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clinical Nature** | Excess or distortion of normal functions | Loss or decrease of normal functions |
| **Examples** | **Hallucinations** (hearing commanding voices) | **Flat Affect** (expressionless face, monotone voice) |
| **Beliefs** | **Delusions** (believing neighbors are poisoning food) | **Avolition** (complete lack of motivation/willpower) |
| **Speech & Logic** | Disorganized, incoherent speech (word salad) | **Alogia** (extremely poverty of speech, short answers) |
| **Social Aspect** | Paranoia (distrusting everyone) | **Asociality** (complete withdrawal from family/friends)|
| *Response to Meds* | Responds well to standard antipsychotics | Responds poorly, requires rehabilitation |

## Crisis Intervention & Safety De-escalation Protocol

If a schizophrenia patient is in active psychosis (experiencing acute delusions or hallucinations), execute the following safety guidelines:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Keep your voice calm, slow, and low.** Speak in short, simple sentences. Psychotic patients are highly sensitive to loud tones, which they perceive as threats.
* **Acknowledge their feelings, but not their delusion.** Do not validate the false belief, nor directly challenge it. Say: *"I understand you feel scared right now, but I do not see anyone tracking you. You are safe here with me."*
* **Create a low-stimulation zone.** Keep the patient in a quiet room with minimal lighting and no crowds. High noise levels increase auditory hallucinations.
* **Keep a safe physical distance.** Give them space; do not corner or physically touch them without permission.

#### Hindi
* **शांत और धीमी आवाज में बोलें:** जोर से चिल्लाने से मरीज आक्रामक हो सकता है।
* **भावनाओं को स्वीकारें, वहम को नहीं:** कहें: *"मुझे पता है कि तुम्हें डर लग रहा है, पर यहां कोई कैमरा नहीं है। तुम मेरे साथ सुरक्षित हो।"*
* **दूरी बनाए रखें:** मरीज को घेरने या अचानक छूने की कोशिश न करें।

#### Hinglish
* **Aawaz ko calm aur slow rakhein:** Short aur simple sentences me dheere se baat karein. Tez bolne ya daantne se patient violent ho sakta hai.
* **Feeling ko acknowledge karein, delusion ko nahi:** Unhe galat prove karne ya ladne ke bajay soft tone me bolein—*"Mujhe samajh aa raha hai ki aapko darr lag raha hai, par yahan police nahi hai. Aap mere sath safe hain."*
* **Stimulation kam karein:** Patient ko crowd, shor aur bright lights se door shaant kamre me rakhein. Shor badhne se aawajein (hallucinations) aur tez ho jati hain.
* **Physical distance rakhein:** Patient ko touch karne ki koshish na karein warna wo darr ke maare self-defense me attack kar sakte hain.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT argue, mock, or laugh at their delusions.** Saying *"You are crazy, there is no one on the roof"* will make them lose trust in you and can trigger physical violence.
* **Do NOT whisper or look suspiciously around the patient.** Paranoid patients will assume you are conspiring against them.
* **Do NOT make sudden movements.** Avoid cornering the patient.

#### Hindi
* **बहस या मजाक न उड़ाएं:** यह कहने से बचें कि *"तुम पागल हो गए हो।"*
* **फुसफुसाएं नहीं:** मरीज के सामने कानाफूसी न करें, वे सोच सकते हैं कि आप उनके खिलाफ साजिश कर रहे हैं।

#### Hinglish
* **Behas ya mazak bilkul na udayein:** Kabhi ye na bolein—*"Tum pagal ho gaye ho, chhat par koi nahi hai."* Isse wo aapko bhi apna dushman samajh lenge.
* **Kanaphusi (whispering) na karein:** Patient ke samne kisi aur se dheere-dheere ya ishare me baat na karein. Paranoid patient ko lagega ki aap unhe marne ki planning kar rahe hain.
* **Achanak close na aayein:** Unhe corner karne ki koshish na karein.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Psychiatric Clinical History:** Diagnosis is based on DSM-5 criteria: active symptoms present for at least 1 month and continuous signs of disturbance for 6 months.
* **Clozapine Blood Monitoring (Absolute Requirement):** If the patient is prescribed **Clozapine** (for treatment-resistant schizophrenia), they **must get a Complete Blood Count (CBC) test done every week for the first 6 months.** Clozapine can cause **Agranulocytosis** (a fatal drop in white blood cells/neutrophils), leaving the body vulnerable to deadly infections.
* **MRI Brain & Toxicology Screen:** Done to rule out brain tumors or drug-induced psychosis (from cannabis/methamphetamine).

## Medical & Pharmacological Management

### Clinical Interventions

#### English
* **Antipsychotic Medications:** Must be taken daily, lifelong.
   * **First-Generation (Typical):** e.g., Haloperidol (high risk of muscle stiffness/tremors).
   * **Second-Generation (Atypical):** e.g., Risperidone, Olanzapine, Aripiprazole (first-line, lower muscle side effects but cause weight gain and high blood sugar).
   * **Clozapine:** Reserved strictly for treatment-resistant cases; requires mandatory WBC monitoring.
* **Medication Compliance Warning:** Families must monitor drug intake directly. Stopping antipsychotics causes rapid relapse into severe psychosis.

#### Hindi
* **एंटीसाइकोटिक्स (Antipsychotics):** यह इलाज जीवनभर चलता है। दवा (Risperidone) रोज देना आवश्यक है।
* **क्लोज़ापीन (Clozapine) चेतावनी:** यदि यह दवा चल रही है, तो रक्त जांच (CBC) हर सप्ताह या महीने में कराना अनिवार्य है (सफेद रक्त कोशिकाएं कम होने का खतरा)।

#### Hinglish
* Antipsychotic tablets (jaise **Risperidone**, Olanzapine, Aripiprazole) daily khani hoti hai. Yeh lifelong treatment hai.
* **Clozapine warning:** Agar patient Clozapine goli le raha hai, to unka **CBC test rojana/weekly** karwana compulsory hai taaki WBC count check ho sake (agranulocytosis risk).
* **Compliance:** Dawa ko regular khilayein, patient ke bolne par ki "mai thik hu" dawa kabhi band na karein.

1. **Typical Antipsychotics (First-Gen):** D2 blockers (e.g., Haloperidol, Fluphenazine).
2. **Atypical Antipsychotics (Second-Gen):** D2/5HT2 blockers (e.g., Risperidone, Olanzapine, Quetiapine, Aripiprazole).
3. **Reserve Atypical Antipsychotic:** e.g., Clozapine.
4. **Anticholinergics:** Reduce drug-induced muscle stiffness (e.g., Trihexyphenidyl / Pacitane).

## Prevention of Relapse
* **Strict Adherence:** Never skip doses. Use long-acting depot injections (given once a month) if the patient refuses oral pills.
* **Family Psychoeducation:** Educate family members to reduce critical comments or high emotional expression, which are proven chemical triggers for relapse.
* **Avoid Cannabis/Drugs:** Marijuana/hashish chemically accelerates schizophrenia onset and triggers severe psychotic episodes.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Schizophrenia
* **Self-Harm & Suicide:** Up to $10\%$ of schizophrenia patients commit suicide, often due to commanding voices telling them to jump or harm themselves.
* **Severe Vagrancy:** Becoming homeless, losing job, and severe self-neglect.
* **Drug-Induced EPS:** Muscle rigidity, lockjaw, or continuous pacing (akathisia) from wrong drug dosages.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient exhibits physical violence, threatens self-harm, or is holding a weapon due to paranoid delusions.**
* **The patient on Clozapine develops a high fever, severe sore throat, and mouth ulcers (early signs of fatal agranulocytosis).**
* **The patient develops extreme muscle stiffness, high fever, and confusion (Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome - NMS, a fatal reaction to antipsychotics).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is schizophrenia?**
   Schizophrenia is a chronic, severe psychiatric brain disorder that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves, causing them to lose touch with reality.

2. **What is the difference between hallucinations and delusions?**
   A hallucination is a false sensory perception (like hearing voices that do not exist). A delusion is a fixed, false belief held with absolute conviction (like believing neighbors are spies).

3. **Why do schizophrenia patients hear voices?**
   It is caused by chemical imbalances, particularly excess **Dopamine** in the brain's auditory pathway, which makes the brain perceive internally generated thoughts as external voices.

4. **Why does Clozapine require weekly blood tests?**
   Clozapine can cause **Agranulocytosis**—a dangerous drop in white blood cells (neutrophils). Weekly CBC tests are mandatory to detect this before it causes a fatal infection.

5. **How should I de-escalate a paranoid schizophrenic patient?**
   Speak in a calm, slow, low voice. Do not argue with or laugh at their delusions. Keep a safe physical distance and reduce surrounding noise/crowds.

6. **What are positive and negative symptoms of schizophrenia?**
   Positive symptoms add abnormal behaviors (hallucinations, delusions). Negative symptoms represent lost normal functions (flat facial expression, social withdrawal, lack of motivation).

7. **Where can I get free schizophrenia medicines in India?**
   Generic antipsychotic medications are distributed completely free of charge at all state district hospitals under the NMHP and DMHP programs.

8. **Can cannabis (ganja/weed) trigger schizophrenia?**
   Yes. Cannabis use chemically alters dopamine pathways, accelerating the onset of schizophrenia in genetically vulnerable individuals and triggering severe relapses.

9. **What is a "depot" antipsychotic injection?**
   It is a long-acting injection (like Haloperidol Decanoate) given into the muscle once every 2 to 4 weeks. It slowly releases medicine, ensuring treatment for patients who refuse daily pills.

10. **Why do antipsychotic medicines cause muscle stiffness?**
    By blocking dopamine receptors in the movement control center of the brain (basal ganglia), antipsychotics can mimic Parkinson’s disease, causing tremors and stiffness.

11. **What is Trihexyphenidyl (Pacitane) used for?**
    It is an anticholinergic drug prescribed alongside antipsychotics to prevent and treat side effects like muscle stiffness, tremors, and lockjaw.

12. **Why do atypical antipsychotics cause weight gain?**
    Atypical antipsychotics (like Olanzapine) alter histamine and serotonin receptors, significantly increasing appetite and raising the risk of diabetes and cholesterol.

13. **Is schizophrenia the same as split personality or multiple personality disorder?**
    No. Split personality (Dissociative Identity Disorder) is extremely rare. Schizophrenia means a split from reality, not multiple personalities.

14. **Can a patient with schizophrenia live a normal life?**
    Yes. With strict daily medication, regular counseling, family support, and vocational rehabilitation, many patients can manage their symptoms and live productively.

15. **What is the price of generic Olanzapine (10mg) at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Olanzapine (10mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹18 per strip, compared to ₹80-₹120 in private pharmacies.

16. **Why do schizophrenia patients have a high risk of suicide?**
    About $5-10\%$ commit suicide. It is often triggered by "command hallucinations" (voices commanding them to jump or hurt themselves) or deep depression when they realize their diagnosis.

17. **What is Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS)?**
    NMS is a rare, life-threatening reaction to antipsychotics characterized by extreme muscle rigidity ("lead-pipe" stiffness), high fever, sweating, and confusion, requiring ICU care.

18. **Can family stress trigger a schizophrenia relapse?**
    Yes. High "Expressed Emotion" in families (constant criticism, hostility, or over-involvement) creates chronic stress that chemically triggers relapses.

19. **How does the RPWD Act 2016 help schizophrenia patients in India?**
    It recognizes chronic mental illness as a disability, allowing patients to get disability certificates, free treatment access, reservation in government jobs, and pension benefits.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover schizophrenia hospital stays?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for psychiatric ward stays, acute psychotic episode management, drug toxicity treatments, and ECT.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** People with schizophrenia are highly dangerous, violent, and should be permanently locked in asylums.
  * **Fact:** Most schizophrenia patients are quiet, passive, and scared. They are much more likely to be victims of abuse than perpetrators. Violence only occurs if they are untreated and acting on paranoid delusions.
* **Myth:** Schizophrenia is caused by black magic, djinn possession, or past life sins.
  * **Fact:** Schizophrenia is a medical brain disorder linked to genetics, neurodevelopmental issues, and dopamine imbalance. It requires medicine, not exorcism.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key or metal cures the voices (hallucinations) in the head.
  * **Fact:** Auditory hallucinations are real brain perceptions driven by dopamine overload. Sucking keys is a useless myth. Antipsychotics are required.
* **Myth:** If a schizophrenia patient behaves normally for 3 months, they are cured and can stop all medicines.
  * **Fact:** Normal behavior is proof that the medicines are working. Stopping them will cause a relapse of psychosis. Schizophrenia requires lifelong medication.
* **Myth:** Schizophrenia is caused by poor parenting or lack of moral character.
  * **Fact:** Bipolar and schizophrenia are neuro-biological disorders. Parenting style does not cause schizophrenia.
* **Myth:** Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) destroys the patient's brain and makes them permanently dumb.
  * **Fact:** Modern ECT is a highly controlled, painless medical procedure done under anesthesia. It is a life-saving therapy for patients with severe suicidal or catatonic schizophrenia.
* **Myth:** Branded antipsychotics are much safer and cause less weight gain than generic ones.
  * **Fact:** Branded and generic antipsychotics contain the exact same chemical molecules and carry the same metabolic side effects, but generics are 80% cheaper.
* **Myth:** Schizophrenia only affects poor, uneducated people.
  * **Fact:** Schizophrenia affects all socioeconomic classes equally, with a worldwide prevalence of about 1%, regardless of education or wealth.
* **Myth:** Keeping a schizophrenia patient in chains is the only way to control their agitation.
  * **Fact:** Chaining is illegal and abusive. Acute agitation can be managed safely and humanely using standard medical sedatives in a hospital setting.
* **Myth:** A person with schizophrenia can never make decisions or sign legal documents.
  * **Fact:** During stable periods (remission), many patients are completely rational, competent, and legally capable of making decisions.
