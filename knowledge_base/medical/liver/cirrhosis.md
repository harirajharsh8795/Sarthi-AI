# Liver Cirrhosis (Liver ka Sikudna) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Decompensated Liver Disease and Ascites Guide)

## Overview

### English
Liver Cirrhosis is an advanced, late-stage scarring (fibrosis) of the liver caused by long-term liver damage (chronic injury due to alcohol abuse, chronic Hepatitis B or C, or Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease [NAFLD]). As healthy liver tissue is progressively replaced by non-functioning scar tissue, blood flow through the liver is blocked, leading to **Portal Hypertension**. Cirrhosis is classified into **Compensated** (liver still functions, asymptomatic) and **Decompensated** (presence of complications like ascites [fluid in abdomen], hepatic encephalopathy [ammonia brain toxicity], or variceal bleeding). Diagnosis utilizes ultrasound elastography (FibroScan) and blood LFTs. While scarring is irreversible, further damage can be halted, and advanced stages require liver transplantation.

## Quick Summary
Liver cirrhosis is late-stage irreversible hepatic scarring. Symptoms include yellow eyes (jaundice), abdominal swelling (ascites), spider angiomas, dark stools, and confusion. Diagnostics use FibroScan, platelet counts, and LFTs. Management requires absolute alcohol cessation, low-sodium diets, diuretics, and transplant.

---

## Definition
* **Liver Cirrhosis:** Diffuse disorganization of normal liver structure by regenerative nodules and fibrotic bands of scar tissue.
* **Portal Hypertension:** Increased blood pressure within the portal vein system that drains blood from the gastrointestinal tract to the liver, caused by structural blockages in the cirrhotic liver.
* **Ascites:** The pathological accumulation of protein-containing fluid in the peritoneal (abdominal) cavity, a primary complication of decompensated cirrhosis.

---

## Pathological Progression from Fatty Liver to Cirrhosis
The liver undergoes gradual destruction over decades:
1. **Steatosis (Fatty Liver):** Excess fat accumulates in liver cells. Reversible.
2. **Steatohepatitis (NASH/ASH):** Fat triggers active inflammation and cellular injury.
3. **Fibrosis (Scarring):** Chronic inflammation forces stellate cells to produce collagen, forming early scar tissue (fibrous bands).
4. **Cirrhosis:** Intersecting fibrous bands seal off nodules of liver cells, permanently reorganizing liver structure. **Irreversible.**

## Symptoms of Liver Cirrhosis

### English
* Yellowing of skin and eyes (jaundice); swelling of the abdomen (ascites) and legs (edema); vomiting of blood (hematemesis due to ruptured esophageal varices) or black, tarry stools (melena); easy bruising and bleeding (due to low platelets and clotting factor loss); tiny red spider-like veins on the skin (spider angiomas); confusion, slurred speech, and hand tremors (hepatic encephalopathy).

## First Aid & Home Care Protocol (For Cirrhosis Patients)

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Adhere to a strict low-sodium (salt) diet.** Salt causes the body to retain fluid, worsening ascites (stomach swelling) and leg edema. Limit daily sodium to $< 2\text{ grams}$ (less than half a teaspoon of salt). Do not add salt to cooked food.
* **Measure abdominal girth and weight daily.** A sudden weight gain of $>1\text{ kg}$ in a day indicates rapid fluid buildup, requiring diuretic dose adjustment.
* **Take Lactulose syrup daily** as prescribed. Lactulose keeps bowel movements loose (2-3 soft stools/day), which clears toxic ammonia from the gut, preventing brain confusion (hepatic encephalopathy).
* **Consume high-calorie, moderate-protein meals** (small, frequent feeds).

#### Hindi
* **नमक का सेवन बिल्कुल बंद करें:** जलोदर (Ascites) को बढ़ने से रोकने के लिए दिनभर में 2 ग्राम से कम नमक लें।
* **लैक्टुलोज (Lactulose) सिरप रोजाना लें:** दिन में 2-3 बार शौच साफ होने से मस्तिष्क की विषाक्तता (encephalopathy) से बचाव होता है।
* **नियमित वजन और पेट का घेरा (abdominal girth) नापें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Namak (Salt) strictly band karein:** Pet me paani bharne (Ascites) ko control karne ke liye khane me extra namak bilkul na dalein. Limit daily salt $< 2$ grams.
* **Daily weight aur pet ki sujan napein:** Subah khali pet weight check karein. Agar 1 din me weight 1kg se zyada badhe, to ye paani bharne ka sign hai.
* **Lactulose Syrup daily lein:** Rojana doctor ki prescription se lactulose syrup piyein taaki din me 2-3 baar pet saaf ho. Pet saaf rehne se body ka toxic Ammonia wash-out ho jata hai aur dimaag shanti me rehta hai.
* **Sharab (Alcohol) se completely door rahein.**

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT drink a drop of alcohol.** Alcohol acts as a direct cell poison, accelerating liver failure and causing rapid death.
* **Do NOT take sleeping pills** (sedatives/benzodiazepines). In cirrhosis, the liver cannot metabolize these drugs; they accumulate in the blood, triggering deep coma (hepatic encephalopathy).
* **Do NOT take paracetamol or NSAID painkillers** (like Diclofenac) without specialist clearance. They worsen kidney failure (hepatorenal syndrome) and trigger internal bleeding.

#### Hindi
* **शराब का सेवन 100% बंद करें:** एक बूंद शराब भी जानलेवा हो सकती है।
* **नींद की गोलियां (Sedatives) न लें:** ये सीधे कोमा का कारण बन सकती हैं।
* **बिना डॉक्टर के दर्दनिवारक दवाएं न लें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Alcohol ki ek boond bhi na lein:** Sharab peena band na karne par liver fast fail hoga aur patient ki maut ho jayegi.
* **Neend ki dawaiyan (sleeping pills) na lein:** Liver in dawaiyon ko filter nahi kar pata jisse ye dimaag ko sunn karke deep coma me bhej deti hain.
* **NSAID painkillers/Paracetamol na lein:** Yeh medicines kidney fail (hepatorenal syndrome) kar sakti hain aur internal stomach bleeding trigger kar sakti hain.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Liver Function Test (LFT):** Checks for elevated Bilirubin, elevated SGOT/SGPT, and critically **low Serum Albumin** (low albumin is the primary cause of fluid leaking into the abdomen).
* **FibroScan (Transient Elastography):** Measures liver stiffness (scarring/fibrosis) in kilopascals (kPa). Values $> 12.5\text{ kPa}$ indicate cirrhosis.
* **Abdominal Ultrasound/CT:** Evaluates liver size (shrunken, nodular), spleen enlargement, and fluid (ascites).
* **Upper GI Endoscopy:** Checks for dilated veins (**Esophageal Varices**) in the food pipe, which are prone to bursting and causing fatal bleeding.

## Medical & Surgical Management

### Management of Ascites & Varices

#### English
* **Diuretics:** Spironolactone and Furosemide are given daily to remove excess water.
* **Endoscopic Variceal Ligation (EVL):** A gastroenterologist uses bands to tie off bleeding veins in the esophagus during endoscopy.

#### Hindi
* **मूत्रवर्धक दवाएं (Diuretics):** अतिरिक्त पानी निकालने के लिए दवाएं (Spironolactone) देना।
* **एंडोस्कोपी बैंडिंग (EVL):** भोजन नली की फूली हुई नसों को एंडोस्कोपी द्वारा बांधना।

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to cirrhosis management:*
1. **Aldosterone Antagonists (Diuretics):** First-line for ascites (e.g., Spironolactone).
2. **Loop Diuretics:** Combined with spironolactone (e.g., Furosemide).
3. **Ammonia Detoxicants:** Bind and flush out blood ammonia (e.g., Lactulose syrup, Rifaximin antibiotic).
4. **Beta-Blockers (Non-selective):** Lower portal blood pressure to prevent variceal bleeding (e.g., Propranolol, Carvedilol).

## Prevention of Liver Cirrhosis
* **Absolute Alcohol Abstinence:** Stop alcohol intake completely.
* **Manage Fatty Liver:** Control weight, exercise daily, and keep triglycerides normal.
* **Vaccinate for Hepatitis B:** Get vaccinated to prevent chronic HBV-induced cirrhosis.
* **Avoid Self-Medication:** Limit use of hepatotoxic drugs and heavy-metal traditional cures.

---

## Possible Complications of Decompensated Cirrhosis
* **Variceal Hemorrhage:** Severe, life-threatening vomiting of fresh red blood from burst veins.
* **Hepatic Encephalopathy:** Brain toxicity from ammonia buildup, causing confusion and coma.
* **Hepatorenal Syndrome (HRS):** Complete kidney failure triggered by liver dysfunction.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient vomits fresh red blood or coffee-ground material (esophageal variceal burst).**
* **The patient passes black, tarry, sticky stools (indicates active internal GI bleed).**
* **The patient develops sudden confusion, severe sleepiness, slurred speech, or becomes unresponsive.**
* **The patient develops a high fever with abdominal pain (indicates Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis - SBP, a fatal infection of ascites fluid).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is liver cirrhosis?**
   Liver cirrhosis is the late stage of progressive liver scarring (fibrosis) caused by chronic liver diseases like alcoholism, hepatitis, or fatty liver.

2. **What is Portal Hypertension?**
   It is high blood pressure in the portal vein system. The scarred liver blocks blood flow, causing blood to pool in gastrointestinal veins, leading to swelling and bleeding.

3. **Why does liver cirrhosis cause abdominal swelling (ascites)?**
   The damaged liver cannot produce enough Albumin protein. Low albumin, combined with portal hypertension, causes fluid to leak out of blood vessels and pool in the abdominal cavity.

4. **Why is Lactulose syrup prescribed daily in liver cirrhosis?**
   Lactulose is a laxative that makes the gut environment acidic, converting toxic ammonia into non-absorbable ammonium. This is flushed out in stool, preventing brain confusion (hepatic encephalopathy).

5. **Why should cirrhosis patients follow a low-sodium (salt) diet?**
   Sodium makes the body hold onto water. Eating salt causes rapid accumulation of fluid in the abdomen (ascites) and legs, worsening swelling.

6. **What is a FibroScan and what do the values mean?**
   A FibroScan is a non-invasive ultrasound check measuring liver stiffness. A score higher than 12.5 kPa indicates advanced scarring (cirrhosis).

7. **Where can BPL cardholders get a free liver transplant in India?**
   Eligible cardholders can receive cashless liver transplants, donor surgeries, and ICU care at empaneled tertiary hospitals under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY card.

8. **Why are sleeping pills dangerous for liver cirrhosis patients?**
   The liver cannot break down sedatives. Taking them leads to drug buildup in the blood, which can trigger sudden hepatic coma (encephalopathy) and respiratory failure.

9. **What are esophageal varices?**
   These are enlarged, fragile blood vessels in the food pipe caused by portal hypertension. If pressure rises, they can rupture, causing severe, life-threatening vomiting of blood.

10. **Can liver cirrhosis be reversed?**
    The hard scar tissue of cirrhosis is generally permanent and cannot be reversed. However, further scarring can be stopped by treating the cause (stopping alcohol, treating hepatitis).

11. **Why does liver cirrhosis cause easy bruising and bleeding?**
    The liver is responsible for producing clotting factors that help blood clot. A damaged liver stops producing these factors, and portal hypertension destroys platelets in the spleen, raising bleeding risks.

12. **What is Hepatorenal Syndrome (HRS)?**
    HRS is a type of kidney failure that occurs in patients with advanced liver cirrhosis. The liver dysfunction alters blood flow to the kidneys, causing them to shut down.

13. **Why is fever with abdominal pain a medical emergency in ascites?**
    It indicates Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP)—a life-threatening bacterial infection of the fluid trapped in the abdomen, requiring immediate IV antibiotics.

14. **What is EVL banding?**
    Endoscopic Variceal Ligation (EVL) is a procedure where a doctor places small rubber bands around swollen veins in the food pipe during endoscopy to stop or prevent bleeding.

15. **What is the price of generic Lactulose syrup at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Lactulose syrup is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹100 to ₹140 per bottle, compared to ₹350+ for branded versions in private pharmacies.

16. **Why are NSAID painkillers like Diclofenac banned in cirrhosis?**
    NSAIDs reduce blood flow to the kidneys, triggering kidney failure (HRS), and cause stomach ulcers, which can lead to fatal bleeding in portal hypertension.

17. **What is the difference between compensated and decompensated cirrhosis?**
    Compensated means the liver is scarred but still does its job, and the patient has no symptoms. Decompensated means the liver is failing, presenting with ascites, bleeding, or confusion.

18. **How does fatty liver progress to cirrhosis?**
    Fatty liver (steatosis) leads to inflammation (NASH). Over years, chronic inflammation destroys liver cells, replacing them with fibrous scars that eventually develop into cirrhosis.

19. **What is the role of Rifaximin in liver cirrhosis?**
    Rifaximin is an antibiotic that stays in the gut to kill ammonia-producing bacteria. It is combined with Lactulose to prevent recurrences of brain confusion (hepatic encephalopathy).

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover ascites fluid drainage (paracentesis)?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for therapeutic paracentesis (needle drainage of abdominal fluid), diagnostic lab checks, and ICU stays.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Drinking moderate amounts of beer or wine does not damage a cirrhotic liver.
  * **Fact:** A cirrhotic liver cannot tolerate any alcohol. Even a single drink can trigger acute liver failure and variceal bleeding. Absolute abstinence is mandatory.
* **Myth:** Liver cirrhosis can be cured completely with herbal syrups or nose drops.
  * **Fact:** Hard scar tissue cannot be dissolved by any syrup or alternative cure. These claims are false and can cause heavy-metal poisoning, worsening liver failure.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures the brain confusion (hepatic encephalopathy).
  * **Fact:** Confusion is caused by toxic ammonia buildup in the brain. It requires Lactulose syrup and Rifaximin to clear it. Sucking keys is a useless myth.
* **Myth:** Abdominal swelling in cirrhosis is just normal bloating due to gas.
  * **Fact:** Swelling in cirrhosis is caused by liters of fluid trapped in the abdomen (ascites). It requires diuretics or needle drainage, not gas pills.
* **Myth:** Liver transplant requires a whole liver from a deceased donor.
  * **Fact:** A living donor (usually a relative) can donate a portion (lobe) of their healthy liver. Both the donor's and the patient's liver portions will grow back to normal size in a few months.
* **Myth:** If your LFT enzymes (SGOT/SGPT) are normal, you cannot have cirrhosis.
  * **Fact:** In advanced cirrhosis, liver cells are already replaced by scar tissue, so there are few cells left to leak enzymes. SGOT/SGPT can be completely normal. Check FibroScan.
* **Myth:** Liver cirrhosis is a contagious disease that spreads by sharing food.
  * **Fact:** Cirrhosis itself is not contagious. However, if it was caused by Hepatitis B or C, those viruses can transmit through blood or unprotected sex, not by sharing food.
* **Myth:** A cirrhosis patient should follow a zero-protein diet.
  * **Fact:** Severe protein restriction worsens muscle wasting (sarcopenia) and increases mortality. Give moderate amounts of high-quality protein (dahi, paneer) unless in deep hepatic coma.
* **Myth:** Ascites fluid drainage (tapping) makes the body weak and should be avoided.
  * **Fact:** Tapping (paracentesis) is a safe, life-saving procedure done to relieve extreme pressure on the lungs and stomach when breathing becomes difficult.
* **Myth:** Liver cirrhosis only happens to chronic alcoholics.
  * **Fact:** Cirrhosis can happen to non-alcoholics due to chronic Hepatitis B/C infections or advanced Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD) caused by diabetes and obesity.
