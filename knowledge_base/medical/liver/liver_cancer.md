# Liver Cancer (Liver Cancer) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Hepatocellular Carcinoma and Transplant Guide)

## Overview

### English
Liver Cancer, primarily **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)**, is a highly aggressive primary malignancy of the liver cells. It almost exclusively arises in the background of chronic liver injury and scarring—specifically **Liver Cirrhosis** caused by chronic Hepatitis B or C infection, alcohol abuse, or severe Non-Alcoholic Steatohepatitis (NASH). Symptoms include rapid weight loss, jaundice, right-upper abdominal pain, and worsening ascites (fluid in abdomen). Diagnosis is confirmed via triphasic abdominal CT/MRI and elevated tumor marker **Alpha-Fetoprotein (AFP)**. Curative treatments include surgical liver resection or liver transplantation (within Milan criteria). Palliative options include Transarterial Chemoembolization (TACE) and systemic immunotherapies.

## Quick Summary
Liver cancer is primary hepatic malignancy, mostly HCC. Symptoms feature weight loss, jaundice, right-upper abdominal pain, and ascites. Diagnostics utilize triphasic CT scans, MRIs, and AFP levels. Management uses surgical resection, liver transplantation, TACE, or systemic chemotherapy.

## Definition
* **Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC):** The most common primary liver cancer, originating directly from hepatocytes (liver cells).
* **Alpha-Fetoprotein (AFP):** A glycoprotein tumor marker measured in blood; levels $> 400\text{ ng/mL}$ in a patient with a liver mass strongly support HCC diagnosis.
* **TACE (Transarterial Chemoembolization):** A minimally invasive urology/oncology procedure where a catheter injects chemotherapy drugs and blood-vessel-blocking particles directly into the artery supplying the liver tumor, cutting off its nutrient supply.

---

## The Milan Criteria for Liver Transplantation
For patients with liver cancer and cirrhosis, liver transplant is the best cure if the tumor fits within the **Milan Criteria**:
1. **Single Tumor:** Size $\le 5\text{ cm}$ in diameter.
2. **Multiple Tumors:** Up to 3 nodules, each $\le 3\text{ cm}$ in diameter.
3. **No Vascular Invasion:** No tumor cells inside the portal veins.
4. **No Metastasis:** No spread to lymph nodes or other organs.
5. *If the patient fits these criteria, transplant removes both the cancer and the cirrhotic liver, preventing recurrence.*

## Symptoms of Liver Cancer

### English
* Unexplained, rapid weight loss and severe loss of appetite (anorexia); yellowing of the skin and eyes (jaundice); pain, swelling, or fullness on the right upper side of the abdomen; a hard mass or lump felt directly below the right rib cage (hepatomegaly); worsening of pre-existing ascites (fluid in abdomen); persistent fever, fatigue, and nausea.

## First Aid & Home Care Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Ensure high-calorie, small, frequent meals.** Cancer cells consume massive energy, causing muscle wasting (cachexia). Keep the patient nourished with easily digestible protein (paneer, egg whites).
* **Follow a strict low-sodium diet** if ascites (fluid in abdomen) is present.
* **Manage pain effectively** using doctor-prescribed medications. Avoid self-medicating.
* **Adhere to a strict medication schedule** for underlying cirrhosis (like Lactulose syrup to prevent hepatic encephalopathy).

#### Hindi
* **पोषक और उच्च कैलोरी वाला भोजन लें:** कैंसर के कारण होने वाली कमजोरी से बचने के लिए थोड़े-थोड़े अंतराल पर खाएं।
* **लैक्टुलोज (Lactulose) सिरप नियमित रूप से लें।**
* **नमक का सेवन सीमित रखें।**

#### Hinglish
* **High-calorie soft food lein:** Cancer cells body ki energy chura lete hain jisse muscle loss (cachexia) hota hai. Thoda-thoda khana rojana kai baar khayein (paneer, dahi, ubla aloo).
* **Lactulose syrup daily lein:** Dimaag me ammonia toxicity se bachane ke liye daily lactulose syrup lekar pet saaf rakhein.
* **Low-salt diet lein:** Agar pet me sujan (ascites) ho to khane me extra namak bilkul na dalein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT take paracetamol or NSAID painkillers** (like Diclofenac) without oncology clearance. The cancerous liver cannot process these drugs, risking acute liver failure and bleeding.
* **Do NOT drink alcohol.**
* **Do NOT go for alternative "cancer-curing" herbs or magic drops.** These traditional preparations contain heavy metals that destroy the remaining healthy liver tissue, causing rapid death.

#### Hindi
* **पैरासिटामोल या इबुप्रोफेन स्वयं से न लें:** कैंसर प्रभावित लिवर इन दवाओं को नहीं पचा पाता।
* **झाड़-फूंक या चमत्कारी दावों वाली देशी दवाओं में समय बर्बाद न करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Painkillers (Combiflam/Diclofenac) khud se na lein:** Cancerous liver kafi weak hota hai. Painkillers lene se kidney fail (hepatorenal syndrome) ho sakti hai aur stomach bleeding ho sakti hai.
* **Sharab (Alcohol) se completely door rahein.**
* **Herbal cancer medicines ke chakkar me na padein:** "10 din me cancer cure" karne ka dava karne wali fake herbal medicine na lein. Inme heavy metals hote hain jo liver ko turant damage karke patient ki maut ka kaaran bante hain.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Triphasic Abdominal CT or Contrast MRI:** The definitive diagnostic scan. HCC has a classic signature: **Rapid enhancement (wash-in) during the arterial phase, followed by washout during the venous phase.** (Often avoids the need for a liver biopsy, which carries bleeding risk).
* **Serum AFP (Alpha-Fetoprotein):** Tumor marker blood test. Normal is $< 8.0\text{ ng/mL}$; values $> 400\text{ ng/mL}$ in cirrhosis are highly diagnostic for HCC.
* **LFT & Platelet Count:** Evaluates liver function and portal hypertension status.

## Medical & Surgical Management

### Curative Surgical Treatments

#### English
* **Surgical Resection (Hepatectomy):** Removing the tumor segment, done only if the patient has no cirrhosis and normal portal pressure.
* **Liver Transplantation:** The absolute best cure for patients with cirrhosis and early-stage HCC (within Milan criteria), replacing the entire diseased organ.

#### Hindi
* **लिवर रिसेक्शन (सर्जरी):** कैंसर वाले हिस्से को काटकर निकालना।
* **लिवर ट्रांसप्लांट (प्रत्यारोपण):** सिरोसिस और शुरुआती कैंसर वाले मरीजों के लिए सबसे प्रभावी इलाज।

#### Hinglish
* **Hepatectomy (Surgical Resection):** Liver ke tumor wale portion ko cut karke nikalna (agar liver non-cirrhotic ho).
* **Liver Transplantation:** Milan criteria ke andar hone par kharab liver ko poora change karna.

### Palliative Interventions (Non-resectable cases)

#### English
* **TACE (Transarterial Chemoembolization):** A radiologist uses a catheter to inject chemotherapy drugs and lipid blocks directly into the tumor artery, killing the cancer cells by starvation.
* **Systemic Immunotherapy:** e.g., Atezolizumab + Bevacizumab combination therapy.

#### Hindi
* **TACE थेरेपी:** ट्यूमर की धमनी (artery) में कीमोथेरेपी दवाएं डालकर उसे सिकोड़ना।

#### Hinglish
* **TACE Therapy:** Vascular radiologist catheter ke jariye tumor ki artery me chemotherapy inject karke use seal kar dete hain taaki tumor starvation se sikud jaye.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to liver cancer management:*
1. **Targeted Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors (TKIs):** Block tumor blood vessel growth (e.g., Sorafenib, Lenvatinib).
2. **Systemic Immunotherapies:** Boost immune response against cancer (e.g., Atezolizumab, Nivolumab, Durvalumab).
3. **Monoclonal Antibodies (VEGF Inhibitors):** Block tumor blood supply (e.g., Bevacizumab).
4. **Osmotic Laxatives:** Prevent hepatic coma (e.g., Lactulose).

## Prevention of Liver Cancer
* **Hepatitis B Vaccination:** Prevents chronic HBV infection, which is responsible for 50% of global HCC cases.
* **Hepatitis C Treatment:** Cure chronic HCV with DAAs within 12 weeks to eliminate liver cancer risk.
* **Stop Alcohol Abuse:** Prevent alcoholic cirrhosis and malignancy.
* **Reversal of Fatty Liver:** Stop progression of NASH to cirrhosis.

---

## Possible Complications of Liver Cancer
* **Tumor Rupture:** Sudden catastrophic bleeding into the abdomen, causing shock and death.
* **Portal Vein Thrombosis:** Cancer cells invading and blocking the major portal vein, causing liver shutdown.
* **Hepatic Failure:** Progressive loss of liver function leading to coma.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient develops sudden, severe abdominal pain, chest pain, cold sweating, and dizziness (indicates tumor rupture and bleeding).**
* **The patient vomits blood, passes black, sticky stools, or loses consciousness.**
* **The patient shows sudden confusion, extreme sleepiness, or slurred speech.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Viral Hepatitis Control Program (NVHCP):** Prevents HCC by screening and providing free antiviral treatments for Hepatitis B and C.
* **National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular Diseases and Stroke (NPCDCS):** Coordinates cancer screening and oncology referrals at state government hospitals.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless surgical packages for liver transplants, hepatectomies (cancer resection), TACE procedures, immunotherapy drug cycles, and ICU cancer care.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Offers generic oncology support drugs, lactulose, and vitamins at highly subsidized rates.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is liver cancer?**
   Liver cancer is a primary malignancy of the liver cells, most commonly Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC), arising in a scarred liver (cirrhosis).

2. **What causes liver cancer?**
   The primary causes are chronic Hepatitis B or C infections, chronic alcohol abuse, and advanced non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NASH), all leading to liver cirrhosis.

3. **What is the AFP test?**
   AFP (Alpha-Fetoprotein) is a blood tumor marker. An AFP level $> 400\text{ ng/mL}$ in a patient with a liver mass on CT scan is highly diagnostic for HCC.

4. **Why is a triphasic CT scan done for liver cancer?**
   A triphasic CT scans the liver during three blood flow phases. HCC has a classic vascular signature (rapid wash-in during the arterial phase and washout in the venous phase), allowing diagnosis without a risky biopsy.

5. **What is TACE therapy?**
   TACE (Transarterial Chemoembolization) is a procedure where a catheter is inserted into the groin and guided to the liver tumor's blood supply. Chemotherapy is injected, and the artery is blocked, starving the tumor.

6. **What is the Milan Criteria?**
   It is a set of rules used to decide if a liver cancer patient is eligible for a transplant: a single tumor $\le 5\text{ cm}$ or up to 3 tumors $\le 3\text{ cm}$ each, with no vascular invasion or spread.

7. **Where can BPL cardholders get free liver cancer surgery in India?**
   Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY card, eligible patients can get completely free cashless hepatectomies, TACE procedures, or liver transplants at empaneled hospitals.

8. **Why does liver cancer cause abdominal pain?**
   The growing tumor stretches the liver capsule, which contains sensitive pain nerves, causing a constant, dull ache in the upper right side of the abdomen.

9. **Can a liver transplant cure liver cancer?**
   Yes. If the cancer is detected early and meets the Milan criteria, a transplant is the best cure because it removes both the cancer and the underlying cirrhotic liver.

10. **Why are biopsy checks avoided in suspected liver cancer?**
    Liver tumors are highly vascular. Inserting a biopsy needle carries a high risk of severe bleeding and can cause cancer cells to leak and spread along the needle track.

11. **How does Hepatitis B cause liver cancer?**
    The Hepatitis B virus integrates its DNA directly into the host's liver cell DNA. This can cause genetic mutations that trigger cancer, even before cirrhosis develops.

12. **What is the difference between primary and secondary liver cancer?**
    Primary liver cancer (HCC) starts in the liver cells. Secondary liver cancer (metastasis) starts in another organ (like colon or breast) and spreads to the liver.

13. **Why is a low-sodium diet necessary if a liver cancer patient has ascites?**
    Sodium causes the body to retain water, which leaks into the abdomen (ascites). Restricting salt helps prevent fluid buildup and breathing difficulty.

14. **What is Sorafenib?**
    Sorafenib is an oral targeted chemotherapy drug (tyrosine kinase inhibitor) used to slow down tumor growth and blood vessel formation in advanced liver cancer.

15. **What is the price of generic Sorafenib at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Sorafenib is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, costing only ₹3,000 to ₹5,000 per month, compared to ₹20,000+ in private pharmacies.

16. **Why does liver cancer cause sudden severe abdominal pain?**
    Sudden, sharp abdominal pain can indicate that the liver tumor has ruptured and is bleeding directly into the abdominal cavity, which is a life-threatening emergency.

17. **Can a living donor donate a liver for a cancer transplant?**
    Yes. If the patient fits the Milan criteria, a healthy relative can donate a portion of their liver, which will grow back to normal size in both the donor and recipient.

18. **Why should liver cancer patients avoid sleeping pills?**
    The damaged liver cannot metabolize sedatives. Taking them leads to drug buildup, which can trigger sudden hepatic coma (encephalopathy).

19. **What is the role of the NVHCP program?**
    NVHCP is a national health program providing free screening and curable drugs for Hepatitis B and C, preventing these infections from progressing to liver cancer.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover immunotherapy for liver cancer?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for advanced immunotherapy drugs (like Atezolizumab and Bevacizumab) for eligible cardholders.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Drinking heavy amounts of beer does not cause liver cancer, only country liquor does.
  * **Fact:** All alcohol contains ethanol, which causes liver inflammation, cirrhosis, and mutations, leading directly to liver cancer (HCC).
* **Myth:** Liver cancer can be cured in 15 days using special herbal leaves.
  * **Fact:** No herbal therapy can cure or shrink liver tumors. These false claims delay surgical care, allowing the cancer to spread and become incurable.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures the tumor or abdominal pain.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys has no medical effect on cancer cells or liver capsules. It is a dangerous superstition. Seek oncology care.
* **Myth:** A liver biopsy is mandatory to confirm liver cancer before treatment.
  * **Fact:** Triphasic CT or MRI scans are so accurate for HCC that a biopsy is often avoided to prevent bleeding and tumor cell seeding.
* **Myth:** Liver cancer is highly contagious and spreads by touching the patient.
  * **Fact:** Cancer is not contagious. However, if the cause was Hepatitis B or C, those viruses can spread through blood or sex, not by casual touch.
* **Myth:** If you get a liver transplant, the cancer will always return immediately.
  * **Fact:** If the transplant is done early within the Milan criteria, the cure rate is over 70-80% with a very low risk of recurrence.
* **Myth:** Liver cancer only happens to old people.
  * **Fact:** Chronic Hepatitis B contracted at birth can cause liver cancer in young adults in their 20s and 30s, even without cirrhosis.
* **Myth:** Chemotherapy is the only treatment option for liver cancer.
  * **Fact:** Early-stage liver cancer is treated with curative options like surgical resection (hepatectomy) or liver transplantation.
* **Myth:** Drinking lemon water cures liver tumors.
  * **Fact:** Lemon water provides Vitamin C but has no effect on cancer cells. It requires clinical surgical, local ablation, or systemic oncology treatments.
* **Myth:** If a liver cancer patient develops ascites, they will die in a few days.
  * **Fact:** While ascites indicates advanced (decompensated) liver disease, it can be managed with diuretics, salt restriction, and tapping, extending life.
