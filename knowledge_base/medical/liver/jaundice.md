# Jaundice (Piliya) ki Pehchan, Kaaran aur Upachar (Hyperbilirubinemia and Liver Function Guide)

## Overview

### English
Jaundice, clinically termed **Icterus**, is a yellowish discoloration of the skin, mucous membranes, and the sclera (whites of the eyes) caused by elevated levels of bilirubin in the blood (**Hyperbilirubinemia**, typically visible when serum bilirubin exceeds **$2.5 - 3.0\text{ mg/dL}$**; normal is $< 1.2\text{ mg/dL}$). Jaundice is not a disease itself but a clinical sign of an underlying disorder. It is classified pathologically into three types: **Pre-hepatic (Hemolytic)** (excess RBC breakdown), **Hepatic (Hepatocellular)** (liver cell injury due to viral hepatitis, alcohol, or cirrhosis), and **Post-hepatic (Obstructive)** (bile flow blockage due to gallstones or pancreatic tumors). Diagnostic workup requires Liver Function Tests (LFT) and abdominal ultrasound.

## Quick Summary
Jaundice is hyperbilirubinemia causing yellow skin/sclera. Classifications are hemolytic (pre-hepatic), hepatocellular (hepatic), and obstructive (post-hepatic). Symptoms feature yellow discoloration, dark urine, pale stools, and skin itching. Diagnostics measure serum bilirubin fractions and SGPT/SGOT. Management addresses the specific cause.

---

## Definition
* **Hyperbilirubinemia:** An excess of bilirubin in the blood, classified as conjugated (direct) or unconjugated (indirect) bilirubin.
* **Bilirubin:** A yellowish pigment formed during the normal breakdown of red blood cells (hemoglobin), processed by the liver and excreted in bile.
* **Obstructive Jaundice:** Jaundice caused by a physical blockage in the bile ducts (e.g., gallstones, cholangiocarcinoma), preventing bile from draining into the intestines.

---

## Pathological Classification & Diagnostic Markers

It is clinically vital to distinguish the three pathological sites of jaundice:

| Parameter | Pre-Hepatic (Hemolytic) | Hepatic (Hepatocellular) | Post-Hepatic (Obstructive) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Cause** | Excess RBC rupture (Malaria, G6PD) | Liver cell damage (Hepatitis, Cirrhosis) | Bile duct block (Gallstones, Cancer) |
| **Bilirubin Type** | **Unconjugated (Indirect) High** | Mixed (Direct & Indirect) | **Conjugated (Direct) High** |
| **SGPT / SGOT** | Normal | **Critically Elevated ($> 500$)** | Mildly Elevated |
| **Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP)** | Normal | Mildly Elevated | **Critically Elevated ($> 3$x Normal)** |
| **Stool Color** | Normal / Dark | Normal / Pale | **Clay-Colored (White/Grey)** |
| **Urine Color** | Normal | Dark (Tea-Color) | **Dark (Tea-Color)** |

## Symptoms of Jaundice (Piliya)

### English
* Yellowing of the sclera (whites of the eyes) appearing first, followed by yellowing of the skin; dark, tea-colored or cola-colored urine; pale, clay-colored, or white stools (in obstructive jaundice); severe skin itching (pruritus due to bile salt accumulation); loss of appetite, nausea, and metallic taste; abdominal pain in the upper right quadrant.

## First Aid & Liver Care Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Ensure complete bed rest** during acute jaundice. Physical activity puts extra blood flow demands on the liver, worsening cell injury.
* **Follow a low-fat, high-carbohydrate diet.** The liver cannot produce enough bile to digest fats. Eat small, frequent meals of easily digestible starch (boiled rice, khichdi, bananas, sugarcane juice). Avoid all fried food, oil, and ghee.
* **Maintain hydration** by drinking clean, purified water and fresh fruit juices.
* **Check the color of your urine and stool daily** to monitor bile flow.

#### Hindi
* **पूर्ण आराम करें:** लिवर पर दबाव कम करने के लिए बेड रेस्ट बहुत जरूरी है।
* **वसा रहित (low-fat) भोजन लें:** उबले चावल, खिचड़ी, केला खाएं। तेल, घी, तला भोजन 100% बंद करें।
* **साफ उबला पानी पिएं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Complete Bed Rest karein:** Acute phase me physical rest bohot zaroori hai taaki liver cells ko recover hone ka time mile.
* **Low-fat, High-carb khana khayein:** Khane me tel, ghee, butter aur fried food bilkul band karein kyunki swollen liver fat ko digest nahi kar sakta. Diet me ubla chawal, khichdi, kela, dahi aur ganna juice lein.
* **Clean water piyein:** Paani boil karke ya filter karke piyein.
* **Urine aur stool color track karein:** Daily urine aur latrine ka color notice karein (clay color stool block nali ko dikhata hai).

### Don't (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT take Paracetamol or painkillers.** A damaged liver cannot metabolize these drugs; taking paracetamol can cause rapid progression to acute liver failure.
* **Do NOT drink alcohol.** Alcohol is a direct hepatotoxin.
* **Do NOT go for nose-drops, nose-bleeding, or herbal healers** (jhad-phook). These delay hospital diagnosis of serious causes like gallstone obstruction or viral hepatitis.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **LFT (Liver Function Test):** Measures total, direct (conjugated), and indirect (unconjugated) bilirubin. Checks SGPT/SGOT (hepatitis marker) and Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP - bile block marker).
* **Abdominal Ultrasound:** Checks if the bile ducts are dilated, indicating obstructive jaundice (due to a gallstone or tumor block).
* **Viral Serology:** Checks for Hepatitis A, B, C, and E.
* **CBC & Reticulocyte Count:** Done for hemolytic jaundice to check for malaria or blood cell disorders (anemia).

---

## Medical & Surgical Management
* **For Hepatocellular Jaundice (Hepatitis):** Treatment is supportive rest for HAV/HEV, and specific antiviral drugs for HBV/HCV.
* **For Obstructive Jaundice (Gallstones/Tumor):** Requires surgical intervention to clear the block:
   * **ERCP:** An endoscopic procedure to remove gallstones blocking the common bile duct.
   * **Surgical Decompression:** Placing a stent to bypass pancreatic tumor blockages.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to jaundice management:*
1. **Antivirals:** Target underlying viral hepatitis (e.g., Tenofovir, Sofosbuvir).
2. **Ursodeoxycholic Acid (UDCA):** Helps dissolve gallstones and improves bile flow in chronic liver disease.
3. **Bile Acid Sequestrants:** Control severe skin itching (e.g., Cholestyramine).
4. **Vitamins (Fat-soluble):** Supplemented if bile block prevents fat absorption (Vitamins A, D, E, K).

## Prevention of Jaundice
* **Drink Clean Water:** Protect against waterborne Hepatitis A and E.
* **Vaccinate for Hepatitis B:** Universal vaccination protects against chronic HBV.
* **Avoid Alcohol Abuse:** Prevent alcoholic hepatitis and cirrhosis.
* **Safe Food Hygiene:** Wash raw food and hands before cooking.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Jaundice
* **Acute Liver Failure:** Hepatic coma and death within days (high risk in pregnant HEV patients).
* **Biliary Sepsis:** Infected bile buildup in obstructive jaundice, causing shock.
* **Kernicterus:** Severe brain damage in newborns when indirect bilirubin crosses the blood-brain barrier.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient shows sudden confusion, extreme sleepiness, or flapping hand tremors (hepatic encephalopathy).**
* **The patient is a pregnant woman who develops yellow eyes and vomiting.**
* **The patient develops high fever with chills and severe right-upper abdominal pain (cholangitis/infection).**
* **The patient vomits blood or passes black, sticky stools.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is jaundice?**
   Jaundice (Piliya) is the yellowish discoloration of the eyes and skin caused by the buildup of a pigment called bilirubin in the blood.

2. **What is Bilirubin and where does it come from?**
   Bilirubin is a yellow waste pigment produced when old red blood cells are broken down. The liver normally processes and removes it through bile.

3. **What is the difference between direct and indirect bilirubin?**
   Indirect (unconjugated) bilirubin is the raw form traveling from blood to liver. Direct (conjugated) bilirubin is processed by the liver to be soluble in water.

4. **Why do jaundice patients have clay-colored stools?**
   Bilirubin gives stool its normal brown color. If the bile duct is blocked (obstructive jaundice), bilirubin cannot reach the intestines, making stool look pale, white, or clay-colored.

5. **Why should I avoid taking paracetamol during jaundice?**
   A swollen, inflamed liver cannot process paracetamol. Taking it puts a toxic load on the liver, which can cause sudden, acute liver failure.

6. **What is Obstructive Jaundice?**
   It is jaundice caused by a physical blockage in the bile ducts (like a gallstone or pancreatic tumor) that prevents bile from draining into the gut.

7. **How is newborn (neonatal) jaundice treated?**
   Neonatal jaundice is common. It is treated using **Phototherapy** (blue-green light) which breaks down indirect bilirubin under the skin into a water-soluble form that the baby can excrete in urine.

8. **Where can I get a free liver function test (LFT) in India?**
   LFT blood tests are performed completely free of charge at all government primary health centers (PHCs) and district hospitals.

9. **Can a pregnant woman get severe complications from jaundice?**
   Yes. Jaundice caused by Hepatitis E in pregnancy carries a very high risk of rapid liver failure (fulminant hepatitis), which can be fatal for both mother and baby.

10. **Why does jaundice cause severe skin itching?**
    In bile blockages, bile salts accumulate in the blood and settle under the skin, irritating nerve endings and causing constant, severe itching.

11. **Is sugarcane juice a cure for jaundice?**
    Sugarcane juice provides hydration and easily digestible glucose, supporting liver recovery, but it cannot cure infections like Hepatitis B/C or gallstones. It is supportive, not a cure.

12. **Why does the urine turn dark yellow in jaundice?**
    Excess processed bilirubin in the blood is filtered out by the kidneys and excreted in the urine, turning it a dark yellow, tea, or cola color.

13. **What is the Widal test used for in jaundice?**
    The Widal test is for typhoid, not jaundice. For jaundice, doctors check LFT (Bilirubin, SGPT/SGOT, ALP) and abdominal ultrasound.

14. **What is ERCP?**
    ERCP (Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography) is a specialized endoscopy procedure used to find and remove gallstones blocking the bile duct.

15. **What is the price of generic UDCA (Ursodeoxycholic Acid) at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic UDCA (150mg/300mg) tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, costing only ₹150 to ₹220 per strip, compared to ₹600+ for branded versions.

16. **Why does malaria cause jaundice?**
    Malaria parasites destroy red blood cells rapidly (hemolysis). This releases massive amounts of raw bilirubin into the blood, overloading the liver and causing hemolytic jaundice.

17. **What is Hepatic Encephalopathy?**
    It is a brain dysfunction that occurs when the liver fails to filter ammonia from the blood. The ammonia travels to the brain, causing confusion, flapping hand tremors, and coma.

18. **Can traditional nose-drops cure jaundice?**
    No. Nasal drops or traditional "bleeding" methods have no scientific basis and can cause severe infections, delaying proper medical treatment.

19. **What is the difference between jaundice and hepatitis?**
    Hepatitis is the inflammation of the liver (the cause). Jaundice is the yellowing of the skin and eyes (the symptom/sign of that cause).

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover neonatal phototherapy?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for newborn nursery stays, phototherapy treatments, pediatric ICU care, and all diagnostic tests.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Jaundice is caused by eating too much yellow food like turmeric (haldi).
  * **Fact:** Jaundice is caused only by high bilirubin levels in the blood due to liver, blood, or bile duct disorders. Turmeric does not cause or worsen jaundice.
* **Myth:** Piliya jhadwane (tantra-mantra) or putting herbal drops in the nose cures jaundice instantly.
  * **Fact:** Most acute jaundice (Hep A/E) is self-limiting and recovers on its own, leading to beliefs in magic cures. True obstructive jaundice (stones/cancer) will turn fatal if not treated medically.
* **Myth:** A person with jaundice should follow a strict zero-salt, zero-water diet.
  * **Fact:** Dehydration is dangerous. Drink plenty of boiled, clean water. Restrict salt only if the patient has fluid buildup (ascites) in the abdomen.
* **Myth:** Giving children paracetamol is safe to treat jaundice fever.
  * **Fact:** Paracetamol is highly toxic to an inflamed liver. Avoid all painkillers during jaundice. Use normal tap water cloth sponging to lower fever.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures jaundice.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys has no scientific effect on bilirubin processing. It is a useless superstition. Consult a doctor.
* **Myth:** Jaundice only happens to alcoholics.
  * **Fact:** Jaundice can happen to anyone due to waterborne viral infections (Hep A/E), bloodborne viruses (Hep B/C), gallstones, or blood disorders like malaria.
* **Myth:** Newborn jaundice means the mother did not care for the baby properly.
  * **Fact:** Neonatal jaundice is a normal physiological process because the newborn's liver is immature. It resolves with phototherapy and frequent feeding.
* **Myth:** All liver tonics in the market can cure jaundice fast.
  * **Fact:** OTC liver tonics do not cure viral infections or gallstone blocks. Only targeted medical care and liver rest (bed rest) can cure jaundice.
* **Myth:** Jaundice spreads through the air when the patient talks or breathes.
  * **Fact:** Jaundice is not an airborne infection. It only spreads if the cause is waterborne Hepatitis A/E (fecal-oral) or bloodborne Hepatitis B/C, not by breathing.
* **Myth:** If you had jaundice once, you can never get it again.
  * **Fact:** Jaundice is a symptom. You can get it multiple times due to different causes, such as contracting a different hepatitis virus or developing gallstones.
