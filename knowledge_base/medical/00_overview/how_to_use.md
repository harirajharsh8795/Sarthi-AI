# Swasthya Swasthya Sahayika ka Upyog Kaise Karein (How to Use the Medical Knowledge Base)

## Overview

### English
This guide explains how to effectively use the Saarthi AI Medical Knowledge Base to find reliable health information. It details how the folder structure is organized, how to perform search queries in English, Hindi, or Hinglish, how to locate critical warnings, and how to verify information with physical healthcare services. This offline library is designed to make navigation simple for users of all digital literacy levels.

## Quick Summary
To search the database, input your symptom or topic in simple conversational terms (e.g., "sir dard" or "fever precautions"). Use the Cross References at the bottom of each page to navigate between symptoms, anatomy, and treatments. Pay close attention to "Emergency Warning Signs" on every disease page to know when a condition is critical.

---

## Definition
* **Semantic Search:** A search method that focuses on the meaning behind search terms rather than just matching exact keywords, allowing the AI to understand conversational Hindi/Hinglish queries.
* **Offline Navigation:** Browsing files locally stored on a device without requiring active cellular networks or Wi-Fi connectivity.

---

## Anatomy / Background
How the Knowledge Base is designed for quick retrieval:
1. **Hierarchical Folders:** Structured by medical categories (Symptoms, Anatomy, Chronic Diseases, First Aid) to locate specific files quickly.
2. **Standardized Sections:** Every file follows a strict template, ensuring you always know where to find symptoms, treatments, or prevention.
3. **Multilingual Content:** Every page starts with an overview in English, Hindi, and Hinglish for instant comprehension.

---

## Causes

### Factors Driving the Design of this Guide
* The need to guide users away from panic search behaviors in health emergencies.
* Empowering low-digital-literacy users in rural areas to browse health articles efficiently.
* Reducing cognitive overload by using a uniform layout across all medical files.

### Contributing Implementation Goals
* Making it easy for regional health workers (ASHAs/ANMs) to quickly retrieve reference manuals.
* Restructuring medical topics so they can be parsed by local text-to-speech engines.

### Risk Factors of Incorrect Usage
* **Lifestyle:** Blindly trying home remedies for chest pain thinking it is simple gas or acidity.
* **Environmental:** Neglecting safety guidelines for water purification during disease outbreaks.

## Symptoms

### Signs of Ineffective Search Queries
* Receiving "no results found" because of searching complex chemical formulations instead of common disease names.
* Feeling confused by multiple documents without reading the "Quick Summary" first.

### Indicators of Successful Database Navigation
* Finding the exact disease profile and understanding early vs. advanced symptoms.
* Correctly mapping symptoms to "When to Consult a Doctor" timelines.

### Severe Technical/Search Errors
* Unresolved links, formatting errors, or using wrong folders for unrelated queries.
* Missing critical emergency warnings due to skipping page headers.

### Navigation Guides by Age
* **Children:** Guide parents to focus on vaccination, nutrition, and child health folders.
* **Women:** Navigation guides for pregnancy, menstrual health, and women's wellness.
* **Elderly:** Guides focusing on chronic diseases, arthritis, and dosage precautions warnings.

## Diagnosis

### Medical Query Analysis
* Assess what type of query you have: an emergency (First Aid), a symptom check (Symptoms), or general information (Anatomy/Diseases).

### Physical Path Selection
* Browse the folder corresponding to your query (e.g., go to `medical/first_aid/` if someone is choking).

### Query Translation & Keywords
* Search using simple terms. The system is tuned for common Indian terms and colloquial Hinglish (e.g., "bhukar", "saap kaatna").

### Differential Navigation
* Compare overlapping symptom profiles (e.g., checking both `fever.md` and `malaria.md` to see differences).

## Treatment

### Action Plan 1: Basic Symptom Search
* Open the `medical/symptoms/` folder, find your symptom, and read the "Common Causes" and "Home Care" sections.

### Action Plan 2: Handling Emergencies
* Immediately open `medical/first_aid/` files (e.g., `cpr.md`, `burns.md`) and follow step-by-step instructions.

### Action Plan 3: Chronic Disease Management
* Review the `medical/chronic_diseases/` files for guidelines on daily habits, diet, and regular clinical monitoring.

### Action Plan 4: Locating Government Benefits
* Check the "Government Health Programs" section at the bottom of files to find free treatment schemes in India.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug search boundaries:*
1. **Generic Drug Search:** How to look up drug categories (like Antipyretics) instead of specific commercial brand names.
2. **Prescription Warnings:** Instruction on reading drug caution notes and checking if a medicine requires a Schedule H doctor prescription.
3. **Jan Aushadhi Lookup:** Tips on finding low-cost alternative medicines using the generic drug name.

## Prevention

### Daily Search Best Practices
* Check the "Last Updated" date in the YAML metadata to ensure you are reading current health guidelines.
* Do not save old screenshots of articles; always reference the live offline file to avoid outdated information.

### Safety Habits
* Cross-check details with your community health worker (ASHA/ANM) whenever you seek medical care.

---

## Possible Complications
* **Self-Diagnosis Risks:** Attempting to self-medigate by matching symptoms without a clinical laboratory test.
* **Emergency Delay:** Wasting critical minutes reading first-aid files during a severe heart attack instead of calling an ambulance.
* **Information Misinterpretation:** Confusing generic educational tips with actual personalized prescriptions.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Do NOT waste time searching the database. Seek immediate emergency care at a hospital or call 108 if a person has:
* **Difficulty breathing, severe chest pain, or sudden loss of speech.**
* **Severe physical injuries, heavy blood loss, or poison ingestion.**
* **High fever with neck stiffness or unconsciousness.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* To confirm any medical condition described in this knowledge base.
* If symptoms do not improve after following home care tips for 2-3 days.
* Whenever you need personalized medication dosage or treatment changes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **How do I search for a topic if I don't know the exact medical name?**
   You can search using common terms in Hindi, English, or Hinglish (e.g., "dast" for diarrhea, "vomiting" for vomiting).

2. **Does this database require internet to work?**
   No. The Saarthi AI Medical Knowledge Base is fully offline and saved locally on your device.

3. **Where do I find the emergency first aid instructions?**
   All first aid topics are kept inside the `medical/first_aid/` folder (e.g., `burns.md`, `snake_bite.md`).

4. **Why is there no medicine dosage mentioned in the files?**
   Medicines require specific dosages based on age, weight, and clinical tests. Giving wrong doses is dangerous, so we do not list dosages.

5. **How can I find out about free government operations or treatments?**
   Every disease page has a "Government Health Programs" section listing relevant schemes like Ayushman Bharat.

6. **What should I do if a link in the file doesn't open?**
   Make sure you are browsing within the correct folder structure. You can open files directly using any local file explorer.

7. **How do I know if the information is correct and updated?**
   Each file has a "Last Updated" date in the metadata at the top, referencing official sources like WHO and MoHFW India.

8. **What does the "window period" mean in diagnostic files?**
   It indicates the time frame within which a test can accurately detect a disease after exposure.

9. **Can this app help me find a doctor near me?**
   For doctor registries, check official government health portals or visit your nearest primary health center (PHC).

10. **Can I copy or share these medical files?**
    Yes, these markdown files are open-source and can be copied or shared for educational purposes.

11. **Where do I find information about childhood vaccines?**
    Go to `medical/child_health/vaccination.md` to see the complete immunization schedule.

12. **What should I do if someone is unconscious?**
    Immediately check the `medical/first_aid/cpr.md` file for recovery position and CPR steps, and call 108.

13. **Can I search for local remedies for common cold?**
    Yes, open `medical/symptoms/cough.md` and read the "Home Care" section.

14. **How are the files structured?**
    Every document follows the same structure: Overview, Symptoms, Causes, Diagnosis, Treatment, Prevention, FAQs, and Disclaimer.

15. **What do the keywords at the top of the page do?**
    They help the search engine index and retrieve the document when you type similar search terms.

16. **Why do we have a myths vs facts section in every file?**
    To clear common health misconceptions and prevent dangerous traditional practices.

17. **Can health workers use this database for teaching?**
    Yes, it is designed as a simplified reference tool for ASHAs, ANMs, and community leaders.

18. **Where can I learn about safe pregnancy care?**
    Go to `medical/women_health/pregnancy.md` or check files in the `sexual_health` folder.

19. **What does CD4 mean in HIV files?**
    CD4 refers to the immune helper cells. You can read details in `medical/infectious_diseases/aids.md`.

20. **Can I use this app for veterinary or animal health?**
    No, this database is strictly for human medical and public health education.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Searching medical apps is a replacement for visiting a doctor.
  * **Fact:** Medical apps provide general knowledge; only a licensed doctor can legally diagnose and treat diseases.
* **Myth:** You must search using complex English medical terms to get results.
  * **Fact:** Saarthi AI is optimized for simple Hinglish and Hindi words (e.g., "bukhar", "khansi").
* **Myth:** First-aid instructions must only be read during an actual emergency.
  * **Fact:** First-aid guides should be read beforehand so you know what to do when an emergency occurs.
* **Myth:** The database will prescribe custom antibiotic doses if you type your age.
  * **Fact:** Saarthi AI will never prescribe medicines or dosages to ensure patient safety and prevent AMR.
* **Myth:** All health websites and offline apps are equally trustworthy.
  * **Fact:** Many sources contain outdated or incorrect tips. Saarthi AI uses verified ICMR and WHO guidelines.
* **Myth:** If a symptom guide matches my condition, it is 100% certain I have that disease.
  * **Fact:** Many diseases share identical symptoms. Only diagnostic tests and doctor evaluations can confirm.
* **Myth:** The home care sections describe clinical cures.
  * **Fact:** Home care guides are for comfort and temporary relief, not clinical cures.
* **Myth:** The government schemes section has application forms.
  * **Fact:** The schemes section provides guidance and helpline numbers; actual registration must be done at government portals.
* **Myth:** You can buy prescription medicines based on the app's drug category descriptions.
  * **Fact:** Pharmacies will reject app screenshots; they require physical prescriptions signed by registered doctors.
* **Myth:** The database covers all diseases in the world.
  * **Fact:** The database focuses on common conditions, public health concerns, and symptoms relevant to the Indian population.

## Conversation Examples

### Conversation 1
* **User:** App me mujhe sugar ki dawai ka naam batao.
* **Assistant:** Saarthi AI par sugar (Diabetes) ke dawaiyon ki category (jaise Metformin or Insulin) ke baare me padha ja sakta hai. Lekin patient ki health aur sugar level ke hisab se sahi dose aur dawai sirf doctor hi prescribe kar sakte hain.

### Conversation 2
* **User:** How do I find the child vaccination chart in this offline database?
* **Assistant:** You can navigate to the `medical/child_health/` folder and open the `vaccination.md` file. It contains the complete National Immunization Schedule (NIS) for children.

### Conversation 3
* **User:** Mujhe sar me dard hai aur ulti ho rahi hai. Kis folder me dekhu?
* **Assistant:** Aap `medical/symptoms/` folder me `headache.md` aur `vomiting.md` files dekh sakte hain. Lekin agar dard achanak aur bahut tez hai, to turant doctor se milein.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** Can I search the database using my local language?
* **Assistant:** Yes. You can type your queries in English, Hindi, or Hinglish (e.g., "khansi ka ilaj" or "tb warning signs") and the system will retrieve the correct files.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Mer pitaji ko saans lene me dikkat ho rha hai, kya is app me check karu?
* **Assistant:** Saans lene me achanak dikkat hona ek medical emergency ho sakti hai. App par search karne me waqt kharab na karein. Turant ambulance (108) bulayein aur hospital ke emergency room me jayein.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** How do I know when a file was last updated?
* **Assistant:** Every markdown file contains metadata at the very top. Look for the "last_updated" field to check the date.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Kya Jan Aushadhi Kendras ke bare me information isme milegi?
* **Assistant:** Haan, general overview aur sabhi medicine-related files ke government schemes section me Jan Aushadhi Pariyojana ki jankari di gayi hai.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Is it safe to follow the home care tips for diarrhea?
* **Assistant:** The home care section for diarrhea recommends ORS and fluids to prevent dehydration, which is standard first aid. However, if stools contain blood or fever develops, consult a doctor immediately.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** What does "differential diagnosis" mean in the diagnostic sections?
* **Assistant:** It is the clinical process doctors use to distinguish your condition from other diseases that cause similar symptoms (e.g., separating dengue from malaria).

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Mujhe malaria ke bare me padhna hai. Kaha milega?
* **Assistant:** Aap `medical/infectious_diseases/` folder me `malaria.md` file open karke padh sakte hain.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Abha card kya hota hai aur iska yahan kya use hai?
* **Assistant:** ABHA (Ayushman Bharat Health Account) sarkari digital health account hai. Iski aur anya yojanao ki details aap humare "Government Health Programs" section me padh sakte hain.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** I cannot see any pictures in the anatomy files. Is this normal?
* **Assistant:** Yes. The database is optimized for quick text retrieval and works fully offline. You can read the detailed descriptions in the "Anatomy / Background" section.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Kya is app par search confidential hai?
* **Assistant:** Haan, yeh database fully offline hai aur aapke device par store hai. Aapka search data kisi third-party server par nahi jata.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** Antibiotics ke side effects kaha likhe hain?
* **Assistant:** Aap `medical/medicines/` folder me `antibiotics.md` file open karke clinical categories, use guidelines, aur warnings ke baare me padh sakte hain.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** What should I do if a family member has high fever with a stiff neck?
* **Assistant:** Do not search symptoms. Fever with a stiff neck is a severe warning sign (meningitis indicator). Take them to the emergency ward immediately.
