# Poisoning ka Pratham Upachar (Poisoning First Aid, Chemical Exposure, and Toxicology)

## Overview

### English
Poisoning is injury or death caused by swallowing, inhaling, touching, or injecting various drugs, chemicals, venoms, or gases. First aid depends on the route of exposure. For ingested poisons, **inducing vomiting is strictly contraindicated** in most cases (especially for acids, alkalis, or kerosene) as it causes secondary burns to the esophagus and airway. Immediate steps include identifying the substance, moving the victim to fresh air for inhaled gases (carbon monoxide), flushing skin/eyes with water for 20 minutes for chemical contact, and seeking immediate emergency hospital care for antidotes or gastric lavage.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
Poisoning involves toxic exposure through ingestion, inhalation, or contact. Key symptoms include vomiting, seizures, difficulty breathing, and chemical burns around the mouth. First aid forbids inducing vomiting for caustic substances. Move inhaled cases to fresh air. Flush eye/skin contact with water for 15-20 minutes. Diagnostics check toxic blood levels. Management utilizes specific antidotes and activated charcoal.

---

## Definition
* **Poisoning:** Exposure to a substance that is toxic or harmful, leading to physiological disruption or organ damage.
* **Corrosive/Caustic Poison:** Highly acidic (pH $< 2$) or alkaline (pH $> 11.5$) substances (like toilet cleaners, bleach, sulfuric acid) that destroy human tissues instantly on contact.
* **National Poisons Information Centre (NPIC):** A specialized unit at AIIMS New Delhi providing 24/7 telephonic guidance (Helpline: 1800-116-117) to manage acute poisoning cases.

---

## Toxicology & Route-Specific Care

Toxicants enter the body through four pathways:
1. **Ingestion (Swallowing):** Insecticides (organophosphates common in India), household chemicals, or drug overdoses. *Vomiting risks aspiration into lungs.*
2. **Inhalation (Breathing):** Carbon monoxide (from generators or charcoal fires in closed rooms) or pesticide sprays.
3. **Absorption (Skin/Eye Contact):** Agri-chemicals absorbed through skin or acid splashes in eyes.
4. **Injection:** Venomous stings/bites or drug abuse.

## Symptoms of Poisoning

### English
* **Ingested:** Nausea, abdominal pain, and continuous vomiting; chemical burns, redness, or typical smell around the mouth; drowsiness, confusion, or complete unconsciousness; sudden seizures or fits.
* **Inhaled:** Severe shortness of breath, coughing, wheezing; dizziness, headache, and confusion; irritation in the eyes.
* **Skin/Eye:** Severe redness, burning pain, and peeling skin; watering, redness, and inability to open eyes.

### Hindi
* **निगलने पर:** मतली, पेट में तेज दर्द और उल्टी; मुंह के आसपास जलने के निशान या अजीब गंध; अत्यधिक सुस्ती या बेहोशी; दौरे पड़ना।
* **सांस द्वारा अंदर जाने पर:** सांस लेने में तकलीफ, खांसी, सिरदर्द और चक्कर आना।
* **त्वचा/आँख पर:** गंभीर लाली, जलन, दर्द और त्वचा का छिलना।

### Hinglish
* **Khane par:** Ulti hona, pet me severe pain; mouth ke aaspas jalne ke nishan ya ajeeb smell aana; behad neend (drowsiness) aana ya behosh ho jana; fits (doure) padna.
* **Saans me jane par:** Saans phoolna, lagatar khansi aana, sir dard, chakkar aur confusion hona.
* **Skin/Eye me jane par:** Tez jalan, laal hona, skin nikalna; aankh me sujan aur kholne me dikkat.

## First Aid Action Plan (Route-Specific Protocol)

### For Ingested Poisons (Swallowed)

#### English
* **Do NOT induce vomiting.** If the poison was a corrosive chemical (like acid, bleach, or phenyl), vomiting will cause it to burn the throat and mouth a second time on the way up, or enter the lungs (aspiration).
* **If the patient is unconscious:** Place them on their left side (karwat/recovery position) to prevent them from choking on their own saliva or vomit.
* **Collect the container:** Save the chemical bottle, pill strip, or leftover food to show the doctor for identifying the antidote.
* **Call NPIC AIIMS (1800-116-117)** for instant toxicological guidance.

#### Hindi
* **उल्टी न कराएं:** एसिड या फिनाइल निगलने पर उल्टी कराने से खाद्य नली दोबारा जलती है।
* **मरीज को करवट दिलाएं:** बेहोश होने पर बाईं करवट लेटाएं।
* **कंटेनर इकट्ठा करें:** जहर की बोतल या दवा के पत्ते को डॉक्टर को दिखाने के लिए संभाल कर रखें।

#### Hinglish
* **Vomiting (ulti) bilkul na karwayein:** Acid, phenyl, toilet cleaner ya kerosene (mitti ke tel) pine par ulti karwane se windpipe aur foodpipe dobara burn ho jati hain aur saans nali block ho jati hai.
* **Karwat dilayein:** Patient behosh ho to left side me karwat (recovery position) dilayein taaki saliva saans nali me na jaye.
* **Bottle ko sambhalein:** Zeher ki bottle, packet, ya bachi hui tablet strip ko hospital le jaane ke liye sambhal kar rakhein.

### For Inhaled Poisons (Gases/Fumes)

#### English
* Immediately move the victim into fresh, open air. Loosen tight clothing around their neck. If they are not breathing, begin CPR immediately. Do not enter a gas-filled room without proper respiratory mask protection.

#### Hindi
* पीड़ित को तुरंत खुली, ताजी हवा में ले जाएं। यदि सांस बंद हो, तो तुरंत सीपीआर शुरू करें।

#### Hinglish
* Patient ko turant band kamre se nikal kar khuli, taaji hawa me le jayein. Kapde dheele karein aur saans band hone par CPR shuru karein.

### For Skin and Eye Chemical Contact

#### English
* Flush the affected skin or eyes immediately with large amounts of gently running tap water for at least 15 to 20 minutes. Remove contaminated clothing while washing. Do not use neutralizing chemicals on skin.

#### Hindi
* प्रभावित त्वचा या आंखों को कम से कम 15-20 मिनट तक बहते पानी से धोएं। दूषित कपड़े निकालें।

#### Hinglish
* Chemical wali skin ya aankh ko behte nal ke paani se kam se kam 15-20 minutes tak continuously dhoyein. Wash karte waqt kapde nikal dein.

## Medical & Emergency Management

### Gastric Lavage (Stomach Wash)

#### English
* Done in the hospital by inserting a nasogastric tube to wash out stomach contents. *Contraindicated for corrosive acids/alkalis due to risk of stomach perforation.*

#### Hindi
* अस्पताल में राइल्स ट्यूब (nasogastric tube) डालकर पेट की सफाई (stomach wash) करना।

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories and antidotes used by toxicologists:*
1. **Specific Antidotes:** Reverse the effect of specific poisons (e.g., Atropine + Pralidoxime [PAM] for organophosphate pesticides; Acetylcysteine for Paracetamol overdose; Naloxone for opioid overdose).
2. **Adsorbents:** Bind to toxins in the stomach (e.g., Activated Charcoal powder).
3. **Anticonvulsants:** Stop seizures caused by neurotoxic poisons (e.g., Diazepam, Midazolam).
4. **Antiemetics:** Used clinically in hospital to control severe non-corrosive vomiting.
5. **Intravenous Fluids:** Hydrate the body and flush out filtered toxins through the kidneys.

## Prevention of Poisoning Accidents
* **Lock Chemicals:** Store all pesticides, acids, toilet cleaners, and medications in locked cupboards, out of reach of children.
* **Original Containers:** Never store kerosene, pesticides, or phenyl in soft drink bottles, as children can mistake them for water or juice.
* **Check Labels:** Read labels carefully before taking medications. Never take medicines in the dark.
* **Ventilation:** Do not run petrol/diesel generators inside closed rooms or sleep with charcoal heaters (angithi) burning in unventilated bedrooms.

---

## Possible Complications of Severe Poisoning
* **Multihop/Multi-organ Failure:** Pesticides cutting off nervous control, stopping the diaphragm, leading to respiratory failure.
* **Esophageal Strictures:** Permanent scarring and narrowing of the food pipe after swallowing acid/alkali, making swallowing food impossible.
* **Aspiration Pneumonia:** Vomit or chemicals entering the lungs, causing severe chemical lung burns.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Call 108 (Ambulance) or go to the nearest emergency department immediately if you observe:
* **The patient ceases breathing, has no pulse, or goes into cardiac arrest.**
* **The patient develops continuous seizures, severe confusion, or coma.**
* **The patient has swallowed a pesticide, organophosphate, or rodent poison (zinc phosphide).**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Poisons Information Centre (NPIC):** Run by the Department of Pharmacology at AIIMS New Delhi, providing 24/7 free advice on poisoning management to doctors and the public.
* **National Programme for Prevention and Control of Trauma:** Upgrades ICU facilities at district hospitals to handle acute poisoning emergencies.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless ICU treatment packages, ventilator support, and antidote therapies for accidental or intentional poisoning.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is the first aid for a person who swallowed pesticide?**
   Do not induce vomiting. Call the AIIMS Poisons Helpline (1800-116-117) immediately, identify the chemical name from the packet, and transport the patient to a hospital emergency room.

2. **Why is inducing vomiting dangerous in poisoning?**
   If the poison is corrosive (acid/phenyl) or petroleum-based (kerosene), vomiting will cause it to burn the throat a second time, or get inhaled into the lungs, causing fatal chemical pneumonia.

3. **What is the Poisons Helpline number in India?**
   The National Poisons Information Centre (NPIC) at AIIMS New Delhi can be reached 24/7 at **1800-116-117** or **011-26593677**.

4. **What is Activated Charcoal and how does it help?**
   Activated charcoal is a fine black powder that acts like a sponge. When swallowed in the hospital, it binds to poison particles in the gut, stopping them from entering the bloodstream.

5. **What should I do if acid splashes into someone's eyes?**
   Immediately flush the eyes with gently running clean water for at least 15-20 minutes, keeping the eyelids open. Seek emergency eye doctor help.

6. **Why is carbon monoxide poisoning called a silent killer?**
   Carbon monoxide is a colorless, odorless gas. Running generators or burning coal inside a closed room produces it, causing people to sleep comfortably, go into a coma, and die without realizing.

7. **What is a "stomach wash" (gastric lavage)?**
   It is a hospital procedure where a plastic tube is inserted through the nose into the stomach to wash out the swallowed poison before it is absorbed.

8. **Where can BPL cardholders get free poisoning treatment in India?**
   Free treatment, antidotes, and ICU care are provided at all government medical colleges and district hospitals under Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY.

9. **What is the antidote for a paracetamol overdose?**
   The specific clinical antidote for paracetamol toxicity is **N-Acetylcysteine (NAC)**, which protects the liver from fatal damage.

10. **Why should chemicals never be stored in soft drink bottles?**
    Children identify drinks by the bottle shape. Storing phenyl, kerosene, or pesticides in Coca-Cola or Sprite bottles is the leading cause of accidental child poisoning in India.

11. **Can I give milk to a poisoning victim?**
    No. Do not give milk, raw eggs, or water unless specifically instructed by a poison control specialist, as some chemicals absorb faster when mixed with fats in milk.

12. **What is the antidote for pesticide (organophosphate) poisoning?**
    The specific antidotes are **Atropine** (which blocks the excess acetylcholine) and **Pralidoxime (PAM)** (which reactivates the cholinesterase enzyme).

13. **What is the first aid for inhaled toxic smoke?**
    Immediately move the person to fresh air. If they are coughing or dizzy, give oxygen if available. Start CPR if they stop breathing.

14. **Why does organophosphate pesticide poisoning cause pinpoint pupils?**
    These chemicals block the cholinesterase enzyme, causing a massive buildup of acetylcholine, which over-stimulates the parasympathetic system, constricting the pupils to pinpoints.

15. **Can a person survive poisoning if they are already in a coma?**
    Yes. With immediate hospital ICU care, mechanical ventilation, and specific antidotes, many comatose poisoning patients recover completely.

16. **How do I clean skin exposed to dry chemical powder?**
    Brush the dry powder off the skin with a cloth first, then flush the area with large amounts of running water. Do not wet dry chemicals directly on skin without brushing, as some react with water to burn.

17. **What is the price of generic Atropine injections in India?**
    Generic Atropine injections are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, often costing only ₹5 to ₹10 per ampoule.

18. **Why is Metformin stopped after contrast scans to prevent acidosis?**
    Contrast scans stress kidneys, which can cause Metformin to accumulate, leading to lactic acid buildup (acidosis) in the blood.

19. **What is uremic encephalopathy?**
    It is brain poisoning caused by the buildup of urea and waste products in the blood when the kidneys fail, leading to confusion and coma.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover ventilator support for poisoning cases?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers completely cashless packages including ICU stay, ventilator charges, medications, and antidote therapies.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Force the patient to drink salt water or soap water to make them vomit the poison.
  * **Fact:** Inducing vomiting can cause the poison to burn the esophagus again or enter the lungs (aspiration). Salt water can also cause fatal sodium toxicity. Never force vomiting.
* **Myth:** Giving the patient cow urine (gomutra) will neutralize any poison.
  * **Fact:** Cow urine cannot neutralize chemical poisons or pesticides. It introduces bacteria and delays life-saving hospital antidote treatment.
* **Myth:** All poisons have the same antidote (like activated charcoal).
  * **Fact:** Charcoal only stops absorption. Severe poisoning requires specific clinical antidotes (like Atropine for pesticides or Acetylcysteine for paracetamol).
* **Myth:** Carbon monoxide poisoning only happens in factories.
  * **Fact:** It commonly happens at home when people run generators or burn coal heaters (angithi) in closed, unventilated bedrooms overnight.
* **Myth:** If the patient swallowed poison but feels completely fine, you do not need to go to the hospital.
  * **Fact:** Many poisons (like paracetamol or rat poison) do not cause symptoms for the first 12-24 hours, but silently destroy the liver or kidneys. Get checked immediately.
* **Myth:** Pouring vinegar on a chemical skin burn will neutralize it.
  * **Fact:** Mixing acid (vinegar) with an alkaline burn creates a chemical reaction that releases extreme heat, worsening the skin burn. Wash with plain running water only.
* **Myth:** Sucking on the skin can pull out swallowed chemical poisons.
  * **Fact:** You cannot suck out swallowed poisons, and trying to do so only puts the rescuer at risk.
* **Myth:** Poisoning is always intentional (suicide).
  * **Fact:** Millions of cases are accidental, especially in children drinking household cleaners stored in juice bottles, or farmers inhaling pesticide sprays.
* **Myth:** If you eat mud, it will absorb the poison.
  * **Fact:** Mud does not act like activated medical charcoal and contains lockjaw (tetanus) bacteria. Never feed mud to a patient.
* **Myth:** CPR should not be done on a poisoning victim.
  * **Fact:** If the patient stops breathing and has no pulse, perform CPR. Use a pocket mask or face shield for breaths to protect yourself from chemical residue on their lips.
