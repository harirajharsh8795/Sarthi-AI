# Kutte ke Katne ka Pratham Upachar (Dog Bite First Aid, Rabies Prevention, and Vaccines)

## Overview

### English
A dog bite is a physical injury that carries a high risk of bacterial infections (Pasteurella, Capnocytophaga) and the fatal Rabies virus, which is transmitted through the saliva of infected animals. Rabies has a 99.9% fatality rate once symptoms develop, making immediate Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) essential. First aid requires washing the wound immediately with soap and running tap water for at least 15 minutes, applying antiseptic, and consulting a doctor for the Anti-Rabies Vaccine (ARV) and Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG) for severe (Category III) bites. The wound should never be sutured (stitched) immediately unless absolutely necessary, to prevent trapping the virus inside.

## Quick Summary
A dog bite poses a severe risk of rabies and bacterial infections. First aid requires washing the wound under running water with soap for 15 minutes. Bites are graded into Categories I, II, and III. Immediate medical management involves the rabies vaccine (ARV) on days 0, 3, 7, 14, and 28, along with Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG) for Category III wounds. Prevention focuses on vaccinating pets.

---

## Definition
* **Rabies:** A vaccine-preventable, zoonotic, viral disease affecting the central nervous system, caused by lyssaviruses and transmitted through the bite of an infected animal.
* **Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP):** The immediate treatment of a bite victim after rabies exposure to prevent the virus from entering the central nervous system, consisting of wound washing, vaccine, and immunoglobulin.
* **Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG):** Passive antibodies injected directly into and around Category III wounds to neutralize the rabies virus instantly before active vaccine antibodies develop.

---

## Pathology & Classification of Exposure (WHO Guidelines)

The rabies virus enters the body through saliva and travels along peripheral nerves to the brain at a rate of 12-24 mm/day. Post-exposure prophylaxis depends on the category of bite:
* **Category I:** Touching or feeding animals, licks on intact skin. (PEP: None needed. Just wash the skin).
* **Category II:** Nibbling of uncovered skin, minor scratches or abrasions without bleeding. (PEP: Wash wound immediately + start active Anti-Rabies Vaccine schedule).
* **Category III:** Single or multiple transdermal bites or scratches with bleeding; licks on broken skin; contamination of mucous membranes with saliva (e.g., licks on lips/eyes). **(PEP: Wash wound immediately + inject Rabies Immunoglobulin RIG around the wound + start active Anti-Rabies Vaccine schedule).**

## First Aid Action Plan (Immediate Steps)

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Wash the wound immediately** under running tap water with soap for at least 15 minutes. The mechanical action of washing with soap washes away up to 90% of the rabies virus particles.
* **Apply an antiseptic** containing povidone-iodine, alcohol, or chlorhexidine to kill remaining virus particles.
* **Seek medical help instantly** to start the anti-rabies vaccination series.
* **Get a Tetanus toxoid injection** if you have not received one in the past 5 years.

#### Hindi
* **घाव को तुरंत धोएं:** नल के बहते पानी के नीचे साबुन से कम से कम 15 मिनट तक धोएं।
* **एंटीसेप्टिक लगाएं:** पोविडोन-आयोडीन (povidone-iodine) या डिटॉल लगाएं।
* **तुरंत अस्पताल जाएं:** एंटी-रेबीज वैक्सीन (ARV) शुरू करें।

#### Hinglish
* **Ghaw ko turant dhoyein:** Behte nal ke pani ke niche sabun se kam se kam 15 minutes tak continuously ragad kar dhoyein. Sabun virus ke envelope ko destroy karta hai.
* **Antiseptic lagayein:** Dhone ke baad Betadine (povidone-iodine) ya alcohol-based sanitizer lagayein.
* **Turant hospital jayein:** Anti-Rabies Vaccine (ARV) lagwana shuru karein.
* **Tetanus ka injection lein:** Tetanus dose zaroori lagwayein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT apply home remedies** like red chili powder, mustard oil, lime (chuna), or plant juices. They cause chemical burns, severe pain, and delay proper vaccination.
* **Do NOT cover the wound tightly** with bandages or dressings. Keep it open to let secretions drain.
* **Do NOT get the wound stitched (sutured) immediately.** Stitching forces the rabies virus deeper into the tissues and nerves. If stitching is essential due to heavy bleeding, it must be done only *after* injecting Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG) into the wound.

* **घरेलू नुस्खे न लगाएं:** मिर्च पाउडर, सरसों का तेल, चूना या मिट्टी न लगाएं।
* **घाव को कसकर न बांधें:** इसे पट्टी से कसकर न ढकें।
* **तुरंत टांके न लगवाएं:** इससे वायरस अंदर नसों में गहरा चला जाता है।

#### Hinglish
* **Mirch powder ya chuna na lagayein:** Ghaw par lal mirch powder, sarso ka tel, ya chuna bilkul na lagayein. Isse chemical burns aur severe pain hota hai.
* **Bandage tight na karein:** Ghaw ko tight patti se na bandhein, use open ya loose rakhein.
* **Tanke (Stitches) turant na lagwayein:** Turant tanke lagane se virus nasso me andar lock ho jata hai. Agar bleeding rokne ke liye tanke lagana compulsory ho, to pehle wound me RIG (Immunoglobulin) inject hona zaroori hai.

## Anti-Rabies Vaccine (ARV) Schedule (WHO PEP Protocol)
*The vaccine must be administered intramuscularly (deltoid muscle of arm, never in the buttocks):*
* **Dose 1 (Day 0):** On the day of the bite (as soon as possible).
* **Dose 2 (Day 3):** Three days after the first dose.
* **Dose 3 (Day 7):** Seven days after the first dose.
* **Dose 4 (Day 14):** Fourteen days after the first dose.
* **Dose 5 (Day 28):** Twenty-eight days after the first dose (Optional/recommended for high-risk Category III).
* *Note: If a patient has been fully vaccinated in the past, they only require 2 booster doses on Day 0 and Day 3.*

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to rabies and dog bite management:*
1. **Active Immunisation (ARV):** Rabies vaccine that stimulates the patient's own immune system to make antibodies (e.g., Purified Chick Embryo Cell Vaccine - PCECV, Rabipur).
2. **Passive Immunisation (RIG):** Provides ready-made antibodies for immediate protection in Category III bites (e.g., Human Rabies Immunoglobulin - HRIG, Equine Rabies Immunoglobulin - ERIG).
3. **Antiseptics:** Kill pathogens locally in the wound (e.g., Povidone-iodine 10% solution).
4. **Tetanus Toxoid Vaccine:** Prevents secondary tetanus infection from dog saliva.
5. **Antibiotics (Oral):** Prevent secondary bacterial wound infections (e.g., Amoxicillin-Clavulanate).

## Prevention of Rabies
* **Vaccinate Pets:** Ensure your pet dogs and cats receive their annual rabies vaccines.
* **Avoid Stray Animals:** Educate children not to tease street dogs, touch sleeping dogs, or play with mother dogs feeding puppies.
* **Report Strays:** Inform municipal authorities about aggressive stray dogs for vaccination and sterilization programs.
* **Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis:** Vets, animal handlers, and forest workers should get pre-exposure rabies vaccines (doses on Day 0, 7, and 21 or 28).

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Bites
* **Rabies Encephalitis:** Fatal brain inflammation leading to hydrophobia (fear of water), aerophobia, convulsions, coma, and death.
* **Sepsis:** Bacterial wound infection entering the bloodstream, causing multi-organ failure.
* **Severe Scarring/Deformity:** Deep tissue damage from dog claws and teeth.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient exhibits hydrophobia (spasms in the throat when seeing or trying to drink water).**
* **The patient becomes extremely agitated, aggressive, aerophobic (fear of wind/air drafts), or starts showing paralysis.**
* **The dog bite has caused severe, uncontrolled active bleeding from a major artery.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Rabies Control Programme (NRCP):** Aims to prevent human deaths from rabies by ensuring free availability of anti-rabies vaccines (ARV) and rabies immunoglobulins (RIG) at all government district hospitals and community health centers.
* **Universal Immunization Programme (UIP):** Focuses on spreading awareness about immediate wound washing and rabies vaccine compliance.
* **PM Bhartiya Janaushadhi Pariyojana:** Offers generic wound dressings, antiseptics, and supportive antibiotics at very low prices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a dog bite emergency first aid?**
   Wash the wound immediately under running tap water with soap for at least 15 minutes, apply an antiseptic (Betadine), and visit a hospital for the anti-rabies vaccine.

2. **Why is washing the wound with soap for 15 minutes so important?**
   The rabies virus has a fatty outer envelope. Soap dissolves this envelope, destroying the virus, and running water mechanically flushes up to 90% of the virus particles out of the wound.

3. **What is the difference between Category II and Category III bites?**
   Category II involves minor scratches without bleeding. Category III involves deep, bleeding bites, licks on broken skin, or saliva contact with lips/eyes, requiring both vaccine and immunoglobulin.

4. **What is Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG) and when is it needed?**
   RIG contains ready-made rabies antibodies. It is injected directly into and around Category III bleeding wounds to neutralize the virus immediately, as the vaccine takes 7-14 days to start producing antibodies.

5. **Where is the rabies vaccine injected?**
   The vaccine is injected into the deltoid muscle of the upper arm in adults. For young children, it is injected into the anterolateral thigh. It should never be given in the buttocks, as fat absorbs it too slowly.

6. **What is the rabies vaccine schedule in India?**
   The standard PEP schedule under the NCDC consists of doses given on Day 0 (day of bite), Day 3, Day 7, Day 14, and Day 28.

7. **Do I need rabies vaccines if my pet dog bit me?**
   If the pet is fully vaccinated and kept indoors, the risk is low. However, you should still consult a doctor. The dog should be observed for 10 days; if it remains healthy, the vaccine series can sometimes be adjusted.

8. **Where can I get free rabies vaccines in India?**
   Anti-rabies vaccines (ARV) are provided completely free of cost at all government community health centers (CHCs), civil hospitals, and medical colleges under the NRCP.

9. **Why is it bad to apply red chili powder or lime to a dog bite?**
   These substances cause chemical burns, damage tissue, increase pain, and do not kill the virus. They only delay proper clinical washing and vaccination.

10. **Can rabies be cured once symptoms appear?**
    No. Once clinical symptoms of rabies (like hydrophobia or fear of air drafts) appear in a human, there is no cure, and the disease is 100% fatal. This is why immediate vaccination is life-saving.

11. **Why should a dog bite wound not be stitched immediately?**
    Stitching closes the wound, forcing the virus deep into the muscle tissues where it can attach to nerves easily. Keep the wound open.

12. **Can a lick from a street dog cause rabies?**
    If the lick is on intact, healthy skin, the risk is zero (Category I). If the lick is on a cut, scratch, or on the lips/eyes, it is Category III and requires full PEP.

13. **Do I need a tetanus shot after a dog bite?**
    Yes. Dog saliva contains various bacteria. A tetanus toxoid injection should be taken within 24 hours if you haven't had one in the last 5 years.

14. **What is the "10-day observation rule" for the dog?**
    If the biting dog is a pet and can be watched, observe it for 10 days. If the dog shows no signs of rabies (loss of appetite, aggression, paralysis) and survives 10 days, it did not have active rabies during the bite.

15. **Can puppies transmit rabies?**
    Yes. Even a small puppy can transmit rabies if it has been bitten by an infected animal. Treat all puppy bites with the same medical precaution.

16. **Why does rabies cause hydrophobia (fear of water)?**
    The virus causes severe, painful spasms in the throat muscles (larynx) when trying to swallow. Eventually, even the sight or thought of water triggers these painful throat spasms, causing panic.

17. **What is Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP)?**
    It is a preventive rabies vaccine series (3 doses on Days 0, 7, and 21 or 28) given to high-risk people like veterinarians, dog catchers, and forest rangers.

18. **Can a rabbit or rat bite transmit rabies?**
    Small rodents like rats, mice, squirrels, and rabbits rarely carry rabies and are not known to transmit it to humans, though they still require normal wound washing.

19. **What is the role of the National Rabies Control Programme (NRCP)?**
    NRCP is a government initiative that ensures free availability of vaccines (ARV) and immunoglobulins (RIG) in public health centers, trains doctors, and promotes dog vaccination.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover dog bite treatment?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless treatment packages for severe animal bites, surgical wound cleaning, ICU stays, and immunoglobulin administration.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Applying red chili powder, lime, or oil on the dog bite burns the rabies virus.
  * **Fact:** These substances cause severe chemical burns and pain but do not kill the virus. Only washing with soap and running water for 15 minutes destroys the virus.
* **Myth:** If the dog does not look mad or foam at the mouth, it does not have rabies.
  * **Fact:** A dog can carry the rabies virus in its saliva and transmit it days before showing any clinical signs of madness or foaming. Treat all street dog bites immediately.
* **Myth:** Rabies vaccine requires 14 injections in the stomach.
  * **Fact:** This is an obsolete method. Modern rabies vaccines (ARV) require only 4 to 5 small, painless injections in the arm (deltoid muscle).
* **Myth:** Sucking on the wound with your mouth will extract the virus.
  * **Fact:** Sucking on the wound introduces the virus directly into your mouth, risking brain infection through your lips and gums. Never suck a bite wound.
* **Myth:** Hydrophobia is just a mental fear of water that can be treated with counseling.
  * **Fact:** Hydrophobia is a physical spasm of the throat muscles caused by the virus attacking the brain's nerves. It is involuntary and fatal.
* **Myth:** A fully vaccinated domestic pet dog can never transmit rabies.
  * **Fact:** While the risk is extremely low, if the pet was bitten by a rabid stray without your knowledge, or the vaccine failed, it can transmit the disease. Get a doctor's evaluation.
* **Myth:** Applying a metal key on the bite area stops rabies.
  * **Fact:** Metal has no anti-viral properties. Sucking or pressing keys on the wound is a useless myth that delays vaccination.
* **Myth:** Rabies is highly contagious and spreads through coughing.
  * **Fact:** Rabies does not spread through human coughing. It is transmitted only through saliva contact with broken skin or mucous membranes (bites/licks).
* **Myth:** Alternative medicine powders can cure rabies after symptoms start.
  * **Fact:** Rabies is 100% fatal once symptoms start. No alternative medicine can cure it. Immediate vaccination after the bite is the only life-saving way.
* **Myth:** You should stitch the dog bite wound immediately to stop scarring.
  * **Fact:** Stitching forces the virus deep into the tissue nerves, increasing the risk of rabies. Keep the wound open unless bleeding is life-threatening.
