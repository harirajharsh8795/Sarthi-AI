# CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) ki Pehchan aur Prakriya (CPR First Aid and AED Guide)

## Overview

### English
Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) is a life-saving emergency procedure performed when a person's heart stops beating (cardiac arrest). It combines chest compressions (to manually pump blood through the body to keep the brain alive) and rescue breaths (to deliver oxygen to the lungs). An Automated External Defibrillator (AED) should be used as soon as available to deliver an electrical shock to restore normal heart rhythm. Immediate bystander CPR can double or triple the chances of survival after cardiac arrest. The standard adult protocol involves 30 chest compressions (at a rate of 100-120 per minute, depth of 2-2.4 inches) followed by 2 rescue breaths.

## Quick Summary
CPR restores blood circulation and breathing. It is used during cardiac arrest. Symptoms of cardiac arrest are unconsciousness, lack of breathing, and no pulse. First aid delivers 30 chest compressions and 2 rescue breaths. compressions are performed at 100-120/min at a depth of 2-2.4 inches. If an AED is available, follow its voice prompts. Prevention focuses on early bystander action.

---

## Definition
* **Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR):** An emergency procedure consisting of chest compressions and rescue breaths to manually maintain circulatory flow and oxygenation during cardiac arrest.
* **Cardiac Arrest:** A sudden loss of heart function, breathing, and consciousness, usually caused by an electrical disturbance in the heart. (Different from a heart attack, which is a blood flow blockage).
* **AED (Automated External Defibrillator):** A portable electronic device that automatically diagnoses life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias (VF/VT) and is able to treat them through defibrillation (shock).

---

## Pathology & Time Sensitivity
Without oxygen, brain cells die within minutes:
1. **Clinical Death vs. Biological Death:** Clinical death occurs the moment the heart stops beating. Biological death (permanent brain cell death) begins in 4 to 6 minutes.
2. **Immediate Intervention:**
   * **Within 1 minute:** CPR start has over 90% survival chance.
   * **4 - 6 minutes:** Brain damage starts.
   * **10 minutes:** Brain death is certain if no CPR is performed.
   * *Bystander CPR acts as a manual heart pump, buying time until paramedics arrive.*

## Symptoms of Cardiac Arrest (When to do CPR)

### English
* The person is completely unresponsive (does not wake up or react when you shake their shoulders and shout).
* The person is not breathing, or is only gasping/gurgling abnormally (agonal breathing).
* No pulse is felt on the carotid artery in the neck (check for no more than 10 seconds).

* व्यक्ति पूरी तरह से बेहोश है (कंधे हिलाने और चिल्लाने पर कोई प्रतिक्रिया नहीं)।
* व्यक्ति सांस नहीं ले रहा है, या केवल असामान्य रूप से हांफ (agonal breathing) रहा है।
* गर्दन की मुख्य धमनी (carotid artery) पर कोई पल्स (धड़कन) महसूस नहीं हो रही है।

* Neck ki side me carotid artery par koi pulse (dhadkan) mehsus nahi ho rahi (sirf 10 seconds tak check karein).

## CPR Action Plan (Adult Protocol - Hands-Only & Standard)

### Step-by-Step Procedure

#### English
1. **Verify Scene Safety:** Ensure the area is safe for you and the victim.
2. **Check Responsiveness:** Tap the shoulders and shout, "Are you okay?"
3. **Call for Help:** Ask someone to call **108** and get an **AED**. If alone, call 108 first, put the phone on speaker, and start CPR.
4. **Check Breathing & Pulse:** Look at the chest for rise and fall, and check the neck pulse for 5-10 seconds. If no pulse/breathing, begin compressions.
5. **Chest Compressions (30 times):**
   * Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest (lower half of breastbone). Place your other hand on top, interlocking fingers.
   * Keep your elbows locked and shoulders directly over your hands.
   * **Push hard and fast:** Compress at a depth of **2 to 2.4 inches (5 to 6 cm)** and a rate of **100 to 120 compressions per minute** (matching the beat of the song "Staying Alive"). Allow the chest to fully recoil after each push.
6. **Give Rescue Breaths (2 times):**
   * Open the airway using head-tilt, chin-lift. Pinch the nose shut, seal your mouth over the victim's mouth, and blow for 1 second until the chest rises. Give 2 breaths.
7. **Repeat the cycle:** 30 compressions and 2 breaths. If you cannot give breaths, do **Hands-Only CPR** (continuous compressions).

#### Hindi
1. **घटनास्थल सुरक्षित करें:** मरीज को सख्त, समतल जमीन पर लेटाएं।
2. **सहायता बुलाएं:** किसी से 108 नंबर पर कॉल करने और एईडी (AED) लाने को कहें।
3. **छाती दबाएं (30 बार):** छाती के बीच में दोनों हाथों को आपस में जोड़कर रखें। कोहनी सीधी रखते हुए **2 से 2.4 इंच गहरा** दबाएं। गति **100-120 प्रति मिनट** होनी चाहिए।
4. **सांस दें (2 बार):** सिर पीछे झुकाएं, नाक बंद करें, और मुंह से मुंह सटाकर 2 बार हवा फूंकें। 30:2 का चक्र जारी रखें।

#### Hinglish
1. **Zameen par letaayein:** Patient ko kisi thos, flat zameen (hard floor) par seedha letayein.
2. **Help ke liye call karein:** Kisi se kahein ki wo **108** par ambulance call karein aur **AED** lekar aayein.
3. **Chest Compressions (30 baar):**
   * Apne ek hath ki heel ko chest ke center (breastbone) par rakhein, dusra hath uske upar rakh kar fingers lock karein.
   * Elbows ko bilkul seedha (lock) rakhein aur pure body weight se press karein.
   * **Tezi aur gehrayi se dabayein:** Compress ki depth **2 se 2.4 inches (5-6 cm)** honi chahiye aur speed **100 se 120 compressions per minute** (Stayin' Alive song ki beat par). Chest ko wapas upar aane dein.
4. **Rescue Breaths (2 baar):**
   * Head-tilt, chin-lift se saans ki nali kholyein. Naak band karke, patient ke muh me 1-1 second ke liye 2 baar air blow karein taaki chest upar uthe.
5. **Cycle repeat karein:** 30 compressions aur 2 breaths ka cycle continuous chalayein. Agar artificial saans nahi de sakte, to sirf continuous chhati dabate rahein (**Hands-Only CPR**).

## Automated External Defibrillator (AED) Use

### English
1. **Turn on the AED** immediately. Follow the loud voice prompts.
2. **Apply the pads** to the victim's bare chest as shown in the pictures (one on right upper chest, one on left lower chest).
3. **Clear the victim:** Ensure no one is touching the victim while the AED analyzes the heart rhythm.
4. **Deliver shock if advised:** If the machine says "Shock advised," press the orange shock button. Ensure everyone is clear before pressing.
5. **Resume CPR:** Immediately resume chest compressions for 2 minutes after the shock, until the AED analyzes again.

### Hindi
1. **एईडी (AED) चालू करें:** मशीन के ध्वनि निर्देशों (voice prompts) का पालन करें।
2. **पैड लगाएं:** एक पैड छाती के दाईं ओर ऊपर और दूसरा बाईं ओर नीचे चिपकाएं।
3. **दूरी बनाएं:** जब मशीन हार्ट रिदम की जांच करे, तो मरीज को न छुएं। "शॉक" बटन दबाने का निर्देश मिलने पर बटन दबाएं और तुरंत सीपीआर शुरू करें।

#### Hinglish
1. **AED machine turn-on karein:** Machine ke voice prompts (bolne wali aawaz) ko dhayan se sunkar follow karein.
2. **Pads lagayein:** Patient ki bare chest par ek pad right side me upar aur dusra pad left side me niche lagayein.
3. **Door rahein (Clear):** Jab machine heart rhythm check kare, to patient ko koi touch na kare. Agar machine kahe "Shock advised", to orange button dabakar shock dein aur shock lagte hi dobara CPR compressions shuru karein.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. During CPR and advanced cardiac life support (ACLS), the following clinical drug categories are administered by medical professionals in hospitals:*
1. **Vasopressors:** Constrict blood vessels to shunt blood to the brain and heart (e.g., Epinephrine/Adrenaline injection).
2. **Antiarrhythmics:** Restore normal heart rhythm in shockable arrest states (e.g., Amiodarone, Lidocaine).
3. **Electrolyte Correctors:** Used if arrest is triggered by high potassium or low calcium (e.g., Calcium Gluconate).
4. **Thrombolytics:** Injected if the cardiac arrest was caused by a massive pulmonary blood clot.

## Prevention of Sudden Cardiac Arrest
* **Treat Coronary Artery Disease:** Manage underlying atherosclerosis, hypertension, and diabetes.
* **Stop Smoking:** Quitting tobacco prevents plaque rupture that triggers sudden cardiac arrest.
* **Know AED Locations:** Public spaces (airports, metro stations, malls) in India are increasingly equipped with AEDs; know where they are located.
* **Learn CPR:** Get certified in basic life support (BLS) through hands-on training workshops.

---

## Possible Complications of CPR
* **Rib Fractures:** Breaking of ribs due to deep chest compressions (common in elderly, but acceptable as it is a life-saving procedure).
* **Gastric Inflation:** Air entering the stomach instead of lungs during rapid rescue breaths, causing vomiting.
* **Aspiration:** Inhaling stomach vomit into the lungs, leading to chemical pneumonia.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Call 108 (Ambulance) or go to the nearest emergency department immediately if you observe:
* **A person collapses suddenly, loses consciousness, and ceases normal breathing.**
* **Severe, squeezing chest pain radiating to arm, back, or neck, with sweating (cardiac arrest pre-cursor).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is CPR?**
   CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) is an emergency procedure combining chest compressions and rescue breaths to manually circulate oxygenated blood to the brain when the heart stops.

2. **What is the difference between a heart attack and cardiac arrest?**
   A heart attack is a plumbing problem where a blood vessel is blocked, but the heart is still beating. Cardiac arrest is an electrical problem where the heart stops beating completely.

3. **What is the compression-to-breath ratio in CPR?**
   The standard ratio for adults is 30 chest compressions followed by 2 rescue breaths, repeated continuously.

4. **How deep should chest compressions be for adults?**
   Compressions must be between 2 to 2.4 inches (5 to 6 cm) deep to successfully squeeze the heart against the spine and pump blood.

5. **What is the correct compression rate in CPR?**
   Compressions should be delivered at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute, which matches the rhythm of the popular song "Stayin' Alive."

6. **What is Hands-Only CPR?**
   It is CPR performing only continuous chest compressions without rescue breaths, recommended for untrained bystanders or when no barrier mask is available.

7. **What is an AED?**
   An Automated External Defibrillator (AED) is a portable device that analyzes the heart's rhythm and delivers an electric shock if needed to restore a normal heartbeat.

8. **Where can I find an AED in India?**
   AEDs are increasingly installed in public transport hubs like major airports, metro stations, large corporate offices, and malls.

9. **Can doing CPR break the patient's ribs?**
   Yes. Pressing 2 inches deep can fracture ribs, especially in elderly patients. However, a broken rib is treatable, whereas cardiac arrest without CPR is fatal.

10. **Why should I let the chest fully recoil after each compression?**
    Chest recoil allows the heart chambers to fill with blood again. If you do not let the chest rise completely, the heart cannot pump enough blood on the next push.

11. **How do I perform CPR on an infant under 1 year of age?**
    Use 2 fingers in the center of the chest to compress about 1.5 inches deep. Keep the compression-to-breath ratio at 30:2 (or 15:2 if two rescuers are present).

12. **Why is the carotid artery checked for a pulse?**
    The carotid artery in the neck is closest to the heart and is the last pulse to disappear during cardiac arrest, making it the most reliable site to check.

13. **What is "agonal breathing" during cardiac arrest?**
    It is abnormal, irregular gasping or snoring sounds made by a dying brain. It is not normal breathing and is a sign to start CPR immediately.

14. **Is it safe to use an AED on a wet surface or in the rain?**
    Move the patient to a dry area if possible and dry their chest before applying pads, as water can conduct electricity and reduce the shock's effectiveness.

15. **What should I do if the AED says "No shock advised"?**
    It means the heart is in a non-shockable rhythm (like asystole/flatline). Immediately resume chest compressions (CPR) for 2 minutes until the AED checks again.

16. **Why should no one touch the patient while the AED is analyzing?**
    If someone touches the patient, the AED might read the other person's heart rhythm instead, leading to an incorrect diagnosis.

17. **Can I be sued for performing CPR on a stranger in India?**
    No. India has a "Good Samaritan Law" that protects bystanders who provide emergency first aid to victims of accidents or medical emergencies from legal liability.

18. **When should I stop performing CPR?**
    Stop only when: the patient starts showing signs of life (moving, breathing), professional medical help (paramedics) takes over, or you are physically exhausted.

19. **What is the role of Adrenaline in cardiac arrest?**
    Adrenaline (Epinephrine) is a vasopressor injected intravenously every 3-5 minutes during advanced CPR to constrict blood vessels and increase blood flow to the brain and heart.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover cardiac arrest emergency care?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for emergency ICU stays, ventilator support, and cardiac resuscitation treatments at all empaneled hospitals.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** CPR is only done by doctors and nurses.
  * **Fact:** Most cardiac arrests happen at home. Immediate bystander CPR is the most critical link in survival before doctor arrival. Anyone can learn and perform it.
* **Myth:** You must give mouth-to-mouth rescue breaths to do CPR.
  * **Fact:** If you are untrained or uncomfortable, "Hands-Only" CPR (chest compressions only) is highly effective and preferred over doing nothing.
* **Myth:** An AED will shock anyone, even if their heart is still beating normally.
  * **Fact:** The AED is smart. It only delivers a shock if it detects life-threatening shockable rhythms (like Ventricular Fibrillation). It will never shock a normal heart.
* **Myth:** If you break a rib during CPR, you must stop immediately.
  * **Fact:** Keep going. A fractured rib is painful but treatable. Stopping chest compressions will lead to permanent brain death.
* **Myth:** Cough CPR (coughing hard) can save you if you are alone during a cardiac arrest.
  * **Fact:** This is a dangerous internet hoax. Cardiac arrest causes instant unconsciousness within seconds, making coughing impossible. Call 108 immediately.
* **Myth:** Cardiac arrest and heart attack are the same thing.
  * **Fact:** A heart attack is a blood block (awake patient). Cardiac arrest is an electrical shutdown (unconscious, not breathing patient). Cardiac arrest requires CPR.
* **Myth:** Pressing the chest lightly is safer to prevent injuries.
  * **Fact:** Light compressions do not squeeze the heart enough to pump blood to the brain. You must compress at least 2 inches deep for adults.
* **Myth:** You should wait for the ambulance before starting CPR.
  * **Fact:** Brain death begins in 4-6 minutes. Ambulances take longer. Immediate bystander CPR is essential to keep the brain alive.
* **Myth:** CPR will always wake the patient up immediately.
  * **Fact:** CPR rarely wakes a patient up. Its purpose is to keep oxygen flowing to the brain until doctors can shock the heart back to normal.
* **Myth:** You can use an AED on a patient wearing a metal necklace.
  * **Fact:** Do not place AED pads directly over metal jewelry or pacemakers. Place pads at least 1 inch away from any metal.
