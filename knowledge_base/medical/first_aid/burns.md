# Jale ka Pratham Upachar (Burns First Aid, Classification, and Care)

## Overview

### English
Burns are tissue damage resulting from heat, overexposure to the sun or other radiation, chemical contact, or electrical contact. They are classified into three levels based on depth: First-Degree (superficial redness of the epidermis), Second-Degree (partial thickness damage causing painful blisters), and Third-Degree (full-thickness destruction of all skin layers, often painless due to nerve damage). Immediate first aid requires cooling the burn under cool, running tap water for at least 10-15 minutes, avoiding the application of home remedies (butter, toothpaste, oils), and covering with a clean dressing before seeking medical care.

## Quick Summary
Burns are tissue injuries classified as first, second, or third degree. Immediate first aid requires running cool water for 10-15 minutes. Blisters should never be popped. Avoid toothpaste, butter, or oils. Diagnostics involve clinical examination of total burn surface area (Rule of Nines). Management includes silver sulfadiazine cream, pain relievers, and IV fluids for severe cases.

---

## Definition
* **First-Degree Burn:** A superficial burn affecting only the epidermis, characterized by redness, minor swelling, and pain.
* **Second-Degree Burn (Partial-Thickness):** A burn extending into the dermis, characterized by redness, extreme pain, and fluid-filled blisters.
* **Third-Degree Burn (Full-Thickness):** A severe burn destroying all layers of the skin, causing a leathery white, brown, or charred appearance, often painless due to destroyed nerve endings.

---

## Pathology & Rule of Nines
Burns damage the skin barrier and cause fluid loss:
1. **Loss of Epidermal Barrier:** The skin prevents bacteria from entering and holds body fluids. When destroyed, massive amounts of fluid leak out, and pathogens easily enter, risking sepsis.
2. **Rule of Nines (Adults):** Clinicians calculate the Total Body Surface Area (TBSA) burned to estimate fluid resuscitation needs:
   * Head & Neck: 9%
   * Each Arm: 9%
   * Each Leg: 18%
   * Anterior Trunk (Chest/Abdomen): 18%
   * Posterior Trunk (Back): 18%
   * Perineum: 1%

## Symptoms & Classifications

### English
* **First-Degree:** Redness, dry skin, mild swelling, pain when touched, no blisters. (Heals in 3-6 days).
* **Second-Degree:** Intense pain, swelling, fluid-filled blisters (bullae), wet-looking pink/red skin. (Heals in 2-3 weeks, may scar).
* **Third-Degree:** Leathery white, dark brown, or charred black skin; lack of pain in the center of the burn (surrounding areas may be painful); visible fat, muscle, or bone exposed.

### Hindi
* **प्रथम श्रेणी (First-Degree):** त्वचा का लाल होना, सूखापन, हल्की सूजन, छूने पर दर्द, कोई छाले नहीं।
* **द्वितीय श्रेणी (Second-Degree):** तीव्र दर्द, सूजन, पानी से भरे छाले (blisters), त्वचा का गीला और लाल दिखना।
* **तृतीय श्रेणी (Third-Degree):** त्वचा का चमड़े जैसा सफेद, गहरा भूरा या कोयले की तरह काला होना; जले हुए हिस्से में दर्द महसूस न होना (तंत्रिका क्षति के कारण)।

## First Aid Action Plan (Thermal/Heat Burns)

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Cool the burn immediately** under cool, running tap water for 10 to 15 minutes. This stops the burning process and reduces swelling and pain.
* **Remove jewelry and tight clothing** (like rings, watches) gently from the burned area before it starts swelling.
* **Cover the burn loosely** with a sterile non-stick bandage or a clean, dry, lint-free cloth (like a clean bedsheet).
* **Elevate the burned limb** above the heart level if possible, to reduce swelling.

#### Hindi
* **तुरंत ठंडा करें:** प्रभावित हिस्से को 10-15 मिनट तक ठंडे बहते नल के पानी के नीचे रखें।
* **गहने और तंग कपड़े हटाएं:** सूजन शुरू होने से पहले अंगूठियां, घड़ियां आदि धीरे से निकालें।
* **ढीला ढकें:** साफ, सूखे और बिना रोएंदार कपड़े से ढकें।

#### Hinglish
* **Turant thanda karein:** Jale hue area par behte nal ka normal thanda paani 10-15 minutes tak dalein.
* **Ornaments hatayein:** Sujan shuru hone se pehle rings, watch aur tight kapde dheere se nikal dein.
* **Clean kapde se cover karein:** Jale hue area ko loosely kisi saaf, dry aur soft cotton kapde se cover karein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT use ice, ice water, or ice packs.** Extreme cold constricts blood vessels, worsening tissue damage (frostbite-like damage).
* **Do NOT apply toothpaste, butter, flour, turmeric, or oils.** They trap the heat inside the tissues, make the burn deeper, and cause severe bacterial infections.
* **Do NOT pop or puncture blisters.** Popping blisters breaks the sterile barrier, leading to rapid infection.
* **Do NOT try to pull away clothing** that is stuck to the burned skin. Cut around it.

#### Hindi
* **बर्फ का उपयोग न करें:** बर्फ का पानी रक्त वाहिकाओं को सिकोड़ता है, जिससे घाव और गहरा हो जाता है।
* **घरेलू नुस्खे न लगाएं:** टूथपेस्ट, मक्खन, आटा, हल्दी या तेल न लगाएं। यह गर्मी को अंदर रोकता है और संक्रमण पैदा करता है।
* **छाले न फोड़ें:** छालों को फोड़ने से संक्रमण का खतरा काफी बढ़ जाता है।

#### Hinglish
* **Barf (Ice) na lagayein:** Barf lagane se blood vessels tight ho jati hain, jisse skin tissue aur damage ho jata hai.
* **Toothpaste, makkhan, ya oil na lagayein:** Yeh home remedies skin ke andar heat ko lock kar deti hain aur severe bacterial infection karti hain.
* **Chhale (Blisters) na phodein:** Chhale phodne se germs andar chale jate hain aur infection ho jata hai.
* **Chipe kapde ko na kheencheir:** Agar kapda skin par chipak gaya hai, to use kheechein nahi, aaspas se cut kar dein.

## Medical Management of Severe Burns

### Fluid Resuscitation (Parkland Formula)

#### English
* For severe burns ($>15-20\%$ TBSA), patients require immediate intravenous (IV) fluid resuscitation in the hospital to prevent hypovolemic burn shock:
  \[\text{Fluid Volume (Ringer's Lactate)} = 4 \text{ mL} \times \text{Weight (kg)} \times \text{TBSA (\%)}\]
* Half of this total volume is given in the first 8 hours, and the rest in the next 16 hours.

#### Hindi
* गंभीर रूप से जले मरीजों के लिए अस्पताल में तुरंत ड्रिप (Ringer's Lactate) लगाना ताकि डिहाइड्रेशन (burn shock) से बचाया जा सके।

#### Hinglish
* Severe burns (>15%) me hospital me immediate IV fluids (Ringer's Lactate drip) di jati hai taaki fluid loss se hone wale "Burn Shock" se patient ki jaan bachaye ja sake.

### Clinical Wound Care

#### English
* Apply topical antibacterial ointments (such as Silver Sulfadiazine) to prevent infection. Clean the wound under sterile conditions. Administer a Tetanus toxoid injection immediately if not updated.

#### Hindi
* संक्रमण को रोकने के लिए एंटीबैक्टीरियल क्रीम (जैसे Silver Sulfadiazine) लगाना। टेटनस (Tetanus) का टीका देना।

#### Hinglish
* Doctor ki salah se antibacterial cream (jaise Silver Sulfadiazine) lagayein. Tetanus injection zaroori lagwayein.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to burn wound care:*
1. **Topical Antibacterial Agents:** Prevent bacterial infection on raw skin (e.g., Silver Sulfadiazine, Mupirocin).
2. **Systemic Analgesics (Pain Relievers):** Control intense pain (e.g., Paracetamol, Tramadol, or IV Morphine for severe hospital cases).
3. **Intravenous Fluids:** Crystalloids used to restore blood volume (e.g., Ringer's Lactate).
4. **Tetanus Toxoid Injection:** Vaccine to prevent tetanus infection from wound contamination.
5. **Soothing Dressings:** Sterile paraffin gauze or collagen sheets used to promote skin regrowth.

## Prevention of Burn Accidents
* **Kitchen Safety:** Turn pot handles inward toward the back of the stove. Keep children away from the cooking area.
* **Electrical Safety:** Cover unused electrical sockets. Do not overload power strips.
* **Geyser/Hot Water Safety:** Always test bath water temperature with your elbow before putting a child in the tub.
* **Fire Safety:** Install smoke alarms in the house and keep a fire extinguisher near the kitchen.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Burns
* **Hypovolemic Shock:** Severe dehydration and loss of blood volume leading to organ failure and death.
* **Systemic Sepsis:** Bacterial infection entering the raw wound, spreading to the blood, and causing septic shock.
* **Joint Contractures:** Scar tissue tightening the skin over joints, permanently locking the joint in a bent position.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **A burn that covers more than 10% of the patient’s body, or any third-degree burn (charred/white skin).**
* **Burns involving the face, eyes, ears, hands, feet, groin, or major joints.**
* **Soot around the nose/mouth, singed nasal hairs, or difficulty breathing (indicates inhalation injury/throat swelling).**
* **An electrical burn (risk of sudden heart rhythm arrest) or a chemical burn.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Programme for Prevention and Control of Burn Injuries (NPPCBI):** Establishes specialized burn care units at state medical colleges and district hospitals, offering free surgery and rehabilitation services.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless hospitalization packages for severe burn wound debridement, skin grafting surgeries, and ICU care.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Offers generic Silver Sulfadiazine creams, sterile gauze dressings, bandages, and painkillers at very low prices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is the first thing I should do for a heat burn?**
   Immediately place the burned area under cool, running tap water for 10 to 15 minutes. Do not use ice or apply home remedies.

2. **Why is using ice on a burn bad?**
   Ice causes severe vasoconstriction (blood vessel narrowing), which cuts off blood supply to the injured skin, worsening tissue damage.

3. **Why should I avoid applying toothpaste to a burn?**
   Toothpaste contains chemicals and menthol that trap the heat inside the skin tissues, making the burn deeper and introducing harmful bacteria.

4. **Should I pop the blisters that form after a burn?**
   No. Blisters act as a sterile, protective barrier over the raw skin underneath. Popping them opens the wound to bacterial infection.

5. **What is the difference between a second-degree and third-degree burn?**
   A second-degree burn affects the upper layers, forming painful blisters. A third-degree burn destroys all skin layers, turning skin white or charred, and is often painless because nerves are destroyed.

6. **What is the "Rule of Nines"?**
   It is a clinical system used to estimate the percentage of the body surface area burned in an adult, helping calculate the amount of IV fluids needed.

7. **Where can BPL cardholders get free burn care in India?**
   Under the NPPCBI program and Ayushman Bharat, free burn care, ICU stays, and skin surgeries are provided at government medical colleges and district hospitals.

8. **How does Ringer's Lactate help burn patients?**
   Ringer's Lactate is an IV fluid that closely matches body electrolytes. It replaces the massive fluids lost through the damaged skin barrier, preventing shock.

9. **Why is an electrical burn dangerous even if the skin looks normal?**
    Electrical current travels through internal tissues (muscles, nerves) and can cause unseen internal damage, muscle breakdown (rhabdomyolysis), or cardiac arrest.

10. **Why do throat/inhalation burns require emergency intubation?**
    Inhaling hot smoke causes rapid swelling of the airway and vocal cords. If not intubated immediately, the throat can close completely, causing suffocation.

11. **Can a chemical burn be washed with water?**
    Yes. Most chemical burns (acids/alkalis) should be flushed with large amounts of running water for at least 20-30 minutes to wash away the chemical.

12. **Why is a tetanus shot given after a burn?**
    A burn creates raw, damaged tissue that is vulnerable to *Clostridium tetani* bacteria. A tetanus injection prevents this fatal nerve infection.

13. **What is skin grafting?**
    It is a surgical procedure where healthy skin is taken from an unburned part of the patient's body (like the thigh) and transplanted to cover the raw, burned area.

14. **Why do burn scars tighten over joints?**
    As deep burns heal, the scar tissue contracts (shrinks), which can pull the joints tight and limit movement, a complication called joint contracture.

15. **What is the price of generic Silver Sulfadiazine cream in India?**
    Generic Silver Sulfadiazine cream is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹15 to ₹25, compared to ₹80+ for branded versions.

16. **Why does third-degree burn not hurt in the center?**
    The intense heat of a third-degree burn completely destroys the nociceptors (pain-sensing nerve endings) in the skin, making the center numb.

17. **How do I clean a minor burn wound daily?**
    Wash it gently with mild soap and clean water daily, pat dry with a sterile gauze, apply antibiotic ointment, and cover with a non-stick bandage.

18. **Can I use cotton wool directly on a burn wound?**
    No. Loose cotton fibers stick to the raw wound surface, making it extremely painful to remove during dressings and increasing the risk of infection.

19. **What is a chemical burn first aid protocol?**
    Remove contaminated clothing immediately and flush the area with running water for 20-30 minutes. Do not try to neutralize acid with alkali (causes more heat).

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover skin grafting surgeries?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers completely cashless packages for burn debridement, skin grafting, and post-operative plastic surgeries.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Put butter, ghee, or coconut oil on a fresh burn immediately.
  * **Fact:** Butter and oils trap heat, keeping the skin burning internally, making the wound deeper, and introducing bacteria. Use cool running water only.
* **Myth:** Toothpaste is the best cooling agent for burns.
  * **Fact:** Toothpaste dries out the wound, seals in the heat, and causes chemical irritation. It makes it very painful for the doctor to clean the wound.
* **Myth:** Pop all blisters immediately so the skin can dry.
  * **Fact:** Blisters are protective. Popping them exposes raw tissues, leading to severe bacterial infections. Let them heal naturally.
* **Myth:** If a burn does not hurt, it is not serious.
  * **Fact:** A complete lack of pain usually means it is a severe third-degree burn that has destroyed the nerves. It is a critical emergency.
* **Myth:** Pouring ice-cold water is better than normal tap water.
  * **Fact:** Ice-cold water constricts blood vessels, worsening tissue ischemia (lack of blood). Use normal, cool tap water ($15^\circ\text{C}$ to $25^\circ\text{C}$).
* **Myth:** Apply mud or cow dung on a burn to cool it.
  * **Fact:** Mud and cow dung contain millions of bacterial spores (including tetanus) and cause fatal infections. Never apply them.
* **Myth:** A burn wound should always be kept open to the air to heal.
  * **Fact:** Exposed wounds dry out, delay cell growth, and are open to air bacteria. Keep the wound clean, moist with ointment, and covered.
* **Myth:** Once a burn heals, you do not need physiotherapy.
  * **Fact:** Deep burns over joints require physiotherapy during healing to prevent the scar tissue from tightening and locking the joint (contractures).
* **Myth:** Only fire burns are dangerous.
  * **Fact:** Electrical, chemical, and hot steam (scalds) burns can be just as dangerous and cause deep internal injuries.
* **Myth:** Burn scars can be completely removed with a simple cream.
  * **Fact:** Creams cannot remove deep burn scars. Significant scarring requires laser therapy, pressure garments, or reconstructive surgery.
