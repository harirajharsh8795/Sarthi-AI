# Angina Pectoris (Chest Pain) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Angina Pectoris and Coronary Spasm Guide)

## Overview

### English
Angina Pectoris, commonly known as angina, is a clinical syndrome characterized by squeezing, pressure, heaviness, or pain in the chest. It occurs when the heart muscle (myocardium) does not receive enough oxygen-rich blood, a condition called myocardial ischemia, usually due to narrowing of the coronary arteries (atherosclerosis). Angina is classified into **Stable Angina** (predictable, triggered by physical exertion or stress, relieved by rest or sublingual Nitroglycerin) and **Unstable Angina** (unpredictable, occurs at rest, increases in severity, and is a medical emergency indicating an impending heart attack).

## Quick Summary
Angina is chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart. Key classifications are stable (relieved by rest) and unstable (emergency). Symptoms include chest pressure, pain radiating to the left arm or jaw, and shortness of breath. Diagnostics utilize ECG, Troponin, and Angiography. Management includes Nitroglycerin, Beta-blockers, Aspirin, and angioplasty.

---

## Definition
* **Angina Pectoris:** Chest pain or discomfort due to myocardial ischemia, representing a mismatch between myocardial oxygen supply and demand.
* **Stable Angina:** Coronary artery constriction where pain is predictable, occurring during exertion and resolving with rest or nitrates.
* **Unstable Angina (Acute Coronary Syndrome):** A rupture of an atherosclerotic plaque leading to partial arterial obstruction, presenting as chest pain at rest or rapidly worsening pain.

---

## Pathology & Classification of Angina
Ischemia triggers pain pathways in the heart:
1. **Myocardial Oxygen Mismatch:** When exertion raises heart rate, the heart needs more oxygen. Narrowed arteries cannot supply this excess, causing anaerobic metabolism and lactic acid release, which irritates cardiac pain nerves.
2. **Stable vs. Unstable Angina Characteristics:**
   * **Stable:** Predictable pattern. Pain lasts $< 5-10$ minutes. Relieved by rest.
   * **Unstable:** New onset or worsening pain. Pain lasts $> 20$ minutes. Occurs at rest. Does NOT resolve with rest or Nitroglycerin. *Requires immediate emergency hospitalization.*
   * **Prinzmetal's (Variant) Angina:** Rare coronary artery spasm causing pain at rest, unrelated to blockages.

## First Aid & Emergency Protocol

### For Stable Angina Attack (Known Patient)

#### English
1. **Stop all physical activity** immediately. Sit down and rest in a comfortable position.
2. **Administer one tablet of Nitroglycerin (0.4mg) sublingually** (under the tongue). Do not swallow or chew the tablet.
3. If pain does not resolve within 5 minutes, take a second Nitroglycerin tablet and **call 108 immediately**, treating it as a suspected heart attack.

#### Hindi
1. **सभी शारीरिक गतिविधियां रोकें:** आराम से बैठें।
2. **जीभ के नीचे नाइट्रोग्लिसरीन (Nitroglycerin) रखें:** गोली चबाएं या निगलें नहीं, जीभ के नीचे पिघलने दें।
3. 5 मिनट में दर्द ठीक न होने पर तुरंत 108 बुलाएं।

#### Hinglish
1. **Saari physical activities turant rokein:** Shanti se ek jagah aaram se baith jayein.
2. **Nitroglycerin tablet sublingually lein:** Ek tablet ko jeebh ke niche (under the tongue) rakhein. Ise chabayein ya niglein nahi, apne aap dissolve hone dein.
3. Agar 5 minutes me pain thik na ho, to immediate **108 ambulance** call karein, ise heart attack ka warning sign manein.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Electrocardiogram (ECG):** Checks for ST-segment depression or T-wave inversion during pain, indicating ischemia.
* **Cardiac Troponin T/I Test:** Blood test to rule out myocardial infarction (heart attack). Troponin remains normal in angina.
* **Coronary Angiography:** Gold standard catheter-based scan to visualize the exact location and percentage of blockages in coronary arteries.

---

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to angina management:*
1. **Vasodilators (Nitrates):** Dilate coronary arteries and veins to reduce heart workload (e.g., Sublingual Nitroglycerin, Isosorbide Mononitrate).
2. **Antiplatelets:** Prevent blood clots from forming on blockages (e.g., Aspirin, Clopidogrel).
3. **Beta-Blockers:** Lower heart rate and blood pressure, reducing myocardial oxygen demand (e.g., Metoprolol, Atenolol).
4. **Calcium Channel Blockers:** Relax coronary arteries, useful in variant spasms (e.g., Amlodipine, Diltiazem).
5. **Statins:** Stabilize arterial plaques and lower cholesterol (e.g., Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin).

---

## Prevention of Angina Episodes
* **Control Risk Factors:** Keep HbA1c $< 7.0\%$, Blood Pressure $< 130/80$ mmHg, and LDL Cholesterol $< 70$ mg/dL.
* **Quit Smoking:** Nicotine constricts coronary arteries and accelerates plaque formation.
* **Heart-Healthy Diet:** Follow a low-fat, low-sodium (salt $<5$g/day) Mediterranean diet.
* **Regular Mild Exercise:** 30 minutes of brisk walking daily (consult your cardiologist before starting any exercise program).

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Angina
* **Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack):** Plaque rupture leading to complete arterial blockage and heart tissue death.
* **Congestive Heart Failure:** Chronic lack of blood flow weakening the heart muscle, making it unable to pump blood.
* **Cardiac Arrhythmias:** Ischemic damage disrupting the heart's electrical wiring, causing fatal rhythms.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Chest pain that occurs at rest, lasts longer than 15-20 minutes, or is not relieved by Nitroglycerin.**
* **Chest pain accompanied by severe breathlessness, cold sweating, nausea, or fainting.**
* **A feeling of crushing pressure or weight on the chest, like "an elephant sitting on the chest."**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Angina Pectoris?**
   Angina is chest pain or discomfort caused when the heart muscle does not get enough oxygen-rich blood due to narrowed coronary arteries.

2. **What is the difference between stable and unstable angina?**
   Stable angina is predictable, triggered by exercise, and relieved by rest. Unstable angina is unpredictable, occurs at rest, is more severe, and is a medical emergency.

3. **How do I use Nitroglycerin during an angina attack?**
   Place one Nitroglycerin tablet under your tongue (sublingually) at the start of chest pain. Let it dissolve completely. Do not chew or swallow it.

4. **Can Nitroglycerin cause side effects?**
   Yes. Nitroglycerin dilates blood vessels, which can cause a sudden drop in blood pressure, leading to a temporary headache, dizziness, or flushing.

5. **Is angina the same as a heart attack?**
   No. Angina is a temporary decrease in blood flow without permanent heart muscle damage. A heart attack is a complete blockage causing permanent muscle death.

6. **What diagnostic tests confirm angina?**
   Confirming tests include an Electrocardiogram (ECG) during pain, cardiac stress test (TMT), and Coronary Angiography to view blockages.

7. **Where can BPL patients get free angiogram and angioplasty in India?**
   Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, eligible patients can get free cashless angiography and angioplasty at all empaneled government and private hospitals.

8. **Why does physical exercise trigger stable angina?**
   Exercise makes the heart beat faster, increasing its demand for oxygen. Narrowed arteries cannot meet this demand, causing oxygen shortage (ischemia) and pain.

9. **Can stress cause angina?**
   Yes. Severe emotional stress releases adrenaline, which raises blood pressure and heart rate, increasing the heart's oxygen needs and triggering angina.

10. **Why should people taking Sildenafil (Viagra) avoid Nitroglycerin?**
    Both drugs dilate blood vessels. Taking them together can cause a dangerous, sudden drop in blood pressure, leading to fainting or cardiac arrest.

11. **What is Coronary Angiography?**
    It is a minimally invasive imaging procedure using a catheter and contrast dye to map blockages in the heart’s arteries under an X-ray screen (Cath Lab).

12. **Why are beta-blockers prescribed for angina?**
    Beta-blockers slow down the heart rate and lower blood pressure, reducing the workload of the heart and its oxygen requirements, preventing attacks.

13. **Can gas or acidity feel like angina?**
    Yes. Acid reflux (GERD) or gas can cause a burning sensation in the chest. However, any new chest pain should be evaluated by a doctor to rule out heart disease.

14. **What is Prinzmetal's or Variant Angina?**
    It is a type of angina caused by a sudden temporary spasm (squeezing) of a coronary artery, rather than a permanent plaque blockage. It usually happens at rest.

15. **What is the price of generic Nitroglycerin tablets in India?**
    Generic sublingual Nitroglycerin or Isosorbide Mononitrate tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹15 per strip.

16. **Why does stable angina pain go away with rest?**
    Resting lowers the heart rate, reducing its oxygen demand to a level that the narrowed arteries can easily supply, which stops the ischemic pain.

17. **What is the role of Aspirin in angina?**
    Aspirin is an antiplatelet (blood thinner). It prevents platelets from clotting at the site of narrowed arteries, reducing the risk of a complete heart block.

18. **Can women experience different angina symptoms than men?**
    Yes. Women often present with atypical symptoms like shortness of breath, nausea, back or jaw pain, rather than classic crushing chest pain.

19. **What is CABG (Bypass Surgery)?**
    Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG) is a surgery where a healthy blood vessel (from the leg or chest) is grafted to bypass blocked coronary arteries, restoring blood flow.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover bypass surgery (CABG) costs?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for CABG bypass surgery, post-operative ICU care, and cardiologist consultations.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Angina only happens to elderly men who are overweight.
  * **Fact:** Angina can affect women and young adults, especially those with family history, diabetes, high stress, or history of smoking.
* **Myth:** If you have chest pain, drinking warm water with lemon will dissolve the heart block.
  * **Fact:** Lemon water cannot clear cholesterol plaques in coronary arteries. Do not delay medical help with home remedies during chest pain.
* **Myth:** Stable angina is completely cured after taking Nitroglycerin.
  * **Fact:** Nitroglycerin only dilates vessels to relieve immediate pain. It does not dissolve blockages. Angina requires lifestyle changes and block management.
* **Myth:** Angina chest pain is always on the left side of the chest.
  * **Fact:** Angina pain is usually felt behind the breastbone (retrosternal) in the center of the chest, and can radiate to jaw, neck, back, or both arms.
* **Myth:** You should chew Nitroglycerin tablets quickly for faster relief.
  * **Fact:** Nitroglycerin must be placed under the tongue. It is absorbed directly through the sublingual blood vessels. Swallowing it reduces its effectiveness.
* **Myth:** Angina patients should never do any physical exercise.
  * **Fact:** Controlled, regular aerobic exercise (like walking) is highly beneficial for heart health. Consult your cardiologist for a safe exercise plan.
* **Myth:** If my cardiac troponin test is normal, I don't have heart disease.
  * **Fact:** Troponin is only elevated when heart cells die (heart attack). In angina, troponin is normal. A normal test does not rule out blockages.
* **Myth:** Angioplasty is a permanent cure and you can start smoking again after it.
  * **Fact:** Stents can get blocked again (restenosis) if you continue smoking, eat fatty foods, or stop taking blood thinners. Lifestyle control is lifelong.
* **Myth:** Heart pain is always a sharp, needle-like piercing pain.
  * **Fact:** Angina is rarely sharp. It is usually described as a dull, heavy pressure, tightness, squeezing, or burning sensation in the chest.
* **Myth:** Sano (rock salt) does not cause angina or high blood pressure.
  * **Fact:** All salts contain sodium, which raises blood pressure and strains coronary blood flow. Restrict rock salt just like table salt.
