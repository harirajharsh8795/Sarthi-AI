# Stroke (Brain Attack) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Brain Stroke and FAST Protocol Guide)

## Overview

### English
A Stroke, also known as a Brain Attack or Cerebrovascular Accident (CVA), is a critical medical emergency that occurs when the blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, depriving brain tissue of oxygen and nutrients. Within minutes, brain cells begin to die. Strokes are classified into **Ischemic Stroke** (caused by a blood clot blocking a brain artery, accounting for 87% of cases) and **Hemorrhagic Stroke** (caused by a ruptured blood vessel bleeding into the brain). A Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA) is a temporary "mini-stroke" that resolves quickly but serves as a warning sign. Immediate identification using the **FAST** protocol and urgent hospital transport are key.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
A stroke is brain cell death from interrupted blood flow. It is classified as ischemic (clot) or hemorrhagic (bleed). Symptoms follow the FAST protocol (Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 108). Diagnostics use emergency CT scans or MRIs. Management utilizes clot-busting tPA injection within 4.5 hours for ischemic stroke, or surgical decompression for hemorrhagic stroke.

---

## Definition
* **Stroke (CVA):** Rapidly developing loss of brain function due to disturbance in the blood supply to the brain.
* **Ischemic Stroke:** A stroke caused by an obstruction (thrombus or embolus) in a blood vessel supplying blood to the brain.
* **Hemorrhagic Stroke:** A stroke caused by the rupture of a blood vessel (often due to high blood pressure or aneurysm) bleeding directly into the brain tissue.
* **FAST Protocol:** A rapid screening tool for stroke identification: **F**ace Drooping, **A**rm Weakness, **S**peech Difficulty, **T**ime to call 108.

---

## Pathology & The 4.5-Hour Thrombolysis Window
Time is brain: every second of delay destroys millions of neurons:
1. **The Ischemic Penumbra:** When a brain artery is blocked, a central core of tissue dies instantly. Surrounding this is the "penumbra"—a zone of stunned but salvageable brain tissue. Saving this penumbra requires restoring blood flow immediately.
2. **The 4.5-Hour Golden Window:** For Ischemic stroke, clot-dissolving medications (such as Alteplase / tPA) can only be safely injected intravenously within **4.5 hours** of the first symptom. Beyond this window, the risk of bleeding into the dead brain tissue out-weighs the benefit.

## Symptoms of a Stroke (FAST Protocol)

### English
* **F - Face Drooping:** One side of the face droops or is numb. When asked to smile, the person's smile is uneven.
* **A - Arm Weakness:** One arm feels weak or numb. When asked to raise both arms, one arm drifts downward.
* **S - Speech Difficulty:** Speech is slurred, garbled, or hard to understand. The person cannot repeat a simple sentence.
* **T - Time to call 108:** If the person shows any of these signs, even if they go away, call 108 immediately and get them to a stroke-ready hospital.
* *Other Symptoms:* Sudden severe headache ("thunderclap headache" in hemorrhage); sudden confusion; loss of balance or difficulty walking.

### Hindi
* **F - Face (चेहरा):** चेहरे का एक तरफ लटक जाना या सुन्न होना। मुस्कुराने पर चेहरा टेढ़ा दिखना।
* **A - Arm (हाथ):** एक हाथ में कमजोरी या सुन्नता होना। दोनों हाथ उठाने पर एक हाथ नीचे की ओर गिरना।
* **S - Speech (आवाज):** आवाज का लड़खड़ाना, साफ न बोल पाना या समझने में कठिनाई।
* **T - Time (समय):** तुरंत 108 पर कॉल करने का समय।

### Hinglish
* **F - Face Drooping:** Face ka ek side achanak tedha ho jana ya sunn padna. Smile karne par face ek side khinch jata hai.
* **A - Arm Weakness:** Ek hath ya pair me achanak kamzori ya sunn-pan aana. Dono hath upar karne par ek hath niche behne lagta hai.
* **S - Speech Difficulty:** Bolne me ladkhadahat (slurred speech) hona, aawaz saaf na nikalna ya dusro ki baat samajh na pana.
* **T - Time to call 108:** Agar inme se koi bhi lakshan dikhe, to bina ek second gawaye **108 ambulance** call karein aur "Stroke-ready" hospital bhagein.

## First Aid Action Plan (Immediate Steps)

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Check the time** when the first symptom started. This is critical information for the emergency doctor to decide if clot-busting drugs can be given.
* **Help the person lie down** on their side (recovery position) if they are unconscious, with their head slightly elevated. This keeps the airway clear if they vomit.
* **Keep the patient calm** and loosen any tight clothing.
* **Call 108 immediately** and transport them to a hospital equipped with a 24-hour CT Scan and Neurologist.

#### Hindi
* **लक्षण शुरू होने का समय नोट करें:** यह डॉक्टर के लिए सबसे महत्वपूर्ण जानकारी है।
* **मरीज को करवट दिलाएं:** बेहोश होने पर सुरक्षित स्थिति (recovery position) में लेटाएं।
* **तुरंत 108 बुलाएं:** 24 घंटे सीटी स्कैन वाले अस्पताल ले जाएं।

#### Hinglish
* **Symptom shuru hone ka time note karein:** Doctor ko sabse pehle batayein ki pehla lakshan exact kis samay shuru hua tha (Golden Window tracking).
* **Patient ko karwat dilakar letayein:** Agar patient behosh ho ya unhe chakkar aa rahe ho, to left side me karwat (recovery position) dilayein taaki ulti hone par saans ki nali block na ho. Head ko thoda upar rakhein.
* **108 call karein:** Aise hospital jayein jahan 24-hour CT scan machine aur Neurologist available ho.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT give the patient Aspirin.** Unlike a heart attack, where Aspirin is life-saving, in a stroke you do not know if it is ischemic (clot) or hemorrhagic (bleeding). Giving Aspirin to a hemorrhagic stroke patient can worsen brain bleeding and cause death.
* **Do NOT give any food or liquid** (like water or tea) by mouth, as stroke often paralyzes throat muscles, causing severe choking (aspiration).
* **Do NOT wait to see if symptoms improve.** Every minute of delay causes permanent brain damage.

#### Hindi
* **एस्पिरिन (Aspirin) बिल्कुल न दें:** सीटी स्कैन के बिना एस्पिरिन देने से हैमरेजिक स्ट्रोक में ब्लीडिंग बढ़ सकती है, जिससे मौत हो सकती है।
* **मुंह से कुछ भी न दें:** पानी या दवा न पिलाएं, गला पैरालाइज होने से फेफड़ों में जा सकता है।
* **लक्षण ठीक होने का इंतजार न करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Aspirin (aspirin) tablet bilkul na dein:** Heart attack me hum aspirin dete hain, par **stroke me CT scan se pehle aspirin dena strictly forbidden (mana) hai**. Agar patient ko bleeding stroke (hemorrhagic) hai, to aspirin dene se dimaag me bleeding badh jayegi aur patient ki turant maut ho sakti hai.
* **Khane ya peene ko kuch na dein:** Stroke me gale ki muscles paralyze ho sakti hain, jisse paani ya medicine saans ki nali me phans kar suffocation kar sakti hai.
* **Wait bilkul na karein:** "Thodi der me thik ho jayega" soch kar ghar par wait karna sabse badi galti hai.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Emergency Non-Contrast Brain CT Scan:** The absolute first-line test. Done immediately upon arrival to rule out Hemorrhagic Stroke (bleeding shows up instantly as white area on CT). **If CT shows no bleed, it confirms Ischemic Stroke, clearing the way for thrombolysis.**
* **Brain MRI (DWI sequence):** Highly sensitive scan to detect early ischemic changes within minutes of stroke onset.

## Medical & Surgical Management

### For Ischemic Stroke (Clot Blockage)

#### English
* **Intravenous Thrombolysis (tPA):** Clot-busting medication (Alteplase, Tenecteplase) is injected within 4.5 hours of symptom onset to dissolve the clot and restore blood flow.
* **Mechanical Thrombectomy:** For large vessel blocks, a neuro-interventionist uses a catheter to mechanically retrieve and pull the clot out of the brain artery within 6 to 24 hours of onset.

#### Hindi
* **थ्रोम्बोलिसिस (Thrombolysis):** 4.5 घंटे के भीतर थक्के को पिघलाने वाला इंजेक्शन (Alteplase) लगाना।
* **मैकेनिकल थ्रोम्बेक्टोमी:** कैथेटर से थक्के को बाहर निकालना।

#### Hinglish
* **Thrombolysis Injection:** 4.5 ghante ke andar hospital pahunchne par doctor clot pighlane wala injection (Alteplase/Tenecteplase) veins me lagate hain.
* **Mechanical Thrombectomy:** Agar dimaag ki badi nali block ho, to vascular neurosurgeon catheter daal kar clot ko mechanically kheench kar bahar nikalte hain.

### For Hemorrhagic Stroke (Brain Bleed)

#### English
* Emergency blood pressure control to limit bleed growth. If the bleed is large, a neurosurgeon performs a craniotomy (opening the skull) to remove the blood clot and relieve brain pressure (decompression).

#### Hindi
* रक्तचाप (BP) को नियंत्रित करना। गंभीर मामलों में मस्तिष्क का दबाव कम करने के लिए क्रेनियोटॉमी (craniotomy) सर्जरी करना।

#### Hinglish
* Immediate BP control karna. Dimaag me high pressure se bachane ke liye surgeon skull (sir ki haddi) cut karke clot nikalte hain (Craniotomy surgery).

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories used in stroke emergencies:*
1. **Thrombolytics:** Dissolve fibrin clots in ischemic stroke (e.g., Alteplase, Tenecteplase).
2. **Antihypertensives (IV):** Lower blood pressure rapidly in emergencies (e.g., Labetalol, Nicardipine).
3. **Antiplatelets:** Prescribed only *after* CT scan confirms ischemic stroke (e.g., Aspirin, Clopidogrel).
4. **Osmotic Diuretics:** Reduce brain swelling (e.g., Mannitol, Hypertonic Saline).
5. **Neuroprotectants:** Protect brain cells from damage.

## Prevention of Stroke
* **Strict BP Control:** Hypertension is the leading cause of stroke. Keep blood pressure $< 130/80$ mmHg.
* **Manage Atrial Fibrillation:** Irregular heartbeat causes clots in the heart that travel to the brain. Take anticoagulants (Warfarin/Apixaban) as prescribed.
* **Stop Smoking & Limit Alcohol:** Smoking doubles ischemic stroke risk.
* **Low-Cholesterol Diet:** Keep LDL cholesterol $< 100$ mg/dL to prevent carotid artery plaques.

---

## Possible Complications of Stroke
* **Permanent Hemiplegia (Paralysis):** Complete loss of movement on one side of the body.
* **Aphasia:** Permanent difficulty speaking or understanding spoken language due to damage to the brain's speech centers (Broca/Wernicke areas).
* **Vascular Dementia:** Loss of cognitive function, memory, and reasoning power due to brain cell death.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Sudden drooping of one side of the face, weakness in one arm/leg, or slurred speech.**
* **A sudden, excruciatingly painful headache ("worst headache of life" - thunderclap headache).**
* **Sudden loss of balance, double vision, or unexplained confusion.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular Diseases and Stroke (NPCDCS):** Upgrades government district hospitals with "Stroke Units" and free CT scan facilities.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a brain stroke?**
   A stroke occurs when blood supply to a part of the brain is blocked (ischemic) or when a blood vessel in the brain bursts (hemorrhagic), causing brain cells to die within minutes.

2. **What does the FAST protocol stand for?**
   FAST is a quick stroke check: **F**ace drooping, **A**rm weakness, **S**peech difficulty, **T**ime to call 108.

3. **Why should I NOT give Aspirin to a suspected stroke patient at home?**
   You cannot distinguish between a clot stroke and a bleeding stroke without a CT scan. If the patient has a bleeding stroke (hemorrhagic), Aspirin will stop clotting and cause fatal brain bleeding.

4. **What is the "Golden Window" for stroke treatment?**
   The Golden Window is **4.5 hours** from the start of symptoms. Within this time, clot-busting injections (tPA) can be safely given to dissolve the clot and prevent permanent paralysis.

5. **What is a Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA)?**
   A TIA, or mini-stroke, is a temporary blockage of blood flow to the brain. Symptoms last only a few minutes or hours and resolve completely, but it is a warning sign of a major stroke.

6. **What is a hemorrhagic stroke?**
   It is a stroke caused by a ruptured blood vessel bleeding into the brain, commonly triggered by uncontrolled high blood pressure or an aneurysm.

7. **Why is a CT scan done immediately upon arrival at the hospital?**
   An emergency CT scan is required to rule out a brain bleed. If the CT scan shows no bleed (hemorrhage), the doctor can safely administer clot-busting thrombolytic drugs.

8. **Where can BPL cardholders get free stroke treatment in India?**
   Eligible patients can get free cashless stroke treatments, CT scans, ICU care, and brain surgeries under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme at all empaneled hospitals.

9. **What is mechanical thrombectomy?**
   It is an advanced catheter procedure done in a cath lab where a surgeon inserts a wire into the brain artery to physically catch and pull out a large blood clot.

10. **Why should stroke patients not drink water or eat food?**
    A stroke often paralyzes the throat muscles (dysphagia). Drinking or eating can cause liquids or pills to slip into the windpipe, causing severe choking and aspiration pneumonia.

11. **How does high blood pressure cause a stroke?**
    High blood pressure weakens the tiny blood vessels in the brain over years. Eventually, these weak vessels can rupture (hemorrhagic stroke) or promote plaque buildup that blocks blood (ischemic stroke).

12. **Why does Atrial Fibrillation increase stroke risk?**
    In AF, the heart's upper chambers quiver chaotically instead of pumping. This causes blood to pool and form clots, which can pump out of the heart and travel directly to block brain arteries.

13. **Can a person recover from paralysis after a stroke?**
    Yes. Immediate medical treatment combined with early, intensive physiotherapy and rehabilitation can help the brain relearn movements (neuroplasticity), restoring function over months.

14. **What is a "thunderclap headache"?**
    It is a sudden, extremely painful headache that peaks within 60 seconds, often described as the "worst headache of life," which is a key warning sign of a ruptured brain aneurysm.

15. **What is the price of generic Alteplase injection in India?**
    While branded Alteplase/tPA costs ₹30,000 to ₹40,000, under the government's NPCDCS program, it is provided completely free of cost in designated government stroke units.

16. **Why does brain swelling occur after a stroke?**
    Dead brain cells swell with fluid (cerebral edema). Doctors use osmotic drugs like **Mannitol** or perform surgery to temporarily remove a piece of the skull to prevent the swollen brain from being crushed.

17. **Can smoking double the risk of a stroke?**
    Yes. Smoking damages blood vessels, raises blood pressure, makes blood thicker, and accelerates plaque buildup in the neck arteries (carotids), doubling stroke risk.

18. **What is a carotid duplex ultrasound?**
    It is a non-invasive ultrasound of the neck arteries checking for cholesterol plaques that block blood flow to the brain, which is a major cause of stroke.

19. **What is aphasia?**
    Aphasia is a language disorder caused by stroke damage to the brain's communication centers, leaving the patient unable to speak clearly, find words, or understand spoken speech.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover post-stroke physiotherapy?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for hospital stay, acute stroke management, surgeries, and initial inpatient physical rehabilitation and speech therapy.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** A stroke is the same as a heart attack.
  * **Fact:** A heart attack is a blockage in the heart's blood supply. A stroke is a blockage or bleed in the brain's blood supply. They are different organs and require different first aid.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a stroke patient's finger and squeezing blood out will cure the stroke.
  * **Fact:** Squeezing fingers has no medical effect on a brain clot or bleed. This is a dangerous myth that wastes precious time. Call 108 immediately.
* **Myth:** Stroke only happens to people who are old or paralyzed.
  * **Fact:** Strokes can happen at any age, including children and young adults, due to genetic clotting disorders, drug abuse, or undetected aneurysms.
* **Myth:** If stroke symptoms go away after 10 minutes, you do not need to go to the hospital.
  * **Fact:** Temporary symptoms indicate a mini-stroke (TIA). A TIA carries a very high risk of a massive, permanent stroke within the next 48 hours. Seek immediate care.
* **Myth:** You should give the stroke patient a glass of hot tea or milk to comfort them.
  * **Fact:** Throat paralysis is common in stroke. Giving any liquids by mouth can cause the fluid to go into the lungs, causing suffocation. Keep them fasting.
* **Myth:** Tying a tight cloth around the head stops brain bleeding.
  * **Fact:** Head bandages cannot stop bleeding inside the skull. Only immediate clinical BP control and neurosurgery can treat brain hemorrhage.
* **Myth:** Stroke paralysis is always permanent and can never improve.
  * **Fact:** With early thrombolysis, proper medical care, and daily physical therapy, many patients recover significant movement and speech.
* **Myth:** Checking a stroke patient's pulse is the only way to diagnose a stroke.
  * **Fact:** A stroke patient's pulse can be completely normal. Diagnosis relies on the FAST protocol (face, arm, speech check) and a brain CT scan.
* **Myth:** Stroke is contagious and can spread by touching a paralyzed person.
  * **Fact:** Stroke is not an infectious disease and is not contagious. It is a vascular brain emergency.
* **Myth:** If the patient has a stroke, massage their head with hot mustard oil.
  * **Fact:** Massaging has no effect on brain clots. It delays reaching the hospital, causing more brain cells to die. Proceed to the hospital immediately.
