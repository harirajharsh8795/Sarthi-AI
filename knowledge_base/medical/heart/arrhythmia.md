# Arrhythmia (Irregular Heartbeat) ki Pehchan, Prakar aur Upachar (Heart Rhythm and Palpitations Guide)

## Overview

### English
Cardiac Arrhythmia is a group of clinical conditions characterized by an abnormal or irregular heartbeat—either too fast (**Tachycardia**, rest heart rate $> 100$ bpm), too slow (**Bradycardia**, rest heart rate $< 60$ bpm), or completely chaotic (**Atrial Fibrillation** or **Ventricular Fibrillation**). Arrhythmias occur due to malfunctions in the heart's electrical conduction system. While some arrhythmias are harmless, others like Ventricular Fibrillation (VF) are directly life-threatening, causing sudden cardiac arrest. Diagnosis relies heavily on Electrocardiography (ECG), 24-hour Holter monitoring, and Electrophysiological Studies (EPS).

## Quick Summary
An arrhythmia is an irregular heartbeat. The primary types are tachycardia (fast), bradycardia (slow), and fibrillation (chaotic). Symptoms include palpitations (dhadkan mehsus hona), dizziness, chest pain, and fainting. Diagnostics use ECG and 24-hour Holter monitoring. Management involves antiarrhythmic medications (Amiodarone), pacemakers, and cardioversion.

---

## Definition
* **Arrhythmia:** Any deviation from the normal digital sinus rhythm of the heartbeat, including abnormalities of rate, regularity, or site of origin of impulses.
* **Atrial Fibrillation (AF):** A common supraventricular arrhythmia characterized by rapid, chaotic electrical signals in the atria, leading to poor blood flow and a high risk of blood clots and stroke.
* **Pacemaker:** A small, surgically implanted electronic device that monitors and sends electrical impulses to the heart muscle to maintain a normal heart rate in bradycardia.

---

## Pathology & Classification of Arrhythmias
Malfunction in the Sinoatrial (SA) node or conducting fibers causes rhythm errors:
1. **SA Node Malfunction:** The SA node is the natural pacemaker. If it fires too slowly (Sick Sinus Syndrome) or blocks signals (AV Block), bradycardia occurs.
2. **Re-entry Pathways:** Extra electrical loops in the heart tissue override the SA node, causing rapid firing (Tachycardia / SVT).
3. **Major Types of Arrhythmias:**
   * **Sinus Tachycardia:** Fast heart rate ($>100$ bpm) due to fever, exercise, or anxiety.
   * **Atrial Fibrillation (AF):** Chaotic quivering of atria. **AF raises stroke risk by 5 times due to blood pooling and clotting in the left atrium.**
   * **Ventricular Fibrillation (VF):** Chaotic quivering of ventricles. Zero blood is pumped. *Causes instant collapse and cardiac arrest.*

### English

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Electrocardiogram (ECG):** The primary diagnostic tool. Shows the electrical waves of the heart (P-QRS-T segments).
* **Holter Monitor (24-48 Hour):** A portable ECG device worn by the patient for 24-48 hours to record heart activity during normal daily routines, capturing silent or occasional arrhythmias.
* **Electrophysiology Study (EPS):** An invasive catheter-based study where electrodes are passed into the heart to map its electrical pathways and trigger/identify the source of abnormal rhythms.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to cardiac rhythm management:*
1. **Class III Antiarrhythmics:** Block potassium channels to prolong cardiac action potential (e.g., Amiodarone).
2. **Beta-Blockers:** Slow heart rate by blocking adrenaline (e.g., Metoprolol, Bisoprolol).
3. **Calcium Channel Blockers:** Slow conduction through the AV node (e.g., Verapamil, Diltiazem).
4. **Anticoagulants (Blood Thinners):** Mandatory in Atrial Fibrillation to prevent stroke (e.g., Warfarin, Apixaban, Dabigatran).
5. **Cardiotonic Glycosides:** Slow AV conduction and increase heart contractility (e.g., Digoxin).

## Prevention of Arrhythmia Episodes
* **Avoid Stimulants:** Limit caffeine (coffee, energy drinks) and avoid alcohol, nicotine, and recreational drugs, which irritate the heart's electrical system.
* **Manage Stress:** Practice yoga, meditation, and deep breathing.
* **Maintain Electrolyte Balance:** Keep potassium, magnesium, and calcium levels normal in blood (eat green vegetables, bananas).
* **Treat Underlying Heart Disease:** Manage high BP, diabetes, and coronary artery blockages.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Arrhythmias
* **Ischemic Stroke:** Blood clots forming in the quivering left atrium of AF patients traveling to the brain, causing paralysis.
* **Sudden Cardiac Death:** Ventricular fibrillation stopping blood flow completely.
* **Tachycardia-Induced Cardiomyopathy:** Persistent high heart rate weakening the heart muscle, leading to heart failure.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Sudden collapse, loss of consciousness, and cessation of normal breathing (VF cardiac arrest).**
* **Palpitations accompanied by chest pain, severe dizziness, or difficulty breathing.**
* **A resting pulse rate consistently above 140 bpm or below 40 bpm, with confusion or extreme fatigue.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is cardiac arrhythmia?**
   Arrhythmia is a condition where the heart beats with an irregular, abnormally fast (tachycardia), or abnormally slow (bradycardia) rhythm due to electrical conduction issues.

2. **What are palpitations?**
   Palpitations are the physical sensation of feeling your heart pounding, fluttering, or racing in your chest, neck, or throat, which can be a sign of arrhythmia.

3. **What is Atrial Fibrillation (AF) and why is it dangerous?**
   AF is a chaotic, rapid quivering of the heart's upper chambers (atria). It is dangerous because blood pools in the atria, forming clots that can travel to the brain and cause an ischemic stroke.

4. **What is a pacemaker and how does it help?**
   A pacemaker is a small electronic device surgically implanted under the chest skin. It monitors the heart rate and sends electrical signals to make it beat at a normal rate if it goes too slow.

5. **What is a Holter Monitor?**
   A Holter monitor is a small, portable ECG device that you wear for 24 to 48 hours to continuously record your heart's electrical rhythm during daily activities.

6. **Why does hyperkalemia (high potassium) cause arrhythmias?**
   Potassium regulates the electrical charge of heart muscle cells. High blood potassium levels disrupt these charges, leading to slow AV blocks or sudden fatal ventricular fibrillation.

7. **Where can BPL cardholders get a free pacemaker in India?**
   Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, eligible BPL cardholders can get free cashless surgeries for permanent pacemaker implantation at empaneled hospitals.

8. **What is cardiac ablation?**
   Cardiac ablation is a catheter-based procedure where a doctor uses heat (radiofrequency) or cold (cryoablation) to destroy the tiny areas of heart tissue causing the abnormal electrical signals.

9. **Can caffeine and alcohol trigger arrhythmias?**
   Yes. Excess caffeine, energy drinks, nicotine, and alcohol act as cardiac stimulants, irritating the heart muscle and triggering premature beats or atrial fibrillation.

10. **What is the difference between tachycardia and bradycardia?**
    Tachycardia is a resting heart rate above 100 beats per minute. Bradycardia is a resting heart rate below 60 beats per minute.

11. **Why do patients with Atrial Fibrillation need blood thinners?**
    Blood thinners (anticoagulants) prevent blood clots from forming in the quivering left atrium, reducing the risk of stroke.

12. **What is an ICD (Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator)?**
    An ICD is an advanced implantable device that continuously monitors heart rhythm. If it detects a fatal rhythm like VF, it automatically delivers an internal electrical shock to save the patient’s life.

13. **Can anxiety cause an irregular heartbeat?**
    Yes. Anxiety and panic attacks release adrenaline, which naturally increases heart rate and can cause harmless premature atrial or ventricular contractions (ectopics).

14. **How do doctors check for arrhythmias in a clinic?**
    Diagnosis starts with a physical pulse check, followed by an ECG. If the arrhythmia is occasional, a Holter monitor or loop recorder is used.

15. **What is the price of generic Amiodarone tablets in India?**
    Generic Amiodarone (200mg) tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹15 to ₹25 per strip of 10 tablets.

16. **Why does sudden thyroid dysfunction cause tachycardia?**
    Hyperthyroidism (excess thyroid hormone) increases the sensitivity of the heart to adrenaline, leading to sinus tachycardia or atrial fibrillation.

17. **What is cardioversion?**
    Cardioversion is a medical procedure where a controlled electrical shock or medication is used to reset an abnormally fast heart rate back to a normal rhythm.

18. **Is a resting heart rate of 52 bpm always dangerous?**
    Not always. In well-conditioned athletes, a slow heart rate of 40-50 bpm is normal because their heart muscle is strong and pumps blood efficiently.

19. **What is SVT (Supraventricular Tachycardia)?**
    SVT is a rapid heart rate (often 150-250 bpm) originating in the chambers above the ventricles, causing sudden episodes of palpitations and lightheadedness.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover cardiac ablation procedures?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for electrophysiology studies (EPS) and cardiac ablation therapy.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** If you feel palpitations, you are having a massive heart attack.
  * **Fact:** Palpitations can be caused by anxiety, caffeine, dehydration, or simple arrhythmias. They do not necessarily mean you are having a heart attack.
* **Myth:** Pacemaker patients cannot use microwave ovens or mobile phones.
  * **Fact:** Modern pacemakers are shielded against household microwave radiation. Mobile phones are safe but should be kept at least 6 inches away from the pacemaker (use opposite ear).
* **Myth:** Arrhythmia is only a disease of old age.
  * **Fact:** Young adults and children can have congenital electrical pathway issues, leading to SVT or channelopathies like Long QT syndrome.
* **Myth:** You can cure Atrial Fibrillation completely with garlic and ginger juices.
  * **Fact:** Garlic has minor vascular benefits but cannot stop chaotic AF signals or prevent stroke. AF requires proper antiarrhythmics and blood thinners.
* **Myth:** A slow heart rate is always a sign of a very healthy heart.
  * **Fact:** A resting heart rate below 60 is healthy for athletes, but in elderly individuals, it can indicate a blocked electrical node (AV block) requiring a pacemaker.
* **Myth:** If my ECG is normal, I definitely do not have an arrhythmia.
  * **Fact:** A standard ECG only records the heart for 10 seconds. If your arrhythmia happens occasionally, it will be missed. You need a 24-hour Holter monitor.
* **Myth:** Pacemakers must be replaced every year.
  * **Fact:** Modern pacemaker batteries last between 7 to 15 years, after which a minor surgical procedure is done to replace the battery unit.
* **Myth:** Blood thinners like aspirin and warfarin will dissolve existing blockages.
  * **Fact:** Blood thinners prevent new blood clots from forming. They do not dissolve the cholesterol plaques causing artery blockages.
* **Myth:** Cardioversion electrical shock is highly painful and dangerous.
  * **Fact:** Cardioversion is done under light sedation, so the patient feels no pain. It is a highly safe, controlled clinical procedure.
* **Myth:** Taking daily multivitamins will prevent all arrhythmias.
  * **Fact:** Multivitamins cannot repair electrical conduction damage. Specific mineral balance (potassium/magnesium) is key, but must be managed under medical advice.
