# Atherosclerosis (Artery Blockage) ki Pehchan, Kaaran aur Upachar (Arterial Plaque and Cholesterol Guide)

## Overview

### English
Atherosclerosis is a chronic, progressive vascular disease characterized by the narrowing and hardening of arteries due to the buildup of fatty plaques. These plaques are composed of cholesterol, lipids, cellular waste products, calcium, and fibrin (plaque). As the plaque grows, it restricts the flow of oxygen-rich blood to vital organs. Atherosclerosis is the primary cause of Coronary Artery Disease (CAD, causing angina and heart attacks), Carotid Artery Disease (causing stroke), and Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD, causing leg pain while walking). Major risk factors include high LDL cholesterol, hypertension, diabetes, smoking, and lack of exercise.

## Quick Summary
Atherosclerosis is the buildup of plaques in arteries. It limits blood flow. Key indicators are high LDL cholesterol ($> 100$ mg/dL) and arterial calcium. Symptoms depend on the affected organ (angina for heart, leg pain for limbs, numbness for brain). Diagnostics use lipid profile scans, CT calcium scoring, and angiography. Management involves statin medications, low-fat diets, exercise, and stenting.

---

## Definition
* **Atherosclerosis:** Hardening and narrowing of the arteries due to lipid and calcium-rich plaque deposition in the tunica intima (inner lining) of arteries.
* **Plaque:** A sticky deposit of fat, cholesterol, calcium, and necrotic cells that adheres to the arterial wall.
* **Ischemia:** An inadequate blood supply to an organ or part of the body, especially the heart muscles, caused by arterial blockages.

---

## Pathology of Plaque Formation & Progression
Plaque grows over decades through endothelial injury:
1. **Endothelial Injury:** High blood pressure, smoking, or high blood sugar damages the delicate inner lining (endothelium) of the artery.
2. **LDL Infiltration:** Bad cholesterol (LDL) enters the damaged lining and becomes oxidized.
3. **Foam Cell Creation:** White blood cells (macrophages) rush to clean up the LDL, eat too much fat, and turn into dead "foam cells," which settle on the artery wall.
4. **Plaque Hardening:** Calcium deposits settle on the fat, turning it hard.
5. **Plaque Rupture:** If the fibrous cap of the plaque rips open, a blood clot forms instantly on top of it, completely blocking the artery and triggering a **Heart Attack** or **Stroke**.

## Symptoms of Atherosclerosis (Based on Affected Organs)

### English
* **In Heart Arteries (CAD):** Squeezing chest pain (angina) during exercise; shortness of breath; fatigue.
* **In Brain Arteries (Carotid):** Sudden weakness or numbness in arms/legs; slurred speech; drooping face; temporary vision loss (TIA/Stroke).
* **In Leg Arteries (PAD):** Sharp pain or cramping in the calves or thighs while walking, which resolves with rest (intermittent claudication).

### Hindi
* **हृदय धमनियों में (CAD):** शारीरिक श्रम के दौरान छाती में दर्द (angina); सांस फूलना।
* **मस्तिष्क धमनियों में (Carotid):** हाथ-पैर में अचानक कमजोरी या सुन्नता; बोलने में कठिनाई (Stroke)।
* **पैरों की धमनियों में (PAD):** चलने पर पिंडलियों या जांघों में तेज दर्द और ऐंठन (claudication)।

## First Aid & Emergency Protocol

### For Suspected Plaque Rupture (Heart Attack or Stroke Emergency)

#### English
1. **Check for stroke** using FAST (Face, Arms, Speech, Time) or **Heart Attack** (chest pain + cold sweating).
2. **Do not give solid foods.**
3. **If Heart Attack:** Administer one tablet of chewable **Aspirin (300mg)** immediately.
4. Call **108** for emergency cardiac transport.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Fasting Lipid Profile:** Measures Total Cholesterol, LDL (Bad), HDL (Good), and Triglycerides. **LDL $> 130$ mg/dL is a high risk factor.**
* **CT Calcium Scoring:** A non-invasive CT scan checking the amount of calcified plaque in the heart's arteries, giving a "Agatston Score" (Score $> 400$ indicates high risk of heart event).
* **Duplex Ultrasound (Carotid):** Uses sound waves to measure the thickness of carotid artery walls and check blood flow to the brain.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to plaque stabilization and cholesterol management:*
1. **HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitors (Statins):** Block cholesterol synthesis in the liver, lower LDL, and stabilize plaques to prevent rupture (e.g., Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin).
2. **Antiplatelets (Blood Thinners):** Prevent blood clots on plaques (e.g., Aspirin, Clopidogrel).
3. **Cholesterol Absorption Inhibitors:** Reduce gut absorption of cholesterol (e.g., Ezetimibe).
4. **PCSK9 Inhibitors:** Injectable monoclonal antibodies for severe cholesterol lowering (e.g., Alirocumab, Evolocumab).
5. **Vasodilators:** Relax blood vessels to improve flow (e.g., Nitrates).

---

## Prevention of Plaque Buildup
* **Maintain LDL Targets:** Keep LDL $< 100$ mg/dL (and strictly $< 70$ mg/dL if you already have coronary artery disease).
* **Quit Tobacco:** Smoking causes direct chemical damage to the vascular endothelium, speeding up lipid deposits.
* **Low-Cholesterol Diet:** Avoid trans fats, fried foods, red meat, and palm oil. Eat high-fiber foods (oats, legumes).
* **Daily Activity:** Walk briskly for 30-40 minutes daily.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Atherosclerosis
* **Acute Myocardial Infarction:** Complete blockage of a coronary artery, destroying heart muscle.
* **Ischemic Stroke:** Complete blockage of a brain artery, causing permanent brain tissue death and paralysis.
* **Critical Limb Ischemia:** Blocked leg arteries leading to gangrene (rotting toes) requiring amputation.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Crushing chest pain radiating to left arm/neck, with cold sweating and breathlessness.**
* **Sudden weakness in one side of the face/body, slurred speech, or difficulty speaking.**
* **A toe or foot that suddenly turns cold, pale, numb, or blue, with severe resting pain (acute limb blockage).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is atherosclerosis?**
   Atherosclerosis is a disease where fatty deposits called plaques (made of cholesterol, calcium, and fat) build up inside the arteries, narrowing them and restricting blood flow.

2. **What is the difference between arteriosclerosis and atherosclerosis?**
   Arteriosclerosis is the general term for the hardening and loss of elasticity of arteries. Atherosclerosis is a specific type of arteriosclerosis caused by plaque buildup.

3. **What is LDL cholesterol and why is it called "bad"?**
   LDL (Low-Density Lipoprotein) is called bad cholesterol because excess LDL carries cholesterol into the artery walls, where it deposits and forms plaques.

4. **How do statin drugs help in atherosclerosis?**
   Statins lower LDL cholesterol production in the liver. More importantly, they "stabilize" existing plaques, making their fibrous caps stronger so they do not rupture and cause a heart attack.

5. **Can atherosclerosis blockages be reversed?**
   While blockages cannot be 100% dissolved naturally, intensive statin therapy, a strict low-fat diet, and lifestyle changes can shrink plaques slightly and halt their progression.

6. **What is CT Calcium Scoring?**
   It is a quick, non-invasive CT scan that measures the amount of calcified plaque in your coronary arteries. A higher score means a higher risk of a heart attack.

7. **Where can BPL cardholders get free bypass surgery in India?**
   Eligible patients can get free cashless bypass surgery (CABG) or angioplasty at all government and empaneled private hospitals under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme.

8. **Why is smoking a major risk factor for artery blockages?**
   Cigarette smoke contains toxins that chemically damage the endothelium (artery lining), making it sticky and easy for LDL cholesterol to collect and form plaques.

9. **What is Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD)?**
   PAD is a type of atherosclerosis where arteries in the legs are blocked, causing sharp leg cramps or pain when walking (claudication) due to lack of blood.

10. **What happens when a plaque ruptures?**
    When a plaque ruptures, the body treats it as an injury and forms a blood clot on top of it. This clot can completely block the artery in seconds, causing a heart attack or stroke.

11. **How does high blood pressure accelerate atherosclerosis?**
    High blood pressure puts constant physical stress on the artery walls, causing tiny tears in the endothelial lining where cholesterol can easily accumulate.

12. **Why are blood thinners like Aspirin prescribed to plaque patients?**
    If a plaque begins to crack or rupture, Aspirin stops platelets from sticking together to form a large blood clot, preventing a complete blockage of the artery.

13. **Can a person have atherosclerosis without any symptoms?**
    Yes. Atherosclerosis develops silently over decades. A person may have 50-60% blockages without any symptoms until a plaque ruptures or blockages reach 70% or more.

14. **What is the target LDL cholesterol level for heart patients?**
    For individuals with diagnosed heart disease, the target LDL level is strictly **below 70 mg/dL** (and under 55 mg/dL for very high-risk patients) to prevent new events.

15. **What is the price of generic Atorvastatin in India?**
    Generic Atorvastatin (10mg/20mg) tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹18 per strip, compared to ₹70+ for branded versions.

16. **Why does diabetes speed up artery clogging?**
    High blood sugar levels damage endothelial cells and cause LDL particles to become smaller and denser, making them more likely to enter and clog artery walls.

17. **What is Carotid Artery Disease?**
    It is the narrowing of the carotid arteries in the neck that supply blood to the brain. Blockage here can cut off blood flow, causing a stroke.

18. **Can eating garlic dissolve artery plaques?**
    No. Garlic has minor blood pressure-lowering and antioxidant benefits, but there is no scientific evidence that it can dissolve or clear existing cholesterol plaques.

19. **What is the difference between angioplasty and bypass surgery?**
    Angioplasty uses a balloon and wire mesh (stent) to push the plaque aside and open the artery from inside. Bypass surgery (CABG) uses a grafted vein to bypass the blocked segment.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover carotid artery surgeries?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for carotid endarterectomy (surgical plaque removal) and carotid stenting to prevent stroke.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Artery blockages only happen to people who eat pure ghee and butter.
  * **Fact:** Ghee raises cholesterol, but genetics, smoking, high BP, diabetes, and eating trans fats (vanaspati oil, bakery items) are equally responsible for plaque.
* **Myth:** Drinking apple cider vinegar daily will dissolve all cholesterol plaques.
  * **Fact:** Vinegar cannot dissolve plaques. Plaques are locked inside the artery walls under a fibrous cap. Only statins and lifestyle changes can manage them.
* **Myth:** If you are thin and have a normal weight, your arteries cannot be blocked.
  * **Fact:** Thin people can have high LDL cholesterol due to genetics (Familial Hypercholesterolemia) or smoking, leading to severe atherosclerosis.
* **Myth:** High cholesterol is the only cause of artery blockage.
  * **Fact:** Cholesterol is the building block, but plaque only forms if the artery lining is damaged by smoking, diabetes, or high blood pressure first.
* **Myth:** Once you start taking cholesterol tablets (statins), you can eat as much junk food as you want.
  * **Fact:** Statins cannot compensate for a terrible diet. Continuous high-fat intake will overpower the medicine, causing blockages to grow.
* **Myth:** You can completely clear blocked arteries in 15 days using alternative herbal mixtures.
  * **Fact:** Plaque formation takes 20-30 years. There is no medical or alternative cure that can clear it in weeks. Avoid fake claims.
* **Myth:** Stents never get blocked again.
  * **Fact:** Stents can get blocked again (restenosis) if the patient stops taking blood thinners, continues smoking, or does not control their diabetes.
* **Myth:** Only men get atherosclerosis.
  * **Fact:** Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of death in both men and women. Women's risk rises rapidly after menopause due to loss of estrogen protection.
* **Myth:** All chest pain is acid gas reflux.
  * **Fact:** Many heart attacks are misdiagnosed as gas, leading to death. Get any crushing or exertional chest pain checked immediately by ECG.
* **Myth:** Rock salt is 100% safe and does not damage arteries.
  * **Fact:** Rock salt contains sodium which raises blood pressure, causing endothelial tears and promoting plaque growth. Limit all salts.
