# Hridaya ki Sanrachna, Bimariyan aur Upachar (Heart Anatomy, Diseases, and Care)

## Overview

### English
The heart is a muscular organ located in the chest, slightly to the left of the midline. It acts as the central pump of the cardiovascular system, continuously circulating blood throughout the body to deliver oxygen and nutrients while removing metabolic waste. The heart is susceptible to various conditions, including Coronary Artery Disease (CAD), heart attacks, angina, heart failure, and arrhythmias. Maintaining cardiovascular health requires a balanced diet, exercise, and strict control of blood pressure and cholesterol.

## Quick Summary
The heart pumps blood through four chambers (atria and ventricles) and four valves. Key diseases include Coronary Artery Disease (blocked arteries), Heart Attack (tissue death from lack of blood), Angina (exertion chest pain), Heart Failure (weak pumping), and Arrhythmia (unstable rhythm). Symptoms include chest pain, shortness of breath, and sweating. Diagnostic tests include ECG, Echocardiogram, and Angiography. Preventive care focuses on reducing salt, exercising, and avoiding tobacco.

---

## Definition
* **Human Heart:** A hollow, cone-shaped muscular organ that pumps blood through the circulatory system by rhythmic contraction and dilation.
* **Coronary Artery Disease (CAD):** The narrowing or blockage of the coronary arteries, usually caused by plaque buildup (atherosclerosis), which restricts blood flow to the heart muscle.
* **Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack):** Permanent damage to a portion of the heart muscle caused by a complete blockage of coronary blood flow.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The heart's anatomy is divided into chambers, valves, and blood vessels:
1. **Four Chambers:** 
   * **Left and Right Atria (Upper Chambers):** Receive incoming blood.
   * **Left and Right Ventricles (Lower Chambers):** Pump blood out of the heart. The left ventricle is the thickest chamber as it pumps blood to the entire body.
2. **Four Valves (Tricuspid, Pulmonary, Mitral, Aortic):** Act as one-way gates to prevent the backward flow of blood.
3. **Coronary Arteries:** The network of blood vessels surrounding the heart that supply oxygen-rich blood directly to the heart muscles.

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Heart Diseases
* **Atherosclerosis:** Buildup of fats, cholesterol, and other substances (plaque) in and on artery walls, restricting blood flow.
* **Ischemia:** Reduced blood flow to the heart muscle, depriving it of necessary oxygen.

### Secondary & Genetic Factors
* Family history of early heart disease.
* Congenital heart defects (structural heart issues present from birth).

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** Smoking and tobacco use, high salt and saturated fat diet, physical inactivity, and obesity.
* **Clinical Conditions:** Chronic high blood pressure (hypertension), diabetes, and high LDL cholesterol levels.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** Mild chest discomfort during exertion, shortness of breath while walking, easy fatigue.
* **Common Symptoms:** Tightness, pressure, or squeezing pain in the center of the chest (angina); pain radiating to the left shoulder, arm, neck, or jaw; excessive unexplained sweating (diaphoresis).
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Persistent shortness of breath even at rest, swelling in the ankles, feet, or abdomen (signs of heart failure), irregular or racing heartbeat (palpitations).
* **Emergency Symptoms:** Sudden, crushing chest pain lasting more than a few minutes, severe difficulty breathing, dizziness, lightheadedness, or loss of consciousness.

## Diagnosis

### Medical History & Physical Exam
* Evaluation of risk factors (smoking, family history), checking blood pressure, and listening to heart sounds using a stethoscope to detect murmurs.

### Electrocardiogram (ECG / EKG)
* Records the electrical signals of the heart. Used to detect heart attacks, rhythm abnormalities, and signs of previous heart damage.

### Echocardiogram (Echo)
* An ultrasound of the heart that provides real-time images of the heart chambers, valves, and pumping efficiency (Ejection Fraction - EF).

### Cardiac Angiography
* A test that uses dye and special X-rays to look inside the coronary arteries to find the exact location and severity of blockages.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **Antiplatelets (Blood Thinners):** Prevent blood clots from forming in coronary arteries (e.g., Aspirin, Clopidogrel).
2. **Statins (Cholesterol-Lowering Drugs):** Reduce LDL cholesterol and stabilize plaque inside arteries (e.g., Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin).
3. **Beta-Blockers:** Lower heart rate and blood pressure, reducing the workload on the heart (e.g., Metoprolol, Atenolol).
4. **ACE Inhibitors & ARBs:** Relax blood vessels to lower blood pressure and protect heart structure (e.g., Ramipril, Telmisartan).
5. **Nitrates (Vasodilators):** Relieve chest pain by widening coronary blood vessels (e.g., Sorbitrate/Isosorbide Dinitrate).

## Prevention

### English
* **Diet:** Restrict daily salt intake to under 5 grams (1 teaspoon). Avoid trans-fats, processed foods, and reuse of frying oil. Eat fresh fruits and vegetables.
* **Exercise:** Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity physical activity (like brisk walking) per week.
* **Tobacco Cessation:** Completely stop smoking and chewing tobacco, as it damages artery walls and doubles the risk of heart attacks.

## Possible Complications
* **Cardiac Arrest:** Sudden loss of heart function, breathing, and consciousness, caused by an electrical disturbance in the heart.
* **Chronic Heart Failure:** Progressive weakening of the heart, leading to fluid accumulation in the lungs and body tissues.
* **Stroke:** Clots traveling from the heart to the brain, causing brain tissue damage.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Crushing chest pain or pressure lasting more than 5 minutes.**
* **Chest pain radiating to the left arm, neck, or jaw accompanied by cold sweat.**
* **Sudden severe shortness of breath or dizziness/fainting.**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you experience chest pain, heaviness, or burning sensation during walking or climbing stairs.
* For regular screening of blood pressure, blood sugar, and lipid profile if you are over 30 years of age.
* Before starting any heavy exercise routine if you have a family history of heart disease.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a heart attack?**
   A heart attack occurs when blood flow to a part of the heart is completely blocked, leading to the death of heart muscle cells.

2. **Is chest pain always a sign of a heart attack?**
   No, chest pain can also be caused by acidity, muscle strain, or lung issues. However, cardiac chest pain usually increases with exertion and is accompanied by sweating or shortness of breath.

3. **What is the difference between Angina and a Heart Attack?**
   Angina is temporary chest pain caused by reduced blood flow during exertion, which resolves with rest. A heart attack is a complete blockage where pain does not go away with rest and causes permanent muscle damage.

4. **What is Ejection Fraction (EF) in an Echo report?**
   Ejection Fraction measures the percentage of blood pumped out of the left ventricle with each contraction. A normal EF ranges from 50% to 70%. Below 40% suggests heart failure.

5. **Can high blood pressure cause heart failure?**
   Yes. Chronic high blood pressure forces the heart to pump harder against high resistance, causing the heart muscles to thicken and eventually weaken.

6. **What is an Angioplasty?**
   Angioplasty is a minimally invasive procedure where a doctor inserts a catheter with a balloon and a metal mesh tube (stent) to open a blocked coronary artery.

7. **How does smoking damage the heart?**
   Tobacco smoke contains chemicals that damage the lining of blood vessels, promote plaque buildup, and cause blood vessels to constrict, raising blood pressure.

8. **What is sorbitrate and when is it used?**
   Sorbitrate (Isosorbide Dinitrate) is a medicine placed under the tongue to quickly widen blood vessels and relieve acute angina chest pain.

9. **Can diabetes increase the risk of heart disease?**
   Yes, high blood sugar damages blood vessels and the nerves that control the heart, significantly increasing the risk of heart attacks.

10. **What is a Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG) surgery?**
    Commonly called Bypass Surgery, CABG uses healthy blood vessels from another part of the body (leg or chest) to bypass blocked coronary arteries and restore blood flow.

11. **What is a cardiac arrest?**
    Cardiac arrest is an electrical malfunction of the heart that causes it to suddenly stop beating altogether, requiring immediate CPR.

12. **What should I do if someone collapses from suspected cardiac arrest?**
    Immediately call 108, start CPR (chest compressions), and look for an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) if available.

13. **Why is reusing frying oil bad for the heart?**
    Reheating oil repeatedly creates trans-fatty acids and harmful chemical compounds that raise LDL (bad) cholesterol and block arteries.

14. **What is the normal blood pressure range?**
    The normal blood pressure range for a healthy adult is below 120/80 mmHg.

15. **What are statins?**
    Statins are a class of medications that help lower LDL (bad) cholesterol levels in the blood and prevent plaque buildup inside arteries.

16. **Why do doctors recommend Aspirin during a suspected heart attack?**
    Aspirin acts as an antiplatelet drug that prevents blood clots from growing larger, helping maintain some blood flow through the blocked artery.

17. **Can stress trigger a heart attack?**
    Yes, a sudden severe psychological shock or physical strain can rupture a plaque and cause a blockage.

18. **How does salt intake affect blood pressure?**
    Excess salt causes the body to retain water, increasing the volume of blood in circulation and raising blood pressure against blood vessel walls.

19. **What is an ECG test?**
    An Electrocardiogram (ECG) is a quick, non-invasive test that measures the electrical activity of the heart to check for rhythm issues or active damage.

20. **Can heart disease be reversed?**
    While damaged heart muscle cannot easily recover, strict control of cholesterol, blood pressure, healthy diet, and lifestyle changes can stabilize and stop the progression of blockages.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Heart attack and cardiac arrest are the same thing.
  * **Fact:** A heart attack is a circulation problem (blocked blood vessel); a cardiac arrest is an electrical problem (heart stops beating).
* **Myth:** Only elderly people need to worry about heart attacks.
  * **Fact:** Heart attacks are rising in young Indian adults due to stress, bad diet, smoking, and lack of exercise.
* **Myth:** If I take cholesterol medication, I can eat anything I want.
  * **Fact:** Medications only manage cholesterol; a high-fat diet will still build blockages and increase heart risk.
* **Myth:** All chest pain on the left side is a heart attack.
  * **Fact:** Acidity, muscle strain, or lung infections can also cause left-side chest pain. Testing is necessary to confirm.
* **Myth:** Heart disease is strictly hereditary; if my parents are healthy, I am safe.
  * **Fact:** Family history is a risk factor, but lifestyle choices (smoking, poor diet, obesity) are the primary causes of heart disease.
* **Myth:** Women are safe from heart attacks.
  * **Fact:** Cardiovascular disease is a leading cause of death in women, especially post-menopause.
* **Myth:** Exercising hard can cure heart blockages.
  * **Fact:** Moderate exercise is healthy, but sudden high-intensity exertion in a person with blockages can trigger a heart attack.
* **Myth:** You should lie down and rest during a heart attack.
  * **Fact:** Lying down makes breathing harder. Keep the person sitting upright, loosen tight clothing, and call 108 immediately.
* **Myth:** Red wine is a proven cure for preventing heart attacks.
  * **Fact:** There is no clinical evidence that drinking alcohol cures or prevents heart diseases; alcohol abuse increases heart failure risk.
* **Myth:** A person who survives a heart attack should never exercise again.
  * **Fact:** Regular, doctor-supervised cardiac rehabilitation and walking are essential for recovery and strengthening the heart.
