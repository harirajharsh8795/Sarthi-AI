# Kankal Tantra ki Sanrachna, Bimariyan aur Dekhbhal (Skeleton Anatomy, Diseases, and Bone Care)

## Overview

### English
The human skeleton is the internal framework of the body, composed of 206 bones in an adult. It provides structural shape, protects vital internal organs, enables movement in coordination with muscles, stores essential minerals (calcium and phosphorus), and produces blood cells within the bone marrow. The skeletal system is vulnerable to diseases such as Osteoporosis (decreased bone density), Osteoarthritis (cartilage wear-and-tear), Fractures, Osteomyelitis (bone infection), and Rickets (soft bones due to Vitamin D deficiency). Maintaining skeletal health requires a diet rich in calcium and Vitamin D, regular weight-bearing exercises, and proper posture.

## Quick Summary
The adult skeleton contains 206 bones divided into axial and appendicular parts. Key skeletal disorders include Osteoporosis (weak bones prone to fractures), Osteoarthritis (joint cartilage degeneration), and Fractures. Symptoms include bone pain, localized swelling, joint stiffness, deformity, and loss of height. Diagnostics include X-rays, CT scans, and DEXA bone density scans. Prevention focuses on calcium, Vitamin D3, sunlight exposure, and weight-bearing exercises.

---

## Definition
* **Human Skeleton:** The structural framework of bones and cartilage that supports and protects the human body.
* **Osteoporosis:** A systemic skeletal disease characterized by low bone mass and micro-architectural deterioration of bone tissue, leading to increased bone fragility and fracture susceptibility.
* **Osteoarthritis:** A degenerative joint disease characterized by breakdown of articular cartilage and changes in subchondral bone, causing joint pain and stiffness.

---

## Anatomy / Background
The human skeleton is divided into two primary sections:
1. **Axial Skeleton (80 bones):** Formed along the body's central axis. Includes the skull (protecting the brain), vertebral column (spine protecting the spinal cord), and rib cage (protecting the heart and lungs).
2. **Appendicular Skeleton (126 bones):** Formed of limbs and girdles. Includes the pectoral girdle (shoulders), pelvic girdle (hips), and the bones of the arms, hands, legs, and feet.
3. **Bone Structure:** Bones consist of a hard outer compact layer, a spongy inner cancellous layer, and a central cavity containing **bone marrow** (where red and white blood cells are synthesized).

## Causes

### Primary Causes of Skeletal Diseases
* **Hormonal Changes:** Drop in estrogen levels in women post-menopause, causing rapid calcium loss from bones (Post-menopausal Osteoporosis).
* **Cartilage Degeneration:** Mechanical wear and tear over time wearing down the protective cartilage at the ends of bones (Osteoarthritis).
* **Nutritional Deficiency:** Inadequate intake of Calcium and Vitamin D3, preventing the body from hardening bone matrices (Rickets/Osteomalacia).

### Secondary & Infectious Factors
* Bacterial invasion (*Staphylococcus aureus*) reaching bone tissues through blood or open wounds, causing bone decay (Osteomyelitis).
* Severe physical trauma or sudden mechanical impact exceeding bone strength, causing a break (Fracture).

### Risk Factors
* **Lifestyle:** Lack of weight-bearing exercise (causing bone mass loss), high consumption of carbonated sodas, heavy smoking, and lack of sunlight exposure.
* **Clinical:** Long-term use of steroid tablets (like Prednisolone) which thin out bone structure.

## Symptoms

### English
* **Early Signs:** Recurrent lower back pain, gradual loss of grip strength, minor joint crepitus (crackling sounds) during movement, slouched posture.
* **Common Symptoms:** Severe bone pain or deep joint aching; stiffness in joints (especially in the morning); localized swelling and tenderness around bones or joints; difficulty bending or straightening limbs.
* **Advanced Symptoms:** Loss of height over time (due to compression fractures in the spine); development of a stooped back (dowager's hump); severe bone deformity; minor falls causing major fractures (hip, wrist, or spine).
* **Emergency Symptoms:** Severe, sudden pain with visible bone deformity after a fall; bone protruding through the skin (compound fracture); inability to move a limb or feel sensation below a joint.

## Diagnosis

### Physical & Range of Motion Exam
* Assessment of gait (walking style), palpating bones for localized pain, and testing the flexibility and range of motion of joints.

### Imaging Diagnostics
* **X-ray:** The primary test to detect bone fractures, joint space narrowing in osteoarthritis, or bone infections.
* **CT & MRI:** Used to diagnose complex joint tears, stress fractures not visible on X-ray, or bone tumors.

### Dual-Energy X-ray Absorptiometry (DEXA Scan)
* The gold standard test to measure **Bone Mineral Density (BMD)**.
* Evaluated using a **T-score**:
   * **Normal:** -1.0 or higher.
   * **Osteopenia (low bone density):** Between -1.0 and -2.5.
   * **Osteoporosis:** -2.5 or lower.

### Blood & Biochemical Screening
* **Serum Calcium & Vitamin D3 levels:** Essential minerals for bone density.
* **Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP):** Elevated levels suggest bone turnover disorders like Paget's disease or rickets.

### Surgical Interventions

#### English
* **Fracture Reduction & Fixation:** Aligning broken bones and securing them with metal plates, screws, or rods.
* **Joint Replacement (Arthroplasty):** Replacing a severely damaged joint (hip or knee) with an artificial prosthesis.
* **Vertebroplasty:** Injecting bone cement into fractured spinal vertebrae to stabilize spine collapse.

#### Hindi
* **अस्थि संलयन (Fracture Fixation):** टूटी हुई हड्डियों को आपस में मिलाकर मेटल प्लेट, स्क्रू या रॉड की मदद से स्थिर करना।

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines or recommend dosages. The following describes clinical drug categories only:*
1. **Calcium & Vitamin D3 Supplements:** Standard nutritional therapies to maintain bone mineralization (e.g., Calcium Carbonate + Calcitriol).
2. **Bisphosphonates:** Retard bone breakdown (resorption) by osteoclasts, increasing bone density in osteoporosis (e.g., Alendronate, Zoledronic Acid injection).
3. **SERMs (Selective Estrogen Receptor Modulators):** Mimic estrogen's bone-protective effects in post-menopausal women (e.g., Raloxifene).
4. **Teriparatide:** A synthesized parathyroid hormone that stimulates new bone formation (anabolic agent).
5. **Chondroprotective Agents:** Supplements used to support joint cartilage health in early osteoarthritis (e.g., Glucosamine, Chondroitin).

### English
* **Adequate Calcium Intake:** Aim for 1000-1200 mg of calcium daily through dietary sources or supplements.
* **Sunlight Exposure:** Spend at least 15 minutes in morning sun daily to maintain healthy Vitamin D levels.
* **Avoid Bone depleters:** Quit smoking and limit carbonated drinks, as they interfere with calcium absorption.
* **Prevent Falls:** Keep home pathways clear of clutter to prevent slips and fractures in the elderly.

## Possible Complications
* **Atypical Fractures:** Fractures occurring without any major trauma due to extremely weak bone structure in severe osteoporosis.
* **Aseptic Necrosis:** Death of bone tissue (most commonly in the femoral head of the hip) due to temporary loss of blood supply.
* **Chronic Disability:** Permanent loss of mobility and bedridden state in elderly patients after hip fractures.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Visible bone ends protruding through an open wound after trauma (high risk of bone infection).**
* **Inability to move limbs, feet, or hands after a fall or spine injury.**
* **Severe swelling, cold skin, and lack of pulse in a limb after a fracture (indicates compartment syndrome).**

---

## When to Consult a Doctor
* If you experience chronic, deep bone pain that is worse at night or during rest.
* If you have joint pain that increases during walking and causes a grinding sensation.
* If you are a woman over 50 (post-menopause) or take steroid medicines, to get a baseline DEXA scan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **How many bones are in the human body?**
   An adult human body has 206 bones. A newborn baby has around 270 to 300 bones, which gradually fuse together as they grow.

2. **What is Osteoporosis?**
   Osteoporosis is a silent disease where bones lose density, become weak and brittle, and can break easily from a minor fall or even a cough.

3. **What is a DEXA scan?**
   A DEXA scan is a low-dose X-ray that measures Bone Mineral Density (BMD). It is used to diagnose osteopenia and osteoporosis.

4. **Why do post-menopausal women have weak bones?**
   Post-menopause, women experience a sharp drop in estrogen, a hormone that protects bones. This leads to rapid bone loss and increases osteoporosis risk.

5. **What is the difference between Osteoarthritis and Rheumatoid Arthritis?**
   Osteoarthritis is wear-and-tear damage to joint cartilage from aging or overuse. Rheumatoid Arthritis is an autoimmune disease where the immune system attacks joints.

6. **How does Vitamin D help bones?**
   Vitamin D acts like a key that allows the intestines to absorb calcium from food. Without Vitamin D, the calcium you eat cannot be absorbed by the body.

7. **What is a compound (open) fracture?**
   A compound fracture is when a broken bone breaks through the skin, creating an open wound. This is a medical emergency due to high risk of bone infection.

8. **Can a lack of calcium cause muscle cramps?**
   Yes. Calcium is required for muscle contraction and relaxation. Low calcium levels cause nerve hyper-excitability, leading to painful muscle cramps and spasms.

9. **What is bone marrow?**
   Bone marrow is the spongy tissue inside large bones (like hips and thighs) that contains stem cells that produce red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets.

10. **Where can I get free treatment for fractures in India?**
    Eligible families can get completely free, cashless treatment for fracture surgeries and orthopedic implants under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme.

11. **Why does smoking weaken bones?**
    Smoking reduces blood supply to bones, slows the production of bone-forming cells (osteoblasts), and decreases calcium absorption from the diet.

12. **What is joint crepitus?**
    Crepitus is the cracking, popping, or grinding sound/sensation in a joint during movement, often caused by cartilage wearing away in osteoarthritis.

13. **How does weight-bearing exercise strengthen bones?**
    Weight-bearing exercises force your body to work against gravity. This stress signals bone-forming cells to deposit more minerals, making bones denser.

14. **What is Osteomyelitis?**
    Osteomyelitis is a serious bacterial or fungal infection in a bone, which can reach the bone through the blood or from an open fracture/wound.

15. **What does a DEXA scan T-score of -2.8 mean?**
    A T-score of -2.8 indicates Osteoporosis (defined as -2.5 or lower). This means you have significantly low bone density and a high risk of fractures.

16. **Why are carbonated sodas bad for bones?**
    Many sodas contain high levels of phosphoric acid, which can increase calcium excretion in urine and interfere with calcium absorption in the gut.

17. **What is Osteomalacia?**
    Osteomalacia is the softening of bones in adults, usually caused by severe Vitamin D deficiency, leading to bone pain and muscle weakness. In children, this is called Rickets.

18. **Can a person live a normal life after a hip replacement?**
    Yes. Modern hip replacement surgery is highly successful. It relieves chronic pain and restores mobility, allowing patients to perform daily activities comfortably.

19. **What is rhabdomyolysis and does it affect bones?**
    Rhabdomyolysis is the breakdown of muscle tissue, not bones. However, severe muscle breakdown releases proteins that can damage the kidneys, leading to calcium imbalances.

20. **Can you get Vitamin D from sitting behind a glass window?**
    No. Glass blocks UVB rays, which are the specific wavelengths of sunlight required by the skin to synthesize Vitamin D. You must sit in direct sunlight.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Bones are dead, dry structures inside our body.
  * **Fact:** Bones are living tissues that constantly break down and rebuild themselves, contain blood vessels, and produce blood cells daily.
* **Myth:** Drinking milk is the only way to get enough calcium.
  * **Fact:** While milk is a great source, you can also get calcium from yogurt, paneer, leafy greens (spinach, kale), ragi, tofu, and almonds.
* **Myth:** Joint pain is a normal part of aging and cannot be managed.
  * **Fact:** While joint wear is common, therapies, weight loss, exercises, and joint replacement surgeries can successfully manage pain and restore mobility.
* **Myth:** Osteoporosis only affects women.
  * **Fact:** Men can also develop osteoporosis, especially if they smoke, abuse alcohol, have low testosterone, or take steroid medicines.
* **Myth:** If you have osteoporosis, you should stop exercising to avoid fractures.
  * **Fact:** Lack of exercise makes bones even weaker. Safe, low-impact exercises like walking and resistance bands strengthen bones and improve balance, preventing falls.
* **Myth:** A minor crack in a bone does not require a doctor visit.
  * **Fact:** Even minor cracks (stress fractures) need medical evaluation and stabilization to prevent them from becoming complete fractures.
* **Myth:** Taking calcium supplements can cause kidney stones in everyone.
  * **Fact:** Taking calcium in recommended doses with plenty of water is safe. Stones usually occur due to dehydration or genetic conditions, not standard calcium intake.
* **Myth:** Splints or casts can be wet during bathing.
  * **Fact:** Plaster casts must be kept dry. Water weakens the plaster and causes skin irritation and infection under the cast.
* **Myth:** Cracking your knuckles causes osteoarthritis.
  * **Fact:** Cracking knuckles releases gas bubbles from joint fluid; scientific studies show it does not cause osteoarthritis.
* **Myth:** Traditional bone-setters are safer and faster than orthopedic doctors.
  * **Fact:** Unscientific bone-setting can lead to bones healing in wrong angles (malunion), permanent joint stiffness, or severe nerve damage. Always see a qualified orthopedic surgeon.

### Conversation 1
* **User:** Meri dadi 70 saal ki hain, wo bathroom me phisal kar gir gayi hain aur unke hip me tez dard hai. Wo khadi nahi ho pa rahi hain.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Elderly logo me girne ke baad hip me tez dard hona Hip Fracture ka sanket hai. Unhe bilkul hilayein nahi aur khada karne ki koshish na karein. Turant 108 helpline par call karke ambulance bulayein. Ayushman Bharat card se iska operation free ho sakta hai.

### Conversation 2
* **User:** Doctor ne mere DEXA scan ka T-score -2.7 bataya hai. Kya meri bones normal hain?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, T-score -2.7 aane ka matlab hai ki aapko Osteoporosis (haddiyon ka khokhla hona) hai. Normal score -1.0 se upar hota hai. Aapki bones kamzor hain aur fracture ka risk zyada hai. Aapko doctor se milkar bone density badhane wali medicines (Bisphosphonates) shuru karni chahiye.

### Conversation 3
* **User:** Kya calcium ki tablet khane se kidney stone (pathri) ho sakti hai?
* **Assistant:** Agar aap bina doctor ki salah ke aur bina zaroorat ke calcium ki high doses rojana lete hain, to stone ka risk ho sakta hai. Lekin agar aap normal dose me calcium lete hain aur rojana 3-4 liters paani peete hain, to kidney stone ka koi risk nahi hai.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** Mere ghutno me chalte samay kat-kat ki aawaz aati hai aur halka dard rehta hai. Kya mujhe gathiya hai?
* **Assistant:** Ghutno se kat-kat ki aawaz aana (crepitus) aur dard hona Osteoarthritis (gathiya) ka shuruati lakshan ho sakta hai, jisme joints ka cartilage ghis jata hai. Aap ghutno ka ek X-ray karayein aur doctor se consult karein. Weight control me rakhein aur soft walking karein.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Kya Vitamin D3 ki tablet rojana khani chahiye?
* **Assistant:** Nahi. Vitamin D3 (Cholecalciferol 60,000 IU) ki high dose tablet/capsule aamtaur par hafte me ek baar khayi jati hai, rojana nahi. Rojana lene se Vitamin D toxicity ho sakti hai. Dawa ki dose hamesha doctor ki salah se hi tay karein.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** What is the difference between splint and cast?
* **Assistant:** A splint is a temporary, half-support made of plaster or metal, used in the first few days to allow swelling to go down. A cast is a complete, hard plaster or fiberglass ring that completely surrounds the limb to keep the bone still while it heals.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Mere 10 saal ke bete ke pair thode tedhe (bow legs) dikhte hain. Kya karein?
* **Assistant:** Baccho me pairon ka tedha hona Rickets (Vitamin D aur calcium ki kami) ke karan ho sakta hai. Government ki RBSK scheme ke tehat iski free checkup aur treatment sarkari hospital me hota hai. Pediatrician se consult karein.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Kya osteoporosis ko completely theek kiya ja sakta hai?
* **Assistant:** Osteoporosis ko fully reverse karna mushkil hai, lekin regular treatment (calcium, D3, Bisphosphonates) aur weight exercises se bones ko further weak hone se roka ja sakta hai aur density ko kafi hadd tak sudhara ja sakta hai.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Haddi tootne par plaster kitne din tak lagana padta hai?
* **Assistant:** Haddi tootne (fracture) ke baad plaster lagane ka samay haddi ke size aur location par depend karta hai. Aamtaur par wrist ya arm fracture me 4-6 hafte, aur leg bones me 6-12 hafte tak plaster lagaya jata hai.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Does Ayushman Bharat card cover total knee replacement surgery?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment expenses for total knee replacement and hip replacement surgeries, including the cost of artificial implants, are covered at all empaneled hospitals.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Meri koki (wrist) slip hone par toot gayi hai aur sujan bohot hai. Kya pehla aid karein?
* **Assistant:** Wrist me sujan aur dard hone par use bilkul na hilayein. Cardboard ya lakdi ki khapchi (splint) banakar kapde se wrist ko support dein. Baraf (ice pack) se sujan kam karein aur turant doctor ke paas checkup aur X-ray ke liye le jayein.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** How can I increase bone density naturally after menopause?
* **Assistant:** Post-menopause me bone density badhane ke liye diet me calcium rich foods (milk, paneer, ragi) badhayein, rojana dhoop me baithein, aur regular walking ya light weight training karein. Agar density bohot kam hai, to doctor hormonally support ya medicines shuru karenge.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Kya steroid tablets khane se haddiyan kamzor hoti hain?
* **Assistant:** Haan, long-term steroid tablets (jaise Prednisolone) khane se bones se calcium tezi se leak hota hai, jisse bones khokhli ho jati hain. Steroids hamesha doctor ki dekh-rekh me hi lein aur sath me calcium aur D3 supplements zaroori hain.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** What is the role of NPHCE program for elderly bone care?
* **Assistant:** The National Programme for Health Care of the Elderly (NPHCE) offers free physiotherapy, geriatric clinics, and free assistive devices (walking sticks, walkers) at government district hospitals to help senior citizens keep their bones active.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Mere pair ki haddi me injury ke baad pas (pus) ban raha hai aur bukhar hai.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Fracture ya injury ke baad bone me pus aur bukhar aana Osteomyelitis (haddi me infection) ka sanket hai. Yeh ek severe condition hai jo haddi ko gala sakti hai. Turant kisi orthopedic surgeon se milein aur IV antibiotics shuru karayein.
