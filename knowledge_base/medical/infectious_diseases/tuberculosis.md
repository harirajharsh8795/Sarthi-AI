# Tuberculosis (TB/Tuberculosis) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Mycobacterium tuberculosis and DOTS Guide)

## Overview

### English
Tuberculosis (TB) is a major infectious disease caused by the bacterium **Mycobacterium tuberculosis**, primarily affecting the lungs (Pulmonary TB) but capable of infecting any organ (Extra-pulmonary TB, e.g., lymph nodes, spine, brain). It is transmitted through airborne droplets when an active TB patient coughs or sneezes. The hallmark symptoms are a chronic cough lasting $> 2$ weeks, evening rise of temperature (fever), night sweats, and unexplained weight loss. Diagnosis relies on sputum acid-fast bacilli (AFB) microscopy, chest X-rays, and highly sensitive molecular tests like **CBNAAT (GeneXpert)**. Treatment is a structured 6-month multi-drug regimen (Rifampicin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol) administered under the **DOTS** protocol to prevent Drug-Resistant TB (MDR-TB).

## Quick Summary
Tuberculosis is a bacterial lung disease. Symptoms include a chronic cough over 2 weeks, evening fever, night sweats, and weight loss. First aid targets face masking and immediately getting a CBNAAT sputum test. Diagnostics use AFB smear, GeneXpert, and chest X-rays. Management utilizes a 6-month daily combination drug regimen (HRZE).

---

## Definition
* **Pulmonary TB:** Tuberculosis infection localized within the lung parenchyma, which is highly infectious.
* **Extra-Pulmonary TB:** TB infection occurring outside the lungs (e.g., pleural space, meninges, spine [Pott's disease], lymph nodes).
* **CBNAAT (GeneXpert):** Cartridge-Based Nucleic Acid Amplification Test, a rapid PCR-based molecular diagnostic tool that detects TB bacterial DNA and screens for Rifampicin drug resistance within 2 hours.
* **DOTS (Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course):** The WHO-recommended strategy where health workers directly observe the patient swallowing each dose of TB medication to ensure compliance and prevent drug resistance.

---

## Active vs. Latent TB Infection (LTBI)
It is critical to distinguish between these two states:
* **Latent TB (LTBI):** The bacteria are asleep (dormant) in the body, walled off by the immune system. The person is not sick, has no symptoms, and **cannot spread TB to others**.
* **Active TB Disease:** The bacteria multiply because the immune system weakens. The person feels sick, has symptoms, and **can easily spread TB through the air**. *Latent TB can reactivate into Active TB at any time, especially in patients with HIV or diabetes.*

## First Aid & Sputum Collection Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Wear a face mask** (surgical or N95) to prevent transmitting droplets to family members. Keep the room well-ventilated with windows open.
* **Get tested immediately** if you have a cough for $>2$ weeks. Proceed to the nearest government DOTS center.
* **Collect sputum correctly:** Collect the first morning sputum sample. Cough deeply from the chest, not just saliva from the mouth, and spit it into the sterile container provided.
* **Maintain high protein nutrition** (milk, eggs, paneer, pulses) to help the body rebuild tissues.

#### Hindi
* **मास्क पहनें:** घर के अन्य सदस्यों को बचाने के लिए मरीज मास्क पहने।
* **बलगम की जांच सही तरीके से करें:** सुबह का पहला गहरा बलगम सैंपल लें, केवल थूक न दें।
* **पोषक आहार लें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Mask zaroori pehnein:** Ghar ke logo ko bachane ke liye N95 ya surgical mask pehnein.
* **Sputum (balgam) test karwayein:** 15 din se zyada khansi hone par government DOTS center par balgam checkup karayein.
* **Morning sputum sample lein:** Subah uthte hi bina brush kiye chest ke deep se khans kar aane wala balgam sample box me daal kar lab me dein, muh ki normal thuk (saliva) na dein.
* **High-protein diet lein:** Doodh, paneer, ande aur daal khayein taaki body recover kar sake.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT spit in open public places.** Spitting spreads the bacteria in the air, infecting others. Use a closed spit pot containing a disinfectant (bleach/phenyl) or spit in a toilet flushing immediately.
* **Do NOT stop TB medications prematurely.** Stopping drugs after 1-2 months when symptoms improve is highly dangerous. The remaining bacteria will mutate, leading to **Multi-Drug Resistant TB (MDR-TB)**, which requires 2 years of highly toxic injections and drugs.
* **Do NOT smoke or drink alcohol** during TB treatment.

#### Hindi
* **खुले में न थूकें:** सार्वजनिक स्थानों पर थूकने से हवा में बैक्टीरिया फैलता है।
* **दवा बीच में न छोड़ें:** लक्षण ठीक होने पर भी 6 महीने का पूरा कोर्स करें, वरना एमडीआर-टीबी (MDR-TB) हो सकता है।

#### Hinglish
* **Khule me na thookein:** Khule me thookne se TB bacteria hawa me kai ghante tak tairta rehta hai. Toilet me thook kar flush karein ya disinfectant pot use karein.
* **Dawa beech me na chodein:** 1-2 mahine me khansi theek hone par dawai band karne ki galti bilkul na karein. Dawai beech me chodne se bache hue bacteria strong hokar **MDR-TB (Multi-Drug Resistant)** ban jate hain jiska treatment 2 saal chalta hai aur toxic hota hai.

## Medical & DOTS Management (NTEP India Guidelines)

### Standard 6-Month Regimen for Drug-Sensitive Pulmonary TB

#### English
* **Intensive Phase (Months 1 - 2):** Daily dose of 4 drugs: **Rifampicin (R), Isoniazid (H), Pyrazinamide (Z), and Ethambutol (E)** - (HRZE).
* **Continuation Phase (Months 3 - 6):** Daily dose of 3 drugs: **Rifampicin (R), Isoniazid (H), and Ethambutol (E)** - (HRE).
* *Administered as Fixed-Dose Combinations (FDC) adjusted to the patient's weight band.*

#### Hindi
* **शुरुआती 2 महीने:** चार दवाएं रोजाना लेना (Rifampicin + Isoniazid + Pyrazinamide + Ethambutol)।
* **अगले 4 महीने:** तीन दवाएं रोजाना लेना (Rifampicin + Isoniazid + Ethambutol)।

#### Hinglish
* **Pehle 2 Mahine (Intensive Phase):** Daily basis par 4 main drugs ka combination (HRZE: Rifampicin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol) diya jata hai.
* **Agley 4 Mahine (Continuation Phase):** Daily basis par 3 drugs (HRE: Rifampicin, Isoniazid, Ethambutol) di jati hain. Yeh poora course 6 mahine ka hota hai.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories used in TB management:*
1. **First-line Anti-TB Drugs (ATT):** e.g., Rifampicin (inhibits RNA polymerase), Isoniazid (inhibits cell wall synthesis), Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol.
2. **Second-line Drugs (for MDR-TB):** e.g., Bedaquiline, Delamanid, Linezolid, Moxifloxacin, Kanamycin.
3. **Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6):** Prescribed daily alongside Isoniazid to prevent peripheral neuropathy (burning hands/feet).

---

## Prevention of Tuberculosis
* **BCG Vaccine (Bacille Calmette-Guérin):** A live-attenuated vaccine given to all newborns at birth (or up to 1 year of age) under the government UIP. It protects children against severe forms like TB meningitis and miliary TB.
* **Cough Etiquette:** Cover mouth and nose when coughing.
* **UV Light & Ventilation:** TB bacteria are killed by direct sunlight and UV light. Ensure sunlit, ventilated rooms.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated TB
* **Permanent Lung Cavitation:** Destruction of lung tissue leading to chronic breathing trouble (COPD) and lung failure.
* **Pott's Disease (Spine TB):** Destruction of vertebrae leading to spinal collapse and paralysis of limbs.
* **TB Meningitis:** Infection of brain linings, causing high mortality or permanent neurological damage.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient coughs up large volumes of fresh red blood (massive hemoptysis).**
* **Sudden severe chest pain, shortness of breath, and oxygen levels dropping (indicates lung collapse/pneumothorax).**
* **A TB patient develops a high fever, severe headache, confusion, stiff neck, or seizures (TB meningitis).**

---

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Tuberculosis Elimination Programme (NTEP):** Provides completely free CBNAAT/sputum testing and free daily FDC medicines (ATT) at all government DOTS centers.
* **Nikshay Poshan Yojana:** A direct benefit transfer (DBT) scheme where the government sends **₹500 per month** to the bank account of every active TB patient on treatment to help them buy nutritious food.
* **Nikshay Portal:** The national web-based registry used to notify and track every TB patient in India across private and public sectors.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is Tuberculosis (TB)?**
   TB is a contagious bacterial infection caused by *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*, primarily affecting the lungs but capable of infecting bones, lymph nodes, and the brain.

2. **How does TB transmit?**
   TB spreads through the air when a person with active pulmonary TB coughs, sneezes, spits, or speaks, releasing tiny bacterial droplets that others inhale.

3. **What is the difference between latent and active TB?**
   Latent TB means bacteria are dormant in the body; you have no symptoms and cannot spread the virus. Active TB means the bacteria are multiplying, making you sick and highly contagious.

4. **What is the CBNAAT test?**
   CBNAAT (GeneXpert) is a highly sensitive molecular PCR test. It detects TB bacterial DNA in sputum and screens for resistance to the primary drug Rifampicin within 2 hours.

5. **What is DOTS therapy?**
   DOTS (Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course) is a program where a designated healthcare worker or volunteer directly watches the patient swallow their daily TB medicines to ensure compliance.

6. **Why is stopping TB medicines early dangerous?**
   Stopping early allows the surviving bacteria to mutate and become resistant to first-line drugs, causing Multi-Drug Resistant TB (MDR-TB), which is much harder and more toxic to treat.

7. **Where can I get free TB tests and medicines in India?**
   Under the NTEP program, all sputum tests, chest X-rays, CBNAAT checks, and 6-month daily daily medicines are provided completely free of charge at all government DOTS clinics.

8. **What is the Nikshay Poshan Yojana?**
   It is a government program that pays ₹500 monthly directly into the bank account of every TB patient on active treatment to help them afford a high-protein diet.

9. **Why do TB patients take Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6) daily?**
    The TB drug Isoniazid can cause a deficiency of Vitamin B6, leading to peripheral neuropathy (numbness, burning sensation in hands and feet). Pyridoxine is given to prevent this.

10. **Why does Rifampicin turn urine orange/red?**
    Rifampicin is a red-colored drug. It is excreted through bodily fluids, safely turning urine, sweat, saliva, and tears orange-red. This is normal and not a cause for concern.

11. **What is the BCG vaccine?**
    BCG is a vaccine given to newborns at birth under the UIP. It prevents severe, life-threatening forms of pediatric TB, such as TB meningitis.

12. **Can TB occur outside the lungs?**
    Yes. This is called Extra-pulmonary TB. It can affect lymph nodes (scrofula), the spine (Pott's disease), the brain (TB meningitis), kidneys, and abdomen.

13. **How long is a TB patient contagious after starting medicines?**
    With daily compliance, most pulmonary TB patients become non-infectious within 2 to 3 weeks of starting the standard 6-month ATT regimen.

14. **What is MDR-TB and how is it treated?**
    MDR-TB (Multi-Drug Resistant TB) is tuberculosis resistant to at least Rifampicin and Isoniazid. It requires a longer treatment (18-24 months) using second-line injections and newer drugs like Bedaquiline.

15. **What is the price of generic TB drugs at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    While the government distributes them free at DOTS clinics, generic anti-TB drugs are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at highly subsidized generic rates.

16. **What is hemoptysis?**
    Hemoptysis is the medical term for coughing up blood from the lungs or respiratory tract, which is a key symptom of active pulmonary TB.

17. **Can a person with TB get married or have children?**
    Yes. Once cured, a person can marry and have children safely. If infected during pregnancy, treatment must continue, as most first-line TB drugs are safe for the fetus.

18. **Why is smoking strictly banned during TB treatment?**
    Smoking damages the cilia (filters) in the lungs and weakens lung defenses, worsening TB lung damage and significantly raising the risk of treatment failure.

19. **What is the Mantoux test?**
    The Mantoux test is a skin test where a small amount of PPD (purified protein derivative) is injected under the skin. A swelling after 48-72 hours indicates exposure to TB bacteria (useful for latent TB screening).

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover spinal TB surgeries?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for extra-pulmonary TB hospitalizations, spinal stabilization surgeries for Pott's disease, and all supportive care.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** TB only affects poor and malnourished people.
  * **Fact:** TB bacteria spread through the air. Anyone who inhales the droplets can get TB, regardless of wealth, social status, or clean habits.
* **Myth:** TB is a hereditary disease passed down through genetics.
  * **Fact:** TB is an infectious bacterial disease spread through airborne droplets. It is not genetic or hereditary.
* **Myth:** If your cough stops after 1 month of DOTS, you are completely cured.
  * **Fact:** The bacteria are only suppressed, not eradicated. You must complete the full 6-month course. Stopping early causes MDR-TB.
* **Myth:** Spitting on the road is harmless because sunlight kills TB instantly.
  * **Fact:** While UV light kills TB, sputum clumps protect the bacteria, allowing them to remain active in dust for hours, infecting passing citizens. Spitting in public is illegal.
* **Myth:** TB only occurs in the lungs.
  * **Fact:** TB can affect almost any organ in the body, including lymph nodes, bones, kidneys, reproductive tract, skin, and brain.
* **Myth:** A person with TB should be permanently isolated from the family.
  * **Fact:** Permanent isolation is unnecessary. Once the patient starts daily DOTS medicines, they become non-infectious in 2-3 weeks and can live safely with the family while wearing a mask.
* **Myth:** TB is incurable and always leads to death.
  * **Fact:** With regular daily DOTS treatment, first-line TB is over 95% curable. Early diagnosis is key.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures TB cough.
  * **Fact:** This is a dangerous superstition. TB requires specific multi-drug antibiotics (ATT) to kill the Mycobacterium bacteria.
* **Myth:** TB medicines cause permanent liver damage and should be avoided.
  * **Fact:** While TB drugs can cause temporary liver enzyme changes, they are monitored by doctors. The benefit of curing TB far outweighs this risk. Never stop drugs without clearance.
* **Myth:** If you had the BCG vaccine at birth, you can never get pulmonary TB as an adult.
  * **Fact:** The BCG vaccine protects children against severe forms like TB meningitis, but its protection against adult pulmonary TB declines over years. Adults still need precautions.
