# Typhoid (Miyadi Bukhar) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Salmonella typhi and Antibiotic Guide)

## Overview

### English
Typhoid Fever, also known as Enteric Fever and commonly called Miyadi Bukhar in India, is a systemic bacterial infection caused by the ingestion of food or water contaminated with **Salmonella enterica serovar Typhi** (or Paratyphi). The disease is characterized by a sustained, high fever that follows a **step-ladder pattern** (rising daily), severe headache, abdominal pain, constipation (in adults) or diarrhea (in children), and a temporary rash called **rose spots** on the chest/abdomen. Diagnosis is confirmed via blood culture (gold standard) or serological tests like the **Widal test**. Treatment requires a complete course of specific antibiotics (e.g., Azithromycin or Ceftriaxone) to prevent life-threatening complications like intestinal perforation and bleeding.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
Typhoid is a bacterial enteric fever. Symptoms feature step-ladder fever, headache, abdominal pain, and rose spots. First aid emphasizes intensive hydration and Paracetamol. Avoid self-medicating with low-dose antibiotics. Diagnostics use blood culture or Widal tests. Management uses targeted antibiotics (Azithromycin).

---

## Definition
* **Enteric Fever:** A collective term for typhoid fever (caused by S. typhi) and paratyphoid fever (caused by S. paratyphi A, B, or C).
* **Step-Ladder Fever:** A classical temperature pattern in the first week of typhoid where the body temperature climbs higher each day, peaking in the evening and dropping slightly in the morning.
* **Rose Spots:** Transient, blanching, rose-colored macules (2-4mm diameter) appearing on the chest and abdomen in $20-30\%$ of typhoid patients during the second week of illness.

---

## Pathological Stages & Intestinal Perforation Risk
Typhoid bacteria invade the lymphatic tissues of the intestines:
1. **First Week:** The bacteria enter the small intestine, penetrate the mucosal lining, and are carried by macrophages to the liver, spleen, and bone marrow, where they multiply. Fever begins to climb in a step-ladder pattern.
2. **Second Week:** The bacteria enter the bloodstream (bacteremia), causing sustained high fever ($103^\circ\text{F} - 104^\circ\text{F}$), abdominal distension, and rose spots.
3. **Third Week (Critical Phase):** **The bacteria colonize Peyer's Patches (lymph tissues in the ileum). If untreated, these tissues inflame, ulcerate, and rupture, causing severe intestinal bleeding or Intestinal Perforation (hole in the gut lining).** *This causes gut contents to leak into the abdomen, leading to peritonitis, sepsis, and death. Urgent surgery is required.*

## First Aid & Initial Care

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Maintain continuous hydration** by drinking boiled/purified water, ORS solution, and warm soups to replenish fluids lost during high fever.
* **Use Paracetamol** to lower fever.
* **Get tested (Blood Culture/Widal)** if you have a sustained fever for $>4-5$ days. Early antibiotic start prevents intestinal complications.
* **Follow a soft, easily digestible diet** (porridge, khichdi, boiled potatoes, bananas). Avoid spicy, high-fiber, or raw foods, which can irritate the inflamed intestines.

#### Hindi
* **हाइड्रेशन बनाए रखें:** उबला पानी, सूप या ओआरएस (ORS) पिएं।
* **केवल पैरासिटामोल (Paracetamol) लें:** बुखार के लिए पैरासिटामोल दें।
* **नरम और सुपाच्य भोजन लें:** खिचड़ी, दलिया खाएं।

#### Hinglish
* **Hydration maintain rakhein:** Bukhar me dher sara paani (boiled/purified), nariyal pani ya ORS pilayein.
* **Sirf Paracetamol lein:** Bukhar aur sir dard kam karne ke liye paracetamol tablet dein.
* **Soft diet khayein:** Intestines me sujan hoti hai, isiliye oily, fried aur heavy high-fiber khana bilkul band karein. Sirf ubla aloo, khichdi, daliya aur kela khayein.
* **Widal/Blood culture test karayein:** Agar bukhar 5 din se zyada ho to immediately doctor ke paas ja kar enteric fever test karayein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT take arbitrary, low-dose antibiotics.** Taking random antibiotics (like single doses of Ciprofloxacin or Azithromycin) before testing masks the infection, leading to a negative blood culture while the bacteria silently damage the intestines, causing sudden perforation.
* **Do NOT eat raw salads, street foods, or unpasteurized dairy.** These are the primary sources of Salmonella bacteria.
* **Do NOT stop antibiotics early.** Complete the full 7-14 day course prescribed by the doctor, even if the fever drops to normal, to prevent relapse and becoming a chronic carrier.

#### Hindi
* **अधूरी एंटीबायोटिक खुराक न लें:** बुखार ठीक होने पर भी डॉक्टर द्वारा दी गई एंटीबायोटिक का पूरा 7-14 दिन का कोर्स करें, वरना बीमारी दोबारा लौट सकती है (relapse)।
* **कच्चा सलाद, चाट-पकौड़ी या खुला पानी न पिएं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Bina test ke antibiotics na lein:** Padosi ke kehne par 1-2 dose antibiotic le kar choddna sabse dangerous hai. Isse test report negative aayegi aur bacteria aanto ko nuksan pahunchata rahega.
* **Bahar ka khana/salad band karein:** Street food, bina dhula salad aur khule matke ka paani bilkul na piyein, inme Salmonella bacteria ho sakta hai.
* **Antiviral aur antibiotic beech me na chodein:** Bukhar normal hote hi course beech me na rokein. Antibiotics ka 7-14 din ka full course complete karein taaki relapse na ho.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Blood Culture:** The gold standard test. Most accurate during the first week of fever to isolate *Salmonella typhi*.
* **Widal Test:** A serological test checking for antibodies against Salmonella O and H antigens. **Most reliable only after the 7th-10th day of fever.** (Look for titer values $> 1:160$ for active infection).
* **Typhidot Test:** A rapid test detecting IgM and IgG antibodies, giving results in a few hours.

## Medical & Antibiotic Management

### First-Line Oral Antibiotics (for uncomplicated typhoid)

#### English
* **Azithromycin** (500mg once daily for 7 days) or **Cefixime** (400mg twice daily) are the standard oral drugs of choice due to high fluoroquinolone resistance in India.

#### Hindi
* **एज़िथ्रोमाइसिन (Azithromycin)** या सेफिक्साइम (Cefixime) टैबलेट 7 दिनों तक देना।

#### Hinglish
* India me fluoroquinolone resistance high hone ke karan, doctors oral tablets me **Azithromycin (500mg daily 7 days)** ya Cefixime (400mg twice daily) likhte hain.

### Intravenous Antibiotics (for severe/hospitalized cases)

#### English
* IV **Ceftriaxone** (1-2g daily for 10-14 days) is the standard treatment in hospital settings for patients unable to tolerate oral drugs or showing systemic complications.

#### Hindi
* गंभीर मामलों में अस्पताल में IV सेफ्ट्रिएक्सोन (Ceftriaxone) ड्रिप द्वारा देना।

#### Hinglish

2. **Macrolides:** e.g., Azithromycin.

4. **Antipyretics:** Lower fever (e.g., Paracetamol).

## Prevention of Typhoid
* **Typhoid Conjugate Vaccine (TCV):** Highly effective single-dose vaccine (e.g., Typbar-TCV).
   * **Pediatric Schedule (IAP):** Recommended as a single dose for all infants at **9 months** of age, providing long-lasting protection.
* **Safe Water (WASH):** Drink only boiled or chlorinated water. Store water in clean, covered containers.
* **Food Hygiene:** Eat freshly cooked, hot food. Wash hands with soap after using the toilet and before preparing food.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Typhoid
* **Intestinal Perforation:** Rupture of the ileum wall, causing peritonitis (severe abdominal infection), septic shock, and death.
* **Gastrointestinal Hemorrhage:** Severe internal bleeding from gut ulcers, presenting as dark tarry stools or blood in vomit.
* **Typhoid State:** Severe neuropsychiatric symptoms (muttering delirium, picking at bedclothes) due to bacterial endotoxins affecting the brain.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Sudden, severe, sharp pain starting in the abdomen (stomach), accompanied by a rigid, hard stomach wall (indicates intestinal perforation).**
* **The patient vomits blood, passes black, tarry stools, or shows sudden extreme weakness and paleness.**
* **The patient becomes confused, delirious (badbadana), or cannot wake up.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is typhoid fever?**
   Typhoid (Miyadi Bukhar) is a bacterial infection caused by *Salmonella typhi*, transmitted through contaminated food or water, causing sustained high fever and gut inflammation.

2. **What is a step-ladder fever pattern?**
   It is the classic temperature rise in typhoid where the fever climbs a little higher each day (like steps of a ladder), peaking in the evening and dropping slightly in the morning.

3. **Why is the Widal test unreliable in the first week of fever?**
   The Widal test detects antibodies made by your body against the bacteria. It takes 7-10 days for your immune system to make enough antibodies to show up on the test. A blood culture is best in the first week.

4. **What are "Rose Spots" in typhoid?**
   Rose spots are small, flat, rose-pink spots (2-4mm) that appear on the chest or abdomen of some typhoid patients during the second week of fever, fading when pressed.

5. **Why is stopping typhoid antibiotics early dangerous?**
   If you stop antibiotics early, some bacteria survive. This can cause the fever to return (relapse) or turn you into a chronic carrier who sheds bacteria in feces, infecting others.

6. **What is intestinal perforation and why is it fatal?**
   The bacteria cause ulcers in the small intestine. If untreated, the ulcer can eat through the gut wall, creating a hole (perforation). Stool and bacteria leak into the abdomen, causing fatal sepsis.

7. **Where can BPL cardholders get free typhoid treatment in India?**
   Eligible patients can get free cashless hospital stays, IV ceftriaxone, ICU care, and emergency surgeries under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY card.

8. **What is the Typhoid Conjugate Vaccine (TCV)?**
   TCV is a highly effective vaccine given as a single dose to infants at 9 months of age, providing long-lasting protection against Salmonella typhi.

9. **Why is a high-fiber diet banned during typhoid?**
    The bacteria inflame and weaken the intestinal walls. High-fiber foods (raw salads, whole grains, nuts) require hard gut contractions to digest, which can rupture the weak gut wall, causing a perforation. Eat soft foods only.

10. **How does typhoid transmit?**
    It transmits through the fecal-oral route. Eating food or drinking water contaminated with the urine or feces of an active patient or a chronic carrier spreads the disease.

11. **Who was "Typhoid Mary"?**
    Mary Mallon was an asymptomatic chronic carrier of Salmonella typhi in the US who worked as a cook, infecting over 50 people with typhoid through food contamination.

12. **What is the Widal titer level indicating active infection?**
    A titer of 1:160 or higher for both anti-O (active infection) and anti-H (past exposure/vaccination) antibodies is generally considered diagnostic for typhoid in endemic areas.

13. **Can typhoid cause liver and spleen enlargement?**
    Yes. The bacteria multiply in the reticuloendothelial system, causing the liver (hepatomegaly) and spleen (splenomegaly) to swell and enlarge, which is checked during abdominal exams.

14. **What is the incubation period of typhoid?**
    The incubation period is usually **1 to 2 weeks** (ranging from 5 to 21 days) after consuming contaminated water or food before symptoms show.

15. **What is the price of generic Cefixime and Azithromycin in India?**
    Generic Cefixime (200mg) and Azithromycin (500mg) tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹30 to ₹50 per strip, compared to ₹120+ for branded versions.

16. **Why does typhoid cause bradycardia (slow heart rate) despite high fever?**
    Normally, heart rate rises with fever. Typhoid exhibits "relative bradycardia" (Faget's sign) where the pulse remains unusually slow despite high temperature, caused by bacterial toxins.

17. **Can a pregnant woman take Ceftriaxone for typhoid?**
    Yes. Ceftriaxone is safe during pregnancy. Fluoroquinolones (like Ciprofloxacin) are avoided during pregnancy due to fetal joint damage risk.

18. **How do I sanitize a typhoid patient's toilet?**
    Clean the toilet seat and flush handle daily using household bleach (sodium hypochlorite) or phenyl disinfectant to kill Salmonella bacteria shed in urine and feces.

19. **What is the Typhidot test?**
    Typhidot is a rapid test that checks for specific IgM and IgG antibodies against outer membrane proteins of Salmonella typhi, giving results in 2-3 hours.

20. **Is typhoid contagious from person to person?**
    Yes, indirectly. It does not spread through coughing, but if an infected person does not wash hands with soap after using the toilet and prepares food, they will infect others.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Typhoid is caused by getting wet in the rain or drinking cold water in winter.
  * **Fact:** Typhoid is caused only by the *Salmonella typhi* bacteria from food/water contaminated with fecal matter, not by weather changes.
* **Myth:** Drink only sugarcane juice and raw milk to cure typhoid.
  * **Fact:** Raw milk can contain Salmonella and other bacteria, worsening gut infection. Sugarcane juice provides glucose but does not kill bacteria. Take doctor-prescribed antibiotics.
* **Myth:** The Widal test positive on Day 2 of fever confirms typhoid.
  * **Fact:** Widal checks for antibodies, which take 7-10 days to build. A positive Widal on Day 2 indicates a past infection or vaccine antibodies, not current typhoid. Do a blood culture.
* **Myth:** If you feel better after 3 days of antibiotics, you can stop the medicine.
  * **Fact:** Stopping early leaves some bacteria alive. They will multiply, cause a relapse, and build antibiotic resistance. Complete the 7-14 day course.
* **Myth:** Typhoid vaccine only protects you for 1 year.
  * **Fact:** Newer Typhoid Conjugate Vaccines (TCV) provide long-lasting, highly effective protection for several years after a single dose.
* **Myth:** People with typhoid should be given raw salad and green vegetables to recover.
  * **Fact:** Raw salad and fiber irritate the swollen intestines, raising the risk of gut perforation. Eat only soft, well-cooked starch foods (khichdi, boiled potato).
* **Myth:** Typhoid is a mild disease that can be treated with home remedies.
  * **Fact:** Untreated typhoid has a 10-20% mortality rate due to intestinal perforation and bleeding. It is a severe bacterial infection requiring antibiotics.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures typhoid fever.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Salmonella typhi requires specific antibiotics (Azithromycin/Ceftriaxone) to be destroyed.
* **Myth:** Once cured, you can never get typhoid again.
  * **Fact:** Re-infection is common if you consume contaminated water or food again. Practice food hygiene and get vaccinated.
* **Myth:** Typhoid bacteria only live in the stomach.
  * **Fact:** The bacteria enter the blood and travel to the liver, spleen, bone marrow, and gallbladder, where they can live for years in chronic carriers.
