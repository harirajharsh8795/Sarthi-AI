# Viral Hepatitis (Piliya) ki Pehchan, Prakar aur Upachar (Viral Hepatitis A, B, C, E and Liver Care)

## Overview

### English
Viral Hepatitis is a systemic infection characterized by inflammation of the liver cells, primarily caused by five distinct hepatotropic viruses: **Hepatitis A (HAV), B (HBV), C (HCV), D (HDV), and E (HEV)**.
* **HAV and HEV:** Transmitted via the fecal-oral route (contaminated food/water). They cause acute, self-limiting infections presenting as jaundice and raise risk of acute liver failure in pregnant women (especially HEV).
* **HBV and HCV:** Transmitted through blood, contaminated needles, or sexual contact. They often progress to chronic liver disease, leading to cirrhosis and hepatocellular carcinoma (liver cancer).
* **Prevention:** Highly effective vaccines exist for HAV and HBV. Chronic HBV is managed with life-saving lifelong antivirals (Tenofovir), while chronic HCV is completely curable with a 12-week course of Direct-Acting Antivirals (Sofosbuvir/Daclatasvir).

### Hindi

* **हेपेटाइटिस A और E:** दूषित भोजन और पानी (fecal-oral route) से फैलते हैं। ये तीव्र (acute) पीलिया का कारण बनते हैं।
* **हेपेटाइटिस B और C:** संक्रमित रक्त, सुइयों या यौन संपर्क से फैलते हैं। ये क्रोनिक लिवर सिरोसिस (cirrhosis) और लिवर कैंसर का कारण बन सकते हैं।
* **इलाज:** हेपेटाइटिस C का 12 सप्ताह के एंटीवायरल (Sofosbuvir) कोर्स से पूर्ण इलाज संभव है। भारत सरकार (NVHCP) के तहत इसका मुफ्त इलाज होता है।

* **Hepatitis B aur C:** Infected blood transfusion, contaminated needles/syringes sharing, ya unprotected sex ke jariye phailte hain. Yeh chronic (life-long) liver damage, Cirrhosis aur Liver Cancer ka main kaaran hain.
* **Treatment:** Hepatitis B ke liye lifelong antivirals (Tenofovir) lagti hain aur Hepatitis C ka 12-week ki antiviral tablets (Sofosbuvir + Daclatasvir) se 100% cure (poora ilaaj) ho jata hai. Government ke NVHCP program me ye medicines free milti hain.

## Quick Summary
Hepatitis is viral liver inflammation. Key classifications are fecal-oral (A, E) and bloodborne (B, C). Symptoms include yellow skin/eyes (jaundice), dark urine, clay-colored stools, and right-upper abdominal pain. Diagnostics use Liver Function Tests (elevated SGPT/bilirubin) and viral serologies. Management focuses on supportive rest for acute types and specific antivirals for chronic types.

---

## Definition
* **Viral Hepatitis:** Liver inflammation caused by hepatotropic viruses, resulting in hepatocyte injury and necrosis.
* **Hepatitis B Surface Antigen (HBsAg):** The primary serological marker in blood used to diagnose active Hepatitis B infection.
* **Direct-Acting Antivirals (DAAs):** Highly effective oral medications that target specific steps in the HCV replication cycle, curing over 95% of Hepatitis C patients.

## Symptoms of Viral Hepatitis

### English
* Yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes (jaundice); dark, tea-colored urine; pale, clay-colored stools; severe nausea, vomiting, and loss of appetite (anorexia); pain and discomfort in the upper right side of the abdomen (liver area); fever, joint pain, and extreme fatigue.

## First Aid & Home Care Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Ensure complete bed rest** during the acute phase. The inflamed liver needs reduced workload.
* **Follow a low-fat, high-carbohydrate diet.** Eat small, frequent meals consisting of easily digestible carbohydrates (boiled rice, khichdi, bananas, sugarcane juice). High-fat food strains the liver.
* **Maintain strict hand hygiene** (washing hands with soap and water after using the toilet) to prevent spreading Hepatitis A and E to family members.
* **Drink only boiled or purified water** to prevent fecal-oral transmission.

#### Hindi
* **पूर्ण आराम करें:** यकृत पर दबाव कम करने के लिए आराम आवश्यक है।
* **वसा रहित आहार लें:** उबले चावल, खिचड़ी, केला खाएं। वसायुक्त और तला भोजन बंद करें।
* **साफ पानी पिएं:** पानी उबालकर पिएं।

#### Hinglish
* **Complete Bed Rest:** Inflammation ke dauran liver par pressure kam karne ke liye physical rest zaroori hai.
* **Low-fat Diet khayein:** Oily, fried aur masaledar khana bilkul band karein. Diet me carbohydrate rich aur easily digestible food (khichdi, kela, dahi, ubla aloo) lein.
* **Clean water piyein:** Paani hamesha boil karke ya filter karke hi piyein taaki Hepatitis A/E family me na phaile.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT take Paracetamol or other painkillers** without doctor clearance. The liver is inflamed and cannot metabolize drugs; taking paracetamol can trigger acute, fatal liver failure (hepatotoxicity).
* **Do NOT drink alcohol.** Alcohol is a direct liver toxin and will accelerate liver cell death.
* **Do NOT go for traditional jaundice-curing methods** (jhad-phook, nose-drops, herbal oils). They delay clinical care, causing acute liver failure.

#### Hindi
* **पैरासिटामोल (Paracetamol) न लें:** सूजे हुए लिवर पर पैरासिटामोल लेने से लिवर फेल हो सकता है।
* **शराब का सेवन बिल्कुल न करें।**
* **झाड़-फूंक या पारंपरिक नुस्खों में समय बर्बाद न करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Paracetamol/Painkillers khud se na lein:** Liver pehle se damaged aur swollen hota hai. Khud se paracetamol ya painkillers lene se liver completely fail (acute liver failure) ho sakta hai.
* **Sharab (Alcohol) bilkul na piyein:** Alcohol liver ke liye poison hai, ise peene se liver cells turant dead hone lagte hain.
* **Jhad-phook ke chakkar me na padein:** Piliya jhadwane ya herbal drops nose me dalne jaise andhvishwas se bachein. Isse treatment me deri hoti hai aur liver damage badhta hai.

## Medical & Antiviral Management
* **For Hepatitis B (Chronic):** Long-term treatment with oral nucleoside analogs like **Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF)** or **Entecavir** to suppress viral replication, prevent cirrhosis, and lower liver cancer risk.
* **For Hepatitis C (Chronic):** Curable with 12 weeks of oral Direct-Acting Antiviral (DAA) combinations: **Sofosbuvir + Daclatasvir** or **Sofosbuvir + Velpatasvir**.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories used in hepatitis management:*
1. **HCV Direct-Acting Antivirals:** Stop HCV replication (e.g., Sofosbuvir, Daclatasvir, Velpatasvir).
2. **HBV Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors:** Suppress HBV DNA (e.g., Tenofovir, Entecavir).
3. **Topical/Systemic Antipruritics:** Control skin itching caused by bile buildup (e.g., Cholestyramine).
4. **Hepatoprotective Supplements:** Support cell recovery (e.g., Ursodeoxycholic Acid - UDCA).

## Prevention of Hepatitis
* **Vaccination:**
   * **Hepatitis B Vaccine:** Given under government Universal Immunization Programme (birth dose, followed by doses at 6, 10, and 14 weeks).
   * **Hepatitis A Vaccine:** Available privately in a 2-dose schedule.
* **Safe Water & Sanitation:** Avoid open defecation; drink clean water.
* **Safe Injection Practices:** Ensure disposable needles are used. Screen blood donations for HBV and HCV.
* **Safe Sex:** Use latex condoms consistently.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Hepatitis
* **Fulminant Hepatic Failure:** Sudden complete liver shutdown within days (high risk in pregnant women infected with Hep E).
* **Decompensated Liver Cirrhosis:** Scarred liver causing ascites (fluid in abdomen), portal hypertension, and bleeding.
* **Hepatocellular Carcinoma:** Chronic Hepatitis B/C leading to primary liver cancer.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **A pregnant woman develops jaundice, extreme fatigue, or vomiting (Hepatitis E emergency).**
* **The patient shows sudden confusion, slurred speech, or flapping tremors of hands (hepatic encephalopathy).**
* **The patient vomits fresh blood or passes black, tarry stools (ruptured varices).**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Viral Hepatitis Control Program (NVHCP):** A national initiative under the NHM providing completely free screening, viral load diagnostics, lifelong free Tenofovir tablets for Hepatitis B, and free curable Sofosbuvir+Daclatasvir medicines for Hepatitis C at government treatment centers.
* **Universal Immunization Programme (UIP):** Provides free birth doses of the Hepatitis B vaccine to all newborns at birth to prevent chronic carrier states.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Offers generic liver support drugs, diuretics, and vitamins at very low prices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is viral hepatitis?**
   Viral hepatitis is an inflammation of the liver caused by one of five hepatotropic viruses (A, B, C, D, E), commonly presenting as jaundice.

2. **How does Hepatitis E spread and why is it dangerous in pregnancy?**
   Hepatitis E spreads through contaminated drinking water. In pregnant women (especially in the third trimester), HEV can cause rapid, fatal liver failure (fulminant hepatitis), risking both mother and baby.

3. **What is the difference between Hepatitis B and Hepatitis C?**
   Both spread through blood and sex. Hepatitis B can be prevented by a vaccine but is managed with lifelong suppressive drugs. Hepatitis C has no vaccine but is 100% curable with a 12-week tablet course.

4. **Where can I get free treatment for Hepatitis B and C in India?**
   Under the government's National Viral Hepatitis Control Program (NVHCP), free DNA/RNA PCR testing and free antiviral medicines are provided at all government medical colleges.

5. **Why should I avoid taking paracetamol when I have jaundice?**
   The liver is inflamed and cannot clear drugs. Taking paracetamol (even normal doses) puts a toxic load on the liver, which can trigger sudden, acute liver failure.

6. **What is the HBsAg test?**
   HBsAg (Hepatitis B Surface Antigen) is the primary blood screening test. A positive result confirms an active, current Hepatitis B infection.

7. **How does chronic hepatitis lead to liver cirrhosis?**
   Chronic infection (HBV/HCV) causes slow, continuous liver inflammation over 20-30 years, replacing healthy liver cells with non-functioning scar tissue (cirrhosis).

8. **Is there a vaccine for Hepatitis B?**
   Yes. The Hep B vaccine is highly effective and given free to all infants at birth, followed by doses at 6, 10, and 14 weeks under the government UIP.

9. **Can Hepatitis C be completely cured?**
   Yes. Modern Direct-Acting Antiviral (DAA) tablets like Sofosbuvir and Daclatasvir taken daily for 12 weeks cure over 95% of Hepatitis C patients.

10. **Why do hepatitis patients have dark urine and clay-colored stools?**
    The inflamed liver cannot excrete bilirubin into the intestines (making stool lose its normal brown color and look clay-like). The excess bilirubin accumulates in the blood and is filtered out by kidneys, turning urine dark yellow.

11. **How do I prevent Hepatitis A?**
    Drink boiled or chlorinated water, wash hands with soap after using the toilet and before preparing food, and get the Hepatitis A vaccine.

12. **Can a person get Hepatitis B from sharing food?**
    No. Hepatitis B and C do not spread through sharing food, water, cups, hugging, kissing, coughing, or sneezing. They only spread through blood-to-blood contact or sexual fluids.

13. **What is the role of the NVHCP program?**
    NVHCP is a national health program providing free screening, diagnostic viral load tests, and highly effective generic antiviral treatments for Hepatitis B and C.

14. **Why is alcohol strictly banned in hepatitis?**
    The liver is already struggling with viral inflammation. Alcohol is a direct hepatotoxin (liver poison) that accelerates liver cell death and scarring.

15. **What is the price of generic Sofosbuvir at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    While government centers distribute it completely free, generic Sofosbuvir (400mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a highly subsidized price compared to private pharmacies.

16. **What is Hepatic Encephalopathy?**
    It is a brain dysfunction that occurs when a damaged liver cannot filter toxic ammonia from the blood. The ammonia travels to the brain, causing confusion, tremors, and coma.

17. **Can a mother transmit Hepatitis B to her newborn baby?**
    Yes. Transmission during delivery is common. To prevent this, the newborn must receive the Hepatitis B vaccine birth dose and Hepatitis B Immunoglobulin (HBIG) within 12 hours of birth.

18. **Why does liver disease cause skin itching?**
    When bile flow is blocked, bile salts build up in the bloodstream and settle under the skin, irritating nerves and causing severe, chronic itching.

19. **What is the difference between acute and chronic hepatitis?**
    Acute hepatitis is a short-term infection (usually HAV/HEV) that resolves in a few weeks. Chronic hepatitis is a long-term infection (lasting over 6 months, usually HBV/HCV) causing progressive liver damage.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover liver transplant surgeries?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless surgery packages for liver transplants, donor surgeries, ICU stays, and post-operative immunosuppressive medications.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Sugarcane juice is the only medicine required to cure all types of hepatitis.
  * **Fact:** Sugarcane juice provides hydration and glucose, which supports the liver, but it cannot cure chronic viral infections like Hep B or C. They require antivirals.
* **Myth:** Piliya jhadwane (tantra-mantra) or putting oil in the nose cures hepatitis.
  * **Fact:** These are dangerous superstitions. Acute Hep A/E resolve on their own, making people believe in magic cures. True chronic Hep B/C will silently destroy the liver if not treated medically.
* **Myth:** Hepatitis B spreads through saliva during casual kissing.
  * **Fact:** The risk of Hep B transmission through casual kissing or sharing cups is extremely low, as the virus requires blood-to-blood or sexual contact to transmit.
* **Myth:** A person with chronic Hepatitis B can never get married or have children.
  * **Fact:** They can marry and have children safely. The partner must be vaccinated, and the newborn must receive the vaccine and HBIG at birth.
* **Myth:** All liver tonics in the market can cure chronic Hepatitis C.
  * **Fact:** Over-the-counter liver tonics cannot cure Hepatitis C. Only specific Direct-Acting Antiviral (DAA) drugs can kill the virus.
* **Myth:** If you had jaundice once, you are immune to all types of hepatitis.
  * **Fact:** Having Hep A only protects you from Hep A. You can still contract Hep B, C, or E. Get vaccinated for Hep B.
* **Myth:** Hepatitis patients should eat a zero-salt diet with only boiled white food.
  * **Fact:** A zero-salt diet is only required in advanced cirrhosis with fluid accumulation (ascites). For normal hepatitis, a balanced, low-fat diet is sufficient.
* **Myth:** Hepatitis C is a hereditary disease passed down generations.
  * **Fact:** Hepatitis C is an infectious viral disease spread through blood contact. It is not hereditary or genetic.
* **Myth:** Healthy-looking people cannot have chronic Hepatitis B.
  * **Fact:** Chronic Hep B is a silent infection. People can carry and transmit the virus for 20 years without showing any symptoms, until cirrhosis develops. Test HBsAg.
* **Myth:** Traditional herbal medicine is 100% safe for an inflamed liver.
  * **Fact:** Many herbal preparations contain heavy metals or hepatotoxic compounds that can overload and destroy an already struggling liver. Avoid self-medicating.
