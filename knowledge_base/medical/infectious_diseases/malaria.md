# Malaria (Malaria Fever) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Plasmodium Parasite and Chloroquine Guide)

## Overview

### English
Malaria is a life-threatening, mosquito-borne parasitic infection caused by protozoan parasites of the genus **Plasmodium** and transmitted by the bite of an infected female **Anopheles** mosquito. In India, the primary causative species are **Plasmodium vivax** (causes relapsing malaria due to dormant liver stages) and **Plasmodium falciparum** (causes severe, fatal malaria, including cerebral malaria). The classic symptom is cyclic high fever accompanied by violent shaking chills, followed by intense sweating. Diagnosis is confirmed via rapid diagnostic tests (RDT) or peripheral blood smear (thick/thin smears) microscopy. Treatment utilizes **Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT)** for falciparum malaria and Chloroquine + Primaquine for vivax malaria.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
Malaria is a parasitic mosquito-borne infection. Symptoms include cyclic high fever, rigors (shivering), profuse sweating, and anemia. First aid focuses on cold sponging, hydration, and Paracetamol. Diagnostics utilize peripheral blood smear microscopy or Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDT). Management uses ACT (Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy) or Chloroquine with Primaquine.

---

## Definition
* **Malaria:** An acute febrile illness caused by Plasmodium parasites (P. falciparum, P. vivax, P. malariae, P. ovale).
* **Cerebral Malaria:** A severe clinical complication of P. falciparum infection where parasitized red blood cells block the tiny capillaries in the brain, leading to encephalopathy, seizures, and coma.
* **Radical Cure:** The complete eradication of both blood-stage parasites and dormant liver-stage parasites (hypnozoites in P. vivax) using Primaquine to prevent relapse.

---

## Parasite Life Cycle & Red Blood Cell Destruction
The parasite multiplies in the liver and blood:
1. **Liver Stage (Exo-erythrocytic):** The mosquito injects sporozoites into the blood, which travel to the liver, multiply, and turn into merozoites. In P. vivax, some remain dormant as **hypnozoites** (relapse source).
2. **Blood Stage (Erythrocytic):** Merozoites burst out of the liver and infect red blood cells (RBCs). They multiply inside RBCs, causing them to rupture (lyse) simultaneously. **The synchronous rupture of RBCs releases toxic waste (hemozoin) into the blood, triggering the classic cyclic chills and high fever.**
3. **Severe Anemia:** Massive destruction of RBCs leads to severe anemia (low hemoglobin).

## Symptoms of Malaria

### English
* Cyclic high fever ($103^\circ\text{F} - 105^\circ\text{F}$) starting with severe cold shakes (rigors/chills); fever peaks for 2-6 hours, followed by profuse sweating and a rapid drop in body temperature; severe headache, muscle pain, and vomiting; fatigue and pale skin (anemia); yellowing of eyes (mild jaundice from RBC rupture).

## First Aid & Initial Care

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Perform cold sponging** immediately on the forehead, neck, and armpits using normal tap water to reduce dangerously high fever ($>103^\circ\text{F}$).
* **Ensure adequate hydration** by drinking ORS solution, warm water, and soups to replace fluids lost during the sweating phase.
* **Get a blood test (RDT/Blood Smear)** done immediately at the first sign of shivering fever. Early diagnosis prevents progression to cerebral malaria.
* **Take Paracetamol** for fever.

#### Hindi
* **ठंडे पानी की पट्टियां रखें:** सिर और शरीर पर पट्टियां रखकर बुखार कम करें।
* **हाइड्रेशन बनाए रखें:** पर्याप्त पानी और ओआरएस (ORS) पिएं।
* **तुरंत खून की जांच कराएं (RDT/Blood Smear)।**

#### Hinglish
* **Cold Sponging karein:** Shivering wala fever aane par sir aur body par normal tap water ki patti (sponging) karein taaki temperature down ho ske.
* **Hydration maintain karein:** Sweating phase me dher sara fluid loss hota hai. ORS aur saaf paani pilayein.
* **Blood Test turant karayein:** Bukhar aate hi nearest lab me Malaria RDT ya Blood Smear test karayein taaki early stage me ilaaj ho sake.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT self-medicate with Chloroquine or ACT.** Taking incorrect dosages of antimalarials leads to drug resistance and treatment failure.
* **Do NOT use heavy blankets during the hot/sweating phase.** Cover the patient with a light sheet to allow body heat to escape.
* **Do NOT delay hospital transport** if the patient is confused, vomiting continuously, or cannot swallow tablets.

#### Hindi
* **खुद से मलेरिया की दवा न खाएं:** गलत खुराक से दवा का बेअसर (resistance) होने का खतरा रहता है।
* **तेज बुखार में मोटे कंबल न ओढ़ें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Chloroquine khud se na lein:** Bina test aur doctor ki prescription ke chloroquine ya artesunate tablets na khayein. Adhi-adhuri dose lene se parasite drug-resistant ho jata hai.
* **Mote kambal na odhayein:** Sweating aur high fever phase me heavy kambal na odhayein, body ko normal air lagne dein taaki heat release ho sake.

## Medical & Antimalarial Management (WHO & NVBDCP Guidelines)

### For Plasmodium Vivax Malaria

#### English
* Treated with oral **Chloroquine** ($25$ mg/kg total dose divided over 3 days) to clear blood-stage parasites, followed by **Primaquine** (0.25 mg/kg daily for 14 days) to kill dormant liver stages (hypnozoites) and prevent relapse. *Contraindicated in G6PD deficiency.*

#### Hindi
* तीन दिन तक **क्लोरोक्वीन (Chloroquine)** देना, उसके बाद लिवर से वायरस साफ करने के लिए 14 दिन तक **प्राइमाक्वीन (Primaquine)** देना।

#### Hinglish
* P. vivax me 3 din tak **Chloroquine** tablet di jati hai, aur liver me chhupe parasites (hypnozoites) ko maarne ke liye 14 din tak **Primaquine** di jati hai taaki relapse (dobara bukhar aana) na ho.

### For Plasmodium Falciparum Malaria (Severe)

#### English
* Treated with **Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT)** (Artesunate + Sulfadoxine-Pyrimethamine) orally for 3 days. Severe falciparum malaria requires intravenous (IV) **Artesunate** injections in the ICU.

#### Hindi
* **एसीटी (ACT - Artesunate)** थेरेपी देना। गंभीर मामलों में IV आर्टिसुनेट (Artesunate) इंजेक्शन देना।

#### Hinglish
* P. falciparum me 3 din ka **ACT (Artesunate + Sulfadoxine-Pyrimethamine)** course diya jata hai. Agar severe ya cerebral malaria ho, to hospital me immediate IV Artesunate injections lagaye jate hain.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories used in malaria management:*
1. **Artemisinin Derivatives:** Rapid blood-stage parasite killers (e.g., Artesunate, Artemether, Lumefantrine).
2. **Quinoline Derivatives:** Classic antimalarials (e.g., Chloroquine, Mefloquine, Quinine).
3. **8-Aminoquinolines:** Eradicate liver hypnozoites (e.g., Primaquine). *Must check G6PD enzyme status before giving.*
4. **Antipyretics:** Reduce fever (e.g., Paracetamol).

---

## Prevention of Malaria
* **Vector Control:** Eliminate standing water in puddles, gutters, and pots around the house where Anopheles mosquitoes breed (they breed in stagnant clean/dirty water).
* **Bed Nets:** Sleep under Long-Lasting Insecticidal Nets (LLINs) or mosquito nets.
* **Repellents:** Use DEET or picaridin repellents on exposed skin.
* **Chemoprophylaxis:** Vets or travelers to high-risk malaria areas can take weekly Mefloquine or daily Doxycycline under doctor guidance.

---

## Possible Complications of Severe Malaria
* **Cerebral Malaria:** Parasites blocking brain blood vessels, causing convulsions, coma, and death.
* **Blackwater Fever:** Massive intravascular hemolysis (RBC rupture) releasing hemoglobin into urine, turning urine dark black and causing acute kidney failure.
* **Severe Hypoglycemia:** Critical drop in blood sugar, often worsened by Quinine treatment.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient develops sudden confusion, extreme drowsiness, fits (seizures), or goes into a coma (cerebral malaria).**
* **Shortness of breath, rapid breathing, and lips turning blue (ARDS/lung congestion).**
* **The patient passes dark black or red urine (hemoglobinuria).**
* **Continuous vomiting and inability to keep fluids or tablets down.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme (NVBDCP):** Distributes free insecticidal bed nets (LLINs) in endemic zones, coordinates DDT/malathion spraying, and ensures free availability of RDT kits and antimalarial drugs at all PHCs/CHCs.
* **Malaria Elimination Demonstration Project (MEDP):** Targets zero malaria transmission in India by 2030.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless packages for severe malaria ICU care, IV Artesunate injections, blood transfusions for severe anemia, and kidney dialysis for blackwater fever.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Offers generic Paracetamol, Chloroquine, and supportive multi-vitamins at very low generic prices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is malaria?**
   Malaria is a life-threatening blood disease caused by Plasmodium parasites, transmitted by the bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito.

2. **Why does malaria cause shivering chills and fever?**
   The parasites multiply inside red blood cells until they burst simultaneously. This rupture releases toxic parasite waste (hemozoin) into the blood, causing sudden shivering chills and high fever.

3. **What are P. vivax and P. falciparum?**
   P. vivax is the most common malaria species in India, causing relapses due to dormant liver stages. P. falciparum is the most dangerous species, causing severe complications like cerebral malaria.

4. **What is cerebral malaria?**
   Cerebral malaria is a severe complication of *P. falciparum* where sticky, infected red blood cells block the tiny capillaries supplying the brain, causing seizures, coma, and death.

5. **What is the 14-day Primaquine course for P. vivax?**
   Primaquine is a medicine that kills the dormant parasite stages (hypnozoites) in the liver. A complete 14-day course is mandatory to prevent the malaria fever from relapsing months later.

6. **Why must G6PD status be checked before taking Primaquine?**
   Primaquine can trigger massive, acute red blood cell destruction (hemolysis) in patients with a genetic G6PD enzyme deficiency, causing life-threatening anemia.

7. **Where can I get free malaria tests and medicines in India?**
   Confidential rapid antigen tests (RDT) and all antimalarials (ACT/Chloroquine) are provided completely free at all government PHCs, Community Health Centers, and civil hospitals.

8. **What is Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT)?**
   ACT is a combination of two antimalarials (an artemisinin derivative and a long-acting partner drug) used to treat falciparum malaria to prevent drug resistance.

9. **How do I prevent Anopheles mosquito breeding?**
   Anopheles mosquitoes breed in standing water. Empty, drain, or cover all water containers, pots, coolers, and puddles around your house weekly.

10. **Why does Quinine cause a drop in blood sugar?**
    Quinine (an older antimalarial) stimulates pancreatic beta cells to release excess insulin, causing severe hypoglycemia (low blood sugar), which must be monitored.

11. **Can a pregnant woman take Chloroquine?**
    Yes, Chloroquine is safe for pregnant women. However, Primaquine is strictly contraindicated during pregnancy due to the risk of fetal G6PD deficiency and hemolysis.

12. **What is the difference between a thick and thin blood smear?**
    A thick blood smear uses a larger drop of blood and is used to detect if parasites are present. A thin smear is used to identify the specific Plasmodium species and calculate parasite density.

13. **How does mosquito netting protect against malaria?**
    Anopheles mosquitoes bite mostly at night. Sleeping under long-lasting insecticidal nets (LLINs) kills mosquitoes on contact and prevents them from biting you.

14. **What is "Blackwater Fever"?**
    It is a complication of severe falciparum malaria where massive RBC destruction releases hemoglobin into the bloodstream, which is filtered by kidneys, turning the urine dark black/red.

15. **What is the price of generic Chloroquine and Primaquine in India?**
    Generic Chloroquine and Primaquine tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹20 per strip, compared to ₹80+ for branded versions.

16. **Why does malaria cause jaundice?**
    The massive destruction of red blood cells by the parasites releases excess bilirubin into the blood, causing yellowing of the skin and eyes (hemolytic jaundice).

17. **Can a traveler take daily medicine to prevent malaria?**
    Yes. Chemoprophylaxis involves taking weekly Mefloquine or daily Doxycycline starting before travel to high-risk areas, continuing during the stay, and for 4 weeks after leaving.

18. **How does malaria cause spleen enlargement (splenomegaly)?**
    The spleen acts as a blood filter. In malaria, the spleen works overtime to filter and destroy millions of damaged red blood cells, causing it to swell and enlarge.

19. **What is the role of the NVBDCP program?**
    NVBDCP is a national health program that conducts vector control, distributes insecticidal nets (LLINs), monitors outbreaks, and provides free diagnostics and antimalarials.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover cerebral malaria ICU care?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for severe malaria hospitalizations, pediatric ICU stays, ventilator support, and emergency dialysis.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Eating raw papaya or drinking goat milk cures malaria instantly.
  * **Fact:** Goat milk and papaya have no anti-parasitic properties against Plasmodium. Malaria requires specific antimalarials (Chloroquine/ACT) to clear the blood infection.
* **Myth:** Malaria mosquitoes only breed in dirty sewer drains.
  * **Fact:** Anopheles mosquitoes can breed in clean, stagnant rainwater puddles, coolers, and storage pots. Keep all water containers covered.
* **Myth:** If the fever drops after 2 days, you can stop the Chloroquine tablets.
  * **Fact:** You must complete the full 3-day course of Chloroquine and the 14-day course of Primaquine. Stopping early causes the parasite to return stronger and build drug resistance.
* **Myth:** Malaria and typhoid are the exact same disease.
  * **Fact:** Malaria is a parasitic infection spread by mosquitoes. Typhoid is a bacterial infection spread by contaminated food and water. They need different diagnostics and treatments.
* **Myth:** You can get malaria from drinking contaminated water.
  * **Fact:** Malaria does not spread through drinking water. It is transmitted only through the bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito.
* **Myth:** Tying a key to the body prevents malaria fever.
  * **Fact:** Sucking or tying keys on the body is a useless superstition. It has no effect on blood parasites. Use insecticidal bed nets.
* **Myth:** Severe cerebral malaria only affects old people.
  * **Fact:** Children under 5 years and pregnant women have the lowest immunity and are at the highest risk of developing fatal cerebral malaria.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a lemon will cure malaria fever.
  * **Fact:** Lemon has vitamin C but cannot kill Plasmodium parasites in the blood. Use ACT or Chloroquine.
* **Myth:** A normal blood test rules out malaria completely.
  * **Fact:** If the blood sample was taken during the dormant phase when parasites are inside the liver, the blood smear might be negative. Repeat the test during the shivering fever phase.
* **Myth:** Malaria has been completely eliminated from India.
  * **Fact:** While cases have dropped, malaria remains endemic in many states, especially in forested and rural areas during monsoons. Target is elimination by 2030.
