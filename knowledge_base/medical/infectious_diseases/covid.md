# COVID-19 (Corona) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (SARS-CoV-2 and Oxygen Monitoring Guide)

## Overview

### English
COVID-19 (Coronavirus Disease 2019) is a highly infectious respiratory illness caused by the **Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2)** virus. It primarily spreads through respiratory droplets and aerosols. Symptoms range from mild fever, dry cough, and loss of taste/smell (anosmia) to severe viral pneumonia, hypoxemia, and Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS) requiring mechanical ventilation. Clinical management focuses on symptom control and home isolation for mild cases, while severe cases require oxygen therapy, corticosteroids, and antivirals. Vaccination remains the most effective preventive measure globally.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
COVID-19 is a viral respiratory disease. Symptoms include fever, dry cough, anosmia, and dyspnea. First aid targets home isolation, paracetamol for fever, and continuous monitoring of $SpO_2$ using a pulse oximeter. If $SpO_2$ drops below 92%, seek emergency hospital care. Diagnostics use RT-PCR or Rapid Antigen tests. Management utilizes antivirals, steroids, and oxygen therapy.

---

## Definition
* **COVID-19:** A respiratory disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, first identified in December 2019.
* **Anosmia / Ageusia:** The temporary loss of the sense of smell (anosmia) and sense of taste (ageusia), which are highly specific diagnostic markers for COVID-19.
* **ARDS (Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome):** A life-threatening lung injury where fluid builds up in the lungs' air sacs, preventing oxygen from passing into the bloodstream.

---

## Pathology of Lung Damage & Hypoxemia
The virus targets lung cells and disrupts oxygen exchange:
1. **ACE2 Receptor Binding:** The SARS-CoV-2 spike protein binds to ACE2 receptors on Type II alveolar cells in the lungs, entering and destroying them.
2. **Cytokine Storm:** In severe cases, the immune system overreacts, releasing massive inflammatory proteins (cytokines). This causes severe leakage in lung capillaries, filling the alveoli with inflammatory fluid.
3. **Happy Hypoxia:** A dangerous phenomenon where a patient's oxygen level drops dangerously low ($< 85\%$), but they feel comfortable and do not experience breathlessness, until sudden respiratory collapse occurs. *This is why regular monitoring with a pulse oximeter is mandatory.*

## Home Care & Oxygen Monitoring Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Isolate immediately** in a well-ventilated single room with a separate bathroom for 7 to 10 days. Wear a triple-layered N95 mask.
* **Monitor Oxygen Saturation ($SpO_2$)** every 6 hours using a Pulse Oximeter. Normal $SpO_2$ is $95\% - 100\%$.
* **Perform Proning:** If $SpO_2$ falls to $93\%-94\%$, lie face down on your stomach (prone position) with pillows under your chest and shins. This improves lung ventilation and raises oxygen levels.
* **Stay hydrated** by drinking warm water, soups, and taking Paracetamol for fever.

#### Hindi
* **तुरंत होम आइसोलेशन में जाएं:** हवादार कमरे में रहें और N95 मास्क पहनें।
* **ऑक्सीजन की निगरानी करें:** पल्स ऑक्सीमीटर से हर 6 घंटे में SpO2 मापें।
* **प्रोनिंग (Proning) करें:** ऑक्सीजन का स्तर (93-94%) गिरने पर पेट के बल लेटें (pronning)।

#### Hinglish
* **Immediate Home Isolation:** Apne aap ko ek well-ventilated room me 7-10 days ke liye isolate karein. N95 mask zaroori pehnein.
* **Pulse Oximeter use karein:** Har 6 ghante me digital pulse oximeter se **SpO2 (oxygen level)** check karein. Normal SpO2 95% se upar hona chahiye.
* **Prone Position (Proning) letayein:** Agar oxygen level 93-94% ke paas drop ho, to stomach (pet) ke bal letayein (Proning). Chest aur pairon ke niche pillow lagayein. Isse lungs ki capacity badhti hai.
* **Warm liquids piyein:** Gunguna paani piyein aur bukhar ke liye Paracetamol lein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT take oral steroids** (like Dexamethasone, Prednisolone) at home for mild symptoms. Taking steroids early in the viral phase suppresses immunity, allowing the virus to multiply rapidly, raising the risk of fatal fungal infections (Black Fungus/Mucormycosis).
* **Do NOT rush to buy oxygen cylinders** or concentrate on self-medication without clinical advice.
* **Do NOT ignore a drop in $SpO_2$ below 92%** even if the patient feels completely fine (Happy Hypoxia).

#### Hindi
* **शुरुआत में स्टेरॉयड (Steroids) न लें:** बिना डॉक्टर के शुरुआती दिनों में डेक्सामेथासोन जैसी दवाएं लेने से ब्लैक फंगस (Mucormycosis) का खतरा बढ़ता है।
* **SpO2 92% से कम होने पर लापरवाही न करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Steroid tablets khud se na lein:** Bukhar ke pehle 5-7 dino me bina doctor ki salah ke Dexamethasone ya Prednisolone tablets bilkul na lein. Steroid immunity daba dete hain jisse virus tezi se badhta hai aur **Black Fungus (Mucormycosis)** ka khatra ho jata hai.
* **Happy Hypoxia ignore na karein:** Agar SpO2 level 92% se niche jaye par patient comfortable dikhe, tab bhi use ignore na karein, ye happy hypoxia hai jo fatal ho sakta hai.

## Medical & Hospital Management

### Oxygen Therapy & Ventilation

#### English
* Severe COVID-19 ($SpO_2 < 90\%$) requires hospitalization for non-invasive ventilation (NIV), High-Flow Nasal Cannula (HFNC) oxygen therapy, or mechanical intubation in the ICU.

#### Hindi
* गंभीर मामलों में अस्पताल में ऑक्सीजन बेड या वेंटिलेटर पर भर्ती करना।

#### Hinglish
* Severe cases (SpO2 < 90%) me hospital me high-flow oxygen, HFNC machine, ya ICU me mechanical ventilator support zaroori hota hai.

### Pharmacological Therapy

#### English
* **Antivirals:** Paxlovid (Nirmatrelvir/Ritonavir) or Remdesivir IV infusions are given within the first 5 days for patients at high risk of progression.
* **Corticosteroids:** Administered clinically (IV Dexamethasone) only for patients requiring supplemental oxygen, to control the cytokine storm.

#### Hindi
* मध्यम से गंभीर मामलों में एंटीवायरल (Remdesivir) और स्टेरॉयड (Dexamethasone) इंजेक्शन लगाना।

#### Hinglish
* Clinical protocol ke tehat hospital me antivirals (Remdesivir/Paxlovid) aur steroids (Dexamethasone) ke injections aur blood thinners (Heparin) diye jaate hain.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories used in COVID-19 management:*
1. **Antivirals:** Inhibit viral replication (e.g., Nirmatrelvir/Ritonavir [Paxlovid], Remdesivir IV, Molnupiravir).
2. **Corticosteroids:** Suppress hyper-inflammation in severe cases (e.g., Dexamethasone, Methylprednisolone).
3. **Antipyretics:** Manage fever (e.g., Paracetamol).
4. **Anticoagulants:** Prevent micro-clots in lungs (e.g., Low Molecular Weight Heparin - LMWH, Enoxaparin).
5. **Monoclonal Antibodies:** Neutralize the spike protein (e.g., Casirivimab/Imdevimab).

## Prevention of COVID-19
* **Vaccination:** Complete the primary series and booster doses of COVID-19 vaccines (e.g., Covishield, Covaxin, or Corbevax).
* **Masking & Distance:** Wear N95 masks in crowded indoor spaces; maintain 6 feet distance from others.
* **Hand Hygiene:** Wash hands with soap and water frequently or use alcohol-based sanitizers.
* **Ventilation:** Keep indoor windows open to dilute viral aerosols.

---

## Possible Complications of COVID-19
* **ARDS (Respiratory Failure):** Severe damage to lung tissue requiring ventilator support.
* **Thromboembolism:** Blood clots forming in veins (DVT) or traveling to lungs (Pulmonary Embolism), causing sudden cardiac death.
* **Long COVID:** Chronic fatigue, breathlessness, brain fog, and muscle aches lasting for months after virus clearance.
* **Mucormycosis (Black Fungus):** Rare, fatal fungal infection of sinuses/eyes/brain, triggered by steroid abuse in diabetic patients.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The pulse oximeter shows $SpO_2$ consistently below 92% on room air.**
* **The patient has severe difficulty breathing, chest pain, or lips and face turn blue.**
* **The patient develops confusion, extreme drowsiness, or cannot wake up.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is COVID-19?**
   COVID-19 is a highly infectious respiratory disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, spreading through airborne droplets.

2. **What is a normal oxygen level on a pulse oximeter?**
   A normal $SpO_2$ reading for a healthy individual is between 95% and 100%. A reading below 92% is an emergency.

3. **What is "Happy Hypoxia"?**
   It is a condition where a patient’s blood oxygen levels drop dangerously low, but they do not feel breathless or uncomfortable, leading to sudden, unexpected lung collapse.

4. **How does "Proning" help during COVID-19?**
   Proning (lying face down on your stomach) helps expand the back areas of your lungs, improves oxygen flow into the blood, and helps raise $SpO_2$ levels.

5. **Why is taking steroids at home dangerous for mild COVID-19?**
   Taking steroids early in a mild infection suppresses the immune system, allowing the virus to multiply. In diabetics, it raises blood sugar, triggering fatal Black Fungus (Mucormycosis).

6. **What is the difference between RT-PCR and Rapid Antigen Test?**
   RT-PCR is a highly accurate molecular test that detects the virus’s genetic material (takes 12-24 hours). Rapid Antigen Test checks for viral proteins and gives results in 15 minutes but is less sensitive.

7. **Where can BPL cardholders get free COVID-19 ICU care in India?**
   Eligible patients can get free cashless ICU stays, ventilator support, and medications at all government and empaneled private hospitals under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme.

8. **What are the symptoms of "Black Fungus" (Mucormycosis)?**
   Symptoms include one-sided facial swelling, nasal blockage with black discharge, severe headache, eye pain, drooping eyelid, or loss of vision.

9. **What is "Long COVID"?**
   Long COVID refers to symptoms like chronic fatigue, shortness of breath, joint pain, and memory issues (brain fog) that persist for weeks or months after recovering from the virus.

10. **Why does COVID-19 increase the risk of blood clots?**
    The virus causes severe inflammation in the lining of blood vessels (endothelial cells), making platelets sticky and triggering micro-clots in the lungs, brain, and legs.

11. **Are COVID-19 booster doses necessary?**
    Yes. Immunity from the primary vaccine doses declines over months. A booster dose reactivates antibodies, maintaining protection against new variants.

12. **What is the WHO-recommended isolation period for mild cases?**
    WHO recommends isolating for at least 7-10 days from the start of symptoms. You can end isolation if you are fever-free for 3 days without using medicine.

13. **How do I clean my pulse oximeter?**
    Wipe the finger sensor inside the oximeter with a clean cloth dampened with 70% isopropyl alcohol after each use. Let it dry completely.

14. **Why is Paxlovid prescribed for COVID-19?**
    Paxlovid is an oral antiviral combination of Nirmatrelvir and Ritonavir. Taken within 5 days of symptoms, it blocks viral protein cutting, reducing hospitalizations by 89%.

15. **What is the price of generic Paracetamol and Vitamin C at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Paracetamol (500mg/650mg) and chewable Vitamin C tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹3 to ₹6 per strip.

16. **Can a pregnant woman get vaccinated against COVID-19?**
    Yes. Pregnant women are at higher risk for severe COVID-19 complications. Vaccination is safe and highly recommended to protect both mother and baby.

17. **What is the role of Remdesivir in COVID-19?**
    Remdesivir is an intravenous antiviral drug. It mimics viral genetic blocks, stopping replication. It is clinically used to shorten recovery time in hospitalized patients.

18. **Why should we not take antibiotics like Azithromycin for COVID-19?**
    COVID-19 is a viral disease. Antibiotics only kill bacteria. Overusing Azithromycin does not cure COVID-19 and leads to global antibiotic resistance.

19. **How does N95 mask protect against COVID-19?**
    An N95 respirator filters at least 95% of airborne particles and aerosols, providing much higher protection than loose surgical or cloth masks.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover post-COVID lung fibrosis care?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for post-COVID lung damage therapy, oxygen support rehabilitation, and specialist consultations.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Drinking warm alcohol or cow urine will protect you from COVID-19.
  * **Fact:** Alcohol and cow urine have no effect on the SARS-CoV-2 virus. They can cause severe poisoning or stomach damage. Use N95 masks and vaccines.
* **Myth:** If you can hold your breath for 10 seconds without coughing, you do not have COVID-19.
  * **Fact:** This is a fake test. Many people with active COVID-19 can hold their breath, and people with asthma cannot. Only a PCR swab test confirms infection.
* **Myth:** COVID-19 vaccines contain metallic chips to track citizens.
  * **Fact:** This is a ridiculous conspiracy theory. Vaccines contain only viral proteins or mRNA, lipids, and salts to stimulate natural immunity.
* **Myth:** Inhaling steam at 100°C will kill the virus inside your lungs.
  * **Fact:** High-temperature steam cannot reach the lungs without burning your airways (trachea). It does not cure COVID-19 and can cause severe throat burns.
* **Myth:** COVID-19 is just a normal, mild winter flu.
  * **Fact:** While many have mild symptoms, COVID-19 is far more infectious, has a higher mortality rate, and causes severe systemic blood clotting compared to normal flu.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a camphor tablet (kapoor) will raise your oxygen level instantly.
  * **Fact:** Camphor has no effect on blood oxygen levels. It only has a strong smell that clears nasal passages temporarily. If oxygen drops, use proning or clinical oxygen.
* **Myth:** Only elderly people get severe COVID-19.
  * **Fact:** Young adults with no underlying health issues can develop severe cytokine storms and die from ARDS. Everyone should take precautions.
* **Myth:** Taking daily double doses of vitamin C prevents COVID-19 completely.
  * **Fact:** Vitamins support overall immunity, but they cannot block the virus from entering your lungs. Only vaccination and masks prevent infection.
* **Myth:** You cannot get COVID-19 twice.
  * **Fact:** Re-infection is common because the virus mutates into new variants (like Omicron) that can bypass antibodies from past infections.
* **Myth:** Using hand sanitizer is better than washing hands with soap.
  * **Fact:** Washing hands with soap and water for 20 seconds is superior, as soap physically breaks down the virus's outer membrane and washes it away.
