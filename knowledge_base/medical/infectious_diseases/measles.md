# Measles (Khasra) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Measles Virus and Vitamin A Guide)

## Overview

### English
Measles, clinically known as Rubeola and commonly called Khasra in India, is a highly contagious viral respiratory infection caused by the **Measles Virus** (a morbillivirus of the paramyxovirus family). It spreads rapidly via airborne droplets and aerosols, remaining active in the air for up to 2 hours. The disease presents with fever, dry cough, coryza (runny nose), conjunctivitis (red watery eyes), the pathognomonic **Koplik spots** inside the mouth, and a characteristic maculopapular rash spreading from head to toe. A key therapeutic intervention is **Vitamin A supplementation**, which reduces mortality and blindness in children. The **MMR/MR vaccine** provides excellent lifelong immunity.

## Quick Summary
Measles is a highly contagious viral disease. Symptoms include high fever, coryza, conjunctivitis, Koplik spots, and a descending red rash. First aid focuses on isolation, hydration, and Vitamin A supplementation. Never use aspirin in children. Diagnostics are based on clinical exam and IgM serology. Management is supportive.

---

## Definition
* **Measles (Rubeola):** An acute, highly contagious viral disease characterized by fever, respiratory symptoms, Koplik spots, and a maculopapular rash.
* **Koplik Spots:** Small, blue-white spots with a red background appearing on the inner cheek lining (buccal mucosa) near the molars, appearing 1-2 days *before* the skin rash. They are a definitive diagnostic sign of measles.
* **SSPE (Subacute Sclerosing Panencephalitis):** A rare, fatal, progressive brain inflammation that develops 7-10 years after a measles infection due to persistent viral mutation in brain cells.

---

## Clinical Progression & The "3 C's"
Measles follows a specific clinical progression timeline:
1. **Incubation Phase:** 10-14 days after virus exposure with no symptoms.
2. **Prodromal Phase (Days 1 - 4):** High fever accompanied by the classic **3 C's**: **C**ough (dry), **C**oryza (runny nose), and **C**onjunctivitis (red, watery eyes). Inside the mouth, **Koplik spots** appear.
3. **Eruptive Phase (Days 4 - 8):** A flat, red rash (maculopapular) begins behind the ears and on the forehead. It spreads downward over the face, neck, trunk, and limbs to the hands and feet. The fever peaks at this time.
4. **Resolution Phase:** The rash fades in the order it appeared, leaving a brown staining and fine peeling (desquamation) of the skin.

## First Aid & Vitamin A Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Isolate the child immediately** in a separate room for at least 4 days after the rash appears to prevent spreading this highly contagious virus.
* **Administer Vitamin A supplementation immediately.** WHO mandates 2 doses of oral Vitamin A to all children diagnosed with measles (Dose: 50,000 IU for infants $<6$ months; 100,000 IU for 6-12 months; 200,000 IU for children $>12$ months, given on Day 1 and Day 2). This prevents blindness and halves the risk of fatal pneumonia.
* **Keep the patient hydrated** with water, breast milk (for infants), and ORS.
* **Protect eyes from bright light** (photophobia is common) by dimming the room or using sunglasses.

#### Hindi
* **मरीज को अलग रखें (Isolation):** चकत्ते दिखने के कम से कम 4 दिन बाद तक आइसोलेशन में रखें।
* **विटामिन A (Vitamin A) की खुराक दें:** खसरे की पुष्टि होते ही तुरंत डॉक्टर की सलाह पर विटामिन ए की दो खुराकें दें।
* **हाइड्रेशन बनाए रखें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Immediate Isolation:** Rash aane ke kam se kam 4 din baad tak bachhe ko alag room me rakhein taaki virus ghar ke baki bachho ya neighbors me na phaile.
* **Vitamin A dose dein (Sabse important):** Khashra confirm hone par doctor ki salah se immediate **Vitamin A** ki do oral doses lagatar 2 din dein (day 1 aur day 2). Vitamin A eyes ko damage hone se bachata hai aur death rate ko 50% kam kar deta hai.
* **Fluids aur ORS dein:** Bukhar me hydration maintain karne ke liye ORS aur gunguna pani pilayein.
* **Aankhon ko tez roshni se bachayein:** Room me dim light rakhein kyunki laal aankhon me roshni chubhti hai (photophobia).

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT give Aspirin to children.** Viral infections combined with aspirin cause the fatal **Reye's Syndrome** (brain and liver failure). Use Paracetamol only.
* **Do NOT apply home-made herbal pastes** or oils on the rash, which can cause skin breakdown and bacterial infection.
* **Do NOT skip the second dose of Vitamin A.**

#### Hindi
* **बच्चों को एस्पिरिन (Aspirin) कभी न दें।**
* **चकत्तों पर तेल या कोई देशी लेप न लगाएं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Aspirin (aspirin) tablet bilkul na dein:** Buckhar kam karne ke liye bachho ko aspirin dene se liver aur brain damage (Reye's syndrome) ho sakta hai. Bukhar ke liye sirf Paracetamol syrup dein.
* **Rashes par paste na lagayein:** Dano par haldi, tel ya koi desi lep na lagayein, isse skin me infection ho sakta hai.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Clinical Presentation:** The presence of the "3 C's," Koplik spots, and a descending rash is often sufficient for diagnosis.
* **Measles IgM Antibody Test:** An ELISA test on a blood sample taken within 3 days of rash onset confirms acute infection.
* **Throat/Nose Swab RT-PCR:** Done to detect viral RNA for molecular surveillance.

---

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to measles management:*
1. **Vitamin A (Retinol):** Mandatory pediatric mucosal repair agent and immune booster.
2. **Analgesics & Antipyretics:** Lower fever and relieve body aches (e.g., Paracetamol). *Do not use Aspirin.*
3. **Topical Soothing Agents:** Manage dry skin (e.g., Calamine lotion).
4. **Antitussives:** Control severe dry cough.

## Prevention of Measles
* **Measles-Rubella (MR) Vaccine:** Part of the Indian government's routine Universal Immunization Programme (UIP).
   * **1st Dose:** Given at 9 to 12 months of age.
   * **2nd Dose:** Given at 16 to 24 months of age.
* **MMR Vaccine:** Provides additional protection against Mumps (given privately).
* **Post-Exposure Immunization:** Non-vaccinated exposed individuals can get the MR vaccine within 72 hours of exposure to prevent or reduce infection.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Measles
* **Otitis Media:** Middle ear infection, which can cause permanent hearing loss.
* **Severe Laryngotracheobronchitis (Croup):** Inflammation of the airways causing breathing difficulty.
* **Subacute Sclerosing Panencephalitis (SSPE):** A rare, fatal, degenerative brain disorder developing years after infection.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The child has rapid, shallow breathing, chest retractions (saans lete waqt pasli chalna), or grunting sounds.**
* **The child develops a high fever, severe headache, stiff neck, extreme drowsiness, or seizures (encephalitis).**
* **The child has severe persistent diarrhea, is unable to drink fluids, or has sunken eyes (severe dehydration).**
* **The child develops white patches on the cornea of the eye or starts losing vision.**

---

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **Universal Immunization Programme (UIP):** Provides free MR (Measles-Rubella) vaccines to all children at 9 months and 16-24 months at all government health sub-centers and Anganwadis.
* **MR Elimination Campaign:** A massive countrywide campaign by the MoHFW to eliminate measles and control congenital rubella syndrome (CRS) through vaccination drives.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless hospitalization packages for severe measles complications (severe pneumonia, croup, encephalitis) and pediatric ICU care.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Offers generic Paracetamol syrup, Vitamin A capsules, ORS, and calamine lotion at highly subsidized rates.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is measles?**
   Measles (Khasra) is a highly contagious viral respiratory infection caused by the measles virus, causing high fever, cough, and a widespread red rash.

2. **How does measles spread?**
   It spreads through airborne droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes. The virus can remain active in the air or on surfaces for up to 2 hours.

3. **What are Koplik spots?**
   Koplik spots are small, blue-white spots with red halos that appear on the inside of the cheeks near the molars 1-2 days before the measles rash breaks out.

4. **Why is Vitamin A given to children with measles?**
   Measles virus depletes the body’s Vitamin A reserves, leading to blindness and severe mucosal damage. Supplementation prevents eye damage and halves the risk of fatal pneumonia.

5. **What is the MR vaccine?**
   The MR (Measles-Rubella) vaccine protects against both measles and rubella. In India, it is given free at 9 months and 16-24 months of age.

6. **What are the "3 C's" of measles?**
   The "3 C's" are the initial warning symptoms of measles: **C**ough (dry), **C**oryza (runny nose), and **C**onjunctivitis (red, watery eyes).

7. **Where can I get free Vitamin A and MR vaccines in India?**
   Vitamin A syrup and MR vaccines are provided completely free of charge to all children at government PHCs, sub-centers, and Anganwadis.

8. **Why is Aspirin banned in children with measles?**
   Giving Aspirin to children during viral infections like measles or flu can cause Reye’s Syndrome, a rare but life-threatening disease that causes swelling in the liver and brain.

9. **Can adults get measles?**
   Yes. Adults who were never vaccinated or never had the disease can contract measles, and they often experience much more severe complications than children.

10. **What is the measles rash pattern?**
    The flat red rash (maculopapular) begins on the forehead and behind the ears, then spreads downward over the face, neck, trunk, limbs, and finally reaches the hands and feet.

11. **How long is a person with measles contagious?**
    A person is contagious from 4 days *before* the rash appears until 4 days *after* the rash breaks out. Keep the patient isolated during this time.

12. **Can measles cause permanent brain damage?**
    Yes. Measles can cause acute encephalitis (brain swelling) during the infection, and a rare, fatal degenerative brain disease called SSPE years later.

13. **Is rubella the same as measles?**
    No. Rubella (German measles) is caused by a different virus and is usually milder, but it is dangerous for pregnant women as it causes birth defects (Congenital Rubella Syndrome).

14. **What is the age for MR vaccination in India?**
    Under the government UIP schedule, the first dose is given at 9-12 months and the second dose at 16-24 months.

15. **What is the price of generic Vitamin A and Paracetamol at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Vitamin A solutions and Paracetamol syrups are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹5 to ₹10 per bottle.

16. **Why does measles cause photophobia?**
    The viral infection causes acute inflammation of the conjunctiva (inner eyelid lining) and cornea, making the eyes highly sensitive and painful in bright light.

17. **What is SSPE?**
    SSPE (Subacute Sclerosing Panencephalitis) is a rare, fatal, progressive brain disorder that develops 7-10 years after a measles infection due to a persistent mutated virus in brain cells.

18. **Can a child get measles after the first dose of vaccine?**
    Yes. The first dose provides about 85-90% protection. The second booster dose is required to raise protection to over 97%, ensuring complete immunity.

19. **What is the MR Elimination campaign?**
    It is a government health mission aimed at vaccinating all children aged 9 months to 15 years with the MR vaccine to completely eliminate measles from India.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover measles encephalitis hospitalizations?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for severe measles complications, including viral croup, encephalitis, and ICU admissions.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Khasra is a holy visit of a goddess and you should not give any medicines or vaccines.
  * **Fact:** Khasra is a dangerous, scientific viral infection (Measles). Not giving medicine (especially Vitamin A) can lead to permanent blindness, hearing loss, and death.
* **Myth:** Do not wash or bathe the child during measles.
  * **Fact:** Keeping the child clean is important. You can sponge the child with lukewarm water to reduce fever. Avoid scrubbing the rash.
* **Myth:** Putting breast milk in a child's red eyes cures measles conjunctivitis.
  * **Fact:** Squirting breast milk or other liquids into the eyes can cause severe secondary bacterial eye infections. Use sterile eye drops prescribed by a doctor.
* **Myth:** The measles vaccine causes autism in children.
  * **Fact:** This is a completely debunked myth based on a fraudulent 1998 study. Multiple international studies involving millions of children have proven that the MMR vaccine does not cause autism.
* **Myth:** One dose of the measles vaccine is enough for lifetime protection.
  * **Fact:** Two doses of the MR/MMR vaccine are mandatory to achieve over 97% protection. The first dose alone leaves 10-15% of children unprotected.
* **Myth:** You can cure measles by feeding the child raw eggs.
  * **Fact:** Raw eggs do not cure measles. Feed soft, easily digestible warm foods. Ensure they receive the doctor-prescribed Vitamin A doses.
* **Myth:** Measles is a mild childhood disease that everyone should get naturally to build immunity.
  * **Fact:** Measles is a dangerous infection that kills over 100,000 children globally every year due to severe pneumonia and brain swelling. Vaccination is the only safe way to build immunity.
* **Myth:** A child with measles rash should be kept in a hot, closed room with zero fans.
  * **Fact:** A hot, stuffy room makes the child uncomfortable and raises body temperature. Keep the room well-ventilated but avoid direct drafts.
* **Myth:** You can get measles from a dog or cat.
  * **Fact:** Measles is a human-only virus. It does not infect or spread through pets or other animals.
* **Myth:** Measles rash only affects the skin.
  * **Fact:** The measles virus infects the entire respiratory lining, intestines, and immune system, causing severe lung damage and gut diarrhea.
