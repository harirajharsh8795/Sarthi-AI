# AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (AIDS and Opportunistic Infections Guide)

## Overview

### English
Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is the most advanced clinical stage of infection caused by the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV). AIDS is clinically defined when an HIV-positive individual's CD4 T-cell count falls below **200 cells/$\mu$L** (normal: $500 - 1500$) or when they develop one or more AIDS-defining opportunistic infections (e.g., Pneumocystis pneumonia, Tuberculosis, esophageal candidiasis, or Kaposi's sarcoma). Once considered fatal, AIDS is now a manageable chronic condition due to life-saving Antiretroviral Therapy (ART), which suppresses viral replication and restores immune function. In India, NACO provides completely free diagnosis and lifelong ART.

### Hindi
एड्स (AIDS - Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome / अर्जित प्रतिरक्षा न्यूनता सिंड्रोम) मानव इम्युनोडेफिशिएंसी वायरस (HIV) संक्रमण का सबसे उन्नत चरण है। चिकित्सकीय रूप से, एड्स तब घोषित किया जाता है जब किसी एचआईवी-पॉजिटिव व्यक्ति की CD4 टी-कोशिकाओं (T-cells) की संख्या **200 cells/$\mu$L** से कम हो जाती है, या जब उन्हें कोई गंभीर अवसरवादी संक्रमण (opportunistic infection) जैसे तपेदिक (TB) या फेफड़ों का संक्रमण हो जाता है। एंटीरेट्रोवाइरल थेरेपी (ART) के माध्यम से इसे नियंत्रित किया जा सकता है। भारत में, नाको (NACO) मुफ्त एआरटी दवाएं प्रदान करता है।

## Quick Summary
AIDS is the advanced stage of HIV infection. It is defined by CD4 counts under 200 cells/$\mu$L and opportunistic infections. Symptoms include chronic fever, rapid weight loss, persistent diarrhea, and oral thrush. Diagnostics use ELISA, Western Blot, and CD4 flow cytometry. Management utilizes lifelong Antiretroviral Therapy (ART) and NACO clinics.

---

## Definition
* **AIDS (Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome):** The severe clinical syndrome representing the end-stage of chronic HIV infection, characterized by profound immunosuppression.
* **CD4 Cells (helper T-cells):** White blood cells that organize the immune system's response to infections. They are the primary targets destroyed by HIV.
* **Opportunistic Infections (OIs):** Infections that occur more frequently and are more severe in people with weakened immune systems (e.g., cryptococcal meningitis, toxoplasmosis).

---

## Clinical Stages of HIV to AIDS Progression

The progression from HIV to AIDS follows three main stages:
1. **Acute HIV Infection (Stage 1):** Within 2-4 weeks of exposure, patients experience flu-like symptoms (fever, rash, sore throat) with very high viral loads.
2. **Clinical Latency (Stage 2 - Chronic HIV):** The virus multiplies at low levels. Patients are often asymptomatic, which can last 10-15 years without treatment.
3. **AIDS (Stage 3):** The immune system is completely destroyed. **CD4 count drops $< 200$ cells/$\mu$L.** The patient becomes highly vulnerable to opportunistic infections and cancers, leading to death if untreated.

## Symptoms of AIDS (Advanced Immunosuppression)

### English
* Rapid, unexplained weight loss ("HIV wasting syndrome"); recurring high fever and profuse night sweats; extreme, unexplained fatigue; chronic diarrhea lasting for more than one week; sores or white patches in the mouth, tongue, or throat (oral candidiasis/thrush); persistent dry cough and breathlessness (pneumonia); purple or dark spots on the skin (Kaposi's sarcoma).

### Hindi
* तेजी से और बिना कारण वजन कम होना (Wasting syndrome); बार-बार तेज बुखार आना और रात में अत्यधिक पसीना आना; लगातार 1 सप्ताह से अधिक समय तक दस्त रहना; मुंह, जीभ या गले में सफेद पैच (oral thrush); फेफड़ों में संक्रमण के कारण सूखी खांसी और सांस फूलना।

### Hinglish
* Achanak tezi se bina kisi kaaran weight (vajan) ghatna (HIV wasting syndrome); lagatar high fever aur raat me behod pasina aana; 1 week se zyada time tak dast (diarrhea) rehna; muh, tongue ya throat me white dhabbe padna (oral thrush/fungus); lagatar khansi aur saans phoolna.

## Diagnostic Criteria
* **HIV Antibody/Antigen Testing:** Initial screening by ELISA/Rapid test, confirmed by Western Blot or PCR RNA viral load testing.
* **CD4 T-lymphocyte Count:** Flow cytometry measuring CD4 cells. **Count $< 200$ cells/$\mu$L confirms AIDS.**
* **Opportunistic Infection Screen:** Sputum check for tuberculosis, chest X-rays for pneumonia, and CSF checks for cryptococcal meningitis.

## Medical Management & NACO Guidelines

### Antiretroviral Therapy (ART)

#### English
* Lifelong, daily combination of three antiretroviral drugs (typically Dolutegravir, Tenofovir, and Lamivudine - TLD regimen) to block viral replication, allowing CD4 counts to recover.
* **Treat All Policy:** India follows the WHO "Treat All" policy, meaning ART must be started immediately upon HIV diagnosis, regardless of CD4 count.

#### Hindi
* आजीवन रोजाना तीन दवाओं का संयोजन (TLD regimen) लेना। भारत में एचआईवी की पुष्टि होते ही तुरंत एआरटी (ART) शुरू की जाती है।

#### Hinglish
* Lifelong daily bases par teen antiretroviral medicines ka group (TLD regimen: Tenofovir + Lamivudine + Dolutegravir) lena. NACO ki guidelines ke according, HIV confirm hote hi CD4 count chahe jitna bhi ho, turant ART treatment shuru kiya jata hai.

### Prophylactic Treatments

#### English
* Daily Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole (Co-trimoxazole) is prescribed to prevent Pneumocystis pneumonia and toxoplasmosis when CD4 count is $< 200$ cells/$\mu$L.

#### Hindi
* अवसरवादी संक्रमणों से बचने के लिए को-ट्रिमोक्साजोल (Co-trimoxazole) दवा देना।

#### Hinglish
* CD4 count 200 se kam hone par opportunistic bacterial/fungal infections se bachane ke liye daily Co-trimoxazole tablet di jati hai.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories used in HIV/AIDS management:*
1. **Integrase Strand Transfer Inhibitors (INSTIs):** Block viral DNA integration (e.g., Dolutegravir).
2. **Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NRTIs):** Block reverse transcription (e.g., Tenofovir, Lamivudine, Abacavir).
3. **Non-Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NNRTIs):** e.g., Efavirenz, Nevirapine.
4. **Protease Inhibitors (PIs):** Block viral packaging (e.g., Lopinavir, Ritonavir, Atazanavir).
5. **Prophylactic Antimicrobials:** Prevent opportunistic infections (e.g., Co-trimoxazole, Fluconazole).

## Prevention of HIV Transmission
* **Safe Sexual Practices:** Consistently and correctly use latex condoms during every sexual encounter.
* **Avoid Needle Sharing:** Never share syringes or needles. Use sterile disposable syringes.
* **Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP):** High-risk HIV-negative individuals can take daily Tenofovir+Emtricitabine to prevent infection.
* **Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP):** Emergency medicines taken within 72 hours of accidental exposure (e.g., condom break, needle stick) for 28 days.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated AIDS
* **Severe Sepsis & Death:** Minor infections turning fatal due to lack of immune cells.
* **Cryptococcal Meningitis:** Fungal brain infection causing severe headache, stiff neck, and coma.
* **AIDS-Related Lymphoma:** Aggressive blood cancers developing due to immune failure.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **A patient with HIV/AIDS develops a high fever with severe headache, confusion, and a stiff neck (meningitis).**
* **Severe, progressive difficulty breathing, chest pain, and lips turning blue (pneumonia/respiratory failure).**
* **The patient develops sudden seizures, weakness in limbs, or loss of consciousness.**

---

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National AIDS Control Organisation (NACO):** Coordinates free HIV testing, counseling (ICTC centers), free distribution of condoms, and free lifelong Antiretroviral Therapy (ART centers) at government medical centers.
* **National AIDS Control Programme (NACP Phase V):** Aims to achieve the 95-95-95 targets: 95% of people living with HIV knowing their status, 95% of diagnosed people on ART, and 95% on ART achieving viral suppression.
* **HIV/AIDS Prevention and Control Act, 2017:** Legally prohibits discrimination against HIV-positive individuals in employment, education, healthcare, and housing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is the difference between HIV and AIDS?**
   HIV is the virus that infects the body. AIDS is the third, most advanced stage of HIV infection when the immune system is severely damaged (CD4 count $< 200$).

2. **What is a CD4 count and why is it important?**
   CD4 cells are white blood cells that fight infections. Measuring their count tells how strong the immune system is. Normal is 500-1500; under 200 is diagnosed as AIDS.

3. **What is Antiretroviral Therapy (ART)?**
   ART is a combination of daily medications that stops HIV from copying itself, allowing the immune system (CD4 cells) to recover and preventing AIDS.

4. **Is there a cure for AIDS?**
   No. There is currently no permanent cure for HIV/AIDS. However, with regular daily ART, patients can suppress the virus, live healthy lives, and have a normal life expectancy.

5. **Where can I get free HIV testing and medicines in India?**
   NACO provides completely free, confidential HIV testing at ICTC centers and free lifelong ART medicines at government ART clinics.

6. **What are opportunistic infections?**
   These are severe infections (like TB, fungal meningitis, or Pneumocystis pneumonia) that target people with weak immune systems but rarely make healthy people sick.

7. **How is HIV transmitted?**
   HIV spreads through unprotected sex, sharing contaminated needles/syringes, transfusion of infected blood, and from an infected mother to child during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

8. **What is Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)?**
   PEP is emergency antiretroviral medicine taken within 72 hours of potential HIV exposure (e.g., condom break, needle prick) for 28 days to prevent infection.

9. **Can a pregnant HIV-positive woman give birth to a healthy baby?**
   Yes. If the mother takes regular ART throughout pregnancy and delivery, and the baby receives preventive medicines for a few weeks, the risk of transmission drops to under 1%.

10. **What is PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis)?**
    PrEP is a daily medicine taken by HIV-negative people who are at very high risk of exposure (e.g., serodiscordant couples) to prevent contracting the virus.

11. **Why does AIDS cause rapid weight loss?**
    The active virus and chronic opportunistic infections raise the body’s metabolic rate, destroying muscle and fat tissue (HIV wasting syndrome).

12. **Can HIV spread through sharing food or hugging?**
    No. HIV is not spread through sweat, tears, saliva, hugging, shaking hands, sharing toilets, or sharing food and water.

13. **What is the "Treat All" policy of NACO?**
    Launched in 2017, it mandates that every person testing positive for HIV must be started on free ART immediately, regardless of their CD4 count.

14. **What is the "Window Period" in HIV testing?**
    It is the time between HIV exposure and when a test can detect the virus. For antibody tests, it is usually 3-12 weeks. PCR tests can detect it within 10-33 days.

15. **What is the price of generic HIV medicines at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    While NACO supplies them completely free, generic anti-retroviral drugs are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at highly subsidized generic rates.

16. **Why do AIDS patients take Co-trimoxazole tablets daily?**
    Co-trimoxazole is a preventive antibiotic given to patients with CD4 counts under 200 to protect them from dangerous lung infections (Pneumocystis pneumonia).

17. **What is the ELISA test?**
    ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay) is the standard initial blood screening test used to detect HIV antibodies in the bloodstream.

18. **Can mosquitoes transmit HIV?**
    No. Mosquitoes do not transmit HIV. The virus cannot survive or multiply inside the mosquito’s gut, and mosquitoes do not inject blood from a previous feed.

19. **What legal protection do HIV patients have in India?**
    The HIV/AIDS Act, 2017, makes it illegal to discriminate against HIV-positive people in jobs, schools, hospitals, or renting homes. It also guarantees right to privacy.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover AIDS hospitalization?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for opportunistic infection treatments, HIV-related surgeries, ICU stays, and diagnostic checks.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** HIV and AIDS are the exact same thing.
  * **Fact:** HIV is the virus. AIDS is the late stage of infection. A person with HIV who takes regular ART may never develop AIDS in their lifetime.
* **Myth:** You can contract HIV by sharing a glass of water or swimming pools.
  * **Fact:** HIV cannot survive in water or air. It does not spread through casual contact, sharing utensils, or using public pools.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a wound of an HIV patient can cure them.
  * **Fact:** Sucking does not cure HIV and exposes the rescuer to infected blood. Never suck on any wound.
* **Myth:** If both partners are HIV-positive, they do not need to use condoms.
  * **Fact:** Condoms are still required to prevent cross-infection with different, drug-resistant strains of HIV and other sexually transmitted infections (STIs).
* **Myth:** HIV only spreads among sex workers or gay men.
  * **Fact:** Anyone who has unprotected sex or shares needles can contract HIV, regardless of gender or sexual orientation. Heterosexual transmission is common.
* **Myth:** You can easily tell if someone has HIV/AIDS by looking at them.
  * **Fact:** HIV-positive people taking regular ART look and feel completely healthy. Only a blood test can confirm HIV status.
* **Myth:** Alternative herbal syrups can completely cure HIV/AIDS.
  * **Fact:** There is no herbal cure for HIV. Alternative medicines claiming to cure HIV are fraudulent and dangerous if they cause patients to stop their daily ART.
* **Myth:** You can get HIV from a mosquito bite.
  * **Fact:** Mosquitoes cannot transmit HIV. The virus cannot replicate inside them and is digested in their stomach.
* **Myth:** HIV-positive mothers should never breastfeed their babies.
  * **Fact:** If the mother is on active ART with suppressed viral load, breast-feeding is safe and recommended, especially in resource-limited areas. Consult a pediatrician.
* **Myth:** Testing positive for HIV means your life is over and you will die soon.
  * **Fact:** With modern daily Antiretroviral Therapy (ART), HIV-positive individuals can live active, healthy lives for decades, with a normal life expectancy.
