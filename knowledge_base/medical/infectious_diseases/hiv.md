# HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (HIV Screening and Lifelong ART Guide)

## Overview

### English
Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) is a retrovirus that targets and destroys the host's immune system, specifically CD4 T-lymphocytes (helper T-cells). If left untreated, progressive destruction of these cells leads to Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS). HIV is transmitted through unprotected sexual contact, sharing contaminated syringes, transfusion of unscreened blood, and vertical transmission from mother to child. While HIV cannot be cured, modern Antiretroviral Therapy (ART) suppresses the viral load to undetectable levels, allowing patients to live long, healthy lives and preventing transmission.

## Quick Summary
HIV is a retrovirus that destroys CD4 cells. Symptoms start with a flu-like illness (fever, sore throat, rash), followed by a long asymptomatic phase. First aid/PEP must be taken within 72 hours of accidental exposure. Diagnostics use ELISA screening, confirmed by Western Blot. Management involves lifelong daily combination ART under NACO clinics.

---

## Definition
* **HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus):** A lentivirus (a subgroup of retrovirus) that causes chronic, progressive infection leading to immune failure.
* **Viral Load:** The measure of the number of viral particles (copies of HIV RNA) per milliliter of blood, indicating how active the virus is.
* **PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis):** Emergency antiretroviral drugs taken within 72 hours of potential HIV exposure for 28 days to prevent infection.

---

## Transmission Modes & Viral Replication Cycle
The virus multiplies inside CD4 cells:
1. **Binding & Fusion:** The virus binds to CD4 receptors on the host T-cell surface and fuses with the cell membrane.
2. **Reverse Transcription:** Viral RNA is converted into double-stranded DNA using the viral enzyme *Reverse Transcriptase*.
3. **Integration:** Viral DNA enters the host cell nucleus and integrates into the host's DNA using the enzyme *Integrase*.
4. **Assembly & Budding:** New viral proteins are cut by the *Protease* enzyme, assembled, and bud out to infect other CD4 cells.
5. *ART drugs are designed to block each of these specific replication steps.*

## Symptoms of HIV (Acute vs. Chronic Phase)

### English
* **Acute Phase (Stage 1 - first 2-4 weeks):** Flu-like illness including high fever, sore throat, generalized red skin rash, muscle/joint pain, and swollen lymph nodes in the neck/armpits.
* **Chronic Phase (Stage 2 - Latency):** Often asymptomatic (no symptoms) for 5 to 15 years, though the virus is silently replicating.
* **AIDS Phase (Stage 3):** Chronic fever, rapid weight loss, persistent diarrhea, and oral thrush (fungal throat infection).

### Hindi
* **तीव्र चरण (Acute Stage 1):** संक्रमण के 2-4 सप्ताह बाद फ्लू जैसे लक्षण जैसे बुखार, गले में खराश, त्वचा पर लाल चकत्ते (rash), और गर्दन में गांठें (lymph nodes) सूजना।
* **क्रोनिक चरण (Stage 2):** बिना किसी लक्षण के 5-15 वर्षों तक रहना (Asymptomatic phase)।
* **एड्स चरण (Stage 3):** लगातार बुखार, अत्यधिक वजन घटना, और मुंह में छाले।

### Hinglish
* **Acute Phase (Stage 1):** Katne ya exposure ke 2-4 weeks baad flu-like symptoms aana: Jaise tez bukhar, gale me kharash, body par rashes, joints me dard, aur neck/gale me sujan (swollen lymph nodes).
* **Chronic Phase (Stage 2):** Isme patient ko 5-15 saal tak koi symptom nahi hota, par virus andar hi andar CD4 cells ko destroy karta rehta hai.
* **AIDS Phase (Stage 3):** CD4 count < 200 hone par rapid weight loss, chronic diarrhea, aur oral thrush (gale me fungal infection).

## First Aid & Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)

### Immediate Action for Accidental Exposure (Needle Stick / Condom Break)

#### English
1. **Wash the exposure site immediately** with soap and water. If exposed in the eyes or mouth, flush with large amounts of water. **Do NOT squeeze or rub the needle prick site**, as this causes micro-tears that ease viral entry.
2. **Do NOT apply bleach**, alcohol, or harsh disinfectants on the wound.
3. **Go to a government hospital/ART center within 72 hours** (strictly, the sooner the better) to start **Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)**. PEP involves taking combination ART drugs daily for 28 days to stop the virus from taking hold in the body.

#### Hindi
1. **घाव को तुरंत धोएं:** साबुन और पानी से धोएं। घाव को दबाकर खून निकालने की कोशिश न करें।
2. **72 घंटे के भीतर अस्पताल जाएं:** तुरंत पोस्ट-एक्सपोजर प्रोफिलैक्सिस (PEP) दवाएं शुरू करें।

#### Hinglish
1. **Ghaw ko turant dhoyein:** Needle prick ya cut lagne par area ko soap aur paani se wash karein. **Ghaw ko daba kar blood nikalne ki koshish na karein**, isse tissue tears me virus fast enters hota hai. Bleech ya acid na lagayein.
2. **72 Hours ke andar PEP shuru karein:** Bina kisi sharm ke, exposure ke **72 ghante ke andar** government hospital ke ART center jayein aur **Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)** kit shuru karwayein. Yeh 28 days ka course hota hai jo infection hone se rokkta hai.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **ELISA Antibody/Antigen (4th Gen) Test:** The standard screening test, detecting both HIV antibodies and the p24 antigen.
* **Western Blot Test:** The confirmatory test used to confirm diagnosis if the ELISA screening is positive.
* **CD4 Count Flow Cytometry:** Measures the immune strength. Normal: $500 - 1500$ cells/$\mu$L.
* **Viral Load PCR Test:** Measures the copies of HIV RNA in the blood. Suppressing this to $< 50$ copies/mL (undetectable) is the goal of treatment.

---

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories used in HIV management:*
1. **Integrase Inhibitors (INSTIs):** Block viral integration (e.g., Dolutegravir, Raltegravir).
2. **Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors (NRTIs):** Block viral DNA synthesis (e.g., Tenofovir, Lamivudine, Emtricitabine, Abacavir).
3. **Protease Inhibitors (PIs):** Block viral assembly (e.g., Ritonavir, Lopinavir, Darunavir).
4. **PrEP Combinations:** Pre-exposure prophylaxis (e.g., Tenofovir + Emtricitabine [Truvada]).

## Prevention of HIV
* **Safe Sexual Practices:** Use latex condoms consistently and correctly during every sexual encounter.
* **Do Not Share Needles:** Intravenous drug users must never share syringes or needles.
* **Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP):** High-risk HIV-negative individuals take daily Truvada to prevent infection.
* **Safe Blood Transfusions:** Ensure blood is screened at registered blood banks.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated HIV
* **Opportunistic Infections (AIDS):** Severe, fatal infections like Tuberculosis, PCP pneumonia, and Toxoplasmosis.
* **AIDS-Related Malignancies:** Kaposi's sarcoma, non-Hodgkin lymphoma.
* **HIV-Associated Dementia:** Cognitive decline and motor dysfunction due to viral brain damage.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **An HIV-positive patient develops a sudden severe headache, stiff neck, confusion, or seizures (opportunistic brain infection).**
* **Sudden shortness of breath, rapid breathing, and lips turning blue (severe PCP pneumonia).**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National AIDS Control Organisation (NACO):** Coordinates free confidential counseling/testing (ICTC centers) and distributes free lifelong ART medicines (TLD regimen) at government medical colleges.
* **HIV/AIDS Act, 2017:** A central law protecting HIV patients from discrimination in jobs, education, and healthcare, and guaranteeing their right to confidentiality.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless hospitalization packages for severe HIV-related opportunistic infections and ICU care.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is HIV?**
   HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) is a virus that targets and destroys CD4 helper T-cells of the immune system, leading to AIDS if untreated.

2. **How is HIV diagnosed?**
   HIV is diagnosed using ELISA blood screening tests. If positive, the result is confirmed using a highly specific Western Blot test.

3. **What is Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)?**
   PEP is an emergency 28-day antiretroviral medicine course started within 72 hours of potential HIV exposure to prevent the virus from infecting the body.

4. **What is Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP)?**
   PrEP is a daily medicine taken by HIV-negative people who are at very high risk of exposure (like those with HIV-positive partners) to prevent infection.

5. **Where can I get free HIV tests and medicines in India?**
   Confidential HIV testing is free at all government ICTC centers. NACO provides completely free lifelong ART medicines at government ART centers.

6. **What does "Undetectable = Untransmittable" (U=U) mean?**
   If an HIV-positive person takes regular ART and suppresses their blood viral load to undetectable levels, they cannot transmit the virus to their sexual partners.

7. **How does HIV transmit?**
   It transmits through unprotected sex, sharing contaminated syringes, blood transfusions from unscreened donors, and from mother to child during childbirth/breastfeeding.

8. **Can HIV spread through saliva or kissing?**
   No. HIV is not present in high enough concentrations in saliva to transmit. Casual kissing, sharing food, water, or hugging does not spread the virus.

9. **Can a pregnant HIV-positive woman have a healthy baby?**
   Yes. With regular ART during pregnancy and delivery, and infant prophylaxis, the risk of transmission to the newborn is reduced to under 1%.

10. **Why is the HIV/AIDS Act, 2017 important?**
    It legally bans discrimination against HIV-positive people in employment, education, and housing, and protects their right to medical privacy.

11. **What is the "Window Period"?**
    It is the time between HIV exposure and when a test can detect antibodies. Standard ELISA tests have a window period of 3 to 12 weeks.

12. **Why is squeezing a needle-prick wound bad?**
    Squeezing causes local tissue damage and micro-tears, which makes it easier for the virus to enter local cells and access blood vessels. Wash with soap and water instead.

13. **Is there a vaccine for HIV?**
    No. There is currently no vaccine available for HIV due to the virus’s rapid mutation rate, though research is ongoing.

14. **What is the standard first-line ART regimen in India?**
    The standard NACO first-line regimen is the TLD tablet (Tenofovir + Lamivudine + Dolutegravir), taken once daily.

15. **What is the price of generic HIV drugs at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    While NACO supplies them completely free, generic antiretrovirals are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at highly discounted generic rates.

16. **Why does HIV target CD4 cells?**
    The virus’s outer gp120 protein specifically binds to the CD4 receptors on the surface of T-helper immune cells to enter and replicate inside them.

17. **Can mosquitoes transmit HIV?**
    No. HIV cannot survive or replicate inside mosquitoes, and mosquitoes do not inject blood from a previous host.

18. **What are opportunistic infections in HIV?**
    These are severe infections (like TB, cryptococcal meningitis) that strike only when the CD4 count drops below 200, representing advanced immune failure.

19. **What should I do if I miss a dose of my ART medicine?**
    Take the missed dose as soon as you remember. If it is almost time for your next dose, skip the missed one. Do not double the dose. Compliance must be $>95\%$.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover HIV hospitalization?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for opportunistic infection treatments, HIV-related surgeries, and cardiac/respiratory ICU stays.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** HIV and AIDS are the exact same thing.
  * **Fact:** HIV is the virus. AIDS is the late stage of infection. A person with HIV who takes regular ART may never develop AIDS in their lifetime.
* **Myth:** You can get HIV from sharing a glass of water or public toilet seats.
  * **Fact:** HIV cannot survive on dry surfaces or in water. It does not spread through casual contact, sharing utensils, or public restrooms.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a needle-prick wound immediately after exposure prevents HIV.
  * **Fact:** Sucking puts the virus directly in contact with your mouth lining, risking brain infection. Wash with soap and water, and get PEP.
* **Myth:** If you are on ART, you do not need to use condoms.
  * **Fact:** Condoms are still needed to prevent transmission of other STIs (syphilis, gonorrhea) and protect against different drug-resistant strains of HIV.
* **Myth:** Only gay men and sex workers get HIV.
  * **Fact:** Anyone who has unprotected sex or shares needles can contract HIV, regardless of gender or sexual orientation. Heterosexual transmission is highly common.
* **Myth:** You can easily tell if someone has HIV by looking at them.
  * **Fact:** HIV-positive people taking daily ART look and feel completely healthy. Only a blood test can confirm HIV status.
* **Myth:** Alternative herbal therapies can completely cure HIV/AIDS.
  * **Fact:** There is no herbal cure for HIV. Stopping ART for herbal treatments will cause the viral load to spike, destroying your immunity.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures rabies, and similar metal touch cures HIV.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys or touching metals has no effect on any viral infection. Use scientifically proven PEP and ART.
* **Myth:** HIV-positive mothers must never breastfeed.
  * **Fact:** If the mother is on active ART with suppressed viral load, breastfeeding is safe and recommended, especially in resource-limited areas.
* **Myth:** Testing positive for HIV means your life is over.
  * **Fact:** With modern daily Antiretroviral Therapy (ART), HIV-positive individuals can live active, healthy lives for decades, with a normal life expectancy.
