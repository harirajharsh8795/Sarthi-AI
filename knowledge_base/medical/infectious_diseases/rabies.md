# Rabies (Rabies) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Immediate First Aid (Rabies Post-Exposure Prophylaxis PEP Guide)

## Overview

### English
Rabies is a highly fatal viral zoonotic disease caused by the **Rabies Virus (a Lyssavirus)** and transmitted through the saliva of an infected animal, most commonly via dog bites, scratches, or licks on broken skin. The virus targets the central nervous system, causing acute, progressive encephalomyelitis. **Once clinical symptoms develop, Rabies is 100% fatal; there is no cure.** However, rabies is 100% preventable if immediate Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) is started. PEP requires immediate, thorough wound washing with soap and running water for 15 minutes, local infiltration of **Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG)** into the wound, and a series of **anti-rabies vaccines**.

## Quick Summary
Rabies is a fatal viral encephalitis. Transmission occurs via animal saliva (bites/scratches). Symptoms include hydrophobia, aerophobia, hallucinations, and paralysis. First aid requires immediate washing with soap and running water for 15 minutes. Never suture the wound. Diagnostics check animal observation and PCR. Management utilizes PEP vaccination.

## Definition
* **Rabies:** A zoonotic viral disease of the central nervous system, almost always fatal once clinical symptoms manifest.
* **Hydrophobia:** The involuntary, painful spasms of the throat muscles (laryngeal spasms) triggered by the sight, sound, or mention of water, preventing swallowing, typical of furious rabies.
* **RIG (Rabies Immunoglobulin):** Ready-made antibodies injected directly into and around the wound to neutralize the rabies virus locally before the vaccine-induced antibodies develop.

---

## WHO Bite Category Classification & PEP Protocol

The type of Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) required depends on the severity of exposure:

* **Category I:** Touching or feeding animals, licks on intact skin. **(Action: None required, wash skin).**
* **Category II:** Nibbling of uncovered skin, minor scratches or abrasions without bleeding. **(Action: Immediate wound washing + Complete Anti-Rabies Vaccine [ARV] series).**
* **Category III:** Single or multiple transdermal bites or scratches (bleeding), licks on broken skin, or exposure to bats. **(Action: Immediate wound washing + RIG infiltration into the wound + Complete ARV series).**

## Symptoms of Rabies (Clinical Phase)

### English
* **Early Phase (Prodromal):** Pain, tingling, or burning sensation (paresthesia) directly at the animal bite wound site; low-grade fever, headache, and general malaise.
* **Neurological Phase (Furious Rabies - 80% of cases):** Hydrophobia (spasms when trying to drink water); aerophobia (spasms triggered by air drafts/wind); hyper-excitability, agitation, hallucinations, and excessive salivation ("foaming at the mouth").
* **Paralytic Phase (Dumb Rabies - 20% of cases):** Progressive muscle weakness starting at the bite site, leading to paralysis, coma, and respiratory arrest.

* **उग्र रेबीज (Furious Rabies):** हाइड्रोफोबिया (पानी का डर/गले में ऐंठन); एरोफोबिया (हवा से डर); अत्यधिक उत्तेजना और मुंह से झाग निकलना।
* **लकवाग्रस्त रेबीज (Paralytic Rabies):** शरीर में धीरे-धीरे लकवा (paralysis) मारना।

## Immediate Emergency First Aid (Dog/Animal Bite)

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Wash the wound immediately** under a running tap using soap (any washing soap or bath soap) for **at least 15 minutes**. Soap dissolves the lipid envelope of the rabies virus, inactivating up to $90\%$ of the virus locally.
* **Apply an antiseptic** like Povidone-Iodine (Betadine) or spirit after washing.
* **Go to the nearest government hospital** or ARV clinic immediately to start the Anti-Rabies Vaccine (ARV) series.
* **Get Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG)** injected directly into the wound if the bite caused bleeding (Category III).

#### Hindi
* **घाव को तुरंत धोएं:** बहते नल के पानी और साबुन से कम से कम 15 मिनट तक लगातार धोएं। साबुन वायरस को नष्ट करता है।
* **एंटीसेप्टिक (Betadine) लगाएं।**
* **तुरंत सरकारी अस्पताल जाएं:** एंटी-रेबीज वैक्सीन (ARV) लगवाएं।

#### Hinglish
* **Ghaw ko turant dhoyein (Sabse bada life saving step):** Bite hote hi ghaw ko behte nal ke niche rakh kar sabun (soap) se **15 minutes tak lagatar dhoyein**. Sabun me mojud chemicals rabies virus ke outer layer ko destroy kar dete hain jisse virus passive ho jata hai.
* **Antiseptic lagayein:** Dhone ke baad Betadine ya alcohol antiseptic lagayein.
* **Turant hospital jayein:** Bin deri kiye government hospital me **Anti-Rabies Vaccine (ARV)** ki first dose lagwayein (Day 0 dose).
* **RIG injection lagwayein:** Agar bite deep hai aur khoon nikla hai (Category III), to ghaw ke andar **Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG)** injection infiltration zaroori karwayein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT suture (stitch) the wound.** Suturing pushes the rabies virus deeper into sensory nerves, accelerating brain infection. If stitching is absolutely necessary to stop severe bleeding, do it only *after* injecting RIG, using loose, wide stitches.
* **Do NOT cover the wound** with bandages, plasters, or dressings. Keep it open.
* **Do NOT apply home remedies** like red chili powder, turmeric, lime (chuna), mustard oil, or animal dung. These cause severe chemical burns and introduce secondary bacterial infections.

#### Hindi
* **घाव पर टांके (suturing) न लगवाएं:** टांके लगाने से वायरस नसों के अंदर गहराई तक चला जाता है। घाव को खुला रखें।
* **लाल मिर्च, चूना, मिट्टी या हल्दी न लगाएं:** इससे इन्फेक्शन बढ़ सकता है।

#### Hinglish
* **Ghaw par taanke (suture) na lagwayein:** Animal bite ke ghaw par stitch bilkul na lagwayein. Suture karne se virus nerves ke aur deep chala jata hai aur fast brain tak pahunchta hai. Ghaw ko khula chodd dein.
* **Bandage na karein:** Ghaw par plaster ya tight bandage na bandhein.
* **Desi nuskhe bilkul na karein:** Ghaw par lal mirch powder, chuna, haldi, mitti ya gobar na lagayein. Yeh skin ko burn kar dete hain aur severe bacterial infection karte hain.

## Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) Immunization Schedule

### Intramuscular (IM) Schedule (Essen Regimen)
* Given in the deltoid muscle (shoulder) on **5 doses: Day 0, 3, 7, 14, and 28**. (Day 0 is the day of the first vaccine injection).

### Intradermal (ID) Schedule (Thai Red Cross Regimen - Standard in Indian Government Centers)
* Given in the skin (intradermal) on **4 visits: Day 0, 3, 7, and 28** (2 sites on each day). This is highly cost-effective and uses very small doses.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to rabies prevention:*
1. **Anti-Rabies Vaccines (ARV):** Cell-culture derived inactive vaccines (e.g., Purified Chick Embryo Cell Vaccine [PCECV], Purified Vero Cell Rabies Vaccine [PVRV]).
2. **Equine Rabies Immunoglobulin (ERIG):** Highly potent passive antibodies derived from horses (requires skin test first to check allergy).
3. **Human Rabies Immunoglobulin (HRIG):** Human-derived antibodies (no allergy testing needed, highly expensive).
4. **Tetanus Toxoid (TT):** Vaccine given alongside ARV to prevent tetanus infection.

---

## Prevention of Rabies
* **Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP):** High-risk professionals (veterinarians, animal handlers, forest rangers) should receive a 3-dose preventive vaccine schedule (Days 0, 7, and 21 or 28).
* **Vaccinate Pets:** Ensure all domestic dogs and cats receive their primary rabies vaccine and annual boosters.
* **Stray Dog Vaccination:** Support local community stray dog sterilization and vaccination programs.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Rabies
* **Acute Encephalomyelitis & Death:** Once the virus reaches the brain, it triggers complete nervous system dysfunction, leading to cardiac arrest, asphyxiation due to vocal cord spasms, and 100% certainty of death within 2-10 days of symptom onset.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **A person who had an animal bite weeks ago develops difficulty swallowing, fear of drinking water, or panic when air blows on their face.**
* **An animal bite patient develops sudden severe aggression, confusion, hallucinations, or foaming at the mouth.**

---

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Rabies Control Programme (NRCP):** Coordinates free availability of anti-rabies vaccines (ARV) and Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG) at all government district hospitals and community health centers.
* **Integrated Rabies Surveillance:** Encourages monitoring of the biting animal for 10 days. If the animal remains healthy for 10 days, it indicates it did not have rabies during the bite (though vaccine schedule should not be stopped without doctor clearance).
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless hospitalization packages for severe animal bites requiring surgical debridement under anesthesia and RIG therapy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is rabies?**
   Rabies is a fatal viral disease caused by the rabies virus (Lyssavirus) transmitted through the saliva of infected animals, attacking the brain.

2. **Why is rabies 100% fatal but 100% preventable?**
   Once symptoms start (hydrophobia, throat spasms), the virus has destroyed brain tissues, and there is no cure, leading to death. However, taking the PEP vaccine immediately after a bite kills the virus before it reaches the brain, preventing the disease completely.

3. **What is the first aid for a dog bite?**
   Wash the bite wound immediately under running tap water with soap for 15 minutes, apply Betadine, do not stitch or bandage, and proceed to a hospital for rabies vaccine.

4. **Why does soap wash remove the rabies virus?**
   The rabies virus has a fatty outer envelope (lipid membrane). Soap acts as a surfactant, dissolving this envelope, which deactivates and flushes the virus out of the wound.

5. **What is Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG) and when is it needed?**
   RIG is a solution of ready-made antibodies. It is mandatory for Category III bites (bleeding wounds) and must be injected directly inside the wound to neutralize the virus locally.

6. **Can I stitch a dog bite wound?**
   No. Dog bite wounds should never be sutured. Stitching pushes the virus deep into nerves. If bleeding is life-threatening, apply loose stitches only after injecting RIG.

7. **How many injections are required for the rabies vaccine?**
   Under the standard government intradermal schedule, 4 visits are required on Days 0, 3, 7, and 28. The intramuscular schedule requires 5 doses on Days 0, 3, 7, 14, and 28.

8. **Should I observe the biting dog for 10 days?**
   Yes. If the dog/cat stays completely healthy and alive for 10 days after the bite, it means it did not have active rabies in its saliva during the bite. However, **do not delay starting your vaccine on Day 0 while waiting to observe the dog**.

9. **Where can I get free rabies vaccines in India?**
   Anti-rabies vaccines (ARV) and Rabies Immunoglobulin (RIG) are provided completely free of cost at all government community health centers and civil hospitals under the NRCP program.

10. **What is "Hydrophobia"?**
    Hydrophobia is the fear of water. It is a neurological symptom of rabies where trying to drink water triggers violent, extremely painful muscle spasms in the throat, making swallowing impossible.

11. **Can rabies spread through a scratch without bleeding?**
    Yes. A scratch that breaks the top layer of skin (Category II exposure) can allow the virus in the animal's claws (contaminated with saliva) to infect the nerves. It requires vaccination.

12. **Why is a rabies vaccine not given in the gluteal muscle (buttocks)?**
    Injecting the vaccine into the buttocks can cause the medicine to be absorbed by fat tissue instead of muscle, leading to poor antibody production and vaccine failure. It must be given in the shoulder (deltoid muscle).

13. **Is rabies transmitted by monkey or bat bites?**
    Yes. Monkey, bat, cat, and jackal bites can transmit rabies. All wild mammal bites require immediate first aid and PEP vaccination.

14. **Can rabies transmit if an animal licks my intact skin?**
    No. Licking on healthy, unbroken skin is a Category I exposure and does not transmit rabies. Just wash the area with soap and water. However, licks on cuts or wounds require immediate PEP.

15. **What is the price of generic Anti-Rabies Vaccine (ARV) in India?**
    While government hospitals provide it free, generic ARV vials are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹60 to ₹80 per vial.

16. **What is "Aerophobia" in rabies?**
    It is the panic and throat spasms triggered by a draft of air or wind blowing on the patient's face, caused by hyper-sensitive sensory nerves.

17. **Can a person get rabies years after a dog bite?**
    Yes. The virus travels slowly along nerves to the brain. The incubation period is usually 1-3 months, but in rare cases, it can take up to a year or more.

18. **Why is applying red chili powder on a dog bite dangerous?**
    Chili powder does not kill the virus. It causes chemical burns, damages the local tissue, and pushes the virus deeper into the open nerves.

19. **What is the difference between Equine and Human RIG?**
    Equine RIG (ERIG) is derived from horses and is cheaper but requires an allergy skin test first. Human RIG (HRIG) is derived from humans, does not cause allergic reactions, but is very expensive.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover dog bite treatment?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for severe animal bite wound care, surgical cleaning, and Rabies Immunoglobulin treatment.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Sucking blood out of a dog bite wound removes the rabies virus.
  * **Fact:** Sucking a wound is extremely dangerous. It creates micro-tears in the mouth, allowing the virus direct access to the brain, and does not clean the wound. Wash with soap and running water.
* **Myth:** Applying red chili powder, turmeric, or lime (chuna) on the wound kills rabies.
  * **Fact:** These home remedies do not kill the virus. They cause tissue damage and severe pain, making it easier for the virus to infect nerves.
* **Myth:** If the dog that bit you is a domestic pet, you do not need vaccination.
  * **Fact:** Even domestic dogs must be vaccinated. If a pet's vaccination card is not up to date, or if the dog was bitten by a stray, you must take the rabies vaccine.
* **Myth:** Tying a key or a copper coin over a dog bite wound cures rabies.
  * **Fact:** Tying metals has no medical effect. It is a useless superstition that delays vaccine care, leading to 100% certain death.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures rabies.
  * **Fact:** This is a highly dangerous myth. Rabies is a viral brain infection that can only be prevented by vaccine and RIG.
* **Myth:** Rabies only happens if the biting dog is actively foaming at the mouth.
  * **Fact:** An animal can transmit rabies days before it shows any symptoms (like foaming or aggression). Treat all stray animal bites as suspect.
* **Myth:** The rabies vaccine requires 14 injections in the stomach.
  * **Fact:** This is an obsolete method from decades ago. Modern rabies vaccines are painless cell-culture vaccines given in 4 or 5 doses in the shoulder (arm) or skin.
* **Myth:** Once a person shows fear of water (hydrophobia), they can be cured in the ICU.
  * **Fact:** Once hydrophobia develops, the disease is 100% fatal. ICU care can only provide sedation. Immediate PEP before symptoms start is the only way to survive.
* **Myth:** Rabies can spread through the air or by touching a patient's dry skin.
  * **Fact:** Rabies does not spread through the air or casual skin contact. It requires contact with infected saliva or brain tissue.
* **Myth:** If you get vaccinated against rabies, you are immune to dog bites forever.
  * **Fact:** The vaccine provides protection for a limited time. If you get bitten again years later, you still require a booster vaccine dose on Day 0 and Day 3.
