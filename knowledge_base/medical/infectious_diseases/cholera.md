# Cholera (Haiza) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Vibrio cholerae and Rehydration Guide)

## Overview

### English
Cholera, commonly known as Haiza in India, is an acute, life-threatening diarrheal infection caused by the ingestion of food or water contaminated with the bacterium **Vibrio cholerae**. It is characterized by the sudden onset of painless, massive watery diarrhea (classic "rice-water stools") and vomiting. The rapid loss of fluids and electrolytes (up to 1 liter/hour) can lead to severe dehydration, hypovolemic shock, and death within hours if untreated. The cornerstone of therapy is immediate rehydration using Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS) or intravenous fluids (Ringer's Lactate) for severe cases. Prevention relies on clean drinking water, proper sanitation, and oral cholera vaccines (OCV).

## Quick Summary
Cholera is an acute bacterial diarrheal disease. Symptoms feature painless, voluminous "rice-water stools," vomiting, and signs of severe dehydration (sunken eyes, dry mouth). First aid centers on immediate ORS hydration. Never use anti-motility drugs. Diagnostics analyze stool culture. Management uses IV fluids and antibiotics (Doxycycline).

---

## Definition
* **Cholera:** An acute diarrheal illness caused by toxigenic Vibrio cholerae serogroup O1 or O139, producing a potent enterotoxin that triggers massive fluid secretion in the small intestine.
* **Rice-Water Stools:** The classic watery, non-smelly, pale grey diarrhea containing flecks of mucus and epithelial cells, resembling water in which rice has been washed.
* **Hypovolemic Shock:** A life-threatening condition where severe fluid loss leaves the heart unable to pump enough blood, leading to organ failure.

---

## Pathology of Fluid Loss & Dehydration Stages
The cholera toxin forces intestinal cells to secrete massive fluids:
1. **Cholera Toxin Action:** The bacteria colonize the small intestine and release cholera toxin (CT). This toxin locks adenylate cyclase active, causing massive excretion of sodium, potassium, bicarbonate, and water into the gut lumen.
2. **WHO Dehydration Classification:**
   * **No Dehydration:** Patient is alert; normal eyes; drinks normally. (Treat with ORS).
   * **Some Dehydration:** Restless/irritable; sunken eyes; thirsty (drinks eagerly); skin pinch goes back slowly. (Treat with ORS under monitoring).
   * **Severe Dehydration (Emergency):** Lethargic or unconscious; sunken eyes; dry mouth; skin pinch goes back very slowly ($> 2$ seconds); unable to drink. **(Requires immediate IV Ringer's Lactate resuscitation).**

## Symptoms of Cholera (Haiza)

### English
* Sudden, painless, voluminous watery diarrhea ("rice-water stools"); continuous watery vomiting; rapid heart rate and loss of skin elasticity (turgor); sunken eyes, dry tongue, and absence of tears; extreme thirst; decreased or absent urine output (oliguria/anuria); severe muscle cramps (due to potassium/sodium loss).

## First Aid & Rehydration Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Start ORS (Oral Rehydration Salts) immediately.** Dissolve 1 packet of WHO-ORS in 1 liter of clean drinking water. Give the patient small sips continuously. Do not wait to go to the hospital to start rehydration.
* **Give Zinc supplements** (20mg daily for 10-14 days) to children under 5 to reduce diarrhea severity.
* **Continue breastfeeding** infants and feeding soft home foods (khichdi, bananas) as tolerated.
* **Transport to hospital immediately** if the patient is lethargic, vomiting continuously, or cannot drink.

#### Hindi
* **तुरंत ओआरएस (ORS) शुरू करें:** 1 पैकेट ओआरएस को 1 लीटर साफ पानी में घोलें और लगातार पिलाएं।
* **जिंक (Zinc) पूरक दें:** 5 वर्ष से कम उम्र के बच्चों को 20mg जिंक टैबलेट दें।
* **स्तनपान जारी रखें।**

#### Hinglish
* **ORS solution turant shuru karein:** 1 packet WHO-ORS ko 1 Liter saaf peene ke paani me gholyein aur patient ko thoda-thoda lagatar pilate rahein. Rehydration shuru karne me late na karein.
* **Zinc tablet dein:** 5 saal se chhote bachho ko 14 din tak rojana 20mg Zinc tablet dein.
* **Soft food khilayein:** Bachho ko dhoodh (breastfeed) aur khichdi, kela khilana jari rakhein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT give anti-motility drugs** (like Loperamide or Lomotil). These drugs stop bowel movement, trapping the toxic cholera bacteria and toxins inside the gut, which can cause toxic megacolon and worsen sepsis.
* **Do NOT dilute ORS incorrectly.** Never mix ORS with juice, milk, or soft drinks, and do not add extra sugar or salt to the ORS solution.
* **Do NOT give plain water only** in large quantities, as it lacks electrolytes and can trigger seizures (hyponatremia).

#### Hindi
* **दस्त रोकने की दवा (Loperamide) न दें:** लोपेरामाइड जैसी दवाएं आंतों की गति रोकती हैं, जिससे जहर शरीर में ही जमा होकर गंभीर संक्रमण करता है।
* **ओआरएस में चीनी/नमक अलग से न मिलाएं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Loperamide/Lomotil (dast rokne ki tablet) bilkul na dein:** Yeh medicines bowel movement ko lock karti hain, jisse cholera bacteria aur unka zeher body ke andar hi lock ho jata hai, jo severe sepsis aur death ka kaaran banta hai.
* **ORS me alag se sugar/salt na dalein:** Ghol me alag se salt/sugar na milayein aur na hi ise juice me gholein.

## Medical & Emergency Management

### Intravenous Fluid Resuscitation

#### English
* For severe dehydration or shock, insert large-bore IV lines and rapidly infuse **Ringer's Lactate** (100 mL/kg fluid given over 3 hours for adults, 6 hours for infants) to restore blood pressure and kidney filtration.

#### Hindi
* गंभीर डिहाइड्रेशन में अस्पताल में तुरंत ड्रिप (Ringer's Lactate) लगाना (100 mL/kg)।

#### Hinglish
* Severe dehydration me hospital me immediate IV fluids (Ringer's Lactate) ki speed drip chadhayi jati hai (100 mL/kg) taaki blood pressure aur gurde (kidney) shut-down hone se bachaya ja sake.

### Antibiotic Therapy

#### English
* Given to reduce stool volume and shorten bacterial shedding (e.g., Single-dose **Doxycycline 300mg** is the drug of choice for adults; Azithromycin for pregnant women and children).

#### Hindi
* संक्रमण की अवधि कम करने के लिए एंटीबायोटिक (Doxycycline) देना।

#### Hinglish
* Bacteria ko kill karne aur dast ki duration kam karne ke liye doctor **Doxycycline (300mg single dose)** or Azithromycin antibiotic likhte hain.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to cholera management:*
1. **Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS):** Glucose-electrolyte solution to restore hydration.
2. **Crystalloid IV Fluids:** Rapid volume expanders (e.g., Ringer's Lactate, Normal Saline).
3. **Antimicrobial Agents:** Target Vibrio cholerae (e.g., Doxycycline, Azithromycin, Ciprofloxacin).
4. **Zinc Supplements:** Pediatric mucosal repair agent (Zinc Sulfate).
5. **Antiemetics:** Control vomiting in hospital settings (e.g., Ondansetron).

## Prevention of Cholera
* **Safe Water (WASH):** Drink only boiled, chlorinated, or filtered water. Store drinking water in closed containers.
* **Food Hygiene:** Eat only piping hot, freshly cooked food. Wash raw fruits and vegetables thoroughly. Avoid raw street food.
* **Sanitation:** Practice safe disposal of feces; wash hands with soap and water after using the toilet and before eating.
* **Oral Cholera Vaccines (OCV):** Get vaccinated during outbreaks (e.g., Shanchol or Euvichol vaccines, taken in 2 oral doses 2 weeks apart).

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Cholera
* **Hypovolemic Burn Shock:** Severe loss of blood volume leading to cardiac arrest and death.
* **Acute Renal Failure (AKI):** Lack of blood flow destroying kidney tubules, causing complete uremia.
* **Severe Hypokalemia:** Low potassium stopping the heart muscle from beating.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient is lethargic, sleepy, unresponsive, or goes into a coma.**
* **The patient is unable to drink ORS or vomits every sip they try to swallow.**
* **The patient has completely stopped passing urine for over 8-12 hours, accompanied by muscle twitching.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Center for Disease Control (NCDC):** Coordinates cholera surveillance and deploys rapid response teams during seasonal outbreaks (monsoon).

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is cholera?**
   Cholera is a highly infectious bacterial disease caused by *Vibrio cholerae*, leading to sudden, severe, watery diarrhea and dehydration.

2. **What are "rice-water stools"?**
   It is the classic symptom of cholera where the diarrhea looks like pale, cloudy white water in which rice has been washed, and has no fecal odor.

3. **What is the most important treatment for cholera?**
   Immediate rehydration using Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS) is the most critical, life-saving treatment. Do not wait for a doctor to start ORS.

4. **Why are anti-diarrheal drugs like Lomotil dangerous in cholera?**
   They paralyze gut movement, trapping the toxic cholera bacteria inside. This allows the toxin to build up, worsening shock and sepsis.

5. **What is the WHO-ORS formula?**
   WHO-ORS contains sodium chloride, glucose, potassium chloride, and trisodium citrate, which maximizes water absorption in the intestines.

6. **How does Ringer's Lactate help in severe cholera?**
   Ringer's Lactate is an IV fluid that replaces lost volume and corrects the metabolic acidosis caused by bicarbonate loss in watery stools.

7. **Where can I get free ORS and cholera treatment in India?**
   ORS packets and cholera treatments (including IV fluids) are provided completely free at all government PHCs, CHCs, and government hospitals.

8. **How does cholera transmit?**
   It transmits through the fecal-oral route, usually by drinking water or eating food contaminated with the feces of an infected person.

9. **What is the role of Zinc in pediatric cholera?**
   Zinc helps repair the damaged mucosal lining of the intestines, reducing the severity and duration of diarrhea in children under 5.

10. **Why does cholera cause severe muscle cramps?**
    Massive watery diarrhea flushes out essential potassium and sodium. Low potassium (hypokalemia) causes painful cramps in the calves and abdomen.

11. **Can cholera cause death in a single day?**
    Yes. In severe untreated cases, a person can lose up to 10-12 liters of fluid in a day, leading to hypovolemic shock and death within 3 to 18 hours.

12. **What is the antibiotic used to treat cholera in adults?**
    A single oral dose of **Doxycycline (300mg)** is the first-line antibiotic to kill the bacteria and reduce diarrheal output.

13. **Is there a vaccine for cholera?**
    Yes. Oral Cholera Vaccines (OCV) like Shanchol and Euvichol are given orally in 2 doses, 14 days apart, providing good protection during outbreaks.

14. **How do I chlorinate drinking water at home?**
    Add 1 chlorine tablet (halogen tablet) or 3-5 drops of liquid bleach per 20 liters of water. Stir well and wait 30 minutes before drinking.

15. **What is the price of WHO-ORS packets in India?**
    WHO-ORS packets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹4 to ₹6 per packet, compared to ₹20+ in private pharmacies.

16. **Why does severe cholera cause kidney failure?**
    Extreme dehydration drops blood pressure, depriving the kidneys of blood flow. This causes acute tubular necrosis, stopping urine production.

17. **Can a pregnant woman take Doxycycline for cholera?**
    No. Doxycycline is avoided in pregnancy due to risk of bone/tooth damage in the fetus. Pregnant women are treated with Azithromycin.

18. **How do I perform a skin turgor test for dehydration?**
    Pinch the skin on the patient's abdomen or forearm for a second, then release. If it takes more than 2 seconds to flatten (slow recoil), they are severely dehydrated.

19. **What is the Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme (IDSP)?**
    It is a government network that tracks reports of multiple cases of watery diarrhea in an area to detect and stop cholera outbreaks before they spread.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover cholera hospitalization?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for acute diarrheal disease treatment, pediatric dehydration ICU care, and all IV medications.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Cholera is caused by eating too many sour mangoes in summer.
  * **Fact:** Cholera is caused only by the *Vibrio cholerae* bacteria from water or food contaminated with feces, not by mangoes.
* **Myth:** Drink only pure cow milk to cure cholera diarrhea.
  * **Fact:** Milk is hard to digest during cholera and can worsen diarrhea. Drink only WHO-ORS solution and clean water.
* **Myth:** Lomotil is a magical drug that stops cholera instantly.
  * **Fact:** Lomotil is highly dangerous in cholera. It traps the bacteria and toxins in your intestines, which can cause toxic shock and death. Avoid it.
* **Myth:** If you are vomiting, you should stop drinking ORS.
  * **Fact:** Continue giving ORS. Even if the patient vomits, some fluid is still absorbed. Give small, frequent sips (1 teaspoon every 2 minutes).
* **Myth:** Plain water is enough to treat cholera dehydration.
  * **Fact:** Plain water lacks electrolytes. Drinking only water can cause a dangerous drop in blood sodium (hyponatremia), triggering seizures. Use ORS.
* **Myth:** You should starve a person with cholera to let the stomach rest.
  * **Fact:** Starvation weakens the patient and delays gut recovery. Continue feeding soft, easily digestible foods like khichdi and bananas.
* **Myth:** Cholera spreads through the air when an infected person coughs.
  * **Fact:** Cholera does not spread through the air. It spreads only when fecal bacteria contaminate food or drinking water.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a lemon will cure cholera instantly.
  * **Fact:** Lemon cannot kill Vibrio cholerae in the gut. Only proper rehydration (ORS) and antibiotics can cure cholera.
* **Myth:** Chlorine tablets make water toxic and dangerous to drink.
  * **Fact:** Chlorine tablets in correct doses are completely safe and are the most effective way to kill cholera bacteria in drinking water.
* **Myth:** Cholera has been completely eradicated from India.
  * **Fact:** Cholera outbreaks still occur seasonally, especially during monsoons, in areas with poor drinking water filtration and open drainage.
