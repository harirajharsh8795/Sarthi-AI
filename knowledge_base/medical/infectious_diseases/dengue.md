# Dengue (Dengue Fever) ki Pehchan, Lakshan aur Upachar (Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever and Platelet Guide)

## Overview

### English
Dengue Fever, commonly known as "Breakbone Fever" due to severe muscle and joint pain, is a mosquito-borne viral infection caused by the **Dengue Virus (DENV 1-4)** and transmitted by the bite of an infected female **Aedes aegypti** mosquito. While most cases present with acute fever, retro-orbital (behind the eye) pain, and rash, some progress to life-threatening complications: **Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF)** and **Dengue Shock Syndrome (DSS)**. These are marked by capillary leakage (fluid escaping blood vessels), pleural effusion, severe thrombocytopenia (platelet count dropping below **150,000 cells/$\mu$L**), and bleeding. Management relies entirely on hydration and fever control using Paracetamol. NSAIDs (Aspirin, Ibuprofen) are strictly contraindicated due to bleeding risk.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
Dengue is a viral mosquito-borne fever. Symptoms include sudden high fever, retro-orbital pain, severe joint pain, and bleeding gums. First aid requires intensive hydration (ORS) and Paracetamol. Avoid all NSAID painkillers (Aspirin, Ibuprofen, Diclofenac) as they promote internal bleeding. Diagnostics monitor platelet count via daily CBC and NS1/IgM tests. Management focuses on preventing shock.

---

## Definition
* **Dengue Fever:** An acute febrile illness caused by one of four closely related dengue virus serotypes (DENV-1, DENV-2, DENV-3, DENV-4).
* **Thrombocytopenia:** A clinical state characterized by an abnormally low number of platelets in the blood (Normal: $1.5 - 4.5\text{ Lakhs/}\mu\text{L}$), raising the risk of bleeding.
* **Capillary Leak Syndrome:** A pathological state where fluid leaks out of blood capillaries into surrounding body cavities (lungs/abdomen), leading to low blood volume and shock.

---

## Clinical Progression & Critical Phase
Dengue progresses through three distinct phases:
1. **Febrile Phase (Days 1 - 5):** Sudden high fever ($104^\circ\text{F}$), joint pain, retro-orbital pain. (Hydrate with ORS).
2. **Critical Phase (Days 3 - 7):** **The fever drops to normal ($98.6^\circ\text{F}$), but the risk of shock peaks.** Capillaries leak fluid, hematocrit rises, and platelet counts drop rapidly. *This is when complications like pleural effusion (fluid in lungs) and DSS develop. Close monitoring is required.*
3. **Recovery Phase (Days 7 - 10):** Fluid is reabsorbed back into the blood, platelet counts recover, and a typical "white spots on red background" recovery rash appears on the skin.

## First Aid & Hydration Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Maintain intensive oral hydration.** Ask the patient to drink ORS solution, coconut water, fresh juices, and warm soups continuously. Dehydration is the primary cause of dengue shock.
* **Give Paracetamol only** for fever and pain. Keep the dose within safe limits ($< 3-4$ grams/day).
* **Monitor Platelet Count daily** using a Complete Blood Count (CBC) test once the fever starts dropping, under doctor supervision.
* **Use physical cooling** (damp cloth on forehead/body sponge) to control high fever.

#### Hindi
* **अत्यधिक तरल पदार्थ लें (Hydration):** लगातार ओआरएस (ORS), नारियल पानी, नींबू पानी पिएं।
* **केवल पैरासिटामोल (Paracetamol) लें:** बुखार के लिए केवल पैरासिटामोल का उपयोग करें।
* **दैनिक सीबीसी (CBC) जांच कराएं:** प्लेटलेट्स की निगरानी करें।

#### Hinglish
* **Dher saara liquid piyein (Hydration):** ORS, nariyal paani, nimbu paani, aur daal ka paani lagatar pilate rahein. Body me paani ki kami na hone dein.
* **Sirf Paracetamol dein:** Fever aur body ache ke liye sirf paracetamol tablet dein.
* **Daily Platelet checkup (CBC):** Bukhar utarne ke baad rojana CBC blood test karakar platelets count aur Hematocrit level monitor karein.
* **Cold Sponging:** Normal paani ki patti rakhein sir aur body par bukhar kam karne ke liye.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT take Aspirin, Ibuprofen, Diclofenac, or Mefenamic Acid.** These NSAID painkillers act as blood thinners and irritate the stomach lining. In a patient with low platelets, taking these drugs can cause severe, fatal internal stomach bleeding.
* **Do NOT demand immediate platelet transfusion** just because the count drops below 100,000. Transfusion is generally not recommended unless platelets drop below **10,000 cells/$\mu$L** or there is active, severe bleeding.
* **Do NOT self-medicate with antibiotics.** Dengue is a viral infection; antibiotics do not work.

#### Hindi
* **दर्दनिवारक (NSAIDs) बिल्कुल न लें:** एस्पिरिन, इबुप्रोफेन या डाइक्लोफेनाक लेने से पेट में घातक ब्लीडिंग हो सकती है।
* **जल्दबाजी में प्लेटलेट्स न चढ़ाएं:** प्लेटलेट्स 10,000 से कम होने या ब्लीडिंग होने पर ही ट्रांसफ्यूजन की जरूरत होती है।

#### Hinglish
* **Aspirin, Ibuprofen, Diclofenac ya Mefenamic acid (Meftal) bilkul na lein:** Yeh painkillers blood ko patla karti hain. Dengue me platelets pehle se low hote hain, agar ye dawaiyan li jayein, to stomach me internal bleeding shuru ho sakti hai jo fatal ho sakti hai.
* **Platelet transfusion ke liye jaldi na karein:** Platelets 1 Lakh se niche aane par panic na ho. Jab tak count **10,000** se kam na ho ya active bleeding (khoon behna) na ho, tab tak platelets chadhane (transfusion) ki zarurat nahi hoti.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Dengue NS1 Antigen Test:** A blood test performed in the first 1-5 days of fever to detect viral proteins.
* **Dengue IgM/IgG Antibody Test:** Done after day 5 of fever to detect antibodies indicating active or past infection.
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC):** Monitored daily. Checks for **Thrombocytopenia** (platelet count dropping) and **Hematocrit (Hct) elevation** (rising hematocrit indicates blood hemoconcentration due to capillary fluid leakage, a key sign of shock).

## Medical & Emergency Management

### Intravenous Fluid Resuscitation

#### English
* For Dengue Shock Syndrome, patients require rapid IV infusions of crystalloids (Normal Saline or Ringer's Lactate) at controlled rates to restore vascular volume and prevent multi-organ failure.

#### Hindi
* डेंगू शॉक सिंड्रोम (DSS) होने पर अस्पताल में तुरंत IV सलाइन ड्रिप चढ़ाना।

#### Hinglish
* Dengue Shock (DSS) hone par hospital me immediate IV saline/Ringer's Lactate drip lagayi jati hai taaki blood volume normalize ho sake.

### Platelet Transfusion Criteria

#### English
* Prophylactic platelet transfusion is clinically indicated only if platelet count drops below **10,000/$\mu$L** in the absence of bleeding, or below **50,000/$\mu$L** in patients with active hemorrhage or scheduled for surgery.

#### Hindi
* प्लेटलेट्स केवल 10,000 से कम होने पर या रक्तस्राव होने पर ही चढ़ाना।

#### Hinglish
* Platelets chadhane (transfusion) ki zarurat tabhi hoti hai jab platelet count **10,000** se niche gir jaye ya fir gums/urine se active bleeding ho rahi ho.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to dengue management:*
1. **Analgesics & Antipyretics:** Manage pain and fever safely (e.g., Paracetamol). *Do not use NSAIDs.*
2. **Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS):** Electrolyte replenishment.
3. **Crystalloids (IV):** Restore blood volume (e.g., Normal Saline, Ringer's Lactate).
4. **Colloids (IV):** Used clinically in severe shock if crystalloids fail to restore BP.

## Prevention of Dengue
* **Mosquito Breeding Control:** Eliminate standing water in flower pots, coolers, discarded tires, and buckets around the house, as Aedes mosquitoes breed only in clean standing water.
* **Personal Protection:** Wear full-sleeved shirts, long trousers, and socks during daytime (Aedes mosquitoes are day-biters).
* **Mosquito Repellents:** Apply DEET or picaridin-based insect repellents on exposed skin. Use mosquito nets at home.

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated/Severe Dengue
* **Dengue Shock Syndrome (DSS):** Critical drop in blood pressure due to fluid leakage, causing multi-organ failure.
* **Severe Hemorrhage:** Massive internal stomach or brain bleeding.
* **Acute Liver Failure:** Severe dengue causing hepatitis and liver dysfunction.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Severe, continuous abdominal (stomach) pain and persistent vomiting (more than 3 times in 24 hours).**
* **Active bleeding from the gums, nose, vomit (black coffee-color), or blood in stool/urine.**
* **The patient becomes extremely weak, confused, lethargic, or cold and clammy skin (signs of shock).**
* **Sudden breathing difficulty or rapid breathing.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Vector Borne Disease Control Programme (NVBDCP):** Coordinates vector control measures, fogging operations, and free diagnostic facilities across states.
* **National Dengue Day (May 16):** An initiative by the MoHFW to raise public awareness about Aedes breeding control before monsoons.
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Covers cashless hospitalization packages for severe dengue, DSS/DHF ICU care, platelet transfusions, and IV fluids.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Offers generic Paracetamol, WHO-ORS packets, and supportive medicines at highly subsidized prices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is dengue fever?**
   Dengue is a viral infection transmitted by the bite of an infected female *Aedes aegypti* mosquito, causing high fever, joint pain, and low platelets.

2. **Why is Aedes mosquito called a "day biter"?**
   Aedes mosquitoes are most active during the day, especially in the early morning (after sunrise) and late afternoon (before sunset). Wear full sleeves during these times.

3. **What is the normal platelet count and why does it drop in dengue?**
   Normal platelet count is 1.5 to 4.5 Lakhs/$\mu$L. Dengue virus infects bone marrow cells, suppressing platelet production, and causes antibodies to destroy platelets in the blood.

4. **Why are painkillers like Ibuprofen banned in dengue?**
   NSAID painkillers like Ibuprofen, Diclofenac, and Aspirin thin the blood and damage the stomach lining. In a patient with low platelets, this can cause fatal internal stomach bleeding.

5. **What is the difference between Dengue Fever and Dengue Shock Syndrome (DSS)?**
   Dengue fever is a self-limiting illness with joint pain and fever. DSS is a life-threatening stage where fluid leaks out of blood vessels, causing a critical drop in blood pressure and organ failure.

6. **What is the "Critical Phase" in dengue?**
   The Critical Phase occurs between day 3 and day 7 of illness, when the fever starts dropping. This is when the risk of capillary leakage, low platelets, and shock is highest.

7. **Where can I get a free dengue test in India?**
   Dengue NS1/IgM diagnostic tests are performed completely free of charge at all government district civil hospitals and sentinel laboratories.

8. **When does a dengue patient need a platelet transfusion?**
   Transfusion is generally required only if platelets drop below 10,000/$\mu$L in stable patients, or below 50,000/$\mu$L if there is active bleeding or scheduled surgery.

9. **Can papaya leaf extract cure dengue?**
    Papaya leaf extract has shown some properties that may help increase platelets, but it is not an antiviral cure. Hydration with ORS and water is the primary life-saving treatment.

10. **Why does high hematocrit indicate danger in dengue?**
    Hematocrit measures the percentage of red blood cells in the blood. A rising hematocrit indicates that fluid is leaking out of the blood vessels, making the blood thick, which is a warning sign of shock.

11. **How do I prevent Aedes mosquito breeding at home?**
    Change water in air coolers, flower vases, and pet bowls weekly. Cover all water storage drums. Throw away empty tins, tires, and pots that can collect rainwater.

12. **Can a person get dengue twice?**
    Yes. There are 4 different strains of the dengue virus (DENV 1-4). A person can be infected up to four times, and the second infection is often more severe (raising DHF risk).

13. **Is there a vaccine for dengue in India?**
    While some dengue vaccines (like Dengvaxia) are approved in other countries, they are not widely used in India, and vector control remains the primary prevention method.

14. **What is the retro-orbital pain in dengue?**
    It is a classic symptom of dengue characterized by a dull, aching pain behind the eyes, which worsens when the patient moves their eyes.

15. **What is the price of generic Paracetamol and ORS at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Paracetamol (650mg) and WHO-ORS packets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹3 to ₹5 per strip or packet.

16. **Why does dengue cause severe joint pain?**
    The viral infection causes acute inflammation in muscle tissues and joints, earning it the name "breakbone fever" due to the intensity of the pain.

17. **What is the 20WBCT test?**
    It is a simple blood clotting test used in vipers. For dengue, doctors rely on CBC, NS1 ELISA, and monitoring hematocrit.

18. **Can a pregnant woman transmit dengue to her baby?**
    Yes. Vertical transmission can occur, especially if the mother is infected close to delivery, risking stillbirth or low birth weight.

19. **What is the role of the NVBDCP program?**
    NVBDCP is a national health program that conducts mosquito control fogging, monitors vector-borne outbreaks, and provides free diagnostic sentinel labs.

20. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover platelet transfusion costs?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for dengue hospitalization, ICU bed charges, IV fluids, and platelet component transfusions.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Drinking goat milk or kiwi juice is the only way to cure dengue.
  * **Fact:** Goat milk and kiwi have no antiviral properties against dengue. While they provide hydration, drinking plain water and WHO-ORS is the scientifically proven way to save lives.
* **Myth:** If the fever drops to normal, the patient is completely out of danger.
  * **Fact:** The drop in fever marks the start of the "Critical Phase" (Days 3-7) when platelets drop and shock/internal bleeding are most likely to occur. Monitor closely.
* **Myth:** Platelets must be transfused immediately if the count drops below 80,000.
  * **Fact:** Transfusion is not recommended above 10,000 unless there is active bleeding. Unnecessary transfusions can cause lung fluid overload and reactions.
* **Myth:** Any painkiller (like Combiflam or Flexon) is safe for dengue body pain.
  * **Fact:** Combiflam contains Ibuprofen, which acts as a blood thinner. Taking it during low platelets can cause fatal internal stomach bleeding. Use Paracetamol only.
* **Myth:** Dengue mosquitoes breed in dirty sewer drains.
  * **Fact:** Aedes mosquitoes breed only in clean, stagnant water, such as water collected in coolers, flower pots, tires, and roof drums. Keep home water containers clean.
* **Myth:** Fogging operations completely eliminate all dengue mosquitoes.
  * **Fact:** Fogging only kills flying adult mosquitoes. It does not kill the mosquito larvae in standing water. Eliminating standing water is key.
* **Myth:** Dengue mosquitoes bite only at night.
  * **Fact:** Aedes mosquitoes are day-biters. They bite mostly in the early morning and late afternoon. Use repellents during the day.
* **Myth:** Having dengue once makes you immune to all future dengue infections.
  * **Fact:** Recovery only protects you from that specific strain. You can be infected by the other three strains, and second infections carry a higher risk of severe DHF.
* **Myth:** Antibiotics are necessary to cure dengue fever fast.
  * **Fact:** Dengue is a virus. Antibiotics have no effect on viruses. Overusing antibiotics can cause stomach irritation and drug resistance.
* **Myth:** If you do not have bleeding from the nose, you do not have severe dengue.
  * **Fact:** Severe dengue (capillary leak/shock) can occur internally without visible bleeding, presenting as severe stomach pain and low blood pressure.

* **Assistant:** Dengue patient ko dehydration se bachne ke liye daily kam se kam **3 se 4 Liters** fluids pina chahiye. Isme normal water ke sath ORS solution, nariyal paani, nimbu paani, fresh juices, aur watery soups shamil karein taaki body me electrolytes balance rahein.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Dengue infection confirm karne ke liye NS1 aur IgM test kab karwana chahiye?
* **Assistant:** Fever shuru hone ke pehle 1 se 5 dino ke andar **NS1 Antigen** test karana chahiye jo virus ki presence batata hai. Fever shuru hone ke 5 din ke baad **IgM/IgG Antibody** test karana chahiye jo body me dengue ke khilaf bani antibodies check karta hai.
