## Quick Summary
This general medical FAQ guide provides answers to common questions about basic symptoms (fever, pain, coughing), first aid, medication safety, and health guidelines. Normal values include temperature $98.6^\circ$F, blood pressure $120/80$ mmHg, and pulse 60-100 bpm. Symptoms of emergency require immediate 108 calls. Prevention relies on vaccines, low-sodium diets, and regular exercise.

---

## Key Clinical Vital Signs (Normal Values)
Before evaluating symptoms, it is important to know the normal parameters of the human body:
* **Body Temperature:** $97.8^\circ\text{F} - 99.1^\circ\text{F}$ (Average: $98.6^\circ\text{F}$ or $37^\circ\text{C}$). Fever is clinically defined as $\ge 100.4^\circ\text{F}$ ($38^\circ\text{C}$).
* **Blood Pressure:** Normal is under $120/80$ mmHg. High BP (Hypertension) is defined as $\ge 140/90$ mmHg.
* **Pulse Rate (Heart Rate):** $60 - 100$ beats per minute (bpm) at rest.
* **Respiration Rate:** $12 - 20$ breaths per minute in adults.
* **Oxygen Saturation ($SpO_2$):** $95\% - 100\%$ on room air. Levels under $92\%$ indicate hypoxemia requiring clinical attention.

## Common Symptoms & Basic Self-Care

### English
* **Fever:** Hydrate with water/ORS. Place damp cloth on forehead. Take Paracetamol under dosage guidance.
* **Cough/Cold:** Use steam inhalation and warm saline gargles. Stay hydrated.
* **Diarrhea:** Drink ORS solution after every loose stool. Eat bananas and rice.

## Medicines Safety Rules
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. Follow these strict rules for clinical safety:*
1. **Never Self-Medicate Antibiotics:** Antibiotics only kill bacteria, not viruses (like cold/flu). Using them without prescription leads to **Antibiotic Resistance**, making future infections untreatable.
2. **OTC Painkiller Warnings:** Common painkillers like Ibuprofen or Diclofenac (NSAIDs) can cause severe stomach bleeding and kidney damage if taken frequently without doctor supervision.
3. **Paracetamol Dose Limits:** Do not exceed 4 grams (8 tablets of 500mg) of paracetamol within 24 hours to prevent acute liver toxicity.
4. **Store Safely:** Keep all medicines in their original packing, away from moisture and direct sunlight, and out of reach of children.

## Preventive Health Guidelines
* **Balanced Nutrition:** Follow a diet low in sodium (salt $< 5$g/day) and refined sugars; eat fresh fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
* **Regular Exercise:** Perform 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise (e.g., 30 minutes brisk walking daily) per week.
* **Routine Screenings:** Get annual blood sugar, blood pressure, and lipid profile tests after reaching 30-35 years of age.
* **Vaccinations:** Complete the full schedule of adult vaccines (e.g., annual flu shot, tetanus booster every 10 years).

---

## Possible Complications of Neglected Chronic Conditions
* **Untreated Hypertension:** Leads to hemorrhagic stroke (paralysis) and coronary artery disease (heart attacks).
* **Uncontrolled Diabetes:** Causes diabetic foot gangrene, permanent blindness (retinopathy), and kidney failure.
* **Untreated Infections:** Can progress to sepsis, causing multi-organ failure and a critical drop in blood pressure.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Call 108 (Ambulance) or go to the nearest emergency department immediately if you observe:
* **Crushing chest pain radiating to left arm or neck, accompanied by cold sweating and shortness of breath.**
* **Sudden weakness or numbness in one side of the face/body, and difficulty speaking clearly (Stroke).**
* **Difficulty breathing, gasping for air, or blue lips/tongue (Cyanosis).**
* **Loss of consciousness, severe confusion, or sudden seizures.**

## Government Health Schemes (India)
* **Ayushman Bharat (AB-PMJAY):** Provides cashless health cover of up to ₹5 Lakhs per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalizations at all empaneled government and private hospitals.
* **PM Bhartiya Janaushadhi Pariyojana:** Offers high-quality generic medicines, surgical items, and diagnostics support at generic stores at 50% to 90% cheaper prices than branded versions.
* **Ayushman Bharat Health Account (ABHA):** A digital health ID that stores and shares your medical records (prescriptions, lab reports, discharge summaries) digitally across healthcare providers.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a normal human body temperature?**
   The normal body temperature is around 98.6°F (37°C). A temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher is considered a fever.

2. **Why should I not buy antibiotics over-the-counter?**
   Antibiotics only cure bacterial infections. Taking them for viral diseases (like common cold or flu) is useless and causes antibiotic resistance, meaning the drugs will not work when you have a real infection.

3. **What is a normal blood pressure reading?**
   A normal blood pressure reading is under 120/80 mmHg. A reading of 140/90 mmHg or higher indicates high blood pressure (hypertension).

4. **What is the first aid for a minor burn?**
   Cool the burn immediately under cool running tap water for 10-15 minutes. Do not apply ice, butter, or toothpaste, as they trap heat and damage skin. Cover with a clean cloth.

5. **What is the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme?**
   It is a government health scheme providing cashless hospitalisation cover of up to ₹5 Lakhs per year per family for poor and vulnerable families at empaneled hospitals.

6. **What are generic medicines?**
   Generic medicines contain the exact same active ingredients, strength, and quality as branded medicines but are sold at much lower prices because they do not have marketing costs.

7. **How do I register for an ABHA card?**
   You can register for an Ayushman Bharat Health Account (ABHA) card online using your Aadhaar card or mobile number on the official NDHM portal or at government clinics.

8. **What should I do if blood sugar drops below 70 mg/dL?**
   If conscious, follow the Rule of 15: eat 15g of fast-acting sugar (3 teaspoons of sugar in water), wait 15 minutes, and recheck. If unconscious, go to the emergency room.

9. **What is the difference between a cold and the flu (influenza)?**
   A cold is mild, causing runny nose and sore throat. The flu is more severe, causing sudden high fever, body aches, dry cough, and extreme fatigue.

10. **Why should I restrict salt intake?**
    High sodium (salt) intake causes the body to retain water, increasing blood volume and raising blood pressure, which damages the heart and kidneys.

11. **What is the normal range of blood oxygen ($SpO_2$)?**
    For a healthy individual, the normal oxygen level is between 95% and 100%. A level below 92% requires immediate medical attention.

12. **Why does physical exercise lower diabetes risk?**
    Exercise forces muscle cells to absorb glucose directly from the blood for energy without needing insulin, improving overall insulin sensitivity.

13. **What is a stroke and what are its signs?**
    A stroke is a blockage or bleed in brain blood vessels. Remember FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 108.

14. **Why should we not take painkillers daily?**
    NSAID painkillers (like Diclofenac or Ibuprofen) block prostaglandins, which protect the stomach lining and dilate kidney vessels, leading to ulcers and kidney failure.

15. **How long does it take for a vaccine to build immunity?**
    It typically takes about 2 weeks after vaccination for the body to build protective antibodies against the disease.

16. **What is the PM Jan Aushadhi program?**
    It is a government initiative selling high-quality generic medicines at special stores (Kendra) at prices 50-90% lower than branded market equivalents.

17. **What is the first aid for a choking person?**
    Perform the Heimlich maneuver: stand behind the person, wrap your arms around their waist, make a fist, and press inward and upward quickly on their abdomen.

18. **Why does hydration help during a fever?**
    High body temperature increases water loss through sweating and breathing. Drinking fluids prevents dehydration and helps cool the body.

19. **What is the difference between an ECG and an Echo?**
    An ECG checks the heart's electrical pathways (wiring). An Echocardiogram is an ultrasound checkup showing the physical heart muscle, valves, and pumping chambers.

20. **Can stress raise blood pressure permanently?**
    Yes. Chronic stress releases hormones like cortisol and adrenaline, which constrict blood vessels and raise heart rate, leading to chronic hypertension.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Antibiotics are the best cure for a common cold.
  * **Fact:** Cold is caused by viruses. Antibiotics only kill bacteria. Taking them for a cold does not help and can cause side effects and drug resistance.
* **Myth:** Sendha namak (rock salt) is completely safe and does not raise blood pressure.
  * **Fact:** Rock salt contains sodium chloride, just like normal table salt. It raises blood pressure just as much and must be restricted.
* **Myth:** Apply toothpaste or mustard oil on a fresh burn to soothe it.
  * **Fact:** Toothpaste and oils contain chemicals that trap heat, increase skin damage, and cause severe wound infections. Use cool running water only.
* **Myth:** If you feel healthy, you do not need to check your blood pressure.
  * **Fact:** High BP is a "silent killer" with no symptoms. You can have dangerously high BP for years without knowing, until it causes a stroke. Check regularly.
* **Myth:** Generic medicines are cheap because they are of low quality.
  * **Fact:** Generic medicines are cheap because they do not have expensive drug development and marketing costs. They are highly tested and equal in quality.
* **Myth:** You should force a seizing patient to smell a shoe or onion to wake them up.
  * **Fact:** Seizures stop on their own in 1-3 minutes. Smelling shoes does nothing. First aid requires turning them on their side (karwat) to keep airways clear.
* **Myth:** Drinking alcohol keeps the body warm in winter.
  * **Fact:** Alcohol dilates blood vessels in the skin, making you feel warm temporarily, but actually causes the core body heat to escape faster, risking hypothermia.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a metal key stops a dog bite from turning into rabies.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys has no effect on the rabies virus. Wash the wound with soap under running water for 15 minutes and get the anti-rabies vaccine.
* **Myth:** Diabetic patients can eat unlimited honey or jaggery instead of sugar.
  * **Fact:** Honey and jaggery contain glucose and raise blood sugar levels just as rapidly as white sugar. They must be strictly limited.
* **Myth:** You should give water to an unconscious person to help them wake up.
  * **Fact:** Giving water to an unconscious person can cause the liquid to enter their lungs (aspiration), leading to suffocation or pneumonia.

## Conversation Examples

### Conversation 1
* **User:** Mujhe pichle 2 din se 101°F bukhar hai aur badan me dard hai. Kya mai medical store se antibiotic kharid kar kha lu?
* **Assistant:** Nahi, aapko bina doctor ke checkup ke khud se antibiotic nahi khani chahiye. Bukhar aur badan dard aamtaur par viral infection se hote hain, jin par antibiotics kaam nahi karti. Isse antibiotic resistance ka khatra hota hai. Aap abhi bukhar ke liye Paracetamol (500mg) le sakte hain, dher saara paani piyein, aur aaram karein. Agar bukhar 3 din se zyada rahe, to doctor ko dikhayein.

### Conversation 2
* **User:** What is the benefit of making an ABHA card?
* **Assistant:** The Ayushman Bharat Health Account (ABHA) card creates a unique 14-digit digital ID. It allows you to store all your medical prescriptions, lab reports, and doctor discharge summaries digitally in one place. You can share these reports instantly with any doctor in India, removing the need to carry heavy paper files.

### Conversation 3
* **User:** Mere relative ko achanak chest me bhari dabaav mehsus ho raha hai, pasina aa raha hai aur dard left arm me ja raha hai. Kya ye gas ka dard hai?
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Thanda pasina aana aur chest pain ka left arm me jana Heart Attack ka classic lakshan hai. Ise gas ka dard samajh kar ignore na karein. Aap patient ko turant litayein, unhe tight kapde dheele karein, aur agar ghar par **Aspirin (300mg)** tablet hai, to use immediate chabane (chew) ke liye dein. Bina ek bhi second waste kiye 108 call karke emergency hospital le jayein.

### Conversation 4
* **User:** PM Jan Aushadhi Kendra par dawa itni sasti kyu hoti hai? Kya inki quality kharab hoti hai?
* **Assistant:** Jan Aushadhi Kendra par milne wali dawaiyan "Generic" hoti hain. Branded companies apni dawaiyon ki marketing aur advertisements par karodon kharch karti hain, isiliye wo mehngi hoti hain. Generic dawaiyon ka koi marketing kharch nahi hota, isiliye ye 50% se 90% sasti hoti hain. Inki quality WHO standards ke according highly tested hoti hai.

### Conversation 5
* **User:** Mere relative achanak behosh ho gaye hain aur digital machine par unka BP 190/120 dikha raha hai. Hum kya karein?
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** BP ka 190/120 mmHg se upar jana aur behoshi aana "Hypertensive Emergency" hai, jisse brain stroke ka khatra hota hai. Patient behosh hain, isiliye unke muh me paani ya koi dawa na dalein. Unhe turant nearest hospital ke emergency room me lekar bhagein jahan IV BP control ki dawa di ja sake.

### Conversation 6
* **User:** eAG kya hota hai sugar test report me?
* **Assistant:** eAG ka matlab hai "Estimated Average Glucose". Yeh aapke HbA1c percentage ko daily home glucometer ke normal blood sugar readings (mg/dL) me convert karke batata hai, jisse aapko samajhne me asani ho ki pichle 3 mahine me average sugar kitna tha.

### Conversation 7
* **User:** Kya sendha namak (rock salt) khane se high BP nahi badhta?
* **Assistant:** Yeh ek bada myth hai. Sendha namak, kala namak ya sea salt me bhi normal namak ki tarah hi "Sodium" hota hai. Blood pressure ko sodium hi badhta hai. Isiliye high BP patients ko kisi bhi tarah ka namak ho, uska rojana intake 1 chammach (5g) se kam hi rakhna chahiye.

### Conversation 8
* **User:** Does Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY card cover cashless treatments in private hospitals?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Under the Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY scheme, cashless treatment benefits are provided at all government hospitals and all **empaneled private hospitals** across India. You can check the list of empaneled private hospitals on the official PM-JAY website.

### Conversation 9
* **User:** Agar chot lagne par bleeding ho rahi ho, to kya karein?
* **Assistant:** Sabse pehle bleeding wale area ko saaf pani se dhoyein. Fir us par ek saaf cotton ka kapda ya bandage rakhkar 5-10 minutes tak continuously direct pressure (dabayein) banaye rakhein. Hath-pair me chot ho to use heart level se upar uthayein. Agar bleeding na ruke, to doctor ke paas jayein.

### Conversation 10
* **User:** Fasting blood sugar 114 mg/dL aaya hai. Kya mujhe diabetes hai?
* **Assistant:** Fasting sugar 114 mg/dL aana Pre-diabetes (high risk category) ko dikhata hai (normal range 70-99 mg/dL, diabetes $\ge 126$ mg/dL). Aapko diabetes nahi hai par risk high hai. Aap diet me sweet kam karein aur regular walk shuru karein taaki diabetes hone se bacha ja sake.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** Kya regular paracetamol khana kidneys ke liye safe hai?
* **Assistant:** Paracetamol limited dose me (fever ya normal pain me) kidneys ke liye safe hai. Par strong painkillers jaise Diclofenac, Aceclofenac, ya Ibuprofen (NSAIDs) kidneys ke blood flow ko block karti hain aur chronic use se kidney fail karti hain.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** What is the price of generic Metformin and Amlodipine at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?
* **Assistant:** Generic Metformin (500mg) and Amlodipine (5mg) tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, costing only ₹10 to ₹15 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹40-₹60 for branded versions.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** Sucking on a metal key stops a dog bite from turning into rabies?
* **Assistant:** No, this is a dangerous myth. Sucking keys or applying chili powder on the wound does not kill the rabies virus. Wash the wound immediately under running tap water with soap for 15 minutes, apply antiseptic, and visit a doctor to start the anti-rabies vaccine series.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** What is the role of National Health Mission (NHM) in free diagnostics?
* **Assistant:** Under the NHM Free Diagnostics Services Initiative, the government provides free essential blood tests, urine tests, X-rays, and ECG services at all primary health wellness centers and district hospitals for poor patients.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** Relative ka achanak face ek side tedha ho gaya hai aur wo bol nahi pa rahe hain.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Face ka ek side tedha hona (face drooping) aur aawaz ladkharana (speech difficulty) Brain Stroke (paralysis) ka sign hai. Aap bina ek bhi minute gawaye unhe immediate hospital ke emergency room me stroke center le jayein. Fast treatment se paralysis completely reverse ho sakta hai.
