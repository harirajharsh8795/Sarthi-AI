# Menstruation (Menstruation/Periods) and Menstrual Hygiene (ASHA Suvidha Pads and Heavy Bleeding Warnings)

## Overview

### English
Menstruation, clinically termed **Masik Dharm** (periods), is the cyclical shedding of the endometrial lining of the uterus, occurring typically every **21 to 35 days** (average cycle length is 28 days). Pathophysiologically, it is regulated by the hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian axis (estrogen and progesterone levels drop at the end of the luteal phase). Key clinical guidelines focus on:
1. **Menstrual Hygiene:** Correct pad change frequency to prevent ascending vaginal infections.
2. **Abnormal Uterine Bleeding (AUB) / Menorrhagia:** Heavy bleeding saturating one or more pads every hour for consecutive hours, signaling uterine fibroids or hormonal imbalances.
3. **Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS):** A rare but fatal bacterial infection linked to keeping highly absorbent tampons inserted for $> 8$ hours.

## Quick Summary
Menstruation represents the cyclic shedding of the endometrium. Menorrhagia features heavy blood loss requiring pad changes every hour. Toxic shock syndrome is a fatal tampon-associated bacterial emergency. Diagnostics monitor hemoglobins and pelvic ultrasounds. Management balances endometrial proliferation.

## Definition
* **Menorrhagia:** Abnormally heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding, defined clinically as blood loss $> 80\text{ ml}$ per cycle or bleeding that lasts longer than 7 days.
* **Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS):** A life-threatening systemic infection caused by toxin-producing *Staphylococcus aureus*, linked to prolonged tampon retention, presenting with sudden high fever, hypotension, and a sunburn-like rash.
* **Dysmenorrhea:** Painful menstrual cramps caused by uterine contractions mediated by high levels of prostaglandins (hormone-like substances).

Different products require specific hygiene behaviors to prevent pelvic infections:

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sanitary Pads** | Cellulose/Cotton, worn externally on underwear | **Change every 4 - 6 hours** (even with light flow) | Rashes, Bacterial Vaginosis, yeast infections from blood decay | Use CDSCO-tested generic **Suvidha biodegradable pads** (₹1 each) |

## Symptoms & Danger Signs of Menstrual Disorders

### English
* **Dysmenorrhea (Normal Cramps):** Dull, throbbing lower abdominal pain starting 1-2 days before periods, resolving by Day 3.
* **Menorrhagia (Heavy Bleeding):** Bleeding that saturates one or more sanitary pads/tampons every hour for consecutive hours; passing blood clots larger than a coin; bleeding lasting $> 7$ days.
* **TSS Warning Signs (Tampon Users):** **Sudden high fever $> 102\text{°F}$ ($38.9\text{°C}$), rapid drop in blood pressure causing fainting, a red sunburn-like rash on palms/soles, vomiting, and diarrhea.**

## Step-by-Step Menstrual Hygiene & Pain Protocol

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Change sanitary pads every 4-6 hours.** Do not wear a single pad all day, even if the flow is very light. Blood is a rich medium for bacterial growth; trapped moisture destroys vaginal pH, leading to vaginitis and UTIs.
* **Support rural hygiene (ASHA schemes).** Purchase highly subsidized sanitary napkins from your village ASHA worker under the government's Menstrual Hygiene Scheme (MHS).
* **Manage period pain safely.** Take **Mefenamic Acid (250-500mg)** or Ibuprofen at the onset of cramps. These NSAIDs block prostaglandin synthesis, reducing uterine spasms.
* **Wash the external genitals correctly.** Wash the vulva with plain warm water. **Wipe from front to back** after using the toilet to prevent fecal bacteria from entering the vagina.

#### Hindi
* **सैनेटरी पैड हर ४-६ घंटे में बदलें:** चाहे रक्तस्राव कम हो या अधिक।
* **आशा (ASHA) योजना का लाभ लें:** ग्रामीण क्षेत्रों में आशा कार्यकर्ताओं से ₹६ में ६ पैड का पैक खरीदें।
* **दर्द के लिए मेफेनामिक एसिड (Mefenamic Acid) लें:** यह आंतों/गर्भाशय के दर्द को कम करती है।
* **आगे से पीछे की ओर पोंछें (front to back wipe)।**

#### Hinglish
* **Sanitary Pad har 4-6 ghante me badlein:** Agar flow bilkul kam hai to bhi pad change karein. Jamne wala blood bacteria grow karta hai jo vaginal infections (vaginitis) aur **UTI** karte hain.
* **ASHA scheme ka use karein:** Rural areas me government MHS scheme ke tehat ASHA didi se **₹6 me 6 sanitary pads** ka packet lein.
* **Period pain ke liye Mefenamic Acid lein:** Pet me severe marod (dysmenorrhea) hone par doctor ki advice se **Mefenamic Acid (250/500mg)** tablet lein. Yeh prostaglandin chemical ko block karke uterus cramps ko shant karti hai.
* **Front to back wash karein:** Toilet ke baad aage se piche ki taraf saaf karein taaki rectum ke bacteria vagina me enter na karein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT leave a tampon inserted for more than 8 hours.** Leaving highly absorbent tampons inside the vagina for long periods creates a perfect breeding ground for *Staphylococcus aureus*, which releases deadly toxins directly into the blood, triggering fatal **Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS)**.
* **Do NOT use dirty rags, ash, or sand wrapped in cloth** instead of sanitary pads. This is a leading cause of pelvic inflammatory disease (PID), tubal blockage, and permanent infertility in rural women.
* **Do NOT ignore bleeding after sexual intercourse (Postcoital Bleeding).** This is a critical warning sign of Cervical Cancer, requiring a Pap smear checkup immediately.

#### Hindi
* **योनि टैम्पोन (Tampon) को ८ घंटे से अधिक समय तक अंदर न छोड़ें:** इससे टीएसएस (TSS - बैक्टीरियल जहर) का गंभीर खतरा रहता है।
* **पीरियड्स में गंदे कपड़े, राख या रेत का प्रयोग न करें:** इससे गर्भाशय में संक्रमण (PID) होकर बांझपन हो सकता है।
* **संभोग के बाद योनि से खून आने (Postcoital bleeding) को नजरअंदाज न करें:** यह गर्भाशय ग्रीवा के कैंसर (Cervical Cancer) का संकेत हो सकता है।

#### Hinglish
* **Tampon ko 8 ghante se zyada vagina me na choddhein:** Tampon ko long-term choddne se *Staphylococcus* bacteria grow hote hain jo blood me zehar failate hain (**TSS**). Ise night me sote waqt lagane se bachein.
* **Gande kapde ya raakh (ash) use na karein:** Pads ki jagah ganda kapda use karne se pelvic area me infection (**PID**) phail jata hai, jisse bachhadani ki tubes block ho jati hain aur female permanently baanjh (infertile) ho sakti hai.
* **Sex ke baad bleeding (Postcoital bleeding) ko ignore na karein:** Yeh Cervical Cancer ka warning sign hai. immediate Pap Smear test karwayein.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Pelvic Ultrasound (TVS/Abdominal):** Mandatory check to detect uterine fibroids, endometrial polyps, or adenomyosis causing heavy bleeding.
* **Complete Blood Count (CBC):** Checked to diagnose iron-deficiency anemia caused by heavy blood loss (Menorrhagia).
* **Thyroid Profile (TSH):** Rules out hypothyroidism, a common systemic cause of heavy, irregular periods.

---

## Medical & Surgical Management
* **Antifibrinolytic Therapy:** Tranexamic Acid (500mg/1g) tablets prescribed during heavy periods to promote clotting and reduce blood loss.
* **Hormonal Management:** Combined Oral Contraceptive Pills (COCPs) or progesterone-releasing intrauterine devices (Mirena IUD) inserted to thin the uterine lining.
* **Hysteroscopy & D&C (Dilation and Curettage):** Surgical scraping of the uterine lining to diagnose cancer and stop acute heavy bleeding.

## Prevention of Menstrual Complications
* **Maintain Genital Hygiene:** Keep the vulva dry and clean.
* **Iron-Rich Diet:** Eat spinach, beetroot, apples, and eggs to prevent anemia from monthly blood loss.
* **Avoid Tampon Overuse.**

## Possible Complications of Unmanaged Menstrual Disorders
* **Severe Anemia:** Low hemoglobin leading to heart strain, dizziness, and fatigue.
* **Ascending Pelvic Infection (PID):** Spread of bacteria from unhygienic pads into the uterus and fallopian tubes.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **A female saturates one or more sanitary pads every hour for 2 or more consecutive hours (acute menorrhagia shock risk).**
* **A tampon user develops a sudden high fever, chills, dizziness, a red sunburn-like rash, or faints (Toxic Shock Syndrome emergency).**
* **Severe, agonizing lower abdominal pain that does not respond to painkillers.**
* **Vaginal bleeding occurs in a female who is pregnant or has missed her period.**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is menorrhagia?**
   Menorrhagia is the clinical term for abnormally heavy or prolonged menstrual bleeding, which can lead to severe anemia.

2. **Why is changing sanitary pads every 4-6 hours mandatory?**
   Menstrual blood is a rich medium for bacterial growth. Keeping a wet pad on for long periods allows bacteria to multiply, leading to skin rashes, vaginitis, and UTIs.

3. **What is Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS) and how does it happen?**
   TSS is a life-threatening bacterial infection. It occurs when a tampon is left inside the vagina for too long ($> 8$ hours), allowing *Staphylococcus* bacteria to produce toxins that enter the bloodstream.

4. **Why is using dirty rags during periods dangerous?**
   Dirty rags contain bacteria and fungi. Using them during menstruation allows pathogens to ascend into the uterus and fallopian tubes, causing Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID) and permanent infertility.

5. **How does Mefenamic Acid help menstrual cramps?**
   Mefenamic Acid is an NSAID that blocks the production of prostaglandins, which are chemicals that cause the uterine muscles to contract and cramp.

6. **What is the price of generic Suvidha pads at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
   Generic biodegradable **Suvidha Sanitary Pads** are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only **₹1 per pad** (₹4 for a pack of 4 pads).

7. **How does a pelvic ultrasound help diagnose heavy bleeding?**
   A pelvic ultrasound uses sound waves to create images of the uterus, allowing doctors to detect fibroids, polyps, or thickening of the uterine lining (endometrium).

8. **What is the difference between primary and secondary dysmenorrhea?**
   Primary dysmenorrhea is normal period pain caused by prostaglandins. Secondary dysmenorrhea is severe pain caused by an underlying medical condition, such as endometriosis or fibroids.

9. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover fibroid removal surgery?**
   Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for diagnostic hysteroscopies, uterine fibroid myomectomy surgeries, C-section deliveries, and ICU care.

10. **What is a menstrual cup and how is it cleaned?**
    A menstrual cup is a reusable silicone cup inserted into the vagina to collect blood. It is cleaned by emptying and rinsing it during the cycle, and boiling it in water for 5 minutes between cycles.

11. **Why does an underactive thyroid (hypothyroidism) cause heavy periods?**
    Low thyroid hormone levels disrupt the hormonal signaling required for ovulation. Anovulatory cycles lead to progesterone deficiency and excessive thickening of the endometrium, causing heavy bleeding.

12. **What are the warning signs of Toxic Shock Syndrome?**
    Warning signs include a sudden high fever ($> 102\text{°F}$), a rapid drop in blood pressure (causing dizziness or fainting), a red sunburn-like rash on the palms and soles, vomiting, and diarrhea.

13. **What is the role of ASHA workers in the Menstrual Hygiene Scheme?**
    ASHA workers distribute subsidized sanitary pads to adolescent girls in rural villages, educate them on menstrual hygiene, and refer those with severe symptoms to clinics.

14. **Why is wiping front to back after toilet use important?**
    It prevents E. coli and other fecal bacteria from the anus from being dragged forward into the vagina and urethra, reducing the risk of painful UTIs and vaginitis.

15. **What is Postcoital Bleeding and why is it a red flag?**
    Postcoital bleeding is vaginal bleeding after intercourse. It is a critical warning sign of Cervical Cancer and requires a Pap smear and pelvic examination immediately.

16. **How does Tranexamic Acid reduce heavy bleeding?**
    Tranexamic Acid is an antifibrinolytic drug. It slows the breakdown of blood clots in the uterine lining, reducing the amount of menstrual blood loss by up to $50\%$.

17. **Can a woman get pregnant during her period?**
    Although unlikely, it is possible. Sperm can survive inside the female body for up to 5 days. If a woman has a short cycle and ovulates early, she can conceive from sex during her period.

18. **Why does chronic anemia cause fatigue and shortness of breath?**
    Anemia is a low red blood cell count. With less hemoglobin to carry oxygen, the heart must work harder, causing dizziness, extreme fatigue, and breathlessness on exertion.

19. **How is a diagnostic D&C (Dilation and Curettage) done?**
    A doctor dilates the cervix and uses a spoon-shaped instrument (curette) to gently scrape the uterine lining. The tissue is sent to a lab to rule out endometrial cancer.

20. **Is generic Mefenamic Acid as effective as branded Meftal Spas?**
    Yes. Generic Mefenamic Acid tablets contain the exact same active drug and relieve uterine spasms just as effectively, at an 80% lower cost.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Menstrual blood is toxic, impure, and dirty blood that must be flushed out completely.
  * **Fact:** Menstrual blood is normal blood mixed with the clean, shed tissue of the uterine lining. It is not toxic or impure; it is a normal sign of reproductive health.
* **Myth:** You should not take a bath or wash your hair during your periods.
  * **Fact:** Bathing and washing your hair are completely safe and important for hygiene during menstruation. Warm baths can help relax muscles and relieve cramps.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures severe menstrual cramps and heavy bleeding instantly.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Cramps require warm compression, exercise, or NSAIDs like Mefenamic Acid.
* **Myth:** Using a menstrual cup will tear the hymen and make a girl lose her virginity.
  * **Fact:** Virginity is a social concept, not a physical one. Using a menstrual cup can stretch the hymen, but it does not affect virginity. Cups are safe for all ages.
* **Myth:** A girl should not enter the kitchen, touch pickles, or pray during her periods because she is impure.
  * **Fact:** These are non-scientific social taboos. Menstruation is a normal biological process. Touching pickles or cooking does not cause food to spoil.
* **Myth:** Branded expensive sanitary pads are the only safe option; government pads are useless.
  * **Fact:** Generic Suvidha pads are CDSCO-approved, biodegradable, sterile, and highly absorbent, providing the same safety at a cost of only ₹1 per pad.
* **Myth:** Periods must last exactly 28 days for every woman; any variation is a disease.
  * **Fact:** A normal cycle can range from 21 to 35 days. The duration of bleeding can normally vary between 3 and 7 days.
* **Myth:** Taking painkillers for period cramps will damage your ovaries and cause infertility.
  * **Fact:** Taking standard NSAIDs (like Mefenamic Acid) for 1-2 days a month to relieve severe cramps is highly safe and has no effect on future fertility.
* **Myth:** If you miss a period, it always means you are pregnant.
  * **Fact:** Missing a period can be caused by hormonal imbalances (PCOS), extreme stress, rapid weight loss, thyroid issues, or intense exercise.
* **Myth:** Bleeding between periods is normal and just a sign of hormonal adjustment.
  * **Fact:** Bleeding between cycles (intermenstrual bleeding) is abnormal and requires clinical evaluation to rule out cervical polyps, infection, or cancer.

Iske tootne par periods kafi heavy aur prolong aate hain (Menorrhagia). TSH checkup mandatory hai.

### Conversation 11
* **User:** What is the price of generic Suvidha Sanitary Pads at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?
* **Assistant:** Generic biodegradable **Suvidha Sanitary Pads** are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost, costing only **₹1 per pad** (₹4 for a pack of 4 pads), compared to ₹40-₹80 for branded pads in private stores.

### Conversation 12
* **User:** "Tranexamic Acid" tablet heavy bleeding me kaise help karti hai?
* **Assistant:** Tranexamic acid ek antifibrinolytic drug hai. Yeh uterus ki blood vessels ke clotting mechanism ko fast karti hai aur clots ko melt hone se rokti hai. Ise periods ke dauran khane se heavy blood loss 40-50% tak control ho jata hai. Ise urology/gynecology checkup ke baad hi lein.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** relative ko periods ke beech ke dino me achanak spotting ya halki bleeding ho rahi hai, unke cycles normal hain.
* **Assistant:** **Warning:** Do cycles ke beech me bleeding hona (Intermenstrual Bleeding) cervical polyps, uterine infection, ya hormonal dysregulation ka sign hai. relative ko ignore karne se rokein aur pelvic ultrasound karwayein.

### Conversation 14
* **User:** rural area me cotton cloth (gande kapde) use karne ke kya complications hain?
* **Assistant:** Gande ya geely kapde use karne se bacteria fallopian tubes me enter kar jate hain. Isse tubes block ho jati hain, jisse ectopic pregnancy (tubes me bachha rukna) ya permanent baanjhpan (infertility) ho jata hai. ASHA didi se safe generic pads hi khareedein.

### Conversation 15
* **User:** kya generic contraceptive pills brand medicines jitni hi safe hoti hain?
* **Assistant:** Yes. Generic Combined Oral Contraceptive Pills (COCPs) me exact same active hormone profiles hote hain. Yeh cycle ko regulate karti hain aur endometrial hyperplasia ko rokti hain, aur Jan Aushadhi centers par branded se 80% discounted rates par milti hain.
