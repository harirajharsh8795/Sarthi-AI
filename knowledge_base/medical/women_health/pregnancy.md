# Maternal Pregnancy Care (Garbhavastha/Pregnancy) and Antenatal Safety (Preeclampsia, Gestational Diabetes and Trimester Care Guide)

## Overview

### English
Maternal Pregnancy Care involves the clinical monitoring, nutritional support, and emergency prevention throughout the three gestation trimesters (**40 weeks total**). Pathophysiologically, pregnancy alters maternal hemodynamics, increasing blood volume and endocrine demands. Essential protocols require **at least 4 Antenatal Care (ANC) visits**, daily **Folic Acid supplementation** in the 1st trimester to prevent neural tube defects, and routine screening for:
1. **Preeclampsia:** High blood pressure ($> 140/90\text{ mmHg}$) and protein in urine after 20 weeks.
2. **Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM):** High blood sugar developing during pregnancy.
*Emergency red flags include vaginal bleeding, sudden facial swelling, and severe lower abdominal pain (Ectopic Pregnancy warning).*

## Quick Summary
Maternal pregnancy care spans three trimesters under strict ANC monitoring. Preeclampsia features gestational hypertension with proteinuria. First aid requires daily folic acid, iron-calcium, and blood pressure checks. Diagnostics monitor ultrasounds and glucose tolerances. Management coordinates safe deliveries.

## Definition
* **Antenatal Care (ANC):** The systemic clinical monitoring of a pregnant woman at regular intervals, aimed at early detection and treatment of pregnancy complications.
* **Preeclampsia:** A pregnancy-specific hypertensive disorder characterized by high blood pressure ($> 140/90\text{ mmHg}$) and protein in the urine (proteinuria) after 20 weeks of gestation.
* **Gestational Diabetes (GDM):** A type of diabetes first diagnosed during pregnancy, where placental hormones block insulin, raising maternal blood sugar levels.

## Trimester Care & Clinical Protocols

Monitoring pregnancy requires different interventions at each phase:

| Gestational Phase | Weeks Range | Primary Clinical Focus | Essential Scans & Blood Tests | Daily Medications |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **First Trimester** | Weeks $1 - 12$ | Rule out **Ectopic Pregnancy**, check viability, establish gestation date | **Urine Pregnancy Test (UPT)**, Pelvic Ultrasound (dating scan) | **Folic Acid ($5\text{ mg}$)** (prevents brain/spine defects) |
| **Second Trimester** | Weeks $13 - 28$ | Monitor fetal growth, screen for anomalies, check BP for preeclampsia | **Anomaly Scan (Weeks 18-20)**, Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) | **Iron + Folic Acid (IFA)**, **Calcium + Vitamin D3** (take at separate times) |
| **Third Trimester** | Weeks $29 - 40$ | Check fetal presentation (head down), prepare for delivery, monitor BP | Growth Scan (ultrasound), fetal doppler, CBC | Continue IFA, Calcium, and start birth planning |

## Symptoms & Danger Signs of Pregnancy Complications

### English
* **Preeclampsia Warning Signs:** Sudden swelling of the face, hands, and feet (edema); persistent, severe headache; blurry vision or seeing spots; severe pain under the right ribs.
* **Other Danger Signs (Emergency):** **Any amount of vaginal bleeding or spotting; sudden leak of watery fluid from the vagina (premature rupture of membranes); decreased or absent fetal movements (baby kicking less than 10 times in 2 hours in the 3rd trimester); severe lower abdominal pain.**

### Hindi
* **प्री-एकलम्प्सिया (Preeclampsia) के लक्षण:** चेहरे, हाथों और पैरों पर अचानक अत्यधिक सूजन आना; गंभीर सिरदर्द; आंखों के सामने धुंधलापन छाना (हाई बीपी का संकेत)।
* **आपातकालीन खतरे के संकेत (Emergency):** **योनि से रक्तस्राव (blood) होना; अचानक पानी का रिसाव होना (water breaking); गर्भ में शिशु की हलचल का कम होना (३ घंटे में १० से कम किक); पेट में तेज दर्द।**

### Hinglish
* **Preeclampsia (High BP) ke Symptoms:** Achanak face, hands aur feet par heavy sujan (edema) aana; lagatar severe sir dard hona; aankho ke samne dhundhla-pan (blurry vision) aana (BP high hone ka sign).

## Step-by-Step Antenatal Care & Safety Protocol

If a pregnant woman experiences complications, execute this safety protocol:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Attend all 4 mandatory ANC visits.** The government provides free checkups: Visit 1 (before 12 weeks), Visit 2 (14-26 weeks), Visit 3 (28-34 weeks), and Visit 4 (36-40 weeks) at all health centers.
* **Take Iron and Calcium supplements separately.** Take Iron (IFA) in the morning on an empty stomach (with lemon water for better absorption), and Calcium at night after dinner. *Calcium blocks iron absorption in the gut; taking them together renders both drugs useless.*
* **Monitor fetal movements daily in the 3rd trimester.** Count kicks after meals. If the baby kicks less than 10 times in 2 hours, drink a glass of cold water or walk around, and count again. If still low, contact a doctor immediately.
* **Get vaccinated with Tetanus Toxoid (TT).** Receive two doses of TT or Td vaccine during pregnancy to protect both mother and newborn from fatal tetanus.

#### Hindi
* **कम से कम ४ प्रसव पूर्व जांच (ANC visits) अवश्य कराएं:** ये सरकारी अस्पतालों में पूरी तरह मुफ्त हैं।
* **आयरन और कैल्शियम की गोलियां अलग-अलग समय पर लें:** कैल्शियम पेट में आयरन के अवशोषण (absorption) को रोकता है।
* **गर्भावस्था के आखिरी ३ महीनों में रोज शिशु की हलचल (kicks) गिनें।**
* **टिटनेस (TT) के दो टीके अवश्य लगवाएं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Kam se kam 4 ANC checkups karwayein:** Government hospitals me prasal purv jaanch bilkul free hoti hai.
* **Iron aur Calcium alag-alag time par lein:** Iron (IFA) ki goli morning me khali pet lein aur Calcium ki goli raat ko dinner ke baad lein. Dono ko ek sath na khayein, kyunki calcium iron ke absorption ko blocks kar deta hai jisse dono dawaiyan be-asar ho jati hain.
* **3rd Trimester me daily baby kicks count karein:** Khana khane ke baad counting karein. Agar 2 ghante me baby 10 bar se kam kick kare, to immediate doctor se contact karein.
* **Tetanus (TT/Td) ke 2 injections lagwayein:** Baby ko tetanus infection se bachane ke liye pregnancy me TT vaccine zaroori lagwayein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT self-medicate.** Never take over-the-counter painkillers (like NSAIDs/Ibuprofen) during pregnancy. NSAIDs taken in the 3rd trimester can cause premature closure of the baby's ductus arteriosus (heart artery), leading to fatal fetal heart failure. Only use Paracetamol under doctor advice.
* **Do NOT ignore high blood pressure.** If your BP is $> 140/90\text{ mmHg}$, treat it immediately. Left untreated, it can progress to **Eclampsia** (seizures/convulsions in the mother), which is fatal for both mother and baby.
* **Do NOT smoke or consume alcohol.**

#### Hindi
* **बिना डॉक्टर के लिखे कोई दवा (painkillers) न खाएं:** आखिरी ३ महीनों में ब्रूफेन (Ibuprofen) लेने से शिशु के दिल की धमनी बंद हो सकती है।
* **हाई ब्लड प्रेशर (BP $> १४०/९०$) को नजरअंदाज न करें:** यह एकलम्प्सिया (दौरे पड़ना) में बदल सकता है जो जानलेवा है।

#### Hinglish
* **Bina doctor ki advice ke painkiller (NSAIDs/Ibuprofen) na khayein:** Pregnancy ke 3rd trimester me Ibuprofen khane se baby ke heart ki artery (**Ductus Arteriosus**) achanak time se pehle band ho jati hai, jisse baby ko heart failure ho sakta hai. Pain ke liye sirf safe Paracetamol hi lein.
* **High BP (Blood Pressure) ko ignore na karein:** Agar BP 140/90 se upar hai, to ye maternal seizures (doure/eclampsia) kar sakta hai jo mother aur baby dono ke liye fatal hai.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Transvaginal & Abdominal Ultrasound:** Dating scan (first trimester), Anomaly Scan (weeks 18-20 to check for birth defects), and Growth Scan (third trimester).
* **OGTT (Oral Glucose Tolerance Test):** Blood test done between weeks 24-28; drinking 75g glucose water and testing blood sugar after 2 hours diagnoses Gestational Diabetes.
* **Urine Dipstick Test:** Performed at every ANC visit to screen for protein (proteinuria) for preeclampsia.

---

## Medical & Surgical Management
* **Antihypertensive Therapy:** Safe drugs like Labetalol or Methyldopa prescribed to lower blood pressure.
* **Emergency Cesarean Section (C-Section):** Surgical delivery performed immediately if fetal distress or preeclampsia complications arise.
* **Magnesium Sulfate (IV):** Administered in the hospital to prevent and treat seizures in severe preeclampsia/eclampsia.

## Prevention of Pregnancy Complications
* **Pre-conception Folic Acid:** Start taking Folic Acid $400\text{ mcg}$ daily 1 month before trying to conceive to prevent brain/spine defects.
* **Monitor Weight Gain:** Normal weight gain is **$11 - 16\text{ kg}$** for a healthy mother.
* **Ensure Clean Drinking Water.**

---

## Possible Complications of Unmanaged Pregnancy
* **Stillbirth & Miscarriage:** Due to uncontrolled blood sugar or high BP.
* **Maternal Mortality:** Due to postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) or eclampsia seizures.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The pregnant mother experiences any amount of vaginal bleeding or sudden gush of water.**
* **The mother develops a sudden, severe headache, blurry vision, and facial swelling (Preeclampsia).**
* **The mother has a seizure (fit) or loses consciousness (Eclampsia).**
* **Fetal movements decrease significantly or stop completely.**
* **Severe, sharp, one-sided lower abdominal pain in the first trimester (Ectopic Pregnancy rupture).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is maternal pregnancy care?**
   It refers to the medical, nutritional, and diagnostic monitoring of a pregnant woman to ensure a healthy pregnancy and safe delivery.

2. **Why is Folic Acid taken in the first trimester?**
   Folic Acid is essential for the early development of the baby's brain and spinal cord. Taking it prevents serious birth defects called **Neural Tube Defects** (like spina bifida).

3. **What is preeclampsia and why is it dangerous?**
   Preeclampsia is high blood pressure combined with protein in the urine after 20 weeks of pregnancy. If untreated, it can cause maternal seizures (eclampsia), kidney failure, and fetal death.

4. **Why must Iron and Calcium tablets be taken at different times?**
   Calcium competes with iron for absorption in the intestines. Taking them together blocks iron absorption, rendering both tablets ineffective. Take Iron in the morning and Calcium at night.

5. **How does Gestational Diabetes develop?**
   Hormones produced by the placenta block the action of maternal insulin, causing insulin resistance. If the mother's pancreas cannot produce enough insulin, blood sugar rises, causing GDM.

6. **What is the price of generic Folic Acid at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
   Generic Folic Acid (5mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹5 to ₹10 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹30-₹50 in private pharmacies.

7. **How often should a pregnant woman count baby kicks in the third trimester?**
   In the 3rd trimester, the baby should kick at least **10 times in a 2-hour period** during active counting. If kicks are fewer, contact a doctor immediately.

8. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover C-section deliveries?**
   Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for normal and C-section deliveries, neonatal care, hospital stays, and post-op medicines.

9. **What is an Anomaly Scan?**
   An Anomaly Scan (or level-2 ultrasound) is performed between weeks 18 and 20 of pregnancy. It is a detailed scan to check the baby's organs for physical birth defects.

10. **Why are ACE inhibitor blood pressure tablets banned in pregnancy?**
    ACE inhibitors can cross the placenta and cause fetal kidney failure, lung underdevelopment, and skull deformities. Only safe alternatives like Labetalol or Methyldopa are used.

11. **What is the Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK)?**
    JSSK is a government scheme that guarantees completely free delivery, free C-section surgeries, free diagnostics, free drugs, and free transport to and from the hospital at all government facilities.

12. **Can a pregnant woman take Ibuprofen for pain?**
    No. Ibuprofen (an NSAID) taken in the 3rd trimester can cause premature closure of the baby's ductus arteriosus (heart artery), leading to fetal heart failure. Use Paracetamol instead.

13. **What is Eclampsia?**
    Eclampsia is a severe complication of preeclampsia characterized by the onset of tonic-clonic seizures (fits) or coma in a pregnant woman, representing a major emergency.

14. **How is the OGTT test done?**
    The patient drinks a solution of 75g of glucose in water, and blood sugar is tested after 2 hours. A reading $> 140\text{ mg/dL}$ diagnoses Gestational Diabetes.

15. **What is an Ectopic Pregnancy?**
    It is a life-threatening condition where a fertilized egg implants outside the uterus, usually in the fallopian tube. If the tube ruptures, it causes severe internal bleeding and pelvic pain.

16. **Why is the Tetanus Toxoid (TT) vaccine given during pregnancy?**
    The vaccine stimulates the mother to produce antibodies against tetanus, which are passed to the baby. This protects the newborn from neonatal tetanus, a fatal infection.

17. **What is the Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY)?**
    It is a government cash-incentive scheme that provides ₹5,000 in direct bank transfers to pregnant and lactating mothers for health and nutritional support.

18. **How much weight gain is normal during pregnancy?**
    For a mother with a normal pre-pregnancy weight, a healthy weight gain is between **$11\text{ kg}$ and $16\text{ kg}$** over the course of the 9 months.

19. **What is the role of ASHA didis during pregnancy in villages?**
    ASHA didis register pregnant women, distribute free iron-folic acid tablets, arrange free transportation for deliveries under JSSK, and accompany mothers to health centers.

20. **Is generic Labetalol safe for high BP in pregnancy?**
    Yes. Generic Labetalol is a beta-blocker that is highly safe and effective for managing pregnancy-induced hypertension, costing very little at generic clinics.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** A pregnant woman must eat for two people to ensure the baby grows big.
  * **Fact:** Eating for two leads to excessive weight gain and raises the risk of gestational diabetes and C-section. A mother only needs about 300 extra calories a day in the 2nd and 3rd trimesters.
* **Myth:** If you have heartburn during pregnancy, it means the baby is growing a lot of hair.
  * **Fact:** Heartburn is caused by progesterone hormone relaxing the esophageal sphincter, allowing stomach acid to rise, and the growing uterus pressing on the stomach.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures high BP and pregnancy seizures instantly.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. High BP requires medical antihypertensives (Labetalol) and eclampsia fits require Magnesium Sulfate in a hospital.
* **Myth:** Ultrasound scans are dangerous and emit radiation that causes birth defects.
  * **Fact:** Ultrasounds use sound waves, not X-ray radiation. They are highly safe and essential for monitoring fetal development.
* **Myth:** A pregnant woman should take both iron and calcium tablets together at breakfast.
  * **Fact:** Taking them together blocks iron absorption. Always take Iron on an empty stomach in the morning and Calcium at night.
* **Myth:** Senda namak (rock salt) water washes cure vaginal bleeding in pregnancy.
  * **Fact:** Vaginal bleeding indicates a pregnancy complication (like placenta previa or miscarriage). Using salt washes is useless and delays critical care.
* **Myth:** Branded expensive pregnancy vitamins are better than government-supplied IFA tablets.
  * **Fact:** Government-supplied IFA tablets contain high-potency generic iron and folic acid that meet strict CDSCO quality standards, completely free.
* **Myth:** A mother who delivers via C-section should not breastfeed her baby for the first 3 days.
  * **Fact:** C-section mothers must breastfeed as soon as possible (within 1 hour) using comfortable positions like the football hold.
* **Myth:** You can determine the baby's gender by the shape of the mother's stomach.
  * **Fact:** The shape of the stomach is determined by the mother's muscle tone, uterine structure, and baby's position, not the baby's gender. Gender determination is illegal in India.
* **Myth:** A pregnant woman should rest in bed 24/7 and avoid all physical work.
  * **Fact:** Unless advised for high-risk complications (like placenta previa), light physical activity and walking are highly beneficial for a smooth delivery.

* **Assistant:** Level-2 Anomaly scan pregnancy ke **18-20 weeks** me hota hai. Yeh scan baby ke internal organs (heart, brain, kidneys, spine) ko structurally examine karta hai taaki baby me koi major birth defect or genetic abnormality (jaise Down syndrome signs) early rule out ho sake.

### Conversation 13
* **User:** relative ko delivery ke baad achanak continuous heavy bleeding ho rahi hai aur BP 90/60 ho gaya hai.
* **Assistant:** **Emergency:** Delivery ke baad heavy bleeding hona **PPH (Postpartum Hemorrhage)** hai. Yeh garbhasay contract na hone se (uterine atony) hota hai aur mother ki death ka main reason hai. relative ko immediately flat letayein aur nearest emergency hospital shift karwayein jahan IV Oxytocin/Misoprostol shuru ki ja sake.

* **Assistant:** PMMVY ke tehat pregnant mother ko ASHA didi ke through register karna hota hai. Verification ke baad **₹5,000** ki cash incentive 2 installments me direct pregnant woman ke bank account (DBT) me transfer hoti hai taaki wo nutrient diet khareed sake.
