# Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STD/Sexually Transmitted Diseases) ki Pehchan, Symptoms aur Ilaaj (Syphilis, Gonorrhea and Herpes Guide)

## Overview

### English
A Sexually Transmitted Disease (STD) is the clinical manifestation of a sexually transmitted infection (STI) that has progressed to cause tissue damage, chronic symptoms, or systemic illness. STDs are caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites. Key clinical STDs include:
1. **Syphilis:** A bacterial infection (*Treponema pallidum*) characterized by painless chancres progressing to systemic cardiovascular/neuro-syphilis.
2. **Gonorrhea & Chlamydia:** Bacterial infections causing purulent urethral/vaginal discharge, leading to pelvic inflammatory disease (PID) and sterility.
3. **Genital Herpes:** A lifelong viral infection (HSV-2) causing recurrent, painful fluid-filled blisters.
Management includes **Syndromic treatment**, partner notifications, and NACO free services.

## Quick Summary
STDs represent symptomatic infections transmitted via sexual contact. Bacterial types feature gonorrhea (purulent discharge) and syphilis (painless chancres); viral types include herpes (painful blisters). First aid for acute exposures requires immediate syndromic antibiotics. Diagnostics check VDRL serology and multiplex PCR. Management combines partner treatments.

## Definition
* **Chancre:** A painless, firm, circular skin ulcer that appears at the primary site of a Syphilis infection, representing a highly contagious stage.
* **Purulent Discharge:** A thick, opaque fluid (usually yellow or green) containing pus, dead cells, and bacteria, secreted from the urethra or cervix during Gonorrhea.
* **Syndromic Kit:** Color-coded drug kits containing specific antibiotics/antifungals distributed by the government of India to treat STDs based on visible symptoms immediately.

## Clinical Comparison of Major STDs

Understanding the differences is crucial to starting the correct syndromic kit:

| STD Type | Causative Agent | Signature Genital Symptom | Diagnostic Screening Test | First-Line Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gonorrhea** | *Neisseria gonorrhoeae* (Bacteria) | **Thick, purulent yellow-green discharge** from penis/vagina | Gram stain / PCR Swab | **Ceftriaxone IM injection** + Azithromycin |
| **Syphilis** | *Treponema pallidum* (Bacteria) | **Single, painless, firm ulcer (Chancre)** on genitals | **VDRL / RPR blood test** | **Benzathine Penicillin G** injection |
| **Chlamydia** | *Chlamydia trachomatis* (Bacteria)| Watery, cloudy discharge, painful urination | PCR Urine/Swab test | **Azithromycin** (1g single dose) or Doxycycline |
| **Genital Herpes**| Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV-2) | **Multiple, painful, watery blisters** that rupture | HSV-2 IgG/IgM blood test | **Acyclovir** (Antiviral therapy) |
| **Trichomoniasis**| *Trichomonas vaginalis* (Parasite)| Frothy, foul-smelling yellow-green vaginal fluid | Wet mount microscopy | **Metronidazole** (2g single oral dose) |

## Symptoms of Major STDs

### English
* **Bacterial STDs (Gonorrhea/Chlamydia/Syphilis):** Burning sensation during urination; pain in the testicles (men); deep pelvic pain during intercourse (women); a painless circular sore on the penis, vulva, or mouth; yellow/green genital discharge.
* **Viral STDs (Herpes/HPV):** Clusters of painful, burning fluid-filled blisters on the genitals or anus; flu-like fever and swollen groin lymph nodes during outbreaks; cauliflower-like skin bumps (warts).

* **बैक्टीरियल एसटीडी के लक्षण:** पेशाब के दौरान तेज जलन; अंडकोश में दर्द; योनि या लिंग से पीला-हरा मवाद निकलना; बिना दर्द वाले लाल घाव (chancre) होना।
* **वायरल एसटीडी (Herpes/HPV) के लक्षण:** गुप्तांगों पर दर्दनाक पानी से भरे छाले; बुखार और जांघों की ग्रंथियों में सूजन; मस्से (warts) होना।

## Emergency Syndromic & Safety Protocol (First Aid)

If exposed to unsafe sex or showing symptoms of STD, execute this protocol immediately:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Treat both partners simultaneously.** If you are diagnosed with an STD (like Gonorrhea or Syphilis), your partner must take the exact same treatment course, even if they have zero symptoms. Otherwise, you will constantly re-infect each other.
* **Go to a government Suraksha Clinic** located at all civil hospitals. NACO provides free diagnosis, counseling, and color-coded syndromic kits.
* **Use Penicillin G for Syphilis.** Injectable Benzathine Penicillin G is the gold standard cure for Syphilis.
* **Complete the entire antibiotic course.** Do not stop taking tablets early when symptoms fade.

#### Hindi
* **दोनों साथियों का एक साथ इलाज करें:** अन्यथा दोबारा संक्रमण (re-infection) हो जाएगा।
* **सरकारी सुरक्षा क्लीनिक (Suraksha Clinic) जाएं:** यहाँ मुफ्त कलर-कोडेड किट (syndromic kits) मिलती हैं।
* **सिफलिस के लिए पेनिसिलिन (Penicillin G) का टीका लगवाएं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Husband-Wife dono ka ilaaj ek sath karwayein:** Agar aapko koi STD hai, to bina symptoms ke bhi partner ko same dawa khilayein. Agar sirf ek ilaaj karwayega, to dusre se fir se re-infection ho jayega (ping-pong effect).
* **Government Suraksha Clinic jayein:** Har government district hospital me **Suraksha Clinic** hoti hai jahan STDs ke liye free **Color-Coded Kits** (jaise Grey Kit, Green Kit) di jati hain.
* **Syphilis me Penicillin G lagwayein:** Syphilis ka primary cure Benzathine Penicillin G injection hai jo doctor ki dekh-rekh me lagta hai.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT have any sexual intercourse** (vaginal, anal, or oral) during the treatment phase. Wait at least 7 days after both partners have finished their antibiotic course before resuming.
* **Do NOT squeeze or pop Herpes blisters or Syphilis sores.** This releases millions of pathogens, spreading the infection to other parts of your body (auto-inoculation) and to your partner.
* **Do NOT self-medicate with random antibiotics.**

#### Hindi
* **इलाज के दौरान यौन संबंध बिल्कुल न बनाएं:** कम से कम ७ दिनों तक परहेज करें।
* **हर्पीज के छालों या सिफलिस के घावों को दबाएं या फोड़ें नहीं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Ilaaj ke dauran sex bilkul na karein:** Jab tak dono partners ki medicines complete na ho jayein aur 7 din na nikal jayein, tab tak physical relations strictly avoid karein.
* **Herpes ke chhalo ko press ya phodein na:** Chhalo ko nail se phodne se unka paani baki skin par lag kar naye chhale bana deta hai aur virus haatho me phail jata hai.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **VDRL / RPR Blood Test:** Screening test for Syphilis; monitored to check treatment response.
* **TPHA Test:** Confirmatory test for Syphilis.
* **NAAT (Nucleic Acid Amplification Test):** Gold standard PCR test using urine or genital swabs to detect Chlamydia and Gonorrhea DNA.

## Medical & Color-Coded Syndromic Management (India)
Under NACO, patients are treated immediately using color-coded kits based on symptoms:
* **Grey Kit (Kit 1):** For Urethral/Cervical Discharge (contains Azithromycin 1g + Cefixime 400mg).
* **Green Kit (Kit 2):** For Vaginitis (contains Secnidazole 2g + Fluconazole 150mg).
* **Red Kit (Kit 5):** For Painless Genital Ulcers/Syphilis (contains Azithromycin 2g).
* **Acyclovir Therapy:** Daily oral tablets to suppress Herpes outbreaks (HSV cannot be cured, only suppressed).

## Prevention of STIs
* **Consistent Condom Use:** Correctly roll on a latex condom for every sexual act.
* **Vaccination:** Complete the 3-dose Hepatitis B vaccine and HPV vaccine (Gardasil-9).
* **Avoid Multiple Partners.**

## Possible Complications of Untreated STDs
* **Infertility & Pelvic Damage:** Untreated Gonorrhea/Chlamydia causes PID, scarring the fallopian tubes, leading to ectopic pregnancy or sterility.
* **Congenital Syphilis:** A pregnant mother with untreated Syphilis transmits it to the baby, causing stillbirth, bone deformities, or death.
* **Neuro-Syphilis:** Late-stage Syphilis damages the brain, spinal cord, causing dementia and paralysis.

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **A female develops high fever, severe lower abdominal pain, and foul vaginal discharge (indicates acute Pelvic Inflammatory Disease / Pelvic Peritonitis emergency).**
* **The patient has a known exposure to Syphilis/HIV (requires immediate PEP/antibiotics within 72 hours).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is the difference between an STI and an STD?**
   An STI (Sexually Transmitted Infection) is when bacteria or viruses enter the body. An STD (Sexually Transmitted Disease) occurs when the infection progresses to cause visible symptoms or organ damage.

2. **What is Gonorrhea and what are its symptoms?**
   Gonorrhea is a bacterial infection. Symptoms include a thick, yellow-green pus-like discharge from the penis or vagina, and severe burning during urination.

3. **How is Syphilis treated?**
   Primary Syphilis is treated with a single intramuscular injection of **Benzathine Penicillin G**. If allergic, doctors use daily Doxycycline.

4. **Can Genital Herpes be cured permanently?**
   No. Genital Herpes (HSV-2) cannot be cured. The virus stays dormant in nerve cells. However, daily **Acyclovir** antiviral tablets can suppress outbreaks and prevent transmission.

5. **What is a "Suraksha Clinic" in India?**
   Suraksha Clinics are specialized government departments at civil hospitals that provide free, confidential testing, counseling, and medicines (syndromic kits) for STDs.

6. **What is the Grey Kit (Kit 1) used for?**
   The Grey Kit is a government-provided kit containing Cefixime (400mg) and Azithromycin (1g) used to treat urethral or cervical discharge (Gonorrhea/Chlamydia).

7. **Why must both partners be treated for an STD?**
   If only one partner is treated, the untreated partner will pass the infection back during the next sexual contact, causing a continuous loop of re-infection.

8. **What is the window period for Syphilis testing?**
   The window period for VDRL/RPR antibodies is typically **3 to 6 weeks** after exposure. Testing too early can yield a false-negative.

9. **Can Syphilis affect the brain?**
    Yes. If left untreated for years, Syphilis can progress to Tertiary Syphilis (Neurosyphilis), causing brain damage, dementia, and spinal cord paralysis.

10. **How does Trichomoniasis present?**
    It is a parasitic infection causing a frothy, foul-smelling yellow-green vaginal discharge with vaginal itching and redness, treated with Metronidazole.

11. **What is the price of generic Metronidazole at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Metronidazole (400mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹15 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹40-₹60 in private pharmacies.

12. **Can a pregnant mother pass Syphilis to her baby?**
    Yes. This is called Congenital Syphilis. It can cause miscarriage, stillbirth, or severe bone and brain deformities in the newborn baby. Screening is mandatory in pregnancy.

13. **What is the VDRL test?**
    The Venereal Disease Research Laboratory (VDRL) test is a blood screening check that detects antibodies produced in response to a Syphilis infection.

14. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover STD treatments?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for severe pelvic inflammatory disease admissions, diagnostic hysteroscopy, and surgical wound repairs.

15. **Why do condoms offer less protection against Herpes?**
    Herpes is spread by skin-to-skin contact. If Herpes blisters are located on the scrotum, pubic area, or thighs, contact with these areas can transmit the virus even if a condom is worn on the penis.

16. **What is the first-line treatment for Chlamydia?**
    Chlamydia is treated with a single oral dose of **Azithromycin (1g)** or a 7-day course of Doxycycline (100mg twice daily).

17. **Can Gonorrhea cause blindness in newborn babies?**
    Yes. If a mother has Gonorrhea during birth, the bacteria can infect the baby's eyes during delivery (ophthalmia neonatorum), causing blindness. Doctors apply erythromycin ointment to newborns to prevent this.

18. **Why should I avoid having sex during STD treatment?**
    Having sex during treatment can spread the infection back and forth, delay healing of genital tissue, and cause pain. Wait at least 7 days after finishing medicines.

19. **What is the role of NACO in STD control?**
    NACO runs national prevention programs, distributes free Nirodh condoms, and operates Suraksha Clinics to provide free, standardized syndromic treatments.

20. **Is generic Penicillin G as effective as branded versions?**
    Yes. Generic Benzathine Penicillin G injections meet strict CDSCO quality standards and cure Syphilis just as effectively, at a fraction of the cost.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** You can catch an STD by sitting on a public toilet seat.
  * **Fact:** STD bacteria and viruses (like HIV, Syphilis, Gonorrhea) cannot survive on dry surfaces or toilet seats. They require warm mucosal membranes to transmit.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures Syphilis or Gonorrhea.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Bacterial STDs require specific antibiotics (like Penicillin G or Azithromycin).
* **Myth:** If you have an STD, you will always see discharge or sores immediately.
  * **Fact:** Many STDs, especially Chlamydia and Gonorrhea in women, are completely symptomless (silent). Regular testing is the only way to detect them.
* **Myth:** Viral STDs like Herpes can be cured by taking a 7-day antibiotic course.
  * **Fact:** Antibiotics only kill bacteria. Viral infections like Herpes cannot be cured, but can be managed with antivirals like Acyclovir.
* **Myth:** Senda namak (rock salt) washes cure vaginal discharge and STDs.
  * **Fact:** Rock salt has no chemical effect on STI pathogens. Douching with salt water worsens infections by destroying healthy vaginal bacteria.
* **Myth:** Married couples do not need to worry about STDs.
  * **Fact:** If either partner was exposed before marriage, or has multiple partners outside the marriage, STDs can easily transmit within marriage.
* **Myth:** The VDRL test is only for men.
  * **Fact:** VDRL is a blood test for Syphilis and is mandatory for both men and women, especially during pregnancy checkups.
* **Myth:** Branded expensive antibiotics are better at treating Gonorrhea than government kits.
  * **Fact:** NACO syndromic kits contain high-potency WHO-approved generic medicines that cure infections just as effectively, completely free.
* **Myth:** You can get Herpes by sharing a swimming pool with an infected person.
  * **Fact:** The Herpes virus cannot survive in chlorinated pool water. It only spreads through direct skin-to-skin contact with active sores or saliva.
* **Myth:** If a sore heals on its own, it means the Syphilis is cured.
  * **Fact:** The primary Syphilis chancre heals on its own in 3-6 weeks, but the bacteria remain in the blood, progressing silently to damage organs. Complete treatment is essential.
