# Emergency Contraceptive Pill (Emergency Pill/i-Pill) ki Pehchan aur Rules (Morning After Pill and Failure Guide)

## Overview

### English
The Emergency Contraceptive Pill (ECP), commonly called the **Morning-After Pill** (popularly known as **i-Pill** or **Unwanted-72** in India), is a high-dose hormonal formulation used to prevent pregnancy after unprotected sexual intercourse, condom breakage, or sexual assault. The standard pill contains **$1.5\text{ mg}$ of Levonorgestrel** (a synthetic progestin). It works primarily by inhibiting or delaying ovulation. **Efficacy is highly time-sensitive:** it prevents $95\%$ of pregnancies if taken within 24 hours, dropping to $58\%$ if taken between 48-72 hours. It is completely ineffective once implantation has occurred.

## Quick Summary
Emergency contraceptive pills delay ovulation to prevent fertilization. Efficacy is time-critical (within 72 hours). Symptoms of use feature nausea, vomiting, spotting, and delayed periods. First aid for post-pill vomiting within 2 hours requires repeating the dose. Diagnostics check for ectopic pregnancy if lower abdominal pain occurs. Management is supportive.

---

## Definition
* **Emergency Contraception:** Contraceptive measures taken after sexual intercourse to prevent pregnancy.
* **Ovulation Delay:** The pharmacological blocking or postponing of the release of a mature egg from the ovary, preventing fertilization by active sperm.
* **Meningitis/Ectopic Risk:** If the pill fails, the risk of the pregnancy implanting in the fallopian tube (ectopic pregnancy) is slightly increased, which is a life-threatening emergency.

---

## Mechanism of Action & Time Sensitivity

The emergency pill works *before* pregnancy starts, not after:
1. **Blocks Ovulation:** The high dose of Levonorgestrel suppresses the LH surge, stopping the ovary from releasing an egg.
2. **Thickens Cervical Mucus:** It thickens vaginal fluids, making it difficult for remaining sperm to swim up.
3. **Time Curve of Efficacy:**
   * **$< 24$ Hours:** **$95\%$ Effective**
   * **$24 - 48$ Hours:** **$85\%$ Effective**
   * **$48 - 72$ Hours:** **$58\%$ Effective**
   * **$> 72$ Hours:** Extremely low efficacy; requires a doctor for Copper-T insertion.
   * *If the egg is already fertilized and implanted in the uterus, the pill is 100% ineffective and will not cause an abortion.*

## Symptoms & Side Effects of Emergency Pill

### English
* **Immediate (First 48 hours):** Severe nausea and vomiting; dizziness and fatigue; lower abdominal cramps and breast tenderness.
* **Menstrual Disturbances (Next 1-3 weeks):** Irregular spotting or bleeding within a week of taking the pill; the next menstrual period may be 3-7 days early or late.
* **Ectopic Warning:** Sudden, sharp, one-sided lower abdominal pain 2-3 weeks later (indicating tubal pregnancy).

### Hindi
* **त्वरित दुष्प्रभाव:** मतली और उल्टी आना; चक्कर आना और पेट में मरोड़।
* **मासिक धर्म में बदलाव:** मासिक धर्म के बीच में हल्की ब्लीडिंग (spotting) होना; अगला मासिक धर्म चक्र १ सप्ताह आगे या पीछे होना।
* **खतरे के संकेत:** पेट के एक तरफ तेज असहनीय दर्द होना।

### Hinglish
* **Immediate Side Effects:** Heavy nausea (jee machlana) aur vomiting (ulti) hona; chakkar aana aur lower abdominal pain.
* **Periods me disturbance:** Pill lene ke 1 week ke andar spotting (halki bleeding) hona; agla period normal date se 3-7 din pehle ya late aana.
* **Warning Sign:** Pill failure hone par uterine ke bahar pregnancy tharne (ectopic pregnancy) se lower abdomen me ek taraf tez chubhan wala dard hona.

## Safety Guidelines & Crisis Dosing

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Take the pill as soon as possible** after condom breakage or unprotected sex. Every hour of delay increases pregnancy risk.
* **Repeat the dose if you vomit within 2 hours** of taking the pill. The hormone has not been absorbed, requiring an anti-emetic (like Domperidone) followed by a second emergency pill.
* **Perform a pregnancy test** if your period is delayed by more than **7 days** past its expected date.
* **Consult a gynecologist** for a Copper-T IUD insertion if the 72-hour window has passed (effective up to 5 days after sex).

#### Hindi
* **जितनी जल्दी हो सके गोली लें:** असुरक्षित संबंध के तुरंत बाद लेना सर्वोत्तम है।
* **२ घंटे के भीतर उल्टी होने पर दोबारा दवा लें:** यदि दवा खाने के २ घंटे में उल्टी हो जाए, तो एक और गोली खानी होगी।
* **पीरियड ७ दिन लेट होने पर जांच करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Jitna fast ho sake pill lein:** Condom phatne ke turant baad goli khana sabse best hai.
* **2 ghante me ulti hone par double dose lein:** Agar pill khane ke **2 ghante ke andar ulti (vomiting) ho jaye**, to turant ulti ki dawa (Ondansetron) khakar ek aur emergency pill khayein, kyunki pehli dawa body absorb nahi kar payi thi.
* **Period 7 din late hone par test karein:** Expected date se 7 din upar hone par home pregnancy test card se checkup karein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT use the emergency pill as regular birth control.** It contains a massive dose of hormones ($1.5\text{ mg}$ levonorgestrel, equal to half a pack of daily pills). Using it weekly causes severe hormonal imbalance, ovarian cyst risks, and chronic menstrual cycle disturbance.
* **Do NOT take the emergency pill if you are already pregnant.** It cannot terminate an existing pregnancy (it is not a Mifepristone/Misoprostol abortion pill).
* **Do NOT ignore severe one-sided lower abdominal pain** 2-3 weeks after taking the pill.
* **Do NOT use emergency pills repeatedly in a single menstrual cycle.**

#### Hindi
* **नियमित गर्भनिरोधक के रूप में न लें:** आई-पिल को महीने में बार-बार खाना गंभीर हार्मोनल गड़बड़ी पैदा करता है।
* **यह गर्भपात की गोली (abortion pill) नहीं है।**

#### Hinglish
* **Regular birth control ki tarah use na karein:** Emergency pill ko month me baar-baar khana strictly contraindicated hai. Isme heavy hormone dose hoti hai jo ovary ke normal functions ko block karke periods ko completely irregular (kharab) kar deti hai.
* **Yeh garbhpat (Abortion) ki dawa nahi hai:** Agar pregnancy pehle hi thar chuki hai, to ye pill use destroy nahi karegi. Abortion ke liye alag medicines (Mifepristone/Misoprostol) gynecologist ki prescription par di jati hain.
* **Ek hi cycle me bar-bar pill na lein.**

## Diagnostic Evaluation of Failure
* **Urine Pregnancy Test (UPT):** Done if menstruation is delayed.
* **Transvaginal Ultrasound (TVS):** Done if the pregnancy test is positive and the patient has abdominal pain, to rule out ectopic pregnancy.

---

## Medical Management of Failure
* If a pregnancy test is positive after pill failure and the patient wishes to terminate, a gynecologist prescribes **Medical Method of Abortion (MMA)** pills (Mifepristone + Misoprostol) if the pregnancy is under 7-9 weeks, or performs surgical aspiration.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to emergency contraception:*
1. **Progestin Emergency Contraceptives:** e.g., Levonorgestrel 1.5mg (i-Pill, Unwanted-72, 72-Card).
2. **Selective Progesterone Receptor Modulator:** e.g., Ulipristal Acetate 30mg (effective up to 120 hours / 5 days).
3. **Anti-emetics:** Prevent post-pill vomiting (e.g., Ondansetron, Domperidone).

---

## Prevention of Emergency Pill Side Effects
* **Take the Pill with Food:** Consuming the tablet with a small meal reduces the risk of drug-induced stomach irritation and vomiting.
* **Adopt Regular Methods:** Switch to daily weekly pills (Saheli) or Copper-T to avoid the need for emergency hormonal shocks.

---

## Possible Complications of Pill Abuse
* **Severe Hormonal Dysregulation:** Anovulatory cycles, delayed periods, or heavy bleeding.
* **Ruptured Ectopic Pregnancy:** If failure leads to tubal pregnancy, it can rupture, causing internal bleeding, shock, and death.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **Severe, sharp, one-sided lower abdominal pain in the female partner 2-3 weeks after taking the emergency pill (ectopic pregnancy warning).**
* **Heavy, continuous vaginal bleeding (soaking more than 2 pads per hour) after pill intake.**
* **Severe dizziness, fainting, or cold sweating.**

## Government Health Programs (India)
* **National Family Planning Programme:** Distributes emergency contraceptive pills (**E-Pills**) completely free of cost to couples at all government sub-centers and PHCs.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Offers high-quality generic Levonorgestrel (1.5mg) emergency contraceptive pills at highly subsidized rates (₹15 to ₹25), compared to ₹90-₹110 for branded i-Pill.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is an emergency contraceptive pill?**
   It is a high-dose hormonal pill containing Levonorgestrel used to prevent pregnancy after unprotected sex if taken within 72 hours.

2. **How does the morning-after pill work?**
   It stops or delays the ovary from releasing an egg (ovulation). If there is no egg, the sperm cannot fertilize it.

3. **What is the time limit to take the i-Pill?**
   You must take the pill within **72 hours (3 days)** of unprotected sex. The sooner it is taken, the more effective it is.

4. **What should I do if I vomit after taking the i-Pill?**
   If you vomit within **2 hours** of taking the pill, you must take another emergency pill immediately, as the first one was not absorbed.

5. **Can the emergency pill cause an abortion?**
   No. ECPs are not abortion pills. If the egg is already fertilized and implanted in the womb, the pill will not work and will not harm the fetus.

6. **Why can't I take the i-Pill every week?**
   The i-Pill contains a massive dose of hormones. Taking it frequently disrupts your natural hormonal cycle, causing irregular periods, weight gain, and severe mood swings.

7. **Where can I get a cheap emergency pill in India?**
   Generic emergency contraceptive pills (Levonorgestrel 1.5mg) are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹15 to ₹25.

8. **Can the emergency pill cause ectopic pregnancy?**
   If the pill fails to prevent pregnancy, there is a slightly higher risk that the pregnancy will develop in the fallopian tube (ectopic pregnancy), which is dangerous.

9. **Why is my period late after taking the emergency pill?**
   The high dose of hormones delays ovulation. This commonly delays your next period by 3 to 7 days, which is normal.

10. **Does the i-Pill protect against HIV and other STIs?**
    No. Emergency pills have zero effect on viruses and bacteria. Only latex condoms protect against HIV and STIs.

11. **What is the difference between i-Pill and Unwanted-72?**
    Both are brand names for the exact same drug, **Levonorgestrel 1.5mg**. They work in the same way and have the same safety guidelines.

12. **Is Ulipristal Acetate better than Levonorgestrel?**
    Ulipristal Acetate (30mg) is effective for up to **120 hours (5 days)** after sex and has a higher success rate than Levonorgestrel, but it requires a doctor's prescription.

13. **Can I take 2 emergency pills together for double safety?**
    No. Taking a double dose does not increase efficacy but will cause severe nausea, continuous vomiting, and hormonal disturbances. Take only one.

14. **Why is a pregnancy test necessary after taking the ECP?**
    Since emergency pills are not 100% effective, you should take a home pregnancy test if your period is delayed by more than 7 days.

15. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover emergency gynecology care?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for ectopic pregnancy surgeries, emergency diagnostic ultrasounds, and post-abortion ICU care.

16. **Can a breastfeeding mother take the emergency pill?**
    Yes. Levonorgestrel emergency pills are safe during breastfeeding as only trace amounts enter breast milk, which does not harm the baby.

17. **What is "Spotting" after taking the ECP?**
    It is light vaginal bleeding that occurs 3 to 7 days after taking the pill. It is a common, harmless hormonal side effect.

18. **Can I take the emergency pill if I am on other medications?**
    Some drugs (like epilepsy medicines or rifampicin) can reduce the pill's effectiveness. Consult a doctor for alternative emergency methods like Copper-T.

19. **What is the role of the government's E-Pill program?**
    The government family planning clinics distribute emergency pills free of cost to couples to prevent unwanted pregnancies and reduce unsafe abortions.

20. **Is generic Levonorgestrel as effective as branded i-Pill?**
    Yes. Generic Levonorgestrel (1.5mg) tablets contain the exact same hormone and meet the same quality standards as branded versions, but cost 80% less.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** The i-Pill is a safe birth control tablet that you can take after every sex act.
  * **Fact:** The i-Pill is for emergencies only. Taking it frequently causes severe ovarian dysregulation and irregular bleeding. Use condoms or daily pills instead.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key prevents pregnancy, so emergency pills are not needed.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Only ECPs or Copper-T within the time limit can prevent pregnancy after condom failure.
* **Myth:** The emergency pill is an abortion pill that can terminate a 1-month pregnancy.
  * **Fact:** The emergency pill only prevents fertilization. It cannot abort an existing pregnancy. Abortion requires Mifepristone/Misoprostol under doctor care.
* **Myth:** If you take the i-Pill, you are protected from pregnancy for the next 1 week.
  * **Fact:** The pill only protects against the sex act that occurred *before* taking it. If you have sex the day after taking the pill, you can get pregnant.
* **Myth:** The emergency pill causes permanent infertility in young women.
  * **Fact:** ECPs do not affect future fertility. The hormonal disruption is temporary, and your normal fertility returns in the next cycle.
* **Myth:** Taking the emergency pill causes severe birth defects in the baby if it fails.
  * **Fact:** If the pill fails and you carry the pregnancy to term, studies show Levonorgestrel does not cause any birth defects or harm the fetus.
* **Myth:** You must wait for the morning to take the "morning-after" pill.
  * **Fact:** You should take it immediately. The name "morning-after" is a metaphor. Taking it within 12 hours gives the highest pregnancy protection.
* **Myth:** Generic emergency pills from Jan Aushadhi are weak and do not work.
  * **Fact:** Generic Levonorgestrel (1.5mg) tablets have the exact same chemical composition and are equally effective, but are 80% cheaper.
* **Myth:** The emergency pill is 100% effective in all women.
  * **Fact:** ECPs can fail, especially if taken late, if the woman is overweight (body weight $>75\text{ kg}$ reduces Levonorgestrel efficacy), or if she vomits.
* **Myth:** The emergency pill is highly toxic and can cause sudden death.
  * **Fact:** While it causes temporary side effects like nausea and irregular periods, the hormone is safe and does not cause death or organ failure.
