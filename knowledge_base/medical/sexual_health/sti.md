# Sexually Transmitted Infections (STI/Sexually Transmitted Infections) ki Pehchan aur Asymptomatic Infection Rules (Screening, HPV Vaccine and Prevention Guide)

## Overview

### English
A Sexually Transmitted Infection (STI) is the colonization of the body's genitourinary tract or mucous membranes by pathogenic bacteria, viruses, or parasites acquired through sexual contact. The critical clinical distinction of STIs is that **the majority of infections are completely asymptomatic (silent)**. For example, up to $80\%$ of Chlamydia cases in women show zero symptoms while silently causing tubal scarring and permanent infertility. Safe sex protocols emphasize proactive diagnostic screening, vaccinations (**HPV** and **Hepatitis B**), and starting **Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)** or syndromic kits immediately after high-risk unprotected exposure.

### Hindi

## Quick Summary
STIs represent asymptomatic mucosal colonizations transmitted sexually. The silent nature of pathogens like chlamydia requires proactive screening to prevent tubal scarring. First aid for high-risk exposure utilizes on-demand PEP. Diagnostics use molecular PCR swabs. Management focuses on preventive vaccines.

## Definition
* **Asymptomatic Infection:** An active infection in the body that presents no outward signs or symptoms, allowing the patient to transmit it to others unknowingly.
* **HPV (Human Papillomavirus):** A double-stranded DNA virus responsible for genital warts and $99\%$ of all cervical cancers worldwide, preventable via vaccination.
* **Partner Notification:** The clinical practice of identifying, testing, and treating the sexual partners of a patient diagnosed with an STI.

## Symptoms of Common STIs (Symptomatic Phase)

When STIs progress to show symptoms, they present in specific patterns:

### English
* **Silent (Asymptomatic) Phase:** Zero pain, discharge, or lesions; patient feels completely healthy but can transmit the infection.
* **Urethral/Vaginal discharge:** Purulent yellow-green fluid; burning urination; pain in pelvic area.
* **Genital Ulcers/Sores:** Single painless ulcer (Syphilis); cluster of painful watery blisters (Herpes simplex).

### Hindi
* **लक्षण रहित चरण (Asymptomatic):** कोई दर्द, रिसाव या छाला न होना; मरीज पूरी तरह स्वस्थ महसूस करता है लेकिन संक्रमण फैला सकता है।
* **स्राव (Discharge) होना:** पीला या सफेद बदबूदार पानी निकलना।
* **घाव और मस्से होना।**

### Hinglish
* **Asymptomatic Phase (Silent stage):** Koi pain, discharge ya chhale na hona; patient khud ko 100% healthy samajhta hai par virus dusro ko transmit kar sakta hai.
* **Vaginal/Penis discharge:** Badbudar pus-like liquid leak hona; peshab ke dauran tez jalan.
* **Genital Ulcers (Ghaw):** Painless chancre (Syphilis) ya dardnak watery daane (Herpes).

## Safety, Screening & Vaccination Protocols (First Aid)

If you have multiple partners or had a condom break, execute this protection protocol:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Get vaccinated against HPV.** The **HPV Vaccine** (Gardasil-9) is recommended for both boys and girls aged **9 to 26 years** (most effective before their first sexual encounter). It prevents cervical, penile, anal, and throat cancers.
* **Get vaccinated against Hepatitis B.** A 3-dose vaccine schedule provides lifelong protection against sexually transmitted Hepatitis B liver damage.
* **Screen annually if you are sexually active** with multiple partners. Do not wait for symptoms to show. Request a Chlamydia/Gonorrhea PCR urine test and VDRL blood test.
* **Complete the Partner Treatment.** Treat both partners together.

#### Hindi
* **एचपीवी (HPV) का टीका लगवाएं:** ९ से २६ वर्ष की आयु में गर्भाशय ग्रीवा (cervix) और लिंग के कैंसर से बचने के लिए टीका लगवाएं।
* **हेपेटाइटिस बी (Hepatitis B) का टीका लगवाएं।**
* **प्रतिवर्ष नियमित जांच कराएं।**

#### Hinglish
* **HPV Vaccine (Gardasil-9) lagwayein:** Cervical cancer (females me) aur penile/throat cancer (males me) se bachne ke liye **9 se 26 saal ki age** me HPV vaccine lagwayein, khaskar first sexual relation se pehle.
* **Hepatitis B vaccine lagwayein:** Liver failure aur cancer se bachne ke liye Hepatitis B ki 3 doses lagwayein.
* **Rojana/Saal me ek baar test karwayein:** Agar aapke multiple partners hain, to bina symptoms ke bhi saal me 1 bar HIV, VDRL aur urine PCR test karwayein.

### Don't (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT use oil-based lubricants** (like coconut oil, lotions, vaseline) with latex condoms. Oil degrades latex instantly, causing microscopic holes or tears, allowing STI pathogens to pass. Use water-based lube.
* **Do NOT assume a "normal" looking genital area is safe.** A partner can look clean and healthy but carry silent Chlamydia or HPV.
* **Do NOT ignore missed periods or pelvic pain** after unsafe sex.

#### Hindi
* **कंडोम के साथ नारियल तेल या लोशन का प्रयोग न करें:** इससे लैटेक्स में सूक्ष्म छेद हो जाते हैं।
* **गुप्तांगों को देखकर यह न समझें कि साथी सुरक्षित है।**

#### Hinglish
* **Condom par sarso ya coconut oil na lagayein:** Oil-based lubricants latex rubber ko melt karke condom me micro-holes (halkey chhed) kar dete hain jisse virus pass ho jata hai. Humesha **Water-based lubricant** use karein.
* **Partner ki skin dekh kar safe na samjhein:** Genital area clean dikhne par bhi partner me silent Chlamydia ya HPV infection ho sakta hai. Safely Condom use karein.

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **Multiplex NAAT (PCR) Swab:** Gold standard molecular test to detect active Chlamydia, Gonorrhea, and Mycoplasma DNA.
* **VDRL / RPR blood test:** Screening for Syphilis antibodies.
* **HBsAg Blood Test:** Screens for Hepatitis B surface antigen.

---

## Medical & Preventive Management
* **Syndromic Kit Management (India):** Government clinics use color-coded kits: Grey Kit (Cefixime + Azithromycin) for discharge, Green Kit (Secnidazole + Fluconazole) for vaginitis.
* **HPV Vaccination:** 2 doses (ages 9-14) or 3 doses (ages 15-26) of recombinant HPV vaccine.

## Prevention of STIs
* **Consistent Condom Use:** Use a new latex condom for every sexual act (vaginal, anal, oral).
* **Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP):** Daily oral pills for high-risk HIV-negative people.
* **Regular Screening.**

## Possible Complications of Untreated STIs
* **Tubal Infertility:** Chlamydia/Gonorrhea scars the fallopian tubes, preventing pregnancy or causing fatal ectopic pregnancies.
* **Cervical Cancer:** Caused by high-risk HPV strains 16 and 18.
* **Chronic Liver Cirrhosis:** Caused by chronic Hepatitis B infection.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient has a known high-risk exposure (condom break, assault) to an HIV-positive or Syphilis-positive source (requires PEP/antibiotics within 72 hours).**
* **A female develops high fever, severe lower abdominal pain, and foul vaginal discharge (acute Pelvic Inflammatory Disease emergency).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is an STI?**
   An STI (Sexually Transmitted Infection) is an infection passed from one person to another through sexual contact, which often does not show any symptoms.

2. **Why are STIs called "silent infections"?**
   Because many STIs (like Chlamydia, HPV, and Hepatitis B) can stay in the body for months or years without causing any pain, discharge, or visible sores, while silently causing internal organ damage.

3. **What is Chlamydia and why is it dangerous for women?**
   Chlamydia is a bacterial STI. In $80\%$ of women, it is asymptomatic. If left untreated, it travels up to the fallopian tubes, causing severe scarring and permanent infertility.

4. **What is the HPV vaccine (Gardasil-9) and who should take it?**
   Gardasil-9 is a vaccine protecting against 9 strains of Human Papillomavirus. It is recommended for boys and girls aged 9 to 26 to prevent cervical and penile cancers.

5. **Where can I get a free STI treatment kit in India?**
   Free STI kits (like Grey or Green kits) are distributed by the government at all **Suraksha Clinics** located inside civil hospitals.

6. **Can Hepatitis B spread through sex?**
   Yes. Hepatitis B is a highly infectious viral STI that spreads through semen, vaginal fluids, and blood. It can cause chronic liver failure and liver cancer.

7. **How does the Hepatitis B vaccine schedule work?**
   The vaccine is given in 3 doses: the first dose, the second dose 1 month later, and the third dose 6 months after the first. It provides lifelong protection.

8. **Can a condom protect against HPV?**
   Condoms provide high protection but are not 100% effective against HPV because the virus can be present on the skin of the scrotum or thighs, which are not covered by the condom.

9. **What is the window period for Hepatitis B testing?**
   The HBsAg test can usually detect Hepatitis B in the blood **4 to 6 weeks** after exposure.

10. **Why should both partners get treated for Chlamydia?**
    If only one partner takes antibiotics, they will get re-infected during their next sexual contact. Partners must be treated together and avoid sex for 7 days.

11. **What is the price of generic Azithromycin at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Azithromycin (500mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹30 to ₹40 per strip of 3 tablets, compared to ₹80-₹120 in private stores.

12. **Can untreated STIs cause ectopic pregnancy?**
    Yes. Untreated Chlamydia or Gonorrhea causes Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID), which damages the cilia in the fallopian tubes, causing the embryo to implant in the tube.

13. **Is the HPV vaccine safe?**
    Yes. The HPV vaccine is highly safe and has been tested in millions of adolescents worldwide. It does not cause infertility or neurological diseases.

14. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover vaginal infection treatments?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for severe pelvic infections, diagnostic hysteroscopy, and C-section deliveries.

15. **Why is using coconut oil as a lubricant bad for condoms?**
    Coconut oil degrades latex rubber, causing the condom to weaken and tear easily during sex, which increases the risk of STI transmission.

16. **What is the first-line treatment for Gonorrhea?**
    The first line of treatment is a single intramuscular injection of **Ceftriaxone (500mg)** combined with oral Azithromycin (1g).

17. **Can a pregnant mother pass an STI to her newborn baby?**
    Yes. Chlamydia and Gonorrhea can infect the baby's eyes during birth, causing blindness. Syphilis can cause Congenital Syphilis, which can lead to stillbirth.

18. **Why is NAAT (PCR) better than normal culture tests for STIs?**
    NAAT (Nucleic Acid Amplification Test) is a molecular DNA test. It is highly sensitive and can detect even tiny amounts of Chlamydia or Gonorrhea DNA, avoiding false negatives.

19. **What is the role of NACO Suraksha Clinics?**
    Suraksha Clinics provide free, confidential STI testing, counseling, free Nirodh condoms, and free syndromic medicine kits to patients nationwide.

20. **Is generic Doxycycline as effective as branded doxycycline?**
    Yes. Generic Doxycycline capsules contain the exact same active antibiotic and cure Chlamydia infections just as effectively, at an 80% lower cost.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** If you look clean and have no genital discharge, you do not have an STI.
  * **Fact:** Up to $80\%$ of Chlamydia and early HIV infections show zero symptoms. You can be infected and transmit the infection without knowing it.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures Chlamydia or Gonorrhea instantly.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Bacterial STIs can only be cured by specific antibiotics under medical advice.
* **Myth:** The HPV vaccine is a way to promote unsafe sex among teenagers.
  * **Fact:** The HPV vaccine is cancer prevention. It protects teenagers from cervical and penile cancers before they are exposed to the virus.
* **Myth:** You can get an STI from sharing a swimming pool or food.
  * **Fact:** STI pathogens cannot survive outside the human body. They do not transmit through water, sharing food, or hugging.
* **Myth:** Using two condoms together provides double protection against STIs.
  * **Fact:** Two condoms slide against each other, creating friction that tears both layers. Use only one condom at a time.
* **Myth:** Antibiotics can cure viral STIs like Herpes or Hepatitis B.
  * **Fact:** Antibiotics only kill bacteria. Viral STIs require antivirals or are prevented by vaccines.
* **Myth:** Douching with vinegar after sex washes away STI pathogens.
  * **Fact:** Douching pushes bacteria deeper into the cervix, worsening infections and destroying vaginal health. Use condoms instead.
* **Myth:** Generic STI kits from government clinics are expired or weak.
  * **Fact:** Government Suraksha kits contain high-potency WHO-approved generic medicines that are fresh, tested, and highly effective.
* **Myth:** Married women do not need the HPV vaccine.
  * **Fact:** Married women can still contract HPV if their partner was exposed before marriage. The vaccine is highly protective for all women up to age 26.
* **Myth:** If your first STI screening is negative at 1 week post-exposure, you are completely safe.
  * **Fact:** You are in the window period. You must repeat the blood tests at 12 weeks (3 months) to confirm you are negative for Syphilis and HIV.
