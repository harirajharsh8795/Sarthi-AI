# Contraception (Contraception/Garbh Nirodhak) ki Pehchan aur Tarike (Contraceptive Methods and Safety Guide)

## Overview

### English
Contraception, commonly known as birth control or family planning, refers to any method, drug, or device used to prevent pregnancy. Contraceptive methods are classified into:
1. **Barrier Methods:** (e.g., male/female Condoms - the only method protecting against both pregnancy and STIs).
2. **Hormonal Methods:** (e.g., Combined Oral Contraceptive Pills [OCPs], Progestin-only pills, injections, implants).
3. **Intrauterine Devices (IUDs):** (e.g., Copper-T or hormonal IUD Mirena).
4. **Permanent Sterilization:** (e.g., Male Vasectomy, Female Tubectomy).
*Clinical guidelines require screening for deep vein thrombosis (DVT) risk before prescribing combined estrogen-progestin pills.*

### Hindi
गर्भनिरोधक (Contraception / गर्भनिरोधक या परिवार नियोजन), अनचाहे गर्भधारण (unintended pregnancy) को रोकने वाली विधियां, दवाएं या उपकरण हैं। इन्हें चार श्रेणियों में बांटा गया है: **बैरियर विधि** (कंडोम - जो गर्भ और एसटीआई दोनों रोकता है), **हार्मोनल विधि** (गर्भनिरोधक गोलियां, इंजेक्शन), **अंतर्गर्भाशयी उपकरण** (कॉपर-टी / Copper-T), और **स्थायी नसबंदी** (पुरुष नसबंदी/महिला नसबंदी)। हार्मोनल गोलियों के सेवन के दौरान गहरी नस के थक्के (**DVT / रक्त के थक्के**) के खतरे की जांच आवश्यक है, विशेष रूप से धूम्रपान करने वाली और 35 वर्ष से अधिक उम्र की महिलाओं में।

### Hinglish

Inke 4 main types hain: **Barrier Methods** (jaise Condoms jo pregnancy aur STIs dono se bachate hain), **Hormonal Methods** (jaise daily oral contraceptive pills ya hormonal injections), **IUDs** (jaise uterine cavity me fit hone wali Copper-T), aur **Permanent Methods** (jaise male ki Vasectomy ya female ki Tubectomy). Hormonal pills ke use me **DVT (blood clot)** ka risk dhyan rakhna zaroori hota hai.

## Quick Summary
Contraception prevents pregnancy through barrier, hormonal, IUD, or permanent methods. Hormonal methods carry side effects like DVT blood clots (especially in smokers). First aid for contraceptive failures uses emergency pills. Diagnostics monitor hormone profiles and IUD positioning. Management matches patient health history.

## Definition
* **IUD (Intrauterine Device):** A small, T-shaped plastic device inserted into the uterus by a clinician to prevent fertilization (e.g., Copper-T blocks sperm motility).
* **DVT (Deep Vein Thrombosis):** A blood clot that forms in a deep vein, usually in the lower leg or thigh, a rare but critical side effect of estrogen-containing birth control pills.
* **LARC (Long-Acting Reversible Contraception):** Birth control methods that last for years without user intervention (e.g., IUDs, implants).

## Symptoms of Hormonal Pill Side Effects & IUD Complications

### English
* **Hormonal Pill Side Effects:** Nausea, mild headaches, and breast tenderness; irregular breakthrough bleeding (spotting between periods); **Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)** signs: sudden pain, swelling, warmth, and redness in one calf muscle.
* **Copper-T Complications:** Heavy menstrual bleeding and painful cramps (dysmenorrhea) during the first 3 months; pelvic infection; feeling the Copper-T thread missing (indicates expulsion or migration).

### Hindi
* **हार्मोनल गोलियों के दुष्प्रभाव:** मतली, सिरदर्द, मासिक धर्म के बीच खून के धब्बे (spotting) दिखना; पैरों की नसों में खून का थक्का जमना (**DVT** - एक पैर में तेज दर्द और सूजन)।
* **कॉपर-टी की समस्याएं:** शुरुआत के 3 महीनों में भारी रक्तस्राव और पेट में मरोड़; कॉपर-टी का खिसक जाना (thread missing)।

### Hinglish
* **Hormonal Pills ke Side Effects:** Nausea (jee machlana), mild headache (sir dard) aur periods ke beech me spotting (halki bleeding) hona; **DVT (Deep Vein Thrombosis) warning:** Ek pair me sudden tej dard, sujan aur garmi mehsus hona (blood clot ka sign).
* **Copper-T ki Complications:** Pehle 3 mahine me periods me heavy bleeding aur abdominal cramps hona; Copper-T ka dhaaga (thread) mehsus na hona (khisak jana).

## Safety Guidelines & Contraindications

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Consult a gynecologist before starting combined hormonal pills.** Estrogen is contraindicated in women aged $>35$ who smoke, or those with high blood pressure, as it increases the risk of stroke and heart attack.
* **Take the daily oral pill at the exact same time every day.** Missing a pill by $>12$ hours requires backup contraception (condoms) for 7 days.
* **Check the Copper-T thread monthly** after your period to ensure it hasn't shifted.
* **Choose Vasectomy over Tubectomy** for permanent contraception. Vasectomy is safer, less invasive, takes 10 minutes (NSV), and is highly effective.

#### Hindi
* **दवा शुरू करने से पहले डॉक्टर से मिलें:** 35 से अधिक उम्र की धूम्रपान करने वाली महिलाएं ओसीपी (OCP) न लें।
* **रोजाना एक ही समय पर गोली खाएं।**
* **मासिक धर्म के बाद कॉपर-टी के धागे की जांच करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Pills shuru karne se pehle doctor se consult karein:** Agar aapki age 35 se zyada hai aur aap smoking (biri/cigarette) karte hain, to combined hormonal pills na lein warna blood clot (DVT) ya stroke ho sakta hai.
* **Daily OCP ek hi time par lein:** Agar aap daily goli leti hain (jaise Mala-D), to rojana exact same time par lein. 12 ghante se late hone par 7 din tak backup ke liye condom zaroori pehnein.
* **Copper-T thread daily check karein:** Periods ke baad fingers se check karein ki Copper-T ka dhaaga apni jagah par hai ya nahi.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT rely on hormonal pills for STI protection.** Hormonal methods do not create any physical barrier. **Only condoms protect against STIs.**
* **Do NOT ignore sudden calf pain and swelling** while taking birth control pills. This is a medical emergency (DVT).
* **Do NOT undergo permanent vasectomy without realizing it is irreversible.** It is a permanent decision.
* **Do NOT pull the Copper-T thread** yourself if you feel it.

#### Hindi
* **गोलियों पर एसटीआई सुरक्षा के लिए भरोसा न करें:** गोलियां एड्स (HIV) या सिफलिस से नहीं बचातीं।
* **पैरों में तेज दर्द और सूजन को नजरअंदाज न करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **STIs/HIV se bachne ke liye pills par bharosa na karein:** Garbh-nirodhak goliya sirf pregnancy rokti hain, HIV ya syphilis se protection **sirf Condoms** hi dete hain.
* **Pair ke dard ko ignore na karein:** Painkiller goli khate waqt agar pair ki calf muscle me sudden pain aur sujan ho, to use ignore na karein (DVT risk).
* **Copper-T thread ko khud se na kheencheik.**

## Diagnostic Evaluation before Starting Contraception
* **Blood Pressure Check:** Mandatory before starting estrogen pills (estrogen raises BP).
* **Pelvic Ultrasound:** Done before Copper-T insertion to check uterine cavity shape and post-insertion to verify correct positioning.
* **Coagulation Profile:** Screen for clotting disorders (like Factor V Leiden) if the family history has DVT.

---

## Medical & Surgical Management
* **LARC Insertion (Copper-T):** Inserted through the cervix into the uterine cavity by a trained gynecologist.
* **No-Scalpel Vasectomy (NSV):** A minor 10-minute male sterilization procedure done under local anesthesia. The vas deferens tubes (sperm ducts) are cut and tied through a tiny puncture, requiring no stitches.
* **Tubectomy:** Surgical cutting and tying of the fallopian tubes in females.

## Medicines
*Saarthi AI does not prescribe medicines. The following describes clinical drug categories related to contraception:*
1. **Combined Oral Contraceptive Pills (COCPs):** e.g., Estrogen + Progestin (Mala-D, Mala-N).
2. **Progesterone-only Pills (POPs):** e.g., Levonorgestrel, Desogestrel (safe for breastfeeding mothers).
3. **Non-Hormonal Weekly Pills:** e.g., Centchroman (Saheli / Chhaya - does not cause weight gain or DVT).
4. **Hormonal Injections:** e.g., Depot Medroxyprogesterone Acetate (DMPA - Antara).

## Prevention of Contraceptive Complications
* **Switch to Non-Hormonal Pills (Saheli):** Centchroman is a once-a-week non-steroidal pill developed in India (CDRI) that has zero risk of blood clots or weight gain.
* **Stay Active on Flights:** If taking OCPs, walk and stretch during long flights to prevent DVT.
* **Post-Vasectomy Semen Analysis:** A man is not sterile immediately after vasectomy. **Use backup protection (condoms) for 3 months (or 20 ejaculations)** until a semen test shows zero active sperm.

---

## Possible Complications of Contraceptive Failure
* **Ectopic Pregnancy:** High risk if pregnancy occurs with a Copper-T inside, where the fetus grows in the fallopian tube, risking internal rupture and death.
* **Pulmonary Embolism:** If a DVT blood clot breaks and travels to the lungs, causing sudden death.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **A female on birth control pills develops sudden chest pain, shortness of breath, or coughs up blood (Pulmonary Embolism).**
* **Sudden severe swelling and pain in one leg (DVT).**
* **Severe, sharp, one-sided lower abdominal pain in a woman using Copper-T (Ectopic pregnancy warning).**

---

## Government Family Planning Programs (India)
* **National Family Planning Programme:** Provides free Copper-T (380A/375), daily pills (**Mala-N**), non-hormonal weekly pills (**Chhaya / Saheli**), and injectable contraceptives (**Antara**) free at all government PHCs.
* **Compensation Schemes for Sterilization:** The government provides direct financial incentives (compensation) to males undergoing NSV (approx. ₹2000-₹3000) and females undergoing tubectomy.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Offers generic oral pills, emergency pills, and condoms at 80% discounted rates.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is contraception?**
   Contraception includes methods, devices, or drugs used to prevent pregnancy during sexual intercourse.

2. **What is Copper-T (IUD) and how long does it last?**
   Copper-T is a T-shaped intrauterine device inserted in the womb. Copper acts as a spermicide. Copper-T 380A provides protection for up to 10 years.

3. **Why do hormonal birth control pills cause blood clots (DVT)?**
   Estrogen in combined pills increases the liver's production of clotting factors, raising the risk of deep vein blood clots, especially in smokers over 35.

4. **What is "Saheli" (Chhaya) contraceptive pill?**
   Saheli (Centchroman) is a non-steroidal, non-hormonal weekly pill developed in India. It does not cause weight gain, mood swings, or blood clots.

5. **Is a man sterile immediately after a vasectomy?**
   No. Active sperm remain in the ducts after surgery. The couple must use condoms for **3 months** until a semen test confirms zero sperm.

6. **What should I do if I miss a daily contraceptive pill?**
   Take the missed pill as soon as you remember. If it has been more than 12-24 hours, take the pill and use backup condoms for the next 7 days.

7. **Where can I get free birth control in India?**
   All methods—including Copper-T, Mala-N pills, Saheli pills, and Antara injections—are provided completely free at all government primary health centers (PHCs).

8. **What is "Antara" injection?**
   Antara is a government-provided hormonal injection (DMPA) given once every 3 months into the arm or hip to prevent pregnancy.

9. **Can birth control pills protect against HIV/STIs?**
   No. Hormonal pills only prevent pregnancy. They do not block viral or bacterial transmission. Only latex condoms protect against HIV and STIs.

10. **What are the warning signs of DVT?**
    Signs include sudden, severe pain, swelling, warmth, and redness in one calf muscle of the leg.

11. **What is the difference between Vasectomy and Tubectomy?**
    Vasectomy is male sterilization (tying the sperm ducts), a 10-minute outpatient procedure. Tubectomy is female sterilization (tying fallopian tubes), requiring abdominal surgery.

12. **Can a breastfeeding mother take combined contraceptive pills?**
    No. Estrogen in combined pills can reduce breast milk production. Breastfeeding mothers should use Progestin-Only Pills (POPs) or Copper-T.

13. **What is the price of generic birth control pills at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic oral contraceptive strips are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹20 per month.

14. **What is an ectopic pregnancy?**
    It is a dangerous condition where a fertilized egg implants outside the uterus, usually in the fallopian tube. It is a medical emergency that can cause internal rupture.

15. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover vasectomy and tubectomy?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for permanent sterilization surgeries, complications repair, and all pre/post-operative care.

16. **How does Copper-T affect menstruation?**
    In the first 3 months after insertion, Copper-T can cause heavier menstrual bleeding, spotting, and increased back pain. This usually normalizes later.

17. **Can a woman get pregnant immediately after removing Copper-T?**
    Yes. Unlike hormonal injections, fertility returns immediately after a gynecologist removes the Copper-T device from the uterus.

18. **Why is smoking dangerous while taking birth control pills?**
    Smoking, combined with estrogen pills, severely damages blood vessels and dangerously increases the risk of stroke, heart attack, and deep vein thrombosis.

19. **What is LARC?**
    Long-Acting Reversible Contraception (LARC) includes methods like IUDs and hormonal implants that provide highly effective pregnancy prevention for 3 to 10 years without daily action.

20. **Is vasectomy reversible?**
    A vasectomy should be considered permanent and irreversible. While reversal surgery exists, it is complex, expensive, and does not guarantee success.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Contraceptive pills cause permanent infertility, and women cannot conceive after stopping them.
  * **Fact:** Pills do not cause infertility. Once you stop taking daily pills, your normal hormonal cycle and fertility return within 1 to 3 months.
* **Myth:** Vasectomy makes a man weak, destroys his strength, and causes erectile dysfunction.
  * **Fact:** Vasectomy only cuts the sperm ducts. It has no effect on testosterone production, physical strength, erection, or climax.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key prevents pregnancy, so contraceptives are not needed.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Pregnancy can only be prevented by barrier, hormonal, IUD, or permanent sterilization methods.
* **Myth:** Copper-T can travel from the uterus to the heart or brain.
  * **Fact:** Copper-T is placed in the uterus. While it can rarely perforate the uterine wall, it cannot enter the bloodstream or travel to the heart or brain.
* **Myth:** If you miss a pill, you should take 4 tablets together the next day.
  * **Fact:** Taking 4 pills together will cause severe vomiting, nausea, and hormone toxicity. Follow the 1-2 missed pill rules and use condoms.
* **Myth:** Natural calendar methods (withdrawing before climax) are 100% safe.
  * **Fact:** Withdrawal has a high failure rate ($22\%$ in normal use) because pre-ejaculatory fluid (pre-cum) can contain active sperm.
* **Myth:** Antara injection causes permanent bone damage in all women.
  * **Fact:** Antara (DMPA) can cause a temporary loss of bone mineral density, which recovers after stopping the injection. It is safe for most women under doctor advice.
* **Myth:** Generic birth control pills from government clinics are cheap and cause more side effects.
  * **Fact:** Generic government pills (Mala-N, Chhaya) contain the exact same hormones and undergo strict safety testing. They are highly effective and safe.
* **Myth:** You must take a break from contraceptive pills every 1 year to "detox" the body.
  * **Fact:** There is no medical need for a pill break unless you want to get pregnant. Breaks increase the risk of accidental pregnancy.
* **Myth:** Tubectomy is a 100% painless and minor procedure compared to vasectomy.
  * **Fact:** Tubectomy requires entry into the female abdominal cavity, carrying higher surgical risks and recovery times than male vasectomy.
