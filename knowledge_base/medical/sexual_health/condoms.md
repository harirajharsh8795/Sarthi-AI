# Condoms (Condoms/Nirodh) ki Pehchan, Sahi Upyog aur Savdhaniyan (Barrier Contraception and STI Prevention Guide)

## Overview

### English
A Condom is a thin, form-fitting barrier sheath (commonly made of **latex**, polyurethane, or polyisoprene) worn over the erect penis (male condom) or inserted into the vagina (female condom) during sexual intercourse. It acts as a primary physical barrier preventing the exchange of bodily fluids (semen, vaginal secretions, blood), thereby offering **dual protection**: preventing unintended pregnancy (efficacy rate of $98\%$ when used perfectly) and significantly reducing the risk of transmitting Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs), including HIV, Gonorrhea, Chlamydia, and HPV.

### Hindi
कंडोम (Condom / कंडोम या निरोध), यौन संबंध के दौरान इस्तेमाल किया जाने वाला एक पतला, सुरक्षात्मक आवरण (sheath) है (जो आमतौर पर लैटेक्स से बना होता है)। यह पुरुष के लिंग पर (male condom) या महिला की योनि में (female condom) लगाया जाता है। यह **दोहरी सुरक्षा (Dual Protection)** प्रदान करता है: अनचाहे गर्भधारण से बचाव ($98\%$ प्रभावशीलता) और एचआईवी (HIV), सिफलिस और गोनोरिया जैसे यौन संचारित रोगों (STIs) के प्रसार को रोकना। इसके इस्तेमाल के समय तेल आधारित लुब्रिकेंट (oil-based lubricants) का उपयोग कभी न करें।

Unwanted Pregnancy (garbh) se bachav, aur 2. **STIs/STDs** (jaise HIV, Syphilis, Gonorrhea) se protection. Correct use aur oil-based lubricants se bachna iske liye mandatory hai.

## Quick Summary
Condoms are barrier contraceptives offering dual protection against pregnancy and STIs. Symptoms of incorrect use feature slipping, breaking, and local latex allergy itching. First aid for a broken condom requires immediate emergency contraception (pill) within 72 hours. Diagnostics check STI status. Management uses proper water-based lubrication.

## Definition
* **Barrier Contraception:** Methods of birth control that physically block sperm from entering the uterus (e.g., condoms, diaphragms).
* **Latex Degradation:** The chemical breakdown of latex rubber molecules caused by exposure to oil-based substances (like vaseline or coconut oil), causing the rubber to weaken and tear instantly.
* **Dual Protection:** The simultaneous prevention of both unintended pregnancy and sexually transmitted infections (STIs) by a single device.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Correct Condom Application (Male Condom)

To ensure maximum efficacy and prevent tearing or slippage:
1. **Check Expiry & Packaging:** Verify the expiration date. Feel the packet for an "air cushion" (pillow effect) to ensure the seal has not been punctured.
2. **Open Carefully:** Tear along the jagged edge. **Do NOT use teeth, scissors, or long fingernails**, as they easily make micro-tears in the latex.
3. **Check the Roll Direction:** Ensure the condom is rolled up (it should look like a small hat, not a pacifier). The rolled edge must face outward.
4. **Pinch the Tip:** Pinch the reservoir tip (teat) at the top of the condom to remove trapped air. *If air is trapped, the pressure during ejaculation will pop and burst the condom.*
5. **Roll Down:** Place the condom on the tip of the erect penis and unroll it all the way down to the base.
6. **Post-Ejaculation Withdrawal:** Withdraw the penis immediately after ejaculation **while still erect**, holding the condom firmly at the base to prevent semen leakage.
7. **Safe Disposal:** Wrap in tissue paper and throw in a dustbin. Do not flush down the toilet.

## Symptoms of Incorrect Use & Allergies

### English
* **Slippage or Breakage:** Condom slipping off inside the vagina during sex, or tearing/rupturing during intercourse (due to dry friction or oil use).
* **Latex Allergy:** Severe itching, redness, swelling, or burning sensation in the genital area (both partners) after condom use. (Requires switching to polyurethane/non-latex condoms).

### Hindi
* **फटना या खिसकना:** संभोग के दौरान कंडोम का खिसक जाना या अचानक फट जाना (सूखे घर्षण के कारण)।
* **लैटेक्स एलर्जी:** कंडोम इस्तेमाल करने के बाद जननांगों में तेज खुजली, लाली और सूजन होना।

### Hinglish
* **Condom Phatna ya Nikalna:** Sex ke dauran condom ka phat jana ya slip hokar vagina ke andar chhoot jana (friction ya oil use karne se).
* **Latex Allergy:** Condom use karne ke baad vaginal ya penis area me tez khujli, rashes ya sujan aana. (Iske liye non-latex polyurethane condoms use karein).

## First Aid for Broken Condom Crisis (Emergency Contraception)

If a condom breaks, slips off, or leaks during intercourse, execute this protocol immediately:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Administer an Emergency Contraceptive Pill (ECP)** (such as Levonorgestrel 1.5mg, commonly sold as **i-Pill** or **72-Card**) to the female partner **within 72 hours** (3 days) of the intercourse. The sooner the pill is taken, the higher the efficacy ($95\%$ if taken within 24 hours).
* **Gently wash the external genitals** with plain water. Do not douche or scrub inside.
* **Consult a gynecologist** for an emergency Copper-T IUD insertion if the 72-hour window has passed (effective up to 5 days after intercourse).
* **Screen for STIs** in 2-3 weeks.

#### Hindi
* **इमरजेंसी गर्भनिरोधक गोली (i-Pill) दें:** असुरक्षित यौन संबंध या कंडोम फटने के **72 घंटे (3 दिन) के भीतर** महिला साथी को आई-पिल (i-Pill) खिलाएं।
* **बाहरी जननांगों को साफ पानी से धोएं।**

#### Hinglish
* **i-Pill (Emergency Pill) khilayein:** Condom phatne ke **72 ghante (3 din) ke andar** female partner ko i-Pill (Levonorgestrel 1.5mg) khilayein. 24 ghante ke andar lene par ye 95% pregnancy ko rok deti hai.
* **External area wash karein:** Genital area ko plain water se wash karein.
* **Emergency Copper-T lagwayein:** Agar 72 ghante nikal chuke hain, to gynecologist se contact karke 5 din ke andar Copper-T lagwa sakte hain.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT use oil-based lubricants.** Avoid Vaseline, baby oil, body lotions, butter, or coconut oil. Oil degrades latex instantly, causing the condom to tear in seconds. **Only use water-based or silicone-based lubricants.**
* **Do NOT double up.** Never wear two condoms at the same time. The friction between the two latex surfaces causes both to tear rapidly.
* **Do NOT wash or reuse a condom.** Condoms are strictly single-use devices. Washing them destroys the latex structure.
* **Do NOT push vaginal douches or chemicals** inside to wash out semen; this pushes sperm further into the cervix and destroys vaginal pH.

#### Hindi
* **तेल आधारित लुब्रिकेंट का उपयोग न करें:** नारियल तेल, वैसलीन या लोशन कंडोम को तुरंत फाड़ देते हैं।
* **एक साथ दो कंडोम (double condom) न पहनें।**
* **कंडोम का दोबारा उपयोग (reuse) कभी न करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Oil-based lubricants (nariyal tel/vaseline) na lagayein:** Condom ke upar kabhi bhi coconut oil, vaseline, body lotion ya thuk (saliva) na lagayein. Oil se latex rubber chemically melt hokar turant phat jata hai. Humesha **Water-based lubricant** hi use karein.
* **Double Condoms ek sath na pehnein:** Do condom ek sath pehnne se friction ke karan dono phat jayenge.
* **Reuse bilkul na karein:** Ek condom sirf ek baar ke liye hota hai. Ise wash karke use karne se latex bilkul khatam ho jata hai.
* **Vaginal douching na karein.**

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **STI Panel Screening:** If a condom breaks with a partner of unknown STI status, test for HIV, Hepatitis B, Syphilis, Chlamydia, and Gonorrhea at 2 weeks and 3 months.
* **Urine Pregnancy Test (UPT):** If menstruation is delayed by $> 5$ days after condom failure.

---

## Medical & Preventive Management
* **Dual Protection Strategy:** Combine condom use (for STI protection) with another contraceptive method (like daily oral contraceptive pills or IUDs) for absolute protection against pregnancy.
* **Latex-Free Options:** For patients with latex allergy, prescribe **Polyurethane** or **Polyisoprene** condoms.

## Prevention of Condom Failure
* **Correct Storage:** Store condoms in a cool, dry place. Do not keep them in wallets or car glove compartments where friction and heat degrade the latex.
* **Ensure Adequate Lubrication:** Use water-based lube to prevent dry friction tears.
* **Check Expiry Dates.**

---

## Possible Complications of Condom Failure
* **Unintended Pregnancy:** Leading to emotional stress or surgical abortions.
* **STI Transmission:** Risk of contracting incurable infections like HIV or Herpes.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient experienced a condom tear with a known HIV-positive partner (requires immediate PEP therapy within 72 hours).**
* **Severe abdominal pain or heavy bleeding in the female partner 2-3 weeks after emergency pill intake (possible ectopic pregnancy).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a condom and how does it work?**
   A condom is a thin barrier sheath worn during sex to physically block semen and vaginal fluids, preventing pregnancy and STIs.

2. **Why is it called "Dual Protection"?**
   Because a condom is the only contraceptive that simultaneously protects against both unintended pregnancy and sexually transmitted infections (STIs/HIV).

3. **Can I use coconut oil as a lubricant with a latex condom?**
   No. Coconut oil, vaseline, and lotions degrade latex rubber chemically, causing the condom to tear within seconds. Use only water-based lubricants.

4. **Why do condoms break during sex?**
   Common causes include: using oil-based lubricants, using expired condoms, not pinching the air tip, tearing the packet with teeth, or dry friction (lack of lubrication).

5. **What is the i-Pill and when should it be taken?**
   The i-Pill is an emergency contraceptive tablet containing Levonorgestrel. It must be taken within 72 hours of a condom break to prevent pregnancy.

6. **What should I do if a condom gets stuck inside the vagina?**
   Wash your hands, relax, sit in a squatting position, and gently insert your fingers to pull out the condom. If unable, visit a gynecologist immediately.

7. **Where can I get free condoms in India?**
   Free government condoms branded as "Nirodh" are distributed at all government clinics, civil hospitals, and NACO counseling centers (ICTC).

8. **Can I wear two condoms together for extra safety?**
   No. Wearing two condoms causes friction between the latex layers, making both condoms highly likely to tear during intercourse.

9. **What is a latex allergy?**
   Some people are allergic to latex rubber. It causes itching, redness, and swelling. If you have it, switch to polyurethane or polyisoprene condoms.

10. **How effective are condoms in preventing HIV?**
    When used correctly and consistently, male latex condoms reduce the risk of HIV transmission by over 85-90%.

11. **Why must I pinch the tip of the condom before rolling it on?**
    Pinching removes trapped air. If air is left inside, the pressure from ejaculation will pop and burst the condom.

12. **Can I reuse a condom if I wash it with soap?**
    No. Condoms are strictly single-use. Washing destroys the strength of the latex, making it useless. Use a new condom for every sex act.

13. **What is the price of generic emergency pills at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Levonorgestrel (1.5mg) emergency pills are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹15 to ₹25, compared to ₹80-₹110 for branded i-Pill.

14. **What is PEP for HIV?**
    Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) is a 28-day course of antiviral medicines started within 72 hours of a condom break with an HIV-positive partner to prevent infection.

15. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover emergency abortions?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless surgical and medical packages for therapeutic abortions, obstetric emergencies, and gynecological ICU care.

16. **How should condoms be stored?**
    Store them in a cool, dry place. Do not keep them in wallets, pockets, or car glove boxes as friction and heat degrade the latex rubber.

17. **Can condoms prevent all STIs?**
    Condoms highly prevent fluids-based STIs (HIV, Gonorrhea). However, they offer less protection against skin-to-skin STIs (Herpes, Genital Warts, Syphilis) if the sores are outside the covered area.

18. **What is a female condom?**
    It is a polyurethane sheath inserted inside the vagina before sex, held in place by a flexible ring. It provides female-controlled protection against pregnancy and STIs.

19. **How long after ejaculation should the penis be withdrawn?**
    Withdraw the penis immediately after ejaculation while it is still erect, holding the condom rim firmly at the base of the penis to prevent semen leak.

20. **Can saliva be used as a lubricant?**
    Saliva is not a good lubricant. It dries up quickly, increasing friction tears, and can transmit throat infections to the genitals. Use water-based lube.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Condoms have tiny micro-pores that allow the HIV virus to pass through easily.
  * **Fact:** This is a false rumor. Latex is non-porous. The gaps between latex molecules are thousands of times smaller than the HIV virus. Condoms block HIV completely.
* **Myth:** Applying mustard oil (sarso tel) on a condom makes sex smoother and safer.
  * **Fact:** Mustard oil degrades latex, causing the condom to burst. Use water-based lubricants only.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key prevents pregnancy, so condoms are not required.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Pregnancy can only be prevented by barrier methods (condoms) or hormonal contraceptives.
* **Myth:** Condoms only need to be worn right before ejaculation (the climax).
  * **Fact:** Pre-ejaculatory fluid (pre-cum) can contain active sperm and STIs. Condoms must be worn before any genital contact starts.
* **Myth:** Using two condoms together is twice as safe.
  * **Fact:** Two condoms slide against each other, creating dry friction that tears both. Use only one condom at a time.
* **Myth:** Branded imported condoms are 100% tear-proof compared to government Nirodh.
  * **Fact:** All condoms sold in India must meet the same strict quality standards set by the CDSCO. Government Nirodh is highly safe and effective.
* **Myth:** Condoms cause permanent erectile dysfunction in men.
  * **Fact:** Condoms have no effect on penile nerves or erection physiology. Temporary loss of erection is usually psychological anxiety (performance anxiety).
* **Myth:** If you store condoms in your wallet, they stay safe for years.
  * **Fact:** Friction from sitting and body heat in a wallet degrades the latex fast, making the condom brittle and prone to tearing.
* **Myth:** Female condoms cannot prevent STIs.
  * **Fact:** Female condoms are made of strong polyurethane and cover both the vaginal walls and external labia, offering excellent protection against STIs.
* **Myth:** Condoms are only for teenagers; married couples do not need them.
  * **Fact:** Condoms are essential for married couples for family planning (child spacing) and protecting against STIs if either partner travels.
