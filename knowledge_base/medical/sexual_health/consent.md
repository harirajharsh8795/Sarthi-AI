# Sexual Consent (Sammati/Maanjuri) ki Pehchan aur Kanooni Niyam (Enthusiastic Consent and Legal Boundaries Guide)

## Overview

### English
Sexual Consent is a clear, voluntary, and mutual agreement to engage in specific sexual activities. According to clinical, ethical, and legal frameworks, consent must be **FRIES**: **F**reely given, **R**eversible (can be withdrawn at any moment), **I**nformed (no lies or hidden facts like stealthing), **E**nthusiastic (wanted, not just tolerated), and **S**pecific (agreeing to one act does not mean agreeing to others). Legally in India, under the **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023**, consent obtained through coercion, fear, intoxication (alcohol/drugs), or false promise of marriage is legally void (invalid) and classified as sexual assault or rape.

### Hindi
यौन सहमति (Sexual Consent / सहमति या मंजूरी), किसी भी यौन गतिविधि में शामिल होने के लिए एक स्पष्ट, स्वैच्छिक (voluntary) और आपसी समझौता है। सहमति के लिए **FRIES** सिद्धांत का पालन होना चाहिए: सहमति स्वतंत्र रूप से दी गई हो (Freely given), प्रतिवर्ती हो (Reversible - जिसे कभी भी वापस लिया जा सके), सूचित हो (Informed), उत्साही हो (Enthusiastic), और विशिष्ट हो (Specific)। भारतीय कानून (**BNS 2023**) के अनुसार, नशे की हालत में, डर दिखाकर या शादी के झूठे वादे पर ली गई सहमति पूरी तरह से अमान्य (void) है और इसे बलात्कार (rape) माना जाता है।

### Hinglish

Consent hamesha **FRIES** niyam par chalta hai: Freely given (bina kisi dabaw ke), Reversible (kisi bhi waqt mana karne ka right), Informed (koi jhoot ya dhoka na ho), Enthusiastic (mann se, dabaw me nahi), aur Specific (ek chiz ke liye haan kehna sabhi ke liye haan nahi hota). Indian Law (**BNS 2023**) ke under nashi (alcohol) me, darr ke pressure me ya shadi ke jhoote wade par li gayi manjoori invalid hai aur use rape/assault mana jata hai.

## Quick Summary
Consent is a voluntary, reversible agreement for sexual activity. Consent cannot be given by intoxicated, sleeping, or underage persons. Signs of absent consent include silence, freezing, crying, or drug influence. First aid for non-consensual acts requires immediate medical and legal rescue. Diagnostics check for physical trauma. Management utilizes professional psychological therapy.

## Definition
* **FRIES Consent:** An acronym defining the legal and ethical standards of consent: Freely Given, Reversible, Informed, Enthusiastic, Specific.
* **Stealthing:** The non-consensual removal of a condom during sexual intercourse, which legally invalidates consent and constitutes sexual assault.
* **Age of Consent:** The legally defined minimum age at which a person is considered capable of giving valid consent to sexual activity (established as **18 years** in India under POCSO and BNS).

---

## The FRIES Principles of Consent

Valid consent must meet all five criteria of the **FRIES** model:

| Principle | Meaning | Violation Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Freely Given** | Done without pressure, guilt, force, or threats | Partner agrees only because they are afraid of an argument |
| **Reversible** | Consent can be withdrawn at **any moment**, even during the act | Continuing to touch the partner after they say *"Stop"* or *"No"* |
| **Informed** | No lies, manipulation, or hidden details | Removing the condom secretly during sex (**Stealthing**) |
| **Enthusiastic**| Doing what you *want* to do, not what you feel *obliged* to do | Partner lies silently, waiting for the act to finish |
| **Specific** | Agreeing to one act (e.g., kissing) is not consent for others | Assuming kissing means they want penetration |

## Symptoms of Absent Consent & Sexual Assault

### English
* **Physical & Verbal signs of No Consent:** Saying *"No,"* *"Stop,"* *"I'm not sure,"* or *"I want to wait"*; physical resistance (pushing away, pulling clothes close); **silence or freezing** (tonic immobility - a fear response where the patient cannot move or speak); crying or shaking.
* **Inability to Consent:** The patient is sleeping, semi-conscious, or heavily intoxicated with alcohol or drugs. (Legally, an intoxicated person **cannot** consent).

### Hindi
* **सहमति न होने के संकेत:** *"नहीं,"* *"रुको,"* या *"मेरा मन नहीं है"* कहना; हाथ पीछे खींचना या चुपचाप जम जाना (tonic immobility - डर के कारण हिल न पाना); रोना।
* **सहमति देने में असमर्थता:** मरीज का सोए होना, बेहोश होना या अत्यधिक नशे (alcohol) में होना।

### Hinglish
* **No Consent ke Signs:** *"Nahi,"* *"Roko,"* ya *"Mera mann nahi hai"* bolna; physical resist karna (dhakka dena ya hath hatana); sudden shant ya freeze ho jana (tonic immobility - darr se sunn ho jana); rona ya kaapna.
* **Consent na de paane ki condition:** Agar partner soya hua ho, behosh ho, ya sharab/nasha (intoxicated) me ho. *Nashe me di gayi manjoori kanoonan invalid hoti hai.*

## Emergency Protocol for Non-Consensual Sexual Assault (First Aid)

If a person has been subjected to non-consensual sexual activity or assault, execute this care protocol immediately:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Ensure physical safety first.** Move to a safe, secure location immediately.
* **Do NOT shower, wash, change clothes, or brush teeth** before medical examination. Washing destroys crucial DNA evidence (semen, skin cells) needed for legal prosecution.
* **Go to a government hospital emergency department** for a medical examination. Ask for a forensic examination (DNA swab).
* **Take Emergency Contraception (i-Pill)** within 72 hours to prevent pregnancy.
* **Access HIV PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis)** within 72 hours to prevent HIV transmission.
* **Call the National Women Helpline (181)** or Police (112) for legal help.

#### Hindi
* **सुरक्षित स्थान पर जाएं।**
* **नहाएं या कपड़े न बदलें:** चिकित्सा जांच से पहले नहाने से डीएनए (DNA) सबूत नष्ट हो जाते हैं।
* **तुरंत सरकारी अस्पताल जाएं:** 72 घंटे के भीतर इमरजेंसी गर्भनिरोधक (i-Pill) और एचआईवी से बचाव की दवा (PEP) लें।
* **हेल्पलाइन १८१ पर कॉल करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Sabse pehle safe place par jayein.**
* **Nahayein ya kapde na badlein:** Medical checkup aur forensic test se pehle nahane ya vaginal wash karne se DNA evidence (semen/skin cells) destroy ho jate hain jo kanoonan case ke liye zaroori hain.
* **Government Hospital emergency jayein:** 72 ghante ke andar **i-Pill** (pregnancy rokne ke liye) aur **HIV PEP medicines** (HIV se bachne ke liye) shuru karwayein.
* **Women Helpline 181 ya Police 112 ko call karein.**

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT blame yourself.** Assault is 100% the fault of the perpetrator. Lack of physical struggle does not mean consent; the brain often freezes in fear.
* **Do NOT delay medical care.** The window for HIV PEP and emergency contraception is strictly **72 hours**.

#### Hindi
* **खुद को दोषी न मानें:** डर के कारण शांत हो जाना सहमति नहीं है।
* **इलाज में देरी न करें।**

#### Hinglish
* **Apne aap ko doshi na manein:** Darr ke mare shant ho jana (freeze hona) aapki manjoori nahi thi. Crime 100% samne wale ka hai.
* **Treatment me deri na karein:** HIV PEP aur i-Pill ka time limit strictly **72 ghante** hi hota hai.

## Diagnostic & Forensic Evaluation
* **Forensic DNA Swabbing:** Swabs taken from the vagina, cervix, or skin to extract the perpetrator's DNA profile.
* **Toxicology Screen:** Blood and urine tested if drug-facilitated sexual assault (date rape drugs like GHB or Ketamine) is suspected.
* **STI Screening:** Baseline blood and swab checks for STIs.

---

## Medical & Psychological Management
* **HIV PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis):** A 28-day antiviral regimen (e.g., Tenofovir + Lamivudine + Dolutegravir) started within 72 hours.
* **Trauma-Informed Counseling:** Intensive therapy with a clinical psychologist to manage Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) and trauma.

## Prevention of Consent Violations
* **Enthusiastic Communication:** Always check in with your partner. Ask: *"Is this okay?"* or *"Do you want to slow down?"*
* **Respect the "No":** A "No" or hesitation must be respected immediately without sulking, anger, or pressure.
* **Understand Stealthing:** Ensure condoms are never removed without explicit disclosure.

## Possible Complications of Non-Consensual Acts
* **PTSD (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder):** Severe anxiety, flashbacks, and nightmares.
* **Unwanted Pregnancy & STIs.**
* **Clinical Depression & Suicidal Ideation.**

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The victim has severe genital or physical injuries, heavy bleeding, or is in shock.**
* **The victim expresses active suicidal thoughts or attempts self-harm.**

## Government Health Programs & Laws (India)
* **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023:** Section 63 defines rape, stating that consent obtained through coercion, intoxication, or false promise of marriage is legally invalid.
* **POCSO Act 2012:** Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act states that the legal age of consent is strictly **18 years**. Any sexual activity with a minor under 18 is statutory rape, regardless of their "consent."
* **National Women Helpline (181 / 1091):** Provides 24/7 free legal, police, and medical rescue support to female victims of assault.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is sexual consent?**
   Sexual consent is a clear, voluntary, specific, and enthusiastic agreement between partners to engage in a sexual act.

2. **What does the FRIES acronym stand for?**
   It stands for: **F**reely given, **R**eversible, **I**nformed, **E**nthusiastic, and **S**pecific.

3. **Can consent be withdrawn during sex?**
   Yes. Consent is reversible. A partner has the absolute right to say *"Stop"* or *"No"* at any moment during sex, and the other partner must stop immediately.

4. **Is consent valid if the person is drunk?**
   No. Legally and medically, an intoxicated person (drunk or drugged) is incapable of giving valid consent. Sexual activity under such state is classified as assault.

5. **What is "Stealthing"?**
   Stealthing is secretly removing a condom during sex without the partner's knowledge. It is a violation of consent and is legally treated as sexual assault/rape.

6. **What is the legal age of consent in India?**
   The legal age of consent in India is strictly **18 years** under the POCSO Act. Any sexual activity with anyone under 18 is a crime, even if they agreed to it.

7. **Where can a victim of sexual assault get free help in India?**
   Victims can call the National Women Helpline at **181** or the police emergency response system at **112** for free immediate help.

8. **Why should a victim not shower before medical checkup?**
   Showering, washing, or changing clothes washes away DNA evidence like semen, saliva, or hair. Keeping this evidence intact is crucial to identify and prosecute the offender.

9. **What is PEP for HIV and when must it be started?**
   Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) is a 28-day course of antiviral drugs that must be started **within 72 hours** of assault to prevent contracting HIV.

10. **Does silence mean consent?**
    No. Silence does not mean consent. In fearful situations, the brain can freeze (tonic immobility), making the victim unable to speak or move. Consent must be verbal and enthusiastic.

11. **Is sex under a false promise of marriage legally rape in India?**
    Yes. Under Section 69 of the BNS 2023, obtaining sexual consent through deceitful means, including a false promise of marriage without intention to marry, is a punishable offense.

12. **What is the POCSO Act?**
    POCSO (Protection of Children from Sexual Offences) is a strict Indian law enacted in 2012 to protect minors (under 18) from sexual abuse, exploitation, and statutory rape.

13. **What is the difference between active and passive consent?**
    Passive consent is letting something happen out of fear or pressure. Active consent is enthusiastic, voluntary communication showing clear desire for the act.

14. **What is a date rape drug?**
    These are sedative chemicals (like Ketamine or Rohypnol) secretly slipped into drinks to make the victim confused, helpless, or unconscious to commit assault without consent.

15. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover emergency care for assault victims?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for trauma surgery, emergency gynecology checkups, IV medications, and psychological therapy.

16. **Can marital partners withdraw consent?**
    Yes. Being married does not give permanent consent. A spouse has the complete right to say no to any sexual act, and the other must respect it.

17. **What is the role of a forensic nurse?**
    A forensic nurse is trained to collect DNA swabs, document physical injuries, and preserve evidence in a sexual assault case while providing compassionate care.

18. **How does trauma therapy help after assault?**
    Psychologists use therapies like CBT and EMDR to help survivors process the trauma, reduce flashbacks, and manage anxiety or PTSD.

19. **What is the price of generic emergency pills at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Levonorgestrel (1.5mg) emergency contraceptive pills are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹15 to ₹25.

20. **Can a minor give consent under parental permission?**
    No. Parental permission cannot validate sexual activity with a minor. Under Indian law, any sexual act with a person under 18 is statutory rape.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** If she did not scream or fight back, it means she consented to the sex.
  * **Fact:** Extreme fear triggers a biological survival response called **Tonic Immobility** (freezing), where the victim is physically unable to scream or move. Silence is not consent.
* **Myth:** If you agree to go to someone's bedroom, it means you have consented to have sex.
  * **Fact:** Going to a room is only consent to enter the room. Consent for sex must be explicit, specific, and enthusiastic, and can be withdrawn at any time.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key protects you from pregnancy, so emergency pills are not needed after assault.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Emergency pills (i-Pill) must be taken within 72 hours to prevent pregnancy.
* **Myth:** Married women cannot experience sexual assault or rape by their husbands.
  * **Fact:** Consent applies to every sexual act, regardless of relationship status. Sex without consent within marriage is still a severe violation of bodily autonomy.
* **Myth:** A person can consent even if they are heavily drunk or passed out.
  * **Fact:** An intoxicated or unconscious person cannot give legal or ethical consent. Sex with a passed-out person is rape.
* **Myth:** Stealthing (secretly removing a condom) is just a normal sexual preference, not an assault.
  * **Fact:** Stealthing is a severe violation of consent that exposes the partner to pregnancy and STIs. It is legally treated as sexual assault.
* **Myth:** Only women can be victims of sexual assault or consent violations.
  * **Fact:** Anyone can experience sexual assault or consent violations, including men and non-binary individuals. Support services are available for all.
* **Myth:** You can buy PEP medicines easily at any chemist without a doctor.
  * **Fact:** PEP contains high-potency antivirals that require a doctor's prescription and liver/kidney screening to ensure safety.
* **Myth:** Statutory rape only applies if the minor was forced.
  * **Fact:** Under POCSO, any sexual contact with a minor under 18 is statutory rape, even if the minor willingly agreed.
* **Myth:** If you consented to sex once, you have permanently consented for the future.
  * **Fact:** Consent is specific and active. Agreeing to sex yesterday does not mean consenting to sex today. Always check in with your partner.
