# Pregnancy (Pregnancy/Garbhavastha) ki Pehchan, Symptoms aur Dehgrekh (Antenatal Care, Preeclampsia and Nutrition Guide)

## Overview

### English
Pregnancy (clinically termed gestation) is the physiological state of carrying a developing embryo or fetus within the female uterus. It spans approximately **40 weeks** (divided into three trimesters). Key management revolves around **Antenatal Care (ANC)** to monitor fetal development and screen for life-threatening maternal complications:
1. **Preeclampsia:** Pregnancy-induced high blood pressure with protein in the urine, which can progress to life-threatening seizures (Eclampsia).
2. **Gestational Diabetes (GDM):** High blood sugar detected during pregnancy.
3. **Ectopic Pregnancy:** Implantation outside the uterus.
*Critical prevention requires daily **Folic Acid (400 mcg)** supplementation starting pre-conception to prevent fetal brain/spinal cord defects (Neural Tube Defects - NTDs).*

### Hindi
गर्भावस्था (Pregnancy / गर्भावस्था या गर्भधारण), महिला के गर्भाशय (uterus) में एक विकासशील भ्रूण या गर्भ को धारण करने की शारीरिक स्थिति है। यह लगभग **40 सप्ताह (9 महीने)** तक चलती है। इसके प्रबंधन में **प्रसव पूर्व देखभाल (Antenatal Care - ANC)** महत्वपूर्ण है, जिसमें गंभीर जटिलताओं की जांच की जाती है: **प्री-एक्लेमप्सिया (Preeclampsia - गर्भावस्था में उच्च रक्तचाप)**, **गर्भकालीन मधुमेह (Gestational Diabetes - शुगर बढ़ना)** और **अस्थानिक गर्भावस्था (Ectopic Pregnancy - गर्भाशय के बाहर बच्चा ठहरना)**। शिशु के मस्तिष्क दोषों को रोकने के लिए दैनिक **फॉलिक एसिड (Folic Acid)** का सेवन अनिवार्य है।

Baby ki spinal cord defects rokkne ke liye **Folic Acid** tablet khana compulsory hai.

## Quick Summary
Pregnancy spans 40 weeks. Diagnostics track urine hCG, OGTT sugar levels, and fetal ultrasounds. Symptoms include missed periods, morning sickness, and preeclampsia warnings (headache, leg swelling). First aid for eclampsia seizures requires lateral positioning. Management combines ANC checkups with daily folic acid/iron.

---

## Definition
* **hCG (Human Chorionic Gonadotropin):** A hormone produced by the placenta shortly after implantation, detected in urine pregnancy test cards (UPT).
* **Neural Tube Defect (NTD):** Congenital brain and spinal cord malformations (like Spina Bifida) caused by folic acid deficiency in early pregnancy.
* **Eclampsia:** The onset of generalized tonic-clonic seizures in a woman with preeclampsia, representing an obstetric emergency.

## Antenatal Care (ANC) Checkup Schedule & Diagnostics

To ensure a safe delivery, the government mandates at least 4 ANC visits:

| ANC Visit | Recommended Timing | Essential Tests | Clinical Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st Visit** | **Within $12\text{ Weeks}$ (1st Trimester)**| Urine Pregnancy Test (UPT), Blood Group, Hb%, HIV/Syphilis screen | Confirms pregnancy, screens for anemia and maternal infections |
| **2nd Visit** | $14 - 26\text{ Weeks}$ (2nd Trimester)| **USG Obstetric Scan**, Blood Pressure, Urine Protein | Monitors fetal growth, screens for congenital anomalies and Preeclampsia |
| **3rd Visit** | $28\text{ Weeks}$ (3rd Trimester)| **OGTT (75g Oral Glucose)**, Td (Tetanus) injection | Diagnoses **Gestational Diabetes** and prevents maternal/neonatal tetanus |
| **4th Visit** | $32 - 36\text{ Weeks}$ | Fetal Heart Rate monitoring, Presentation check | Confirms baby's position (head down) for normal delivery |

## Symptoms & Danger Signs of Pregnancy

### English
* **Normal Symptoms:** Missed menstrual period; morning sickness (nausea/vomiting); frequent urination; breast tenderness and fatigue; mild swelling in ankles at term.
* **Critical Danger Signs (Emergency):** **Sudden severe headache, blurred vision**, and massive face/hand swelling (signs of high blood pressure / Preeclampsia); **any vaginal bleeding or spot** (abortion or ectopic sign); sudden decrease in baby's movement.

### Hindi
* **सामान्य लक्षण:** मासिक धर्म का बंद होना; सुबह जी मिचलाना और उल्टी होना; स्तन भारी होना।
* **खतरे के संकेत (Emergency):** **तेज सिरदर्द, धुंधला दिखना** और चेहरे पर सूजन (Preeclampsia); **योनि से खून बहना**; गर्भ में शिशु की हलचल कम होना।

### Hinglish
* **Normal Symptoms:** Periods ka miss hona; subah uthte hi ulti ya jee machlana (morning sickness); fatigue (thakan) aur bar-bar peshab aana.
* **Danger Signs (Emergency):** **Achanak tez sir dard hona, aankhon me dhundla dikhna** aur face/hands par heavy sujan aana (high BP/Preeclampsia ke signs); **vaginal bleeding (khoon aana) ya spotting**; baby ka move karna achanak kam ya band hona.

## Safety & Nutritional Protocol during Pregnancy

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Start Folic Acid (400 mcg daily) immediately** when planning pregnancy or as soon as UPT is positive, continuing through the 1st trimester. This prevents neural tube birth defects in the baby.
* **Take Iron-Folic Acid (IFA) and Calcium tablets daily** from the 14th week (2nd trimester) until delivery. *Take iron and calcium at different times (e.g., iron in morning, calcium in evening), as calcium blocks iron absorption.*
* **Sleep on your left side.** This prevents the heavy uterus from pressing on the inferior vena cava, optimizing blood flow to the placenta and baby.
* **Eat a diet rich in iron** (spinach, beetroot, pomegranate) to prevent maternal anemia (keep Hemoglobin $> 11\text{ g/dL}$).

#### Hindi
* **फॉलिक एसिड (Folic Acid) तुरंत शुरू करें:** शुरुआती ३ महीनों में इसे दैनिक लें।
* **आयरन और कैल्शियम की गोलियां अलग-अलग समय पर लें:** कैल्शियम, आयरन के अवशोषण (absorption) को रोकता है।
* **बाईं करवट (left side) लेकर सोएं।**

#### Hinglish
* **Folic Acid (400mcg daily) immediate shuru karein:** UPT positive aate hi pehle 3 mahine daily folic acid tablet khana mandatory hai taaki baby me dimaag aur spinal cord ka normal development ho.
* **Iron aur Calcium tablet ek sath na lein:** 14th week se daily Iron (IFA) aur Calcium ki tablets shuru karein. Calcium, Iron ke absorption ko body me block karta hai, isiliye **Iron subah khayein aur Calcium shaam ko**.
* **Left side karwat lekar soyein:** Left side sone se baby ko oxygen aur blood supply maximum milti hai.
* **Diet me Iron badhayein:** Palak, chukandar aur anar khayein taaki anemia (khoon ki kami) na ho.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT self-medicate.** Many common drugs (like ibuprofen, certain acne medicines, tetracyclines) are **teratogenic** (cause severe birth defects). Take only doctor-prescribed pregnancy-safe medicines.
* **Do NOT ignore a blood pressure reading of $\ge 140/90\text{ mmHg}$.**
* **Do NOT perform heavy lifting** or strenuous physical labor.
* **Do NOT consume raw papaya or pineapple**, as they contain enzymes (papain) that can trigger uterine contractions and miscarriage.

#### Hindi
* **बिना डॉक्टर की सलाह के कोई भी दवा न लें:** कई दवाएं शिशु में जन्मजात विकलांगता (teratogenic) पैदा कर सकती हैं।
* **कच्चा पपीता या अनानास न खाएं:** ये गर्भपात (abortion) करा सकते हैं।

#### Hinglish
* **Bina doctor advice koi dawa na khayein:** Pregnancy me painkiller (Ibuprofen) ya acne tablets lena strictly banned hai. Yeh dawaiyan **Teratogenic** hoti hain, yaani baby me birth defects ya apangta kar sakti hain. Sirf doctor-prescribed safe tablets hi lein.
* **Kaccha papita aur ananas na khayein:** Inme papain enzyme hota hai jo uterine contractions badha kar abortion/miscarriage kara sakta hai.
* **X-ray scan bilkul na karwayein (radiation hazard).**

## Diagnostic Evaluation of Complications
* **OGTT (Oral Glucose Tolerance Test):** Done at 24-28 weeks: measure blood sugar, drink 75g glucose, and test blood sugar again after 2 hours. Diagnostic for GDM if 2-hour sugar is $\ge 140\text{ mg/dL}$.
* **Urine Dipstick for Protein:** Checked at every ANC visit to screen for proteinuria (Preeclampsia marker).
* **Target Scan (Anomaly Scan):** Done at 18-20 weeks to check for physical structural defects in the fetus.

---

## Medical & Obstetric Management
* **Antihypertensive Therapy:** If BP is $\ge 140/90\text{ mmHg}$, prescribe pregnancy-safe BP medicines (e.g., Labetalol, Methyldopa).
* **Magnesium Sulfate ($MgSO_4$) Protocol:** The emergency drug of choice given intravenously to stop and prevent eclamptic seizures during labor.
* **Insulin Therapy:** The preferred safe drug for Gestational Diabetes if diet control fails (metformin is secondary).

## Prevention of Maternal Complications
* **Pre-Conception Planning:** Start folic acid 1 month before stopping contraception.
* **Regular Blood Pressure Monitoring:** Detect preeclampsia before seizures start.
* **Maintain Genital Hygiene:** Prevent Vaginal infections (UTI/Vaginosis) which can cause preterm labor.

---

## Possible Complications of Unmanaged Pregnancy
* **Seizures (Eclampsia) & Stroke:** Fatal for both mother and baby.
* **IUGR (Intrauterine Growth Restriction):** The baby stops growing due to poor placental blood flow.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs (Obstetric Crisis)
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The pregnant mother develops a seizure/fit (Eclampsia).** *First aid: Turn her to her left side to keep her airway open, do not put anything in her mouth, and proceed to hospital for Magnesium Sulfate.*
* **Sudden vaginal bleeding or continuous leaking of fluid.**
* **Severe, constant abdominal pain (placental abruption warning).**
* **Sudden severe headache, blurred vision, and high blood pressure.**

## Government Maternal Health Schemes (India)
* **Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY):** Provides direct cash benefits (₹5,000) to pregnant women in their bank accounts to support nutrition and compensation for wage loss.
* **Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK):** Guarantees **completely free and cashless deliveries** (both normal and C-section), free medicines, free diagnostics, free blood, and free transport from home to hospital for all pregnant women.
* **Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA):** Provides free, comprehensive diagnostic and ANC checkups by specialists on the **9th of every month** at all government hospitals.
* **Jan Aushadhi Kendras:** Provides generic iron, calcium, folic acid, and pregnancy test kits at 80% discounted rates.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **How is pregnancy confirmed?**
   Pregnancy is confirmed using a Urine Pregnancy Test (UPT) kit, which detects the hCG hormone in the first morning urine, showing a double pink line.

2. **Why is Folic Acid important in early pregnancy?**
   Folic acid is essential for the baby's early brain and spinal cord development. Deficiency causes neural tube defects (NTDs) like Spina Bifida, where the spine is exposed.

3. **What is Preeclampsia and what are its symptoms?**
   Preeclampsia is pregnancy-induced high blood pressure ($\ge 140/90\text{ mmHg}$) and protein in the urine. Symptoms include severe headaches, blurred vision, and facial swelling.

4. **Why shouldn't I take calcium and iron tablets together?**
   Calcium competes with iron for absorption in the intestines. Taking them together reduces iron absorption. Take iron in the morning and calcium in the evening.

5. **What is Gestational Diabetes (GDM)?**
   GDM is high blood sugar first diagnosed during pregnancy. It is checked using a 75g Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) at 24-28 weeks.

6. **What is Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK)?**
   JSSK is a government scheme providing completely free, cashless normal or C-section deliveries, free medicines, blood, and free transport at all government hospitals.

7. **Why is sleeping on the left side recommended for pregnant women?**
   Sleeping on the left side improves blood flow to the placenta and baby by preventing the heavy uterus from compressing the mother's main vena cava vein.

8. **What is Eclampsia?**
   Eclampsia is the occurrence of seizures (fits) in a pregnant woman with preeclampsia. It is a life-threatening emergency for both mother and baby.

9. **Can a pregnant woman undergo an X-ray scan?**
   No. X-ray radiation carries a high risk of causing congenital birth defects or childhood cancers. Use ultrasounds (USG) instead, which are completely safe.

10. **What is an Anomaly Scan (Target Scan) and when is it done?**
    It is a detailed ultrasound scan performed at 18-20 weeks to examine the fetus's physical structure and check for any birth defects.

11. **What is the price of generic Folic Acid at Jan Aushadhi Kendras?**
    Generic Folic Acid (5mg) is available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras for only ₹10 to ₹15 per strip of 10 tablets, compared to ₹50-₹80 in private stores.

12. **Why is raw papaya banned during pregnancy?**
    Raw or semi-ripe papaya contains latex and papain enzymes. These enzymes act like prostaglandins, causing uterine contractions that can trigger miscarriage.

13. **What is the treatment for gestational diabetes?**
    The first line of management is medical nutritional therapy (diet control). If blood sugar remains high, the safest drug of choice is insulin.

14. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover C-section deliveries?**
    Yes, the PM-JAY scheme covers cashless packages for normal deliveries, C-sections, high-risk pregnancy management, neonatal ICU care, and surgical complications.

15. **What is the Td vaccine schedule in pregnancy?**
    Pregnant women receive 2 doses of the Tetanus and Diphtheria (Td) vaccine. The first is given as soon as pregnancy is confirmed, and the second is given 4 weeks later.

16. **Why does anemia occur in pregnancy?**
    During pregnancy, blood volume increases by 50%, requiring extra iron to make hemoglobin. If dietary iron and supplements are low, severe anemia develops.

17. **What is a biochemical screening test (Double Marker)?**
    It is a maternal blood test done at 11-13 weeks, combined with an NT scan, to calculate the risk of chromosomal abnormalities like Down Syndrome.

18. **Why does gestational hypertension require safe drugs?**
    Standard BP drugs like Enalapril or Losartan can cause fetal kidney failure. Safe drugs like Labetalol or Methyldopa are used instead.

19. **What is the cash benefit under the PMMVY scheme?**
    The PMMVY scheme provides ₹5,000 cash in three installments to pregnant women for their first child to support nutrition.

20. **Is generic Iron as effective as branded iron capsules?**
    Yes. Generic Iron Folic Acid (IFA) distributed by government centers contains the exact same active iron salts and is highly effective in treating anemia.

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Pregnant women must eat for two people to ensure the baby grows.
  * **Fact:** Eating for two leads to gestational diabetes and obesity. A pregnant woman only needs about 300-350 extra calories per day in the second and third trimesters.
* **Myth:** Drinking coconut water or milk makes the baby's skin color fair.
  * **Fact:** Skin color is determined by genetics. Coconut water is excellent for hydration but has zero effect on the baby's skin color.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key prevents preeclampsia or high blood pressure in pregnancy.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. High blood pressure requires medical screening, diet adjustments, and safe BP medicines.
* **Myth:** A pregnant woman should avoid taking iron and calcium pills because they make the baby's skin dark or cause delivery complications.
  * **Fact:** This is a dangerous myth. Iron and calcium supplements prevent maternal anemia, postpartum hemorrhage, and weak bones in the baby.
* **Myth:** An ultrasound scan is harmful and can deform the baby's limbs.
  * **Fact:** Ultrasounds use high-frequency sound waves, not radiation. They are completely safe and do not cause any harm to the baby.
* **Myth:** Sleeping on the back is the best position during the third trimester.
  * **Fact:** Sleeping on your back compresses maternal blood vessels, reducing blood flow to the baby. Sleep on your left side.
* **Myth:** You can determine the baby's gender by the shape of the pregnant belly.
  * **Fact:** Belly shape is determined by the mother's abdominal muscle tone and baby's position, not gender. Gender prediction is illegal in India.
* **Myth:** Branded imported prenatal vitamins are better than government-provided IFA tablets.
  * **Fact:** Government-distributed IFA tablets meet national pharmacopoeia standards and are highly effective in preventing maternal anemia.
* **Myth:** Papaya and pineapple must be avoided even in their fully ripe forms.
  * **Fact:** Fully ripe papaya is safe in moderation. However, raw or semi-ripe papaya must be avoided due to high latex levels.
* **Myth:** Normal delivery is impossible if the mother had a C-section in her first pregnancy.
  * **Fact:** Under strict medical supervision, many women can successfully have a Vaginal Birth After Cesarean (VBAC) if the uterine scar is stable.
