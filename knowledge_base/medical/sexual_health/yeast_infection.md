# Vaginal Yeast Infection (Yeast Infection/Yeast Infection) ki Pehchan aur Upachar (Candidiasis, Clotrimazole and Antifungals Guide)

## Overview

### English
A Vaginal Yeast Infection, clinically termed **Vulvovaginal Candidiasis (VVC)** (popularly known as vaginal thrush), is a fungal infection caused by the opportunistic overgrowth of yeast, primarily **Candida albicans**. Candida is a normal part of the vaginal microflora, but it multiplies rapidly when the vaginal ecosystem is disrupted (e.g., due to high estrogen levels, uncontrolled diabetes, or antibiotic use which kills protective Lactobacillus). Symptoms feature intense vaginal/vulvar itching, burning, and a signature **thick, white, odorless, cottage-cheese-like discharge**. Treatment utilizes local azole antifungals (Clotrimazole) or oral Fluconazole.

## Definition
* **Vulvovaginal Candidiasis (VVC):** A clinical fungal infection of the vagina and vulva causing inflammation, intense itching, and vaginal discharge.
* **Oppotunistic Pathogen:** A microbe (like Candida) that normally lives harmlessly in the body but causes disease when the host's immune system or natural flora is weakened.
* **KOH Mount:** A diagnostic test where a smear of vaginal fluid is mixed with Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) on a glass slide to dissolve human cells, leaving fungal structures visible under a microscope.

## Etiology: Triggers of Fungal Overgrowth

Understanding what causes yeast to multiply helps prevent recurrent infections:

| Triggers of Overgrowth | Pathological Mechanism | Preventive Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Broad-Spectrum Antibiotics**| Kills good **Lactobacillus bacteria**, removing the natural acidic protection | Avoid antibiotic abuse for normal colds |
| **Uncontrolled Diabetes** | High glucose in vaginal secretions feeds the yeast, accelerating growth | **Keep blood sugar controlled (HbA1c $< 7\%$)** |
| **High Estrogen Levels** | Estrogen increases vaginal glycogen (food for yeast), common in pregnancy | Maintain strict hygiene during pregnancy |
| **Douching & Soap Abuse** | Alters vaginal pH and washes away protective Lactobacillus | **Wash with plain water only** (vulva area) |
| **Immunosuppression** | Weak immune system cannot control yeast levels (HIV or steroid users) | Medical management of underlying immunity |

## Symptoms of Vaginal Yeast Infection

### English
* **Primary:** Intense, persistent itching and burning sensation in the vagina and vulva; thick, white, clumpy, odorless vaginal discharge (looks like cottage cheese/curds).
* **Secondary:** Redness, swelling, and soreness of the vulva skin; pain during urination (burning as urine hits raw skin); pain during sexual intercourse (dyspareunia).

## Safe Hygiene & Antifungal Application Protocol (First Aid)

If experiencing a vaginal yeast infection, execute this rescue protocol:

### Do's (What to do)

#### English
* **Apply topical Antifungals correctly.** Standard treatment includes inserting a **Clotrimazole Vaginal Suppository (100mg/200mg/500mg)** deep into the vaginal canal at bedtime using the provided applicator. The patient must lie down immediately after insertion to prevent the tablet from sliding out.
* **Take a single dose of Fluconazole (150mg)** orally if prescribed by a doctor for rapid relief. (Note: oral Fluconazole is contraindicated in pregnancy; pregnant women must use local Clotrimazole creams/tablets only).
* **Maintain complete dryness.** Gently dry the genital area with a clean cotton towel after washing, and wear loose cotton underwear.
* **Complete the full course.** If a 6-day suppository course is prescribed, finish all 6 days even if the itching disappears on Day 2.

* **एंटीफंगल दवा (Clotrimazole) का सही उपयोग करें:** रात को सोते समय वेजाइनल सपोजिटरी (गोली) को योनि के भीतर गहराई से डालें।
* **गर्भवती महिलाएं केवल स्थानीय क्रीम (Clotrimazole) का ही उपयोग करें:** गर्भावस्था में ओरल Fluconazole वर्जित है।
* **त्वचा को हमेशा सूखा (dry) रखें।**
* **दवा का कोर्स पूरा करें।**

#### Hinglish

* **Vaginal area dry rakhein:** Wash ke baad tissue ya soft cotton kapde se pat-dry karein aur loose cotton underwear pehnein.

### Don'ts (What NOT to do)

#### English
* **Do NOT scratch the area.** Scratching the raw vulva skin causes micro-tears, leading to secondary bacterial infections and severe swelling (edema).
* **Do NOT perform Vaginal Douching.** Never wash inside the vagina with Dettol, Betadine, vinegar, or soaps. Douching worsens yeast infections by wiping out natural flora.
* **Do NOT wear tight jeans, synthetic leggings, or damp underwear.** They trap sweat and moisture, creating a perfect climate for yeast growth.
* **Do NOT have sexual intercourse during the active infection.**

* **संभोग से परहेज करें।**

## Diagnostic Evaluation
* **KOH Mount Slide Test:** Vaginal discharge is placed on a slide with $10\%$ Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) and examined under a microscope. The presence of budding yeast and **pseudohyphae** (long branching fungal cells) confirms Candidiasis.
* **Vaginal pH Testing:** A vaginal pH strip is checked: in pure yeast infections, the pH remains **acidic ($< 4.5$)**, helping rule out Bacterial Vaginosis (where pH is $> 4.5$).
* **Fasting Blood Sugar & HbA1c:** Checked if the patient has recurrent yeast infections ($> 4$ episodes a year) to rule out undiagnosed Diabetes.

## Medical & Preventive Management
* **Antifungal Suppositories:** Clotrimazole vaginal inserts (e.g., 100mg for 6 days, or 500mg single dose).
* **Oral Antifungal Therapy:** Fluconazole 150mg (single oral dose) for uncomplicated infections.
* **Diabetes Management:** Strict insulin/oral drug management to lower glucose levels feeding the yeast.

## Prevention of Recurrent Yeast Infections
* **Limit Antibiotic Use:** Take antibiotics only when absolutely necessary and prescribed by a doctor.
* **Maintain Blood Sugar Control:** Keep diabetes in check.
* **Avoid Scented Sanitary Products:** Scented tampons/pads irritate the vaginal mucosa.
* **Wipe Front to Back.**

---

## Possible Complications of Untreated Yeast Infections
* **Severe Vulvar Fissures:** Deep, painful cracks in the skin due to chronic scratching.
* **Secondary Bacterial Infection:** Cellulitis or painful abscesses in the vulva.

---

## Emergency Warning Signs
Go to the nearest emergency department immediately or call 108 if you observe:
* **The patient has severe vaginal swelling, deep bleeding cracks (fissures), and high fever.**
* **The yeast infection does not improve after multiple courses of antifungals (requires screening for immune diseases like HIV or severe uncontrolled diabetes).**

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a vaginal yeast infection?**
   A vaginal yeast infection (vaginal candidiasis) is a fungal infection caused by an overgrowth of Candida yeast in the vagina, causing intense itching, burning, and discharge.

2. **What does a yeast infection discharge look like?**
   The signature discharge is thick, white, and clumpy, resembling cottage cheese or curd, and has no odor.

3. **Why do antibiotics cause yeast infections?**
   Antibiotics kill the good Lactobacillus bacteria that keep the vaginal environment acidic. Without this acidic protection, the yeast grows rapidly, causing an infection.

4. **How does Clotrimazole suppository insert work?**
   Clotrimazole vaginal tablets are inserted deep into the vagina at bedtime. The tablet dissolves overnight, releasing antifungal medicine directly to kill the yeast.

5. **Why is oral Fluconazole dangerous in pregnancy?**
   Oral Fluconazole can cross the placenta and is associated with a risk of birth defects and miscarriage. Pregnant women must only use safe local Clotrimazole creams or inserts.

6. **Can a yeast infection be treated with normal antibiotics?**
   No. Yeast is a fungus, not a bacteria. It must be treated with antifungal medicines like Clotrimazole or Fluconazole, not antibiotics.

7. **Where can I get cheap generic yeast infection medicines in India?**
   Generic Clotrimazole vaginal suppositories and Fluconazole tablets are available at PM Jan Aushadhi Kendras at a very low cost.

8. **How does uncontrolled diabetes trigger yeast infections?**
   High blood sugar raises glucose levels in vaginal fluids. Fungal yeast feeds on this glucose, causing rapid overgrowth and recurrent infections.

9. **What is a KOH mount test?**
   It is a lab test where vaginal discharge is mixed with potassium hydroxide (KOH) on a slide. The chemical dissolves human cells, allowing the doctor to see yeast spores and branches under a microscope.

10. **Why should I avoid tight jeans during a yeast infection?**
    Tight clothing traps heat and moisture in the groin area. Fungi grow best in warm, wet conditions. Loose cotton clothing keeps the area dry.

11. **Can a man get a yeast infection from a woman?**
    Yes. Although not a classic STI, a man can get redness, itching, and swelling on the head of the penis (balanitis) after sex with an infected partner.

12. **What is the difference between Yeast Infection and Bacterial Vaginosis (BV)?**
    Yeast infection causes severe itching and a thick, white, odorless clumpy discharge with acidic pH. BV causes a thin, watery grey discharge with a strong fishy odor and alkaline pH.

14. **Why should I not scratch the itchy area?**
    Scratching the sensitive vulvar skin breaks the skin, causing painful cracks (fissures) and allowing bacteria to enter, leading to a secondary bacterial infection.

15. **Does the Ayushman Bharat card cover vaginal abscess drainage?**

16. **Is vaginal douching good for curing yeast infections?**
    No. Douching (washing inside the vagina) is highly dangerous. It wipes out protective bacteria and makes the yeast infection more severe. Wash only the outside with plain water.

17. **How does lactic acid gel help?**
    Lactic acid gels help temporarily restore the vagina's normal acidic pH, supporting the growth of good Lactobacillus bacteria.

19. **What are pseudohyphae?**
    Pseudohyphae are long, branching chains of yeast cells visible under a microscope in active Candida infections.

20. **Is generic Clotrimazole cream safe for external vulvar itching?**

## Common Myths vs Facts

* **Myth:** Yeast infections are always sexually transmitted and a sign of cheating.
  * **Fact:** Yeast infections are caused by an imbalance of the body's natural flora. They can happen to young girls and virgins, and are not classic STIs.
* **Myth:** Putting yogurt (dahi) inside the vagina cures yeast infections safely.
  * **Fact:** While yogurt contains Lactobacillus, commercial yogurt also contains sugars and other bacteria. Inserting it can worsen the infection and cause chemical irritation. Use sterile Clotrimazole tablets.
* **Myth:** Sucking on a key cures vaginal yeast infections instantly.
  * **Fact:** Sucking keys is a useless superstition. Fungal infections require active antifungal medications.
* **Myth:** Douching with Dettol or bleach cures yeast infections.
  * **Fact:** Dettol and bleach cause severe chemical burns and destroy the vaginal lining. They are highly dangerous. Wash only with plain water.
* **Myth:** Senda namak (rock salt) water washes balance vaginal pH.
  * **Fact:** Rock salt has no chemical effect on yeast and can severely dehydrate and irritate the vaginal mucosa.
* **Myth:** You must take a 7-day antibiotic course to cure a yeast infection.
  * **Fact:** Antibiotics do not kill fungi. Taking them will actually cause or worsen a yeast infection. You must take antifungals.
* **Myth:** Yeast infections are only a female issue; men can never get them.
  * **Fact:** Men can get yeast infections on the penis glans (Candidal Balanitis), causing redness, itching, and white discharge under the foreskin.
* **Myth:** Generic Fluconazole tablets from Jan Aushadhi clinics are weak.
  * **Fact:** Generic Fluconazole contains the exact same active antifungal compound and cures infections just as effectively, at an 80% lower cost.
* **Myth:** A yeast infection will always go away on its own without treatment.
  * **Fact:** While mild infections can sometimes resolve, moderate to severe infections require antifungal treatment to prevent painful skin cracks and secondary infections.
* **Myth:** Using scented body spray on the genitals cures the yeast odor.
  * **Fact:** Body sprays contain alcohol and fragrances that cause severe chemical burns and allergic reactions on sensitive genital skin.
