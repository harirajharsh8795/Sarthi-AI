# Immigration and Citizenship Law in India

## Overview (English)
Immigration in India is governed primarily by laws regulating the entry, stay, and exit of foreign nationals, as well as the acquisition, termination, and regulation of Indian citizenship. The legal framework provides guidelines for Visas, Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) status, deportation of illegal immigrants, and naturalization. Understanding these laws is essential for foreign nationals visiting or residing in India, and for persons of Indian origin navigating their rights and obligations.

## Legal Background
### History & Purpose
India's immigration and citizenship framework was largely developed post-independence to distinguish citizens from aliens, manage refugees post-partition, and control the entry and stay of foreign nationals. Over the years, the laws have been amended to introduce concepts like Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) and update naturalization rules.

### Applicable Acts
1. **The Citizenship Act, 1955**: Governs the acquisition and determination of Indian citizenship.
2. **The Foreigners Act, 1946**: Empowers the government to regulate the entry, presence, and departure of foreigners.
3. **The Passports Act, 1967**: Regulates the issuance of passports and travel documents to Indian citizens.
4. **Registration of Foreigners Act, 1939**: Mandates the registration of foreigners visiting or residing in India.
5. **The Passport (Entry into India) Act, 1920**: Requires persons entering India to be in possession of valid passports.

### Relevant Constitutional Articles
- **Article 5 to 11**: Deals with Citizenship at the commencement of the Constitution and empowers Parliament to regulate the right of citizenship by law.
- **Article 21**: Right to life and personal liberty applies to non-citizens (foreigners) as well.
- **Article 14**: Right to equality before the law, which is available to both citizens and foreigners within Indian territory.

### Relevant Sections
- **Section 3 of the Foreigners Act, 1946**: Power to make orders to prohibit, regulate or restrict entry of foreigners.
- **Section 14 of the Foreigners Act, 1946**: Penalty for contravention of the Act, such as overstaying a visa.
- **Section 3, 4, 5, 6 of the Citizenship Act, 1955**: Acquisition of citizenship by birth, descent, registration, and naturalization.
- **Section 7A of the Citizenship Act, 1955**: Registration of Overseas Citizens of India.
- **Section 8, 9, 10 of the Citizenship Act, 1955**: Renunciation, termination, and deprivation of citizenship (India does not allow dual citizenship).

## Applicability
- **Covered**: All foreign nationals intending to visit, reside, work, or study in India; Indian citizens holding passports; Persons of Indian Origin seeking OCI status.
- **Exempt**: Citizens of Nepal and Bhutan do not require visas to enter India (with some exceptions like entering from China).
- **Special Conditions**: OCI cardholders have lifelong visas and are exempt from foreigner registration (FRRO), but they cannot vote, buy agricultural land, or hold certain constitutional offices.

## Rights of Citizens (English)
Indian citizens have fundamental rights, including the right to vote, contest in elections, hold public office, and possess an Indian passport. Foreigners have the right to life and personal liberty (Article 21) and equality before the law (Article 14) but do not have rights like freedom of movement and residence (Article 19) in the same way citizens do.

## Duties & Responsibilities (English)
Foreigners must possess valid travel documents, register with FRRO if their visa requires it (usually for stays exceeding 180 days), obey local laws, and leave before their visa expires. OCI cardholders must carry their OCI card and old/new passport while traveling. Indian citizens have a duty to not acquire foreign citizenship if they wish to remain Indian citizens, as India strictly prohibits dual citizenship.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure (English)
1. **Visa Application**: A foreign national applies for an Indian e-Visa or regular Visa at an Indian Mission abroad.
2. **Entry into India**: The foreigner presents their passport and valid visa at the immigration checkpoint in India.
3. **FRRO Registration**: For Long Term Visas (Employment, Student, Medical), the foreigner must register online at the e-FRRO portal within 14 days of arrival if the stay exceeds 180 days.
4. **Visa Extension/Conversion**: If required, apply for visa extension or conversion through the e-FRRO portal well before the expiry date.
5. **Exit**: The foreigner must leave India before the visa expires. In case of FRRO registration, an exit permit may be required depending on the visa category.

## Required Documents
- **For Visa**: Valid Passport (at least 6 months validity), passport size photos, proof of address, financial proof, specific documents based on visa type (e.g., Employment contract for Employment Visa, Admission letter for Student Visa).
- **For OCI**: Proof of Indian origin (parents'/grandparents' Indian passport or birth certificate), foreign passport, naturalization certificate.
- **For FRRO Registration**: Passport, Visa, Form C (issued by hotel/landlord), photo, proof of purpose (letter from employer/university).

## Government Authorities
- **Bureau of Immigration (BoI)**: Manages immigration checkpoints and FRRO services.
- **Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO)**: Handles visa extensions, registration, and residence permits for foreigners in India.
- **Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA)**: The nodal ministry governing immigration, citizenship, and OCI matters.
- **Indian Missions Abroad (Embassies/High Commissions)**: Issue visas and handle consular services.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Overstaying Visa**: Under Section 14 of the Foreigners Act, 1946, overstaying can result in imprisonment up to 5 years and a fine. Practically, it leads to heavy financial penalties (calculated based on the duration of overstay) and potential blacklisting or deportation.
- **Illegal Entry/Forged Documents**: Entering without a valid passport/visa or using forged documents is punishable with imprisonment under the Passport (Entry into India) Act and IPC (or equivalent BNS sections for forgery).
- **Failure to Register with FRRO**: Can lead to fines, refusal of visa extension, or deportation.

## Exceptions
- **OCI Cardholders**: Do not need a visa to visit India and are exempt from FRRO registration regardless of the length of their stay.
- **Nepal and Bhutan Citizens**: Generally do not need a passport or visa to enter India, provided they enter from their respective countries or have valid identity documents.
- **Diplomats**: Subject to different rules governed by the Vienna Convention and hold Diplomatic Passports/Visas.

## Important Timelines
- **FRRO Registration**: Must be done within 14 days of arrival in India for applicable long-term visas.
- **Visa Extension**: Application should ideally be submitted at least 60 days before the current visa expires.
- **Surrender of Indian Passport**: Must be done within 3 years of acquiring foreign citizenship to avoid heavy penalties.
- **OCI Application**: Can take 1 to 3 months to process.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Overstaying even by a single day thinking it won't matter (leads to fines and exit delays).
- Working in India on a Tourist Visa (strictly prohibited and leads to deportation and blacklisting).
- Failing to inform FRRO about a change of address or change of passport while residing in India.
- Indian citizens acquiring foreign citizenship and continuing to travel on their Indian passport.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can I get dual citizenship in India?**
   No, the Indian Constitution strictly prohibits dual citizenship. If you acquire another country's citizenship, your Indian citizenship automatically terminates. (भारत में दोहरी नागरिकता की अनुमति नहीं है। / India mein dual citizenship allowed nahi hai.)

2. **What is an OCI card?**
   Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) is a lifelong visa status for Persons of Indian Origin. It is not dual citizenship. (OCI भारतीय मूल के लोगों के लिए आजीवन वीजा है। / OCI Indian origin ke logo ke liye life-long visa hai.)

3. **Can an OCI cardholder buy agricultural land in India?**
   No, OCI cardholders cannot purchase agricultural land, plantation property, or farmhouses in India. (नहीं, OCI कार्डधारक कृषि भूमि नहीं खरीद सकते। / Nahi, OCI holders kheti ki zameen nahi kharid sakte.)

4. **What happens if my Indian visa expires while I am in India?**
   Overstaying is a legal offense. You will face heavy fines, potential deportation, and may be blacklisted from entering India again. (वीजा समाप्त होने के बाद रुकना अपराध है और जुर्माना लग सकता है। / Visa expire hone ke baad overstay karne par fine aur deportation ho sakta hai.)

5. **How do I register with the FRRO?**
   FRRO registration is completely online via the e-FRRO portal (indianfrro.gov.in). No physical visit is required unless called for an interview. (FRRO पंजीकरण पूरी तरह से ऑनलाइन होता है। / FRRO registration e-FRRO portal par online kiya jata hai.)

6. **Do I need to register with FRRO if I am on an e-Tourist visa?**
   Generally, e-Tourist visas allow a stay of up to 90 or 180 days (depending on nationality) per visit, and FRRO registration is not required or permitted on tourist visas. (ई-पर्यटक वीजा पर FRRO पंजीकरण की आवश्यकता नहीं है। / e-Tourist visa par FRRO registration zaroori nahi hota.)

7. **Can I change my visa type while in India?**
   Generally, no. Visas are non-convertible. You usually have to leave India, apply for a new visa type in your home country, and re-enter. There are very few exceptions (like converting to a medical or spouse visa under specific rules). (आमतौर पर भारत में रहते हुए वीजा का प्रकार नहीं बदला जा सकता। / India mein rehte hue visa type change karna allow nahi hai.)

8. **What is Form C?**
   Form C is a mandatory document that must be filed by the host/hotel/landlord within 24 hours of a foreigner's arrival at their premises. (फॉर्म सी मकान मालिक द्वारा विदेशी के आगमन पर भरा जाता है। / Form C landlord ko foreigner ke aane ke 24 hours mein police ko dena hota hai.)

9. **Can a foreigner work in India on a Business Visa?**
   No. A Business Visa is for attending meetings, setting up a business, or technical meetings. Regular employment requires an Employment (E) Visa. (बिजनेस वीजा पर नौकरी करना मना है। / Business Visa par salary wali job karna illegal hai.)

10. **I just got US citizenship. Can I travel to India on my Indian passport?**
    No. Traveling on an Indian passport after acquiring foreign citizenship is a punishable offense. You must surrender the Indian passport and apply for a visa or OCI. (विदेशी नागरिकता लेने के बाद भारतीय पासपोर्ट पर यात्रा करना अपराध है। / Foreign citizenship lene ke baad Indian passport use karna illegal hai.)

11. **How long does it take to get an OCI card?**
    It typically takes 1 to 3 months, depending on document verification and the specific Indian Mission. (OCI कार्ड बनने में 1 से 3 महीने लग सकते हैं। / OCI card banne mein 1 se 3 months lagte hain.)

12. **Are there any restrictions on where a foreigner can travel in India?**
    Yes. Foreigners need a Protected Area Permit (PAP) or Restricted Area Permit (RAP) to visit certain areas like parts of North East India, J&K, and Andaman Islands. (कुछ क्षेत्रों में जाने के लिए विदेशियों को विशेष परमिट चाहिए। / Kuch restricted areas (jaise North East) jane ke liye special permit lagta hai.)

13. **Can a foreigner open a bank account in India?**
    Yes, foreigners holding a valid Long Term Visa and FRRO registration can open a bank account. Tourist visa holders have limited options (like NRO accounts for specific purposes). (लंबी अवधि के वीजा वाले विदेशी बैंक खाता खोल सकते हैं। / Long term visa wale foreigners India mein bank account khol sakte hain.)

14. **What is an Exit Permit?**
    If a foreigner loses their passport, overstays, or has a child born in India, they need an Exit Permit from the FRRO to leave the country legally. (निकास परमिट देश छोड़ने के लिए आवश्यक है यदि आपका वीजा समाप्त हो गया है। / Agar visa overstay ho gaya ho, toh India se bahar jane ke liye Exit Permit lena padta hai.)

15. **Can an OCI card be cancelled?**
    Yes, the Government of India can cancel an OCI card for reasons like fraud, showing disaffection to the Constitution, or national security. (हां, सरकार OCI कार्ड रद्द कर सकती है। / Haan, government fraud ya security reasons se OCI card cancel kar sakti hai.)

16. **How can a foreigner acquire Indian citizenship?**
    Foreigners can apply for Indian citizenship through naturalization (requires living in India for 12 years) or registration (e.g., marrying an Indian citizen and living in India for 7 years). (विदेशी प्राकृतिककरण या पंजीकरण द्वारा नागरिकता ले सकते हैं। / Foreigners naturalization ya registration (jaise Indian se shadi) ke through citizenship apply kar sakte hain.)

17. **What is the penalty for not surrendering the Indian passport?**
    Failing to surrender the Indian passport within 3 years of taking foreign citizenship attracts a penalty of up to Rs. 50,000 or more, plus penalties for travel on that passport. (पासपोर्ट सरेंडर न करने पर भारी जुर्माना लगता है। / Indian passport surrender nahi karne par bhaari fine lagta hai.)

18. **Can a foreign student work in India?**
    No. The Student Visa does not permit employment in India. Taking up a job while on a student visa is a violation. (छात्र वीजा पर काम करने की अनुमति नहीं है। / Student Visa par job karna allowed nahi hai.)

19. **What if a child is born in India to foreign parents?**
    The child does not automatically get Indian citizenship. The parents must register the birth with their embassy, get a passport for the child, and apply for an Indian visa/exit permit. (विदेशी माता-पिता के भारत में जन्मे बच्चे को स्वतः नागरिकता नहीं मिलती। / Foreign parents ke bachhe ko India mein paida hone se automatically citizenship nahi milti.)

20. **Can I use an agent for FRRO services?**
    The e-FRRO portal is highly user-friendly and designed to be used directly by the applicant. It is best to apply directly to avoid fraud. (ई-FRRO के लिए एजेंट की आवश्यकता नहीं है। / e-FRRO services ke liye directly online apply karein, agent ki zaroorat nahi hai.)

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth**: OCI means Dual Citizenship.
   **Fact**: India does not allow dual citizenship. OCI is just a lifelong visa and residency status, not actual citizenship.
2. **Myth**: A foreigner marrying an Indian gets Indian citizenship immediately.
   **Fact**: No, they must live in India for at least 7 years on an appropriate visa/OCI before applying for citizenship by registration.
3. **Myth**: Overstaying by a few days is ignored.
   **Fact**: Even a one-day overstay is heavily penalized and requires an exit permit.
4. **Myth**: Foreigners can travel anywhere in India with a valid visa.
   **Fact**: Certain regions require special PAP/RAP permits.
5. **Myth**: I can work remotely for a foreign company on a Tourist Visa in India.
   **Fact**: Technically, Tourist Visas are strictly for recreation, sightseeing, and casual visits. Working, even remotely, falls into a grey area but is generally not permitted if it constitutes residing and earning in India.
6. **Myth**: OCI cardholders can vote in Indian elections.
   **Fact**: OCI cardholders do not have voting rights in India.
7. **Myth**: E-Visa can be extended while in India.
   **Fact**: E-Visas are strictly non-extendable and non-convertible.
8. **Myth**: Surrendering the Indian passport happens automatically when you get a foreign one.
   **Fact**: You must manually apply for a Surrender Certificate at the Indian Consulate.
9. **Myth**: If my passport expires, my Indian Visa also expires.
   **Fact**: Your visa remains valid. You can travel by carrying both your new valid passport and the old passport containing the valid visa.
10. **Myth**: Only foreigners need to worry about FRRO.
    **Fact**: Indian landlords/hosts must also file Form C for foreign guests, failing which the landlord faces legal action.
