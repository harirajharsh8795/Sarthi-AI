# Live-in Relationships in India: Legal Status, Rights, and Procedures

## Overview
**English:** A live-in relationship involves two consenting adults living together as a couple without being legally married. In India, there is no specific national legislation governing live-in relationships overall, but the Supreme Court has granted it legal recognition under the Right to Life (Article 21). Partners (especially women and children) are afforded protection under acts like the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act (PWDVA) and Section 125 of the CrPC (now BNSS Sec 144). Recently, states like Uttarakhand have mandated the registration of live-in relationships through the Uniform Civil Code.

**Hindi (Unicode):** लिव-इन रिलेशनशिप (Live-in Relationship) में दो बालिग व्यक्ति अपनी मर्जी से बिना कानूनी शादी किए एक कपल की तरह साथ रहते हैं। भारत में इसके लिए कोई एक राष्ट्रीय कानून नहीं है, लेकिन सुप्रीम कोर्ट ने इसे जीवन के अधिकार (Article 21) के तहत कानूनी मान्यता दी है। इसमें महिलाओं और बच्चों को घरेलू हिंसा से महिला संरक्षण अधिनियम और CrPC की धारा 125 (अब BNSS की धारा 144) के तहत सुरक्षा और भरण-पोषण का अधिकार मिलता है। उत्तराखंड जैसे कुछ राज्यों ने अब इसे पंजीकृत (register) करना अनिवार्य कर दिया है।

## Definition
**English:** A living arrangement in which an unmarried couple lives together in a long-term relationship that resembles a marriage ("nature of marriage"). The Supreme Court defines it under the criteria of shared household, pooling of resources, and public representation as a couple.
**Hindi (Unicode):** यह एक ऐसी व्यवस्था है जिसमें एक अविवाहित जोड़ा लंबे समय तक शादी जैसी प्रकृति (nature of marriage) वाले रिश्ते में साथ रहता है। सुप्रीम कोर्ट इसे एक साझा घर, संसाधनों को साझा करने और समाज में एक जोड़े के रूप में रहने के आधार पर परिभाषित करता है।
**Hinglish:** Ek aisi living arrangement jisme unmarried couple lambe time tak ek sath "marriage-like" relationship mein rehte hain. Supreme Court ise shared household aur public mein couple ki tarah behave karne ke basis par define karta hai.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Understanding the legalities of a live-in relationship is crucial because it ensures that women are not left destitute, children are not branded illegitimate, and both partners are aware of their legal liabilities, especially regarding property, maintenance, and domestic violence.
**Hindi (Unicode):** इसके कानूनी पहलुओं को समझना इसलिए जरूरी है ताकि महिलाओं को बेसहारा न छोड़ा जाए, बच्चों को अवैध न माना जाए, और दोनों भागीदारों को संपत्ति, भरण-पोषण और घरेलू हिंसा से जुड़े उनके अधिकारों और जिम्मेदारियों का पता हो।
**Hinglish:** Iske legal rules samajhna zaroori hai taaki women ko destitute na chhoda jaye, bachchon ko illegitimate na samjha jaye, aur dono partners ko apne property, maintenance aur domestic violence se jude rights aur liabilities pata hon.

## Legal Background
**History:** Historically considered taboo, the legal stance shifted with landmark judgments (e.g., Badri Prasad vs. Dy. Director of Consolidation 1978, Indra Sarma vs. VKV Sarma 2013) where the Supreme Court recognized long-term cohabitation as a presumption of marriage.
**Purpose:** To protect vulnerable partners (usually women) from exploitation and to safeguard the future of children born out of such relationships.
**Applicable Acts:** 
- Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (Section 2(f) - 'relationship in the nature of marriage')
- Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023 (Section 144, replacing CrPC 125 for maintenance)
- Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 (Section 16 for legitimacy of children)
- Uttarakhand Uniform Civil Code, 2024 (Mandatory registration of live-in relationships)
**Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 21 (Protection of Life and Personal Liberty).

## Applicability
**Who is covered:** Adult heterosexual and homosexual couples (though specific maintenance and domestic violence laws currently explicitly cover heterosexual female partners). Both must be of legal age of majority.
**Who is exempt/Invalid conditions:** If one partner is already married and the other knows (adulterous live-in). Minors cannot legally be in a live-in relationship. If consent is obtained by fraud.
**Special conditions:** To claim rights under PWDVA, the relationship must be in the "nature of marriage" (shared age, cohabitation for a significant period, pooling of finances, sexual relationship, intention to have children).

3. **Right to Shelter:** The right to reside in a shared household.
4. **Rights of Children:** Children are considered legitimate and have rights to self-acquired and ancestral property of parents.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
### For Registration (Under specific State Laws like Uttarakhand UCC)
**English:**
1. **Prepare Documents:** Gather Aadhaar, age proof, and address proof for both partners.
2. **Submission:** Submit a joint statement/application to the Registrar of the local jurisdiction.
3. **Verification:** The Registrar conducts a summary inquiry to verify age, consent, and marital status.
4. **Issuance:** A registration certificate is issued within 30 days.
5. **Termination:** If separating, a formal statement of termination must be submitted.

**Hindi (Unicode):**
1. **दस्तावेज़ तैयार करें:** दोनों के लिए आधार, आयु प्रमाण और पते का प्रमाण।
2. **आवेदन:** स्थानीय रजिस्ट्रार के पास संयुक्त आवेदन जमा करें।
3. **सत्यापन:** रजिस्ट्रार उम्र, सहमति और वैवाहिक स्थिति को सत्यापित करेगा।
4. **सर्टिफिकेट:** 30 दिनों के भीतर रजिस्ट्रेशन सर्टिफिकेट जारी किया जाएगा।
5. **समाप्ति:** अलग होने पर, समाप्ति (termination) का औपचारिक बयान जमा करना होगा।

**Hinglish:**

4. **Certificate Issue:** 30 days mein certificate issue ho jayega.
5. **Termination:** Alag hone par termination statement deni padti hai.

### For Claiming Maintenance/Protection
1. File a complaint with the Protection Officer or nearest Police Station.
2. Approach the Magistrate's court under PWDVA or BNSS Sec 144.
3. Submit evidence of the "nature of marriage" (joint accounts, rent agreements, etc.).
4. The court assesses the evidence and orders maintenance or protection.

## Required Documents
- Proof of Identity (Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Passport)
- Proof of Age (Birth Certificate, 10th Marksheet - Both must be 18+)
- Proof of Shared Household (Joint rent agreement, utility bills in both names)
- Joint bank account statements (to prove pooling of resources, if required for court cases)
- Photographs showing public representation as a couple.

## Government Authorities
- **Registrar of Live-in Relationships:** (In states where mandated by law)
- **Protection Officers:** Appointed under the Domestic Violence Act.
- **Family Courts / Judicial Magistrates:** For handling maintenance, child custody, and domestic violence claims.
- **Police / Women's Cell:** For filing immediate complaints of abuse.
- **National/State Legal Services Authority (NALSA/SLSA):** For free legal aid.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Failure to Register (Where mandated, e.g., Uttarakhand):** Imprisonment up to 3-6 months and/or fine up to ₹25,000.
- **Domestic Violence:** Punishable under Section 31 of PWDVA (breach of protection order) with imprisonment up to 1 year or a fine up to ₹20,000, or both. Plus penalties under BNS for cruelty/assault.
- **False Information to Registrar:** Imprisonment up to 3 months or fine up to ₹25,000, or both.

## Exceptions
- **Walk-in, Walk-out relationships:** Brief affairs, one-night stands, or just spending weekends together do not qualify as a live-in relationship in the "nature of marriage" and don't attract maintenance rights.
- **Adulterous Relationships:** If one partner is legally married to someone else, the unmarried partner generally cannot claim maintenance (though courts decide case-by-case on grounds of fraud).
- **Same-sex couples:** Currently, the specific statutory provisions for maintenance (under BNSS 144 and PWDVA) are interpreted largely for heterosexual relationships, though Constitutional protections exist.

## Important Timelines
- **Registration Certificate Issuance:** Usually within 30 days of application (under state-specific laws).
- **Maintenance Application:** Can be filed anytime during or after the separation, but prompt filing is recommended.
- **Termination Statement:** Notice should be given immediately upon deciding to separate (where registration is mandated).

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Assuming all live-in relationships automatically grant property/maintenance rights.
- Not keeping documented proof of shared living (rent agreements, joint accounts).
- Entering a live-in relationship without verifying the marital status of the partner.
- Failing to register the relationship in states where it is legally mandated, leading to criminal penalties.
- Concealing a previous marriage while entering a live-in relationship.

## Frequently Asked Questions
**1. Is live-in relationship legal in India?**
Yes, it is legally recognized under Article 21. Supreme Court has stated it is not an offense.
हां, यह कानूनी रूप से मान्य है। सुप्रीम कोर्ट ने कहा है कि यह कोई अपराध नहीं है।
Haan, ye bilkul legal hai aur Article 21 ke under protected hai.

**2. Can police arrest couples in a live-in relationship?**
No, police cannot arrest consenting adults merely for living together. It is not illegal.
नहीं, पुलिस केवल साथ रहने के लिए बालिग जोड़ों को गिरफ्तार नहीं कर सकती।
Nahi, police consenting adults ko sirf live-in mein rehne ke liye arrest nahi kar sakti.

**3. Is it mandatory to register a live-in relationship?**
It is not mandatory pan-India. However, states like Uttarakhand have made it mandatory under their Uniform Civil Code.
पूरे भारत में यह अनिवार्य नहीं है, लेकिन उत्तराखंड जैसे राज्यों ने इसे अनिवार्य कर दिया है।
Pan-India compulsory nahi hai, par Uttarakhand jaise states mein UCC ke under compulsory hai.

**4. Can a female partner claim maintenance?**
Yes, if the relationship is in the "nature of marriage", she can claim maintenance under PWDVA and BNSS.
हां, अगर रिश्ता शादी की तरह है, तो महिला भरण-पोषण की मांग कर सकती है।
Haan, agar relationship marriage jaisa hai, toh PWDVA ke under maintenance claim kar sakti hai.

**5. Are children born out of a live-in relationship legitimate?**
Yes, the Supreme Court has declared them legitimate with rights to parents' property.
हां, सुप्रीम कोर्ट ने उन्हें वैध माना है और उन्हें संपत्ति का अधिकार है।
Haan, SC ke according bachche legitimate hain aur unhe property rights milte hain.

**6. Can a married person be in a live-in relationship?**
Living with someone while being legally married to another is not a legally protected live-in relationship and could attract legal complications.
शादीशुदा होते हुए किसी और के साथ लिव-इन में रहना कानूनी रूप से सुरक्षित नहीं है।
Married hote hue live-in mein rehna legally protected nahi hai.

**7. How to prove a live-in relationship in court?**
Provide joint rent agreements, joint bank accounts, shared utility bills, and evidence of living together for a long period.
साझा रेंट एग्रीमेंट, जॉइंट बैंक अकाउंट और लंबे समय तक साथ रहने के सबूत पेश करें।
Joint rent agreement, bank accounts aur shared bills court mein proof ke tarah use hote hain.

**8. Does a live-in partner get a share in ancestral property?**
No, a live-in partner does not automatically inherit ancestral property, but the children born from the relationship do.
नहीं, पार्टनर को पैतृक संपत्ति नहीं मिलती, लेकिन बच्चों को मिलती है।
Nahi, partner ko ancestral property nahi milti, par unke bachchon ko right milta hai.

**9. Can a couple adopt a child while in a live-in relationship?**
The law regarding joint adoption by live-in couples is stringent; CARA guidelines primarily allow legally married couples or single individuals to adopt.
लिव-इन जोड़ों द्वारा संयुक्त गोद लेने के नियम सख्त हैं; मुख्य रूप से विवाहित जोड़े या अकेले व्यक्ति गोद ले सकते हैं।
Live-in couples ke liye joint adoption difficult hai, CARA rules mostly married couples ko allow karte hain.

**10. What happens if one partner walks out?**
If the relationship is not in the nature of marriage, either can walk out without obligations. Otherwise, maintenance claims may arise.
अगर रिश्ता शादी जैसा है, तो भरण-पोषण का दावा हो सकता है, अन्यथा कोई भी बाहर निकल सकता है।
Agar relation deep hai toh maintenance claim ho sakta hai, warna normally alag ho sakte hain.

**11. Does the Domestic Violence Act apply to live-in relationships?**
Yes, the PWDVA applies to relationships in the "nature of marriage".
हां, घरेलू हिंसा अधिनियम शादी जैसे रिश्तों पर लागू होता है।
Haan, DV Act "nature of marriage" wale live-in relationships par apply hota hai.

**12. Can a live-in agreement be legally enforced?**
Yes, a cohabitation agreement outlining financial and property terms can be enforced under the Indian Contract Act.
हां, वित्तीय मामलों से जुड़ा सहवास समझौता कॉन्ट्रैक्ट एक्ट के तहत लागू किया जा सकता है।
Haan, cohabitation agreement contract act ke under enforce kiya ja sakta hai.

**13. Is there a minimum time period to qualify for rights?**
There is no strict fixed timeframe, but courts look for a "reasonable period" of cohabitation to establish the nature of marriage.
कोई निश्चित समयसीमा नहीं है, लेकिन कोर्ट लंबे समय तक साथ रहने को आधार मानता है।
Koi fixed time nahi hai, par court ek "reasonable period" ki cohabitation dekhti hai.

**14. What if the parents oppose the relationship?**
If both partners are consenting adults, parents cannot legally force them to separate. They can seek police protection if threatened.
अगर दोनों बालिग हैं, तो माता-पिता कानूनी रूप से उन्हें अलग नहीं कर सकते। वे पुलिस सुरक्षा ले सकते हैं।
Agar adults hain, toh parents force nahi kar sakte. Threat hone par police protection le sakte hain.

**15. How to end a registered live-in relationship?**
By submitting a formal statement of termination to the Registrar who registered it.
रजिस्ट्रार को समाप्ति का औपचारिक बयान जमा करके।
Registrar ko formal termination statement dekar end kar sakte hain.

**16. Are same-sex live-in relationships protected?**
They are not illegal, but they currently do not enjoy the same statutory maintenance or domestic violence protections explicitly designed for heterosexual women.
वे अवैध नहीं हैं, लेकिन वर्तमान में उन्हें भरण-पोषण के समान वैधानिक अधिकार नहीं मिलते हैं।
Illegal nahi hain, par maintenance aur DV act ki specific protections currently unhe nahi milti.

**17. What if a minor is in a live-in relationship?**
It is illegal and falls under child protection laws like POCSO, attracting severe criminal charges.
यह अवैध है और POCSO जैसे कानूनों के तहत गंभीर अपराध माना जाता है।
Ye illegal hai aur POCSO act ke under severe crime maana jata hai.

**18. Can a live-in partner claim a nominee status in bank accounts?**
Yes, anyone can be made a nominee, regardless of the legal relationship status.
हां, किसी को भी नॉमिनी बनाया जा सकता है।
Haan, bank accounts aur insurance mein live-in partner ko nominee banaya ja sakta hai.

**19. How is a cohabitation agreement different from marriage?**
It only defines financial and property arrangements, unlike marriage which brings personal laws into effect.
यह केवल वित्तीय व्यवस्था तय करता है, जबकि शादी में पर्सनल लॉ लागू होते हैं।
Ye sirf financial terms define karta hai, shaadi ki tarah personal laws apply nahi hote.

**20. What is palimony?**
Palimony refers to the maintenance or alimony paid to a partner after separation from a long-term live-in relationship.
यह लंबे समय तक चले लिव-इन रिश्ते के टूटने के बाद दिए जाने वाले भरण-पोषण को कहते हैं।
Ye long-term live-in relation ke end hone par diye jane wale maintenance ko kehte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Live-in relationships are illegal in India.
   **Fact:** They are completely legal and recognized under Article 21 of the Constitution.
2. **Myth:** Children from live-in relationships are illegitimate.
   **Fact:** The Supreme Court has ruled that children born from long-term live-in relationships are legitimate.
3. **Myth:** Police can raid and arrest couples living together.
   **Fact:** Police cannot arrest consenting adults for living together unless there is a specific criminal complaint.
4. **Myth:** Live-in partners automatically get half the property.
   **Fact:** No, property rights are not automatic. They depend on financial contributions and specific agreements.
5. **Myth:** Domestic Violence Act only applies to married women.
   **Fact:** The PWDVA covers women in relationships "in the nature of marriage", including live-ins.
6. **Myth:** You must live together for exactly 3 years to get legal rights.
   **Fact:** There is no legally defined timeframe; courts evaluate the circumstances on a case-by-case basis.
7. **Myth:** A married man can legally have a live-in partner.
   **Fact:** This is an adulterous relationship and is not given the legal protections of a standard live-in relationship.
8. **Myth:** Registration of live-in relationship is mandatory everywhere.
   **Fact:** Currently, it is mandatory only in specific states like Uttarakhand under their UCC.
9. **Myth:** Hotels can legally deny rooms to unmarried couples.
   **Fact:** There is no law that prohibits unmarried couples from staying in hotels.
10. **Myth:** Same-sex couples cannot live together.
    **Fact:** Same-sex cohabitation is legal, though statutory marriage/maintenance rights may differ.

## Conversation Examples
**Example 1: Legality of the Relationship**
**Citizen:** Is it a crime to live with my girlfriend without marriage? Our families are threatening us.
**Assistant:** No, it is not a crime. The Supreme Court of India recognizes live-in relationships between consenting adults under Article 21. If your families are threatening you, you can approach the local police or high court for a protection order.
**Citizen:** Kya police hume pareshan kar sakti hai?
**Assistant:** Nahi, agar aap dono consenting adults hain (18+), toh police sirf sath rehne ke liye aapko pareshan ya arrest nahi kar sakti. 

**Example 2: Maintenance Rights**
**Citizen:** I lived with my partner for 5 years, and we acted like husband and wife. Now he left me. Can I claim maintenance?
**Assistant:** Yes, if your relationship was in the "nature of marriage" (shared household, pooled finances), you can claim maintenance under the Domestic Violence Act or Section 144 of the BNSS.
**Citizen:** Iske liye mujhe court mein kya dikhana hoga?
**Assistant:** Aapko joint rent agreement, bank statements, ya photos jaise proofs dikhane honge jo sabit karein ki aap dono ek couple ki tarah rehte the.

**Example 3: Registration Rules**
**Citizen:** I heard we need to register our live-in relationship now. Is that true?
**Assistant:** If you reside in Uttarakhand, yes, it is mandatory under their new Uniform Civil Code. Failure to register can lead to penalties. In most other states, registration is not legally required yet.
**Citizen:** Register karne ka kya process hai waha?
**Assistant:** Aapko apne documents (Aadhaar, age proof) ke sath local Registrar ko joint application deni hogi. Verification ke baad 30 din mein certificate mil jata hai.

**Example 4: Child Rights**
**Citizen:** Will our child get a share in my partner's ancestral property?
**Assistant:** Yes, the Supreme Court has ruled that children born out of a live-in relationship are legitimate and are entitled to a share in their parents' property, including ancestral property.
**Citizen:** Bacche ke birth certificate me kya likha jayega?
**Assistant:** Birth certificate mein dono parents ka naam legally likhwaaya jaa sakta hai.

**Example 5: Domestic Violence**
**Citizen:** My live-in boyfriend beats me and has thrown me out of our rented house. What are my rights?
**Assistant:** You are protected under the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act. You have the right to reside in the shared household, and you can file a police complaint or approach a magistrate for a protection and residence order.
**Citizen:** Mujhe turant kya karna chahiye?
**Assistant:** Aap 1091 (Women Helpline) ya 112 par call karke police ki madad le sakti hain aur Protection Officer se mil sakti hain.

**Example 6:**
**Citizen:** What happens if my partner is already married but told me he was divorced?
**Assistant:** If your consent was obtained by fraud, you can file a criminal case for cheating and rape under false pretext of marriage. However, claiming maintenance might be complex and depends on the court's discretion regarding the fraud.

**Example 7:**
**Citizen:** Can we open a joint bank account as a live-in couple?
**Assistant:** Yes, RBI guidelines allow any two individuals to open a joint bank account without needing to prove a marital relationship.

**Example 8:**
**Citizen:** Do we need a lawyer to draft a cohabitation agreement?
**Assistant:** While not strictly necessary, it is highly recommended to have a lawyer draft it to ensure it clearly covers financial distributions and is legally sound under the Indian Contract Act.

**Example 9:**
**Citizen:** Can a landlord legally refuse to rent to a live-in couple?
**Assistant:** Landlords have the right to choose their tenants based on their preferences, but they cannot evict you forcibly without following the legal eviction process outlined in the state's Rent Control Act once an agreement is signed.

**Example 10:**
**Citizen:** Kya hum legally bacha adopt kar sakte hain?
**Assistant:** CARA guidelines live-in couples ke joint adoption ko strictly allow nahi karti hain, par individual capacity mein as a single parent adoption kiya ja sakta hai.

**Example 11:**
**Citizen:** What if we break up? How is the property divided?
**Assistant:** Unlike divorce, there are no specific laws for asset division in a live-in relationship. Assets are usually divided based on whose name is on the title or the cohabitation agreement, if any.

**Example 12:**
**Citizen:** Mujhe aur mere partner ko ghar walo se jaan ka khatra hai, kya karein?
**Assistant:** Aap turant apne state ke High Court mein ek writ petition file karke police protection maang sakte hain. Court couples ko protection grant karti hai.

**Example 13:**
**Citizen:** My partner died without a will. Do I inherit their property?
**Assistant:** No, a live-in partner is not considered a legal heir under personal succession laws (like Hindu Succession Act). Without a will, you do not have an automatic right to inherit their property.

**Example 14:**
**Citizen:** Kya hotel wale hume room dene se mana kar sakte hain?
**Assistant:** Law mein aisi koi rok nahi hai. Par hotel ki apni internal policy ho sakti hai. Aap pehle se confirm kar lein ya couple-friendly hotels book karein.

**Example 15:**
**Citizen:** How do we prove "nature of marriage" for a maintenance case?
**Assistant:** You need to show pooling of resources (joint accounts), long-term cohabitation (rent agreements), shared duties, and that society viewed you as a couple (photos, invitations, witness testimonies).
