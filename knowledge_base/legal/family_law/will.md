# Will (Wasiyat): Legal Validity, Drafting, and Execution in India

## Overview

**English**: A Will (testament) is a legal document by which a person (the testator) expresses their wishes regarding the distribution of their property and assets after their death. In India, making a Will ensures that your self-acquired property is inherited by the people you choose, avoiding potential family disputes and the default rules of intestate succession. It provides absolute clarity on estate distribution.

**Hindi (Unicode)**: वसीयत (Will) एक कानूनी दस्तावेज़ है जिसके माध्यम से कोई व्यक्ति (वसीयतकर्ता) अपनी मृत्यु के बाद अपनी संपत्ति और संपदा के वितरण के संबंध में अपनी इच्छा व्यक्त करता है। भारत में, वसीयत बनाने से यह सुनिश्चित होता है कि आपकी स्व-अर्जित संपत्ति आपके द्वारा चुने गए लोगों को मिले, जिससे संभावित पारिवारिक विवादों और निर्वसीयत उत्तराधिकार के डिफ़ॉल्ट नियमों से बचा जा सके। यह संपत्ति वितरण पर पूर्ण स्पष्टता प्रदान करता है।

**Hinglish**: Will (Wasiyat) ek aisa legal document hai jiske through ek person (testator) apni death ke baad apni property aur assets ke distribution ki wishes declare karta hai. India mein Will banane se family disputes avoid hote hain aur yeh ensure hota hai ki aapki self-acquired property unhi ko mile jinhe aap dena chahte hain, na ki religion ke default succession laws ke hisaab se.

## Quick Summary

**English**: A Will takes effect only after the death of the testator. It can be changed, modified, or revoked any number of times during the testator's lifetime. While registration of a Will is not legally mandatory in India, it is highly recommended to prove its authenticity. It requires the signatures of the testator and at least two independent witnesses. 

**Hindi (Unicode)**: वसीयत केवल वसीयतकर्ता की मृत्यु के बाद ही प्रभावी होती है। इसे वसीयतकर्ता के जीवनकाल में कितनी भी बार बदला, संशोधित या रद्द किया जा सकता है। यद्यपि भारत में वसीयत का पंजीकरण कानूनी रूप से अनिवार्य नहीं है, लेकिन इसकी प्रामाणिकता साबित करने के लिए इसकी अत्यधिक अनुशंसा की जाती है। इसमें वसीयतकर्ता और कम से कम दो स्वतंत्र गवाहों के हस्ताक्षर होने आवश्यक हैं।

**Hinglish**: Will sirf testator ki death ke baad effect mein aati hai. Isey life mein kitni baar bhi change, modify, ya cancel (revoke) kiya ja sakta hai. ## Definition

**English**: According to Section 2(h) of the Indian Succession Act, 1925, a "Will" means the legal declaration of the intention of a testator with respect to his property which he desires to be carried into effect after his death.

**Hindi (Unicode)**: भारतीय उत्तराधिकार अधिनियम, 1925 की धारा 2(h) के अनुसार, "वसीयत" का अर्थ एक वसीयतकर्ता के उस इरादे की कानूनी घोषणा है जो वह अपनी संपत्ति के संबंध में करता है और जिसे वह अपनी मृत्यु के बाद लागू करना चाहता है।

**Hinglish**: Indian Succession Act, 1925 ke Section 2(h) ke anusar, "Will" ka matlab hai ek person (testator) ki apni property ko lekar wo legal declaration jo wo chahta hai ki uski death ke baad poori ki jaye.

**English**:

2. **Overrides Default Laws**: Prevents the application of default religious succession laws (e.g., Hindu Succession Act) which might distribute property in a way the deceased did not intend.
3. **Protects Vulnerable Dependents**: Allows provision for minor children, disabled dependents, or specific charities.
4. **Appoints Executors**: Allows the testator to appoint a trusted person (Executor) to manage and distribute the estate.

## Legal Background

The concept and execution of a Will in India are primarily governed by the Indian Succession Act, 1925, with specific variations for different religious communities based on personal laws.

**Relevant Acts & Statutes:**
- **Indian Succession Act, 1925 (ISA)**: The primary legislation. Parts IV and VI govern testamentary succession for Hindus, Buddhists, Sikhs, Jains, Christians, and Parsis. 
- **Hindu Succession Act, 1956**: Section 30 clearly states that any Hindu may dispose of by Will or other testamentary disposition any property, which is capable of being so disposed of by him/her, in accordance with the provisions of the Indian Succession Act, 1925.
- **Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937**: Governs Muslims. Under Islamic law, a Muslim cannot bequeath more than one-third (1/3rd) of their total estate through a Will without the consent of the legal heirs. The rest must devolve as per Sharia succession rules.
- **The Registration Act, 1908**: Section 18 makes the registration of a Will optional. Section 40 and 41 deal with the presentation and registration of Wills.

**Relevant Sections of the Indian Succession Act, 1925:**
- **Section 2(h)**: Defines a Will.
- **Section 59**: Person capable of making Wills (sound mind, not a minor).
- **Section 62**: Will may be revoked or altered.
- **Section 63**: Execution of unprivileged Wills (requires signature and two witnesses).
- **Section 65 & 66**: Privileged Wills (made by soldiers, airmen, mariners in expedition).
- **Section 213**: Right as executor or legatee established via Probate (mandatory in certain jurisdictions like Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai for Hindus/Parsis).

## Applicability

**Who is Covered:**
- Any person of sound mind who has reached the age of majority (18 years) can make a Will.
- A person who is ordinarily insane may make a Will during an interval when they are of sound mind.
- Deaf, mute, or blind individuals can make a Will if they understand what they are doing.

**Special Conditions & Exemptions:**
- **Muslims**: Mostly exempt from the Indian Succession Act regarding Wills. Governed by uncodified Sharia law. Can only will up to 1/3rd of the estate; oral wills are permitted but written is preferred.
- **Ancestral Property**: A Hindu coparcener can will away their *share* of the undivided joint family property (Section 30, Hindu Succession Act), but they cannot will away the entire ancestral property. Only self-acquired property can be willed away entirely.
- **Intoxication/Illness**: A Will made by a person whose mind is clouded by intoxication, severe illness, or coercion is void.

## Rights of Citizens

**English**: 
- **Right to Modify**: You have the absolute right to amend, modify (via Codicil), or completely revoke your Will at any time before your death.
- **Right to Bequeath**: You can give your self-acquired property to anyone, including non-relatives, friends, or charitable trusts.
- **Right to Confidentiality**: The contents of a registered Will remain strictly confidential in the Sub-Registrar’s office until the testator's death.

## Duties & Responsibilities

**English**:
- **Testator's Duty**: Must ensure they are of sound mind and acting voluntarily without any force or fraud. Should clearly describe all properties and beneficiaries.
- **Witnesses' Duty**: Must see the testator sign the Will (or receive an acknowledgment of the signature) and must sign the Will in the presence of the testator. They do not need to read the contents of the Will.
- **Executor's Duty**: After the testator's death, the Executor must clear any debts/taxes of the deceased and distribute the remaining estate exactly as per the Will.

- **वसीयतकर्ता का कर्तव्य**: यह सुनिश्चित करना चाहिए कि वे स्वस्थ दिमाग के हैं और बिना किसी दबाव या धोखाधड़ी के स्वेच्छा से कार्य कर रहे हैं। संपत्तियों और लाभार्थियों का स्पष्ट वर्णन करना चाहिए।

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English**: 
1. **Drafting the Will**: Write down the distribution of assets clearly. It can be handwritten or typed. No stamp paper is required; it can be written on plain paper.
2. **Appointing an Executor**: Name a reliable person to execute the Will after your demise.
3. **Execution (Signing)**: The testator must sign or affix their thumb impression on each page.
4. **Attestation by Witnesses**: At least two independent witnesses must sign the Will, confirming they saw the testator sign it. Beneficiaries should ideally *not* be witnesses.
5. **Medical Certificate (Optional but recommended)**: Attach a doctor's certificate stating the testator is of sound mind.
6. **Registration (Optional but highly recommended)**: Take the drafted Will, along with the two witnesses, to the local Sub-Registrar of Assurances. Pay the requisite registration fee. The Sub-Registrar will record it, providing strong legal evidence of its genuineness.
7. **Safe Custody**: Keep the original safe, or deposit it in a bank locker, and inform the executor of its location.

## Required Documents

To register a Will at the Sub-Registrar’s office:
1. Original drafted Will (on plain paper).
2. Proof of Identity of the Testator (Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, Passport).
3. Proof of Address of the Testator.
4. Proof of Identity of the two witnesses.
5. Medical Fitness Certificate (from a registered medical practitioner, ensuring sound mental health of the testator at the time of execution).
6. Passport-sized photographs of the testator and both witnesses.
7. Proof of ownership of properties mentioned (Index II, Title Deeds) – though Sub-Registrars typically do not verify title, it is good practice to carry them.

---

## Government Authorities

1. **Sub-Registrar of Assurances**: The authority under the State Revenue Department where the Will is registered.
2. **Civil Courts / District Courts**: Handle disputes related to the Will, Will challenges, and the issuance of Letters of Administration.
3. **High Courts**: Issue 'Probate' (an official copy of the Will certified by the court) which is mandatory in certain metropolitan cities (Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai).

---

## Penalties & Punishments

- **Forgery of a Will**: Under Section 467 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) [formerly IPC Sec 467], forging a Will is a serious criminal offense punishable by life imprisonment, or imprisonment up to 10 years, and a fine.
- **Using a Forged Will as Genuine**: Punishable under Section 471 of BNS.
- **Concealing or Destroying a Will**: Under Section 477 of BNS, fraudulently destroying or canceling a Will carries a punishment of up to 7 years imprisonment and a fine.

*(Note: BNS replaced the Indian Penal Code (IPC) effective July 2024. The fundamental nature of these crimes remains severely punishable).*

---

## Exceptions

- **Privileged Wills**: Soldiers, airmen, or mariners engaged in active warfare or expeditions can make "Privileged Wills" (Sections 65 & 66, ISA). These can be made orally in front of two witnesses or written without the strict need for attestation/signatures that apply to normal civilians.
- **Muslim Personal Law**: The 1/3rd restriction. A Muslim cannot will more than 1/3rd of their property without the consent of the other legal heirs. If they do, the excess bequest is invalid unless heirs consent after the testator's death.

---

## Important Timelines

- **Validity During Lifetime**: A Will is valid until revoked. There is no expiry date for a Will as long as the testator is alive.
- **Registration Timeframe**: A Will can be registered at *any time* during the life of the testator. Unlike other documents (which have a 4-month window), Section 27 of the Registration Act allows a Will to be presented at any time.
- **Probate Application**: If Probate is required, there is technically no strict limitation period under the Limitation Act, but unreasonable delay (usually beyond 3 years from death) must be explained to the court.

---

## Common Legal Mistakes

1. **Not Using Independent Witnesses**: Having a beneficiary (a person getting property in the Will) sign as a witness. Under Section 67 of ISA, a bequest to an attesting witness is generally void.
2. **Ambiguous Property Descriptions**: Writing "my house" instead of specifying the exact address, survey number, and flat number.
3. **Ignoring Ancestral Property Rules**: Trying to Will away the entire ancestral property without realizing you can only Will your specific share in it.
4. **Forgetting to Update**: Not updating the Will after acquiring new properties or after major life events (like marriage, birth of a child, or death of a beneficiary).
5. **No Medical Certificate**: Older testators not attaching a doctor's certificate, leaving the Will vulnerable to "unsound mind" challenges in court.

## Safety Tips

**English**: 
- Always register your Will to minimize the chances of it being challenged for forgery.
- Always include a "Residuary Clause" (e.g., "Any other assets I acquire in the future will go to X") to cover forgotten or future properties.
- Store a digital copy and inform a trusted executor about the physical location of the original Will.

**Hindi (Unicode)**:
- जालसाजी की चुनौती से बचने के लिए हमेशा अपनी वसीयत को पंजीकृत (Register) कराएं।
- भविष्य में अर्जित या भूली हुई संपत्ति को कवर करने के लिए हमेशा एक "अवशिष्ट खंड" (Residuary Clause) शामिल करें।
- मूल वसीयत की एक डिजिटल प्रतिलिपि सुरक्षित रखें और एक विश्वसनीय व्यक्ति को मूल दस्तावेज़ के स्थान के बारे में सूचित करें।

**Hinglish**:
- Apni Will ko hamesha register karwayein taaki baad mein koi fraud ka ilzaam na laga sake.
- Ek "Residuary Clause" zaroor dalein (jaise: "Future mein agar main kuch aur kharidu toh wo X ko milega").
- Original Will ki digital copy banayein aur executor ko batayein ki original kahan rakhi hai.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Is it mandatory to register a Will in India?**
**English**: No, registration is optional under Section 18 of the Registration Act. However, it is highly recommended to prevent challenges regarding forgery or tampering.
**Hindi**: नहीं, पंजीकरण अधिनियम की धारा 18 के तहत वसीयत का पंजीकरण वैकल्पिक है। हालांकि, जालसाजी से बचने के लिए इसकी अत्यधिक अनुशंसा की जाती है।
**Hinglish**: Nahi, registration optional hai. Par forgery se bachne ke liye isko register karwana best rehta hai.

**2. Can a Will be written on plain paper, or is stamp paper needed?**
**English**: A Will can be legally written on plain paper. No stamp paper or stamp duty is required for drafting a Will.
**Hindi**: वसीयत को कानूनी रूप से सादे कागज पर लिखा जा सकता है। इसके लिए किसी स्टाम्प पेपर की आवश्यकता नहीं है।
**Hinglish**: Will plain paper pe likhi ja sakti hai, iske liye koi stamp paper ki zarurat nahi hoti.

**3. Who can be a witness to a Will?**
**English**: Any adult of sound mind can be a witness. However, they should ideally be independent individuals who are not beneficiaries in the Will.
**Hindi**: कोई भी स्वस्थ दिमाग वाला वयस्क गवाह हो सकता है। लेकिन ध्यान रहे कि गवाह ऐसा व्यक्ति होना चाहिए जिसे वसीयत में कोई संपत्ति न मिल रही हो।
**Hinglish**: Koi bhi adult aur sound mind ka insaan witness ban sakta hai. Par better hai ki wo koi beneficiary na ho.

**4. How many times can I change my Will?**
**English**: You can change, modify, or completely revoke your Will as many times as you want during your lifetime. The last validly executed Will before death is considered the final one.
**Hindi**: आप अपने जीवनकाल में जितनी बार चाहें अपनी वसीयत बदल सकते हैं। मृत्यु से पहले बनाई गई अंतिम वैध वसीयत ही मान्य होती है।
**Hinglish**: Aap apni life mein kitni baar bhi Will change kar sakte hain. Last bani hui valid Will hi final maani jayegi.

**5. What is a Codicil?**
**English**: A Codicil is a legal document used to make minor changes, additions, or deletions to an existing Will without having to rewrite the entire document. It must be executed just like a Will.
**Hindi**: कोडिसिल एक कानूनी दस्तावेज़ है जिसका उपयोग पूरी वसीयत को फिर से लिखे बिना मौजूदा वसीयत में मामूली बदलाव करने के लिए किया जाता है।
**Hinglish**: Codicil ek document hai jisse aap purani Will mein chote changes kar sakte hain bina poori Will dubara likhe.

**6. What happens if I die without making a Will (Intestate)?**
**English**: If you die without a Will, your assets will be distributed according to the succession laws applicable to your religion (e.g., Hindu Succession Act for Hindus).
**Hindi**: यदि आप वसीयत के बिना मर जाते हैं, तो आपकी संपत्ति आपके धर्म पर लागू उत्तराधिकार कानूनों के अनुसार वितरित की जाएगी।
**Hinglish**: Agar aap bina Will banaye guzar jate hain, toh property aapke religion ke succession laws ke hisaab se distribute hogi.

**7. Can a joint property be willed?**
**English**: You can only Will your specific share in a joint property. You cannot bequeath the entire joint property.
**Hindi**: आप संयुक्त संपत्ति में केवल अपना विशिष्ट हिस्सा ही वसीयत कर सकते हैं। पूरी संपत्ति की वसीयत नहीं की जा सकती।
**Hinglish**: Aap joint property mein sirf apna hissa (share) hi Will mein de sakte hain, poori property nahi.

**8. Is a video Will legally valid in India?**
**English**: A video recording alone is not a legally valid Will under the Indian Succession Act. However, a video recording of the physical execution/signing of a written Will serves as excellent supplementary evidence in court.
**Hindi**: केवल वीडियो रिकॉर्डिंग कानूनी रूप से वैध वसीयत नहीं है। लेकिन लिखित वसीयत पर हस्ताक्षर करते समय की गई वीडियो रिकॉर्डिंग अदालत में एक मजबूत सबूत हो सकती है।
**Hinglish**: Sirf video record karna Will nahi maana jata. Par written Will sign karte waqt video banana ek strong court evidence hai.

**9. Do I need a lawyer to draft a Will?**
**English**: No, you do not legally need a lawyer. You can draft it yourself. However, for complex estates, consulting a lawyer ensures legal clarity and prevents loopholes.
**Hindi**: नहीं, कानूनी रूप से वकील की आवश्यकता नहीं है। लेकिन जटिल संपत्तियों के मामले में, वकील से परामर्श करना बेहतर होता है।
**Hinglish**: Lawyer mandatory nahi hai, aap khud likh sakte hain. Par complex property ke liye lawyer ki help lena safe rehta hai.

**10. What is a Probate?**
**English**: Probate is a copy of a Will certified under the seal of a court of competent jurisdiction with a grant of administration to the estate of the testator.
**Hindi**: प्रोबेट एक सक्षम न्यायालय की मुहर के तहत प्रमाणित वसीयत की प्रति है, जो निष्पादक को संपत्ति के प्रबंधन का अधिकार देती है।
**Hinglish**: Probate court dwara certified Will ki copy hoti hai jo executor ko legal power deti hai property distribute karne ki.

**11. Is Probate mandatory everywhere in India?**
**English**: No. For Hindus, Buddhists, Sikhs, and Jains, probate is only mandatory for Wills made within the jurisdictions of the High Courts of Mumbai, Kolkata, and Chennai, or for properties situated within these limits.
**Hindi**: नहीं। हिंदुओं के लिए यह केवल मुंबई, कोलकाता और चेन्नई के उच्च न्यायालयों के अधिकार क्षेत्र में अनिवार्य है।
**Hinglish**: Nahi, Hindus ke liye yeh sirf Mumbai, Kolkata, aur Chennai mein zaroori hai.

**12. Can a Muslim person will their entire property?**
**English**: No. Under Sharia law, a Muslim can only bequeath up to one-third of their estate through a Will. The remaining two-thirds must go to legal heirs unless the heirs consent to the Will after the testator's death.
**Hindi**: नहीं। शरिया कानून के तहत, एक मुस्लिम अपनी संपत्ति का केवल एक-तिहाई हिस्सा ही वसीयत कर सकता है।
**Hinglish**: Nahi, Muslim law ke hisaab se sirf 1/3rd property hi Will ki ja sakti hai, baaki legal heirs ko milti hai.

**13. How can a Will be challenged in court?**
**English**: A Will can be challenged on grounds of lack of testamentary capacity (unsound mind), fraud, coercion, undue influence, forgery, or improper execution (lack of witnesses).
**Hindi**: वसीयत को अस्वस्थ दिमाग, धोखाधड़ी, दबाव, अनुचित प्रभाव, जालसाजी या अनुचित निष्पादन (गवाहों की कमी) के आधार पर अदालत में चुनौती दी जा सकती है।
**Hinglish**: Will ko court mein unsound mind, fraud, zabardasti, ya fake signatures ke ground pe challenge kiya ja sakta hai.

**14. Can a nominee override a Will?**
**English**: No. A nominee is merely a trustee or custodian of the asset (like a bank account or shares) until it is handed over to the legal heirs mentioned in the Will. The Will supersedes a nomination.
**Hindi**: नहीं। नॉमिनी केवल संपत्ति का ट्रस्टी होता है। वसीयत में उल्लिखित कानूनी उत्तराधिकारी ही वास्तविक मालिक होते हैं।
**Hinglish**: Nahi, nominee sirf trustee hota hai. Property ka asli maalik wahi hoga jiska naam Will mein hai.

**15. Can I Will my property to a minor?**
**English**: Yes, you can. However, you should appoint a legal guardian in the Will who will manage the property until the minor turns 18.
**Hindi**: हाँ, आप कर सकते हैं। लेकिन आपको वसीयत में एक कानूनी अभिभावक नियुक्त करना चाहिए जो नाबालिग के 18 वर्ष का होने तक संपत्ति का प्रबंधन करेगा।
**Hinglish**: Haan kar sakte hain, par minor ke 18 saal ka hone tak property manage karne ke liye ek guardian appoint karna padega.

**16. What happens to debts after the testator dies?**
**English**: The debts must be paid off from the deceased person's estate before any property is distributed to the beneficiaries. The executor is responsible for clearing these dues.
**Hindi**: लाभार्थियों को कोई भी संपत्ति वितरित करने से पहले मृतक की संपत्ति से कर्ज का भुगतान किया जाना चाहिए।
**Hinglish**: Property batne se pehle deceased ke saare loans aur debts unki property se hi chukaye jayenge.

**17. Do both witnesses need to be present at the same time?**
**English**: It is not strictly legally necessary for both to be present at the exact same moment, but they both must have seen the testator sign or received an acknowledgment of the signature from the testator. However, having them both present simultaneously is the best practice.
**Hindi**: दोनों गवाहों का एक ही समय उपस्थित होना सबसे अच्छा अभ्यास है, हालांकि उन्हें वसीयतकर्ता को हस्ताक्षर करते हुए देखना आवश्यक है।
**Hinglish**: Best practice yahi hai ki dono witnesses ek hi time pe present ho aur testator ko sign karte hue dekhein.

**18. Can an NRI make a Will in India?**
**English**: Yes, an NRI can make a Will regarding their properties located in India. The procedure is the same, and they can execute it in front of the Indian Embassy officials in their residing country if they cannot travel to India.
**Hindi**: हाँ, एक एनआरआई भारत में स्थित अपनी संपत्तियों के संबंध में वसीयत बना सकता है।
**Hinglish**: Haan, NRI apni Indian properties ke liye Will bana sakte hain. Wo Indian Embassy mein bhi execute kar sakte hain.

**19. Does marriage revoke a previous Will?**
**English**: Under Section 69 of the Indian Succession Act, marriage generally revokes any previously made Will (applicable to Christians and Parsis, but NOT to Hindus, Buddhists, Sikhs, and Jains).
**Hindi**: भारतीय उत्तराधिकार अधिनियम की धारा 69 के तहत, विवाह आम तौर पर पहले की गई वसीयत को रद्द कर देता है (ईसाइयों और पारसियों पर लागू, लेकिन हिंदुओं पर नहीं)।
**Hinglish**: Christians aur Parsis ke case mein shaadi ke baad purani Will cancel ho jati hai, par Hindus ke case mein nahi hoti.

**20. What is a mutual Will?**
**English**: A mutual Will is usually made by a married couple where they leave their respective estates to each other. It takes effect on the death of the first person.
**Hindi**: आपसी वसीयत (Mutual Will) आमतौर पर एक विवाहित जोड़े द्वारा बनाई जाती है जहां वे अपनी संपत्ति एक-दूसरे के लिए छोड़ देते हैं।
**Hinglish**: Mutual Will husband-wife banate hain jisme wo apni property ek dusre ko de dete hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth**: Registration of a Will is mandatory to make it legally valid.
   **Fact**: False. Registration is optional in India. A valid, witnessed unregistered Will holds full legal weight, though registration aids in proving authenticity.
2. **Myth**: You need a highly expensive stamp paper to write a Will.
   **Fact**: False. A Will can be written on a simple blank piece of paper. No stamp duty is required.
3. **Myth**: A nominee of a bank account becomes the absolute owner of the money.
   **Fact**: False. A nominee is just a custodian. The ultimate right belongs to the legal heir determined by the Will or succession law.
4. **Myth**: I cannot change my Will once I register it.
   **Fact**: False. You can cancel or amend a registered Will any number of times by drafting a new Will or a Codicil.
5. **Myth**: A doctor's certificate is legally mandatory.
   **Fact**: False. It is not legally mandatory, but it is highly recommended as it preemptively defeats claims that the testator was not of sound mind.
6. **Myth**: Only old people should make a Will.
   **Fact**: False. Life is unpredictable. Any adult with self-acquired assets should consider making a Will to secure their family's future.
7. **Myth**: I can Will away my ancestral property exactly as I wish.
   **Fact**: False. You can only Will away your specific undivided share in ancestral property, not the entire property.
8. **Myth**: The witnesses must read the Will to know what is written inside.
   **Fact**: False. Witnesses only attest to the *signature* of the testator, not the *contents* of the document.
9. **Myth**: Video recording replaces a written Will.
   **Fact**: False. An oral video recording is not a valid Will for ordinary citizens. It only serves as supplementary evidence to a written Will.
10. **Myth**: A beneficiary can also be a witness to save time.
    **Fact**: False. If a beneficiary signs as an attesting witness, the bequest to them becomes legally void under Section 67 of the ISA.

## Conversation Examples

**Example 1:**
**Citizen**: Is it necessary to print the Will, or can I write it by hand?
**Assistant**: You can absolutely write it by hand! A handwritten Will (often called a holographic Will) is completely valid in India as long as it is signed by you and attested by two witnesses. In fact, handwriting can sometimes make it harder to forge.

**Example 2:**
**Citizen**: क्या मैं अपनी वसीयत में किसी अनाथालय को अपनी संपत्ति दे सकता हूँ?
**Assistant**: जी हाँ, आप अपनी स्व-अर्जित संपत्ति (Self-acquired property) का कोई भी हिस्सा या पूरी संपत्ति किसी भी व्यक्ति, धर्मार्थ संस्था या अनाथालय को वसीयत के जरिए दे सकते हैं।

**Example 3:**
**Citizen**: Maine apne bete ko nominee banaya hai bank mein. Kya mujhe Will ki zarurat hai?
**Assistant**: Haan, Will bahot zaroori hai. Nominee sirf ek trustee hota hai jo bank se paisa receive karta hai. Asli haqdaar wahi hoga jiska naam Will mein likha hoga ya jo legal heir hoga. Will nomination ko override karti hai.

**Example 4:**
**Citizen**: My uncle forced my grandfather to sign a Will. What can we do?
**Assistant**: A Will signed under coercion, fraud, or undue influence is void under Section 61 of the Indian Succession Act. You can challenge this Will in the civil court by providing evidence of the coercion.

**Example 5:**
**Citizen**: क्या वसीयत बनाने के लिए वकील की फीस बहुत ज्यादा होती है?
**Assistant**: वसीयत आप खुद सादे कागज पर लिख सकते हैं, इसके लिए वकील की आवश्यकता नहीं है। यदि आप कानूनी सलाह लेते हैं, तो वकील की फीस जटिलता के आधार पर भिन्न हो सकती है, लेकिन ड्राफ्टिंग अपने आप में मुफ्त की जा सकती है।

**Example 6:**
**Citizen**: Agar main apni Will ko register karwa lun, toh kya meri family ko mere death ke baad pata chal jayega?
**Assistant**: Nahi, jab tak aap zinda hain, registered Will ki details Sub-Registrar office mein completely confidential rehti hain. Kisi ko nahi pata chalega jab tak aap khud na batayein.

**Example 7:**
**Citizen**: I want to change my executor. Do I need to write a whole new Will?
**Assistant**: No, you don't need to write a completely new Will. You can simply make a "Codicil," which is a supplementary document that modifies your existing Will to change the executor. The Codicil must also be signed and witnessed properly.

**Example 8:**
**Citizen**: मेरी पुश्तैनी ज़मीन है, क्या मैं उसे अपने एक ही बेटे को वसीयत कर सकता हूँ?
**Assistant**: नहीं। पुश्तैनी संपत्ति (Ancestral Property) में जन्म से ही सभी कानूनी उत्तराधिकारियों का अधिकार होता है। आप केवल उस संपत्ति में अपने हिस्से (Share) की वसीयत कर सकते हैं, पूरी संपत्ति की नहीं।

**Example 9:**
**Citizen**: Kya dono witnesses ka ek sath hona zaroori hai jab main sign karun?
**Assistant**: Kanoon ke hisaab se unhe aapko sign karte hue dekhna chahiye ya aapki acknowledgment leni chahiye. Sabse best aur safe yahi hai ki aap aur dono witnesses ek hi time par ek dusre ke samne sign karein.

**Example 10:**
**Citizen**: What if one of my witnesses dies before me?
**Assistant**: Your Will remains perfectly valid. However, if the Will is challenged in court later, proving it might become slightly tougher. It is a good practice to choose witnesses who are younger than you.

**Example 11:**
**Citizen**: क्या मुझे हर संपत्ति के लिए अलग वसीयत बनानी होगी?
**Assistant**: नहीं, आप अपनी सभी संपत्तियों (बैंक खाते, घर, आभूषण आदि) को एक ही वसीयत में शामिल कर सकते हैं। बस सभी संपत्तियों का विवरण स्पष्ट रूप से लिखें।

**Example 12:**
**Citizen**: Main ek Muslim hun. Kya main apni saari property apni beti ko Will kar sakta hun?
**Assistant**: Sharia law ke mutabiq, aap apni property ka sirf 1/3rd hissa hi Will ke through de sakte hain. Baaki 2/3rd hissa Islamic succession rules ke hisaab se saare legal heirs mein batega, jab tak ki dusre heirs apni consent na de dein.

**Example 13:**
**Citizen**: Do I need to pay stamp duty based on property value while registering a Will?
**Assistant**: No. Unlike property sale deeds or gift deeds, a Will is completely exempt from stamp duty. You only need to pay a nominal registration/processing fee at the Sub-Registrar's office.

**Example 14:**
**Citizen**: अगर मुझे बाद में वसीयत रद्द करनी हो तो क्या प्रक्रिया है?
**Assistant**: आप एक नई वसीयत बनाकर या पुरानी वसीयत को फाड़कर/नष्ट करके उसे रद्द कर सकते हैं। यदि पुरानी वसीयत पंजीकृत थी, तो नई वसीयत में स्पष्ट रूप से लिखें कि आप पुरानी सभी वसीयतों को रद्द कर रहे हैं, और नई वसीयत को भी पंजीकृत कराएं।

**Example 15:**
**Citizen**: Mere paas abhi koi property nahi hai par aage kharidunga. Kya main aaj Will bana sakta hun?
**Assistant**: Haan, aap Will bana sakte hain. Aap apni Will mein ek 'Residuary Clause' daal sakte hain, jiska matlab hai ki "future mein jo bhi property main acquire karunga, wo is person ko milegi".

**English**: While there is no central online portal exclusively for writing Wills, citizens must use state-level **Inspector General of Registration (IGR)** portals to book an appointment with the Sub-Registrar for Will registration. For probate matters, cases are filed through the **eCourts** system. Legal aid regarding Will drafting for marginalized citizens is available via **NALSA (National Legal Services Authority)**.

**Hinglish**: Will likhne ka koi direct online government portal nahi hai, par register karne ke liye aapko apne state ke **IGR portal** pe jake Sub-Registrar ka appointment book karna hoga. Agar court mein Probate ka matter hai, toh wo **eCourts** ke through track hota hai. Garib logo ke liye **NALSA** free legal advice deta hai Will drafting pe.
