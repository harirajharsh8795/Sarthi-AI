# Divorce in Indian Family Law: Procedures, Rights, and Regulations

## Overview
**English:** Divorce is the legal dissolution of a marriage by a court or other competent body. In India, divorce is governed by personal laws based on the religion of the individuals or secular laws like the Special Marriage Act. The process ensures that the legal and financial ties between spouses are severed fairly, addressing alimony, child custody, and property division.

## Quick Summary
**English:** Divorce in India can be obtained either by mutual consent (where both parties agree) or contested (where one party seeks divorce on grounds like cruelty, adultery, desertion, etc.). The legal framework requires filing a petition in the Family Court, followed by counseling, evidence recording, and final judgment.

## Definition
**English:** A legal decree pronounced by a court of competent jurisdiction dissolving a valid marriage, rendering both parties legally single and free to remarry, while comprehensively determining the ancillary issues of maintenance, child custody, and asset distribution.

## Legal Background
The legal framework for divorce in India is diverse, reflecting the country's pluralistic society. Each religion has its specific personal laws, while secular laws accommodate inter-faith marriages.

### Applicable Acts and Sections
1.  **The Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 (HMA)**
    *   Applicable to Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs.
    *   **Section 13(1):** Grounds for contested divorce (Adultery, Cruelty, Desertion for 2+ years, Conversion, Mental Disorder, Leprosy, Venereal Disease, Renunciation, Presumption of Death).
    *   **Section 13(2):** Special grounds available only to the wife (Bigamy by husband, Rape/Sodomy/Bestiality, Non-resumption of cohabitation after maintenance decree, Repudiation of marriage before turning 18).
    *   **Section 13B:** Divorce by Mutual Consent. Requires living separately for 1 year or more and mutual agreement that they cannot live together.
2.  **The Special Marriage Act, 1954 (SMA)**
    *   Applicable to all citizens irrespective of religion (civil marriages).
    *   **Section 27:** Grounds for contested divorce (similar to HMA).
    *   **Section 28:** Divorce by mutual consent (living separately for 1 year).
3.  **The Dissolution of Muslim Marriages Act, 1939**
    *   Applicable to Muslim women seeking divorce (Khula / Faskh).
    *   **Section 2:** Grounds for decree for dissolution of marriage (Whereabouts of husband unknown for 4 years, failure to provide maintenance for 2 years, imprisonment of husband for 7+ years, cruelty, etc.).
    *   *Note:* The Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Marriage) Act, 2019 criminalized instant Triple Talaq (Talaq-e-Biddat).
4.  **The Indian Divorce Act, 1869**
    *   Applicable to Christians.
    *   **Section 10:** Grounds for dissolution of marriage (Adultery, Cruelty, Desertion, etc.).
    *   **Section 10A:** Dissolution of marriage by mutual consent (living separately for 2 years, often read down to 1 year by various High Courts).
5.  **The Parsi Marriage and Divorce Act, 1936**
    *   Applicable to Parsis.
    *   **Sections 32 & 32B:** Grounds for divorce and mutual consent divorce.

### Relevant Constitutional Articles
*   **Article 14:** Equality before the law (ensuring gender neutrality in modern interpretations of divorce laws).
*   **Article 21:** Protection of life and personal liberty (right to live with human dignity, right to exit a broken marriage).
*   **Article 25:** Freedom of conscience and free profession, practice, and propagation of religion (basis for personal laws).

## Applicability
*   **Who is covered:** Any legally married citizen or resident in India whose marriage is registered or recognized under personal laws or the Special Marriage Act.
*   **Who is exempt:** Individuals whose marriage is void ab initio (e.g., bigamy, prohibited degrees of relationship without custom exception). They seek an annulment, not a divorce.
*   **Special conditions:** A divorce petition generally cannot be filed within the first year of marriage, except in cases of exceptional hardship to the petitioner or exceptional depravity by the respondent (e.g., Section 14 of HMA).

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

### A. Divorce by Mutual Consent (Under Sec 13B HMA, Sec 28 SMA, Sec 10A IDA)
**English:**
1.  **Drafting the Petition:** Both spouses jointly draft a petition stating they have lived separately for over a year and mutually agree to dissolve the marriage. Settlements regarding alimony and child custody are drafted.
2.  **First Motion:** The joint petition is filed in the Family Court. Both parties appear before the judge to record their statements.
3.  **Cooling-Off Period:** The court grants a 6-month cooling-off period (statutory period) to reconsider the decision. (The Supreme Court has ruled this can be waived in exceptional circumstances).
4.  **Second Motion:** After the cooling-off period, but before 18 months from the first motion, parties file the second motion.
5.  **Final Hearing and Decree:** Statements are recorded again. If the judge is satisfied that consent is free and not forced, the decree of divorce is granted.

### B. Contested Divorce
**English:**
1.  **Filing the Petition:** One spouse files a petition under grounds like cruelty, adultery, or desertion.
2.  **Summons to Respondent:** The court issues a notice/summons to the other spouse to appear.
3.  **Appearance and Written Statement:** The respondent files a reply (Written Statement) defending the allegations or making counter-claims.
4.  **Mediation/Counseling:** The judge refers the parties to the mediation center to attempt reconciliation.
5.  **Framing of Issues & Evidence:** If mediation fails, the court frames legal issues. Both parties submit evidence and cross-examine witnesses.
6.  **Final Arguments and Judgment:** After hearing arguments, the court delivers the final judgment, granting or denying the divorce.

## Required Documents
1.  Original Marriage Certificate or proof of marriage (invitation card, photographs).
2.  Proof of address for both husband and wife (Aadhar Card, Passport, Voter ID).
3.  Recent passport-size photographs of both parties.
4.  Birth certificates of children, if any.
5.  Proof of living separately (for mutual consent divorce).
6.  Income Tax Returns for the last 3 years, Salary Slips, and Bank Statements (crucial for maintenance calculations as per Rajnesh v. Neha guidelines).
7.  Comprehensive Affidavit of Assets and Liabilities.
8.  Evidence supporting contested grounds (medical records for cruelty, police complaints, emails/messages, etc.).

## Government Authorities
1.  **Family Courts:** The primary judicial body instituted under the Family Courts Act, 1984, for handling matrimonial disputes.
2.  **District Courts:** Where Family Courts are not established, the District Judge handles divorce matters.
3.  **Mediation and Conciliation Centres:** Attached to courts, aiming to resolve family disputes amicably without lengthy litigation.
4.  **High Court & Supreme Court:** Appellate authorities for challenging Family Court decrees or seeking transfer of cases.

## Penalties & Punishments
While divorce itself is a civil remedy, matrimonial disputes often involve criminal elements. The officially defined punishments are:
1.  **Cruelty by Husband or his Relatives (Section 85, BNS / Section 498A, IPC):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine.
2.  **Bigamy (Section 82, BNS / Section 494, IPC):** Marrying again during the lifetime of a spouse. Imprisonment up to 7 years and a fine.
3.  **Domestic Violence Act, 2005:** Breach of a protection order by the respondent is a cognizable and non-bailable offense punishable with imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine up to Rs. 20,000, or both.
4.  **Triple Talaq (Muslim Women Protection of Rights on Marriage Act, 2019):** Pronouncement of instant Triple Talaq is void and illegal, punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine.

## Exceptions
1.  **Divorce within 1 year of marriage:** Not permitted under HMA/SMA unless there is a case of exceptional hardship (e.g., extreme physical abuse) or exceptional depravity.
2.  **Customary Divorce:** Section 29(2) of HMA saves customs. If a community has an established, continuous, and unvaried custom of divorce outside the court (e.g., through a Panchayat), it is legally recognized, though increasingly courts insist on a formal judicial decree for clarity.

## Important Timelines
*   **Filing within 1 year:** Generally barred.
*   **Separation period (Mutual Consent):** Minimum 1 year under HMA and SMA. (2 years under Indian Divorce Act, often read as 1 year).
*   **Cooling-off Period:** 6 months (Can be waived by the court).
*   **Maximum period for Second Motion:** Must be filed within 18 months of the First Motion; else, the petition is dismissed.
*   **Appeal Period:** A decree of divorce by a Family Court can be appealed in the High Court within 30 to 90 days from the date of the order, depending on the specific Act. Do not remarry before the appeal period expires.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1.  **Remarrying immediately:** Marrying before the appeal period expires or while an appeal is pending makes the second marriage void and constitutes bigamy.
2.  **Hiding Income:** Filing a false affidavit of income and assets. The Supreme Court strictly penalizes perjury and concealment of financial resources.
3.  **Using Children as Pawns:** Courts severely frown upon parental alienation or denying visitation rights to the other parent.
4.  **Abandoning the Matrimonial Home:** Leaving the home without legal advice can be construed as 'desertion' by the other party.
5.  **Not securing alimony in writing:** Relying on verbal promises for child support or alimony during mutual consent instead of incorporating it into the court decree.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can I get a divorce within 6 months of marriage?**
   **English:** Generally, no. A divorce petition cannot be filed within the first year of marriage to allow the couple time to adjust. However, in cases of extreme physical or mental cruelty (exceptional hardship), you can seek special permission from the court to file early.
   **Hindi:** आम तौर पर, नहीं। विवाह के पहले वर्ष के भीतर तलाक की याचिका दायर नहीं की जा सकती। हालांकि, अत्यधिक क्रूरता के मामलों में आप जल्दी फाइल करने के लिए अदालत से विशेष अनुमति ले सकते हैं।
   **Hinglish:** Normally nahi. Shadi ke pehle saal mein divorce file nahi hota. Par agar extreme cruelty (hardship) ka case hai, toh court se special permission lekar file kar sakte hain.

2. **Is the 6-month cooling-off period mandatory in mutual divorce?**
   **English:** It is a statutory requirement, but the Supreme Court has ruled that Family Courts can waive this period if the parties have already lived separately for a long time (e.g., over 18 months) and all mediation efforts have failed.
   **Hindi:** यह एक वैधानिक आवश्यकता है, लेकिन सुप्रीम कोर्ट ने फैसला दिया है कि पारिवारिक अदालतें इस अवधि को माफ कर सकती हैं यदि पक्ष पहले ही लंबे समय से अलग रह रहे हों और मध्यस्थता के सभी प्रयास विफल हो गए हों।
   **Hinglish:** Yeh law mein hai, par Supreme Court ne kaha hai ki agar couple pehle se hi bohot lamba time alag reh chuka hai aur mediation fail ho chuki hai, toh court is 6-month period ko waive (khatam) kar sakti hai.

3. **What is the difference between alimony and maintenance?**
   **English:** Maintenance usually refers to the monthly financial support given to a spouse during or after the divorce proceedings. Alimony often refers to a lump-sum permanent settlement paid after the divorce decree is granted.
   **Hindi:** भरण-पोषण (Maintenance) आम तौर पर तलाक की कार्यवाही के दौरान या बाद में जीवनसाथी को दी जाने वाली मासिक वित्तीय सहायता को संदर्भित करता है। एलिमनी अक्सर तलाक की डिक्री दिए जाने के बाद भुगतान किए गए एकमुश्त स्थायी निपटान को संदर्भित करती है।
   **Hinglish:** Maintenance ka matlab hai case ke dauran ya baad mein milne wala monthly kharcha. Alimony ka matlab zyada tar ek-saath milne wala lump-sum amount hota hai jo final divorce par milta hai.

4. **Who gets the custody of a child after divorce?**
   **English:** Child custody is decided purely based on the "welfare of the child." Mothers are often preferred for children under 5 years, but fathers have equal rights to claim custody or visitation. Joint custody is also increasingly awarded.
   **Hinglish:** Child custody hamesha "bacche ki bhalayi" (welfare of the child) ke basis par decide hoti hai. 5 saal se chote bacche ke liye mother ko preference milti hai, par father ko bhi equal right aur visitation milta hai.

5. **Can a husband claim maintenance from his wife?**
   **English:** Yes, under Section 24 of the Hindu Marriage Act, a husband can claim maintenance from his wife if he has no independent source of income and she is employed and capable of supporting him.
   **Hindi:** हाँ, हिंदू विवाह अधिनियम की धारा 24 के तहत, एक पति अपनी पत्नी से भरण-पोषण का दावा कर सकता है यदि उसके पास आय का कोई स्वतंत्र स्रोत नहीं है और वह कार्यरत है।

6. **What happens if one spouse does not agree to divorce?**
   **English:** If one spouse refuses to consent, mutual divorce is not possible. The other spouse must file for a contested divorce, proving specific legal grounds like cruelty, desertion, or adultery with solid evidence.
   **Hinglish:** Agar ek spouse nahi maanta, toh mutual divorce nahi ho sakta. Dusre spouse ko contested divorce file karke cruelty, desertion ya adultery jaisa koi ground court mein prove karna padega.

7. **How is property divided after a divorce in India?**
   **English:** India does not have a "community property" law for automatic 50-50 division. Properties held in joint names are divided based on contribution. Properties solely owned by one spouse remain theirs, though the wife has a right to residence until legally divorced and can claim maintenance considering the husband's assets.
   **Hinglish:** India mein 50-50 property division ka strict law nahi hai. Joint name wali property unke contribution ke hisab se batti hai. Single name property usi ki rehti hai, par wife maintenance aur right to residence claim kar sakti hai.

8. **Is an online or WhatsApp Talaq legally valid?**
   **English:** No. The Supreme Court has banned instant Triple Talaq (Talaq-e-Biddat), making it entirely illegal and void. Furthermore, it is a criminal offense punishable by law.
   **Hindi:** नहीं। सुप्रीम कोर्ट ने तत्काल तीन तलाक (तलाक-ए-बिद्दत) पर प्रतिबंध लगा दिया है, जिससे यह पूरी तरह से अवैध और शून्य हो गया है।

9. **Can I remarry immediately after the Family Court grants divorce?**
   **English:** No. You must wait for the statutory appeal period to expire (usually 30 to 90 days depending on the applicable Act). If the other party files an appeal in the High Court, you cannot remarry until the appeal is disposed of.
   **Hinglish:** Nahi. Aapko appeal period (30 se 90 din) khatam hone ka wait karna hoga. Agar dusri party High Court chali gayi, toh jab tak wahan case chalega, aap dobara shadi nahi kar sakte.

10. **What is Restitution of Conjugal Rights (RCR)?**
    **English:** Under Section 9 of HMA, if one spouse has withdrawn from the society of the other without reasonable excuse, the aggrieved spouse can file for RCR, requesting the court to order the other spouse to live together.
    **Hinglish:** Agar ek spouse bina valid reason ke ghar chhod de, toh dusra spouse Section 9 (RCR) file kar sakta hai jisme court spouse ko wapas sath rehne ka order de sakti hai.

11. **Do I need a lawyer for mutual consent divorce?**
    **English:** While it is possible to file as a party in person, it is highly recommended to hire a lawyer to ensure the petition, settlement terms, and affidavits are legally sound and prevent future litigation.

12. **Can divorce be granted based on an incurable mental disorder?**
    **English:** Yes, under Section 13(1)(iii) of the HMA, if a spouse is suffering continuously or intermittently from a mental disorder of such a kind that the petitioner cannot reasonably be expected to live with them.

13. **What is 'desertion' as a ground for divorce?**
    **English:** Desertion means the intentional permanent forsaking and abandonment of one spouse by the other without reasonable cause and without consent, for a continuous period of at least two years immediately preceding the petition.

14. **How long does a contested divorce take?**
    **English:** A contested divorce can take anywhere from 2 to 5 years, or even longer, depending on the complexity of the case, the amount of evidence, and the caseload of the Family Court.

15. **What if the husband refuses to pay court-ordered maintenance?**
    **English:** If the husband fails to pay maintenance, the wife can file an execution petition. The court can attach his salary, bank accounts, or property, and can even sentence him to civil imprisonment for non-compliance.

16. **Can the grounds for divorce be changed from contested to mutual?**
    **English:** Yes, at any stage of a contested divorce proceeding, if the parties reach a settlement, they can withdraw the contested petition and jointly file an application converting it into a mutual consent divorce.

17. **Is counseling mandatory in divorce proceedings?**
    **English:** Yes, the Family Courts Act mandates that at the first instance, the court shall make every endeavor to assist and persuade the parties in arriving at a settlement, often through a court-appointed counselor or mediator.

18. **Can a working woman claim maintenance?**
    **English:** Yes, if the court finds that her independent income is insufficient for her to maintain the same standard of living she enjoyed in her matrimonial home, she can still claim maintenance.

19. **What is 'cruelty' in legal terms?**
    **English:** Cruelty includes both physical violence and mental torture. Constant taunts, denying marital intercourse, forcing a wife to abort, making false allegations, and severe disrespect to the spouse or their parents constitute mental cruelty.

20. **Is 'irretrievable breakdown of marriage' a valid ground for divorce?**
    **English:** It is not explicitly stated in the statutes (HMA/SMA), but the Supreme Court of India can grant a divorce on this ground using its inherent powers under Article 142 of the Constitution to do "complete justice."

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** The wife automatically gets 50% of the husband’s property upon divorce.
   **Fact:** India does not have a 50% automatic division rule. Property division depends on ownership and financial contribution, though courts ensure adequate maintenance.
2. **Myth:** Fathers never get child custody; it always goes to the mother.
   **Fact:** Courts decide custody solely on the child’s welfare. Fathers frequently win custody or joint custody if it serves the child's best interests.
3. **Myth:** You can get a legal divorce on WhatsApp or over email.
   **Fact:** Only a decree passed by a competent civil/family court constitutes a valid legal divorce in India.
4. **Myth:** A husband doesn't have to pay alimony if the wife is working.
   **Fact:** If the wife’s income is not sufficient to match the lifestyle of the matrimonial home, the husband is still liable to pay the deficit as maintenance.
5. **Myth:** Mutual consent divorce can be completed in a few days.
   **Fact:** It takes a minimum of 6 months due to the statutory cooling-off period, unless specifically waived by the court.
6. **Myth:** If you live apart for 5 years, you are automatically divorced.
   **Fact:** Separation does not mean divorce. A formal decree from a court is mandatory to dissolve the marriage legally.
7. **Myth:** Cruelty only means physical beating.
   **Fact:** Mental cruelty (continuous verbal abuse, humiliation, false accusations) is a very strong and commonly used ground for divorce.
8. **Myth:** You can file for divorce immediately after the wedding if you don't like the spouse.
   **Fact:** By law, you cannot file a divorce petition within the first year of marriage without proving exceptional hardship.
9. **Myth:** The husband cannot claim maintenance.
   **Fact:** Under Section 24 of the HMA, a husband without income can claim maintenance from an earning wife.
10. **Myth:** Getting an annulment is the same as getting a divorce.
    **Fact:** Divorce ends a valid marriage. Annulment declares that a valid marriage never existed in the first place (e.g., due to fraud, bigamy).

## Conversation Examples

**Scenario 1: Understanding Mutual Divorce**
**Citizen:** Meri aur meri wife ki bilkul nahi banti, hum 2 saal se alag reh rahe hain. Kya hume court ke chakkar kaatne padenge?
**Assistant:** Agar aap dono aapas mein sehmat hain, toh aap 'Mutual Consent Divorce' (Section 13B, HMA) ke tahat joint petition file kar sakte hain. Isme aapko settlement terms likh kar First Motion file karna hoga. Isme court ke zyada chakkar nahi lagane padte, process relatively fast aur peaceful hota hai.

**Scenario 2: Contested Divorce and Cruelty**
**Citizen:** My husband constantly abuses me emotionally and threatens my family. Can I file for divorce even if he says no?
**Assistant:** Yes, you absolutely can. You can file for a Contested Divorce under Section 13(1) of the Hindu Marriage Act on the grounds of "Mental Cruelty." You will need to provide evidence, such as messages, medical records, or witness statements, to prove the cruelty in the Family Court.

**Scenario 3: Custody of a Young Child**
**Citizen:** Mera beta 3 saal ka hai. Mere husband keh rahe hain ki divorce ke baad baccha unke paas rahega kyunki main working nahi hu. Kya yeh sach hai?
**Assistant:** Nahi, yeh bilkul galat hai. Indian courts ke mutabiq 5 saal se chote bacche ki custody aam taur par maa ko hi milti hai. Agar aap working nahi hain, toh court aapke husband ko order karegi ki woh aapko aur bacche dono ko monthly maintenance dein taaki aap bacche ki parvarish kar sakein.

**Scenario 4: Cooling-off Period Waiver**
**Citizen:** Hum dono 3 saal se alag hain aur humne mutual divorce file kiya hai. Kya hume 6 mahine ka wait karna zaroori hai?
**Assistant:** Supreme Court ki guidelines ke anusaar, agar aap 18 mahine se zyada samay se alag reh rahe hain aur mediation ke baad bhi reconciliation ka koi chance nahi hai, toh aap Family Court mein ek application laga kar 6 mahine ka 'cooling-off period' waive (maaf) karne ki request kar sakte hain.

**Scenario 5: Alimony Calculations**
**Citizen:** How does the court decide how much maintenance my ex-husband has to pay me?
**Assistant:** The Family Court uses guidelines set by the Supreme Court (Rajnesh v. Neha). Both parties must file an 'Affidavit of Assets and Liabilities'. The court checks the husband's income, the wife's needs, the living standard during the marriage, and child support requirements to fix a reasonable monthly maintenance amount.

**Scenario 6: Husband Claiming Maintenance**
**Citizen:** I lost my job due to an accident, and my wife wants a divorce. She earns very well. Can I ask for maintenance?
**Assistant:** Yes. Under Section 24 of the Hindu Marriage Act, the law is gender-neutral regarding interim maintenance. If you have no independent income to support yourself and your wife has a sufficient income, you can file an application seeking maintenance from her during the proceedings.

**Scenario 7: Not appearing in court**
**Citizen:** Meri patni ne mere khilaf divorce case daala hai, par main court nahi jaunga. Toh kya hoga?
**Assistant:** Agar aapko court ka summons mila hai aur aap jaan-boojh kar pesh nahi hote, toh judge 'Ex-Parte' (ek-tarfa) faisla suna sakte hain. Iska matlab court aapki patni ki baaton ko sach maan kar divorce decree pass kar degi aur maintenance bhi tay kar sakti hai, jo aapko manna padega.

**Scenario 8: Bigamy without Divorce**
**Citizen:** My wife and I have been separated for 4 years. Can I just marry someone else now?
**Assistant:** No, you cannot. Merely living apart does not end the marriage legally. You must obtain a legal decree of divorce from the Family Court. If you remarry without a legal divorce, it is an offense of Bigamy under Section 494 of the IPC (now Section 82, BNS), and the second marriage will be void.

**Scenario 9: False 498A Cases**
**Citizen:** Meri wife ne mujh par aur mere parents par jhutha 498A (dowry cruelty) ka case laga diya hai divorce lene ke liye. Main kya karu?
**Assistant:** Aapko turant High Court ya Sessions Court mein Anticipatory Bail (agrim jamanat) ke liye apply karna chahiye. Saath hi, aap unke lagaye gaye jhuthe aaropon ka saboot ikattha karein. Supreme Court ke anusar, jhuthe case lagana apne aap mein 'mental cruelty' hai, jiske aadhar par aap divorce claim kar sakte hain.

**Scenario 10: Withdrawal of Mutual Consent**
**Citizen:** Humne mutual divorce file kiya tha (first motion), par ab mera husband keh raha hai ki woh second motion mein sign nahi karega. Ab kya hoga?
**Assistant:** Mutual divorce mein second motion hone tak kisi bhi party ko apni sehmati wapas lene (withdraw) ka adhikar hai. Agar aapke husband sign nahi karte, toh mutual divorce ki petition dismiss ho jayegi. Aise mein aapko contested divorce file karna padega.

**Scenario 11: NRI Divorce**
**Citizen:** I got married in India, but my husband and I live in the USA. Can we file for divorce in a US court?
**Assistant:** If you both agree and submit to the US court's jurisdiction, a foreign decree is generally valid. However, if it's a contested matter and the grounds don't align with Indian law (Section 13 of HMA or Section 13 of CPC), an Indian court might not recognize the foreign decree. It is safer to file through a Power of Attorney in an Indian court or ensure the foreign decree meets Indian legal standards.

**Scenario 12: Changing Lawyer**
**Citizen:** Mera lawyer theek se case nahi lad raha, kya main beech case mein apna vakil badal sakta hu?
**Assistant:** Haan, aapko apna lawyer badalne ka pura haq hai. Aapko apne purane lawyer se 'NOC' (No Objection Certificate) leni hogi. Uske baad aapka naya lawyer court mein apna 'Vakalatnama' file kar ke case aage badha sakta hai.

**Scenario 13: Refusal to Pay Maintenance**
**Citizen:** Court ne mere ex-husband ko 10,000 rupaye mahina maintenance dene ko bola hai, par woh 4 mahine se paise nahi de raha.
**Assistant:** Aap apne lawyer ke zariye Family Court mein 'Execution Petition' file karein. Court aapke ex-husband ke bank accounts ya salary attach (freeze) kar sakti hai. Agar fir bhi woh nahi dete, toh court unhe civil jail bhej sakti hai.

**Scenario 14: Restitution of Conjugal Rights**
**Citizen:** Meri patni naraz hokar mayke chali gayi hai aur wapas aane se mana kar rahi hai. Mujhe divorce nahi chahiye, main use wapas lana chahta hu.
**Assistant:** Aap Family Court mein Section 9 (Hindu Marriage Act) ke tahat 'Restitution of Conjugal Rights' (RCR) ki petition file kar sakte hain. Isme court unhe notice bhejegi aur poochegi ki woh bina kisi valid reason ke aapse alag kyun reh rahi hain, aur unhe wapas aane ka aadesh de sakti है.

**Scenario 15: Property bought in wife's name**
**Citizen:** Maine apni kamayi se ek flat kharida tha par tax bachane ke liye apni wife ke naam kar diya tha. Ab divorce ho raha hai, flat kiska hoga?
**Assistant:** Benami Transactions Act ke tahat, agar aapne apni legally wedded wife ke naam par property kharidi hai uske benefit ke liye, toh title unhi ka mana jata hai. Halaanki, divorce case mein aap court ko dikha sakte hain ki saara paisa aapne diya tha. Yeh ek complex civil title dispute ban jata hai jo properly litigate karna padega.
