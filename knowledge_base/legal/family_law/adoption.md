# Legal Framework for Adoption in India (भारत में गोद लेने की कानूनी प्रक्रिया)

## Overview
**English:** Adoption in India is a deeply regulated legal process designed to ensure the safety, welfare, and best interests of the child. Unlike informal adoptions, legal adoption severs all ties of the child with their biological family and gives them the exact same rights as a biological child of the adoptive parents. The legal framework is primarily governed by the Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 (HAMA) for Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs, and the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 (JJ Act) for secular adoptions across all religions. The Central Adoption Resource Authority (CARA) is the nodal agency overseeing all adoptions.
**Hindi Unicode:** भारत में गोद लेना एक विनियमित कानूनी प्रक्रिया है जिसका उद्देश्य बच्चे की सुरक्षा, कल्याण और सर्वोत्तम हितों को सुनिश्चित करना है। अनौपचारिक गोद लेने के विपरीत, कानूनी रूप से गोद लेने से बच्चे के अपने जैविक परिवार के साथ सभी संबंध टूट जाते हैं और उसे दत्तक माता-पिता के जैविक बच्चे के समान अधिकार मिल जाते हैं। यह प्रक्रिया मुख्य रूप से हिंदू दत्तक ग्रहण और भरण-पोषण अधिनियम, 1956 (HAMA) और किशोर न्याय अधिनियम, 2015 (JJ Act) द्वारा शासित है। केंद्रीय दत्तक ग्रहण संसाधन प्राधिकरण (CARA) गोद लेने की देखरेख करने वाली नोडल एजेंसी है।

## Quick Summary

## Definition
**English:** "Adoption" is the legal process through which the adopted child is permanently separated from their biological parents and becomes the lawful child of their adoptive parents with all the rights, privileges, and responsibilities that are attached to a biological child.
**Hindi Unicode:** "दत्तक ग्रहण" या गोद लेना वह कानूनी प्रक्रिया है जिसके माध्यम से गोद लिया गया बच्चा अपने जैविक माता-पिता से स्थायी रूप से अलग हो जाता है और सभी अधिकारों और जिम्मेदारियों के साथ अपने दत्तक माता-पिता का कानूनी बच्चा बन जाता है।
**Hinglish:** "Adoption" ek legal process hai jisme baccha apne biological parents se permanently alag hokar apne adoptive parents ka legal child ban jata hai, aur use property, care, aur society mein wahi rights milte hain jo ek biological bacche ko milte hain.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Formal adoption is vital to prevent child trafficking, ensure the child receives legal inheritance rights, and secure official identity documents (like Aadhaar, Passport) with the adoptive parents' names. Informal handovers hold no legal validity.
**Hindi Unicode:** बच्चे की तस्करी को रोकने, बच्चे को कानूनी विरासत के अधिकार सुनिश्चित करने और दत्तक माता-पिता के नाम के साथ आधिकारिक पहचान दस्तावेज (जैसे आधार, पासपोर्ट) प्राप्त करने के लिए औपचारिक गोद लेना महत्वपूर्ण है।
**Hinglish:** Formal adoption isliye important hai taaki child trafficking ruk sake, bacche ko property mein legal hak mil sake, aur uske Aadhaar ya Passport par adoptive parents ka naam officially chadh sake. Informal adoption ki court mein koi value nahi hoti.

## Legal Background
The legal mechanisms governing adoption in India are structured to cater to both personal laws and a uniform secular law.
*   **The Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 (HAMA):**
    *   Applicable only to Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and Sikhs.
    *   Requires a simple giving and taking ceremony.
    *   Registration of an adoption deed (Section 16) provides a presumption of validity.
    *   Sections 7-11 govern the capacity of males and females to adopt, and conditions for adoption (e.g., age difference of 21 years if adopting a child of the opposite sex).
*   **The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 (JJ Act):**
    *   A secular law applicable to all Indian citizens regardless of religion.
    *   Governed by Sections 56 to 73.
    *   Section 56: Provides for adoption of orphan, abandoned, and surrendered children.
    *   Section 57: Eligibility of prospective adoptive parents.
    *   Section 58: Detailed procedure for adoption.
*   **CARA Guidelines 2022:**
    *   Provides the detailed administrative framework, documentation requirements, age limits of parents, and the mandate to use the Child Adoption Resource Information and Guidance System (CARINGS) portal.

## Applicability
*   **Who is Covered:**
    *   Married couples with at least 2 years of stable marital relationship.
    *   Single females can adopt a child of any gender.
    *   Single males can only adopt a male child.
    *   Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs), and Foreign Nationals can adopt under the JJ Act.
*   **Who is Exempt/Ineligible:**
    *   Couples with less than two years of marriage.
    *   Individuals with life-threatening medical conditions.
    *   Couples having three or more children (except in cases of special needs children, relative adoption, or step-parent adoption).
*   **Special Conditions:**
    *   The composite age of the prospective adoptive parents dictates the age of the child they can adopt. E.g., for a child up to 4 years, the composite age of the couple must not exceed 90 years.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English:** Adoptive parents must provide unconditional care, education, and health support. They are legally bound not to discriminate between an adopted and a biological child. Under HAMA, an adoption once made cannot be cancelled by the parents.
**Hindi Unicode:** दत्तक माता-पिता को बिना किसी शर्त के देखभाल, शिक्षा और स्वास्थ्य सहायता प्रदान करनी चाहिए। वे कानूनी रूप से बाध्य हैं कि वे जैविक और गोद लिए गए बच्चे के बीच भेदभाव न करें।
**Hinglish:** Adoptive parents ki duty hai ki wo bacche ko acchi education aur care dein. HAMA ke under, ek baar baccha god le liya toh aap use wapas nahi kar sakte.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Registration:** Register online on the CARINGS portal with necessary documents.
2. **Home Study:** A Social Worker from a Specialized Adoption Agency (SAA) conducts a Home Study Report (HSR) within 30 days to assess suitability.
3. **Referral:** Based on seniority, prospective parents are shown profiles of children legally free for adoption. They have 48 hours to reserve a child.
4. **Matching & Pre-adoption Foster Care:** The parents visit the SAA, interact with the child, and take the child in pre-adoption foster care.
5. **Court Order:** The SAA files an adoption petition in the District Court or Family Court. The court verifies documents and passes an Adoption Order within 60 days.
6. **Birth Certificate:** Post court order, a new birth certificate is issued for the child reflecting the adoptive parents' names.

**Hindi Unicode:**
1. **पंजीकरण:** आवश्यक दस्तावेजों के साथ CARINGS पोर्टल पर ऑनलाइन पंजीकरण करें।
2. **होम स्टडी:** एक सामाजिक कार्यकर्ता 30 दिनों के भीतर आपकी उपयुक्तता का आकलन करने के लिए होम स्टडी रिपोर्ट तैयार करता है।
3. **रेफरल:** वरिष्ठता के आधार पर, माता-पिता को गोद लेने के लिए कानूनी रूप से स्वतंत्र बच्चों के प्रोफाइल दिखाए जाते हैं।
4. **प्री-अडॉप्शन फोस्टर केयर:** माता-पिता एजेंसी जाते हैं, बच्चे से मिलते हैं और उसे फोस्टर केयर में घर लाते हैं।
5. **न्यायालय का आदेश:** जिला न्यायालय में याचिका दायर की जाती है, जो 60 दिनों के भीतर गोद लेने का आदेश पारित करता है।

## Required Documents
*   PAN Card and Aadhaar Card / Passport.
*   Marriage Certificate (for couples).
*   Proof of Income (IT Returns for the last 3 years, Salary Slips).
*   Medical Fitness Certificate of prospective parents.
*   Photographs of the prospective adoptive parents.
*   Two reference letters from persons who know the family well (not immediate relatives).
*   Divorce Decree / Death Certificate of spouse (if applicable).

## Government Authorities
*   **CARA (Central Adoption Resource Authority):** The apex body under the Ministry of Women & Child Development.
*   **SARA (State Adoption Resource Agency):** Monitors adoptions at the state level.
*   **SAA (Specialised Adoption Agency):** Authorized child care institutions that house children and facilitate matching.
*   **CWC (Child Welfare Committee):** The authority that declares a child "legally free for adoption".
*   **District Courts / Family Courts:** Issue the final and binding legal Adoption Order.

## Penalties & Punishments
*   **Section 80 of JJ Act (Punitive Measures):** Any person or organization offering or receiving an orphan, abandoned, or surrendered child for the purpose of adoption without following the provisions of the JJ Act is liable for imprisonment up to 3 years, or a fine of ₹1 Lakh, or both.
*   **Trafficking:** Buying or selling a child under the guise of adoption invokes severe trafficking laws under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS).

## Exceptions
*   **Step-Parent Adoption:** A step-parent can adopt the biological child of their spouse without age-difference restrictions, requiring consent from the other biological parent.
*   **Relative Adoption:** A person can adopt the child of a relative (paternal or maternal aunt/uncle, grandparents) with a slightly simplified procedure under the JJ Act, though a court order remains mandatory.

## Important Timelines
*   **Home Study Report (HSR):** Must be completed within 30 days of registration and assignment to an SAA.
*   **Child Reservation:** 48 hours to reserve a child upon receiving a referral.
*   **Filing Court Petition:** Within 10 days of accepting the child.
*   **Court Disposal Time:** The court is mandated to dispose of the adoption application within 60 days of filing (as per Section 61, JJ Act).
*   **Follow-up Reports:** SAAs must upload post-adoption follow-up reports every 6 months for a period of 2 years.

## Common Legal Mistakes
*   **Hospital Handovers:** Taking a child directly from a biological mother or a hospital staff member without CARA intervention. This is illegal and does not confer any legal rights.
*   **Unregistered Deeds:** Under HAMA, executing a notarized paper without a proper religious ceremony (Datta Homa/giving and taking) or failing to register the adoption deed.
*   **Paying Brokers:** Paying middlemen, lawyers, or NGOs directly to "buy" or source a child. Only official CARA fees are legally permissible.

## Safety Tips
**English:** Always route your adoption application exclusively through the CARINGS portal. Never trust brokers or touts offering immediate adoption for money.
**Hindi Unicode:** हमेशा CARINGS पोर्टल के माध्यम से ही गोद लेने के लिए आवेदन करें। कभी भी ऐसे दलालों पर भरोसा न करें जो पैसे के लिए तुरंत बच्चा गोद दिलाने की पेशकश करते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Adoption ke liye sirf official CARA/CARINGS website (cara.nic.in) ka hi use karein. Kisi bhi agent ya hospital staff ko paise dekar baccha na lein, yeh illegal hai aur jail ho sakti hai.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can a single man adopt a female child in India?**
   **English:** No. Under the JJ Act and CARA guidelines, a single male is strictly prohibited from adopting a female child. He can only adopt a male child.
   **Hindi Unicode:** नहीं। जेजे अधिनियम के अनुसार, एक अकेला पुरुष किसी बालिका को गोद नहीं ले सकता। वह केवल बालक को ही गोद ले सकता है।
   **Hinglish:** Nahi, law ke hisaab se ek single unmarried man kisi ladki ko god nahi le sakta. Wo sirf ladke ko god le sakta hai.

2. **Is it legal to adopt a child directly from a hospital or a relative without informing authorities?**
   **English:** No. Direct adoptions from hospitals are illegal and amount to child trafficking. Even relative adoptions must be processed legally through the court under the JJ Act or HAMA to be valid.
   **Hindi Unicode:** नहीं। अस्पताल से सीधे बच्चे को गोद लेना अवैध है। रिश्तेदारों से गोद लेने की प्रक्रिया को भी कानूनी रूप से अदालत के माध्यम से पूरा किया जाना चाहिए।

3. **Can Muslims, Christians, or Parsis adopt legally in India?**

4. **What is the maximum age limit for parents to adopt a newborn?**

5. **Does the biological mother have a say once the child is legally adopted?**

12. **Can live-in couples adopt a child?**
    **English:** No. According to CARA regulations, only couples with a minimum of two years of stable marital relationship are eligible to apply together as prospective adoptive parents.
    **Hindi Unicode:** नहीं। CARA नियमों के अनुसार, केवल वे जोड़े जिनकी कम से कम दो साल की स्थिर शादी हो, एक साथ आवेदन करने के पात्र हैं।
    **Hinglish:** Nahi, live-in couples legally baccha god nahi le sakte. Adopt karne ke liye aapki 2 saal ki stable marriage honi zaroori hai.

13. **What happens if I adopt a child through an unregistered deed under HAMA?**
    **English:** An unregistered deed does not automatically prove adoption. Under Section 16 of HAMA, a registered adoption deed raises a legal presumption of validity. An unregistered deed may face severe legal challenges regarding inheritance and valid adoption.
    **Hindi Unicode:** अपंजीकृत डीड स्वचालित रूप से गोद लेने को साबित नहीं करती है। HAMA की धारा 16 के तहत, पंजीकृत डीड कानूनी मान्यता देती है।
    **Hinglish:** Agar aapne sirf notary paper par adoption deed banwayi aur registrar ke paas register nahi karwayi, toh property ke disputes mein us adoption ko challenge kiya ja sakta hai.

14. **How long is the wait time on CARINGS?**
    **English:** The waiting period for adopting a normal, healthy child up to 2 years of age can be quite long, often spanning 2 to 3 years, due to the high volume of registered parents versus legally free children.
    **Hindi Unicode:** एक स्वस्थ छोटे बच्चे को गोद लेने की प्रतीक्षा अवधि अक्सर 2 से 3 वर्ष तक हो सकती है क्योंकि पंजीकृत माता-पिता की संख्या उपलब्ध बच्चों से बहुत अधिक है।
    **Hinglish:** Healthy chote bacche ke liye waiting list bahut lambi hai. Normally 2 se 3 saal lag sakte hain kyunki apply karne wale parents jyada hain.

15. **Can an NRI adopt an Indian child?**
    **English:** Yes, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs) can adopt Indian children under the inter-country adoption framework regulated by CARA and the JJ Act.
    **Hindi Unicode:** हाँ, अनिवासी भारतीय (NRI) और OCI, CARA द्वारा विनियमित अंतर-देशीय गोद लेने के ढांचे के तहत भारतीय बच्चों को गोद ले सकते हैं।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, NRIs aur OCIs CARA ke inter-country adoption rules ke hisaab se India se baccha god le sakte hain. Iska process thoda alag aur lamba hota hai.

16. **Are transgender persons allowed to adopt?**
    **English:** The legal landscape is evolving. Currently, the JJ Act does not explicitly bar transgender individuals, and they may apply as single parents. However, specific CARA regulations and societal evaluations during home studies apply.
    **Hindi Unicode:** वर्तमान में, जेजे अधिनियम स्पष्ट रूप से ट्रांसजेंडर व्यक्तियों को रोकता नहीं है, और वे एकल माता-पिता के रूप में आवेदन कर सकते हैं।
    **Hinglish:** Law mein expressly mana nahi kiya gaya hai. Transgender log as single parent apply kar sakte hain, par agency unka home study aur financial stability check karegi.

17. **Can adoptive parents return the child if they face issues?**
    **English:** No. Once the final adoption order is passed by the court, the adoption is irrevocable. The parents have the same legal responsibilities as they would for a biological child. Abandoning the child is a criminal offense.
    **Hindi Unicode:** नहीं। एक बार अदालत का आदेश पारित होने के बाद, गोद लेना अपरिवर्तनीय है। बच्चे को छोड़ना एक आपराधिक कृत्य है।
    **Hinglish:** Ek baar court order milne ke baad bacche ko 'return' nahi kiya ja sakta. Agar parents bacche ko abandon karte hain, toh unpar criminal charges lagenge.

18. **What is a "special needs" child in CARA?**
    **English:** A special needs child is one with a physical or mental disability, or a severe medical condition. The waiting period for adopting special needs children is significantly shorter, and parents can choose a child directly from the portal without waiting for a referral.
    **Hindi Unicode:** विशेष आवश्यकता वाले बच्चे शारीरिक या मानसिक विकलांगता या गंभीर चिकित्सा स्थिति वाले होते हैं। ऐसे बच्चों को गोद लेने की प्रतीक्षा अवधि बहुत कम होती है।
    **Hinglish:** Special needs category mein wo bacche aate hain jinhe koi disability ya medical issue hota hai. In baccho ko god lene ka process fast hota hai aur waiting nahi hoti.

19. **What is pre-adoption foster care?**
    **English:** After the parents accept a child and sign the matching documents, the child is placed in their care temporarily before the final court order is passed. This is known as pre-adoption foster care.
    **Hindi Unicode:** अंतिम अदालती आदेश पारित होने से पहले बच्चे को अस्थायी रूप से माता-पिता की देखभाल में रखा जाता है। इसे प्री-अडॉप्शन फोस्टर केयर कहा जाता है।
    **Hinglish:** Court order aane se pehle, papers sign karke bacche ko ghar laane ka jo temporary period hota hai, use pre-adoption foster care kehte hain.

20. **Can older children be adopted?**
    **English:** Yes. Children up to 18 years of age who are legally free can be adopted. Older prospective parents (e.g., composite age up to 110 years for couples) are eligible to adopt older children.
    **Hindi Unicode:** हाँ। कानूनी रूप से स्वतंत्र 18 वर्ष तक के बच्चों को गोद लिया जा सकता है।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, bade baccho (18 saal tak) ko bhi adopt kiya ja sakta hai. Bade parents (jinki age zyada hai) specially bade baccho ko hi god le sakte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Adopting a child from a distant relative secretly is fine as long as they agree.
   **Fact:** Any adoption without a court order or formal registered deed (for Hindus) is legally invalid and can be challenged, denying the child their fundamental inheritance rights.
2. **Myth:** You can buy a child if you pay enough money to an NGO or hospital.
   **Fact:** Buying a child is a severe criminal offense under anti-trafficking laws and the JJ Act. Legal adoption involves strict, transparent procedures via CARA.
3. **Myth:** Single mothers cannot adopt boys.
   **Fact:** Single females are fully eligible to adopt a child of any gender, including boys. It is single men who cannot adopt girls.
4. **Myth:** Once an adopted child turns 18, their biological parents can claim them back.
   **Fact:** Legal adoption severs all rights of biological parents permanently. They have no legal claim over the child at any age.
5. **Myth:** Adoptive parents can change the child's date of birth.
   **Fact:** The date of birth is fixed based on medical examinations or prior records by the CWC and cannot be altered arbitrarily by the adoptive parents.
6. **Myth:** Muslims and Christians cannot adopt in India, they can only be guardians.
   **Fact:** Before 2015, they could only be guardians under the G&W Act. With the JJ Act 2015, Muslims, Christians, and Parsis can completely and legally adopt.
7. **Myth:** You need to own a house to adopt a child.
   **Fact:** You do not need to own a house; you only need to show stable income and a safe living environment (even if rented).
8. **Myth:** If you already have a boy, you cannot adopt another boy.
   **Fact:** Under HAMA, you cannot adopt a child of the same gender as your living biological child. Under JJ Act, however, there are fewer restrictions on gender, though CARA generally encourages adopting the opposite gender.
9. **Myth:** The adoption process in India can be completed in one month.
   **Fact:** Legal adoption through CARA takes an average of 2-3 years for healthy young children due to rigorous checks and long waiting lists.
10. **Myth:** The child's original birth certificate is just modified with a pen.
    **Fact:** A brand new birth certificate is legally issued by the municipal authorities replacing the biological parents' names with the adoptive parents' names based on the Court Order.

## Conversation Examples

**Example 1:**
**Citizen:** I want to adopt my deceased sister's son. Do I still need to go to court?
**Assistant:** Yes. While relative adoptions are allowed under both HAMA and the JJ Act, it is highly recommended to secure a valid court order or a registered adoption deed (if both parties are Hindus) to ensure the child gets full legal rights and faces no property disputes in the future.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** Haan. Relative adoption mein bhi court order (JJ Act) ya registered deed (HAMA) ki zaroorat hoti hai taaki bacche ko property mein aage chalkar koi dikkat na aaye.

**Example 2:**
**Citizen:** Meri shadi ko 1 saal hua hai, kya main abhi apply kar sakti hu?
**Assistant:** As per CARA guidelines, married couples must have at least two years of a stable marital relationship to be eligible to apply for adoption. You will need to wait one more year to register.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** CARA ke rules ke anusaar, couples ki shadi ko kam se kam 2 saal poore hone chahiye taaki marriage ki stability confirm ho sake. Aapko ek saal aur rukna hoga apply karne ke liye.

**Example 3:**
**Citizen:** Hospital ka staff mujhe 2 lakh rupaye mein ek anath baccha de raha hai. Kya ye legal hai?
**Assistant:** No, this is absolutely illegal and is considered child trafficking. You and the hospital staff can be jailed for up to 3 years under Section 80 of the JJ Act. Please only adopt through the official CARINGS portal.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** Bilkul nahi, ye baccho ki taskari (trafficking) hai aur gair-kanooni hai. Isme aapko aur staff ko JJ Act ke Section 80 ke tahat 3 saal ki jail ho sakti hai. Hamesha CARA ke through hi legally baccha god lein.

**Example 4:**
**Citizen:** I am an unmarried 30-year-old male. Can I adopt a baby girl?
**Assistant:** No. Under the JJ Act and CARA regulations, a single prospective adoptive male is not permitted to adopt a female child. You are only eligible to adopt a male child.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** Nahi, kanoon ke anusar ek single male kisi bacchi (girl child) ko god nahi le sakta. Aap sirf ladke (male child) ko hi god le sakte hain.

**Example 5:**
**Citizen:** How much official fee do I have to pay to CARA?
**Assistant:** CARA has prescribed fees that you pay to the adoption agency, not directly to CARA. It is approximately ₹40,000 for Indian citizens, covering the Home Study Report, childcare corpus, and post-adoption follow-ups.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** Official fee lagbhag ₹40,000 hoti hai jo aapko agency ko deni hoti hai. Isme Home Study aur bacche ki care ke charges shamil hote hain. Extra paise maangna illegal hai.

**Example 6:**
**Citizen:** Kya musalman baccha god le sakte hain? Hamaare personal law mein to mana hai.
**Assistant:** Yes, you can. While Muslim personal law emphasizes guardianship (Kafala), the Juvenile Justice Act, 2015 is a secular law. The Supreme Court has ruled that any Indian citizen, including Muslims, can legally adopt a child under the JJ Act, giving the child full rights.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** Haan, aap le sakte hain. Supreme Court ke anusar JJ Act 2015 ek secular law hai, iske tahat Muslim families legally baccha god le sakti hain aur bacche ko property ke saare rights milenge.

**Example 7:**
**Citizen:** Mera apna 5 saal ka beta hai. Kya main ek aur beta god le sakta hu?
**Assistant:** Under the Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act (HAMA), you cannot adopt a son if you already have a biological son. However, under the JJ Act via CARA, while the system prioritizes placing children in families with no children, it is possible, though wait times may be longer.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** HAMA law ke under agar aapka ek beta hai toh aap dusra beta god nahi le sakte. Lekin CARA/JJ Act ke throuh aap apply kar sakte hain, haalan ki preference unko milti hai jinke paas bacche nahi hain.

**Example 8:**
**Citizen:** How do we prove our income for adoption?
**Assistant:** You need to upload your Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last three years, salary slips, and a bank statement on the CARINGS portal to prove your financial stability.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** Income proof ke liye aapko pichle 3 saal ke ITR (Income Tax Returns), salary slips aur bank statements CARINGS portal par upload karne honge.

**Example 9:**
**Citizen:** Kya adoption court order ke baad bacche ka birth certificate badla jata hai?
**Assistant:** Yes. After the District Court passes the final adoption order, the specialized adoption agency applies for a new birth certificate. The new certificate will have your names as the mother and father.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** Haan, Court order milne ke baad municipal corporation naya birth certificate issue karti hai, jisme biological parents ki jagah aapka (adoptive parents) naam likha hota hai.

**Example 10:**
**Citizen:** The agency is taking too long. Can I contact an NGO to get a baby faster?
**Assistant:** No. Legal adoptions can only be processed through Specialized Adoption Agencies (SAAs) registered on the CARINGS portal. Private NGOs cannot bypass CARA. Any faster route bypassing the portal is illegal.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** Nahi. Legal adoption sirf CARINGS portal aur registered agencies (SAA) ke throuh hi hota hai. Kisi private NGO se baccha lena illegal hai.

**Example 11:**
**Citizen:** I have a severe heart condition. Will my application be rejected?

**Citizen:** What happens in the Home Study?
**Assistant:** A qualified social worker will visit your home to interact with you and your family. They will assess your living conditions, financial stability, emotional readiness, and motivation to adopt, submitting a Home Study Report (HSR) within 30 days.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** Home study mein agency ka social worker aapke ghar aakar check karta hai ki aapka ghar kaisa hai, aap emotionally aur financially kitne stable hain baccha paalne ke liye.

**Citizen:** Kya single aurat baccha god le sakti hai?

**Example 14:**
**Citizen:** Are there follow-up visits after we get the baby?
**Assistant:** Yes. After the child is placed with you, the social worker will conduct post-adoption follow-up visits and submit reports every 6 months for a period of 2 years to ensure the child is adjusting well.
**Hindi/Hinglish:** Haan, baccha ghar aane ke baad 2 saal tak, har 6 mahine mein agency ka social worker aapke ghar visit karega yeh check karne ke liye ki baccha theek aur khush hai ya nahi.
