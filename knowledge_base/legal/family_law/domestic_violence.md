# Domestic Violence Laws in India: Complete Guide to Rights and Protection

## Overview
**English:** Domestic violence in India is addressed primarily through the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (PWDVA), which provides civil remedies, and the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS), which imposes criminal penalties. It covers physical, emotional, verbal, sexual, and economic abuse within a shared household.
**Hindi (Unicode):** भारत में घरेलू हिंसा से निपटने के लिए मुख्य रूप से घरेलू हिंसा से महिलाओं का संरक्षण अधिनियम, 2005 (PWDVA) है, जो नागरिक उपचार प्रदान करता है, और भारतीय न्याय संहिता, 2023 (BNS) है, जो आपराधिक दंड लगाता है। इसमें साझे घर में शारीरिक, भावनात्मक, मौखिक, यौन और आर्थिक दुर्व्यवहार शामिल है।
**Hinglish:** India mein domestic violence ko handle karne ke liye Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (PWDVA) aur Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023 hai. Yeh physical, emotional, verbal, sexual aur economic abuse ko cover karta hai jo shared household mein hota hai.

## Quick Summary
**English:** A woman facing abuse can seek protection, residence, monetary relief, and custody orders under PWDVA, while the abuser can be criminally prosecuted under Section 85 of BNS (formerly IPC 498A).
**Hindi (Unicode):** दुर्व्यवहार का सामना कर रही महिला PWDVA के तहत संरक्षण, निवास, मौद्रिक राहत और कस्टडी आदेश मांग सकती है, जबकि दुर्व्यवहार करने वाले पर BNS की धारा 85 (पहले IPC 498A) के तहत आपराधिक मुकदमा चलाया जा सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Abuse face kar rahi woman PWDVA ke under protection, residence, maintenance aur custody orders claim kar sakti hai, jabki abuser par BNS Section 85 (purana IPC 498A) ke under criminal case chalaya ja sakta hai.

## Definition
**English:** Domestic Violence includes any act, omission, or conduct that harms or endangers the health, safety, life, limb, or well-being (mental or physical) of an aggrieved person, or tends to do so, including physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse.
**Hindi (Unicode):** घरेलू हिंसा में कोई भी ऐसा कार्य, चूक या आचरण शामिल है जो किसी व्यथित व्यक्ति के स्वास्थ्य, सुरक्षा, जीवन, अंग या भलाई (मानसिक या शारीरिक) को नुकसान पहुंचाता है या खतरे में डालता है, जिसमें शारीरिक, यौन, मौखिक, भावनात्मक और आर्थिक दुर्व्यवहार शामिल है।
**Hinglish:** Domestic violence ka matlab hai koi bhi aisi harkat jo kisi victim ki health, safety, ya well-being ko physical, mental, ya economic harm pahunchaye.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Recognizing and addressing domestic violence is crucial for safeguarding fundamental human rights, ensuring women's safety, preventing long-term psychological trauma, and holding perpetrators accountable.
**Hindi (Unicode):** मौलिक मानवाधिकारों की रक्षा, महिलाओं की सुरक्षा सुनिश्चित करने, दीर्घकालिक मनोवैज्ञानिक आघात को रोकने और अपराधियों को जवाबदेह ठहराने के लिए घरेलू हिंसा को पहचानना और उसका समाधान करना महत्वपूर्ण है।
**Hinglish:** Domestic violence ko rokna zaroori hai taaki auraton ki safety ensure ho sake, human rights bache rahein aur abusers ko saza mil sake.

## Legal Background
**History & Purpose:** Historically, domestic abuse was tackled under IPC 498A. Recognizing the need for immediate civil reliefs (like a roof over one's head and financial support), the PWDVA was enacted in 2005. Recently, IPC has been replaced by the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023.
**Applicable Acts:** 
- Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act (PWDVA), 2005
- Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023
- Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023
- Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023
**Relevant Sections:**
- PWDVA Section 3: Definition of Domestic Violence.
- PWDVA Sections 18-22: Protection orders, Residence orders, Monetary reliefs, Custody orders, Compensation orders.
- BNS Section 85 (Replaces IPC 498A): Cruelty by husband or relatives of husband.
- BNS Section 86: Cruelty defined.
- BNSS Section 173 (Replaces CrPC 154): Filing an FIR for cognizable offenses.

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** Any woman who is or has been in a domestic relationship with the respondent (husband, live-in partner, or relatives of the husband/partner). It also covers sisters, widows, or mothers living in a shared household. Children can also be included through the mother's application.
- **Who is exempt:** The law is currently gender-specific under PWDVA, protecting primarily women. Men cannot file a case under PWDVA against their wives, though they may seek other remedies under family law or general criminal law.
- **Special Conditions:** The aggrieved person and the respondent must have lived together in a "shared household" at some point in a domestic relationship (marriage, relationship in the nature of marriage, consanguinity, adoption, or joint family).

## Rights of Citizens
**English:** Victims have the right to reside in the shared household, the right to free legal aid, the right to protection orders against the abuser, and the right to privacy and confidentiality during proceedings.
**Hindi (Unicode):** पीड़ितों को साझे घर में रहने का अधिकार, मुफ्त कानूनी सहायता का अधिकार, दुर्व्यवहार करने वाले के खिलाफ संरक्षण आदेश का अधिकार, और कार्यवाही के दौरान गोपनीयता का अधिकार है।
**Hinglish:** Victims ko shared ghar mein rehne ka right, free legal aid, protection orders, aur privacy ka right milta hai.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English:** Citizens have a duty to report incidents of domestic violence if they witness them. Protection Officers must assist the victim in filing the Domestic Incident Report (DIR) and medical examinations.
**Hindi (Unicode):** नागरिकों का कर्तव्य है कि यदि वे घरेलू हिंसा की घटनाओं को देखते हैं तो उसकी रिपोर्ट करें। संरक्षण अधिकारियों को घरेलू घटना रिपोर्ट (DIR) दर्ज करने और चिकित्सा परीक्षण में पीड़िता की सहायता करनी चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Citizens ki duty hai ki domestic violence dekhein toh report karein. Protection Officers ka farz hai victim ki help karna report (DIR) file karne mein.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:** 
1. **Filing Complaint:** Approach Police, Protection Officer, or Service Provider to file a Domestic Incident Report (DIR).
2. **Magistrate Approach:** File an application before the Judicial Magistrate under Section 12 of PWDVA.
3. **Notices & Counseling:** Magistrate issues notice to the respondent. Counseling may be ordered.
4. **Interim Orders:** Magistrate may pass ex-parte interim protection, residence, or maintenance orders.
5. **Evidence & Hearing:** Both parties submit evidence.
6. **Final Judgment:** Magistrate passes final orders (Protection, Residence, Monetary relief).
7. **Criminal Route:** Simultaneously, an FIR can be filed under BNS Section 85 (Cruelty) at the local police station.

**Hindi (Unicode):**
1. **शिकायत दर्ज करना:** घरेलू घटना रिपोर्ट (DIR) दर्ज करने के लिए पुलिस, संरक्षण अधिकारी या सेवा प्रदाता से संपर्क करें।
2. **मजिस्ट्रेट के पास जाना:** PWDVA की धारा 12 के तहत न्यायिक मजिस्ट्रेट के समक्ष एक आवेदन दाखिल करें।
3. **नोटिस और काउंसलिंग:** मजिस्ट्रेट प्रतिवादी को नोटिस जारी करता है। काउंसलिंग का आदेश दिया जा सकता है।
4. **अंतरिम आदेश:** मजिस्ट्रेट एकतरफा अंतरिम संरक्षण, निवास या भरण-पोषण आदेश पारित कर सकता है।
5. **साक्ष्य और सुनवाई:** दोनों पक्ष साक्ष्य प्रस्तुत करते हैं।
6. **अंतिम निर्णय:** मजिस्ट्रेट अंतिम आदेश (संरक्षण, निवास, मौद्रिक राहत) पारित करता है।
7. **आपराधिक मार्ग:** साथ ही, स्थानीय पुलिस स्टेशन में BNS की धारा 85 (क्रूरता) के तहत FIR दर्ज की जा सकती है।

**Hinglish:**
1. **Complaint Filing:** Police, Protection Officer, ya Service Provider ke paas Domestic Incident Report (DIR) file karein.
2. **Magistrate Approach:** PWDVA Section 12 ke under Magistrate ke paas application lagayein.
3. **Notices & Counseling:** Magistrate respondent ko notice bhejta hai.
4. **Interim Orders:** Magistrate jaldi relief ke liye interim orders paas kar sakta hai.
5. **Evidence & Hearing:** Dono parties proof submit karti hain.
6. **Final Judgment:** Court final orders paas karta hai.
7. **Criminal Route:** Sath mein BNS Section 85 ke under Police station mein FIR bhi ho sakti hai.

## Required Documents
1. Proof of Identity (Aadhaar, Voter ID).
2. Marriage Certificate or Proof of live-in relationship (photographs, joint accounts).
3. Proof of shared household (Rent agreement, utility bills).
4. Medical reports (if physical abuse occurred).
5. Police complaints (NC/FIR copies, if any).
6. Evidence of abuse (WhatsApp chats, emails, audio/video recordings).
7. Bank statements (to claim monetary relief/maintenance).

## Government Authorities
- **Police:** For immediate rescue and filing FIR under BNS 85.
- **Protection Officers (PO):** Appointed by state governments to assist victims in filing cases and implementing court orders.
- **Service Providers:** Registered NGOs that provide medical, legal, and shelter assistance.
- **Judicial Magistrate First Class / Metropolitan Magistrate:** Hears PWDVA cases.
- **National / State Commission for Women:** Can be approached for intervention.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **BNS Section 85 (Cruelty by Husband/Relatives):** Imprisonment for a term which may extend to 3 years and a fine.
- **Breach of Protection Order (PWDVA Section 31):** If the respondent violates a protection order, it is a cognizable and non-bailable offense, punishable with imprisonment up to 1 year, or a fine up to Rs. 20,000, or both.
- **Dowry Death (BNS Section 80, replacing IPC 304B):** Imprisonment for not less than 7 years, extending up to life imprisonment.

## Exceptions
- General wear and tear of married life or trivial quarrels do not amount to domestic violence or cruelty under BNS Section 85.
- Reasonable self-defense actions are not considered abuse.

## Important Timelines
- **First Hearing:** Magistrate must fix the first hearing within 3 days of receiving the application.
- **Case Disposal:** Effort must be made to dispose of every application within 60 days from the first hearing.
- **Appeal:** An appeal against the Magistrate's order can be filed in the Sessions Court within 30 days.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Delaying the filing of a complaint or medical examination after an incident of physical abuse.
- Leaving the shared household voluntarily without securing a legal residence order, which may weaken the claim.
- Not keeping copies of crucial evidence (chats, medical reports) in a safe place.
- Failing to involve the Protection Officer, leading to procedural delays.
- Using false allegations, which can backfire and result in counter-cases for defamation or perjury.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Can a live-in partner file a domestic violence case?**
**English:** Yes, PWDVA covers relationships "in the nature of marriage," meaning live-in partners can file a case and seek protection and maintenance.
**Hindi:** हां, PWDVA में "विवाह की प्रकृति" वाले संबंध शामिल हैं, जिसका अर्थ है कि लिव-इन पार्टनर मामला दर्ज कर सकते हैं और संरक्षण मांग सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, live-in partners bhi PWDVA ke under case file kar sakti hain aur protection maang sakti hain.

**Q2: Can I get my husband removed from the house?**
**English:** Yes, the Magistrate can pass a residence order directing the respondent to remove himself from the shared household to protect the victim.
**Hindi:** हां, मजिस्ट्रेट पीड़िता की सुरक्षा के लिए प्रतिवादी को साझे घर से बाहर निकलने का निर्देश देते हुए निवास आदेश पारित कर सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, court residence order paas karke husband ko shared ghar se bahar nikalne ka order de sakta hai.

**Q3: What if I have no proof of physical abuse?**
**English:** Domestic violence includes emotional, verbal, and economic abuse. You can provide evidence like abusive messages, witness testimonies, or recordings.
**Hindi:** घरेलू हिंसा में भावनात्मक, मौखिक और आर्थिक दुर्व्यवहार भी शामिल है। आप अपमानजनक संदेश या गवाह के बयान जैसे सबूत दे सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Emotional aur verbal abuse bhi domestic violence hai. Aap messages ya witnesses ko as proof use kar sakte hain.

**Q4: Is an FIR required to file a case under PWDVA?**
**English:** No, PWDVA is a civil law mechanism. An FIR is not required to seek protection, maintenance, or residence orders.
**Hindi:** नहीं, PWDVA एक दीवानी कानून तंत्र है। संरक्षण या भरण-पोषण के लिए FIR आवश्यक नहीं है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, PWDVA civil law hai. Protection ya maintenance ke liye FIR ki zaroorat nahi hoti.

**Q5: Can I claim maintenance under PWDVA?**
**English:** Yes, you can claim monetary relief under Section 20 of PWDVA to cover expenses incurred and losses suffered due to domestic violence.
**Hindi:** हां, आप घरेलू हिंसा के कारण हुए खर्चों और नुकसान को कवर करने के लिए PWDVA की धारा 20 के तहत मौद्रिक राहत का दावा कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, aap Section 20 ke under financial losses aur expenses cover karne ke liye maintenance claim kar sakti hain.

**Q6: Against whom can a DV complaint be filed?**
**English:** It can be filed against any adult male partner and his relatives. A complaint can also be filed against female relatives of the husband/partner.
**Hindi:** यह किसी भी वयस्क पुरुष साथी और उसके रिश्तेदारों के खिलाफ दायर किया जा सकता है। पति की महिला रिश्तेदारों के खिलाफ भी शिकायत दर्ज की जा सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Yeh husband/partner aur uske relatives (including female relatives) ke khilaf file kiya ja sakta hai.

**Q7: How much time does it take to get a protection order?**
**English:** The law mandates that the Magistrate must try to dispose of the application within 60 days. Interim orders can be passed within a few days.
**Hindi:** कानून के अनुसार मजिस्ट्रेट को 60 दिनों के भीतर आवेदन का निपटारा करना चाहिए। अंतरिम आदेश कुछ ही दिनों में पारित किए जा सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Court ko 60 days mein case dispose karna hota hai. Interim order kuch hi din mein mil sakta hai.

**Q8: What happens if the husband disobeys the protection order?**
**English:** Breach of a protection order is a criminal offense under Section 31 of PWDVA, punishable with up to 1 year in jail.
**Hindi:** संरक्षण आदेश का उल्लंघन PWDVA की धारा 31 के तहत एक आपराधिक अपराध है, जिसमें 1 साल तक की जेल हो सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Agar husband protection order todta hai, toh use 1 saal tak ki jail ho sakti hai.

**Q9: Can a mother-in-law file a DV case against her daughter-in-law?**
**English:** No, under PWDVA, a mother-in-law cannot file a case against a daughter-in-law. She can, however, file a case against her son.
**Hindi:** नहीं, PWDVA के तहत, सास बहू के खिलाफ मामला दर्ज नहीं कर सकती है। हालांकि, वह अपने बेटे के खिलाफ मामला दर्ज कर सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, mother-in-law daughter-in-law ke khilaf DV case nahi kar sakti, par apne bete ke khilaf kar sakti hai.

**Q10: Do I need a lawyer to file a DV case?**
**English:** While it is advisable to have a lawyer, you can also file a case through a Protection Officer or Service Provider without a lawyer.
**Hindi:** यद्यपि वकील होना उचित है, लेकिन आप वकील के बिना संरक्षण अधिकारी के माध्यम से भी मामला दर्ज कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Lawyer hona better hai, par aap Protection Officer ke through bhi bina lawyer case file kar sakte hain.

**Q11: Can a working woman claim maintenance under DV Act?**
**English:** Yes, a working woman can claim maintenance if her income is not sufficient to maintain the lifestyle she is accustomed to in the shared household.
**Hindi:** हां, एक कामकाजी महिला भरण-पोषण का दावा कर सकती है यदि उसकी आय उस जीवन शैली को बनाए रखने के लिए पर्याप्त नहीं है जिसकी उसे आदत है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, working woman bhi maintenance claim kar sakti hai agar uski income sufficient nahi hai.

**Q12: Can I get child custody under the DV Act?**
**English:** Yes, Section 21 of the PWDVA allows the Magistrate to grant temporary custody of children to the aggrieved woman to prevent them from abuse.
**Hindi:** हां, PWDVA की धारा 21 मजिस्ट्रेट को बच्चों को दुर्व्यवहार से बचाने के लिए व्यथित महिला को बच्चों की अस्थायी कस्टडी देने की अनुमति देती है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, DV Act ke Section 21 ke under Magistrate bacchon ki temporary custody maa ko de sakta hai.

**Q13: What is a Domestic Incident Report (DIR)?**
**English:** A DIR is an official report prepared by a Protection Officer or Service Provider documenting the details of the domestic violence incidents.
**Hindi:** DIR संरक्षण अधिकारी द्वारा तैयार की गई एक आधिकारिक रिपोर्ट है जिसमें घरेलू हिंसा की घटनाओं का विवरण होता है।
**Hinglish:** DIR ek official report hai jo Protection Officer banata hai jisme domestic violence ki details hoti hain.

**Q14: Can a divorced wife file a DV case?**
**English:** Generally, a divorced wife cannot file a fresh DV case for incidents happening after the divorce, but she can file for incidents that occurred during the marriage.
**Hindi:** आमतौर पर, एक तलाकशुदा पत्नी तलाक के बाद होने वाली घटनाओं के लिए नया डीवी मामला दर्ज नहीं कर सकती है, लेकिन वह विवाह के दौरान हुई घटनाओं के लिए कर सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Divorced wife divorce ke baad ki incidents ke liye DV case nahi kar sakti, par marriage ke time ki abuse ke liye kar sakti hai.

**Q15: Can a brother abuse his sister, and will it fall under DV Act?**
**English:** Yes, sisters can file a case against brothers if they live in a shared household and have a domestic relationship by consanguinity.
**Hindi:** हां, बहनें भाइयों के खिलाफ मामला दर्ज कर सकती हैं यदि वे एक साझे घर में रहती हैं और रक्त संबंध से उनका घरेलू रिश्ता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, sister apne brother ke khilaf DV case kar sakti hai agar wo shared ghar mein sath rehte hain.

**Q16: Is verbal abuse considered domestic violence?**
**English:** Yes, verbal and emotional abuse, including insults, ridicule, name-calling, or continuous demands for dowry, are considered domestic violence.
**Hindi:** हां, मौखिक और भावनात्मक दुर्व्यवहार, जिसमें अपमान, मज़ाक उड़ाना, गाली देना या दहेज की निरंतर मांग शामिल है, घरेलू हिंसा माना जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, gaali dena, insult karna ya dowry ki demand karna bhi verbal and emotional domestic violence hai.

**Q17: Will the court send us for mediation?**
**English:** The Magistrate may order the parties to undergo counseling or mediation under Section 14 of the PWDVA to explore amicable settlement, provided there is no severe threat.
**Hindi:** मजिस्ट्रेट PWDVA की धारा 14 के तहत पक्षों को सौहार्दपूर्ण समझौते का पता लगाने के लिए काउंसलिंग या मध्यस्थता से गुजरने का आदेश दे सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Court parties ko counseling ya mediation ke liye bhej sakti hai settlement explore karne ke liye.

**Q18: What is economic abuse?**
**English:** Depriving a woman of financial resources, stopping her from working, forcing her out of the house, or taking away her salary/assets is economic abuse.
**Hindi:** महिला को वित्तीय संसाधनों से वंचित करना, उसे काम करने से रोकना, या उसके वेतन/संपत्ति को छीन लेना आर्थिक दुर्व्यवहार है।
**Hinglish:** Aurat ko paise na dena, job karne se rokna, ya uski salary chheen lena economic abuse kahlata hai.

**Q19: Can an NRI husband be prosecuted under the DV Act?**
**English:** Yes, the DV Act applies if the domestic violence occurred in India or if the woman was subjected to abuse by an NRI husband while visiting or living in a shared household. Summons can be served abroad.
**Hindi:** हां, यदि घरेलू हिंसा भारत में हुई है तो एनआरआई पति पर मुकदमा चलाया जा सकता है। विदेश में समन भेजा जा सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, NRI husband par bhi DV Act lag sakta hai agar abuse hua ho. Summons bahar bhi bheje ja sakte hain.

**Q20: Can I get my Stridhan back under the DV Act?**
**English:** Yes, the Magistrate can direct the respondent to return the Stridhan, jewelry, clothes, and other items belonging to the aggrieved woman.
**Hindi:** हां, मजिस्ट्रेट प्रतिवादी को स्त्रीधन, गहने, कपड़े और व्यथित महिला की अन्य वस्तुओं को वापस करने का निर्देश दे सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, court order de sakta hai ki husband aapka Stridhan aur jewelry wapas kare.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** The DV Act only protects legally wedded wives.
   **Fact:** It protects legally wedded wives, live-in partners, mothers, sisters, and widows living in a shared household.
2. **Myth:** You must have physical injuries to prove domestic violence.
   **Fact:** Emotional, verbal, sexual, and economic abuses are fully recognized forms of domestic violence.
3. **Myth:** You need to file for divorce to file a DV case.
   **Fact:** A DV case can be filed while continuing to stay in the marriage or shared household.
4. **Myth:** Men can file a DV case under PWDVA against their wives.
   **Fact:** PWDVA is exclusively for the protection of women. Men cannot invoke this specific Act.
5. **Myth:** Filing a DV case means the husband will immediately go to jail.
   **Fact:** PWDVA is civil in nature. Jail only happens if a Protection Order is breached, or if an FIR is filed under BNS Section 85.
6. **Myth:** A working woman cannot claim maintenance.
   **Fact:** A working woman can claim maintenance if her income is inadequate.
7. **Myth:** Mothers-in-law can sue daughters-in-law under the DV Act.
   **Fact:** Only an aggrieved woman can sue adult male members and female relatives of the husband. Mothers-in-law cannot initiate DV proceedings against a daughter-in-law.
8. **Myth:** The shared household must be owned by the husband.
   **Fact:** The woman has a right to reside in the shared household regardless of whether the husband owns it or it is a rented/joint family property.
9. **Myth:** Verbal taunts don't count as violence.
   **Fact:** Constant taunting, name-calling, or not having a male child constitutes emotional violence.
10. **Myth:** It takes years to get any relief under the DV Act.
    **Fact:** Magistrates are empowered to grant ex-parte interim relief (maintenance/residence) almost immediately on the first few hearings.

## Conversation Examples

**Scenario 1: Filing a complaint for physical abuse**
**Citizen:** My husband beat me yesterday and locked me out of the house. What should I do?
**Assistant:** You should immediately call 112 or the Women's Helpline at 1091. You can visit the nearest police station to file an FIR under BNS Section 85 for cruelty. Simultaneously, you can contact a Protection Officer to file an application under the DV Act for a Residence Order to get back into your home.
**Hindi translation:** (नागरिक: मेरे पति ने मुझे पीटा और घर से बाहर निकाल दिया। मुझे क्या करना चाहिए? / सहायक: आपको तुरंत 112 या 1091 पर कॉल करना चाहिए। आप BNS धारा 85 के तहत FIR दर्ज कर सकती हैं और PWDVA के तहत निवास आदेश मांग सकती हैं।)

**Scenario 2: Understanding Economic Abuse**
**Citizen:** Mera husband mujhe ghar kharch nahi deta aur meri salary bhi le leta hai. Kya ye crime hai?
**Assistant:** Haan, yeh Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act ke under 'Economic Abuse' (Aarthik durvyavahar) maana jata hai. Aap Magistrate ke paas Section 20 ke under monetary relief aur maintenance ke liye case file kar sakti hain, jisse aapko financial support mil sake.

**Scenario 3: Protection Orders**
**Citizen:** I fear my in-laws will harm me if I stay in the house, but I have nowhere to go.
**Assistant:** You can file an application under Section 12 of the PWDVA. The Magistrate can pass a 'Protection Order' preventing them from committing any violence, and a 'Residence Order' ensuring you cannot be evicted from the shared household. If there is a severe threat, the court can even order the abusers to remove themselves from the house.

**Scenario 4: Live-in Relationships**
**Citizen:** Hum shaadi shuda nahi hain, par 3 saal se live-in mein the. Ab wo mujhe physically abuse kar raha hai. Kya main DV Act use kar sakti hu?
**Assistant:** Bilkul. PWDVA un sabhi relationships ko cover karta hai jo "nature of marriage" mein hain. Ek live-in partner DV Act ke under protection, maintenance aur residence orders claim kar sakti hai. Aap turant Protection Officer ya Police ko report karein.

**Scenario 5: Stridhan Recovery**
**Citizen:** Mere in-laws ne mera saara jewelry aur stridhan rakh liya hai. Kaise wapas lu?
**Assistant:** Under the DV Act, the Magistrate has the power to direct your husband and in-laws to return all your Stridhan, jewelry, and personal belongings. You must mention this specifically in your application under Section 12.

**Scenario 6: Interim Relief**
**Citizen:** Case chalne tak mera kharch kaise chalega?
**Assistant:** Court aapko 'Interim Maintenance' grant kar sakti hai. Case ki final hearing hone tak, aapka husband legally bound hoga aapko har mahine ek fixed amount dene ke liye taaki aapka kharch chal sake.

**Scenario 7: Verbal and Emotional Abuse**
**Citizen:** My husband constantly taunts me for not bringing enough dowry and verbally abuses me. Does this count as violence?
**Assistant:** Yes, continuous verbal abuse and taunting, especially regarding dowry, strictly falls under 'Emotional and Verbal Abuse' as defined in the DV Act. You have full legal grounds to file a complaint against him.

**Scenario 8: Brother abusing Sister**
**Citizen:** Mera bhai mujhe regularly marta hai aur ghar se nikalne ki dhamki deta hai. Kya main complaint kar sakti hu?
**Assistant:** Haan, PWDVA blood relatives ko bhi cover karta hai jo ek shared household mein rehte hain. Aap apne bhai ke khilaf domestic violence ka case file karke Protection Order aur Residence Order le sakti hain.

**Scenario 9: False Cases Myth**
**Citizen:** I heard that husbands easily go to jail immediately if a DV case is filed.
**Assistant:** That is a myth. The DV Act is primarily a civil law providing remedies like protection and maintenance. The husband only faces jail time if an FIR is registered under criminal laws like BNS Section 85, or if he breaches a specific Protection Order given by the Magistrate.

**Scenario 10: Role of Protection Officer**
**Citizen:** Protection Officer kaun hota hai aur kaise help karta hai?
**Assistant:** Protection Officer (PO) State Government dwara appoint kiye jate hain. PO aapko Domestic Incident Report (DIR) file karne mein, medical aid dilane mein, aur court mein Magistrate ke paas application lagane mein free of cost help karte hain.

**Scenario 11: Child Custody fear**
**Citizen:** If I file a DV case, will my husband take my children away?
**Assistant:** No, on the contrary, under Section 21 of the DV Act, the Magistrate can grant you temporary custody of your children to protect them from the abusive environment. The husband will not be allowed to forcibly take them.

**Scenario 12: Cost of Legal Aid**
**Citizen:** Main lawyer afford nahi kar sakti, case kaise ladu?
**Assistant:** Har woman ko Free Legal Aid ka right hai. Aap District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) ko approach kar sakti hain, jo aapko free lawyer provide karenge. Iske alawa, Protection Officer bhi aapki madad karenge bina kisi fees ke.

**Scenario 13: Single incident vs Continuous**
**Citizen:** Does it have to be daily abuse to file a case? It only happened twice.
**Assistant:** No, you do not have to wait for it to become a daily occurrence. Even a single act of physical, mental, or economic abuse that threatens your safety or well-being is enough to file a complaint under the DV Act.

**Scenario 14: Working Woman Maintenance**
**Citizen:** Main job karti hu, kya mujhe phir bhi maintenance mil sakta hai?
**Assistant:** Haan. Agar aapki income us standard of living ko maintain karne ke liye kafi nahi hai jo aap shared household mein enjoy karti thi, toh court aapko additional maintenance grant kar sakti hai aapke husband ki income ke basis par.

**Scenario 15: Mediation push**
**Citizen:** Will the police force me to compromise?
**Assistant:** The police or counselors may suggest mediation to resolve the conflict amicably, but they cannot force you to compromise, especially if you face physical danger. You have the full right to insist on a legal Protection Order and FIR.

## Government Services
- **National Commission for Women (NCW):** Has a dedicated helpline and online portal to register complaints of domestic violence.
- **Women Helpline - 1091:** A toll-free 24/7 helpline across India for women in distress.
- **National Legal Services Authority (NALSA):** Provides free legal services to women under the Legal Services Authorities Act. Access via the NALSA portal or local courts.
- **One Stop Centres (Sakhi):** Established by the Ministry of Women and Child Development to provide integrated support (medical, legal, psychological) to women affected by violence under one roof.
