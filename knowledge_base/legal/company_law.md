# Company Law in India: Registration, Compliance, and Administration

## Overview

**English:** Company Law in India, governed primarily by the Companies Act, 2013, regulates the incorporation, functioning, management, and dissolution of companies. It ensures corporate governance, transparency, and the protection of shareholders, investors, and creditors. Whether you are starting a private limited company or a one-person company, understanding this framework is critical.

## Definition

**English:** A 'Company' under Section 2(20) of the Companies Act, 2013 is an incorporated association which is an artificial person created by law, having a separate entity, with a perpetual succession and a common seal.

**Hindi:** कंपनी अधिनियम, 2013 की धारा 2(20) के तहत एक 'कंपनी' एक निगमित संघ है जो कानून द्वारा बनाया गया एक कृत्रिम व्यक्ति है, जिसकी एक अलग इकाई, शाश्वत उत्तराधिकार और एक सामान्य मुहर होती है।

## Legal Background

### History & Purpose
The company law in India has evolved from the Companies Act, 1956 to the much modernized Companies Act, 2013. The new Act was introduced to improve corporate governance, empower shareholders, increase transparency, enhance accountability of management, and facilitate easier doing of business. It strictly regulates corporate frauds and strengthens the institutional structure of the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT).

### Applicable Acts
1. **The Companies Act, 2013** - The primary legislation.
2. **The Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008** - For LLPs.
3. **The Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956** - For listed companies.
4. **The Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999** - For foreign investments (FDI).
5. **The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016** - For corporate insolvency and liquidation.

### Relevant Constitutional Articles
- **Article 19(1)(g):** Right to practice any profession, or to carry on any occupation, trade or business (subject to reasonable restrictions).
- **Article 14:** Right to Equality (applicable to companies as legal persons).

### Relevant Sections of Companies Act, 2013
- **Section 3:** Formation of company.
- **Section 7:** Incorporation of company.
- **Section 13:** Alteration of Memorandum.
- **Section 135:** Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR).
- **Section 149:** Company to have Board of Directors (Minimum numbers, Women Director, Independent Director).
- **Section 166:** Duties of directors.
- **Section 447:** Punishment for fraud.

## Applicability
- **Covered:** Private Limited Companies, Public Limited Companies, One Person Companies (OPC), Section 8 Companies (NGOs), Government Companies, Nidhi Companies, Producer Companies, and Foreign Companies operating in India.
- **Exempt:** Sole proprietorships, unregistered partnerships, HUFs (Hindu Undivided Families), and cooperative societies (unless explicitly registered under the Companies Act).
- **Special Conditions:** Listed public companies have additional compliance burdens from SEBI. Certain provisions like CSR (Sec 135) only apply to companies crossing specific net worth, turnover, or profit thresholds.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure (Company Registration)

**English:**
1. **Apply for Digital Signature Certificate (DSC):** Directors must obtain a Class-3 DSC.
2. **Name Approval:** Apply for company name via the SPICe+ Part A form on the MCA portal.
3. **Drafting Documents:** Prepare the Memorandum of Association (MOA) and Articles of Association (AOA).
4. **Filing SPICe+ Form (Part B):** Submit the comprehensive form along with MOA, AOA, AGILE-PRO-S (for GST, EPFO, ESIC, Bank Account), and INC-9.
5. **Payment of Fees:** Pay the prescribed ROC fees and Stamp Duty online.
6. **Certificate of Incorporation (COI):** Upon verification, the ROC issues the COI with the Corporate Identification Number (CIN), PAN, and TAN.

**Hindi:**
1. **डिजिटल सिग्नेचर सर्टिफिकेट (DSC) के लिए आवेदन:** निदेशकों को क्लास-3 DSC प्राप्त करना होगा।
2. **नाम अनुमोदन:** MCA पोर्टल पर SPICe+ Part A फॉर्म के माध्यम से कंपनी के नाम के लिए आवेदन करें।
3. **दस्तावेज़ प्रारूपण:** मेमोरेंडम ऑफ एसोसिएशन (MOA) और आर्टिकल्स ऑफ एसोसिएशन (AOA) तैयार करें।
4. **SPICe+ फॉर्म (Part B) दाखिल करना:** MOA, AOA, AGILE-PRO-S और INC-9 के साथ व्यापक फॉर्म जमा करें।
5. **शुल्क का भुगतान:** निर्धारित ROC शुल्क और स्टाम्प ड्यूटी का ऑनलाइन भुगतान करें।
6. **निगमन का प्रमाण पत्र (COI):** सत्यापन के बाद, ROC CIN, PAN और TAN के साथ COI जारी करता है।

**Hinglish:**

6. **Certificate of Incorporation (COI):** Verification ke baad, ROC Corporate Identification Number (CIN), PAN, aur TAN ke sath COI issue karta hai.

## Required Documents
- Identity Proof of Directors/Shareholders (PAN Card mandatory for Indian Nationals).
- Address Proof of Directors/Shareholders (Aadhar, Passport, Voter ID, or Driving License).
- Residence Proof (Bank Statement, Electricity Bill, Mobile Bill - not older than 2 months).
- Passport size photographs.
- Registered Office Proof (Rent Agreement, NOC from owner, and recent Utility Bill).
- MOA and AOA (electronically drafted via SPICe+).

## Government Authorities
- **Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA):** Central authority regulating corporate affairs.
- **Registrar of Companies (ROC):** Regional offices handling company registration and compliance.
- **Regional Directors (RD):** Supervise the working of ROCs.
- **National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT):** Quasi-judicial body for corporate disputes, insolvency, and mergers.
- **National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT):** Hears appeals against NCLT orders.
- **Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI):** Regulates listed public companies.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Non-filing of Annual Returns (Sec 92):** Penalty of ₹10,000 and ₹100 per day of continuing default, subject to a maximum of ₹2,00,000 for the company and ₹50,000 for the officer in default.
- **Failure to hold AGM (Sec 99):** Fine up to ₹1,00,000 and ₹5,000 for every day the default continues.
- **Fraud (Sec 447):** Imprisonment ranging from 6 months to 10 years, and a fine not less than the amount involved in the fraud, up to 3 times the amount. If public interest is involved, minimum imprisonment is 3 years.
- **CSR Non-Compliance (Sec 135):** Penalty of twice the unspent amount (up to ₹1 Crore) for the company and 1/10th of the unspent amount (up to ₹2 Lakhs) for defaulting officers.
- **Acceptance of Illegal Deposits (Sec 76A):** Fine from ₹1 Crore to ₹10 Crores and imprisonment up to 7 years.

## Exceptions
- **Small Companies:** Enjoy lesser compliance burden (e.g., need to hold only 2 board meetings a year instead of 4, no need to prepare cash flow statement).
- **One Person Company (OPC):** No requirement for AGM. Only one director and one member required.
- **Section 8 Companies:** Exempted from adding "Limited" or "Private Limited" to their names; have relaxed notice periods for AGMs.
- **Startups (DPIIT Recognized):** Relaxed norms for issuing ESOPs, sweat equity, and accepting deposits from members.

## Important Timelines
- **Company Name Reservation:** Valid for 20 days (for new companies) or 60 days (for name change).
- **First Board Meeting:** Within 30 days of incorporation.
- **Appointment of Auditor:** Within 30 days of incorporation (First Auditor).
- **Commencement of Business (Form INC-20A):** To be filed within 180 days of incorporation.
- **Annual General Meeting (AGM):** Within 6 months from the closure of the financial year (for first AGM, within 9 months).
- **Filing Financial Statements (AOC-4):** Within 30 days of the AGM.
- **Filing Annual Return (MGT-7/7A):** Within 60 days of the AGM.
- **Director KYC (DIR-3 KYC):** Annually by 30th September.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Mixing Personal and Company Funds:** Using company accounts for personal expenses piercing the corporate veil.
- **Missing Compliance Deadlines:** Failing to file AOC-4 or MGT-7 on time leading to heavy additional fees.
- **Ignoring Statutory Registers:** Not maintaining minutes of board meetings and required registers at the registered office.
- **Not Filing INC-20A:** Failing to file the declaration of commencement of business, which can lead to ROC striking off the company.
- **Issuing Shares without Proper Procedure:** Violating private placement or rights issue rules under the Act.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a Private Limited Company?**
**English:** It is a privately held business entity with limited liability. It restricts the transferability of shares and limits the number of members to 200.
**Hindi:** यह सीमित देयता वाली एक निजी तौर पर आयोजित व्यावसायिक इकाई है। यह शेयरों की हस्तांतरणीयता को प्रतिबंधित करता है और सदस्यों की संख्या को 200 तक सीमित करता है।
**Hinglish:** Yeh limited liability wali ek privately held business entity hai. Yeh shares ke transfer ko restrict karti hai aur members ki maximum limit 200 hoti hai.

2. **How many directors are required for a Private Limited Company?**
**English:** A minimum of two directors and a maximum of fifteen directors are required. At least one director must be a resident of India.
**Hindi:** कम से कम दो निदेशकों और अधिकतम पंद्रह निदेशकों की आवश्यकता होती है। कम से कम एक निदेशक भारत का निवासी होना चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Minimum do directors aur maximum 15 directors required hote hain. Kam se kam ek director Indian resident hona chahiye.

**Hinglish:** Specific financial limit cross karne wali companies ko apne pichle 3 saal ke average net profit ka 2% social development activities par kharch karna hota hai.

11. **How is a company different from an LLP?**
**English:** A Company is highly regulated, has stricter compliances, and allows equity funding easily. An LLP is a mix of partnership and company, with less compliance and flexibility in internal management.
**Hindi:** एक कंपनी अत्यधिक विनियमित होती है और अनुपालन सख्त होते हैं। एक एलएलपी साझेदारी और कंपनी का मिश्रण है, जिसमें कम अनुपालन होता है।
**Hinglish:** Company heavily regulated hoti hai aur equity funding easy hoti hai. LLP partnership aur company ka mix hai, jisme compliance kam aur internal management flexible hota hai.

12. **What is the Commencement of Business certificate?**
**English:** It is a mandatory declaration (Form INC-20A) filed by directors within 180 days of incorporation, stating that subscribers have paid the initial capital into the company's bank account.
**Hindi:** यह निगमन के 180 दिनों के भीतर निदेशकों द्वारा दायर एक अनिवार्य घोषणा है, जिसमें कहा गया है कि ग्राहकों ने प्रारंभिक पूंजी का भुगतान कर दिया है।
**Hinglish:** Yeh incorporation ke 180 days ke andar file hone wali mandatory declaration (INC-20A) hai, jo prove karti hai ki promoters ne initial capital company ke bank account mein daal di hai.

13. **Can a company be closed completely?**
**English:** Yes, a company can be closed legally through Strike Off (Fast Track Exit under Sec 248) if it has no operations/liabilities, or through Liquidation under the IBC.
**Hindi:** हां, यदि कोई संचालन/देनदारी नहीं है, तो एक कंपनी को कानूनी रूप से स्ट्राइक ऑफ या आईबीसी के तहत परिसमापन के माध्यम से बंद किया जा सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar company mein operations ya liabilities nahi hain toh use Sec 248 ke under Strike Off karke, ya IBC ke under liquidate karke legally close kiya ja sakta hai.

14. **What is a One Person Company (OPC)?**
**English:** OPC is a type of company that can be formed with just one director and one member, enjoying limited liability status. It requires a nominee member.
**Hindi:** ओपीसी एक प्रकार की कंपनी है जिसे केवल एक निदेशक और एक सदस्य के साथ बनाया जा सकता है। इसके लिए एक नॉमिनी सदस्य की आवश्यकता होती है।
**Hinglish:** OPC aisi company hai jo sirf ek director aur ek member se ban sakti hai, limited liability ke fayde ke sath. Isme ek nominee member compulsory hota hai.

15. **Are statutory audits mandatory for private companies?**
**English:** Yes, statutory audit by a practicing Chartered Accountant is absolutely mandatory for all registered companies, regardless of their turnover or profit.
**Hindi:** हां, टर्नओवर या लाभ की परवाह किए बिना, सभी पंजीकृत कंपनियों के लिए एक सीए द्वारा वैधानिक ऑडिट बिल्कुल अनिवार्य है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, chahe turnover zero kyu na ho, sabhi registered companies ke liye practicing CA se statutory audit karwana 100% mandatory hai.

16. **Who can be an auditor of a company?**
**English:** Only a Chartered Accountant (CA) or a firm of CAs holding a valid certificate of practice in India can be appointed as an auditor of a company.
**Hindi:** केवल एक चार्टर्ड अकाउंटेंट (सीए) या सीए की फर्म जिसके पास भारत में अभ्यास का वैध प्रमाण पत्र है, उसे ही नियुक्त किया जा सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Sirf ek valid Certificate of Practice hold karne wala CA ya CA firm hi company ka auditor appoint ho sakta hai.

17. **Can directors be held personally liable for company debts?**
**English:** Generally no, due to the concept of limited liability. However, in cases of fraud, tax evasion, or personal guarantees, the corporate veil can be pierced, making directors personally liable.
**Hindi:** आम तौर पर सीमित देयता के कारण नहीं। हालांकि, धोखाधड़ी के मामलों में, निदेशकों को व्यक्तिगत रूप से उत्तरदायी ठहराया जा सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Normally nahi (limited liability ki wajah se). Par agar fraud, tax chori, ya personal guarantee ka case ho, toh corporate veil hata kar directors ko personally liable thehraya ja sakta hai.

18. **What is SPICe+?**
**English:** SPICe+ (Simplified Proforma for Incorporating Company Electronically Plus) is an integrated web form on the MCA portal offering 10 services to seamlessly incorporate a company.
**Hindi:** SPICe+ एमसीए पोर्टल पर एक एकीकृत वेब फॉर्म है जो कंपनी को मूल रूप से शामिल करने के लिए 10 सेवाएं प्रदान करता है।
**Hinglish:** SPICe+ MCA portal par ek integrated web form hai jo ek sath 10 services (jaise PAN, TAN, GST, PF) provide karta hai company register karne ke liye.

19. **What is a Section 8 Company?**
**English:** A Section 8 Company is registered for promoting commerce, art, science, sports, education, research, social welfare, religion, charity, or environmental protection, and prohibits dividend payments.
**Hindi:** धारा 8 कंपनी सामाजिक कल्याण या दान को बढ़ावा देने के लिए पंजीकृत है, और सदस्यों को लाभांश भुगतान को प्रतिबंधित करती है।
**Hinglish:** Section 8 company social welfare, education, ya charity promote karne ke liye banayi jati hai. Inhe NGO bhi kehte hain, aur yeh members ko dividend pay nahi kar sakti.

20. **Can I convert my Sole Proprietorship into a Private Limited Company?**
**English:** Yes, you can transition by incorporating a new Private Limited Company and officially taking over the business of the proprietorship firm through a takeover agreement.
**Hindi:** हां, आप एक नई प्राइवेट लिमिटेड कंपनी बनाकर और टेकओवर समझौते के माध्यम से संक्रमण कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, aap ek nayi Private Limited company incorporate karke aur takeover agreement sign karke apni proprietorship ko company mein convert kar sakte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** Minimum 1 Lakh capital is needed to start a company.
   **Fact:** The minimum paid-up capital requirement of ₹1,00,000 for private companies was abolished by the Companies (Amendment) Act, 2015. You can start with a nominal amount.
2. **Myth:** OPC and Sole Proprietorship are exactly the same.
   **Fact:** OPC has separate legal entity and limited liability, whereas a sole proprietorship doesn't have a separate legal identity from its owner.
3. **Myth:** Only big businesses need to file MCA annual returns.
   **Fact:** Every registered company, even if it has zero turnover or didn't do any business, MUST file annual returns (AOC-4 & MGT-7).
4. **Myth:** Directors can freely use company funds for personal expenses.
   **Fact:** No, company funds belong strictly to the company. Using them for personal expenses without proper procedure is illegal and could lead to prosecution.
5. **Myth:** A Private Limited Company can sell its shares to the public.
   **Fact:** Private companies cannot invite the public to subscribe to their shares; only Public Limited Companies can do so through an IPO.
6. **Myth:** You need a commercial office space to register a company.
   **Fact:** You can register a company at a residential address. A NOC from the owner and a utility bill are sufficient.
7. **Myth:** GST registration is mandatory for all companies upon incorporation.
   **Fact:** GST is mandatory only if your turnover crosses the threshold or if you fall under compulsory registration criteria. It is optional at incorporation.
8. **Myth:** Closing a company is as easy as starting one.
   **Fact:** Winding up or striking off a company is a complex, time-consuming legal process that requires clearance from various departments.
9. **Myth:** A company can never be criminally prosecuted.
   **Fact:** While a company cannot be jailed, it can be heavily fined, and its directors/officers in default can face criminal imprisonment for fraud.
10. **Myth:** Family members cannot be the directors in the same company.
    **Fact:** There is no restriction. Husband, wife, and family members can be the directors and shareholders of the same Private Limited Company.

## Conversation Examples

**Conversation 1:**
**Citizen:** I want to start a software startup. Should I go for LLP or Private Limited?
**Assistant:** If you plan to raise funds from angel investors or venture capitalists in the future, a Private Limited Company is the best choice because investors prefer equity shares. If you don't need external funding and want less compliance, LLP is better.

**Conversation 2:**
**Citizen:** Mere paas abhi business operations nahi hain. Kya phir bhi annual filing karni padegi?
**Assistant:** Haan bilkul. Company Act ke hisaab se ROC ki annual filing (AOC-4, MGT-7) aur ITR file karna mandatory hai, chahe aapka turnover zero hi kyu na ho. Filing nahi karne par daily penalty lagti hai.

**Conversation 3:**
**Citizen:** How much time does it take to register a company in India?
**Assistant:** Generally, if all your documents are correct, name approval and incorporation through the SPICe+ form can take about 7 to 10 working days.

**Conversation 4:**
**Citizen:** Can a government employee be a director in a private company?
**Assistant:** Usually, civil service rules prohibit government employees from holding an office of profit or engaging in private business. You need explicit prior permission from your department, which is rarely granted.

**Conversation 5:**
**Citizen:** Kya mujhe har saal auditor appoint karna hoga?
**Assistant:** Ek baar jab first auditor appoint hota hai, uske baad 1st AGM mein auditor 5 saal ke liye appoint hota hai. Har saal appointment zaroori nahi, bas ratification pehle hota tha, jo ab hata diya gaya hai.

**Conversation 6:**
**Citizen:** What happens if I don't file the Form INC-20A?
**Assistant:** Form INC-20A (Commencement of Business) must be filed within 180 days. If not filed, a penalty applies, the company cannot borrow money, and the ROC has the power to strike off the company's name.

**Conversation 7:**
**Citizen:** Sir, company bandh karne ka kya process hai agar koi activity na ki ho?
**Assistant:** Agar company ne pichle 2 saal se koi business nahi kiya hai, toh aap Nill liabilities ke sath STK-2 form file karke 'Fast Track Exit' scheme ke under company ko strike off karwa sakte hain.

**Conversation 8:**
**Citizen:** Are there any benefits for startups under this law?
**Assistant:** Yes, recognized startups (DPIIT registered) get benefits like fast-track patent processing, income tax exemptions for 3 consecutive years, easier winding-up norms, and exemption from angel tax.

**Conversation 9:**
**Citizen:** Main apni wife ke sath company start karna chahta hu. Kya yeh allowed hai?
**Assistant:** Ji haan, husband aur wife milkar asani se Private Limited Company register kar sakte hain. Aap dono Directors aur Shareholders dono ban sakte hain.

**Conversation 10:**
**Citizen:** What is the limit on maximum shareholders in a Private Company?
**Assistant:** A Private Limited Company can have a maximum of 200 members (shareholders). Employees who hold shares are excluded from this limit.

**Conversation 11:**
**Citizen:** Can a foreign national own 100% shares in an Indian private company?
**Assistant:** Yes, 100% Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) is allowed under the automatic route in most sectors. So a foreign national or foreign company can own the shares, but one director must be an Indian resident.

**Conversation 12:**
**Citizen:** Company banane ke liye kitna stamp duty dena hota hai?
**Assistant:** Stamp duty state to state vary karti hai. Maharashtra aur Madhya Pradesh mein alag rates hain. SPICe+ form bharte waqt state ke hisaab se system auto-calculate kar leta hai.

**Conversation 13:**
**Citizen:** How many board meetings are compulsory in a year?
**Assistant:** For a regular private company, at least 4 board meetings must be held in a year, with a gap of not more than 120 days between two consecutive meetings. For Small Companies or OPCs, 2 meetings (one per half year) are sufficient.

**Conversation 14:**
**Citizen:** Kya company apne directors ko loan de sakti hai?
**Assistant:** Section 185 ke under, company apne directors ya unke relatives ko loan nahi de sakti. Isme kuch strict exceptions aur rules hain, toh hamesha CA/CS se consult karna chahiye.

**Conversation 15:**
**Citizen:** Can I change my company's name later?
**Assistant:** Yes, you can change the company name by passing a Special Resolution in an EGM, getting approval from the Central Government (ROC), and filing Form INC-24.
