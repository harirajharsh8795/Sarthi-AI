# Filing a Consumer Complaint in India (उपभोक्ता शिकायत प्रक्रिया / Upbhokta Shikayat Prakriya)

## Overview
**English:** The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 empowers Indian consumers to file complaints against sellers, service providers, or manufacturers for defective goods, deficiency in services, unfair trade practices, or misleading advertisements. The process has been highly modernized, allowing digital filing through the E-Daakhil portal, significantly reducing the dependency on lawyers and making justice accessible to common citizens. 
**Hindi:** उपभोक्ता संरक्षण अधिनियम, 2019 भारतीय उपभोक्ताओं को दोषपूर्ण वस्तुओं, सेवा में कमी, अनुचित व्यापार प्रथाओं या भ्रामक विज्ञापनों के खिलाफ विक्रेताओं या निर्माताओं के खिलाफ शिकायत दर्ज करने का अधिकार देता है। प्रक्रिया को आधुनिक बनाया गया है, जिससे ई-दाखिल पोर्टल के माध्यम से डिजिटल फाइलिंग की अनुमति मिलती है।
**Hinglish:** Consumer Protection Act, 2019 ke tehat agar aapko defective goods, service mein deficiency, ya misleading ads milte hain, toh aap seller ya manufacturer ke khilaf complaint file kar sakte hain. E-Daakhil portal se ab aap bina kisi lawyer ke online bhi case file kar sakte hain, jo justice ko aam aadmi ke liye accessible banata hai.

## Quick Summary
**English:** Any individual who buys goods or services for personal use can file a complaint. The process involves sending a legal notice, filing a complaint online or offline based on claim value, attending hearings, and receiving compensation. 
**Hindi:** कोई भी व्यक्ति जो व्यक्तिगत उपयोग के लिए सामान या सेवाएँ खरीदता है, शिकायत दर्ज कर सकता है। इस प्रक्रिया में कानूनी नोटिस भेजना, दावे के मूल्य के आधार पर ऑनलाइन या ऑफलाइन शिकायत दर्ज करना, सुनवाई में भाग लेना और मुआवजा प्राप्त करना शामिल है।
**Hinglish:** Jo bhi personal use ke liye saman ya service leta hai, wo complaint kar sakta hai. Process mein legal notice bhejna, claim value ke hisaab se online/offline case file karna, aur compensation lena shamil hai.

## Definition
**English:** A Consumer Complaint is a formal legal or administrative grievance raised by a consumer against a seller or service provider seeking redressal, compensation, or replacement under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019.
**Hindi:** उपभोक्ता शिकायत एक औपचारिक कानूनी शिकायत है जो उपभोक्ता द्वारा विक्रेता या सेवा प्रदाता के खिलाफ उपभोक्ता संरक्षण अधिनियम, 2019 के तहत निवारण, मुआवजे या प्रतिस्थापन की मांग के लिए उठाई जाती है।
**Hinglish:** Consumer complaint ek formal legal case hai jo ek consumer seller ya service provider ke khilaf file karta hai, taaki use uska paisa, replacement, ya compensation mil sake.

## Why It Matters
**English:** It ensures accountability in the marketplace. Without a robust complaint mechanism, consumers would be left vulnerable to corporate exploitation, low-quality goods, and financial fraud. 
**Hindi:** यह बाजार में जवाबदेही सुनिश्चित करता है। एक मजबूत शिकायत तंत्र के बिना, उपभोक्ता कॉर्पोरेट शोषण, कम गुणवत्ता वाले सामान और वित्तीय धोखाधड़ी के प्रति संवेदनशील रह जाएंगे।
**Hinglish:** Ye market mein accountability banata hai. Agar strong complaint system na ho, toh companies aur sellers consumers ka asani se exploitation kar sakte hain aur fraud badh jayega.

## Legal Background
The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 (CPA 2019) replaces the older 1986 Act, expanding consumer rights, especially concerning e-commerce and misleading ads.
- **Section 2(7) of CPA, 2019:** Defines a "Consumer" as anyone who buys goods or avails services for consideration (payment). It explicitly includes offline and online transactions.
- **Section 34, 47, 58 of CPA, 2019 (Read with 2021 Rules):** Defines Pecuniary Jurisdiction (financial limits) for District, State, and National Commissions.
- **Section 35 of CPA, 2019:** Details the manner in which a complaint shall be made.
- **Section 94 of CPA, 2019:** Deals with the prevention of unfair trade practices in e-commerce.

## Applicability
**Who is Covered:**
- Any person who pays or promises to pay for goods or services.
- E-commerce buyers, telecom subscribers, medical patients, insurance policyholders, real estate buyers.
- A registered consumer association acting on behalf of a consumer.
- Central Authority (CCPA) or the Central/State Government.

**Who is Exempt (Not a Consumer):**
- Individuals purchasing goods for commercial resale.
- Individuals availing free services (e.g., free treatment at a government hospital).
- Buyers who have not paid or promised to pay (unless they are beneficiaries using the service with the buyer's approval).

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Send a Legal Notice:** Send a registered formal notice to the defaulting party asking them to rectify the issue within 15-30 days.
2. **Draft the Complaint:** If unresolved, draft a consumer complaint detailing facts, deficiency, and requested relief (compensation).
3. **Choose the Jurisdiction:** Based on the claim amount (District <= 50L, State 50L-2Cr, National > 2Cr) and geographical location (where the consumer lives/works or opposite party resides).
4. **Filing:** File physically at the commission or online via E-Daakhil portal. Pay the nominal court fee (No fee up to Rs 5 Lakhs claim).
5. **Admission & Hearing:** The commission admits the case, sends a notice to the opposite party (who has 30-45 days to reply).
6. **Evidence & Arguments:** Both sides submit affidavits of evidence and argue the matter.
7. **Final Order:** The commission passes an order (refund, replacement, compensation, or penalty).
**Hindi:**
1. **कानूनी नोटिस भेजें:** 15-30 दिनों में समस्या के समाधान के लिए विपक्षी दल को नोटिस भेजें।
2. **शिकायत का मसौदा तैयार करें:** समाधान न होने पर तथ्यों और मुआवजे की मांग के साथ शिकायत लिखें।
3. **अधिकार क्षेत्र चुनें:** दावे की राशि के आधार पर (जिला <= 50 लाख, राज्य 50 लाख-2 करोड़, राष्ट्रीय > 2 करोड़)।
4. **फाइलिंग:** ई-दाखिल पोर्टल या सीधे अदालत में मामूली शुल्क के साथ शिकायत दर्ज करें (5 लाख तक कोई शुल्क नहीं)।
5. **सुनवाई और आदेश:** दोनों पक्ष सबूत पेश करते हैं, और फोरम अंतिम आदेश पारित करता है।
**Hinglish:**
1. **Notice Bhejna:** Pehle seller ko 15-30 din ka legal notice bhejein taaki wo problem solve kar de.
2. **Drafting:** Agar wo nahi maane, toh facts aur compensation claim likh kar complaint draft karein.
3. **Forum Choose Karein:** Claim value ke hisaab se District (50 lakh tak), State (50L-2Cr), ya National (> 2Cr) decide karein.
4. **File Karein:** E-Daakhil pe online ya offline file karein. 5 lakh tak ki claim par koi court fee nahi lagti.
5. **Hearing & Order:** Court dono parties ko sunkar final order (refund, replacement, ya penalty) deti hai.

## Required Documents
- Copy of the formal legal notice sent to the opposite party and its postal delivery receipt.
- Proof of purchase: Invoice, bill, receipt, or online order confirmation.
- Proof of payment: Bank statement, credit card statement, UPI transaction ID.
- Warranty or Guarantee cards.
- Communications: Emails, WhatsApp chats, call recordings, or SMS exchanged with the seller/customer care.
- Affidavits supporting the complaint.
- ID Proof of the complainant (Aadhaar, PAN).

## Government Authorities
- **Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA):** Regulates matters relating to violation of consumer rights, unfair trade practices, and misleading advertisements.
- **District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission:** Handles complaints where the value of goods/services paid as consideration does not exceed Rs. 50 Lakhs.
- **State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission:** Handles complaints between Rs. 50 Lakhs and Rs. 2 Crores, and appeals from District Commissions.
- **National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (NCDRC):** Handles complaints exceeding Rs. 2 Crores and appeals from State Commissions.
- **National Consumer Helpline (NCH - 1915):** The first level of grievance redressal via pre-litigation conciliation.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Misleading Advertisements:** Section 21 allows CCPA to impose a penalty of up to Rs. 10 Lakhs on manufacturers/endorsers, extending to Rs. 50 Lakhs for subsequent violations. Imprisonment up to 2 years (extending to 5 years for repeat offenses).
- **Adulterated Goods:** Section 90 prescribes imprisonment from 6 months to life, and fines ranging from Rs. 1 Lakh to Rs. 10 Lakhs depending on the severity of injury caused by the adulteration.
- **Spurious Goods:** Section 91 prescribes similar strict penalties including license cancellation.
- **Failure to Comply with Commission Orders:** Section 72 prescribes imprisonment of 1 month to 3 years, or a fine of Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 1 Lakh, or both.

## Exceptions
- **Commercial Use:** Buying goods for commercial purpose or resale is not covered under the CPA. Exception: Goods bought exclusively to earn a livelihood by means of self-employment (e.g., a tailor buying a sewing machine).
- **Free Services:** Services provided free of charge are generally excluded (e.g., government hospitals offering completely free treatment).
- **Civil Disputes:** Disputes involving complex questions of civil/criminal law, title of property, or breach of complex commercial contracts are directed to Civil Courts.

## Important Timelines
- **Limitation Period:** The complaint must be filed within exactly 2 (two) years from the date on which the cause of action arose (Section 69 of CPA).
- **Notice Period Reply:** Opposite party gets 30 days to reply after being served notice by the Commission (extendable by 15 days).
- **Appeal Timeline:** Appeal to State or National Commission must be filed within 30 days of the respective lower commission's order.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Missing the 2-Year Deadline:** Filing a case after 2 years of the issue without a valid condonation of delay application.
- **Not Sending a Legal Notice:** Jumping to court without trying to resolve the issue directly with the company first.
- **Filing in the Wrong Forum:** Filing a 10 Lakh claim in the State Commission instead of the District Commission.
- **Missing E-commerce Jurisdiction:** Filing where the seller is located instead of where the consumer resides (The new Act allows filing where the consumer lives/works).

## Safety Tips

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can I file a consumer complaint without a lawyer?**
   **English:** Yes. The Consumer Protection Act allows citizens to represent themselves. You do not strictly need an advocate, especially for standard matters.

2. **What is the fee to file a consumer case?**
   **English:** There is zero fee for claims up to Rs. 5 Lakhs. For claims between 5-10 lakhs, the fee is Rs. 200, and it scales nominally up to Rs. 7,500 for massive claims at the National level.
   **Hindi:** 5 लाख रुपये तक के दावों के लिए कोई शुल्क नहीं है।
   **Hinglish:** 5 lakh tak ke claim ke liye zero fee hai. Uske upar fees Rs. 200 se shuru hoti hai.

3. **Where can I complain against Amazon or Flipkart?**
   **English:** You can complain via the National Consumer Helpline (1915) or file an online case via the E-Daakhil portal. E-commerce platforms are fully accountable under the CPA 2019.
   **Hindi:** आप राष्ट्रीय उपभोक्ता हेल्पलाइन या ई-दाखिल पर शिकायत कर सकते हैं।

    **English:** Yes. Non-Resident Indians can file complaints for goods/services purchased in India, and can use the online E-Daakhil portal or authorize an agent.
    **Hindi:** हाँ। एनआरआई भारत में खरीदी गई वस्तुओं या सेवाओं के लिए ऑनलाइन ई-दाखिल के माध्यम से शिकायत दर्ज कर सकते हैं।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, NRIs bhi India mein li gayi service ya product ke khilaf online E-Daakhil se complaint file kar sakte hain.

20. **Can I withdraw my complaint later?**
    **English:** Yes. If the opposite party settles the matter with you out of court, you can easily file an application to withdraw the complaint at any time.
    **Hindi:** हाँ। यदि विपक्षी दल अदालत के बाहर समझौता कर लेता है, तो आप कभी भी शिकायत वापस ले सकते हैं।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, agar company aapki baat maan leti hai aur compromise ho jata hai, toh aap apni complaint waapas le sakte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** You always need an expensive lawyer to go to consumer court.
   **Fact:** No, the CPA allows you to represent yourself or use an authorized representative. The process is simplified.
2. **Myth:** Filing a case will take 10-15 years.
   **Fact:** While delays happen, consumer courts follow a summary procedure. Cases are generally decided much faster (1-3 years) compared to civil courts.
3. **Myth:** "Goods once sold will not be taken back" is legally binding.
   **Fact:** This statement holds no legal weight. If a product is defective, the seller is bound by law to repair, replace, or refund.
4. **Myth:** I must travel to the company's headquarters to file a case.
   **Fact:** Since 2019, you can file the case in the district where you live or work.
5. **Myth:** Consumer courts charge heavy fees.
   **Fact:** There is no fee for claims up to Rs. 5 Lakhs, and nominal fees (Rs. 200+) for higher amounts.
6. **Myth:** E-commerce companies are not liable for 3rd party sellers.
   **Fact:** Under e-commerce rules, platforms hold accountability for unfair trade practices and cannot simply wash their hands of defective deliveries.
7. **Myth:** I can file a case 5 years after buying the product.
   **Fact:** The limitation period is strict. You must file within 2 years of the cause of action.
8. **Myth:** Government hospitals can be sued in consumer court.
   **Fact:** If the service is provided completely free of charge, it falls outside the purview of the Consumer Protection Act.
9. **Myth:** Only the buyer can file a complaint.
   **Fact:** A beneficiary (e.g., someone gifted the item) can file a complaint with the buyer's approval.
10. **Myth:** Verbal promises by the seller are binding in court.
    **Fact:** Without written proof, emails, or call recordings, verbal promises are extremely difficult to prove in court.

## Conversation Examples

**Dialogue 1: Online Fraud / Wrong Delivery**
*Citizen:* I ordered a smartphone on a major e-commerce site, but received a soap bar. Customer care is rejecting my refund. What should I do?
*Assistant (English):* First, register a grievance on the National Consumer Helpline (1915). If they still refuse, send a formal legal notice to the platform. Finally, you can file a complaint on the E-Daakhil portal for deficiency in service and unfair trade practices. 
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Sabse pehle 1915 (NCH) par call karke complaint likhwayein. Agar wo refund na dein, toh unhe ek legal notice bhejein. Phir aap ghar baithe E-Daakhil portal pe deficiency in service ka case daal sakte hain.

**Dialogue 2: Builder Delay**
*Citizen:* I paid for a flat in 2020. The builder promised delivery in 2023 but hasn't finished it yet. Can I approach consumer court?
*Assistant (English):* Yes, delay in handing over possession of a flat by a builder is considered a deficiency in service. You can file a case in the State or National Commission (depending on the value) seeking a refund with interest or immediate possession with compensation.
*Assistant (Hindi):* हाँ, बिल्डर द्वारा फ्लैट का पज़ेशन देने में देरी 'सेवा में कमी' है। आप अपने दावे की राशि के अनुसार राज्य या राष्ट्रीय आयोग में केस दर्ज करके ब्याज के साथ रिफंड या मुआवजे की मांग कर सकते हैं।

**Dialogue 3: Jurisdiction Question**
*Citizen:* The company is located in Mumbai, but I live in Delhi. Do I need to travel to Mumbai to file the case?
*Assistant (English):* No. Under the Consumer Protection Act 2019, you can file the case at the District Consumer Commission in Delhi, exactly where you reside or work.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Nahi, naye kanoon ke hisaab se aap Delhi mein apne ghar ya office ke jurisdiction wale District Forum mein case file kar sakte hain. Mumbai jaane ki zaroorat nahi hai.

**Dialogue 4: Lawyer Requirement**
*Citizen:* Do I need to hire a lawyer to file a case in the District Forum?
*Assistant (English):* No, it is not mandatory. The consumer courts are designed to be accessible. You can draft your complaint, submit the documents, and argue the facts yourself. However, for complex matters involving huge amounts, consulting an advocate is advisable.
*Assistant (Hindi):* नहीं, यह अनिवार्य नहीं है। उपभोक्ता अदालतें आम जनता के लिए सुलभ बनाई गई हैं। आप खुद शिकायत लिख सकते हैं और बहस कर सकते हैं। लेकिन जटिल मामलों में वकील की सलाह लेना बेहतर होता है।

**Dialogue 5: Defective Electronics**
*Citizen:* Maine AC kharida tha 2 mahine pehle, wo cooling nahi kar raha aur company repair bhi nahi kar rahi.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Ye deficiency in service hai. Aapke paas agar warranty card aur bill hai, toh pehle company ko legal notice bhejein ki 15 din mein thik karein. Agar nahi karte, toh District Consumer Commission mein replacement aur mental agony ke compensation ke liye file karein.
*Assistant (English):* This is a deficiency in service. If you have the bill and warranty, send a legal notice giving them 15 days to resolve it. If they fail, file a complaint in the District Commission seeking AC replacement and compensation for mental agony.

**Dialogue 6: Insurance Claim Rejection**
*Citizen:* My health insurance claim was rejected citing 'pre-existing disease', but I declared everything.
*Assistant (English):* If the rejection is arbitrary, you can challenge it in a consumer court. Gather all your medical records, policy documents, and the proposal form. Send a legal notice to the insurance company and then file a complaint for unfair rejection.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Agar unhone bina sahi reason ke claim reject kiya hai, toh aap consumer court mein case kar sakte hain. Apni medical file aur policy documents jama karein aur unko legal notice bhejein.

**Dialogue 7: Bank ATM Dispute**
*Citizen:* ATM se 10,000 deduct ho gaye par cash nahi nikla. Bank refund nahi de raha.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* RBI guidelines ke anusar, bank ko 5 din ke andar refund karna hota hai, warna Rs. 100/day penalty lagti hai. Agar bank mana kare, toh pehle Banking Ombudsman ko complain karein. Wahan se fail ho toh Consumer Court mein case file karein.
*Assistant (English):* As per RBI, banks must reverse failed ATM transactions within 5 days, failing which they pay Rs. 100/day penalty. Complain to the Banking Ombudsman first. If unresolved, approach the consumer court.

**Dialogue 8: No Bill Issued**
*Citizen:* The shopkeeper refused to give a GST bill and gave a kutcha receipt. Can I sue?
*Assistant (English):* Refusing to issue a proper cash memo/bill is an unfair trade practice. While it makes proving the purchase harder, you can still complain to the CCPA (Central Consumer Protection Authority) or the state GST department about this malpractice.
*Assistant (Hindi):* पक्का बिल न देना अनुचित व्यापार प्रथा है। आप सीसीपीए (CCPA) या जीएसटी विभाग से इस धोखाधड़ी की शिकायत कर सकते हैं।

**Dialogue 9: Missing the Limitation Period**
*Citizen:* It has been 3 years since the company cheated me. Can I file a case now?
*Assistant (English):* The general limitation period is 2 years from the date of the incident. However, if you have a very strong and valid reason for the delay, you can file an application for 'Condonation of Delay'. It is up to the judge to accept it.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Waise limit 2 saal ki hoti hai. Par agar aapke paas delay ka koi bahut genuine legal reason hai, toh aap 'Condonation of Delay' application laga sakte hain. Judge agree karein toh case chal sakta है।

**Dialogue 10: Adulterated Food**
*Citizen:* I found an insect inside a sealed cold drink bottle. What should I do?
*Assistant (English):* Do not open or throw the bottle. Take clear photos and videos. Keep the purchase receipt. You can file a complaint with the FSSAI and simultaneously approach the consumer court for heavy compensation and punitive damages against the manufacturer.
*Assistant (Hindi):* बोतल को खोलें या फेंकें नहीं। स्पष्ट फोटो और वीडियो लें और रसीद सुरक्षित रखें। आप FSSAI से शिकायत कर सकते हैं और भारी मुआवजे के लिए उपभोक्ता अदालत भी जा सकते हैं।

**Dialogue 11: Free Medical Camp**
*Citizen:* I received wrong treatment at a free medical camp organized by an NGO. Can I go to consumer court?
*Assistant (English):* Generally, no. Since no 'consideration' (payment) was made by you, you do not qualify as a consumer under the Act for free services. You may have to explore civil or criminal negligence laws instead.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Kyunki aapne free camp mein ilaaj karaya aur paise nahi diye, aap Consumer Act ke under cover nahi hote. Aapko civil court ya police mein negligence ka case dekhna padega.

**Dialogue 12: Appeal Process**
*Citizen:* The District Commission rejected my claim. Is this the end?
*Assistant (English):* No. You have the right to file an appeal before the State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission within 30 days of the District Commission's order.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Nahi, aap District Forum ke order ke khilaf 30 din ke andar State Commission mein appeal daal sakte hain.

**Dialogue 13: Education Fees Refund**
*Citizen:* I paid coaching fees for 1 year, but left after 1 month because the teaching was bad. They won't refund the rest.
*Assistant (English):* Consumer courts have repeatedly ruled that coaching institutes cannot forfeit the entire fee if a student leaves midway. Send a legal notice demanding a pro-rata refund for the un-availed period, and approach the consumer forum if they refuse.
*Assistant (Hindi):* उपभोक्ता अदालतों ने फैसला दिया है कि कोचिंग संस्थान बची हुई अवधि की फीस नहीं रोक सकते। आप बची हुई अवधि के रिफंड के लिए कानूनी नोटिस भेजें और फिर अदालत जाएँ।

**Dialogue 14: Used Car Purchase**
*Citizen:* Maine dealer se second-hand car li thi 'as is' basis par, par usne meter temper kiya tha.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Agar meter tampering (odometer fraud) hui hai, toh ye unfair trade practice and cheating hai. 'As is' clause fraud ko protect nahi karta. Aap unpar E-Daakhil ke through consumer court mein case daal sakte hain.
*Assistant (English):* Meter tampering is an unfair trade practice and fraud. The "as is" clause does not protect a seller from active fraud. You can file a case against the dealer in consumer court.

**Dialogue 15: Overcharging above MRP**
*Citizen:* A restaurant charged me Rs. 40 for a water bottle whose MRP is Rs. 20.
*Assistant (English):* If it's a normal retail shop, charging above MRP is illegal. However, the Supreme Court has ruled that hotels and restaurants *can* charge above MRP for water/drinks because they are providing a composite service (ambience, cutlery, service), not just selling goods.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Agar dukaan wala extra le toh illegal hai. Par Supreme Court ke hisaab se, restaurant ya hotel mein ambiance aur service included hoti hai, isliye wahan MRP se zyada charge karna legally allow kar diya gaya hai.

**English:**
