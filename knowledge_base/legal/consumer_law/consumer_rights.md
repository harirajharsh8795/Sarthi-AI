# Consumer Rights in India: A Comprehensive Legal Guide

## Overview

**English:** The Consumer Protection Act, 2019, forms the bedrock of consumer rights in India. It aims to protect the interests of consumers and establish authorities for timely and effective administration and settlement of consumers' disputes. The law covers all goods and services, whether bought offline or online, and holds manufacturers, service providers, and sellers accountable for defective goods, deficient services, unfair trade practices, and misleading advertisements.

## Definition

**English:** Under Section 2(7) of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, a "Consumer" is any person who buys any goods or hires/avails of any services for a consideration which has been paid or promised or partly paid and partly promised. It includes any user of such goods or beneficiary of services when such use is made with the approval of the buyer. However, it does not include a person who obtains goods for resale or for any commercial purpose.

## Why It Matters

**English:** Understanding consumer rights is critical because it prevents market exploitation, ensures product safety, holds massive corporations accountable, and provides a structured mechanism to claim compensation for financial, physical, or mental harassment caused by defective products or deficient services.

## Legal Background

**History:** The first Consumer Protection Act in India was enacted in 1986, marking a significant milestone in recognizing consumers as a distinct stakeholder group. As the economy liberalized, the market grew complex, bringing in e-commerce, direct selling, and telemarketing. To address these modern challenges, the Parliament enacted the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, which repealed the 1986 Act, bringing a more robust and expansive framework.

**Purpose:** The primary purpose is to provide for the protection of the interests of consumers and to establish authorities for timely and effective administration and settlement of consumers' disputes.

**Applicable Acts:**
- Consumer Protection Act, 2019
- Bureau of Indian Standards Act, 2016
- Legal Metrology Act, 2009
- Sale of Goods Act, 1930
- E-Commerce Rules, 2020 (under CPA 2019)

**Relevant Constitutional Articles:**
- Article 21 (Protection of Life and Personal Liberty): Courts have interpreted the right to safe and hazardous-free products as part of the right to a safe life.

**Relevant Sections of Consumer Protection Act, 2019:**
- Section 2(7): Definition of Consumer.
- Section 2(9): Defines Consumer Rights (Safety, Information, Choice, Representation, Redressal, Education).
- Section 2(10): Defines "Defect" in goods.
- Section 2(11): Defines "Deficiency" in service.
- Section 2(47): Defines "Unfair Trade Practice".
- Section 10: Establishment of Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA).
- Section 34, 47, 58: Jurisdiction of District, State, and National Commissions respectively.
- Section 82 to 87: Product Liability.

## Applicability

**Who is Covered:**
- Any person buying goods (offline or online, through electronic means, teleshopping, direct selling or multi-level marketing) for personal use.
- Any person hiring services (banking, telecom, transport, medical, housing construction, entertainment, electricity, etc.) for personal use.
- Beneficiaries of such goods or services using them with the buyer's approval.
- Individuals buying goods for self-employment (e.g., a tailor buying a sewing machine to earn a livelihood).

**Who is Exempt (Not a Consumer):**
- Persons buying goods strictly for resale.
- Persons buying goods or availing services for large-scale commercial purposes.
- Persons obtaining goods/services free of charge (with exceptions like subsidized healthcare in some scenarios, though completely free government hospital services are usually outside the purview).
- Persons availing services under a contract of personal service (e.g., master-servant relationship).

**Special Conditions:**
- E-commerce platforms (Amazon, Flipkart, etc.) are strictly covered and have specific duties regarding return, refund, and grievance redressal under the Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020.

**English:**
1. **Right to Safety:** Protection against marketing of goods/services hazardous to life and property.
2. **Right to Information:** Right to be informed about quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard, and price.
3. **Right to Choose:** Assured access to a variety of goods and services at competitive prices.
4. **Right to be Heard:** Right to represent and be heard at appropriate consumer forums.
5. **Right to seek Redressal:** Right to seek compensation against unfair trade practices or exploitation.
6. **Right to Consumer Education:** Right to acquire knowledge and skills to be an informed consumer.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English:**
1. **Send a Legal Notice (Optional but Recommended):** Send a formal written notice to the seller/manufacturer outlining the grievance and demanding a resolution within a specific timeframe (e.g., 15 days).
2. **Register on National Consumer Helpline (NCH):** Call 1915 or use the NCH app/website for pre-litigation resolution.
3. **Draft the Complaint:** If unresolved, draft a consumer complaint detailing the transaction, defect/deficiency, evidence, and relief sought.
4. **Determine Jurisdiction:**
   - District Commission: Claims up to ₹50 Lakhs.
   - State Commission: Claims from ₹50 Lakhs to ₹2 Crores.
   - National Commission (NCDRC): Claims exceeding ₹2 Crores.
5. **File via E-Daakhil:** Use the E-Daakhil portal to file the complaint online, upload documents, and pay the nominal fee online.
6. **Admission & Notice:** The Commission admits the complaint and sends a notice to the opposite party giving them 30-45 days to reply.
7. **Mediation (Optional):** The Commission may refer the case to a Consumer Mediation Cell if there is scope for early settlement.
8. **Evidence and Arguments:** Both parties submit evidence (affidavits) and present their arguments.
9. **Final Order:** The Commission passes an order for repair, replacement, refund, and/or compensation.

## Required Documents

- Copy of the Bill, Invoice, or Cash Receipt.
- Proof of Payment (Bank statement, UPI receipt, credit card statement).
- Warranty or Guarantee Card.
- Copy of the Legal Notice sent to the seller/manufacturer and the Delivery Receipt (Postal tracking).
- Any correspondence (emails, chat logs, WhatsApp messages, letters) with the seller/customer care.
- Photographs or videos of the defective product.
- Expert opinion or lab test report (if applicable, e.g., food adulteration).
- An Affidavit stating that the facts in the complaint are true.

## Government Authorities

1. **Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA):** An apex regulatory body established to regulate matters relating to violation of consumer rights, unfair trade practices, and false/misleading advertisements.
2. **District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission:** First level of consumer court for claims up to ₹50 Lakhs.
3. **State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission:** Appellate authority for District Commissions and original jurisdiction for claims between ₹50 Lakhs to ₹2 Crores.
4. **National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (NCDRC):** Apex consumer court located in New Delhi, appellate authority for State Commissions, and original jurisdiction for claims above ₹2 Crores.
5. **Consumer Mediation Cells:** Attached to every Commission for amicable out-of-court settlement.

## Penalties & Punishments

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019, provides stringent penalties:
- **Misleading Advertisements:** CCPA can impose a penalty of up to ₹10 Lakhs on a manufacturer or an endorser (celebrity) for a false or misleading advertisement. For every subsequent offence, the penalty may extend to ₹50 Lakhs.
- **Prohibition on Endorsers:** CCPA can prohibit the endorser of a misleading ad from endorsing any product for up to 1 year; extending to 3 years for subsequent offences.
- **Manufacturing/Selling Adulterated Goods:** 
  - If no injury is caused: Imprisonment up to 6 months and fine up to ₹1 Lakh.
  - Causing non-grievous injury: Imprisonment up to 1 year and fine up to ₹3 Lakhs.
  - Causing grievous injury: Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine up to ₹5 Lakhs.
  - Resulting in death: Imprisonment of not less than 7 years (may extend to life) and a fine of not less than ₹10 Lakhs.
- **Failure to Comply with Commission's Order (Section 72):** Imprisonment for a term not less than 1 month, but which may extend to 3 years, or with fine not less than ₹25,000, which may extend to ₹1,00,000, or both.

## Exceptions

- **Commercial Purchases:** A person who buys goods for resale or commercial profit is not a consumer, except when the goods are bought exclusively for earning a livelihood by self-employment (e.g., a commercial taxi bought by a person who drives it himself for a living).
- **Free Services:** Services rendered free of charge (like a friend repairing your computer for free) do not fall under the Act.
- **Statutory Taxes & Duties:** Disputes regarding the collection of municipal taxes, income tax, or statutory fees do not come under consumer protection.
- **B2B Transactions:** Business-to-Business disputes are generally settled under commercial laws, arbitration, or civil courts, not consumer courts.

## Important Timelines

- **Limitation Period for Filing Complaint:** A complaint must be filed within **2 years** from the date the cause of action arose (e.g., the date of purchase, or the date the defect was discovered, or the date the service was denied).
- **Notice Period to Opposite Party:** The Commission issues a notice to the opposite party directing them to give their version of the case within **30 days**, extendable by 15 days.
- **Appeal to State Commission:** Any person aggrieved by an order made by the District Commission may appeal against such order to the State Commission within **45 days** from the date of the order.
- **Appeal to NCDRC:** An appeal from the State Commission to the National Commission must be filed within **30 days**.
- **Appeal to Supreme Court:** An appeal from the NCDRC to the Supreme Court must be filed within **30 days**.

## Common Legal Mistakes

1. **Not Collecting/Losing the Bill:** Relying only on verbal promises and failing to demand a GST invoice. Without a bill, proving a transaction is extremely difficult.
2. **Ignoring the Limitation Period:** Waiting for years while negotiating with the company's customer care and missing the 2-year legal deadline to file a case.
3. **Accepting Unreasonable Terms in Contracts:** Signing dotted lines on terms that waive consumer rights (though courts often strike down one-sided, unfair contracts).
4. **Filing in Wrong Jurisdiction:** Filing a case in a forum that lacks territorial or pecuniary jurisdiction, leading to delays and rejection.
5. **Not Preserving Evidence:** Throwing away defective goods, damaged packaging, or deleting email/chat conversations with the company.

- जब आपको ऑनलाइन ऑर्डर किया गया कोई महंगा उत्पाद मिले तो अनबॉक्सिंग वीडियो रिकॉर्ड करें।

**Hinglish:**
- Mehngi cheezein kharidte waqt hamesha traceable payment (UPI, Cards) use karein, cash avoid karein.
- Google par customer care number search karke fake numbers/phishing links par bharosa na karein. Official app use karein.
- Online order ki hui mehngi cheez ka parcel kholte waqt hamesha unboxing video banayein (proof ke liye).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Do I need a lawyer to file a case in the Consumer Court?**
**English:** No, you do not need a lawyer. The Consumer Protection Act allows consumers to appear in person or through an authorized representative to present their case. The procedure is designed to be simple and citizen-friendly.
**Hindi:** नहीं, आपको वकील की आवश्यकता नहीं है। आप अपना केस खुद लड़ सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, consumer court mein lawyer ki zarurat nahi hoti, aap apna case khud file aur argue kar sakte hain.

**Q2: What is the fee to file a case?**
**English:** As of current rules, there is ZERO fee for cases valued up to ₹5 Lakhs filed before the District Commission. For higher amounts, a very nominal fee ranging from ₹200 to ₹7500 is applicable depending on the claim amount.
**Hindi:** 5 लाख रुपये तक के दावों के लिए कोई शुल्क नहीं है।
**Hinglish:** ₹5 Lakh tak ke claims ke liye court fee ZERO hai. Uske upar nominal fees lagti hai.

**Q3: Can I file a complaint online?**
**English:** Yes, you can file a complaint online through the E-Daakhil portal (edaakhil.nic.in). It allows you to upload documents, pay the fee online, and track the status of your case without visiting the court initially.
**Hindi:** हां, आप ई-दाखिल पोर्टल के जरिए ऑनलाइन शिकायत दर्ज कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, aap E-Daakhil portal ke through online complaint file kar sakte hain.

**Q4: Where should I file the case? My seller is in another state.**
**English:** Under the 2019 Act, you have the right to file the complaint at the District Commission where YOU (the complainant) reside or work for gain. You do not need to travel to the seller's location.
**Hindi:** 2019 अधिनियम के तहत, आप उस जिला आयोग में शिकायत दर्ज कर सकते हैं जहां आप निवास करते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Naye law ke hisaab se, aap apne residence ya work location wale district court mein case file kar sakte hain, seller ki location par jaane ki zarurat nahi.

**Q5: What is product liability?**
**English:** Product liability means the responsibility of a product manufacturer or product seller, of any product or service, to compensate for any harm caused to a consumer by such defective product manufactured or sold or by deficiency in services.
**Hindi:** उत्पाद दायित्व का अर्थ है किसी दोषपूर्ण उत्पाद से होने वाले नुकसान के लिए निर्माता या विक्रेता की जिम्मेदारी।
**Hinglish:** Product liability matlab agar kisi defective product se aapko koi nuksaan hota hai, toh manufacturer ya seller ko uska compensation dena hoga.

**Q6: Can I sue for mental agony?**
**English:** Yes, Consumer Commissions frequently award compensation for mental agony, physical harassment, and the legal costs incurred by the consumer, in addition to the refund or replacement.
**Hindi:** हां, उपभोक्ता आयोग अक्सर मानसिक पीड़ा के लिए मुआवजा देते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, refund ke alawa Consumer Courts mental agony aur harassment ke liye bhi extra compensation grant karte hain.

**Q7: I bought goods at a discounted sale with a "No Return, No Exchange" tag. Can I still complain if it is defective?**
**English:** Yes. "No Return, No Exchange" boards or terms are legally invalid if the goods have an inherent manufacturing defect. Sellers cannot contract out of their liability to provide non-defective goods.
**Hindi:** हां। यदि सामान में विनिर्माण दोष है, तो "नो रिटर्न" बोर्ड कानूनी रूप से अमान्य हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan. Agar product defective hai, toh "No Return" ka tag legally invalid hai. Company ko defective product replace ya refund karna hi hoga.

**Q8: Does medical negligence fall under consumer protection?**
**English:** Yes. Medical services, when availed for a consideration (payment), fall under the purview of the Consumer Protection Act. Patients can file cases against doctors or hospitals for medical negligence as a 'deficiency in service'.
**Hindi:** हां, भुगतान लेकर दी जाने वाली चिकित्सा सेवाएं उपभोक्ता संरक्षण के अंतर्गत आती हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar aapne fee pay ki hai, toh medical negligence (doctor ki laparwahi) ko 'deficiency in service' maankar consumer court mein case kiya ja sakta hai.

**Q9: What if the company has closed down or gone bankrupt?**
**English:** If a company undergoes insolvency proceedings under the IBC (Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code), a moratorium is placed, and consumer cases against them cannot proceed. Consumers must file their claims before the Resolution Professional as creditors.
**Hindi:** यदि कंपनी दिवालिया हो जाती है, तो उपभोक्ताओं को आईबीसी के तहत लेनदार के रूप में अपना दावा प्रस्तुत करना होगा।
**Hinglish:** Agar company bankrupt ho gayi hai (jaise Jaypee Infratech), toh aapko NCLT/Resolution Professional ke paas as a creditor apna claim file karna padega.

**Q10: Are coaching institutes covered under this Act?**
**English:** Yes. Educational institutions and coaching centers that charge fees but fail to provide promised infrastructure, classes, or refuse justified fee refunds fall under 'deficiency in service' and unfair trade practices.
**Hindi:** हां, फीस लेने वाले लेकिन वादे पूरी न करने वाले कोचिंग संस्थान इसके अंतर्गत आते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar coaching centre fees lekar padhai nahi karata ya refund policy follow nahi karta, toh unpar consumer case ho sakta hai.

*(Note: FAQs 11 to 20 focus on specific sectoral issues like banking, real estate, telecom, aviation, and e-commerce, echoing similar principles of deficiency and unfair trade practices, consistently affirming the consumer's right to redressal as per the 2019 Act.)*

**Q11: Can a shopkeeper charge more than the MRP?**
**English:** No. Maximum Retail Price (MRP) is the highest price that can be charged. Charging above MRP is a legal offense. You can bargain for a price lower than the MRP.
**Hindi:** नहीं। एमआरपी से अधिक शुल्क लेना एक कानूनी अपराध है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, MRP se zyada charge karna illegal hai. MRP maximum price hota hai, usse zyada koi nahi le sakta.

**Q12: Are delivery charges extra over MRP online?**
**English:** Yes, an e-commerce site can charge delivery fees over the MRP, but the product's actual price cannot exceed the MRP. The delivery fee must be clearly stated before purchase.
**Hindi:** हां, ई-कॉमर्स साइटें एमआरपी से अधिक डिलीवरी शुल्क ले सकती हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, online shopping mein MRP ke upar delivery charge lag sakta hai, lekin product ki price MRP se zyada nahi ho sakti.

**Q13: What if my flight is severely delayed or canceled?**
**English:** Under DGCA guidelines and Consumer Protection Act, you are entitled to meals, refreshments, alternative flights, or full refunds depending on the length of the delay. Failure to provide this is a deficiency in service.
**Hindi:** उड़ान में देरी होने पर आप भोजन, वैकल्पिक उड़ान या धनवापसी के हकदार हैं।
**Hinglish:** Flight cancel ya delay hone par aapko DGCA rules ke according refund ya alternate flight milni chahiye, warna consumer court ja sakte hain.

**Q14: Can restaurants forcefully charge a 'Service Charge'?**
**English:** The CCPA guidelines state that service charge is voluntary and at the discretion of the consumer. A restaurant cannot mandate it, and forcing it is considered an unfair trade practice.
**Hindi:** नहीं, सेवा शुल्क स्वैच्छिक है और रेस्तरां इसे अनिवार्य नहीं कर सकते।
**Hinglish:** CCPA rules ke anusar Service Charge dena aapki marzi hai. Restaurant ise forcefully bill mein add karke nahi maang sakta.

**Q15: Can I claim insurance if the company wrongly rejects it?**
**English:** Yes. Wrongful repudiation of insurance claims is one of the most common consumer disputes. If an insurance company rejects a valid claim on flimsy grounds, the Consumer Court can order them to pay the claim amount with interest.
**Hindi:** हां। यदि बीमा कंपनी गलत तरीके से दावों को खारिज करती है तो आप उपभोक्ता न्यायालय जा सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar insurance company galat reason dekar claim reject kare, toh aap Consumer court se claim ka paisa aur interest le sakte hain.

**Q16: Is an Aadhaar card required to file a complaint?**
**English:** While identity proof is required to establish your credentials, Aadhaar is not strictly mandatory for filing a consumer case, though it is highly recommended for E-Daakhil registration.
**Hindi:** शिकायत दर्ज करने के लिए आधार सख्ती से अनिवार्य नहीं है, हालांकि पहचान प्रमाण आवश्यक है।
**Hinglish:** Identity proof zaroori hai, par Aadhaar strictly mandatory nahi hai. E-Daakhil par register karne ke liye kaafi useful rehta hai.

**Q17: What is CCPA and how is it different from Consumer Courts?**
**English:** The CCPA is an executive regulatory body that can take suo-moto action, initiate class-action lawsuits, and ban misleading ads. Consumer Courts are quasi-judicial bodies that adjudicate individual complaints.
**Hindi:** सीसीपीए एक नियामक निकाय है जो स्वतः संज्ञान ले सकता है, जबकि उपभोक्ता न्यायालय व्यक्तिगत शिकायतों का फैसला करते हैं।
**Hinglish:** CCPA ek regulatory body hai jo khud se (suo-moto) fake ads aur unfair practices par action le sakti hai. Consumer courts individual complaints sunte hain.

**Q18: What is 'E-commerce' under the CPA 2019?**
**English:** It means buying or selling of goods or services, including digital products, over a digital or electronic network. The E-commerce rules make marketplaces (like Amazon) share liability in certain dispute scenarios.
**Hindi:** इसका अर्थ है डिजिटल या इलेक्ट्रॉनिक नेटवर्क पर वस्तुओं या सेवाओं की खरीद या बिक्री।
**Hinglish:** E-commerce matlab internet ke through goods/services kharidna ya bechna. Naye rules in platforms ko bhi responsible banate hain.

**Q19: Can a builder be sued in Consumer Court for delayed possession?**
**English:** Yes. Housing construction falls under 'service'. Severe delay in handing over possession amounts to a deficiency in service, and buyers can claim a refund with interest or compensation.
**Hindi:** हां। आवास निर्माण 'सेवा' के अंतर्गत आता है और बिल्डरों पर मुकदमा किया जा सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan. Agar builder flat ka possession time par nahi deta, toh ye 'deficiency in service' hai, aur aap refund with interest claim kar sakte hain.

**Q20: Can I complain against a government department (like Electricity or Water board)?**
**English:** Yes. If you pay a tariff for electricity, water, or telecom services provided by a government body, you are a consumer. You can sue them for deficient service.
**Hindi:** हां। यदि आप सरकारी निकायों द्वारा प्रदान की जाने वाली सेवाओं के लिए शुल्क देते हैं, तो आप एक उपभोक्ता हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar aap Bijli ya Pani ka bill pay karte hain aur service kharab hai, toh aap government department par bhi consumer case kar sakte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** You must hire an expensive lawyer to go to Consumer Court.
   **Fact:** The process is simplified. You can draft and argue your own case in person or authorize a trusted relative/friend.
2. **Myth:** Consumer cases take 10-15 years to resolve.
   **Fact:** The law mandates disposal of cases within 3 to 5 months. While pendency exists, it is much faster than civil courts.
3. **Myth:** I can't do anything if I bought an item on a 'sale'.
   **Fact:** A product sold at a discount still carries an implied warranty of merchantability. You can demand replacement for manufacturing defects.
4. **Myth:** If I lose the original physical bill, my case is void.
   **Fact:** While a physical bill is strong evidence, digital invoices, bank transaction SMS, credit card statements, and warranty cards are fully valid proofs.
5. **Myth:** The seller says "Company is responsible, not me."
   **Fact:** Both the manufacturer and the seller are jointly liable. You can make both of them parties to your complaint.
6. **Myth:** Consumer courts only handle goods, not services.
   **Fact:** Services like banking, insurance, education, medical, transport, and telecom are fully covered.
7. **Myth:** Online shopping from foreign sites isn't covered.
   **Fact:** Any e-commerce entity systematically offering goods/services to Indian consumers falls under the jurisdiction of Indian Consumer Protection Laws.
8. **Myth:** If a celebrity endorses a product, it must be safe.
   **Fact:** Celebrities can be misled too. The CPA 2019 now holds endorsers legally liable if they endorse false claims without doing due diligence.
9. **Myth:** You have to file the case where the company's headquarters are located.
   **Fact:** Under the 2019 Act, you can file the complaint at the district where you reside or work.
10. **Myth:** A printed "Goods once sold will not be taken back" on the receipt is a binding contract.
    **Fact:** This is legally void if the good is defective. It is considered an unfair trade practice.

## Conversation Examples

**Dialogue 1: Defective Mobile Phone**
*Citizen:* Maina ek naya mobile liya tha 2 din pehle, screen apne aap black ho gayi. Shopkeeper bol raha hai "Service center jao, meri responsibility nahi". (I bought a new mobile 2 days ago, the screen went black. Shopkeeper says go to the service center, not his responsibility.)
*Assistant:* Shopkeeper aapse apni responsibility se bhaag nahi sakta. Consumer Protection Act ke mutabiq, seller aur manufacturer dono responsible hain. Aap pehle National Consumer Helpline (1915) par call karke shopkeeper ke khilaaf complaint darj karein. Agar wo replace nahi karta, toh aap E-Daakhil ke zariye Consumer Court mein case file kar sakte hain. Aapke paas bill zaroor hona chahiye.

**Dialogue 2: Hospital Overcharging / Negligence**
*Citizen:* The hospital charged me for procedures they didn't even perform on my father. They refuse to revise the bill.
*Assistant:* This constitutes a severe deficiency in service and an unfair trade practice. First, demand a detailed itemized bill and medical records. Send a formal legal notice to the hospital management demanding a refund of the overcharged amount. If they ignore it, you can file a complaint with the District or State Consumer Commission (depending on the claim amount) seeking a refund and compensation for mental agony.

**Dialogue 3: E-commerce Refusing Refund**
*Citizen:* Maine online shoes order kiye the, size galat aaya. "No return policy" bolkar website refund nahi de rahi. (I ordered shoes online, wrong size arrived. Website is denying refund citing "No return policy".)
*Assistant:* Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020 ke anusar, agar wrong, defective, ya spurious (fake) item deliver hua hai, toh e-commerce platform aapko return aur refund deny nahi kar sakta. "No Return" policy aise cases mein invalid hoti hai. Aap pehle platform ke Grievance Officer ko mail karein. Agar 1 month mein resolution na mile, toh E-Daakhil par complaint file karein.
