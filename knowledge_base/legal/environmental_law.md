# Environmental Law in India (Paryavaran Kanoon / पर्यावरण कानून)

## Overview
**English:** Environmental law in India encompasses a comprehensive framework of legislations, constitutional provisions, and judicial precedents designed to protect the environment, prevent pollution, and ensure sustainable development. It empowers citizens to take legal action against environmental degradation.

## Legal Background

### History & Purpose
Following the UN Conference on the Human Environment (Stockholm, 1972) and the Bhopal Gas Tragedy (1984), India rapidly strengthened its environmental jurisprudence to provide an overarching legal framework for environmental protection.

### Relevant Constitutional Articles
- **Article 21:** Right to Life, heavily interpreted by the Supreme Court to include the Right to a Clean Environment.
- **Article 48A:** Directive Principle stating the State shall endeavor to protect and improve the environment and safeguard forests and wildlife.
- **Article 51A(g):** Fundamental Duty of every citizen to protect and improve the natural environment including forests, lakes, rivers, and wildlife, and to have compassion for living creatures.

### Applicable Acts
- **The Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 (EPA):** The umbrella legislation for environmental protection.
- **The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974.**
- **The Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981.**
- **The National Green Tribunal Act, 2010 (NGT Act):** Established specialized environmental courts.
- **The Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972.**
- **The Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980.**
- **The Biological Diversity Act, 2002.**

### Relevant Sections under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS)
- **Section 270 (BNS):** Public nuisance (replaces IPC Section 268) – applicable when pollution causes common injury, danger, or annoyance to the public.
- **Section 278 (BNS):** Making atmosphere noxious to health (replaces IPC Section 278) – vitiating the atmosphere so as to make it noxious to the health of persons in the neighborhood.
- **Section 277 (BNS):** Fouling water of public spring or reservoir (replaces IPC Section 277).

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** All citizens, residents, private corporations, public sector undertakings, and government agencies operating within the territory of India.
- **Who is exempt:** No one is fully exempt. However, certain strategic defense projects and specific national security infrastructure may receive accelerated clearances or partial exemptions from public hearing requirements, though they still require environmental safeguards.
- **Special conditions:** Special Economic Zones (SEZs) and certain industrial categories (Green or White categories as per CPCB) have simplified compliance, while Red category industries have stringent monitoring.

## Rights of Citizens
**English:** Citizens have the right to breathe clean air, access safe drinking water, demand information on environmental clearances, and file complaints or Public Interest Litigations (PILs) against polluters.

**Hinglish:** Citizens ki constitutional duty hai ki wo natural resources ko protect karein, kachra na failayein, waste ko sahi se manage karein, aur illegal environmental damage ki report karein.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

### Filing an Environmental Complaint
**English:**
1. **Identify the Authority:** Determine if the issue is local (Municipal Corporation) or industrial (State Pollution Control Board - SPCB).
2. **Collect Evidence:** Gather photographs, videos, location tags, and witness statements. For noise pollution, record the decibel levels if possible.
3. **First Complaint:** File a written complaint to the local Police Station (under BNS for public nuisance) or the Municipal Ward Officer.
4. **Escalation to SPCB:** If unresolved, file a formal complaint on the SPCB's online portal or via registered post.
5. **Filing in NGT:** If the SPCB fails to act, hire an environmental lawyer to file a petition before the National Green Tribunal within a period of 6 months from the date the cause of action first arose.
6. **Public Interest Litigation (PIL):** For large-scale environmental damage, a PIL can be filed directly in the High Court or Supreme Court.

**Hindi Unicode (चरण-दर-चरण प्रक्रिया):**
1. **प्राधिकरण की पहचान करें:** तय करें कि मुद्दा स्थानीय (नगर निगम) है या औद्योगिक (राज्य प्रदूषण नियंत्रण बोर्ड - SPCB)।
2. **सबूत इकट्ठा करें:** तस्वीरें, वीडियो, लोकेशन टैग और गवाहों के बयान इकट्ठा करें।
3. **पहली शिकायत:** स्थानीय पुलिस स्टेशन या नगर निगम वार्ड अधिकारी को लिखित शिकायत दर्ज करें।
4. **SPCB को वृद्धि:** यदि समाधान नहीं होता है, तो SPCB के ऑनलाइन पोर्टल पर या पंजीकृत डाक से औपचारिक शिकायत दर्ज करें।
5. **NGT में फाइलिंग:** यदि SPCB कार्रवाई नहीं करता है, तो कारण उत्पन्न होने के 6 महीने के भीतर राष्ट्रीय हरित अधिकरण में याचिका दायर करने के लिए एक पर्यावरण वकील को नियुक्त करें।
6. **जनहित याचिका (PIL):** बड़े पैमाने पर पर्यावरणीय क्षति के लिए सीधे उच्च न्यायालय या सर्वोच्च न्यायालय में जनहित याचिका दायर की जा सकती है।

## Required Documents
- Identity Proof of the complainant (Aadhaar, Voter ID).
- Written complaint detailing the date, time, location, and nature of the pollution.
- Photographic or videographic evidence (preferably geo-tagged and time-stamped).
- Copies of any prior complaints made to local authorities.
- If approaching NGT, a formally drafted petition, affidavit, and prescribed fee receipt.
- Laboratory analysis reports (if dealing with water/soil contamination, tested by certified labs).

## Government Authorities
- **MoEFCC (Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change):** Central policy-making body.
- **CPCB (Central Pollution Control Board):** Sets national standards for air and water quality.
- **SPCB (State Pollution Control Boards) / DPCC (Delhi Pollution Control Committee):** Enforce standards, grant consent to establish/operate industries, and monitor compliance.
- **National Green Tribunal (NGT):** Fast-track specialized court for environmental disputes.
- **Local Police:** For immediate action regarding public nuisance, loud music, or illegal tree felling under BNS.
- **Municipal Corporations:** For solid waste management and local sanitation issues.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Environment (Protection) Act, 1986:** Violation can result in imprisonment up to 5 years, or a fine up to ₹1,00,000, or both. Continued violation attracts an additional fine of ₹5,000 per day.
- **Water Act & Air Act:** Imprisonment from 1.5 to 6 years and fines for discharging pollutants beyond prescribed standards.
- **NGT Act:** NGT can impose severe financial penalties (environmental compensation) which can run into crores of rupees depending on the extent of damage ("Polluter Pays" principle). Non-compliance with NGT orders can lead to imprisonment up to 3 years or fine up to ₹10 Crores (₹25 Crores for companies).
- **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS):**
  - Section 278 (Noxious atmosphere): Fine which may extend to ₹1,000.
  - Section 277 (Fouling water): Imprisonment up to 3 months, or fine up to ₹500, or both.

## Exceptions
- Government-sanctioned projects that have valid Environmental Clearances (EC) are exempt from general prohibition, provided they operate strictly within the mandated conditions.
- Traditional rights of forest-dwelling Scheduled Tribes and other traditional forest dwellers are protected under the Forest Rights Act, 2006, exempting them from certain restrictions of the Forest Conservation Act.
- Diesel generator usage during severe grid failures, when explicitly permitted by emergency municipal orders, may be temporarily exempt from noise/air pollution bans.

## Important Timelines
- **NGT Petition Filing:** Must be filed within 6 months from the date the cause of action first arose.
- **Appeal in NGT:** An appeal against an order of the SPCB or MoEFCC must be filed within 30 days (extendable by 60 days for sufficient cause).
- **Appeal to Supreme Court:** Any person aggrieved by an NGT decision can appeal to the Supreme Court within 90 days.
- **RTI Responses:** When seeking environmental data via RTI, authorities must respond within 30 days (48 hours if it concerns the life and liberty/immediate severe health hazard of a person).

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Delay in Filing:** Waiting for years before approaching the NGT, causing the petition to be dismissed on grounds of limitation.
- **Lack of Hard Evidence:** Filing complaints based purely on verbal allegations without photos, videos, or expert reports.
- **Approaching Wrong Forum:** Filing a civil suit in a lower civil court for an environmental issue that strictly falls under NGT jurisdiction (Civil courts are barred from hearing matters covered by the NGT Act).
- **Ignoring Local Authorities:** Jumping directly to the NGT or High Court without first exhausting administrative remedies (complaining to SPCB/Municipality).

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **Can I complain against my neighbor for burning garbage?**
   *English:* Yes. Burning garbage, especially plastic, is illegal under the Solid Waste Management Rules and the EPA. You can complain to the municipal corporation or local police.
   *Hindi:* हाँ। कचरा, विशेषकर प्लास्टिक जलाना, ठोस अपशिष्ट प्रबंधन नियमों और EPA के तहत अवैध है। आप नगर निगम या स्थानीय पुलिस से शिकायत कर सकते हैं।
   *Hinglish:* Haan. Kachra, especially plastic jalana illegal hai. Aap municipality ya local police ko complaint kar sakte hain.

2. **What are the permissible noise levels in residential areas?**
   *English:* As per the Noise Pollution (Regulation and Control) Rules, 2000, the limit in residential areas is 55 dB during the day (6 AM to 10 PM) and 45 dB at night (10 PM to 6 AM).
   *Hindi:* ध्वनि प्रदूषण नियमों के अनुसार, आवासीय क्षेत्रों में सीमा दिन के दौरान 55 dB और रात में 45 dB है।
   *Hinglish:* Residential areas mein din mein 55 dB aur raat mein 45 dB ki limit allowed hai.

3. **How do I stop someone from cutting a tree illegally?**
   *English:* Immediately call the local police (Dial 112) and the municipal or forest department's tree officer. Unauthorized tree cutting violates state Preservation of Trees Acts.
   *Hindi:* तुरंत स्थानीय पुलिस (112) और नगर निगम या वन विभाग के ट्री ऑफिसर को कॉल करें।
   *Hinglish:* Turant 112 par police ko aur municipal tree officer ko call karein. Bina permission ped kaatna illegal hai.

4. **Is it mandatory for industries to treat their wastewater?**
   *English:* Absolutely. Under the Water Act, all industries must operate Effluent Treatment Plants (ETP) and meet discharge standards before releasing wastewater into the environment.
   *Hindi:* बिल्कुल। जल अधिनियम के तहत, सभी उद्योगों को अपशिष्ट जल उपचार संयंत्र (ETP) संचालित करना चाहिए।
   *Hinglish:* Bilkul. Water Act ke hisaab se sabhi factories ko ganda paani saaf karne ke liye ETP lagana zaroori hai.

5. **Who can file a case in the National Green Tribunal?**
   *English:* Any aggrieved person, organization, or citizen concerned about environmental protection can file a petition in the NGT.
   *Hindi:* कोई भी पीड़ित व्यक्ति, संगठन या नागरिक जो पर्यावरण संरक्षण के बारे में चिंतित है, NGT में याचिका दायर कर सकता है।
   *Hinglish:* Koi bhi citizen ya NGO jo environment protect karna chahta hai, wo NGT mein case file kar sakta hai.

6. **Do I need a lawyer to file a complaint with the Pollution Control Board?**
   *English:* No, you do not need a lawyer to file an administrative complaint with the SPCB. A simple written application or online form is sufficient.
   *Hindi:* नहीं, SPCB में शिकायत दर्ज करने के लिए वकील की आवश्यकता नहीं है।
   *Hinglish:* Nahi, SPCB mein complaint karne ke liye lawyer ki zaroorat nahi hoti, aap simple letter ya online portal se kar sakte hain.

7. **What is the penalty for loud DJs playing after 10 PM?**
   *English:* Playing loud music after 10 PM without special permission violates noise pollution rules. Police can confiscate the equipment and impose fines under local laws and BNS.
   *Hindi:* रात 10 बजे के बाद तेज संगीत बजाना नियमों का उल्लंघन है। पुलिस उपकरण जब्त कर सकती है और जुर्माना लगा सकती है।
   *Hinglish:* Raat 10 baje ke baad bina permission DJ bajana illegal hai. Police equipment seize kar sakti hai aur fine laga sakti hai.

8. **Can civil courts hear environmental disputes?**
   *English:* Generally, no. Section 29 of the NGT Act bars civil courts from hearing matters that fall under the jurisdiction of the National Green Tribunal.
   *Hindi:* आमतौर पर नहीं। NGT अधिनियम की धारा 29 सिविल अदालतों को उन मामलों की सुनवाई से रोकती है जो NGT के अधिकार क्षेत्र में आते हैं।
   *Hinglish:* Normally nahi. NGT Act ke under, environmental matters mostly NGT handle karta hai, civil courts nahi.

9. **What is "Polluter Pays" principle?**
   *English:* It is a legal principle stating that those who produce pollution should bear the costs of managing it to prevent damage to human health or the environment.
   *Hindi:* यह एक कानूनी सिद्धांत है जिसमें कहा गया है कि जो लोग प्रदूषण फैलाते हैं उन्हें पर्यावरण को होने वाले नुकसान की भरपाई करनी चाहिए।
   *Hinglish:* Ye ek legal rule hai jiska matlab hai ki jo pollution karega, wahi nuksan bharne ka paisa dega.

10. **How can I check if a factory near me has an Environmental Clearance?**
    *English:* You can check the Parivesh portal of the MoEFCC, the respective SPCB website, or file an RTI application to obtain this information.
    *Hindi:* आप MoEFCC के परिवेश पोर्टल, संबंधित SPCB वेबसाइट की जांच कर सकते हैं, या RTI दायर कर सकते हैं।
    *Hinglish:* Aap MoEFCC ke Parivesh portal ya SPCB website par check kar sakte hain, ya RTI file karke information le sakte hain.

11. **Are construction activities allowed at night?**
    *English:* Construction causing noise pollution at night is generally banned in residential areas. Permissible times are usually between 6 AM and 10 PM.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** The police cannot do anything about loud DJ music; only the NGT can.
   **Fact:** The local police have full authority to act immediately against noise pollution under local laws and the BNS.
2. **Myth:** I must prove that pollution made me sick to file a case.
   **Fact:** You do not need to show personal medical injury. The mere violation of environmental norms is sufficient to complain.
3. **Myth:** Trees inside my private boundary wall can be cut without permission.
   **Fact:** In most urban areas, you need permission from the municipal Tree Officer to cut mature trees, even on your private property.
4. **Myth:** Environmental cases drag on for decades like civil cases.
   **Fact:** The NGT is mandated to dispose of applications or appeals finally within 6 months of filing as far as possible.
5. **Myth:** Only affected villagers can protest against a polluting factory.
   **Fact:** Any public-spirited citizen or NGO can file a petition in the NGT or a PIL in the High Court for environmental protection.
6. **Myth:** All industries are allowed to release some smoke.
   **Fact:** Industries can only emit emissions strictly within the parameters and limits defined in their 'Consent to Operate' granted by the SPCB.
7. **Myth:** NGT handles every single environmental issue.
   **Fact:** NGT only handles civil cases involving substantial questions relating to the environment arising out of specific scheduled Acts. It doesn't handle criminal trials for wildlife poaching.
8. **Myth:** Burning dry leaves in winter is a safe and legal way to dispose of them.
   **Fact:** Burning leaves causes severe air pollution and is strictly prohibited and penalised in urban jurisdictions.
9. **Myth:** Ground water belongs to the person who owns the land and they can pump unlimited amounts.
   **Fact:** Extraction of groundwater for commercial or industrial use requires NOC from the Central Ground Water Authority (CGWA).
10. **Myth:** Banning single-use plastic is just a guideline, not a law.
    **Fact:** The ban on specific single-use plastics is a legally binding notification under the Environment (Protection) Act, and violations carry fines and imprisonment.

## Conversation Examples

**Dialogue 1: Garbage Burning**
*Citizen:* Sir, mere padosi roz sham ko park ke paas kachra aur plastic jalate hain. Saans lena mushkil ho gaya hai.
*Assistant:* Yeh Solid Waste Management Rules aur Environment Protection Act ke khilaf hai. Aap iski photo ya video banakar apne Municipal Corporation ward officer ya local police ko 112 par call karke 'public nuisance' ki complaint darj karwa sakte hain.

**Dialogue 2: Illegal Tree Felling**
*Citizen:* My RWA is cutting down two huge Peepal trees inside our society without any reason. What can I do?
*Assistant:* Unauthorized tree felling is an offense. Immediately demand to see the permission from the municipal Tree Officer. If they don't have it, call 112 for police assistance and inform the Forest Department/Municipal Corporation immediately to halt the cutting.

**Dialogue 3: Noise Pollution at Night**
*Citizen:* Raat ke 1 baje tak pados ke ground mein DJ baj raha hai. Police kuch nahi kar rahi.
*Assistant:* Noise Pollution Rules ke hisaab se 10 PM ke baad loudspeakers allowed nahi hain. Aap 112 par dubara call karein aur agar action na ho, toh evidence ke saath SP (Superintendent of Police) ya District Magistrate ko written complaint dein.

**Dialogue 4: Industrial Effluents in River**
*Citizen:* Ek factory ka ganda, neela paani seedha naddi mein ja raha hai. Mujhe kya karna chahiye?
*Assistant:* Yeh Water Act ka violation hai. Aap turant iski video banayein (location ke saath) aur State Pollution Control Board (SPCB) ke regional office mein written complaint dein. Aap CPCB ke online portal par bhi grievance dal sakte hain.

**Dialogue 5: Approaching NGT**
*Citizen:* The municipality is dumping the entire city's waste into the nearby lake. No official is listening. Can I go to court?
*Assistant:* Yes, since the administrative authorities are failing, this is a perfect case for the National Green Tribunal (NGT). You should hire an environmental lawyer and file an original application in the NGT for a permanent injunction and restoration of the lake.

**Dialogue 6: Dust from Construction**
*Citizen:* Mere ghar ke samne bahut bada construction chal raha hai aur unhone green net nahi lagaya hai. Bahut dhool ud rahi hai.
*Assistant:* Construction sites par dust control measures (jaise green net lagana aur paani chidakna) mandatory hain. Aap iski complaint local municipality, RERA authority, ya State Pollution Control Board ko kar sakte hain.

**Dialogue 7: Vehicle Emission Issue**
*Citizen:* Is there any portal where I can complain about buses that are emitting huge clouds of black smoke?
*Assistant:* Yes, you can take a clear picture of the bus with its number plate and report it to your city's Traffic Police via their official app or X (formerly Twitter) handle. The Regional Transport Office (RTO) can cancel their permit.

**Dialogue 8: E-Waste Disposal**
*Citizen:* I have a lot of old computers and batteries. Can I throw them in the regular garbage?
*Assistant:* No, throwing e-waste in regular garbage is illegal under E-Waste (Management) Rules. You must give them to authorized e-waste dismantlers or drop them at official municipal collection centers.

**Dialogue 9: Groundwater Extraction**
*Citizen:* Ek builder ne society ke paas illegal borewell lagaya hai aur roz tankaro se paani bech raha hai.
*Assistant:* Commercial use ke liye bina CGWA (Central Ground Water Authority) ki permission ke borewell lagana illegal hai. Aap District Magistrate (DM) ya CGWA ki website par iski complaint darj karein.

**Dialogue 10: Protection of Wetlands**
*Citizen:* Kuch log shehar ke bahar ek jheel/wetland mein mitti bhar rahe hain plot kaatne ke liye.
*Assistant:* Wetlands (Conservation and Management) Rules ke under wetlands ko barna ya waha construction karna strictly prohibited hai. Aapko turant State Wetland Authority aur District Collector ko suchit karna chahiye.

**Dialogue 11: RTI for Environment Data**
*Citizen:* Mujhe kaise pata chalega ki mere gaon ke paas wali factory ko pollution board se clearance mili hai ya nahi?
*Assistant:* Aap Right to Information (RTI) Act ke under State Pollution Control Board (SPCB) mein ek application laga kar us factory ki 'Consent to Establish' aur 'Consent to Operate' ki copy maang sakte hain.

**Dialogue 12: Action against SPCB**
*Citizen:* SPCB is not acting on my complaint against a polluting factory despite multiple reminders. What is the next step?
*Assistant:* If the SPCB is inactive, you can approach the National Green Tribunal (NGT) under Section 14 of the NGT Act. You can also file a writ petition in the High Court seeking a writ of mandamus to force the SPCB to perform its statutory duty.

**Dialogue 13: Stubble Burning**
*Citizen:* Kisaan khet mein fasal ke bache hue hisse (stubble) jala rahe hain, dhuaan bahut hai.
*Assistant:* Fasal ka kachra jalana (stubble burning) NGT ke orders aur Air Act ke khilaf hai. Halanki, sarkar kisano ko subsidy aur machinery deti hai ise rokne ke liye. Aap local Tehsildar ya agriculture officer ko inform kar sakte hain.

**Dialogue 14: DG Set Pollution**
*Citizen:* The society's diesel generator (DG) set makes too much noise and emits thick smoke.
*Assistant:* DG sets must comply with CPCB emission and noise standards and require acoustic enclosures (soundproof canopies). You can complain to the State Pollution Control Board (SPCB) to inspect and penalize the management.

**Dialogue 15: Animal Cruelty & Environment**
*Citizen:* Kuch log society mein stray dogs ko zahar dekar maar rahe hain, kya yeh environmental law mein aata hai?
*Assistant:* Yeh Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act aur BNS ke under serious crime hai, aur Constitution ke Article 51A(g) ka violation hai (compassion for living creatures). Aap turant police FIR darj karwayein.
