## Legal Background
The necessity of a legal disclaimer stems from the principles of tort law, specifically negligence and misrepresentation, as well as statutory frameworks governing electronic contracts and information intermediaries. 
- **Information Technology Act, 2000 (Section 79):** Provides a safe harbor for intermediaries, stating they shall not be liable for any third-party information, data, or communication link made available or hosted by them, subject to certain conditions. Saarthi AI acts as an information provider and aggregator.
- **Indian Contract Act, 1872 (Section 10 & 11):** Establishes the fundamentals of valid contracts and consent, highlighting that terms of use and disclaimers form a binding agreement when a user accesses the platform.
- **Advocates Act, 1961 (Section 29):** States that only advocates entered in the roll of a State Bar Council are entitled to practice the profession of law. Saarthi AI is an AI technology and does not practice law.
- **Consumer Protection Act, 2019:** Ensures that services are not deficient, but disclaimers explicitly clarify that the "service" provided is information, not legal representation, thus mitigating claims of deficiency in legal service.
The legal frameworks under the new Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), and Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) also mandate precise reliance on official documents for evidentiary or procedural validity, further reinforcing the need to consult original texts.

## Exceptions

## Important Timelines
- **Limitation Act, 1963:** Every legal remedy has an expiration date. For example, civil suits for breach of contract generally must be filed within 3 years. Relying on AI without checking the specific limitation period for your unique case facts can result in your case being time-barred.
- **RTI Appeals:** 30 days for first appeal, 90 days for second appeal.
- **Filing FIR:** Should be done immediately; delays require satisfactory explanation to the court.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Assuming AI is a Lawyer:** Believing that an AI's interpretation of a statute perfectly matches how a judge will interpret it in a specific context.
- **Missing Deadlines:** Reading about a right but failing to realize the limitation period has expired.
- **DIY Lawyering in Complex Cases:** Attempting self-representation in criminal or complex civil matters based solely on internet research.
- **Misinterpreting Nuance:** Failing to understand exceptions or provisos in legal sections that change the outcome completely.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** AI legal tools are exact replacements for human lawyers.
   **Fact:** AI provides structured information and explanations; human lawyers provide strategic counsel, empathy, court representation, and accountability, which AI cannot offer.
   
2. **Myth:** If a legal article says I can get bail, I will automatically get it.
   **Fact:** Bail is often a matter of judicial discretion depending on the specific facts, gravity of the offense, and the judge's assessment, regardless of theoretical eligibility.
   
3. **Myth:** I can sign a contract drafted entirely by AI without reading it.
   **Fact:** Every contract binds you legally. Using an AI draft without reviewing or understanding the clauses can lead to severe financial or legal liabilities.
   
4. **Myth:** Legal disclaimers are just standard text and hold no real power in court.
   **Fact:** Explicit terms of use and disclaimers form a binding electronic contract and heavily restrict liability under the Information Technology Act.
   
5. **Myth:** Knowing the law is enough to win a case.
   **Fact:** Knowing the law is only part of the equation; presenting evidence properly under the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) and procedural tactics are equally vital.
   
6. **Myth:** The police must immediately follow whatever the AI says the law is.
   **Fact:** Police follow operational protocols and the CRPC/BNSS. Pointing out AI information does not override their on-ground procedural authority; only a court order can.
   
7. **Myth:** Using legal terms makes my arguments stronger.
   **Fact:** Misusing legal jargon can confuse the issue and annoy judges or officials. Clarity and factual accuracy are much more powerful than unnecessary complex words.
   
8. **Myth:** Any law I read online is currently valid.
   **Fact:** Laws are frequently amended, struck down by the Supreme Court, or replaced (e.g., IPC replaced by BNS). Always check if the law is currently in force.
   
9. **Myth:** I can sue the AI platform if the information is slightly inaccurate.
   **Fact:** By using the service, you agree to this disclaimer which explicitly waives the right to hold the platform liable for informational inaccuracies.
   
10. **Myth:** Only rich people can afford lawyers, so AI is my only hope.
    **Fact:** While AI is a great free resource for knowledge, free legal aid is a statutory right in India through NALSA for marginalized groups, providing actual human lawyers at no cost.
