# Fundamental and Legal Rights of Citizens in India

## Overview

**English**: The Constitution of India and various statutory laws guarantee a comprehensive framework of rights to its citizens. These rights ensure dignity, equality, freedom, and justice for every individual. With the introduction of the new criminal laws—Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), and Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA)—the legal framework has been modernized to provide speedier justice, enhanced victim protections, and a stronger emphasis on human rights during legal procedures. Understanding these rights empowers citizens to protect themselves against exploitation, state overreach, and private offenses.

**Hindi (Unicode)**: भारत का संविधान और विभिन्न वैधानिक कानून अपने नागरिकों को अधिकारों का एक व्यापक ढांचा प्रदान करते हैं। ये अधिकार प्रत्येक व्यक्ति के लिए सम्मान, समानता, स्वतंत्रता और न्याय सुनिश्चित करते हैं। नए आपराधिक कानूनों—भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS), भारतीय नागरिक सुरक्षा संहिता (BNSS), और भारतीय साक्ष्य अधिनियम (BSA)—के लागू होने से कानूनी ढांचे को आधुनिक बनाया गया है ताकि त्वरित न्याय, पीड़ितों को बेहतर सुरक्षा और कानूनी प्रक्रियाओं के दौरान मानवाधिकारों पर अधिक जोर दिया जा सके। इन अधिकारों को समझना नागरिकों को शोषण, राज्य के अतिक्रमण और निजी अपराधों से खुद को बचाने में सशक्त बनाता है।

## Legal Background

### History and Purpose
The framework of Fundamental Rights was inspired by the US Bill of Rights and the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. It was drafted to undo historic social inequalities and protect the diverse population of India. The recent overhaul of criminal laws (BNS, BNSS, BSA) aims to shift the focus from "punishment" (colonial era) to "justice" and citizen protection.

### Relevant Constitutional Articles
- **Article 14**: Equality before the law.
- **Article 15**: Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.
- **Article 19**: Protection of certain rights regarding freedom of speech, expression, assembly, association, movement, and profession.
- **Article 21**: Protection of life and personal liberty (Includes right to privacy, speedy trial, free legal aid).
- **Article 22**: Protection against arrest and detention in certain cases.
- **Article 32**: Right to Constitutional Remedies (Filing writ petitions in the Supreme Court).

### Relevant Sections (BNS, BNSS, BSA 2023)
- **BNSS Section 35**: Arrest without warrant (Strict conditions applied to police).
- **BNSS Section 50**: Person arrested to be informed of grounds of arrest and right to bail.
- **BNSS Section 54**: Examination of arrested person by medical officer.
- **BNSS Section 173 (Zero FIR)**: Right to file an FIR at any police station regardless of jurisdiction.
- **BSA Section 61**: Admissibility of electronic records, protecting citizens from tampered digital evidence.

## Applicability

- **Who is covered**: All citizens of India enjoy Fundamental Rights. Certain rights (like Article 14 and 21) are available to non-citizens (foreigners) as well.
- **Who is exempt**: Enemy aliens do not enjoy protection against arrest and detention under Article 22. Certain rights are restricted for members of Armed Forces and Police under Article 33 to maintain discipline.
- **Special Conditions**: During a National Emergency (Article 352), certain Fundamental Rights (like Article 19) can be suspended. Article 20 and 21 cannot be suspended under any circumstances.

## Duties & Responsibilities

**English**: Rights come with responsibilities. Article 51A outlines Fundamental Duties, including abiding by the Constitution, promoting harmony, protecting public property, developing a scientific temper, and safeguarding the environment. Citizens must also comply with legal procedures, report crimes (under BNSS), and provide truthful evidence (under BSA).

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure (Seeking Remedy for Rights Violation)

**English**:
1. **Filing a Complaint (FIR)**: If a cognizable offense occurs, file an FIR under BNSS Section 173. You can file a Zero FIR at any station.
2. **e-FIR**: For certain offenses, BNSS allows electronic registration of FIRs.
3. **Legal Aid**: If you cannot afford a lawyer, apply for free legal aid through NALSA.
4. **Writ Petition**: If a Fundamental Right is violated by the state, directly approach the High Court (Article 226) or Supreme Court (Article 32).
5. **Human Rights Commission**: For severe police atrocities or state abuses, file a complaint online with the NHRC or State Human Rights Commission.

2. **e-FIR**: कुछ अपराधों के लिए, BNSS इलेक्ट्रॉनिक FIR की अनुमति देता है।

## Required Documents
- Identity Proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport) for filing official complaints.
- Address Proof.
- Digital Evidence (WhatsApp chats, emails) authenticated under Section 61 of the BSA.
- Medical certificates (in case of physical injury or assault, mandated by BNSS for medico-legal cases).
- Copies of previous complaints or legal notices.

## Government Authorities
- **Local Police Station**: First point of contact for criminal complaints.
- **Magistrate Courts**: To enforce rights against illegal detention.
- **High Courts & Supreme Court**: Protectors of Fundamental Rights.
- **National Legal Services Authority (NALSA)**: For free legal representation.
- **National Human Rights Commission (NHRC)**: Investigates gross rights violations.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Violation of SC/ST Rights**: Punishable under the SC/ST (Prevention of Atrocities) Act with strict non-bailable provisions.
- **Illegal Detention by Police**: Officers can be prosecuted under BNS for wrongful confinement, and departmental action is initiated.
- **Denial of FIR**: Police officers refusing to register FIRs for cognizable offenses can face imprisonment up to 2 years under specific provisions of the BNS.

## Exceptions
- **Reasonable Restrictions**: Freedom of Speech (Article 19) is subject to restrictions for sovereignty, security of the state, friendly relations with foreign states, public order, and defamation.
- **Preventive Detention**: Under Article 22, the state can detain individuals without trial for up to 3 months (can be extended by advisory board) if they are a threat to national security.

## Important Timelines
- **Production before Magistrate**: Within 24 hours of arrest (excluding travel time).
- **Filing Charge Sheet**: Must be filed within 60 or 90 days depending on the severity of the crime; otherwise, the accused gets default bail.
- **Medical Examination**: Must be conducted immediately upon arrest under BNSS guidelines.
- **RTI Appeals**: First appeal within 30 days of the decision from the Public Information Officer.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Refusing to sign an FIR after reading it (It is a legal duty).
- Assuming the police can arrest for non-cognizable offenses without a warrant.
- Not informing a family member or lawyer immediately upon arrest.
- Giving a forced confession to the police (Confessions to police are generally not admissible in court under BSA, unless it leads to discovery of facts).
- Failing to collect a free copy of the FIR immediately after registration.

## Safety Tips

**English**: Always insist on a memo of arrest signed by a witness. Do not hand over your original documents unless court-ordered. Know the difference between cognizable (serious, no warrant needed) and non-cognizable (warrant needed) offenses.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **Can the police arrest me without a warrant?**
   **English**: Yes, but only for cognizable offenses like murder, robbery, or severe assault. For non-cognizable offenses, they must have a magistrate's warrant. Under BNSS, the police must justify the necessity of arrest for offenses carrying punishment under 7 years.
   **Hindi**: हाँ, लेकिन केवल संज्ञेय अपराधों जैसे हत्या या लूट के लिए। असंज्ञेय अपराधों के लिए उन्हें वारंट चाहिए।
   **Hinglish**: Haan, par sirf serious (cognizable) crimes mein. Chote crimes ke liye magistrate ka warrant chahiye. BNSS ke mutabiq 7 saal se kam saza wale cases mein arrest zaruri hone par hi hogi.

2. **Do I have the right to remain silent?**
   **English**: Yes, Article 20(3) of the Constitution protects you against self-incrimination. You cannot be forced to be a witness against yourself or confess to a crime. However, you must provide your basic identity details.
   **Hindi**: हाँ, संविधान का अनुच्छेद 20(3) आपको खुद के खिलाफ गवाही देने से बचाता है।
   **Hinglish**: Haan, Article 20(3) aapko self-incrimination se bachata hai. Koi aapko apna gunah kubool karne ke liye force nahi kar sakta, par basic identity batani hoti hai.

3. **What is a Zero FIR?**
   **English**: A Zero FIR can be filed at any police station regardless of where the crime occurred. It is then transferred to the police station with the correct jurisdiction. This ensures immediate action, especially in heinous crimes against women.
   **Hindi**: जीरो FIR किसी भी पुलिस स्टेशन में दर्ज की जा सकती है, भले ही अपराध कहीं भी हुआ हो।
   **Hinglish**: Zero FIR aap kisi bhi police station mein file kar sakte hain, chahe crime kahin bhi hua ho. Baad mein police use sahi jurisdiction wale station transfer kar deti hai.

4. **Can women be arrested at night?**
   **English**: Generally, no. Under BNSS Section 43, no woman can be arrested after sunset and before sunrise. In exceptional circumstances, a female police officer must obtain prior permission from a Judicial Magistrate.
   **Hindi**: सामान्यतः नहीं। सूर्यास्त के बाद और सूर्योदय से पहले किसी महिला को गिरफ्तार नहीं किया जा सकता।
   **Hinglish**: Normally nahi. Sunset ke baad aur sunrise se pehle aurat ko arrest nahi kar sakte. Emergency mein lady police ko Magistrate se permission leni padti hai.

5. **Is it mandatory to get a copy of the FIR?**
   **English**: Yes, BNSS mandates that a copy of the FIR must be provided to the informant immediately and free of cost. Refusal to provide this is a violation of your legal rights.
   **Hindi**: हाँ, FIR की एक प्रति शिकायतकर्ता को तुरंत और मुफ्त दी जानी चाहिए।
   **Hinglish**: Haan, FIR ki copy complainant ko free aur turant milni chahiye. Yeh aapka right hai.

6. **What if the police refuse to file my FIR?**
   **English**: If the police refuse, you can send the complaint in writing by post to the Superintendent of Police (SP). If still unaddressed, you can approach a Magistrate under BNSS Section 175(3) to order an investigation.
   **Hindi**: आप SP को लिखित शिकायत भेज सकते हैं या मजिस्ट्रेट के पास जा सकते हैं।
   **Hinglish**: Agar police FIR na likhe, toh SP ko speed post se complaint bhejo. Phir bhi na ho, toh Magistrate ke paas BNSS 175(3) mein appeal karo.

7. **Do I need to hire an expensive lawyer?**
   **English**: No. Article 39A guarantees equal justice and free legal aid. If you belong to weaker sections, women, children, or have low income, NALSA will provide you a competent lawyer free of cost.
   **Hindi**: नहीं। NALSA के माध्यम से मुफ्त कानूनी सहायता उपलब्ध है।
   **Hinglish**: Nahi. Agar aap afford nahi kar sakte, toh NALSA aapko free lawyer provide karega, yeh aapka Constitutional right hai.

8. **Can I be punished twice for the same crime?**
   **English**: No. Article 20(2) protects you from "Double Jeopardy." No person can be prosecuted and punished for the same offense more than once by the courts.
   **Hindi**: नहीं। अनुच्छेद 20(2) आपको एक ही अपराध के लिए दोहरे दंड से बचाता है।
   **Hinglish**: Nahi. Article 20(2) "Double Jeopardy" se bachata hai. Ek crime ke liye do baar court saza nahi de sakti.

9. **What are my rights regarding handcuffs?**
   **English**: Handcuffing is not a routine procedure. According to Supreme Court rulings and BNSS guidelines, handcuffs should only be used if the accused is a habitual offender, has a history of violence, or is likely to escape.
   **Hindi**: हथकड़ी लगाना नियमित प्रक्रिया नहीं है, यह केवल भागने या हिंसा के जोखिम पर उपयोग की जाती है।
   **Hinglish**: Har case mein handcuff (hathkadi) lagana allowed nahi hai. Sirf agar accused bhagne ka risk ho ya violent ho tabhi lagayi ja sakti hai.

10. **Can I record a police officer demanding a bribe?**
    **English**: Yes. You have the right to record public servants performing official duties in public spaces. This acts as electronic evidence under the BSA. However, do not physically obstruct their lawful duty.
    **Hindi**: हाँ, आप सार्वजनिक स्थान पर रिश्वत मांगने वाले अधिकारी को रिकॉर्ड कर सकते हैं।
    **Hinglish**: Haan, public duty par agar police bribe maange toh aap record kar sakte hain. BSA ke under yeh electronic evidence mana jata hai.

11. **How long can the police keep me in custody?**
    **English**: Police cannot detain anyone beyond 24 hours without presenting them before a Magistrate. The Magistrate will then decide whether to grant bail, order police custody (remand), or judicial custody.
    **Hindi**: पुलिस मजिस्ट्रेट के सामने पेश किए बिना 24 घंटे से अधिक हिरासत में नहीं रख सकती।
    **Hinglish**: Police aapko 24 ghante se zyada lockup mein bina Magistrate ke samne pesh kiye nahi rakh sakti. Yeh sakht niyam hai.

12. **Can a traffic cop take away my car keys?**
    **English**: No. Traffic police do not have the legal right to snatch your car or bike keys. Their duty is to ask for your license and issue a challan if you violate traffic rules.
    **Hindi**: नहीं, ट्रैफिक पुलिस को आपकी चाबियां छीनने का कोई कानूनी अधिकार नहीं है।
    **Hinglish**: Nahi, traffic police ko aapki gadi ya bike ki chabi nikalne ka right nahi hai. Wo sirf challan kaat sakte hain.

13. **Is a confession made to the police valid in court?**
    **English**: Under the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), a confession made directly to a police officer while in custody is generally not admissible in court unless it leads to the discovery of a new, material fact or evidence.
    **Hindi**: पुलिस को दिया गया कबूलनामा आमतौर पर अदालत में स्वीकार्य नहीं होता है।
    **Hinglish**: Police ke samne kiya gaya confession court mein generally valid nahi hota, jab tak usse koi naya saboot na mile.

14. **What should I do if police beat me in custody?**
    **English**: You have the right to medical examination every 48 hours in custody. If assaulted, inform the Magistrate immediately when produced. You can also file a complaint with the NHRC or State Police Complaints Authority.
    **Hindi**: हिरासत में आप मेडिकल जांच की मांग कर सकते हैं। मारपीट होने पर तुरंत मजिस्ट्रेट को बताएं।
    **Hinglish**: Agar police maare, toh Magistrate ke samne peshi ke waqt turant batao. Custody mein har 48 hours mein medical checkup aapka haq hai.

15. **Can private companies violate my fundamental rights?**
    **English**: Fundamental Rights are primarily enforced against the "State" (government). However, rights against untouchability (Art 17) and human trafficking (Art 23) apply against private individuals too. For other private disputes, you must use civil or criminal laws (like BNS).
    **Hindi**: मौलिक अधिकार मुख्य रूप से राज्य के खिलाफ लागू होते हैं, निजी व्यक्तियों के खिलाफ कुछ अपवादों को छोड़कर।
    **Hinglish**: Fundamental Rights mostly Government ke khilaf hote hain. Private company ke dispute ke liye aap civil court ya BNS ke law use karenge.

16. **Are my phone and chats protected by privacy laws?**
    **English**: Yes. The Right to Privacy is recognized as a Fundamental Right under Article 21. Your phone cannot be arbitrarily tapped or seized without a proper legal warrant or procedure authorized by law.
    **Hindi**: निजता का अधिकार अनुच्छेद 21 के तहत एक मौलिक अधिकार है।
    **Hinglish**: Haan, Right to Privacy Article 21 mein hai. Bina proper warrant ya legal procedure ke aapka phone tap ya seize nahi kiya ja sakta.

17. **What is a Habeas Corpus petition?**
    **English**: It is a writ filed in the High Court or Supreme Court to demand the physical production of a person who has been illegally detained or kidnapped. The court will order the police to produce the person.
    **Hindi**: यह अवैध रूप से हिरासत में लिए गए व्यक्ति को पेश करने के लिए अदालत में दायर एक रिट है।
    **Hinglish**: Habeas Corpus ek writ hai. Agar koi illegally gayab ya detain kar liya jaye, toh court police ko order deti hai ki us insaan ko turant court mein pesh karein.

18. **Can my employer force me to work 15 hours a day?**
    **English**: No. This violates labor laws and your Right against Exploitation. Standard working hours are regulated (usually 8-9 hours), and overtime must be compensated. Forced labor without consent is unconstitutional.
    **Hindi**: नहीं। यह श्रम कानूनों और शोषण के खिलाफ आपके अधिकार का उल्लंघन करता है।
    **Hinglish**: Nahi. Yeh labor laws aur aapke exploitation ke khilaf right ka violation hai. Jabardasti overwork karwana illegal hai.

19. **What happens if I cannot afford to pay for bail?**
    **English**: If an accused is indigent and unable to furnish bail within a week of the bail order, BNSS allows the court to release them on a personal bond without sureties. Also, undertrials who have served up to half of the maximum sentence for the alleged crime must be released on personal bond.
    **Hindi**: यदि आप जमानत नहीं दे सकते हैं, तो अदालत आपको व्यक्तिगत बांड पर रिहा कर सकती है।
    **Hinglish**: Agar bail ke paise nahi hain aur 1 hafta guzar jaye, toh court personal bond par chhod sakti hai. Aadhe se zyada saza lockup mein kat chuki ho toh bhi release milni chahiye.

20. **Can I challenge an unjust law passed by the government?**
    **English**: Yes. Under the power of Judicial Review, citizens can challenge the constitutional validity of any law or government order in the High Courts (Article 226) or Supreme Court (Article 32) if it violates Fundamental Rights.
    **Hindi**: हाँ। आप न्यायिक समीक्षा की शक्ति के तहत उच्च न्यायालय या सर्वोच्च न्यायालय में किसी भी अनुचित कानून को चुनौती दे सकते हैं।
    **Hinglish**: Haan. Agar koi law aapke rights chheenta hai, toh aap High Court ya Supreme Court mein use challenge kar sakte hain. Isko Judicial Review kehte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth**: Police can arrest anyone at any time based on mere suspicion.
   **Fact**: For minor offenses, police need a warrant. For cognizable offenses, they must have credible information or reasonable suspicion, not just baseless doubts, and must follow BNSS procedures.
2. **Myth**: FIR can only be filed where the crime happened.
   **Fact**: A Zero FIR can be filed anywhere across India.
3. **Myth**: You have no rights once you are inside a police lockup.
   **Fact**: You retain your right to life, legal representation, medical examination, and protection against torture.
4. **Myth**: Confessions beaten out of an accused in police custody guarantee a conviction.
   **Fact**: Confessions made to police are strictly inadmissible as proof of guilt under the BSA.
5. **Myth**: Fundamental Rights cannot be restricted by the government under any circumstances.
   **Fact**: Article 19 freedoms come with "reasonable restrictions" such as public order, decency, and state security.
6. **Myth**: Traffic police can confiscate your driving license on the spot for any offense.
   **Fact**: They can seize it only for specific severe offenses (like drunk driving or jumping red lights) and must give an acknowledgment receipt.
7. **Myth**: Legal aid is only for extreme poverty cases.
   **Fact**: Free legal aid under NALSA is available to women, children, SC/ST, industrial workmen, and persons with an annual income below a specific state threshold.
8. **Myth**: A police officer can refuse to register an FIR saying it's not their jurisdiction.
   **Fact**: Refusing a cognizable FIR is a punishable offense; they must register a Zero FIR.
9. **Myth**: If you don't answer police questions, you will go to jail.
   **Fact**: You have the Right to Silence against self-incrimination. You don't have to admit guilt.
10. **Myth**: The government can take your private land without any compensation.
    **Fact**: Under Article 300A, a person cannot be deprived of property save by authority of law, and fair compensation is required.

## Conversation Examples

**Dialogue 1: Zero FIR**
- **Citizen (Hinglish)**: Sir, mera phone aur bag dusre shahar mein chori ho gaya. Kya main yahan FIR kar sakta hu?
- **Assistant (Hinglish)**: Haan bilkul. Aap yahan 'Zero FIR' darj karwa sakte hain. Police ye nahi keh sakti ki crime unke area mein nahi hua. Baad mein wo khud is FIR ko sahi station transfer kar denge.

**Dialogue 2: Police Refusing FIR**
- **Citizen (English)**: The local police are refusing to file my FIR for an assault. What are my options?
- **Assistant (English)**: Under BNSS Section 175, you should write your complaint and send it via registered post to the Superintendent of Police (SP). If no action is taken, you can directly petition the local Magistrate to direct the police to register the FIR.

**Dialogue 3: Right against Handcuffing**
- **Citizen (Hindi)**: क्या पुलिस बिना किसी कारण के मुझे हथकड़ी लगा सकती है?
- **Assistant (Hindi)**: नहीं। सुप्रीम कोर्ट के निर्देशों के अनुसार, हथकड़ी लगाना एक अपवाद है, नियम नहीं। यदि आप भागने की कोशिश नहीं कर रहे हैं या हिंसक नहीं हैं, तो पुलिस आमतौर पर आपको हथकड़ी नहीं लगा सकती।

**Dialogue 4: Right to Lawyer**
- **Citizen (Hinglish)**: Agar mujhe arrest kar liya jaye toh kya main apne lawyer se baat kar sakta hu?
- **Assistant (Hinglish)**: Haan. BNSS Section 39 ke mutabiq aapko arrest ke waqt apne lawyer ya family member ko inform karne ka right hai. Police ko aapko is right ke baare mein batana compulsory hai.

**Dialogue 5: Arrest of Women**
- **Citizen (English)**: Can male police officers arrest a female suspect at midnight?
- **Assistant (English)**: Absolutely not. According to BNSS Section 43, a woman cannot be arrested after sunset and before sunrise. Also, the arrest must be made by a female police officer. Exceptions strictly require a Magistrate's prior written permission.

**Dialogue 6: Traffic Police Harassment**
- **Citizen (Hinglish)**: Traffic police ne meri gadi ki chabi nikal li kyunki maine helmet nahi pehna tha. Kya ye sahi hai?
- **Assistant (Hinglish)**: Nahi, ye illegal hai. Motor Vehicles Act ke andar traffic police ko chabi nikalne ka koi adhikar nahi hai. Wo sirf challan kaat sakte hain. Aap unki is action ki complaint senior officers se kar sakte hain.

**Dialogue 7: Self-Incrimination**
- **Citizen (English)**: If the police interrogate me, do I have to confess to everything they accuse me of?
- **Assistant (English)**: No. Article 20(3) of the Constitution grants you the Right to Silence against self-incrimination. You are not bound to give answers that expose you to a criminal charge. 

**Dialogue 8: Free Legal Aid**
- **Citizen (Hindi)**: मेरे पास वकील की फीस देने के पैसे नहीं हैं। क्या मुझे न्याय नहीं मिलेगा?
- **Assistant (Hindi)**: आपको न्याय जरूर मिलेगा। NALSA (राष्ट्रीय विधिक सेवा प्राधिकरण) के तहत महिलाओं, बच्चों, SC/ST, और कम आय वाले व्यक्तियों को मुफ्त वकील (Legal Aid) दिया जाता है। आप स्थानीय कोर्ट में NALSA डेस्क पर जा सकते हैं।

**Dialogue 9: Max Time in Police Custody**
- **Citizen (Hinglish)**: Police kisi ko lockup mein kitne din tak rakh sakti hai?
- **Assistant (Hinglish)**: Arrest hone ke 24 ghante ke andar police ko accused ko Magistrate ke samne pesh karna hi padta hai. 24 ghante se zyada lockup mein rakhna illegal hai bina court ke remand order ke.

**Dialogue 10: Police Bribe Recording**
- **Citizen (English)**: A government official is asking for a bribe. Can I record him on my phone? Will it be valid in court?
- **Assistant (English)**: Yes, you can record a public official taking a bribe in the course of public duty. Under the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), electronic evidence like audio and video recordings are fully admissible in court.

**Dialogue 11: Writ of Habeas Corpus**
- **Citizen (Hinglish)**: Mere bhai ko police kal utha le gayi aur ab bata nahi rahi wo kahan hai. Main kya karu?
- **Assistant (Hinglish)**: Yeh illegal detention lagta hai. Aap turant lawyer ke zariye High Court ya Supreme Court mein 'Habeas Corpus' writ file karein. Court police ko order karegi ki aapke bhai ko 24 ghante mein judge ke samne pesh kiya jaye.

**Dialogue 12: Fundamental Duties**
- **Citizen (Hindi)**: क्या नागरिकों के सिर्फ अधिकार होते हैं या कोई कर्तव्य भी हैं?
- **Assistant (Hindi)**: संविधान के अनुच्छेद 51A में नागरिकों के 11 मौलिक कर्तव्य (Fundamental Duties) बताए गए हैं, जैसे संविधान का पालन करना, सार्वजनिक संपत्ति की रक्षा करना, और देश की एकता बनाए रखना।

**Dialogue 13: Workplace Exploitation**
- **Citizen (English)**: My boss makes me work 16 hours a day and holds my original degree certificates. Is this legal?
- **Assistant (English)**: No, this is entirely illegal and violates your Article 23 right against forced labor and exploitation. You can file a complaint with the Labor Commissioner and the police for wrongful retention of your property.

**Dialogue 14: Confession under Pressure**
- **Citizen (Hinglish)**: Police lockup mein dande maar ke confession likhwa le toh kya main permanently fas jaunga?
- **Assistant (Hinglish)**: Nahi. Police custody mein dabaw mein diya gaya confession court mein BSA ke under valid proof nahi mana jata. Jab aap Magistrate ke samne pesh hon, toh unhe maarpit ki baat turant batayein.

**Dialogue 15: Cyber Crime and Privacy**
- **Citizen (English)**: Someone hacked my email and is spreading fake photos. Is my right violated?
- **Assistant (English)**: Yes, your Right to Privacy (Article 21) is violated. You should immediately call 1930 or log on to cybercrime.gov.in to file an official complaint under the BNS and IT Act provisions.
