## Legal Background
The foundation of this knowledge base relies on officially enacted Indian laws, recently overhauled to reflect modern needs.
*   **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023:** Replaces the Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860. It defines criminal offenses and punishments.
*   **Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023:** Replaces the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), 1973. It outlines the procedural aspects of arrests, investigations, and trials.
*   **Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023:** Replaces the Indian Evidence Act, 1872. It governs the admissibility of evidence in courts, including digital evidence.
*   **The Constitution of India:** The supreme law of the land, providing fundamental rights under Part III (Articles 12-35) which are frequently referenced across this knowledge base.
*   **Purpose:** To present these complex statutes in a simplified, structured, and legally accurate format for AI retrieval and human understanding.

## Applicability
*   **Who is covered:** All citizens of India, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), and in certain cases, foreign nationals residing in or visiting India who need to understand Indian laws.
*   **Who is exempt:** The knowledge base is not applicable for laws of other jurisdictions outside the territory of India.
*   **Special conditions:** The knowledge base provides information, not attorney-client privilege or direct legal representation. Users must consult a registered advocate for binding legal advice.

## Required Documents
When using the knowledge base to prepare for a legal process, always keep the following generic documents ready:
1. Valid Government ID (Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, Passport).
2. Proof of Address (Utility bills, Rent agreement).
3. All relevant evidence (Photographs, chat screenshots, emails, audio/video recordings).
4. Previous correspondence with authorities (if any).
5. Medical certificates (in case of physical injury or assault).

## Government Authorities
Depending on the topic, the knowledge base will direct you to:
*   **Police Stations:** For filing FIRs/NCRs under BNSS.
*   **Courts:** District Courts, High Courts, Supreme Court for judicial remedies.
*   **Lok Adalats:** For amicable settlement of disputes.
*   **Specialized Tribunals:** NCLT for corporate issues, Consumer Forums for consumer disputes, RERA for real estate.
*   **Cyber Crime Cells:** Specialized units for digital offenses.

## Penalties & Punishments
The knowledge base lists specific penalties as defined by the latest laws (BNS, 2023). It does not invent or estimate punishments. Users must look at the 'Penalties & Punishments' section of individual topic files to understand the legal consequences of actions, which include imprisonment terms, fines, or community service as mandated by law.

## Exceptions
*   The knowledge base does not cover internal military laws (Court Martial).
*   It does not cover highly specific corporate tax planning strategies.
*   Information provided may be subject to recent High Court or Supreme Court rulings not yet updated in the base. Always check the 'last_updated' field in the document frontmatter.

## Important Timelines
*   **FIR Filing:** Should be done as soon as possible after the incident to avoid 'delay in FIR' defense.
*   **Bail Applications:** Can be filed immediately upon anticipation of arrest (Anticipatory) or after arrest (Regular).
*   **Appeals:** Usually 30 to 90 days depending on the court and the specific act, governed by the Limitation Act, 1963.
*   **RTI Replies:** 30 days from the date of receipt of the application by the Public Information Officer.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. **Relying on outdated laws:** Using IPC/CrPC sections instead of the new BNS/BNSS sections for offenses committed after July 1, 2024.
2. **Delaying action:** Waiting too long to report a crime or file a civil suit, which can lead to the case being barred by limitation.
3. **Destroying evidence:** Deleting chats or formatting devices before preserving digital evidence properly under the BSA, 2023.
4. **Signing unread documents:** Agreeing to terms or police statements without reading or understanding them.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Reading this knowledge base makes me a lawyer.
   **Fact:** No, it makes you a legally aware citizen. Only a law degree and Bar Council enrollment make you a lawyer.
2. **Myth:** The AI's advice is legally binding in court.
   **Fact:** The AI provides information, not binding legal advice. Courts rely on physical documents, arguments by advocates, and official statutes.
3. **Myth:** Old laws like IPC are completely useless now.
   **Fact:** IPC still applies to offenses committed before July 1, 2024. The new laws (BNS) apply prospectively.
4. **Myth:** The knowledge base only has English content.
   **Fact:** The knowledge base is heavily multilingual, incorporating Hindi Unicode and Hinglish to cater to a wider Indian demographic.
5. **Myth:** All legal procedures shown can be completed online.
   **Fact:** While digital portals exist, many critical legal procedures still require physical presence at a police station or court.
6. **Myth:** The AI can file an FIR for me.
   **Fact:** The AI can guide you on *how* to file an FIR, but you must physically approach the police or use official state police portals to file it.
7. **Myth:** If a section isn't mentioned here, it doesn't exist.
   **Fact:** This is a curated knowledge base covering common and significant laws; it may not contain every obscure legal sub-section.
8. **Myth:** Legal language is too difficult for common people.
   **Fact:** This knowledge base is explicitly designed to break down complex legal jargon into simple, actionable language.
9. **Myth:** You have to pay to access this legal knowledge.
   **Fact:** The foundational legal information provided by this system is intended for free public empowerment.
10. **Myth:** The procedures described guarantee a win in court.
    **Fact:** The procedures are administrative and legal steps; judicial outcomes depend on evidence, facts, and judicial discretion.
