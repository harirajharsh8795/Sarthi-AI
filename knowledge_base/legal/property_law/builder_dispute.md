# Builder-Buyer Disputes and Legal Remedies

## Overview
**English:** A builder-buyer dispute typically arises when a real estate developer fails to deliver possession of a property on time, demands hidden charges, provides substandard construction quality, or alters the building plan without the buyer's consent. The introduction of the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA) has drastically transformed the landscape of real estate by introducing strict compliance for builders and an expedited grievance redressal mechanism for homebuyers. Buyers can approach RERA authorities, Consumer Courts, or criminal courts (under the new BNS laws) in cases of severe fraud.

## Quick Summary
**English:** Homebuyers possess powerful legal tools against defaulting builders under RERA and the Consumer Protection Act. If a builder delays possession, a buyer can either claim a refund with interest or seek delayed possession compensation. Filing a complaint via the respective state RERA portal is the most effective administrative remedy. In case of criminal breach of trust or organized cheating, an FIR can be lodged under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS).

## Definition
**English:** A Builder-Buyer Dispute constitutes a legal conflict arising from a breach of the 'Agreement for Sale' or statutory obligations by a real estate promoter/developer, predominantly involving delayed delivery, quality deficiencies, or financial misappropriation.

## Legal Background
The legal framework governing builder disputes in India is primarily robust due to specialized legislation:
- **Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA):** 
  - *Section 18:* Empowers the allottee (buyer) to demand a refund with interest if the builder fails to complete or give possession of the apartment in accordance with the terms of the agreement.
  - *Section 31:* Allows aggrieved persons to file a complaint with the Authority or the adjudicating officer for violations.
  - *Section 12:* Obligates the promoter to compensate buyers if they made an advance/deposit based on false advertisements.
  - *Section 14(3):* Fixes the builder's liability for structural defects for 5 years from possession.
- **Consumer Protection Act, 2019:** Can be invoked for 'deficiency in service'. Buyers can approach District, State, or National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions depending on the pecuniary jurisdiction.
- **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023:** 
  - *Section 316 (Criminal Breach of Trust):* Applicable if the builder diverts funds collected for a specific project to another project.
  - *Section 318 (Cheating):* If the builder never intended to build the property and solely aimed to dupe buyers.
- **Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016:** Homebuyers are considered financial creditors and can initiate corporate insolvency resolution processes against defaulting developers.

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** Any individual or entity that has been allotted a plot, apartment, or building by a promoter. Real estate agents are also accountable under RERA.
- **Who is exempt:** Properties not registered under RERA (though still covered under Consumer Protection), purely commercial disputes between two developers, or secondary market resale purchases (unless the developer is directly involved in transferring the original title).
- **Special Conditions:** RERA specifically applies to projects where the land area exceeds 500 square meters or the number of apartments exceeds eight.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Send Legal Notice:** Draft and send a formal legal notice to the builder demanding possession or refund.
2. **File RERA Complaint:** If the builder ignores the notice, visit your state's RERA website and file a complaint under Section 31, submitting all proofs (agreement, receipts).
3. **Mediation/Conciliation:** Many state RERA bodies initially refer the matter to a conciliation forum to reach a settlement.
4. **Adjudication:** If mediation fails, the RERA Authority conducts hearings and passes an order for possession or refund with interest.
5. **Execution:** If the builder defies the RERA order, the buyer can file an execution petition to recover the due amount as arrears of land revenue.
6. **NCLT (Alternative):** If the builder is completely bankrupt, homebuyers (collectively meeting the threshold) can file a plea in the NCLT under IBC.

## Government Authorities
- **State RERA Authorities:** Every state has a dedicated Real Estate Regulatory Authority (e.g., MahaRERA, UP RERA, HRERA).
- **RERA Appellate Tribunal:** For filing appeals against RERA Authority orders.
- **Consumer Courts (DCDRC, SCDRC, NCDRC):** For claiming deficiency in service and asking for compensation.
- **National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT):** For insolvency proceedings against completely defunct builders.
- **Local Police / EOW (Economic Offences Wing):** For lodging criminal complaints against massive real estate frauds.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Failure to Register Project:** Penalty up to 10% of the estimated cost of the real estate project (Section 59, RERA).
- **Non-compliance with RERA Orders:** Imprisonment up to 3 years or fine up to 10% of the estimated cost of the project, or both, for promoters (Section 64, RERA).
- **Criminal Breach of Trust (Section 316 BNS):** Imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to five years, or with fine, or with both.
- **Cheating (Section 318 BNS):** Imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.

## Exceptions
- Force Majeure: Builders can claim an extension of time for possession in case of natural disasters, wars, or other unforeseen calamities (e.g., the COVID-19 pandemic led to blanket extensions by RERA authorities).
- Default by Buyer: If the buyer consistently fails to make scheduled payments, the builder has the right to terminate the agreement and forfeit the earnest money as per the contractual terms.

## Important Timelines
- **Defect Liability Period:** 5 years from the date of handing over possession.
- **RERA Complaint Disposal:** The Authority is mandated to dispose of complaints within 60 days from the date of filing.
- **Appeal to RERA Tribunal:** Must be filed within 60 days of the RERA Authority's order.
- **Consumer Court Limitation:** Complaint must be filed within 2 years from the date the cause of action arose.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. **Stopping EMI Payments:** Stopping bank EMIs abruptly because the builder delayed possession will harm your CIBIL score and make you a defaulter to the bank.
2. **Signing Arbitration Clauses Blindly:** Many builders bury one-sided arbitration clauses in the agreement. However, the Supreme Court has ruled that homebuyers can still approach RERA/Consumer forums despite arbitration clauses.
3. **Accepting Possession Without OC:** Taking physical possession without an Occupancy Certificate (OC) is illegal and strips you of many rights.
4. **Delaying Legal Action:** Waiting for years on the builder's false verbal promises instead of sending a legal notice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can I file a RERA complaint if the project is not registered with RERA?**
   Yes, you can file a complaint with RERA even for unregistered projects if they were supposed to be registered under the law. RERA authorities can penalize the builder for non-registration and still hear your grievance.

2. **Is it better to go to a Consumer Court or RERA for delayed possession?**
   RERA is specifically designed for real estate disputes and usually resolves cases faster than consumer courts. However, if you are seeking very high punitive compensation for mental agony, the NCDRC (Consumer Court) is often preferred.

3. **What is the current interest rate RERA awards on refunds?**
   The interest rate is usually the State Bank of India's Marginal Cost of Lending Rate (MCLR) plus 2%. This varies slightly from state to state according to state RERA rules.

4. **Can I stop paying my home loan EMI if the builder delays the project?**
   No, your home loan agreement with the bank is separate from your agreement with the builder. Stopping EMIs will ruin your credit score and the bank can initiate recovery proceedings against you.

5. **What is an Occupancy Certificate (OC) and why is it important?**
   An OC is a document issued by the local municipal authority stating that the building is constructed as per approved plans and is safe to live in. Taking possession without an OC is illegal and can lead to eviction by civic bodies.

6. **The builder is forcing me to pay hidden charges before giving keys. What should I do?**
   Do not pay any charges that are not explicitly mentioned in the Agreement for Sale. Demand a written invoice and file a complaint with RERA for unfair trade practices if they withhold possession.

7. **My builder promised a swimming pool but did not build one. Is this a violation?**
   Yes. Under Section 14 of RERA, the builder cannot make any alterations to the sanctioned plans, layout plans, and specifications without the written consent of two-thirds of the allottees.

8. **Can the builder cancel my allotment if I delay one payment?**
   A builder can cancel the allotment for non-payment, but they must follow the due process defined in the RERA agreement, which usually requires them to send multiple demand notices before cancellation.

9. **Can homebuyers collectively file a case against a builder?**
   Yes, homebuyers can form an association and file a joint complaint in RERA or a class-action suit in the NCDRC or NCLT for collective relief.

10. **How much advance can a builder legally ask for before signing an agreement?**
    Under Section 13 of RERA, a promoter cannot accept a sum of more than 10% of the cost of the apartment as an advance payment without first entering into a written agreement for sale and registering it.

11. **What happens if the RERA order is in my favor but the builder doesn't pay?**
    You can file an execution application with the RERA authority. RERA can issue a recovery certificate to the District Magistrate, who can seize the builder's properties and recover your money as arrears of land revenue.

12. **Can I claim tax benefits on a home loan if possession is delayed?**
    You can claim tax deduction under Section 24 of the Income Tax Act for pre-construction interest in 5 equal installments starting from the year you get possession, but prolonged delays complicate claiming standard deductions.

13. **Is it mandatory to hire a lawyer for a RERA complaint?**
    No, you can file a RERA complaint online and represent yourself. However, engaging a lawyer is advisable for drafting the complaint effectively and handling cross-examinations.

14. **What is the Defect Liability Period under RERA?**
    It is 5 years from the date of possession. If any structural defect or defect in workmanship is brought to the notice of the promoter within this period, they must rectify it within 30 days without further charge.

15. **Can I go to police if the builder runs away with my money?**
    Yes, this constitutes a criminal offense. You can file an FIR under Section 316 (Criminal Breach of Trust) and Section 318 (Cheating) of the BNS.

16. **Will I get compensation for mental harassment by the builder?**
    Consumer courts are generally more liberal in awarding compensation for mental agony compared to RERA, which strictly focuses on interest-based refunds for delays.

17. **What is the difference between carpet area and super built-up area?**
    Carpet area is the actual usable floor area within the apartment walls. Super built-up area includes common areas like lobbies, lifts, and stairs. RERA mandates that properties must be sold strictly on carpet area.

18. **Can the builder change the size of my apartment during construction?**
    Minor variations might be allowed based on the agreement, but significant changes require the buyer's consent. If the carpet area is reduced, the builder must refund the excess amount paid.

19. **What is a tripartite agreement?**
    It is a legal agreement involving three parties—the homebuyer, the builder, and the bank financing the home loan. It ensures the bank's rights over the property if the buyer defaults on the loan.

20. **Can I transfer my property before getting possession?**
    Yes, you can sell your rights to another buyer (assignment), but the builder may charge transfer fees if explicitly agreed upon in the initial contract. RERA prevents arbitrary exorbitant transfer fees.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** A builder can indefinitely delay a project if they cite "lack of funds".
   **Fact:** Lack of funds is not a force majeure event. The builder is liable to pay delay penalties or refund the money with interest under RERA.

2. **Myth:** Once an arbitration clause is signed, I cannot go to RERA.
   **Fact:** The Supreme Court has clarified that statutory remedies under RERA or Consumer Protection Act are available to buyers despite an arbitration clause in the agreement.

3. **Myth:** Builders can ask for any amount of advance booking money.
   **Fact:** By law, builders cannot ask for more than 10% of the total property cost as advance without executing a registered agreement for sale.

4. **Myth:** RERA only applies to new projects launched after 2016.
   **Fact:** RERA also applies to ongoing projects that had not received a completion certificate prior to the implementation of the Act.

5. **Myth:** If I take a bank loan, the bank is responsible for checking if the project is legally clear.
   **Fact:** While banks do their due diligence, the ultimate legal responsibility and risk lie with the homebuyer. Always do your independent verification.

6. **Myth:** Taking possession for "fit-outs" is completely safe.
   **Fact:** Taking possession without a valid Occupancy Certificate (OC) is illegal and waives many of your legal rights regarding structural defects.

7. **Myth:** I can stop paying my home loan EMI if the builder halts construction.
   **Fact:** Stopping EMIs makes you a defaulter with your bank. The bank will initiate action against you, not the builder.

8. **Myth:** Consumer courts are the only place for builder disputes.
   **Fact:** RERA is a specialized, faster tribunal for real estate disputes, making it the primary forum for homebuyers today.

9. **Myth:** Developers can easily alter the project plan after selling flats.
   **Fact:** Any alteration to sanctioned plans requires written consent from two-thirds of the buyers in the project.

10. **Myth:** All real estate agents are trustworthy if they are hired by a big developer.
    **Fact:** Real estate agents must be individually registered with RERA. Always verify the agent's RERA registration number before dealing with them.

## Conversation Examples

**Example 1: Delayed Possession**
- **Citizen:** Builder ne 2 saal se flat ka possession delay kar diya hai. Mujhe kya karna chahiye?
- **Assistant:** Agar builder ne possession mein delay kiya hai, toh aap apne state ki RERA website par Section 31 ke under complaint file kar sakte hain. Aapke paas do options hain: ya toh aap interest ke saath refund maang sakte hain, ya phir jab tak possession nahi milta tab tak delay compensation (interest) claim kar sakte hain.

**Example 2: Hidden Charges**
- **Citizen:** The builder is asking for an extra 2 lakh rupees for 'water connection' right before giving keys. It was not in the agreement.
- **Assistant:** You are not legally bound to pay any hidden charges that are not explicitly stated in your Agreement for Sale. You should demand a formal invoice in writing. If the builder refuses possession based on this, you can file a complaint with RERA for unfair trade practices.

**Example 3: Structural Defects**
- **Citizen:** Maine flat ka possession liya aur 6 mahine mein hi deewar mein cracks aane lage. Builder repair karne se mana kar raha hai.
- **Assistant:** RERA Act ke Section 14(3) ke mutabiq, possession milne ke 5 saal ke andar agar koi structural defect aata hai, toh builder ko use 30 din ke andar free mein theek karna hota hai. Aap turant builder ko ek written legal notice bhejein, aur agar wo nahi maanta toh RERA mein shikayat darj karein.

**Example 4: Refund Claim**
- **Citizen:** The project is halted for 3 years. I want to cancel my booking and get a refund. Will they deduct money?
- **Assistant:** Under Section 18 of RERA, if the builder fails to complete the project on time, you have the absolute right to seek a full refund along with interest. They cannot legally deduct cancellation charges if the fault lies with their delay.

**Example 5: Unregistered Project**
- **Citizen:** Mera project RERA mein registered nahi hai, toh kya main complaint kar sakta hu?
- **Assistant:** Haan, bilkul. Agar project eligible tha par builder ne register nahi karwaya, tab bhi aap RERA mein complaint file kar sakte hain. RERA authority builder par non-registration ki penalty lagayegi aur aapki complaint bhi sunegi.

**Example 6: Builder Forcing Arbitration**
- **Citizen:** Builder is saying I can't go to RERA because there is an arbitration clause in my contract. Is that true?
- **Assistant:** No, that is false. The Supreme Court of India has firmly established that the existence of an arbitration clause does not bar a consumer or homebuyer from approaching RERA or Consumer Courts. These are statutory rights that cannot be overridden by private contracts.

**Example 7: Bank EMI vs Builder Delay**
- **Citizen:** Builder kaam nahi kar raha, toh kya main bank ki EMI rok du?
- **Assistant:** Nahi, kripya apni EMI na rokein. Aapka bank ke saath loan agreement builder ke agreement se alag hota hai. EMI rokne se aapka CIBIL score kharab hoga aur bank aappe recovery case kar dega. Aap RERA se refund claim karke loan clear kar sakte hain.

**Example 8: Changing Project Layout**
- **Citizen:** Builder plan change karke hamare park ki jagah ek aur tower bana raha hai. Kya ye allowed hai?
- **Assistant:** RERA ke tahat, builder kisi bhi sanctioned plan ya layout mein badlaav tab tak nahi kar sakta jab tak use project ke 2/3rd (do-tihai) buyers ki written consent (s सहमति) na mil jaye. Aap is violation ke khilaf turant RERA mein stay order ke liye apply kar sakte hain.

**Example 9: Cheating / Fraud**
- **Citizen:** The builder took money from 500 people, never bought the land, and his office is closed.
- **Assistant:** This is a clear case of severe criminal fraud. All buyers should immediately form an association and file an FIR with the Economic Offences Wing (EOW) of your local police under Section 316 (Criminal Breach of Trust) and Section 318 (Cheating) of the BNS. You can also file a case in the NCLT to trace his assets.

**Example 10: Buying Resale Property**
- **Citizen:** I bought an under-construction flat from an original buyer (resale). Does RERA protect me?
- **Assistant:** Yes. Once the builder recognizes the transfer and issues an endorsement or new agreement in your name, you step into the shoes of the original allottee and enjoy all the same rights under RERA against the builder.

**Example 11: Execution of Order**
- **Citizen:** RERA ne mere favor mein order pass kiya refund ka, par builder 3 mahine se paise nahi de raha.
- **Assistant:** Agar builder RERA order ka palan nahi kar raha, toh aapko RERA Authority mein 'Execution Petition' file karni hogi. Authority District Magistrate (DM) ko Recovery Certificate issue karegi, jo builder ki property attach/zabt karke aapka paisa recover karenge.

**Example 12: Super Built-up Area scam**
- **Citizen:** Builder sold me a 1000 sq ft flat, but I measured it and it's only 700 sq ft. He says 300 is 'super area'.
- **Assistant:** Under RERA, promoters are strictly mandated to sell property based only on 'Carpet Area' (the actual usable area within walls). Charging for super built-up area indiscriminately is illegal. You can demand a refund for the excess amount paid or file a complaint.

**Example 13: Builder Demanding Cash**
- **Citizen:** Builder registry ke time cash demand kar raha hai tax bachane ke liye.
- **Assistant:** Ye bilkul gair-kanooni (illegal) hai. Property transaction mein cash dena black money promote karta hai aur aage chal kar aapko title disputes ya income tax notices mein fasa sakta hai. Saari payments hamesha bank account/cheque ke through hi karein.

**Example 14: Filing RERA Complaint Process**
- **Citizen:** How do I physically file a RERA complaint? Do I need to go to court?
- **Assistant:** You do not need to visit a court initially. You can file a RERA complaint entirely online through your state's official RERA portal. You will need to upload your Agreement for Sale, payment proofs, and ID. A nominal filing fee is required, which can also be paid online.

**Example 15: Compensation for Mental Agony**
- **Citizen:** Builder ne 5 saal latkaya, main RERA mein delay interest ke alawa mental torture ka paisa maang sakta hu?
- **Assistant:** RERA specifically possession delay ke liye standardized interest deta hai. Agar aapko heavy compensation (mental agony aur harassment ke liye) chahiye, toh aap apne case ki valuation ke hisaab se Consumer Court (NCDRC/SCDRC) approach kar sakte hain, wahan compensation claim thoda broad hota hai.

## Government Services
- **MahaRERA / UP RERA / Other State RERA Portals:** Web portals for real estate project verification and online complaint filing. (e.g., https://maharera.mahaonline.gov.in)
- **e-Daakhil:** Government portal for filing online consumer complaints against builders for deficiency in service (https://edaakhil.nic.in).
- **NCLT e-Filing:** For filing insolvency and bankruptcy pleas collectively against major defaulting developers.
