# Property Mutation (Dakhil Kharij)

## Overview

### English
Property mutation is the legal process of updating the ownership details of a specific property in the local municipal or land revenue records. It is carried out after the transfer of property ownership due to sale, inheritance, gift, or partition. While property registration makes you the legal owner, mutation ensures that you are recognized by the local authorities for tax purposes. Without mutation, the property tax receipts and utility bills will continue to be generated in the name of the previous owner.

### Hindi (हिंदी)
संपत्ति दाखिल-खारिज (म्यूटेशन) स्थानीय नगर पालिका या भूमि राजस्व रिकॉर्ड में किसी विशिष्ट संपत्ति के स्वामित्व विवरण को अद्यतन (अपडेट) करने की कानूनी प्रक्रिया है। यह बिक्री, विरासत, उपहार या विभाजन के कारण संपत्ति के स्वामित्व हस्तांतरण के बाद किया जाता है। जबकि संपत्ति का पंजीकरण आपको कानूनी मालिक बनाता है, दाखिल-खारिज यह सुनिश्चित करता है कि कर उद्देश्यों के लिए स्थानीय अधिकारियों द्वारा आपको मान्यता दी जाए। दाखिल-खारिज के बिना, संपत्ति कर रसीदें और बिजली/पानी के बिल पिछले मालिक के नाम पर ही बनते रहेंगे।

Ye property ki sale, inheritance (virasat), gift ya partition ke baad kiya jata hai. ## Definition

### English
Mutation of property, commonly known as 'Dakhil Kharij' in North India, 'Intkal' in Punjab/Haryana, or 'Ferfar' in Maharashtra, refers to the recording of the transfer of title of a property from one person to another in the revenue records of the local municipal corporation or revenue department. It is the administrative procedure by which the government updates its property tax payer database.

### Hindi (हिंदी)
संपत्ति के म्यूटेशन, जिसे आमतौर पर उत्तर भारत में 'दाखिल-खारिज', पंजाब/हरियाणा में 'इंतकाल', या महाराष्ट्र में 'फेरफार' के रूप में जाना जाता है, का तात्पर्य स्थानीय नगर निगम या राजस्व विभाग के राजस्व रिकॉर्ड में एक व्यक्ति से दूसरे व्यक्ति को संपत्ति के शीर्षक हस्तांतरण को दर्ज करना है। यह वह प्रशासनिक प्रक्रिया है जिसके द्वारा सरकार अपने संपत्ति कर दाता डेटाबेस को अद्यतन करती है।

### Hinglish

Ye ek administrative process hai jisse government apna property tax payer database update karti hai.

### English
1. **Property Tax:** Enables the new owner to legally pay property taxes in their own name.
2. **Utilities:** Mandatory for applying for electricity, water, and gas connections.
3. **Compensation:** In case of land acquisition by the government, compensation is paid to the person whose name is in the revenue records.
4. **Resale:** Prospective buyers usually ask for mutation records to verify that there are no pending tax dues or hidden disputes.

## Legal Background

- **Applicable Acts:** 
  - The Transfer of Property Act, 1882
  - The Registration Act, 1908
  - Various State Land Revenue Codes (e.g., UP Revenue Code 2006, Maharashtra Land Revenue Code 1966)
  - State Municipal Corporation Acts
- **Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 300A (Right to Property - Constitutional Right)
- **Key Judicial Principle:** As per various Supreme Court judgments (e.g., Sawarni v. Inder Kaur, 1996), mutation entries do not convey or extinguish title to property, nor do they have any presumptive value on title. They are strictly for fiscal purposes (revenue collection).

---

## Applicability

- **Who is covered:** Any individual or entity that acquires property through sale deed, gift deed, relinquishment deed, exchange, inheritance, will, or partition.
- **Who is exempt:** Temporary tenants or leaseholders (unless it's a perpetual lease specifically requiring mutation as per state laws).
- **Special Conditions:** In agricultural land, mutation is extremely critical as it serves as the primary record for issuing Kisan Credit Cards, subsidies, and crop insurance. For flats/apartments, mutation in the municipal corporation is necessary, whereas in housing societies, a share certificate transfer is also required.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

### English
1. **Application Submission:** Submit the mutation application form online (via state land records portal) or offline at the local municipal office or Tehsildar office.
2. **Document Upload/Attachment:** Attach the registered sale deed/will, Aadhaar, affidavit, and tax clearance receipts.
3. **Proclamation (Ishtehar):** The revenue authority issues a public notice calling for objections within a specific period (usually 15-30 days).
4. **Verification:** The Patwari/Lekhpal verifies the documents and physically inspects the property if needed.
5. **No Objection:** If no objections are received within the stipulated time, the Tehsildar/Circle Officer approves the mutation.
6. **Order Generation:** The revenue record is updated, and a mutation certificate (Khatauni/Record of Right extract) is issued to the new owner.

### Hindi (हिंदी)
1. **आवेदन जमा करना:** म्यूटेशन आवेदन पत्र ऑनलाइन (राज्य भूमि रिकॉर्ड पोर्टल के माध्यम से) या स्थानीय नगरपालिका कार्यालय या तहसीलदार कार्यालय में ऑफलाइन जमा करें।
2. **दस्तावेज संलग्न करना:** पंजीकृत बिक्री विलेख/वसीयत, आधार, शपथ पत्र और कर निकासी रसीदें संलग्न करें।
3. **उद्घोषणा (इश्तेहार):** राजस्व प्राधिकरण एक विशिष्ट अवधि (आमतौर पर 15-30 दिन) के भीतर आपत्तियों के लिए सार्वजनिक नोटिस जारी करता है।
4. **सत्यापन:** पटवारी/लेखपाल दस्तावेजों का सत्यापन करता है और आवश्यकता पड़ने पर संपत्ति का भौतिक निरीक्षण करता है।
5. **कोई आपत्ति नहीं:** यदि निर्धारित समय के भीतर कोई आपत्ति प्राप्त नहीं होती है, तो तहसीलदार/सर्किल अधिकारी म्यूटेशन को मंजूरी दे देता है।
6. **आदेश जारी होना:** राजस्व रिकॉर्ड को अपडेट किया जाता है, और नए मालिक को म्यूटेशन प्रमाण पत्र (खतौनी/अधिकार का रिकॉर्ड) जारी किया जाता है।

## Required Documents

- Copy of the Registered Sale Deed / Gift Deed / Will.
- Identity Proof (Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID).
- Latest Property Tax Receipt (NOC from the tax department).
- Indemnity Bond and Affidavit on Stamp Paper of appropriate value.
- Death Certificate of the previous owner (in case of inheritance).
- Succession Certificate or Legal Heir Certificate (for intestate succession).
- Copy of the previous mutation order/Khatauni.
- Court Order copy (if mutation is based on a court decree).

---

## Government Authorities

- **Patwari / Lekhpal / Talathi:** Grassroots revenue official who maintains the village/ward land records and performs initial verification.
- **Revenue Inspector (Kanoongo):** Supervises the Patwari and forwards the verified report.
- **Tehsildar / Circle Officer / Mamlatdar:** The sanctioning authority who passes the final mutation order.
- **Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) / Assistant Collector:** The first appellate authority if mutation is contested or rejected.
- **Municipal Commissioner / Zonal Officer:** For urban properties within city limits.

---

## Penalties & Punishments

- There is no direct "criminal punishment" for merely delaying property mutation.
- **Penalty for Delay:** States usually levy a late fee or monetary penalty if mutation is applied for after the statutory period (e.g., Rs. 250 to Rs. 5000 depending on the delay and state rules).
- **Fraudulent Mutation:** Forging documents to get mutation done is a serious criminal offense punishable under sections of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) for forgery and cheating, which can lead to imprisonment.

---

## Exceptions

- **Disputed Title:** If a title suit is pending in a civil court regarding the property, the mutation process is usually stayed or kept pending until the court passes a final decree.
- **Leasehold Properties:** For long-term lease properties (e.g., 99-year lease from DDA or MHADA), a separate 'transfer of lease' process is required before the municipal mutation can occur.

---

## Important Timelines

- **Application Deadline:** Ideally within 3 to 6 months from the date of property registration or death of the owner.
- **Processing Time:** 15 to 45 working days, depending on the state and method of application.
- **Notice Period for Objections:** Usually 15 to 30 days after issuing the Ishtehar.
- **Appeal Timeline:** If rejected, an appeal must typically be filed before the SDM within 30 to 60 days of the Tehsildar's order.

---

## Common Legal Mistakes

- **Assuming Registry is Enough:** Thinking that executing a sale deed completes the process and ignoring mutation completely.
- **Ignoring Tax Dues:** Applying for mutation without clearing previous property tax arrears, leading to instant rejection.
- **Not Checking Previous Mutation:** Buying property without verifying if the seller had mutated the property in their own name first.
- **Delaying Inheritance Mutation:** Heirs failing to mutate ancestral property for decades, causing massive legal hurdles during subsequent sales.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Is property mutation compulsory?**
Yes, it is practically compulsory for paying property tax, transferring utility bills, and avoiding future legal complications, though it is a separate process from property registration.

**2. Does mutation confer legal title/ownership?**
No. Mutation does not grant or extinguish legal title. The title is proven by registered sale deeds or court decrees. Mutation is solely for government revenue collection.

**3. What is the fee for mutation?**
The fee varies widely by state, typically ranging from a nominal ₹25 to ₹1000 depending on the property type and state revenue rules.

**4. How long does the mutation process take?**
Typically, it takes anywhere between 15 to 45 days if all documents are in order and no objections are filed during the public notice period.

**5. Can mutation be done online?**
Yes, most Indian states have digitized their land records and offer online mutation applications via portals like Bhulekh (UP), Dharani (Telangana), and e-Swathu (Karnataka).

**6. What happens if I don't mutate the property?**
You will not receive property tax bills in your name, you cannot get water/electricity connections, and selling the property in the future will be extremely difficult.

**7. How is mutation done after the death of the owner?**
The legal heirs must submit an application along with the death certificate, succession certificate/legal heir certificate, and an affidavit to the Tehsildar or municipal authority.

**8. Can a mutation application be rejected?**
Yes. Common reasons include incomplete documents, unpaid previous tax dues, title disputes pending in civil court, or mismatched property details.

**9. What should I do if my mutation application is rejected?**
You can file an appeal against the Tehsildar’s/Zonal Officer's order before the Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) or the higher municipal appellate authority within 30-60 days.

**10. Do I need to mutate an apartment/flat?**
Yes, flats must be mutated in the local municipal corporation records to ensure property tax is assessed in your name. You must also notify the housing society.

**11. What is 'Kharij' in Dakhil Kharij?**
'Dakhil' means entry, and 'Kharij' means removal. Dakhil Kharij means removing the name of the previous owner and entering the name of the new owner in the records.

**12. Is mutation required for gifted property?**
Yes, once the gift deed is registered, the person receiving the gift (donee) must apply for mutation to update the revenue records.

**13. Do I need a lawyer for property mutation?**
It is not legally mandatory to hire a lawyer. The process is administrative, and many citizens do it themselves online, though legal help ensures error-free document submission.

**14. Can mutation be cancelled later?**
If it is proven that mutation was obtained through fraudulent documents, or if a civil court passes a decree contrary to the mutation, the revenue officer can cancel or revise the mutation.

**15. My property is in a joint name. How is mutation done?**
The mutation will reflect the names of all joint owners exactly as mentioned in the registered sale deed.

**16. What is the difference between Registry and Mutation?**
Registry transfers the legal title and ownership under the Transfer of Property Act. Mutation updates the government's tax records to acknowledge the new owner for tax purposes.

**17. What documents are needed for agricultural land mutation?**
Registered sale deed, previous Khatauni (RoR), Aadhaar, Khasra details, and a formal application to the local Tehsildar/Patwari.

**18. Can NRIs apply for property mutation online?**
Yes, NRIs can use state land portals to apply. They may need to provide an Aadhar or authorize someone locally via a registered Power of Attorney (PoA) if physical presence is requested.

**19. Will I get a penalty for late mutation?**
Some states impose a nominal late fee if mutation is not applied for within the stipulated time (usually 3 to 6 months post-registry).

**20. Does a Will require mutation after the testator's death?**
Yes. The beneficiary of the Will must apply for mutation by submitting the Will (preferably probated, if required by local laws) and the death certificate.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** Registration of sale deed automatically completes the mutation process.
   **Fact:** In most states, they are separate processes. You must manually apply for mutation after getting the registered deed, though some states are now integrating both.
2. **Myth:** Mutation proves I am the legal owner of the property.
   **Fact:** Mutation is only a record for tax collection. It does not establish absolute legal title.
3. **Myth:** If I don't do mutation, my registry becomes invalid.
   **Fact:** Your registered title remains completely valid, but you will face severe administrative and resale issues without mutation.
4. **Myth:** Mutation is not required for ancestral property.
   **Fact:** It is strictly required. Heirs must get their names mutated to apply for agricultural loans or sell the land.
5. **Myth:** Mutation costs a percentage of the property value.
   **Fact:** Mutation is usually a nominal fixed administrative fee, unlike stamp duty which is a percentage of the property value.
6. **Myth:** You cannot challenge a mutation once approved.
   **Fact:** You can appeal to a higher revenue authority (SDM/Collector) or challenge the title in a civil court.
7. **Myth:** Apartments don't require mutation, only land does.
   **Fact:** Apartments also require mutation in the municipal corporation for property tax assessment.
8. **Myth:** Mutation can be done with a Notarized Agreement to Sell.
   **Fact:** No. A registered sale deed or a court decree is mandatory for property mutation.
9. **Myth:** Electricity bills can be transferred without mutation.
   **Fact:** Most electricity boards now strictly demand a mutation certificate or tax receipt in the new owner's name.
10. **Myth:** Only the buyer can apply for mutation.
    **Fact:** In case of inheritance, legal heirs apply. In gifts, the donee applies.

## Conversation Examples

**Conversation 1: Basic Process**
**Citizen:** Maine naya ghar kharida hai aur registry ho gayi hai. Kya mujhe Dakhil Kharij karwana padega?
**Assistant:** Haan, registry ke baad dakhil kharij (mutation) karwana zaroori hai. Isse municipal records mein property aapke naam par update ho jayegi aur aage se property tax aapke naam se aayega.

**Conversation 2: Difference between Registry & Mutation**
**Citizen:** What is the exact difference between registry and mutation? I am confused.
**Assistant:** Registry transfers the legal ownership of the property to you. Mutation is simply updating the local government’s record book so they know who to collect property tax from. Both are essential.

**Conversation 3: Timeline**
**Citizen:** Dakhil kharij mein kitna time lagta hai?
**Assistant:** Aam taur par documents sahi hone par isme 15 se 45 din lagte hain. Ek 15-30 din ka public notice (ishtehar) nikalta hai, agar koi objection nahi aata, toh pass ho jata hai.

**Conversation 4: Legal Heir Mutation**
**Citizen:** Pitaji ke nidhan ke baad zameen mere naam karni hai. Process kya hai?
**Assistant:** Aapko Tehsildar office mein 'Virasat' (Inheritance) ke aadhar par mutation apply karna hoga. Iske liye Pitaji ka Death Certificate aur Legal Heir / Succession Certificate dena padega.

**Conversation 5: Non-Mutation Consequences**
**Citizen:** Agar main abhi mutation na karwaun toh kya nuksan hai?
**Assistant:** Agar aap mutation nahi karwayenge, toh bijli/pani ka bill aur property tax pichle owner ke naam par aate rahenge. Future mein agar aap property bechna chahenge, toh koi buyer use kharidega nahi.

**Conversation 6: Application Method**
**Citizen:** Kya mutation online apply kiya ja sakta hai?
**Assistant:** Ji haan, zyadatar states ne apna portal online kar diya hai (jaise UP mein Bhulekh, Telangana mein Dharani). Aap wahan se apply karke status track kar sakte hain.

**Conversation 7: Cost**
**Citizen:** How much does property mutation cost?
**Assistant:** Mutation fees are generally very nominal, typically ranging between ₹25 to ₹1000 depending on your state and whether it is an agricultural or urban property.

**Conversation 8: Rejection Reasons**
**Citizen:** Mera dakhil-kharij application reject ho gaya. Aisa kyun hua?
**Assistant:** Rejection ke kai karan ho sakte hain, jaise pichle owner ka property tax due hona, documents mismatch hona, ya property par pehle se court ka stay hona. Aapko rejection order mein exact reason check karna chahiye.

**Conversation 9: Dispute Objection**
**Citizen:** Someone applied for mutation on my land using fake papers. What should I do?
**Assistant:** You must immediately file a written objection (Aapatti) before the Tehsildar or Circle Officer handling the mutation process. Also, consider filing an FIR for forgery.

**Conversation 10: Apartment Mutation**
**Citizen:** Maine ek flat liya hai housing society mein. Kya mujhe bhi mutation karwana hoga?
**Assistant:** Haan, flat ke liye Municipal Corporation mein property tax ke liye mutation hoga. Iske alawa society ke records mein share certificate bhi transfer karwana padega.

**Conversation 11: NRI Buying Property**
**Citizen:** I am an NRI. Do I need to be physically present for mutation?
**Assistant:** Not necessarily. You can apply online through the state land records portal. If physical presence is needed, your authorized representative can attend on your behalf using a registered Power of Attorney.

**Conversation 12: Joint Property**
**Citizen:** Hum dono bhaiyon ne milkar zameen li hai. Mutation kiske naam se hoga?
**Assistant:** Mutation aap dono bhaiyon ke naam se jointly hoga, theek waise hi jaise aapki registered sale deed mein likha hua hai.

**Conversation 13: Delayed Mutation**
**Citizen:** Maine 5 saal pehle registry karayi thi par mutation nahi karaya. Kya ab kara sakta hu?
**Assistant:** Ji haan, aap ab bhi kara sakte hain. Halaanki, kuch states mein delay ke liye chhota penalty ya late fee lag sakta hai. Aapko apne property tax dues clear karne honge.

**Conversation 14: Mutation on Power of Attorney**
**Citizen:** Kya sirf Notary wali Power of Attorney par mutation ho sakta hai?
**Assistant:** Nahi, revenue department sirf registered transfer documents (jaise Sale Deed ya Gift Deed) ke aadhar par hi mutation karta hai. Notarized agreement valid nahi maana jata.

**Conversation 15: Utility Transfer**
**Citizen:** Bijli ka meter apne naam karane ke liye department kya mangega?
**Assistant:** Bijli department naye meter connection ya naam transfer ke liye aapse Mutation Certificate (ya updated property tax receipt) aur registered registry ki copy mangega.
