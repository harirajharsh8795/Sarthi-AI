# Property Partition (Bantwara) in India: Complete Legal Guide

## Overview
**English:** Property Partition, legally known as the division of joint or co-owned property, is a fundamental right of every co-owner or coparcener in India. It involves dividing a property into distinct shares so that each owner gets their rightful legal portion, which they can independently use, sell, or transfer. A partition can be executed amicably through a Partition Deed or contested through a Partition Suit in a civil court.

## Quick Summary
**English:** A partition separates the interests of co-owners in a joint property. When divided by mutual consent, a Partition Deed is executed and registered. If disputed, a Partition Suit is filed in court, leading to a preliminary and then a final decree. Registration and proper stamping are mandatory for the division of immovable property.

## Definition
**English:** Partition is the intentional severance of joint ownership in property, resulting in the conversion of joint tenancy or coparcenary property into independent, individual ownerships. 

**Hindi (Unicode):** बंटवारा (Partition) संपत्ति में संयुक्त स्वामित्व को समाप्त करने का कानूनी कार्य है, जिसके परिणामस्वरूप संयुक्त संपत्ति स्वतंत्र और व्यक्तिगत स्वामित्व में बदल जाती है।

**Hinglish:** Partition ek legal process hai jiske through joint ownership khatam ki jati hai, aur joint property alag-alag individual ownership mein badal jati hai.

## Why It Matters
**English:** It prevents future family disputes, clearly defines individual boundaries and ownership rights, and provides the absolute title required to sell, lease, or mortgage the property without needing permission from other family members.

**Hindi (Unicode):** यह भविष्य के पारिवारिक विवादों को रोकता है, व्यक्तिगत अधिकारों को स्पष्ट रूप से परिभाषित करता है, और संपत्ति को बेचने, पट्टे पर देने या गिरवी रखने के लिए पूर्ण कानूनी अधिकार प्रदान करता है।

**Hinglish:** Yeh future family disputes ko rokti hai, ownership rights ko clear karti hai, aur property ko sell ya mortgage karne ke liye absolute title deti hai taaki dusre family members ki permission na leni pade.

## Legal Background
**History & Purpose:** Historically, the Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) system held properties jointly. The Partition Act of 1893 and the Hindu Succession Act of 1956 (amended in 2005) modernized these laws, granting equal rights to female heirs and establishing structured procedures for partitioning agricultural, residential, and commercial properties.

**Applicable Acts & Relevant Sections:**
- **Hindu Succession Act, 1956:** Section 6 (as amended in 2005) guarantees daughters equal coparcenary rights in ancestral property. Section 8 details the general rules of succession for males.
- **The Partition Act, 1893:** Section 2 allows for the sale of property and distribution of proceeds if a physical division is unreasonable. Section 4 protects the rights of family members when a share of the family home is sold to a third party.
- **The Registration Act, 1908:** Section 17 mandates that any instrument (like a Partition Deed) creating or extinguishing rights in immovable property worth over Rs. 100 must be registered.
- **Indian Stamp Act, 1899:** Section 2(15) defines an instrument of partition, which dictates the stamp duty payable.
- **Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC):** Order XX Rule 18 details the procedure for court decrees in partition suits. Section 54 governs the partition of revenue-paying estates.
- **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023:** Section 318 covers cheating and fraud (e.g., deceiving a co-owner about property value), and Section 336 covers forgery of documents (e.g., faking a partition deed).

## Applicability
**Who is Covered:**
- Coparceners in a Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) (including daughters since 2005).
- Co-owners or joint owners who purchased property together.
- Legal heirs of a deceased person who died intestate (without a will).

**Who is Exempt / Exceptions:**
- Self-acquired property of a living person cannot be partitioned by their heirs against their will.
- Certain properties governed by specific trust deeds or endowments.

## Rights of Citizens
**English:** Every co-owner has the absolute right to claim their specific share in the property, the right to demand a physical division (metes and bounds), and the right to seek court intervention if mutual consent fails. Daughters have equal coparcenary rights.

**Hindi (Unicode):** प्रत्येक सह-स्वामी को संपत्ति में अपना विशिष्ट हिस्सा मांगने, भौतिक विभाजन (metes and bounds) की मांग करने और आपसी सहमति विफल होने पर कोर्ट की मदद लेने का पूर्ण अधिकार है। बेटियों को पैतृक संपत्ति में समान अधिकार प्राप्त हैं।

**Hinglish:** Har co-owner ko apna specific share claim karne aur property ke physical division ki demand karne ka right hai. Agar mutual consent na ho toh wo court ja sakte hain. Daughters ka ancestral property mein barabar ka right hai.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English:** Co-owners have a duty to maintain the property, pay property taxes proportionally until partition, not cause damage to the property, and disclose all relevant facts/documents truthfully during the partition process.

**Hindi (Unicode):** सह-स्वामियों का कर्तव्य है कि वे बंटवारे तक संपत्ति का रखरखाव करें, संपत्ति कर (Property Tax) का भुगतान करें, संपत्ति को नुकसान न पहुंचाएं, और बंटवारे के दौरान सभी तथ्यों और दस्तावेजों को सच्चाई से साझा करें।

**Hinglish:** Co-owners ki duty hai ki wo bantware tak property maintain karein, tax pay karein, property ko damage na karein, aur partition ke time sabhi documents aur facts sachayi se batayein.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English:**
*Method 1: Mutual Consent (Partition Deed)*
1. **Drafting:** Discuss and finalize the shares of each co-owner. Draft a Partition Deed on non-judicial stamp paper of appropriate value.
2. **Stamping:** Pay the required stamp duty as per the state's Stamp Act.
3. **Execution & Registration:** All co-owners must sign the deed in the presence of two witnesses. Register the deed at the Sub-Registrar’s office where the property is located.
4. **Mutation:** Apply for mutation of names in the municipal/revenue records using the registered deed.

*Method 2: Contested (Partition Suit)*
1. **Legal Notice:** Send a formal legal notice to all other co-owners demanding partition.
2. **Filing Suit:** If the notice is ignored, file a Partition Suit in a civil court with appropriate jurisdiction.
3. **Preliminary Decree:** The court examines the rights and issues a preliminary decree defining the share of each party.
4. **Local Commissioner:** The court appoints a Local Commissioner to evaluate the property and suggest a physical division (metes and bounds).
5. **Final Decree:** Based on the Commissioner's report, the court passes a final decree, officially allotting the shares.

## Required Documents
- Original Title Deeds (Sale Deed, Gift Deed, etc.) of the property.
- Encumbrance Certificate (EC) to prove the property is free of illegal liabilities.
- Death Certificate of the original owner (if the property is ancestral).
- Family Tree Certificate (Vanshavali) issued by the Tehsildar or revenue authority.
- Khata / Patta / Property Tax receipts.
- Aadhar Card and PAN Card of all co-owners.
- Two passport-sized photographs of all executing parties.

## Government Authorities
- **Sub-Registrar Office:** For the registration of the Partition Deed and payment of stamp duty.
- **Civil Courts:** District Court or High Court (depending on pecuniary jurisdiction) for filing Partition Suits.
- **Revenue Department (Tehsildar/Patwari):** For updating land records (Mutation/Dakhil-Kharij) after the partition.
- **Lok Adalat / Mediation Centers:** For amicable out-of-court dispute resolution under the Legal Services Authorities Act.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Unregistered Deeds:** Under Section 49 of the Registration Act, an unregistered partition deed cannot be accepted as evidence in court for proving ownership.
- **Fraudulent Partition:** Creating fake family trees, hiding heirs, or forging signatures is punishable under BNS Section 318 (Cheating) and Section 336 (Forgery), attracting imprisonment up to 3-7 years and fines.
- **Stamp Duty Evasion:** The property document can be impounded, and penalties up to 10 times the original stamp duty amount can be levied by the Collector.

## Exceptions
- Impartible Estates: Certain properties granted by the government or historically designated as impartible cannot be partitioned.
- Property dedicated entirely to a public trust, temple, or Waqf cannot be partitioned among family members.
- If physical division ruins the property's intrinsic value (like a very small house), the court may order the sale of the property under the Partition Act, 1893, and divide the monetary proceeds instead.

## Important Timelines
- **Notice Period:** A standard legal notice usually gives 30 to 60 days to the co-owners to respond before a suit is filed.
- **Limitation Period:** Article 65 of the Limitation Act, 1963 sets a 12-year limitation period to file a suit for possession based on title, especially if another co-owner claims adverse possession. However, for a simple partition where all are in constructive possession, there is technically no fixed limitation period, but it should be done within 12 years of right denial.
- **Registration Time:** A partition deed must be registered within 4 months of its execution date.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Relying entirely on an oral partition (Zubaani Bantwara) without documenting it or doing mutation. Oral partition is very hard to prove in court.
- Not registering the family settlement or partition deed. Unregistered documents have zero legal validity for immovable property transfer.
- Excluding daughters or sisters from the partition of ancestral property. This renders the partition legally challengeable and void.
- Forgetting to execute mutation (Dakhil Kharij) in revenue records after getting the registered partition deed or court decree.

## Safety Tips
**English:** Always perform a title search and obtain an Encumbrance Certificate before finalizing the partition. Ensure all legal heirs, especially females, are included in the deed. Do not sign blank papers. Pay the correct stamp duty to avoid the document being impounded.

**Hindi (Unicode):** बंटवारा तय करने से पहले हमेशा संपत्ति का टाइटल सर्च (Title Search) करें और एन्कम्ब्रेंस सर्टिफिकेट (EC) प्राप्त करें। सुनिश्चित करें कि सभी कानूनी वारिसों, विशेषकर महिलाओं को डीड में शामिल किया गया है। कभी भी कोरे कागज़ पर हस्ताक्षर न करें। दस्तावेज़ को ज़ब्त होने से बचाने के लिए सही स्टाम्प ड्यूटी का भुगतान करें।

**Hinglish:** Partition final karne se pehle property ka title check karein aur Encumbrance Certificate nikalein. Ensure karein ki sabhi legal heirs (specially daughters/sisters) deed mein shamil hain. Blank paper par sign mat karein aur correct stamp duty pay karein.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **Can a daughter claim partition of ancestral property?**
   **English:** Yes, under the 2005 amendment to the Hindu Succession Act, daughters have equal rights as sons in ancestral property and can demand partition.
   **Hindi:** हां, हिंदू उत्तराधिकार अधिनियम के 2005 के संशोधन के तहत, बेटियों को पैतृक संपत्ति में बेटों के समान अधिकार हैं और वे बंटवारे की मांग कर सकती हैं।
   **Hinglish:** Haan, 2005 amendment ke baad daughters ka ancestral property mein sons ke barabar ka right hai aur wo partition demand kar sakti hain.

2. **Can a son claim partition in his father's self-acquired property?**
   **English:** No. A son has no legal right to claim partition in his father's self-acquired property while the father is alive. The father can dispose of it however he wishes.
   **Hindi:** नहीं। पिता के जीवित रहते हुए बेटा उनकी स्व-अर्जित संपत्ति में बंटवारे का दावा नहीं कर सकता। पिता इसे अपनी इच्छा अनुसार किसी को भी दे सकते हैं।
   **Hinglish:** Nahi. Father ke zinda rehte son unki self-acquired property par partition claim nahi kar sakta. Father use jaise chahe dispose kar sakte hain.

3. **What is the difference between ancestral and self-acquired property?**
   **English:** Ancestral property is inherited up to four generations of male lineage without any division. Self-acquired property is purchased from one's own income or received via a direct Will or Gift.
   **Hindi:** पैतृक संपत्ति वह है जो चार पीढ़ियों तक बिना किसी बंटवारे के विरासत में मिलती है। स्व-अर्जित संपत्ति अपनी आय से खरीदी गई या वसीयत/उपहार के माध्यम से प्राप्त होती है।
   **Hinglish:** Ancestral property wo hai jo 4 generations se bina divide hue inherit hoti hai. Self-acquired property apni income se kharidi gayi ya Will/Gift se mili property hoti hai.

4. **Is an oral partition valid?**
   **English:** While oral partition is historically recognized under Hindu law, it is very difficult to prove in court. For immovable property, a written and registered deed is legally necessary for a clear title.
   **Hindi:** हालांकि हिंदू कानून के तहत मौखिक बंटवारे को मान्यता प्राप्त है, लेकिन इसे अदालत में साबित करना बहुत मुश्किल है। अचल संपत्ति के स्पष्ट मालिकाना हक के लिए लिखित और पंजीकृत डीड अनिवार्य है।
   **Hinglish:** Oral partition ko Hindu law mein maanta hai, par court mein prove karna mushkil hai. Immovable property ke clear title ke liye written aur registered deed zaruri hai.

5. **How is stamp duty calculated for a Partition Deed?**
   **English:** Stamp duty is usually calculated on the market value of the largest separated share. The rates vary state by state (e.g., 2% to 5%).
   **Hindi:** स्टाम्प ड्यूटी की गणना आमतौर पर अलग किए गए सबसे बड़े हिस्से के बाजार मूल्य पर की जाती है। इसकी दरें हर राज्य में अलग-अलग (जैसे 2% से 5%) होती हैं।
   **Hinglish:** Stamp duty generally sabse bade separated share ke market value par calculate hoti hai. Rates har state mein alag (2% se 5%) hote hain.

6. **What happens if one co-owner refuses to sign the Partition Deed?**
   **English:** If even one co-owner refuses to sign, a mutual Partition Deed cannot be executed. The only remedy is to file a Partition Suit in a civil court.
   **Hindi:** यदि एक भी सह-स्वामी हस्ताक्षर करने से इंकार करता है, तो आपसी बंटवारा डीड नहीं बन सकती। इसका एकमात्र उपाय सिविल कोर्ट में 'पार्टीशन सूट' दायर करना है।
   **Hinglish:** Agar ek bhi co-owner sign karne se mana kar de, toh mutual deed nahi ban sakti. Fir civil court mein Partition Suit file karna hi ek option bachta hai.

7. **How long does a Partition Suit take in court?**
   **English:** A contested partition suit can take anywhere from 3 to 10 years or more depending on the complexities, number of heirs, and court backlog.
   **Hindi:** विवादित बंटवारा मुकदमे में जटिलताओं, वारिसों की संख्या और कोर्ट के काम के बोझ के आधार पर 3 से 10 वर्ष या उससे अधिक का समय लग सकता है।
   **Hinglish:** Contested suit mein complexities, heirs ki ginti aur court ke backlog ke hisaab se 3 se 10 saal ya usse zyada time lag sakta hai.

8. **What is a Preliminary Decree in a Partition Suit?**
   **English:** A preliminary decree is the court's initial order that officially declares the shares of each party but does not physically divide the property.
   **Hindi:** प्रारंभिक डिक्री कोर्ट का वह शुरुआती आदेश है जो प्रत्येक पक्ष के हिस्से की आधिकारिक घोषणा करता है, लेकिन संपत्ति का भौतिक विभाजन नहीं करता।
   **Hinglish:** Preliminary decree court ka initial order hai jo sabka share legally decide karta hai, par property ko physically divide nahi karta.

9. **What is a Final Decree?**
   **English:** The final decree physically divides the property based on the Commissioner's report. Once stamped and registered, it serves as the absolute proof of ownership for the divided parts.
   **Hindi:** अंतिम डिक्री कमिश्नर की रिपोर्ट के आधार पर संपत्ति का भौतिक विभाजन करती है। स्टाम्प और रजिस्टर होने के बाद, यह विभाजित हिस्सों के मालिकाना हक का पूर्ण प्रमाण है।
   **Hinglish:** Final decree Commissioner ki report ke base par property ka physical division karti hai. Stamp aur register hone ke baad, yeh divided parts ke ownership ka absolute proof ban jati hai.

10. **Can a partitioned property be sold without others' consent?**
    **English:** Yes, once the partition is legally finalized and registered, you become the absolute owner of your specific share and can sell it without anyone's consent.
    **Hindi:** हां, एक बार बंटवारा कानूनी रूप से अंतिम और पंजीकृत हो जाने के बाद, आप अपने हिस्से के पूर्ण स्वामी बन जाते हैं और बिना किसी की सहमति के इसे बेच सकते हैं।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, ek baar partition legally final aur register ho jaye, toh aap apne share ke absolute owner ban jate hain aur kisi ki permission ke bina sale kar sakte hain.

11. **Do stepchildren have a right to claim partition?**
    **English:** A stepchild inherits from their biological parents, not the stepparents, unless the stepparent has formally adopted them or written a Will in their favor.
    **Hindi:** सौतेला बच्चा अपने जैविक माता-पिता से विरासत प्राप्त करता है, न कि सौतेले माता-पिता से, जब तक कि सौतेले माता-पिता ने उन्हें औपचारिक रूप से गोद न लिया हो या वसीयत न लिखी हो।
    **Hinglish:** Stepchild apne biological parents se inherit karta hai. Stepparents ki property mein unka right nahi hota jab tak ki unhe legally adopt na kiya gaya ho ya Will na ho.

12. **Can a widow claim partition in her husband's family property?**
    **English:** Yes, a widow steps into the shoes of her deceased husband. She is a Class I legal heir and is entitled to claim the exact share her husband would have received.
    **Hindi:** हां, विधवा अपने मृत पति की जगह लेती है। वह प्रथम श्रेणी (Class I) की कानूनी वारिस है और वह ठीक उसी हिस्से का दावा कर सकती है जो उसके पति को मिलता।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, widow apne deceased husband ka share claim kar sakti hai. Wo Class I legal heir hoti hai aur apne husband ka equal share claim karne ke liye legally entitled hai.

13. **Is it mandatory to register a court's final decree for partition?**
    **English:** Yes, a final decree that partitions immovable property must be stamped and registered under the Registration Act to ensure the legal title is transferred.
    **Hindi:** हां, अचल संपत्ति का बंटवारा करने वाली अंतिम डिक्री को कानूनी मालिकाना हक हस्तांतरित करने के लिए रजिस्ट्रेशन एक्ट के तहत स्टाम्प और पंजीकृत किया जाना चाहिए।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, immovable property ka final decree Registration Act ke under stamp aur register karana zaruri hai taaki legal title transfer ho sake.

14. **What if physical partition of a house is impossible?**
    **English:** If a house is too small to divide, the Partition Act allows the court to order the sale of the property and distribute the monetary proceeds equally among co-owners.
    **Hindi:** यदि घर इतना छोटा है कि उसे बांटा नहीं जा सकता, तो पार्टिशन एक्ट कोर्ट को संपत्ति बेचने और सह-स्वामियों के बीच पैसे बांटने का आदेश देने की अनुमति देता है।
    **Hinglish:** Agar ghar bahut chhota hai aur divide nahi ho sakta, toh court us property ko sell karke paisa sabhi co-owners mein barabar baantne ka order de sakti hai.

15. **Can an unborn child claim rights in a partition?**
    **English:** Under Hindu law, an unborn child in the mother's womb at the time of partition has a right to a share. The partition must either be postponed or a share kept aside for the child.
    **Hindi:** हिंदू कानून के तहत, बंटवारे के समय मां के गर्भ में पल रहे अजन्मे बच्चे का हिस्से पर अधिकार होता है। बंटवारे को या तो टालना पड़ता है या बच्चे के लिए हिस्सा अलग रखना पड़ता है।
    **Hinglish:** Hindu law ke mutabiq, unborn child jo mother ke womb mein hai, uska bhi share hota hai. Uske liye ya toh partition postpone karna padta hai ya share alag rakhna padta hai.

16. **Can agricultural land be partitioned by a Civil Court?**
    **English:** Generally, partition of agricultural land is done by Revenue Courts (Tehsildar/SDM) as per state-specific land revenue acts, not standard Civil Courts.
    **Hindi:** आम तौर पर, कृषि भूमि का बंटवारा राज्य के भू-राजस्व अधिनियमों के अनुसार राजस्व न्यायालयों (तहसीलदार / एसडीएम) द्वारा किया जाता है, सामान्य सिविल कोर्ट द्वारा नहीं।
    **Hinglish:** Agricultural land ka partition mostly Revenue Courts (Tehsildar/SDM) state laws ke according karte hain, standard Civil Courts nahi.

17. **What is a Family Settlement Agreement (FSA)?**
    **English:** An FSA is a mutual out-of-court agreement among family members to divide property and resolve disputes peacefully without a formal partition suit.
    **Hindi:** पारिवारिक समझौता (FSA) परिवार के सदस्यों के बीच संपत्ति को बांटने और बिना औपचारिक मुकदमे के शांतिपूर्वक विवादों को सुलझाने के लिए एक आपसी समझौता है।
    **Hinglish:** Family Settlement Agreement ek mutual agreement hai jisme family members bina court case ke shanti se property divide karte hain aur disputes resolve karte hain.

18. **Does a registered Will override a partition?**
    **English:** A person can only Will away their self-acquired property or their specific undivided share in ancestral property. They cannot Will away the entire ancestral property.
    **Hindi:** कोई व्यक्ति केवल अपनी स्व-अर्जित संपत्ति या पैतृक संपत्ति में अपने विशिष्ट हिस्से की ही वसीयत कर सकता है। वह पूरी पैतृक संपत्ति की वसीयत नहीं कर सकता।
    **Hinglish:** Ek person sirf apni self-acquired property ya ancestral property mein apne share ki Will bana sakta hai. Wo poori ancestral property ki Will nahi bana sakta.

19. **What is the difference between Relinquishment Deed and Partition Deed?**
    **English:** In a partition, property is divided among all owners. In a relinquishment deed (Release Deed), one co-owner voluntarily gives up their specific share in favor of another co-owner.
    **Hindi:** बंटवारे में संपत्ति सभी स्वामियों के बीच बांटी जाती है। त्याग पत्र (Relinquishment Deed) में, एक सह-स्वामी स्वेच्छा से दूसरे सह-स्वामी के पक्ष में अपना हिस्सा छोड़ देता है।
    **Hinglish:** Partition mein property sabhi owners mein divide hoti hai. Relinquishment deed mein ek co-owner apni marzi se apna share kisi dusre co-owner ke favor mein chhod deta hai.

20. **Can partition be done if original documents are lost?**
    **English:** Yes, you can obtain certified copies of the title deeds from the Sub-Registrar's office, file an FIR for lost documents, and proceed with the partition suit.
    **Hindi:** हां, आप सब-रजिस्ट्रार कार्यालय से टाइटल डीड की प्रमाणित प्रतियां प्राप्त कर सकते हैं, खोए हुए दस्तावेजों के लिए एफआईआर दर्ज कर सकते हैं, और बंटवारे की कार्यवाही आगे बढ़ा सकते हैं।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, aap Sub-Registrar office se certified copies nikalwa sakte hain, lost documents ki FIR file karke partition ka process aage badha sakte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Daughters who are married before 2005 have no right to ancestral property.
   **Fact:** The Supreme Court (Vineeta Sharma vs Rakesh Sharma, 2020) clarified that daughters have equal coparcenary rights regardless of when they were born or married.
2. **Myth:** The eldest son gets a larger share of the property.
   **Fact:** In Indian law, all legal heirs of the same class get an equal share. There is no special privilege for the eldest son.
3. **Myth:** A Partition Deed doesn't need to be registered if the family agrees.
   **Fact:** An unregistered Partition Deed has no legal value for immovable property and cannot be used for mutation or future sales.
4. **Myth:** The head of the family (Karta) can sell the entire ancestral property without others' consent.
   **Fact:** A Karta can only sell ancestral property for 'legal necessity' or 'benefit of estate'. Otherwise, consent of all coparceners is required.
5. **Myth:** Nominees automatically become full owners of the property.
   **Fact:** A nominee is merely a trustee. The property must ultimately be distributed among the legal heirs as per succession laws or a Will.
6. **Myth:** If you possess a property for 5 years, you become its sole owner.
   **Fact:** Adverse possession requires 12 years of uninterrupted, hostile, and open possession to claim ownership, which is very hard to prove against co-owners.
7. **Myth:** Oral partition recorded on plain paper is sufficient.
   **Fact:** A plain paper document without stamp duty and registration is legally invalid for immovable property partition.
8. **Myth:** Self-acquired property automatically becomes ancestral after the owner's death.
   **Fact:** It becomes joint family property for the heirs, but it is not "ancestral" (coparcenary) in the strict legal sense, unless it passes down four generations.
9. **Myth:** Only sons have the duty to maintain parents after claiming property.
   **Fact:** Both sons and daughters who inherit property have a legal and moral obligation to maintain their dependent parents.
10. **Myth:** A partition suit will lock up the property indefinitely and nobody can use it.
    **Fact:** While the suit is pending, the court can appoint a receiver or grant injunctions, but co-owners usually maintain their existing possession and use.

## Conversation Examples

**Scenario 1: Mutual Agreement Setup**
*Citizen:* "Bhai, hum teen bhai-behen hain. Pitaji nahi rahe. Ghar ka bantwara kaise karein bina ladai ke?"
*Saarthi:* "Aap teeno aapsi sehmati se ek 'Partition Deed' banwa sakte hain. Ek lawyer se deed draft karwayein, uspar appropriate stamp duty pay karein, aur Sub-Registrar office mein register kara lein. Phir nagar nigam mein mutation (Dakhil-Kharij) karwa lein."

**Scenario 2: Denied Rights to Daughter**
*Citizen:* "I am married. My brothers are saying I have no share in our grandfather's ancestral land. Is it true?"
*Saarthi:* "No, that is incorrect. Under the Hindu Succession Act (2005 Amendment), daughters have equal coparcenary rights in ancestral property, just like sons, regardless of their marital status. You can claim your legal share."

**Scenario 3: Unregistered Deed Trouble**
*Citizen:* "Humne 10 saal pehle 100 rupaye ke stamp paper par bantwara likh liya tha. Ab mera bhai maan nahi raha. Kya karu?"
*Saarthi:* "100 rupaye ke stamp paper par bina registration ke hua bantwara legally valid nahi maana jata (Registration Act Section 17). Aapko ab civil court mein 'Partition Suit' file karna padega apne hisse ke liye."

**Scenario 4: Father's Self-Acquired Property**
*Citizen:* "Mere papa ne khud ek ghar kharida tha. Wo mujhe usme se hissa nahi de rahe hain. Kya main court ja sakta hu?"
*Saarthi:* "Nahi. Pita ki swayam-arjit (self-acquired) sampatti par unka pura adhikar hota hai. Jab tak wo z जीवित (alive) hain, aap usme legally bantware ka dawa nahi kar sakte."

**Scenario 5: Relinquishing Rights**
*Citizen:* "Main apni beheno ko apna hissa free mein dena chahta hu. Iska kya process hai?"
*Saarthi:* "Iske liye aapko ek 'Relinquishment Deed' (Release Deed/Tyag Patra) banwani hogi. Is deed ko stamp paper par print karke Sub-Registrar office mein register karana hoga. Isme stamp duty Partition Deed se kam lagti hai."

**Scenario 6: Missing Co-owner**
*Citizen:* "Mera ek bhai 10 saal se missing hai, uska kuch pata nahi. Ghar ka bantwara kaise hoga?"
*Saarthi:* "Agar koi vyakti 7 saal tak missing ho, toh law use legally dead maan leta hai (Section 108 of Evidence Act). Aapko court se civil death declaration lena hoga, uske baad uske legal heirs (patni/bacche) ko shamil karke bantwara kiya ja sakta hai."

**Scenario 7: Cost of Partition**
*Citizen:* "Partition deed banwane mein kitna kharcha aata hai?"
*Saarthi:* "Kharcha do cheezon par depend karta hai: Lawyer ki fees aur State ki Stamp Duty. Stamp duty har rajya mein alag hoti hai (lagbhag 2% se 5% property ke hisse ki value par). Registration fees alag se lagti hai."

**Scenario 8: Agricultural Land Partition**
*Citizen:* "Gaon ki khet ki zameen ka bantwara kaise hoga? Kya civil court jana padega?"
*Saarthi:* "Khet (Agricultural land) ke bantware ke liye civil court nahi, balki Revenue Court (Tehsildar ya SDM) ke paas aawedan karna padta hai. Iska process state ke Land Revenue Act ke tahat chalta hai."

**Scenario 9: Forced Sale of Small House**
*Citizen:* "Ghar bahut chota hai, hum 4 bhai hain, physically divide nahi ho sakta. Kya karein?"
*Saarthi:* "Aise mamle mein Court Partition Act, 1893 ke tahat ghar ko market price par bechne (sell) ka aadesh de sakti hai. Uske baad jo paisa aayega, wo chaaro bhaiyon mein barabar baant diya jayega."

**Scenario 10: Step-Mother's Rights**
*Citizen:* "Pitaji ki death ke baad kya meri step-mother (sauteli maa) ka property mein share hai?"
*Saarthi:* "Haan. Agar aapke pitaji ne waisyat (Will) nahi banayi hai, toh unki vidhwa (step-mother) ek Class I legal heir mani jati hai aur unka aapke pitaji ki property mein barabar ka hissa hoga."

**Scenario 11: Pending Home Loan**
*Citizen:* "Ghar par abhi home loan chal raha hai. Kya iska bantwara ho sakta hai?"
*Saarthi:* "Haan, lekin bank ki NOC (No Objection Certificate) leni hogi. Bantware ke baad, jis vyakti ko jo hissa milega, wo us hisse ka bacha hua loan chukane ke liye zimmedar hoga."

**Scenario 12: Threats During Partition**
*Citizen:* "Mera bada bhai bantware ki baat karne par maar-peet ki dhamki de raha hai. Help me."
*Saarthi:* "Aap turant police me jhuthi dhamki aur maar-peet (BNS Section 351) ki complaint darj karein. Property ke liye, aap apne lawyer ke through unhe Legal Notice bhejein aur Civil Court mein Partition Suit file karke 'Stay Order' (Injunction) maangein taaki wo property bech na sakein."

**Scenario 13: Delay in Court Decree**
*Citizen:* "Court se final decree mil gayi hai. Ab aage kya karna hai?"
*Saarthi:* "Court ki Final Decree ko Non-Judicial Stamp paper par print karwayein aur Sub-Registrar ke paas jakar uski registry karwayein. Iske baad, nagar nigam ya panchayat mein property ka mutation apne naam par karwayein."

**Scenario 14: Grandchildren's Right**
*Citizen:* "Kya pote (grandson) ka dada ki ancestral property mein birth se right hota hai?"
*Saarthi:* "Haan, ancestral property (jo 4 generation se aayi ho) mein pote aur poti ka janm (birth) se hi coparcenary right hota hai. Wo apne hisse ki maang kar sakte hain."

**Scenario 15: Discovering Hidden Property**
*Citizen:* "Bantwara 5 saal pehle ho gaya tha. Ab pata chala pitaji ki ek aur zameen thi jo bhai ne chupayi thi. Kya karu?"
*Saarthi:* "Bhai dwara sampatti chupana fraud (BNS Sec 318) hai. Aap us nayi zameen ke liye court mein naya partition suit file kar sakte hain, aur pichle bantware ko fraud ke aadhar par challenge bhi kar sakte hain."
