# Property Registration Process in India (सम्पत्ति पंजीकरण प्रक्रिया)

## Overview
**English:** Property registration is the official and legal process of recording the ownership of a property in the name of the buyer in the government's records. It involves paying the requisite stamp duty and registration charges to the Sub-Registrar of Assurances. Without this legal step, the buyer does not get the legal title to the property, even if they have paid the full purchase amount and possess the physical document.
**Hindi Unicode:** संपत्ति पंजीकरण (प्रॉपर्टी रजिस्ट्री) एक आधिकारिक और कानूनी प्रक्रिया है जिसमें सरकारी रिकॉर्ड में खरीदार के नाम पर संपत्ति के स्वामित्व को दर्ज किया जाता है। इसमें उप-पंजीयक (सब-रजिस्ट्रार) को आवश्यक स्टाम्प शुल्क और पंजीकरण शुल्क का भुगतान करना शामिल है। इस कानूनी कदम के बिना, खरीदार को संपत्ति का कानूनी शीर्षक नहीं मिलता है, भले ही उन्होंने पूरी राशि का भुगतान कर दिया हो।
**Hinglish:** Property registration ek official aur legal process hai jisme government records mein buyer ke naam par property ki ownership register ki jati hai. Isme Sub-Registrar office mein stamp duty aur registration charges pay karna padta hai. Is process ke bina, buyer ko property ka legal title nahi milta, chahe unhone full payment karke possession le liya ho.

## Quick Summary
**English:** The registration of property must be done under Section 17 of the Registration Act, 1908, within four months from the date of execution of the sale deed. It mandates the presence of the buyer, seller, and two witnesses at the Sub-Registrar's office.
**Hindi Unicode:** पंजीकरण अधिनियम, 1908 की धारा 17 के तहत बिक्री विलेख (सेल डीड) के निष्पादन की तारीख से चार महीने के भीतर संपत्ति का पंजीकरण किया जाना चाहिए। इसके लिए सब-रजिस्ट्रार कार्यालय में खरीदार, विक्रेता और दो गवाहों की उपस्थिति अनिवार्य है।
**Hinglish:** Registration Act 1908 ke Section 17 ke under, sale deed banne ke 4 mahine ke andar property ka registration karwana zaroori hai. Iske liye buyer, seller aur do witnesses ka Sub-Registrar office mein present hona compulsory hai.

## Definition
**English:** Registration refers to the recording of an executed document in the official registers maintained by the State Government, conferring legal validity and public notice of a transaction concerning immovable property.
**Hindi Unicode:** पंजीकरण का तात्पर्य राज्य सरकार द्वारा बनाए गए आधिकारिक रजिस्टरों में एक निष्पादित दस्तावेज़ को दर्ज करने से है, जो अचल संपत्ति से संबंधित लेनदेन को कानूनी वैधता और सार्वजनिक नोटिस प्रदान करता है।
**Hinglish:** Registry ka matlab hai state government ke official records mein property ke documents ko darj karna, jisse us transaction ko legal validity milti hai aur public ko uske baare mein official notice milta hai.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Registering a property serves as conclusive proof of ownership, protects against fraud, enables the owner to secure bank loans, and makes the transaction legally enforceable in a court of law. It prevents the seller from selling the same property to multiple individuals.
**Hindi Unicode:** संपत्ति का पंजीकरण स्वामित्व का निर्णायक प्रमाण है, जो धोखाधड़ी से बचाता है, मालिक को बैंक ऋण सुरक्षित करने में सक्षम बनाता है, और लेन-देन को अदालत में कानूनी रूप से लागू करने योग्य बनाता है। यह विक्रेता को एक ही संपत्ति कई लोगों को बेचने से रोकता है।
**Hinglish:** Property register karna ownership ka sabse bada proof hai. Ye fraud se bachata hai, bank loans lene mein madad karta hai, aur court mein legal rights enforce karne ki power deta hai. Ye double-sale fraud (ek hi property do logon ko bechna) ko rokta hai.

## Legal Background
**History:** The concept of formal property registration was institutionalized by the British in India to ensure transparency in land administration and revenue collection, culminating in the Registration Act of 1908.
**Purpose:** To prevent forgery and fraud, ensure public record-keeping, and guarantee the authenticity of title documents.
**Applicable Acts:** 
- The Registration Act, 1908
- The Transfer of Property Act, 1882
- The Indian Stamp Act, 1899
- Indian Contract Act, 1872
**Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 300A (Right to Property - persons not to be deprived of property save by authority of law).
**Relevant Sections:** 
- Section 17 of the Registration Act, 1908: Documents for which registration is compulsory.
- Section 49 of the Registration Act, 1908: Effect of non-registration of documents required to be registered.
- Section 54 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882: Definition of 'Sale' and how made.

## Applicability
**Who is covered:** Any individual, Hindu Undivided Family (HUF), company, or entity acquiring immovable property valued at ₹100 or more.
**Who is exempt:** Certain government grants, specific leases not exceeding one year, and particular types of wills do not require mandatory registration.
**Special conditions:** Agricultural land registration may be restricted for non-agriculturists in states like Himachal Pradesh and Maharashtra. NRIs must follow FEMA guidelines while registering property.

**English:** 
1. The right to inspect public registers for property encumbrances (Encumbrance Certificate).
2. The right to receive the original registered document after processing.
3. The right to file an appeal if the Sub-Registrar refuses registration under Section 71.

2. प्रक्रिया के बाद मूल पंजीकृत दस्तावेज़ प्राप्त करने का अधिकार।
3. धारा 71 के तहत सब-रजिस्ट्रार द्वारा पंजीकरण से इनकार करने पर अपील दायर करने का अधिकार।

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. Title Verification: Check property title and Encumbrance Certificate for the past 15-30 years.
2. Drafting the Deed: A legal expert drafts the Sale Deed on non-judicial stamp paper of appropriate value.
3. Stamp Duty Payment: Pay the required stamp duty online or via franking/e-stamp.
4. Appointment Booking: Schedule an appointment online at the respective Sub-Registrar's office.
5. Appearance: Buyer, seller, and two witnesses appear before the Sub-Registrar on the scheduled date with original IDs and photographs.
6. Execution and Biometrics: Parties sign the registry document, and their biometrics (thumb impressions) and photos are captured.
7. Verification: The Sub-Registrar verifies the documents and payment receipts.
8. Document Collection: The registered document is handed over or sent by post to the buyer after a few days.

**Hindi Unicode:**
1. शीर्षक सत्यापन: पिछले 15-30 वर्षों के संपत्ति के शीर्षक और एन्कम्ब्रेंस सर्टिफिकेट की जांच करें।
2. डीड तैयार करना: एक कानूनी विशेषज्ञ उचित मूल्य के गैर-न्यायिक स्टाम्प पेपर पर सेल डीड तैयार करता है।
3. स्टाम्प शुल्क भुगतान: आवश्यक स्टाम्प शुल्क का ऑनलाइन या ई-स्टाम्प के माध्यम से भुगतान करें।
4. अपॉइंटमेंट बुकिंग: संबंधित सब-रजिस्ट्रार कार्यालय में ऑनलाइन अपॉइंटमेंट बुक करें।
5. उपस्थिति: खरीदार, विक्रेता और दो गवाह निर्धारित तिथि पर मूल आईडी और तस्वीरों के साथ उप-पंजीयक के सामने उपस्थित होते हैं।
6. हस्ताक्षर और बायोमेट्रिक्स: पार्टियां रजिस्ट्री दस्तावेज़ पर हस्ताक्षर करती हैं, और उनके बायोमेट्रिक्स (अंगूठे के निशान) और तस्वीरें ली जाती हैं।
7. सत्यापन: सब-रजिस्ट्रार दस्तावेजों और भुगतान रसीदों का सत्यापन करता है।
8. दस्तावेज़ संग्रह: पंजीकृत दस्तावेज़ कुछ दिनों के बाद खरीदार को सौंप दिया जाता है या डाक द्वारा भेज दिया जाता है।

**Hinglish:**

2. Deed Drafting: Lawyer se Sale Deed draft karwayein correct value ke e-stamp paper par.

4. Appointment Booking: SRO (Sub-Registrar Office) portal par appointment fix karein.
5. Appearance: Buyer, seller aur 2 witnesses original ID ke sath SRO office visit karein.
6. Execution: Sabhi log deed par sign karein, aur biometrics (photos/thumb impressions) record karwayein.
7. Verification: Sub-registrar documents aur payment verify karega.

## Required Documents
- Original Draft of the Sale Deed/Conveyance Deed.
- Proof of Stamp Duty payment (e-Stamp/Challan).
- Identity Proof (Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID) of buyer, seller, and witnesses.
- Passport-size photographs of all parties.
- PAN Card (mandatory if property value exceeds ₹50 Lakhs) or Form 60.
- No Objection Certificate (NOC) if required (e.g., from housing society, development authority).
- Khata Certificate/Extract or Patta.
- Previous chain of registered property documents (Mother Deed).
- Encumbrance Certificate (EC).
- TDS payment challan (Form 26QB) if property value is over ₹50 Lakhs.

## Government Authorities
- **Sub-Registrar Office (SRO):** Primary authority for property registration and fee collection.
- **District Registrar:** Appellate authority if SRO refuses to register a document.
- **Inspector General of Registration (IGR):** State-level head of the registration department.
- **Revenue Department:** Updates mutation records post-registration.
- **Civil Courts:** Resolves title disputes and issues injunctions regarding property sales.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Delay in Registration:** If a document is not registered within 4 months, the Registrar may allow it with a penalty of up to 10 times the registration fee under Section 25 of the Registration Act.
- **Undervaluation (Section 47A Indian Stamp Act):** If property is registered below circle rate to evade tax, a notice is issued, deficit duty is recovered, and penalties up to twice the deficit amount may be imposed.
- **False Statements/Fraud:** Punishable under Section 82 of the Registration Act with imprisonment up to 7 years, or fine, or both. Forgery of valuable security (Section 336, 338 of BNS) attracts imprisonment up to 10 years or life.

## Exceptions
- Wills are not strictly required to be registered under Section 18 of the Registration Act, but registration is highly recommended.
- Sale of immovable property valued under ₹100 does not strictly require mandatory registration (though practically obsolete today).
- Property transferred via court decree or order, though updating records is still necessary.

## Important Timelines
- **Time to Register:** 4 months from the date of execution of the deed.
- **Grace Period (with penalty):** Additional 4 months (Total 8 months).
- **Sub-Registrar's decision on valuation:** Usually resolved within 30-90 days if referred to the Collector.
- **Mutation update:** Should ideally be applied for within 1-3 months post-registration.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Relying only on a Notarized Agreement to Sell or General Power of Attorney (GPA) for ownership transfer (Supreme Court strictly invalidated GPA sales in Suraj Lamp case).
- Registering at a value lower than the government circle rate to save stamp duty, inviting income tax notices and penalties.
- Failing to verify the entire chain of previous title documents (Mother Deeds).
- Skipping the mutation (Dakhil Kharij) process after registry.
- Ignoring the TDS deduction (1%) for properties over ₹50 Lakhs.

## Safety Tips
**English:** Always hire a competent property lawyer for a title search before buying. Ensure payment is made strictly via bank transfers to leave a clear financial trail. Verify the identity of the seller directly, not just through brokers.
**Hindi Unicode:** खरीदने से पहले शीर्षक खोज (टाइटल सर्च) के लिए हमेशा एक सक्षम संपत्ति वकील नियुक्त करें। सुनिश्चित करें कि स्पष्ट वित्तीय रिकॉर्ड के लिए भुगतान पूरी तरह से बैंक हस्तांतरण के माध्यम से किया गया हो। केवल दलालों के माध्यम से नहीं, बल्कि सीधे विक्रेता की पहचान सत्यापित करें।
**Hinglish:** Property kharidne se pehle title search ke liye achha lawyer hire karein. Payment hamesha cheque, RTGS ya NEFT se karein taaki record rahe. Broker ke bharose na rahkar, seller ki original ID aur details khud verify karein.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Is it mandatory to register a property?**
**English:** Yes, under Section 17 of the Registration Act, 1908, it is mandatory to register any sale of immovable property valued at ₹100 or more. Without registration, the title does not legally transfer to the buyer. Therefore, unregistered sale deeds hold no legal weight in court regarding ownership.
**Hindi Unicode:** हाँ, पंजीकरण अधिनियम, 1908 की धारा 17 के तहत ₹100 या उससे अधिक मूल्य की अचल संपत्ति की किसी भी बिक्री को पंजीकृत करना अनिवार्य है। पंजीकरण के बिना, शीर्षक कानूनी रूप से खरीदार को हस्तांतरित नहीं होता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, Registration Act ke Section 17 ke anusar property register karna compulsory hai. Bina registry ke, ownership legally buyer ko transfer nahi hoti. Isliye registry zaroori hai.

**Q2: Can I buy property on GPA (General Power of Attorney)?**
**English:** No, the Supreme Court of India in the Suraj Lamp Industries case ruled that a GPA is not a valid instrument to transfer property titles. You can only get legal title through a registered Sale Deed. A GPA only authorizes someone to act on your behalf, it does not transfer ownership.
**Hindi Unicode:** नहीं, भारत के सर्वोच्च न्यायालय ने सूरज लैंप मामले में फैसला दिया है कि जीपीए संपत्ति का शीर्षक हस्तांतरित करने का वैध साधन नहीं है। आप केवल पंजीकृत सेल डीड के माध्यम से ही कानूनी स्वामित्व प्राप्त कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, Supreme Court ke order ke baad GPA par property kharidna valid nahi hai. Ownership sirf registered sale deed se hi milti hai. GPA sirf kisi ko represent karne ka right deta hai.

**Q3: What happens if I miss the 4-month deadline to register?**
**English:** If you fail to register within four months of execution, you can apply to the Sub-Registrar for an extension of another four months. However, you will be liable to pay a penalty of up to 10 times the original registration fee. After 8 months, registration is generally not permitted without a court order.
**Hindi Unicode:** यदि आप चार महीने के भीतर पंजीकरण करने में विफल रहते हैं, तो आप अगले चार महीनों के विस्तार के लिए उप-पंजीयक को आवेदन कर सकते हैं। हालांकि, आपको मूल पंजीकरण शुल्क का 10 गुना तक जुर्माना देना होगा। 8 महीने के बाद इसे पंजीकृत करना मुश्किल है।
**Hinglish:** Agar aap 4 mahine mein registry nahi karwate, toh agle 4 mahine ke liye delay condone karne ki application de sakte hain. Par isme regular fee ka 10 guna tak penalty lag sakta hai. 8 mahine baad court jana pad sakta hai.

**Q4: Is the presence of both buyer and seller mandatory at the Sub-Registrar's office?**
**English:** Yes, both the buyer and the seller (or their legally authorized power of attorney holders) must be physically present at the Sub-Registrar's office. Their photographs and biometric fingerprints are taken during the process. This ensures that both parties consent to the transaction.
**Hindi Unicode:** हाँ, खरीदार और विक्रेता (या उनके अधिकृत पावर ऑफ अटॉर्नी धारक) दोनों का उप-पंजीयक कार्यालय में भौतिक रूप से उपस्थित होना अनिवार्य है। इस प्रक्रिया के दौरान उनकी तस्वीरें और बायोमेट्रिक उंगलियों के निशान लिए जाते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, buyer aur seller dono ka Sub-Registrar office mein personally present hona zaroori hai. Wahan unke photos aur biometrics (thumb impressions) liye jate hain taaki unki consent verify ho sake.

**Q5: What are the witnesses for, and who can be a witness?**
**English:** Two witnesses are required to attest that the buyer and seller signed the document in their presence. Any adult of sound mind with a valid government ID (like Aadhaar) can be a witness. It is preferable that they are not direct beneficiaries of the property.
**Hindi Unicode:** दो गवाहों की आवश्यकता होती है जो प्रमाणित करते हैं कि खरीदार और विक्रेता ने उनकी उपस्थिति में दस्तावेज़ पर हस्ताक्षर किए हैं। कोई भी वयस्क व्यक्ति जिसके पास वैध सरकारी आईडी हो, गवाह बन सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Do witnesses (gawah) chahiye hote hain jo confirm karte hain ki unke samne sign hue hain. Koi bhi adult jiske paas valid ID proof (Aadhaar) ho, witness ban sakta hai.

**Q6: What is a circle rate, and how does it affect registration?**
**English:** The circle rate (or ready reckoner rate) is the minimum property valuation set by the state government for a specific area. Stamp duty and registration fees must be paid on either the circle rate or the actual agreement value, whichever is higher. Registering below the circle rate is not allowed and attracts tax penalties.
**Hindi Unicode:** सर्किल रेट राज्य सरकार द्वारा किसी विशिष्ट क्षेत्र के लिए निर्धारित न्यूनतम संपत्ति मूल्यांकन है। स्टाम्प ड्यूटी का भुगतान सर्किल रेट या वास्तविक समझौते मूल्य (जो भी अधिक हो) पर किया जाना चाहिए। सर्किल रेट से कम पर पंजीकरण की अनुमति नहीं है।
**Hinglish:** Circle rate government dwara tay kiya gaya minimum property rate hai us area ka. Registry ka kharcha circle rate ya actual deal value (jo zyada ho) us par lagta hai. Circle rate se kam par registry nahi ho sakti.

**Q7: Can a property be registered in any Sub-Registrar office?**
**English:** Generally, a property must be registered at the Sub-Registrar office under whose jurisdiction the property is located. Some states offer the facility of 'Anywhere Registration' within the same district or state, but jurisdiction rules strictly apply in most regions.
**Hindi Unicode:** आमतौर पर, संपत्ति का पंजीकरण उस उप-पंजीयक कार्यालय में किया जाना चाहिए जिसके अधिकार क्षेत्र में संपत्ति स्थित है। कुछ राज्य एक ही जिले के भीतर 'कहीं भी पंजीकरण' की सुविधा प्रदान करते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Normally, property ussi Sub-Registrar office mein register hoti hai jiske jurisdiction mein woh area aata hai. Kuch states mein same district ke kisi bhi office mein registry ki suvidha (Anywhere Registration) di gayi hai.

**Q8: Do I need a lawyer for property registration?**
**English:** While the law does not strictly mandate hiring a lawyer, it is highly recommended. A lawyer conducts title verification, ensures the sale deed is drafted without legal loopholes, and navigates the complex procedural requirements at the Sub-Registrar's office, protecting you from future litigation.
**Hindi Unicode:** हालांकि कानून के अनुसार वकील नियुक्त करना अनिवार्य नहीं है, लेकिन इसकी अत्यधिक अनुशंसा की जाती है। एक वकील शीर्षक सत्यापन करता है, यह सुनिश्चित करता है कि सेल डीड कानूनी खामियों के बिना तैयार की गई है, और आपको भविष्य की मुकदमेबाजी से बचाता है।
**Hinglish:** Kanoon mein lawyer karna compulsory nahi hai, par ye highly recommended hai. Lawyer title clear hai ya nahi check karta hai, sahi Sale Deed draft karta hai aur future disputes se bachata hai.

**Q9: What happens if the seller refuses to register after taking the money?**
**English:** If the seller refuses to execute the registration after signing an Agreement to Sell and accepting consideration, the buyer can file a civil suit for "Specific Performance of Contract" in a civil court. The court can compel the seller to register the property or issue a decree to register it.
**Hindi Unicode:** यदि विक्रेता पैसे लेने के बाद पंजीकरण करने से इनकार करता है, तो खरीदार सिविल कोर्ट में "विशिष्ट प्रदर्शन" (Specific Performance) के लिए मुकदमा दायर कर सकता है। अदालत विक्रेता को संपत्ति पंजीकृत करने के लिए मजबूर कर सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Agar seller paise lekar registry karne se mana kare, toh buyer civil court mein "Specific Performance" ka case daal sakta hai. Court seller ko registry karne ke liye order de sakti hai.

**Q10: What is mutation, and is it the same as registration?**
**English:** No, registration is the transfer of title via the Sub-Registrar, while mutation (Dakhil Kharij) is the updating of the new owner's name in the local municipal or revenue records for property tax purposes. Mutation must be done after registration, as registration alone does not automatically update municipal tax records.
**Hindi Unicode:** नहीं, पंजीकरण सब-रजिस्ट्रार के माध्यम से शीर्षक का हस्तांतरण है, जबकि म्यूटेशन (दाखिल-खारिज) संपत्ति कर उद्देश्यों के लिए स्थानीय नगरपालिका या राजस्व रिकॉर्ड में नए मालिक के नाम को अपडेट करना है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, registry aur mutation alag hain. Registry se title transfer hota hai. Mutation (Dakhil Kharij) ka matlab hai Nagar Nigam ya Tehsil ke records mein apna naam update karwana taaki property tax aapke naam se aaye.

**Q11: Can a registered sale deed be cancelled?**
**English:** Yes, a registered sale deed can be cancelled, but usually only by an order of a competent Civil Court under the Specific Relief Act, 1963, if it was executed under fraud, coercion, or misrepresentation. The parties can also mutually execute a 'Cancellation Deed' and register it.
**Hindi Unicode:** हाँ, एक पंजीकृत सेल डीड को रद्द किया जा सकता है, लेकिन आमतौर पर केवल सक्षम सिविल कोर्ट के आदेश द्वारा यदि इसे धोखाधड़ी या जबरदस्ती के तहत निष्पादित किया गया हो। दोनों पक्ष आपसी सहमति से 'कैंसिलेशन डीड' भी बना सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, sale deed cancel ho sakti hai par aamtaur par Court ke order se agar fraud ya dabav saabit ho. Ya fir dono parties aapas mein agree karke 'Cancellation Deed' register karwa sakti hain.

**Q12: How long does the Sub-Registrar keep the original document?**
**English:** After the registration process is complete, scanning, and uploading into the digital repository, the original registered document is returned to the buyer. This process generally takes anywhere from the same day to a few weeks depending on the state.
**Hindi Unicode:** पंजीकरण प्रक्रिया पूरी होने, स्कैनिंग और डिजिटल रिपोजिटरी में अपलोड होने के बाद, मूल पंजीकृत दस्तावेज़ खरीदार को वापस कर दिया जाता है। इस प्रक्रिया में आमतौर पर कुछ दिन लगते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Registry ka process, scanning aur uploading poora hone ke baad original document buyer ko wapas mil jata hai. State ke hisaab se isme same day se lekar kuch hafton ka time lag sakta hai.

**Q13: What is e-stamping?**
**English:** E-stamping is a computer-based application and a secured electronic way of stamping non-judicial documents and paying stamp duty to the government. It has replaced the traditional physical stamp papers in most states to prevent counterfeiting and ensure transparency.
**Hindi Unicode:** ई-स्टैम्पिंग एक कंप्यूटर-आधारित एप्लिकेशन है और गैर-न्यायिक दस्तावेजों पर मुहर लगाने तथा सरकार को स्टाम्प शुल्क का भुगतान करने का एक सुरक्षित इलेक्ट्रॉनिक तरीका है। इसने पारंपरिक भौतिक स्टाम्प पेपर की जगह ले ली है।
**Hinglish:** E-stamping ek digital aur secure tareeka hai stamp duty pay karne ka. Isne purane physical stamp papers ko replace kar diya hai taaki nakli stamp paper ka fraud roka ja sake.

**Q14: Is it necessary to register a gift deed?**
**English:** Yes, according to Section 17 of the Registration Act, 1908, and Section 123 of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882, the gift of immovable property must be registered to be valid. Unregistered gift deeds hold no legal validity. Stamp duty is often lower for gifts among blood relatives.
**Hindi Unicode:** हाँ, अचल संपत्ति का उपहार वैध होने के लिए पंजीकृत होना चाहिए। अपंजीकृत गिफ्ट डीड की कोई कानूनी वैधता नहीं होती है। रक्त संबंधियों के बीच उपहारों के लिए स्टाम्प शुल्क अक्सर कम होता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, immovable property ka Gift Deed register karna compulsory hai. Bina registry ke gift legal nahi maana jata. Khoon ke rishton (blood relatives) mein gift deed par stamp duty kam lagti hai.

**Q15: Can an NRI register property in India without being present?**
**English:** Yes, a Non-Resident Indian (NRI) can buy or sell property in India without being physically present by issuing a valid registered or notarized Power of Attorney (PoA) to a trusted person in India. This PoA must be authenticated by the Indian Embassy or Consulate in their country of residence.
**Hindi Unicode:** हाँ, एक अनिवासी भारतीय (एनआरआई) भारत में किसी विश्वसनीय व्यक्ति को वैध पंजीकृत या नोटरीकृत पावर ऑफ अटॉर्नी (पीओए) जारी करके शारीरिक रूप से उपस्थित हुए बिना संपत्ति खरीद या बेच सकता है। इसे भारतीय दूतावास द्वारा प्रमाणित किया जाना चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Haan, NRI bina India aaye property kharid ya bech sakte hain. Unhe apne desh mein Indian Embassy se verify karwakar ek Power of Attorney (PoA) India mein kisi trusted person ko bhejni hoti hai.

**Q16: Are registration charges different for men and women?**
**English:** In many Indian states (like Delhi, Haryana, UP, and Rajasthan), the stamp duty is slightly lower (typically by 1% to 2%) if the property is registered in the name of a female buyer or jointly with a female. The registration fee is usually the same (1%) for everyone.
**Hindi Unicode:** कई भारतीय राज्यों में, यदि संपत्ति महिला खरीदार के नाम पर या महिला के साथ संयुक्त रूप से पंजीकृत है, तो स्टाम्प ड्यूटी थोड़ी कम होती है (आमतौर पर 1% से 2% तक)।
**Hinglish:** Bahut se states mein (jaise Delhi, Haryana, UP), agar property aurat ke naam par ya joint name par register hoti hai, toh stamp duty 1-2% kam lagti hai. Par registration fee aamtaur par 1% hi rehti hai.

**Q17: Does the Sub-Registrar verify the authenticity of the title?**
**English:** No, the Sub-Registrar's duty is primarily to verify whether the legal formalities of registration (stamp duty, ID proofs, format) are met. They do not verify if the seller is the absolute legal owner. Title verification is strictly the buyer's responsibility (caveat emptor - buyer beware).
**Hindi Unicode:** नहीं, सब-रजिस्ट्रार का कर्तव्य मुख्य रूप से यह सत्यापित करना है कि पंजीकरण की कानूनी औपचारिकताएं पूरी हो गई हैं या नहीं। वे यह सत्यापित नहीं करते हैं कि विक्रेता पूर्ण कानूनी मालिक है या नहीं। यह खरीदार की जिम्मेदारी है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, Sub-Registrar sirf ye check karta hai ki stamp duty aur ID proof sahi hain ya nahi. Woh title verify nahi karta. Title clear hai ya nahi, ye check karna puri tarah buyer ki zimmedari hai.

**Q18: What is an Encumbrance Certificate (EC)?**
**English:** An Encumbrance Certificate is an official document that records all registered transactions related to a property over a specified period. It serves as evidence that the property is free from legal or financial dues, such as pending mortgages or uncleared loans.
**Hindi Unicode:** एन्कम्ब्रेंस सर्टिफिकेट एक आधिकारिक दस्तावेज़ है जो एक निर्दिष्ट अवधि में संपत्ति से संबंधित सभी पंजीकृत लेनदेन को रिकॉर्ड करता है। यह इस बात का प्रमाण है कि संपत्ति कानूनी या वित्तीय बकाया से मुक्त है।
**Hinglish:** Encumbrance Certificate (EC) ek document hai jo dikhata hai ki pichle kuch saalon mein property par koi loan, mortgage ya dispute toh nahi hai. Property clear hone ka ye bada proof hai.

**Q19: Can properties be registered entirely online?**
**English:** While many states have digitized the pre-registration steps like calculating stamp duty, paying fees online, and booking appointments, the final physical presence of all parties before the Sub-Registrar is still mandatory for biometrics and signatures. End-to-end online registration without physical visits is largely unavailable.
**Hindi Unicode:** जबकि कई राज्यों ने स्टाम्प शुल्क की गणना और ऑनलाइन भुगतान जैसे पूर्व-पंजीकरण चरणों को डिजिटल कर दिया है, बायोमेट्रिक्स और हस्ताक्षर के लिए सब-रजिस्ट्रार के समक्ष सभी पक्षों की अंतिम भौतिक उपस्थिति अभी भी अनिवार्य है।
**Hinglish:** Halanki stamp duty pay karna aur appointment lena online ho gaya hai, par final registry ke liye Sub-Registrar office mein physically jaakar biometrics aur sign karna abhi bhi compulsory hai. Puri tarah online registry nahi hoti.

**Q20: What is the rule of TDS on property purchase?**
**English:** Under Section 194-IA of the Income Tax Act, if the transaction value of the property is ₹50 Lakhs or more, the buyer must deduct 1% (or 0.75% as per periodic amendments) TDS from the payment to the seller and deposit it using Form 26QB. The Sub-Registrar checks this challan during registration.
**Hindi Unicode:** आयकर अधिनियम की धारा 194-आईए के तहत, यदि संपत्ति का लेनदेन मूल्य ₹50 लाख या उससे अधिक है, तो खरीदार को विक्रेता को भुगतान से 1% टीडीएस काटना होगा और इसे फॉर्म 26QB का उपयोग करके जमा करना होगा।
**Hinglish:** Agar property ki value 50 Lakh se zyada hai, toh buyer ko payment karte waqt 1% TDS kaat kar Income Tax department mein jama karna padta hai (Form 26QB). Registry ke waqt iska challan dikhana zaroori hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts

**Myth 1:** An unregistered sale agreement printed on a ₹500 stamp paper is enough to prove ownership.
**Fact:** No, an agreement to sell does not transfer ownership. Only a fully registered Sale Deed gives you absolute ownership rights.

**Myth 2:** GPA (General Power of Attorney) means I am the owner.
**Fact:** The Supreme Court has clearly stated that GPA transfers no ownership. It only authorizes someone to act on your behalf.

**Myth 3:** Paying property tax means I am the legal owner of the property.
**Fact:** Property tax payment and mutation records are for revenue purposes. They do not constitute legal title. Only a registered deed provides legal title.

**Myth 4:** The Sub-Registrar thoroughly checks the past history of the property to ensure no fraud happens.
**Fact:** The Sub-Registrar only checks current document formalities. Verifying the historical title is the buyer's duty.

**Myth 5:** I can declare the property value as ₹10 Lakhs to save stamp duty, even if I bought it for ₹50 Lakhs.
**Fact:** Registering a property below the government's circle rate is illegal and will lead to heavy penalties and income tax notices.

**Myth 6:** Witnesses to a registry also become part-owners of the property.
**Fact:** Witnesses only testify that the documents were signed in their presence. They have no rights or claims over the property.

**Myth 7:** Online stamp duty payment completes the registration process.
**Fact:** Online payment is just step one. Physical presence, biometrics, and signatures at the SRO are mandatory to complete registration.

**Myth 8:** An Encumbrance Certificate completely guarantees that the property has no issues.
**Fact:** An EC only shows *registered* transactions. It will not reflect unregistered agreements, pending civil court cases, or oral disputes.

**Myth 9:** A Will MUST be registered for it to be valid.
**Fact:** Under Section 18 of the Registration Act, registration of a Will is optional, though it is strongly recommended to prevent disputes.

**Myth 10:** Once registered, a Sale Deed cannot be challenged by anyone.
**Fact:** A registered deed can be challenged in a civil court if it involves fraud, coercion, impersonation, or lacks rightful legal title.

## Conversation Examples

**Conversation 1:**
*Citizen:* Sir, main property kharid raha hoon. Registry ke liye SRO office kab jana hoga?
*Assistant:* Property ki Sale Deed banne (execution) ke 4 mahine ke andar aapko Sub-Registrar Office (SRO) jana hoga. Appointment online book kiya ja sakta hai aur saare parties ko present hona hoga.

**Conversation 2:**
*Citizen:* Can I register the property in any SRO in the city?
*Assistant:* Generally, no. You must register it at the specific Sub-Registrar office that has jurisdiction over the area where your property is located, unless your state allows 'Anywhere Registration' within the district.

**Conversation 3:**
*Citizen:* Mere chacha ne mujhe ek plot gift kiya hai. Kya iski registry karani padegi?
*Assistant:* Ji haan, Immovable property ka gift tabhi valid hota hai jab Gift Deed registered ho. Aapko apne area ke Sub-Registrar office mein stamp duty dekar ise register karwana hoga.

**Conversation 4:**
*Citizen:* What happens if I bought a house on Power of Attorney 10 years ago?
*Assistant:* As per the Supreme Court ruling, PoA does not transfer legal title. You do not hold absolute legal ownership. It is highly recommended to execute and register a proper Sale Deed from the original owner if possible.

**Conversation 5:**
*Citizen:* Registry ke liye witnesses kon ho sakte hain?
*Assistant:* Koi bhi do adult vyakti jinke paas valid ID (jaise Aadhaar) ho, witness ban sakte hain. Behtar hoga ki wo aapke dost ya rishtedar hon jinhe transaction ke baare mein pata ho.

**Conversation 6:**
*Citizen:* Mujhe stamp duty kitni pay karni hogi?
*Assistant:* Stamp duty state government tay karti hai, jo aamtaur par property value ka 4% se 7% ke beech hoti hai. Iske alawa 1% registration fee bhi lagti hai. Ye amount circle rate ya deal value, jo zyada ho, uspe calculate hoti hai.

**Conversation 7:**
*Citizen:* What documents should I bring to the Registrar's office?
*Assistant:* Bring the original drafted deed, e-stamp papers/receipts, original ID proofs (Aadhaar/PAN) of all parties, photos, EC, and previous chain of documents. If the property is over ₹50L, bring the TDS challan (Form 26QB).

**Conversation 8:**
*Citizen:* Kya registry ke time mere wife ke naam par concession milega?
*Assistant:* Bahut se states (jaise UP, Delhi) mein agar registry female ke naam par hoti hai, toh stamp duty mein 1-2% ki chhoot milti hai.

**Conversation 9:**
*Citizen:* Maine registry karwa li hai. Ab kya karna hoga?
*Assistant:* Registry ke baad aapko 'Mutation' (Dakhil-Kharij) ke liye apply karna hoga Nagar Nigam ya Tehsil mein, taaki property tax records mein aapka naam update ho jaye.

**Conversation 10:**
*Citizen:* If the seller is an NRI, how will they sign at the SRO?
*Assistant:* The NRI seller can issue a legally valid Power of Attorney (attested by the Indian Embassy in their country) to a trusted person in India. That person can then appear at the SRO and sign on their behalf.

**Conversation 11:**
*Citizen:* Kya unregistered sale agreement ki court mein koi value hai?
*Assistant:* Unregistered sale agreement se ownership transfer nahi hoti, lekin ise contract ke proof ke roop mein istemaal karke aap 'Specific Performance' ka case daal sakte hain taaki seller ko registry karne ke liye force kiya ja sake.

**Conversation 12:**
*Citizen:* Property under-valuation ka notice kya hota hai?
*Assistant:* Agar aapne registry government ke circle rate se kam par karwai hai, toh Sub-Registrar notice bhejkar bachi hui stamp duty aur penalty mang sakta hai.

**Conversation 13:**
*Citizen:* Does the Sub-Registrar check if the seller is a fraud?
*Assistant:* No, the Sub-Registrar's job is only to check documentation and tax payment. Verifying if the seller is the genuine owner and not a fraud is strictly your (the buyer's) responsibility. Hire a lawyer for a title search.

**Conversation 14:**
*Citizen:* Mere builder ne registry karne se mana kar diya, kya karun?
*Assistant:* Aap pehle builder ko legal notice bhejein. Agar wo na mane, toh aap RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority) ya Consumer Court mein complaint file kar sakte hain, ya Civil Court mein Specific Performance suit daal sakte hain.

**Conversation 15:**
*Citizen:* Is PAN card mandatory for registration?
*Assistant:* Yes, quoting the PAN card is mandatory for any immovable property transaction exceeding ₹50 Lakhs. If you don't have a PAN, you must submit Form 60.
