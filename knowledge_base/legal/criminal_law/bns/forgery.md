# Forgery (Jalsazi) under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS)

## Overview

**English:** Forgery is the act of creating, altering, or executing a false document or electronic record with the intent to deceive, commit fraud, or cause injury to the public or any person. Under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, forgery is a serious criminal offense designed to protect the integrity and authenticity of documents and property marks, ensuring trust in legal and commercial transactions.

## Definition

**English:** According to Section 336 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, whoever makes any false document or false electronic record, or part of a document or electronic record, with intent to cause damage or injury to the public or to any person, or to support any claim or title, or to cause any person to part with property, or to enter into any express or implied contract, or with intent to commit fraud or that fraud may be committed, commits forgery.

## Legal Background

The legal framework governing forgery has been updated and streamlined under the new criminal laws of India. 

- **Primary Act:** Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023.
- **Procedural Law:** Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023 (replaces the CrPC).
- **Evidentiary Law:** Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023 (replaces the Indian Evidence Act, focusing strongly on the admissibility of electronic and digital records).

**Relevant Sections under BNS (Chapter XVIII - Offences Relating to Documents and to Property Marks):**
- **Section 336:** Defines forgery and prescribes the general punishment (imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both).
- **Section 337:** Making a false document (explains what constitutes making a false physical or electronic document).
- **Section 338:** Forgery of record of Court or of public register, etc. (Punishable up to 7 years).
- **Section 339:** Forgery of valuable security, will, etc. (Punishable with imprisonment for life, or up to 10 years, and fine).
- **Section 340:** Forgery for purpose of cheating. (Punishable up to 7 years and fine).
- **Section 341:** Forgery for purpose of harming reputation. (Punishable up to 3 years and fine).
- **Section 342:** Forged document or electronic record (Defines a forged document).
- **Section 343:** Using as genuine a forged document or electronic record. (Punishable exactly as if the person had forged it).

---

## Applicability

- **Who is covered:** Any citizen of India or foreigner within Indian territory who commits forgery. It also applies to offenses committed outside India targeting Indian citizens or systems (e.g., cyber forgery of Indian banking records).
- **Who is exempt:** Honest mistakes without 'mens rea' (criminal intent or intent to defraud) do not constitute forgery. A person who signs a document by genuine mistake, without intending to deceive anyone, cannot be held liable for forgery.
- **Special Conditions:** Forgery requires clear proof of fraudulent intention. Just altering a document for correction, with the consent of all parties, is not forgery.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English:**
1. **Filing Complaint:** Submit a written complaint to the local Police Station or Economic Offences Wing (EOW) detailing the forgery. Attach copies of the original and forged documents if available.
2. **FIR Registration:** Police register an FIR under Section 336, 339, 340, etc., of BNS depending on the gravity.
3. **Investigation:** Police collect evidence under BNSS provisions, including sending the documents to the Forensic Science Laboratory (FSL) for handwriting and signature analysis.
4. **Chargesheet:** The police file a chargesheet before the Magistrate.
5. **Trial:** The court examines the forensic reports, witnesses, and electronic evidence (under BSA 2023). 
6. **Judgment:** If proven guilty beyond a reasonable doubt, the court awards punishment and may cancel the validity of the forged document.

## Penalties & Punishments

The punishments under BNS 2023 for forgery-related offenses are strictly categorized:
- **General Forgery (Sec 336 BNS):** Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.
- **Forgery of Court Records or Public Register (Sec 338 BNS):** Imprisonment up to 7 years, and shall also be liable to fine.
- **Forgery of Valuable Security, Will, etc. (Sec 339 BNS):** Imprisonment for life, or imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to 10 years, and shall also be liable to fine.
- **Forgery for Purpose of Cheating (Sec 340 BNS):** Imprisonment up to 7 years, and shall also be liable to fine.
- **Using a Forged Document as Genuine (Sec 343 BNS):** Punished in the same manner as if the offender had forged such document.

---

## Exceptions
- **Lack of Criminal Intent (Mens Rea):** Altering a document with the honest belief that one has the authority to do so, without intending to defraud or cause harm, is not forgery.
- **Consent:** Changes made to a contract or document with the mutual consent and knowledge of all involved parties do not constitute forgery.
- **Clerical Errors:** A genuine clerical or typographical error made without deceitful intent is excluded from criminal liability.

---

## Important Timelines
- **Reporting:** Must be reported immediately upon discovery. Delay in lodging the FIR can weaken the case, and the delay must be satisfactorily explained in court.
- **Limitation Period:** As per BNSS, offenses punishable with imprisonment for a term exceeding 3 years (like forgery of wills or for cheating) generally do not have a strict limitation period for taking cognizance, but undue delay is frowned upon. Offenses punishable up to 3 years have a limitation of 3 years.
- **Forensic Reports:** Usually take 3 to 6 months depending on the workload of the State FSL, which can delay the filing of the final chargesheet.

---

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. **Failing to preserve the original document:** Handing over the only original copy of the authentic document to untrusted parties.
2. **Delay in taking Civil Action:** Relying solely on criminal proceedings (FIR) while failing to file a civil suit to get a stay on the property or transaction.
3. **Using forged documents unknowingly:** Presenting a document to a court or authority without verifying it, which might lead to charges under Section 343 BNS (using forged document as genuine).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What is the primary BNS section for forgery?**
*English:* The primary section for forgery under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) is Section 336. This replaces the old IPC Section 463/465. It defines the offense and prescribes a base punishment of up to 2 years imprisonment.
*Hindi:* भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS) के तहत जालसाज़ी के लिए प्राथमिक धारा 336 है। यह पुरानी IPC धारा 463/465 की जगह लेती है।
*Hinglish:* BNS mein forgery ka main section 336 hai. Ye purane IPC Sec 463/465 ko replace karta hai. Isme 2 saal tak ki saza ho sakti hai.

**Q2: Someone forged my signature on a property will. What is the punishment?**
*English:* Forging a will or a valuable security is a very serious crime covered under Section 339 of the BNS. It is punishable with life imprisonment or up to 10 years of imprisonment, along with a fine.
*Hindi:* वसीयत या मूल्यवान प्रतिभूति की जालसाज़ी BNS की धारा 339 के तहत एक बहुत गंभीर अपराध है। इसमें आजीवन कारावास या 10 साल तक की जेल हो सकती है।
*Hinglish:* Property will forge karna BNS Section 339 ke under ek serious crime hai. Isme life imprisonment ya 10 saal tak ki saza aur fine lag sakta hai.

**Q3: Can a person be punished for just keeping a forged document?**
*English:* Merely possessing a forged document without the intent to use it or without knowing it is forged does not automatically constitute a crime. However, if it is possessed with the intent to use it as genuine, it attracts criminal liability under Section 343 BNS.
*Hindi:* केवल जाली दस्तावेज़ रखना, यदि उसे इस्तेमाल करने का इरादा न हो, अपराध नहीं है। लेकिन असली के रूप में उपयोग करने के इरादे से रखना BNS 343 के तहत अपराध है।
*Hinglish:* Agar aapko pata nahi hai ki document fake hai aur aapne rakha hua hai, toh wo crime nahi hai. Par fraud karne ke intent se rakhna BNS 343 ke under punishable hai.

**Q4: Is forgery a bailable or non-bailable offense?**
*English:* Simple forgery under Section 336 BNS is generally bailable. However, aggravated forms of forgery, such as forgery of a valuable security (Sec 339) or forgery for cheating (Sec 340), are non-bailable and cognizable.
*Hindi:* BNS की धारा 336 के तहत सामान्य जालसाज़ी जमानती है। लेकिन वसीयत या धोखाधड़ी के लिए जालसाज़ी (धारा 339, 340) गैर-जमानती अपराध हैं।
*Hinglish:* Simple forgery (Sec 336) bailable hoti hai. Lekin property papers ya cheating ke liye forgery (Sec 339, 340) non-bailable aur serious offenses hain.

**Q5: How is a digital or electronic forgery treated under BNS?**
*English:* BNS treats electronic records exactly like physical documents. Creating a false electronic record, hacking to alter data, or faking an email for fraud is heavily penalized under BNS combined with the IT Act, 2000. Evidence is presented under the BSA 2023.
*Hindi:* BNS इलेक्ट्रॉनिक रिकॉर्ड को भौतिक दस्तावेज़ों के समान ही मानता है। झूठा इलेक्ट्रॉनिक रिकॉर्ड बनाना भी जालसाज़ी है।
*Hinglish:* BNS mein digital records aur physical documents barabar mane jate hain. Fake emails ya edited PDFs banana bhi forgery hai aur isme BNS aur IT Act dono lagte hain.

**Q6: What is the difference between cheating and forgery?**
*English:* Cheating (BNS Sec 318) involves deceiving someone to deliver property or do an act. Forgery (BNS Sec 336) is specifically the physical or digital act of creating a false document. If a forged document is used to cheat, both charges apply.
*Hindi:* धोखाधड़ी (BNS 318) किसी को धोखा देकर संपत्ति प्राप्त करना है। जालसाज़ी (BNS 336) झूठा दस्तावेज़ बनाने का कार्य है।
*Hinglish:* Cheating (dhokhadhadi) ka matlab kisi ko bewakoof banakar fayda uthana hai. Forgery ka matlab fake document banana hai. Fake document se fraud karne par dono charges lagte hain.

**Q7: Can a company be charged with forgery?**
*English:* Yes, if officers of a company generate forged invoices or fake balance sheets, the individuals responsible (Directors, Managers) will be criminally prosecuted under BNS. The company itself cannot be imprisoned but can face heavy fines.
*Hindi:* हां, यदि किसी कंपनी के अधिकारी जाली चालान या फर्जी बैलेंस शीट बनाते हैं, तो जिम्मेदार व्यक्तियों पर BNS के तहत मुकदमा चलाया जाएगा।
*Hinglish:* Haan, agar company ke directors fake invoices ya balance sheet banate hain, toh unpar BNS ke under criminal case chalega.

**Q8: What should I do if a fake bank account is opened in my name?**
*English:* Immediately inform the bank in writing to freeze the account. File a complaint with the cyber police or local police station regarding identity theft and forgery under BNS and the IT Act.
*Hindi:* तुरंत बैंक को खाता फ्रीज करने के लिए लिखित में सूचित करें। पहचान की चोरी और जालसाज़ी के संबंध में साइबर पुलिस में शिकायत दर्ज करें।
*Hinglish:* Bank ko turant written application de kar account freeze karwayein. Phir police ya cyber cell mein fake ID/forgery aur identity theft ki FIR file karein.

**Q9: Does modifying a valid contract without the other party's knowledge count as forgery?**
*English:* Yes. Altering a material part of a document without lawful authority or consent after it has been executed constitutes making a false document under Section 337 of the BNS.
*Hindi:* हां। निष्पादित होने के बाद कानूनी अधिकार या सहमति के बिना किसी दस्तावेज़ के एक महत्वपूर्ण हिस्से को बदलना BNS की धारा 337 के तहत झूठा दस्तावेज़ बनाना है।
*Hinglish:* Haan bilkul. Agar agreement sign hone ke baad aap bina dusri party ke permission ke usme changes karte hain, toh wo BNS Section 337 ke under forgery maana jayega.

**Q10: How can I prove that my signature was forged?**
*English:* You can request the court to send the document to a handwriting expert at the Forensic Science Laboratory (FSL). The expert will compare the forged signature with your admitted or specimen signatures.
*Hindi:* आप अदालत से दस्तावेज़ को फोरेंसिक साइंस लेबोरेटरी (FSL) के हस्तलेखन विशेषज्ञ के पास भेजने का अनुरोध कर सकते हैं।
*Hinglish:* Aap court mein handwriting expert aur FSL lab testing ki demand kar sakte hain. Expert aapke original aur fake signature ko match karke report dega.

**Q11: Can a police officer refuse to file an FIR for forgery?**
*English:* Since aggravated forgery is a cognizable offense, the police are bound to register an FIR under BNSS. If they refuse, you can approach the Superintendent of Police (SP) or file a private complaint before a Magistrate.
*Hindi:* चूंकि गंभीर जालसाज़ी एक संज्ञेय अपराध है, इसलिए पुलिस FIR दर्ज करने के लिए बाध्य है। मना करने पर आप SP से संपर्क कर सकते हैं या मजिस्ट्रेट के समक्ष शिकायत कर सकते हैं।
*Hinglish:* Serious forgery ek cognizable offense hai aur police FIR file karne se mana nahi kar sakti. Agar kare, toh aap SP office ja sakte hain ya Magistrate court mein private complaint file kar sakte hain.

**Q12: What is the punishment for forging a medical certificate?**
*English:* Forging a medical certificate to gain undue advantage or deceive authorities falls under standard forgery (Section 336 BNS) or forgery for cheating (Section 340 BNS), carrying penalties from 2 to 7 years.
*Hindi:* अनुचित लाभ प्राप्त करने या अधिकारियों को धोखा देने के लिए मेडिकल सर्टिफिकेट में जालसाज़ी करना BNS 336 या 340 के अंतर्गत आता है, जिसमें 2 से 7 साल तक की सजा हो सकती है।
*Hinglish:* Fake medical certificate banakar fayda lena BNS Section 336 ya 340 ke under aata hai. Isme 2 se 7 saal tak ki jail ho sakti hai.

**Q13: Is altering an educational degree considered forgery?**
*English:* Absolutely. Tampering with mark sheets or degree certificates to secure a job or admission is a serious offense under Section 340 BNS (forgery for cheating).
*Hindi:* बिल्कुल। नौकरी या प्रवेश पाने के लिए मार्कशीट या डिग्री प्रमाण पत्र के साथ छेड़छाड़ करना BNS की धारा 340 के तहत एक गंभीर अपराध है।
*Hinglish:* Haan. Job ya admission pane ke liye marksheets ya degree mein her-pher karna cheating aur forgery (BNS Sec 340) ka serious crime hai.

**Q14: Are digital signatures susceptible to forgery?**
*English:* While highly secure, if someone steals your DSC token and password to sign documents unauthorizedly, it constitutes electronic forgery and identity theft.
*Hindi:* हालांकि यह अत्यधिक सुरक्षित है, यदि कोई अनधिकृत रूप से दस्तावेज़ों पर हस्ताक्षर करने के लिए आपका DSC टोकन और पासवर्ड चुराता है, तो यह इलेक्ट्रॉनिक जालसाज़ी है।
*Hinglish:* Wese toh DSC secure hota hai, par agar koi aapka DSC token aur password chura kar use kar le, toh wo bhi electronic forgery aur fraud maana jata hai.

**Q15: Can civil and criminal cases run simultaneously for a forged property deed?**
*English:* Yes. A criminal case determines the punishment for the fraudster, while a civil suit is necessary to cancel the forged deed and reclaim the property rights. Both can run simultaneously.
*Hindi:* हां। एक आपराधिक मामला धोखेबाज़ के लिए सजा निर्धारित करता है, जबकि जाली डीड को रद्द करने और संपत्ति के अधिकारों को पुनः प्राप्त करने के लिए एक सिविल मुकदमा आवश्यक है।
*Hinglish:* Haan bilkul. Criminal case fraud karne wale ko jail bhejega, aur civil case us fake property paper ko cancel karke property aapko wapas dilwayega. Dono sath chal sakte hain.

**Q16: Can I withdraw a forgery case if we settle out of court?**
*English:* Simple forgery might be compoundable with the court's permission, but serious forgery cases (like forging a will or valuable security under Sec 339) are generally non-compoundable, meaning they cannot be settled and withdrawn easily without the High Court's intervention.
*Hindi:* साधारण जालसाज़ी में अदालत की अनुमति से समझौता हो सकता है, लेकिन गंभीर जालसाज़ी के मामले आम तौर पर गैर-समझौता योग्य (non-compoundable) होते हैं।
*Hinglish:* Choti forgery mein court ki permission se settlement ho sakta hai. Par property ya will forge karne jaise bade cases non-compoundable hote hain, inme asani se case wapas nahi hota.

**Q17: Does signing on someone else's behalf count as forgery?**
*English:* If you sign on someone's behalf with a valid Power of Attorney (PoA) and indicate that you are signing as an agent, it is legal. Signing their actual name without authorization to deceive is forgery.
*Hindi:* यदि आप वैध पावर ऑफ अटॉर्नी के साथ किसी की ओर से हस्ताक्षर करते हैं, तो यह कानूनी है। धोखा देने के लिए बिना अधिकार के उनके नाम से हस्ताक्षर करना जालसाज़ी है।
*Hinglish:* Agar aapke paas legal Power of Attorney hai aur aap agent bankar sign karte hain toh theek hai. Par bina permission ke kisi ka direct signature copy karna forgery hai.

**Q18: What is 'making a false document' under BNS 337?**
*English:* It refers to dishonestly making, signing, sealing, or executing a document, altering a document after execution, or obtaining a signature through deception or from a person of unsound mind.
*Hindi:* यह बेईमानी से किसी दस्तावेज़ को बनाने, हस्ताक्षर करने या निष्पादित करने, या निष्पादन के बाद किसी दस्तावेज़ को बदलने को संदर्भित करता है।
*Hinglish:* BNS 337 ka matlab hai beimaani se koi document banana, sign karna, ya kisi pagal ya nashe mein dhut insaan se dhokhe se sign karwana.

**Q19: How are photocopy forgeries treated?**
*English:* Presenting a manipulated or forged photocopy as a genuine copy of an original is considered using a forged document (Sec 343 BNS) and is fully punishable.
*Hindi:* हेरफेर की गई या जाली फोटोकॉपी को मूल की वास्तविक प्रति के रूप में प्रस्तुत करना जाली दस्तावेज़ का उपयोग करना माना जाता है।
*Hinglish:* Agar aap original document ki bajaye fake photocopy banakar usko asli batakar pesh karte hain, toh wo bhi Sec 343 BNS ke under crime hai.

**Q20: Does forgery apply to currency notes?**
*English:* Counterfeiting currency notes is treated as a separate, far more severe offense under the BNS, strictly dealing with offenses against the State and economy, carrying much harsher, often life, sentences.
*Hindi:* नकली मुद्रा नोटों को BNS के तहत एक अलग और कहीं अधिक गंभीर अपराध माना जाता है, जिसमें कठोर सजा होती है।
*Hinglish:* Fake notes ya nakli note chhapna forgery se alag, ek bahut bada crime maana jata hai, jisme life imprisonment tak ki saza hoti hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts

**Myth 1:** Forgery only applies to physical paper documents.
**Fact:** Under BNS and BSA, electronic records, emails, digital signatures, and modified PDFs are fully recognized as documents. Digital forgery is severely punished.

**Myth 2:** If I didn't successfully cheat anyone, a fake document isn't a crime.
**Fact:** The mere act of "making a false document" with the *intent* to commit fraud completes the offense of forgery under BNS Section 336, regardless of success.

**Myth 3:** Signing a document using a different style of your own signature is forgery.
**Fact:** As long as it is your signature and there is no intent to defraud, varying your signature style is not forgery.

**Myth 4:** Forgery cases are only fought in Civil Courts.
**Fact:** Forgery is a severe criminal offense fought in Magistrate and Sessions Courts. Civil courts are only involved to get stay orders or nullify the effect of the forged document.

**Myth 5:** If a document is unregistered, its forgery is not a crime.
**Fact:** Even forging an unregistered agreement, letter, or receipt is a crime. Registration status does not change the criminal nature of creating a false document.

**Myth 6:** Only the person who created the fake document can be punished.
**Fact:** Section 343 BNS penalizes anyone who *uses* a forged document knowing it to be fake, with the exact same punishment as the creator.

**Myth 7:** Police will immediately arrest anyone accused of forgery without investigation.
**Fact:** As per BNSS guidelines, police must conduct a preliminary inquiry, especially in cases where the punishment is less than 7 years, before making an arrest.

**Myth 8:** You cannot prove a signature is forged if there are no witnesses.
**Fact:** Witness testimony is not mandatory. Expert forensic opinion on handwriting and forensic ink age analysis under the BSA is highly admissible and sufficient for conviction.

**Myth 9:** Taking a printout of a digital signature and pasting it on a paper is legal.
**Fact:** Unauthorized lifting and pasting of a digital/physical signature is a classic form of forgery and identity theft.

**Myth 10:** Once a case is filed, the forged property deed gets automatically cancelled.
**Fact:** The criminal court only punishes the offender. You must file a separate civil suit for the cancellation of the forged deed.

## Conversation Examples

**Conversation 1: Reporting a forged check**
*Citizen:* Kisi ne meri checkbook se blank cheque chura kar usme mera fake signature kiya aur bank se paise nikal liye. Mujhe kya karna chahiye?
*Assistant:* Sabse pehle bank ko turant inform karein aur account freeze karwayein. Phir apne nazdiki police station mein BNS Section 339 (Forgery of valuable security) aur 340 (Cheating) ke tahat FIR darj karein. Bank se us clear hue cheque ki copy aur CCTV footage preserve karne ko kahein.

**Conversation 2: Fake property papers**
*Citizen:* My uncle has created a fake will of my late grandfather to claim the entire property. How can I challenge this?
*Assistant:* You must immediately file a criminal complaint under Section 339 of the BNS for forgery of a will. Simultaneously, file a civil suit for a declaration that the will is null and void, and seek a permanent injunction to prevent your uncle from selling the property. You can request a forensic handwriting analysis of the will.

**Conversation 3: Using a fake degree for a job**
*Citizen:* Ek employee ne humari company mein fake college degree dikha kar job le li hai. Kya hum uspar police case kar sakte hain?
*Assistant:* Haan, aap BNS Section 340 (Forgery for purpose of cheating) aur Section 343 (Using forged document as genuine) ke tahat FIR file kar sakte hain. Company use turant terminate karke local police station mein written complaint de sakti hai.

**Conversation 4: Altering an existing contract**
*Citizen:* Maine ek rent agreement sign kiya tha. Landlord ne baad mein rent amount 10,000 se badal kar 20,000 kar diya bina mujhe bataye. Kya ye crime hai?
*Assistant:* Haan, ye BNS Section 337 ke under 'making a false document' (material alteration) ka crime hai. Aap is forgery ke khilaf police complaint kar sakte hain. Aapke paas jo original copy hai, wo ek strong evidence hogi.

**Conversation 5: Forged digital signature**
*Citizen:* Someone hacked my email and used my scanned signature to approve a company transaction. Does BNS cover digital forgery?
*Assistant:* Absolutely. Under BNS Section 336 read with the IT Act, 2000, creating or altering electronic records to commit fraud is a severe offense. You should immediately report this to the Cyber Crime Police.

**Conversation 6: Getting a false medical certificate**
*Citizen:* Agar main chhutti lene ke liye fake medical certificate submit karun, toh kya problem ho sakti hai?
*Assistant:* Ye BNS Section 336 ke under forgery hai. Agar company chahay toh aapke khilaf police complaint kar sakti hai, jisme 2 saal tak ki saza ho sakti hai, aur aapki job bhi ja sakti hai. Aisa bilkul na karein.

**Conversation 7: Proving the forgery in court**
*Citizen:* Court mein kaise sabit hoga ki signature mera nahi hai?
*Assistant:* Court Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) ke tahat FSL (Forensic Science Lab) ke handwriting expert ko document bhejega. Wo aapke asli signatures aur forged signature ka microscopic analysis karke court ko report dega.

**Conversation 8: Delay in filing FIR**
*Citizen:* Mujhe 2 saal baad pata chala ki mere naam ka fake loan agreement bana hua hai. Kya ab FIR ho sakti hai?
*Assistant:* Haan, bilkul ho sakti hai. Forgery ke cases mein jab fraud ka pata chalta hai (discovery of crime), tab se limitation dekha jata hai. Aap turant sabhi documents ke sath police ko complaint dein aur delay ka reason explain karein.

**Conversation 9: Buying property with fake papers unknowingly**
*Citizen:* Maine ek property kharidi, baad mein pata chala ki seller ne fake papers dikhaye the. Kya mere upar bhi forgery ka case banega?
*Assistant:* Nahi, agar aapko nahi pata tha ki papers fake hain aur aapne 'good faith' mein kharidi ki hai (lack of mens rea), toh aap par forgery ka case nahi banega. Aap ulta us seller ke khilaf BNS Section 340 (cheating) ka case darj karein.

**Conversation 10: Fake WhatsApp chats as evidence**
*Citizen:* Someone created a fake WhatsApp chat screenshot showing me agreeing to a deal. Is this forgery?
*Assistant:* Yes, creating a false electronic record (like a manipulated screenshot) to cause injury or support a false claim falls under forgery (BNS Sec 336). The original digital footprints can be verified via forensic analysis under BSA.

**Conversation 11: Punishment for forging a Will**
*Citizen:* Vasiyat (Will) ki jalsazi karne ki BNS mein kya saza hai?
*Assistant:* BNS ki dhara 339 ke tahat vasiyat (will) ya valuable security ki jalsazi ek bahut gambhir apradh hai. Isme ajeevan karawas (life imprisonment) ya 10 saal tak ki jail aur jurmana ho sakta hai.

**Conversation 12: Cyber Crime Portal use**
*Citizen:* Where do I complain if the forgery was done entirely online through fake emails?
*Assistant:* For digital and cyber-related forgery, you can register a complaint directly on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in). The cyber cell will investigate the IP addresses and digital footprints.

**Conversation 13: Withdrawing the case**
*Citizen:* Jisne forgery ki thi wo ab paise wapas kar raha hai. Kya hum case wapas le sakte hain?
*Assistant:* Agar forgery simple nature ki hai toh court ki permission se compromise ho sakta hai. Lekin agar cheating ya property papers ki forgery ka serious FIR (Sec 339/340) hai, toh case non-compoundable hota hai. Aapko case quash karwane ke liye High Court jana padega.

**Conversation 14: Fake ID card**
*Citizen:* Ek ladka police ki fake ID banakar logo se paise ainth raha hai. Ye kaunsa section hai?
*Assistant:* Ye cheating by personation (BNS 319) aur forgery for cheating (BNS 340) ka case hai. Iske alawa public servant ka bhes badalne ke bhi alag criminal charges lagenge. Turant 112 par call karein.

**Conversation 15: Signature done by mistake**
*Citizen:* By mistake I signed on my colleague's form instead of mine because they looked identical. Am I guilty of forgery?
*Assistant:* No. Forgery requires a criminal intention (mens rea) to defraud. Since it was an honest mistake without any intent to cause wrongful gain or loss, it does not constitute forgery.
