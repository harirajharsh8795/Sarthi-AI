# Extortion (Section 308, Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita) - Comprehensive Legal Guide

## Overview
**English:** Extortion is a serious criminal offence under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023. It occurs when a person intentionally puts another individual in fear of physical injury, reputational harm, or false legal accusation, thereby dishonestly inducing them to hand over money, property, or valuable security. It bridges the gap between theft and robbery by involving an element of psychological coercion and fear. 

**Hindi Unicode:** भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS), 2023 के तहत जबरन वसूली (एक्सटॉर्शन) एक गंभीर आपराधिक अपराध है। यह तब होता है जब कोई व्यक्ति जानबूझकर किसी अन्य व्यक्ति को शारीरिक चोट, प्रतिष्ठा को नुकसान या झूठे कानूनी आरोप के डर में डालता है, और इस प्रकार बेईमानी से उन्हें पैसा, संपत्ति या मूल्यवान सुरक्षा सौंपने के लिए प्रेरित करता है। यह मनोवैज्ञानिक दबाव और भय के तत्व को शामिल करके चोरी और डकैती के बीच की खाई को पाटने का काम करता है।

## Quick Summary
**English:** Extortion under Section 308 of the BNS replaces Section 383 of the old IPC. It is a non-bailable, cognizable offence (in aggravated forms) punishable with rigorous imprisonment ranging from 7 years to life imprisonment, depending on the severity of the threat used (e.g., threat of death vs. threat of simple injury).

## Definition
**English:** According to Section 308(1) of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, whoever intentionally puts any person in fear of any injury to that person, or to any other, and thereby dishonestly induces the person so put in fear to deliver to any person any property, or valuable security or anything signed or sealed which may be converted into a valuable security, commits "extortion".

**Hindi Unicode:** भारतीय न्याय संहिता की धारा 308(1) के अनुसार, जो कोई भी जानबूझकर किसी व्यक्ति को या किसी अन्य व्यक्ति को किसी भी प्रकार की चोट के डर में डालता है, और इस प्रकार डराए गए व्यक्ति को बेईमानी से किसी भी व्यक्ति को कोई संपत्ति, या मूल्यवान सुरक्षा या हस्ताक्षरित या मुहरबंद कुछ भी देने के लिए प्रेरित करता है जिसे मूल्यवान सुरक्षा में बदला जा सकता है, वह "जबरन वसूली" करता है।

**Hinglish:** BNS Section 308(1) ke mutabiq, agar koi insaan intentionally kisi ko darr me daalta hai (ki usko ya uske kisi apne ko nuksan pahunchaya jayega), aur is darr ki wajah se us insaan se dishonestly koi property, paise, ya signed papers (jo valuable security ban sakte hain) le leta hai, toh us act ko "extortion" (vasooli) kaha jata hai.

It safeguards personal property and mental well-being.

## Legal Background
Historically, Extortion was governed by Sections 383 to 389 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860. With the implementation of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, the provisions have been restructured, streamlined, and integrated into Section 308. 

- **Applicable Act:** Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023
- **Relevant Section:** Section 308 (Extortion)
- **Constitutional Relevance:** Article 21 (Protection of Life and Personal Liberty) and Article 300A (Right to Property) are indirectly safeguarded by penalizing those who infringe upon them through extortion.

**Breakdown of BNS Section 308:**
- **Section 308(1):** Defines Extortion.
- **Section 308(2):** Punishment for simple extortion. Imprisonment up to 7 years and a fine.
- **Section 308(3):** Extortion by putting a person in fear of death or grievous hurt. Imprisonment up to 10 years and a fine.
- **Section 308(4):** Extortion by threat of accusation of an offence punishable with death, life imprisonment, or imprisonment for 10 years. Punishable with up to 10 years imprisonment; if the offence threatened is an unnatural offence (like under Section 63 BNS), punishment can be life imprisonment.
- **Section 308(5):** Putting a person in fear of injury in order to commit extortion (attempt). Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.

## Applicability
**Who is covered:**
- Every individual present in the territory of India, including citizens, foreign nationals, and stateless persons.
- Cyber criminals committing extortion digitally (e.g., ransomware, sextortion) from inside or outside India against Indian citizens.
- Public servants who abuse their position to extort money (though this may also attract the Prevention of Corruption Act).

**Who is exempt:**
- Genuine legal recovery agents acting strictly within the bounds of law and RBI guidelines (though use of threat or physical force by them constitutes extortion and is illegal).
- No exemptions for police officers, politicians, or influential individuals.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Preserve Evidence:** Do not delete any messages, emails, or block numbers immediately without taking screenshots or recordings.
2. **File a Police Complaint/FIR:** Visit the nearest police station or use the online cyber crime portal (cybercrime.gov.in) if it is digital extortion. The police must register an FIR under Section 308 of BNS.
3. **Investigation:** Police will collect evidence, trace the numbers/IP addresses, and record witness statements under Section 180 BNSS (Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita).
4. **Arrest and Remand:** The accused can be arrested without a warrant as extortion is typically a cognizable offence. The accused will be presented before a Magistrate within 24 hours.
5. **Filing of Chargesheet:** Police will file the police report (chargesheet) within 60 to 90 days.
6. **Trial:** The court frames charges, witnesses are examined, cross-examinations happen, and a judgment is delivered.

## Required Documents
To file a strong case, the victim should prepare:
- Written complaint detailing the date, time, location, and nature of the threat.
- Printouts of WhatsApp chats, SMS, emails, or social media messages.
- Pen drive or CD containing call recordings or CCTV footage of the physical threat.
- Bank statements if money was already transferred under fear.
- Medical certificates if physical injury was inflicted during the threat.
- Proof of identity (Aadhar, PAN) of the complainant.

## Government Authorities
- **Police Department:** Primary authority for registering FIRs and investigating.
- **Cyber Crime Cell:** Specialized unit for digital extortion, ransomware, and sextortion.
- **Magistrate Courts (JMFC/CMM):** Courts where the trial initiates.
- **Sessions Court:** For aggravated extortion involving threats of death or life imprisonment, which may be committed to the Sessions court.
- **Lok Adalat / Mediation Centers:** Generally NOT applicable as extortion is a non-compoundable serious criminal offence.

## Penalties & Punishments
Under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023:
- **Simple Extortion (Sec 308(2)):** Rigorous imprisonment up to 7 years, and a fine.
- **Attempt to Extort (Sec 308(5)):** Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.
- **Extortion with Threat of Death or Grievous Hurt (Sec 308(3)):** Imprisonment up to 10 years, and a fine.
- **Extortion by threat of Accusation of Capital Offence (Sec 308(4)):** Imprisonment up to 10 years. If the threat is about an unnatural offence, punishment can extend to life imprisonment.

## Exceptions
There are very few exceptions in extortion:
- **Bona fide Legal Claim:** If a person genuinely believes they have a legal right to the property and uses lawful means (like a legal notice) to demand it, it is not extortion. However, threatening violence to recover a legal debt IS extortion.
- **Absence of Fear:** If the victim gives the property out of pity, charity, or freely without the element of fear playing a role in inducing the delivery, extortion is not complete.

## Important Timelines
- **Reporting:** Immediate. Delay in FIRs can weaken the case, though valid reasons for delay (like extreme fear or trauma) are accepted by courts.
- **Limitation Period:** As the punishment is generally above 3 years (e.g., 7 years), there is NO period of limitation for taking cognizance of the offence under Section 508 of BNSS.
- **Bail:** Generally, bail for serious extortion sections takes time and is at the discretion of the Sessions Court or High Court.
- **Chargesheet:** Must be filed within 60 or 90 days depending on the severity of the specific sub-section, else the accused gets default bail.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Paying the Extortionist:** Paying up rarely stops the harassment; it usually increases the demands.
- **Deleting Evidence:** Victims often delete compromising photos or chats in a panic. This destroys crucial evidence needed to trace and convict the culprit.
- **Delaying the FIR:** Waiting for weeks to file an FIR gives the accused time to abscond or destroy evidence.
- **Not Informing Cyber Cell:** In digital blackmail, local police might lack technical expertise. Failing to involve the Cyber Crime Cell is a major mistake.
- **Hiding Facts from Lawyers:** Not telling the entire truth to your legal counsel can destroy the case during cross-examination.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **What is the BNS section for extortion?**
   Under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023, extortion is covered under Section 308. This replaces Section 383 of the old Indian Penal Code (IPC).

2. **Is extortion a bailable offence?**
   No, extortion under BNS Section 308 is generally a non-bailable and cognizable offence, especially in its aggravated forms involving threats to life.

3. **What is the difference between theft and extortion?**
   Theft (BNS Section 303) involves taking property without the owner's consent. Extortion involves taking property WITH the owner's consent, but that consent is obtained wrongfully by putting them in fear.

4. **Can a recovery agent be charged with extortion?**
   Yes, if a loan recovery agent uses threats of physical violence, defamation, or false criminal charges to force someone to pay, they can be charged with extortion under BNS Section 308.

5. **What if the threat is fake, but I still got scared and paid?**
   It still constitutes extortion. The key ingredient is that you were put in fear of injury, and you delivered the property because of that fear, regardless of the physical reality of the threat.

6. **What is 'Sextortion'?**
   Sextortion is a form of cyber extortion where a person is blackmailed with the threat of releasing their intimate pictures or videos online unless they pay money. It is punishable under BNS Sec 308 and IT Act provisions.

7. **How is robbery different from extortion?**
   Extortion becomes robbery when the offender is physically present, puts the victim in immediate fear of instant death, instant hurt, or instant wrongful restraint, and takes the property right then and there.

8. **Where can I report cyber extortion?**
   You can report cyber extortion immediately at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (www.cybercrime.gov.in) or by dialing the national helpline number 1930.

9. **What is the punishment for blackmailing someone with false rape charges?**
   This falls under BNS Section 308(4). Extortion by threat of accusation of an offence punishable with life imprisonment or death carries severe penalties, extending up to 10 years of imprisonment.

10. **Do I need to hire a lawyer to file an FIR for extortion?**
    No, you do not need a lawyer to file an FIR. You can directly approach the police station. However, having a lawyer later for the court trial is highly recommended.

11. **Can my employer commit extortion against me?**
    Yes. If an employer threatens to ruin your career with false accusations or physical harm unless you hand over money, blank cheques, or property, it is extortion.

12. **Is simply threatening someone considered extortion?**
    Mere threat without the demand and delivery of property is Criminal Intimidation (BNS Section 351). Extortion requires the actual demand and/or delivery of property or valuable security.

13. **What is meant by 'valuable security' in extortion?**
    Valuable security includes documents like blank signed cheques, property papers, promissory notes, or any legal document that can create or extinguish a legal right.

14. **Can a police officer be guilty of extortion?**
    Absolutely. If a police officer abuses their power and threatens to file false charges unless a bribe is paid, they can be prosecuted for extortion alongside corruption charges.

15. **What if I was forced to sign a blank paper?**
    Forcing someone to sign a blank paper under threat falls under the definition of extortion in BNS 308, as that paper can be converted into a valuable security (like a promissory note).

16. **How long does an extortion trial take in India?**
    Depending on the court's backlog and the complexity of the evidence, a trial can take anywhere from 2 to 5 years, though severe cases may be fast-tracked.

17. **Can I withdraw an extortion case later?**
    Extortion is generally a non-compoundable offence, meaning you cannot settle it privately and withdraw the case without the permission of the High Court under BNSS Section 528 (quashing of FIR).

18. **Are WhatsApp chats admissible as evidence in extortion cases?**
    Yes, WhatsApp chats and audio notes are highly admissible as electronic evidence under the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), provided a Section 63 certificate is attached.

19. **What if the extortionist is a minor?**
    If the offender is below 18, they will be dealt with under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, and the proceedings will take place before the Juvenile Justice Board.

20. **Does the property have to be movable in extortion?**
    Unlike theft, which only applies to movable property, extortion can apply to any property, whether movable or immovable, or valuable security.

## Common Myths vs Facts

**Myth 1:** Extortion only happens to rich business people.
**Fact:** Anyone can be a victim of extortion. Cybercriminals target common citizens daily through loan apps and video call scams for even small amounts.

**Myth 2:** You must suffer physical injury for a crime to be extortion.
**Fact:** No actual physical injury is required. The mere "fear" of injury (physical, reputational, or legal) is enough to constitute the crime.

**Myth 3:** Paying the extortionist will solve the problem.
**Fact:** Paying almost always leads to further demands. It funds their criminal activity and marks you as an easy target.

**Myth 4:** If someone owes you money, you can threaten them to get it back legally.
**Fact:** Threatening violence or false implication to recover a legitimate debt is illegal and amounts to extortion.

**Myth 5:** Extortion is a bailable offence if the amount is small.
**Fact:** Extortion is a serious cognizable offence under BNS. Bail is not a matter of right and is granted at the discretion of the court, regardless of the amount.

**Myth 6:** The police will leak my name if I report sextortion.
**Fact:** The law allows victims of sensitive crimes like sextortion to keep their identities anonymous during reporting and investigation.

**Myth 7:** Only cash demands count as extortion.
**Fact:** Demanding property, vehicles, blank signed cheques, or forcing someone to sign property transfer documents all count as extortion.

**Myth 8:** If I didn't hand over the money, it's not a crime.
**Fact:** If you were threatened but didn't pay, it is still punishable as "Attempt to Extort" under BNS Section 308(5), carrying up to 2 years imprisonment.

**Myth 9:** Extortion laws only apply to Indian citizens.
**Fact:** BNS applies to any person committing the crime within India, and also targets cybercriminals operating from abroad against Indian victims.

**Myth 10:** I can't file an FIR if the threat was made via a private WhatsApp call.
**Fact:** You absolutely can. Voice recordings, call logs, and circumstantial evidence can be used by the police to build a strong case.

## Conversation Examples

**Scenario 1: Threat by a local goon**
**Citizen:** Sir, mere area ka ek local gunda mujhse har mahine 10,000 rupaye haftaa maang raha hai, keh raha hai nahi diya toh dukan jala dega. Mujhe kya karna chahiye? (A local goon is demanding a monthly protection fee of 10,000 rupees, threatening to burn my shop. What should I do?)
**Assistant:** This is a clear case of Extortion under BNS Section 308. Do not pay him. Secretly record his threats if possible, or note the time he visits. Go to the nearest police station immediately and file an FIR. You can also request police protection if you fear for your life.

**Scenario 2: Sextortion trap**
**Citizen:** A girl video called me on WhatsApp, suddenly stripped, and recorded it. Now a man is calling claiming to be a police officer, demanding 50,000 rupees or they will upload it to YouTube. I am very scared.
**Assistant:** Do not panic and strictly DO NOT transfer any money. This is a classic "Sextortion" cyber scam. Block the numbers immediately. Take screenshots of the demands and report it at cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930. The police know about these gangs, and your identity will be kept safe. 

**Scenario 3: Loan app harassment**
**Citizen:** I took a small loan from an app. I repaid it, but now they morphed my face onto explicit photos and are threatening to send them to my contact list if I don't pay 20,000 more.
**Assistant:** This is cyber extortion and a severe crime under BNS Section 308 and the IT Act. Do not pay them another rupee. Warn your close contacts that your phone was hacked. File a complaint with the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal immediately with all screenshots and transaction IDs.

**Scenario 4: Threatening for property papers**
**Citizen:** Mera chacha mujhe jaan se maarne ki dhamki de raha hai ki agar maine pushtaini zameen ke kagaz par sign nahi kiye. (My uncle is threatening to kill me if I don't sign the ancestral property papers.)
**Assistant:** Ye BNS ki dhara 308 ke tehat 'Extortion' aur BNS 351 ke tehat 'Criminal Intimidation' ka sangeen aporadh hai. Kisi bhi kagaz par sign mat kijiye. Turant police station ja kar written complaint dijiye ki aapki jaan ko khatra hai aur aapse zabardasti property documents sign karwaye ja rahe hain.

**Scenario 5: Boss threatening to file a false case**
**Citizen:** My ex-employer is refusing to pay my 3 months' salary. When I asked for it, he said if I don't give him a signed blank cheque, he will file a false theft case against me.
**Assistant:** Threatening to falsely accuse someone of a crime to extract a valuable security (the blank cheque) is an aggravated form of extortion. Do not give the cheque. Preserve any emails or messages where he made this threat. You should file a police complaint for extortion and also approach the labor commissioner regarding your unpaid salary.

**Scenario 6: Attempted extortion without payment**
**Citizen:** A person sent me a letter threatening to kidnap my son unless I leave 5 lakhs at a specific spot. I haven't paid, and my son is safe. Is it still a crime?
**Assistant:** Yes. Under BNS Section 308(5), attempting to commit extortion by putting a person in fear of injury is a punishable offence. Hand over the letter to the police immediately. They can investigate fingerprints, CCTV near your house, and provide security for your son.

**Scenario 7: Fake Police / Customs Officer Scam**
**Citizen:** Mujhe ek call aayi ki mera parcel custom me fasa hai jisme illegal drugs hain. Caller bol raha hai 2 lakh do warna arrest warrant niklega. 
**Assistant:** Ye ek bahut common fraud aur extortion scam hai. Asli police ya customs officers kabhi call par paise transfer karne nahi bolte. Ghabrayein nahi, paise na dein, aur is call ki detail 1930 par cyber crime helpline ko dein.

**Scenario 8: Blackmail using old secrets**
**Citizen:** A former friend found out about a past mistake of mine and is threatening to tell my husband unless I buy him a motorcycle. 
**Assistant:** This is extortion. He is using the fear of reputational harm and family disruption to unlawfully acquire property (the motorcycle). Keep evidence of his demands. You have the right to file an FIR against him under BNS Section 308. 

**Scenario 9: False Rape Case Threat**
**Citizen:** A woman I met on a dating app is demanding 5 lakh rupees. She says if I don't pay, she will file a false rape case against me. 
**Assistant:** Threatening to accuse someone of a crime punishable by life imprisonment (like rape) to extort money is severely punishable under BNS Section 308(4). Gather all your chat history, location data, and call recordings. Approach the police proactively and file a complaint for extortion and criminal intimidation.

**Scenario 10: Forced Money Transfer**
**Citizen:** Teen ladko ne mujhe raste me ghera aur chaku dikha kar mera phone liya aur mere UPI se 50,000 khud ko transfer kar liye. (Three guys cornered me, showed a knife, took my phone and transferred 50,000 via my UPI).
**Assistant:** Ye Extortion aur Robbery (Loot) dono ki category me aata hai, kyunki unhone immediate jaan ka darr dikha kar property (paise) li. Turant apna bank account freeze karwayein aur 112 par call karke local police me FIR darj karwayein.

**Scenario 11: Business Rival**
**Citizen:** A competitor is threatening to release fake negative reviews and manipulated videos of my restaurant to ruin my business unless I pay him 1 Lakh monthly.
**Assistant:** This falls under extortion as they are intentionally threatening injury to your business reputation to extract money. Document the threats and file an FIR with the local police or cyber cell. 

**Scenario 12: Recovery Agent Harassment**
**Citizen:** Bank ka recovery agent mere ghar aakar mere bacchon ke samne mujhe gaaliyan de raha hai aur bol raha hai ki TV aur fridge utha kar le jayega agar aaj EMI nahi di.
**Assistant:** Recovery agents cannot use abusive language or physical threats, nor can they unlawfully take your household items. Using fear to recover money crosses into extortion and criminal intimidation. You can file a police complaint against the agent and send a formal grievance to the bank and RBI Ombudsman.

**Scenario 13: Land Mafia**
**Citizen:** Local land mafia members have parked a tractor on my farm and are threatening to destroy my crops if I don't sell my land to them at a throwaway price.
**Assistant:** This is organized extortion. They are using the threat of destroying your property to force a sale. File a complaint with the Superintendent of Police (SP) directly if the local station doesn't help, and you can also approach a civil court for an immediate injunction (stay order).

**Scenario 14: Withdrawal of Complaint for Money**
**Citizen:** I filed a minor accident case against a man. Now his brother is threatening to break my legs unless I withdraw the case AND pay him 20,000 for their "trouble".
**Assistant:** Threatening you with physical injury to force the withdrawal of a legal case and demanding money is extortion. Report this new threat to the police station where the original accident case is filed. They will add charges of extortion (BNS 308) and intimidating a witness.

**Scenario 15: Blackmail by IT administrator**
**Citizen:** My company's IT admin found out I was searching for new jobs on the company laptop. He is threatening to report me to the CEO for "data theft" unless I give him a cut of my salary.
**Assistant:** This is workplace extortion. Do not pay him. Preserve the chat or email where he makes this demand. Report the incident directly to your HR department and higher management. You also have the right to file a police complaint.

- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** Go to www.cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930 to report cyber extortion, ransomware, and sextortion immediately.

- **NALSA (National Legal Services Authority):** Provides free legal aid for vulnerable victims who cannot afford a lawyer for their court cases.
- **Emergency Response Support System (ERSS):** Dial 112 for immediate police assistance if you are under physical threat.
