# Assault under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) - Comprehensive Legal Guide

## Overview
**English:** Under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, assault is a punishable offense. It is defined as making any gesture, or any preparation, intending or knowing it to be likely that such gesture or preparation will cause any person present to apprehend that the person making it is about to use criminal force.
**Hindi (Unicode):** भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS), 2023 के तहत, 'हमला' (Assault) एक दंडनीय अपराध है। इसका अर्थ है कोई ऐसा इशारा या तैयारी करना, जिससे सामने वाले व्यक्ति को यह लगे कि उस पर आपराधिक बल का प्रयोग होने वाला है।
**Hinglish:** BNS 2023 ke antargat, assault ek dandniya apradh hai. Iska matlab hai koi aisa ishara ya taiyari karna jisse saamne wale insaan ko lage ki us par criminal force (hamla) use hone wala hai.

## Quick Summary
**English:** Assault is not necessarily actual physical contact; the apprehension or fear of impending physical violence is sufficient. The law protects individuals from threats of immediate harm, prescribing strict penalties under BNS.
**Hindi (Unicode):** हमले में वास्तविक शारीरिक संपर्क होना जरूरी नहीं है; शारीरिक हिंसा का डर पैदा करना ही पर्याप्त है। कानून व्यक्तियों को तत्काल नुकसान की धमकियों से बचाता है।
**Hinglish:** Assault me actual physical contact hona zaroori nahi hai; physical violence ka darr paida karna hi kaafi hai. Kanoon logo ko immediate nuksan ki dhamkiyo se bachata hai.

## Definition
**English:** Assault (BNS Section 130) occurs when a person makes a gesture or preparation with the intention or knowledge that it will cause someone present to believe they are about to be subjected to criminal force. Mere words do not amount to assault, but words combined with gestures may.
**Hindi (Unicode):** हमला (BNS धारा 130) तब होता है जब कोई व्यक्ति ऐसा इशारा या तैयारी करता है, जिससे उपस्थित व्यक्ति को यह विश्वास हो जाए कि उस पर आपराधिक बल का प्रयोग होने वाला है। केवल शब्द हमला नहीं होते, लेकिन इशारों के साथ शब्द हमला हो सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Assault (BNS Section 130) tab hota hai jab koi insaan aisa ishara ya taiyari karta hai jisse kisi ko vishwas ho jaye ki us par criminal force use hone wala hai. Sirf shabdo se assault nahi hota, par shabdo ke sath ishare assault maane jate hain.

## Why It Matters

## Legal Background
- **History & Purpose:** Previously governed by Section 351 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), the provision was updated under Section 130 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 to modernise and streamline the legal framework protecting individuals against apprehension of force.
- **Applicable Acts:** Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023; Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023; Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023.
- **Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 21 (Protection of Life and Personal Liberty).
- **Relevant Sections:**
  - **BNS Section 129:** Criminal Force
  - **BNS Section 130:** Assault
  - **BNS Section 131:** Punishment for Assault or Criminal Force (otherwise than on grave provocation)
  - **BNS Section 132:** Assault or Criminal Force to deter public servant from discharge of his duty
  - **BNS Section 133:** Assault or Criminal Force with intent to outrage modesty of a woman
  - **BNS Section 134:** Assault or Criminal Force with intent to dishonor person

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** All citizens, residents, and individuals within the territory of India are protected under this law.
- **Who is exempt:** Generally, no one is exempt, but law enforcement officers acting in the lawful discharge of their duties and within the bounds of the law are protected from assault charges (e.g., using reasonable force to effect an arrest).
- **Special conditions:** Private defense (self-defense) is a valid exception where a person uses proportional force to protect themselves or others from imminent harm.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Immediate Safety:** Move to a safe place and call emergency services (112) if the threat is ongoing.
2. **Medical Attention:** Seek immediate medical help if any physical contact occurred.
3. **Police Complaint:** Visit the nearest police station to file an FIR under BNS Sections 130/131. If the police refuse, file an e-FIR or approach the Superintendent of Police (SP).
4. **Gather Evidence:** Collect CCTV footage, audio/video recordings, and contact details of witnesses.
5. **Magistrate Complaint:** If the police fail to act, file a private complaint before a Judicial Magistrate under the relevant BNSS section (formerly CrPC 156(3)).
6. **Investigation:** The police will investigate, record statements under BNSS, and file a charge sheet.
7. **Trial:** The court will hear both sides, examine evidence, and pass a judgment.

2. **चिकित्सा:** यदि कोई शारीरिक संपर्क हुआ है, तो चिकित्सा सहायता लें।

## Government Authorities
- **Police Department:** For registering FIRs and conducting primary investigations.
- **Judicial Magistrate (First Class):** For hearing the trial and taking cognizance of the offense.
- **Lok Adalat:** Can sometimes mediate if the offense is compoundable and parties agree to a settlement.
- **National/State Legal Services Authority (NALSA/SLSA):** For free legal aid to eligible victims.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **BNS Section 131 (Punishment for Assault/Criminal Force):** Imprisonment up to 3 months, or fine up to ₹500, or both (if not on grave provocation).
- **BNS Section 132 (Assault to deter public servant):** Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.
- **BNS Section 133 (Assault with intent to outrage modesty):** Imprisonment not less than 1 year but which may extend to 5 years, and fine.
- **BNS Section 134 (Assault with intent to dishonor):** Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.

## Exceptions
- **Grave and Sudden Provocation:** If the assault is a direct reaction to grave and sudden provocation by the victim, the punishment may be mitigated (BNS allows reduction of sentence for provocation, usually limiting it to 1 month imprisonment or a ₹200 fine).
- **Private Defense:** Actions taken in legitimate self-defense (proportional to the threat) are entirely exempt from criminal liability under BNS general exceptions.

## Important Timelines
- **Filing FIR:** Should be done immediately or as soon as possible after the incident to ensure the reliability of the complaint.
- **Limitation Period:** Under BNSS, for offenses punishable with imprisonment up to 3 months, the court cannot take cognizance after 1 year from the date of the offense.
- **Medical Examination:** Must be conducted within 24 hours of the incident if injury is claimed.
- **Filing of Charge Sheet:** Usually within 60 to 90 days depending on the severity of the associated charges.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. Delaying the filing of the FIR without a valid reason, weakening the case.
2. Retaliating with excessive and disproportionate physical violence, converting self-defense into an offense.
3. Failing to collect or preserve crucial evidence like CCTV footage before it is overwritten.
4. Not getting a medical examination done if physical force accompanied the assault.
5. Exaggerating the facts of the incident in the police complaint, which can lead to perjury or false case charges.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Q:** What is the difference between assault and criminal force?
   **A:** Assault is creating the apprehension or fear of physical harm (e.g., raising a fist). Criminal force is the actual application of force (e.g., punching someone). BNS treats them distinctly but they are often charged together.
2. **Q:** Can mere words constitute an assault?
   **A:** No, mere words do not amount to an assault. However, the words which a person uses may give to their gestures or preparation such a meaning as may make those gestures or preparations amount to an assault.
3. **Q:** Is assault a bailable offense?
   **A:** Yes, simple assault under BNS Section 131 is generally a bailable, non-cognizable, and compoundable offense.
4. **Q:** Do I need a lawyer to file an FIR for assault?
   **A:** No, you do not need a lawyer to file an FIR. You can simply go to the police station and narrate the incident or provide a written complaint.
5. **Q:** What if the police refuse to register my FIR?
   **A:** You can send the substance of the information in writing by post to the Superintendent of Police (SP). If still unresolved, you can file a complaint before the Magistrate under BNSS.
6. **Q:** Can a woman file a special case for assault?
   **A:** Yes, if the assault is done with the intent to outrage her modesty, it is covered under a specific, more severe section (BNS Section 133), which is cognizable and non-bailable.
7. **Q:** (Hindi) क्या गाली देना हमले में आता है?
   **A:** केवल गालियां देना या अपशब्द कहना हमला (Assault) नहीं माना जाता। लेकिन अगर गालियों के साथ मारने की धमकी या इशारा किया जाए, तो यह हमला माना जाएगा।
8. **Q:** (Hinglish) Agar kisi ne mujhe WhatsApp par dhamki di, toh kya wo assault hai?
   **A:** WhatsApp par dhamki dena criminal intimidation ho sakta hai, par use assault nahi maana jayega jab tak aapko immediate physical attack ka darr na ho.
9. **Q:** Can I hit back if someone assaults me?
   **A:** You have the right to private defense. You can use reasonable and proportional force to protect yourself from imminent danger, but not for retaliation or revenge.
10. **Q:** What is the punishment for assaulting a police officer?
    **A:** Assaulting a public servant to deter them from their duty is a serious offense under BNS Section 132, punishable with up to 2 years of imprisonment.
11. **Q:** Is CCTV footage admissible as evidence?
    **A:** Yes, CCTV footage is strong electronic evidence under the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), provided its authenticity is proven.
12. **Q:** How long does an assault trial take?
    **A:** The duration of the trial depends on the court's backlog and the complexity of the case, but summary trials for simple assault can conclude in a few months to a year.
13. **Q:** Can an assault case be settled outside court?
    **A:** Yes, simple assault is a compoundable offense. With the permission of the court or through Lok Adalat, parties can compromise and close the case.
14. **Q:** What happens if the accused is a minor?
    **A:** If the accused is under 18, they will be dealt with under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, and the proceedings will take place before a Juvenile Justice Board.
15. **Q:** What if the assault resulted in severe injuries?
    **A:** If severe injuries occur, the charges will be upgraded from simple assault to causing 'hurt' or 'grievous hurt' under relevant BNS sections, which carry much harsher penalties.
16. **Q:** Can I claim compensation for assault?
    **A:** Yes, the criminal court can order the convict to pay compensation to the victim under the provisions of the BNSS, in addition to any fine imposed.
17. **Q:** Does raising a stick at a dog amount to assault?
    **A:** No, assault is an offense against a human being. Animal cruelty falls under different laws, such as the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act.
18. **Q:** Is spitting on someone considered assault?
    **A:** Yes, spitting on someone intentionally constitutes criminal force, and making the preparation to spit in their presence can be considered assault.
19. **Q:** (Hinglish) Agar maine self defense me kisi ko mara aur wo seriously injure ho gaya, toh kya mujh par case hoga?
    **A:** Agar aap prove kar sakein ki force proportional thi aur aapki jaan ko khatra tha, toh BNS private defense ke under aapko exemption milega. Par case investigate zaroor hoga.
20. **Q:** What is the definition of 'apprehension' in assault?
    **A:** Apprehension means a reasonable expectation or fear that criminal force is about to be applied immediately.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** Assault always means someone punched or kicked you.
   **Fact:** That is battery or criminal force. Assault is merely creating the fear or apprehension of such physical harm.
2. **Myth:** Verbal threats over the phone are considered assault.
   **Fact:** Phone threats are usually classified as criminal intimidation, not assault, because there is no immediate physical threat.
3. **Myth:** (Hinglish) Police bina warrant assault me arrest kar sakti hai.
   **Fact:** Simple assault non-cognizable hota hai, isliye police bina warrant arrest nahi kar sakti (unless it involves public servants or outraging modesty).
4. **Myth:** Self-defense gives you the right to kill your attacker.
   **Fact:** You can only use proportional force. The right to cause death in self-defense only arises if there is a reasonable apprehension of death or grievous hurt.
5. **Myth:** An assault case cannot be withdrawn once filed.
   **Fact:** Simple assault is a compoundable offense, meaning the victim can choose to compromise and withdraw the case.
6. **Myth:** (Hindi) अगर चोट के निशान नहीं हैं, तो कोई केस नहीं बन सकता।
   **Fact:** हमले (Assault) के मामले में चोट लगना आवश्यक नहीं है। केवल डराना या आपराधिक बल प्रयोग करने का इशारा ही केस दर्ज करने के लिए पर्याप्त है।
7. **Myth:** Assault laws only protect men.
   **Fact:** Assault laws are gender-neutral and protect all individuals, though women have additional protective sections.
8. **Myth:** Pointing an unloaded gun is not assault.
   **Fact:** If the victim believes the gun is loaded and fears immediate harm, it constitutes an assault.
9. **Myth:** You must have eyewitnesses to prove an assault.
   **Fact:** While eyewitnesses strengthen the case, circumstantial evidence, CCTV, or even the victim's credible testimony can secure a conviction.
10. **Myth:** Assault is an unbailable crime.
    **Fact:** Simple assault is a bailable offense, meaning the accused has the right to get bail easily from the police station itself.
