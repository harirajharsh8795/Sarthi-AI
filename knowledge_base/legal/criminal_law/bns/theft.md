# Theft under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) - Section 303

## Overview
**English:** Theft, formerly covered under Section 378/379 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), is now comprehensively addressed under Section 303 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023. It deals with the dishonest removal of movable property out of the possession of any person without their consent. The new law introduces modern provisions, including community service for petty thefts, ensuring a more reformative approach while maintaining strict penalties for organized or repeated property crimes.
**Hindi Unicode:** भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS) की धारा 303 के तहत चोरी (Theft) को परिभाषित और दंडनीय बनाया गया है। यह पहले IPC की धारा 378/379 के तहत था। किसी व्यक्ति की सहमति के बिना उसकी चल संपत्ति (movable property) को बेईमानी से ले जाना चोरी कहलाता है। नए कानून में छोटे अपराधों के लिए सामुदायिक सेवा (community service) का भी प्रावधान है।
**Hinglish:** BNS ki Section 303 ke under chori (theft) ko naye tarike se define kiya gaya hai, jo pehle IPC 378/379 mein tha. Kisi ki bina permission ke uski movable property ko dishonestly move karna theft hai. Naye law mein chhote-mote chori ke cases ke liye community service ka bhi naya rule laya gaya hai, aur repeated offenders par strict penalty hai.

## Quick Summary
**English:** Section 303 of BNS defines theft as dishonestly taking movable property without consent. Punishments range from community service (for petty theft under ₹5,000) to up to 3 years imprisonment or fine, with heavier penalties for theft in a dwelling house, theft by clerks/servants, or repeated offences.
**Hindi Unicode:** BNS की धारा 303 के अनुसार सहमति के बिना चल संपत्ति ले जाना चोरी है। सजा में जुर्माना, 3 साल तक की जेल और ₹5,000 से कम की चोरी के लिए सामुदायिक सेवा शामिल है।
**Hinglish:** Agar koi bina consent ke kisi ka saman (movable property) churata hai, toh BNS Section 303 lagta hai. ₹5,000 se kam ke petty theft par ab community service ho sakti hai. Normal chori mein 3 saal tak ki jail ya fine ho sakta hai.

## Definition
**English:** Whoever, intending to take dishonestly any movable property out of the possession of any person without that person's consent, moves that property in order to such taking, is said to commit theft.
**Hindi Unicode:** जो कोई किसी व्यक्ति के कब्जे से उस व्यक्ति की सम्मति के बिना कोई जंगम सम्पत्ति बेईमानी से ले लेने का आशय रखते हुए, उस सम्पत्ति को ऐसे ले लेने के लिए हटाता है, वह चोरी करता है, यह कहा जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Jo koi insaan dishonestly kisi dusre ki movable property (jo zameen se judi na ho) ko bina uski permission ke apni jagah se hatata hai us intent ke sath ki wo usko le jaye, usne theft (chori) kiya hai.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Protecting movable property is a fundamental aspect of civil rights and economic stability. BNS modernizes the approach to property crimes by balancing deterrence for habitual offenders with reformative justice (community service) for first-time, minor offenders, reducing the burden on prisons.
**Hindi Unicode:** संपत्ति की सुरक्षा नागरिकों का बुनियादी अधिकार है। BNS छोटे अपराधियों को सुधारने के लिए सामुदायिक सेवा जैसे आधुनिक तरीके अपनाता है और आदतन अपराधियों को कड़ी सजा देकर समाज को सुरक्षित बनाता है।
**Hinglish:** Logon ki property safe rakhna law ka main kaam hai. BNS ka naya approach ensure karta hai ki petty thieves ko reform kiya ja sake through community service, aur badi chori karne walon ko jail bheja jaye, taaki society safe rahe.

## Legal Background
* **History & Purpose:** IPC Section 378 & 379 governed theft for over 160 years. The Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) replaced them in 2023 to modernize the legal framework, introducing "community service" as a formally recognized punishment to decongest jails and reform petty offenders.
* **Applicable Acts:** Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023; Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023 (for procedure); Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023 (for evidence).
* **Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 21 (Protection of life and personal liberty), Article 300A (Persons not to be deprived of property save by authority of law).
* **Relevant Sections:**
  * **BNS Section 303(1):** Definition of Theft.
  * **BNS Section 303(2):** Punishment for standard theft (up to 3 years imprisonment, or fine, or both).
  * **BNS Section 303(3):** Theft of an idol or icon from a place of worship (imprisonment up to 3 years, and fine).
  * **BNS Section 303(4):** Theft of cattle or vehicle.
  * **BNS Section 305:** Theft in a dwelling house, tent, or vessel (Up to 7 years imprisonment and fine).
  * **BNS Section 306:** Theft by clerk or servant of property in possession of master (Up to 7 years and fine).

## Applicability
* **Covered:** Only **movable property** (tangible assets, money, vehicles, jewelry, detached trees/crops, domestic animals). It applies irrespective of the value of the property, though the punishment varies.
* **Exempt:** Immovable property (land, house, rooted trees) cannot be stolen (though trespassing or illegal occupation applies). Intangible properties like data or electricity are covered under different specialized laws (IT Act, Electricity Act), although the legal definitions are constantly evolving.
* **Special Conditions:** The property must be moved. Merely intending to steal without moving the object does not constitute the completed act of theft (it may be an attempt). The taking must be *dishonest* and *without consent*.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English:** Provide accurate details, bills, and IMEI/chassis numbers of the stolen items to the police. Do not file false theft cases, as it is punishable under BNS Section 217.
**Hindi Unicode:** पुलिस को चोरी हुए सामान की सही जानकारी, बिल और सीरियल नंबर प्रदान करें। झूठी FIR दर्ज न कराएं, यह कानूनी अपराध है।
**Hinglish:** Police ko saman ki saari correct details dein jaise purchase bill, serial number ya gadi ka number. Fake chori ki complaint kabhi na karein, ye BNS 217 ke under punishable offence hai.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Immediate Reporting:** Call 112 or visit the nearest police station immediately.
2. **File FIR/e-FIR:** Under Section 173 of BNSS, file an FIR. Many states allow e-FIR for standard/petty thefts.
3. **Investigation:** Police inspect the crime scene, check CCTVs, and question witnesses under BNSS procedures.
4. **Recovery & Arrest:** Police trace the property and arrest the accused.
5. **Charge-sheet:** Police submit the charge-sheet to the Magistrate within 60-90 days.
6. **Superdari:** The victim applies to the Magistrate to release the recovered property back to them pending trial.
7. **Trial:** The court examines evidence under the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) and passes judgment.

**Hindi Unicode:**
1. 112 पर कॉल करें या तुरंत पुलिस स्टेशन जाएं।
2. BNSS की धारा 173 के तहत FIR दर्ज कराएं। (छोटे मामलों में e-FIR भी संभव है)।
3. पुलिस CCTV और गवाहों की मदद से जांच करेगी।
4. पुलिस आरोपी को गिरफ्तार कर सामान बरामद करेगी।
5. मजिस्ट्रेट के सामने चार्जशीट पेश की जाएगी।
6. पीड़ित 'सुपरदारी' के माध्यम से अपना सामान अदालत से वापस मांग सकता है।
7. अदालत सबूतों के आधार पर सजा सुनाएगी।

**Hinglish:**
1. Chori hote hi sabse pehle 112 par call karein ya nearest police station jayein.
2. BNSS Section 173 ke under FIR likhwayein. Petty theft mein e-FIR bhi kaam karti hai.
3. Police spot par aakar CCTV check karegi aur investigation start karegi.
4. Accused pakde jane par police charge-sheet court mein file karegi.
5. Apne recovered saman ko wapas pane ke liye court mein 'Superdari' application lagayein.
6. Trial ke baad judge sentence pass karenge.

## Required Documents
* Written complaint detailing the incident.
* Identity proof of the complainant (Aadhaar, Voter ID).
* Proof of ownership (Original bills, invoices, registration certificates for vehicles, IMEI box for phones, photos of jewelry).
* CCTV footage (if available in personal/home cameras).

## Government Authorities
* **Police Department:** For registering FIR, investigation, and recovery.
* **Judicial Magistrate (First Class):** For trying the case, issuing arrest warrants, and granting Superdari.
* **Lok Adalats:** Sometimes used for compounding minor compoundable theft offences with mutual compromise.

## Penalties & Punishments
* **Standard Theft (BNS 303(2)):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both.
* **Petty Theft (Under ₹5,000, first-time offender):** Can be punished with community service upon return of the property.
* **Theft in Dwelling House (BNS 305):** Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.
* **Theft by Clerk/Servant (BNS 306):** Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.
* **Theft after preparation made for causing death/hurt (BNS 307):** Imprisonment up to 10 years and fine.

## Exceptions
* **Joint Property:** A co-owner taking common property without the other's consent is generally not theft unless it causes wrongful loss to the other.
* **Mistake of Fact:** If a person takes another's property genuinely believing in good faith that it belongs to them, it lacks the "dishonest intention" and is not theft (though civil liability may arise).
* **Consent:** If the property was moved with the express or implied consent of the possessor, it is not theft.

## Important Timelines
* **Reporting:** Should be reported as soon as possible. Delay in lodging FIR (without reasonable explanation) can weaken the case.
* **Charge-sheet:** Must be filed by police within 60 days (if punishment is less than 10 years) under BNSS to prevent default bail.
* **Limitation for Cognizance:** As per BNSS Section 508, the court can take cognizance of an offence punishable with up to 3 years imprisonment only within 3 years of the offence. For offences punishable with more than 3 years (e.g., BNS 305/306), there is no limitation period.

## Common Legal Mistakes
* Delaying the FIR thinking the property is permanently lost.
* Failing to provide accurate serial numbers, IMEI, or chassis numbers, making legal recovery impossible.
* Exaggerating the list of stolen items (this compromises the credibility of the entire case).
* Settling with the thief without court permission in non-compoundable situations.

## Safety Tips
**English:** Always lock your vehicles and homes securely. Keep digital copies of bills and IMEI numbers backed up on cloud storage. Use GPS trackers for expensive vehicles.
**Hindi Unicode:** अपने घर और वाहनों को हमेशा सुरक्षित रूप से लॉक करें। कीमती सामानों के बिल और फोन के IMEI नंबर सुरक्षित रखें। वाहनों में GPS ट्रैकर का उपयोग करें।

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is the difference between theft and robbery?**
   **English:** Theft is taking property secretly or without consent. Robbery involves theft with violence, threat of instant death, or hurt.
   **Hindi:** चोरी में बिना बताए संपत्ति ली जाती है। लूट (Robbery) में चोरी के साथ हिंसा या जान का खतरा शामिल होता है।
   **Hinglish:** Chori mein chupke se saman liya jata hai, jabki robbery mein maar-peet ya darakar chori ki jati hai.

2. **Can someone be arrested for stealing something worth ₹500?**
   **English:** Yes, theft is a cognizable offence regardless of value. However, under BNS, petty theft (under ₹5,000) by a first-time offender often results in community service instead of jail.
   **Hindi:** हां, चोरी एक संज्ञेय अपराध है। लेकिन BNS के तहत, ₹5,000 से कम की पहली चोरी पर जेल की बजाय सामुदायिक सेवा की सजा मिल सकती है।
   **Hinglish:** Haan arrest ho sakta hai, par naye BNS law mein agar ₹5,000 se kam ka saman hai aur pehli baar chori ki hai, toh jail ki jagah community service milti hai.

3. **Can I file an online FIR for a stolen mobile phone?**
   **English:** Yes, most state police portals allow e-FIRs for lost or stolen items, especially mobile phones and documents. You can also block the phone via the CEIR portal.
   **Hindi:** हां, आप पुलिस पोर्टल पर e-FIR दर्ज कर सकते हैं। इसके अलावा आप CEIR पोर्टल के जरिए फोन को ब्लॉक कर सकते हैं।
   **Hinglish:** Haan, state police ki website par online e-FIR file ho sakti hai. Phone block karne ke liye government ke CEIR portal ka use karein.

4. **What is 'Superdari' in theft cases?**
   **English:** Superdari is the legal process where the Magistrate hands over the custody of the recovered stolen property back to the rightful owner during the pendency of the trial.
   **Hindi:** सुपरदारी वह कानूनी प्रक्रिया है जिसमें अदालत सुनवाई पूरी होने तक बरामद किया गया सामान उसके असली मालिक को सौंप देती है।
   **Hinglish:** Superdari ek court process hai jismein police dwara baramad kiya gaya chori ka saman, judge sahab trial khatam hone tak asli owner ko wapas de dete hain.

5. **Does theft apply to digital data or electricity?**
   **English:** No, Section 303 of BNS strictly applies to movable physical property. Data theft is covered under the IT Act (Section 66/43), and electricity theft under the Electricity Act, 2003.
   **Hindi:** नहीं, BNS 303 केवल भौतिक संपत्तियों पर लागू होता है। डेटा चोरी IT Act और बिजली चोरी Electricity Act के तहत आती है।
   **Hinglish:** Nahi, BNS Section 303 sirf physical movable property par lagta hai. Data chori IT Act mein aur bijli chori Electricity Act mein punishable hai.

6. **What if the thief is a servant or employee?**
   **English:** Under BNS Section 306, theft by a servant or clerk carries a harsher punishment, with imprisonment up to 7 years, due to the breach of trust involved.
   **Hindi:** BNS की धारा 306 के तहत, अगर नौकर या कर्मचारी चोरी करता है, तो उसे 7 साल तक की जेल हो सकती है क्योंकि इसमें विश्वासघात शामिल है।
   **Hinglish:** Agar naukar ya employee chori kare, toh BNS 306 lagta hai jismein 7 saal tak ki jail ka provision hai, kyunki isme trust toda gaya hai.

7. **What if someone borrows my car and doesn't return it?**
   **English:** If they took it with your consent initially but later refused to return it with dishonest intent, it is generally Criminal Breach of Trust, not standard theft.
   **Hindi:** यदि उन्होंने आपकी सहमति से कार ली थी लेकिन वापस नहीं की, तो यह आपराधिक विश्वासघात (Criminal Breach of Trust) है, चोरी नहीं।
   **Hinglish:** Agar gadi mang ke le gaye aur wapas nahi ki, toh ye Criminal Breach of Trust ka case banta hai, direct chori nahi kyunki initial consent thi.

8. **Can police refuse to register an FIR for theft?**
   **English:** No, theft is a cognizable offence. Police are legally bound to register an FIR under Section 173 of BNSS. If they refuse, you can approach the Superintendent of Police or file a complaint with a Magistrate.
   **Hindi:** नहीं, चोरी एक संज्ञेय अपराध है और पुलिस को FIR दर्ज करनी ही होगी। मना करने पर आप SP या मजिस्ट्रेट से शिकायत कर सकते हैं।
   **Hinglish:** Nahi, police chori ki FIR likhne se mana nahi kar sakti. Agar kare, toh aap SP ko written complaint bhej sakte hain ya Magistrate ke paas ja sakte hain.

9. **What happens if the stolen property is never recovered?**
   **English:** The police will file an 'Untraced Report' (Closure Report) in court. The case can be reopened if the property or accused is found later. You can use the FIR and this report for insurance claims.
   **Hindi:** पुलिस अदालत में 'Untraced Report' (अनट्रेस्ड रिपोर्ट) दाखिल करेगी। आप इस रिपोर्ट का इस्तेमाल बीमा क्लेम के लिए कर सकते हैं।
   **Hinglish:** Police court mein 'Untraced Report' file karti hai. Is report aur FIR ki help se aap apne insurance company se claim le sakte hain.

10. **Is keeping found property considered theft?**
    **English:** If you find property, know the owner, but dishonestly keep it, it is Criminal Misappropriation of Property, not strictly theft. But if you take it from someone's immediate possession, it is theft.
    **Hindi:** गिरी हुई चीज़ पाकर बेईमानी से रख लेना आपराधिक दुर्विनियोग (Criminal Misappropriation) है। लेकिन किसी के पास से चुपके से उठाना चोरी है।
    **Hinglish:** Agar koi giri hui cheez mile aur aap use dishonestly rakh lein, toh wo Criminal Misappropriation hai. Par kisi ke bag ya jeb se nikalna chori (theft) hai.

11. **How does community service work for petty theft?**
    **English:** Under BNS, if the stolen value is under ₹5,000, it's a first offence, and property is returned, a Magistrate can order the offender to perform community service under a designated officer instead of jail.
    **Hindi:** BNS के तहत, ₹5,000 से कम की चोरी पर अगर संपत्ति वापस मिल जाती है, तो मजिस्ट्रेट जेल के बजाय सामुदायिक सेवा का आदेश दे सकता है।
    **Hinglish:** Naye law mein, agar ₹5000 se kam ki chori hai aur first time offence hai, toh judge jail bhejney ke bajaye samaj sewa (community service) karne ko keh sakte hain.

12. **Can a husband be accused of stealing his wife's property?**
    **English:** Yes, if a husband takes the wife's exclusive absolute property (Stridhan) dishonestly without her consent, it constitutes theft or criminal breach of trust.
    **Hindi:** हां, अगर पति पत्नी की निजी संपत्ति (स्त्रीधन) बिना उसकी सहमति और बेईमानी से ले लेता है, तो यह चोरी या विश्वासघात का मामला हो सकता है।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, agar husband wife ka Stridhan (exclusive property) uski permission ke bina dishonestly le leta hai, toh wo theft ya breach of trust mana jayega.

13. **Do I need a lawyer to file a theft FIR?**
    **English:** No, you do not need a lawyer to file an FIR. You just need to provide a written complaint at the police station or file an e-FIR online.
    **Hindi:** नहीं, FIR दर्ज कराने के लिए वकील की आवश्यकता नहीं है। आप सीधे पुलिस को लिखित शिकायत दे सकते हैं।
    **Hinglish:** Nahi, chori ki FIR file karne ke liye lawyer ki zarurat nahi hoti. Aap khud police station mein written application de sakte hain.

14. **What if a child commits theft?**
    **English:** A child under 7 cannot commit an offence. Between 7-12 years, it depends on their maturity. Above 12, they are dealt with under the Juvenile Justice Act, focusing on reform rather than strict BNS penalties.
    **Hindi:** 7 साल से कम का बच्चा अपराध नहीं कर सकता। 12 से ऊपर के बच्चों पर जुवेनाइल जस्टिस एक्ट (Juvenile Justice Act) के तहत कार्रवाई होती है।
    **Hinglish:** 7 saal se chhota bachha law mein offender nahi hota. Bade bachon ko Juvenile Justice Board ke samne pesh kiya jata hai, unhe normal jail nahi bheja jata.

15. **What is the penalty for stealing from a temple or church?**
    **English:** Theft of an idol or icon from a place of worship is explicitly covered under BNS Section 303(3), punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine.
    **Hindi:** धार्मिक स्थल से मूर्ति या प्रतीक की चोरी BNS धारा 303(3) के तहत आती है, जिसमें 3 साल तक की जेल और जुर्माना हो सकता है।
    **Hinglish:** Mandir, masjid ya church se murti ya koi religious item churane par BNS 303(3) lagta hai, jismein 3 saal tak ki jail aur fine dono hote hain.

16. **How do I claim insurance for a stolen vehicle?**
    **English:** Lodge an FIR immediately, inform the insurance company, and submit the FIR copy, registration certificate, and eventually the police 'Untraced Report' if the vehicle isn't recovered.
    **Hindi:** तुरंत FIR दर्ज कराएं, बीमा कंपनी को सूचित करें और FIR कॉपी व गाड़ी के कागजात जमा करें। गाड़ी न मिलने पर पुलिस की 'Untraced Report' दें।
    **Hinglish:** Sabse pehle FIR file karein aur insurance company ko batayein. Agar police gadi nahi dhundh pati toh 'Untraced Report' court se le kar insurance claim ke liye submit karein.

17. **Is snatching considered theft?**
    **English:** Snatching is now defined as a distinct, more severe offence under Section 304 of BNS, carrying up to 3 years imprisonment, distinct from standard theft.
    **Hindi:** झपटमारी (Snatching) को अब BNS की धारा 304 के तहत एक अलग और गंभीर अपराध माना गया है।
    **Hinglish:** Chain snatching ya phone snatching ab direct BNS Section 304 ke andar alag se punishable hai, ye normal chori se zyada serious mana jata hai.

18. **Can the police search a house for stolen goods without a warrant?**
    **English:** Yes, under BNSS, if an investigating officer has reasonable grounds to believe stolen property is inside and obtaining a warrant would cause delay allowing evidence destruction, they can search without a warrant after recording reasons in writing.
    **Hindi:** हां, यदि पुलिस को लगता है कि वारंट लेने में समय लगेगा और सबूत नष्ट हो जाएंगे, तो वे लिखित कारण देकर बिना वारंट तलाशी ले सकते हैं।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, agar police ko pakka shaq hai aur warrant lene ke time mein saman chupaya ja sakta hai, toh wo in-writing reason dekar bina warrant ghar search kar sakte hain.

19. **Can a theft case be withdrawn?**
    **English:** Basic theft may be compoundable (withdrawn by compromise) with the permission of the court under BNSS Section 359, provided the stolen value isn't exceedingly high and it's not a severe aggravated form (like theft by a servant).
    **Hindi:** हां, सामान्य चोरी के मामले में अदालत की अनुमति से समझौता (Compound) किया जा सकता है।
    **Hinglish:** Basic chori ke cases mein agar property wapas mil jaye toh court ki permission se mutual compromise karke case wapas liya ja sakta hai.

20. **Is purchasing stolen property an offence?**
    **English:** Yes, dishonestly receiving or retaining stolen property is a separate, serious offence under BNS Section 317, punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years or fine or both.
    **Hindi:** हां, बेईमानी से चोरी का सामान खरीदना या रखना BNS धारा 317 के तहत अपराध है।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, chori ka saman saste mein jante hue kharidna BNS Section 317 ke under jurm hai, isme bhi 3 saal tak ki jail ho sakti hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** If you steal less than ₹1,000, it's not a crime.
   **Fact:** Any theft, regardless of value, is a crime under BNS 303. Small amounts may get community service, but it is still a criminal conviction.
2. **Myth:** Online money theft is covered under BNS 303.
   **Fact:** Digital fraud and cyber theft are covered under BNS Section 318 (Cheating) or the IT Act, not Section 303 which is for movable physical property.
3. **Myth:** A husband cannot steal from a wife.
   **Fact:** Taking a spouse's exclusive property without consent dishonestly is a crime.
4. **Myth:** You need to find the thief yourself before police register FIR.
   **Fact:** Police must register an FIR against "unknown persons" immediately upon your complaint.
5. **Myth:** Snatching a phone and stealing it from a desk are the same.
   **Fact:** Snatching involves force/surprise on a person and falls under BNS 304 (Snatching), carrying different implications than BNS 303 (Theft).
6. **Myth:** Police keep the recovered stolen property forever.
   **Fact:** You can claim it back through a 'Superdari' application in court.
7. **Myth:** Without a CCTV camera, police will reject the theft complaint.
   **Fact:** CCTV is an aid to investigation, not a legal prerequisite for filing an FIR.
8. **Myth:** Buying stolen goods innocently will put you in jail.
   **Fact:** To be punished under BNS 317, you must have *dishonestly* received it knowing or having reason to believe it is stolen.
9. **Myth:** Theft of a car and theft of a bicycle have the same sections.
   **Fact:** BNS Section 303(4) specifically mentions theft of vehicles, showing nuanced legislative intent.
10. **Myth:** E-FIR is not legally valid in court.
    **Fact:** E-FIRs, once verified and officially registered by the police station, have the exact same legal validity as physical FIRs under BNSS and BSA.

## Conversation Examples

**Conversation 1: Filing a basic complaint**
*Citizen:* Mera mobile bus stand par chori ho gaya hai, main kya karoon?
*Assistant:* Aapko turant 112 par call karke police ko inform karna chahiye. Uske baad apne nearest police station mein jakar ya online state police portal par e-FIR file karein. Apna IMEI number aur purchase bill zaroor dein, aur CEIR portal (ceir.gov.in) par phone ko block karwayein.

**Conversation 2: Stolen property recovery**
*Citizen:* Police ne mera chori hua laptop baramad kar liya hai, par mujhe wapas nahi de rahe.
*Assistant:* Police baramad kiya gaya saman direct aapko nahi de sakti. Aapko Magistrate ki court mein 'Superdari' application lagani hogi. Court ek order pass karegi, jiske baad police aapko wo laptop wapas kar degi.

**Conversation 3: Servant theft**
*Citizen:* Mere naukar ne ghar se ₹50,000 aur jewelry chura li hai. Kya ye normal chori hai?
*Assistant:* Nahi, naukar ya employee dwara ki gayi chori BNS Section 306 ke under aati hai. Ye ek gambhir (serious) offence hai kyunki isme trust toda gaya hai. Isme 7 saal tak ki jail ho sakti hai. Aap turant FIR file karein aur specify karein ki chori servant ne ki hai.

**Conversation 4: Community Service**
*Citizen:* Ek ladke ne dukan se ₹1000 ka saman churaya, ye uski pehli galti thi aur usne saman wapas kar diya. Kya use jail hogi?
*Assistant:* BNS Section 303(2) ke mutabiq, agar chori ₹5,000 se kam ki hai, saman wapas mil gaya hai, aur ye pehla offence hai, toh court use jail bhejne ke bajaye community service (samudayik sewa) karne ki saza de sakti hai.

**Conversation 5: Buying stolen goods**
*Citizen:* Maine ek second-hand phone kharida saste mein, baad mein pata chala wo chori ka tha. Kya mujhe arrest kiya jayega?
*Assistant:* Agar aapne kharidte waqt nahi socha tha ki ye chori ka hai aur apka intention (aashay) galat nahi tha, toh aapne offence nahi kiya. Par BNS Section 317 ke tahat, agar aap jante hue chori ka saman beaimani se kharidte hain, toh aapko 3 saal tak ki saza ho sakti hai. Aapko turant police ko sach bata dena chahiye.

**Conversation 6: E-FIR usage**
*Citizen:* Main Delhi mein rehti hu, mera purse chori ho gaya. Kya mujhe police station jana zaroori hai?
*Assistant:* Delhi Police ki website aur app par aap easily e-FIR file kar sakti hain, khaaskar lost and stolen items ke liye. Aapko physically station jane ki zarurat nahi hai, e-FIR ki copy legally valid hoti hai.

**Conversation 7: Attempt to steal**
*Citizen:* Kisi ne meri bike ka lock todne ki koshish ki par chura nahi paya. Kya ye chori hai?
*Assistant:* Nahi, kyunki property move nahi hui, toh chori complete nahi hui. Ye BNS ke under 'Attempt to commit theft' (chori ka prayas) aur mischief (nuksan) ka mamla banega.

**Conversation 8: Theft of electricity**
*Citizen:* Mere padosi ne mere meter se wire jod kar bijli chori ki hai. Kya BNS 303 lagaoon?
*Assistant:* BNS 303 physical movable property par lagta hai. Bijli ki chori Electricity Act, 2003 ki Section 135 ke under punish ki jati hai. Aapko apne electricity provider ya vigilance department ko complaint karni chahiye.

**Conversation 9: Joint property**
*Citizen:* Mera aur mere bhai ka joint business account aur locker hai. Usne bina bataye paise nikal liye.
*Assistant:* Joint property mein aamtaur par BNS 303 (Theft) nahi lagta jab tak dusre partner ko wrongful loss pahunchane ki beaimani na ho. Ye Criminal Breach of Trust ya partnership dispute ka matter ho sakta hai.

**Conversation 10: Police refusing FIR**
*Citizen:* Police chori ki report likhne se mana kar rahi hai, keh rahi hai chhota amount hai.
*Assistant:* Chori ek cognizable (sangyeya) offence hai aur police BNSS Section 173 ke under FIR likhne ke liye bound hai, chahe amount kuch bhi ho. Aap SP (Superintendent of Police) ko written complaint bhej sakte hain.

**Conversation 11: Untraced Report**
*Citizen:* 6 mahine ho gaye meri gadi chori hue, police ko kuch nahi mila. Insurance wale claim nahi de rahe.
*Assistant:* Aap police se court mein 'Untraced Report' (Final Report) file karne ko kahein. Jab court us report ko accept kar legi, tab wo order aur copy aap insurance company ko claim settlement ke liye de sakte hain.

**Conversation 12: Data Theft**
*Citizen:* Kisi ne meri company ka data pendrive mein copy karke chura liya.
*Assistant:* Pendrive churana BNS 303 (Theft) mein aata hai. Lekin Data ki chori Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000 ki Section 43 aur 66 ke under cyber crime mani jati hai. Aap cyber cell mein report karein.

**Conversation 13: Time limit for FIR**
*Citizen:* Mera bag 1 mahine pehle train mein chori hua tha, kya main ab FIR kar sakta hu?
*Assistant:* Haan, aap abhi bhi Government Railway Police (GRP) ya regular police station mein zero FIR file kar sakte hain. Lekin aapko delay ka reason (jaise health issue ya travel) batana hoga taaki case weak na pade.

**Conversation 14: Difference between Extortion and Theft**
*Citizen:* Kisi ne mujhe dara kar mera wallet le liya. Ye theft hai?
*Assistant:* Nahi, darakar, dhamkakar property lena Extortion (Uddhapan - BNS Sec 308) ya Robbery (Loot - BNS Sec 309) kehlata hai. Theft mein bina daraye, chupke se ya bina consent ke property li jati hai.

**Conversation 15: Compoundable Theft**
*Citizen:* Jisne meri cycle churai thi, wo garib hai aur mafi maang raha hai. Usne cycle wapas kar di. Kya case band ho sakta hai?
*Assistant:* Haan, BNS mein sadharan chori BNSS Section 359 ke tahat compoundable (samjhauta-yogya) ho sakti hai. Aap magistrate ke samne application dekar mutual compromise ke through case ko lawfully close karwa sakte hain.

## Government Services
**English:**
- **CCTNS / e-FIR Portals:** Every state police maintains a portal for online citizen services where e-FIRs for petty thefts can be registered.
- **CEIR (Central Equipment Identity Register):** A telecom portal by the DoT to block and trace stolen mobile phones using IMEI numbers.
- **eCourts Services:** Citizens can track the status of their Superdari applications, charge-sheets, and trial dates using the eCourts website or app.
- **NALSA (National Legal Services Authority):** Provides free legal aid for victims needing help navigating the court process for recovery.
