# Defamation (Maanhani) under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) Section 356

## Overview
**English:** Defamation is the act of communicating false statements about a person that injure their reputation. Under the new Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023, defamation is covered under Section 356 (formerly Sections 499 and 500 of the Indian Penal Code). The BNS introduces community service as an alternative punishment for defamation, alongside imprisonment and fines.

## Definition
**English:** Section 356(1) of BNS defines defamation as making or publishing any imputation concerning any person intending to harm, or knowing or having reason to believe that such imputation will harm, the reputation of such person, by words either spoken or intended to be read, or by signs or by visible representations.

## Legal Background
- **History & Purpose:** Criminal defamation has existed in India since the drafting of the IPC in 1860 (Sections 499 & 500). The intent is to deter malicious falsehoods that ruin reputations. In the transition to BNS, the core principles remain, but modernized penalties (like community service) have been introduced.
- **Applicable Acts:** Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS); Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 (BNSS); Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023 (BSA).
- **Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 21 (Right to Life and Personal Liberty - encompasses right to reputation), Article 19(1)(a) (Freedom of Speech and Expression - subject to reasonable restrictions under 19(2)).
- **Relevant Sections:**
  - **Section 356 BNS:** Defamation and its punishment.
  - **Section 356(2) BNS:** Prescribes punishment including simple imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both, or community service.

## Applicability
- **Covered:** Any individual, company, association, or collection of persons whose reputation is unlawfully tarnished. Imputations against a deceased person can also be defamation if they would harm the reputation of that person if living, and are intended to be hurtful to the feelings of their family.
- **Exempted:** Accurate statements made in good faith, legal proceedings, public performances critiqued honestly, and warnings given in good faith to protect interests.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Notice:** Send a legal notice demanding an unconditional apology and withdrawal of the defamatory statement.
2. **Complaint:** Since it is a non-cognizable offense, police usually do not file an FIR directly. The complainant must file a private complaint before the Judicial Magistrate under the BNSS.
3. **Examination:** The Magistrate will record the statement of the complainant and witnesses.
4. **Summons:** If a prima facie case is made out, the Magistrate summons the accused.
5. **Bail:** The accused can apply for bail as it is a bailable offense.
6. **Trial & Verdict:** Evidence is examined (under BSA). If proven guilty, the accused is sentenced.

- **Judicial Magistrate First Class (JMFC):** For filing criminal complaints.

- **Cyber Crime Cell / Police:** If the defamation involves morphing, deepfakes, or cyber harassment (additional charges under IT Act or BNS may apply, making it cognizable).

## Penalties & Punishments
Under Section 356(2) of the BNS:
- Simple imprisonment for a term which may extend to **two years**, or
- Fine, or
- Both, or
- **Community Service** (newly introduced, allows the court to mandate public service instead of jail/fine).

## Exceptions
Section 356 BNS provides ten exceptions where an imputation is not defamation:
1. Imputation of truth which public good requires to be made or published.
2. Public conduct of public servants.
3. Conduct of any person touching any public question.
4. Publication of reports of proceedings of courts.
5. Merits of a case decided in court or conduct of witnesses.
6. Merits of public performance (e.g., book reviews, movie reviews).
7. Censure passed in good faith by person having lawful authority.
8. Accusation preferred in good faith to authorized person.
9. Imputation made in good faith by person for protection of his or other's interests.
10. Caution intended for good of person to whom conveyed or for public good.

## Important Timelines
- **Limitation Period:** Under BNSS, the limitation period for taking cognizance of defamation (which carries a maximum punishment of 2 years) is generally **3 years** from the date of the offense.
- **Legal Notice Response:** Generally, 15 to 30 days are given in a legal notice for a response or apology.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Filing a police complaint for purely verbal defamation, expecting an FIR (it requires a Magistrate complaint).
- Delaying the complaint beyond the limitation period.
- Failing to preserve electronic evidence (like taking certified screenshots/hashes before the post is deleted).
- Making a counter-defamatory statement in anger, which leads to cross-cases.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What is the BNS section for defamation?**
*Answer:* Defamation is covered under Section 356 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023. This replaces the old IPC Sections 499 and 500. It defines the offense and lays down the punishment.

**2. Can the police arrest someone for defamation without a warrant?**
*Answer:* No. Criminal defamation is a non-cognizable and bailable offense. The police cannot investigate or arrest without a warrant or order from a Magistrate.

**3. Is truth a complete defense against a defamation case?**
*Answer:* Yes, but with a condition. Under Exception 1, truth is a defense only if the statement is made for the "public good." Just because a statement is true doesn't mean you can broadcast it to unnecessarily harass someone's private life.

**4. What is the new punishment introduced for defamation under BNS?**
*Answer:* BNS has introduced "community service" as a punishment. So a person convicted under Section 356 can be sentenced to community service, along with or instead of imprisonment up to 2 years and a fine.

**5. How is civil defamation different from criminal defamation?**
*Answer:* Civil defamation seeks monetary compensation (damages) for the harm caused to reputation. Criminal defamation seeks to punish the offender with imprisonment, fine, or community service. You can file both simultaneously.

**6. Can a dead person be defamed?**
*Answer:* Yes. Imputations against a deceased person can amount to defamation if they would harm their reputation if alive, and if the statement is intended to hurt the feelings of their family or close relatives.

**7. Is sharing or retweeting a defamatory post considered defamation?**
*Answer:* Yes. Reproducing, publishing, sharing, or retweeting defamatory material can make you equally liable for defamation, as it amplifies the harm to the person's reputation.

**8. Can a company file a defamation case?**
*Answer:* Yes. A company, corporation, or an association of persons has a legal reputation and can file a criminal or civil defamation suit if false statements harm their business reputation.

**9. Can I go to the cyber police for WhatsApp defamation?**
*Answer:* Pure defamation is non-cognizable, so cyber police may not register an FIR. However, if the defamation involves obscenity, morphed photos, or criminal intimidation, cyber police can file an FIR under applicable BNS/IT Act sections.

**10. What should I do if someone sends me a legal notice for defamation?**
*Answer:* Do not ignore it. Consult a lawyer. If the statement was a genuine mistake, you may publish an unconditional apology. If you believe your statement is covered under the 10 exceptions (like public good), you can legally contest it.

**11. Is calling someone names in private considered defamation?**
*Answer:* Defamation requires "publication" to a third party. If you say something bad to a person directly when no one else is around, it is not defamation. However, it might amount to criminal intimidation or insult (other BNS sections).

**12. Kya main apne boss par maanhani ka case kar sakta hoon agar wo sabke saamne meri beizzati kare?**
*Answer:* (Hinglish) Agar boss sirf aapke kaam ko lekar daant raha hai (good faith mein), toh Exception 7 ke under wo defamation nahi hai. Par agar wo jhoote arop lagakar sabke saamne apko zaleel karte hain malice ke saath, toh case ban sakta hai.

**13. क्या मैं मानहानि केस में समझौता कर सकता हूँ?**
*Answer:* (Hindi) हाँ, मानहानि एक शमनीय (compoundable) अपराध है। यदि दोनों पक्ष अदालत के बाहर सुलह कर लेते हैं, तो शिकायतकर्ता मामले को वापस ले सकता है।

**14. Are reviews on Google or Zomato considered defamatory?**
*Answer:* Honest reviews based on actual experience fall under the "merits of public performance" or "good faith" exceptions. However, intentionally writing false, malicious reviews to destroy a business is defamation.

**15. Does freedom of speech protect me from defamation?**
*Answer:* Freedom of speech (Article 19(1)(a)) is not absolute. Article 19(2) allows the State to impose reasonable restrictions on this freedom in the interest of public order, decency, or in relation to contempt of court and defamation.

**16. What is 'Innuendo' in defamation?**
*Answer:* Sometimes words are not directly insulting but have a hidden, secondary meaning that is defamatory. If a reasonable person understands the hidden insult, it is called innuendo, and it constitutes defamation.

**17. How much compensation can I claim in a civil defamation suit?**
*Answer:* There is no fixed limit. The compensation amount is decided by the court based on your social standing, the extent of the damage caused, the reach of the defamatory publication, and the financial loss you suffered.

**18. Can an MP or MLA be sued for defamation for things said in Parliament?**
*Answer:* No. Statements made by Members of Parliament or State Legislatures on the floor of the House are protected by constitutional privilege (Articles 105 and 194) and are immune from defamation proceedings in any court.

**19. What is 'Slander' vs 'Libel'?**
*Answer:* Slander refers to spoken or transient defamatory statements. Libel refers to written, printed, or permanent defamatory statements (like videos or articles). Indian criminal law covers both under the single section of Defamation.

**20. Kitne time ke andar defamation ka case file karna hota hai?**
*Answer:* (Hinglish) BNSS ke according, criminal defamation ka case generally offence hone ke 3 saal ke andar court mein file kiya ja sakta hai. Civil suit ke liye limitation period 1 saal hota hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts

**Myth 1:** If I delete the defamatory tweet, I cannot be punished.
**Fact:** Deleting the post does not erase the offense if it was already read by others and the victim has preserved evidence (screenshots/URLs). It may only help mitigate damages.

**Myth 2:** FIR is mandatory for a defamation case.
**Fact:** Police cannot file an FIR for simple defamation as it is non-cognizable. You must file a private complaint before a Magistrate.

**Myth 3:** (Hindi) सच बोलने पर कभी मानहानि नहीं होती।
**Fact:** केवल सच बोलना ही काफी नहीं है, अदालत यह भी देखती है कि क्या वह सच "सार्वजनिक हित" (Public Good) के लिए बोला गया था या सिर्फ किसी को नीचा दिखाने के लिए।

**Myth 4:** You can't be sued if you start the sentence with "In my opinion".
**Fact:** Phrasing a false statement of fact as an opinion does not give you immunity if it maliciously damages someone's reputation.

**Myth 5:** (Hinglish) WhatsApp pe forward kiya hua message bhejne se main bach jaunga, kyunki maine wo likha nahi hai.
**Fact:** Forward karne wala bhi utna hi zimmedar hota hai. Defamatory content ko publish ya circulate karna apne aap mein apradh hai.

**Myth 6:** Civil defamation is the only way to get justice.
**Fact:** You can pursue both civil (for compensation) and criminal (for punishment) cases simultaneously.

**Myth 7:** Only famous people or politicians can file defamation cases.
**Fact:** Any citizen, regardless of their social status, has a right to reputation and can file a defamation case if wronged.

**Myth 8:** Husbands and wives can defame each other.
**Fact:** Communications between spouses during the marriage are legally privileged, but publically defaming a spouse before third parties is actionable.

**Myth 9:** Apologizing automatically dismisses the criminal case.
**Fact:** An apology is a mitigating factor but doesn't automatically stop the trial unless the complainant agrees to compromise and withdraw the case.

**Myth 10:** Lawyers can't be sued for what they argue in court.
**Fact:** Lawyers enjoy absolute privilege during judicial proceedings for statements made in their professional capacity in good faith.

*Assistant:* (Hindi) हाँ, बिल्कुल। बीएनएस के तहत किसी मृत व्यक्ति पर लांछन लगाना भी मानहानि है, यदि उस लांछन से उनकी प्रतिष्ठा को नुकसान पहुंचता और इससे आपके परिवार की भावनाओं को ठेस पहुंची है। आप मजिस्ट्रेट के पास शिकायत दर्ज करा सकते हैं।

**Example 6: Slander by Boss**
*Citizen:* My boss called me incompetent in a closed cabin. Nobody else was there. Can I sue him?
*Assistant:* No. For defamation to occur, the defamatory statement must be "published" to a third party. If he insulted you in a closed room with nobody else present, it does not legally constitute defamation.

**Example 7: True Statements**
*Citizen:* Maine ek aadmi ke corruption ka sachha proof Twitter par daala, public ki bhalai ke liye. Usne mujh par defamation laga diya.
*Assistant:* BNS ki pehli exception (Exception 1) yahi kehti hai ki agar koi baat sach hai aur public ke bhale (public good) ke liye boli gayi hai, toh wo defamation nahi maani jayegi. Aapko court mein bas us baat ki sacchai sabit karni hogi.

**Example 8: Community Service**
*Citizen:* Naye BNS kanoon mein maanhani ke liye community service ka kya matlab hai?
*Assistant:* BNS 356 ke under court jail ya fine ke bajaye doshi ko samaaj ki bhalai ke kaam (jaise hospital, school ya NGO mein madad) karne ki saza de sakti hai. Yeh ek naya sudharatmak (reformative) kadam hai.

**Example 9: Civil vs Criminal**
*Citizen:* Should I file a civil case or a criminal case for defamation?
*Assistant:* It depends on your goal. If you want the person to go to jail or perform community service, file a criminal complaint (BNS 356). If you want monetary compensation for the loss of business or mental agony, file a civil suit. You can also do both.

**Example 10: Police Complaint Confusion**
*Citizen:* (Hinglish) Cyber police ne mera defamation complaint FIR mein convert karne se kyun mana kar diya?
*Assistant:* Kyunki pure defamation (sirf baatein) non-cognizable hai. Par agar usme kisi ki morphed/fake a आपत्तिजनक (obscene) photo lagayi gayi ho, toh wo IT Act aur BNS ke under cognizable ban jata hai, tab police FIR likhti hai.

**Example 11: Defamation in Court**
*Citizen:* Opposing lawyer called me a fraud during court proceedings. Is it defamation?
*Assistant:* Usually not. Statements made in good faith by advocates, witnesses, or judges during judicial proceedings enjoy absolute privilege and are protected under BNS Exceptions.

**Example 12: Withdrawing a Case**
*Citizen:* I filed a defamation case against my neighbor. He has now apologized and paid me for the damages. Can I stop the case?
*Assistant:* Yes. Defamation is a compoundable offense. You can inform the Magistrate that you have reached a settlement (compromise), and the court will dismiss the case and acquit the accused.

**Example 13: Time Limit**
*Citizen:* (Hindi) 4 साल पहले किसी ने मुझे अखबार में बदनाम किया था। क्या मैं आज केस कर सकता हूँ?
*Assistant:* (Hindi) आपराधिक मानहानि का केस दर्ज करने की समय सीमा (Limitation Period) आम तौर पर 3 वर्ष है। 4 साल बाद कोर्ट केस स्वीकार नहीं करेगा, जब तक कि देरी का कोई अत्यंत वैध और असाधारण कारण न हो।

**Example 14: Defaming a Company**
*Citizen:* A rival brand is running false ads saying our food products contain poison.
*Assistant:* This is corporate defamation. A registered company has a reputation to protect. Your company can immediately seek an injunction from a civil court to stop the ads and simultaneously file a criminal complaint under BNS 356.

**Example 15: Notice Reply Ignored**
*Citizen:* (Hinglish) Maine legal notice bheja tha 1 mahine pehle, par usne koi jawab nahi diya. Ab kya step lu?
*Assistant:* Agar notice ka time (usually 15-30 days) khatam ho gaya hai aur koi response ya apology nahi aayi, toh ab aapko apne lawyer ke zariye Judicial Magistrate ke samne private complaint file karni chahiye.
