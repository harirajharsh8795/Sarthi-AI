# Murder under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS): Laws, Procedures, and Punishments

## Overview

**English:**
Under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, murder is classified as one of the most heinous offenses against the human body. The law clearly distinguishes between culpable homicide and murder, setting strict parameters for intentional killing. The transition from the Indian Penal Code (IPC) to the BNS modernizes the linguistic framework while retaining the core principles of criminal liability. Section 101 of the BNS defines Murder, which corresponds to Section 300 of the old IPC, while Section 103 prescribes the punishment for murder, previously Section 302 of the IPC.

## Definition

**English:**
According to Section 101 of the BNS, culpable homicide is murder if the act by which the death is caused is done with the intention of causing death, or with the intention of causing such bodily injury as the offender knows to be likely to cause the death of the person, or if the person inflicting the injury intended it to be sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death. It also includes acts so imminently dangerous that they must, in all probability, cause death.

## Why It Matters

**English:**
Understanding the law of murder under BNS is critical for citizens and legal practitioners alike. It guarantees the right to life by imposing the severest penalties—life imprisonment or death—on those who unlawfully take another's life. It sets boundaries between accidental death, self-defense, culpable homicide, and intentional murder, ensuring justice and maintaining public order.

## Legal Background

- **History:** The laws regarding murder were originally codified in the Indian Penal Code, 1860 (drafted by Lord Macaulay). To modernize and de-colonize the criminal justice system, the IPC was replaced by the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) in 2023, restructuring the chapters to prioritize bodily offenses.
- **Purpose:** To strictly penalize the intentional and unlawful deprivation of human life and to deter potential offenders through severe state-sanctioned punishments.
- **Applicable Acts:** Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023, Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) 2023, Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) 2023.
- **Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 21 (Protection of Life and Personal Liberty). The state can only deprive a person of life through a procedure established by law.
- **Relevant Sections:**
  - **Section 101 BNS:** Definition of Murder.
  - **Section 103 BNS:** Punishment for Murder.
  - **Section 100 BNS:** Definition of Culpable Homicide.
  - **Section 104 BNS:** Punishment for Culpable Homicide not amounting to murder.

## Applicability

- **Who is covered:** All individuals within the territory of India, and Indian citizens committing offenses outside India, subject to extraterritorial jurisdiction rules under BNS.
- **Who is exempt:** Persons acting in good faith exercising the Right of Private Defense (subject to legal limits), acts of judges/public servants acting in official capacity lawfully, accidents without criminal intent, acts of children under 7 years (or 12 years if not mature), and persons of unsound mind at the time of the act.
- **Special conditions:** Certain conditions mitigate murder to culpable homicide not amounting to murder, such as grave and sudden provocation, exceeding the right of private defense in good faith, or sudden fights in the heat of passion without premeditation.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English:**
1. **Registration of FIR:** The process begins with lodging an FIR under Section 103 BNS (via Section 173 BNSS) at the police station.
2. **Investigation:** Police secure the crime scene, gather forensics, conduct inquests, and arrange for a post-mortem examination. Statements are recorded under Section 180 BNSS.
3. **Arrest & Remand:** The accused is arrested without a warrant (cognizable offense) and produced before a Magistrate within 24 hours. The police may seek police custody (remand) for interrogation.
4. **Filing of Charge-sheet:** The police submit the final investigation report (charge-sheet) to the Magistrate within 90 days.
5. **Committal to Sessions Court:** Since murder is exclusively triable by a Sessions Court, the Magistrate commits the case to the Sessions Judge.
6. **Framing of Charges:** The Sessions Court formally reads out the charges. If the accused pleads not guilty, the trial begins.
7. **Prosecution Evidence:** The prosecution examines witnesses (eyewitnesses, forensic experts, doctors) and presents material evidence.
8. **Statement of Accused:** The accused is given a chance to explain the circumstances appearing against them in the evidence.
9. **Defense Evidence:** The accused may present evidence or witnesses in their defense.
10. **Final Arguments & Judgment:** Both sides present final arguments. The judge delivers the verdict (acquittal or conviction). If convicted, a separate hearing for the quantum of sentence is held.

## Penalties & Punishments
Under **Section 103 of BNS**:
- **Whoever commits murder shall be punished with death or imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine.**
- *(Note: A death sentence passed by a Sessions Court must be confirmed by the High Court before it can be executed.)*
Under **Section 103(2) of BNS**:
- When a group of five or more persons acting in concert commits murder on the ground of race, caste or community, sex, place of birth, language, personal belief or any other similar ground, each member of such group shall be punished with death or with imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine. (Mob Lynching provision).

## Exceptions
Under Section 101 BNS, culpable homicide does NOT amount to murder if it falls under specific exceptions:
1. **Grave and Sudden Provocation:** If the offender loses self-control due to grave and sudden provocation, provided the provocation wasn't sought voluntarily.
2. **Exceeding Private Defense:** If the offender, in good faith, exceeds the right of private defense without premeditation.
3. **Public Servant Exceeding Powers:** If a public servant acting for public justice exceeds their legal powers in good faith.
4. **Sudden Fight:** If death occurs in a sudden fight in the heat of passion, without premeditation.
5. **Consent:** If the person whose death is caused, being above 18 years of age, suffers death with their own consent.

## Important Timelines
- **FIR Registration:** Immediate. Delay must be reasonably explained in court.
- **Filing Charge-sheet:** Within 90 days of arrest; otherwise, the accused is entitled to statutory/default bail.
- **Appeal:** An appeal against a conviction must generally be filed in the High Court within 60 days from the date of judgment.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Delay in filing FIR:** Delay can be fatal to the prosecution's case if not explained, as it leaves room for suspicion of fabrication.
- **Contradictory Witness Statements:** Witnesses changing their statements (turning hostile) severely weakens the case.
- **Mishandling the Crime Scene:** Touching objects or moving the body before police arrival destroys vital forensic evidence.
- **Relying solely on circumstantial evidence:** If the chain of circumstances is broken, courts usually acquit the accused.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **Q: What is the difference between Section 302 IPC and Section 103 BNS?**
   **A:** (English) There is no difference in the core legal principle or punishment. Section 302 of the old IPC has simply been renumbered as Section 103 under the new Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023. The punishment remains death or life imprisonment.
   (Hindi) मूल कानूनी सिद्धांत या सजा में कोई अंतर नहीं है। पुरानी IPC की धारा 302 को अब नई भारतीय न्याय संहिता 2023 के तहत धारा 103 के रूप में नया नंबर दिया गया है।
   (Hinglish) Core concept aur punishment same hai. Old IPC Section 302 ko naye BNS law mein Section 103 kar diya gaya hai.

2. **Q: Can bail be granted in a murder case?**
   **A:** (English) Murder is a non-bailable offense. Regular bail is very difficult to get and is completely at the discretion of the Sessions Court or High Court, usually only granted on medical grounds or lack of prima facie evidence.
   (Hindi) हत्या एक गैर-जमानती अपराध है। जमानत मिलना बहुत मुश्किल है और यह अदालत के विवेक पर निर्भर करता है।
   (Hinglish) Murder non-bailable crime hai. Bail milna bahut mushkil hota hai aur mostly High Court se hi milti hai agar evidence weak ho.

3. **Q: Is mob lynching considered murder under the BNS?**
   **A:** (English) Yes, BNS Section 103(2) explicitly recognizes murder by a group of five or more persons on grounds of race, caste, sex, etc. (mob lynching) and prescribes the death penalty or life imprisonment for each member.
   (Hindi) हाँ, BNS की धारा 103(2) विशेष रूप से भीड़ द्वारा हत्या (mob lynching) को मान्यता देती है और प्रत्येक सदस्य के लिए मृत्युदंड या आजीवन कारावास का प्रावधान करती है।
   (Hinglish) Haan, BNS 103(2) mein mob lynching ko specially add kiya gaya hai, jisme 5 ya zyada log milkar kisi ki hate crime ke chalte murder karte hain.

4. **Q: What happens if the murder was committed in self-defense?**
   **A:** (English) If proven that the act was strictly in good faith for private defense and did not exceed legal limits, the accused can be completely acquitted. If it exceeded limits, it may be reduced to culpable homicide.
   (Hindi) यदि यह साबित हो जाता है कि यह कृत्य आत्मरक्षा में किया गया था और कानूनी सीमा को पार नहीं किया, तो आरोपी को बरी किया जा सकता है।
   (Hinglish) Agar prove ho jaye ki murder strictly self-defense mein tha aur limits cross nahi ki, toh court accuse ko free kar sakti hai.

5. **Q: How many years is life imprisonment in a murder case?**
   **A:** (English) Life imprisonment technically means imprisonment for the remainder of the convict's natural life. However, state governments have the power to remit sentences after a minimum of 14 years under the BNSS.
   (Hindi) आजीवन कारावास का अर्थ तकनीकी रूप से दोषी के शेष प्राकृतिक जीवन के लिए कारावास है। हालांकि, राज्य सरकारें 14 साल बाद सजा माफ कर सकती हैं।
   (Hinglish) Life imprisonment ka matlab whole life hota hai, par state government chahe toh 14 saal baad sentence kam (remit) kar sakti hai.

6. **Q: Can a murder charge be withdrawn or compromised?**
   **A:** (English) No. Murder is a non-compoundable offense. The state prosecutes the case, and the victim's family cannot legally agree to a compromise or withdraw the charges.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** If there is no dead body, there can be no murder conviction.
   **Fact:** A conviction can be secured without a body if circumstantial evidence strongly proves the victim was killed by the accused.
2. **Myth:** Section 302 of the IPC still applies for crimes committed today.
   **Fact:** For crimes committed on or after the enforcement of the new laws, Section 103 of the BNS applies, not IPC 302.
3. **Myth:** Murder cases can be compromised if the victim's family accepts money.
   **Fact:** Murder is strictly non-compoundable; out-of-court settlements are legally invalid and punishable if they involve suppressing evidence.
4. **Myth:** The death penalty is given in every murder case.
   **Fact:** The death penalty is awarded only in the "rarest of rare" cases. Life imprisonment is the norm.
5. **Myth:** If you kill someone by accident, it's murder.
   **Fact:** Accidental death without intention or gross negligence is not murder. It may be an accident or culpable homicide.
6. **Myth:** Bail is impossible in a murder case.
   **Fact:** It is very difficult but not completely impossible. High Courts grant bail if the prosecution's case is glaringly weak or on severe medical grounds.
7. **Myth:** A person sentenced to life imprisonment gets out in 14 years automatically.
   **Fact:** Life means the entire natural life. The 14-year mark is only the minimum period after which the state can *consider* remission; it is not automatic.
8. **Myth:** Confession to police is enough to secure a murder conviction.
   **Fact:** A confession made to a police officer is generally not admissible as evidence in court unless it leads to the discovery of new facts.
9. **Myth:** The wife/husband of the accused cannot be a witness.
   **Fact:** Spouses can be competent witnesses against each other under Indian evidence laws.
10. **Myth:** Self-defense gives you a blank cheque to kill an attacker.
    **Fact:** The right of private defense is proportionate to the threat. You cannot kill an unarmed person who just slapped you and claim self-defense.

**Citizen:** Does the new law address mob lynching specifically?
**Saarthi AI:** Yes, it does. Section 103(2) of the BNS specifically introduces severe penalties for mob lynching. When a group of five or more persons commits a murder based on race, caste, sex, or personal belief, every member can be punished with death or life imprisonment.

**Citizen:** Kya murder case mein victim ki family ke saath paise dekar compromise ho sakta hai?
**Saarthi AI:** Nahi. BNS ke mutabiq murder ek non-compoundable crime hai. Iska matlab court kisi bhi tarah ke out-of-court settlement ya compromise ko allow nahi karegi. Crime state (sarkar) ke khilaf maana jata hai, isliye prosecution case chalayega.

**Dialogue 5: Finding a Body**
**Citizen:** If a person goes missing and no body is found, can someone still be convicted of murder?
**Saarthi AI:** Yes. While finding the body makes the case stronger, it is not strictly mandatory. If the prosecution can establish an unbroken chain of circumstantial evidence proving the accused killed the victim and disposed of the body, a conviction can still happen.

**Dialogue 6: Accident vs Murder**
**Citizen:** Mere dost ki gadi se galti se kisi ki maut ho gayi. Kya uspe BNS Section 103 lagega?
**Saarthi AI:** Nahi. Agar death galti se hui hai (accident) bina kisi intention ya criminal knowledge ke, toh wo murder (Section 103) nahi hai. Us par Death by Negligence (Rash driving) ka charge lagega, jiski saza murder se kafi kam hoti hai.

**Dialogue 7: FIR Delay**
**Citizen:** We lodged the FIR for my uncle's murder after 2 days because we were in shock. Will it weaken the case?
**Saarthi AI:** Courts understand human trauma. If you provide a reasonable and truthful explanation for the delay (like shock, fear, or geographical distance) to the judge, the delay will not necessarily weaken the prosecution's case.

**Dialogue 8: Life Imprisonment Meaning**
**Citizen:** Life imprisonment ka actual time kitna hota hai? Kya 14 saal mein rihai mil jati hai?
**Saarthi AI:** Technically, life imprisonment ka matlab jab tak aakhri saans hai tab tak jail. 14 saal sirf ek minimum limit hai jiske baad State Government (Sarkar) case review kar sakti hai remission (chhoot) ke liye. 14 saal baad automatically rihai nahi milti.

**Dialogue 9: Hostile Witness**
**Citizen:** What happens if the sole eyewitness turns hostile in court?
**Saarthi AI:** If a sole eyewitness turns hostile (retracts their statement), it severely damages the prosecution's case. The court will then have to rely entirely on forensic, medical, and circumstantial evidence to reach a conclusion.

**Dialogue 10: Attempt to Murder**
**Citizen:** Agar kisi ne goli chalayi par saamne wale ko sirf chot aayi aur jaan bach gayi, toh kya lagega?
**Saarthi AI:** Aise case mein BNS Section 109 (Attempt to murder) lagega (jo pehle IPC 307 tha). Isme intention murder ka tha, par maut nahi hui. Iski saza 10 saal tak ki jail, aur chot lagne par life imprisonment tak ho sakti hai.

**Dialogue 11: Insanity Defense**
**Citizen:** The person who committed the murder is genuinely mentally ill. Will they be hanged?
**Saarthi AI:** No. If it is medically and legally proven that at the time of the incident, the person was of unsound mind and didn't know that their action was wrong or contrary to law, they can claim the defense of insanity under general exceptions, leading to an acquittal but mandatory psychiatric care.

**Dialogue 12: Weapon Recovery**
**Citizen:** Police ne abhi tak murder weapon (chaku) recover nahi kiya hai. Kya case fail ho jayega?
**Saarthi AI:** Sirf weapon recover na hona case ko fail nahi karta. Agar baki evidences jaise eyewitness, CCTV, ya strong circumstantial evidence clear hai, toh court bina murder weapon ke bhi saza suna sakti hai.

**Dialogue 13: Bail in BNS 103**
**Citizen:** Can my relative get bail in a BNS Section 103 case easily?
**Saarthi AI:** No. Murder under BNS 103 is a strictly non-bailable offense. The Magistrate cannot grant bail. You have to apply to the Sessions Court or High Court, and bail is granted only in exceptional circumstances (e.g., severe lack of evidence or grave medical emergency).

**Dialogue 14: Difference between Culpable Homicide and Murder**
**Citizen:** Mujhe culpable homicide aur murder ka fark samajh nahi aa raha.
**Saarthi AI:** Dono mein maut hoti hai, par difference 'Intention' (iraade) aur degree of probability ka hai. Agar intention clear maarne ka hai ya certainty hai ki is chot se maut hi hogi, toh wo Murder hai. Agar maut hone ki sirf 'sambhavna' (likelihood) thi par clear plan nahi tha, toh wo Culpable Homicide hai.

**Dialogue 15: Death Penalty**
**Citizen:** Will the judge always give the death penalty for murder?
**Saarthi AI:** No, under Indian jurisprudence, the mandatory sentence is life imprisonment. The death penalty is an exception and is awarded only in the "rarest of rare" cases where the crime is exceedingly brutal, calculated, or shocks the conscience of society.
