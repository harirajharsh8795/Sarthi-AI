# Robbery Under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS)

## Overview
**English:** Robbery is a severe criminal offense under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS). It is essentially an aggravated form of either theft or extortion, involving the presence or threat of immediate physical harm, wrongful restraint, or death to accomplish the stealing of property. It is treated as a heinous crime against both property and the human body.
**Hindi Unicode:** भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS) के तहत लूट (Robbery) एक गंभीर आपराधिक अपराध है। यह मूल रूप से चोरी या जबरन वसूली (extortion) का एक उग्र रूप है, जिसमें संपत्ति चुराने के लिए तत्काल शारीरिक नुकसान, गलत तरीके से रोकने, या मौत की धमकी देना शामिल है। इसे संपत्ति और मानव शरीर दोनों के खिलाफ एक जघन्य अपराध माना जाता है।

## Definition
**English:** Under Section 309 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, "Robbery" occurs when in order to commit theft, or in committing theft, or in carrying away property obtained by theft, the offender voluntarily causes or attempts to cause death, hurt, or wrongful restraint, or fear of instant death, hurt, or wrongful restraint. Similarly, extortion becomes robbery if the offender is in the presence of the person put in fear and commits extortion by putting that person in fear of instant death, hurt, or wrongful restraint.
**Hindi Unicode:** भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS), 2023 की धारा 309 के तहत, "लूट" तब होती है जब चोरी करने के लिए, या चोरी करते समय, या चोरी से प्राप्त संपत्ति को ले जाते समय, अपराधी स्वेच्छा से मृत्यु, चोट, या गलत तरीके से रोक, या तत्काल मृत्यु, चोट या गलत तरीके से रोक का डर पैदा करता है। इसी तरह, जबरन वसूली लूट बन जाती है यदि अपराधी डरे हुए व्यक्ति की उपस्थिति में होता है और उस व्यक्ति को तत्काल मृत्यु, चोट या रोक के डर में डालकर जबरन वसूली करता है।

## Why It Matters

## Legal Background
**History:** Historically covered under Section 390 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860, the offense of robbery was designed to bridge the gap between simple property crimes and violent offenses. The BNS, 2023 modernized these provisions to better address contemporary nuances of violent property crimes.
**Purpose:** To protect the life, liberty, and property of individuals from forceful deprivation and to ensure that criminals who escalate property crimes into violent confrontations are subjected to harsh penal consequences.
**Applicable Acts:** Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023; Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023 (for procedural aspects); Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023 (for evidentiary matters).
**Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 21 (Protection of Life and Personal Liberty) - ensuring safety of the victim; Article 22 (Rights of the accused regarding arrest).
**Relevant Sections:**
- BNS Section 309: Defines Robbery and prescribes punishment for it.
- BNS Section 310: Dacoity (when 5 or more persons commit robbery).
- BNS Section 311: Robbery or dacoity with attempt to cause death or grievous hurt.
- BNSS Section 173: Information in cognizable cases (FIR registration).
- BSA Section 61-63: Relevant facts and electronic evidence relating to CCTV or digital logs of the incident.

## Applicability
**Who is covered:** Any individual who commits robbery against any person within the territory of India, or any Indian citizen committing it outside India.
**Who is exempt:** Generally, no one is exempt, save for individuals lacking the legal capacity to commit a crime (e.g., a child under 7 years of age under BNS general exceptions, or persons of unsound mind at the time of the act).
**Special conditions:** If the act of robbery involves 5 or more persons, the offense escalates to Dacoity (BNS Section 310), which carries different legal procedures and harsher minimum penalties.

## Rights of Citizens
**English:** Victims have the right to file an immediate FIR (First Information Report), the right to receive medical attention at the state's expense if injured, and the right to police protection if they face ongoing threats.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. Call Emergency Services: Dial 112 immediately to report the robbery and request police and medical assistance if needed.
2. Preserve the Scene: Do not touch anything where the robbery occurred to preserve fingerprints and evidence.
3. File an FIR: Visit the nearest police station or use e-FIR portals (if applicable) to lodge a formal complaint under BNSS Section 173. Provide detailed descriptions of the culprits, stolen items, and weapons used.
4. Medical Examination: If injured, undergo a medico-legal examination at a government hospital.
5. Investigation: Police will collect evidence, record statements under BNSS, and attempt to trace the culprits.
6. Arrest and Recovery: Upon identifying the suspects, police will arrest them and attempt to recover the stolen property.
7. Charge-sheet and Trial: A charge-sheet will be filed in the Magistrate/Sessions court, followed by a trial where evidence is presented under BSA, 2023.

## Required Documents
- Identity Proof of the complainant (Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, PAN).
- Detailed list of stolen items.
- Ownership proofs of stolen items (bills, IMEI numbers for phones, RC for vehicles, photos of jewelry).
- Medico-Legal Certificate (MLC) if the victim sustained physical injuries.
- Written complaint addressed to the Station House Officer (SHO).

## Government Authorities
- **Police Stations:** First responders and primary investigating authority.
- **Crime Branch / Special Cell:** For organized gangs involved in highway or serial robberies.
- **Magistrate Courts:** For remand, bail hearings, and initial judicial procedures.
- **Sessions Courts:** Robbery cases are often tried before a Court of Session, especially if severe violence is involved.

## Penalties & Punishments
Under BNS Section 309, the punishments are strictly defined:
- **General Robbery:** Rigorous imprisonment for a term which may extend to 10 years, and also liable to fine.
- **Highway Robbery at Night:** If the robbery is committed on the highway between sunset and sunrise, the imprisonment may be extended to 14 years.
- **Attempt to commit Robbery:** Rigorous imprisonment for a term which may extend to 7 years, and fine.
- **Voluntarily causing hurt in committing robbery:** If hurt is caused, the imprisonment may extend to 10 years or up to life imprisonment in extreme aggravated forms under associated sections.

## Exceptions
There are no "exceptions" that justify robbery. However, if a person genuinely reclaims their own property using reasonable force without the intent to cause wrongful loss (mens rea), it might not fall under theft/robbery, though they could be charged with assault or affray. This is highly circumstantial and decided by courts.

## Important Timelines
- **FIR Registration:** Should be done immediately (Zero delay is ideal, though courts consider valid explanations for delay).
- **Filing Charge-sheet:** Typically within 60 to 90 days from the date of arrest, depending on the severity of the associated charges.
- **Limitation Period:** As robbery is punishable with imprisonment for more than 3 years, there is no limitation period for taking cognizance under BNSS.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Delaying the FIR:** Waiting days to report the robbery weakens the case and allows criminals to escape and dispose of the property.
- **Tampering with Evidence:** Cleaning up the crime scene or touching items the robbers touched destroys vital forensic evidence.
- **Exaggerating Losses:** Falsely adding items to the stolen list can backfire during cross-examination, damaging the victim's credibility.
- **Not Notifying Banks/Telecom:** Failing to block stolen credit cards or SIM cards immediately, leading to further financial and identity theft.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is the difference between theft and robbery?**
   Theft is stealing property without the owner's consent. It becomes robbery when force, violence, or the threat of instant death/hurt is used to commit the theft.

2. **चोरी और लूट में क्या अंतर है?**
   चोरी बिना सहमति के संपत्ति लेना है। यह लूट तब बन जाती है जब चोरी करने के लिए बल, हिंसा या तत्काल मृत्यु/चोट की धमकी का उपयोग किया जाता है।

3. **Chori aur robbery mein kya farq hai?**
   Chori chupke se hoti hai. Lekin agar chor dara kar, maar-peet kar ya dhmka kar saaman chheene, toh woh robbery kahlati hai.

4. **Is snatching a mobile phone considered robbery?**
   If the snatcher uses criminal force, causes hurt, or creates a fear of injury while snatching the phone, it is considered robbery. If it's done stealthily without force against the person, it's theft or snatching.

5. **मोबाइल छीनना लूट है या चोरी?**
   यदि मोबाइल छीनते समय बल प्रयोग किया जाता है या चोट पहुंचाई जाती है, तो यह लूट है।

6. **Agar koi raste mein chain chheen le, toh kya woh robbery hai?**
   Agar chain chheente waqt gardan par chot lagti hai ya jhatka lagta hai (force use hota hai), toh police ise robbery (loot) ya snatching ke under BNS mein darj karegi.

7. **What is the minimum punishment for robbery under BNS?**
   The law prescribes rigorous imprisonment that can extend up to 10 years, or 14 years for highway robbery at night, plus a fine.

8. **क्या लूट के मामले में समझौता हो सकता है?**
   नहीं, लूट (Robbery) एक गैर-शमनीय (Non-compoundable) अपराध है, यानी इसमें अदालत के बाहर समझौता नहीं किया जा सकता।

9. **Kya robbery case mein compromise ho sakta hai?**
   Nahi, robbery ek non-compoundable offense hai. Isme victim aur accused ke beech out-of-court compromise legally valid nahi hota.

10. **How many people are required to make it a dacoity instead of robbery?**
    If 5 or more people jointly commit or attempt to commit a robbery, it is termed as dacoity under BNS Section 310.

11. **डकैती और लूट में क्या अंतर है?**
    जब 5 या उससे अधिक लोग मिलकर लूट करते हैं, तो उसे डकैती कहा जाता है।

12. **Agar 6 log milkar loot karein toh use kya kahenge?**
    BNS ke tahat agar 5 ya usse zyada log milkar robbery karte hain, toh use Dacoity (Dakaiti) kaha jaata hai, jiski saza aur bhi kadi hoti hai.

13. **What should I do immediately after being robbed?**
    Ensure your physical safety, call 112, do not touch the crime scene, and file an FIR at the nearest police station.

14. **लूट होने पर सबसे पहले क्या करें?**
    सुरक्षित स्थान पर जाएं, 112 डायल करें, घटनास्थल से छेड़छाड़ न करें और तुरंत FIR दर्ज कराएं।

15. **Loot hone par sabse pehle kya karna chahiye?**
    Apne aap ko safe karein, 112 par police ko call karein, bank cards block karein aur turant FIR likhwayen.

16. **Can I file an online FIR for robbery?**
    For heinous and violent crimes like robbery, physical presence at the police station is generally required to record detailed statements and medical examinations, though you can initiate the complaint online.

17. **क्या मैं लूट की ई-एफआईआर (e-FIR) दर्ज कर सकता हूँ?**
    लूट एक गंभीर अपराध है, इसलिए आमतौर पर विस्तृत बयान और चिकित्सा जांच के लिए पुलिस स्टेशन जाना आवश्यक होता है।

18. **Kya main robbery ki online FIR kar sakta hoon?**
    Robbery ek serious crime hai jisme violence involve hota hai, isliye aapko police station jaakar hi properly FIR file karni padti hai.

19. **What happens if the stolen property is never recovered?**
    The criminal trial for robbery continues based on witness testimonies and other evidence even if the property is not recovered. However, non-recovery makes the prosecution's case slightly harder to prove.

20. **क्या होगा यदि लूटा गया सामान वापस नहीं मिलता?**
    सामान न मिलने पर भी गवाहों और सबूतों के आधार पर केस चलता है और अपराधी को सजा हो सकती है।

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Pickpocketing is a form of robbery.
   **Fact:** Pickpocketing is theft. It only becomes robbery if the pickpocket threatens or hurts the victim upon being caught to escape with the property.
2. **Myth:** Robbery cases can be withdrawn if the robber returns the money.
   **Fact:** Robbery is a non-compoundable state offense; returning money does not erase the crime, and the state will prosecute.
3. **Myth:** Using a toy gun to steal isn't armed robbery.
   **Fact:** If the victim believes the gun is real and is put in fear of instant death, the offense constitutes robbery.
4. **Myth:** Robbery only happens at night.
   **Fact:** Robbery can happen at any time. However, highway robbery at night carries an enhanced punishment.
5. **Myth:** You must fight back to prove it was a robbery.
   **Fact:** No, submitting due to fear of violence is sufficient to prove robbery. Your safety is paramount.
6. **Myth:** You need witnesses to prove robbery.
   **Fact:** While helpful, a victim's sole, credible testimony supported by circumstantial evidence (like injuries) is enough for conviction.
7. **Myth:** Extortion and robbery are completely different and unrelated.
   **Fact:** Robbery is an aggravated form of extortion (or theft). When extortion is committed in the immediate presence of the victim under threat of instant harm, it is robbery.
8. **Myth:** The police won't file an FIR if the stolen amount is very small.
   **Fact:** The amount does not matter. The use of violence/threat makes it a serious cognizable offense, and police must register an FIR.
9. **Myth:** Dacoity is just another word for robbery.
   **Fact:** Legally, Dacoity is a distinct offense defined strictly by the involvement of 5 or more persons committing robbery.
10. **Myth:** Only the person whose property was stolen can file the FIR.
    **Fact:** Anyone who witnessed the robbery or is aware of the crime can report it to the police.

## Conversation Examples

**Scenario 1: Reporting a Highway Robbery**
**Citizen:** Hello, police? Main Highway pe hoon, kuch logon ne meri gaadi rok kar mujhe chaaku dikhaya aur mera laptop aur cash chheen liya. (Hello, police? I am on the highway, some people stopped my car, showed a knife and took my laptop and cash.)
**Call Operator (112):** Ghabraiye mat sir. Aap apni exact location batayein. Main turant PCR bhej raha hoon. Kya aapko koi chot aayi hai? (Don't panic sir. Tell me your exact location. I am sending a PCR immediately. Are you injured?)
**Citizen:** Nahi, main theek hoon, par wo log bikes par the aur aage nikal gaye. (No, I am fine, but they were on bikes and drove ahead.)
**Call Operator (112):** PCR 5 minute mein pahunch rahi hai. Aap gaadi mein hi rahein. Hum area barricade karwa rahe hain. (PCR is reaching in 5 mins. Stay in the car. We are barricading the area.)

**Scenario 2: Differentiating Theft and Robbery**
**Citizen:** Sir, meri dukaan ka taala tod kar raat ko saaman chori ho gaya. Kya yeh robbery (loot) hai? (Sir, the lock of my shop was broken at night and goods were stolen. Is this robbery?)
**Saarthi AI:** Nahi sir, yeh BNS ke tahat 'House-breaking' aur 'Theft' (chori) ka case hai, kyunki jab chori hui tab wahan koi maujood nahi tha jise dhamkaya gaya ho. Robbery tab hoti hai jab aapke samne aapko dara/dhamka kar saaman chheena jaye. (No sir, under BNS this is 'House-breaking' and 'Theft', because no one was present to be threatened. Robbery requires the presence of violence or threat against a person.)

**Scenario 3: Snatching Incident**
**Citizen:** Main market se jaa rahi thi, ek ladke ne jhatka maar kar meri chain chheen li, meri gardan pe chot bhi lagi hai. (I was walking through the market, a boy forcefully snatched my chain, my neck is also injured.)
**Saarthi AI:** Yeh ek serious offense hai. Kyunki isme force use hua aur aapko chot (hurt) lagi hai, yeh BNS ke tahat Robbery/Snatching ki category mein aayega. Aap turant nazdiki police station jaa kar FIR darj karein aur apna medical checkup karwayen. (This is a serious offense. Since force was used and you were hurt, it falls under Robbery/Snatching under BNS. Please go to the nearest police station immediately to file an FIR and get a medical checkup.)

**Scenario 4: Compromise Queries**
**Citizen:** Jin ladkon ne mera phone loota tha unke ghar wale phone wapas de rahe hain aur case wapas lene ko keh rahe hain. Kya main case wapas le sakta hoon? (The families of the boys who robbed my phone are returning it and asking me to withdraw the case. Can I withdraw it?)
**Saarthi AI:** Robbery ek non-compoundable offense hai. Iska matlab law ke according aap is case mein aapas mein samjhauta (compromise) nahi kar sakte. Ek baar FIR darj hone aur charge-sheet file hone ke baad, case court trial ke through hi khatam hoga. Aapko court ko poori sachai batani chahiye. (Robbery is non-compoundable. Meaning you cannot legally compromise. Once FIR is registered, the case can only conclude through a court trial. You must tell the truth to the court.)

**Scenario 5: Document Requirement**
**Citizen:** FIR likhwane ke liye mujhe kya documents dene honge? (What documents do I need to provide to file an FIR?)
**Saarthi AI:** Aapko apna ID proof (Jaise Aadhaar), loote gaye saaman ki list, aur agar phone chori hua hai toh uska IMEI number dena hoga. Agar chot lagi hai toh hospital ki MLC (Medico-Legal Certificate) bhi police record mein lagani hogi. (You need your ID proof, list of robbed items, IMEI number if phone is stolen. If injured, the hospital's MLC must be attached to the police record.)
