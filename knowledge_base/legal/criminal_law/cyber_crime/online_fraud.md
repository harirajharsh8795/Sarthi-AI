# Online Fraud and Financial Cyber Crimes in India

## Overview

**English:** Online fraud refers to any deceitful activity conducted via the internet, computer networks, or mobile telecommunications with the intent of unlawfully extracting money, personal data, or financial assets from victims. This encompasses phishing, UPI scams, fake e-commerce websites, identity theft, and loan app frauds.

**Hinglish:** Online fraud ka matlab hai internet, computer networks ya mobile communications ke zariye ki gayi aisi dhokhadhadi jiska maqsad victims se illegal tarike se paise, personal data ya financial assets nikalna hota hai. ## Legal Background

The legal framework governing online fraud in India primarily rests on the Information Technology Act, 2000 and the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS), which replaced the Indian Penal Code (IPC).

**Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000:**
- **Section 66:** Computer Related Offences (Hacking, data theft).
- **Section 66C:** Punishment for identity theft (using electronic signature, password, or unique identification feature of any other person).
- **Section 66D:** Punishment for cheating by personation by using computer resource.

**Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS):**
- **Section 318:** Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property (formerly IPC 420). This section applies when the fraudster deceives the victim to part with money.
- **Section 319:** Cheating by personation (formerly IPC 419). Applies when a fraudster pretends to be someone else (e.g., a bank official) to commit fraud.
- **Section 336 & 339:** Forgery of electronic records (formerly parts of IPC 468, 471).
- **Section 316:** Criminal Breach of Trust (applicable if an entrusted digital entity misuses funds).

**RBI Guidelines on Customer Liability:**
The Reserve Bank of India circular limits the liability of a customer in unauthorized electronic banking transactions if reported within 3 working days (Zero Liability).

## Applicability

- **Who is covered:** Any Indian citizen, resident, or corporation holding a bank account or digital wallet in India who suffers a financial loss due to online deceit.
- **Exemptions:** Negligence by the user (e.g., sharing OTPs or PINs willingly without coercion) may shift the financial liability to the user according to RBI guidelines, though the criminal liability of the fraudster remains.
- **Special Conditions:** The IT Act has extraterritorial jurisdiction under Section 75, meaning offenses committed outside India by any person targeting a computer or network located in India are punishable under Indian law.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English:**
1. **Immediate Block:** Call your bank’s customer care or use their mobile app to block the affected account, debit/credit card, and UPI ID immediately to prevent further loss.
2. **Call 1930:** Dial the National Cyber Crime Reporting Helpline (1930) and provide transaction details. They will attempt to freeze the fraudulent funds in the suspect's bank account.
3. **Register on NCRP:** Go to cybercrime.gov.in and file a detailed complaint. You will receive an acknowledgment number.
4. **Submit Bank Dispute:** Submit a formal dispute form to your bank along with the NCRP acknowledgment receipt.
5. **FIR Registration:** Based on the NCRP complaint, the local Cyber Police Station will evaluate and convert the complaint into an FIR under BNS 318, 319, and IT Act 66C, 66D.
6. **Investigation:** The police may require you to submit Section 65B (Evidence Act) certificates for electronic records, device logs, and give a statement.
7. **Court Proceedings:** Once the chargesheet is filed, the case moves to the Magistrate Court for trial and recovery of funds.

## Required Documents
- Identity Proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport).
- Bank statement showing the fraudulent transaction (stamped by the bank).
- SMS or Email alerts received during the transaction.
- Screenshots of WhatsApp chats, fake websites, or caller ID details of the fraudster.
- UPI Transaction IDs (e.g., 12-digit UTR number).
- Section 65B Evidence Act Certificate (a self-declaration authenticating digital evidence).
- Complaint acknowledgment copy from National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal.

## Government Authorities
- **Cyber Police Station:** Specialized local police unit handling tech-crimes.
- **Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C):** Nodal agency under MHA managing the 1930 helpline.
- **RBI Banking Ombudsman:** The appellate authority if the bank refuses to refund or fails to resolve the issue within 30 days.
- **Judicial Magistrate First Class (JMFC):** The court where IT Act and BNS cyber fraud trials take place.
- **Adjudicating Officer (IT Act):** For civil compensation claims up to Rs. 5 Crores under the IT Act (usually the IT Secretary of the State).

## Penalties & Punishments
- **BNS Section 318 (Cheating):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, or with fine, or both. If cheating induces delivery of property, imprisonment up to 7 years and a fine.
- **BNS Section 319 (Cheating by Personation):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, or with fine, or both.
- **IT Act Section 66C (Identity Theft):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rupees 1 Lakh.
- **IT Act Section 66D (Cheating by personation using computer resource):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rupees 1 Lakh.

## Exceptions
- The user is fully liable for the loss if they explicitly shared their OTP, PIN, or password out of gross negligence until they report the incident.
- Transactions made via cash handovers for online promises do not fall under cybercrime and are treated as standard cheating (BNS 318).

## Important Timelines
- **Golden Hour / Zero Liability Window:** Must report to the bank within **3 working days** (or 24-48 hours ideally) for maximum chances of a full refund.
- **Partial Liability Window:** Reporting within **4 to 7 working days**. Customer liability is capped (usually between Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 25,000 depending on account type).
- **Total Liability:** Reporting after 7 working days. The refund is subject to the bank's board-approved policy.
- **Bank Resolution:** The bank must resolve the complaint within **90 days** from the date of receipt.
- **Ombudsman Appeal:** Can be filed if the bank rejects the claim or does not reply within **30 days**.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Deleting the fraudulent SMS or WhatsApp chat in panic, destroying digital evidence.
- Failing to immediately report to the bank, relying solely on a police complaint.
- Refusing to submit the Section 65B certificate, rendering digital evidence inadmissible in court.
- Not noting down the 12-digit UPI UTR number or bank reference number.

## Safety Tips

**English:**
1. **Never Share OTP/PIN:** Banks, police, or government officials will never ask for your OTP, UPI PIN, or CVV.
2. **Verify URLs:** Always check that website links start with "https://" before entering payment details.
3. **Beware of Screen Sharing:** Never download remote access apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer on the advice of customer support callers.
4. **Check Unknown Links:** Do not click on links claiming your electricity will be disconnected, or a parcel is stuck at customs.

**Hindi Unicode:**
1. **कभी भी OTP/PIN साझा न करें:** बैंक, पुलिस या सरकारी अधिकारी कभी भी आपका OTP, UPI पिन या CVV नहीं मांगेंगे।

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**Q1: What should I do within the first 5 minutes of realizing I have been scammed online?**

**Hinglish:** RBI ki zero liability protection ke liye 3 din mein bank ko report karna zaroori hai. Badi amounts ke liye bank FIR mangte hain, par initial process ke liye NCRP portal ki complaint receipt kaafi hoti hai.

**Q4: How long does the bank take to resolve a cyber fraud dispute?**
**English:** As per RBI guidelines, the bank must resolve the customer’s complaint within 90 days from the date of reporting. During this period, the bank must conduct its internal investigation and credit the eligible amount back to the victim.
**Hindi Unicode:** आरबीआई के दिशानिर्देशों के अनुसार, बैंक को रिपोर्ट करने की तारीख से 90 दिनों के भीतर ग्राहक की शिकायत का समाधान करना होगा। इस अवधि के दौरान, बैंक को जांच करनी चाहिए और योग्य राशि वापस करनी चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** RBI guidelines ke anusar, bank ko report karne ke date se 90 din ke andar complaint resolve karni hoti hai. Is dauran, bank apni investigation karke eligible amount victim ke account mein credit karta hai.

**Q5: What if the bank refuses to refund my money despite it being a bank security flaw?**
**English:** If the bank rejects your claim or fails to respond within 30 days, you can file a free complaint with the RBI Banking Ombudsman online at the RBI CMS portal. You can also approach the Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission.
**Hindi Unicode:** यदि बैंक आपके दावे को खारिज करता है या 30 दिनों के भीतर जवाब नहीं देता है, तो आप RBI CMS पोर्टल पर RBI बैंकिंग लोकपाल के पास मुफ्त शिकायत दर्ज कर सकते हैं। आप उपभोक्ता विवाद निवारण आयोग से भी संपर्क कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Agar bank aapka claim reject kar de ya 30 din tak reply na kare, toh aap RBI CMS portal par RBI Banking Ombudsman ke paas muft complaint file kar sakte hain. Aap Consumer Court bhi ja sakte hain.

**Q6: Can the police track a fraudster who used a fake Truecaller name and pre-activated SIM?**
**English:** Yes, the Cyber Police use advanced digital forensics, IMEI tracking, IP address logs, and bank KYC trails of the recipient accounts (mule accounts) to track down the syndicates, even if the SIM was acquired with fake IDs.
**Hindi Unicode:** हां, साइबर पुलिस फर्जी आईडी वाले सिम के बावजूद सिंडिकेट को ट्रैक करने के लिए उन्नत डिजिटल फोरेंसिक, आईएमईआई ट्रैकिंग, आईपी एड्रेस लॉग और प्राप्तकर्ता खातों के बैंक केवाईसी ट्रेल्स का उपयोग करती है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, Cyber Police advanced digital forensics, IMEI tracking, IP logs aur recipient accounts ke KYC trail ka use karke criminals ko track kar sakti hai, chahe SIM fake ID par hi kyun na ho.

**Q7: I lost money through a crypto scam. Where do I report?**
**English:** Crypto scams are reported identically to fiat currency frauds. File a complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (NCRP). Additionally, contact the cryptocurrency exchange immediately to freeze the suspect's wallet addresses.
**Hindi Unicode:** क्रिप्टो घोटालों की रिपोर्ट फिएट मुद्रा धोखाधड़ी के समान ही की जाती है। राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध रिपोर्टिंग पोर्टल (NCRP) पर शिकायत दर्ज करें। इसके अतिरिक्त, संदिग्ध के वॉलेट को फ्रीज करने के लिए तुरंत क्रिप्टो एक्सचेंज से संपर्क करें।
**Hinglish:** Crypto scams bhi normal fraud ki tarah NCRP portal par report hote hain. Iske alawa, suspect ke wallet ko freeze karne ke liye turant crypto exchange ko contact karein.

**Q8: What is Section 65B of the Indian Evidence Act (now under Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam)?**
**English:** It is a mandatory certificate signed by the person producing electronic evidence (like screenshots or emails) affirming that the digital record is authentic and was generated by a computer operating normally in their lawful control.
**Hindi Unicode:** यह इलेक्ट्रॉनिक साक्ष्य (जैसे स्क्रीनशॉट) प्रस्तुत करने वाले व्यक्ति द्वारा हस्ताक्षरित एक अनिवार्य प्रमाण पत्र है, जो इस बात की पुष्टि करता है कि डिजिटल रिकॉर्ड प्रामाणिक है और उनके वैध नियंत्रण में सामान्य रूप से काम कर रहे कंप्यूटर द्वारा उत्पन्न किया गया था।
**Hinglish:** Yeh ek mandatory certificate hai jo digital evidence (jaise screenshots) submit karne wale ko sign karna hota hai, ye prove karne ke liye ki record asli hai aur unke control mein chal rahe computer se liya gaya hai.

**Q9: The scammer is blackmailing me with morphed photos. What should I do?**
**English:** Do not pay the extortion money. Paying once usually leads to endless demands. Immediately report it on the cybercrime.gov.in portal under "Women/Child Related Crime" (even if you are male) or general cybercrime, and block the caller.
**Hindi Unicode:** जबरन वसूली के पैसे न दें। एक बार भुगतान करने से आमतौर पर अंतहीन मांगें आती हैं। तुरंत इसे cybercrime.gov.in पोर्टल पर रिपोर्ट करें और कॉल करने वाले को ब्लॉक कर दें।
**Hinglish:** Extortion money bilkul na dein. Ek baar paise dene par demands badhti jayengi. Turant cybercrime.gov.in par report karein aur us caller ko block karein.

**Q10: Are loan app frauds considered online fraud?**
**English:** Yes. Unauthorized instant loan apps that extract your contact list and subsequently blackmail you or charge exorbitant processing fees commit severe offenses under BNS (extortion, cheating) and the IT Act (data theft).
**Hindi Unicode:** हां। अनधिकृत इंस्टेंट लोन ऐप जो आपकी संपर्क सूची निकालते हैं और बाद में आपको ब्लैकमेल करते हैं, बीएनएस (जबरन वसूली, धोखाधड़ी) और आईटी अधिनियम (डेटा चोरी) के तहत गंभीर अपराध करते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan. Unauthorized instant loan apps jo aapki contact list nikal kar blackmail karte hain, wo BNS (extortion, cheating) aur IT Act (data theft) ke antargat serious crime karte hain.

**Q11: How do I know if an e-commerce website is fake?**
**English:** Fake websites often lack an SSL certificate (no 'https://'), have grammatical errors, offer unbelievable discounts, lack verifiable contact details, and only accept prepaid UPI or bank transfers rather than Cash on Delivery.
**Hindi Unicode:** नकली वेबसाइटों में अक्सर एसएसएल प्रमाणपत्र ('https://' नहीं) का अभाव होता है, व्याकरण संबंधी त्रुटियां होती हैं, अविश्वसनीय छूट प्रदान करती हैं, और केवल प्रीपेड यूपीआई या बैंक हस्तांतरण स्वीकार करती हैं।
**Hinglish:** Fake websites mein aksar SSL certificate nahi hota ('https://' missing), grammar mistakes hoti hain, unbelievable discounts hote hain, aur wo Cash on Delivery ki jagah sirf prepaid UPI mangti hain.

**Q12: Can I report a cyber fraud anonymously?**
**English:** The National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal allows anonymous reporting for specific crimes (like CSAM or rape imagery), but for financial frauds, you must register with your real identity to facilitate investigations and recover funds.
**Hindi Unicode:** राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध रिपोर्टिंग पोर्टल विशिष्ट अपराधों के लिए गुमनाम रिपोर्टिंग की अनुमति देता है, लेकिन वित्तीय धोखाधड़ी के लिए, धन की वसूली और जांच की सुविधा के लिए आपको अपनी वास्तविक पहचान के साथ पंजीकरण करना होगा।
**Hinglish:** NCRP portal kuch crimes mein anonymous reporting allow karta hai, par financial frauds ke liye investigation aur paise wapas paane ke liye aapko apni real identity ke sath register karna hoga.

**Q13: My UPI app showed "Enter PIN to receive money". Is this real?**
**English:** No. You NEVER have to enter your UPI PIN to receive money; the PIN is only used for sending money or checking your balance. This is a classic reverse-request phishing scam.
**Hindi Unicode:** नहीं। पैसे प्राप्त करने के लिए आपको कभी भी अपना यूपीआई पिन दर्ज नहीं करना पड़ता है; पिन का उपयोग केवल पैसे भेजने या शेष राशि की जांच करने के लिए किया जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. Paise receive karne ke liye UPI PIN kabhi nahi dalna hota; PIN sirf paise bhejne ya balance check karne ke liye use hota hai. Ye ek classic reverse-request scam hai.

**Q14: What is an Adjudicating Officer under the IT Act?**
**English:** Under Section 46 of the IT Act, state-level IT Secretaries act as Adjudicating Officers. They have the power of a civil court to hear cases of data theft and financial frauds and can award compensation up to Rs. 5 Crores to victims.
**Hindi Unicode:** आईटी अधिनियम की धारा 46 के तहत, राज्य स्तरीय आईटी सचिव न्यायनिर्णायक अधिकारी के रूप में कार्य करते हैं। उन्हें डेटा चोरी और वित्तीय धोखाधड़ी के मामलों की सुनवाई करने और पीड़ितों को 5 करोड़ रुपये तक का मुआवजा देने का अधिकार है।
**Hinglish:** IT Act ke Section 46 ke tehat, state-level IT Secretaries Adjudicating Officers hote hain. Unke paas civil court ki power hoti hai aur wo victims ko Rs. 5 Crore tak ka compensation dila sakte hain.

**Q15: The police recovered my money but it's held in a frozen account. How do I get it?**
**English:** Once the police freeze the fraudulent amount, they submit a report to the local Magistrate. You must file an application under the relevant section of the BNSS (formerly CrPC 457) in the Magistrate Court to get a court order directing the bank to unfreeze and return your funds.
**Hindi Unicode:** एक बार पुलिस धोखाधड़ी की राशि को फ्रीज कर देती है, तो वे मजिस्ट्रेट को रिपोर्ट सौंपते हैं। बैंक को आपके फंड वापस करने का निर्देश देने वाला अदालती आदेश प्राप्त करने के लिए आपको बीएनएसएस (पहले सीआरपीसी 457) के तहत मजिस्ट्रेट कोर्ट में आवेदन दाखिल करना होगा।
**Hinglish:** Jab police fraud amount freeze karti hai, toh wo Magistrate ko report deti hai. Aapko Magistrate Court mein BNSS (pehle CrPC 457) ke tehat application file karni hogi, jiske baad court bank ko paise wapas karne ka order dega.

**Q16: Can I sue the telecom operator for issuing a fake SIM used to scam me?**
**English:** Suing telecom operators is complex. However, if systemic negligence in their KYC process is proven, you can include them as a party in civil compensation claims before the Adjudicating Officer or Consumer Court.
**Hindi Unicode:** दूरसंचार ऑपरेटरों पर मुकदमा करना जटिल है। हालांकि, यदि उनकी केवाईसी प्रक्रिया में व्यवस्थित लापरवाही साबित होती है, तो आप उन्हें उपभोक्ता अदालत में नागरिक मुआवजे के दावों में एक पक्ष के रूप में शामिल कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Telecom operators par case karna mushkil hai. Par agar KYC process mein unki laparwahi sabit hoti hai, toh aap unhe Consumer Court ya Adjudicating Officer ke saamne party bana sakte hain.

**Q17: Is it safe to use public Wi-Fi for banking?**
**English:** No. Public Wi-Fi networks are generally unencrypted, making it easy for hackers to intercept your passwords and session cookies using "Man-in-the-Middle" (MitM) attacks. Always use cellular data for banking.
**Hindi Unicode:** नहीं। सार्वजनिक वाई-फाई नेटवर्क आमतौर पर अनएन्क्रिप्टेड होते हैं, जिससे हैकर्स के लिए आपके पासवर्ड को इंटरसेप्ट करना आसान हो जाता है। बैंकिंग के लिए हमेशा सेलुलर डेटा का उपयोग करें।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. Public Wi-Fi networks usually unencrypted hote hain, jisse hackers "Man-in-the-Middle" attack ke zariye aapke passwords chura sakte hain. Banking ke liye hamesha mobile data use karein.

**Q18: What is a 'Mule Account'?**
**English:** A mule account is a bank account that belongs to a third party (often a poor individual or a student) who is paid a small commission by fraudsters to receive and transfer the stolen money, masking the fraudster's true identity.
**Hindi Unicode:** एक खच्चर (म्यूल) खाता वह बैंक खाता होता है जो किसी तीसरे पक्ष का होता है, जिसे जालसाजों द्वारा चोरी किए गए धन को प्राप्त करने और स्थानांतरित करने के लिए एक छोटा कमीशन दिया जाता है, जिससे जालसाज की वास्तविक पहचान छिप जाती है।
**Hinglish:** Mule account wo bank account hota hai jo kisi third party ka hota hai. Fraudsters unhe thoda commission dekar unke account mein stolen money mangwate hain taaki unki asli identity chhup sake.

**Q19: I clicked a phishing link but didn't enter any data. Am I safe?**
**English:** You are likely safe from credential theft if you didn't enter data. However, clicking the link might have downloaded malware in the background. It is advisable to run a reputed antivirus scan and monitor your device for unusual activity.
**Hindi Unicode:** यदि आपने डेटा दर्ज नहीं किया है तो आप सुरक्षित हैं। हालांकि, लिंक पर क्लिक करने से बैकग्राउंड में मैलवेयर डाउनलोड हो सकता है। एक प्रतिष्ठित एंटीवायरस स्कैन चलाना उचित है।
**Hinglish:** Agar aapne data enter nahi kiya toh aap safe hain. Lekin link click karne se background mein malware download ho sakta hai. Isliye ek achha antivirus scan run karna aur device ko monitor karna behtar hai.

**Q20: What role does I4C play in cyber crime?**
**English:** The Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C) operates the National Cybercrime Reporting Portal and the 1930 helpline. It acts as a bridge between victims, banks, payment gateways, and state police forces to rapidly trace and block illicit financial flows.
**Hindi Unicode:** भारतीय साइबर अपराध समन्वय केंद्र (I4C) राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध रिपोर्टिंग पोर्टल और 1930 हेल्पलाइन का संचालन करता है। यह अवैध वित्तीय प्रवाह को तेजी से ट्रैक करने और रोकने के लिए काम करता है।
**Hinglish:** Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C) NCRP portal aur 1930 helpline chalata hai. Ye victims, banks, payment gateways aur police ke beech bridge ka kaam karta hai taaki fraud amounts ko jaldi block kiya ja sake.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** The police cannot recover money sent to scammers.
   **Fact:** If reported within the "golden hour" via 1930, police can work with banks to freeze the funds before they are withdrawn or transferred to crypto.
2. **Myth:** My bank will refund me no matter what.
   **Fact:** RBI mandates refunds only for third-party breaches or bank-side flaws. If you willingly shared your OTP/PIN, the bank will reject zero-liability claims.
3. **Myth:** Cybercrime only targets uneducated individuals.
   **Fact:** Sophisticated spear-phishing and specialized impersonation scams regularly deceive highly educated professionals and tech experts.
4. **Myth:** Scammers calling from foreign numbers cannot be caught.
   **Fact:** Interpol cooperation and tracking of the financial trail (mule accounts inside India) often lead to domestic arrests of the syndicates.
5. **Myth:** I must find the cyber cell office to complain.
   **Fact:** You can register a cybercrime complaint online via cybercrime.gov.in from the comfort of your home, and then visit the nearest regular police station.
6. **Myth:** A VPN protects me from phishing.
   **Fact:** A VPN encrypts your connection, but if you willingly type your password into a fake website, the VPN cannot stop the fraudster from receiving it.
7. **Myth:** Entering a PIN is required to receive cash-backs.
   **Fact:** Cash-backs are credited directly to your account. You NEVER need to enter a PIN to receive money.
8. **Myth:** Cyber police will ask for my account password to investigate.
   **Fact:** No legitimate investigating officer will ask for your passwords or UPI PINs.
9. **Myth:** Cybercrimes below Rs. 10,000 are not investigated.
   **Fact:** Every complaint registered on the NCRP is tracked, and small amounts often link to mega-syndicates stealing crores.
10. **Myth:** Once an FIR is filed, the money comes back automatically.
    **Fact:** You must follow up and file an application under the BNSS in the Magistrate Court to formally get the frozen money released to your account.

## Government Services
- **Helpline 1930:** The National Cyber Crime Reporting toll-free number for reporting financial frauds immediately.
- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (NCRP):** Visit `cybercrime.gov.in` to officially file cyber crime complaints, track status, and report malicious links/apps.
- **RBI Ombudsman (CMS Portal):** File complaints against banks for failing to address fraud reports at `cms.rbi.org.in`.
- **Sanchar Saathi Portal:** Department of Telecommunications portal to check how many SIM cards are registered on your Aadhaar and block lost/stolen phones.
- **eCourts Services:** Track the status of your cyber fraud case once the police file a chargesheet in the Magistrate Court.
