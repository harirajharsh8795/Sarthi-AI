# Digital Arrest Cyber Fraud (डिजिटल अरेस्ट साइबर धोखाधड़ी)

## Overview

**English:** 'Digital Arrest' is a rising cyber fraud where scammers impersonate law enforcement agencies like the CBI, Police, Customs, or Narcotics Control Bureau via video calls (Skype, WhatsApp) to extort money. They falsely claim the victim's identity has been used in illegal activities and force them to stay on camera until they pay a 'security deposit' or 'penalty' to avoid physical arrest. Note: Under Indian Law, there is no concept of a 'Digital Arrest' over a video call.

Perpetrators use social engineering tactics, morphing technologies, and psychological manipulation to confine victims virtually. They masquerade as authorities and simulate a legal interrogation environment to deceive victims into believing they are under lawful custody pending financial clearance.

## Legal Background

The legal framework addressing Digital Arrest cyber frauds involves multiple statutes targeting impersonation, extortion, forgery, and cyber crimes. 

**History & Purpose:**
With the rise of widespread internet connectivity and digital payments, organized cyber-criminal syndicates have evolved from simple phishing to complex psychological manipulation (social engineering). The purpose of the legal provisions is to penalize unauthorized impersonation of public servants, digital forgery, and extortion carried out using computer resources.

**Applicable Acts:**
- Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS) [Replaced Indian Penal Code, 1860]
- Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act)
- Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 (BNSS) [Replaced Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973]
- Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023 (BSA) [Replaced Indian Evidence Act, 1872]

**Relevant Constitutional Articles:**
- **Article 21:** Right to Life and Personal Liberty. The fake "digital arrest" is an infringement on personal liberty through coercion.
- **Article 22:** Protection against arrest and detention in certain cases. Real arrests have strict constitutional safeguards which these scams obviously violate.

**Relevant Sections (BNS & IT Act):**
- **Section 204 BNS:** Impersonating a public servant. (Whoever pretends to hold any particular office as a public servant).
- **Section 308 BNS:** Extortion. (Intentionally putting a person in fear of any injury to dishonestly induce delivery of property).
- **Section 318 BNS:** Cheating. (Deceiving any person fraudulently or dishonestly inducing the person to deliver any property).
- **Section 319 BNS:** Cheating by personation. 
- **Section 336 BNS:** Forgery. (Making a false document, e.g., fake arrest warrants or ID cards).
- **Section 340 BNS:** Forged document or electronic record used as genuine.
- **Section 66C IT Act:** Identity theft. (Fraudulent use of electronic signature, password, or any other unique identification feature).
- **Section 66D IT Act:** Cheating by personation by using computer resource.

*Note: Arrest procedures are strictly governed by Section 35 to Section 62 of the BNSS, which absolutely mandate physical presence, a legitimate FIR, and grounds of arrest to be communicated, with zero provisions for virtual captivity.*

## Applicability

**Who is covered:**
- Every citizen of India and any person residing in India targeted by these cyber syndicates.
- Banks and financial intermediaries (subject to RBI guidelines on reporting and freezing fraudulent transactions).

**Who is exempt:**
- Genuine law enforcement officers operating within the bounds of BNSS (who will never demand money via digital wallets to "clear a case").

**Special Conditions:**
- If the perpetrators are operating from outside India, cross-border mutual legal assistance treaties (MLAT) and Interpol channels are engaged by the Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C).

## Rights of Citizens

**English:**
1. **Right to Refuse Virtual Detention:** No citizen is legally required to stay on a video call with anyone claiming to be a police officer.
2. **Right to Verification:** Citizens have the right to physically verify the identity of the officer by visiting the nearest local police station.
3. **Right to Proper Notice:** Under Section 35 of BNSS, an arrest requires a proper warrant (in non-cognizable offenses) or a Notice of Appearance (under Section 35(3) BNSS, equivalent to old 41A CrPC), physically served.
4. **Right to Legal Counsel:** The citizen has a fundamental right to consult an advocate.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English:**
1. **Immediate Action:** The moment you suspect a 'Digital Arrest' scam, disconnect the call immediately. Do not pay any money.
2. **Call 1930:** Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline (1930) as quickly as possible. Time is critical (golden hour) to freeze the transferred funds in the fraudsters' accounts.
3. **Online Portal Filing:** Register a detailed complaint on the official website: `cybercrime.gov.in`. Attach all screenshots, bank transaction IDs, and phone numbers used by the scammers.
4. **Bank Notification:** Contact your bank's customer care immediately to report the unauthorized/fraudulent transaction and block your account/cards to prevent further deduction.
5. **FIR Registration:** Based on the online complaint, the local Cyber Cell or police station will register a First Information Report (FIR) under relevant sections of BNS and IT Act.
6. **Investigation:** The police, often assisted by the Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C), will track the IP addresses, bank accounts (mule accounts), and telecom resources to apprehend the culprits.
7. **Court Proceedings:** Once the perpetrators are caught, a charge sheet is filed in the jurisdictional Magistrate's court for trial.
8. **Recovery:** If funds were frozen in time, the victim can file an application before the Magistrate under BNSS for the release of the frozen funds back into their account.

## Required Documents
- Identity Proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID).
- Bank statement showing the fraudulent deduction.
- Transaction SMS or UTR numbers from the bank.
- Screenshots of the WhatsApp/Skype call logs, phone numbers, and any fake documents sent by the scammers (e.g., fake CBI warrant).
- Screen recordings of the video call (if available).

## Government Authorities
- **Local Cyber Police Station:** Primary investigating agency.
- **Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C):** Under the Ministry of Home Affairs, coordinates across states for large-scale cyber frauds.
- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (NCRP):** The central hub for registering complaints.
- **Judicial Magistrate First Class (JMFC) / Chief Judicial Magistrate (CJM):** The court where the trial takes place and applications for release of frozen funds are moved.
- **Reserve Bank of India (RBI):** Regulates banks on timelines and protocols for returning fraudulently siphoned funds.

## Penalties & Punishments
(Note: The punishments are as per the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 and IT Act, 2000)

- **Impersonating a public servant (Sec 204 BNS):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both.
- **Extortion (Sec 308 BNS):** Imprisonment up to 7 years, and fine.
- **Cheating (Sec 318 BNS):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both. If the cheating involves delivery of property, imprisonment up to 7 years, and fine.
- **Cheating by personation (Sec 319 BNS):** Imprisonment up to 5 years, or fine, or both.
- **Forgery for purpose of cheating (Sec 336 & 338 BNS):** Imprisonment up to 7 years, and fine.
- **Identity Theft (Sec 66C IT Act):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rupees 1 Lakh.
- **Cheating by personation by using computer resource (Sec 66D IT Act):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to Rupees 1 Lakh.

## Exceptions
- None. There are no legal exceptions that allow a public servant or any individual to digitally arrest, extort, or demand security deposits over video calls to "avoid arrest". 

## Important Timelines
- **Golden Hour:** The first 1-2 hours after the fraud transaction are crucial. Reporting to 1930 within this time significantly increases the chances of the bank freezing the scammers' accounts before they withdraw the cash.
- **Bank Reporting:** According to RBI guidelines, unauthorized electronic banking transactions must be reported to the bank within 3 working days to limit the customer's liability.
- **Limitation Period:** As the offenses involve imprisonment extending beyond 3 years (e.g., Extortion, Forgery), there is generally no rigid limitation period under BNSS for taking cognizance of these severe offenses, though prompt FIR registration is vital to prevent loss of digital evidence.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Compliance out of fear:** Staying on the call and following the scammer’s instructions, believing them to be real police.
- **Delaying reporting:** Waiting for days to report the scam due to shame or fear, allowing scammers to route the money out of the banking system (often converted to cryptocurrency).
- **Deleting evidence:** Deleting the WhatsApp messages, call logs, or fake documents sent by the scammers out of panic. This destroys crucial evidence needed by the cyber police.
- **Not contacting the bank:** Failing to immediately inform the bank to stop further transactions or block compromised accounts.

## Safety Tips

**English:**
- **Never Pay to Avoid Arrest:** Real law enforcement will never ask you to transfer money to a bank account to "verify" it or to "pay a penalty" to avoid arrest.
- **Do Not Share OTPs or Screen:** Never share your screen over Skype/WhatsApp, and do not share OTPs, passwords, or UPI PINs.
- **Hang Up and Verify:** If someone claims to be the police on a call, hang up immediately. Call your local police station to verify.
- **Beware of Fake Backgrounds:** Scammers use fake police station setups as video backgrounds. Do not be intimidated by uniforms or sirens heard over the call.

- **Fake setup se na darein:** Scammers peeche nakli thane ka background lagate hain aur vardi pehnte hain. Unse darna nahi hai.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a Digital Arrest?**
   It is a scam where fraudsters impersonate law enforcement officers on video calls and falsely claim you are under arrest to extort money. There is no such provision in Indian law.

2. **Can the police arrest me over a WhatsApp or Skype call?**
   Absolutely not. An arrest under the BNSS requires the physical presence of police officers. Arrests cannot be executed over internet video calls.

3. **They showed me an official CBI arrest warrant with my name and Aadhaar. Is it real?**
   No. It is a forged document created using basic photo editing tools. The police do not send arrest warrants via WhatsApp or Skype.

4. **They said a parcel containing drugs was intercepted in my name. What should I do?**
   Disconnect the call. This is the standard script used by digital arrest scammers. If you are innocent, the police will conduct a proper physical investigation, not a video interrogation.

5. **Why do they ask me to transfer money?**
   They claim the money is a "security deposit" to verify your bank accounts and promise to refund it in 30 minutes. Once you transfer the money, they disappear.

6. **I already transferred money. Can I get it back?**
   You might, if you report it immediately to 1930. The cyber police can freeze the destination account. Delay reduces the chances of recovery.

7. **The caller knows my Aadhaar number and PAN. How?**
   Data leaks are common on the internet. Scammers buy your personal details from the dark web to make their fake calls seem authentic and terrifying.

8. **Is it legally required for me to keep my camera on during the call?**
   No. You have no legal obligation to answer such calls, keep your camera on, or isolate yourself in a room as instructed by the scammers.

9. **Can the scammers access my bank account just by calling me?**
   No, unless you share an OTP, a password, or a UPI PIN, or download a screen-sharing app (like AnyDesk) that gives them control over your device.

10. **They threatened to inform my employer and family. What do I do?**
   This is a psychological tactic to isolate you. Inform your family immediately that you are receiving extortion calls. Do not hide it out of fear.

11. **Where do I complain about a digital arrest scam?**
   Call 1930 immediately and file a detailed complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).

12. **Do I need to hire a lawyer if I get such a call?**
   If you have not paid any money and just received a call, you do not need a lawyer. Just report the number. If you lost a huge sum, a lawyer can help follow up with the court to unfreeze recovered funds.

13. **Can the local police station register an FIR for this?**
   Yes. If a cognizable offense like extortion (Sec 308 BNS) or cheating (Sec 318 BNS) has occurred, the local police or cyber cell must register an FIR.

14. **What if the bank refuses to help?**
   Banks are mandated by the RBI to register fraud complaints. If the bank refuses, you can escalate the matter to the Banking Ombudsman through the RBI website.

15. **The scammer was wearing a police uniform. How is that possible?**
   Scammers buy fake uniforms or rent them. They also build small studio sets that look like a real police station to deceive victims.

16. **Is 'Digital Arrest' mentioned in the BNS or BNSS?**
   No. "Digital Arrest" is not a legal term. It is a colloquial term coined to describe this specific type of cyber fraud. 

17. **What is the 'Golden Hour' in cyber fraud?**
   The Golden Hour refers to the critical first few hours after money is transferred. Reporting within this time maximizes the chance of freezing the fraudster's account.

18. **Will I be arrested for receiving an international parcel with illegal items?**
   Mere receipt or someone using your name does not make you automatically guilty. Actual law enforcement will investigate the chain of custody physically.

19. **Can they track my location if I answer a WhatsApp call?**
   They cannot track your precise GPS location just by answering a WhatsApp call. They rely entirely on fear to make you comply.

20. **Should I record the video call if this happens?**
   Yes, if possible, screen record the call. It serves as excellent electronic evidence (admissible under BSA) to prove impersonation and extortion.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** "Digital Arrest" is a new law introduced by the government.
   **Fact:** There is no such law. It is a completely fake concept invented by cybercriminals to scare people.

2. **Myth:** Police can interrogate you on Skype and demand penalty fees.
   **Fact:** Police can never demand a penalty or security deposit to clear your name. Legal fines are paid in courts, never to random bank accounts.

3. **Myth:** A warrant sent on WhatsApp is a legally binding document.
   **Fact:** Warrants are issued by courts and physically executed by police under BNSS. WhatsApp warrants are forged documents.

4. **Myth:** If scammers know my Aadhaar number, they have already hacked my bank.
   **Fact:** Knowing an Aadhaar number does not give access to a bank account. They still need an OTP or your authorization to move money.

5. **Myth:** The money is gone forever once transferred.
   **Fact:** Prompt reporting to 1930 within the Golden Hour can freeze the money in the scammer's mule accounts before it is withdrawn.

6. **Myth:** Scammers only target uneducated people.
   **Fact:** Highly educated professionals, doctors, and tech workers frequently fall for this due to the extreme psychological pressure and sophisticated setups.

7. **Myth:** I must not tell my family because it is a "secret investigation".
   **Fact:** This is exactly what the scammers want. Always involve your family or a lawyer immediately.

8. **Myth:** The police will laugh at me if I report that I got scammed.
   **Fact:** Cyber police are well aware of this massive nationwide scam and have specialized teams (I4C) dealing with it daily.

9. **Myth:** I will get arrested if I disconnect the call.
   **Fact:** Disconnecting the call is your best defense. Nothing will happen to you.

10. **Myth:** The RBI secures all transactions, so I will get a refund automatically.
    **Fact:** You must report the unauthorized transaction within 3 days. Automatic refunds do not happen in cases where the user authorized the transfer (even under duress).

## Government Services

**English:**
- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (NCRP):** `cybercrime.gov.in` allows citizens to report digital arrest frauds online.
- **1930 Helpline:** A 24/7 national helpline integrated with banks and police to freeze fraudulent transactions in real-time.
- **I4C (Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre):** An apex body coordinating efforts against cyber crime syndicates.
- **eCourts Services:** Can be used to track the status of the court case if the perpetrators are caught.
