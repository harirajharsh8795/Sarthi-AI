# Phishing and Identity Theft (Cyber Crime)

## Overview
**English:** Phishing is a severe cybercrime where malicious actors deceive individuals into revealing sensitive personal and financial information, such as passwords, banking credentials, and OTPs. Fraudsters typically impersonate legitimate organizations, banks, or government agencies via emails, phone calls (vishing), or text messages (smishing). Under Indian law, these offenses are heavily penalized through a combination of the Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000, and the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, which work together to prosecute digital impersonation and financial fraud.

## Definition
**English:** Phishing refers to the fraudulent practice of sending emails or other electronic communications purporting to be from a reputable company or individual in order to induce individuals to reveal personal information. When this leads to the unauthorized use of an individual's identity, it constitutes Identity Theft (Section 66C of IT Act) and Cheating by Personation (Section 66D of IT Act).

## Legal Background
The legal mechanisms addressing phishing involve specific penal provisions targeting cyber impersonation, data theft, and financial cheating:
- **Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act):**
  - *Section 43:* Imposes civil liability (compensation) for unauthorized access, downloading, or extraction of data.
  - *Section 66:* Punishes computer-related offenses (dishonestly or fraudulently acting under Section 43).
  - *Section 66C:* Identity Theft (fraudulent use of electronic signature, password, or any other unique identification feature).
  - *Section 66D:* Cheating by personation by using computer resource.
- **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023:**
  - *Section 318:* Cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property (replaces IPC 420).
  - *Section 319:* Cheating by personation (replaces IPC 419).
  - *Section 336:* Forgery, including forgery for the purpose of cheating.
- **RBI Circular (2017) on Customer Protection – Limiting Liability of Customers in Unauthorised Electronic Banking Transactions:** Mandates banks to credit (shadow reversal) the amount involved in unauthorized electronic transactions if reported within 3 working days.

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** Any individual, corporate entity, or organization whose digital identity or credentials have been compromised within or outside India, provided the target computer system/network is located in India (Section 75, IT Act).
- **Who is exempt:** Law enforcement agencies performing lawful interception and ethical hackers holding explicitly authorized mandates from the system owners (under strict conditions).
- **Special Conditions:** Intermediaries (banks, telecom providers, platforms) must provide assistance to investigating agencies under Section 69 of the IT Act.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Immediate Halt:** Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at `1930` immediately after discovering the fraud. Provide the transaction details (transaction ID, bank names, amount).
2. **Bank Notification:** Simultaneously email and call your bank's customer care to block your card/account and formally dispute the transaction.
3. **Portal Registration:** Visit `cybercrime.gov.in`, create an account, and file a detailed complaint under the "Financial Fraud" section. Upload all screenshots and evidence.
4. **FIR Conversion:** The online complaint is routed to the nodal cyber police station. The police will conduct a preliminary inquiry and register an FIR under Section 66C/66D of the IT Act and Section 318/319 of the BNS.
5. **Investigation:** The police will issue notices under Section 94 BNSS to banks, telecom providers, and internet platforms to trace the IP address, money trail, and KYC documents of the accused.
6. **Charge Sheet & Trial:** Upon identifying the accused, a charge sheet is filed before the Judicial Magistrate First Class. The trial proceeds with the presentation of electronic evidence accompanied by a Section 63 BSA (Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam) certificate.

## Required Documents
1. A drafted summary of the incident (Date, Time, Medium of approach).
2. Bank account statements highlighting the disputed transactions.
3. Transaction reference numbers / UTR numbers.
4. Screenshots of the phishing SMS, WhatsApp chat, or fake website URL.
5. Soft copy of original email with complete headers (if via email).
6. Copy of the complainant’s Aadhar Card or PAN Card.
7. Call recordings or CDRs (if available).

## Government Authorities
- **Cyber Crime Cell / Police Station:** Primary investigating authority.
- **National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB):** Manages the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal.
- **Bank Nodal Officers:** Responsible for halting transactions based on police requests.
- **Adjudicating Officer (IT Act):** Appointed at the state level (usually IT Secretary) for deciding civil compensation claims under Section 43/46 of the IT Act.
- **Judicial Magistrate First Class (JMFC):** The judicial authority that conducts the criminal trial.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **IT Act Section 66C (Identity Theft):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine up to ₹1 Lakh.
- **IT Act Section 66D (Cheating by Personation using Computer Resource):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine up to ₹1 Lakh.
- **BNS Section 318 (Cheating):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both. If cheating induces delivery of property, imprisonment may extend to 7 years and fine.
- **BNS Section 319 (Cheating by Personation):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both.

## Exceptions
1. White-hat hacking or penetration testing explicitly authorized by the target organization.
2. Cases where identity usage was with the explicit, informed, and documented consent of the individual.
3. Lack of *mens rea* (dishonest or fraudulent intent), such as accidental clicking or benign forwarding of links without knowledge of malicious payload (though gross negligence might still attract civil liability).

## Important Timelines
- **3 Working Days:** Golden window to report unauthorized transactions to the bank to ensure Zero Customer Liability (as per RBI).
- **4 to 7 Working Days:** Window to report where customer liability is capped at ₹10,000 to ₹25,000 depending on the account type.
- **Beyond 7 Working Days:** Customer liability is determined per the bank's Board-approved policy (often full liability falls on the customer).
- **Statute of Limitations:** None for serious cybercrimes carrying punishments of 3+ years, but immediate reporting is practically necessary to freeze funds.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Deleting the evidence:** Victims often delete the fraudulent SMS or clear their browser history out of panic. This destroys crucial electronic evidence.
- **Delaying the Bank Complaint:** Filing a police complaint but forgetting to officially dispute the transaction with the bank within 3 days, losing the RBI zero-liability protection.
- **Failing to file a Section 63 BSA Certificate:** In court, printouts of electronic records must be accompanied by a certificate under Section 63 of the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam to be admissible as evidence.

## Safety Tips
**English:**
1. Never share your OTP, PIN, CVV, or passwords with anyone claiming to be from a bank or police.

3. Do not click on unverified short links received via SMS/WhatsApp claiming your electricity will be cut or KYC is pending.
4. Always verify the actual URL in your browser before entering credentials.

**Hinglish:** Agar fraud hone ke 3 din ke andar bank ko bata diya jaye aur galti aapki na ho, toh aapka koi paisa nahi katega.

**Q9: How do scammers use 'Screen Sharing' apps for phishing?**
**English:** Scammers convince victims to download apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer under the guise of 'KYC update' or 'customer support'. They then see the OTP on the victim's screen and steal funds.
**Hindi:** जालसाज पीड़ितों को 'केवाईसी अपडेट' के बहाने AnyDesk या TeamViewer जैसे ऐप डाउनलोड करने के लिए मनाते हैं और ओटीपी देख लेते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Scammers KYC update ke naam par AnyDesk jaisi app download karwate hain aur screen share karke OTP padh lete hain.

**Q10: Are SMS headers (like AD-HDFCBK) reliable?**
**English:** Not always. Scammers use illegal SMS spoofing services to send texts with official-looking headers. Always independently verify instructions by calling the official customer care number.
**Hindi:** हमेशा नहीं। जालसाज आधिकारिक दिखने वाले हेडर के साथ टेक्स्ट भेजने के लिए अवैध एसएमएस स्पूफिंग सेवाओं का उपयोग करते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Hamesha nahi. Scammers fake SMS headers ka use kar sakte hain. Bank ka official number hamesha check karein.

**Q11: Can I claim compensation from the bank if their system was hacked?**
**English:** Yes. If the unauthorized transaction occurred due to a deficiency in the bank's security architecture, the bank must refund the entire amount, regardless of when you reported it.
**Hindi:** हां। यदि बैंक की सुरक्षा वास्तुकला में कमी के कारण लेनदेन हुआ है, तो बैंक को पूरी राशि वापस करनी होगी।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar bank ka system hack hua hai aur unki galti hai, toh unhe poora paisa wapas karna padega.

**Q12: What is the punishment for cheating by impersonation under BNS?**
**English:** Under Section 319 of the BNS, cheating by personation carries a punishment of up to 3 years imprisonment, a fine, or both.
**Hindi:** बीएनएस की धारा 319 के तहत, प्रतिरूपण द्वारा धोखाधड़ी के लिए 3 साल तक की जेल, जुर्माना या दोनों का दंड है।
**Hinglish:** BNS Section 319 ke under nakli pehchan banakar dhoka dene par 3 saal tak ki jail aur fine ho sakta hai.

**Q13: How is a digital certificate (BSA Section 63) generated?**
**English:** The person who owns or operates the computer/phone from which the printout (screenshot, email) was taken must sign a statutory certificate declaring that the device was functioning normally.
**Hindi:** जिस व्यक्ति के पास कंप्यूटर/फोन है जिससे प्रिंटआउट लिया गया था, उसे एक वैधानिक प्रमाण पत्र पर हस्ताक्षर करना होगा।
**Hinglish:** Jis mobile ya laptop se screenshot liya gaya hai, uske owner ko ek certificate sign karna hota hai ki device theek kaam kar raha tha.

**Q14: Can the Adjudicating Officer award compensation?**
**English:** Yes, the IT Secretary of a State acts as the Adjudicating Officer. They can award civil compensation up to ₹5 Crores for data theft or financial loss due to contravention of the IT Act.
**Hindi:** हां, राज्य के आईटी सचिव न्यायनिर्णायक अधिकारी के रूप में कार्य करते हैं और मुआवजे का आदेश दे सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, Adjudicating Officer IT Act ke under compensation (muawza) dilwa sakte hain agar data chori ya financial loss hua ho.

**Q15: What is the difference between phishing, smishing, and vishing?**
**English:** Phishing generally implies fraudulent emails. Smishing refers to fraudulent SMS texts. Vishing refers to fraudulent voice calls. All are treated under the same penal provisions.
**Hindi:** फ़िशिंग का तात्पर्य धोखाधड़ी वाले ईमेल से है। स्मिशिंग का मतलब एसएमएस है और विशिंग का मतलब वॉयस कॉल है।
**Hinglish:** Phishing matlab fake email, smishing matlab fake SMS, aur vishing matlab fake call. Kanoon mein sabki saza ek jaisi hai.

**Q16: How long does the cyber police take to investigate?**
**English:** Initial freezing of accounts can happen within hours if reported to 1930. However, identifying the perpetrators and filing a charge sheet can take months depending on the complexity of the digital trail.
**Hindi:** 1930 पर रिपोर्ट करने पर खातों को घंटों के भीतर फ्रीज किया जा सकता है। लेकिन चार्जशीट दाखिल करने में महीनों लग सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Report karne ke kuch ghanto mein account freeze ho sakta hai, par scammer ko pakadne aur charge sheet file hone mein mahino lag sakte hain.

**Q17: Is my PAN card safe if I upload it on random sites?**
**English:** No. Uploading PAN cards on unverified sites can lead to identity theft. Fraudsters use this to open shell bank accounts or apply for instant loans in your name.
**Hindi:** नहीं। असत्यापित साइटों पर पैन कार्ड अपलोड करने से पहचान की चोरी हो सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. Fake sites par PAN card upload karne se identity theft ho sakti hai aur scammer aapke naam par loan le sakta hai.

**Q18: What if the fraudster transferred money to a cryptocurrency wallet?**
**English:** Tracing cryptocurrency is complex but not impossible. Cyber cells coordinate with crypto exchanges to trace the wallet address. Immediate reporting is critical before the funds are washed through mixers.
**Hindi:** क्रिप्टोकरेंसी का पता लगाना जटिल है लेकिन असंभव नहीं है। साइबर सेल क्रिप्टो एक्सचेंजों के साथ समन्वय करते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Crypto transactions trace karna mushkil hai par namumkin nahi. Police crypto exchanges se detail maang sakti hai.

**Q19: I received a blackmail email saying my webcam was hacked. Is it real?**
**English:** Mostly no. This is an extortion/phishing scam called "Sextortion phishing." The scammers use old leaked passwords from databases to scare you. Do not pay. Report it.
**Hindi:** यह एक जबरन वसूली घोटाला है। जालसाज आपको डराने के लिए डेटाबेस से पुराने लीक हुए पासवर्ड का उपयोग करते हैं। भुगतान न करें।
**Hinglish:** Yeh ek sextortion scam hai. Scammer purane leak hue password dikha kar darate hain. Paise na dein aur report karein.

**Q20: Can I track the status of my complaint?**
**English:** Yes, you can track the progress of your complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (`cybercrime.gov.in`) using your acknowledgment number.
**Hindi:** हां, आप अपनी शिकायत की स्थिति को राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध रिपोर्टिंग पोर्टल पर ट्रैक कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, aap apne complaint number se cybercrime portal par status check kar sakte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Cybercrimes can only be reported where the scammer lives.
   **Fact:** Cybercrimes have global jurisdiction. You can report it from your local police station or via the national portal.
2. **Myth:** The bank will automatically refund money lost to phishing.
   **Fact:** Banks only refund under Zero Liability rules if reported within 3 days and if the customer did not willingly share the OTP.
3. **Myth:** Phishing only happens via email.
   **Fact:** It frequently happens via SMS (smishing), WhatsApp, and voice calls (vishing).
4. **Myth:** Cyber Police cannot recover money transferred outside the state.
   **Fact:** The 1930 system integrates all Indian banks and can freeze suspect accounts pan-India instantly.
5. **Myth:** Deleting the fraudulent app/message makes me safe.
   **Fact:** Deleting it destroys digital evidence. Take screenshots and backup evidence before removing the app.
6. **Myth:** Antivirus software makes me 100% immune to phishing.
   **Fact:** Antivirus does not prevent you from willingly giving up your OTP to a convincing caller. Human vigilance is required.
7. **Myth:** Only rich people are targeted for phishing.
   **Fact:** Scammers run automated campaigns targeting thousands of citizens across all income brackets.
8. **Myth:** An FIR is necessary to block a bank card.
   **Fact:** No, banks must block cards instantly upon customer request. FIR is needed for criminal investigation.
9. **Myth:** Using "Incognito mode" protects against phishing links.
   **Fact:** Incognito mode only stops local history saving. It does not block phishing sites or malware.
10. **Myth:** Government portals charge fees to register cyber complaints.
    **Fact:** The National Cyber Crime Portal and 1930 are absolutely free public services.

## Government Services
- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (NCRP):** `cybercrime.gov.in` - The apex portal by the Ministry of Home Affairs to report all cybercrimes. (गृह मंत्रालय द्वारा साइबर अपराध रिपोर्ट करने के लिए शीर्ष पोर्टल)
- **1930 Helpline:** Centralised Citizen Financial Cyber Fraud Reporting System to freeze fraudulent money transfers. (वित्तीय धोखाधड़ी को रोकने के लिए केंद्रीकृत हेल्पलाइन)
- **Sanchar Saathi Portal:** `sancharsaathi.gov.in` - Used to track lost mobile phones (CEIR) and check how many mobile connections are registered in your name to prevent identity theft. (खोए हुए फोन को ट्रैक करने और पहचान की चोरी को रोकने के लिए)
- **RBI Ombudsman:** To escalate complaints if a bank fails to follow the Zero Liability guidelines.
