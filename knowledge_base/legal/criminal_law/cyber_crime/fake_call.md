# Fake Call Fraud and Cyber Scams

## Overview
**English:** Fake call fraud, commonly known as vishing (voice phishing), is a type of cybercrime where perpetrators make phone calls pretending to be bank officials, government representatives, or customer service agents to deceive victims into revealing sensitive personal and financial information. This information is then misused to siphon money from the victim's bank accounts or commit identity theft. With the rise of digital banking, these scams have become highly sophisticated, employing psychological manipulation and urgency to bypass the victim's critical thinking.

## Definition
**English:** Vishing (Voice Phishing) is the criminal practice of using social engineering over the telephone system to gain access to private personal and financial information from the public for the purpose of financial reward. It constitutes cheating by personation, extortion, and cyber fraud under Indian legal frameworks.

## Legal Background
The legal framework combating fake call frauds in India has evolved, especially with the introduction of the new criminal laws. The primary statutes include the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023, the Information Technology (IT) Act 2000, and guidelines issued by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI).

**History & Purpose:** Initially, traditional fraud was covered under the Indian Penal Code (IPC). As technology advanced, the IT Act was enacted to address electronic deception. The recent BNS modernization consolidates and strengthens the provisions against organized cybercrime, cheating by impersonation, and digital extortion, ensuring a faster and more robust justice delivery system.

**Applicable Acts:**
1. The Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023
2. The Information Technology Act, 2000
3. The Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023
4. The Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023
5. Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) Regulations

**Relevant Sections:**
- **BNS Section 318:** Deals with cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property (formerly IPC 420). Covers instances where the victim is tricked into transferring money.
- **BNS Section 319:** Cheating by personation (formerly IPC 416). Applicable when the caller pretends to be a bank official or police officer.
- **BNS Section 308:** Extortion (formerly IPC 383). Relevant in "digital arrest" or blackmail calls.
- **BNS Section 111:** Organized Crime. Applied when a syndicate or call center is operating the scam.
- **IT Act Section 66C:** Punishment for identity theft. Applicable when the scammer uses the victim's electronic signature, password, or any other unique identification feature fraudulently.
- **IT Act Section 66D:** Punishment for cheating by personation by using a computer resource. Specifically targets digital and telephonic impersonation.

## Applicability
**Who is Covered:** Every citizen, resident, or corporation in India who receives a fraudulent call or loses money/data due to telephonic deception is protected under these laws.
**Who is Exempt:** No individual or organization is exempt from prosecution if they participate in cyber fraud. However, telecom operators acting as mere intermediaries may claim safe harbor under Section 79 of the IT Act, provided they comply with government directives and due diligence.
**Special Conditions:** For banking frauds, if the customer reports the unauthorized transaction to the bank within 3 working days, they have zero liability as per RBI guidelines, provided the customer did not deliberately share the OTP/PIN.

## Rights of Citizens
**English:**
1. **Right to Zero Liability:** If unauthorized transactions are reported to the bank within 3 days, the citizen bears no financial loss.

3. **Right to Privacy:** Citizens have the right to refuse to share personal information like Aadhaar, PAN, or banking details over a phone call.
4. **Right to Legal Recourse:** Victims can approach the Cyber Crime Cell or Banking Ombudsman for grievance redressal.

**English:**

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Immediate Action:** Disconnect the fake call immediately. Do not engage further or click any links sent via SMS/WhatsApp during the call.
2. **Call 1930:** Dial the National Cyber Crime Reporting Helpline (1930) immediately. The "Citizen Financial Cyber Fraud Reporting and Management System" will attempt to freeze the defrauded amount in the suspect's bank account.
3. **Inform Your Bank:** Call your bank's official customer care to block your account/cards and report the unauthorized transaction. Ask for a complaint reference number.
4. **File Online Complaint:** Visit `cybercrime.gov.in` and file a detailed complaint under "Report Cyber Fraud". Upload screenshots, call recordings, and bank statements.
5. **Register FIR:** Based on the online complaint, the local Cyber Cell will convert it into an FIR under BNS and IT Act provisions. You can also visit the nearest police station to file a Zero FIR.
6. **Investigation:** The Investigating Officer (IO) will requisition Call Detail Records (CDR) and bank account KYC details of the suspects.
7. **Banking Ombudsman:** If the bank fails to resolve the issue or refund the amount within 30 days (provided you reported within 3 days), file a complaint with the RBI Ombudsman.
8. **Court Proceedings:** Upon charge-sheet filing, the case moves to the Judicial Magistrate First Class (JMFC) or Sessions Court, depending on the severity and BNS sections invoked.

## Required Documents
- Detailed written complaint explaining the sequence of events.
- Call logs showing the fraudster's phone number and time of call.
- SMS logs containing the fraudulent links or OTP messages (do not delete them).
- Bank statements highlighting the unauthorized transactions.
- Copy of identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN).
- Screenshots of any WhatsApp/Telegram communication if the call transitioned to text.
- Bank's acknowledgment of the complaint.

## Government Authorities
1. **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (I4C):** The central agency operating the 1930 helpline and cybercrime.gov.in.
2. **Local Police / Cyber Cell:** The primary law enforcement agency for investigating and arresting the perpetrators.
3. **Reserve Bank of India (RBI) Ombudsman:** For resolving disputes between citizens and banks regarding refunds for unauthorized transactions.
4. **Department of Telecommunications (DoT):** Facilitates the blocking of fraudulent phone numbers through the Sanchar Saathi portal (Chakshu facility).
5. **Telecom Service Providers (Jio, Airtel, Vi, etc.):** Required to cooperate with law enforcement by providing CDRs and blocking numbers.
6. **Courts:** Judicial Magistrate First Class or Sessions Courts for trial.

## Penalties & Punishments
The punishments under the new Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) and IT Act are severe to deter organized cybercrime:
- **Cheating (BNS Sec 318):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, or with fine, or with both. If it involves a massive fraud, imprisonment up to 7 years and a fine.
- **Cheating by Personation (BNS Sec 319):** Imprisonment up to 5 years, or with fine, or both.
- **Extortion (BNS Sec 308):** Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.
- **Organized Crime (BNS Sec 111):** Imprisonment from 5 years to life, and a fine not less than five lakh rupees, if the fraud is part of a larger syndicate operation.
- **Identity Theft (IT Act Sec 66C):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine up to 1 lakh rupees.
- **Cheating by Personation using Computer Resource (IT Act Sec 66D):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine up to 1 lakh rupees.

## Exceptions
- **Authorized Callers:** Genuine calls from your bank for verification (though they will NEVER ask for OTP, PIN, or CVV).
- **Good Faith Actions:** If a person genuinely misdialed and no fraudulent intent is proven, it does not constitute a crime.
- **Telecom Intermediary Exemption:** Telecom companies cannot be held directly liable for the fraud unless they colluded with the scammers or ignored government takedown notices (IT Act Sec 79).

## Important Timelines
- **Reporting for Zero Liability:** Must report unauthorized transactions to the bank within **3 working days**.
- **Partial Liability:** If reported between **4 to 7 working days**, liability is capped based on the RBI guidelines (usually between ₹10,000 to ₹25,000 depending on account type).
- **Full Liability:** If reported after **7 working days**, the refund is subject to the bank's board-approved policy, often resulting in no refund from the bank.
- **FIR Registration:** There is no strict limitation period for serious offenses under BNS (punishable with >3 years), but immediate reporting is necessary for recovery of funds.
- **Ombudsman Complaint:** Can be filed if the bank does not reply within **30 days** of the complaint.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Delaying the Report:** Waiting days to report out of embarrassment, which allows the fraudsters to move the money across multiple layers of accounts or withdraw it in cash, making recovery impossible.
- **Deleting Evidence:** Deleting call logs, SMS, or WhatsApp chats out of panic. This destroys crucial digital evidence.
- **Not Informing the Bank:** Only calling the police but forgetting to block the card/account with the bank.
- **Filing in Wrong Jurisdiction:** Believing that you must travel to the city where the call originated. The BNSS allows filing a Zero FIR anywhere.
- **Trusting Search Engine Numbers:** Googling "Bank Customer Care" and calling the first sponsored ad, which is often another scammer.

## Safety Tips
**English:**
- Never share OTP, CVV, PIN, or UPI PIN with anyone, even if they claim to be from the bank or police.
- The UPI PIN is only required to SEND money, never to RECEIVE money.
- Do not download remote access apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer if asked by an unknown caller.
- Verify callers by hanging up and dialing the official number printed on the back of your debit card.

- यदि किसी अज्ञात कॉलर द्वारा कहा जाए तो एनीडेस्क या टीमव्यूअर जैसे रिमोट एक्सेस ऐप डाउनलोड न करें।
- फोन काटकर अपने डेबिट कार्ड के पीछे छपे आधिकारिक नंबर को डायल करके कॉलर्स को सत्यापित करें।

**Hinglish:**
- Apna OTP, CVV, PIN, ya UPI PIN kisi ko na batayein, chahe wo bank manager ya police hi kyun na ban jaye.
- UPI PIN sirf paise BHEJNE ke liye hota hai, mangwane ke liye nahi.
- Caller ke kehne par AnyDesk ya TeamViewer jaise apps bilkul download na karein.
- Call kaat kar apne ATM card ke peeche likhe official number par call karke verify karein.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What should I do immediately after realizing I gave my OTP to a fake caller?**
**English:** Instantly disconnect the call and dial 1930 to report the cyber fraud. Then, call your bank's official customer care to block your account and cards. Finally, file a formal complaint on cybercrime.gov.in.
**Hindi:** तुरंत कॉल काट दें और साइबर धोखाधड़ी की रिपोर्ट करने के लिए 1930 डायल करें। फिर, अपने खाते और कार्ड को ब्लॉक करने के लिए अपने बैंक के आधिकारिक ग्राहक सेवा पर कॉल करें। अंत में, cybercrime.gov.in पर औपचारिक शिकायत दर्ज करें।
**Hinglish:** Turant call kaate aur 1930 par call karke fraud report karein. Uske baad bank customer care ko call karke account aur cards block karwayein. Last mein cybercrime.gov.in par complaint darj karein.

**Q2: Will I get my money back if I report the fake call fraud?**
**English:** If you report to 1930 immediately, the authorities can freeze the money before it is withdrawn. Additionally, if you report the unauthorized transaction to your bank within 3 working days, RBI guidelines mandate that the bank must refund your money (zero liability).
**Hindi:** यदि आप तुरंत 1930 पर रिपोर्ट करते हैं, तो अधिकारी पैसे निकाले जाने से पहले उसे फ्रीज कर सकते हैं। यदि आप 3 कार्य दिवसों के भीतर बैंक को अनधिकृत लेनदेन की रिपोर्ट करते हैं, तो बैंक को आपका पैसा वापस करना होगा।
**Hinglish:** Agar aap 1930 par turant report karte hain, toh police paise freeze kar sakti hai. Aur agar aap 3 working days ke andar bank ko report karte hain, toh RBI rules ke mutabiq bank ko paise refund karne honge.

**Q3: The caller knew my Aadhaar and PAN details. Does that mean they are genuine?**
**English:** No. Fraudsters often buy leaked data from the dark web or shady data brokers. Just because they know your personal details does not mean they are legitimate. Never confirm or provide additional information.
**Hindi:** नहीं। जालसाज अक्सर डार्क वेब से लीक हुआ डेटा खरीदते हैं। केवल इसलिए कि वे आपके व्यक्तिगत विवरण जानते हैं, इसका मतलब यह नहीं है कि वे वैध हैं।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. Scammers dark web se leaked data khareedte hain. Unhe aapki details pata hone ka matlab yeh nahi ki wo asli hain. Kabhi aur details na batayein.

**Q4: Can the local police refuse to file an FIR for a fake call because the scammer is in another state?**
**English:** No, under the new Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), police are legally obligated to register a Zero FIR regardless of the jurisdiction where the crime occurred or where the criminal is located.
**Hindi:** नहीं, नई भारतीय नागरिक सुरक्षा संहिता (बीएनएसएस) के तहत, पुलिस अधिकार क्षेत्र की परवाह किए बिना जीरो एफआईआर दर्ज करने के लिए कानूनी रूप से बाध्य है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, BNSS ke naye rules ke hisaab se police ko Zero FIR likhni hi padegi, chahe scammer kisi bhi state mein ho.

**Q5: The fake caller posed as a police officer and threatened me with a 'digital arrest'. What is this?**
**English:** 'Digital Arrest' is a completely fake concept invented by scammers. The Indian legal system does not have any provision for a 'digital arrest' over Skype or WhatsApp. Real police will issue a formal summons under BNSS Section 35 (formerly CrPC 41A). Disconnect the call immediately.
**Hindi:** 'डिजिटल अरेस्ट' जालसाजों द्वारा आविष्कार की गई एक पूरी तरह से नकली अवधारणा है। भारतीय कानूनी प्रणाली में स्काइप या व्हाट्सएप पर 'डिजिटल अरेस्ट' का कोई प्रावधान नहीं है। असली पुलिस हमेशा औपचारिक समन जारी करेगी।
**Hinglish:** 'Digital Arrest' scammers ka banaya hua fake concept hai. Indian law mein Skype ya WhatsApp par aisi koi arrest nahi hoti. Asli police hamesha formal notice bhejti hai. Call turant kaat dein.

**Q6: What if the bank refuses to refund my money even after reporting within 3 days?**

**Hindi:** यदि बैंक 30 दिनों के बाद भी रिफंड देने से इनकार करता है या देरी करता है, तो आपको उनके आधिकारिक पोर्टल के माध्यम से भारतीय रिजर्व बैंक (RBI) लोकपाल के पास औपचारिक शिकायत दर्ज करनी चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Agar bank 30 din baad bhi paise wapas nahi karta, toh aap RBI Ombudsman portal (cms.rbi.org.in) par bank ke khilaf complaint kar sakte hain. Apna complaint number sambhal kar rakhein.

**Q7: A fake caller convinced me to download an app. They didn't ask for OTP, but money was deducted. How?**

Once installed and permissions granted, the scammer can see your screen, read incoming OTP SMS invisibly, and control your device to make transactions. Uninstall the app instantly and reset your phone.

**Q8: I received a call saying my electricity will be cut off tonight if I don't pay via a link. Is this real?**

**Hindi:** यह एक क्लासिक फर्जी कॉल घोटाला है। बिजली बोर्ड एसएमएस लिंक के माध्यम से तत्काल वियोग की धमकी देने के लिए कॉल नहीं करते हैं। हमेशा आधिकारिक ऐप या वेबसाइट की जांच करें।

**Q9: Can I track the scammer's location using their phone number?**

**Hindi:** हालांकि अपमानजनक भाषा का उपयोग करने की अनुशंसा नहीं की जाती है, लेकिन एक साइबर अपराधी कभी भी पुलिस से संपर्क नहीं करेगा। आगे के उत्पीड़न से बचने के लिए कॉल को काटना और नंबर को ब्लॉक करना बेहतर है।

**Q12: How do scammers spoof numbers to look like the police or bank?**

**Hindi:** जालसाज आपके स्क्रीन पर दिखाई देने वाले नंबर में हेरफेर करने के लिए वीओआईपी तकनीक और कॉलर आईडी स्पूफिंग सॉफ्टवेयर का उपयोग करते हैं। इससे ऐसा लगता है कि कॉल किसी आधिकारिक हॉटलाइन से आ रही है।

**Hindi:** हां, आपको संचार साथी पोर्टल या cybercrime.gov.in की चक्षु सुविधा पर इसकी रिपोर्ट करनी चाहिए। आपकी रिपोर्ट अधिकारियों को धोखाधड़ी वाले नंबर को ब्लॉक करने में मदद करती है।

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** If I get a call from a 9-digit number, it's definitely a government helpline.
   **Fact:** Scammers use spoofing and VoIP to mask their numbers. The length or appearance of a number does not guarantee its authenticity.
2. **Myth:** Bank officials need my OTP to block my stolen debit card.
   **Fact:** Banks NEVER require an OTP to block a card or account. They only need verification through security questions.
3. **Myth:** If I report a cyber fraud after 2 months, the police will not register an FIR.
   **Fact:** While immediate reporting is crucial for fund recovery, serious offenses under BNS do not have strict limitation periods. The police must register an FIR.
4. **Myth:** Scammers can hack my bank account just by me answering their phone call.
   **Fact:** Just answering a standard voice call cannot hack your phone. Hacking occurs if you share details, click links, or install apps.
5. **Myth:** The police can instantly reverse the transaction and put the money back in my account.
   **Fact:** The police can only 'freeze' the disputed amount in the scammer's account via the 1930 portal. Only the bank, often following a court order, can reverse and refund the money.
6. **Myth:** Scammers only target uneducated or elderly people.
   **Fact:** Cybercriminals use highly sophisticated social engineering that routinely tricks tech-savvy youth, IT professionals, and executives.
7. **Myth:** If the Truecaller app says "SBI Customer Care", it is 100% genuine.
   **Fact:** Caller ID apps rely on crowdsourced data. Scammers organize mass campaigns to save their fake numbers under official names to manipulate these apps.
8. **Myth:** A "digital arrest" by the CBI over Skype is a standard legal procedure.
   **Fact:** There is no legal provision for a digital arrest in India. Real agencies do not conduct arrests over video calls.
9. **Myth:** Telecom companies will automatically refund my money if the scammer used their network.
   **Fact:** Telecom companies only provide network infrastructure. They are not liable for financial refunds; disputes must be raised with your bank or the RBI Ombudsman.
10. **Myth:** Changing my UPI PIN on the app will reverse the fraudulent transaction.
    **Fact:** Changing a PIN secures the account for future, but it does not reverse past transactions. You must report it to 1930 and your bank.

**Scenario 2: The Digital Arrest Threat**
**Citizen:** Someone claiming to be from the Cyber Crime Department video called me on Skype and said I am under 'digital arrest' for a parcel containing illegal items. They want me to transfer 5 lakhs for verification.
**Assistant:** This is a classic extortion scam! There is no legal concept of a 'digital arrest' in India. Real police do not demand money for 'verification' or arrest you over Skype. Disconnect immediately, block the account, and report the incident on cybercrime.gov.in.
**Citizen (Hinglish):** Wo log police uniform mein the aur unhone mujhe Supreme court ka warrant bhi dikhaya WhatsApp par.
**Assistant (Hinglish):** Wo uniform aur warrant dono fake hain, sir. Scammers green screen aur Photoshop use karte hain. Supreme court kabhi WhatsApp par warrant nahi bhejti. Aap unhe block karein aur 1930 par unki detail dein.

**Scenario 3: The Fake Customer Care Number**
**Citizen:** I ordered food but it didn't arrive. I Googled the restaurant's customer care number and called. The person sent a link to process my refund, but money got deducted instead!
**Assistant:** You fell victim to a search engine poisoning scam. Scammers manipulate Google results with fake numbers. Never trust random numbers found on Google for customer care. Report the fraudulent transaction to 1930 immediately and block your card.
**Citizen (Hindi):** लेकिन वो नंबर तो गूगल पर सबसे ऊपर आ रहा था!
**Assistant (Hindi):** जालसाज विज्ञापनों (sponsored ads) का उपयोग करके अपने फर्जी नंबरों को गूगल पर सबसे ऊपर दिखाते हैं। हमेशा आधिकारिक ऐप या वेबसाइट से ही संपर्क विवरण लें। अब तुरंत अपने बैंक को सूचित करें।

**Scenario 4: The AnyDesk Scam**
**Citizen:** A caller said there is a problem with my credit card limit. He asked me to download an app called AnyDesk so he could fix it remotely. Is it safe?
**Assistant:** NO! Do not download AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or any screen-sharing app. It gives the scammer complete control over your phone, allowing them to read your OTPs and steal your money. Hang up immediately.
**Citizen (Hinglish):** Maine app download toh kar liya tha par open nahi kiya.
**Assistant (Hinglish):** Bahut badhiya kiya jo open nahi kiya. Us app ko turant uninstall kar dein. Aur aage se kisi unknown caller ke kehne par koi app download na karein.

**Scenario 5: The KBC Lottery Scam**
**Citizen:** I got a WhatsApp audio call saying I won 25 lakhs in the KBC lottery. They are asking for 10,000 rupees as a processing fee/tax to release the prize money.
**Assistant:** This is a 100% fake lottery scam. Legitimate lotteries or organizations never ask for upfront payments or taxes to release prize money. Taxes are directly deducted from the winnings. Do not pay anything. Block the number.
**Citizen (Hindi):** उन्होंने मुझे अमिताभ बच्चन की आवाज वाला एक ऑडियो भी भेजा था।
**Assistant (Hindi):** वह एआई (AI) द्वारा बनाई गई या रिकॉर्ड की गई फर्जी आवाज है। यह लोगों का विश्वास जीतने की एक चाल है। कृपया कोई पैसा न भेजें और cybercrime.gov.in पर इस नंबर की रिपोर्ट करें।

**Scenario 6: Family Emergency AI Voice Clone**
**Citizen:** I got a call from an unknown number sounding exactly like my son saying he is in jail and needs money for bail immediately.
**Assistant:** This is an AI voice cloning scam. Scammers use short audio clips from social media to clone voices. Disconnect and call your son directly on his actual number. Do not transfer any money.

**Scenario 7: Fake Delivery OTP**
**Citizen:** A delivery boy called saying he has a package but I need to share the OTP sent to my phone to cancel the wrong order.
**Assistant:** Do not share the OTP! Delivery platforms do not require OTPs to cancel orders over a phone call. That OTP is likely for a password reset or bank transaction.

**Scenario 8: Trai Disconnection Threat**
**Citizen:** I received an automated call saying my mobile number will be deactivated by TRAI in 2 hours unless I press 9.
**Assistant:** This is a scam. TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority of India) does not make calls or send messages regarding mobile number disconnection. Hang up and ignore it.

**Scenario 9: Wrong UPI Transfer Reversal**
**Citizen:** Someone called saying they mistakenly sent 5000 rupees to my UPI and asked me to scan a QR code to refund it.
**Assistant:** Stop! You never need to scan a QR code or enter your UPI PIN to RECEIVE money or process a refund. Entering your PIN will deduct money from your account.

**Scenario 10: Credit Card Reward Points**
**Citizen:** A caller offered to convert my expiring credit card reward points into cash and sent a link to fill in my card details.
**Assistant:** Banks do not send links asking for full card details, CVV, and OTP to redeem reward points. Redeem points only through the bank's official net banking portal or app.

**Scenario 11: Fake Loan App Call**
**Citizen:** I uninstalled a loan app, but now they are calling my contacts and morphing my photos.
**Assistant:** This is illegal harassment and extortion (BNS Sec 308). File an FIR immediately and report to the cyber portal. Inform your contacts that your phone was hacked and not to entertain these calls.

**Scenario 12: Electricity Bill Scam**
**Citizen:** SMS said my power will be cut at 9:30 PM. I called the number and they asked me to pay 10 rupees via a link for update.
**Assistant:** It's a phishing link designed to steal your banking credentials. Real electricity boards never demand 10 rupees via SMS links to stop disconnections.

**Scenario 13: Job Offer Scam Call**
**Citizen:** A recruiter called offering a work-from-home job liking YouTube videos, but wants a 2000 rupees registration fee.
**Assistant:** Genuine companies never ask employees to pay money to get a job. This is a task fraud scam. Do not pay them.

**Scenario 14: Fake Stock Tips**
**Citizen:** I got a call offering guaranteed 50% returns in the stock market if I join their VIP WhatsApp group.
**Assistant:** These are unregulated pump-and-dump or fake trading app scams. SEBI strictly prohibits such guaranteed return promises. Report the group.

**Scenario 15: PM Scheme Call**
**Citizen:** A caller said I am eligible for a special PM Yojana loan and asked for my Aadhaar OTP.
**Assistant:** Government schemes are processed through official portals (like Jan Samarth), not via unsolicited phone calls asking for OTPs. Do not share the OTP.

## Government Services
**English:**
- **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (1930):** The primary 24/7 centralized helpline for reporting financial cyber frauds immediately.
- **cybercrime.gov.in:** The official portal to file detailed cybercrime complaints.
- **Sanchar Saathi (Chakshu):** A DoT initiative to report suspected fraudulent calls and SMS for number blocking.
- **RBI Ombudsman (CMS):** For escalating complaints against banks if they fail to resolve fraud refund issues.
- **eCourts Services:** To track the progress of the cybercrime case once the charge-sheet is filed in court.

**Hindi Unicode:**
- **राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध रिपोर्टिंग पोर्टल (1930):** वित्तीय साइबर धोखाधड़ी की तुरंत रिपोर्ट करने के लिए प्राथमिक 24/7 केंद्रीकृत हेल्पलाइन।
- **cybercrime.gov.in:** विस्तृत साइबर अपराध शिकायतें दर्ज करने के लिए आधिकारिक पोर्टल।
- **संचार साथी (चक्षु):** नंबर ब्लॉक करने के लिए संदिग्ध धोखाधड़ी कॉल और एसएमएस की रिपोर्ट करने के लिए दूरसंचार विभाग (DoT) की एक पहल।
- **आरबीआई लोकपाल (CMS):** यदि बैंक धोखाधड़ी रिफंड के मुद्दों को हल करने में विफल रहते हैं तो बैंकों के खिलाफ शिकायतों को आगे बढ़ाने के लिए।
- **ई-कोर्ट सेवाएं:** चार्जशीट अदालत में दायर होने के बाद साइबर अपराध के मामले की प्रगति को ट्रैक करने के लिए।

**Hinglish:**
- **Cyber Crime Helpline (1930):** Financial cyber fraud ko turant report karne ke liye 24/7 official helpline.
- **cybercrime.gov.in:** Online detailed complaint file karne ki government website.
- **Sanchar Saathi (Chakshu):** Fraud calls aur SMS report karne ka portal taaki un numbers ko block kiya ja sake.
- **RBI Ombudsman:** Agar bank paise wapas nahi karta, toh bank ke khilaf complaint yahan karein.
- **eCourts:** Jab police court mein case daal de, toh case ka status check karne ke liye.
