# Identity Theft and Cyber Impersonation (पहचान की चोरी और साइबर प्रतिरूपण / Pehchaan Ki Chori aur Cyber Impersonation)

## Overview
**English:** Identity theft occurs when someone wrongfully acquires and uses another person's personal data (like name, Aadhaar, PAN, banking details, or social media profile) for fraudulent purposes, economic gain, or to cause reputational damage. In the digital age, cyber impersonation has become a critical threat, governed primarily by the Information Technology Act, 2000, and the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023.

**Hindi (Unicode):** पहचान की चोरी तब होती है जब कोई व्यक्ति धोखाधड़ी, आर्थिक लाभ या प्रतिष्ठा को नुकसान पहुंचाने के उद्देश्य से किसी अन्य व्यक्ति के व्यक्तिगत डेटा (जैसे नाम, आधार, पैन, बैंकिंग विवरण, या सोशल मीडिया प्रोफाइल) को गलत तरीके से प्राप्त करता है और उसका उपयोग करता है। डिजिटल युग में, साइबर प्रतिरूपण एक गंभीर खतरा बन गया है, जो मुख्य रूप से सूचना प्रौद्योगिकी अधिनियम, 2000 और भारतीय न्याय संहिता, 2023 द्वारा शासित है।

**Hinglish:** Identity theft tab hota hai jab koi vyakti kisi aur ke personal data (jaise naam, Aadhaar, PAN, bank details, ya social media profile) ko fraud karne, paise kamane, ya badnaami karne ke liye galat tareeqe se haasil karta hai. Digital zamane mein, cyber impersonation ek bada khatra ban gaya hai, jispar Information Technology Act, 2000 aur Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 ke kanoon laagoo hote hain.

## Quick Summary
**English:** Identity theft is a punishable criminal offense. Victims must immediately report the crime to the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (1930) or the nearest cyber police station. Prompt action can freeze fraudulent transactions and initiate takedown of fake profiles. The law provides strong punitive measures, including imprisonment and fines.

Jaldi action lene se fraud transactions ko roka ja sakta hai aur fake profiles ko hatwaya ja sakta hai. Kanoon mein jail aur fine dono ki sazaa ka pravadhan hai.

## Definition
**English:** Under Indian law, particularly Section 66C of the Information Technology Act, identity theft is defined as fraudulently or dishonestly making use of the electronic signature, password, or any other unique identification feature of any other person.

**Hindi (Unicode):** भारतीय कानून के तहत, विशेष रूप से सूचना प्रौद्योगिकी अधिनियम की धारा 66C के अनुसार, पहचान की चोरी को किसी अन्य व्यक्ति के इलेक्ट्रॉनिक हस्ताक्षर, पासवर्ड, या किसी अन्य विशिष्ट पहचान विशेषता का धोखाधड़ी या बेईमानी से उपयोग करने के रूप में परिभाषित किया गया है।

**Hinglish:** Indian kanoon ke mutabiq, khaaskar IT Act ke Section 66C mein, identity theft ka matlab hai kisi aur vyakti ke electronic signature, password, ya kisi bhi unique identification feature ka fraud ya beimaani se istemal karna.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Protecting your identity is crucial to preventing financial ruin, maintaining a clean credit score, and safeguarding your personal reputation. Once compromised, restoring your digital and financial identity requires significant time and legal intervention.

## Legal Background
**History:** Identity theft laws evolved rapidly with the rise of the internet. The IT Act 2000 was amended in 2008 to specifically include Section 66C to address identity theft. Recently, the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023, overhauled the penal laws to better address cybercrimes alongside traditional frauds.

**Purpose:** To penalize unauthorized use of personal credentials, protect citizens' digital privacy, and provide a legal framework for prosecuting cybercriminals and recovering damages.

**Applicable Acts:**
- Information Technology Act, 2000 (IT Act)
- Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)
- Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 (BNSS)
- Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023 (BSA)

**Relevant Constitutional Articles:**
- Article 21: Right to Life and Personal Liberty (interpreted by the Supreme Court to include the Right to Privacy in the K.S. Puttaswamy judgment).

**Relevant Sections:**
- Section 66C, IT Act: Punishment for identity theft.
- Section 66D, IT Act: Punishment for cheating by personation by using computer resource.
- Section 318, BNS: Cheating (replaces Section 415 IPC).
- Section 319, BNS: Cheating by personation (replaces Section 416 IPC).
- Section 336, BNS: Forgery (replaces Section 463 IPC).
- Section 338, BNS: Forgery of record of Court or of public register, etc. (replaces Section 466 IPC).
- Section 340, BNS: Forgery for purpose of cheating (replaces Section 468 IPC).

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** All Indian citizens, residents, and companies whose identity is misused online or offline.
- **Who is exempt:** Law enforcement acting under valid court orders/warrants for investigation purposes.
- **Special conditions:** Cross-border identity theft is subject to extradition treaties and mutual legal assistance treaties (MLATs) since cybercrimes often transcend national boundaries.

## Rights of Citizens
**English:** You have the right to privacy, the right to report cybercrimes without jurisdiction barriers (Zero FIR), the right to request takedowns of fake profiles from intermediaries (social media platforms), and the right to dispute fraudulent bank charges.

**Hindi (Unicode):** आपको निजता का अधिकार, बिना क्षेत्राधिकार की बाधाओं (जीरो एफआईआर) के साइबर अपराधों की रिपोर्ट करने का अधिकार, मध्यस्थों (सोशल मीडिया प्लेटफॉर्म) से फर्जी प्रोफाइल हटाने का अनुरोध करने का अधिकार और धोखाधड़ी वाले बैंक शुल्कों पर विवाद करने का अधिकार है।

**Hinglish:** Aapko privacy ka adhikar hai, bina jurisdiction ke (Zero FIR) cyber crime report karne ka haq hai, social media se fake profile hatwane ka right hai, aur fraud bank charges ke khilaf awaz uthane ka adhikar hai.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Immediate Blocking:** Freeze bank accounts and credit cards by contacting your bank immediately if financial details are compromised.
2. **National Cyber Crime Helpline:** Call 1930 or visit cybercrime.gov.in to register a complaint.
3. **Filing Police Complaint (FIR):** Visit the nearest cyber police station or file a Zero FIR under BNSS regarding identity theft and cheating. Provide all electronic evidence (screenshots, URLs).
4. **Platform Reporting:** Use the in-app reporting tools on platforms (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn) to report the fake profile. Provide a copy of the FIR if required by the intermediary.
5. **Notifying Authorities:** Report lost or stolen physical IDs to respective authorities (UIDAI for Aadhaar, Income Tax Dept for PAN).
6. **Investigation:** Police collect electronic evidence under the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023, issuing notices to intermediaries for IP logs and user data.
7. **Trial:** The accused is prosecuted in the appropriate court based on the charge sheet filed under BNS and IT Act.

1. **तत्काल ब्लॉक करना:** यदि वित्तीय विवरणों से समझौता किया गया है तो तुरंत अपने बैंक से संपर्क करके बैंक खातों और क्रेडिट कार्ड को फ्रीज करें।

## Required Documents
- Written complaint detailing the incident.
- Government ID proof of the victim.
- Screenshots of fake profiles, fraudulent messages, or phishing emails.
- Bank statements highlighting unauthorized transactions.
- URLs of the fraudulent websites or social media profiles.
- Certificate under Section 63 of Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023 (for admissibility of electronic evidence).

- Cyber Crime Police Stations (State Police)
- Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In)
- Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI - for SIM swap frauds)
- Reserve Bank of India (RBI - Ombudsman for banking frauds)

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Section 66C IT Act (Identity Theft):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine up to ₹1,00,000.
- **Section 66D IT Act (Cheating by personation):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and a fine up to ₹1,00,000.
- **Section 319 BNS (Cheating by personation):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, or with fine, or with both.
- **Section 336 & 340 BNS (Forgery for cheating):** Imprisonment which may extend to 7 years, and also liable to fine.

## Exceptions
- Using a pseudonym for legitimate literary, artistic, or whistleblowing purposes where there is no intent to defraud, cheat, or harm any individual's reputation.
- Parody accounts that clearly state they are not the actual person, provided they do not violate specific platform policies or cause legal harm.

## Important Timelines
- **Reporting:** Immediate (within 2-3 hours for financial frauds to increase chances of fund recovery via 1930).
- **Zero Liability Protection (RBI):** If an unauthorized banking transaction is reported within 3 days, the customer has zero liability.
- **Police FIR registration:** Must be done immediately upon knowledge of the crime.
- **Takedown by Intermediaries:** Under IT Rules 2021, platforms must acknowledge complaints within 24 hours and resolve them within 15 days (takedown of impersonating content often required within 24-36 hours of legal order).

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Deleting the fraudulent messages or fake profile chats out of panic, destroying crucial electronic evidence.
- Delaying the reporting of lost identity documents (Aadhaar/PAN).
- Failing to secure a Section 63 BSA certificate for digital evidence.
- Not reporting to the 1930 helpline in time for financial frauds.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is considered identity theft under Indian law?**
   Under Section 66C of the IT Act, using someone's electronic signature, password, or unique ID feature dishonestly is identity theft. It covers fake profiles, stolen PAN/Aadhaar usage, and financial frauds. It is a serious criminal offense. Punishments can include up to 3 years of imprisonment.

2. **Someone created a fake Facebook profile with my photos. What should I do?**
   Take screenshots of the profile and its URL. Report the profile using Facebook's in-app reporting tool for impersonation. File a complaint on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) with the evidence. Inform your contacts about the fake profile.

3. **My PAN card was used to take a loan I didn't apply for. How do I fix this?**
   Immediately file an FIR with the local police or cyber cell regarding identity theft and forgery. Report the fraud to the respective bank/NBFC and provide them the FIR copy. Raise a dispute with credit bureaus like CIBIL to remove the fraudulent loan from your record.

4. **Is cyber impersonation a bailable offense?**
   Offenses under Sections 66C and 66D of the IT Act are cognizable and bailable. However, if serious sections of the BNS (like forgery for cheating) are applied, it may become non-bailable depending on the quantum of the fraud. Consult a lawyer for exact case details.

5. **How long does it take to block a fake social media account?**
   If reported correctly as impersonation, social media platforms usually take it down within 24 to 48 hours. If they fail to do so, a legal notice or police intervention forces them to comply under the IT Rules 2021.

6. **Can I file a case if a scammer used my identity to cheat my friends?**
   Yes, you are a primary victim of identity theft (Sec 66C IT Act) and your friends are victims of cheating (Sec 318 BNS). You must file a police complaint immediately to clear your name and help the police track the scammer.

7. **How can I prevent my Aadhaar from being misused?**
   You can lock your Aadhaar biometrics using the mAadhaar app or UIDAI website. Only unlock it when you need to authenticate. Also, use masked Aadhaar where full Aadhaar numbers are not legally required.

8. **What is SIM swapping and how is it related to identity theft?**
   SIM swapping occurs when a fraudster gets a new SIM card issued in your name using your stolen ID. They then receive your banking OTPs, taking over your financial identity. Contact your telecom provider immediately if your SIM abruptly stops working.

9. **Can a minor be a victim of identity theft?**
   Yes, children's identities are often stolen to create fake profiles or commit cyberbullying. Parents must monitor their children's online presence and report any impersonation to the authorities immediately.

10. **Do I need a lawyer to file a cybercrime complaint?**
    No, you do not need a lawyer to file a complaint on the National Cyber Crime Portal or to register an FIR. However, you may need legal assistance during the court trial or for sending legal notices to intermediaries.

11. **क्या पहचान की चोरी के लिए जेल हो सकती है?**
    हाँ, आईटी अधिनियम की धारा 66C और BNS की धाराओं के तहत पहचान की चोरी साबित होने पर 3 से 7 साल तक की जेल और जुर्माना हो सकता है। यह एक गंभीर अपराध है।

12. **मेरा आधार कार्ड खो गया है, मुझे क्या करना चाहिए?**
    सबसे पहले पुलिस में गुमशुदगी की रिपोर्ट (NCR) दर्ज कराएं। फिर UIDAI की वेबसाइट पर जाकर अपने आधार का गलत इस्तेमाल रोकने के लिए उसे लॉक करें और नए आधार के लिए आवेदन करें।

13. **Cyber crime helpline number kya hai?**
    Financial cyber frauds aur identity theft ke liye national helpline number 1930 hai. Aap cybercrime.gov.in par bhi online complaint darj kar sakte hain.

14. **Agar mere bank account se paise nikal gaye toh kya karun?**
    Turant 1930 par call karein aur apne bank ko inform karein. Agar aap 3 din ke andar report karte hain, toh RBI guidelines ke mutabiq aapki liability zero ho sakti hai.

15. **Fake WhatsApp account mere naam se chal raha hai, kya karein?**
    WhatsApp par us number ko report aur block karein. Apne doston ko status ya message ke zariye inform karein. Cyber police mein complaint darj karein taaki police us number ki janch kar sake.

16. **What evidence is required for cyber impersonation?**
    Screenshots, URLs of fake profiles, chat transcripts, emails, and bank statements are crucial. Under the new Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, electronic records must be accompanied by a Section 63 certificate for court admissibility.

17. **Can I sue the social media company for my fake profile?**
    Social media companies are intermediaries protected under Section 79 of the IT Act. You cannot usually sue them unless they fail to remove the fake profile after receiving a valid court or government order.

18. **How do fraudsters get my personal data?**
    Data is often obtained through phishing emails, data breaches, unsecured Wi-Fi networks, malware, or by scraping public information from your poorly secured social media accounts.

19. **What is a Zero FIR in cyber crimes?**
    Under the BNSS, a Zero FIR allows you to file a police complaint at any police station, regardless of where the crime occurred. It is then transferred to the correct jurisdiction cyber cell.

20. **Can I recover money lost due to identity theft?**
    Yes, if reported immediately to 1930 and the bank, the transaction can be frozen. Civil remedies and court orders post-conviction can also direct the accused to pay compensation.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Only rich or famous people face identity theft.
   **Fact:** Anyone can be a target. Scammers steal common people's IDs for loans, mules accounts, and scams.
2. **Myth:** Identity theft only happens if I lose my wallet.
   **Fact:** Most identity thefts today happen online through phishing, malware, or data breaches.
3. **Myth:** If my bank refunds the money, the crime is solved.
   **Fact:** The bank refund protects your finances, but your compromised identity (PAN/Aadhaar) can still be used for other crimes until secured.
4. **Myth:** Social media platforms automatically delete fake profiles.
   **Fact:** They rely on user reports or legal orders. You must actively report the profile.
5. **Myth:** Police cannot trace cybercriminals.
   **Fact:** Cyber cells use advanced digital forensics, IP tracking, and OSINT tools to trace criminals.
6. **Myth (Hindi):** साइबर पुलिस शिकायत के लिए पैसे लेती है।
   **Fact:** साइबर सेल या पुलिस स्टेशन में शिकायत दर्ज करना बिल्कुल मुफ्त है।
7. **Myth (Hinglish):** Mera account private hai toh data chori nahi ho sakta.
   **Fact:** Data breaches aur malware se private accounts ka data bhi leak ho sakta hai.
8. **Myth:** I should delete all scam emails immediately.
   **Fact:** Keep them safely as electronic evidence for the police investigation.
9. **Myth:** Identity theft is a victimless crime if the bank pays.
   **Fact:** It damages your credit score, causes immense mental stress, and can wrongly implicate you in crimes.
10. **Myth:** Zero FIR is not applicable for cyber crimes.
    **Fact:** Zero FIR applies to all cognizable offenses, including severe cybercrimes, ensuring immediate legal action.

**Example 1: Fake Profile Reporting**
**Citizen:** Someone made a fake Instagram account using my photos and is asking my friends for money. What should I do?
**Assistant:** This is identity theft (Sec 66C IT Act) and cheating by personation. Ask your friends not to pay and to report the profile via Instagram. Take screenshots of the profile and chats, copy the URL, and immediately file a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.

**Example 2: Aadhaar Misuse**
**Citizen (Hindi):** किसी ने मेरे आधार कार्ड का उपयोग करके सिम कार्ड ले लिया है।
**Assistant (Hindi):** यह एक गंभीर अपराध है। तुरंत अपने टेलीकॉम ऑपरेटर को कॉल करके उस नंबर को ब्लॉक करवाएं। अपने आधार को UIDAI वेबसाइट पर लॉक करें और 1930 पर कॉल करके साइबर धोखाधड़ी की रिपोर्ट दर्ज करें।

**Example 3: Financial Fraud via Identity Theft**
**Citizen (Hinglish):** Mere naam par kisi ne loan le liya hai, mujhe CIBIL check karne par pata chala.
**Assistant (Hinglish):** Aap turant cyber police ya local thane mein BNS aur IT Act ke tahat FIR darj karwayein. FIR ki copy us bank ya NBFC ko bhejein jisne loan diya hai aur CIBIL ko dispute raise karein taaki aapka record theek ho sake.

**Example 4: SIM Swap Concern**
**Citizen:** My phone lost network entirely for the last 5 hours, and now I see an email about a bank transaction.
**Assistant:** You might be a victim of a SIM swap fraud. Borrow a phone, call your bank immediately to block all accounts, and call 1930 to freeze the transaction. Then visit your telecom provider to reclaim your number.

**Example 5: Phishing Call**
**Citizen:** A person called claiming to be from the police, asking for my Aadhaar OTP to verify my identity.
**Assistant:** Stop talking to them immediately. The police or any legitimate government authority will NEVER ask for your OTP. Do not share any details. You can report this suspicious number on the Chakshu portal (Sanchar Saathi).

**Example 6: Zero FIR Inquiry**
**Citizen (Hindi):** मैं अभी यात्रा कर रहा हूँ और मेरा पैन कार्ड और लैपटॉप चोरी हो गया है। क्या मैं यहाँ शिकायत कर सकता हूँ?
**Assistant (Hindi):** हाँ, आप BNSS के तहत किसी भी पुलिस स्टेशन में 'जीरो एफआईआर' (Zero FIR) दर्ज करा सकते हैं। बाद में इसे आपके संबंधित अधिकार क्षेत्र वाले थाने में ट्रांसफर कर दिया जाएगा।

**Example 7: WhatsApp Impersonation**
**Citizen (Hinglish):** Mere boss ke naam aur photo se mujhe WhatsApp par message aaya, Amazon gift cards maang rahe hain.
**Assistant (Hinglish):** Yeh ek classic impersonation scam hai. Message ka reply na karein. Apne boss ko unke original number par call karke confirm karein. Us fake number ko WhatsApp par report aur block karein.

**Example 8: Legal Liability of Intermediaries**
**Citizen:** The social media site is refusing to take down my fake profile even after I reported it.
**Assistant:** If they fail to act, you can file a police complaint. The police can issue a legal notice under Section 94 BNSS or IT Rules to the platform, mandating them to remove the impersonating content within 24-36 hours.

**Example 9: Stolen Device Data**
**Citizen:** My laptop was stolen, and now someone is logging into my emails.
**Assistant:** Immediately use another device to change your main email passwords and force 'logout from all devices'. Enable 2FA. File an FIR for theft and unauthorized computer access (Sec 66 IT Act).

**Example 10: Compromised Email**
**Citizen (Hindi):** मेरे ईमेल का पासवर्ड बदल दिया गया है और मुझे एक्सेस नहीं मिल रहा है।
**Assistant (Hindi):** ईमेल प्रदाता के 'अकाउंट रिकवरी' विकल्प का उपयोग करें। यदि इससे काम नहीं बनता है, तो यह धारा 66C के तहत पहचान की चोरी है। तुरंत साइबर सेल में रिपोर्ट करें ताकि आपके संपर्कों को स्पैम या फ्रॉड से बचाया जा सके।

**Example 11: Document Forgery**
**Citizen (Hinglish):** Kisi ne mere signatures forge karke property papers banwa liye hain.
**Assistant (Hinglish):** Yeh BNS ke Section 336 aur 340 (Forgery for cheating) ka serious case hai. Aapko turant FIR darj karni chahiye aur court mein civil injunction file karni chahiye taaki property ki aage sale ruk sake.

**Example 12: Data Breach Notification**
**Citizen:** I received an email saying my data was found in a breach. Should I worry?
**Assistant:** Yes, you should act. Change your passwords immediately, especially for banking and primary emails. Monitor your bank statements closely for the next few months and check your credit report.

**Example 13: Using Biometrics Safely**
**Citizen:** Is it safe to give my thumbprint at private shops for verification?
**Assistant:** Be very cautious. Only use Aadhaar biometrics at authorized centers. Fraudsters have cloned fingerprints using silicon to empty bank accounts via AePS (Aadhaar Enabled Payment System). Keep your biometrics locked on the UIDAI portal.

**Example 14: Recovering Access**
**Citizen (Hindi):** मैंने अपना फेसबुक वापस पा लिया है, क्या मुझे अभी भी पुलिस में जाना चाहिए?
**Assistant (Hindi):** यदि आपके अकाउंट से आपके दोस्तों को कोई धोखाधड़ी वाले संदेश भेजे गए थे, तो भविष्य में किसी भी कानूनी परेशानी से बचने के लिए पुलिस को घटना की सूचना देना सुरक्षित रहता है।

**Example 15: Corporate Identity Theft**
**Citizen (Hinglish):** Kisi ne humari company ki fake website banakar logo se paise loot liye.
**Assistant (Hinglish):** Yeh brand impersonation aur cheating hai. Turant cybercrime portal par website ka URL report karein. Apne customers ko alert karein, aur police ko FIR darj karwayein taaki woh domain host se website block karwa sakein.
