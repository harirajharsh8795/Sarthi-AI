# Social Media Crimes under Indian Law

## Overview / अवलोकन / Overview
**English:** Social media crimes encompass a wide variety of illegal activities committed on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, X (formerly Twitter), and others. These include cyberbullying, cyberstalking, online defamation, impersonation (fake profiles), sharing non-consensual intimate imagery (revenge porn), and creating deepfakes. With the implementation of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, and the existing Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000, India has a robust legal framework to tackle these offences and protect citizens in the digital space.

**Hindi (हिंदी):** सोशल मीडिया अपराधों में फेसबुक, इंस्टाग्राम, व्हाट्सएप और एक्स (ट्विटर) जैसे प्लेटफॉर्म पर की जाने वाली कई तरह की अवैध गतिविधियां शामिल हैं। इनमें साइबर बुलिंग, साइबर स्टॉकिंग, ऑनलाइन मानहानि, फर्जी प्रोफाइल बनाना, बिना सहमति के अंतरंग तस्वीरें साझा करना और डीपफेक बनाना शामिल है। भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS), 2023 और सूचना प्रौद्योगिकी (IT) अधिनियम, 2000 के तहत, भारत में इन अपराधों से निपटने और डिजिटल दुनिया में नागरिकों की सुरक्षा के लिए एक मजबूत कानूनी ढांचा है।

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023 aur IT Act, 2000 ke tehat, India mein in crimes se nipatne aur citizens ko digital space mein safe rakhne ke liye strict laws hain.

## Legal Background
The legal framework against social media crimes primarily relies on the Information Technology Act, 2000, and the newly implemented Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, which replaced the Indian Penal Code (IPC). The Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) governs the procedural aspects, and the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) regulates electronic evidence.

**Applicable Sections:**
*   **Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000:**
    *   **Section 66C:** Identity theft (including fake profiles).
    *   **Section 66D:** Cheating by personation by using computer resource.
    *   **Section 66E:** Violation of privacy (capturing/publishing images of private areas without consent).
    *   **Section 67:** Publishing or transmitting obscene material in electronic form.
    *   **Section 67A:** Publishing or transmitting material containing sexually explicit act.
    *   **Section 67B:** Child pornography.
*   **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023:**
    *   **Section 356:** Defamation (covers online defamation/libel).
    *   **Section 78:** Stalking (specifically includes monitoring a woman's use of the internet, email, or any other form of electronic communication).
    *   **Section 72:** Voyeurism (including capturing and disseminating images).
    *   **Section 352:** Intentional insult with intent to provoke breach of peace.
    *   **Section 353:** Statements conducing to public mischief (fake news, hate speech online).
    *   **Section 318:** Cheating.
    *   **Section 319:** Cheating by personation.

## Applicability
These laws apply to all citizens of India, and even foreign nationals if the crime involves a computer, computer system, or network located in India, or if the victim is an Indian citizen, depending on the jurisdiction defined by the IT Act and BNS.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure / चरण-दर-चरण कानूनी प्रक्रिया / Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Preserve Evidence:** Take clear screenshots of the offensive post, chat, or fake profile. Note down the exact URL/link of the profile or post. Do not block the person immediately before taking evidence if it might delete the chat history (depending on the platform).
2. **Report to Platform:** Use the platform's in-built reporting mechanism to report the account or post for community guideline violations.
3. **Register Complaint on NCRP:** Visit `cybercrime.gov.in`. Register an account, provide details of the incident, upload the evidence (screenshots, URLs), and submit the complaint.
4. **Visit Cyber Police Station:** Alternatively, visit the nearest Cyber Police Station with a written complaint and printed copies of the evidence.
5. **FIR Registration:** Based on the complaint's severity, the police will register an FIR under relevant sections of BNS and IT Act.
6. **Investigation & Takedown:** Police investigate, issue notices to social media platforms for IP logs and user details, and may request the platform to take down the illegal content.
7. **Court Proceedings:** A charge sheet is filed, and the trial proceeds in the designated court.

## Required Documents
*   A detailed written complaint outlining the incident.
*   Clear screenshots of the offending profile, posts, comments, or chats.
*   Exact URLs (links) of the profile and the specific posts.
*   Header details of emails (if applicable).
*   Identity proof of the complainant (Aadhaar, Voter ID, etc.).
*   Bank statements (if financial fraud is involved).
*   Certificate under Section 63 of Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) for electronic evidence (to be provided during trial).

## Government Authorities
*   **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (NCRP):** Primary portal for online complaints.
*   **Cyber Crime Police Stations:** Specialized police units in every district/city.
*   **Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In):** Nodal agency for dealing with cyber security threats.
*   **Judicial Magistrates & Cyber Appellate Tribunal:** For legal proceedings and appeals.

## Penalties & Punishments
*   **Cyber Stalking (BNS Sec 78):** First conviction: Up to 3 years imprisonment and fine. Second/subsequent conviction: Up to 5 years imprisonment and fine.
*   **Identity Theft / Fake Profile (IT Act Sec 66C):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to ₹1 Lakh.
*   **Cheating by Personation (IT Act Sec 66D):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to ₹1 Lakh.
*   **Violation of Privacy (IT Act Sec 66E):** Imprisonment up to 3 years or fine up to ₹2 Lakhs, or both.
*   **Publishing Obscene Material (IT Act Sec 67):** First conviction: Up to 3 years and fine up to ₹5 Lakhs. Subsequent: Up to 5 years and fine up to ₹10 Lakhs.
*   **Publishing Sexually Explicit Material (IT Act Sec 67A):** First conviction: Up to 5 years and fine up to ₹10 Lakhs. Subsequent: Up to 7 years and fine up to ₹10 Lakhs.
*   **Defamation (BNS Sec 356):** Simple imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both, or community service.

## Exceptions
*   Good faith comments on public figures regarding their public duties are generally protected from defamation claims.
*   Platforms (Intermediaries) are generally exempt from liability under Section 79 of the IT Act if they act strictly as mere conduits and follow due diligence/takedown notices properly, unless they actively modified or promoted the content.

## Important Timelines
*   **Content Takedown:** Intermediaries (social media platforms) are required to take down non-consensual intimate images within 24 hours of receiving a complaint. For other legal orders, usually within 36 hours.
*   **Filing Complaint:** While there is no strict limitation period for reporting serious cyber crimes, immediate reporting (within 24-48 hours) is critical before electronic logs are deleted by the platform.
*   **Data Retention:** Intermediaries must preserve information and associated records for at least 180 days for investigation purposes.

## Common Legal Mistakes
*   **Deleting the Chat/Post:** Victims often delete the abusive chat or block the user in panic before taking screenshots or noting URLs, destroying crucial evidence.
*   **Retaliating Online:** Abusing the harasser in return can weaken the victim's case and lead to counter-complaints.
*   **Not Noting URLs:** Screenshots without the specific web address (URL) of the profile or post make it difficult for police to request IP logs from platforms.
*   **Ignoring Privacy Settings:** Keeping profiles completely public makes it easier for criminals to scrape photos for fake profiles or deepfakes.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
1. **What is considered a social media crime?**
   Any illegal act done using social media, like bullying, stalking, fake profiles, hacking, and spreading non-consensual images. It violates laws like BNS and IT Act.
2. **सोशल मीडिया अपराध क्या माना जाता है?**
   सोशल मीडिया का उपयोग करके किया गया कोई भी अवैध कार्य, जैसे डराना, पीछा करना, फर्जी प्रोफाइल बनाना और बिना सहमति के चित्र फैलाना।
3. **Social media crime kya hota hai?**
   Social media ke through kiye gaye illegal acts, jaise cyberbullying, stalking, fake profiles banana, aur revenge porn, social media crime aate hain.
4. **How do I report a fake Facebook or Instagram profile?**
   First, report the profile using the platform's in-app reporting tool. Then, take screenshots, copy the profile URL, and file a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.
5. **मैं फर्जी फेसबुक या इंस्टाग्राम प्रोफाइल की रिपोर्ट कैसे करूं?**
   पहले, ऐप के रिपोर्टिंग टूल का उपयोग करके प्रोफाइल की रिपोर्ट करें। फिर, स्क्रीनशॉट लें, URL कॉपी करें और cybercrime.gov.in पर शिकायत दर्ज करें।
6. **Fake Instagram profile kaise report karein?**
   Pehle app mein profile ko report karein. Phir screenshot lekar aur URL copy karke cybercrime.gov.in par complaint file karein.
7. **Is cyberbullying a punishable offense in India?**
   Yes. While not explicitly named 'cyberbullying', acts of online harassment, stalking, and defamation are punishable under BNS Sec 78, 356, and IT Act Sec 67.
8. **क्या साइबर बुलिंग भारत में दंडनीय अपराध है?**
   हाँ। हालांकि इसे स्पष्ट रूप से 'साइबर बुलिंग' नहीं कहा गया है, लेकिन ऑनलाइन उत्पीड़न, स्टॉकिंग और मानहानि BNS और IT अधिनियम के तहत दंडनीय हैं।
9. **Kya cyberbullying India mein punishable hai?**
   Haan. Online harassment, stalking aur defamation BNS Sec 78, 356 aur IT Act ke under punishable offences hain.
10. **Someone posted my private photos online. What should I do?**
    Immediately take screenshots and URLs. File a complaint on the National Cyber Crime Portal. You have the right to request the platform to take it down within 24 hours under IT Rules.
11. **किसी ने मेरी निजी तस्वीरें ऑनलाइन पोस्ट कर दी हैं। मुझे क्या करना चाहिए?**
    तुरंत स्क्रीनशॉट और URL लें। राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध पोर्टल पर शिकायत दर्ज करें। आपको आईटी नियमों के तहत 24 घंटे के भीतर इसे हटाने का अनुरोध करने का अधिकार है।
12. **Kisi ne meri private photos leak kar di hain, kya karun?**
    Turant screenshots aur URLs save karein. Cyber crime portal par complaint karein. IT Rules ke tehat aap platform se photo 24 hours mein delete karwa sakte hain.
13. **Can I file a cyber crime complaint anonymously?**
    Yes, the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal allows you to report specific crimes like child pornography and rape/gang rape (CP/RGR) related content anonymously.
14. **क्या मैं गुमनाम रूप से साइबर अपराध की शिकायत दर्ज कर सकता हूँ?**
    हाँ, राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध रिपोर्टिंग पोर्टल आपको बाल पोर्नोग्राफी जैसे विशिष्ट अपराधों की गुमनाम रूप से रिपोर्ट करने की अनुमति देता है।
15. **Kya main anonymously cyber crime report kar sakta hu?**
    Haan, NCRP portal par aap child pornography ya sensitive content anonymously report kar sakte hain.
16. **What is the punishment for creating a deepfake to defame someone?**
    Creating deepfakes for harm can attract charges under Section 66D, 66E, 67 of the IT Act and Sections 318, 319, 356 of BNS, leading to imprisonment of up to 3 years or more.
17. **What is online stalking (cyberstalking)?**
    Continuously monitoring a woman's internet activity, emails, or sending unwanted messages despite clear disinterest is cyberstalking, punishable under BNS Section 78.
18. **ऑनलाइन स्टॉकिंग (साइबर स्टॉकिंग) क्या है?**
    किसी महिला की इंटरनेट गतिविधि पर नज़र रखना या बार-बार अवांछित संदेश भेजना साइबर स्टॉकिंग है, जो BNS धारा 78 के तहत दंडनीय है।
19. **Cyberstalking kya hota hai?**
    Internet par kisi aurat ki activity monitor karna ya uski marzi ke khilaf bar-bar message karna cyberstalking hai. BNS Sec 78 ke under yeh crime hai.
20. **Can police trace fake accounts if they are deleted?**
    Yes, if reported promptly, police can obtain IP logs and creation details from social media companies before the data retention period expires.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Fake accounts cannot be traced.
   **Fact:** Police can trace fake accounts using IP addresses, device IDs, and ISP logs provided by the platform.
2. **Myth:** Deleting a defamatory post removes all evidence.
   **Fact:** Digital footprints remain on servers, and screenshots/URLs saved prior to deletion act as strong evidence.
3. **Myth:** Social media crimes are not 'real' crimes.
   **Fact:** They are serious criminal offenses under the BNS and IT Act, carrying severe prison sentences.
4. **Myth:** Only the person who created the offensive post is liable.
   **Fact:** Sharing, forwarding, or extensively amplifying illegal content (like child pornography or fake news causing riots) can also attract criminal liability.
5. **Myth:** I don't need a URL, just the name on the profile is enough for police.
   **Fact:** The exact URL is crucial because multiple profiles can have the same display name.
6. **Myth:** The police cannot take action against foreign platforms like X or Facebook.
   **Fact:** Platforms operating in India are bound by Indian IT Rules and must cooperate with law enforcement.
7. **Myth:** Only women can be victims of cyber crimes.
   **Fact:** Anyone, regardless of gender, can be a victim of hacking, financial fraud, or defamation online. (Though stalking laws currently are gender-specific toward protecting women).
8. **Myth:** You must hire a lawyer to file a cyber crime complaint.
   **Fact:** You can file a complaint directly on the NCRP portal or at a police station for free without a lawyer.
9. **Myth:** Using a VPN makes you completely invisible to the police.
   **Fact:** While a VPN masks your IP, advanced forensics and cooperation with VPN providers or correlating other digital footprints can still identify perpetrators.
10. **Myth:** If my account is hacked and posts illegal content, I will definitely go to jail.
    **Fact:** If you can prove your account was compromised (by reporting the hack immediately), you will not be held liable for the hacker's actions.

*Assistant (English):* This is a serious crime. File a complaint immediately on cybercrime.gov.in. Under IT rules, platforms must take down non-consensual intimate images or deepfakes within 24 hours of receiving a complaint.

*Assistant (English):* Yes, but tracing WhatsApp messages can be complex due to end-to-end encryption. However, you can file a police complaint under BNS Section 356 (Defamation). The police can request metadata from WhatsApp and investigate the sender's phone number.

*Assistant (Hinglish):* Nahi, National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (NCRP) ya police station mein complaint darj karne ki koi fees nahi hoti hai. Yeh bilkul free hai.

**Example 6**

*Assistant (English):* Yes, you can still file a complaint. Explain that you deleted it out of panic. The police can often retrieve deleted data through forensic tools or by requesting logs from the service provider, though it makes the process slightly harder.

**Example 7**

**Example 8**
*Citizen:* Will my parents know if I report online harassment?
*Assistant (English):* If you are a minor, police usually involve parents or guardians. If you are an adult, you can file a complaint independently. For sensitive issues, you can report anonymously on the cyber crime portal.

**Example 9**
*Citizen:* Maine ek online product order kiya tha Facebook ad dekh kar, par fraud ho gaya.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Yeh online financial fraud hai. Turant 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline) par call karein. Transaction details aur Facebook ad ka screenshot lekar cybercrime.gov.in par complaint karein.

**Example 10**
*Citizen:* What is the punishment for revenge porn in India?
*Assistant (English):* Sharing non-consensual sexually explicit material (revenge porn) is punishable under Section 67A of the IT Act. The punishment for the first conviction is imprisonment of up to 5 years and a fine up to ₹10 Lakhs.

**Example 11**
*Citizen:* Kya cyber police sach mein action leti hai?
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Haan, bilkul. Cyber police ke paas advanced tools aur technical expertise hoti hai. Agar aap sahi evidence (screenshots, URL) provide karte hain, toh strict action liya jata hai.

**Example 12**
*Citizen:* Someone is trolling me in the comments of my YouTube channel.
*Assistant (English):* If the trolling crosses into severe abuse, death threats, or defamation, you can take legal action. Document the comments (screenshots/URLs), block/ban the user on YouTube, and if you feel threatened, report to the police.

**Example 14**
*Citizen:* What is Section 66C of the IT Act?
*Assistant (English):* Section 66C of the IT Act deals with identity theft. It makes it illegal to fraudulently or dishonestly make use of the electronic signature, password, or any other unique identification feature of another person, such as creating a fake profile.

## Government Services
**English:**
*   **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (NCRP):** The central website (`cybercrime.gov.in`) for reporting all forms of cyber crime.
*   **1930 Helpline:** A dedicated toll-free helpline for reporting cyber financial frauds.
*   **CERT-In:** For reporting technical vulnerabilities and large-scale cyber attacks.

*   **CERT-In:** तकनीकी कमजोरियों और बड़े पैमाने पर साइबर हमलों की रिपोर्ट करने के लिए।
