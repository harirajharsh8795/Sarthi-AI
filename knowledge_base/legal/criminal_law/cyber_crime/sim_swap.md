# SIM Swap Fraud: Comprehensive Legal Guide

## Overview

**English:** SIM Swap fraud (or SIM splitting) is a sophisticated form of cybercrime where a scammer convinces your telecom operator to port your phone number to a new SIM card in their possession. By doing so, the scammer intercepts all your incoming calls and text messages, most importantly the One-Time Passwords (OTPs) required for banking transactions, allowing them to drain your bank accounts and steal your digital identity. This guide provides a detailed understanding of the legal frameworks, immediate actions required, and preventive measures against SIM Swap frauds in India.

**Hindi (Unicode):** सिम स्वैप फ्रॉड (SIM Swap Fraud) एक गंभीर साइबर अपराध है जहाँ एक जालसाज़ आपके टेलीकॉम ऑपरेटर को धोखा देकर आपका मोबाइल नंबर एक नए सिम कार्ड पर पोर्ट करवा लेता है। इसके बाद, आपके सभी इनकमिंग कॉल और मैसेज (विशेष रूप से बैंकिंग ओटीपी) उनके पास जाने लगते हैं। इसका उपयोग करके वे आपके बैंक खाते खाली कर सकते हैं और आपकी डिजिटल पहचान चुरा सकते हैं। यह गाइड भारत में सिम स्वैप धोखाधड़ी के खिलाफ कानूनी ढांचे, तत्काल आवश्यक कार्रवाई और निवारक उपायों की विस्तृत जानकारी प्रदान करती है।

- **Immediate action:** Call 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline), freeze your bank accounts, and contact your telecom operator immediately to block the SIM.

## Definition

**English:** SIM Swapping is an account takeover fraud that targets a weakness in two-factor authentication and two-step verification in which the second factor or step is an SMS or a call placed to a mobile phone. Legally, it encompasses identity theft, cheating by personation, and unauthorized access to computer resources and electronic communication networks.

**Hindi (Unicode):** सिम स्वैपिंग एक अकाउंट टेकओवर धोखाधड़ी है जो टू-फैक्टर ऑथेंटिकेशन (2FA) की कमजोरी को निशाना बनाती है, जिसमें दूसरा चरण मोबाइल फोन पर भेजा गया एसएमएस या कॉल होता है। कानूनी रूप से, इसमें पहचान की चोरी, प्रतिरूपण द्वारा धोखाधड़ी, और कंप्यूटर संसाधनों व इलेक्ट्रॉनिक संचार नेटवर्क तक अनधिकृत पहुंच शामिल है।

## Legal Background

The prosecution and prevention of SIM Swap frauds in India are governed primarily by the Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000, the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023, and the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023, along with sectoral guidelines from the RBI and DoT.

### Relevant Acts & Sections

#### 1. Information Technology Act, 2000
- **Section 43:** Penalty and compensation for damage to computer, computer system, etc. (Unauthorized access to electronic data).
- **Section 66:** Computer related offences (Dishonestly or fraudulently doing any act referred to in Section 43).
- **Section 66C:** Punishment for identity theft (Fraudulently making use of the electronic signature, password, or any other unique identification feature of any other person).
- **Section 66D:** Punishment for cheating by personation by using computer resource.

#### 2. Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023 (Replaces IPC)
- **Section 318:** Cheating (Deceiving any person fraudulently or dishonestly, inducing them to deliver property).
- **Section 319:** Cheating by personation (Pretending to be some other person to cheat).
- **Section 316:** Criminal breach of trust.
- **Section 338:** Forgery of valuable security, will, etc. (Relevant if fake documents were submitted to the telecom operator for SIM replacement).
- **Section 111:** Organized crime (If the fraud is executed by a sophisticated cybercrime syndicate on a large scale).

#### 3. Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023 (Replaces IEA)
- **Section 61:** Admissibility of electronic records.
- **Section 62 & 63:** Special provisions as to evidence relating to electronic records. Proper hash values and certification must be provided to present SMS logs, emails, and transaction history in court.

#### 4. Regulatory Guidelines
- **RBI Circular on Customer Protection (2017/2020):** Limits liability of customers in unauthorized electronic banking transactions to 'Zero' if reported within 3 working days.
- **DoT (Department of Telecommunications) KYC Directives:** Mandates strict verification protocols for issuing duplicate SIM cards or processing MNP (Mobile Number Portability).

## Applicability

- **Who is covered:** Any individual or corporate entity whose telecom services are registered in India and whose bank accounts/digital assets are compromised due to illegal SIM replacement.
- **Who is exempt:** Customers who are proven to have colluded with the fraudsters or who have exhibited gross negligence (e.g., voluntarily sharing their OTPs or physical SIM card, failing to report the fraud for an extended period after noticing the network loss) may lose the protection of zero-liability.
- **Special Conditions:** The telecom operator may also be held liable for deficiency in service under the Consumer Protection Act if it is proven that they issued a duplicate SIM without conducting the mandated physical KYC verifications or by ignoring obvious red flags.

**English:**

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English:**
1. **Immediate Disconnection:** The moment your phone loses signal unexpectedly and you receive suspicious emails about password changes, use another phone to call your telecom operator and block your number immediately.
2. **Freeze Financial Accounts:** Call your bank(s) and credit card companies to freeze all accounts linked to that mobile number. Request them to stop all outward clearing.
3. **Call 1930:** Dial the National Cyber Crime Reporting Helpline (1930) to report the financial fraud. They can alert the respective banking nodes to block the transferred funds.
4. **Online Complaint:** Register a formal complaint on `cybercrime.gov.in`. Obtain the acknowledgment number.
5. **Register FIR:** Visit the nearest police station or cyber crime police station. Under BNS Section 318, 319, and IT Act Sections 66C/66D, lodge an FIR. Provide the online complaint acknowledgment.
6. **Gather Evidence:** Collect bank statements, emails notifying password changes, and a formal letter from the telecom operator detailing the time of the unauthorized SIM swap.
7. **Submit Dispute to Bank:** Submit a formal dispute form to your bank quoting the FIR and RBI guidelines for reversing the unauthorized transactions.
8. **Consumer Forum (Optional):** If the telecom operator is found negligent in verifying documents before issuing the SIM, file a case in the District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission for compensation.

## Required Documents

- Government-issued ID proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID).
- Stamped bank statements showing the unauthorized deductions.
- Call/SMS logs or emails showing password reset requests.
- Acknowledgment receipt from the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal.
- Copy of the formal complaint submitted to the telecom operator.
- Written application to the Bank Manager detailing the timeline of the fraud.
- Section 63 BSA certificate for all electronic evidence (emails, digital statements).

## Government Authorities

- **Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C):** Under MHA, manages the 1930 helpline and cybercrime.gov.in.
- **Reserve Bank of India (RBI):** Ombudsman handles disputes if your bank refuses to refund the stolen money within 30 days.
- **Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) & DoT:** Sets the guidelines for SIM replacement and KYC protocols that telecom operators must follow.
- **Local Police / Cyber Cell:** For criminal investigation and tracking IP addresses/digital footprints of the fraudsters.
- **Consumer Courts (NCDRC/SCDRC/DCDRC):** For civil suits against telecom operators for deficiency of service.

## Penalties & Punishments

- **Under IT Act, Sec 66C (Identity Theft):** Imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to 3 years and shall also be liable to fine which may extend to ₹1 Lakh.
- **Under IT Act, Sec 66D (Cheating by Personation):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to ₹1 Lakh.
- **Under BNS, 2023 Sec 318 (Cheating):** Depending on the severity and amount, imprisonment for a term which may extend to 3 to 7 years, and fine.
- **Under BNS, 2023 Sec 319 (Cheating by personation):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, or with fine, or both.
- **Under BNS, 2023 Sec 111 (Organized Crime):** If part of a syndicate, punishment can range from 5 years to life imprisonment with a minimum fine of ₹5 Lakhs.

---

## Exceptions

- **Delayed Reporting:** If the victim reports the fraud to the bank after 7 working days, the customer’s liability shall be determined as per the bank's Board-approved policy (which often means the customer bears the loss).
- **Victim Negligence:** If the victim actively shared a 20-digit SIM number, an OTP to initiate porting, or clicked on a phishing link granting remote access, they may lose the protection of RBI's zero-liability mandate.
- **Corporate Accounts:** RBI guidelines on zero liability primarily protect retail individuals; corporate current accounts may have different contractual liabilities.

---

## Important Timelines

- **Immediate/1 Hour:** Golden hour to call 1930 and freeze bank accounts to stop fund routing.
- **3 Working Days:** Maximum timeframe to report unauthorized transactions to the bank to ensure absolute Zero Liability.
- **4 to 7 Working Days:** Customer liability is capped at a specific amount (e.g., ₹10,000 to ₹25,000 depending on account type) if reported in this window.
- **90 Days:** Timeframe within which the bank must resolve the dispute and reverse the money (if eligible) after the complaint is registered.
- **30 Days:** If the bank rejects the claim or does not reply, the customer can approach the RBI Banking Ombudsman.

---

## Common Legal Mistakes

- **Assuming Network Glitch:** Ignoring a dead network for 2-3 days thinking it's a telecom issue, giving fraudsters ample time.
- **Failing to Register FIR:** Banks require an FIR or cybercrime portal acknowledgment to process refunds. Failing to provide this delays the process.
- **Not Informing All Institutions:** Blocking the bank account but forgetting about linked credit cards, mutual funds, or crypto exchanges.
- **Arguing only with Telecom Operator:** Wasting time fighting with the telecom store instead of first calling the bank to secure the funds.

**English:** They often use fake, forged, or stolen ID documents. Sometimes, they bribe corrupt insiders at third-party telecom kiosks to bypass biometric and physical verification.
**Hindi:** वे अक्सर नकली या चोरी किए गए आईडी दस्तावेज़ों का उपयोग करते हैं। कभी-कभी वे वेरिफिकेशन से बचने के लिए टेलीकॉम कियोस्क के भ्रष्ट कर्मचारियों को रिश्वत देते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Wo log fake ya chori kiye gaye ID documents use karte hain, ya phir telecom stores ke corrupt staff ko bribe karke verification bypass kar dete hain.

**Q3: Will my phone show any signs of a SIM swap?**
**English:** Yes, your mobile phone will abruptly lose all network signal (showing "No Service" or "Emergency Calls Only") because your current SIM is deactivated by the operator.
**Hindi:** हाँ, आपके फोन का नेटवर्क अचानक चला जाएगा (नो सर्विस या एमरजेंसी कॉल्स ओनली दिखाई देगा) क्योंकि आपका मौजूदा सिम डीएक्टिवेट हो जाता है।

**Hindi:** हाँ, यदि वे अपने बैंक खातों से जुड़े भारतीय टेलीकॉम नेटवर्क का उपयोग कर रहे हैं। भारत में रहते हुए उन पर भी आरबीआई और बीएनएस (BNS) के तहत समान कानूनी प्रक्रिया और सुरक्षा लागू होती है।

## Common Myths vs Facts

**Myth 1:** My bank is unhackable, so my money is safe even if my SIM is cloned.
**Fact:** SIM swap bypasses the bank's security by intercepting the very OTPs the bank sends to verify it's you. It targets the telecom weakness, not the bank.

**Myth 2:** The telecom company will automatically stop unauthorized SIM changes.
**Fact:** Scammers use fake IDs and advanced social engineering, which often fools basic telecom customer service agents.

**Myth 3:** I am responsible for the lost money because my number was used.
**Fact:** RBI guidelines protect you. If you report the fraud within 3 working days, your liability is zero, as the fraud occurred due to system vulnerabilities, not your negligence.

**Myth 4:** Only rich individuals get targeted by SIM swap frauds.
**Fact:** Scammers target anyone whose data they can buy on the dark web or acquire through phishing, regardless of their net worth.

**Myth 5:** The police cannot do anything about cybercrimes.
**Fact:** The Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (1930) has specialized mechanisms to freeze stolen funds across bank nodes instantly if reported within the golden hour.

**Myth 6:** Changing my bank password regularly prevents SIM swap.
**Fact:** Passwords alone aren't enough because the attacker can use the 'Forgot Password' feature and use the intercepted OTP on your swapped SIM to reset it.

**Myth 7:** A 24-hour SMS block on a new SIM stops all frauds.
**Fact:** While TRAI mandates a 24-hour block on incoming SMS for new/upgraded SIMs, sophisticated scammers sometimes manipulate telecom insiders to lift this block prematurely.

**Myth 8:** If I didn't click any link, I cannot be a victim of SIM swap.
**Fact:** SIM swapping does not require you to click a link or install malware; it happens entirely off-device at the telecom provider's end.

**Myth 9:** Banks will instantly reverse the funds the moment I complain.
**Fact:** Banks require a formal investigation, a copy of the FIR, and up to 90 days to process the refund under RBI guidelines.

**Myth 10:** Consumer courts won't entertain cases against telecom giants.
**Fact:** Numerous judgments by State and National Consumer Commissions have directed telecom operators to pay heavy compensation for issuing duplicate SIMs without proper KYC.

## Conversation Examples

**Example 1: Recognizing the Fraud**
*Citizen:* Mera phone kal se 'No Service' dikha raha hai, aur aaj subah email check kiya toh bank se 50,000 cut gaye hain. Kya karu?
*Assistant:* Yeh SIM Swap fraud ka clear case hai. Scammer ne aapka duplicate SIM nikalwa kar OTPs intercept kar liye hain. Turant kisi aur phone se apne bank aur telecom operator ko call karein aur accounts/SIM block karwayein. Phir 1930 par dial karke cyber fraud report karein.

**Example 2: Determining Liability**
*Citizen:* I reported the unauthorized transaction on the second day. Will the bank force me to bear the loss?
*Assistant:* No. According to the RBI circular on unauthorized electronic banking transactions, if you report the fraud within 3 working days and you did not share any credentials yourself, your liability is strictly zero. The bank must refund you.

**Example 3: Dealing with Telecom Operators**
*Citizen:* Airtel/Jio store wale bol rahe hain unki galti nahi hai, unhone valid ID dekh kar hi SIM diya tha.
*Assistant:* Unka yeh kehna kafi nahi hai. Unhe kanooni taur par prove karna hoga ki unhone proper physical verification kiya tha. Aap unse written logs maang sakte hain aur agar unki negligence milti hai toh Consumer Court mein deficiency of service ka case kar sakte hain.

**Example 4: Filing an FIR**
*Citizen:* Police station mein inspector keh rahe hain ki yeh unke jurisdiction ka matter nahi hai.
*Assistant:* Cyber crimes mein jurisdiction ki koi seema nahi hoti. BNS 2023 aur IT Act ke tahat police ko 'Zero FIR' darj karni hi padegi. Agar wo mana karein, toh aap SP/DCP Cyber ko direct written complaint de sakte hain ya cybercrime.gov.in ka portal use karein.

**Example 5: Preventing Future Attacks**
*Citizen:* Mujhe is fraud se dobara bachne ke liye kya karna chahiye?
*Assistant:* Apne bank aur email accounts par SMS-based OTP ki jagah Google Authenticator ya Microsoft Authenticator app use karna shuru karein. Saath hi, social media par apna phone number aur date of birth private rakhein.

**Example 6: Delay in Reporting**
*Citizen:* Main out of station tha, 10 din baad pata chala ki SIM swap hua aur paise cut gaye. Kya ab refund milega?
*Assistant:* 7 din se zyada delay hone par 'Zero Liability' ka rule automatically apply nahi hota. Ab refund bank ki internal policy par depend karega. Fir bhi, turant 1930 par report karein aur FIR darj karwayein.

**Example 7: Corporate Account Swap**
*Citizen:* Meri company ke current account se SIM swap ke through 5 lakh nikle hain. Does RBI zero liability apply?
*Assistant:* RBI ki zero liability policy primarily retail savings accounts ke liye hai. Corporate/Current accounts ke terms alag hote hain aur contract par depend karte hain. Aapko turant legal action aur FIR proceed karni hogi.

**Example 8: Gathering Evidence**
*Citizen:* Bank wale proof maang rahe hain ki yeh SIM swap tha, main kaise prove karu?
*Assistant:* Aapko apne telecom operator se ek formal letter request karna hoga jisme likha ho ki duplicate SIM kis date aur time par issue hua. Us time ko bank transactions ke time se match karke aap prove kar sakte hain.

**Example 9: Phishing Link vs SIM Swap**
*Citizen:* Maine ek link par click kiya tha jisse ek app download hui, aur paise cut gaye. Kya ye SIM swap hai?
*Assistant:* Nahi, yeh malware/phishing fraud hai (screen mirroring ya SMS forwarding). SIM swap mein aapke phone ka network poori tarah chala jata hai kyunki naya SIM scammer ke device mein activate ho jata hai.

**Example 10: Suspicious SMS Warning**
*Citizen:* Mujhe abhi message aaya hai "Your SIM porting request is initiated" par maine aisi koi request nahi daali.
*Assistant:* Ise bilkul ignore na karein! Turant apne telecom operator (Airtel, Jio, Vi) ke customer care ko call karein aur is request ko cancel karwayein, yeh SIM swap attack ki shuruaat ho sakti hai.

**Example 11: E-SIM Swapping**
*Citizen:* Main physical SIM use nahi karta, mera E-SIM hai. Kya mere sath bhi ye fraud ho sakta hai?
*Assistant:* Haan, agar aapka primary email account hack ho jaye, toh scammer us email ke through E-SIM ka naya QR code request karke apne phone par activate kar sakta hai. Apne email par 2FA hamesha on rakhein.

**Example 12: Role of 1930**
*Citizen:* 1930 par call karne se kya sach me paise wapas mil jate hain?
*Assistant:* 1930 (Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre) turant alag-alag banks ke nodal officers ko alert bhejta hai. Agar aapne fraud ke 1-2 ghante ke andar report kiya (Golden Hour), toh wo un funds ko aage transfer hone se freeze kar sakte hain.

**Example 13: Banking Ombudsman**
*Citizen:* 40 din ho gaye bank ne report lene ke baad bhi refund nahi kiya. Next step kya hai?
*Assistant:* Aap RBI ke Complaint Management System (CMS) portal par jakar Banking Ombudsman ko direct complaint file karein. Wahan apni FIR aur 3 din ke andar kiye gaye intimation ka proof attach karein.

**Example 14: Recovering Access**
*Citizen:* Scammer ne mere Gmail ka password bhi badal diya hai OTP se. Main access kaise wapas lu?
*Assistant:* Pehle apna SIM card telecom operator se restore karwayein. Uske baad Google ke account recovery page par jakar 'Forgot Password' karein aur apne mobile number par OTP mangwa kar account recover karein.

**Example 15: Document Forgery**
*Citizen:* Scammer ne mera fake Aadhaar card banakar SIM liya, iske liye kon si dhara lagegi?
*Assistant:* Fake ID use karne par BNS ki dhara 318 (Cheating), 319 (Cheating by personation), aur 338/339 (Forgery of documents) lagegi, sath hi IT Act ki dhara 66C aur 66D bhi shamil hongi.
