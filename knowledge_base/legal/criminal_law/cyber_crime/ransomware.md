# Ransomware & Cyber Extortion: Legal Rights, Procedures, and Protections in India

## Overview

**English:** Ransomware is a malicious software that infects a computer or network, encrypting the victim's data and making it inaccessible. The attackers demand a "ransom" (often in cryptocurrency) to provide the decryption key. Under Indian law, deploying ransomware involves multiple offences including unauthorized access, data destruction, cheating, and extortion.

## Definition

**English:** Ransomware is defined legally and technically as a class of malware designed to deny access to a computer system or data until a ransom is paid. In legal terms, it constitutes "cyber extortion" and "computer-related offences" involving unauthorized access, data manipulation, and demand of property under threat.

## Legal Background

The legal framework addressing ransomware in India draws from the Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000 and the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS).

*   **History & Purpose:** Initially, traditional extortion laws were applied to ransomware. The IT Act amendments introduced specific penalties for computer-related offences. The recently enforced BNS (replacing IPC) modernizes definitions of extortion and cheating to encompass digital crimes comprehensively.
*   **Information Technology Act, 2000:**
    *   **Section 43 & 66:** Deals with unauthorized access, downloading, extracting data, or introducing computer contaminants (viruses/malware) and prescribes punishment for computer-related offences.
    *   **Section 66C:** Punishment for identity theft.
    *   **Section 66D:** Punishment for cheating by personation by using a computer resource.
    *   **Section 66F:** Covers Cyber Terrorism. If a ransomware attack threatens the unity, integrity, or security of India (e.g., attacking critical national infrastructure like power grids or major hospitals), it is classified as cyber terrorism.
*   **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS):**
    *   **Section 308:** Extortion. Whoever intentionally puts any person in fear of any injury to that person, or to any other, and thereby dishonestly induces the person so put in fear to deliver to any person any property or valuable security.
    *   **Section 318:** Cheating. Covers the fraudulent intent of the attackers.
    *   **Section 351:** Criminal Intimidation. When attackers threaten to release sensitive private data if the ransom is not paid (double extortion).

## Applicability

*   **Who is covered:** All Indian citizens, residents, corporate entities, and government bodies operating within India.
*   **Who is exempt:** No one is exempt from the law. However, certain security researchers working with explicit authorization (Ethical Hackers/Red Teams) conducting sanctioned penetration testing are protected under contractual safe harbors.
*   **Special conditions:** The IT Act has extra-territorial jurisdiction (Section 75). If a foreign national deploys ransomware targeting computers in India, they can be prosecuted under Indian law, subject to extradition treaties and international cooperation.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English:**
1. **Quarantine:** Immediately disconnect the infected device from the internet/LAN to prevent the ransomware from spreading. Do not turn off the device immediately if encryption is ongoing, just isolate it.
2. **Preserve Evidence:** Take photographs of the ransom note on the screen using a mobile phone. Do not delete the ransom note text file.
3. **Call Helpline:** Dial 1930 to inform the Cyber Crime cell immediately.
4. **File Online Complaint:** Go to cybercrime.gov.in and file a detailed complaint under 'Report Other Cyber Crimes'. Attach the photos of the ransom demand.
5. **Report to CERT-In:** If you are a business or organization, report the incident to the Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In) via incident@cert-in.org.in.
6. **Consult Experts:** Check platforms like the "No More Ransom" project (nomoreransom.org) for available free decryption keys.
7. **Police Investigation:** Hand over a cloned copy of your hard drive to the cyber cell for forensic analysis, and follow up for FIR registration.

## Required Documents
*   Photographs/Screenshots of the Ransom Note/Lock Screen.
*   Details of the crypto wallet address where the ransom is demanded.
*   System logs, firewall logs, or email headers (if the ransomware came via a phishing email).
*   Identity Proof (Aadhaar/PAN) of the complainant.
*   Authorization letter (if reporting on behalf of a company).

## Government Authorities
*   **Cyber Crime Cell (State Police):** Primary investigating agency for registering FIRs and tracking criminals.
*   **Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C):** Nodal agency under MHA coordinating cybercrime responses.
*   **CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team):** National nodal agency for responding to computer security incidents.
*   **NCIIPC (National Critical Information Infrastructure Protection Centre):** Handles ransomware attacks on critical sectors (banking, power, defense).

## Penalties & Punishments
*   **IT Act, Section 66 (Computer Related Offences):** Imprisonment up to 3 years or fine up to ₹5 Lakhs, or both.
*   **IT Act, Section 66D (Cheating by Personation):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine up to ₹1 Lakh.
*   **IT Act, Section 66F (Cyber Terrorism):** Imprisonment extending up to Life Imprisonment.
*   **BNS, Section 308 (Extortion):** Imprisonment up to 7 years, and fine. If the extortion involves severe threats of death/grievous hurt, punishment can be up to 10 years or life.
*   **BNS, Section 351 (Criminal Intimidation):** Imprisonment up to 2 years, or with fine, or both.

## Exceptions
*   **Good Faith Security Research:** Actions done in good faith by authorized security auditors to identify vulnerabilities are protected, provided no actual extortion demand is made.
*   **Inadvertent Downloads:** Victims who accidentally download ransomware and infect their employer's network are generally not held criminally liable unless there is proven criminal intent or gross negligence violating company policy.

## Important Timelines
*   **CERT-In Reporting (For Entities):** Mandatory reporting within **6 hours** of noticing the ransomware incident.
*   **Cyber Crime Portal:** Should be reported as immediately as possible to facilitate tracing of cryptocurrency, ideally within **24 hours**.
*   **Data Breach Notification:** If personal data of users is compromised, sector-specific regulators (like RBI for banks, IRDAI for insurance) have timelines varying from 2 to 24 hours.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. **Paying the Ransom:** Paying funds criminal organizations and does not guarantee data recovery. It also prevents law enforcement from tracing the culprits effectively.
2. **Formatting the System Immediately:** Reformatting destroys vital digital evidence, making police investigation impossible.
3. **Failing to Report (Corporate):** Companies hiding ransomware attacks violate CERT-In rules, leading to severe regulatory penalties and liability towards affected customers.
4. **Ignoring Data Protection Laws:** Failing to notify users whose data was compromised in a "double extortion" ransomware attack.

**Hindi:** हालांकि फिरौती देना सीधे तौर पर अपराध नहीं है, लेकिन आतंकवादी संस्थाओं को भुगतान करना आतंकवाद विरोधी कानूनों का उल्लंघन कर सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Ransom pay karna directly crime nahi hai, par agar paisa terrorist groups ko jata hai toh UAPA ke under problem ho sakti hai.

**10. Can I sue the company whose database was breached?**
**English:** Yes, you can approach the Data Protection Board or consumer courts to seek compensation for inadequate security measures leading to your data loss.
**Hindi:** हां, आप डेटा सुरक्षा बोर्ड या उपभोक्ता अदालतों से अपर्याप्त सुरक्षा के लिए मुआवजे की मांग कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar company ki security weak thi aur apka data leak hua, toh aap Data Protection Board ya consumer court ja sakte hain.

**11. What if ransomware hits a hospital?**
**English:** Attacks on critical infrastructure like hospitals are treated as Cyber Terrorism under Section 66F of the IT Act, carrying up to life imprisonment.
**Hindi:** अस्पतालों जैसे महत्वपूर्ण बुनियादी ढांचे पर हमलों को आईटी अधिनियम की धारा 66 एफ के तहत साइबर आतंकवाद माना जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Hospitals par attack ko IT Act ke Section 66F ke under Cyber Terrorism maana jata hai, jisme life imprisonment tak ki saza hai.

**12. Is ransomware considered cyber terrorism?**
**English:** It can be. If it is designed to threaten the unity, integrity, security, or sovereignty of India, or strikes critical infrastructure, it falls under cyber terrorism.
**Hindi:** यह हो सकता है। यदि यह भारत की एकता, अखंडता, सुरक्षा को खतरे में डालता है, तो यह साइबर आतंकवाद के अंतर्गत आता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar attack desh ki security ya bade infrastructure par hota hai, toh isko cyber terrorism ki category mein rakha jata hai.

**13. How long does a cyber investigation take?**
**English:** Due to the use of dark web and cryptocurrencies, investigations are highly complex and can take several months to years, often requiring international cooperation (Interpol).
**Hindi:** डार्क वेब और क्रिप्टोकरेंसी के उपयोग के कारण, जांच अत्यधिक जटिल होती है और इसमें कई महीने या साल लग सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Dark web aur crypto ke use ki wajah se investigation complex hoti hai. Isme kayi mahine ya saal lag sakte hain aur international police ki madad chahiye hoti hai.

**14. Do I need a lawyer to file a cyber complaint?**
**English:** No. You can file a complaint directly on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal or visit a police station without a lawyer.
**Hindi:** नहीं। आप किसी वकील के बिना सीधे राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध रिपोर्टिंग पोर्टल पर या पुलिस स्टेशन में शिकायत दर्ज कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. Aap cybercrime.gov.in par ya 1930 par bina kisi lawyer ke directly complaint file kar sakte hain.

**15. What if the attacker is based outside India?**
**English:** Indian laws have extra-territorial reach. The police coordinate with Interpol and foreign agencies via Mutual Legal Assistance Treaties (MLATs) to track them.
**Hindi:** भारतीय कानूनों की पहुंच विदेश तक है। पुलिस इंटरपोल और विदेशी एजेंसियों के साथ समन्वय करती है।
**Hinglish:** Indian laws extra-territorial apply hote hain. Police Interpol aur foreign agencies ke saath milkar investigate karti hai.

**16. How to preserve digital evidence?**
**English:** Take photos of the screen, do not format the hard drive, note the crypto addresses, and keep logs of network activity. Hand over the physical device to the police.
**Hindi:** स्क्रीन की तस्वीरें लें, हार्ड ड्राइव को फॉर्मेट न करें, क्रिप्टो पतों को नोट करें। भौतिक उपकरण पुलिस को सौंप दें।
**Hinglish:** Screen ki photo lein, hard drive format na karein, aur crypto addresses note karein. Apni device as-is police ko forensic ke liye dein.

**17. Can I negotiate with the hackers?**
**English:** It is strongly advised against. Engaging with criminals confirms your system is active and often leads to higher demands or repeated attacks.
**Hindi:** इसकी सख्त मनाही है। अपराधियों के साथ बातचीत करने से प्रायः उच्च मांग या बार-बार हमले होते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Ye bilkul suggest nahi kiya jata. Hackers se baat karne par wo aur zyada paise maang sakte hain ya dubara attack kar sakte hain.

**18. Does cyber insurance cover ransomware?**
**English:** Many modern cyber insurance policies cover ransomware incidents, covering costs like forensic investigation, legal fees, and sometimes the ransom itself (if legally permissible).
**Hindi:** कई आधुनिक साइबर बीमा पॉलिसियां रैंसमवेयर की घटनाओं को कवर करती हैं, जिसमें फोरेंसिक जांच और कानूनी शुल्क शामिल हैं।
**Hinglish:** Aaj kal cyber insurance policies ransomware attacks cover karti hain, jisme investigation, legal fees, aur recovery costs shamil hote hain.

**19. What to do if official government documents are locked?**
**English:** Inform your departmental head immediately. Government bodies must escalate this to CERT-In and NCIIPC urgently to contain national security threats.
**Hindi:** तुरंत अपने विभागाध्यक्ष को सूचित करें। सरकारी निकायों को राष्ट्रीय सुरक्षा खतरों को रोकने के लिए इसे तुरंत CERT-In को रिपोर्ट करना चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Apne head of department ko turant inform karein. Sarkari data lock hone par matter urgent basis par CERT-In ko report karna hota hai.

**20. Are free decryptors safe to use?**
**English:** Use only verified decryptors provided by trusted entities like the "No More Ransom" project, Kaspersky, or CERT-In. Random online decryptors are often disguised malware.
**Hindi:** केवल "नो मोर रैंसम" प्रोजेक्ट जैसी विश्वसनीय संस्थाओं द्वारा प्रदान किए गए सत्यापित डिक्रिप्टर का उपयोग करें।
**Hinglish:** Sirf trusted sources (jaise No More Ransom ya CERT-In) ke decryptors use karein. Google search karke random decryptors download na karein, wo aur virus ho sakte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Paying the ransom guarantees I will get my files back.
   **Fact:** Criminals often take the money and disappear without providing the decryption key. Never pay.
2. **Myth:** Mac and Linux systems cannot get infected by ransomware.
   **Fact:** Modern ransomware variants are specifically designed to target macOS, Linux servers, and mobile devices.
3. **Myth:** Ransomware only targets large corporations.
   **Fact:** Small businesses and individuals are highly targeted because they often lack robust cybersecurity defenses.
4. **Myth:** Formatting my computer deletes the malware permanently.
   **Fact:** While it deletes the malware, it also permanently deletes your encrypted files. Also, advanced malware can hide in BIOS/UEFI.
5. **Myth:** Having an antivirus guarantees 100% protection against ransomware.
   **Fact:** No security solution is 100% foolproof. Zero-day ransomware attacks can bypass traditional antivirus software.
6. **Myth:** If I report to the police, the hackers will delete my data.
   **Fact:** Hackers operate on automated scripts. Reporting to authorities is crucial and does not directly trigger data deletion by the attackers.
7. **Myth:** I have to pay within the 24-hour countdown timer or lose everything.
   **Fact:** The timer is a psychological manipulation tactic. Focus on isolation and reporting instead of panicking.
8. **Myth:** As a small business, I don't need dedicated offline backups.
   **Fact:** Offline backups (disconnected from the network) are the ONLY surefire way to recover from a ransomware attack without paying.
9. **Myth:** Police don't care about individual ransomware attacks.
   **Fact:** Cyber cells actively log all incidents. Individual reports help build patterns to take down large ransomware syndicates.
10. **Myth:** Cyber insurance will unconditionally cover all ransomware damages.
    **Fact:** Insurers require proof that you maintained basic security standards. Negligence can lead to claim denial.

## Conversation Examples

**Pair 1:**
*Citizen:* My computer screen is locked and showing a red skull asking for Bitcoin. What do I do?
*Assistant (English):* You have been hit by ransomware. Do not pay. Immediately disconnect your computer from the Wi-Fi/Internet, take a picture of the screen using your phone, and call 1930 to report it.

**Pair 2:**
*Citizen:* The hacker is asking for 1 Bitcoin to give my photos back. Should I pay?
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Bilkul nahi. Ransom pay karne ke baad bhi hacker data wapas dega iski koi guarantee nahi hai. Aap cybercrime.gov.in par complaint file karein aur police ko evidence dein.

**Pair 3:**
*Citizen:* Can the police trace the Bitcoin I was asked to send?
*Assistant (English):* Yes, Indian cyber cells use advanced blockchain forensics to track cryptocurrency transactions, often collaborating with crypto exchanges to freeze accounts if the attackers attempt to cash out.

**Pair 4:**
*Citizen:* Muje ransomware aya hai, maine laptop format kar diya. Sahi kiya?
*Assistant (Hindi):* नहीं, यह सही नहीं था। लैपटॉप फॉर्मेट करने से आपके एन्क्रिप्टेड फ़ाइलें हमेशा के लिए डिलीट हो गईं और साइबर पुलिस के लिए फोरेंसिक सबूत भी नष्ट हो गए। 

**Pair 5:**
*Citizen:* My company's database was hit. Do we legally have to tell anyone?
*Assistant (English):* Yes. Under the Information Technology Act regulations, organizations are legally mandated to report cybersecurity incidents, including ransomware, to CERT-In within 6 hours.

**Pair 6:**
*Citizen:* 1930 helpline is not answering, what is the alternate?
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Agar 1930 busy hai, toh aap directly www.cybercrime.gov.in par jaakar online complaint file kar sakte hain, ya apne nazdiki police station mein Cyber Desk par jaa sakte hain.

**Pair 7:**
*Citizen:* Is ransomware a cognizable offence in India?
*Assistant (English):* Yes, under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (Extortion) and the IT Act, it is a cognizable and non-bailable offence, meaning the police can register an FIR and arrest without a warrant.

**Pair 8:**
*Citizen:* The hacker is threatening to leak my private photos if I don't pay. What is this?
*Assistant (English):* This is known as "Double Extortion". It falls under Section 351 of BNS (Criminal Intimidation) and Section 66E of the IT Act (Privacy violation). Report this immediately to the cyber police.

**Pair 9:**
*Citizen:* Kya free decryptor tools internet par safe hain?
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Zyadatar free tools jo random sites par milte hain wo fake aur malicious hote hain. Sirf "nomoreransom.org" ya CERT-In jaisi verified websites ke tools use karein.

**Pair 10:**
*Citizen:* The ransom is only Rs 5000, maybe I should just pay it to avoid hassle?
*Assistant (English):* Even small amounts fund criminal syndicates. Additionally, your payment marks you as a "willing payer," making you a prime target for future, more severe attacks. Do not pay.

**Pair 11:**
*Citizen:* What documents to give to cyber cell for ransomware?
*Assistant (English):* You need to provide a screenshot/photo of the ransom note, the Bitcoin address provided by the attacker, any phishing emails that triggered the attack, your ID proof, and hand over the infected device.

**Pair 12:**
*Citizen:* Hacker has sent me a sample decrypted file to prove they have the key.
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Yeh unka trust build karne ka tactic hai. Par sample decrypt karne ka matlab yeh nahi ki wo poora data decrypt karenge paisa milne ke baad. Police ko sab inform karein.

**Pair 13:**
*Citizen:* Police is asking for my laptop, is it safe to give it?
*Assistant (Hindi):* हां, साइबर फोरेंसिक जांच के लिए पुलिस को आपका लैपटॉप चाहिए। वे यह पता लगाने के लिए सिस्टम लॉग का विश्लेषण करेंगे कि हमलावर ने कैसे प्रवेश किया।

**Pair 14:**
*Citizen:* Can I just factory reset my PC to use it again?
*Assistant (English):* A factory reset will remove the ransomware and allow you to use the PC, but it will permanently delete all your encrypted data. Do this only after giving a cloned copy to the police if an investigation is underway.

**Pair 15:**
*Citizen:* Mera bank account safe hai kya agar laptop lock ho gaya?
*Assistant (Hinglish):* Ransomware usually data lock karta hai, par agar malware mein keylogger bhi hua, toh bank passwords compromise ho sakte hain. Turant kisi dusre device se apne net banking passwords change kar lein.
