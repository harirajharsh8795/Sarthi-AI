# Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act

## Overview
**English:** The Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, enacted in 2012 and amended in 2019, is a comprehensive Indian law designed to protect children (any person below the age of 18 years) from offences of sexual assault, sexual harassment, and pornography. The Act is gender-neutral and prioritizes the best interests of the child, establishing Special Courts for speedy trials and providing child-friendly procedures for reporting, evidence recording, and trial.
**Hindi (Unicode):** यौन अपराधों से बच्चों का संरक्षण (पॉक्सो) अधिनियम, 2012 में लागू किया गया और 2019 में संशोधित, एक व्यापक भारतीय कानून है जिसे बच्चों (18 वर्ष से कम आयु के किसी भी व्यक्ति) को यौन उत्पीड़न, यौन उत्पीड़न और अश्लील साहित्य के अपराधों से बचाने के लिए बनाया गया है। यह अधिनियम लिंग-तटस्थ है और बच्चे के सर्वोत्तम हितों को प्राथमिकता देता है, त्वरित सुनवाई के लिए विशेष न्यायालयों की स्थापना करता है और रिपोर्टिंग, साक्ष्य रिकॉर्डिंग और सुनवाई के लिए बाल-सुलभ प्रक्रियाएं प्रदान करता है।

## Definition
**English:** Under Section 2(d) of the POCSO Act, a "child" is defined as any person below the age of eighteen years. The Act defines various sexual offences including penetrative sexual assault, aggravated penetrative sexual assault, sexual assault, aggravated sexual assault, sexual harassment, and using a child for pornographic purposes.

## Why It Matters
**English:** POCSO is vital because it addresses gaps in earlier laws, making child sexual abuse laws gender-neutral and ensuring mandatory reporting. It protects vulnerable children, ensures harsh penalties (including the death penalty for severe offences), and creates a safe legal environment for victims to seek justice.

## Legal Background
**History:** Enacted in 2012 to comply with India's ratification of the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC) in 1992. It replaced the inadequate provisions of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) concerning child sexual abuse. The POCSO (Amendment) Act, 2019 introduced stricter punishments, including the death penalty.
**Purpose:** To protect children from offences of sexual assault, sexual harassment, and pornography, and to provide for the establishment of Special Courts for the trial of such offences.
**Applicable Acts:** Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012; POCSO Rules, 2020; Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015; Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023; Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) 2023; Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) 2023.
**Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 15(3) (Special provisions for women and children), Article 21 (Right to Life and Personal Liberty), Article 39(e) & (f) (Protection of childhood against exploitation).
**Relevant Sections:**
- Section 3 & 4 POCSO: Penetrative sexual assault and punishment.
- Section 5 & 6 POCSO: Aggravated penetrative sexual assault and punishment (includes death penalty).
- Section 7 & 8 POCSO: Sexual assault and punishment.
- Section 9 & 10 POCSO: Aggravated sexual assault and punishment.
- Section 11 & 12 POCSO: Sexual harassment and punishment.
- Section 13-15 POCSO: Use of child for pornographic purposes.
- Section 19 POCSO: Reporting of offences.
- Section 21 POCSO: Punishment for failure to report or record a case.
- Section 24 & 25 POCSO: Recording of statement of a child by Magistrate.
- Section 33 POCSO: Procedure and powers of Special Court.

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** Every person below 18 years of age (regardless of gender) as a victim. Any person committing the offence (regardless of age, though perpetrators below 18 are tried under the Juvenile Justice Act).
- **Who is exempt:** No exemptions for the offence. Consensual sex involving a person below 18 is still an offence under POCSO because the legal age of consent is 18.
- **Special conditions:** Professionals (doctors, teachers, caretakers) have a heightened responsibility, and aggravated charges apply if a person in a position of trust or authority commits the offence.

3. Institutional Duty: Schools, hospitals, and care homes must immediately report any incidents and cooperate with the authorities.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Reporting:** Inform the SJPU or local police (dial 112 or 1098).
2. **FIR Registration:** Police register a zero FIR or regular FIR without delay.
3. **Statement Recording:** Statement of the child is recorded under Section 161 BNSS (by police, preferably women) and Section 164 BNSS (by Magistrate) in a child-friendly environment.
4. **Medical Examination:** Conducted within 24 hours by a registered medical practitioner, with the consent of parents/guardians, accompanied by a person the child trusts.
5. **Investigation:** Police must complete the investigation and file the charge sheet within 2 months.
6. **Trial:** The Special Court must complete the trial within 1 year from the date of taking cognizance.
7. **Compensation:** The Special Court can order interim and final compensation for the victim.
**Hindi (Unicode):**
1. **रिपोर्टिंग:** SJPU या स्थानीय पुलिस को सूचित करें (112 या 1098 डायल करें)।
2. **FIR पंजीकरण:** पुलिस बिना किसी देरी के शून्य FIR या नियमित FIR दर्ज करती है।
3. **बयान रिकॉर्ड करना:** बच्चे का बयान धारा 161 BNSS (पुलिस द्वारा) और धारा 164 BNSS (मजिस्ट्रेट द्वारा) के तहत बाल-सुलभ वातावरण में दर्ज किया जाता है।
4. **चिकित्सा परीक्षण:** माता-पिता/अभिभावकों की सहमति से 24 घंटे के भीतर एक पंजीकृत चिकित्सक द्वारा किया जाता है।

6. **सुनवाई:** विशेष न्यायालय को संज्ञान लेने की तारीख से 1 वर्ष के भीतर सुनवाई पूरी करनी होगी।

## Government Authorities
1. **Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU):** Specialized police wing for handling children's cases.
2. **Child Welfare Committee (CWC):** Statutory body for care, protection, and rehabilitation of victims.
3. **National/State Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR/SCPCR):** Monitoring authorities.
4. **Special Courts (POCSO Courts):** Designated fast-track courts for POCSO trials.
5. **District Legal Services Authority (DLSA):** Provides free legal aid and compensation.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Penetrative Sexual Assault (Sec 4):** Minimum 10 years imprisonment, extending to life imprisonment, and fine.
- **Aggravated Penetrative Sexual Assault (Sec 6):** Minimum 20 years to life imprisonment, or Death Penalty, and fine.
- **Sexual Assault (Sec 8):** Minimum 3 years, extending to 5 years, and fine.
- **Aggravated Sexual Assault (Sec 10):** Minimum 5 years, extending to 7 years, and fine.
- **Sexual Harassment (Sec 12):** Imprisonment up to 3 years, and fine.
- **Using child for pornography (Sec 14):** 5 to 7 years imprisonment and fine.
- **Failure to report (Sec 21):** Imprisonment up to 6 months, or fine, or both. (For institutions, up to 1 year).
- **Disclosure of victim identity (Sec 23):** Imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine, or both.

## Exceptions
- As per the law, there are NO exceptions. Even if the sexual act was consensual, if one party is under 18, it is a statutory rape and punishable under POCSO.
- Age verification errors by the accused are not a valid defense.

## Important Timelines
- **Medical Examination:** Must be completed within 24 hours of receiving the report.
- **Statement Recording by Magistrate:** As soon as possible.
- **Investigation & Charge Sheet:** Must be completed within 2 months from the date of FIR.
- **Trial Completion:** Must be completed within 1 year from the date of taking cognizance of the offence by the Special Court.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. **Not reporting the crime:** Thinking it is a family matter or fearing social stigma. This is illegal under Section 21.
2. **Washing clothes/bathing the victim:** Destroys crucial forensic DNA evidence.
3. **Publishing details on social media:** Revealing the victim's name or photos is a crime.
4. **Compromising with the accused:** POCSO cases are non-compoundable (cannot be settled outside court).
5. **Delay in filing FIR:** Can weaken the case due to loss of evidence, though delay can be explained in court.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Who can report a POCSO crime?**
   Anyone who has knowledge or suspicion of the crime can and must report it, including the victim, parents, teachers, doctors, or neighbors.
2. **What is the age of consent in India?**
   Under the POCSO Act, the age of consent is strictly 18 years. Any sexual activity with someone below 18 is a crime, regardless of consent.
3. **Is the identity of the child kept secret?**
   Yes, Section 22 and 23 of the POCSO Act strictly prohibit disclosing the identity, name, address, or school of the victim in any media.
4. **Can a POCSO case be withdrawn or settled?**
   No. POCSO offences are non-compoundable. You cannot settle the matter privately or withdraw the case once an FIR is filed.
5. **What if the police refuse to file an FIR?**
   You can approach the Superintendent of Police, file a complaint online on the POCSO e-box, or approach the Magistrate under Section 175(3) BNSS.
6. **Will the child have to face the accused in court?**
   No. Special Courts ensure that the child does not see the accused during trials, often using screens, two-way mirrors, or video conferencing.
7. **Is it mandatory for doctors to report?**
   Yes. Under Section 19, doctors and hospital staff are legally bound to report any signs of child sexual abuse to the police immediately.
8. **What if the abuse happened years ago?**
   You can still report it. While earlier reporting is better for evidence, courts understand trauma delays, and POCSO does not have a strict limitation period for reporting serious offences.
9. **Are boys protected under POCSO?**
   Yes, the POCSO Act is entirely gender-neutral and protects all children below 18, including boys and transgender children.
10. **What is the POCSO e-Box?**
    It is an online portal by NCPCR where children or adults can directly and confidentially report sexual abuse.
11. **Can teachers be punished for not reporting?**
    Yes. If school authorities know about abuse and fail to report it, they can face imprisonment under Section 21 of the Act.
12. **Is viewing child pornography a crime?**
    Yes, under Section 15 of the POCSO Act, possessing, storing, or viewing child pornography with the intent to distribute or fail to destroy it is a punishable offence.
13. **Will the child be interrogated multiple times?**
    No. The Act mandates that the child's statement be recorded only once by the police and once by the Magistrate to prevent repeated trauma.
14. **Is free legal aid available?**
    Yes, the victim and their family are entitled to free legal aid provided by the District Legal Services Authority (DLSA).
15. **Does the victim have to pay for medical treatment?**
    No, emergency medical treatment and psychological support are provided free of cost by government and empanelled private hospitals.
16. **What if a false POCSO case is filed?**
    While the law protects children, if an adult intentionally uses a child to file a false and malicious complaint, courts can take strict action against the adult. However, children are generally protected from prosecution for false reporting to encourage them to speak up.
17. **Can an anonymous complaint be filed?**
    Yes, you can inform the police or Childline anonymously, but an investigation will be required to verify facts before an FIR is lodged.
18. **What is a Special Court?**
    These are designated courts at the district level exclusively meant for fast-tracking POCSO and child abuse cases.
19. **How is compensation decided?**
    The Special Court decides compensation based on the CWC's recommendation, the trauma suffered, and medical needs. Interim compensation can be granted at the FIR stage.
20. **Can parents be accused under POCSO?**
    Yes. If a family member or parent commits the abuse, they are charged with aggravated sexual assault, which carries even harsher penalties.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Boys cannot be victims of sexual abuse.
   **Fact:** Boys are also frequent victims. POCSO is gender-neutral and protects boys equally.
2. **Myth:** Teenage consensual sex is completely legal if both agree.
   **Fact:** The legal age of consent is 18. Any sexual contact below 18 is an offence under POCSO.
3. **Myth:** Family members never abuse children; it's always strangers.
   **Fact:** Statistics show that in a vast majority of cases, the abuser is someone known to the child or a family member.
4. **Myth:** We should settle the matter quietly to protect family honor.
   **Fact:** Settling is illegal and encourages the abuser to harm others. Reporting is mandatory.
5. **Myth:** The child must testify in open court in front of the public.
   **Fact:** POCSO trials are held in-camera (privately), and the child is shielded from the accused and public.
6. **Myth:** If there are no physical injuries, abuse didn't happen.
   **Fact:** Sexual abuse includes inappropriate touching, grooming, and pornography, which may leave no physical scars.
7. **Myth:** Delay in filing FIR means the case is false.
   **Fact:** Courts recognize that trauma, fear, and family pressure often cause delays in reporting child abuse.
8. **Myth:** Only the police can file a POCSO FIR.
   **Fact:** Anyone can report it. You can also use the POCSO e-Box or call 1098.
9. **Myth:** The accused gets bail easily in POCSO.
   **Fact:** POCSO offences are non-bailable. Courts are extremely strict in granting bail to POCSO accused.
10. **Myth:** Taking photos of your naked baby and posting them online is safe.
    **Fact:** It can attract pedophiles and fall under child pornography definitions, leading to misuse and legal trouble.

**Pair 4:**
**Citizen:** Maine galti se ek whatsapp group me child pornography video forward kar di. Kya ye crime hai?
**Assistant:** Haan, POCSO Act Section 14 aur 15 ke under child pornography store karna, dekhna ya forward karna ek bahut bada crime hai. Isme 5-7 saal tak ki jail ho sakti hai. Aap turant aisi videos delete karein aur cyber police me report karein.

**Pair 5:**
**Citizen:** Can the school principal be arrested if they hide an abuse case?
**Assistant:** Absolutely. Under Section 21 of the POCSO Act, if the management or staff of an institution fails to report an offence of child abuse, they are liable for imprisonment up to one year and a fine.

**Pair 6:**
**Citizen:** Police ne FIR darj kar li hai, ab aage kya hoga? (Hindi/Hinglish)
**Assistant:** FIR ke baad police bache ka statement ek lady officer se record karwayegi. Fir 24 ghante mein medical test hoga. Magistrate ke samne (Section 164 BNSS) secret statement record hoga. 2 mahine mein chargesheet aayegi aur Special Court mein private (in-camera) trial chalega.

**Pair 7:**
**Citizen:** Meri pados mein ek uncle ek bache ko gande tareeqe se dekhte hain aur akele mein bulate hain. Kya main report kar sakta hoon?
**Assistant:** Haan, bilkul. Agar aapko doubt bhi hai, tab bhi report karna apka farz hai. Aap anonymously 1098 (Childline) par call kar sakte hain. Police investigation karegi.

**Pair 8:**
**Citizen:** Kya POCSO case me victim ko paise/compensation milta hai?
**Assistant:** Haan, POCSO Act ke Section 33(8) ke under CWC ki recommendation par Special Court victim ke rehabilitation, medical treatment aur trauma relief ke liye interim aur final compensation order karti hai.

**Pair 9:**
**Citizen:** The accused's family is threatening us to take back the case. What to do?
**Assistant:** Intimidating a victim or witness is a separate crime. You should immediately report this to the Investigating Officer or the Special Court. The court can order witness protection programs and cancel the bail of the accused if they are out.

**Pair 10:**
**Citizen:** Mere bache ko court me us insaan ke samne jana padega kya jisne abuse kiya?
**Assistant:** Nahi. POCSO Act ensure karta hai ki bacha accused (aaropi) ke samne na aaye. Courtroom mein parda (screen), video conferencing, ya two-way mirror ka use hota hai taaki bacha bina dare statement de sake.

**Pair 11:**
**Citizen:** Is the death penalty applicable in POCSO?
**Assistant:** Yes, the 2019 amendment introduced the death penalty for cases of 'aggravated penetrative sexual assault' against a child, depending on the severity and brutality of the crime.

**Pair 12:**
**Citizen:** Meri media agency case ki report chaapna chahti hai. Kya hum bache ka face blur karke chhap sakte hain?
**Assistant:** Nahi. POCSO Section 23 ke according, face blur karna kafi nahi hai. Aap bache ka naam, pata, school, family details, ya koi aisi cheez publish nahi kar sakte jisse uski pehchaan ho sake. Ulanghan par jail ho sakti hai.

**Pair 13:**
**Citizen:** Kya bache ka medical test bina uske parents ke ho sakta hai?
**Assistant:** Nahi, medical examination hamesha bache ke parents, guardian, ya kisi aise person ke samne hota hai jispar bacha trust karta ho, aur consent ke baad hi hota hai. (Section 27)

**Pair 14:**
**Citizen:** If a child makes a completely fake story, will they go to jail?
**Assistant:** The law generally protects children from prosecution for false complaints to ensure they are not afraid to report. However, if an adult forced the child to lie to settle personal scores, the adult will face severe legal action.

**Pair 15:**
**Citizen:** Mujhe e-box ke bare mein batayein, kaise use karein?
**Assistant:** POCSO e-Box NCPCR ki website par ek online button hai. Bacha ya adult uspe click karke apni complaint, contact details aur location daal sakta hai. Uske baad trained counselor aapse contact karenge aur police help provide karenge secretly.
