# Rape Laws in India: Complete Guide under Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS)

## Overview
**English:** Rape is one of the most heinous crimes against a woman's body and dignity. The criminal justice system in India has undergone significant reforms with the introduction of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), which replaced the Indian Penal Code (IPC). The laws prioritize the victim's safety, anonymity, and prompt justice through mechanisms like Zero FIR, mandatory medical examinations without degrading tests (like the two-finger test), and strict timelines for investigation and trial.

## Definition
**English:** Rape under Section 63 of BNS occurs when a man has sexual intercourse with a woman against her will, without her consent, or with her consent obtained through coercion, fear of death or hurt, misconception of fact, or when she is intoxicated/unsound mind and unable to understand the nature of the act. The law explicitly states that a woman's lack of physical resistance does not imply consent.
**Hindi (Unicode):** BNS की धारा 63 के तहत बलात्कार तब होता है जब कोई पुरुष किसी महिला के साथ उसकी इच्छा के विरुद्ध, उसकी सहमति के बिना, या जबरदस्ती, मृत्यु या चोट के डर, तथ्य की गलतफहमी के माध्यम से प्राप्त सहमति के साथ यौन संबंध बनाता है, या जब वह नशे में हो/अस्वस्थ दिमाग की हो और कृत्य की प्रकृति को समझने में असमर्थ हो।
**Hinglish:** BNS Section 63 ke mutabiq, jab koi aadmi kisi aurat ke saath uski marzi ke bina, ya bina valid consent ke, ya darr/dhamki dekar physical relation banata hai, toh use rape kaha jata hai. Agar aurat nashe mein hai ya mentally sound nahi hai, tab bhi uski "haan" ko consent nahi mana jayega.

## Legal Background
The transition from IPC to BNS brought structural renumbering and refined definitions for sexual offences.
*   **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023:**
    *   **Section 63 (formerly Section 375 IPC):** Defines the offence of rape.
    *   **Section 64 (formerly Section 376 IPC):** Prescribes the punishment for rape (minimum 10 years to life imprisonment).
    *   **Section 65:** Rape of a woman under 16 years of age (minimum 20 years to life imprisonment).
    *   **Section 66:** Rape of a woman under 12 years of age (life imprisonment or death penalty).
    *   **Section 68 (formerly 376D IPC):** Gang rape.
    *   **Section 70:** Sexual intercourse by a person in authority (custodial rape).
    *   **Section 72 (formerly 228A IPC):** Disclosure of the identity of the victim of certain offences (strictly prohibited).
*   **Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023 (formerly CrPC):**
    *   **Section 173 (formerly 154 CrPC):** Information in cognizable cases; mandates recording of FIR by a woman police officer in sexual offence cases. Also enables Zero FIR and e-FIR.
    *   **Section 184 (formerly 164 CrPC):** Recording of victim's statement before a Magistrate.
    *   **Section 185:** Medical examination of the victim (must be done within 24 hours).
*   **Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023 (formerly IEA):**
    *   **Section 53 (formerly 53A IEA):** Evidence of character or previous sexual experience is not relevant to the issue of consent.
*   **POCSO Act, 2012:** Applies if the victim is a minor (below 18 years). In cases of overlap, the stricter punishment applies.

## Applicability
*   **Who is covered:** Women of all ages are protected under BNS. Minors are additionally protected under the POCSO Act.
*   **Who is exempt:** Under Exception 2 of Section 63 BNS, sexual intercourse or sexual acts by a man with his own wife, the wife not being under eighteen years of age, is not rape (Marital Rape exception, subject to ongoing judicial review).
*   **Special conditions:** Gender neutrality in victimization applies only to minors under POCSO. Adult male victims are not covered under Section 63 BNS for rape (though other assault laws may apply).

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Filing the FIR:** The victim or relative approaches the nearest police station (Zero FIR applicable). The statement is recorded by a female officer under Section 173 BNSS.
2. **Medical Examination:** Under Section 185 BNSS, the victim is taken for a medical exam within 24 hours. Consent of the victim is required.
3. **Statement before Magistrate:** Under Section 184 BNSS, the victim's statement is confidentially recorded before a Judicial Magistrate.
4. **Investigation & Arrest:** Police collect forensic evidence, arrest the accused, and file a chargesheet (report) within 60-90 days.
5. **Fast Track Trial:** The trial is ideally conducted in a fast-track court or in-camera (privately). The victim is protected from aggressive cross-examination about her past sexual history (Section 53 BSA).
6. **Verdict & Sentencing:** Based on evidence, the judge pronounces the verdict.

1. **FIR दर्ज करना:** पीड़िता या रिश्तेदार निकटतम पुलिस स्टेशन जाते हैं (जीरो एफआईआर लागू)। धारा 173 BNSS के तहत महिला अधिकारी द्वारा बयान दर्ज किया जाता है।
2. **चिकित्सा जांच:** धारा 185 BNSS के तहत, पीड़िता को 24 घंटे के भीतर मेडिकल जांच के लिए ले जाया जाता है। पीड़िता की सहमति आवश्यक है।
3. **मजिस्ट्रेट के समक्ष बयान:** धारा 184 BNSS के तहत, न्यायिक मजिस्ट्रेट के समक्ष पीड़िता का बयान गोपनीय रूप से दर्ज किया जाता है।

## Required Documents
1. Any identity proof of the victim (Aadhar, Voter ID) for official records (kept strictly confidential).
2. FIR copy (provided free of cost to the informant).
3. Medico-Legal Certificate (MLC) prepared by the doctor.
4. Clothes worn at the time of the incident (vital for forensic evidence).
5. Any digital evidence (chats, emails, call logs) if the perpetrator was known.

## Government Authorities
*   **Local Police / Mahila Thana (Women Police Stations):** First point of contact for filing FIR.
*   **One Stop Centres (Sakhi Centres):** Provides integrated support including medical, legal, and psychological help to women facing violence.
*   **Judicial Magistrate (First Class):** For recording statements under Section 184 BNSS.
*   **Fast Track Courts:** Special courts designated for speedy trial of sexual offences.
*   **District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) / NALSA:** Provides free legal counsel.

## Penalties & Punishments
(Note: Punishments are strictly as per the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023)
*   **Section 64 BNS (Rape):** Minimum 10 years rigorous imprisonment, up to life imprisonment, and fine.
*   **Section 65 BNS (Rape of a woman under 16 years):** Minimum 20 years rigorous imprisonment, up to life imprisonment, and fine.
*   **Section 66 BNS (Rape of a woman under 12 years):** Rigorous imprisonment for the remainder of natural life, or death penalty, and fine.
*   **Section 68 BNS (Gang Rape):** Minimum 20 years to life imprisonment for remainder of natural life for all involved.
*   **Section 70 BNS (Custodial Rape by police, public servant, hospital staff):** Minimum 10 years up to life imprisonment.
*   **Section 72 BNS (Disclosing Victim's Identity):** Imprisonment up to 2 years and fine.

## Exceptions
*   **Medical Procedures:** Lawful medical procedures conducted with consent do not constitute sexual offences.
*   **Marital Exception:** Under BNS, sexual acts by a man with his own wife (aged 18 or above) is currently exempt from the definition of rape, though courts may treat non-consensual acts as cruelty or domestic violence.

## Important Timelines
*   **Reporting:** Immediate reporting is crucial to preserve forensic evidence (like semen, DNA, injuries). However, there is no absolute "Limitation Period" for reporting rape; delay can be explained in court.
*   **Medical Examination:** Must be conducted within 24 hours of receiving the information of the offence (Section 185 BNSS).
*   **Investigation:** Must ideally be completed within 2 months (60 days) from the recording of information.
*   **Trial:** Must ideally be completed within 2 months from the date of filing the chargesheet.

## Common Legal Mistakes
*   **Washing Clothes or Bathing:** Destroying crucial biological and forensic evidence before the medical examination.
*   **Delay in FIR without explanation:** While delay doesn't invalidate the case, an unexplained long delay gives the defense lawyer room to create doubt.
*   **Hiding facts from the lawyer:** Not providing complete and truthful information to your legal counsel.
*   **Compromise (Out of Court Settlement):** Rape is a non-compoundable offence; it cannot be legally "settled" out of court. Attempting to do so is illegal.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Can an FIR for rape be filed online?**
**English:** Under BNSS, an e-FIR can be initiated for cognizable offences, but for serious crimes like rape, the victim's physical statement and medical examination are mandatory. It's best to call 112 or visit a station.
**Hindi (Unicode):** BNSS के तहत, संज्ञेय अपराधों के लिए ई-एफआईआर शुरू की जा सकती है, लेकिन बलात्कार जैसे गंभीर अपराधों के लिए, पीड़िता का भौतिक बयान और चिकित्सा परीक्षण अनिवार्य है। 112 पर कॉल करना या स्टेशन जाना सबसे अच्छा है।
**Hinglish:** BNSS mein e-FIR ka option hai, par rape jaise serious cases mein victim ka physical statement aur medical test zaroori hota hai. Isliye police station jana ya 112 par call karna best hai.

**Q2: What is a Zero FIR?**
**English:** A Zero FIR means you can file your complaint at any police station across India, regardless of where the crime took place. The police will register it and transfer it to the correct jurisdiction.
**Hindi (Unicode):** जीरो एफआईआर का मतलब है कि आप अपनी शिकायत पूरे भारत में किसी भी पुलिस स्टेशन में दर्ज करा सकते हैं, चाहे अपराध कहीं भी हुआ हो। पुलिस इसे दर्ज करेगी और सही अधिकार क्षेत्र में स्थानांतरित कर देगी।
**Hinglish:** Zero FIR ka matlab hai aap India ke kisi bhi police station mein complain likhwa sakti hain. Police FIR darj karke use sahi station mein transfer kar degi.

**Q3: Is the Two-Finger Test still allowed?**
**English:** No. The Supreme Court of India and the BNS/BNSS framework strictly prohibit the Two-Finger Test. Any doctor conducting it can be heavily penalized.
**Hindi (Unicode):** नहीं। भारत के सर्वोच्च न्यायालय और BNS/BNSS ढांचे ने टू-फिंगर टेस्ट पर सख्ती से प्रतिबंध लगा दिया है। इसे करने वाले किसी भी डॉक्टर को भारी जुर्माना और सजा हो सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Bilkul nahi. Supreme Court aur naye kanoonon ne Two-Finger Test par puri tarah ban laga diya hai. Aisa karne wale doctor ko saza ho sakti hai.

**Q4: Does the victim need a lawyer to file an FIR?**
**English:** No. You do not need a lawyer to file an FIR. The police are legally bound to register it. If you need legal help later, the District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) provides a free lawyer.
**Hindi (Unicode):** नहीं। एफआईआर दर्ज करने के लिए आपको वकील की आवश्यकता नहीं है। पुलिस इसे दर्ज करने के लिए कानूनी रूप से बाध्य है। यदि आपको बाद में कानूनी मदद चाहिए, तो DLSA मुफ्त वकील प्रदान करता है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. FIR file karne ke liye lawyer nahi chahiye. Agar baad mein court case ke liye lawyer chahiye toh DLSA aapko free lawyer dega.

**Q5: Can the police refuse to file a rape FIR?**
**English:** No. Refusal to file an FIR in a cognizable offence like rape is a crime under Section 199 of BNS (formerly Section 166A IPC). The police officer can be jailed for this.
**Hindi (Unicode):** नहीं। बलात्कार जैसे संज्ञेय अपराध में एफआईआर दर्ज करने से इनकार करना BNS की धारा 199 के तहत अपराध है। इसके लिए पुलिस अधिकारी को जेल हो सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. Rape case mein FIR likhne se mana karna ek crime hai (BNS Section 199). Aisa karne wale police officer ko jail ho sakti hai.

**Q6: What if the rape happened months ago? Can I still file an FIR?**
**English:** Yes. There is no strict limitation period for reporting rape. However, you will need to explain the reason for the delay to the court (e.g., trauma, fear, threats).
**Hindi (Unicode):** हाँ। बलात्कार की रिपोर्ट करने के लिए कोई सख्त सीमा अवधि नहीं है। हालाँकि, आपको अदालत को देरी का कारण (जैसे, आघात, डर, धमकियाँ) बताना होगा।
**Hinglish:** Haan. Rape report karne ka koi strict time limit nahi hai. Lekin court mein delay ka reason (jaise darr ya trauma) batana padega.

**Q7: Can a false promise of marriage be considered rape?**
**English:** Under Section 69 of BNS, having sexual intercourse by employing deceitful means, including a false promise of employment, promotion, or marriage, is now a specific criminal offence.
**Hindi (Unicode):** BNS की धारा 69 के तहत, रोजगार, पदोन्नति या विवाह के झूठे वादे सहित छलपूर्ण साधनों का उपयोग करके यौन संबंध बनाना अब एक विशिष्ट आपराधिक अपराध है।
**Hinglish:** BNS Section 69 ke mutabiq, jhootha shadi ka wada karke ya dhoka dekar relation banana ek alag aur serious crime bana diya gaya hai.

**Q8: Is marital rape a crime in India?**
**English:** Under the current BNS framework, non-consensual sexual acts by a man with his adult wife (18 years or above) are exempt from the definition of rape (Section 63 Exception 2). It can, however, be prosecuted under Domestic Violence laws or Cruelty.
**Hindi (Unicode):** वर्तमान BNS ढांचे के तहत, एक पुरुष द्वारा अपनी वयस्क पत्नी (18 वर्ष या उससे अधिक) के साथ गैर-सहमति वाले यौन कृत्य को बलात्कार की परिभाषा (धारा 63 अपवाद 2) से छूट दी गई है।
**Hinglish:** Current BNS kanoon mein, apni 18 saal se upar ki patni ke sath relation banana rape ki category mein nahi aata. Lekin iske khilaf Domestic Violence ka case kiya ja sakta hai.

**Q9: What happens if the accused is a minor?**
**English:** If the accused is under 18, they will be tried under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, not BNS. Depending on the age and severity, they may be tried as adults or sent to a reform home.
**Hindi (Unicode):** यदि आरोपी 18 वर्ष से कम है, तो उस पर किशोर न्याय अधिनियम के तहत मुकदमा चलाया जाएगा, न कि BNS के तहत।
**Hinglish:** Agar accused 18 saal se kam hai, toh us par Juvenile Justice Act ke tahat case chalega, aur use sudhar grah (reform home) bheja ja sakta hai.

**Q10: What if the victim is a minor?**
**English:** If the victim is under 18, the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act applies. The punishment is far more severe, and BNS Sections 65 and 66 mandate massive minimum sentences, up to the death penalty.
**Hindi (Unicode):** यदि पीड़िता 18 वर्ष से कम है, तो POCSO अधिनियम लागू होता है। सजा कहीं अधिक कठोर है, और BNS धारा 65 और 66 मृत्युदंड तक की सजा अनिवार्य करती है।
**Hinglish:** Agar victim minor (18 se kam) hai, toh POCSO Act lagta hai. Isme BNS ke Section 65/66 ke tahat saza bahut strict hai, jisme fansi ki saza bhi shamil hai.

*(Additional 10 FAQs to ensure comprehensiveness)*
**Q11: Will my identity be kept secret?**
**Answer:** Yes, BNS Section 72 strictly prohibits police, media, or any person from disclosing your identity.

**Q12: Can I take back the case after a compromise?**
**Answer:** No. Rape is a non-compoundable crime. Once an FIR is filed, a private compromise has no legal standing and trying to force one is illegal.

**Q13: Who conducts the medical exam?**
**Answer:** A registered medical practitioner, preferably female, conducts the exam at a government or private hospital.

**Q14: Do private hospitals treat rape victims for free?**
**Answer:** Yes, under the law, all hospitals (public or private) are bound to provide free first-aid and medical treatment to victims of sexual assault.

**Q15: What is 'In-camera' trial?**
**Answer:** It means the court proceedings happen in private. Only the judge, the lawyers, the victim, and the accused are present. General public and media are not allowed.

**Q16: Can a boy/man be a victim of rape under BNS?**
**Answer:** Section 63 of BNS is gender-specific, recognizing women as victims. However, male minors are protected under the gender-neutral POCSO Act.

**Q17: Is wearing revealing clothes considered 'consent'?**
**Answer:** Absolutely not. Section 53 of BSA clarifies that a woman's character, past history, or clothing has zero relevance to the issue of consent.

**Q18: What if the police station is outside my city?**
**Answer:** File a Zero FIR at any local police station in your current city.

**Q19: How long does the court trial take?**
**Answer:** BNSS mandates that investigations conclude in 2 months and fast-track courts try to complete the trial in 2 months, though systemic delays can happen.

**Q20: Can I get protection if the accused threatens me?**
**Answer:** Yes. You can request the Magistrate or Police for protection under the Witness Protection Scheme.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** If she didn't fight back or scream, it wasn't rape.
   **Fact:** BNS clearly states that lack of physical resistance does not equal consent. Fear or shock often paralyzes victims.
2. **Myth:** You cannot file an FIR after 24 hours.
   **Fact:** There is no limitation period. You can file an FIR even after months, provided you explain the delay.
3. **Myth:** Prostitutes or sex workers cannot be raped.
   **Fact:** The law protects the bodily autonomy of ALL women. Consent is mandatory for every sexual act, regardless of profession.
4. **Myth:** Marital rape is punishable under BNS Section 63.
   **Fact:** Currently, Exception 2 to Section 63 exempts a husband from rape charges if the wife is above 18, though cruelty laws apply.
5. **Myth:** You must hire a highly-paid private lawyer to win.
   **Fact:** The State prosecutes the accused. A Public Prosecutor is provided, and victims can get free legal aid from DLSA.
6. **Myth:** A medical test is not needed if there are no visible injuries.
   **Fact:** Medical tests collect microscopic forensic evidence (DNA) and are absolutely vital even if there are no bruises.
7. **Myth:** The "Two-finger test" proves if a rape occurred.
   **Fact:** It is unscientific, degrading, and entirely banned by the Supreme Court.
8. **Myth:** False promise of marriage is the exact same as rape.
   **Fact:** BNS separates this into Section 69, punishing "deceitful means," distinct from the core rape Section 63.
9. **Myth:** Private hospitals can refuse to examine rape victims.
   **Fact:** Refusal is a crime. All hospitals must provide immediate free medical assistance.
10. **Myth:** Media can publish the victim's name if they support her.
    **Fact:** No one, not even supporters, can disclose the victim's identity. It is a punishable crime under BNS Section 72.
