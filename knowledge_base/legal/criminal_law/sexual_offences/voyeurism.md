# Voyeurism (BNS Section 77) - Laws, Rights, and Legal Remedies

## Overview
**English:** Voyeurism is a serious criminal offense in India under the new Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023. It involves secretly watching, capturing, or sharing images of a woman engaging in a private act where she has a reasonable expectation of privacy. This law protects the fundamental right to privacy and modesty of women from predatory behaviors like hidden cameras, peeping, and unauthorized recordings.
**Hindi Unicode:** भारत में नई भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS), 2023 के तहत तांक-झांक (वोयेरिज्म) एक गंभीर आपराधिक अपराध है। इसमें किसी महिला को किसी निजी कार्य में संलग्न होने पर गुप्त रूप से देखना, उसकी तस्वीरें/वीडियो लेना या साझा करना शामिल है, जहां उसे गोपनीयता की उचित उम्मीद होती है। यह कानून महिलाओं की निजता और शालीनता के मौलिक अधिकार की रक्षा करता है।
**Hinglish:** India mein naye BNS, 2023 ke mutabiq voyeurism ek sangeen jurm hai. Isme kisi aurat ko private act karte hue chupke se dekhna, uski photo ya video lena ya share karna shamil hai. Yeh kanoon hidden cameras, peeping, aur bina permission recording jaise harkaton se auraton ki privacy aur modesty ko bachata hai.

## Definition
**English:** As per Section 77 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), voyeurism occurs when a man watches, or captures the image of a woman engaging in a private act, in circumstances where she would usually have the expectation of not being observed. It also includes the dissemination (sharing) of such images.
**Hindi Unicode:** भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS) की धारा 77 के अनुसार, वोयेरिज्म तब होता है जब कोई व्यक्ति किसी महिला को किसी निजी कार्य में संलग्न देखता है या उसकी तस्वीर लेता है, ऐसी परिस्थितियों में जहां उसे आम तौर पर यह उम्मीद होती है कि उसे नहीं देखा जा रहा है। इसमें ऐसी छवियों का प्रसार (शेयर करना) भी शामिल है।

Un photos/videos ko share karna bhi isme shamil hai.

## Legal Background
**History:** Previously, this offense was covered under Section 354C of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), which was introduced after the 2013 Criminal Law (Amendment) Act following the Nirbhaya case. With the overhaul of criminal laws in India, IPC 354C has been replaced by BNS Section 77.
**Purpose:** To explicitly criminalize the act of invading a woman's spatial and bodily privacy through visual means, either directly or via technology.
**Applicable Acts:**
- **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023**
- **Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023**
- **Information Technology Act, 2000**
**Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 21 (Right to Life and Personal Liberty, which includes the Right to Privacy, upheld in the *K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India* judgment).
**Relevant Sections:**
- **Section 77 BNS:** Deals directly with the offense of Voyeurism.
- **Section 66E IT Act:** Punishment for violation of privacy (capturing/publishing private area images of any person without consent).

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** The BNS section specifically addresses a **man** committing the act against a **woman**. It is a gender-specific offense under BNS 77. (However, Section 66E of the IT Act is gender-neutral and applies to "any person").
- **Who is exempt:** There are no exemptions for anyone, including landlords, employers, or spouses, if the act is done without consent in circumstances where there is an expectation of privacy.
- **Special Conditions:** If the woman consented to the capture of the image but NOT to its dissemination, and the man disseminates it anyway, it still constitutes voyeurism under BNS Section 77.

## Rights of Citizens
**English:** A victim has the right to file a Zero FIR at any police station regardless of jurisdiction. The victim's identity must be kept confidential during the investigation and trial.
**Hindi Unicode:** पीड़िता को अधिकार है कि वह अधिकार क्षेत्र की परवाह किए बिना किसी भी पुलिस स्टेशन में जीरो एफआईआर (Zero FIR) दर्ज करा सकती है। जांच और मुकदमे के दौरान पीड़िता की पहचान गुप्त रखी जानी चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Victim ko yeh right hai ki wo kisi bhi police station me Zero FIR darj kara sakti hai, chahe jurisdiction koi bhi ho. Investigation aur trial ke dauran victim ki identity chupai jani chahiye.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English:** Citizens have a duty to not circulate or forward such private images or videos. Doing so makes them complicit and criminally liable. Property owners must ensure their premises (like hotel rooms and trial rooms) are free from hidden cameras.
**Hindi Unicode:** नागरिकों का कर्तव्य है कि वे ऐसे निजी चित्रों या वीडियो को प्रसारित या फॉरवर्ड न करें। ऐसा करने से वे भी आपराधिक रूप से उत्तरदायी बन जाते हैं। संपत्ति के मालिकों को यह सुनिश्चित करना चाहिए कि उनके परिसर (जैसे होटल के कमरे और ट्रायल रूम) छिपे हुए कैमरों से मुक्त हों।
**Hinglish:** Citizens ki duty hai ki wo aisi private photos ya videos ko forward ya share na karein. Aisa karne par unpar bhi case ho sakta hai. Hotel ya shop owners ko make sure karna chahiye ki unke trial rooms me hidden cameras na hon.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Preserve Evidence:** Do not touch the hidden camera if found. Take pictures of its placement. Save URLs or messages if the content is online.
2. **Call the Police (112):** Immediately alert local authorities to confiscate the device before evidence is destroyed.
3. **File an FIR/Zero FIR:** Visit the nearest police station to file an FIR under Section 77 BNS and Sec 66E IT Act. 
4. **Cyber Crime Portal:** If images are online, report on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).
5. **Statement:** The victim's statement will be recorded by a female police officer under BNSS procedures.
6. **Magistrate Statement:** Statement under Section 183 BNSS (equivalent to old 164 CrPC) will be recorded before a Magistrate.

**Hindi Unicode:**
1. **सबूत सुरक्षित रखें:** हिडन कैमरा मिलने पर उसे छुएं नहीं। उसकी जगह की तस्वीरें लें। यदि सामग्री ऑनलाइन है तो URL सेव करें।
2. **पुलिस को बुलाएं (112):** सबूत नष्ट होने से पहले डिवाइस को जब्त करने के लिए तुरंत पुलिस को सतर्क करें।
3. **FIR दर्ज करें:** धारा 77 BNS और 66E IT Act के तहत FIR दर्ज कराने के लिए निकटतम पुलिस स्टेशन जाएं।
4. **साइबर क्राइम पोर्टल:** यदि तस्वीरें ऑनलाइन हैं, तो राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध रिपोर्टिंग पोर्टल (cybercrime.gov.in) पर रिपोर्ट करें।
5. **बयान:** BNSS प्रक्रियाओं के तहत महिला पुलिस अधिकारी द्वारा पीड़िता का बयान दर्ज किया जाएगा।
6. **मजिस्ट्रेट के सामने बयान:** मजिस्ट्रेट के समक्ष BNSS की धारा 183 के तहत बयान दर्ज किया जाएगा।

## Required Documents
- Written complaint detailing the incident.
- Evidence of the act (photographs of the hidden camera, screenshots of leaked videos, links, call logs if threatened).
- Identity proof of the complainant (Aadhar card, Voter ID - though not strictly necessary for an emergency FIR, helpful for formal documentation).

## Government Authorities
- **Local Police Station:** The primary responder for criminal acts.
- **Cyber Crime Cell:** For online circulation of voyeuristic materials.
- **Magistrate Courts:** For trial and recording of Section 183 BNSS statements.
- **National/State Commission for Women (NCW/SCW):** Can be approached if police refuse to register the FIR.

## Penalties & Punishments
Under **Section 77 BNS (Voyeurism)**:
- **First Conviction:** Imprisonment of not less than 1 year, extending up to 3 years, AND fine. (This is a Bailable and Cognizable offense).
- **Second or Subsequent Conviction:** Imprisonment of not less than 3 years, extending up to 7 years, AND fine. (This is a Non-Bailable and Cognizable offense).

Under **Section 66E IT Act**:
- Imprisonment up to 3 years OR a fine up to Rs. 2 Lakhs, or BOTH.

## Exceptions
There is a specific explanation in the law regarding consent to capture but not disseminate: If a woman consents to the capturing of the image (e.g., intimate photos between partners) but strictly DOES NOT consent to its dissemination to third persons, and the man disseminates it anyway, it is still treated as voyeurism.

## Important Timelines
- **Filing FIR:** Should be filed immediately to prevent destruction of electronic evidence (cameras, memory cards, logs).
- **Investigation & Charge-sheet:** Under BNSS, police should ideally file the charge-sheet within 60 days to expedite trials in crimes against women.
- **Cyber Content Takedown:** Once reported to Nodal Officers/Cyber portal, platforms must act swiftly (often within 24-36 hours) to remove non-consensual intimate imagery (NCII) under IT Intermediary Guidelines.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Confronting the perpetrator first:** Confronting them might lead them to delete the footage and destroy the memory card, destroying crucial evidence. Call police first.
- **Deleting the received video:** If someone sends you the leaked video of yourself, do not just delete it in panic. Keep a backup securely as evidence.
- **Not filing an FIR out of shame:** The law protects your identity; proceedings can happen in camera. Not reporting empowers the criminal.

## Safety Tips

If they touch, it might be a two-way mirror) and hidden cameras (look for tiny blinking lights in the dark or use smartphone camera apps to detect infrared light).
- Never share passwords of cloud storage to prevent hacking and unauthorized access to private photos.

**1. What is the main difference between BNS Section 77 and the old IPC Section 354C?**

## Common Myths vs Facts
**Myth 1:** Voyeurism is only about secretly looking through a keyhole.
**Fact:** Voyeurism includes digital spying, hidden cameras, secretly recording with phones, and distributing those recorded images under BNS 77.

**Myth 2:** Sharing a leaked private video on a WhatsApp group is not a crime if I didn't record it.
**Fact:** Under BNS 77 and IT Act 66E, simply forwarding or disseminating images where a woman's privacy is violated is equally a crime.

**Myth 3:** Voyeurism laws protect both men and women equally under BNS.
**Fact:** Under BNS Sec 77, the law protects only women against men. However, the IT Act Sec 66E is gender-neutral.

**Myth 4:** If a woman gave consent to be recorded, she cannot claim voyeurism if the video is leaked.
**Fact:** Consent to record does NOT equal consent to distribute. If the video is distributed without her consent, it constitutes voyeurism.

**Myth 5:** Police will seize my phone permanently if I report a crime.
**Fact:** Police may seize your phone initially to extract evidence (like the threatening message or link), but they create a forensic clone and return the device, or you can supply the evidence on a pen drive.

**Myth 6:** I cannot complain if the voyeur is my family member or relative.
**Fact:** The law applies to everyone irrespective of their relationship with the victim. Privacy rights remain intact at home.

**Myth 7:** Online complaints are not taken seriously.
**Fact:** The National Cyber Crime Portal complaints are officially tracked, and an FIR must be registered if a cognizable offense (like BNS 77) is made out.

**Myth 8:** Since it's a bailable offense, the police won't arrest the accused.
**Fact:** Bailable means the accused has a right to bail from the police station upon providing bonds, but formal arrest and booking procedures still occur.

**Myth 9:** I need to prove that the accused was making money off the videos to file a case.
**Fact:** Financial gain is not required. The mere act of capturing or watching without consent in private circumstances is enough to prove voyeurism.

**Myth 10:** Trial room cameras are legal for security.
**Fact:** Cameras inside trial rooms, washrooms, or fitting rooms are strictly illegal and a blatant violation of privacy laws in India.
