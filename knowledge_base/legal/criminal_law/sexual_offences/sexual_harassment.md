# Sexual Harassment Law and Procedures in India (BNS & POSH Act)

## Overview
**English:** Sexual harassment is a grave violation of an individual's fundamental rights to equality, life, and liberty. In India, it is addressed through dual mechanisms: the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) for general criminal offenses in public or private spaces, and the specific POSH Act for workplace-related incidents. The law strictly penalizes unwelcome sexual behaviors, physical contact, demands for sexual favors, and sexually colored remarks.
**Hindi Unicode:** यौन उत्पीड़न (Sexual Harassment) किसी भी व्यक्ति के समानता, जीवन और स्वतंत्रता के मौलिक अधिकारों का गंभीर उल्लंघन है। भारत में, इसे दोहरे तंत्र के माध्यम से संबोधित किया जाता है: सार्वजनिक या निजी स्थानों में सामान्य आपराधिक अपराधों के लिए भारतीय न्याय संहिता (BNS), और कार्यस्थल से संबंधित घटनाओं के लिए विशिष्ट पॉश (POSH) अधिनियम। कानून अवांछित यौन व्यवहार, शारीरिक संपर्क, यौन अनुग्रह की मांग और यौन रंगीन टिप्पणियों को सख्ती से दंडित करता है।
**Hinglish:** Sexual harassment kisi bhi vyakti ke equality aur azaadi ke fundamental rights ka bada violation hai. India mein iske liye BNS (Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita) hai jo public/private jagah par harassment ko cover karta hai, aur POSH Act hai jo workplace harassment ko cover karta hai. Kanoon kisi bhi unwanted sexual behaviour, touch, ya comments ko sakhti se punish karta hai.

## Quick Summary
**English:** Sexual harassment encompasses both physical and non-physical unwanted sexual advances. You can file an FIR under Section 74 of the BNS for general harassment, or file a complaint with your company's Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) under the POSH Act for workplace harassment.
**Hindi Unicode:** यौन उत्पीड़न में शारीरिक और गैर-शारीरिक दोनों तरह की अवांछित यौन हरकतें शामिल हैं। आप सामान्य उत्पीड़न के लिए बीएनएस (BNS) की धारा 74 के तहत एफआईआर (FIR) दर्ज कर सकते हैं, या कार्यस्थल पर उत्पीड़न के लिए पॉश (POSH) अधिनियम के तहत अपनी कंपनी की आंतरिक शिकायत समिति (ICC) में शिकायत दर्ज कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Sexual harassment mein physical aur non-physical unwanted harkatein shamil hoti hain. Aap BNS Section 74 ke andar police station mein FIR darj karwa sakte hain, ya workplace harassment ke case mein POSH Act ke under company ki Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) ko complaint de sakte hain.

## Definition
**English:** Sexual harassment includes any one or more of the following unwelcome acts or behavior (whether directly or by implication): physical contact and advances; a demand or request for sexual favors; making sexually colored remarks; showing pornography; or any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of sexual nature.
**Hindi Unicode:** यौन उत्पीड़न में निम्नलिखित में से कोई एक या अधिक अवांछित कार्य या व्यवहार (चाहे प्रत्यक्ष रूप से या निहितार्थ से) शामिल हैं: शारीरिक संपर्क और अग्रिम; यौन अनुग्रह की मांग या अनुरोध; यौन रंगीन टिप्पणियां करना; अश्लील साहित्य दिखाना; या यौन प्रकृति का कोई अन्य अवांछित शारीरिक, मौखिक या गैर-मौखिक आचरण।
**Hinglish:** Sexual harassment ka matlab hai koi bhi unwanted behavior jaise physical touch karna, sexual favors maangna, sexually colored comments paas karna, pornography dikhana, ya koi bhi aur verbal ya non-verbal harkat jo sexual nature ki ho aur samne wale ko uncomfortable kare.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Addressing sexual harassment is critical for maintaining safe environments, promoting gender equality, and protecting the mental and physical well-being of individuals. Strict enforcement acts as a deterrent and empowers survivors to seek justice without fear of retaliation or stigma.
**Hindi Unicode:** सुरक्षित वातावरण बनाए रखने, लैंगिक समानता को बढ़ावा देने और व्यक्तियों की मानसिक और शारीरिक भलाई की रक्षा के लिए यौन उत्पीड़न को संबोधित करना महत्वपूर्ण है। सख्त प्रवर्तन एक निवारक के रूप में कार्य करता है और बचे लोगों को प्रतिशोध या कलंक के डर के बिना न्याय मांगने का अधिकार देता है।
**Hinglish:** Safe environment banaye rakhne, gender equality promote karne aur logon ki mental aur physical health ko protect karne ke liye sexual harassment ke khilaf awaz uthana zaroori hai. Isse society mein dar khatam hota hai aur victims bina kisi darr ke justice maang sakte hain.

## Legal Background
The legal framework against sexual harassment in India has evolved significantly, particularly post the landmark Vishaka Guidelines, leading to comprehensive laws.
- **Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 14 (Right to Equality), Article 15 (Prohibition of discrimination), and Article 21 (Right to Life and Personal Liberty).
- **Applicable Acts:** Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023, Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act (POSH Act) 2013, Information Technology Act 2000.
- **Relevant Sections (BNS 2023):**
  - Section 74: Sexual Harassment (Physical contact, asking for sexual favors, sexually colored remarks).
  - Section 75: Sexual harassment of a woman by a relative.
  - Section 76: Assault or criminal force to woman with intent to disrobe.
  - Section 77: Voyeurism.
  - Section 78: Stalking.
  - Section 79: Word, gesture or act intended to insult the modesty of a woman.
- **Relevant Sections (IT Act 2000):**
  - Section 67 & 67A: Publishing or transmitting obscene material in electronic form.

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** Women across all spaces (public, private, workplace, digital). The POSH Act specifically covers all women employees (regular, temporary, ad hoc, daily wage) and even women visiting a workplace. Under BNS, victims of sexual harassment are primarily recognized as women, but legal precedents are evolving.
- **Who is exempt:** No individual, regardless of rank, position, or relationship to the victim, is exempt from prosecution if found guilty of sexual harassment.
- **Special conditions:** Workplace sexual harassment rules apply not just to the office premises but also to transportation provided by the employer, office parties, and business trips.

## Rights of Citizens
**English:** You have the right to a safe environment, the right to file an FIR (Zero FIR anywhere), the right to confidentiality, the right to free legal aid, and the right to protection against victimization or retaliation at the workplace.
**Hindi Unicode:** आपको एक सुरक्षित वातावरण का अधिकार है, एफआईआर (कहीं भी जीरो एफआईआर) दर्ज करने का अधिकार है, गोपनीयता का अधिकार है, मुफ्त कानूनी सहायता का अधिकार है, और कार्यस्थल पर उत्पीड़न या प्रतिशोध के खिलाफ सुरक्षा का अधिकार है।
**Hinglish:** Aapko safe environment ka right hai, kahin bhi Zero FIR file karne ka right hai, apni identity chupa kar rakhne (confidentiality) ka right hai, free legal help aur workplace par badle (retaliation) se bachne ka right hai.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English:** Citizens have a duty to report incidents of sexual harassment, cooperate with authorities or internal committees during investigations, and not file false or malicious complaints. Employers have a duty to constitute an ICC, organize awareness programs, and ensure a safe working environment.
**Hindi Unicode:** नागरिकों का यह कर्तव्य है कि वे यौन उत्पीड़न की घटनाओं की रिपोर्ट करें, जांच के दौरान अधिकारियों या आंतरिक समितियों के साथ सहयोग करें, और झूठी या दुर्भावनापूर्ण शिकायतें दर्ज न करें। नियोक्ताओं का यह कर्तव्य है कि वे ICC का गठन करें, जागरूकता कार्यक्रम आयोजित करें और सुरक्षित कार्य वातावरण सुनिश्चित करें।
**Hinglish:** Citizens ki duty hai ki wo harassment ki complaint karein, investigation mein authorities ya ICC ka sath dein, aur jhuthi complaint na karein. Employers ki duty hai ki wo office mein ICC banayein, safety rules lagayein aur awareness programs karein.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:** 
1. **For Workplace Harassment:** Submit a written complaint to the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) or Local Complaints Committee (LCC) within 3 months of the incident. The ICC will conduct an inquiry within 90 days. During inquiry, you can ask for transfer or leave. Based on the report, the employer must act within 60 days.
2. **For Criminal Offense (Public/Private Space):** Visit the nearest police station to file an FIR under Section 74 of BNS (or relevant sections). If the police refuse, you can file a Zero FIR at any station or escalate to the Superintendent of Police (SP) or a Judicial Magistrate.
3. **For Cyber Harassment:** File a complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) with screenshots and links as evidence.
**Hindi Unicode:** 
1. **कार्यस्थल पर उत्पीड़न के लिए:** घटना के 3 महीने के भीतर आंतरिक शिकायत समिति (ICC) या स्थानीय शिकायत समिति (LCC) को लिखित शिकायत प्रस्तुत करें। ICC 90 दिनों के भीतर जांच करेगी। जांच के दौरान आप ट्रांसफर या छुट्टी मांग सकते हैं। रिपोर्ट के आधार पर, नियोक्ता को 60 दिनों के भीतर कार्रवाई करनी होगी।
2. **आपराधिक अपराध (सार्वजनिक/निजी स्थान) के लिए:** बीएनएस (BNS) की धारा 74 (या प्रासंगिक धाराओं) के तहत एफआईआर दर्ज करने के लिए निकटतम पुलिस स्टेशन जाएं। यदि पुलिस मना करती है, तो आप किसी भी स्टेशन पर जीरो एफआईआर (Zero FIR) दर्ज कर सकते हैं या एसपी (SP) या न्यायिक मजिस्ट्रेट को शिकायत कर सकते हैं।
3. **साइबर उत्पीड़न के लिए:** सबूत के तौर पर स्क्रीनशॉट और लिंक के साथ राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध रिपोर्टिंग पोर्टल (cybercrime.gov.in) पर शिकायत दर्ज करें।
**Hinglish:** 
1. **Workplace Harassment:** Incident ke 3 mahine ke andar ICC ya LCC ko written complaint dein. ICC 90 days mein inquiry puri karegi. Report aane ke baad employer ko 60 days mein action lena hoga.
2. **Criminal Offense:** Nearest police station ja kar BNS Section 74 mein FIR darj karein. Police mana kare toh Zero FIR karein ya SP/Magistrate ko approach karein.
3. **Cyber Harassment:** Cybercrime.gov.in par screenshots aur evidence ke sath online complaint file karein.

## Required Documents
- Written complaint detailing the incident(s), date, time, and location.
- Identity proof of the complainant (Aadhaar, Voter ID, etc.).
- Evidences: Screenshots of chats, emails, call recordings, CCTV footage if available.
- Details of witnesses, if any.
- Medical report (if physical assault is involved).

## Government Authorities
- **Police Department:** For registering FIRs and conducting criminal investigations.
- **Internal Complaints Committee (ICC):** Mandatory committee in workplaces with 10 or more employees.
- **Local Complaints Committee (LCC):** Formed at the district level for workplaces with less than 10 employees or if the complaint is against the employer.
- **National / State Commission for Women:** For escalating complaints and seeking intervention.
- **Judicial Magistrate / Criminal Courts:** For trial and prosecution of offenders.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Under Section 74 (BNS):** Rigorous imprisonment for a term which may extend to 3 years, or with fine, or both. If the harassment is asking for sexual favors or sexually colored remarks, imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine, or both.
- **Under Section 76 (Assault to disrobe BNS):** Imprisonment not less than 3 years but may extend to 7 years, and shall also be liable to fine.
- **Under Section 77 (Voyeurism BNS):** First conviction - imprisonment 1 to 3 years and fine. Second/subsequent conviction - 3 to 7 years and fine.
- **Under POSH Act:** Depending on the organization's service rules, penalties can range from a written apology, warning, withholding of promotion/increments, to termination of employment, and deduction of compensation from the salary of the respondent.

## Exceptions
- A mere accidental touch in a crowded place without any sexual intent is not considered sexual harassment.
- Constructive feedback or professional reprimand at the workplace without any sexual undertone does not constitute sexual harassment.
- Malicious and false complaints, proven to be made with a vindictive motive, can lead to disciplinary action against the complainant under the POSH Act.

## Important Timelines
- **Filing POSH Complaint:** Within 3 months from the date of the incident (can be extended by another 3 months by ICC in writing).
- **Conciliation Process (POSH):** Before starting an inquiry, the ICC can try conciliation if requested by the complainant.
- **ICC Inquiry Completion:** Must be completed within 90 days.
- **ICC Report Submission:** Must be issued to the employer and parties within 10 days of completing the inquiry.
- **Employer Action:** The employer must act on the ICC's recommendations within 60 days.
- **Appeal (POSH):** Any party can appeal against the ICC's decision to a court or tribunal within 90 days.
- **FIR Limitation:** BNS offenses like Section 74 should ideally be reported immediately, but courts can condone delays if a valid reason is provided.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Delaying the complaint:** Waiting too long can lead to loss of electronic evidence or make witnesses forget details.
- **Not maintaining a paper trail:** Failing to keep copies of complaints submitted to the HR or police.
- **Deleting evidence:** Blocking the person and deleting chats in anger before saving screenshots as proof.
- **Discussing the case publicly:** Breaching confidentiality during a POSH inquiry can lead to defamation counter-claims or procedural violations.
- **Agreeing to informal settlements:** Accepting a verbal apology without documenting the incident, leaving one vulnerable to repeated harassment.

## Safety Tips
**English:** Always save evidence (take screenshots before blocking). Trust your instincts; if a situation feels unsafe, leave immediately. Know your workplace ICC members. Keep emergency numbers (112, 1091 - Women Helpline) on speed dial. Do not share sensitive personal information online with unknown colleagues.
**Hindi Unicode:** हमेशा सबूत सुरक्षित रखें (ब्लॉक करने से पहले स्क्रीनशॉट लें)। अपनी प्रवृत्ति पर भरोसा करें; यदि कोई स्थिति असुरक्षित लगती है, तो तुरंत वहां से चले जाएं। अपने कार्यस्थल के ICC सदस्यों को जानें। आपातकालीन नंबरों (112, 1091 - महिला हेल्पलाइन) को स्पीड डायल पर रखें। अज्ञात सहकर्मियों के साथ ऑनलाइन संवेदनशील व्यक्तिगत जानकारी साझा न करें।
**Hinglish:** Hamesha proofs save karein (block karne se pehle screenshots lein). Agar koi situation thik na lage toh wahan se turant nikal jayein. Apni company ke ICC members ko janiye. Emergency numbers (112, 1091) ko speed dial par rakhein. Anjaan logon ke sath online personal details share na karein.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is the first thing I should do if I am sexually harassed at work?**
   - **English:** Inform the person clearly that their behavior is unwelcome. If it continues, immediately document the incidents and file a written complaint with your HR or the ICC.
   - **Hindi:** व्यक्ति को स्पष्ट रूप से बताएं कि उनका व्यवहार अवांछित है। यदि यह जारी रहता है, तो तुरंत घटनाओं का दस्तावेजीकरण करें और अपने एचआर या आईसीसी में लिखित शिकायत दर्ज करें।
   - **Hinglish:** Us insaan ko clearly bol dein ki unka behavior unwanted hai. Agar phir bhi wo na mane, toh HR ya ICC ko written complaint zarur dein.

2. **Can I file a police complaint as well as an ICC complaint?**
   - **English:** Yes, you have the right to pursue both criminal proceedings (FIR) and disciplinary action (ICC) simultaneously. One does not stop the other.
   - **Hindi:** हाँ, आपको आपराधिक कार्यवाही (FIR) और अनुशासनात्मक कार्रवाई (ICC) दोनों एक साथ करने का अधिकार है। एक दूसरे को नहीं रोकता है।
   - **Hinglish:** Haan, aap ek hi time par police mein FIR aur company mein ICC complaint dono file kar sakte hain.

3. **What if my company does not have an ICC?**
   - **English:** If your company has 10 or more employees and no ICC, they are violating the law and can be fined up to Rs. 50,000. You can complain to the Local Complaints Committee (LCC) or the police.
   - **Hindi:** यदि आपकी कंपनी में 10 या अधिक कर्मचारी हैं और कोई ICC नहीं है, तो वे कानून का उल्लंघन कर रहे हैं और उन पर 50,000 रुपये तक का जुर्माना लगाया जा सकता है। आप स्थानीय शिकायत समिति (LCC) या पुलिस से शिकायत कर सकते हैं।
   - **Hinglish:** Agar company mein 10 se zyada log hain aur ICC nahi hai, toh un par 50,000 ka fine lag sakta hai. Aap LCC ya police ke paas ja sakte hain.

4. **Is staring continuously considered sexual harassment?**
   - **English:** Yes, continuous unwelcome staring or leering that makes a person uncomfortable falls under non-verbal sexual harassment and can be actionable under BNS Section 79.
   - **Hindi:** हाँ, लगातार अवांछित रूप से घूरना जो किसी व्यक्ति को असहज करता है, गैर-मौखिक यौन उत्पीड़न के अंतर्गत आता है और BNS धारा 79 के तहत कार्रवाई योग्य हो सकता है।
   - **Hinglish:** Haan, kisi ko lagatar ghoorna jisse wo uncomfortable feel kare, sexual harassment mana jata hai aur ispar BNS Section 79 mein action ho sakta hai.

5. **Can men be victims of sexual harassment?**
   - **English:** Under the POSH Act and BNS, the laws are primarily framed for the protection of women. However, companies can have gender-neutral policies, and men can file complaints with HR based on company policies, though BNS sections specifically mention women as victims in most cases.
   - **Hindi:** POSH अधिनियम और BNS के तहत, कानून मुख्य रूप से महिलाओं की सुरक्षा के लिए बनाए गए हैं। हालांकि, कंपनियों की नीतियां जेंडर-न्यूट्रल हो सकती हैं।
   - **Hinglish:** Kanoon zyadatar women ke liye hai, par companies apni policy gender-neutral rakh sakti hain jahan men bhi HR ko complaint kar sakte hain.

6. **What if the harassment happens during a business trip or online?**
   - **English:** The POSH Act extends the definition of workplace to any place visited by the employee arising out of or during employment, including transportation and virtual spaces (online meetings, chats).
   - **Hindi:** POSH अधिनियम कार्यस्थल की परिभाषा को रोजगार के दौरान कर्मचारी द्वारा देखे गए किसी भी स्थान तक विस्तारित करता है, जिसमें परिवहन और आभासी स्थान (ऑनलाइन बैठकें, चैट) शामिल हैं।
   - **Hinglish:** Workplace ka matlab sirf office building nahi hai. Business trip, office cab, aur online Zoom meetings ya WhatsApp chats bhi POSH Act ke andar cover hote hain.

7. **How do I prove sexual harassment if there are no witnesses?**
   - **English:** Circumstantial evidence, consistency in your statements, immediate reporting, behavioral changes noticed by peers, and digital trails (emails, messages) can act as strong evidence even without eyewitnesses.
   - **Hindi:** परिस्थितिजन्य साक्ष्य, आपके बयानों में निरंतरता, तत्काल रिपोर्टिंग, साथियों द्वारा देखे गए व्यवहार परिवर्तन, और डिजिटल निशान (ईमेल, संदेश) प्रत्यक्षदर्शी के बिना भी मजबूत सबूत के रूप में कार्य कर सकते हैं।
   - **Hinglish:** Agar koi witness nahi hai, toh aapke statements ki consistency, electronic messages, aur doston dwara notice kiye gaye behavior changes bhi strong proof maane jate hain.

8. **Can the ICC force me to compromise or settle the matter?**
   - **English:** No, the ICC cannot force a settlement. Conciliation can only be initiated if you explicitly request it in writing, and no monetary settlement can be made as a basis of conciliation.
   - **Hindi:** नहीं, ICC समझौता करने के लिए मजबूर नहीं कर सकती। सुलह तभी शुरू की जा सकती है जब आप लिखित रूप में इसका स्पष्ट अनुरोध करें।
   - **Hinglish:** Nahi, ICC aapko compromise karne ke liye force nahi kar sakti. Settlement tabhi hoga jab aap khud written mein request karein.

9. **What action can be taken if someone files a fake complaint against me?**
   - **English:** The POSH Act contains provisions to punish false and malicious complaints. If the ICC concludes that the complaint was entirely fabricated with malicious intent, disciplinary action can be taken against the complainant.
   - **Hindi:** POSH अधिनियम में झूठी और दुर्भावनापूर्ण शिकायतों को दंडित करने के प्रावधान हैं। यदि शिकायत दुर्भावनापूर्ण इरादे से मनगढ़ंत पाई जाती है, तो शिकायतकर्ता के खिलाफ कार्रवाई की जा सकती है।
   - **Hinglish:** Agar inquiry mein prove hota hai ki complaint intentionally jhuthi thi aur badla lene ke liye ki gayi thi, toh company complainant ke khilaf strict action le sakti hai.

10. **Will my identity remain confidential during the ICC inquiry?**
    - **English:** Yes, under Section 16 of the POSH Act, the identity of the complainant, respondent, witnesses, and inquiry details must be kept strictly confidential. Breaching this can result in penalties.
    - **Hindi:** हाँ, POSH अधिनियम की धारा 16 के तहत, शिकायतकर्ता, प्रतिवादी, गवाहों और जांच के विवरण की पहचान पूरी तरह से गोपनीय रखी जानी चाहिए।
    - **Hinglish:** Haan, law ke mutabiq aapki identity, witnesses aur case ki details strictly private rakhi jayengi. Rule todne wale par penalty lagti hai.

11. **Can I resign during a pending sexual harassment inquiry?**
    - **English:** Yes, you have the right to resign at any time. However, the ICC is still obligated to complete the inquiry and provide its findings to the employer.
    - **Hindi:** हाँ, आपको किसी भी समय इस्तीफा देने का अधिकार है। हालांकि, ICC अभी भी जांच पूरी करने और नियोक्ता को अपने निष्कर्ष प्रदान करने के लिए बाध्य है।
    - **Hinglish:** Haan aap resign kar sakte hain. Par aapke resign karne ke baad bhi ICC case ki inquiry poori karke employer ko report degi.

12. **Is asking a colleague out on a date sexual harassment?**
    - **English:** Asking someone out once politely is generally not harassment. However, persisting after they have said no, or making them uncomfortable, constitutes sexual harassment.
    - **Hindi:** किसी से विनम्रतापूर्वक एक बार डेट पर चलने के लिए पूछना आमतौर पर उत्पीड़न नहीं है। हालांकि, उनके मना करने के बाद भी जिद करना यौन उत्पीड़न है।
    - **Hinglish:** Ek baar politely date ke liye puchna harassment nahi hai. Par agar unhone 'No' bol diya hai, phir bhi aap bar-bar puch rahe hain, toh wo harassment hai.

13. **What is Quid Pro Quo sexual harassment?**
    - **English:** It occurs when someone in a position of power demands sexual favors in exchange for workplace benefits like a promotion, raise, or keeping a job.
    - **Hindi:** यह तब होता है जब सत्ता में बैठा कोई व्यक्ति पदोन्नति, वेतन वृद्धि या नौकरी बनाए रखने जैसे कार्यस्थल लाभों के बदले यौन अनुग्रह की मांग करता है।
    - **Hinglish:** Jab boss ya senior promotion ya job security ke badle mein sexual favors maangta hai, toh use Quid Pro Quo harassment kehte hain.

14. **What is a Hostile Work Environment?**
    - **English:** A hostile work environment is created when persistent, unwelcome sexual conduct makes a workplace intimidating, offensive, or difficult for an employee to work in.
    - **Hindi:** एक शत्रुतापूर्ण कार्य वातावरण तब बनता है जब लगातार, अवांछित यौन आचरण एक कार्यस्थल को डराने वाला, आक्रामक या कर्मचारी के काम करने के लिए कठिन बना देता है।
    - **Hinglish:** Jab office mein bar-bar gande comments, jokes ya harkaton ki wajah se kisi ka kaam karna mushkil ya uncomfortable ho jaye, use Hostile Work Environment kehte hain.

15. **What if the harasser is the CEO or owner of the company?**
    - **English:** If the complaint is against the employer themselves, you should approach the Local Complaints Committee (LCC) set up by the district administration, or file a police complaint.
    - **Hindi:** यदि शिकायत स्वयं नियोक्ता के खिलाफ है, तो आपको जिला प्रशासन द्वारा स्थापित स्थानीय शिकायत समिति (LCC) से संपर्क करना चाहिए, या पुलिस शिकायत दर्ज करनी चाहिए।
    - **Hinglish:** Agar harasser company ka owner ya CEO hai, toh ICC ki jagah aap district ki LCC (Local Complaints Committee) ya direct police ke paas complaint lekar ja sakte hain.

16. **How long does a POSH inquiry take?**
    - **English:** By law, the Internal Complaints Committee must complete its inquiry within 90 days of receiving the complaint.
    - **Hindi:** कानून के अनुसार, आंतरिक शिकायत समिति को शिकायत प्राप्त होने के 90 दिनों के भीतर अपनी जांच पूरी करनी होती है।
    - **Hinglish:** Kanoon ke mutabiq, complaint milne ke 90 din ke andar ICC ko apni inquiry poori karni hoti hai.

17. **What relief can I seek during the pendency of the inquiry?**
    - **English:** You can request the ICC to transfer you or the respondent, grant you paid leave up to 3 months (in addition to your normal leave), or restrict the respondent from evaluating your performance.
    - **Hindi:** आप ICC से आपको या प्रतिवादी को स्थानांतरित करने, 3 महीने तक की वैतनिक छुट्टी (आपकी सामान्य छुट्टी के अतिरिक्त) देने, या प्रतिवादी को आपके प्रदर्शन का मूल्यांकन करने से रोकने का अनुरोध कर सकते हैं।
    - **Hinglish:** Inquiry ke dauran aap transfer, 3 mahine tak ki extra paid leave, ya respondent ko aapke appraisal se door rakhne ki demand kar sakte hain.

18. **Can anonymous complaints be investigated by ICC?**
    - **English:** The POSH Act requires a written, signed complaint for formal inquiry. Anonymous complaints may not trigger a formal inquiry, but HR can conduct general sensitization based on them.
    - **Hindi:** POSH अधिनियम में औपचारिक जांच के लिए एक लिखित, हस्ताक्षरित शिकायत की आवश्यकता होती है। अनाम शिकायतें औपचारिक जांच को ट्रिगर नहीं कर सकती हैं।
    - **Hinglish:** POSH Act ke andar formal inquiry ke liye complaint par sign hona zaroori hai. Anonymous complaint par formal case nahi banta par HR general action le sakti hai.

19. **What compensation is provided under the POSH Act?**
    - **English:** Compensation can be deducted from the respondent's salary considering mental trauma, loss of career opportunity, medical expenses, and the financial status of the respondent.
    - **Hindi:** मानसिक आघात, करियर के अवसर की हानि, चिकित्सा व्यय और प्रतिवादी की वित्तीय स्थिति को ध्यान में रखते हुए प्रतिवादी के वेतन से मुआवजे की कटौती की जा सकती है।
    - **Hinglish:** Respondent ki salary se compensation kaat kar diya ja sakta hai, jisme victim ka mental trauma, career loss aur medical expenses count hote hain.

20. **Can I record conversations as proof of harassment?**
    - **English:** Yes, audio or video recordings can be submitted as evidence to the ICC or police. They are admissible in corroborating your allegations.
    - **Hindi:** हाँ, ऑडियो या वीडियो रिकॉर्डिंग को सबूत के तौर पर ICC या पुलिस को सौंपा जा सकता है। वे आपके आरोपों की पुष्टि करने में स्वीकार्य हैं।
    - **Hinglish:** Haan, aap harassment prove karne ke liye audio ya video recording ICC ya police ko as a proof de sakte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Sexual harassment only involves physical touching.
   **Fact:** No, it includes non-physical behaviors like sexual jokes, staring, showing porn, and making explicit remarks.
2. **Myth:** If you don't say "no" immediately, it's not harassment.
   **Fact:** Fear, shock, or power dynamics can delay a response. Lack of a verbal "no" does not mean consent.
3. **Myth:** POSH Act is only for permanent female employees.
   **Fact:** It covers all women—permanent, temporary, contractual, interns, and even visitors at the workplace.
4. **Myth:** You cannot file a complaint if the incident happened outside the office building.
   **Fact:** The workplace definition includes company cabs, offsites, client meetings, and work-from-home virtual environments.
5. **Myth:** Men cannot be punished for making inappropriate jokes if "they didn't mean it."
   **Fact:** Intent does not matter. The impact of the behavior on the victim determines if it is harassment.
6. **Myth:** False complaints are very common, making POSH anti-men.
   **Fact:** Data shows false complaints are statistically rare. The Act also has strict penalties for malicious false complaints.
7. **Myth:** The employer will just fire the person complaining to protect the company's reputation.
   **Fact:** Victimization or retaliation is strictly prohibited by law. The employee can approach courts if targeted.
8. **Myth:** Police won't take action on eve-teasing without physical assault.
   **Fact:** BNS Sections 74 and 79 strictly punish passing lewd remarks and outraging a woman's modesty.
9. **Myth:** You must have an eyewitness to prove harassment.
   **Fact:** Circumstantial evidence, electronic trails, and consistent testimonies are highly valid proofs.
10. **Myth:** ICC members will always side with the management.
    **Fact:** The ICC must have at least 50% women members and a mandatory external member (NGO/legal expert) to ensure neutrality.

## Conversation Examples

**Scenario 1: Understanding Harassment at Workplace**
*Citizen:* "Mera manager bar-bar mujhe raat ko bina kaam ke WhatsApp par inappropriate messages bhejta hai. Kya yeh POSH mein aayega?"
*Assistant:* "Haan, bilkul. Work hours ke baad bhi supervisor ya colleague ka inappropriate, sexually colored ya unwanted message bhejna POSH Act aur BNS Section 74 ke andar sexual harassment maana jata hai. Aap in messages ke screenshots save karein aur apni company ki Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) ko written complaint dein."

**Scenario 2: Filing complaint when no ICC exists**
*Citizen:* "My office has 15 people but we don't have an ICC. My boss is harassing me. Where do I go?"
*Assistant:* "Since your employer is violating the POSH Act by not forming an ICC, and the harasser is the boss, you should immediately file a complaint with the Local Complaints Committee (LCC) situated at your District Magistrate's office. You can also file an FIR with the police under Section 74 of BNS."

**Scenario 3: Dealing with public harassment (Eve-teasing)**
*Citizen:* "Main roz bus stop par khadi hoti hoon, ek ladka roz mujhe ghoorta hai aur gande gaane gaata hai. Mujhe darr lagta hai. Kya karoon?"
*Assistant:* "Yeh BNS Section 79 (insulting modesty) aur Section 74 ka clear case hai. Aap turant Women Helpline (1091) ya Police (112) ko call karein. Aap nearest police station mein Zero FIR bhi darj karwa sakti hain. Agar possible ho, toh kisi ki madad se uski photo ya video dur se le lein as evidence."

**Scenario 4: Conciliation under POSH**
*Citizen:* "ICC ne mujhse poocha hai ki kya main compromise (conciliation) chahti hoon. Kya mujhe karna chahiye?"
*Assistant:* "Conciliation totally aapki marzi par depend karta hai. ICC aapko force nahi kar sakti. Agar aapko lagta hai ki ek written apology aur warning kaafi hai, tabhi haan kahein. Yaad rakhein, conciliation mein koi paiso ka settlement (monetary compensation) nahi ho sakta. Agar aap chahti hain ki strict action ho, toh aap inquiry aage badhane ko keh sakti hain."

**Scenario 5: Protection against retaliation**
*Citizen:* "Agar main ICC mein complaint karungi, toh mera boss mera appraisal kharab kar dega ya nikal dega. Mujhe protection kaise milegi?"
*Assistant:* "POSH Act ke under 'Protection against Retaliation' ka rule hai. Aap ICC ko request kar sakti hain ki inquiry ke dauran aapki reporting change kar di jaye ya boss ko aapke appraisal se dur rakha jaye. Agar company badle ki bhavna se aapko nikalti hai, toh aap labour court ya civil court mein employer par case kar sakti hain."

**Scenario 6: Definition of Workplace**
*Citizen:* "A client behaved inappropriately with me at a coffee shop during a business meeting. Can I complain to my company's ICC?"
*Assistant:* "Yes, you absolutely can. The POSH Act covers 'extended workplaces' which include any place you visit arising out of or during employment. Your company's ICC is bound to take your complaint, and they can coordinate with the client's company or the police to take action against the individual."

**Scenario 7: Evidence in Cyber Harassment**
*Citizen:* "Someone is sending me obscene pictures on Instagram from a fake ID. What should I do?"
*Assistant:* "Do not delete the chat or block them immediately. First, take screenshots showing the username, time, and the explicit content. Copy the profile URL. Then, report the ID on the platform and file a cyber crime complaint at cybercrime.gov.in. This falls under Section 67/67A of the IT Act and Section 74 of BNS."

**Scenario 8: Anonymous complaints**
*Citizen:* "Can I just drop an anonymous letter to HR about my colleague touching me inappropriately? I don't want my name involved."
*Assistant:* "An anonymous letter can alert the HR, but the ICC cannot launch a formal POSH inquiry without a signed, written complaint from you. The law ensures your identity remains strictly confidential during and after the inquiry. To get justice and see him punished, you must file a formal complaint."

**Scenario 9: False Complaints Fear**
*Citizen:* "A female colleague threatened to file a fake POSH case against me because of a professional disagreement. How do I protect myself?"
*Assistant:* "Maintain all professional communication records (emails, chats) clearly showing the professional disagreement. If she files a complaint, present these to the ICC. The POSH Act states that if an inquiry concludes a complaint was made with malicious intent and is completely false, strict disciplinary action will be taken against the complainant."

**Scenario 10: Asking for leaves during inquiry**
*Citizen:* "Main harassment ki wajah se bahut stress mein hoon aur usi office mein kaam nahi kar paa rahi jab tak inquiry chal rahi hai. Kya mujhe chhutti mil sakti hai?"
*Assistant:* "Haan, POSH Act ke Section 12 ke tehat aap ICC ko written request de sakti hain paid leave ke liye. ICC aapko 3 mahine tak ki extra paid leaves (aapki normal leaves ke alawa) grant karne ka order employer ko de sakti hai, jab tak inquiry pendng hai."

**Scenario 11: Action on ICC Report**
*Citizen:* "ICC ki inquiry poori ho gayi aur unhone us aadmi ko guilty mana hai. Ab aage kya hoga?"
*Assistant:* "ICC apni final report employer ko degi. Employer ko 60 din ke andar ICC ki recommendations par action lena hoga. Action mein warning, promotion rokna, salary deduction, ya naukri se nikalna shamil ho sakta hai, jo aapki company ke service rules par depend karega."

**Scenario 12: Men as victims**
*Citizen:* "I am a male employee, and a female senior manager makes sexually colored remarks at me. The POSH Act says it's for women. What can I do?"
*Assistant:* "While the POSH Act strictly safeguards women, many progressive companies have gender-neutral anti-harassment policies. You should check your employee handbook and file a formal grievance with your HR department. If it amounts to criminal intimidation, police action can also be explored."

**Scenario 13: Stalking vs Harassment**
*Citizen:* "Ek colleague office ke baad mera peecha karke mere ghar tak aata hai, par kuch kehta nahi hai. Kya yeh sexual harassment hai?"
*Assistant:* "Yeh BNS Section 78 ke andar Stalking (peecha karna) ka serious criminal offence hai. Yeh workplace harassment bhi maana ja sakta hai. Aap turant 112 par police ko call karein aur apni ICC ko bhi iski complaint dein."

**Scenario 14: Non-verbal harassment**
*Citizen:* "Mera co-worker hamesha mujhe upar se neeche tak ghoorta hai jisse mujhe bohot ajeeb lagta hai. Wo chuuta nahi hai. Kya main complain kar sakti hoon?"
*Assistant:* "Bilkul. Unwelcome staring, leering, ya ghoorna non-verbal sexual harassment hota hai. Aap is baat ki complain HR ya ICC ko bejhijhak kar sakti hain. Sexual harassment ke liye physical touch hona zaroori nahi hai."

**Scenario 15: Time Limit for complaint**
*Citizen:* "Mere boss ne mere sath 5 mahine pehle badtameezi ki thi. Main tab darr gayi thi. Kya main ab ICC mein case kar sakti hoon?"
*Assistant:* "Normally, POSH complaint incident ke 3 mahine ke andar karni hoti hai. Par agar aapke paas delay ka valid reason hai (jaise trauma ya darr), toh aap ICC ko likh kar de sakti hain. ICC ke paas power hai ki wo time limit ko badha sakti hai agar wo aapke reason se satisfy ho."

## Government Services
**English:** You can report sexual and cyber harassment directly via the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) for online offenses. The Ministry of Women and Child Development runs the "She-Box" (Sexual Harassment electronic Box) online portal where women can directly file complaints regarding workplace sexual harassment. You can also utilize NALSA (National Legal Services Authority) for free legal aid.
**Hindi Unicode:** आप ऑनलाइन अपराधों के लिए सीधे राष्ट्रीय साइबर अपराध रिपोर्टिंग पोर्टल (cybercrime.gov.in) के माध्यम से यौन और साइबर उत्पीड़न की रिपोर्ट कर सकते हैं। महिला एवं बाल विकास मंत्रालय "शी-बॉक्स" (She-Box) ऑनलाइन पोर्टल चलाता है जहाँ महिलाएँ कार्यस्थल पर यौन उत्पीड़न की शिकायत सीधे दर्ज करा सकती हैं। आप मुफ्त कानूनी सहायता के लिए NALSA का भी उपयोग कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Online harassment ke liye aap Cyber Crime portal par ja sakte hain. Workplace harassment ke liye Government ka "She-Box" portal hai jahan direct online complaint file hoti hai. Free legal help ke liye aap NALSA ko approach kar sakte hain.
