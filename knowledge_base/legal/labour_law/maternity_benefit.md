# 🤰 Maternity Benefit & Working Women's Rights in India

## Overview

**English:** The Maternity Benefit Act, now integrated into the Code on Social Security, 2020, safeguards the dignity of motherhood and ensures that working women are not economically disadvantaged during pregnancy and after childbirth. It mandates 26 weeks of paid leave, medical bonuses, and protection against dismissal for eligible working women in the organized and partially unorganized sectors.

## Definition

**English:** "Maternity Benefit" legally refers to the payment made to a woman by her employer at the rate of her average daily wage for the period of her actual absence immediately preceding and including the day of her delivery, and for the period immediately following that day, as per Chapter VI of the Code on Social Security, 2020.

**Hindi (Unicode):** "मातृत्व लाभ" कानूनी रूप से उस भुगतान को संदर्भित करता है जो किसी महिला को उसके नियोक्ता द्वारा उसकी वास्तविक अनुपस्थिति की अवधि के लिए उसके औसत दैनिक वेतन की दर से किया जाता है, जिसमें प्रसव का दिन और उसके ठीक बाद की अवधि शामिल है (सामाजिक सुरक्षा संहिता, 2020 के अध्याय VI के अनुसार)।

**Hinglish:** "Maternity Benefit" ka legal matlab hai wo payment jo employer ek aurat ko uske average daily wage ke hisaab se deta hai, uske pregnancy aur delivery ke aaspas ki chhuttiyon (absence) ke liye, jaisa ki Code on Social Security 2020 mein likha hai.

## Legal Background

### History & Purpose
The Maternity Benefit Act was originally enacted in 1961 to regulate the employment of women in certain establishments for certain periods before and after child-birth. It was significantly amended in 2017 to increase the leave duration from 12 weeks to 26 weeks. It has now been subsumed under the broader Code on Social Security, 2020.

### Applicable Acts
1. **Code on Social Security, 2020** (Chapter VI replaces the old Maternity Benefit Act).
2. **Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act, 2017** (Still contextually referenced for its landmark changes).
3. **Factories Act, 1948** (Cross-reference for crèche mandates).

### Relevant Constitutional Articles
- **Article 42**: The State shall make provision for securing just and humane conditions of work and for maternity relief.
- **Article 15(3)**: Empowers the State to make special provisions for women and children.

### Relevant Sections (Code on Social Security, 2020)
- **Section 59**: Right to payment of maternity benefit (26 weeks standard).
- **Section 60**: Notice of claim for maternity benefit and payment thereof.
- **Section 62**: Payment of medical bonus (currently INR 3,500, subject to revision).
- **Section 64**: Absence from work during pregnancy, etc.
- **Section 65**: Leave for miscarriage (6 weeks) or tubectomy operation (2 weeks).
- **Section 66**: Nursing breaks and Crèche facility (for 50+ employees).
- **Section 68**: Protection from dismissal during maternity leave.

---

## Applicability

### Who is Covered
- Women employed in factories, mines, plantations, and shops or establishments employing 10 or more persons.
- Commissioning mothers (using surrogates) and adoptive mothers (adopting children below 3 months of age) are eligible for 12 weeks of leave.
- Women in unorganized sectors (through specific schemes under the Code on Social Security).

### Who is Exempt
- Women who are already covered under the Employees' State Insurance (ESI) Act, 1948, receive maternity benefits directly from the ESI Corporation, provided their wages are below the ESI wage ceiling (currently ₹21,000/month). However, if ESI benefits are not available, the employer must provide them.

### Special Conditions
- **Eligibility criteria**: The woman must have worked in the establishment for at least **80 days** in the 12 months immediately preceding the expected date of delivery.

**English:**
- **Employee**: Must give written notice in the prescribed format to the employer stating the intention to take maternity leave, along with the expected date of delivery (usually supported by a medical certificate).
- **Employer**: Must inform women in writing and electronically about their maternity benefits upon their initial appointment. Must pay the benefit in advance or within 48 hours of proof of delivery.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English:**
1. **Calculate Eligibility**: Check if you have completed 80 days of continuous work in the past 12 months in the current company.
2. **Submit Notice**: Submit Form 1 (or the company’s internal form) to the HR/Employer stating the leave period (up to 8 weeks before delivery).
3. **Submit Proof**: Attach a medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner confirming the pregnancy and expected delivery date.
4. **Receive Pre-natal Benefit**: The employer must pay the wages for the period preceding the delivery in advance.
5. **Post-delivery Proof**: After childbirth, submit the birth certificate or medical document.
6. **Receive Post-natal Benefit**: The employer must disburse the remaining maternity pay within 48 hours.

**Hindi (Unicode):**
1. **पात्रता की गणना करें**: जांचें कि क्या आपने वर्तमान कंपनी में पिछले 12 महीनों में 80 दिन का काम पूरा कर लिया है।
2. **नोटिस जमा करें**: एचआर/नियोक्ता को फॉर्म 1 जमा करें जिसमें छुट्टी की अवधि (प्रसव से 8 सप्ताह पहले तक) का उल्लेख हो।
3. **प्रमाण संलग्न करें**: गर्भावस्था की पुष्टि करने वाला मेडिकल सर्टिफिकेट संलग्न करें।
4. **प्रसव पूर्व लाभ प्राप्त करें**: नियोक्ता को डिलीवरी से पहले की अवधि के लिए अग्रिम वेतन देना होगा।

6. **प्रसवोत्तर लाभ प्राप्त करें**: नियोक्ता को 48 घंटों के भीतर शेष भुगतान करना होगा।

## Government Authorities
- **Labour Commissioner (Central/State)**: For filing complaints against employers refusing to provide benefits.
- **Industrial Tribunal / Labour Court**: For escalating unresolved disputes regarding illegal termination during pregnancy.
- **Employees' State Insurance Corporation (ESIC)**: For claiming benefits if the employee falls under the ESI Act scheme.

---

## Penalties & Punishments
Under Section 133 of the Code on Social Security, 2020:
- **Failure to pay maternity benefit or illegal dismissal**: An employer who fails to pay the required benefit or illegally dismisses a woman during her maternity leave is punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to **6 months**, or with a fine up to **₹50,000**, or both.
- Repeated offenses attract harsher penalties (up to 2 years imprisonment and higher fines).

---

## Exceptions
- **Third Child Onwards**: For a woman who already has two or more surviving children, the maternity leave is restricted to **12 weeks** (6 weeks pre-delivery and 6 weeks post-delivery).
- **ESI Coverage**: Women drawing wages up to ₹21,000 and covered under ESIC must claim their benefits from ESIC, not directly from the employer, though the employer facilitates the paperwork.
- **Termination for Misconduct**: If a woman is dismissed for "gross misconduct" (as legally defined), the employer can deprive her of the maternity benefit or medical bonus, provided a written order is given.

---

## Important Timelines
- **Eligibility Window**: 80 days of work in the preceding 12 months.
- **Notice Period**: Ideally 2 to 3 months before the expected delivery date, though legally permissible any time during pregnancy.
- **Pre-delivery Leave**: Maximum 8 weeks before the expected delivery date.
- **Payment Deadline**: Within 48 hours of producing proof of delivery for post-delivery dues.
- **Miscarriage Leave**: 6 weeks immediately following the day of miscarriage (requires medical proof).
- **Tubectomy Leave**: 2 weeks immediately following the operation.
- **Appeal Timeline**: If deprived of benefits, the woman has 60 days to appeal to the prescribed authority (Labour Commissioner).

---

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Not Giving Written Notice**: Telling the manager verbally without formal written application or HR portal logging.
- **Assuming Probationers Aren't Eligible**: Probationary employees are legally entitled to maternity benefits if they meet the 80-day criteria.
- **Resigning Instead of Taking Leave**: Some women resign out of guilt or pressure; this forfeits their legal right to 6 months of paid salary.
- **Accepting Unpaid Leave**: Accepting an employer's false claim that the company only offers 3 months of paid and 3 months of unpaid leave. The law strictly mandates 26 weeks of fully *paid* leave.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What is the maximum duration of maternity leave in India?**
**English:** Under the Code on Social Security, 2020, eligible working women are entitled to a maximum of 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for their first two children. For the third child onwards, it is 12 weeks.
**Hindi:** सामाजिक सुरक्षा संहिता, 2020 के तहत, योग्य कामकाजी महिलाएं अपने पहले दो बच्चों के लिए अधिकतम 26 सप्ताह के सवैतनिक मातृत्व अवकाश की हकदार हैं। तीसरे बच्चे के लिए यह 12 सप्ताह है।
**Hinglish:** Pehle do bacchon ke liye aap 26 weeks ki paid leave le sakti hain. Teesre bacche se leave 12 weeks ki hoti hai.

**Q2: Am I eligible for maternity leave if I am on probation?**
**English:** Yes. The law does not distinguish between a probationary, permanent, or contractual employee. As long as you have worked for at least 80 days in the 12 months prior to your expected delivery date, you are eligible.
**Hindi:** हाँ। कानून परिवीक्षाधीन (प्रोबेशन), स्थायी या संविदा कर्मचारी के बीच अंतर नहीं करता है। यदि आपने 80 दिन काम किया है तो आप पात्र हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, probation par hone se koi farq nahi padta. Agar aapne 80 din company me kaam kar liya hai, toh aap eligible hain.

**Q3: Can my employer fire me because I am pregnant?**
**English:** No. Section 68 of the Code strictly prohibits an employer from discharging or dismissing a woman during her maternity leave or on account of her pregnancy. Doing so is a punishable offense.
**Hindi:** नहीं। धारा 68 नियोक्ता को मातृत्व अवकाश के दौरान या गर्भावस्था के कारण महिला को नौकरी से निकालने से सख्ती से रोकती है।
**Hinglish:** Bilkul nahi. Pregnancy ya maternity leave ke dauran aapko job se nikalna gair kanooni hai aur iske liye employer ko saza ho sakti है।

**Q4: Do contract workers get maternity benefits?**
**English:** Yes. The rules apply to women employed either directly or through any agency (contractor). The principal employer is ultimately responsible for ensuring the benefits are paid if the contractor defaults.
**Hindi:** हाँ। नियम सीधे या किसी एजेंसी (ठेकेदार) के माध्यम से कार्यरत महिलाओं पर लागू होते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, contract workers ko bhi benefit milta hai. Agar contractor paise na de, toh principal company ki zimmedari hoti hai pay karna.

**Q5: What happens if I suffer a miscarriage?**
**English:** In case of a miscarriage or medical termination of pregnancy, a woman is entitled to 6 weeks of paid leave immediately following the day of the miscarriage, provided she produces sufficient medical proof.
**Hindi:** गर्भपात होने पर, महिला को चिकित्सा प्रमाण प्रस्तुत करने पर गर्भपात के दिन के ठीक बाद 6 सप्ताह के सवैतनिक अवकाश का अधिकार है।
**Hinglish:** Miscarriage hone par aap 6 weeks ki paid leave le sakti hain. Iske liye doctor ka certificate dena zaroori hota hai.

**Q6: What is the rule for adoptive mothers?**
**English:** A woman who legally adopts a child below the age of three months is entitled to maternity leave of 12 weeks from the date the child is handed over to her.
**Hindi:** एक महिला जो कानूनी रूप से तीन महीने से कम उम्र के बच्चे को गोद लेती है, वह 12 सप्ताह के मातृत्व अवकाश की हकदार है।
**Hinglish:** Agar aap 3 mahine se chote bache ko legally adopt karti hain, toh aapko 12 weeks ki paid leave milegi.

**Q7: What is a commissioning mother, and what is her leave entitlement?**
**English:** A commissioning mother is a biological mother who uses her egg to create an embryo implanted in any other woman (surrogate). She is entitled to 12 weeks of paid maternity leave.
**Hindi:** सरोगेसी के माध्यम से मां बनने वाली (कमीशनिंग मदर) महिला 12 सप्ताह के सवैतनिक मातृत्व अवकाश की हकदार है।
**Hinglish:** Surrogacy ke case me commissioning mother ko 12 weeks ki paid leave ka right hai.

**Q8: Can I start my maternity leave 3 months before delivery?**
**English:** No. The law allows you to take a maximum of 8 weeks of leave prior to your expected date of delivery. The remaining 18 weeks must be taken post-delivery.
**Hindi:** नहीं। कानून आपको आपकी अपेक्षित डिलीवरी की तारीख से पहले अधिकतम 8 सप्ताह की छुट्टी लेने की अनुमति देता है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, kanoon ke hisaab se aap delivery se pehle maximum 8 weeks (lagbhag 2 mahine) chhutti le sakti hain. Baaki chhutti delivery ke baad hogi.

**Q9: Does my company have to provide a crèche?**
**English:** Every establishment having 50 or more employees is mandated to have a crèche facility. Women are allowed four visits a day to the crèche, which includes their regular rest intervals.
**Hindi:** 50 या अधिक कर्मचारियों वाले प्रत्येक प्रतिष्ठान में क्रेच सुविधा होना अनिवार्य है।
**Hinglish:** Agar company me 50 ya usse zyada employees hain, toh crèche (baby care room) hona mandatory hai. Mother ko din me 4 baar visit allowed hai.

**Q10: Can I ask for 'Work from Home' after my maternity leave ends?**
**English:** Yes. The law has a provision that if the nature of work allows it, a woman may request to work from home after her 26-week leave expires, subject to mutual agreement with the employer.
**Hindi:** हाँ। यदि काम की प्रकृति इसकी अनुमति देती है, तो एक महिला 26 सप्ताह की छुट्टी समाप्त होने के बाद नियोक्ता की सहमति से घर से काम करने का अनुरोध कर सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, leave khatam hone ke baad aap WFH mang sakti hain, par yeh employer ki marzi aur mutual agreement par depend karta hai.

**Q11: Will my salary be reduced during maternity leave?**
**English:** No. The maternity benefit is paid at the rate of the "average daily wage" for the period of actual absence. This includes your basic salary, dearness allowance, and standard fixed allowances.
**Hindi:** नहीं। मातृत्व लाभ वास्तविक अनुपस्थिति की अवधि के लिए "औसत दैनिक वेतन" की दर पर भुगतान किया जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, maternity leave ke dauran aapki basic salary aur allowances pure milte hain bina kisi deduction ke.

**Q12: What if the employer refuses to pay the maternity benefit?**
**English:** You can file a formal complaint with the local Inspector appointed under the Act or the Labour Commissioner. If found guilty, the employer faces imprisonment and financial penalties.
**Hindi:** आप स्थानीय श्रम निरीक्षक या श्रम आयुक्त के पास औपचारिक शिकायत दर्ज कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Agar company paise na de, toh aap Labour Commissioner ya Inspector ke paas complaint kar sakti hain.

**Q13: Is the maternity bonus mandatory?**
**English:** Yes, under Section 62 of the Code, every woman is entitled to a medical bonus (currently ₹3500) if no pre-natal and post-natal care is provided free of charge by the employer.
**Hindi:** हाँ, यदि नियोक्ता द्वारा मुफ्त प्रसव पूर्व और प्रसवोत्तर देखभाल प्रदान नहीं की जाती है, तो प्रत्येक महिला चिकित्सा बोनस की हकदार है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar company free medical care nahi deti, toh ₹3500 ka medical bonus dena mandatory hai.

**Q14: What if a woman dies during delivery or soon after?**
**English:** If a woman dies during delivery or the leave period, the employer must pay the maternity benefit up to the day of her death. If the child survives, the benefit must be paid for the entire period. If the child also dies, it is paid up to the date of the child's death to her nominee.
**Hindi:** यदि डिलीवरी या छुट्टी की अवधि के दौरान किसी महिला की मृत्यु हो जाती है, तो नियोक्ता को उसकी मृत्यु के दिन तक का भुगतान करना होगा।
**Hinglish:** Agar mother ki death ho jaye, toh unke nominee ko paise milenge. Agar bacha zinda hai, toh puri chhutti ka paisa nominee ko milega.

**Q15: Do I get paid for holidays falling during my maternity leave?**
**English:** Yes. The maternity benefit is calculated for the entire continuous period of absence, which naturally includes all weekends, public holidays, and national holidays falling within those 26 weeks.
**Hindi:** हाँ। यह लगातार अवधि के लिए है, जिसमें सप्ताहांत (वीकेंड) और सार्वजनिक अवकाश शामिल हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, un 26 weeks ke beech me aane wale saare Sundays aur national holidays ka paisa aapko milega.

**Q16: How many nursing breaks am I allowed?**
**English:** A woman is entitled to two additional nursing breaks of 15 minutes each (excluding commute time to the crèche/home) during her daily work hours until the child reaches 15 months of age.
**Hindi:** एक महिला को अपने दैनिक कार्य घंटों के दौरान 15-15 मिनट के दो अतिरिक्त नर्सिंग ब्रेक का अधिकार है जब तक कि बच्चा 15 महीने का न हो जाए।
**Hinglish:** Jab tak bacha 15 mahine ka nahi ho jata, aapko din me do extra nursing breaks milenge apne regular lunch/tea break ke alawa.

**Q17: Does this act apply to start-ups with less than 10 employees?**
**English:** The traditional Maternity Benefit provisions applied to shops and establishments with 10 or more employees. However, various state laws and the new Code attempt to broaden this. Startups with <10 might not strictly fall under this mandate unless they are factories, but modern startups often offer it via internal policies.
**Hindi:** यह कानून आम तौर पर 10 या अधिक कर्मचारियों वाले प्रतिष्ठानों पर लागू होता है।
**Hinglish:** Agar startup me 10 se kam log hain toh strict legal mandate nahi hota, par aaj kal most companies apne internal HR policy me leave deti hain.

**Q18: What is 'Tubectomy Leave'?**
**English:** A woman undergoing a tubectomy operation (sterilization) is entitled to two weeks of paid leave immediately following the day of her operation, upon producing a medical certificate.
**Hindi:** ट्यूबेक्टॉमी (नसबंदी) ऑपरेशन कराने वाली महिला को मेडिकल सर्टिफिकेट जमा करने पर ऑपरेशन के ठीक बाद 2 सप्ताह के सवैतनिक अवकाश का अधिकार है।
**Hinglish:** Nasbandi (Tubectomy) operation ke baad 2 weeks ki paid leave milti hai.

**Q19: Can a pregnant woman be forced to do strenuous work?**
**English:** No. For a period of one month immediately preceding the 8-week pre-delivery leave, an employer cannot force a pregnant woman to do arduous work or work involving long hours of standing which could interfere with her pregnancy.
**Hindi:** नहीं। प्रसव पूर्व अवकाश से ठीक एक महीने पहले, नियोक्ता किसी गर्भवती महिला को कठिन काम करने के लिए मजबूर नहीं कर सकता।
**Hinglish:** Pregnancy ke dauran employer aapse koi aisa bhari kaam ya lambe time tak khade rehne wala kaam nahi karwa sakta jo aapki health ke liye theek na ho.

**Q20: Are teachers covered under this law?**
**English:** Yes. The Supreme Court of India has ruled that teachers are considered "employees" and educational institutions are "establishments." Thus, teachers are fully entitled to maternity benefits.
**Hindi:** हाँ। भारत के सर्वोच्च न्यायालय ने फैसला दिया है कि शिक्षक भी इस अधिनियम के तहत मातृत्व लाभ के हकदार हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, Supreme Court ke order ke hisaab se private aur government schools ki female teachers bhi maternity leave ki poori haqdaar hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** Maternity leave is only for 3 months in private companies.
   **Fact:** The law mandates 26 weeks (6.5 months) of paid leave for all eligible private and public sector employees for the first two children.
2. **Myth:** If you are on a probation period, HR can deny your maternity leave.
   **Fact:** Probationary status does not matter. If you have worked 80 days in the last 12 months, you are legally entitled.
3. **Myth:** WFH is automatically guaranteed for a year after maternity leave.
   **Fact:** WFH is only an option if the nature of the work permits it, and it requires mutual consent between you and your employer.
4. **Myth:** The employer can deduct basic salary and only pay a stipend during leave.
   **Fact:** You must be paid your "average daily wage" in full. No arbitrary deductions are allowed.
5. **Myth:** Surrogacy doesn't qualify for maternity leave.
   **Fact:** Commissioning mothers (via surrogacy) are legally entitled to 12 weeks of paid leave.
6. **Myth:** If a woman suffers a miscarriage, she has to use her sick leave.
   **Fact:** The law provides a specific 6 weeks of paid "Miscarriage Leave" separate from regular sick leave.
7. **Myth:** Companies with 20 employees don't need to provide a crèche.
   **Fact:** True. The crèche facility is mandatory only for establishments with 50 or more employees.
8. **Myth:** You cannot be fired for *any* reason while on maternity leave.
   **Fact:** You cannot be fired *due to pregnancy*. However, if proven guilty of gross misconduct/fraud unrelated to pregnancy, termination is legally possible, though highly scrutinized.
9. **Myth:** Contract workers have to claim maternity from their third-party agency only.
   **Fact:** The principal employer is jointly liable. If the contractor fails, the principal employer must pay.
10. **Myth:** Men get 2 weeks of mandatory paternity leave in India.
    **Fact:** There is no national statutory law mandating paternity leave in the private sector in India. Only Central Government employees get 15 days of paternity leave.

## Conversation Examples

**Dialogue 1: General Eligibility**
*Citizen:* I just found out I'm pregnant. I joined this company 4 months ago. Am I eligible for maternity leave?
*Saarthi:* Congratulations! Yes, you are eligible. The law requires you to have worked for at least 80 days in the 12 months preceding your expected delivery date. Since you've been there 4 months, you meet this criteria and are entitled to 26 weeks of paid leave.

**Dialogue 2: Leave Duration**
*Citizen:* HR keh rahe hain ki private sector me sirf 12 weeks leave milti hai. Kya yeh sach hai?
*Saarthi:* Nahi, yeh sach nahi hai. 2017 ke amendment ke baad, chahe private sector ho ya public, pehle do bacchon ke liye 26 weeks (lagbhag 6.5 mahine) ki paid maternity leave dena kanoonan anivarya hai. Aap apne HR ko Maternity Benefit Act dikha sakti hain.

**Dialogue 3: Miscarriage Leave**
*Citizen:* I unfortunately had a miscarriage last week. My manager says I have to use my casual leaves. What are my rights?
*Saarthi:* I am so sorry for your loss. Legally, your manager is wrong. Under Section 65 of the Code on Social Security, you are entitled to exactly 6 weeks of paid miscarriage leave immediately following the incident. You just need to submit a medical certificate.

**Dialogue 4: Fear of Dismissal**
*Citizen:* Mujhe dar hai ki agar maine pregnancy ka bataya toh meri company mujhe nikal degi. Kya wo aisa kar sakte hain?
*Saarthi:* Kanoon ke mutabiq (Section 68), koi bhi company kisi aurat ko pregnancy ke karan naukri se nahi nikal sakti. Yeh gair-kanooni hai. Agar wo aisa karte hain, toh aap Labour Commissioner ke paas case file kar sakti hain.

**Dialogue 5: HR Refusing Benefit**
*Citizen:* My employer says they are a small startup with 15 people and don't fall under the Maternity Act. Is this true?
*Saarthi:* No. The Maternity Benefit provisions apply to any shop or establishment employing 10 or more persons. Since your startup has 15 employees, they are legally bound to provide you with the 26-week paid maternity leave.

**Dialogue 6: Contract Worker Rights**
*Citizen:* Main ek contractor ke through IT company me housekeeping ka kaam karti hoon. Mujhe maternity leave milegi?
*Saarthi:* Haan, contract workers ko bhi maternity benefit milne ka poora haq hai. Aapki pehli zimmedari contractor ki hai, lekin agar wo paise nahi deta, toh wo IT company (principal employer) ki zimmedari hoti hai aapko pay karna.

**Dialogue 7: Adoption Leave**
*Citizen:* I am adopting a 2-month-old baby girl next month. Do I get maternity leave?
*Saarthi:* Yes! Legally adopting a child under three months of age makes you eligible for 12 weeks of paid maternity leave, starting from the date the child is handed over to you.

**Dialogue 8: WFH Request**
*Citizen:* Meri 26 weeks ki leave khatam hone wali hai par bacha bohot chhota hai. Kya main Work From Home mang sakti hoon?
*Saarthi:* Haan, kanoon me provision hai ki aap leave ke baad Work From Home (WFH) mang sakti hain, bas shart yeh hai ki aapka kaam ghar se hone layak ho aur aapka employer iske liye agree kare. Aap HR se is bare me formal request kar sakti hain.

**Dialogue 9: Nursing Breaks**
*Citizen:* Returning to work is tough. Does the company have to give me time to nurse my baby?
*Saarthi:* Yes. Until your child is 15 months old, you are legally entitled to two additional nursing breaks of 15 minutes each during your daily work hours, apart from your standard rest/lunch intervals.

**Dialogue 10: Crèche Issue**
*Citizen:* Humari company me 100 log kaam karte hain par koi crèche nahi hai. Kya unhe banana chahiye?
*Saarthi:* Haan, bilkul. Kanoon ke anusaar, jis bhi company me 50 ya usse zyada employees hain, wahan crèche facility hona anivarya (mandatory) hai. Aap HR me iski maang kar sakti hain.

**Dialogue 11: Medical Bonus**
*Citizen:* I paid for all my delivery expenses. Can I ask my company for a medical bonus?
*Saarthi:* Yes, if your employer did not provide you with free pre-natal and post-natal care, you are entitled to a medical bonus (currently ₹3,500) under the law.

**Dialogue 12: Notice Format**
*Citizen:* How do I officially inform my company about taking maternity leave?
*Saarthi:* You need to submit a written notice (often referred to as Form 1 or the company’s internal form). It must state the date from which your absence will begin (up to 8 weeks before delivery) and be accompanied by a medical certificate from your doctor.

**Dialogue 13: Start Date of Leave**
*Citizen:* Mujhe kab se maternity leave leni shuru karni chahiye?
*Saarthi:* Yeh poori tarah aapki health par depend karta hai. Kanoon aapko delivery ki expected date se maximum 8 weeks (2 mahine) pehle chhutti shuru karne ki ijazat deta hai. Baaki chhutti aap delivery ke baad le sakti hain.

**Dialogue 14: Third Child**
*Citizen:* This is my third pregnancy. Will I get 26 weeks of leave again?
*Saarthi:* No. For the third child onwards, the law provides for 12 weeks of paid maternity leave (maximum 6 weeks pre-delivery and 6 weeks post-delivery).

**Dialogue 15: Deduction during leave**
*Citizen:* My payslip during maternity leave shows they deducted my transport allowance. Is that legal?
*Saarthi:* Typically, maternity pay is based on "average daily wage." While basic pay and dearness allowance cannot be deducted, certain specific allowances tied directly to physical presence at work (like daily transport/meal coupons) might be excluded depending on your employment contract. However, your core fixed salary must remain completely unaffected.
