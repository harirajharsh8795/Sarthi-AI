# Payment of Gratuity: Laws, Rights, and Procedures in India

## Overview

**English:**
Gratuity is a statutory monetary benefit provided by an employer to an employee in recognition of their long and continuous service to the organization. Governed primarily by the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, it serves as a crucial social security measure designed to support employees upon retirement, resignation, superannuation, or in the unfortunate event of death or disablement. It is a defined benefit plan, meaning the payout is calculated based on a specific formula tied to the employee's last drawn salary and total years of service, rather than market-linked investments.

## Quick Summary

**English:**
The Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 mandates that any establishment with 10 or more employees must pay gratuity to workers who have completed at least 5 years of continuous service. The standard calculation is 15 days of the last drawn wages for every completed year of service. The current tax-free limit for gratuity is Rs. 20 Lakhs. Employers must disburse the amount within 30 days of it becoming payable; otherwise, they are liable to pay interest.

## Definition

**English:**
Gratuity is legally defined as a lump sum amount paid by an employer to an employee as a mark of appreciation for the services rendered. It is a statutory right under Section 4 of the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, triggered upon termination of employment after continuous service of not less than five years, except in cases of death or disablement where the five-year rule is waived.

## Why It Matters

**English:**
Gratuity matters because it provides financial stability during critical life transitions such as retirement, career changes, or unforeseen tragedies. It acts as a forced long-term savings mechanism that does not require employee contribution. It legally enforces the employer's moral obligation to care for employees who have dedicated a significant portion of their career to building the enterprise.

## Legal Background

The statutory framework governing gratuity in India ensures standardized practices across diverse industries.
*   **Primary Act:** The Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972.
*   **Constitutional Relevance:** Aligns with Directive Principles of State Policy (Article 41 & 43) regarding the right to work, to education, and to public assistance in cases of old age, sickness, and disablement, and securing a living wage.
*   **Section 2(e) - Employee:** Defines who qualifies as an employee. Excludes apprentices.
*   **Section 2A - Continuous Service:** Defines uninterrupted service, including periods of sickness, accident, leave, layoff, strike, or lockout not due to the fault of the employee. For a 5-year requirement, 240 days (or 190 days in certain underground mines/establishments working 6 days a week) in the fifth year is often considered a completed year.
*   **Section 4 - Payment of Gratuity:** The core mandate requiring payment upon superannuation, retirement, resignation, death, or disablement, subject to the 5-year rule.
*   **Section 4A - Compulsory Insurance:** Requires employers to obtain an insurance in the manner prescribed, for his liability for payment towards the gratuity under this Act (notified in select states).
*   **Section 7 - Determination of the Amount of Gratuity:** Outlines the process for calculating and determining the payable amount, and the employer's duty to arrange payment.
*   **Section 7(3A) - Interest:** Mandates payment of simple interest if gratuity is not paid within 30 days.
*   **Section 8 - Recovery of Gratuity:** Allows the Controlling Authority to issue a certificate to the Collector to recover the amount as arrears of land revenue if the employer defaults.
*   **Section 9 - Penalties:** Prescribes punishments for avoiding payment or making false statements.

## Applicability

The Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 applies to:
*   Every factory, mine, oilfield, plantation, port, and railway company.
*   Every shop or establishment within the meaning of any law for the time being in force in relation to shops and establishments in a State, in which ten or more persons are employed, or were employed, on any day of the preceding twelve months.
*   Such other establishments or class of establishments, in which ten or more employees are employed, as the Central Government may, by notification, specify.

**Important condition:** Once the Act applies to an establishment, it continues to apply even if the number of employees falls below 10 subsequently.

**Exemptions/Exceptions:**
*   Apprentices are generally excluded.
*   Employees governed by other Acts providing better gratuity benefits may claim under those better provisions.
*   The 5-year continuous service rule is waived in case of the termination of employment due to death or disablement (due to accident or disease). In case of death, the gratuity is paid to the nominee or legal heir.

## Rights of Citizens

**English:**
*   Right to receive gratuity upon completion of 5 years of continuous service.
*   Right to receive the payment within 30 days from the date it becomes payable.
*   Right to receive simple interest if the employer delays payment beyond 30 days.
*   Right to nominate one or more family members to receive the gratuity in case of death.
*   Right to file a complaint/claim before the Controlling Authority (Assistant Labour Commissioner) if the employer denies or delays payment.
*   Right to tax exemption on gratuity up to Rs. 20 Lakhs under the Income Tax Act.

## Duties & Responsibilities

**English:**
*   **Employee Duties:** Submit Form F for nomination at the time of joining or within 30 days. Apply for gratuity using Form I within 30 days of it becoming payable (though courts have held that delayed application doesn't forfeit the right).
*   **Employer Duties:** Determine the amount of gratuity as soon as it becomes payable. Give notice in Form L to the employee and the Controlling Authority specifying the amount. Pay the amount within 30 days. Maintain proper records of nominations.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English - Claiming Gratuity & Dispute Resolution:**
1. **Trigger Event:** Employee resigns, retires, or is terminated after 5+ years of continuous service.
2. **Application by Employee:** Employee submits a written application (Form I) to the employer within 30 days.
3. **Processing by Employer:** Employer verifies the claim, calculates the amount, and issues a notice of payment (Form L) within 15 days of receiving the application.
4. **Disbursement:** Employer pays the gratuity within 30 days from the last working day.
5. **In Case of Dispute/Denial:** If the employer refuses to pay, pays less, or fails to pay within 30 days, the employee must issue a legal notice to the employer.
6. **Filing Application to Authority:** If unresolved, the employee files Form N before the Controlling Authority (Assistant Labour Commissioner) within 90 days of the occurrence of the cause for the application.
7. **Hearing & Order:** The Authority summons both parties, hears the case, and passes an order determining the payable amount and directing the employer to pay within 30 days of the order.
8. **Recovery Certificate:** If the employer still defaults, the Authority issues a Recovery Certificate to the District Collector to recover the amount as land revenue arrears.

## Required Documents
*   **Form F:** Nomination form (filled at joining).
*   **Form I:** Application for payment of gratuity by an employee.
*   **Form J:** Application for payment of gratuity by a nominee (in case of employee's death).
*   **Form K:** Application for payment of gratuity by a legal heir (if no nomination exists).
*   Copy of the Resignation Letter / Retirement Notice and its acceptance.
*   Relieving Letter / Experience Certificate.
*   Last 3 months' Salary Slips (showing Basic + DA).
*   Bank Account Details (Cancelled cheque / Passbook copy).
*   Identity Proof (Aadhaar Card, PAN Card).
*   In case of death: Death Certificate of the employee, succession certificate (if legal heir).

## Government Authorities
*   **Controlling Authority:** The Assistant Labour Commissioner (ALC) of the central or state jurisdiction, depending on the establishment.
*   **Appellate Authority:** The Deputy Labour Commissioner (DLC) or Joint Labour Commissioner (JLC), to whom appeals against the Controlling Authority's orders can be filed within 60 days.
*   **District Collector:** Executes the recovery certificate issued by the Controlling Authority.
*   **Labour Courts / Industrial Tribunals:** Broad jurisdiction over complex industrial disputes, though the Payment of Gratuity Act provides its own dedicated machinery (Controlling Authority) which is the primary forum.

## Penalties & Punishments
*   **For Default in Payment:** Section 9(2) prescribes imprisonment for a term not less than 6 months but which may extend to 2 years, or with fine not less than Rs. 10,000 but which may extend to Rs. 20,000, or with both, for an employer who contravenes any provision of the Act or rules.
*   **For False Statements:** Section 9(1) prescribes imprisonment for up to 6 months, or a fine up to Rs. 10,000, or both, for making any false statement to avoid payment.
*   **Delayed Payment Interest:** Mandatory simple interest (usually at rates notified by the Government, historically around 10%) on the delayed amount under Section 7(3A).

## Exceptions
*   **Forfeiture of Gratuity (Section 4(6)):**
    *   The gratuity of an employee whose services have been terminated for any act, wilful omission, or negligence causing any damage or loss to the employer's property, shall be forfeited to the extent of the damage or loss.
    *   The gratuity payable may be wholly or partially forfeited if the services are terminated for riotous or disorderly conduct, or any other act of violence on his part.
    *   It may be forfeited if the termination is for any act which constitutes an offence involving moral turpitude, provided such offence is committed in the course of employment.
*   **Teachers:** Educational institutions are covered, and teachers are considered 'employees' eligible for gratuity following the 2009 amendment (retrospective from 1997).

## Important Timelines
*   **Completion of Service:** Must complete 5 years of continuous service (Exceptions: Death/Disablement).
*   **Application (Form I):** Preferably within 30 days of gratuity becoming payable.
*   **Employer's Notice (Form L):** Within 15 days of receipt of application.
*   **Payment Deadline:** Within 30 days from the date it becomes payable (last working day).
*   **Complaint (Form N):** Within 90 days to the Controlling Authority if payment is delayed/denied.
*   **Appeal:** Within 60 days of the Controlling Authority's order to the Appellate Authority (extendable by 60 days for sufficient cause).
*   **Pre-deposit for Appeal:** The employer must deposit the ordered gratuity amount with the Appellate Authority before the appeal is entertained.

## Common Legal Mistakes
*   **Not Filing Form F (Nomination):** Employees often forget to update nominations, causing massive legal hurdles for families in case of death.
*   **Miscalculating 5 Years:** Quitting at 4 years and 10 months and assuming eligibility. (Though some courts have held 4 years and 240 days is sufficient, it is a high-risk gamble and often requires litigation to prove).
*   **Delaying the Claim:** Assuming the employer will automatically pay and waiting years before filing a complaint. While there is no strict limitation period barring statutory rights, huge delays complicate evidence.
*   **Signing Blank Full & Final (F&F) Documents:** Employees signing documents stating "No dues pending" before actual money hits the bank account.
*   **Employers Forfeiting Unlawfully:** Employers deducting notice period pay or arbitrary damages from gratuity without following due process (issuing show-cause notice and quantifying loss).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. Is it mandatory to complete exactly 5 years to get gratuity?**
**English:** Yes, completion of 5 years of continuous service is the primary statutory requirement. However, in cases of death or disablement, this requirement is waived.
**Hindi:** हाँ, 5 वर्ष की निरंतर सेवा पूरी करना प्राथमिक वैधानिक आवश्यकता है। हालांकि, मृत्यु या विकलांगता के मामलों में, इस आवश्यकता को माफ कर दिया गया है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, 5 saal ki continuous service primary rule hai. Par death ya disablement ke case mein yeh rule apply nahi hota.

**2. What happens if I resign after 4 years and 240 days?**
**English:** The Madras High Court and some other courts have interpreted that completing 4 years and 240 days in the 5th year constitutes continuous service for 5 years. However, this is heavily litigated, and employers often deny it, forcing employees to approach the Labour Commissioner.
**Hindi:** मद्रास उच्च न्यायालय और कुछ अन्य अदालतों ने व्याख्या की है कि 5वें वर्ष में 4 वर्ष और 240 दिन पूरे करना 5 वर्ष की निरंतर सेवा माना जाता है। लेकिन यह विवादित है और अक्सर मुकदमेबाजी की आवश्यकता होती है।
**Hinglish:** Kuch High Courts ke mutabiq 4 saal 240 din ko 5 saal mana ja sakta hai, par employers usually isey deny karte hain aur aapko Labour Commissioner ke paas jana pad sakta hai.

**3. Is gratuity calculated on basic salary or in-hand salary?**
**English:** Gratuity is calculated based on your Basic Salary plus Dearness Allowance (DA) drawn last. It does not include HRA, conveyance, or other allowances.
**Hindi:** ग्रेच्युटी की गणना आपके मूल वेतन (Basic Salary) और महंगाई भत्ते (DA) के आधार पर की जाती है। इसमें HRA या अन्य भत्ते शामिल नहीं हैं।
**Hinglish:** Gratuity sirf Basic Salary aur DA (Dearness Allowance) par calculate hoti hai. Isme HRA ya in-hand salary ke baaki allowances add nahi hote.

**4. How is the gratuity amount calculated?**
**English:** The formula is: (15 / 26) * (Last drawn Basic + DA) * (Number of completed years of service). Fractions of a year exceeding 6 months are counted as a full year.
**Hindi:** सूत्र है: (15 / 26) * (अंतिम आहरित मूल वेतन + DA) * (सेवा के पूर्ण वर्षों की संख्या)। 6 महीने से अधिक के अंश को पूरा वर्ष माना जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Formula hai: (15/26) x (Last drawn Basic + DA) x (Total years of service). Agar service mein 6 mahine se zyada ka fraction hai, toh usey poora saal mana jata hai.

**5. Is the gratuity amount taxable?**
**English:** Gratuity received up to Rs. 20 Lakhs is exempt from Income Tax for employees covered under the Payment of Gratuity Act during their lifetime. Amounts exceeding this are taxed as per your slab.
**Hindi:** ग्रेच्युटी भुगतान अधिनियम के तहत आने वाले कर्मचारियों के लिए 20 लाख रुपये तक प्राप्त ग्रेच्युटी आयकर से मुक्त है।
**Hinglish:** Rs. 20 Lakhs tak ki gratuity par koi income tax nahi lagta. Uske upar ka amount aapke tax slab ke hisaab se taxable hota hai.

**6. Can an employer deduct money from my gratuity for a lost laptop?**
**English:** Yes, under Section 4(6), an employer can forfeit gratuity to the extent of damage or loss caused to employer property due to wilful omission or negligence. However, they must prove this loss and provide a hearing.
**Hindi:** हां, धारा 4(6) के तहत, नियोक्ता किसी संपत्ति के नुकसान की सीमा तक ग्रेच्युटी को जब्त कर सकता है। हालांकि, उन्हें इस नुकसान को साबित करना होगा।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar aapne company ki property (jaise laptop) ko wilful damage kiya hai, toh employer utna amount deduct kar sakta hai, par unhe proper process follow karna hoga.

**7. Can an employer deny gratuity citing poor performance?**
**English:** No. Poor performance is not a legal ground for forfeiting or denying gratuity. Gratuity can only be forfeited for acts involving moral turpitude, violence, or wilful damage to property.
**Hindi:** नहीं। खराब प्रदर्शन ग्रेच्युटी से इनकार करने का कानूनी आधार नहीं है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. Poor performance ke naam par gratuity deny nahi ki ja sakti. Yeh statutory right hai.

**8. What if the company shuts down? Do I still get my gratuity?**
**English:** Yes, the liability to pay gratuity remains. If a company goes into liquidation, employees' statutory dues, including gratuity, are treated as preferential payments under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC).
**Hindi:** हाँ, ग्रेच्युटी देने का दायित्व बना रहता है। कंपनी बंद होने पर भी कर्मचारियों के वैधानिक बकाये को प्राथमिकता दी जाती है।
**Hinglish:** Haan. Company band hone par bhi gratuity dena zaroori hai. IBC ke under employees ke dues ko priority di jati hai.

**9. My employer has not paid my gratuity even after 3 months. What should I do?**
**English:** You should first send a legal notice demanding payment with interest. If they fail to comply, file Form N before the Controlling Authority (Assistant Labour Commissioner) of your area.
**Hindi:** आपको पहले एक कानूनी नोटिस भेजना चाहिए। यदि वे अनुपालन नहीं करते हैं, तो अपने क्षेत्र के नियंत्रण प्राधिकारी के समक्ष फॉर्म N दाखिल करें।
**Hinglish:** Pehle apne employer ko legal notice bhejein interest ke sath payment demand karte hue. Agar na maane, toh ALC ke paas Form N file karein.

**10. I am on a contract/fixed-term employment. Am I eligible?**
**English:** Yes, fixed-term employees are eligible for gratuity. Under the new labour codes (Code on Social Security, 2020), the 5-year requirement is proposed to be removed for fixed-term employees, making them eligible on a pro-rata basis, though the implementation depends on the effective date of the Code. Under the 1972 Act, continuous service rules apply.
**Hindi:** हाँ, निश्चित अवधि के कर्मचारी ग्रेच्युटी के पात्र हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, fixed-term (contract) employees bhi eligible hote hain rules ke hisaab se.

**11. Is maternity leave counted as continuous service for gratuity?**
**English:** Yes, any period of maternity leave (up to the legally mandated 26 weeks) is counted as continuous service and cannot be considered a break in service.
**Hindi:** हाँ, मातृत्व अवकाश की अवधि को निरंतर सेवा माना जाता है और इसे सेवा में विराम नहीं माना जा सकता।
**Hinglish:** Haan, maternity leave continuous service ka hissa mani jati hai aur usey break in service nahi mana ja sakta.

**12. What is the role of Form F?**
**English:** Form F is the official nomination form where an employee declares who will receive the gratuity amount in the event of the employee's untimely death.
**Hindi:** फॉर्म F आधिकारिक नामांकन फॉर्म है जहां एक कर्मचारी यह घोषित करता है कि कर्मचारी की असामयिक मृत्यु की स्थिति में ग्रेच्युटी राशि किसे मिलेगी।
**Hinglish:** Form F nomination form hai. Agar employee ki job ke dauran death ho jaye, toh yeh form decide karta hai ki paisa kis family member ko milega.

**13. Can I nominate a friend for my gratuity?**
**English:** If you have a "family" as defined under the Act (spouse, children, dependent parents, etc.), you must nominate them. A nomination made in favor of a non-family member is invalid if you have a family. If you do not have a family, you can nominate anyone, but it must be updated once you acquire a family.
**Hindi:** यदि आपका "परिवार" है, तो आपको उन्हें ही नामित करना होगा। परिवार होने पर बाहरी व्यक्ति का नामांकन अमान्य है।
**Hinglish:** Agar aapki family hai, toh aapko family member ko hi nominate karna padega. Friend ka nomination invalid ho jayega agar family exist karti hai.

**14. Are government employees covered under the Payment of Gratuity Act?**
**English:** Government employees are generally governed by separate service rules (like CCS Pension Rules) which often provide better benefits. However, if they are not covered by separate rules, this Act applies.
**Hindi:** सरकारी कर्मचारी आम तौर पर अलग-अलग सेवा नियमों द्वारा शासित होते हैं जो अक्सर बेहतर लाभ प्रदान करते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Govt employees ke apne alag service rules hote hain (jaise CCS rules) jo better gratuity dete hain.

**15. Does a 1-month gap between two roles in the same company break continuous service?**
**English:** If you formally resigned, received Full & Final settlement, and rejoined after a month, it is a break in service. If it was authorized leave without pay, it is not a break.
**Hindi:** यदि आपने औपचारिक रूप से इस्तीफा दे दिया और एक महीने बाद फिर से जुड़ गए, तो यह सेवा में विराम है।
**Hinglish:** Agar aapne properly resign karke settlement le liya tha aur 1 mahine baad re-join kiya, toh purani service count nahi hogi. Yeh break in service hai.

**16. I was illegally terminated without notice. Will I lose my gratuity?**
**English:** No. Illegal termination does not forfeit your statutory right to gratuity. You are entitled to the gratuity for the years you worked.
**Hindi:** नहीं। अवैध समाप्ति आपके ग्रेच्युटी के वैधानिक अधिकार को समाप्त नहीं करती है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, illegal termination aapki gratuity forfeit nahi kar sakta. Aapne jitne saal kaam kiya hai, uska paisa aapko milega.

**17. Do start-ups have to pay gratuity?**
**English:** Yes. The nature of the business does not matter. Any establishment (including startups) with 10 or more employees is legally bound by the Payment of Gratuity Act.
**Hindi:** हाँ। व्यवसाय की प्रकृति कोई मायने नहीं रखती। 10 या अधिक कर्मचारियों वाला कोई भी स्टार्टअप अधिनियम द्वारा बाध्य है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, start-ups ko bhi gratuity deni hoti hai agar unke paas 10 ya usse zyada employees hain.

**18. How is the 15-day wages calculated for piece-rate workers?**
**English:** For piece-rate workers, the 15 days' wages are computed based on the average of the total wages received by them over the 3 months immediately preceding the termination.
**Hindi:** पीस-रेट श्रमिकों के लिए, 15 दिनों के वेतन की गणना समाप्ति से ठीक पहले के 3 महीनों में प्राप्त कुल वेतन के औसत के आधार पर की जाती है।
**Hinglish:** Piece-rate workers ke liye last 3 mahine ki average salary ke basis par 15 din ka paisa calculate hota hai.

**19. What happens to my gratuity if I pass away while employed?**
**English:** The 5-year continuous service rule is waived. Your employer must pay the calculated gratuity based on your tenure up to the date of death to your nominee (Form F) or legal heir.
**Hindi:** 5 साल की निरंतर सेवा का नियम माफ कर दिया गया है। नियोक्ता को आपके नामित व्यक्ति को ग्रेच्युटी का भुगतान करना होगा।
**Hinglish:** 5 saal ka rule maaf ho jata hai. Aapne jitne time kaam kiya, uske hisaab se calculate karke paisa aapke nominee ko de diya jayega.

**20. Is there a time limit (limitation period) to claim unpaid gratuity?**
**English:** The Act states you should apply within 30 days. However, the Supreme Court has repeatedly held that a delay in applying does not invalidate the claim, as it is a statutory right. Delaying for years, however, complicates evidence gathering.
**Hindi:** हालांकि अधिनियम 30 दिनों के भीतर आवेदन करने के लिए कहता है, सर्वोच्च न्यायालय ने माना है कि देरी से दावा अमान्य नहीं होता है।
**Hinglish:** Waise toh 30 din mein apply karna chahiye, par Supreme Court ne clear kiya hai ki der se apply karne par bhi aapka statutory right khatam nahi hota.

## Common Myths vs Facts

*   **Myth 1:** Gratuity is deducted from my monthly salary.
    *   **Fact:** Gratuity is completely paid by the employer out of their own funds. It is not deducted from your monthly gross salary (unlike PF). If your CTC structure shows gratuity as a deduction, it is merely an accounting representation of the employer's liability, not an actual deduction from your cash-in-hand.
*   **Myth 2:** If I am fired, I don't get gratuity.
    *   **Fact:** Termination does not strip you of your gratuity rights unless the termination was specifically for moral turpitude, violence, or causing wilful damage to company property.
*   **Myth 3:** I have to work exactly 5 years, down to the last day.
    *   **Fact:** Under specific conditions, working 4 years and 240 days (in the fifth year) may be considered as 5 years of continuous service under the Act, though this often requires legal assertion.
*   **Myth 4:** The HR did not give me Form I, so I can't claim gratuity.
    *   **Fact:** You do not need HR to give you the form. You can download Form I, fill it, and send it to the employer via registered post or email.
*   **Myth 5:** Gratuity is completely tax-free.
    *   **Fact:** It is tax-free only up to a cumulative lifetime limit of Rs. 20 Lakhs. Any amount received above this across all your employers combined will be taxed according to your income tax slab.
*   **Myth 6:** Startups and small companies are exempt from paying gratuity.
    *   **Fact:** Any organization with 10 or more employees falls under the Act. There is no exemption for startups.
*   **Myth 7:** Only basic salary is used for calculation; DA is excluded.
    *   **Fact:** The calculation strictly uses Basic Salary PLUS Dearness Allowance (DA).
*   **Myth 8:** If I quit without serving notice, the employer can seize my entire gratuity.
    *   **Fact:** They can only adjust legally valid damages or notice pay defaults, and cannot arbitrarily seize the entire statutory gratuity without establishing the exact monetary loss.
*   **Myth 9:** A nominee has full ownership of the gratuity money after an employee's death.
    *   **Fact:** A nominee merely receives the money in trust. If there are other legal heirs under succession laws, the nominee is legally bound to distribute it among them unless the nominee is the sole legal heir.
*   **Myth 10:** I can claim gratuity while still working.
    *   **Fact:** Gratuity is only payable upon termination of employment (resignation, retirement, superannuation, death, or disablement). You cannot withdraw it while actively employed.

## Government Services
*   **Shram Suvidha Portal:** A central portal by the Ministry of Labour & Employment where employees can check compliance and lodge grievances regarding labour law violations.
*   **Samadhan Portal:** The e-dispute portal for industrial disputes (handled by the Chief Labour Commissioner Central).
*   **State Labour Department Portals:** Every state (e.g., Maharashtra Kamgar, e-Labour Punjab) has its own portal for downloading forms (Form I, Form N) and sometimes e-filing claims before the Controlling Authority.
*   **NALSA (National Legal Services Authority):** Provides free legal aid and counsel for industrial workers earning below certain thresholds to fight their gratuity claims in Labour Courts.
