# Salary and Wage Rights under Indian Labour Law

## Overview
**English:** In India, an employee's right to receive their salary and wages on time and without unauthorized deductions is heavily protected under various labour laws, primarily consolidated now under the Code on Wages, 2019. This framework guarantees minimum wages, ensures timely payment, regulates permissible deductions, and provides legal mechanisms for employees to recover unpaid dues from employers.

## Definition
**English:** "Wages" or "Salary" refers to all remuneration capable of being expressed in terms of money, which would, if the terms of employment, express or implied, were fulfilled, be payable to a person employed in respect of his employment or of work done in such employment, and includes basic pay, dearness allowance, and retaining allowance, but does not include bonuses, house rent allowance (HRA), PF contributions, or gratuity, unless specified.
**Hindi Unicode:** "मजदूरी" या "वेतन" से तात्पर्य धन के रूप में व्यक्त किए जा सकने वाले सभी पारिश्रमिक से है, जो रोजगार के संबंध में किसी नियोजित व्यक्ति को देय होता है, जिसमें मूल वेतन (basic pay), महंगाई भत्ता (dearness allowance) शामिल है, लेकिन बोनस, मकान किराया भत्ता (HRA), PF या ग्रेच्युटी शामिल नहीं है।
**Hinglish:** "Wages" ya "Salary" ka matlab hai woh sabhi remuneration jo paiso mein express ki ja sake, jo employment ke terms fulfill hone par employee ko pay ki jati hai. Isme basic pay, dearness allowance include hota hai, par bonus, HRA, PF contribution aur gratuity include nahi hoti jab tak specifically mentioned na ho.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Timely payment of wages is fundamental to a person's livelihood and dignity. The legal protections ensure that employers do not exploit workers by withholding pay, forcing them into bonded labour, or making arbitrary deductions for operational losses.
**Hindi Unicode:** मजदूरी का समय पर भुगतान व्यक्ति की आजीविका और सम्मान के लिए मौलिक है। कानूनी सुरक्षा यह सुनिश्चित करती है कि नियोक्ता वेतन रोककर, बंधुआ मजदूरी के लिए मजबूर करके, या नुकसान के लिए मनमानी कटौती करके श्रमिकों का शोषण न करें।
**Hinglish:** Salary ka timely payment ek insaan ki livelihood aur dignity ke liye zaruri hai. Legal protections ensure karti hain ki employer salary rok kar ya arbitrary deductions karke workers ka exploitation na karein.

## Legal Background
**History:** The Payment of Wages Act was first enacted in 1936 to regulate the payment of wages to certain classes of employed persons. The Minimum Wages Act followed in 1948 to prevent exploitation of unorganized labour. In 2019, the Code on Wages amalgamated these and other acts into a unified code.
**Purpose:** To standardize minimum wages across sectors, ensure timely payment, cap deductions at 50% of total wages, and provide a robust grievance redressal mechanism.
**Applicable Acts:**
- Code on Wages, 2019 (Amalgamating Payment of Wages Act, 1936; Minimum Wages Act, 1948; Payment of Bonus Act, 1965; Equal Remuneration Act, 1976)
- Industrial Disputes Act, 1947
- Specific Relief Act, 1963 (for recovery suits)
- Indian Penal Code (BNS, 2023) / Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (for criminal breach of trust regarding PF deductions)
**Relevant Constitutional Articles:**
- Article 21 (Right to Life and Livelihood)
- Article 23 (Prohibition of forced labour)
- Article 39(d) (Equal pay for equal work for both men and women)
- Article 43 (Living wage, etc., for workers)
**Relevant Sections:**
- Section 15 of the Code on Wages, 2019 (Fixation of wage period)
- Section 17 of the Code on Wages, 2019 (Time of payment of wages)
- Section 18 of the Code on Wages, 2019 (Deductions which may be made from wages)
- Section 33B of the Industrial Disputes Act (Recovery of money due from an employer)

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** All employees, whether in the organized or unorganized sector, daily wagers, contract workers, corporate employees, factory workers, and gig workers (to the extent defined under specific state rules).
- **Who is exempt:** Independent contractors and freelancers are generally governed by the Indian Contract Act, 1872, not labour laws, unless they can prove an employer-employee relationship.
- **Special conditions:** Managerial and administrative staff might not fall under the definition of "workman" under the Industrial Disputes Act, but they are still covered under the Code on Wages for basic wage protections.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:** Procedure to Recover Unpaid Salary:
1. **Send a Formal Communication:** Email the HR and management regarding the unpaid salary. Keep records.
2. **Legal Notice:** If there is no response within 15 days, hire a lawyer to send a formal legal notice under the Industrial Disputes Act or Companies Act (if applicable) demanding payment within 15-30 days.
3. **Approach the Labour Commissioner:** For workmen, file a complaint with the local Labour Commissioner under Section 33(C) of the Industrial Disputes Act.
4. **File a Civil Suit:** If you are a manager/executive, file a summary suit under Order 37 of the Civil Procedure Code (CPC) for recovery of money in a civil court.
5. **NCLT (Insolvency):** If the company owes you more than Rs. 1 Crore, you can initiate Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP) under the IBC as an operational creditor.
**Hindi Unicode:** बकाया वेतन वसूलने की प्रक्रिया:
1. **औपचारिक संचार:** बकाया वेतन के संबंध में एचआर को ईमेल करें और रिकॉर्ड रखें।
2. **कानूनी नोटिस:** यदि 15 दिनों में कोई प्रतिक्रिया नहीं मिलती है, तो वकील के माध्यम से कानूनी नोटिस भेजें।
3. **श्रम आयुक्त:** 'कर्मचारी' (Workman) स्थानीय श्रम आयुक्त के पास शिकायत दर्ज करा सकते हैं।
4. **दीवानी मुकदमा:** मैनेजर/एग्जीक्यूटिव पैसे की वसूली के लिए सिविल कोर्ट में CPC के आदेश 37 के तहत मुकदमा दायर कर सकते हैं।

## Required Documents
- Employment Contract / Appointment Letter
- Salary Slips (previous months)
- Bank Statements showing previous salary credits
- Resignation Letter and Acceptance (if applicable)
- Emails/Communications demanding the unpaid salary
- Form 16 or TDS certificates
- Attendance records / ID Card

## Government Authorities
- **Labour Inspector / Labour Commissioner:** First point of contact for wage disputes for workmen.
- **Labour Court / Industrial Tribunal:** Adjudicates disputes referred by the Labour Commissioner.
- **Civil Courts:** For recovery suits by non-workmen (executives, managers).
- **National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT):** For operational debt recovery (amount > Rs. 1 Crore) under IBC.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Delayed Payment:** Employers can be directed to pay the unpaid wages along with compensation up to 10 times the amount of the unpaid wages.
- **Minimum Wage Violation:** Fine up to Rs. 50,000. Second offence within 5 years: Imprisonment up to 3 months or fine up to Rs. 1 Lakh, or both (Code on Wages, 2019).
- **PF/ESI Non-deposit:** Deducting PF/ESI from employee salary but not depositing it with the government is a criminal breach of trust under BNS/IPC, punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years.

## Exceptions
- **Authorized Deductions:** Employers CAN legally deduct income tax (TDS), PF, ESI, professional tax, court-ordered attachments, and advance salary adjustments. Total deductions cannot exceed 50% of the gross wages.
- **Notice Period Deficit:** If an employee leaves without serving the mandatory notice period agreed upon in the contract, the employer can deduct the salary for the unserved period as "notice pay".
- **Disciplinary Action:** Suspended employees are entitled to a Subsistence Allowance, not full salary, pending departmental inquiry.

## Important Timelines
- **Wage Payment Time:** Before the 7th of the month (if < 1000 employees) or 10th (if > 1000 employees).
- **Final Settlement after Resignation/Termination:** Within 2 working days of removal/dismissal, or by the next normal pay cycle for resignations.
- **Limitation Period for Filing Suit:** Within 3 years from the date the salary became due, under the Limitation Act, 1963.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Not having a written contract:** Working on verbal promises makes it very hard to prove the agreed salary in court.
- **Not protesting deductions in writing:** If an employer deducts money and you don't object via email immediately, it may be construed as implied consent.
- **Delaying legal action:** Waiting for years on false promises of the employer until the 3-year limitation period expires.
- **Absconding:** Leaving the job without notice or handover gives the employer a legal ground to withhold the final settlement.

Hinglish: Nahi, employer salary delay nahi kar sakta. Law ke hisaab se next month ki 7th ya 10th date tak payment karna mandatory hai.

**12. I resigned but my employer is not accepting my resignation. Can they hold my salary?**
English: Resignation is a unilateral act. Once communicated, the notice period begins. The employer cannot force you to work or hold your salary for past work legally.
Hindi: इस्तीफा एकतरफा कार्य है। एक बार सूचित करने के बाद, नोटिस अवधि शुरू हो जाती है। नियोक्ता कानूनी रूप से आपका वेतन नहीं रोक सकता।
Hinglish: Resignation unilateral hota hai. Inform karne ke baad notice period start ho jata hai. Employer aapko zabardasti rok nahi sakta ya salary hold nahi kar sakta.

**13. Do daily wage labourers have the right to a minimum wage?**
English: Yes, daily wagers are strictly protected under the Minimum Wages Act and must be paid the state-mandated minimum daily wage.
Hindi: हां, दिहाड़ी मजदूरों को न्यूनतम मजदूरी अधिनियम के तहत सख्ती से संरक्षित किया जाता है और उन्हें राज्य द्वारा अनिवार्य न्यूनतम दैनिक मजदूरी का भुगतान किया जाना चाहिए।
Hinglish: Haan, daily wage workers ko Minimum Wages Act ke under strict protection milti hai aur unhe state-mandated minimum wage milna hi chahiye.

**14. What if the company files for bankruptcy while owing me 6 months' salary?**
English: Employees are considered operational creditors under the IBC. Your dues will be settled as per the priority waterfall mechanism in Section 53 of the IBC, though you may not recover 100%.
Hindi: कर्मचारियों को IBC के तहत ऑपरेशनल क्रेडिटर माना जाता है। आपके बकाये का निपटान IBC की धारा 53 के अनुसार किया जाएगा।
Hinglish: Bankruptcy ke case mein employees operational creditors hote hain. Aapko IBC ke rules ke hisaab se payment milegi, par shayad 100% na mile.

**15. Can my employer force me to open an account in a specific bank for my salary?**
English: An employer can request you to open a salary account in a partner bank for administrative ease, but they cannot withhold your salary if you refuse.
Hindi: एक नियोक्ता प्रशासनिक आसानी के लिए आपसे किसी भागीदार बैंक में वेतन खाता खोलने का अनुरोध कर सकता है, लेकिन यदि आप मना करते हैं तो वे आपका वेतन रोक नहीं सकते।
Hinglish: Employer administrative ease ke liye partner bank mein account open karne ko bol sakta hai, par deny karne par salary hold nahi kar sakta.

**16. I am a gig worker (Swiggy/Zomato). Am I entitled to minimum wages?**
English: Gig workers are currently classified as independent partners in India, not employees. Thus, strict minimum wage laws do not currently apply, though the Social Security Code 2020 introduces some benefits.
Hindi: गिग वर्कर्स को भारत में स्वतंत्र भागीदार (independent partners) माना जाता है, कर्मचारी नहीं। इसलिए न्यूनतम मजदूरी कानून लागू नहीं होते।
Hinglish: Gig workers ko employees nahi mana jata, isliye minimum wage laws un par strictly apply nahi hote.

**17. How is Gratuity calculated? Is it part of my monthly salary?**
English: Gratuity is a statutory benefit paid after 5 years of continuous service, calculated as (15 * Last Drawn Basic Salary * Years of Service) / 26. It is not paid in monthly salary but is part of your CTC.
Hindi: ग्रेच्युटी 5 साल की निरंतर सेवा के बाद दिया जाने वाला एक वैधानिक लाभ है। यह मासिक वेतन में नहीं दिया जाता है बल्कि आपके सीटीसी का हिस्सा है।
Hinglish: Gratuity 5 saal lagatar kaam karne ke baad milti hai. Yeh monthly salary ka part nahi hai, par aapke CTC mein dikhayi jati hai.

**18. Are freelancers protected under the Code on Wages?**
English: No, freelancers work on a principal-to-principal basis. They must rely on the Indian Contract Act or MSME Samadhaan (if registered) to recover dues.
Hindi: नहीं, फ्रीलांसर प्रिंसिपल-टू-प्रिंसिपल आधार पर काम करते हैं। उन्हें अपना बकाया वसूलने के लिए भारतीय अनुबंध अधिनियम या MSME समाधान पर निर्भर रहना होगा।
Hinglish: Nahi, freelancers par labour laws apply nahi hote. Unhe apne dues recover karne ke liye Contract Act ya MSME Samadhaan ka use karna padega.

**19. What should I do if my salary cheque bounces?**
English: If an employer issues a salary cheque that bounces, you can file a criminal case under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act within 30 days of the bounce notice.
Hindi: यदि वेतन का चेक बाउंस हो जाता है, तो आप बाउंस नोटिस के 30 दिनों के भीतर नेगोशिएबल इंस्ट्रूमेंट्स एक्ट की धारा 138 के तहत आपराधिक मामला दर्ज कर सकते हैं।
Hinglish: Agar salary cheque bounce ho jaye, toh aap 30 din ke andar Negotiable Instruments Act ke Section 138 ke under criminal case file kar sakte hain.

**20. Can a female employee be paid less than a male employee for the same work?**
English: Absolutely not. The Equal Remuneration Act (now part of the Code on Wages) strictly prohibits gender-based wage discrimination for the same or similar nature of work.
Hindi: बिल्कुल नहीं। समान पारिश्रमिक अधिनियम (अब मजदूरी संहिता का हिस्सा) समान कार्य के लिए लिंग आधारित वेतन भेदभाव को सख्ती से रोकता है।
Hinglish: Bilkul nahi. Equal Remuneration law ke under same work ke liye men aur women ko alag-alag salary dena illegal hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Employers can hold the first month's salary as a security deposit.
   **Fact:** This is absolutely illegal. Salary must be paid within the stipulated wage period.
2. **Myth:** If you are on probation, the company doesn't have to pay you.
   **Fact:** Probationers are fully entitled to their contracted wages.
3. **Myth:** Unpaid salary cannot be claimed after leaving the company.
   **Fact:** You have up to 3 years under the Limitation Act to file a legal suit for unpaid wages.
4. **Myth:** The employer can deduct my entire salary if I cause a loss.
   **Fact:** Total deductions cannot exceed 50% of your gross salary under any circumstances.
5. **Myth:** HR can refuse to accept my resignation.
   **Fact:** Resignation is a right. Simply emailing it serves as formal notice; acceptance is not legally required to start the notice period.
6. **Myth:** Labour Courts are only for blue-collar factory workers.
   **Fact:** Many white-collar workers (non-managerial) qualify as 'workmen' under the ID Act and can use Labour Courts.
7. **Myth:** Companies don't have to pay minimum wages if they are in financial loss.
   **Fact:** Minimum wage payment is an absolute statutory obligation, regardless of profit or loss.
8. **Myth:** Interns have the exact same wage rights as full-time employees.
   **Fact:** Interns generally do not fall under the definition of employees for minimum wage protection.
9. **Myth:** You cannot file a case for unpaid salary if you don't have a written contract.
   **Fact:** You can still file a case using emails, ID cards, bank statements, and witness testimonies as proof of employment.
10. **Myth:** Only the government can take action against EPF fraud by employers.
    **Fact:** Employees can directly file police complaints for criminal breach of trust regarding PF deductions.

## Conversation Examples

**Example 1: Delayed Salary**
*Citizen:* Sir, meri company 3 mahine se salary nahi de rahi, bol rahe hain funding aayegi tab denge.
*Assistant:* Company ka funding delay aapki salary rokne ka legal ground nahi hai. Aap pehle HR ko formal email likhiye. Agar 15 din mein jawab na aaye, toh lawyer ke through legal notice bhejein. Agar aap workman category mein aate hain, toh aap Labour Commissioner ke paas complaint bhi file kar sakte hain.

**Example 2: Illegal Deductions**
*Citizen:* My boss deducted Rs 10,000 from my salary because he said my performance was poor. Is this allowed?
*Assistant:* No, this is illegal. Under the Code on Wages, employers cannot deduct money for 'poor performance' arbitrarily. Deductions are strictly regulated (like PF, TDS). You should formally object via email and demand the balance amount.

**Example 3: Notice Period Pay**
*Citizen:* Maine ek din mein resign kar diya kyunki boss abuse karta tha. Ab wo meri pichle mahine ki salary notice pay mein kaat rahe hain.
*Assistant:* Agar contract mein notice period mandatory tha aur aapne serve nahi kiya, toh employer normally notice pay deduct kar sakta hai. But agar aapke paas workplace harassment ka proof hai, toh aap Labour Court mein constructive dismissal ka case kar sakte hain.

**Example 4: Full and Final Settlement**
*Citizen:* It's been 60 days since I left my job, but they haven't processed my Full and Final (FnF) settlement.
*Assistant:* By law, wages should be cleared within two working days of termination, or usually the next pay cycle for resignations. Send a strong email to HR citing the Payment of Wages guidelines. If they ignore, issue a legal notice.

**Example 5: PF Not Deposited**
*Citizen:* Meri salary slip mein PF deduct hota hai par EPFO portal pe update nahi hua pichle 1 saal se.
*Assistant:* Yeh criminal breach of trust (IPC 406/409) hai. Aap EPFO ke grievance portal (EPFiGMS) par complaint darj karein. Aap local police station mein FIR bhi file karwa sakte hain company ke directors ke khilaaf.

**Example 6: Intern Stipend**
*Citizen:* I completed a 3-month internship but they are refusing to pay the promised stipend of 5k per month.
*Assistant:* While standard labour laws might not cover interns, you have a valid contract. You can send a legal notice for breach of contract, or file a complaint on the national consumer/civil grievance portals if it was part of an educational arrangement.

**Example 7: Minimum Wage Violation**
*Citizen:* Mai ek factory mein kaam karta hu, mujhe din ka 200 rupaye milta hai. Kya yeh theek hai?
*Assistant:* Nahi, yeh gair kanooni ho sakta hai. Har rajya (state) ka ek minimum daily wage fix hota hai, jo aam taur par 200 rupaye se zyada hota hai. Aap apne area ke Labour Inspector se iski shikayat kar sakte hain, wo factory malik par fine laga sakte hain.

**Example 8: Cheque Bounce**
*Citizen:* They gave my final settlement via cheque but it bounced due to insufficient funds.
*Assistant:* You must send a legal notice under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act within 30 days of receiving the cheque bounce memo from your bank. If they don't pay within 15 days of the notice, you can file a criminal case.

**Example 9: Maternity Salary**
*Citizen:* I am on maternity leave but my company stopped my salary saying I am not working.
*Assistant:* This is illegal under the Maternity Benefit Act. Eligible women are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave. If they refuse, you can file a complaint with the Labour Commissioner immediately.

**Example 10: Withholding Certificates**
*Citizen:* Company meri 2 mahine ki salary bhi nahi de rahi aur experience letter bhi rok liya hai.
*Assistant:* Experience letter aur salary rokna unka tareeqa ho sakta hai pressure banane ka. Aap turant ek legal notice draft karwayein. Experience letter withhold karna unfair labour practice maana jata hai.

**Example 11: Fined for Late Arrival**
*Citizen:* Main traffic ki wajah se 15 minute late tha, company ne half day ka paisa kaat liya.
*Assistant:* Yeh kanoonan galat hai. Code on Wages ke anusar actual absence ka paisa kat sakta hai, par 15 minute late aane par half-day ki arbitrary penalty illegal hai. HR ko likhit mein complain karein.

**Example 12: No Written Contract**
*Citizen:* I worked for a startup for 4 months without a formal contract. Now they are not paying me.
*Assistant:* Even without a written contract, you can establish an employer-employee relationship using emails, Slack/WhatsApp messages, attendance logs, and any initial payments made to your bank. Collect all evidence and send a legal notice.

**Example 13: Forced Bank Account**
*Citizen:* Employer bol raha hai salary chahiye toh usi ke partner bank me naya account kholna padega.
*Assistant:* Employer administrative convenience ke liye request kar sakta hai, lekin yeh compulsory nahi hai. Wo kisi particular bank me account na kholne par aapki salary hold nahi kar sakte.

**Example 14: TDS Deducted but Not Deposited**
*Citizen:* Meri salary se tax (TDS) kata gaya, par mera Form 26AS update nahi hua.
*Assistant:* Yeh Income Tax Act ka violation hai. Aap Income Tax Department portal par grievance raise kar sakte hain. Employer par heavy penalty aur prosecution ho sakta hai.

**Example 15: Overtime Pay**
*Citizen:* Maine pichle hafte har din 12 ghante kaam kiya, par overtime ka paisa nahi diya HR ne.
*Assistant:* Factory Act aur Shops & Establishments Act ke tahat standard working hours se zyada kaam karne par double rate se overtime pay milna chahiye. Aap apne HR se likhit me claim karein.
