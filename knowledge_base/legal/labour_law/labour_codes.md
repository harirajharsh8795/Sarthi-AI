# New Labour Codes in India: Complete Guide

## Overview
**English:** The Government of India has consolidated 29 central labour laws into four comprehensive Labour Codes: The Code on Wages (2019), The Industrial Relations Code (2020), The Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code (2020), and The Code on Social Security (2020). These codes aim to simplify, modernize, and rationalize labour regulations, ensuring minimum wages, social security for all workers (including gig and platform workers), and a safer working environment while promoting ease of doing business.

Inka main motive labour laws ko simple banana aur har worker (chahe wo gig worker ho ya factory worker) ko minimum wage, social security aur safe working conditions provide karna hai.

## Definition
**English:** The Labour Codes are a set of four legislative acts passed by the Parliament of India that amalgamate, simplify, and rationalize the previous complex web of 29 central labour enactments, governing wages, industrial relations, social security, and occupational safety.
**Hindi (Unicode):** श्रम संहिताएं (Labour Codes) भारत की संसद द्वारा पारित चार विधायी अधिनियमों का एक समूह है, जो वेतन, औद्योगिक संबंधों, सामाजिक सुरक्षा और व्यावसायिक सुरक्षा को नियंत्रित करने वाले 29 केंद्रीय श्रम अधिनियमों के पिछले जटिल जाल को मिलाते हैं, सरल बनाते हैं और युक्तिसंगत बनाते हैं।

## Legal Background
**History:** The Second National Commission on Labour (2002) recommended consolidating central labour laws into broader groups to simplify the archaic and overlapping laws dating back to the pre-independence era.
**Purpose:** To enhance social security coverage, ensure decent working conditions, provide universal minimum wages, reduce litigation, and facilitate ease of doing business to generate employment.
**Applicable Acts:**
- The Code on Wages, 2019 (Repeals Payment of Wages Act, Minimum Wages Act, Payment of Bonus Act, Equal Remuneration Act).
- The Industrial Relations Code, 2020 (Repeals Trade Unions Act, Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, Industrial Disputes Act).
- The Code on Social Security, 2020 (Repeals EPF Act, ESI Act, Maternity Benefit Act, Payment of Gratuity Act, Unorganised Workers' Social Security Act, etc. - total 9 acts).
- The Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code (OSH), 2020 (Repeals Factories Act, Mines Act, Dock Workers Act, Building and Other Construction Workers Act, etc. - total 13 acts).
**Relevant Constitutional Articles:** Article 14 (Equality before law), Article 19(1)(c) (Right to form associations or unions), Article 21 (Right to life and personal liberty), Article 23 (Prohibition of forced labour), Article 24 (Prohibition of child labour), Article 39 (Equal pay for equal work), Article 41 (Right to work, education and public assistance), Article 42 (Provision for just and humane conditions of work and maternity relief), Article 43 (Living wage for workers).

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** All employees and workers in the organized and unorganized sectors, including contract workers, gig workers, platform workers, inter-state migrant workers, and fixed-term employees.
- **Who is exempt:** Certain provisions may exempt specific establishments based on the number of employees (e.g., OSH Code applies to manufacturing units with 20+ workers if power is used, or 40+ if power is not used; IR Code increases the threshold for prior government permission for lay-offs from 100 to 300 workers).
- **Special Conditions:** The definition of "wages" is standardized across all codes, mandating that basic pay must be at least 50% of total remuneration, affecting PF and Gratuity calculations.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English (Claiming Minimum Wages / Filing a Complaint):**
1. Document your employment (appointment letter, pay slips, attendance record).
2. If wages paid are below the minimum or delayed, submit a written complaint to the employer.
3. If unresolved, file a complaint with the Inspector-cum-Facilitator appointed under the Code on Wages.
4. The Inspector will facilitate a resolution or initiate an inquiry.
5. In case of non-compliance, the matter is forwarded to the competent Appellate Authority or Labour Court as specified by the state rules.

## Required Documents
- Aadhaar Card (mandatory for social security registration under Section 142 of Social Security Code).
- Appointment Letter (now mandatory for all employees).
- Salary Slips / Wage Register extracts.
- UAN (Universal Account Number) for EPF.
- e-Shram card (for unorganized and gig workers).
- Bank statements showing salary credits.

## Government Authorities
- **Inspector-cum-Facilitator:** Replacing the traditional 'Labour Inspector', focuses on advising employers on compliance before taking penal action.
- **Central Board of Trustees (CBT) & ESIC:** For administering provident fund and state insurance.
- **National / State Social Security Board:** Specifically constituted for the welfare of unorganized workers, gig workers, and platform workers.
- **Industrial Tribunal:** For resolving industrial disputes.
- **Appellate Authority:** For hearing appeals against the orders of compounding officers or lower authorities.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Non-payment of Minimum Wages:** Fine up to ₹50,000. Repeat offence within 5 years: Imprisonment up to 3 months and/or fine up to ₹1,00,000.
- **Failure to pay EPF/ESI contributions (Social Security Code):** Imprisonment of 1 to 3 years and fine of ₹1,00,000.
- **Contravention of Safety Provisions (OSH Code) leading to death:** Imprisonment up to 2 years or fine up to ₹5,00,000, or both. A portion of the fine may be given to the victim's family.
- **Illegal Strikes/Lockouts (IR Code):** Fine ranging from ₹1,000 to ₹10,000 or imprisonment up to 1 month.

## Exceptions
- The OSH Code does not apply to offices of the Central/State Government, except where explicitly notified.
- Micro and Small Enterprises may have relaxed compliance norms, such as filing a single unified annual return.
- Charitable institutions and specific NGOs may be exempted from certain provisions of the Industrial Relations Code based on government notification.

## Important Timelines
- **Wage Payment:** Wages must be paid within 2 working days in case of employee removal, dismissal, retrenchment, or resignation.
- **Notice for Strike:** A 14-day prior notice is mandatory for striking in *any* establishment (previously only for public utility services).
- **Gratuity:** Fixed-term employees get gratuity based on the actual period of service (pro-rata), without needing to complete the traditional 5-year period. Regular employees still need 5 years for gratuity.
- **Maternity Leave:** 26 weeks of paid leave under the Social Security Code.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Employers not restructuring salary components, failing to realize that basic pay must be at least 50% of the gross salary.
- Gig workers failing to register on the e-Shram or designated portals, missing out on state benefits.
- Employees resigning without serving the required notice period, assuming old rules apply.
- Not providing mandatory appointment letters, which is now a punishable offence under the OSH Code.
- Unions calling for flash strikes without giving the mandatory 14-day notice.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What are the 4 new Labour Codes?**
The new Labour Codes are: Code on Wages (2019), Industrial Relations Code (2020), Social Security Code (2020), and Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code (2020). They replace 29 older labour laws.
2. **When will the Labour Codes be implemented?**
As of now, the Codes have been passed by Parliament and received Presidential assent. Their actual implementation date depends on the Central and State governments notifying the rules.
3. **How does the new wage definition affect my take-home salary?**
Under the new Codes, allowances cannot exceed 50% of total wages. Therefore, Basic Pay must be at least 50%. This increases PF and Gratuity deductions, which may slightly reduce your monthly take-home pay but increases your retirement savings.
4. **Are gig workers covered under the new codes?**
Yes, for the first time, gig workers and platform workers (like food delivery agents) are legally defined and covered under the Code on Social Security for benefits like life and disability cover, health, and maternity benefits.
5. **Do I still have to wait 5 years for Gratuity?**
For regular employees, the 5-year rule remains. However, for fixed-term employees, gratuity will be paid on a pro-rata basis for the duration of their contract, even if it is less than 5 years.
6. **Can women work night shifts now?**
Yes, under the OSH Code, women are permitted to work in all establishments for all types of work, including night shifts (before 6 AM and after 7 PM), provided the employer ensures adequate safety, security, and consent.
7. **Is it harder to go on strike now?**
Yes, the IR Code mandates that a 14-day prior notice must be given for any strike across all industrial establishments, not just public utility services. Also, strikes are prohibited during pending conciliation proceedings.
8. **What is an 'Inspector-cum-Facilitator'?**
The old role of 'Labour Inspector' is modified to 'Inspector-cum-Facilitator'. They will now not only inspect but also advise employers on how to comply with the law before initiating punitive actions.
9. **What happens to my leaves under the new codes?**
Employees can now carry forward unused annual leave up to 30 days. Also, you become eligible for annual leave after working for 180 days in a year, reduced from the previous 240 days.
10. **Do employers need permission to fire employees?**
Under the new IR Code, companies with up to 300 workers can lay off, retrench, or close down without prior government permission. The earlier limit was 100 workers.
11. **Will minimum wage apply to all workers?**
Yes, the Code on Wages ensures a statutory national minimum wage (floor wage) across all employments in both organized and unorganized sectors, removing the restricted schedule of employments under the old Minimum Wages Act.
12. **What happens if my company delays my full and final settlement?**
The Code on Wages mandates that in cases of resignation, dismissal, or retrenchment, the employer must clear the full and final settlement within 2 working days.
13. **Is there any change in maternity leave?**
The core benefits of the Maternity Benefit Act are retained in the Social Security Code, offering 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two children.
14. **How do I register as an unorganized worker?**
Unorganized workers, including gig workers, can register on the government's e-Shram portal using their Aadhaar card and bank account details to access social security schemes.
15. **Can an employer deduct fines from my salary?**
Deductions can be made for specific reasons (like absence from duty, damage to property), but the total deductions in a wage period cannot exceed 50% of the wages.
16. **Are IT sector employees covered under these codes?**
Yes, the new codes apply to employees across all sectors, including IT and ITeS, governing their working hours, leaves, and social security.
17. **What is a Fixed-Term Employee?**
A fixed-term employee is hired for a specific duration. Under the new codes, they must receive the same hours of work, wages, allowances, and statutory benefits as a permanent employee doing the same work.
18. **Is an appointment letter mandatory?**
Yes, the OSH Code makes it a statutory obligation for employers to issue formal appointment letters to all employees to curb exploitation.
19. **What is the maximum working hours allowed?**
The daily working limit can be extended to 12 hours (with consent and appropriate breaks), but the weekly limit remains strictly 48 hours. Any work beyond this attracts overtime pay at twice the normal wage rate.
20. **Who manages the social security fund for gig workers?**
A dedicated National Social Security Board will be established to manage the fund for unorganized, gig, and platform workers, funded by contributions from aggregators (1-2% of their turnover) and government grants.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** My take-home salary will drastically reduce, and I will lose money.
   **Fact:** While take-home pay might slightly decrease due to higher PF deductions, the deducted amount goes into your own retirement corpus, increasing your long-term savings.
2. **Myth:** Gig workers are now regular employees of the aggregators.
   **Fact:** No, gig workers are recognized for social security benefits, but they are not classified as traditional "employees" on the payroll of the aggregators.
3. **Myth:** Strikes are completely banned under the new laws.
   **Fact:** Strikes are not banned, but the procedure is regulated. A mandatory 14-day notice is required before striking.
4. **Myth:** Employers can fire anyone without notice now.
   **Fact:** Due process, notice periods, and retrenchment compensation must still be followed. Only the threshold for government permission for mass lay-offs was increased to 300 workers.
5. **Myth:** The 12-hour workday means I have to work 72 hours a week.
   **Fact:** No, the maximum weekly working hours are capped at 48 hours. If you work 12 hours a day, you will work for 4 days and get 3 days off in a week.
6. **Myth:** Only factory workers get minimum wages.
   **Fact:** The Code on Wages extends minimum wage rights universally to all workers in all sectors.
7. **Myth:** Fixed-term employees do not get gratuity.
   **Fact:** Fixed-term employees are now eligible for pro-rata gratuity based on their contract duration, without waiting for 5 years.
8. **Myth:** Women cannot work in hazardous occupations.
   **Fact:** Women can work in all occupations and shifts, provided the employer implements adequate safety measures and obtains their consent.
9. **Myth:** I have to work 240 days to get paid annual leave.
   **Fact:** The threshold has been reduced to 180 days of work in a calendar year to qualify for paid annual leave.
10. **Myth:** Overtime is paid at the normal hourly rate.
    **Fact:** Overtime must be paid at exactly twice (2x) the normal rate of wages.

**Example 6: Night Shift for Women**
**Citizen:** Kya naye kanoon mein auraton ko night shift me kaam karne ki permission hai?
**Assistant:** Ji haan, OSH Code ke tahat mahilayein raat ki shift (sham 7 baje se subah 6 baje tak) me kaam kar sakti hain. Par employer ko unki safety, security, transport facilities aur unki written consent (sammati) leni anivarya hai.

**Example 7: Appointment Letter Issue**
**Citizen:** I got a job at a local manufacturing unit, but they refuse to give an appointment letter. Is this legal?
**Assistant:** No, it is illegal. The OSH Code mandates that every employer must provide a formal appointment letter to all employees. Failure to do so is a punishable offence.

**Example 8: Leave Policy Change**
**Citizen:** Meri company kehti hai ki chhutti lene ke liye 240 din kaam karna padega. Kya ye sahi hai?
**Assistant:** Naye niyam (OSH Code) ke mutabiq, ab aapko paid annual leave paane ke liye ek saal me kewal 180 din kaam karna hoga, na ki 240 din. Aap is naye kanoon ke baare me apni company ko bata sakte hain.

**Example 9: Strike Notice**
**Citizen:** Our union wants to go on an immediate strike tomorrow against the management. Can we?
**Assistant:** No. Under the Industrial Relations Code, it is now mandatory for workers in *any* industrial establishment to give a 14-day prior notice to the management before going on strike. An immediate flash strike would be considered illegal.

**Example 10: Salary Restructuring**
**Citizen:** My company restructured my salary and reduced my allowances, increasing basic pay to 50%. Why?
**Assistant:** They are complying with the new Code on Wages. The new law defines that allowances cannot exceed 50% of the total remuneration. If they do, the excess is treated as wages. To avoid compliance issues, companies are adjusting Basic Pay to be at least 50% of the gross salary.

**Example 11: Maternity Leave**
**Citizen:** Mujhe maternity leave kitni milni chahiye naye code ke hisaab se?
**Assistant:** Social Security Code ke tahat, aapko pehle do bacchon ke liye 26 hafte (weeks) ki paid maternity leave milne ka adhikar hai, jo purane kanoon ke jaisa hi barkarar rakha gaya hai.

**Example 12: E-Shram Portal**
**Citizen:** I am a carpenter working independently. How do I get government benefits?
**Assistant:** As an unorganized worker, you should register on the government's e-Shram portal using your Aadhaar card. This will issue you an e-Shram card with a 12-digit UAN, making you eligible for accident insurance and other social security schemes under the new Code.

**Example 13: Workplace Injury**
**Citizen:** Factory me kaam karte waqt mere bhai ka accident ho gaya. Employer medical bill nahi de raha.
**Assistant:** OSH Code aur Social Security Code dono employer par duty lagate hain ki wo safe working environment dein. Agar employee ESI me covered hai, toh sara medical kharcha ESI uthayega. Agar nahi, toh employer ko compensation dena padega. Aap Inspector-cum-Facilitator se iski shikayat kar sakte hain.

**Example 14: PF Deductions**
**Citizen:** Meri salary me se PF toh katta hai par account me jama nahi hota. Kya karu?
**Assistant:** Ye ek serious offence hai. Social Security Code ke tahat, agar employer employee ka hissa kaat kar EPF me jama nahi karta, toh use 1 se 3 saal tak ki jail aur ₹1,00,000 tak ka jurmana ho sakta hai. Aap EPFO portal ya grievance cell par turant iski shikayat darj karein.

**Example 15: Contract Worker Equality**
**Citizen:** Main as a contract worker kaam karta hu par regular employees ke comparison me mujhe bahut kam paise milte hain kaam same hone par bhi.
**Assistant:** Code on Wages ensure karta hai "Equal Pay for Equal Work". Agar aapka aur regular employee ka kaam ek jaisa hai, toh employer aapko kam wage nahi de sakta. Aap is bhedbhav ke khilaf Labour Inspector se appeal kar sakte hain.
