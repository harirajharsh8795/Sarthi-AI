# Filing a Police Complaint: Complete Legal Guide

## Overview

**English**: A police complaint is the first formal step to report a crime or an offense to the law enforcement authorities. It sets the criminal justice system in motion. In India, reporting a crime is both a right and a civic duty. The process is governed by the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023 (formerly CrPC). Depending on the severity of the crime (cognizable vs. non-cognizable), the police will register either a First Information Report (FIR) or a Non-Cognizable Report (NCR). The introduction of e-FIRs and Zero FIRs has made it easier for citizens to report crimes from anywhere.

**Hindi**: पुलिस शिकायत (Police Complaint) किसी अपराध या जुर्म की सूचना कानून प्रवर्तन अधिकारियों को देने का पहला औपचारिक कदम है। यह आपराधिक न्याय प्रणाली को गति प्रदान करता है। भारत में, अपराध की रिपोर्ट करना एक अधिकार और नागरिक कर्तव्य दोनों है। यह प्रक्रिया भारतीय नागरिक सुरक्षा संहिता (BNSS), 2023 (पूर्व में CrPC) द्वारा शासित होती है। अपराध की गंभीरता (संज्ञेय बनाम असंज्ञेय) के आधार पर, पुलिस प्रथम सूचना रिपोर्ट (FIR) या असंज्ञेय रिपोर्ट (NCR) दर्ज करेगी। ई-एफआईआर और जीरो एफआईआर की शुरुआत ने नागरिकों के लिए कहीं से भी अपराधों की रिपोर्ट करना आसान बना दिया है।

## Definition

**English**: A Police Complaint is an allegation made orally, in writing, or electronically to a police officer, with a view to his taking action under the BNSS, that some person, whether known or unknown, has committed an offense. It does not include a police report (which is the charge sheet filed after investigation).

## Legal Background

**History & Purpose**:
Historically, the procedure for registering complaints was governed by the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (CrPC). The purpose was to provide a structured mechanism for citizens to seek state intervention against criminal acts. With the introduction of the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) in 2023, the procedure has been modernized to include electronic complaints, time-bound investigations, and stricter accountability.

**Applicable Acts**:
1. Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023 (Replaced CrPC)
2. Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023 (Replaced IPC)
3. Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023 (Replaced Indian Evidence Act)
4. Information Technology Act, 2000 (For cybercrimes)

**Relevant Constitutional Articles**:
- **Article 14**: Right to Equality before the law (ensuring police cannot refuse to file a complaint based on discrimination).
- **Article 21**: Right to Life and Personal Liberty (protecting the victim's right to seek justice and the accused's right to fair procedure).

**Relevant Sections under BNSS 2023**:
- **Section 173 BNSS (Formerly Section 154 CrPC)**: Information in cognizable cases (Registration of FIR). It mandates that information regarding a cognizable offense must be recorded by the police. It also introduces the provision for electronic FIR (e-FIR), which must be signed within 3 days.
- **Section 174 BNSS (Formerly Section 155 CrPC)**: Information as to non-cognizable cases (NCR) and investigation of such cases.
- **Section 175 BNSS (Formerly Section 156 CrPC)**: Police officer's power to investigate a cognizable case without an order of a Magistrate.
- **Section 175(3) BNSS (Formerly Section 156(3) CrPC)**: Power of Magistrate to order an investigation if police refuse to register an FIR.

## Applicability

**Who is covered**:
- Any citizen of India or foreigner present in India who is a victim of a crime.
- Any person who has witnessed a crime.
- Any person who has knowledge of a crime being committed.

**Who is exempt**:
- Foreign diplomats (enjoy diplomatic immunity from prosecution, though complaints can be filed *against* their actions, prosecution requires central government intervention).
- President and Governors (during their term of office, no criminal proceedings can be instituted against them under Article 361 of the Constitution).

**Special Conditions**:
- Complaints against police officers or public servants for acts done in the discharge of official duties often require prior sanction from the government under Section 218 BNSS (formerly 197 CrPC).
- Crimes against women (e.g., rape, sexual harassment) must be recorded by a woman police officer or any woman officer under Section 173(1) Proviso BNSS.

## Rights of Citizens

**English**:
1. **Right to Register Zero FIR**: You can file an FIR at any police station, irrespective of where the crime occurred. It will be transferred to the correct jurisdiction later.
2. **Right to Free Copy**: You are entitled to a free copy of the FIR immediately after it is registered.
3. **Right against Refusal**: The police cannot refuse to register an FIR for a cognizable offense. Refusal is punishable.
4. **Right to Inform via Electronic Means**: You can send information electronically (e-FIR), but you must physically sign it within 3 days.
5. **Right to Read Before Signing**: You have the right to read the drafted complaint and ensure it accurately reflects your statement before signing.

## Duties & Responsibilities

**English**:
1. **Duty to Report**: Citizens are legally bound to report certain serious offenses (like murder, robbery, treason) to the police under Section 33 BNSS (formerly Section 39 CrPC).
2. **Duty of Truthfulness**: It is a punishable offense under BNS to provide false information to the police to cause harassment to someone.
3. **Cooperation**: The complainant must cooperate with the investigating officer by providing facts, evidence, and appearing when summoned.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English**:
**Step 1: Identify the nature of the crime** (Cognizable vs Non-Cognizable).
**Step 2: Approach the Police Station / Portal**. Visit the nearest police station or use the state's online citizen portal for e-FIR.
**Step 3: Narrate the incident**. Provide a written complaint or narrate it orally to the officer in charge, who will reduce it to writing.
**Step 4: Verification**. Read the written complaint carefully. Ensure dates, times, and names are correct.
**Step 5: Sign the complaint**.
**Step 6: Receive Receipt/Copy**. Obtain a stamped copy of the complaint or a free copy of the FIR.
**Step 7: Follow up**. Note the FIR/NCR number and the name of the Investigating Officer (IO) for future updates.
**Alternative (If Police Refuse)**: Send the complaint in writing via registered post to the Superintendent of Police (SP) or Deputy Commissioner of Police (DCP). If still no action, file an application before the Judicial Magistrate under Section 175(3) BNSS.

## Required Documents
1. A written draft of the complaint (optional, but recommended).
2. Valid Identity Proof (Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Passport, etc. - to establish complainant's identity).
3. Any evidence related to the crime (photographs, CCTV footage, medical reports, screenshots of chats, financial statements in case of fraud).
4. For cybercrimes, transaction details and bank statements are crucial.

## Government Authorities
1. **Police Station (SHO - Station House Officer)**: Primary authority to receive and register complaints.
2. **Superintendent of Police (SP) / Commissioner of Police (CP)**: Appellate authority if the SHO refuses to register an FIR.
3. **Judicial Magistrate First Class (JMFC)**: Authority to order an investigation if police refuse to act.
4. **National/State Women's Commission**: For complaints related to crimes against women.
5. **Cyber Crime Cells**: Specialized units for handling internet-based crimes.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Filing a False Complaint**: Under Section 217 of BNS (False charge of offense made with intent to injure), a person can be imprisoned for up to 7 years and fined, depending on the severity of the false charge.
- **Police Refusing to Register FIR**: Under Section 199 BNS (Public servant disobeying direction of law with intent to cause injury), a police officer can be imprisoned for up to 1 year, or fine, or both. For refusing FIR in crimes against women, rigorous imprisonment up to 1 year is mandated.

## Exceptions
- **Non-Cognizable Offenses**: Police cannot register an FIR or investigate without a Magistrate's order. They will only record an NCR and direct the complainant to the court.
- **Civil Disputes**: Matters purely civil in nature (like simple breach of contract without criminal intent) cannot be the subject of a police complaint; they belong in civil courts.
- **Delay in FIR**: While an FIR should be filed promptly, an unreasonable, unexplained delay can be fatal to the prosecution's case. However, delay in cases of sexual assault is often viewed with leniency by courts if a valid reason is provided.

## Important Timelines
- **Filing Complaint**: As soon as possible after the incident. No strict statutory limitation for filing FIRs in serious crimes, but delay must be explained.
- **Limitation for Cognizable Offenses**: Under Section 505 BNSS (formerly 468 CrPC), court cannot take cognizance of an offense after:
  - 6 months, if the offense is punishable with a fine only.
  - 1 year, if the offense is punishable with imprisonment up to 1 year.
  - 3 years, if the offense is punishable with imprisonment between 1-3 years.
- **e-FIR Signature**: Must be physically signed by the complainant within **3 days** of electronic submission, otherwise, it may not be processed.
- **Investigation Report**: Police must file the charge sheet (police report) ideally within 60 or 90 days (depending on the crime severity) to prevent the accused from getting default bail. For rape cases, investigation must be completed within 2 months.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. **Delaying the filing**: Waiting too long to file a complaint without a valid reason creates doubt about its authenticity.
2. **Exaggerating facts**: Adding false details or exaggerating the incident can ruin the entire case during cross-examination in court.
3. **Not taking a copy**: Leaving the police station without a stamped receipt or a free copy of the FIR/NCR.
4. **Filing in wrong jurisdiction and refusing Zero FIR**: Thinking you can't file if you are away from the crime scene. Always insist on a Zero FIR if needed.
5. **Signing without reading**: Blindly signing the complaint drafted by the police without verifying the facts written.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What is the difference between FIR and NCR?**
**English**: An FIR is registered for cognizable offenses (serious crimes like murder, theft) where police can arrest without a warrant. An NCR is for non-cognizable offenses (like simple hurt, verbal abuse) where police need a magistrate's order to investigate.
**Hindi**: FIR संज्ञेय अपराधों के लिए दर्ज की जाती है जहां पुलिस बिना वारंट के गिरफ्तार कर सकती है। NCR असंज्ञेय अपराधों के लिए है जहां जांच के लिए मजिस्ट्रेट के आदेश की आवश्यकता होती है।
**Hinglish**: FIR cognizable offenses ke liye hoti hai jahan police bina warrant arrest kar sakti hai. NCR non-cognizable ke liye hoti hai jahan magistrate ka order chahiye.

**Q2: Can the police refuse to file my FIR?**
**English**: No, police are legally bound to register an FIR if the complaint discloses a cognizable offense. If they refuse, you can approach the SP or a Magistrate.
**Hindi**: नहीं, यदि शिकायत संज्ञेय अपराध का खुलासा करती है तो पुलिस एफआईआर दर्ज करने के लिए बाध्य है।
**Hinglish**: Nahi, agar complaint mein cognizable offense hai toh police ko FIR file karni hi hogi.

**Q3: What should I do if the local police station refuses my complaint?**
**English**: You should send the substance of the information in writing and by post to the Superintendent of Police (SP) concerned. If the SP is satisfied that a cognizable offense is disclosed, they will investigate or direct an officer to do so.
**Hindi**: आपको पुलिस अधीक्षक (एसपी) को लिखित रूप में और डाक द्वारा जानकारी भेजनी चाहिए।
**Hinglish**: Aapko SP ko registered post se complaint bhejni chahiye.

**Q4: Is there a fee for filing a police complaint?**
**English**: No, filing a police complaint, FIR, or NCR is completely free of cost.
**Hindi**: नहीं, पुलिस शिकायत, एफआईआर या एनसीआर दर्ज करना बिल्कुल मुफ्त है।
**Hinglish**: Nahi, police complaint ya FIR file karna bilkul free hai.

**Q5: Can I file an FIR online?**
**English**: Yes, for certain offenses like theft of vehicles or lost items, many states provide an e-FIR facility. Under BNSS, e-FIR is recognized for cognizable offenses but requires physical signature within 3 days.
**Hindi**: हां, कई राज्य ई-एफआईआर की सुविधा प्रदान करते हैं।
**Hinglish**: Haan, kuch offenses ke liye e-FIR file ki ja sakti hai online.

**Q6: What is a Zero FIR?**
**English**: A Zero FIR can be filed at any police station irrespective of the place of the incident. It is numbered 'Zero' and later transferred to the police station having actual jurisdiction.
**Hindi**: जीरो एफआईआर किसी भी पुलिस स्टेशन में दर्ज की जा सकती है, फिर इसे संबंधित स्टेशन में स्थानांतरित कर दिया जाता है।
**Hinglish**: Zero FIR kisi bhi police station mein file ho sakti hai, baad mein jurisdiction wale station transfer ho jati hai.

**Q7: Who can file an FIR?**
**English**: Any person who is a victim, an eyewitness, or just has knowledge of the crime can file an FIR. It is not necessary that only the victim must file it.
**Hindi**: कोई भी व्यक्ति जो पीड़ित है, चश्मदीद गवाह है, या जिसे अपराध की जानकारी है, एफआईआर दर्ज कर सकता है।
**Hinglish**: Victim, eyewitness ya jisko crime ki knowledge ho, wo FIR file kar sakta hai.

**Q8: Can a minor file a police complaint?**
**English**: A minor cannot independently file a legally binding complaint, but they can inform the police. Usually, a parent, guardian, or a designated welfare officer files it on their behalf.
**Hindi**: एक नाबालिग स्वतंत्र रूप से शिकायत दर्ज नहीं कर सकता, अभिभावक को यह करना होता है।
**Hinglish**: Minor direct file nahi kar sakta, parents ya guardian ko minor ke behalf par karna hota hai.

**Q9: What if I don't know the name of the culprit?**
**English**: You can still file an FIR against "unknown persons." The police will investigate to identify the culprits.
**Hindi**: आप "अज्ञात व्यक्तियों" के खिलाफ एफआईआर दर्ज कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish**: Aap "unknown persons" ke against FIR file kar sakte hain, police unhe dhoondegi.

**Q10: Is it mandatory to hire a lawyer to file a police complaint?**
**English**: No, you do not need a lawyer to file a police complaint. The police are supposed to assist you in reducing your information to writing.
**Hindi**: नहीं, पुलिस शिकायत दर्ज करने के लिए वकील की आवश्यकता नहीं है।
**Hinglish**: Nahi, FIR file karne ke liye lawyer ki zaroorat nahi hoti.

**Q11: How long does a police investigation take after an FIR?**
**English**: The law mandates completing the investigation without unnecessary delay. For cases where the accused is in custody, the charge sheet must be filed within 60 or 90 days. For child rape, it is 2 months.
**Hindi**: कानून अनावश्यक देरी के बिना जांच पूरी करने का आदेश देता है।
**Hinglish**: Investigation bina delay ke complete karni hoti hai, custody cases mein 60-90 din mein charge sheet file karni padti hai.

**Q12: Can I withdraw an FIR?**
**English**: An FIR cannot be "withdrawn" at the police station once registered. It can only be quashed by the High Court under Section 528 BNSS (formerly 482 CrPC), or the case can be compounded in court if the offense allows it.
**Hindi**: दर्ज होने के बाद एफआईआर वापस नहीं ली जा सकती, इसे केवल हाईकोर्ट रद्द कर सकता है।
**Hinglish**: FIR ek baar register hone ke baad police station se wapas nahi li ja sakti.

**Q13: What happens if I file a fake FIR?**
**English**: Filing a false FIR is a criminal offense under Section 217 BNS. You can face imprisonment up to 7 years and a heavy fine.
**Hindi**: झूठी एफआईआर दर्ज करना दंडनीय अपराध है, इसमें 7 साल तक की जेल हो सकती है।
**Hinglish**: Fake FIR file karna crime hai, isme 7 saal tak ki jail ho sakti hai.

**Q14: Are oral complaints valid?**
**English**: Yes. Section 173 BNSS states that oral information regarding a cognizable offense given to an officer in charge must be reduced to writing by him and read over to the informant.
**Hindi**: हां, मौखिक शिकायत वैध है, पुलिस अधिकारी इसे लिखेगा और आपको पढ़कर सुनाएगा।
**Hinglish**: Haan, oral complaint valid hai, police officer usko likhkar aapko padhkar sunayega.

**Q15: What is a DD Entry or Station Diary Entry?**
**English**: A Daily Diary (DD) entry or General Diary entry is a record of all events at a police station. A non-cognizable complaint or a missing person report is often just entered in the DD, not as an FIR.
**Hindi**: यह पुलिस स्टेशन की डायरी है जिसमें घटनाओं का रिकॉर्ड रखा जाता है।
**Hinglish**: Yeh station ki diary hoti hai jisme events record hote hain.

**Q16: Do I need medical proof before filing an FIR for assault?**
**English**: No. You must file the FIR first. The police will then issue an injury letter and send you for a Medico-Legal Case (MLC) checkup at a government hospital.
**Hindi**: नहीं, पहले एफआईआर दर्ज करें, पुलिस आपको मेडिकल के लिए भेजेगी।
**Hinglish**: Nahi, pehle FIR file karein, police MLC ke liye hospital bhejegi.

**Q17: What if the police write something different from what I said?**
**English**: You have the right to read the drafted complaint. If it is incorrect, refuse to sign it until it is rectified to accurately reflect your statement.
**Hindi**: यदि पुलिस ने गलत लिखा है, तो तब तक हस्ताक्षर न करें जब तक इसे सुधारा न जाए।
**Hinglish**: Agar police ne kuch galat likha hai, toh sign mat kijiye jab tak correct na ho.

**Q18: Can NRIs file a police complaint in India from abroad?**
**English**: Yes, NRIs can file complaints via email, e-FIR portals, or authorize someone in India (via Power of Attorney) to file it on their behalf.
**Hindi**: हां, एनआरआई ई-एफआईआर या प्रतिनिधि के माध्यम से शिकायत कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish**: Haan, NRIs email ya e-FIR ke through complain kar sakte hain.

**Q19: Is a WhatsApp chat acceptable as proof for filing an FIR?**
**English**: Yes, WhatsApp chats, emails, and call recordings are electronic records and can be submitted as prima facie evidence to support your complaint. A Section 63 BSA (formerly 65B Evidence Act) certificate may be needed later in court.
**Hindi**: हां, व्हाट्सएप चैट प्राथमिक साक्ष्य के रूप में स्वीकार्य हैं।
**Hinglish**: Haan, WhatsApp chats proof ke taur par use ho sakti hain.

**Q20: Can an FIR be filed for a crime that happened years ago?**
**English**: Yes, if the offense is serious (punishable with more than 3 years of imprisonment), there is no limitation period. However, you must explain the reason for the delay to the police and court.
**Hindi**: हां, गंभीर अपराधों के लिए कोई समय सीमा नहीं है, लेकिन देरी का कारण बताना होगा।
**Hinglish**: Haan, serious crimes ke liye salon baad bhi FIR ho sakti hai, bas delay ka reason batana padega.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth**: You can only file a complaint in the police station where the crime occurred.
   **Fact**: You can file a Zero FIR at any police station in India.
2. **Myth**: Filing a police complaint costs money.
   **Fact**: It is a completely free public service.
3. **Myth**: Women must go to the police station to file an FIR.
   **Fact**: Women have the right to file an FIR from their residence or any convenient place in the presence of a woman police officer.
4. **Myth**: Police will always arrest the accused immediately after an FIR.
   **Fact**: Arrest is not automatic. Under BNSS, for offenses with punishment less than 7 years, a notice of appearance is usually issued first.
5. **Myth**: A lawyer is mandatory for drafting an FIR.
   **Fact**: No lawyer is needed. The police are duty-bound to write down your oral statement.
6. **Myth**: e-FIRs don't require any physical interaction.
   **Fact**: Under new laws, e-FIRs require the complainant to sign physically within 3 days for the FIR to be formally registered.
7. **Myth**: Only the victim can file an FIR.
   **Fact**: Anyone with knowledge of a cognizable offense can file an FIR.
8. **Myth**: Police can refuse FIR if they think the case is fake.
   **Fact**: The Supreme Court (Lalita Kumari case) mandated that if a cognizable offense is disclosed, FIR registration is mandatory; truthfulness is investigated later.
9. **Myth**: You can cancel an FIR at the police station if you compromise.
   **Fact**: The police cannot cancel an FIR. Only the High Court can quash it.
10. **Myth**: Delay in filing FIR means the case is dismissed.
    **Fact**: Delay is a factor considered by the court, but if explained reasonably (e.g., trauma, fear), the case is fully valid.
