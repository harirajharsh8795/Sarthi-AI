# Arrest Laws in India: Rights, Procedures, and Protections

## Overview
**English:** An arrest is a formal legal procedure where a law enforcement agency, typically the police, deprives an individual of their liberty due to an alleged involvement in a criminal offense or to prevent a crime. The Indian legal framework, guided by the Constitution and the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023, provides strict guidelines to ensure that arrests are not arbitrary and the fundamental rights of the citizen are protected.

## Legal Background
The legal framework governing arrests in India has been modernized to balance state power with citizen rights.
- **History:** Previously governed by the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) 1973, arrest laws evolved significantly after the landmark Supreme Court judgment in *D.K. Basu vs. State of West Bengal*, which laid down mandatory guidelines to prevent custodial torture.
- **Purpose:** To ensure suspects are brought to justice while safeguarding innocent people from arbitrary detention, harassment, and custodial violence.
- **Applicable Acts:**
  - Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023 (Replaces CrPC)
  - Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023 (Replaces IPC)
  - Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023 (Replaces Indian Evidence Act)
- **Relevant Constitutional Articles:**
  - Article 21: Right to Life and Personal Liberty.
  - Article 22: Protection against arrest and detention in certain cases.
- **Relevant Sections (BNSS 2023):**
  - Section 35: When police may arrest without warrant.
  - Section 36: Procedure of arrest and duties of officer making arrest.
  - Section 37: Designated police officer at police station.
  - Section 39: Right of arrested person to meet an advocate of his choice during interrogation.
  - Section 47: Search of place entered by person sought to be arrested.
  - Section 48: Pursuit of offenders into other jurisdictions.
  - Section 50: Person arrested to be informed of grounds of arrest and of right to bail.
  - Section 58: Person arrested not to be detained more than twenty-four hours.

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** All citizens, residents, and foreign nationals present in the territory of India who are accused of committing a crime or are reasonably suspected of planning to commit a cognizable offense.
- **Who is exempt:** Foreign diplomats possessing diplomatic immunity (under the Vienna Convention), and the President and Governors during their term of office (Article 361). Judicial officers cannot be arrested without prior intimation to the District Judge or the High Court.
- **Special Conditions:** Armed Forces personnel acting in the discharge of their official duties have special protections and generally cannot be arrested without the consent of the Central Government.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Apprehension:** The police officer stops and informs the person that they are under arrest.
2. **Grounds of Arrest:** The officer must inform the person of the exact crime they are accused of.
3. **Arrest Memo:** The officer prepares an arrest memo at the spot, stating the date and time, signed by an independent witness and counter-signed by the arrested person.
4. **Information to Relative:** The police must immediately inform a relative or friend nominated by the arrested person.
5. **Medical Examination:** The arrested person is taken for a medical examination to document any existing injuries.
6. **Station House Entry:** The arrest is recorded in the General Diary at the police station.
7. **Magistrate Presentation:** The person must be physically presented before the nearest Judicial Magistrate within 24 hours of arrest.
8. **Remand/Bail:** The Magistrate decides whether to grant bail, send the person to Police Custody (remand for interrogation), or Judicial Custody (jail).

## Required Documents
- **Arrest Warrant:** Required only for non-cognizable offenses.
- **Arrest Memo:** Mandatory document detailing the time, date, and witness of the arrest.
- **Medical Report:** Prepared by a registered medical practitioner detailing the physical condition of the arrested person.
- **FIR Copy:** If an FIR has been registered, the accused is generally entitled to know its contents, though an official copy is provided at the Magistrate's discretion or during court proceedings.
- **Inspection Memo:** If the police seize any items from the arrested person, an inspection memo listing the items must be provided.

## Government Authorities
- **State Police / Local Police Station:** The primary enforcing body making the arrest.
- **Judicial Magistrate:** The authority who reviews the legality of the arrest within 24 hours.
- **State Human Rights Commission (SHRC) / NHRC:** Bodies that investigate complaints of illegal arrest or custodial torture.
- **Legal Services Authority (NALSA/SALSA):** Provides free legal aid and lawyers to arrested persons who cannot afford them.

## Penalties & Punishments
*Note: These penalties refer to the actions of the arrested person or officers.*
- **Resisting Arrest:** Under BNS, obstructing a public servant in discharge of public functions can lead to imprisonment for up to 3 months, or fine, or both.
- **Absconding (Escaping Custody):** Escaping from lawful custody is a separate crime punishable by imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.
- **Illegal Arrest by Police:** A police officer making an illegal or malafide arrest can be prosecuted for wrongful confinement under BNS and face disciplinary action, suspension, or dismissal.

## Exceptions
- **Women:** Generally, women cannot be arrested after sunset and before sunrise. If exceptional circumstances exist, the police must obtain prior permission from the Judicial Magistrate First Class and the arrest must be made by a woman police officer.
- **Children (Juveniles):** Minors (under 18) are not "arrested" but "apprehended" by the Special Juvenile Police Unit or a designated Child Welfare Police Officer, and they must be brought before the Juvenile Justice Board, not a regular criminal court.
- **Non-Cognizable Offenses:** Police cannot arrest a person without a warrant from a Magistrate in cases of minor, non-cognizable offenses.

## Important Timelines
- **Communication of Arrest:** Immediate notification to family/friends.
- **Magistrate Presentation:** Maximum 24 hours from the time of arrest (excluding travel time).
- **Medical Examination:** Must occur immediately after arrest and every 48 hours while in police custody.
- **Police Custody (Remand) Limit:** Under BNSS, police custody can be requested in phases up to 15 days, and in special terrorism/organized crime cases, this can extend further based on the specific act (like UAPA).
- **Default Bail:** If police fail to file the charge sheet within 60 or 90 days (depending on the severity of the crime), the accused gets an absolute right to default bail.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Refusing to Sign the Arrest Memo:** Signing the arrest memo does not mean you admit guilt; it only acknowledges that you have been formally informed of your arrest. Refusing to sign can create unnecessary friction.
- **Confessing to Police in Fear:** A confession made to a police officer while in custody is generally not admissible as evidence in court unless recorded by a Magistrate.
- **Not Asking for a Medical Examination:** Failing to get a medical examination allows police brutality to go undocumented.
- **Running Away:** Fleeing turns a potentially minor inquiry into a serious offense of escaping lawful custody.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Can the police arrest me without a warrant?**
**English:** Yes, if you are accused of a cognizable offense (serious crimes like murder, theft, rape), the police can arrest you without a warrant under BNSS. For non-cognizable offenses, they must have a warrant from a Magistrate.

**English:** Generally, a woman cannot be arrested after sunset and before sunrise. If there is an extreme emergency, a female police officer must obtain prior written permission from a Judicial Magistrate to make a night arrest.
**Hindi Unicode:** आम तौर पर, किसी महिला को सूर्यास्त के बाद और सूर्योदय से पहले गिरफ्तार नहीं किया जा सकता है। यदि कोई अत्यधिक आपात स्थिति है, तो रात में गिरफ्तारी करनेObjक के लिए एक महिला पुलिस अधिकारी को न्यायिक मजिस्ट्रेट से पूर्व लिखित अनुमति प्राप्त करनी होगी।
**Hinglish:** Normally, kisi aurat ko sunset ke baad aur sunrise se pehle arrest nahi kiya ja sakta. Agar bahut emergency ho, toh female officer ko pehle Magistrate se written permission leni padti hai.

**Q4. What should I do if the police refuse to let me call my family?**
**English:** Informing a relative or friend is your statutory right under Section 36 of BNSS. If the police refuse, you must report this violation directly to the Magistrate when you are presented before them within 24 hours.
**Hindi Unicode:** किसी रिश्तेदार या मित्र को सूचित करना बीएनएसएस की धारा 36 के तहत आपका वैधानिक अधिकार है। यदि पुलिस मना करती है, तो आपको 24 घंटे के भीतर उनके सामने पेश होने पर इस उल्लंघन की रिपोर्ट सीधे मजिस्ट्रेट को करनी चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Apne family ya friend ko inform karna BNSS Section 36 ke under aapka right hai. Agar police mana karti hai, toh 24 hours mein jab aapko Magistrate ke paas pesh kiya jaye, tab aap unhe iski complaint zarur karein.

**Q5. Can the police beat me or use force during arrest?**
**English:** No, the police cannot use unnecessary force or beat you during an arrest. They can only use minimum necessary force to restrain you if you resist or try to flee. Custodial violence is completely illegal.
**Hindi Unicode:** नहीं, पुलिस गिरफ्तारी के दौरान अनावश्यक बल का प्रयोग नहीं कर सकती या आपको पीट नहीं सकती। यदि आप विरोध करते हैं या भागने की कोशिश करते हैं, तो वे आपको रोकने के लिए केवल न्यूनतम आवश्यक बल का उपयोग कर सकते हैं। हिरासत में हिंसा पूरी तरह से अवैध है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, police arrest ke dauran bewajah force use nahi kar sakti ya aapko peet nahi sakti. Agar aap bhagne ki koshish karein ya resist karein tabhi minimum force use kar sakte hain. Custody mein maarna illegal hai.

**Q6. What happens after 24 hours of arrest?**
**English:** The police cannot keep you in their custody for more than 24 hours without a Magistrate's permission. You must be physically produced before a Magistrate, who will decide whether to grant bail or extend custody.
**Hindi Unicode:** मजिस्ट्रेट की अनुमति के बिना पुलिस आपको 24 घंटे से अधिक अपनी हिरासत में नहीं रख सकती है। आपको भौतिक रूप से मजिस्ट्रेट के सामने पेश किया जाना चाहिए, जो यह तय करेगा कि जमानत दी जाए या हिरासत बढ़ाई जाए।
**Hinglish:** 24 ghante ke baad police aapko bina Magistrate ki permission ke custody mein nahi rakh sakti. Aapko Magistrate ke aage pesh karna zaroori hai, jo decide karenge ki bail deni hai ya custody badhani hai.

**Q7. Is it mandatory to have a medical checkup after arrest?**
**English:** Yes, the police are legally required to have you medically examined by a registered medical practitioner immediately after arrest to document any injuries, ensuring you are not harmed in custody.
**Hindi Unicode:** हां, पुलिस कानूनी रूप से आपको किसी भी चोट का दस्तावेजीकरण करने के लिए गिरफ्तारी के तुरंत बाद एक पंजीकृत चिकित्सक द्वारा चिकित्सकीय रूप से जांच करने के लिए बाध्य है, यह सुनिश्चित करने के लिए कि आपको हिरासत में नुकसान न पहुंचे।
**Hinglish:** Haan, arrest ke turant baad ek registered doctor se medical checkup karwana police ke liye mandatory hai taaki koi chot ho toh record ho jaye aur custody mein violence na ho.

**Q8. What if I cannot afford a lawyer after being arrested?**
**English:** If you cannot afford a lawyer, you have the right to free legal aid. The Magistrate will appoint a government-funded lawyer from the Legal Services Authority to defend you at the state's expense.
**Hindi Unicode:** यदि आप वकील का खर्च नहीं उठा सकते हैं, तो आपको मुफ्त कानूनी सहायता का अधिकार है। मजिस्ट्रेट राज्य के खर्च पर आपका बचाव करने के लिए कानूनी सेवा प्राधिकरण से सरकार द्वारा वित्त पोषित वकील नियुक्त करेगा।
**Hinglish:** Agar aap lawyer afford nahi kar sakte, toh aapko free legal aid ka right hai. Magistrate aapko Legal Services Authority se ek sarkari lawyer denge jiska kharcha sarkar uthayegi.

**Q9. Can the police handcuff me?**
**English:** Handcuffing is not a routine procedure and is generally prohibited for ordinary citizens. It is only permitted if there is a clear, documented risk that the person is a hardened criminal, may become violent, or might escape.
**Hindi Unicode:** हथकड़ी लगाना एक नियमित प्रक्रिया नहीं है और आम नागरिकों के लिए यह निषिद्ध है। इसकी अनुमति केवल तभी दी जाती है जब यह स्पष्ट, प्रलेखित जोखिम हो कि व्यक्ति एक खूंखार अपराधी है, हिंसक हो सकता है, या भाग सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Handcuff (hathkadi) lagana normal nahi hai aur aam logo ke liye allowed nahi hai. Yeh sirf tabhi lagai jati hai jab risk ho ki vyakti dangerous criminal hai, violent ho sakta hai ya bhag sakta hai.

**Q10. Can I be arrested on mere suspicion?**
**English:** The police can arrest you on reasonable suspicion of a cognizable offense, but the suspicion must be based on credible information or facts. Arbitrary arrest without concrete justification is illegal.
**Hindi Unicode:** पुलिस आपको किसी संज्ञेय अपराध के उचित संदेह पर गिरफ्तार कर सकती है, लेकिन संदेह विश्वसनीय जानकारी या तथ्यों पर आधारित होना चाहिए। ठोस औचित्य के बिना मनमानी गिरफ्तारी अवैध है।
**Hinglish:** Police aapko cognizable offense ke reasonable shaq par arrest kar sakti hai, par shaq kisi solid proof ya info par based hona chahiye. Bina kisi solid wajah ke arrest karna illegal hai.

**Q11. What is the difference between Police Custody and Judicial Custody?**
**English:** In Police Custody, the accused stays in the police station lock-up and can be interrogated by the police. In Judicial Custody, the accused is sent to a central jail under the control of the Magistrate, and police cannot interrogate them without court permission.
**Hindi Unicode:** पुलिस हिरासत में, आरोपी पुलिस स्टेशन के लॉक-अप में रहता है और पुलिस उससे पूछताछ कर सकती है। न्यायिक हिरासत में, आरोपी को मजिस्ट्रेट के नियंत्रण में केंद्रीय जेल भेजा जाता है, और अदालत की अनुमति के बिना पुलिस उनसे पूछताछ नहीं कर सकती।
**Hinglish:** Police Custody mein accused thane ke lock-up mein rehta hai aur police interrogate kar sakti hai. Judicial Custody mein accused ko jail bheja jata hai aur police bina court permission ke interrogate nahi kar sakti.

**Q12. Do I have to confess to the police if arrested?**
**English:** Absolutely not. You have a constitutional right against self-incrimination (Article 20(3)). You can remain silent. Any confession made to the police while in custody cannot be used against you in court.
**Hindi Unicode:** बिल्कुल नहीं। आपके पास आत्म-दोषारोपण (अनुच्छेद 20(3)) के खिलाफ संवैधानिक अधिकार है। आप चुप रह सकते हैं। हिरासत के दौरान पुलिस को दिया गया कोई भी कबूलनामा अदालत में आपके खिलाफ इस्तेमाल नहीं किया जा सकता।
**Hinglish:** Bilkul nahi. Aapke paas silence maintain karne ka constitutional right hai. Custody mein police ko diya gaya koi bhi confession court mein aapke khilaf use nahi ho sakta.

**Q13. Can traffic police arrest me?**
**English:** Traffic police generally cannot arrest you for routine traffic violations like jumping a red light or not wearing a helmet. However, for serious offenses like severe drunk driving or causing a fatal accident, they can detain you and hand you over to the regular police.
**Hindi Unicode:** यातायात पुलिस आम तौर पर आपको लाल बत्ती पार करने या हेलमेट न पहनने जैसे नियमित यातायात उल्लंघनों के लिए गिरफ्तार नहीं कर सकती है। हालांकि, गंभीर नशे में गाड़ी चलाने या घातक दुर्घटना का कारण बनने जैसे गंभीर अपराधों के लिए, वे आपको हिरासत में ले सकते हैं और नियमित पुलिस को सौंप सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Traffic police normal challan jaise red light jump ke liye arrest nahi kar sakti. Par drunk driving ya severe accident ke cases mein wo aapko detain karke local police ko hand over kar sakte hain.

**Q14. What is a preventive arrest?**
**English:** Preventive arrest is when the police detain someone to prevent them from committing a crime or disrupting public order. Under BNSS Section 170 (previously CrPC 151), they can hold the person for up to 24 hours.
**Hindi Unicode:** निवारक गिरफ्तारी तब होती है जब पुलिस किसी व्यक्ति को अपराध करने या सार्वजनिक व्यवस्था को बाधित करने से रोकने के लिए हिरासत में लेती है। बीएनएसएस धारा 170 के तहत, वे व्यक्ति को 24 घंटे तक रख सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Preventive arrest matlab police kisi ko aage koi crime ya dange karne se rokne ke liye detain karti hai. BNSS Section 170 ke under wo person ko 24 hours tak rakh sakte hain.

**Q15. Can my personal belongings be taken away during arrest?**
**English:** Yes, the police can search you and seize your personal belongings. However, they must provide you with a written 'Inspection Memo' or receipt detailing exactly what was taken, which must be returned when you are released (unless it is evidence).
**Hindi Unicode:** हां, पुलिस आपकी तलाशी ले सकती है और आपका निजी सामान जब्त कर सकती है। हालांकि, उन्हें आपको एक लिखित 'निरीक्षण मेमो' या रसीद प्रदान करनी होगी जिसमें सटीक विवरण हो कि क्या लिया गया था, जिसे रिहा होने पर वापस किया जाना चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Haan, police search karke aapka personal saman le sakti hai. Par unko ek written receipt deni hogi ki kya kya liya gaya hai, jo release hone par wapas milta hai (agar wo evidence na ho).

**Q16. What is the role of the State Police Complaints Authority (SPCA)?**
**English:** The SPCA is a specialized body where you can file complaints against police officers for serious misconduct, including illegal arrest, custodial violence, or extortion.
**Hindi Unicode:** SPCA एक विशेष निकाय है जहां आप अवैध गिरफ्तारी, हिरासत में हिंसा, या जबरन वसूली सहित गंभीर कदाचार के लिए पुलिस अधिकारियों के खिलाफ शिकायत दर्ज कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** SPCA ek aisi body hai jahan aap police officers ke khilaf illegal arrest, maar-peet ya rishwat maangne jaise serious misconduct ki complaint darj kar sakte hain.

**Q17. How is an arrest different from detention?**
**English:** Arrest is formal, recorded, and involves specific criminal charges with strict procedural rights like the 24-hour Magistrate rule. Detention is a temporary restriction of movement for questioning and may not always lead to a formal arrest.
**Hindi Unicode:** गिरफ्तारी औपचारिक, प्रलेखित है, और इसमें 24 घंटे के मजिस्ट्रेट नियम जैसे सख्त प्रक्रियात्मक अधिकारों के साथ विशिष्ट आपराधिक आरोप शामिल हैं। हिरासत पूछताछ के लिए आंदोलन का एक अस्थायी प्रतिबंध है और हमेशा औपचारिक गिरफ्तारी नहीं हो सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Arrest ek formal process hai jisme record banta hai aur proper charges lagte hain. Detention temporarily rokna hota hai questioning ke liye, jo zaroori nahi ki arrest mein badle.

**Q18. Are the D.K. Basu guidelines still applicable under the new laws?**
**English:** Yes, the foundational principles of the D.K. Basu guidelines regarding arrest transparency, medical checkups, informing relatives, and wearing name tags have been fully integrated into the BNSS.
**Hindi Unicode:** हां, गिरफ्तारी में पारदर्शिता, मेडिकल चेकअप, रिश्तेदारों को सूचित करने और नाम टैग पहनने के संबंध में डी.के. बसु के दिशा-निर्देशों के मूलभूत सिद्धांतों को पूरी तरह से बीएनएसएस में एकीकृत किया गया है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, D.K. Basu guidelines jaise name tag pehnna, relatives ko batana aur medical checkup karna, ye sab ab naye BNSS law mein add kar diye gaye hain.

**Q19. Can I film my own arrest if it happens in a public place?**
**English:** Citizens generally have the right to record police interactions in public spaces as long as they do not physically obstruct the officers from performing their duties. However, police might seize your phone as evidence.
**Hindi Unicode:** नागरिकों को आम तौर पर सार्वजनिक स्थानों पर पुलिस बातचीत को रिकॉर्ड करने का अधिकार है, जब तक कि वे अधिकारियों को उनके कर्तव्यों का पालन करने में शारीरिक रूप से बाधा नहीं डालते हैं। हालांकि, पुलिस आपके फोन को सबूत के तौर पर जब्त कर सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Public place mein aap police interaction record kar sakte hain bas unke kaam mein physical rukawat nahi aani chahiye. Par police aapka phone evidence ke roop mein seize kar sakti hai.

**Q20. What is illegal confinement?**
**English:** Illegal or wrongful confinement occurs when a police officer detains a person without legal authority, fails to present them before a Magistrate within 24 hours, or holds them without an official arrest record.
**Hindi Unicode:** अवैध या गलत कारावास तब होता है जब कोई पुलिस अधिकारी किसी व्यक्ति को कानूनी अधिकार के बिना हिरासत में लेता है, 24 घंटे के भीतर उसे मजिस्ट्रेट के सामने पेश करने में विफल रहता है, या आधिकारिक गिरफ्तारी रिकॉर्ड के बिना रखता है।
**Hinglish:** Illegal confinement tab hota hai jab police kisi ko bina legal authority ke detain karti hai, 24 ghante mein Magistrate ke paas nahi le jati, ya bina kisi official record ke thane mein baitha ke rakhti hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** The police can arrest anyone anytime they want.
   **Fact:** Police can only arrest based on reasonable suspicion for cognizable offenses, or with a warrant for non-cognizable offenses. Arbitrary arrests are illegal.
2. **Myth:** Refusing to answer police questions during an arrest is a crime.
   **Fact:** You have the Right to Silence under the Constitution. You are not forced to confess or answer self-incriminating questions.
3. **Myth:** You get exactly one phone call after being arrested, like in the movies.
   **Fact:** Indian law doesn't specify "one phone call." Instead, it gives you the right to have a nominated friend/relative informed about your arrest by the police.
4. **Myth:** Signing the arrest memo means you admit to the crime.
   **Fact:** Signing it only proves that the procedural step of arrest happened at that specific time and place. It is not an admission of guilt.
5. **Myth:** Women can never be arrested.
   **Fact:** Women can be arrested, but generally only by female officers and not between sunset and sunrise, except in extraordinary cases with a Magistrate's permission.
6. **Myth:** If you are innocent, you should resist arrest to prove it.
   **Fact:** Resisting arrest is a separate crime. You should comply calmly and fight the allegations in court or before the Magistrate.
7. **Myth:** Handcuffs are mandatory for all arrests.
   **Fact:** Handcuffing is strictly restricted and used only for dangerous criminals who are a flight risk or violent.
8. **Myth:** Confessions in the police lock-up are solid proof in court.
   **Fact:** Confessions made solely to the police in custody are generally inadmissible in court unless backed by concrete physical evidence discovered due to that statement.
9. **Myth:** The 24-hour limit to see a Magistrate includes travel time.
   **Fact:** The 24-hour limit excludes the time required for the journey from the place of arrest to the Magistrate's court.
10. **Myth:** The police can deny you a lawyer if the crime is very severe.
    **Fact:** The Right to Legal Counsel is a fundamental right applicable to all offenses, regardless of severity.

## Conversation Examples

**Scenario 1: Asking for grounds of arrest**
*Citizen:* Sir, why are you asking me to sit in the police jeep? Am I being arrested? (Sir, aap mujhe police jeep mein baithne ko kyun keh rahe hain? Kya mujhe arrest kiya ja raha hai?)
*Officer:* Yes, you are under arrest.
*Citizen:* Under Section 50 of the BNSS, I have the right to know the exact grounds of my arrest. Please tell me what offense I am accused of. (BNSS Section 50 ke hisaab se mujhe apna jurm janne ka right hai. Kripya batayein mujh par kya charge hai?)

**Scenario 2: Insisting on an Arrest Memo**
*Citizen:* Officer, if I am being arrested, please prepare the Arrest Memo immediately. (Officer, agar aap mujhe arrest kar rahe hain, toh please turant Arrest Memo banaiye.)
*Officer:* We will do that later at the station.
*Citizen:* The Supreme Court's D.K. Basu guidelines require it to be prepared at the spot of arrest and signed by a witness. I will sign it once you prepare it here. (Supreme court ke guidelines ke hisaab se yeh spot par banna chahiye aur witness ke sign hone chahiye.)

**Scenario 3: Requesting to inform family**
*Citizen:* I want my brother to know about this. (Main chahta hoon mere bhai ko iske baare mein pata chale.)
*Officer:* You can tell him when you get bail.
*Citizen:* No sir, Section 36 of BNSS makes it my statutory right to have a relative informed immediately. Please allow me to inform him or you can call him from the station phone. (Nahi sir, Section 36 ke under mera right hai ki family ko turant inform kiya jaye. Please unhe call karne dijiye.)

**Scenario 4: Dealing with night arrest of a woman**
*Officer:* We need you to come to the station right now. (Raat ke 10 baj rahe hain.)
*Citizen (Woman):* Sir, the sun has set. As per the law, a woman cannot be arrested at night without a Magistrate's special order and the presence of a female officer. Do you have the order? (Sir, sunset ho chuka hai. Bina Magistrate ke special order aur female officer ke aap mujhe arrest nahi kar sakte. Kya aapke paas order hai?)

**Scenario 5: Requesting medical examination**
*Citizen:* I have been assaulted by the crowd before you arrived. (Aapke aane se pehle bheed ne mujhe mara hai.)
*Officer:* Just sit in the lock-up.
*Citizen:* Sir, I am requesting a medical examination under the law. My injuries need to be documented immediately by a registered doctor. (Sir, mujhe law ke under medical checkup chahiye. Meri chotein turant doctor se record honi chahiye.)

**Scenario 6: Right to Legal Aid**
*Citizen:* I don't have money for a lawyer. (Mere paas lawyer ke liye paise nahi hain.)
*Magistrate:* You need someone to represent you.
*Citizen:* Your Honor, I request free legal aid from the Legal Services Authority as it is my fundamental right. (Your Honor, main Legal Services Authority se free legal aid ki request karta hoon kyunki yeh mera fundamental right hai.)

**Scenario 7: Facing an illegal demand for money**
*Officer:* Give me Rs. 10,000 and I will let you go without arrest.
*Citizen:* I will not pay any bribe. If I am accused of a crime, follow the legal procedure and present me before a Magistrate. (Main koi rishwat nahi dunga. Agar mujh par charge hai toh legal procedure follow karein aur Magistrate ke paas le chalein.)

**Scenario 8: 24-hour limit violation**
*Citizen:* I have been in this lock-up for two days without seeing a judge. (Main do din se is lock-up mein hoon bina judge ko dekhe.)
*Officer:* We are still investigating.
*Citizen:* Keeping me for more than 24 hours without a Magistrate's order is illegal confinement. I demand to be produced in court immediately. (24 ghante se zyada bina Magistrate ke order ke rakhna illegal confinement hai. Mujhe turant court mein pesh karein.)

**Scenario 9: Refusing self-incrimination**
*Officer:* Just write down that you stole the bike and we will be lenient.
*Citizen:* I respectfully decline. I have the right to remain silent and I will only speak after consulting my advocate. (Main inkaar karta hoon. Mera right to silence hai aur main apne lawyer se baat karne ke baad hi kuch kahunga.)

**Scenario 10: Non-cognizable offense issue**
*Officer:* You abused your neighbor, I am arresting you.
*Citizen:* Simple verbal abuse is a non-cognizable offense. You cannot arrest me without a warrant from a Magistrate. (Normal gaali-galoch ek non-cognizable offense hai. Aap mujhe bina Magistrate ke warrant ke arrest nahi kar sakte.)

**Scenario 11: Being forced to sign blank papers**
*Officer:* Sign this blank paper for the arrest memo, we will fill in the details later.
*Citizen:* I will not sign any blank paper. Please fill in the exact time, date, and location of the arrest, and only then I will counter-sign it. (Main kisi blank paper par sign nahi karunga. Please exact time, date aur location bhariye, tabhi main sign karunga.)

**Scenario 12: Requesting to meet lawyer during interrogation**
*Citizen:* Before answering these questions, I want my lawyer present.
