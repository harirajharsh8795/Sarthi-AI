# Police Interrogation (पुलिस पूछताछ / Police Puch-tach)

## Overview

**English:** Police interrogation is a critical investigative procedure where law enforcement officials question individuals—suspects, witnesses, or victims—to gather facts, extract information, and collect evidence related to a criminal offense. Under the new Indian criminal justice system, governed by the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), the process aims to be transparent while upholding the fundamental rights of the citizen. The law ensures that while the police have the authority to investigate, they cannot employ coercion, torture, or intimidation.

**Hindi Unicode:** पुलिस पूछताछ एक महत्वपूर्ण जांच प्रक्रिया है जहाँ कानून प्रवर्तन अधिकारी किसी आपराधिक अपराध से संबंधित तथ्य जुटाने, जानकारी निकालने और सबूत इकट्ठा करने के लिए व्यक्तियों (संदिग्धों, गवाहों या पीड़ितों) से सवाल करते हैं। भारतीय नागरिक सुरक्षा संहिता (BNSS) द्वारा शासित नई भारतीय आपराधिक न्याय प्रणाली के तहत, इस प्रक्रिया का उद्देश्य नागरिक के मौलिक अधिकारों को बनाए रखते हुए पारदर्शी होना है। कानून यह सुनिश्चित करता है कि पुलिस के पास जांच करने का अधिकार है, लेकिन वे जबरदस्ती, यातना या धमकी का इस्तेमाल नहीं कर सकते।

## Quick Summary

**English:** An individual called for questioning has the Right to Silence against self-incrimination, the Right to Legal Counsel, and protection against physical or mental torture. The police must issue a formal notice (under Section 35 BNSS) for attendance, and special protections are granted to women, minors, senior citizens, and persons with disabilities, who cannot be summoned to the police station arbitrarily.

## Definition

**English:** Police Interrogation is the systematic questioning of a person by law enforcement officers to elicit information or a confession relevant to a criminal investigation, strictly regulated by procedural laws to prevent the abuse of power and protect human rights.

## Why It Matters

**English:** Understanding your rights during police questioning is vital because statements made to the police can heavily influence the trajectory of a criminal case. Awareness prevents false confessions, curbs police brutality, ensures legal representation, and maintains the delicate balance between the state's duty to solve crimes and the individual's constitutional liberty.

## Legal Background

The framework for police interrogation is established through Constitutional mandates and statutory laws, recently updated to remove colonial-era redundancies. 

*   **Article 20(3) of the Constitution of India:** Provides the fundamental Right against Self-Incrimination ("No person accused of any offence shall be compelled to be a witness against himself").
*   **Article 21:** Ensures the Right to Life and Personal Liberty, protecting individuals from custodial torture and cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment.
*   **Article 22:** Grants protection against arbitrary arrest and detention, ensuring the right to consult a legal practitioner.
*   **Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 (BNSS):**
    *   **Section 35:** Replaces Section 41A of CrPC. Notice of appearance before a police officer. It mandates that if an arrest is not required, the police MUST issue a notice directing the person to appear before them.
    *   **Section 179:** Replaces Section 160 CrPC. Police officer's power to require the attendance of witnesses. It strictly dictates that no male under 15 years or above 60 years, no woman, no person with acute illness or disability shall be required to attend at any place other than the place where they reside.
    *   **Section 180:** Replaces Section 161 CrPC. Examination of witnesses by police. The person is bound to answer truly all questions EXCEPT those which have a tendency to expose them to a criminal charge (Right to Silence). Audio-video recording of statements is encouraged/mandated for certain heinous crimes.
    *   **Section 181:** Replaces Section 162 CrPC. Statements to police are not to be signed by the maker. You CANNOT be forced to sign a statement recorded by the police during an investigation.
*   **Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023 (BSA):**
    *   **Section 23:** (Replacing Section 25 IEA). Confessions made to a police officer are NOT admissible as evidence in a court of law against the accused.
    *   **Section 24:** (Replacing Section 26 IEA). Confessions made while in police custody are inadmissible unless made in the immediate presence of a Magistrate.

## Applicability

*   **Who is covered:** Any individual suspected of committing a cognizable or non-cognizable offense, eye-witnesses, material witnesses, or anyone believed to be acquainted with the facts of the case.
*   **Who is exempt (from station visits):** Women of any age, boys under 15 years, men over 60 years, mentally or physically disabled persons, and persons with severe illnesses MUST be interrogated at their residence, not at the police station.
*   **Special conditions:** In cases of sexual offenses against women, the statement of the victim must be recorded by a woman police officer at the victim's residence or a place of her choice, in the presence of parents, guardians, or social workers.

## Rights of Citizens

**English:**
1.  **Right to Notice:** You have the right to receive a written notice (Section 35 BNSS) before being called for questioning.
2.  **Right to Silence:** You can refuse to answer questions that might incriminate you (Article 20(3)).
3.  **Right to Legal Counsel:** You have the right to have your lawyer present during interrogation, though not throughout the entire questioning.
4.  **Right against Torture:** Absolute protection from physical violence, mental harassment, or custodial abuse.
5.  **Right to Refuse Signature:** You cannot be legally compelled to sign any statement written by the police during the investigation (Section 181 BNSS).

## Duties & Responsibilities

**English:**
1.  Comply with valid written summons or notices issued by the police.
2.  Provide true factual information regarding the incident, except for self-incriminating details.
3.  Do not destroy evidence, abscond, or tamper with witnesses.
4.  Maintain decorum and do not verbally or physically assault investigating officers.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure

**English:**
1.  **Issuance of Notice:** Police issues a written notice under Section 35 BNSS detailing the time, place, and purpose of attendance.
2.  **Appearance:** The citizen appears at the designated police station (or police visits the residence for exempted categories).
3.  **Identification:** The Investigating Officer (IO) introduces themselves. The citizen has the right to ask for their identity and designation.
4.  **Questioning:** The IO asks questions regarding the case. Audio-video recording may take place as mandated by BNSS for certain crimes.
5.  **Recording of Statement:** The IO records the answers in writing under Section 180 BNSS.
6.  **Conclusion:** The interrogation ends. The citizen is allowed to leave if not formally arrested. No signature is taken on the statement.

## Required Documents
*   Valid Government ID Proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, Passport, PAN Card) for identity verification.
*   The original written notice/summons received from the police.
*   Relevant documents, medical records, or evidence related to the case (if you are a victim or witness).
*   Contact details of your advocate/legal counsel.

## Government Authorities
*   **Local Police Station:** The primary point of investigation and interrogation.
*   **Superintendent of Police (SP) / Commissioner of Police (CP):** Higher authorities for escalating complaints of police misconduct, torture, or illegal detention.
*   **Magistrate Courts:** Judicial authority where one can file a complaint if abused or falsely implicated during interrogation.
*   **State / National Human Rights Commission (SHRC/NHRC):** For reporting gross human rights violations during custodial questioning.

## Penalties & Punishments
*   **For the Citizen:** Non-compliance with a Section 35 BNSS notice can lead to the police proceeding with a formal arrest. Giving explicitly false information to a public servant can attract charges under Section 217 BNS.
*   **For the Police:** An officer compelling a confession through hurt/grievous hurt is punishable under Section 115/116 of BNS (up to 7 or 10 years imprisonment). Wrongful confinement is punishable under Section 127 BNS.

## Exceptions
*   **Terrorism/National Security:** In cases under UAPA, MCOCA, or NDPS, the rights regarding bail and presence of lawyers are significantly restricted, and in specific local acts, statements made to officers of a certain rank *may* be admissible in court.
*   **Customs/DRI:** Statements given to Customs officers or Directorate of Revenue Intelligence (DRI) under specific acts are generally treated differently than those given to regular police officers and can sometimes be used as evidence.

## Important Timelines
*   **Appearance Time:** The notice will specify the exact date and time. It is usually given with a reasonable buffer (e.g., 24-48 hours) to allow the person to arrange legal counsel or travel.
*   **Custodial Interrogation:** If arrested, maximum police custody for interrogation cannot exceed 15 days in normal crimes (though BNSS introduces varied blocks within the first 40/60 days, up to a max 15 days total custody). The person must be presented before a Magistrate within 24 hours of arrest.

## Common Legal Mistakes
*   Going to the police station based on a "phone call" without a formal written notice under Section 35 BNSS.
*   Signing blank papers or statements out of fear. (You are legally immune from signing Section 180 BNSS statements).
*   Losing temper, arguing aggressively, or physically resisting the police officers.
*   Confessing to a crime under pressure, thinking it will "solve the matter quickly".
*   Not informing family members or lawyers about the interrogation schedule and location.

## Safety Tips

**English:**
*   Always demand a formal written notice before visiting the police station.
*   Share your live location and the Investigating Officer's details with a trusted family member or lawyer.
*   Remember that you have the right to remain silent if a question implicates you in a crime.
*   Do not sign any documents, especially statements written in a language you do not understand.
*   If subjected to violence, immediately request a medical examination and report the abuse to the Magistrate.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**Q1: Can the police call me for interrogation over a phone call?**
**English:** No. The police must issue a formal written notice under Section 35 of the BNSS. You should politely request a written summons before attending.
**Hindi Unicode:** नहीं। पुलिस को BNSS की धारा 35 के तहत एक औपचारिक लिखित नोटिस जारी करना चाहिए। उपस्थित होने से पहले आपको विनम्रतापूर्वक लिखित समन का अनुरोध करना चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. Police ko Section 35 BNSS ke under formal written notice bhejna zaroori hai. Aap politely written notice mang sakte hain.

**Q2: Am I required to sign the statement the police writes down?**
**English:** Absolutely not. Section 181 of the BNSS clearly states that statements made to the police during an investigation shall not be signed by the person making them.
**Hindi Unicode:** बिल्कुल नहीं। BNSS की धारा 181 स्पष्ट रूप से कहती है कि जांच के दौरान पुलिस को दिए गए बयानों पर बयान देने वाले व्यक्ति द्वारा हस्ताक्षर नहीं किए जाएंगे।
**Hinglish:** Bilkul nahi. Section 181 BNSS clear kehta hai ki police ko diye gaye statements par aapko sign karne ki zaroorat nahi hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1.  **Myth:** The police can arrest you if you don't answer all their questions.
    **Fact:** You have the constitutional Right to Silence (Article 20(3)). You cannot be arrested merely for refusing to answer self-incriminating questions.
2.  **Myth:** You must sign the statement written by the police.
    **Fact:** Section 181 of the BNSS explicitly prohibits taking the signature of the person on a statement recorded by the police during an investigation.
3.  **Myth:** Confessing to the police will close the case and you can go home.
    **Fact:** Confessions to the police are inadmissible in court (Section 23 BSA). They do not close the case and might just be used to extract further evidence against you.
4.  **Myth:** The police can call women to the police station at any time for questioning.
    **Fact:** Section 179 BNSS dictates that women of any age must be questioned at their residence, not at the police station.
5.  **Myth:** A phone call from an Inspector is a legally binding summons.
    **Fact:** Only a written notice under Section 35 of the BNSS or a court summons is legally binding.
6.  **Myth:** Lawyers are not allowed to be present during police interrogation.
    **Fact:** The law allows your lawyer to be present during interrogation, although they may not be allowed to intervene in every single question continuously.
7.  **Myth:** The police have the right to beat a suspect slightly to get the truth.
    **Fact:** Absolute False. Physical or mental torture is illegal and unconstitutional. It violates human rights and can lead to the dismissal and prosecution of the officer.
8.  **Myth:** Only criminals are called for police interrogation.
    **Fact:** Witnesses, complainants, victims, and experts are also routinely called for questioning to establish facts and gather evidence.
9.  **Myth:** You have no right to ask the police officer for their identity.
    **Fact:** You have every right to know the name, rank, and ID of the officer interrogating you. They must wear clear name tags (D.K. Basu guidelines).
10. **Myth:** Anything you say can and will be used against you in court exactly like in American movies.
    **Fact:** In India, statements (confessions) made directly to the police are generally inadmissible as substantive evidence of guilt, unlike the "Miranda rights" application shown in Hollywood.

## Conversation Examples

**Scenario 1: Receiving a Phone Call from Police**
*Citizen (C):* Hello?
*Assistant (A) as Police:* Is this Rahul? I am Inspector Verma from the local station. Come to the station in 1 hour for some questioning.
*Citizen (C):* Sir, I am willing to cooperate with the investigation. Please issue a formal written notice under Section 35 of the BNSS to my address, and I will be present at the stipulated time.

**Scenario 2: Insistence on Signature**
*Assistant (A) as IO:* I have written down your answers. Sign at the bottom of this page.
*Citizen (C):* Sir, I have answered truthfully, but under Section 181 of the BNSS, I am not legally required to sign a statement recorded by the police. I respectfully decline to sign it.

**Scenario 3: Right to Silence**
*Assistant (A) as IO:* Were you the one who planned the theft with the gang? Answer me!
*Citizen (C):* Sir, I invoke my constitutional right to silence under Article 20(3). I decline to answer this question as it may incriminate me. I need to consult my lawyer.

**Scenario 4: Questioning a Woman at the Station**
*Assistant (A) as Police:* Madam, you need to come to the police station immediately to record your statement regarding yesterday's fight in your neighborhood.
*Citizen (C):* Sir, as per Section 179 of the BNSS, women cannot be called to the police station for questioning. I request you to send a woman officer to my residence to record my statement.

**Scenario 5: Requesting Lawyer's Presence**
*Assistant (A) as IO:* We are going to start the interrogation now. Sit down.
*Citizen (C):* Sir, before we begin, I would like to exercise my right to have my legal counsel present. My lawyer is on the way; please wait for 15 minutes.

**Scenario 6: Denial of Basic Amenities**
*Citizen (C):* Sir, it has been 6 hours of questioning. I need to use the washroom and get some drinking water.
*Assistant (A) as IO:* No breaks until you tell us what we want to hear.
*Citizen (C):* Sir, denying water and restroom access violates my basic human rights. Please allow me a short break, or I will have to report this mental harassment to the Magistrate.

**Scenario 7: Asking for Officer's Identity**
*Citizen (C):* Excuse me, before I answer your questions, could you please show me your ID card and tell me your name and designation?
*Assistant (A) as IO:* Why do you need to know? Just answer.
*Citizen (C):* As per the Supreme Court guidelines, I have the right to know the identity of the officer interrogating me. 

**Scenario 8: Summons for a Senior Citizen**
*Assistant (A) as Police:* Namaste Uncle, please come to the station tomorrow regarding the property dispute complaint.
*Citizen (C):* Beta, I am 65 years old. Under Section 179 of the BNSS, senior citizens above 60 cannot be called to the station. Please visit my home to record my statement.

**Scenario 9: Clarifying Language Issues**
*Assistant (A) as IO:* I have typed your statement in English. Read it.
*Citizen (C):* Sir, I only understand Hindi properly. Please explain to me in Hindi what you have written before we proceed, and as per the law, I will not sign it.

**Scenario 10: Police Threatening with Arrest**
*Assistant (A) as IO:* If you don't confess to taking the money, I will arrest you right now!
*Citizen (C):* Sir, an arrest must follow legal procedures under BNSS, and I have not committed any offense. Extorting a confession through threats is illegal, and any such statement is inadmissible in court.

**Scenario 11: Seizure of Mobile Phone**
*Assistant (A) as IO:* Give me your mobile phone. We are keeping it.
*Citizen (C):* Sir, if my phone is required for the investigation, please provide me with a formal seizure memo detailing the confiscation, signed and stamped, so I have a legal record of it.

**Scenario 12: Late Night Interrogation**
*Assistant (A) as IO:* We need to question you at 2:00 AM tonight. Be ready.
*Citizen (C):* Sir, routine questioning of witnesses should be done during daytime. Unless I am formally arrested, I request that the interrogation be scheduled during regular daytime hours.

**Scenario 13: Being Forced to Admit Guilt**
*Assistant (A) as IO:* Just admit you hit him, and we will let you go.
*Citizen (C):* Sir, I did not hit him. I will not make a false confession just to be let go, especially since police confessions are invalid in court.

**Scenario 14: Accompanying Person**
*Assistant (A) as IO:* You have to come inside alone. Your brother must leave the station.
*Citizen (C):* Sir, he will not interfere with the interrogation. He can wait in the waiting area or reception outside the room while I cooperate with you.

**Scenario 15: Post-Interrogation Release**
*Citizen (C):* Sir, the questioning is over, and I am not under arrest. May I leave now?
*Assistant (A) as IO:* Yes, you may leave. But you have to come back tomorrow if we call you.
*Citizen (C):* I will cooperate, sir. Please issue another formal notice under Section 35 BNSS if my attendance is required again.
