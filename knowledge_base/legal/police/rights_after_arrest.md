# Rights After Arrest

## Overview
**English:** In India, an arrest does not strip a citizen of their fundamental human rights. The Indian Constitution and the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) provide a robust framework of rights to protect arrested individuals from illegal detention, custodial violence, and arbitrary police action. Knowing these rights is crucial for ensuring a fair legal process and preventing police abuse.

The police must prepare an arrest memo and cannot subject the arrested person to torture.

## Definition
**English:** "Rights After Arrest" refer to the statutory and constitutional safeguards guaranteed to a person taken into police custody, ensuring they are treated with dignity, provided legal representation, and protected against unlawful confinement.

## Why It Matters
**English:** These rights are the bedrock of personal liberty. They prevent secret arrests, mitigate the risk of custodial torture, ensure that family members are aware of the individual's whereabouts, and guarantee that the legal system acts as a check on police powers immediately after an arrest.

## Legal Background
* **Relevant Constitutional Articles:**
  * **Article 21:** Right to Life and Personal Liberty (No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law).
  * **Article 22(1):** Right to be informed of grounds of arrest and right to consult a legal practitioner.
  * **Article 22(2):** Right to be produced before the nearest Magistrate within 24 hours of arrest.
* **Relevant Sections (Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita - BNSS):**
  * **Section 47 BNSS (formerly Sec 50 CrPC):** Person arrested to be informed of grounds of arrest and of right to bail (in bailable offences).
  * **Section 48 BNSS (formerly Sec 50A CrPC):** Obligation of person making arrest to inform about the arrest and the place where the arrested person is being held to a nominated person (friend, relative, etc.).
  * **Section 53 BNSS (formerly Sec 54 CrPC):** Examination of arrested person by medical officer.
  * **Section 58 BNSS (formerly Sec 57 CrPC):** Person arrested not to be detained more than twenty-four hours without judicial scrutiny.
  * **Section 38 BNSS (formerly Sec 41D CrPC):** Right of arrested person to meet an advocate of his choice during interrogation.
* **Supreme Court Guidelines (D.K. Basu vs State of West Bengal):** Mandates visible identification for arresting officers, preparation of Arrest Memo, Information to relatives, and entry in the daily diary.

## Applicability
* **Who is covered:** Every citizen of India and foreign nationals arrested within the Indian territory for ordinary criminal offenses.
* **Who is exempt:** Persons arrested under Preventive Detention laws (e.g., NSA, UAPA in certain contexts may have extended timelines) and Enemy Aliens are exempted from the strict 24-hour rule under Article 22(3), though they still retain basic human rights against torture.
* **Special Conditions:** Women cannot generally be arrested after sunset and before sunrise. If exceptional circumstances exist, prior permission of the Judicial Magistrate is required, and the arrest must be made by a female police officer (Section 43 BNSS).

## Rights of Citizens
**English:**
1. **Right to Information:** To know the exact charges or grounds for arrest.
2. **Right to Inform:** To have a relative or friend informed about the arrest and location.
3. **Right to Legal Counsel:** To meet and consult a lawyer during interrogation.
4. **Right to 24-Hour Production:** To be presented before a Magistrate within 24 hours (excluding travel time).
5. **Right to Medical Exam:** To demand a medical examination to record pre-existing injuries and prevent custodial violence.
6. **Right to Silence:** Protection against self-incrimination (Article 20(3)).

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **The Arrest:** The police officer executes the arrest. They must wear a visible name tag.
2. **Arrest Memo:** An Arrest Memo is immediately prepared, detailing the time, date, and place. It must be attested by at least one witness (family member or local respectable person) and countersigned by the arrested person.
3. **Notification:** The police must inform the nominated relative/friend about the arrest and the police station where the person is held.
4. **Medical Examination:** Under Sec 53 BNSS, a medical examination by a registered medical practitioner must be conducted to record any injuries.
5. **Interrogation & Lawyer Visit:** During questioning, the arrested person has the right to have their lawyer present for some part of the interrogation.
6. **Magistrate Production:** Within 24 hours, the police must produce the accused before the Judicial Magistrate. The Magistrate decides whether to grant bail, order Police Remand (PC), or Judicial Custody (JC).

## Required Documents
* **Arrest Memo:** Crucial document proving the exact time of arrest.
* **Inspection Memo (Optional but recommended):** Documenting major or minor injuries at the time of arrest.
* **Copy of FIR / Remand Application:** The accused's lawyer will need this to argue for bail.
* **Medical Report:** Generated by the medical officer.

## Government Authorities
* **Police Officials (SHO, IO):** Responsible for executing the arrest legally.
* **Judicial Magistrate:** The authority who monitors the legality of the arrest within 24 hours.
* **State/District Legal Services Authority (SLSA/DLSA):** Provides free legal aid if the arrested person cannot afford a lawyer.

## Penalties & Punishments
* **For Police:** If police officers violate these rights, they can face departmental inquiry, suspension, or criminal charges for wrongful confinement (Section 127 BNS) and criminal intimidation. The Magistrate can also take judicial notice of illegal detention.
* **For Citizens:** Resisting lawful arrest or assaulting a public servant can lead to additional severe charges under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS).

## Exceptions
* **Preventive Detention:** Under laws like the National Security Act (NSA), a person can be detained for months without a trial, and normal 24-hour production rules do not apply similarly.
* **Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act (UAPA):** Under UAPA, police custody can be extended up to 30 days, unlike the standard 15 days in ordinary criminal law.

## Important Timelines
* **24 Hours:** Maximum time a person can be held in police custody without Magistrate approval (excluding travel time).
* **15 Days (Standard):** Maximum period of Police Custody (PC) a Magistrate can grant initially for ordinary offenses.
* **60 or 90 Days:** The default bail period if the police fail to file a charge sheet (Section 187 BNSS).

## Common Legal Mistakes
* **Resisting Arrest:** Arguing or fighting with the police during arrest.
* **Not Demanding the Arrest Memo:** This allows police to illegally detain someone for days before officially showing the arrest.
* **Signing Confessions:** Signing a confession out of fear. A confession made to a police officer in custody is generally not admissible in court.
* **Refusing Medical Exam:** The medical exam is your protection against custodial violence; never refuse it.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. **Can the police arrest someone without a warrant?**
**English:** Yes, for cognizable offenses (serious crimes like murder, theft, rape), the police can arrest without a warrant. For non-cognizable offenses, a warrant is required.

**Hinglish:** Haan, serious (cognizable) cases jaise murder ya chori mein police bina warrant arrest kar sakti hai. Chhote cases mein warrant zaroori hota hai.

2. **What if the police refuse to let me call my family?**
**English:** This is a direct violation of Section 48 of the BNSS and Supreme Court guidelines. You must inform the Magistrate about this violation when you are produced before them within 24 hours.
**Hindi Unicode:** यह BNSS की धारा 48 और सुप्रीम कोर्ट के दिशानिर्देशों का सीधा उल्लंघन है। जब आपको 24 घंटे के भीतर पेश किया जाए तो आपको मजिस्ट्रेट को इस उल्लंघन के बारे में सूचित करना चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Ye BNSS Section 48 aur SC guidelines ka violation hai. Jab aapko 24 hours baad Judge ke samne laya jaye, tab aap Judge ko is bare mein zarur batayein.

3. **Can a woman be arrested at night?**

**English:** The arresting officer must bear an accurate, visible, and clear identification and name tag with their designation. Plainclothes officers can arrest, but they must show their ID.

**English:** Detention beyond 24 hours without judicial permission is illegal. You or your lawyer can file a Habeas Corpus writ petition in the High Court for immediate release.

**Hinglish:** Nahi. Aapko sirf Arrest Memo sign karna chahiye, wo bhi time/date check karke. Kabhi bhi blank paper ya na samajh aane wali language wale paper pe sign na karein.

7. **Are confessions made in police custody valid in court?**
**English:** Generally, a confession made to a police officer while in custody is entirely inadmissible as evidence in court under the Indian Evidence Act (now Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam).
**Hindi Unicode:** आम तौर पर, पुलिस हिरासत में पुलिस अधिकारी को दिया गया कोई भी कबूलनामा भारतीय साक्ष्य अधिनियम (अब भारतीय साक्ष्य अधिनियम) के तहत अदालत में सबूत के तौर पर बिल्कुल भी स्वीकार्य नहीं है।
**Hinglish:** Generally nahi. Police custody mein police ko diya gaya confession court mein valid evidence nahi mana jata (under BSA/Evidence Act).

8. **Can the police beat me in custody?**
**English:** Absolutely not. Custodial torture is a severe crime. If you are beaten, request an immediate medical examination from the Magistrate and lodge a complaint against the officers.
**Hindi Unicode:** बिल्कुल नहीं। हिरासत में यातना एक गंभीर अपराध है। यदि आपके साथ मारपीट की जाती है, तो मजिस्ट्रेट से तत्काल चिकित्सा परीक्षण का अनुरोध करें और अधिकारियों के खिलाफ शिकायत दर्ज करें।
**Hinglish:** Bilkul nahi. Custody mein maar-peet illegal hai. Agar police aesa kare, toh Judge ke samne aate hi medical checkup ki maang karein aur complaint karein.

9. **Can the police handcuff me?**
**English:** Handcuffing is not permitted as a routine measure. It can only be used if the person is a repeat offender, violent, or there is a clear risk of escape, usually requiring a Magistrate's permission.
**Hindi Unicode:** नियमित उपाय के रूप में हथकड़ी लगाने की अनुमति नहीं है। इसका उपयोग केवल तभी किया जा सकता है जब व्यक्ति आदतन अपराधी हो, हिंसक हो, या भागने का स्पष्ट जोखिम हो।
**Hinglish:** Normally police handcuff (hathkadi) nahi laga sakti. Ye sirf tab allowed hai jab accused hardcore criminal ho, violent ho, ya bhaagne ka dar ho.

10. **What is an Arrest Memo?**
**English:** It is a legal document prepared at the time of arrest detailing the arrestee's name, time, place, and reason for arrest. It acts as proof of the exact time custody began.
**Hindi Unicode:** यह गिरफ्तारी के समय तैयार किया गया एक कानूनी दस्तावेज है जिसमें गिरफ्तार व्यक्ति का नाम, समय, स्थान और गिरफ्तारी का कारण बताया जाता है। यह हिरासत शुरू होने के सटीक समय के प्रमाण के रूप में कार्य करता है।
**Hinglish:** Ye ek official document hai jo arrest ke time banta hai, jisme time, date aur jagah likhi hoti hai. Ye proof hota hai ki police ne aapko kis time pakda tha.

11. **Do I have the right to remain silent?**
**English:** Yes. Article 20(3) of the Constitution protects you from self-incrimination. You can refuse to answer questions that might prove you guilty.
**Hindi Unicode:** हाँ। संविधान का अनुच्छेद 20(3) आपको स्वयं के खिलाफ गवाही देने से बचाता है। आप ऐसे सवालों के जवाब देने से इनकार कर सकते हैं जो आपको दोषी साबित कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, Constitution ka Article 20(3) aapko right to silence deta hai. Aap un sawalon ka jawab dene se mana kar sakte hain jinse aap fas sakte hain.

12. **Can I choose my own lawyer?**
**English:** Yes. You have a fundamental right under Article 22 to consult a legal practitioner of your choice. If you cannot afford one, the State will provide a free legal aid lawyer.
**Hindi Unicode:** हाँ। अनुच्छेद 22 के तहत अपनी पसंद के कानूनी वकील से परामर्श करने का आपका मौलिक अधिकार है। यदि आप खर्च नहीं उठा सकते, तो राज्य मुफ्त कानूनी सहायता वकील प्रदान करेगा।
**Hinglish:** Haan, Article 22 ke under aapko apne pasand ka lawyer lene ka haq hai. Agar paise nahi hain, toh sarkar aapko free lawyer (legal aid) degi.

13. **Can the police search my phone during arrest?**
**English:** The police can seize your phone as evidence. However, forcing you to reveal your password or biometrics is a complex legal area, generally protected under the right against self-incrimination.
**Hindi Unicode:** पुलिस आपके फोन को सबूत के तौर पर जब्त कर सकती है। हालांकि, आपको अपना पासवर्ड बताने के लिए मजबूर करना एक जटिल कानूनी क्षेत्र है, जिसे आम तौर पर आत्म-अपराध के खिलाफ अधिकार के तहत संरक्षित किया जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Police aapka phone seize (zabt) kar sakti hai evidence ke liye. Par zabardasti password mangna right against self-incrimination ke under debatable hai.

14. **What is the difference between Police Custody and Judicial Custody?**
**English:** Police custody means you are locked up in a police station and can be interrogated. Judicial custody means you are in a regular jail under the Magistrate's control, and police need court permission to question you.
**Hindi Unicode:** पुलिस हिरासत का मतलब है कि आप पुलिस स्टेशन में बंद हैं और पूछताछ की जा सकती है। न्यायिक हिरासत का मतलब है कि आप मजिस्ट्रेट के नियंत्रण में जेल में हैं।
**Hinglish:** Police custody ka matlab aap police lockup mein hain aur unhe interrogation ka haq hai. Judicial custody ka matlab aap jail mein hain, jahan police bina Judge ki permission ke aapse puchtach nahi kar sakti.

15. **Can my family visit me in police custody?**
**English:** Your family can visit you in police custody subject to reasonable restrictions and the permission of the investigating officer, usually to provide food, clothing, or legal documents.
**Hindi Unicode:** आपका परिवार उचित प्रतिबंधों और जांच अधिकारी की अनुमति के अधीन पुलिस हिरासत में आपसे मिल सकता है, आमतौर पर भोजन, कपड़े या कानूनी दस्तावेज प्रदान करने के लिए।
**Hinglish:** Haan, family police station aakar mil sakti hai, par iske liye Investigating Officer (IO) ki permission chahiye hoti hai.

16. **Is a medical exam free of cost?**
**English:** Yes. The medical examination conducted under Section 53 of the BNSS upon arrest is completely free and done at a government hospital.
**Hindi Unicode:** हाँ। गिरफ्तारी पर BNSS की धारा 53 के तहत किया गया चिकित्सा परीक्षण पूरी तरह से मुफ्त है और एक सरकारी अस्पताल में किया जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, arrest ke baad Section 53 BNSS mein jo medical checkup hota hai, wo sarkari hospital mein bilkul free hota hai.

17. **What should I do if the police take me to an unknown location?**
**English:** Demand to be taken to a designated police station. Memorize landmarks if possible. Inform the Magistrate about this illegal transit when produced.
**Hindi Unicode:** किसी निर्दिष्ट पुलिस स्टेशन ले जाने की मांग करें। यदि संभव हो तो स्थलों को याद रखें। पेश होने पर मजिस्ट्रेट को इस अवैध पारगमन के बारे में सूचित करें।
**Hinglish:** Turant demand karein ki aapko official police station le jayen. Raaste yaad rakhne ki koshish karein aur Judge ke samne pesh hote hi is baat ki shikayat karein.

18. **Can I get bail directly from the police station?**
**English:** Yes, if the offense is a 'Bailable Offense' (e.g., minor scuffle), the police are legally bound to grant you bail at the police station itself upon furnishing a bail bond.
**Hindi Unicode:** हाँ, यदि अपराध 'जमानती अपराध' है, तो पुलिस कानूनी रूप से आपको जमानत बांड प्रस्तुत करने पर पुलिस स्टेशन में ही जमानत देने के लिए बाध्य है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar case 'Bailable' (chhota jurm) hai, toh police khud lockup se bail bond bharwa kar aapko chhod sakti hai.

19. **What is transit remand?**
**English:** If you are arrested in one state for a crime committed in another, the police must get a 'transit remand' from a local Magistrate to legally transport you across states within the 24-hour window.
**Hindi Unicode:** यदि आपको किसी अन्य राज्य में किए गए अपराध के लिए एक राज्य में गिरफ्तार किया जाता है, तो पुलिस को आपको राज्यों में कानूनी रूप से ले जाने के लिए स्थानीय मजिस्ट्रेट से 'ट्रांजिट रिमांड' प्राप्त करना होगा।
**Hinglish:** Agar police aapko dusre state mein arrest karti hai, toh unhe local Judge se 'transit remand' leni padti hai taki wo legal tarike se aapko 24 hours ke andar travel karwa sakein.

20. **Can I complain against a police officer who violated my rights?**
**English:** Yes. You can complain to the Magistrate, the State Police Complaints Authority (SPCA), the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC), or file an FIR against the officer.
**Hindi Unicode:** हाँ। आप मजिस्ट्रेट, राज्य पुलिस शिकायत प्राधिकरण (SPCA), राष्ट्रीय मानवाधिकार आयोग (NHRC) से शिकायत कर सकते हैं या अधिकारी के खिलाफ FIR दर्ज कर सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, aap Judge, State Police Complaints Authority ya Human Rights Commission (NHRC) mein police wale ke khilaf complaint kar sakte hain.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** The police can keep you in the lockup for as many days as they want.
   **Fact:** They can only keep you for a maximum of 24 hours. Any time beyond that strictly requires a Magistrate's order.
2. **Myth:** You must answer every single question the police ask during interrogation.
   **Fact:** You have the Right to Silence under Article 20(3). You don't have to answer questions that self-incriminate.
3. **Myth:** Only rich people get lawyers during police questioning.
   **Fact:** The right to legal counsel is fundamental. If you have no money, the State Legal Services Authority provides one for free.
4. **Myth:** The police can arrest a woman at 2 AM if the crime is serious.
   **Fact:** Women cannot be arrested after sunset without the prior written permission of a Judicial Magistrate, executed by a female officer.
5. **Myth:** Police lockups have no cameras, so anything can happen.
   **Fact:** The Supreme Court (Paramvir Singh Saini case) mandates CCTV cameras with audio recording in all police stations, specifically at entry/exit and lockups.
6. **Myth:** If you refuse to sign a police confession, you will be beaten.
   **Fact:** Custodial torture is illegal, and police confessions are largely invalid in court. Never sign blank papers.
7. **Myth:** You cannot ask for a doctor unless you are severely bleeding.
   **Fact:** You have a legal right (Section 53 BNSS) to a medical examination upon arrest to record your physical condition, regardless of visible injuries.
8. **Myth:** Police do not need a reason to arrest you.
   **Fact:** Under Section 47 BNSS, it is mandatory for the police to communicate the full particulars of the offense or the exact grounds for arrest immediately.
9. **Myth:** Bail is only granted by High Courts.
   **Fact:** Bail for bailable offenses is granted by the police station itself. For non-bailable offenses, the local Magistrate or Sessions Court grants bail.
10. **Myth:** The police don't have to tell anyone you are arrested.
    **Fact:** The police are legally bound (Section 48 BNSS) to inform your nominated relative or friend immediately upon your arrest.

## Conversation Examples

**Dialogue 1: Asking for Grounds of Arrest**
*Citizen:* Officer, on what charges are you arresting me? (Sir, aap mujhe kis jurm mein arrest kar rahe hain?)
*Assistant:* (As Police) Under Section 47 of the BNSS, you are being arrested on charges of cheating under Section 318 of BNS. Here is the copy of the FIR.

**Dialogue 2: Demanding an Arrest Memo**
*Citizen:* Can you please prepare the Arrest Memo before we leave? (Kya aap chalne se pehle Arrest Memo bana sakte hain?)
*Assistant:* Yes, the Arrest Memo is being prepared. It will note the time as 4:30 PM. Please read it carefully, check the time, and sign it.

**Dialogue 3: Calling a Relative**
*Citizen:* I need to inform my brother about this arrest immediately. (Mujhe apne bhai ko is arrest ke bare mein abhi batana hai.)
*Assistant:* Under Section 48 BNSS, you have the right to inform a relative. You can use the station phone, or I will make the call and inform him of your location.

**Dialogue 4: Requesting a Lawyer**
*Citizen:* I will not answer further questions until my lawyer arrives. (Main apne lawyer ke aane tak aage koi jawab nahi dunga.)
*Assistant:* You have the right to meet your lawyer during interrogation under Section 38 BNSS. We will halt the main questioning until they arrive.

**Dialogue 5: Medical Examination**
*Citizen:* I want a medical examination done before I am put in the lockup. (Lockup mein jaane se pehle mujhe apna medical checkup karwana hai.)
*Assistant:* We are taking you to the government hospital for a mandatory medical checkup under Section 53 BNSS to record your health status.

**Dialogue 6: Women Arrest at Night**
*Citizen (Female):* It is 10 PM. You cannot arrest me without a Magistrate's order. (Raat ke 10 baj rahe hain. Aap mujhe bina Judge ke order ke arrest nahi kar sakte.)
*Assistant:* You are correct. Women cannot be arrested after sunset. However, we have a special written order from the Judicial Magistrate, and a female officer is executing the arrest.

**Dialogue 7: Handcuffing Objection**
*Citizen:* Why are you handcuffing me? I am not running away. (Aap mujhe hathkadi kyun laga rahe hain? Main bhaag nahi raha.)
*Assistant:* Handcuffing is generally illegal for ordinary arrests. If you are cooperating, we will remove them.

**Dialogue 8: Refusing to Sign Blank Papers**
*Citizen:* This paper is blank. I will not sign it. (Ye paper khali hai. Main ispar sign nahi karunga.)
*Assistant:* You have the right to refuse signing blank documents. You should only sign the Arrest Memo and documents where the text is clearly written and explained to you.

**Dialogue 9: 24-Hour Rule Violation**
*Citizen:* I have been here since yesterday morning. When will you produce me in court? (Main kal subah se yahan hoon. Aap mujhe court mein kab pesh karenge?)
*Assistant:* The 24-hour limit ends at 10 AM. You will be presented before the Judicial Magistrate in 30 minutes, well within your constitutional right.

**Dialogue 10: Police Threatening Violence**
*Citizen:* If you hit me, I will report you to the Magistrate tomorrow. (Agar aapne mujhe maara, toh main kal Judge se shikayat karunga.)
*Assistant:* Custodial violence is strictly prohibited. If an officer assaults you, you must report it to the Magistrate, and the officer can face severe criminal charges.

**Dialogue 11: Minor Offense Bail**
*Citizen:* This is a minor bailable offense. Can I get bail here? (Ye bailable case hai. Kya mujhe yahin se bail mil sakti hai?)
*Assistant:* Yes, for bailable offenses, the Station House Officer (SHO) can grant you bail directly from the police station upon submitting a bail bond.

**Dialogue 12: Right to Silence**
*Citizen:* I don't want to say anything that might implicate me. (Main aesa kuch nahi kehna chahta jisse main fas jaun.)
*Assistant:* You are exercising your Right to Silence under Article 20(3). You cannot be forced to be a witness against yourself.

**Dialogue 13: Plainclothes Officer Arrest**
*Citizen:* You are in plain clothes. Show me your police ID before I go with you. (Aap civil dress mein hain. Pehle apna Police ID dikhayein.)
*Assistant:* As per DK Basu guidelines, here is my official ID card and name badge. Now please cooperate with the arrest.

**Dialogue 14: Confession in Police Custody**
*Citizen:* Will the judge punish me because I signed a confession here? (Kya Judge mujhe isliye saza dega kyunki maine yahan confession pe sign kiya hai?)
*Assistant:* No. Confessions made to the police in custody are generally not admissible as evidence in court. You can retract it before the Magistrate.

**Dialogue 15: Free Legal Aid Request**
*Citizen:* I have no money for a lawyer. What should I do in court today? (Mere paas lawyer ke liye paise nahi hain. Aaj court mein kya karun?)
*Assistant:* Tell the Magistrate immediately that you cannot afford a lawyer. The court will appoint a legal aid lawyer for you free of cost through the Legal Services Authority.

## Government Services
**English:**
* **National Legal Services Authority (NALSA):** Provides free legal aid to eligible individuals (women, SC/ST, low-income) for bail and trial. Toll-free helpline: 15100.
* **eCourts Portal:** Once an FIR is registered, the case status, remand orders, and bail hearing dates can be tracked online using the eCourts services.
* **National Human Rights Commission (NHRC):** A dedicated portal (hrcnet.nic.in) to file complaints against police for custodial violence or illegal detention.

**Hindi Unicode:**
* **राष्ट्रीय विधिक सेवा प्राधिकरण (NALSA):** योग्य व्यक्तियों (महिलाओं, SC/ST, कम आय वाले) को मुफ्त कानूनी सहायता प्रदान करता है। टोल-फ्री हेल्पलाइन: 15100।
* **eCourts पोर्टल:** FIR दर्ज होने के बाद, केस की स्थिति, रिमांड आदेश और जमानत की तारीखों को ऑनलाइन ट्रैक किया जा सकता है।
* **राष्ट्रीय मानवाधिकार आयोग (NHRC):** हिरासत में हिंसा या अवैध हिरासत के लिए पुलिस के खिलाफ शिकायत दर्ज करने के लिए एक समर्पित पोर्टल।

**Hinglish:**
* **NALSA:** Agar paise nahi hain, toh free lawyer ke liye 15100 par call karein (women aur garib logo ke liye free).
* **eCourts Portal:** Apne case ki status, next hearing date, aur Judge ka order eCourts app/website par online check kar sakte hain.
* **NHRC Portal:** Agar police ne lockup mein maara ya galat tarike se rakha, toh NHRC portal par online complaint daal sakte hain.
