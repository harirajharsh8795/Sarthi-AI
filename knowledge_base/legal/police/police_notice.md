# Police Notice (Notice of Appearance before Police Officer)

## Overview
**English:** A Police Notice, specifically under Section 35 of the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023 (formerly Section 41A of the CrPC), is a formal document issued by a police officer requiring a person to appear before them. It is issued when the police investigate a cognizable offense where an immediate arrest is not required. The primary objective is to prevent unnecessary arrests while ensuring the person's cooperation in the ongoing investigation.
**Hindi Unicode:** पुलिस नोटिस (विशेष रूप से भारतीय नागरिक सुरक्षा संहिता (BNSS) की धारा 35 के तहत) एक औपचारिक दस्तावेज है जो पुलिस अधिकारी द्वारा जारी किया जाता है, जिसमें व्यक्ति को उनके सामने पेश होने का निर्देश दिया जाता है। यह तब जारी किया जाता है जब संज्ञेय अपराध में तुरंत गिरफ्तारी आवश्यक नहीं होती है। इसका मुख्य उद्देश्य अनावश्यक गिरफ्तारी को रोकना और जांच में व्यक्ति का सहयोग सुनिश्चित करना है।

## Quick Summary
**English:** If you receive a police notice, you are legally bound to comply and appear before the officer at the specified time and place. As long as you comply, you will generally not be arrested. Non-compliance can lead to a court-authorized arrest.
**Hindi Unicode:** यदि आपको पुलिस नोटिस मिलता है, तो आपको कानूनी रूप से इसका पालन करना होगा और निर्धारित समय और स्थान पर अधिकारी के सामने पेश होना होगा। जब तक आप पालन करते हैं, तब तक आपको गिरफ्तार नहीं किया जाएगा। पालन न करने पर न्यायालय द्वारा गिरफ्तारी का आदेश दिया जा सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Agar aapko police notice milta hai, toh aapko bataye gaye time aur jagah par police ke samne present hona padega. Agar aap cooperate karte hain, toh mostly arrest nahi hota. Lekin ignore karne par court se arrest warrant issue ho sakta hai.

## Definition
**English:** A Police Notice is a written directive issued by a law enforcement officer, mandating an individual against whom a reasonable complaint has been made, or credible information exists regarding a cognizable offense, to appear for questioning.
**Hindi Unicode:** पुलिस नोटिस एक लिखित निर्देश है जो कानून प्रवर्तन अधिकारी द्वारा जारी किया जाता है, जो ऐसे व्यक्ति को पूछताछ के लिए उपस्थित होने का आदेश देता है जिसके खिलाफ कोई उचित शिकायत की गई हो या संज्ञेय अपराध के संबंध में विश्वसनीय जानकारी हो।
**Hinglish:** Police Notice ek written order hai jo police officer un logon ko bhejti hai jinke khilaf koi complaint aayi ho ya kisi serious (cognizable) offense ki jankari mili ho, taaki unse questioning ki ja sake.

## Why It Matters
**English:** It protects citizens from arbitrary and unjustified arrests, upholding the fundamental right to personal liberty under Article 21 of the Constitution. It shifts the focus from immediate arrest to fair investigation, saving individuals from the social stigma and trauma of being imprisoned unnecessarily.
**Hindi Unicode:** यह नागरिकों को मनमानी और अनुचित गिरफ्तारी से बचाता है, और संविधान के अनुच्छेद 21 के तहत व्यक्तिगत स्वतंत्रता के मौलिक अधिकार को बनाए रखता है। यह तत्काल गिरफ्तारी से ध्यान हटाकर निष्पक्ष जांच पर केंद्रित करता है, जिससे लोग अनावश्यक रूप से जेल जाने के सामाजिक कलंक और आघात से बचते हैं।

## Legal Background
**History & Purpose:** Originally introduced as Section 41A in the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) via an amendment to curb police misuse of arrest powers in cases where punishment is up to 7 years. Now, under the new criminal laws of India, it is governed by the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023.
**Applicable Acts:**
- Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023
- Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023 (for substantive offenses)
- Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023 (for evidence procedures)
**Relevant Constitutional Articles:**
- Article 21: Protection of Life and Personal Liberty.
- Article 22: Protection against arrest and detention in certain cases.
**Relevant Sections:**
- **Section 35(1) BNSS:** Power to issue notice of appearance. (Replaces Section 41A CrPC).
- **Section 35(3) BNSS:** Protection from arrest if the notice terms are complied with.
- **Section 35(4) BNSS:** Power to arrest if the person fails to comply or identify themselves, subject to court orders.

## Applicability
**Who is covered:** Any individual suspected of a cognizable offense where the punishment is imprisonment for 7 years or less, and immediate arrest is not legally required to prevent tampering of evidence, absconding, or committing further offenses.
**Who is exempt:** Persons accused of heinous crimes (punishment exceeding 7 years, life imprisonment, or death) do not typically get this notice; they can be arrested immediately under Section 35 of BNSS without a notice.
**Special conditions:** If the police officer believes an arrest is necessary even for an offense punishable up to 7 years, they must record the specific reasons in writing before making the arrest.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Issuance:** The Investigating Officer (IO) issues a formal written notice under Section 35 BNSS.
2. **Service:** The notice is served to the individual personally, electronically, or at their residence.
3. **Acknowledgment:** The receiver must sign an acknowledgment of receiving the notice.
4. **Appearance:** The individual appears at the police station with their lawyer (optional but recommended).
5. **Questioning:** The IO records the person's statement.
6. **Compliance Recording:** The IO records that the person has cooperated. No arrest is made.
7. **Further Notices:** If more information is needed, subsequent notices can be issued.
8. **Breach:** If the individual ignores the notice, the IO can approach the magistrate for an arrest warrant.

## Required Documents
- Original and copy of the received Police Notice.
- Valid Government ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, Voter ID).
- Any documents, evidence, or records requested in the notice.
- Anticipatory bail order (if you have already secured one from the court).
- Authorization letter (Vakalatnama) if your lawyer is accompanying you.

## Government Authorities
- **State Police Departments:** Local police stations and specialized cells (Cyber Crime, EOW).
- **Investigating Officer (IO):** The primary officer responsible for your case.
- **Judicial Magistrate:** Approves arrest warrants if notice is defied.
- **High Courts & Sessions Courts:** For filing Anticipatory Bail if harassment is apprehended.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Ignoring the Notice:** Unjustified failure to comply with the terms of the notice under Section 35(4) BNSS gives the police the power to arrest the person for the offense mentioned in the notice, subject to competent court orders.
- **Giving False Information:** Punishable under Section 214 of BNS (Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita).
- **Tampering with Evidence:** Punishable under Section 238 of BNS.

## Exceptions
- If the police have strong reasons to believe that an arrest is necessary to prevent the destruction of evidence, to stop the person from committing another crime, or to ensure their presence in court, they can bypass the notice and arrest directly, provided they record their reasons in writing.
- Heinous crimes with punishments over 7 years do not legally mandate a Section 35 BNSS notice prior to arrest.

## Important Timelines
- **Appearance Date:** Usually, 3 to 7 days from the date of receiving the notice are given to appear, though it can be immediate depending on the urgency.
- **Response Time:** If you cannot appear due to a valid medical emergency, you must reply to the notice immediately requesting a new date.
- **Anticipatory Bail:** Must be filed before the date of appearance if you strongly suspect foul play or illegal arrest.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. **Ignoring the Notice:** Thinking that throwing away the paper will make the problem disappear. This is the fastest way to get an arrest warrant.
2. **Going Alone Without Legal Advice:** Answering trick questions without a lawyer can lead to self-incrimination.
3. **Losing Your Temper:** Arguing or fighting with the Investigating Officer at the station.
4. **Not Taking Acknowledgment:** Failing to take a stamped acknowledgment from the police station that you appeared and cooperated.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a Section 35 BNSS Notice?**
It is a legal notice issued by police asking a suspect to appear for investigation instead of arresting them immediately. It ensures cooperation without jail time.
2. **Do I need to go to the police station?**
Yes, it is legally mandatory to comply with the notice and appear at the designated place and time.
3. **Can I be arrested if I appear?**
Generally, no. As long as you comply with the terms of the notice and cooperate, Section 35(3) BNSS protects you from arrest.
4. **What happens if I don't go?**
The police can record your non-compliance and approach a Magistrate to secure an arrest warrant against you.
5. **Can I take a lawyer with me?**
Yes, the Supreme Court has ruled that a person has the right to have their advocate present at a visible, though not necessarily audible, distance during interrogation.
6. **Can I ask for more time to appear?**
Yes, if you have a valid reason (e.g., medical emergency, out of station), you can submit a written request via your lawyer or email asking for an extension.
7. **Is a notice sent on WhatsApp legally valid?**
Yes, under modern legal procedures and IT laws, electronic service of notices via WhatsApp or Email is legally recognized by Indian Courts.
8. **What if the notice has no FIR number?**
A notice can be issued based on a complaint or suspicion even before a formal FIR is registered, often referring to a General Diary (GD) entry.
9. **Can a woman be called to the police station?**
Special provisions exist. Under BNSS, women, minors under 15, persons over 60, and persons with disabilities should ideally be questioned at their residence, not the police station.
10. **Does receiving a notice mean I am a criminal?**
No. It only means you are required for questioning. You are presumed innocent until proven guilty in a court of law.
11. **Should I apply for Anticipatory Bail?**
If you have a strong apprehension that the police intend to arrest you despite issuing a notice (e.g., political rivalry, false dowry case), you should apply for anticipatory bail.
12. **Can the police seize my phone during the appearance?**
Yes, if the investigating officer believes your phone contains evidence related to the crime, they can seize it, but they must provide you with a seizure memo.
13. **Do I have to answer all questions?**
You must answer questions truthfully, but you have the constitutional right to remain silent on questions that could expose you to criminal charges (self-incrimination).
14. **How do I know the notice is not a scam?**
Fake notices often ask for money, "digital arrest," or immediate transfer of funds. Real police never ask for money to cancel a notice. Verify the IO's credentials physically.
15. **Can I challenge the notice in court?**
Yes, if the notice is arbitrary, malicious, or violates your fundamental rights, you can challenge it by filing a writ petition in the High Court under Article 226.
16. **Will this appear on my police verification for a passport?**
Just receiving a notice does not usually affect a passport. However, if an FIR is registered and a charge sheet is filed, it will show up in the verification.
17. **Can I record the interrogation on my phone?**
No, recording police operations inside a police station without permission is generally prohibited and can lead to confiscation of your device.
18. **What is a "digital arrest"?**
There is no legal concept called "digital arrest." If someone on a video call claims you are digitally arrested and demands money, it is a cybercrime scam. Disconnect and report to 1930.
19. **What if the police demand a bribe to settle the notice?**
Do not pay. Record the demand if possible and report it to the Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB) or Vigilance department of your state.
20. **Is the police notice applicable for civil disputes?**
No. Police cannot issue this notice for purely civil matters (like a simple breach of contract or tenant eviction). It is only for cognizable criminal offenses.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** Receiving a police notice means my arrest is confirmed.
**Fact:** Actually, it means the opposite. It means the police do not intend to arrest you immediately, provided you cooperate.
2. **Myth:** If I ignore the notice, the police will forget about it.
**Fact:** Ignoring it gives the police the legal right to procure an arrest warrant from a judge.
3. **Myth:** I must answer every question even if it proves my guilt.
**Fact:** Article 20(3) of the Constitution protects you from being a witness against yourself.
4. **Myth:** Police notices are always delivered by a constable in uniform.
**Fact:** Notices can be sent via registered post, email, or even WhatsApp.
5. **Myth:** I cannot leave my city ever again after getting a notice.
**Fact:** You can travel, but you must inform the Investigating Officer and ensure you are available on the dates you are summoned.
6. **Myth:** Only suspects get a police notice.
**Fact:** Witnesses or persons who might have information about the crime can also be summoned under Section 193 BNSS (formerly 160 CrPC).
7. **Myth:** I can send my friend to the police station on my behalf.
**Fact:** No, the person named in the notice must appear physically. A lawyer can accompany you, but cannot replace you.
8. **Myth:** Police can beat me if I don't give the answers they want.
**Fact:** Custodial violence is strictly illegal. You have the right to a medical examination if assaulted.
9. **Myth:** Women must go to the police station at night if summoned.
**Fact:** Women cannot be called to the police station for questioning; they must be questioned at their home, and strictly not between sunset and sunrise.
10. **Myth:** If I am innocent, I don't need a lawyer for the police notice.
**Fact:** Even innocent people can be trapped by leading questions. A lawyer ensures your rights are protected during the legal process.

*Citizen (Suresh):* I didn't go for the notice last week. Police came to my house today.
*Saarthi AI:* By ignoring the notice, you violated Section 35 BNSS, which gives the police grounds to arrest you. You need to contact a criminal defense lawyer immediately.

**Example 12: Notice in Civil Dispute**
*Citizen (Ravi):* My landlord sent a police notice because I delayed rent.
*Saarthi AI:* Police cannot interfere in civil matters like rent disputes. Check if the landlord filed a false criminal complaint (like cheating/fraud) to get the notice issued. Consult a lawyer.

**Example 13: Travelling Abroad**
*Citizen (Tanya):* I have a scheduled flight to Dubai next week, but I got a notice today.
*Saarthi AI:* You must inform the IO about your travel plans and show your tickets. If you leave without informing, it may be treated as absconding, and a Look Out Circular (LOC) could be issued.

**Example 14: Accused vs Witness**
*Citizen (Mohit):* Does getting this notice mean I am the accused?
*Saarthi AI:* Not necessarily. Sometimes people are called simply because they might have information useful to the investigation. Ask your lawyer to clarify your status with the IO.

**Example 15: Bribe Demand**
*Citizen (Deepak):* The constable said if I pay 10,000 rupees, he will cancel the notice.
*Saarthi AI:* A notice cannot be legally "cancelled" for money. This is extortion/bribery. Do not pay. Consult your lawyer and consider complaining to higher police officials or the Anti-Corruption Bureau.
