# Zero FIR: Registration of Offence Irrespective of Jurisdiction

## Overview
**English:** A "Zero FIR" is a First Information Report that can be registered at any police station across India, regardless of where the actual crime took place. It is designed to ensure that victims of serious, cognizable crimes do not face jurisdictional hurdles when seeking urgent police intervention. Once registered, it is given the serial number "0" (hence the name) and is later transferred to the police station that has the actual jurisdiction to investigate the case.
**Hindi Unicode:** 'जीरो एफआईआर' (Zero FIR) एक ऐसी प्रथम सूचना रिपोर्ट है जिसे भारत भर के किसी भी पुलिस स्टेशन में दर्ज किया जा सकता है, चाहे अपराध कहीं भी हुआ हो। इसे यह सुनिश्चित करने के लिए डिज़ाइन किया गया है कि गंभीर, संज्ञेय अपराधों के पीड़ितों को तत्काल पुलिस हस्तक्षेप की मांग करते समय अधिकार क्षेत्र (Jurisdiction) की बाधाओं का सामना न करना पड़े। दर्ज होने के बाद, इसे "0" (शून्य) क्रमांक दिया जाता है और बाद में संबंधित पुलिस स्टेशन में स्थानांतरित कर दिया जाता है।

## Definition
**English:** A Zero FIR refers to an FIR that is registered at a police station irrespective of the area where the offence was committed. The legal mandate dictates that the police cannot refuse to register an FIR of a cognizable offence citing lack of territorial jurisdiction. It ensures the legal machinery is set in motion immediately.
**Hindi Unicode:** जीरो एफआईआर उस एफआईआर को कहते हैं जो उस क्षेत्र की परवाह किए बिना किसी पुलिस स्टेशन में दर्ज की जाती है जहां अपराध किया गया था। कानूनी जनादेश यह निर्धारित करता है कि पुलिस क्षेत्रीय अधिकार क्षेत्र की कमी का हवाला देते हुए किसी संज्ञेय अपराध की एफआईआर दर्ज करने से इनकार नहीं कर सकती है।

## Why It Matters

## Legal Background
The legal framework governing FIRs in India has been modernized with the transition from the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), 1973 to the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023.

- **Purpose:** To prevent the loss of time in jurisdictional disputes among police stations, thereby ensuring prompt investigation and the preservation of evidence.
- **Applicable Acts:**
  - Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023.
  - Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023 (for substantive offences).
- **Relevant Sections:**
  - **Section 173 of BNSS, 2023:** This section corresponds to the old Section 154 of the CrPC. It explicitly mandates that every information relating to the commission of a cognizable offence, irrespective of the area where the offence is committed, may be given orally or by electronic communication to an officer in charge of a police station. This provides strict statutory backing to the concept of Zero FIR.
  - **Section 175 of BNSS, 2023:** Deals with police officers' power to investigate cognizable cases, empowering them to act upon transferred Zero FIRs immediately.
- **History & Precedent:** The concept gained massive prominence after the Justice J.S. Verma Committee recommendations following the 2012 Delhi gang-rape case. The Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) issued strict advisories in 2013, 2015, and 2020 mandating the registration of Zero FIRs.

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** Any individual (victim, witness, or person with knowledge of the crime) reporting a cognizable offence anywhere in India.
- **Who is exempt:** It does not apply to non-cognizable offences (minor offences), where the police are required to enter the information in the daily diary (NC Register) and direct the complainant to the Magistrate.
- **Special conditions:** The police station recording the Zero FIR must transfer it to the jurisdictional police station under intimation to the complainant. In transit, they can take preliminary steps (like sending a victim for a medical exam or securing a crime scene).

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Approach the Police Station:** Visit the nearest police station immediately after a cognizable offence occurs.
2. **Narrate the Incident:** Clearly state the facts to the duty officer/Station House Officer (SHO). Emphasize that the crime is cognizable.
3. **Demand Zero FIR:** If the officer claims lack of jurisdiction, politely remind them of their statutory duty under Section 173 of BNSS, 2023, to register a Zero FIR.
4. **Drafting:** The officer writes down the information. No regular serial number is assigned; it is marked '0'.
5. **Read and Sign:** Read the drafted report carefully and sign it.
6. **Obtain Copy:** Collect your free copy of the Zero FIR.
7. **Transfer:** The police station will forward the Zero FIR to the correct jurisdictional police station through official channels.
8. **Follow up:** Contact the jurisdictional police station a few days later to get the regular FIR number (assigned by them) and updates on the investigation.

## Required Documents
- Any form of written complaint detailing the incident.
- Identification proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID) - optional but recommended.
- Medical reports, if immediate medical assistance was sought prior to reporting.
- Digital evidence (photos, videos) if available.

## Government Authorities
- **Police Station (Registering):** Responsible for registering the Zero FIR and immediate medical/evidence assistance.
- **Police Station (Jurisdictional):** Responsible for registering the regular FIR based on the transferred Zero FIR and conducting the complete investigation.
- **Superintendent of Police (SP) / DCP:** Can be approached if the SHO refuses to register a Zero FIR.
- **Magistrate Court:** Under BNSS, a Magistrate can order the police to register an FIR if they fail to do their duty.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **For Citizens:** Filing a false Zero FIR carries the same penalties as a regular false FIR (Section 217 of BNS - False charge of offence made with intent to injure).
- **For Police Officers:** Refusal to register an FIR for a cognizable offence, especially involving women or vulnerable sections, can lead to departmental action and criminal prosecution under Section 166A of the BNS (Public servant disobeying direction under law).

## Exceptions
- **Non-Cognizable Offences:** Zero FIR does not apply to non-cognizable (less serious) offences. The police will only record it in the non-cognizable register (NCR) and direct you to the magistrate.
- **Civil Disputes:** Financial disputes without an element of cheating/fraud, property boundaries, etc., cannot be registered as Zero FIRs.

## Important Timelines
- **Filing:** Must be filed as quickly as possible after the crime. Delay in filing must be logically explained to prevent suspicion of an afterthought.
- **Transfer:** Usually, the registering police station transfers the Zero FIR within 24-48 hours to the jurisdictional police station.
- **Action:** The jurisdictional police station must register it and initiate investigation immediately upon receipt.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Thinking you must travel back to the city where the crime occurred to report it.
- Confusing a Zero FIR with an e-FIR (Zero FIR requires physical presence or formal communication for serious crimes; e-FIRs are generally for non-heinous crimes/lost items).
- Failing to collect the free copy of the Zero FIR.
- Not following up with the jurisdictional police station after the transfer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. What is the main difference between a regular FIR and a Zero FIR?**
**English:** A regular FIR is filed at the police station that holds territorial jurisdiction over the crime scene and is given a sequential serial number. A Zero FIR is filed at a police station outside the jurisdiction and is numbered "0" before being transferred to the correct station.
**Hindi:** नियमित एफआईआर उस पुलिस स्टेशन में दर्ज की जाती है जिसके अधिकार क्षेत्र में अपराध हुआ है और उसे एक क्रमिक क्रमांक दिया जाता है। जीरो एफआईआर अधिकार क्षेत्र से बाहर के स्टेशन में दर्ज की जाती है और इसे सही स्टेशन पर स्थानांतरित करने से पहले "0" नंबर दिया जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Regular FIR us police station mein hoti hai jiska area hota hai aur use ek serial number milta hai. Zero FIR area se bahar darj hoti hai aur transfer hone se pehle ise "0" number diya jata hai.

**Q2. Can the police refuse to register a Zero FIR?**
**English:** No. Under Section 173 of the BNSS, 2023, the police are legally mandated to register information about a cognizable offence regardless of jurisdiction. Refusal can lead to severe departmental and penal action against the officer.
**Hindi:** नहीं। बीएनएसएस, 2023 की धारा 173 के तहत, पुलिस अधिकार क्षेत्र की परवाह किए बिना संज्ञेय अपराध की जानकारी दर्ज करने के लिए कानूनी रूप से बाध्य है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. BNSS, 2023 ke Section 173 ke under, police kisi bhi cognizable crime ki info darj karne ke liye legally bound hai. Inkaar karne par officer par action ho sakta hai.

**Q3. Is Zero FIR only for crimes against women?**
**English:** No, this is a common misconception. While it gained prominence after the 2012 Nirbhaya case, a Zero FIR can be filed for any cognizable offence (like murder, theft, fatal accidents), regardless of the victim's gender.
**Hindi:** नहीं, यह एक आम गलतफहमी है। यह किसी भी संज्ञेय अपराध (जैसे हत्या, चोरी, दुर्घटना) के लिए दायर की जा सकती है, चाहे पीड़ित का लिंग कुछ भी हो।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, yeh ek common galatfehmi hai. Yeh kisi bhi cognizable crime (jaise murder, chori, accident) ke liye file ki ja sakti hai, chahe victim ka gender kuch bhi ho.

**Q4. What should I do if the SHO refuses a Zero FIR?**
**English:** If the Station House Officer refuses, you can escalate the matter by sending the complaint in writing (via registered post) to the Superintendent of Police (SP) or Deputy Commissioner of Police (DCP). You can also approach a Magistrate.
**Hindi:** यदि एसएचओ मना करता है, तो आप पुलिस अधीक्षक (एसपी) या पुलिस उपायुक्त (डीसीपी) को लिखित रूप में (पंजीकृत डाक के माध्यम से) शिकायत भेज सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Agar SHO mana kare, toh aap SP ya DCP ko written mein (registered post se) complaint bhej sakte hain. Aap Magistrate ke paas bhi ja sakte hain.

**Q5. Can a Zero FIR be filed online?**
**English:** Filing an FIR entirely online (e-FIR) is usually restricted to specific non-heinous crimes (like vehicle theft or lost property) in certain states. For serious cognizable crimes requiring a Zero FIR, physical reporting or formal communication is generally required, though state-specific citizen portals might offer preliminary reporting features.
**Hindi:** पूरी तरह से ऑनलाइन एफआईआर (ई-एफआईआर) आमतौर पर कुछ राज्यों में विशिष्ट गैर-जघन्य अपराधों तक ही सीमित है। गंभीर अपराधों के लिए भौतिक उपस्थिति आवश्यक है।
**Hinglish:** Online e-FIR usually sirf chhote crimes ke liye hoti hai. Serious crimes ke liye Zero FIR police station jaakar hi karni padti hai.

**Q6. Does a Zero FIR have a time limit?**
**English:** There is no specific time limit to file a Zero FIR, but all FIRs should be filed as soon as possible. Unexplained delays can weaken the case, as courts often suspect that a delayed FIR is an afterthought or fabricated.
**Hindi:** कोई विशिष्ट समय सीमा नहीं है, लेकिन जितनी जल्दी हो सके एफआईआर दर्ज की जानी चाहिए। अस्पष्ट देरी मामले को कमजोर कर सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Koi specific time limit nahi hai, par FIR jaldi se jaldi darj karni chahiye. Bina wajah ki delay se case kamzor ho sakta hai.

**Q7. What happens after a Zero FIR is transferred?**
**English:** Once the Zero FIR reaches the jurisdictional police station, they register a fresh, regular FIR with a new serial number based on the contents of the Zero FIR. They then commence the formal investigation.
**Hindi:** एक बार जब जीरो एफआईआर संबंधित पुलिस स्टेशन में पहुंच जाती है, तो वे एक नई नियमित एफआईआर दर्ज करते हैं और औपचारिक जांच शुरू करते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Jab Zero FIR sahi police station pahunchti hai, toh wo log ek nayi regular FIR naye number ke sath register karte hain aur investigation shuru karte hain.

**Q8. Can the police arrest a suspect based on a Zero FIR?**
**English:** Usually, the registering police station forwards the matter. However, if the situation is critical and the suspect is likely to abscond, the police can take preventive action, secure the suspect, or perform an immediate medical examination before transferring the case.
**Hindi:** आमतौर पर, मामला आगे बढ़ाया जाता है। हालांकि, यदि स्थिति गंभीर है, तो पुलिस मामले को स्थानांतरित करने से पहले तत्काल कार्रवाई कर सकती है या संदिग्ध को सुरक्षित कर सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Usually, matter aage bheja jata hai. Par agar situation serious hai, toh police turant action le sakti hai aur suspect ko arrest bhi kar sakti hai case transfer hone se pehle.

**Q9. Do I need a lawyer to file a Zero FIR?**
**English:** Absolutely not. The procedure is straightforward, and any citizen can walk into a police station and report a cognizable crime. A lawyer is not required for the registration of an FIR.
**Hindi:** बिल्कुल नहीं। प्रक्रिया सीधी है, और कोई भी नागरिक पुलिस स्टेशन जाकर शिकायत दर्ज करा सकता है। वकील की आवश्यकता नहीं है।
**Hinglish:** Bilkul nahi. Procedure simple hai, aur koi bhi citizen police station jakar complain kar sakta hai. Lawyer ki zaroorat nahi hoti.

**Q10. Can I file a Zero FIR for a lost mobile phone?**
**English:** No. A lost mobile phone is generally not a cognizable offence (unless it is a case of robbery or theft by force). For lost items, you file a Missing Article Report or an NCR, often available online in many states.
**Hindi:** नहीं। खोया हुआ मोबाइल फोन आमतौर पर संज्ञेय अपराध नहीं है। इसके लिए आप मिसिंग आर्टिकल रिपोर्ट दर्ज कराते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. Khoya hua phone usually cognizable crime nahi hai. Iske liye aap Missing Article Report (NCR) file karte hain, jo online bhi ho jati hai.

**Q11. Is there any fee to register a Zero FIR?**
**English:** No, the registration of any FIR, including a Zero FIR, is completely free of charge. The police are also required to provide you with a free copy immediately.
**Hindi:** नहीं, किसी भी एफआईआर का पंजीकरण पूरी तरह से निःशुल्क है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, koi bhi FIR darj karwana bilkul free hai. Police ko aapko ek free copy bhi deni hoti hai.

**Q12. What if the police say I should go to the correct station because it's only 5 km away?**
**English:** Distance is irrelevant. Section 173 of BNSS legally mandates them to register the FIR irrespective of jurisdiction. You must insist on a Zero FIR, citing the law, especially in emergency situations.
**Hindi:** दूरी अप्रासंगिक है। बीएनएसएस की धारा 173 उन्हें अधिकार क्षेत्र की परवाह किए बिना एफआईआर दर्ज करने का आदेश देती है।
**Hinglish:** Distance se farq nahi padta. BNSS ka Section 173 unhe jurisdiction ke bina FIR darj karne ka order deta hai. Aapko Zero FIR ke liye insist karna chahiye.

**Q13. How do I track a Zero FIR once transferred?**
**English:** Ensure you take the diary number, dispatch number, and the details of the police station where it is being transferred. You can then contact or visit the jurisdictional police station to get the new FIR number.
**Hindi:** सुनिश्चित करें कि आप डायरी नंबर, प्रेषण नंबर और उस पुलिस स्टेशन का विवरण लें जहां इसे स्थानांतरित किया जा रहा है।
**Hinglish:** Dispatch number aur us police station ki details le lein jahan case transfer ho raha hai. Phir wahan contact karke naya FIR number lein.

**Q14. Can a Zero FIR be cancelled?**
**English:** Only the High Court can quash an FIR (including one originating as a Zero FIR) under Section 528 of the BNSS (inherent powers). The police themselves cannot 'cancel' it once registered, though they can file a closure report if no evidence is found.
**Hindi:** केवल उच्च न्यायालय ही बीएनएसएस की धारा 528 के तहत एफआईआर को रद्द कर सकता है। पुलिस स्वयं इसे 'रद्द' नहीं कर सकती।
**Hinglish:** Sirf High Court hi FIR ko quash (cancel) kar sakta hai. Police khud register hone ke baad ise cancel nahi kar sakti, par evidence na milne par closure report file kar sakti है.

**Q15. Does the registering police station investigate the crime at all?**
**English:** Their role is usually limited to preliminary, emergency steps—such as preserving the crime scene, facilitating medical exams, and recording the first version of events—before transferring the case to the jurisdictional station which conducts the detailed investigation.
**Hindi:** उनकी भूमिका आमतौर पर प्रारंभिक, आपातकालीन कदमों तक सीमित होती है, इससे पहले कि मामला संबंधित स्टेशन को स्थानांतरित किया जाए जो विस्तृत जांच करता है।
**Hinglish:** Unka role usually preliminary emergency steps tak limited hota hai, jaise crime scene bachana ya medical checkup karwana. Baki detailed investigation sahi police station karta hai.

**Q16. Will I be intimidated or harassed for filing a Zero FIR?**
**English:** Law strictly prohibits police harassment. If faced with intimidation, citizens should report the officer's conduct to senior officers (SP/DCP) or the State Police Complaints Authority.
**Hindi:** कानून पुलिस उत्पीड़न को सख्ती से रोकता है। यदि कोई अधिकारी डराता है, तो नागरिकों को वरिष्ठ अधिकारियों या राज्य पुलिस शिकायत प्राधिकरण को इसकी रिपोर्ट करनी चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Kanoon police harassment ko roktak hai. Agar koi daraye, toh citizens ko senior officers ya State Police Complaints Authority ko report karni chahiye.

**Q17. What documents should I carry when demanding a Zero FIR?**
**English:** A written complaint detailing the crime, time, location, and a valid ID proof (like Aadhaar) are helpful. However, a police officer cannot refuse to record a verbal complaint if no written document is presented.
**Hindi:** अपराध का विवरण देने वाली एक लिखित शिकायत और एक वैध आईडी प्रमाण सहायक होते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Crime ki written complaint aur valid ID proof (jaise Aadhaar) helpful hote hain. Par police verbal complaint likhne se mana nahi kar sakti.

**Q18. Are Traffic Accidents eligible for Zero FIR?**
**English:** Yes, if an accident involves severe injury or death (making it a cognizable offence), you can file a Zero FIR at any nearby police station if the jurisdictional station is far or unknown.
**Hindi:** हां, यदि किसी दुर्घटना में गंभीर चोट या मृत्यु शामिल है (जो इसे एक संज्ञेय अपराध बनाता है), तो आप जीरो एफआईआर दर्ज करा सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Haan, agar accident mein serious chot aayi hai ya death hui hai, toh aap kisi bhi paas ke police station mein Zero FIR darj karwa sakte hain.

**Q19. Is the Zero FIR concept recognized all over India?**
**English:** Yes, the BNSS applies to all states and union territories in India, making the concept of Zero FIR universally applicable across the country.
**Hindi:** हां, बीएनएसएस भारत के सभी राज्यों और केंद्र शासित प्रदेशों पर लागू होता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, BNSS India ke sabhi states aur union territories mein lagu hota hai, isliye Zero FIR poore desh mein valid hai.

**Q20. Can I file a Zero FIR days after the crime?**
**English:** Yes, you can, but any delay must be satisfactorily explained in your complaint (e.g., medical trauma, fear, lack of awareness). Unjustified delay is often used by the defense to argue that the case is false.
**Hindi:** हां, आप कर सकते हैं, लेकिन आपकी शिकायत में किसी भी देरी का संतोषजनक ढंग से स्पष्टीकरण दिया जाना चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Haan, kar sakte hain, par delay ka valid reason batana hoga (jaise trauma ya darr). Bina wajah delay se case par doubt hota hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Zero FIR is only for rape victims.
   **Fact:** It applies to ALL cognizable offences (murder, theft, kidnapping, etc.), regardless of the victim's gender.
2. **Myth:** Zero FIR is an online FIR.
   **Fact:** No, it is a physical FIR filed at a non-jurisdictional police station. e-FIR is a different concept.
3. **Myth:** The police station that registers the Zero FIR will investigate the whole case.
   **Fact:** They only take preliminary actions and transfer the FIR to the police station with actual jurisdiction.
4. **Myth:** A Zero FIR has less legal weight than a regular FIR.
   **Fact:** It holds the exact same legal weight as a regular FIR under Section 173 of the BNSS.
5. **Myth:** You must pay a fee to get a Zero FIR transferred.
   **Fact:** FIR registration and transfer are completely free public services.
6. **Myth:** Non-cognizable crimes can also be registered as Zero FIRs.
   **Fact:** Only cognizable (serious) crimes can be registered as FIRs (including Zero FIRs).
7. **Myth:** You need a court order to file a Zero FIR.
   **Fact:** No court order is needed. It is your statutory right to file it directly at a police station.
8. **Myth:** If you don't know the exact location of the crime, you cannot file a Zero FIR.
   **Fact:** Zero FIR is designed precisely for situations where jurisdiction is unclear or crime occurred during transit (e.g., on a train).
9. **Myth:** A Zero FIR gets cancelled if it takes too long to transfer.
   **Fact:** It does not get cancelled. The legal obligation remains until it is officially registered at the jurisdictional station.
10. **Myth:** Only the victim can file a Zero FIR.
    **Fact:** Anyone with knowledge of the cognizable crime (witness, relative, friend) can file the FIR.

## Conversation Examples

**Example 1: Jurisdiction Refusal**
**Citizen:** Inspector saab, mera bag train mein chori ho gaya jab train Kanpur cross kar rahi thi. Main abhi Delhi mein utra hoon.
**Assistant:** Chori ek cognizable offence hai. Aap Delhi ke railway police station mein 'Zero FIR' darj karwa sakte hain. Unhe bataiye ki Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita ki dhara 173 ke tehat wo jurisdiction ke bina FIR darj karne ke liye badhya hain.

**Example 2: Police Demanding to go to another station**
**Citizen:** The police officer here says the crime happened 2 kilometers away in the next sector, which falls under a different station. They won't register my complaint.
**Assistant:** They cannot refuse. Politely inform them that you wish to register a 'Zero FIR' immediately due to the urgency of the matter. If they still refuse, you can send your written complaint to the DCP/SP of that area via registered post.

**Example 3: Medical Emergency Context**
**Citizen:** Accident hua hai highway par, hum victim ko paas ke hospital laye hain. Local thana keh raha hai accident unke area mein nahi hua.
**Assistant:** Aise serious accident mein turant police action zaroori hai. Local thana mana nahi kar sakta. Unse 'Zero FIR' darj karne aur hospital aakar preliminary statement lene ka anurodh karein. Yeh unki legal duty hai.

**Example 4: Woman Harassment on Bus**
**Citizen:** My sister was harassed on a bus traveling between two cities. We don't know the exact highway location where it happened.
**Assistant:** That is exactly why the Zero FIR exists. You can go to the police station in your destination city. They will register the Zero FIR and later figure out the jurisdiction to transfer the case.

**Example 5: Is it free?**
**Citizen:** Police wale zero FIR file karne ke liye charge maang rahe hain, keh rahe hain post se bhejna padega dusre thane.
**Assistant:** Yeh bilkul gair-kanooni hai. FIR (chahe regular ho ya Zero) darj karna aur transfer karna bilkul muft (free) hai. Aap aisi demand ki shikayat unke senior officer (SP ya DCP) se ya anti-corruption helpline par kar sakte hain.

**Example 6: Delay in transferring**
**Citizen:** Maine 5 din pehle Zero FIR darj karayi thi, par ab tak transfer nahi hui dusre thane mein.
**Assistant:** Aap us police station ke Station House Officer (SHO) se miliye jahan aapne Zero FIR darj karayi thi aur dispatch details maangiye. Agar wo madad nahi karte, toh DCP/SP office mein written complaint dijiye ki transfer mein deri ho rahi hai.

**Example 7: Knowing the new FIR number**
**Citizen:** Mujhe kaise pata chalega ki meri Zero FIR transfer hone ke baad uska naya number kya hai?
**Assistant:** Jab Zero FIR sahi thane mein pahunchti hai, toh wo use ek naya (regular) FIR number dete hain. Aap us jurisdictional thane mein call karke ya personally visit karke apni Zero FIR ki copy dikha kar naya number le sakte hain.

**Example 8: E-FIR vs Zero FIR**
**Citizen:** Can I just file a Zero FIR online for a murder case?
**Assistant:** No, severe crimes like murder require immediate physical intervention. E-FIRs are generally for non-heinous crimes like vehicle theft. For a murder case, you must physically go to a police station to file a Zero FIR so they can secure evidence immediately.

**Example 9: Civil disputes**
**Citizen:** Mera tenant rent nahi de raha aur makan khali nahi kar raha. Main dusre shahar mein hoon, kya wahan Zero FIR karu?
**Assistant:** Nahi, rent ka vivad (dispute) ek civil matter hai, cognizable crime nahi. Zero FIR sirf serious criminal offences ke liye hoti hai. Aapko apne property wale area ke civil court mein eviction suit file karna hoga.

**Example 10: Missing person**
**Citizen:** Mera bhai kal se laapata hai. Hum abhi dusre state mein relatives ke yahan hain.
**Assistant:** Aap turant paas ke police station mein 'Missing Person' ki report darj karwaiye. Agar kidnapping ka shaq (suspicion) hai, toh aap wahan Zero FIR bhi darj karwa sakte hain, jo baad mein sahi thane ko bhej di jayegi.

**Example 11: Minor scuffle**
**Citizen:** Padosi ne gaali galoch ki, mujhe Zero FIR karni hai jurisdiction se bahar.
**Assistant:** Gaali galoch aamtaur par Non-Cognizable offence (NCR) mein aata hai. Zero FIR sirf cognizable (gambhir) apradhon ke liye hoti hai. Aapko magistrate ke paas ya sahi jurisdiction wale thane mein shikayat karni hogi.

**Example 12: Need for lawyer**
**Citizen:** Do I need to take a lawyer with me to force the police to write a Zero FIR?
**Assistant:** While a lawyer can help articulate your case, it is not legally required. Any citizen can demand a Zero FIR. If you face resistance, mentioning Section 173 of the BNSS and the Supreme Court guidelines usually suffices.

**Example 13: Time limit on transfer**
**Citizen:** How much time does the police have to transfer a Zero FIR?
**Assistant:** Although there isn't a strict hourly limit in the statute, police protocols mandate that the Zero FIR should be forwarded to the jurisdictional police station "forthwith" or as soon as practicable, typically within 24 to 48 hours.

**Example 14: Suspect fleeing**
**Citizen:** Agar main Zero FIR karun aur jurisdiction wale thane ko transfer hone tak criminal bhaag gaya toh?
**Assistant:** Zero FIR darj karne wala thana preliminary action le sakta hai. Agar unhe lagta hai ki suspect bhaag jayega, toh wo transfer se pehle preventive arrest ya detention kar sakte hain.

**Example 15: Cybercrime jurisdiction**
**Citizen:** Mera bank account hack ho gaya aur paise nikal gaye. Main kis thane mein jau?
**Assistant:** Cyber crimes ke liye jurisdiction ka mudda alag hota hai. Aap iski shikayat online National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) par kar sakte hain. Agar aap physically thane jaate hain, toh wo IT Act ke under turant FIR darj karne ke liye badhya hain, jise Zero FIR ki tarah treat kiya ja sakta hai.
