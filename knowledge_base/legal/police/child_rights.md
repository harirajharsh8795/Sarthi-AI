# Child Rights and Juvenile Justice Policies in India

## Overview
**English:** In India, child rights encompass a broad spectrum of legal protections designed to ensure the safety, survival, and holistic development of individuals under the age of 18. These rights are deeply embedded in the Constitution of India and specialized legislations such as the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, and the POCSO Act, 2012. The objective is to shield children from exploitation, abuse, and arbitrary legal penalization, while focusing on reformative rather than punitive justice for minors who conflict with the law.
**Hindi (हिंदी):** भारत में, बाल अधिकारों में 18 वर्ष से कम आयु के व्यक्तियों की सुरक्षा, अस्तित्व और समग्र विकास सुनिश्चित करने के लिए डिज़ाइन किए गए कानूनी संरक्षणों का एक व्यापक स्पेक्ट्रम शामिल है। ये अधिकार भारत के संविधान और किशोर न्याय (बच्चों की देखभाल और संरक्षण) अधिनियम, 2015 और पॉक्सो (POCSO) अधिनियम, 2012 जैसे विशेष कानूनों में गहराई से अंतर्निहित हैं। उद्देश्य बच्चों को शोषण, दुर्व्यवहार और मनमाने कानूनी दंड से बचाना है, जबकि कानून के साथ संघर्ष करने वाले नाबालिगों के लिए दंडात्मक के बजाय सुधारात्मक न्याय पर ध्यान केंद्रित करना है।

Iska main aim bacchon ko exploitation aur abuse se bachana hai, aur agar koi minor crime karta hai toh usko punish karne ki bajay reform (sudhaar) karne par focus karna hai.

## Definition
**English:** A "Child" according to Indian legal jurisprudence (specifically Section 2(12) of the JJ Act, 2015) is defined as a person who has not completed eighteen years of age. A "Juvenile" refers to a child below eighteen years who is alleged to have committed an offence. Child Rights refer to the guaranteed statutory, constitutional, and human rights ensuring their welfare.
**Hindi (हिंदी):** भारतीय कानूनी न्यायशास्त्र (विशेष रूप से जेजे अधिनियम, 2015 की धारा 2 (12)) के अनुसार "बच्चा" उसे परिभाषित किया गया है जिसने अठारह वर्ष की आयु पूरी नहीं की है। एक "किशोर" का तात्पर्य अठारह वर्ष से कम उम्र के बच्चे से है जिस पर अपराध करने का आरोप है। बाल अधिकारों का तात्पर्य उनके कल्याण को सुनिश्चित करने वाले गारंटीकृत वैधानिक, संवैधानिक और मानवाधिकारों से है।
**Hinglish:** Indian law (JJ Act 2015, Section 2(12)) ke mutabiq, "Child" wo hai jiski umar 18 saal se kam hai. "Juvenile" ka matlab us bacche se hai jisne koi crime kiya ho. Child Rights ka matlab un guaranteed legal aur constitutional rights se hai jo unki safety aur well-being ensure karte hain.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Protecting child rights is critical because children are the most vulnerable demographic. Proper legal implementation ensures they are not tried as adults (except in specific heinous cases for 16-18 year-olds), preventing their exploitation in standard prisons. It aims to rehabilitate and integrate them back into society safely.
**Hindi (हिंदी):** बाल अधिकारों की रक्षा करना महत्वपूर्ण है क्योंकि बच्चे सबसे संवेदनशील जनसांख्यिकीय हैं। उचित कानूनी कार्यान्वयन यह सुनिश्चित करता है कि उन पर वयस्कों की तरह मुकदमा नहीं चलाया जाता है (16-18 वर्ष के बच्चों के लिए विशिष्ट जघन्य मामलों को छोड़कर), मानक जेलों में उनके शोषण को रोकना। इसका उद्देश्य उन्हें सुरक्षित रूप से समाज में पुनर्वास और एकीकृत करना है।
**Hinglish:** Child rights ko protect karna isliye zaroori hai kyunki bacche sabse vulnerable hote hain. ## Legal Background
### Constitutional Articles
- **Article 15(3):** Empowers the State to make special provisions for women and children.
- **Article 21A:** Right to free and compulsory education for all children aged 6 to 14 years.
- **Article 24:** Prohibition of employment of children in factories, mines, or hazardous environments below the age of 14.
- **Article 39(e) & (f):** Directs the State to protect children from abuse, exploitation, and ensure they develop in a healthy manner.

### Applicable Acts & Sections
- **Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015:** Primary framework for CNCP and CCL.
- **Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012:** Special law to deal with sexual abuse against minors.
- **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS, 2023):** Replaces IPC. Contains provisions like kidnapping, child trafficking, and specific protections. (e.g., Section 96 BNS replaces Section 363 IPC for kidnapping).
- **Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS, 2023):** Replaces CrPC. Governs the procedural aspects. Under BNSS, specific care is mandated during the arrest and interrogation of minors.
- **Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Amendment Act, 2016:** Bans all child labour under 14 years.

## Applicability
- **Covered:** Any individual who has not completed 18 years of age at the time of the commission of the offence or when seeking protection.
- **Exempt:** Minors cannot be sentenced to death or life imprisonment without the possibility of release.
- **Special Conditions:** Under the JJ Act 2015 Amendment, a child aged between 16 to 18 years who commits a "Heinous Offence" (crimes with a minimum punishment of 7 years or more) may be tried as an adult, but ONLY after a preliminary assessment by the JJB regarding their mental and physical capacity to understand the consequences of their actions.

## Rights of Citizens (Children)
**English:** 
1. Right to Bail: Bail is a right for juveniles, regardless of whether the offence is bailable or non-bailable, unless release brings them into association with criminals.
2. Right to Privacy: Absolute prohibition on publishing the name, address, or photos of a child in conflict with the law or a child victim.
3. No Handcuffing: A juvenile cannot be handcuffed or tied up by the police.
4. Civil Clothes: Police handling juveniles must ideally be in plain clothes, not in uniform.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English:** Parents/Guardians have a duty to ensure the child attends legal proceedings when summoned, usually before the JJB or CWC, and to monitor the child's behavior if released on bail. The State has the responsibility to provide safe observation homes.
**Hindi (हिंदी):** माता-पिता/अभिभावकों का यह कर्तव्य है कि जब जेजेबी या सीडब्ल्यूसी के समक्ष बुलाया जाए, तो वे बच्चे की कानूनी कार्यवाही में उपस्थिति सुनिश्चित करें, और यदि जमानत पर रिहा किया जाता है तो बच्चे के व्यवहार की निगरानी करें।
**Hinglish:** Parents aur guardians ki duty hai ki CWC ya JJB dwara bulaye jane par bacche ko time par pesh karein. Agar baccha bail par bahar hai, toh uski activities par dhyan rakhna parents ki responsibility hai.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:**
1. **Apprehension:** If a minor commits an offence, they are apprehended (not 'arrested' in the traditional sense) by the Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU) or a designated Child Welfare Police Officer.
2. **Intimation:** The police must immediately inform the parents/guardians and the local Probation Officer.
3. **Production:** The child must be produced before the Juvenile Justice Board (JJB) within 24 hours. They cannot be kept in a regular police lock-up or standard jail.
4. **Assessment & Custody:** The JJB assesses the case. The child is either granted bail (handed over to parents) or sent to an Observation Home (never a jail).
5. **Inquiry:** The JJB conducts a child-friendly inquiry, usually completing it within 4 to 6 months.
**Hindi (हिंदी):**
1. **पकड़ना:** यदि कोई नाबालिग अपराध करता है, तो उसे विशेष किशोर पुलिस इकाई (SJPU) द्वारा पकड़ा जाता है (पारंपरिक अर्थों में 'गिरफ्तार' नहीं किया जाता)।
2. **सूचना:** पुलिस को तुरंत माता-पिता और परिवीक्षा अधिकारी को सूचित करना चाहिए।
3. **प्रस्तुति:** बच्चे को 24 घंटे के भीतर किशोर न्याय बोर्ड (JJB) के समक्ष पेश किया जाना चाहिए। उन्हें नियमित पुलिस लॉक-अप में नहीं रखा जा सकता।
4. **आकलन और हिरासत:** जेजेबी मामले का आकलन करता है। बच्चे को या तो जमानत दी जाती है या बाल सुधार गृह भेजा जाता है (जेल कभी नहीं)।
5. **पूछताछ:** जेजेबी एक बाल-सुलभ पूछताछ करता है, जो आमतौर पर 4 से 6 महीने में पूरी होती है।
**Hinglish:**
1. **Apprehension:** Crime ke baad, minor ko Special Juvenile Police (SJPU) pakadti hai. Inhe normal criminals ki tarah arrest nahi kehte.
2. **Intimation:** Police turant parents aur Probation Officer ko inform karti hai.
3. **Production:** Bacche ko 24 ghante ke andar Juvenile Justice Board (JJB) ke samne pesh karna hota hai. Unhe normal lock-up mein nahi rakhte.
4. **Custody:** JJB decide karta hai ki bail deni hai ya Observation Home bhejna hai. Jail kabhi nahi bhej sakte.
5. **Inquiry:** JJB apni inquiry 4-6 mahine mein complete karti hai, jo ekdum child-friendly environment mein hoti hai.

## Required Documents
- Birth Certificate, Aadhar Card, or School ID to prove age (Age determination is the crucial first step).
- FIR/GD entry copy.
- Social Investigation Report by Probation Officer.
- Undertaking bond by parents/guardians for bail.

## Government Authorities
1. **Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU):** Designated police officers in every station to handle minors.
2. **Juvenile Justice Board (JJB):** Consists of a Metropolitan Magistrate and two social workers. Deals with Children in Conflict with Law (CCL).
3. **Child Welfare Committee (CWC):** Deals with Children in Need of Care and Protection (CNCP) like abandoned, lost, or abused children.
4. **District Child Protection Unit (DCPU):** Monitors the implementation of the JJ Act at the district level.

## Penalties & Punishments
(Officially defined under JJ Act 2015 and BNS)
- **Cruelty to a Child (Sec 75 JJ Act):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine up to Rs 1 Lakh. If it causes mental/physical incapacitation, 3-10 years imprisonment.
- **Employment of Child for Begging (Sec 76 JJ Act):** Imprisonment up to 5 years and fine up to Rs 1 Lakh.
- **Using a Child for Vending, Smuggling Drugs (Sec 78 JJ Act):** Rigorous imprisonment up to 7 years and fine up to Rs 1 Lakh.
- **Corporal Punishment (Sec 82 JJ Act):** Fine of Rs 10,000 for first offence, dismissal from service for subsequent.
- **Minor committing an offence:** Will NOT get death penalty or life imprisonment. Maximum punishment is sending to a Special Home for up to 3 years (unless tried as an adult for heinous crimes aged 16-18).

## Exceptions
- If a person was a juvenile at the time of committing the offence, but is caught after turning 18, they will still be treated as a juvenile under the JJ Act and cannot be sent to an adult prison.
- Age is calculated precisely as of the date of the offence, not the date of apprehension.

## Important Timelines
- **Production:** Must be produced before JJB/CWC within 24 hours of apprehension.
- **Social Investigation Report:** Must be filed by Probation Officer within 15 days.
- **Inquiry Completion:** Non-heinous cases must be completed by JJB within 4 months (extendable by 2 months).
- **Preliminary Assessment (16-18 heinous):** Must be completed within 3 months.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- **Faking Age:** Parents often try to produce fake birth certificates to claim juvenility. The JJB conducts strict age determination tests (ossification tests) if documents are suspicious.
- **Hiding Medical Conditions:** Not informing the JJB about a child's psychiatric or medical history during apprehension.
- **Media Naming:** Media houses or citizens illegally naming a juvenile offender on social media, violating Section 74 of JJ Act.

## Safety Tips
**English:** Always ensure your child is aware of Good Touch and Bad Touch. Do not leave minors unattended with distant acquaintances. Educate them about cyber safety, online grooming, and the POSH/POCSO laws. If police detain a minor, immediately demand to see the Child Welfare Police Officer.

Agar police kisi minor ko detain karti hai, toh turant Child Welfare Police Officer se milne ki demand karein aur unhe normal lockup mein rakhne ka virodh karein.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Can a 17-year-old be sent to a regular jail for murder?**
**English:** Under normal circumstances, no. However, if the JJB conducts a preliminary assessment and determines the 17-year-old had the mental capacity to commit a heinous offence, they can be transferred to a Children's Court and potentially tried as an adult. Until age 21, they remain in a Place of Safety, after which they may be transferred to a standard jail.
**Hindi:** सामान्य परिस्थितियों में, नहीं। हालांकि, यदि जेजेबी प्रारंभिक मूल्यांकन करता है और पाता है कि 17 वर्षीय किशोर में जघन्य अपराध करने की मानसिक क्षमता थी, तो उन पर वयस्क के रूप में मुकदमा चलाया जा सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Normally nahi. Lekin agar JJB assessment mein paati hai ki crime heinous tha aur minor ko apne action ka pura andaza tha, toh us par adult court mein case chal sakta hai.

**Q2: Can police handcuff a child?**
**English:** Absolutely not. The law strictly prohibits handcuffing, tying with a rope, or using physical force against any child in conflict with the law under any circumstances.
**Hindi:** बिल्कुल नहीं। कानून किसी भी परिस्थिति में कानून के साथ संघर्ष करने वाले किसी भी बच्चे को हथकड़ी लगाने या रस्सी से बांधने पर सख्त रोक लगाता है।
**Hinglish:** Bilkul nahi. Law strictly ban karta hai ki kisi bhi bacche ko hathkadi lagayi jaye ya rassi se bandha jaye.

**Q3: Is bail guaranteed for a juvenile?**
**English:** Yes, under Section 12 of the JJ Act, bail is a right for a juvenile regardless of the severity of the offence. The only exception is if releasing the child would bring them into association with criminals or expose them to moral danger.
**Hindi:** हां, जेजे अधिनियम की धारा 12 के तहत, अपराध की गंभीरता की परवाह किए बिना किशोर के लिए जमानत एक अधिकार है। अपवाद केवल तब है जब रिहाई से बच्चा अपराधियों के संपर्क में आ सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, JJ Act ke Section 12 ke tahat minor ko bail milna uska right hai, chahe crime kitna bhi bada ho. Sirf tab bail rukti hai agar bahar aane se bacche ko kisi criminal gang ka khatra ho.

**Q4: Where is a minor kept if bail is denied?**
**English:** If bail is denied, the minor is sent to an Observation Home, not a police lock-up or a regular jail. The Observation Home focuses on counselling and education.
**Hindi:** यदि जमानत से इनकार किया जाता है, तो नाबालिग को सुधार गृह (ऑब्जर्वेशन होम) भेजा जाता है, पुलिस लॉक-अप या नियमित जेल में नहीं।
**Hinglish:** Agar bail deny hoti hai, toh minor ko Observation Home bheja jata hai. Wahan unhe lock-up mein nahi balki counselling aur education di jati hai.

**Q5: What is a Zero FIR in child abuse cases?**
**English:** A Zero FIR allows a victim (or their guardian) to file a police complaint at any police station regardless of jurisdiction. This is especially vital in POCSO cases to ensure immediate action is taken.
**Hindi:** एक जीरो एफआईआर पीड़िता (या उनके अभिभावक) को अधिकार क्षेत्र की परवाह किए बिना किसी भी पुलिस स्टेशन में शिकायत दर्ज करने की अनुमति देती है।
**Hinglish:** Zero FIR ka matlab hai ki aap kisi bhi police station mein case file kar sakte hain, chahe crime kahin bhi hua ho. POCSO cases mein ye turant action ke liye zaroori hai.

**Q6: Are juvenile court proceedings open to the public?**
**English:** No. JJB proceedings are strictly confidential and in-camera. Only the child, their parents/guardians, lawyers, and board members are allowed.
**Hindi:** नहीं। जेजेबी की कार्यवाही पूरी तरह से गोपनीय होती है। केवल बच्चा, माता-पिता, वकील और बोर्ड के सदस्यों को अनुमति है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi. JJB ki proceedings in-camera (band kamre mein) hoti hain. Public ya media ko allow nahi kiya jata.

**Q7: How is the age of a juvenile determined?**
**English:** Age is determined primarily through matriculation certificates, birth certificates, or school records. If these are absent or disputed, a medical ossification test (bone test) is conducted by a medical board.
**Hindi:** आयु का निर्धारण मुख्य रूप से जन्म प्रमाण पत्र या स्कूल के रिकॉर्ड के माध्यम से किया जाता है। यदि ये नहीं हैं, तो मेडिकल ओसिफिकेशन टेस्ट (हड्डी परीक्षण) किया जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Umar pata karne ke liye birth certificate ya 10th ki marksheet dekhi jati hai. Agar paper nahi hain, toh doctors bone test (ossification) karke umar batate hain.

**Q8: Can a minor's identity be revealed in the news?**
**English:** No. Section 74 of the JJ Act and Section 23 of POCSO Act strictly prohibit the disclosure of the name, school, address, or photos of a minor victim or offender.
**Hindi:** नहीं। जेजे एक्ट और पॉक्सो एक्ट किसी भी नाबालिग पीड़ित या अपराधी की पहचान उजागर करने पर सख्त प्रतिबंध लगाते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, media ya news mein kisi bhi minor victim ya offender ka naam aur photo dikhana illegal hai.

**Q9: What happens if a minor commits a crime but is caught after turning 18?**
**English:** The relevant date is the date of the commission of the offence. If they were under 18 when the crime occurred, they will be treated as a juvenile and tried by the JJB.
**Hindi:** प्रासंगिक तिथि अपराध करने की तिथि है। यदि अपराध के समय वे 18 वर्ष से कम थे, तो उन्हें किशोर माना जाएगा।
**Hinglish:** Crime ke din ki umar dekhi jati hai. Agar crime karte waqt umar 18 se kam thi, toh arrest 20 saal mein hone par bhi JJB mein hi case chalega.

**Q10: Who deals with abandoned or runaway children?**
**English:** Abandoned, orphaned, or runaway children are classified as Children in Need of Care and Protection (CNCP). They are presented before the Child Welfare Committee (CWC).
**Hindi:** छोड़े गए या भागे हुए बच्चों को सीएनसीपी के रूप में वर्गीकृत किया जाता है। उन्हें बाल कल्याण समिति (CWC) के समक्ष पेश किया जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Jo bacche ghar se bhaag gaye hain ya abandoned hain, unhe CWC ke samne pesh kiya jata hai. Unhe criminal nahi maana jata.

**Q11: Can parents be punished if a minor drives and causes an accident?**
**English:** Yes. Under Section 199A of the Motor Vehicles Act, the guardian or owner of the vehicle is presumed guilty if a juvenile commits a traffic offence, and can face up to 3 years in prison.
**Hindi:** हाँ। मोटर वाहन अधिनियम की धारा 199A के तहत, यदि कोई किशोर यातायात अपराध करता है, तो वाहन का मालिक या अभिभावक दोषी माना जाता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan, Motor Vehicles Act ke tahat agar minor gaadi chalata hai aur accident karta hai, toh parents ko 3 saal tak ki jail aur fine ho sakta hai.

**Q12: Is child labour completely banned?**
**English:** Yes, employment of children below 14 years is completely prohibited in all occupations, except helping in a non-hazardous family business after school hours.
**Hindi:** हाँ, 14 वर्ष से कम उम्र के बच्चों का रोजगार पूरी तरह से प्रतिबंधित है, सिवाय स्कूल के बाद गैर-खतरनाक पारिवारिक व्यवसाय में मदद करने के।
**Hinglish:** Haan, 14 saal se kam umar ke bacchon se kaam karwana illegal hai. Sirf school ke baad apne safe family business mein help allow hai.

**Q13: Do children have a right to free legal aid?**
**English:** Yes. The State Legal Services Authority (SLSA) provides free legal representation to any child brought before the JJB or CWC.
**Hindi:** हाँ। राज्य विधिक सेवा प्राधिकरण (SLSA) जेजेबी या सीडब्ल्यूसी के समक्ष लाए गए किसी भी बच्चे को मुफ्त कानूनी सहायता प्रदान करता है।
**Hinglish:** Haan. Agar kisi minor ka case JJB mein hai, toh unhe sarkaari kharch par free lawyer (legal aid) milta hai.

**Q14: What is a Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU)?**
**English:** It is a unit in every district police setup, comprising officers specially trained to handle minors sensitively, without uniform, using child-friendly language.
**Hindi:** यह हर जिले में पुलिस की एक विशेष इकाई है, जिसमें पुलिस अधिकारी बच्चों को संवेदनशील रूप से संभालने के लिए प्रशिक्षित होते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Har district mein ek special police team hoti hai jo bacchon ke cases handle karti hai. Ye police civil dress mein hoti hai aur bacchon se pyaar se baat karti hai.

**Q15: Can a child be asked to sign a confession?**
**English:** No. Any confession made by a child to a police officer is not admissible in court. The police cannot force a child to sign any statement.
**Hindi:** नहीं। किसी पुलिस अधिकारी के सामने बच्चे द्वारा किया गया कोई भी कबूलनामा अदालत में स्वीकार्य नहीं है।
**Hinglish:** Nahi, police kisi minor se zabardasti koi confession paper sign nahi karwa sakti. Wo court mein valid nahi hota.

**Q16: What is the role of a Probation Officer?**
**English:** The Probation Officer conducts a social investigation to understand the child's family background, psychological state, and reasons for committing the offence, submitting this report to the JJB.
**Hindi:** परिवीक्षा अधिकारी बच्चे की पारिवारिक पृष्ठभूमि और मनोवैज्ञानिक स्थिति को समझने के लिए जांच करता है और यह रिपोर्ट जेजेबी को सौंपता है।
**Hinglish:** Probation Officer bacche ki family background aur uski mansik sthiti ka pata lagata hai aur JJB ko report deta hai ki bacche ne crime kyun kiya.

**Q17: How long can a child be kept in an Observation Home?**
**English:** A child can be kept in an observation home only during the pendency of the inquiry. The inquiry must ideally conclude within 4 to 6 months.
**Hindi:** जांच लंबित रहने तक ही बच्चे को बाल सुधार गृह में रखा जा सकता है, जिसे 4 से 6 महीने में पूरा होना चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Jab tak JJB inquiry chalu hai tab tak hi bacche ko Observation Home mein rakha jata hai, jo 4 se 6 mahine mein poori karni hoti hai.

**Q18: What is the punishment for selling tobacco to a minor?**
**English:** Under Section 77 of the JJ Act, giving or selling intoxicating liquor, narcotics, or psychotropic substances to a minor is punishable by up to 7 years in prison.
**Hindi:** जेजे अधिनियम की धारा 77 के तहत, नाबालिग को नशीली शराब या तंबाकू बेचना 7 साल तक की जेल के साथ दंडनीय है।
**Hinglish:** Agar koi dukandar minor ko sharab, bidi, ya drugs bechta hai, toh JJ Act ke tahat usko 7 saal tak ki jail ho sakti hai.

**Q19: Are old criminal records of a juvenile cleared?**
**English:** Yes. According to Section 24 of the JJ Act, a juvenile who has served their sentence is entitled to a clean slate. The records of conviction are erased to ensure they don't face future stigma.
**Hindi:** हाँ। सजा काटने के बाद, किशोर के पुराने आपराधिक रिकॉर्ड मिटा दिए जाते हैं ताकि उनके भविष्य पर कोई प्रभाव न पड़े।
**Hinglish:** Haan, saza poori hone ke baad juvenile ka criminal record mita diya jata hai taaki aage uski naukri ya life mein problem na aaye.

**Q20: What happens if a false POCSO case is filed?**
**English:** The POCSO Act has provisions (Section 22) against false complaints with the intent to defame. If proven malicious, the person filing the false complaint can face up to 6 months in prison.
**Hindi:** यदि पॉक्सो के तहत झूठी शिकायत दुर्भावनापूर्ण साबित होती है, तो शिकायत दर्ज करने वाले व्यक्ति को 6 महीने तक की जेल हो सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Agar koi revenge lene ke liye POCSO ka jhutha case karta hai, toh court jhuthi complaint karne wale ko 6 mahine ki saza de sakti hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Minors cannot be arrested under any condition.
   **Fact:** Minors can be 'apprehended' (not formally arrested) for committing crimes, but they follow a different legal procedure under the JJB.
2. **Myth:** A 17-year-old murderer will get a life sentence.
   **Fact:** Even if tried as an adult for a heinous crime, a minor cannot be given the death penalty or a mandatory life sentence without possibility of release.
3. **Myth:** You can publish the photo of a brave child victim.
   **Fact:** Section 74 of JJ Act prohibits publishing photos of any minor victim, even if they are praised for bravery in a crime context.
4. **Myth:** Parents are never legally responsible for a minor's crime.
   **Fact:** In specific cases, like underage driving, parents/vehicle owners are held fully liable and face imprisonment.
5. **Myth:** The Juvenile Justice Board functions exactly like a normal court.
   **Fact:** The JJB is highly informal. Members often sit across a table, do not wear robes, and focus on rehabilitation, not punishment.
6. **Myth:** A juvenile caught with adult co-accused will be tried together.
   **Fact:** No, the trial of a juvenile must always be separated from adult co-accused and sent to the JJB.
7. **Myth:** Police can beat a minor to extract a confession.
   **Fact:** Beating a child is severely punishable. Confessions made to police are not admissible anyway.
8. **Myth:** Bail is very hard to get for minors.
   **Fact:** Bail is almost absolute for minors, denied only if release puts the child at risk.
9. **Myth:** Only biological parents can take a minor on bail.
   **Fact:** If parents are unavailable, legal guardians or even fit persons/institutions can take the child on bail.
10. **Myth:** Child labour laws only apply to factories.
    **Fact:** The amended law bans employment of children under 14 in ALL occupations, domestic or commercial.
