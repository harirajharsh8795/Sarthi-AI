## Legal Background
The terminology used in Indian courts is derived from a mix of English common law, historical Islamic law (in property and revenue matters), and modern statutory drafting. Key frameworks include:
- **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023:** Replaced the Indian Penal Code (IPC). Defines criminal offenses.
- **Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS) 2023:** Replaced the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC). Outlines the procedure for criminal justice.
- **Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) 2023:** Replaced the Indian Evidence Act. Dictates how facts are proven in court.
- **Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC):** Governs civil court proceedings.
- **Constitution of India:** The supreme law containing fundamental rights and writs (Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, etc.).

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** All Indian citizens, residents, legal practitioners, law students, and foreign nationals interacting with the Indian legal system.
- **Who is exempt:** None. Ignorance of the law (ignorantia juris non excusat) is no excuse for anyone.
- **Special conditions:** Certain terms may have different interpretations under specific local laws or personal laws (e.g., Hindu Marriage Act vs. Muslim Personal Law).

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:** How to deal with an unfamiliar legal document:
1. **Identify the Source:** Determine if it is a police notice, court summons, or private contract.
2. **Highlight Unknown Terms:** Mark any words you do not understand.
3. **Consult the Glossary:** Look up the terms in this glossary or a standard legal dictionary.
4. **Contextualize:** Read the term in the context of the sentence.
5. **Seek Professional Help:** If the implication is serious (e.g., FIR, warrant), immediately consult a registered advocate.
**Hindi Unicode:** अपरिचित कानूनी दस्तावेज से कैसे निपटें:
1. **स्रोत की पहचान करें:** निर्धारित करें कि यह पुलिस नोटिस, कोर्ट समन या निजी अनुबंध है।
2. **अज्ञात शब्दों को हाइलाइट करें:** उन शब्दों को चिह्नित करें जिन्हें आप नहीं समझते हैं।
3. **शब्दावली देखें:** इस शब्दावली या एक मानक कानूनी शब्दकोश में शब्दों को देखें।
4. **संदर्भित करें:** वाक्य के संदर्भ में शब्द को पढ़ें।
5. **पेशेवर मदद लें:** यदि प्रभाव गंभीर है (जैसे, एफआईआर, वारंट), तो तुरंत एक पंजीकृत वकील से परामर्श लें।

1. **Source check karein:** Pata lagayein ki ye police notice hai, court summons hai ya private contract.
2. **Unknown words mark karein:** Jo words samajh na aayein unhe highlight karein.

## Required Documents
When consulting a lawyer for understanding terms, bring:
1. The original legal document (Notice/Summons/Contract).
2. Any previous correspondence related to the matter.
3. Your government ID (Aadhaar/PAN) for creating a Vakalatnama (Power of Attorney).

## Government Authorities
- **Police Stations:** First point of contact for criminal terminology (FIR, NCR, Bail).
- **Courts (District, High Court, Supreme Court):** Where legal terms are interpreted and enforced.
- **Lok Adalat:** Alternative dispute resolution forum using simpler terminology.
- **NALSA/SLSA/DLSA:** Legal Services Authorities that help explain terms to marginalized sections.

## Penalties & Punishments
While a glossary itself doesn't carry punishments, misunderstanding terms can lead to:
- Contempt of Court (under Contempt of Courts Act, 1971) if a summons or order is ignored.
- Ex-parte decree (a ruling against you) if you fail to appear because you didn't understand a notice.
- Financial penalties for breach of contract due to misunderstanding clauses.

## Exceptions
- Some archaic terms (like Latin maxims) are being phased out in favor of simpler language, though they still appear in higher court judgments.
- Plain language drafting is encouraged but not strictly legally mandated for all historical statutes.

## Important Timelines
- **Summons Response:** Usually 30 days in civil suits (can be extended to 90 days).
- **Bail Application:** Can be filed immediately after arrest.
- **Appeal:** Typically 30 to 90 days depending on the court hierarchy and matter.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. Assuming "Bail" means you are proven innocent (it only means temporary release).
2. Confusing "Cognizable" (police can arrest without warrant) with "Non-Cognizable" (warrant required).
3. Ignoring a legal notice because it uses complex language.
4. Signing a "Vakalatnama" without understanding it transfers power to the lawyer.

## Safety Tips
**English:** Always demand a translation. Never sign a document written in a language you cannot read. Beware of fake legal notices sent via WhatsApp to extort money.
**Hindi Unicode:** हमेशा अनुवाद की मांग करें। कभी भी ऐसी भाषा में लिखे गए दस्तावेज़ पर हस्ताक्षर न करें जिसे आप पढ़ नहीं सकते। पैसे ऐंठने के लिए व्हाट्सएप के माध्यम से भेजे गए फर्जी कानूनी नोटिसों से सावधान रहें।
**Hinglish:** Hamesha translation maange. Kabhi bhi aisi language ke document par sign na karein jo aap padh nahi sakte. Paise thagne ke liye WhatsApp par aane wale fake legal notices se bachke rahein.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is an FIR (First Information Report)?**
   *English:* A written document prepared by police when they receive information about a cognizable offence. It is the first step in a criminal case.
   *Hindi Unicode:* एक लिखित दस्तावेज जो पुलिस द्वारा तब तैयार किया जाता है जब उन्हें किसी संज्ञेय अपराध के बारे में जानकारी मिलती है। यह आपराधिक मामले में पहला कदम है।
   *Hinglish:* FIR ek written document hai jo police tab banati hai jab unhe kisi serious crime ki info milti hai. Ye criminal case ka first step hai.

2. **What does 'Cognizable Offence' mean?**
   *English:* A serious crime (like murder, rape, robbery) where police can arrest the suspect without a warrant from a magistrate.
   *Hindi Unicode:* एक गंभीर अपराध (जैसे हत्या, बलात्कार, डकैती) जहां पुलिस मजिस्ट्रेट के वारंट के बिना संदिग्ध को गिरफ्तार कर सकती है।
   *Hinglish:* Ek serious crime jahan police bina warrant ke arrest kar sakti hai.

3. **What is 'Non-Cognizable Offence' (NCR)?**
   *English:* Less serious offences (like slight assault, public nuisance) where police cannot arrest without a warrant and need a magistrate's permission to investigate.
   *Hindi Unicode:* कम गंभीर अपराध जहां पुलिस वारंट के बिना गिरफ्तार नहीं कर सकती है और जांच के लिए मजिस्ट्रेट की अनुमति की आवश्यकता होती है।
   *Hinglish:* Kam serious crime jismein police bina warrant arrest nahi kar sakti aur investigation ke liye magistrate ki permission chahiye.

4. **What is 'Bail' (Zamanat)?**
   *English:* Temporary release of an accused person awaiting trial, usually on condition of depositing a sum of money or providing a surety.
   *Hindi Unicode:* मुकदमे की प्रतीक्षा कर रहे एक आरोपी व्यक्ति की अस्थायी रिहाई, आमतौर पर एक निश्चित राशि जमा करने या जमानतदार प्रदान करने की शर्त पर।
   *Hinglish:* Trial ka wait kar rahe accused ki temporary release, jiske liye security ya bond dena padta hai.

5. **What is 'Anticipatory Bail'?**
   *English:* Bail granted by a court before an actual arrest is made, when a person anticipates being arrested for a non-bailable offence.
   *Hindi Unicode:* वास्तविक गिरफ्तारी होने से पहले अदालत द्वारा दी गई जमानत, जब किसी व्यक्ति को गैर-जमानती अपराध के लिए गिरफ्तार होने की आशंका होती है।
   *Hinglish:* Arrest hone se pehle court se li gayi bail, jab kisi ko lagta hai ki us par jhootha case ban sakta hai.

6. **What is a 'Warrant'?**
   *English:* A legal document issued by a judge or magistrate authorizing police to make an arrest, search premises, or carry out some other action.
   *Hindi Unicode:* एक न्यायाधीश या मजिस्ट्रेट द्वारा जारी एक कानूनी दस्तावेज जो पुलिस को गिरफ्तारी करने, परिसर की तलाशी लेने या कोई अन्य कार्रवाई करने के लिए अधिकृत करता है।
   *Hinglish:* Court ka order jo police ko kisi ko arrest karne ya search karne ki power deta hai.

7. **What is a 'Summons' (Saman)?**
   *English:* An official order from a court instructing a person to appear before it on a specified date and time.
   *Hindi Unicode:* अदालत का एक आधिकारिक आदेश जो किसी व्यक्ति को एक निर्दिष्ट तिथि और समय पर उसके सामने उपस्थित होने का निर्देश देता है।
   *Hinglish:* Court ka official bulawa ki aapko is date aur time par court mein present hona hai.

8. **What does 'Sub Judice' mean?**
   *English:* A matter that is currently under consideration by a judge or court and is therefore prohibited from public discussion elsewhere.
   *Hindi Unicode:* एक मामला जो वर्तमान में किसी न्यायाधीश या अदालत द्वारा विचाराधीन है।
   *Hinglish:* Ek case jo abhi court mein chal raha hai aur us par koi decision nahi aaya hai.

9. **What is a 'Vakalatnama'?**
   *English:* A document by which a client authorizes a lawyer to represent them and plead their case in court.
   *Hindi Unicode:* एक दस्तावेज जिसके द्वारा एक मुवक्किल एक वकील को अदालत में उनका प्रतिनिधित्व करने और उनका मामला लड़ने के लिए अधिकृत करता है।
   *Hinglish:* Ek document jisse aap kisi lawyer ko apna case ladne ki power dete hain.

10. **What is 'Affidavit' (Halafnama)?**
    *English:* A written statement of facts voluntarily made by a person under an oath or affirmation before an authorized officer.
    *Hindi Unicode:* एक अधिकृत अधिकारी के समक्ष शपथ या प्रतिज्ञान के तहत किसी व्यक्ति द्वारा स्वेच्छा से दिया गया तथ्यों का लिखित बयान।
    *Hinglish:* Ek written statement jo koi person oath lekar deta hai ki usne jo likha hai wo sach hai.

11. **What is an 'Injunction' (Stay Order)?**
    *English:* A court order requiring a person to do or cease doing a specific action.
    *Hindi Unicode:* एक अदालती आदेश जिसके तहत किसी व्यक्ति को कोई विशिष्ट कार्य करने या न करने के लिए कहा जाता है।
    *Hinglish:* Court ka order jo kisi ko kuch karne se rokata hai (Stay order).

12. **What does 'Ex-Parte' mean?**
    *English:* A legal proceeding brought by one person in the absence of and without representation or notification of other parties.
    *Hindi Unicode:* अन्य पक्षों की अनुपस्थिति और बिना प्रतिनिधित्व या अधिसूचना के एक व्यक्ति द्वारा लाई गई कानूनी कार्यवाही।
    *Hinglish:* Ek-tarfa decision jo court tab deti hai jab dusri party court mein present nahi hoti.

13. **What is 'Habeas Corpus'?**
    *English:* A writ requiring a person under arrest to be brought before a judge or into court, to secure the person's release unless lawful grounds are shown for their detention.
    *Hindi Unicode:* एक रिट जिसमें गिरफ्तार व्यक्ति को न्यायाधीश या अदालत के सामने लाने की आवश्यकता होती है।
    *Hinglish:* Ek court order jismein police ko illegal tarike se arrest kiye gaye person ko court mein produce karna padta hai.

14. **What is 'Alimony' (Guzara Bhatta)?**
    *English:* Financial support that a person is ordered by a court to give to their spouse during separation or following divorce.
    *Hindi Unicode:* वित्तीय सहायता जिसे अदालत द्वारा किसी व्यक्ति को अलगाव या तलाक के बाद अपने जीवनसाथी को देने का आदेश दिया जाता है।
    *Hinglish:* Wo paisa jo court divorce ya separation ke baad ek partner ko dusre ko dene ke liye kehti hai.

15. **What is an 'Acquittal' (Bari karna)?**
    *English:* A judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which the person has been charged.
    *Hindi Unicode:* एक निर्णय कि कोई व्यक्ति उस अपराध का दोषी नहीं है जिसका उस पर आरोप लगाया गया है।
    *Hinglish:* Court ka faisla ki accused nirdosh hai aur use charges se mukt kiya jata hai.

16. **What is 'Conviction' (Doshi Karar)?**
    *English:* A formal declaration that someone is guilty of a criminal offense, made by the verdict of a jury or the decision of a judge in a court of law.
    *Hindi Unicode:* अदालत में न्यायाधीश के निर्णय द्वारा की गई एक औपचारिक घोषणा कि कोई व्यक्ति आपराधिक अपराध का दोषी है।
    *Hinglish:* Court ka official faisla ki accused doshi hai aur use saza milni chahiye.

17. **What is 'Perjury'?**
    *English:* The offense of willfully telling an untruth in a court after having taken an oath or affirmation.
    *Hindi Unicode:* शपथ या प्रतिज्ञान लेने के बाद अदालत में जानबूझकर झूठ बोलने का अपराध।
    *Hinglish:* Court mein kasm khane ke baad jaanboojh kar jhooth bolna ya jhoothi gawahi dena.

18. **What does 'Pro Bono' mean?**
    *English:* Legal work undertaken voluntarily and without payment, usually as a public service.
    *Hindi Unicode:* स्वैच्छिक रूप से और बिना भुगतान के किया गया कानूनी कार्य, आमतौर पर सार्वजनिक सेवा के रूप में।
    *Hinglish:* Jab lawyer bina fees ke free mein case ladta hai public welfare ke liye.

19. **What is a 'Charge Sheet' (Challan)?**
    *English:* A final report prepared by law enforcement agencies after completing an investigation, containing the charges against the accused.
    *Hindi Unicode:* जांच पूरी करने के बाद कानून प्रवर्तन एजेंसियों द्वारा तैयार की गई एक अंतिम रिपोर्ट, जिसमें आरोपी के खिलाफ आरोप शामिल हैं।
    *Hinglish:* Police investigation complete hone ke baad court mein submit ki gayi final report jismein sare charges likhe hote hain.

20. **What is 'Defamation' (Maanhani)?**
    *English:* The action of damaging the good reputation of someone, either in writing (libel) or spoken (slander).
    *Hindi Unicode:* किसी की अच्छी प्रतिष्ठा को नुकसान पहुंचाने की क्रिया, या तो लिखित में या बोलकर।
    *Hinglish:* Kisi ki image ya reputation ko jhoothi baaton se kharab karna (likh kar ya bol kar).

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Bail means the case is over and the person is innocent.
   **Fact:** Bail is merely temporary release during the trial. The case continues.
2. **Myth:** A Summons is a warrant for arrest.
   **Fact:** A summons is just an order to appear in court or produce a document. It is not an arrest warrant.
3. **Myth:** You cannot file an FIR if you don't know the attacker's name.
   **Fact:** An FIR can be registered against "unknown persons."
4. **Myth:** A lawyer can guarantee a 100% win if you sign the Vakalatnama.
   **Fact:** No lawyer can legally guarantee a court outcome.
5. **Myth:** An Affidavit is proof of absolute truth.
   **Fact:** An affidavit is just a sworn statement; it can still be challenged and proven false (perjury).
6. **Myth:** Police can enter your house anytime without a warrant for any reason.
   **Fact:** Police need a search warrant for most searches, except during hot pursuit in serious cognizable crimes.
7. **Myth:** Anticipatory bail can be obtained after you are arrested.
   **Fact:** Anticipatory bail must be secured *before* the arrest takes place. Regular bail applies post-arrest.
8. **Myth:** An ex-parte decree cannot be challenged.
   **Fact:** You can apply to set aside an ex-parte decree if you have a valid reason for non-appearance.
9. **Myth:** Sub-judice matters cannot be reported by the media at all.
   **Fact:** Objective reporting of court proceedings is allowed; media trials and pre-judging are prohibited.
10. **Myth:** Only poor people can get pro bono lawyers.
    **Fact:** While aimed at the marginalized, any lawyer can choose to take any case pro bono based on public interest.

## Conversation Examples

**Scenario 1: Understanding an FIR**
*Citizen:* Sir, police ne meri 'FIR' likhne se mana kar diya aur kaha 'NCR' katega. Ye kya hota hai? (Sir, the police refused to file my FIR and said they will file an NCR. What is this?)
*Saarthi AI:* FIR (First Information Report) serious (cognizable) crimes ke liye hoti hai jahan police turant action le sakti hai. NCR (Non-Cognizable Report) chhote crimes ke liye hoti hai jahan police ko court ki permission chahiye investigation ke liye. Aap magistrate ke paas complaint file kar sakte hain.

**Scenario 2: Getting a Summons**
*Citizen:* Mujhe court se ek 'Summons' aaya hai. Kya mujhe arrest kiya jayega? (I received a Summons from the court. Will I be arrested?)
*Saarthi AI:* Nahi. 'Summons' sirf ek official bulawa hota hai ki aapko us date par court mein present hona hai ya koi document dena hai. Arrest warrant alag hota hai. Aapko time par court jana chahiye.

**Scenario 3: Meaning of Bail**
*Citizen:* Mere dost ko court ne bail de di hai. Kya ab wo nirdosh sabit ho gaya? (My friend got bail from court. Is he proven innocent now?)
*Saarthi AI:* Nahi, 'Bail' ka matlab nirdosh hona nahi hai. Bail ka matlab hai ki trial (mukadma) chalne tak unhe temporary release mil gayi hai. Case abhi bhi chal raha hai.

**Scenario 4: Anticipatory Bail**
*Citizen:* Mujhe dar hai ki mere khilaf jhootha case banega aur arrest ho sakta hu. Main kya karu? (I fear a false case will be filed and I might be arrested. What should I do?)
*Saarthi AI:* Aisi situation mein aap 'Anticipatory Bail' (Agrim Zamanat) ke liye Sessions Court ya High Court mein apply kar sakte hain arrest hone se pehle. Ek lawyer se turant sampark karein.

**Scenario 5: Meaning of Charge Sheet**
*Citizen:* Police ne news mein kaha ki unhone 'Charge Sheet' file kar di hai. Iska kya matlab hai? (Police said on news they filed a Charge Sheet. What does it mean?)
*Saarthi AI:* 'Charge Sheet' ya Challan ek final investigation report hoti hai jo police court mein deti hai. Isme likha hota hai ki accused ke khilaf kya evidence mile hain aur kin sections (dhaara) mein case chalega.

**Scenario 6: Sworn Statements**
*Citizen:* Court ne kaha hai ki mujhe 'Affidavit' dena hoga. Ye kaise banta hai? (Court asked me to give an Affidavit. How is it made?)
*Saarthi AI:* 'Affidavit' (Halafnama) ek written document hota hai jis par aap sign karke kasam khate hain ki jo likha hai wo sach hai. Ye Notary Public ya Oath Commissioner ke saamne banta hai.

**Scenario 7: Defamation**
*Citizen:* Kisi ne Facebook par mere baare mein jhooth likh kar meri image kharab ki hai. Kya legal word hai iske liye? (Someone ruined my image by posting lies on Facebook. What is the legal term?)
*Saarthi AI:* Ise 'Defamation' (Maanhani) kehte hain. Likhit roop mein ise 'Libel' kaha jata hai. Aap iske khilaf civil suit ya criminal complaint (under BNS) kar sakte hain.

**Scenario 8: Sub Judice matters**
*Citizen:* Kya main apne chalte hue court case ki details social media par daal sakta hu? (Can I post details of my ongoing court case on social media?)
*Saarthi AI:* Agar case 'Sub Judice' hai (court mein chal raha hai), toh us par aisi comments karna jo case ko effect karein, Contempt of Court (Court ki avmanana) maana ja sakta hai. Behtar hai public comments se bachein.

**Scenario 9: Habeas Corpus**
*Citizen:* Police ne mere bhai ko 3 din se pakad rakha hai aur kuch nahi bata rahe. Main kis kanooni raste ka use karu? (Police held my brother for 3 days and telling nothing. What legal route to use?)
*Saarthi AI:* Aap High Court ya Supreme Court mein 'Habeas Corpus' ki writ file kar sakte hain. Ye court ko power deti hai ki wo police ko order de ki arrested person ko turant court ke saamne laya jaye.

**Scenario 10: Vakalatnama**
*Citizen:* Lawyer ne ek blank paper pe sign karne ko bola jise wo 'Vakalatnama' keh raha tha. Kya karu? (Lawyer asked me to sign a blank paper calling it Vakalatnama. What to do?)
*Saarthi AI:* Kabhi bhi blank Vakalatnama par sign na karein. 'Vakalatnama' wo document hai jisse aap lawyer ko apna case ladne ki power dete hain. Usme details bhari honi chahiye.

**Scenario 11: Alimony**
*Citizen:* Divorce ke baad jo paisa milta hai use legally kya kehte hain? (What is the legal term for money received after divorce?)
*Saarthi AI:* Ise 'Alimony' (Guzara Bhatta) ya 'Maintenance' kehte hain. Ye court tay karti hai dono parties ki financial condition dekh kar.

**Scenario 12: Injunction**
*Citizen:* Mera padosi meri zameen par building bana raha hai, mujhe ise turant rokna hai. Kya word hai iske liye? (Neighbor building on my land, need to stop it. What is the word?)
*Saarthi AI:* Aapko court se 'Injunction' (Stay Order) lana hoga. Ye ek court order hota hai jo kisi kaam ko turant rokne ka nirdesh deta hai.

**Scenario 13: Ex-Parte**
*Citizen:* Main pichli 2 dates par court nahi gaya, ab lawyer keh raha hai 'Ex-Parte' order ho gaya. Ye kya hai? (I missed 2 court dates, lawyer says Ex-Parte order passed. What is it?)
*Saarthi AI:* 'Ex-Parte' ka matlab hai court ne aapki gair-haziri (absence) mein dusri party ki baat sun kar faisla suna diya hai. Aapko ise set aside (radd) karane ki application lagani hogi.

**Scenario 14: Perjury**
*Citizen:* Agar gawah court mein jhooth bole toh kya kanooni term hai? (If a witness lies in court, what is the legal term?)
*Saarthi AI:* Ise 'Perjury' (Jhoothi gawahi dena) kehte hain. Ye ek serious criminal offence hai jiske liye alag se saza aur fine dono ho sakte hain.

**Scenario 15: Pro Bono**
*Citizen:* Main lawyer ki fees nahi de sakta, kya koi aisi term hai jahan free help milti hai? (I can't pay lawyer fees, is there a term for free help?)
*Saarthi AI:* Haan, ise 'Pro Bono' kehte hain jab lawyer free mein case ladta hai. Iske alawa aap 'Legal Aid' ke liye District Legal Services Authority (DLSA) mein apply kar sakte hain.
