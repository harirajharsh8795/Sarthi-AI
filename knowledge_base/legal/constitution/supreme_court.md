# The Supreme Court of India: Complete Legal Guide

## Overview
**English:** The Supreme Court of India is the highest judicial court and the final court of appeal under the Constitution of India. It is the ultimate interpreter of the Constitution and the guardian of citizens' fundamental rights. Established on January 28, 1950, it holds immense power to ensure justice, including the unique power under Article 142 to pass any decree necessary for doing "complete justice."

**Hindi Unicode:** भारत का सर्वोच्च न्यायालय (Supreme Court of India) भारतीय संविधान के तहत सर्वोच्च न्यायिक न्यायालय और अपील का अंतिम न्यायालय है। यह संविधान का अंतिम व्याख्याकार और नागरिकों के मौलिक अधिकारों का रक्षक है। 28 जनवरी, 1950 को स्थापित, यह न्याय सुनिश्चित करने के लिए अपार शक्ति रखता है, जिसमें "पूर्ण न्याय" करने के लिए आवश्यक कोई भी डिक्री पारित करने के लिए अनुच्छेद 142 के तहत अद्वितीय शक्ति शामिल है।

**Hinglish:** Supreme Court of India desh ki sabse badi judicial body aur final court of appeal hai under the Constitution of India. Yeh Constitution ka ultimate interpreter hai aur citizens ke fundamental rights ka guardian hai. Jan 28, 1950 ko establish hui, iske paas justice ensure karne ki immense power hai, jisme Article 142 ke under "complete justice" deliver karne ki unique power bhi shamil hai.

## Quick Summary
**English:** As the Apex Court, the Supreme Court hears appeals against High Court judgments, directly accepts writ petitions for fundamental rights violations (under Article 32), and resolves disputes between states or the central government. Its decisions are binding on all courts across India.

**Hindi Unicode:** शीर्ष न्यायालय के रूप में, सर्वोच्च न्यायालय उच्च न्यायालय के फैसलों के खिलाफ अपील सुनता है, मौलिक अधिकारों के उल्लंघन (अनुच्छेद 32 के तहत) के लिए सीधे रिट याचिका स्वीकार करता है, और राज्यों या केंद्र सरकार के बीच विवादों का समाधान करता है। इसके फैसले भारत भर के सभी न्यायालयों पर बाध्यकारी हैं।

**Hinglish:** Apex Court hone ke naate, Supreme Court High Court judgments ke khilaf appeals sunti hai, fundamental rights violations ke liye direct writ petitions (Article 32 ke under) accept karti hai, aur states ya central government ke beech disputes resolve karti hai. Iske decisions poore India ke sabhi courts par binding hote hain.

## Definition
**English:** The Supreme Court of India is the apex judicial body established under Part V, Chapter IV of the Constitution of India (Articles 124 to 147), possessing original, appellate, and advisory jurisdictions.

**Hindi Unicode:** भारत का सर्वोच्च न्यायालय भारतीय संविधान के भाग V, अध्याय IV (अनुच्छेद 124 से 147) के तहत स्थापित शीर्ष न्यायिक निकाय है, जिसके पास मूल, अपीलीय और सलाहकार क्षेत्राधिकार हैं।

**Hinglish:** Supreme Court of India Constitution ke Part V, Chapter IV (Articles 124 se 147) ke under establish ki gayi apex judicial body hai, jiske paas original, appellate, aur advisory jurisdiction hoti hai.

## Why It Matters
**English:** It is the final protector of your fundamental rights. Without the Supreme Court, there would be no ultimate authority to keep the legislature and executive in check, interpret complex laws, or ensure uniform justice across the nation.

**Hindi Unicode:** यह आपके मौलिक अधिकारों का अंतिम रक्षक है। सर्वोच्च न्यायालय के बिना, विधायिका और कार्यपालिका को नियंत्रण में रखने, जटिल कानूनों की व्याख्या करने या पूरे देश में समान न्याय सुनिश्चित करने के लिए कोई अंतिम अधिकार नहीं होगा।

**Hinglish:** Yeh aapke fundamental rights ka final protector hai. Supreme Court ke bina, legislature aur executive ko check mein rakhne, complex laws ko interpret karne, ya pure desh mein uniform justice ensure karne ke liye koi ultimate authority nahi hoti.

## Legal Background
- **History:** Inaugurated on January 28, 1950, replacing the Federal Court of India and the Privy Council.
- **Purpose:** To serve as the final court of appeal, protect fundamental rights, and act as the guardian of the Constitution.
- **Relevant Constitutional Articles:**
  - **Article 124:** Establishment and constitution of Supreme Court.
  - **Article 129:** Supreme Court to be a court of record.
  - **Article 131:** Original jurisdiction (disputes between Govt of India and States).
  - **Article 132-134:** Appellate jurisdiction in appeals from High Courts.
  - **Article 136:** Special leave to appeal by the Supreme Court (SLP).
  - **Article 32:** Remedies for enforcement of Fundamental Rights (Writ Petitions).
  - **Article 141:** Law declared by Supreme Court to be binding on all courts.
  - **Article 142:** Enforcement of decrees and orders of Supreme Court and orders as to discovery, etc. (Complete Justice).
  - **Article 143:** Power of President to consult Supreme Court (Advisory Jurisdiction).
- **Relevant Acts:** Supreme Court (Number of Judges) Act, 1956; Contempt of Courts Act, 1971.

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** All citizens of India, corporations, foreigners (for certain fundamental rights like Article 21), State Governments, and the Union Government.
- **Who is exempt:** The President and Governors have specific immunities under Article 361 for official acts, but their actions can still be judicially reviewed.
- **Special conditions:** Access via Article 32 is strictly for Fundamental Rights violations.

## Rights of Citizens
**English:** Citizens have the right to approach the Supreme Court directly under Article 32 if their fundamental rights are violated. They also have the right to appeal High Court decisions through Special Leave Petitions (SLP).
**Hindi Unicode:** नागरिकों को यह अधिकार है कि यदि उनके मौलिक अधिकारों का उल्लंघन होता है तो वे अनुच्छेद 32 के तहत सीधे सर्वोच्च न्यायालय जा सकते हैं। उन्हें विशेष अनुमति याचिका (एसएलपी) के माध्यम से उच्च न्यायालय के फैसलों की अपील करने का भी अधिकार है।
**Hinglish:** Citizens ke paas right hai ki agar unke fundamental rights violate hote hain, toh woh Article 32 ke under seedhe Supreme Court approach kar sakte hain. Unhe SLP ke through High Court decisions ke khilaf appeal karne ka bhi right hai.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English:** Citizens must respect the dignity of the Court. Filing frivolous or vexatious petitions wastes judicial time and can lead to heavy penalties. Truthful representation of facts is a strict duty.
**Hindi Unicode:** नागरिकों को न्यायालय की गरिमा का सम्मान करना चाहिए। तुच्छ या परेशान करने वाली याचिकाएं दायर करने से न्यायिक समय बर्बाद होता है और भारी जुर्माना हो सकता है। तथ्यों का सत्य प्रतिनिधित्व एक सख्त कर्तव्य है।
**Hinglish:** Citizens ko Court ki dignity ki respect karni chahiye. Frivolous petitions file karne se judicial time waste hota hai aur heavy penalties lag sakti hain. Facts ka sacha representation ek strict duty hai.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:** 
1. **Consultation:** Consult an Advocate-on-Record (AoR). Only an AoR can file cases in the Supreme Court.
2. **Drafting:** The petition (SLP, Writ, or Appeal) is drafted with complete facts, grounds, and prayers.
3. **Filing:** The petition is filed at the Supreme Court registry. Defect curing is done if the registry finds formatting or documentary errors.
4. **Listing:** The case is listed for admission hearing before a bench.
5. **Admission Hearing:** The bench decides whether to admit the case. If admitted, notices are issued to the opposite parties.
6. **Final Hearing & Judgment:** After replies and rejoinders, arguments are heard, and judgment is pronounced.

**Hindi Unicode:**
1. **परामर्श:** एक एडवोकेट-ऑन-रिकॉर्ड (AoR) से परामर्श लें। केवल AoR ही सर्वोच्च न्यायालय में मामला दायर कर सकता है।
2. **प्रारूपण:** याचिका (एसएलपी, रिट, या अपील) को पूर्ण तथ्यों, आधारों और प्रार्थनाओं के साथ तैयार किया जाता है।
3. **दाखिल करना:** याचिका सुप्रीम कोर्ट रजिस्ट्री में दाखिल की जाती है। यदि रजिस्ट्री को कोई त्रुटि मिलती है तो उसे दूर किया जाता है।
4. **सूचीबद्ध करना:** मामले को प्रवेश सुनवाई के लिए बेंच के सामने सूचीबद्ध किया जाता है।
5. **प्रवेश सुनवाई:** बेंच तय करती है कि मामले को स्वीकार करना है या नहीं। यदि स्वीकार किया जाता है, तो विरोधी पक्षों को नोटिस जारी किए जाते हैं।
6. **अंतिम सुनवाई और फैसला:** जवाब और प्रत्युत्तर के बाद, बहस सुनी जाती है और फैसला सुनाया जाता है।

**Hinglish:**
1. **Consultation:** Ek Advocate-on-Record (AoR) se consult karein. Sirf AoR hi SC mein case file kar sakta hai.
2. **Drafting:** Petition (SLP, Writ, ya Appeal) complete facts, grounds, aur prayers ke saath draft ki jaati hai.
3. **Filing:** Petition SC registry mein file hoti hai. Registry ke objections cure kiye jaate hain.
4. **Listing:** Case ko admission hearing ke liye bench ke samne list kiya jata hai.

6. **Final Hearing & Judgment:** Replies aur rejoinders ke baad, arguments sune jaate hain aur judgment pass hota hai.

## Required Documents
- Certified copies of lower court/High Court judgments.
- Vakalatnama signed in favor of the Advocate-on-Record.
- Affidavit supporting the petition.
- Identity proofs of the petitioner.
- Synopsis and List of Dates detailing the case timeline.

## Government Authorities
- **Chief Justice of India (CJI):** Master of the Roster, allocates cases to different benches.
- **Supreme Court Registry:** Administrative wing managing filings, listings, and records.
- **Supreme Court Legal Services Committee (SCLSC):** Provides free legal aid to eligible persons.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Contempt of Court:** Up to 6 months of simple imprisonment, or a fine up to Rs. 2,000, or both under the Contempt of Courts Act, 1971.
- **Exemplary Costs:** Imposed on petitioners for filing frivolous litigations or wasting court time (can range from thousands to lakhs of rupees).

## Exceptions
- The Supreme Court generally does not entertain an SLP if the petitioner has not exhausted alternative remedies available in lower courts, unless there is a gross miscarriage of justice or a fundamental right violation.

## Important Timelines
- **SLP against High Court order:** Normally 90 days from the date of the judgment/order.
- **SLP if High Court refuses certificate to appeal:** 60 days.
- **Review Petition:** Must be filed within 30 days of the judgment or order.
- **Curative Petition:** No strict time limit, but must be filed within a reasonable time after the dismissal of the Review Petition.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Filing a writ petition in the Supreme Court directly for matters that do not involve fundamental rights.
- Delay in filing an SLP (laches) without a valid application for condonation of delay.
- Approaching a lawyer who is not an Advocate-on-Record (AoR) for filing.
- Suppressing material facts in the petition.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Who is the head of the Supreme Court?**
   The Chief Justice of India (CJI) is the head of the Supreme Court and the highest-ranking judicial officer in India.

2. **Can I file a case directly in the Supreme Court?**
   Generally no, but under Article 32, you can file directly if your Fundamental Rights are violated.

3. **What is an SLP?**
   SLP stands for Special Leave Petition under Article 136, allowing you to appeal against any judgment from any court or tribunal in India.

4. **Who is an Advocate-on-Record (AoR)?**
   An AoR is a lawyer who has passed an exam conducted by the Supreme Court. Only an AoR can file a case in the Supreme Court on behalf of a party.

5. **Is the Supreme Court's decision final?**
   Yes, judgments of the Supreme Court are binding on all courts in India under Article 141. However, one can file a Review Petition.

6. **What is a Review Petition?**
   A petition filed under Article 137 requesting the Supreme Court to review its own judgment. It must be filed within 30 days.

7. **What is a Curative Petition?**
   It is the last judicial resort available after a review petition is dismissed, filed to prevent abuse of process or cure gross miscarriage of justice.

8. **Can the Supreme Court punish for its contempt?**
   Yes, under Article 129, the Supreme Court is a court of record and can punish for its contempt.

9. **What is Public Interest Litigation (PIL)?**
   A PIL is a petition filed for the protection of public interest. It can be filed by any public-spirited citizen under Article 32.

10. **How many judges sit in the Supreme Court?**
    The sanctioned strength is 34 judges, including the Chief Justice of India.

11. **What is a Constitution Bench?**
    A bench of five or more judges constituted to decide cases involving substantial questions of law as to the interpretation of the Constitution.

12. **Are Supreme Court hearings open to the public?**
    Yes, proceedings are generally open to the public, and recently, the Supreme Court has started live-streaming its Constitution Bench hearings.

13. **Can I argue my own case in the Supreme Court?**
    Yes, you can appear as a party-in-person, but you must take permission from the Court after an interaction with a court committee.

14. **What is the fee for filing an SLP?**
    Court fees are nominal and depend on the type of case, but professional fees for AoRs and Senior Advocates vary widely.

15. **Does the Supreme Court provide free legal aid?**
    Yes, the Supreme Court Legal Services Committee (SCLSC) provides free legal aid to marginalized sections, women, children, and poor individuals.

16. **What is the original jurisdiction of the SC?**
    Under Article 131, disputes between the Centre and States, or between two or more States, go directly to the Supreme Court.

17. **What is the advisory jurisdiction?**
    Under Article 143, the President of India can seek the Supreme Court's opinion on questions of law or public importance.

18. **Can the Supreme Court transfer cases between states?**
    Yes, to ensure fair justice, the SC can transfer criminal or civil cases from one High Court or lower court in one state to another.

19. **What is Article 142?**
    It grants the Supreme Court the unique power to pass any order necessary to do "complete justice" in a pending matter.

20. **Where is the Supreme Court located?**
    The Supreme Court of India is located at Tilak Marg, New Delhi.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** Any lawyer can file a case in the Supreme Court.
   **Fact:** Only an Advocate-on-Record (AoR) can file a vakalatnama and petitions in the Supreme Court.
2. **Myth:** You can appeal any High Court order in the Supreme Court as a matter of right.
   **Fact:** The Supreme Court grants Special Leave (SLP) at its discretion; it is not an absolute right.
3. **Myth:** The President of India can override a Supreme Court judgment.
   **Fact:** The President cannot override judgments, though they possess the power to grant pardons under Article 72 in criminal convictions.
4. **Myth:** Supreme Court judges are elected by the public.
   **Fact:** Judges are appointed by the President on the recommendation of the Supreme Court Collegium.
5. **Myth:** A PIL can only be filed by the victim.
   **Fact:** A PIL can be filed by any citizen acting in good faith for a public cause.
6. **Myth:** Review petitions involve a fresh hearing of the whole case.
   **Fact:** Review petitions are generally decided in chambers by circulation, not in open court, unless ordered otherwise.
7. **Myth:** The Supreme Court only hears cases in English.
   **Fact:** The official language of the SC is English, but translated copies of judgments in regional languages are now being made available.
8. **Myth:** The Supreme Court is accountable to the Prime Minister.
   **Fact:** The Judiciary is an independent pillar of democracy, separate from the Executive.
9. **Myth:** Once an SLP is filed, the lower court order is automatically stayed.
   **Fact:** There is no automatic stay; you must specifically pray for a stay order and the SC must grant it.
10. **Myth:** You need to pay lakhs to even enter the Supreme Court.
    **Fact:** Legal aid is freely available for those who cannot afford lawyers.

## Conversation Examples

1. **Citizen:** Mera High Court ka case dismiss ho gaya, ab main kya karoon?
   **Assistant:** Aap Supreme Court mein Special Leave Petition (SLP) file kar sakte hain. Lekin iske liye aapko ek Advocate-on-Record (AoR) appoint karna hoga. Dhyan rahe, SLP 90 dino ke andar file karni hoti hai.

2. **Citizen:** Can I file a writ petition directly in the Supreme Court?
   **Assistant:** Yes, you can file a writ petition under Article 32 directly in the Supreme Court, but only if there is a violation of your Fundamental Rights guaranteed by the Constitution.

3. **Citizen:** Supreme Court mein case khud lad sakta hu bina lawyer ke?
   **Assistant:** Haan, aap 'party-in-person' ke roop mein apna case lad sakte hain. Par iske liye aapko pehle Supreme Court ki ek committee se interaction karna hoga jo ye tay karegi ki aap apna case khud argue karne ke kabil hain ya nahi.

4. **Citizen:** PIL kaun file kar sakta hai?
   **Assistant:** Koi bhi nagrik jo public interest (jan-hit) ki raksha karna chahta hai, PIL file kar sakta hai. Isme zaroori nahi ki petitioner khud victim ho, bas intention bona fide honi chahiye.

5. **Citizen:** What happens if the Supreme Court dismisses my Review Petition?
   **Assistant:** If your Review Petition is dismissed, the last legal remedy available is a Curative Petition. However, this is entertained only in very rare cases where there is a gross violation of principles of natural justice.

6. **Citizen:** Agar Government of India aur ek State ke beech jhagda ho, toh case kahan jayega?
   **Assistant:** Aise mamlo mein Article 131 ke under Supreme Court ki 'Original Jurisdiction' hoti hai. Yeh case seedhe Supreme Court mein jayega.

7. **Citizen:** Kya President Supreme Court se advice le sakte hain?
   **Assistant:** Yes, under Article 143, President kisi public importance ya law ke question par Supreme Court ki advisory opinion maang sakte hain.

8. **Citizen:** SC ka contempt kya hota hai?
   **Assistant:** Contempt of Court ka matlab hai court ke orders ko na manna ya court ki dignity ko lower karna. Supreme Court Article 129 ke under iske liye saza de sakti hai.

9. **Citizen:** Mujhe free lawyer chahiye Supreme Court ke liye.
   **Assistant:** Aap Supreme Court Legal Services Committee (SCLSC) se sampark kar sakte hain. Agar aap eligible hain (jaise women, children, ya low-income group), toh aapko free legal aid mil sakti hai.

10. **Citizen:** How to track my case in the Supreme Court?
    **Assistant:** You can track your case status online through the official Supreme Court of India website or the eCourts portal using your diary number or case number.

11. **Citizen:** What is an Advocate-on-Record?
    **Assistant:** An AoR is a specially qualified lawyer who has passed the Supreme Court's rigorous AoR exam. Only they have the authority to formally file documents in the Supreme Court.

12. **Citizen:** Kya Article 142 ka use aam aadmi karwa sakta hai?
    **Assistant:** Article 142 Supreme Court ko "complete justice" deliver karne ki extraordinary power deta hai. SC ise aam aadmi ke cases mein bhi use kar sakti hai, jaise family disputes mein mutual consent divorce turant grant karna.

13. **Citizen:** What is the retirement age of a Supreme Court judge?
    **Assistant:** A judge of the Supreme Court of India retires at the age of 65 years.

14. **Citizen:** Kya Supreme Court High Court ke decisions ko overrule kar sakti hai?
    **Assistant:** Haan, Supreme Court desh ki sabse badi adalat hai. Woh kisi bhi High Court ke decision ko modify, reverse ya overrule kar sakti hai.

15. **Citizen:** How many judges hear a normal case?
    **Assistant:** Generally, cases are heard by a Division Bench comprising two judges. Important cases may be heard by three judges, and constitutional matters by a Constitution Bench of five or more judges.
