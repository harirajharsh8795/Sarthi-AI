# Prime Minister of India: Constitutional Role, Powers, and Procedures

## Overview
**English:** The Prime Minister (PM) of India is the head of the government and the leader of the executive branch of the Republic of India. While the President is the constitutional head of the state (de jure), the Prime Minister is the actual executive head (de facto). The PM leads the Council of Ministers and is the chief advisor to the President of India.

## Legal Background

### History and Purpose
The office of the Prime Minister of India was established upon the adoption of the Constitution of India in 1950, modeled heavily on the Westminster system of the United Kingdom. The purpose is to have a centralized democratic authority elected by the people to govern the nation, balanced by the ceremonial authority of the President.

### Applicable Acts
1. The Constitution of India, 1950
2. The Representation of the People Act (RPA), 1951
3. Salaries and Allowances of Ministers Act, 1952

### Relevant Constitutional Articles
- **Article 74:** There shall be a Council of Ministers with the Prime Minister at the head to aid and advise the President, who shall, in the exercise of his functions, act in accordance with such advice.
- **Article 75(1):** The Prime Minister shall be appointed by the President and the other Ministers shall be appointed by the President on the advice of the Prime Minister.
- **Article 75(2):** The Ministers shall hold office during the pleasure of the President.
- **Article 75(3):** The Council of Ministers shall be collectively responsible to the House of the People (Lok Sabha).
- **Article 78:** It shall be the duty of the Prime Minister to communicate to the President all decisions of the Council of Ministers relating to the administration of the affairs of the Union and proposals for legislation.
- **Article 352:** National Emergency can be proclaimed by the President only after receiving a written recommendation from the Union Cabinet, headed by the PM.

## Applicability
**Who can be Prime Minister:**
- Must be a citizen of India.
- Must be a member of either the Lok Sabha or the Rajya Sabha. (If a non-member is appointed, they must get elected to either house within 6 months).
- Must have completed 25 years of age if a member of the Lok Sabha, or 30 years of age if a member of the Rajya Sabha.
**Exemptions/Disqualifications:**
- Persons holding an Office of Profit under the Government of India.
- Individuals convicted of certain criminal offenses as per the Representation of the People Act, 1951 (e.g., sentenced to imprisonment for 2 or more years).
- Persons of unsound mind or undischarged insolvents.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure (Appointment Process)
**English:**
1. **General Elections:** Citizens elect Members of Parliament (MPs) for the Lok Sabha.
2. **Result Declaration:** The Election Commission of India announces the results.
3. **Majority Formation:** The party or coalition securing a majority (272+ seats) elects its leader.
4. **Presidential Invitation:** The President of India formally invites the elected leader to form the government.
5. **Oath Taking:** The Prime Minister takes the Oath of Office and Oath of Secrecy administered by the President at Rashtrapati Bhavan.
6. **Proving Majority (If required):** If it is a coalition or minority government, the PM must pass a Floor Test in the Lok Sabha within a timeframe set by the President.
**Hindi Unicode:**
1. **आम चुनाव:** नागरिक लोकसभा के लिए संसद सदस्यों (सांसदों) का चुनाव करते हैं।
2. **परिणाम घोषणा:** भारत निर्वाचन आयोग परिणामों की घोषणा करता है।
3. **बहुमत गठन:** बहुमत (272+ सीटें) हासिल करने वाला दल या गठबंधन अपना नेता चुनता है।
4. **राष्ट्रपति का निमंत्रण:** भारत के राष्ट्रपति औपचारिक रूप से निर्वाचित नेता को सरकार बनाने के लिए आमंत्रित करते हैं।

6. **बहुमत साबित करना:** यदि आवश्यक हो तो लोकसभा में फ्लोर टेस्ट पास करना।

## Required Documents
For filing a nomination to become an MP (the prerequisite to becoming PM):
1. Form 2A (Nomination Paper).
2. Certified extract of electoral roll showing the candidate's name.
3. Form 26 Affidavit (detailing assets, liabilities, and criminal background).
4. Security deposit (as mandated by ECI).
5. Oath or affirmation under Article 84(a) of the Constitution.

## Government Authorities
1. **President of India:** Appointing authority for the PM.
2. **Election Commission of India (ECI):** Conducts elections.
3. **Parliament (Lok Sabha & Rajya Sabha):** The legislature where the PM and government are held accountable.
4. **Supreme Court of India:** Resolves disputes regarding elections and constitutional validity of the PM's actions.
5. **Prime Minister's Office (PMO):** Administrative agency providing secretarial assistance to the PM.

## Penalties & Punishments
The Prime Minister is subject to the rule of law just like any citizen. Important legal liabilities include:
1. **Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988:** The PM can be prosecuted for corruption. (Requires sanction of the President/Lokpal in specific instances).
2. **Anti-Defection Law (Tenth Schedule):** If a PM changes political parties post-election, they can be disqualified from Parliament, automatically causing them to lose the PM post.
3. **Representation of the People Act, 1951:** If found guilty of corrupt electoral practices (Section 123), the PM's election can be set aside by the High Court/Supreme Court, and they can be barred from contesting elections for up to 6 years.

## Exceptions
1. **Hung Parliament:** When no party gets a clear majority, the President exercises discretionary power to appoint the person most likely to secure a majority.
2. **Death in Office:** If a PM dies in office, the Council of Ministers automatically dissolves. The President can appoint a Caretaker PM until the ruling party elects a new leader.
3. **Caretaker Government:** When the Lok Sabha is dissolved, the President asks the PM to continue as a caretaker PM until the new elections are concluded. During this period, the PM cannot take major policy decisions.

## Important Timelines
- **Term Limit:** 5 years (concurrent with the life of the Lok Sabha). However, the term can end earlier if the government loses a confidence vote.
- **Maximum Gap Between Sessions:** The PM must advise the President to summon Parliament so that there is no more than a 6-month gap between two sessions.
- **Time to Get Elected:** A non-member appointed as PM has exactly 6 months to get elected to either House of Parliament.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. **Assuming PM is the Head of State:** The PM is the Head of Government; the President is the Head of State.
2. **Assuming PM has a fixed term:** The PM has no constitutionally fixed tenure. They serve purely "during the pleasure of the President", which is constitutionally interpreted as "so long as they have Lok Sabha majority."
3. **Misunderstanding Article 74:** Some believe the President can act independently on executive matters. The 42nd and 44th Amendments make the PM's and Cabinet's advice binding on the President.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Who appoints the Prime Minister?**
   **Answer:** The President of India appoints the Prime Minister under Article 75 of the Constitution. Usually, the leader of the majority party in the Lok Sabha is invited. 
2. **Can a Rajya Sabha member become Prime Minister?**
   **Answer:** Yes. A member of either the Lok Sabha or Rajya Sabha can become PM. Indira Gandhi and Manmohan Singh were Rajya Sabha members when they became PM. 
3. **What is the minimum age to become the Prime Minister?**
   **Answer:** It is 25 years if the person is a member of the Lok Sabha, and 30 years if they are a member of the Rajya Sabha.
4. **Is the PM's term strictly 5 years?**
   **Answer:** No. The PM holds office as long as they enjoy the majority support in the Lok Sabha. They can be removed anytime via a No-Confidence Motion.
5. **What is a No-Confidence Motion?**
   **Answer:** It is a parliamentary motion moved in the Lok Sabha. If passed, it means the PM has lost the majority, and the entire Council of Ministers must resign.
6. **Can the PM dissolve the Lok Sabha?**
   **Answer:** The PM can advise the President to dissolve the Lok Sabha before its 5-year term expires. The President is generally bound to act on this advice.
7. **Who heads the NITI Aayog?**
   **Answer:** The Prime Minister is the ex-officio Chairman of the NITI Aayog, which is the premier policy 'Think Tank' of the Government of India.
8. **What is the PMO?**
   **Answer:** The Prime Minister's Office (PMO) consists of the immediate staff of the PM, as well as multiple levels of support staff reporting to the Principal Secretary.
9. **Can a non-MP become PM?**
   **Answer:** Yes, but they must become a Member of Parliament (either house) within six months of their appointment.
10. **Kya PM President ko hata sakte hain? (Can PM remove the President?)**
    **Answer:** No. The President can only be removed through a formal impeachment process by the Parliament under Article 61.
11. **PM ki salary kaun decide karta hai? (Who decides PM's salary?)**
    **Answer:** The Parliament of India decides the salary and allowances of the Prime Minister under the Salaries and Allowances of Ministers Act, 1952.
12. **Who takes over if the PM suddenly dies?**
    **Answer:** The Council of Ministers automatically dissolves. The President usually appoints the senior-most cabinet minister as Caretaker PM until a new leader is elected.
13. **Is the PM answerable to the Courts?**
    **Answer:** Yes. While the President enjoys certain immunities under Article 361, the Prime Minister is subject to the rule of law and can be sued or prosecuted.
14. **How can I contact the PM of India?**
    **Answer:** Citizens can write to the PM via the PMO Grievance Portal on pmindia.gov.in, or through the CPGRAMS portal.
15. **Does the PM have veto power over state laws?**
    **Answer:** No. State subjects are under the jurisdiction of the State Legislature and the Chief Minister, subject to the Governor's and President's specific constitutional powers.
16. **Can the PM declare war?**
    **Answer:** The power to declare war lies with the President as the Supreme Commander of the Armed Forces, but it is done on the binding advice of the Cabinet headed by the PM.
17. **What is the difference between PM and Chief Minister (CM)?**
    **Answer:** The PM is the head of the Union government, while a CM is the head of a State government. Their constitutional positions are similar but operate at different federal levels.
18. **क्या प्रधानमंत्री के पास असीमित शक्तियां हैं? (Does PM have absolute power?)**
    **Answer:** नहीं, पीएम संविधान से बंधे हैं। उनके कार्यों की न्यायिक समीक्षा (Judicial Review) की जा सकती है, और वे संसद के प्रति जवाबदेह हैं।
19. **What happens during a Caretaker Government?**
    **Answer:** A caretaker PM manages routine day-to-day administration but cannot make major policy decisions or announce new financial grants.
20. **Who was the first PM of India?**
    **Answer:** Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru was the first Prime Minister of independent India, taking office on August 15, 1947.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** The PM is directly elected by the people of India.
   **Fact:** The people elect MPs. The MPs of the majority party elect their leader, who is appointed as PM by the President.
2. **Myth:** The PM can unilaterally dismiss a Supreme Court Judge.
   **Fact:** A judge can only be removed by an impeachment motion passed by a special majority in both houses of Parliament.
3. **Myth:** The PM has a fixed 5-year tenure.
   **Fact:** The PM's tenure depends entirely on the confidence of the Lok Sabha.
4. **Myth:** Only a member of the Lok Sabha can become PM.
   **Fact:** Members of the Rajya Sabha, and even non-members (for up to 6 months), can become PM.
5. **Myth:** The PM makes all laws in India.
   **Fact:** The PM can propose laws, but they must be passed by both houses of Parliament and assented to by the President.
6. **Myth:** The PM is the Head of State.
   **Fact:** The President is the Head of State; the PM is the Head of Government.
7. **Myth:** PM Cares is a government-owned fund with government money.
   **Fact:** PM CARES is a public charitable trust, and its contributions are entirely voluntary public donations.
8. **Myth:** The PM's advice is optional for the President.
   **Fact:** Under Article 74 (as amended), the advice of the Council of Ministers, headed by the PM, is binding on the President.
9. **Myth:** PM can dismiss a state government whenever they want.
   **Fact:** President's Rule (Article 356) can only be imposed based on a Governor's report and must be approved by Parliament.
10. **Myth:** The PM is immune from criminal prosecution while in office.
    **Fact:** Unlike the President and Governors (Article 361), the PM does not enjoy absolute immunity from civil and criminal proceedings.
