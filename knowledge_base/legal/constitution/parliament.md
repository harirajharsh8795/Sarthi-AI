# The Parliament of India (भारतीय संसद): Powers, Functions, and Procedures

## Overview

**English:**
The Parliament of India is the supreme legislative body of the Republic of India. It is a bicameral legislature composed of the President of India and two houses: the Rajya Sabha (Council of States) and the Lok Sabha (House of the People). The Parliament has the absolute power to make laws, amend the Constitution, oversee the union executive, and manage the country's finances. It serves as the ultimate democratic platform where the voices of the citizens are represented through elected Members of Parliament (MPs).

**Hindi Unicode:**

## Definition

**English:**
Under Article 79 of the Constitution of India, the Parliament is the Union Legislature comprising the President and two Houses. The Lok Sabha represents the people directly through adult suffrage, while the Rajya Sabha represents the states and union territories, functioning as a revising chamber and ensuring federal balance.

**Hindi Unicode:**

## Legal Background

### Applicable Constitutional Articles
- **Part V, Chapter II (Articles 79-122) of the Indian Constitution:**
  - **Article 79:** Constitution of Parliament.
  - **Article 80:** Composition of the Council of States (Rajya Sabha). Maximum 250 members (12 nominated by President).
  - **Article 81:** Composition of the House of the People (Lok Sabha). Maximum 552 members.
  - **Article 84:** Qualification for membership of Parliament (Citizen of India, 30+ years for RS, 25+ years for LS).
  - **Article 85:** Sessions of Parliament, prorogation and dissolution.
  - **Article 102:** Disqualifications for membership (including holding an office of profit).
  - **Article 105:** Powers, privileges, etc., of the Houses of Parliament and of the members and committees thereof.
  - **Article 107-111:** Legislative procedures (Introduction and passing of Bills, Joint sittings, Assent to Bills).
  - **Article 112:** Annual financial statement (Budget).
  - **Tenth Schedule:** Provisions as to disqualification on ground of defection (Anti-Defection Law).

### Purpose
To provide a democratic structure where elected representatives legislate on subjects under the Union List and Concurrent List, maintain checks and balances on the executive, and represent the diverse demographic and geographic interests of India.

---

## Applicability

- **Who is Covered:** All citizens of India (as voters or potential candidates), elected Members of Parliament (MPs), Union Government Ministers, and the President.
- **Who is Exempt:** No one is exempt from laws passed by Parliament unless specifically stated in the legislation or protected by specific Constitutional provisions (e.g., Scheduled Areas under 5th/6th Schedules).
- **Special Conditions:** The Parliament can legislate on State List subjects under special circumstances (e.g., National Emergency under Article 352, or if Rajya Sabha passes a resolution under Article 249).

1. **Right to Vote:** Citizens above 18 years have the right to elect Lok Sabha members.
2. **Right to Contest:** Citizens meeting age and legal criteria can contest parliamentary elections.
3. **Right to Information:** Citizens can track parliamentary proceedings, access Hansard (debates), and know the voting records of MPs.
4. **Right to Petition:** Citizens can submit petitions to parliamentary committees regarding public grievances or pending bills.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure: How a Bill Becomes a Law

**English:**
1. **Drafting:** The concerned Ministry drafts the legislative proposal (Bill).
2. **First Reading:** The Bill is introduced in either House (except Money Bills, which only start in Lok Sabha). The title and objectives are read.
3. **Committee Stage:** The Bill is often referred to a Departmentally Related Standing Committee for detailed scrutiny and public feedback.
4. **Second Reading:** The House debates the Bill clause by clause. Amendments can be proposed and voted upon here.
5. **Third Reading:** The final draft of the Bill is put to a vote. If it gets the required majority, it passes to the other House.
6. **Second House:** The other House goes through the same three readings. If they amend it, it goes back to the originating House.
7. **Presidential Assent:** Once both Houses pass the Bill (or via Joint Sitting in case of a deadlock), it goes to the President. Upon receiving assent, the Bill becomes an Act (Law).

**Hindi Unicode:**
1. **मसौदा तैयार करना:** संबंधित मंत्रालय विधायी प्रस्ताव (विधेयक) का मसौदा तैयार करता है।
2. **प्रथम पठन:** विधेयक किसी भी सदन में पेश किया जाता है (धन विधेयकों को छोड़कर)। शीर्षक और उद्देश्य पढ़े जाते हैं।
3. **समिति चरण:** विधेयक को अक्सर विस्तृत जांच और सार्वजनिक प्रतिक्रिया के लिए स्थायी समिति के पास भेजा जाता है।
4. **द्वितीय पठन:** सदन विधेयक पर खंडवार बहस करता है। संशोधन प्रस्तावित किए जा सकते हैं।
5. **तृतीय पठन:** विधेयक के अंतिम मसौदे पर मतदान होता है। यदि इसे आवश्यक बहुमत मिल जाता है, तो यह दूसरे सदन में जाता है।

## Government Authorities
1. **President of India:** Summons, prorogues the Houses, and dissolves the Lok Sabha. Gives assent to Bills.
2. **Speaker of Lok Sabha:** Presiding officer of the lower house, maintains decorum, decides if a bill is a Money Bill, and presides over Joint Sittings.
3. **Chairman of Rajya Sabha (Vice-President):** Presiding officer of the upper house.
4. **Parliamentary Secretariat:** Independent administrative body managing the day-to-day operations of both Houses.
5. **Election Commission of India (ECI):** Conducts elections for the Parliament.

---

## Penalties & Punishments
1. **Anti-Defection Law (Tenth Schedule):** An MP is disqualified if they voluntarily give up the membership of their party or vote contrary to the party whip without permission.
2. **Breach of Privilege (Article 105):** If a member or citizen breaches the privileges of the House (e.g., disrespecting the chair, publishing confidential committee reports prematurely), the House can punish them with reprimand, suspension, or even imprisonment.
3. **Corrupt Electoral Practices:** Candidates found guilty of bribing voters or hiding criminal records face disqualification and up to 2 to 7 years in prison under the Representation of the People Act and Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS).

---

## Exceptions
- **Money Bills (Article 110):** Can only be introduced in Lok Sabha. Rajya Sabha can only delay a Money Bill for 14 days and make recommendations, which Lok Sabha can accept or reject.
- **Joint Sitting (Article 108):** In case of a deadlock between the two Houses on an ordinary bill, the President can summon a joint sitting. However, joint sittings cannot be called for Money Bills or Constitutional Amendment Bills.
- **Constitutional Amendments (Article 368):** Must be passed by each House separately with a special majority. No joint sitting is allowed.

---

## Important Timelines
- **Maximum Gap Between Sessions:** Not more than 6 months (Article 85). Parliament must meet at least twice a year (usually meets for Budget, Monsoon, and Winter sessions).
- **Rajya Sabha Member Term:** 6 years (1/3rd members retire every 2 years).
- **Lok Sabha Term:** 5 years from the date of its first meeting, unless dissolved earlier.
- **Money Bill Delay Limit:** Rajya Sabha must return a Money Bill within 14 days; otherwise, it is deemed passed.

---

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. **Confusing Parliament with Government:** The Government (Executive) is just a part of the Parliament. The Parliament includes the opposition and holds the Government accountable.
2. **Assuming Rajya Sabha can block a budget:** Rajya Sabha has no real power over Money Bills or the Union Budget; it can only discuss them.
3. **Thinking the President is not part of Parliament:** The President is an integral part of Parliament (Article 79), even though they do not sit in the Houses.
4. **Believing Parliament can amend the basic structure of the Constitution:** The Supreme Court (Kesavananda Bharati case) ruled that Parliament cannot alter the "Basic Structure" of the Constitution.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. What is the difference between Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha?**
**English:** The Lok Sabha is directly elected by the people and has a 5-year term. The Rajya Sabha is indirectly elected by state MLAs, represents states, is a permanent house, and its members have a 6-year term.
**Hindi:** लोकसभा सीधे जनता द्वारा चुनी जाती है और इसका कार्यकाल 5 वर्ष होता है। राज्यसभा अप्रत्यक्ष रूप से राज्य के विधायकों द्वारा चुनी जाती है, यह एक स्थायी सदन है और इसके सदस्यों का कार्यकाल 6 वर्ष होता है।
**Hinglish:** Lok Sabha ke members ko public direct vote dekar chunti hai aur inka term 5 saal hota hai. Rajya Sabha ke members ko state ke MLAs chunte hain aur yeh permanent house hai jiska term 6 saal hota hai.

**Q2. Who presides over the Lok Sabha?**
**English:** The Speaker of the Lok Sabha presides over its sessions. They maintain order, decide who gets to speak, and have the final say on Money Bills.
**Hindi:** लोकसभा अध्यक्ष इसके सत्रों की अध्यक्षता करते हैं। वे व्यवस्था बनाए रखते हैं और धन विधेयकों पर उनका अंतिम निर्णय होता है।
**Hinglish:** Lok Sabha ka Speaker house ko chalata hai. Wahi decide karta hai ki house mein discipline kaise maintain rahega aur Money bill kaunsa hai.

**Q3. Can the President of India make laws without Parliament?**
**English:** Only when Parliament is not in session, the President can issue Ordinances (Article 123). However, these ordinances must be approved by Parliament within 6 weeks of its reassembly.
**Hindi:** केवल जब संसद सत्र में नहीं होती है, तब राष्ट्रपति अध्यादेश (अनुच्छेद 123) जारी कर सकते हैं। हालांकि, इन अध्यादेशों को संसद के फिर से समवेत होने के 6 सप्ताह के भीतर अनुमोदित किया जाना चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Jab Parliament ka session nahi chal raha ho, tabhi President Ordinance nikal sakte hain. Lekin session shuru hone ke 6 weeks ke andar usko Parliament se pass karwana zaroori hai.

**Q4. What is a Money Bill?**
**English:** A Money Bill relates strictly to financial matters like taxation, government expenditure, and borrowing. It can only be introduced in the Lok Sabha with the President's prior recommendation.
**Hinglish:** Money Bill sirf taxes, government spending aur udhaar lene se juda hota hai. Yeh sirf Lok Sabha mein hi pesh kiya ja sakta hai aur iske liye President ki permission zaroori hoti hai.

**Q5. What happens if Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha disagree on a Bill?**
**English:** For ordinary bills, the President can call a Joint Sitting of both Houses to resolve the deadlock by a majority vote. For Constitutional Amendments, the bill fails if not passed by both houses separately.
**Hinglish:** Normal bills ke liye President Joint Sitting bula sakte hain jahan dono houses milkar vote karte hain. Lekin Constitution change karne wale bill dono houses se alag-alag pass hone chahiye, warna bill fail ho jata hai.

**Q6. Can a non-member of Parliament become a Minister?**
**English:** Yes, a non-member can be appointed as a Minister or Prime Minister, but they must get elected to either House of Parliament within 6 months, or they lose their post.
**Hinglish:** Haan, koi aam insaan bhi Minister ya PM ban sakta hai, lekin usko 6 mahine ke andar Lok Sabha ya Rajya Sabha ka election jeetna padega.

**Q7. What is the Zero Hour in Parliament?**
**English:** It is an informal Indian parliamentary innovation starting immediately after the Question Hour, where MPs can raise matters of urgent public importance without prior notice.
**Hindi:** यह प्रश्नकाल के तुरंत बाद शुरू होने वाला समय है, जहाँ सांसद बिना पूर्व सूचना के तत्काल सार्वजनिक महत्व के मामले उठा सकते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Question hour ke theek baad Zero Hour shuru hota hai jisme MPs bina kisi prior notice ke achanak se public importance ke mudde utha sakte hain.

**Q8. What is a No-Confidence Motion?**
**English:** A motion moved in the Lok Sabha expressing a lack of confidence in the Council of Ministers. If passed, the Prime Minister and the entire cabinet must resign.
**Hinglish:** Yeh motion sirf Lok Sabha mein laya jata hai. Agar No-Confidence Motion pass ho jaye, toh PM aur unki saari sarkar ko istifa (resign) dena padta hai.

**Q9. How are Rajya Sabha members elected?**
**English:** They are elected by the elected members of State Legislative Assemblies (MLAs) using a system of proportional representation by means of a single transferable vote.
**Hindi:** उन्हें राज्य विधानसभाओं (विधायकों) के निर्वाचित सदस्यों द्वारा एकल संक्रमणीय मत के माध्यम से आनुपातिक प्रतिनिधित्व प्रणाली का उपयोग करके चुना जाता है।

**Q10. Can the Rajya Sabha be dissolved?**
**English:** No, the Rajya Sabha is a permanent body and is not subject to dissolution. However, one-third of its members retire every second year.
**Hinglish:** Nahi, Rajya Sabha kabhi dissolve (bhang) nahi hoti. Yeh permanent house hai, bas iske ek-tihai (1/3rd) members har 2 saal mein retire hote hain.

**Q11. What is the quorum required to hold a Parliament session?**
**English:** According to Article 100, the quorum is one-tenth of the total number of members of the House, including the presiding officer.
**Hinglish:** Parliament chalane ke liye kam se kam 10% (one-tenth) members ka aana zaroori hai. Ise quorum kehte hain.

**Q12. What is the Anti-Defection Law?**
**English:** Added via the 52nd Amendment (10th Schedule), it disqualifies an MP if they switch parties after the election or vote against their party's whip.
**Hinglish:** Agar koi MP election jeetne ke baad apni party badal leta hai, ya party ke against vote karta hai, toh Anti-Defection Law ke tahat uski MP post cancel ho jati hai.

**Q13. How does Parliament control the national budget?**
**English:** The government cannot spend any money from the Consolidated Fund of India without the Parliament passing the Appropriation Bill and Finance Bill (Budget).
**Hindi:** सरकार विनियोग विधेयक और वित्त विधेयक (बजट) को संसद द्वारा पारित किए बिना भारत की संचित निधि से कोई पैसा खर्च नहीं कर सकती है।

**Q14. What are Parliamentary Committees?**
**English:** Committees made up of MPs that handle complex and specific tasks like scrutinizing bills, auditing government spending (PAC), and investigating specific issues.
**Hinglish:** Yeh MPs ki choti teams hoti hain jo bills ko deeply check karti hain, government ke kharche audit karti hain aur specific topics par research karti hain.

**Q15. Can the Supreme Court interfere in Parliamentary proceedings?**
**English:** Article 122 prohibits courts from inquiring into the validity of any proceedings in Parliament on the ground of alleged procedural irregularity. However, passed laws are subject to judicial review.
**Hinglish:** Supreme Court Parliament ki internal proceedings mein dakhal nahi de sakti. Lekin jab bill pass hokar kanoon ban jata hai, tab court check kar sakti hai ki kanoon constitution ke khilaf toh nahi.

**Q16. What is a Private Member’s Bill?**
**English:** A bill introduced by any MP who is not a Minister. It generally requires one month's notice and has a very low success rate of passing.
**Hindi:** यह किसी भी सांसद द्वारा पेश किया गया विधेयक है जो मंत्री नहीं है। इसके लिए एक महीने के नोटिस की आवश्यकता होती है।

**Q17. How is the Speaker of Lok Sabha elected?**
**English:** The Speaker is elected by the members of the newly constituted Lok Sabha from amongst themselves by a simple majority.
**Hinglish:** Nayi Lok Sabha banne ke baad, sabhi MPs milkar voting ke through apne beech me se hi kisi ek ko Speaker chunte hain.

**Q18. What is 'Prorogation' of a session?**
**English:** Prorogation means the termination of a session of the House by an order made by the President, unlike an adjournment which only pauses a sitting.
**Hinglish:** Session khatam hone ki official announcement ko Prorogation kehte hain, jo President karte hain. Iska matlab us season ka house band ho gaya.

**Q19. What is the maximum strength of Lok Sabha?**
**English:** The maximum permissible strength is 552. Currently, it is fixed at 543 elected members (the Anglo-Indian nominated seats were abolished by the 104th Amendment).
**Hindi:** लोकसभा की अधिकतम अनुमत शक्ति 552 है। वर्तमान में, यह 543 निर्वाचित सदस्यों पर निर्धारित है।

**Q20. Do MPs have immunity from court cases?**
**English:** MPs have immunity from civil arrest during sessions (and 40 days before/after). Under Article 105, they are completely immune from court proceedings for anything said or any vote given inside the Parliament.
**Hinglish:** MPs ko session ke dauran civil cases me arrest nahi kiya ja sakta. Aur house ke andar unhone jo bhi bola ya vote diya, uspe court me case nahi ho sakta.

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** The Prime Minister is the head of Parliament.
   **Fact:** The President is the head of Parliament, and the Speaker/Chairman are the presiding officers of the houses. The PM is the Leader of the House (usually Lok Sabha) and head of the executive.
2. **Myth:** Rajya Sabha can reject a Money Bill.
   **Fact:** Rajya Sabha can only delay a Money Bill for 14 days; it cannot reject or amend it without Lok Sabha's approval.
3. **Myth:** Any MP can introduce the Budget.
   **Fact:** Only a Minister (specifically the Finance Minister) can present the Annual Financial Statement (Budget) with the President's recommendation.
4. **Myth:** The Parliament can amend any part of the Constitution freely.
   **Fact:** Parliament cannot alter the "Basic Structure" of the Constitution (as per the Kesavananda Bharati case).
5. **Myth:** Parliament sessions happen all year round.
   **Fact:** Parliament usually meets in three sessions: Budget (Feb-May), Monsoon (July-Aug), and Winter (Nov-Dec).
6. **Myth:** An MP can vote however they want on any bill.
   **Fact:** If an MP votes against their party whip, they can be disqualified under the Anti-Defection Law.
7. **Myth:** Court can summon the Speaker for what happens inside the Lok Sabha.
   **Fact:** Article 122 provides absolute immunity to parliamentary proceedings from judicial scrutiny regarding procedure.
8. **Myth:** The Vice-President is a member of the Rajya Sabha.
   **Fact:** The Vice-President is the ex-officio Chairman of Rajya Sabha but is NOT a member of the House, hence cannot vote except in a tie.
9. **Myth:** Joint sittings are common for all deadlocked bills.
   **Fact:** Joint sittings are rare and only apply to ordinary bills, never to Constitutional Amendments or Money Bills.
10. **Myth:** Ordinances are permanent laws made by the President.
    **Fact:** Ordinances are temporary. They expire 6 weeks after Parliament reconvenes unless passed as a regular Act.

## Conversation Examples

**Scenario 1: Citizen asking about the Budget**
*Citizen:* Aaj TV par Finance Minister budget padh rahi thi. Kya Parliament ke sabhi MPs ye decide karte hain?
*Assistant:* Haan. Finance Minister sirf budget present karti hain Lok Sabha mein. Uske baad sabhi MPs us par lambi debate karte hain. Jab tak Lok Sabha us budget aur taxation proposals ko pass nahi karti, sarkar ek rupaya bhi kharch nahi kar sakti. Isko Appropriation aur Finance Bill kehte hain.

**Scenario 2: Understanding Rajya Sabha's Role**
*Citizen:* Maine Lok Sabha elections me vote diya hai, par Rajya Sabha ke members ko kaun chunta hai?
*Assistant:* Rajya Sabha ke members ko aam janta direct vote nahi karti. Aap jin MLAs (Vidhayak) ko apne state assembly ke liye chunte hain, wahi MLAs voting karke Rajya Sabha ke MPs ko chunte hain. Isliye isko 'Council of States' kaha jata hai kyunki ye states ko represent karte hain.

**Scenario 3: Laws and Assent**
*Citizen:* Ek bill Lok Sabha aur Rajya Sabha dono se pass ho gaya hai. Kya wo ab law ban gaya?
*Assistant:* Not yet. Dono houses se pass hone ke baad bill President of India ke paas jata hai sign hone ke liye. Jab President apni assent (manzoori) de dete hain, tabhi wo bill officially 'Act' ya kanoon banta hai.

**Scenario 4: Money Bill Confusion**
*Citizen:* Opposition keh rahi hai ki naya tax bill Rajya Sabha mein fail ho jayega. Kya isse bill ruk jayega?
*Assistant:* Nahi. Tax related bills ko 'Money Bill' kaha jata hai. Constitution ke Article 110 ke mutabiq, Money Bill par final power sirf Lok Sabha ke paas hoti hai. Rajya Sabha ise maximum 14 din tak rok sakti hai, par reject nahi kar sakti. 14 din baad ye apne aap pass mana jata hai.

**Scenario 5: MPs and Immunity**
*Citizen:* Ek MP ne Parliament ke andar bahut controversial statement di thi. Us par defamation case kyun nahi hota?
*Assistant:* Article 105 of the Constitution MPs ko 'Parliamentary Privilege' deta hai. Iska matlab hai ki Parliament ke andar bole gaye kisi bhi shabd ya diye gaye vote par desh ke kisi bhi court mein case nahi chalaya ja sakta, taki wo bina darr ke apne mudde rakh sakein.

**Scenario 6: Ordinance Route**
*Citizen:* Parliament to band hai, to phir sarkar ne naya ban (law) kaise laagu kar diya?
*Assistant:* Jab Parliament session me nahi hoti aur emergency me kanoon chahiye hota hai, toh President Article 123 ke tahat 'Ordinance' (Adhyadesh) jaari kar sakte hain. Ye kanoon ki tarah hi kaam karta hai. Lekin jab Parliament wapas khulegi, toh 6 weeks ke andar ise pass karwana zaroori hai, warna ye expire ho jayega.

**Scenario 7: Questioning Ministers**
*Citizen:* Agar mujhe kisi ministry ke kaam par doubt hai, toh uski details kaise nikalwa sakte hain?
*Assistant:* Aapke area ke MP 'Question Hour' mein us minister se seedha sawal pooch sakte hain. Har roz Parliament shuru hote hi pehla ghanta Question Hour hota hai jahan ministers ko MPs ke oral ya written sawalon ka jawab dena padta hai. Ye public ke liye sarkar ko accountable banata hai.

**Scenario 8: Passing of Constitutional Amendments**
*Citizen:* Kya majority government akele Constitution badal sakti hai?
*Assistant:* Aam taur par nahi. Constitution amend karne ke liye (Article 368) dono houses mein 'Special Majority' chahiye hoti hai (matlab house ke 50% total members + 2/3rd present and voting). Aur kuch important federal cases mein, aadhi states ki assemblies ka pass karna bhi zaroori hota hai.

**Scenario 9: Joint Sitting**
*Citizen:* Kya hoga agar Lok Sabha bill pass karde aur Rajya Sabha reject karde? (Non-money bill)
*Assistant:* Agar aam (ordinary) bill par deadlock ho jaye aur 6 mahine beet jayein, to President Article 108 ke tahat ek 'Joint Sitting' bula sakte hain. Yahan Lok Sabha aur Rajya Sabha dono ek sath baithte hain aur majority vote se faisla hota hai. Lok Sabha ki numbers zyada hone ke karan aam taur par unka palda bhari rehta hai.

**Scenario 10: Anti-Defection**
*Citizen:* MP ne election kisi aur party se jeeta aur ab wo ruling party join kar raha hai bina resign kiye. Kya ye allowed hai?
*Assistant:* Nahi, 10th Schedule (Anti-Defection Law) ke mutabiq, agar koi MP election jeetne ke baad apni marzi se party chodta hai ya party ke instructions ke against vote karta hai, toh Speaker uski MP ki post ko turant cancel kar sakte hain (disqualify kar sakte hain).

**Scenario 11: Becoming a Minister**
*Citizen:* Kya main election hare bina Prime Minister ban sakta hu?
*Assistant:* Agar ruling party chahe toh kisi bhi citizen ko PM ya minister bana sakti hai, bhale hi wo MP na ho. Lekin condition ye hai ki us insaan ko 6 mahine ke andar Lok Sabha ya Rajya Sabha ka election jeet kar MP banna padega (Article 75).

**Scenario 12: Standing Committees**
*Citizen:* Parliament mein bills jaldi me pass ho jate hain, unki checking kahan hoti hai?
*Assistant:* Bills par deep research 'Parliamentary Standing Committees' karti hain. In committees mein dono houses ke sabhi partiyo ke MPs hote hain. Wo expert advice lete hain, public se feedback mangte hain, aur phir bill mein changes suggest karte hain. Ye kaam camera ke piche detail mein hota hai.

**Scenario 13: Impeachment**
*Citizen:* Kya Parliament President of India ya Supreme Court judges ko hata sakti hai?
*Assistant:* Haan. President ko 'Impeachment' ke zariye (Article 61) aur Supreme Court/High Court judges ko ek special removal motion ke zariye hataya ja sakta hai, jiske liye Parliament dono houses mein special majority chahiye hoti hai. Ye process bahut mushkil aur strict hai.

**Scenario 14: Dissolving Lok Sabha**
*Citizen:* Lok Sabha 5 saal se pehle kaise bhang (dissolve) ho sakti hai?
*Assistant:* Agar ruling party Lok Sabha mein apna majority support kho de, aur koi aur party sarkar na bana paye, to PM President ko Lok Sabha dissolve karne ki salah de sakte hain. Aise mein mid-term elections karwane padte hain.

**Scenario 15: Knowing your MP's work**
*Citizen:* Main kaise check karu ki mera MP Parliament mein kaam kar bhi raha hai ya nahi?
*Assistant:* Aap `loksabha.nic.in` ya 'PRS Legislative Research' website par ja kar apne MP ka attendance record, unhone kitne sawal pooche, aur kin debates mein hissa liya, sab bilkul free mein check kar sakte hain.
