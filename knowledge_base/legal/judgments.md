# Judgments in Indian Law

## Overview
**English:** A judgment is the final decision of a court of law in a legal proceeding. It officially records the court’s findings, the legal reasoning behind the decision, and the final outcome, which could be an acquittal, conviction, or a civil decree. Understanding a judgment is crucial as it determines the rights, liabilities, and future legal recourse available to the parties involved.
**Hindi:** निर्णय (जजमेंट) एक कानूनी कार्यवाही में कानून की अदालत का अंतिम फैसला होता है। यह आधिकारिक तौर पर अदालत के निष्कर्षों, फैसले के पीछे के कानूनी तर्क और अंतिम परिणाम को दर्ज करता है, जो बरी, दोषसिद्धि या दीवानी डिक्री हो सकता है। निर्णय को समझना महत्वपूर्ण है क्योंकि यह शामिल पक्षों के अधिकारों, देनदारियों और भविष्य के कानूनी विकल्पों को निर्धारित करता है।
**Hinglish:** Judgment ek legal case mein court ka final faisla hota hai. Yeh officially court ke findings, decision ke pichhe ke legal reasoning, aur final outcome (jaise bari hona, saza, ya civil decree) ko record karta hai. Ek judgment ko samajhna bahut zaroori hai kyunki yeh parties ke rights, liabilities, aur aage ke legal options decide karta hai.

## Quick Summary
**English:** Judgments determine the fate of civil and criminal cases. After hearing all arguments and examining evidence, the judge pronounces the judgment in open court. It includes the facts of the case, points for determination, the decision thereon, and the reasons for the decision.
**Hindi:** निर्णय दीवानी और आपराधिक मामलों का भाग्य तय करते हैं। सभी दलीलें सुनने और सबूतों की जांच करने के बाद, न्यायाधीश खुली अदालत में निर्णय सुनाते हैं। इसमें मामले के तथ्य, निर्धारण के बिंदु, उस पर निर्णय और निर्णय के कारण शामिल होते हैं।
**Hinglish:** Judgments civil aur criminal cases ka anjaam tay karte hain. Saari arguments sunne aur evidence dekhne ke baad, judge open court mein faisla sunate hain. Isme case ke facts, determination ke points, final decision, aur us decision ka reason hota hai.

## Definition
**English:** In Indian law, particularly under Section 2(9) of the Code of Civil Procedure (CPC) and relevant provisions of the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), a judgment is defined as the statement given by the Judge on the grounds of a decree or order.
**Hindi:** भारतीय कानून में, विशेष रूप से नागरिक प्रक्रिया संहिता (सीपीसी) की धारा 2(9) और भारतीय नागरिक सुरक्षा संहिता (बीएनएसएस) के प्रासंगिक प्रावधानों के तहत, निर्णय को न्यायाधीश द्वारा एक डिक्री या आदेश के आधार पर दिए गए बयान के रूप में परिभाषित किया गया है।
**Hinglish:** Indian law mein, CPC ke Section 2(9) aur BNSS ke provisions ke mutabiq, judgment judge dwara decree ya order ke grounds par diya gaya ek statement hota hai.

## Why It Matters
**English:** A judgment matters because it resolves the dispute, sets a precedent for future cases, determines penalties or compensations, and opens the door for higher appellate review if parties are dissatisfied. It is the core function of the judicial system.
**Hindi:** निर्णय इसलिए मायने रखता है क्योंकि यह विवाद को सुलझाता है, भविष्य के मामलों के लिए एक मिसाल कायम करता है, दंड या मुआवजे का निर्धारण करता है, और यदि पक्ष असंतुष्ट हैं तो उच्च अपीलीय समीक्षा का द्वार खोलता है। यह न्यायिक प्रणाली का मुख्य कार्य है।
**Hinglish:** Judgment isliye zaroori hai kyunki yeh dispute ko solve karta hai, aage ke cases ke liye example set karta hai, penalties ya compensation tay karta hai, aur parties ke dissatisfaction par appeal ka rasta kholta hai.

## Legal Background
**History:** The concept of formally written judgments evolved from common law traditions. Over decades, Indian courts have standardized the structure of judgments to ensure transparency, accountability, and clarity for litigants.
**Purpose:** To provide a reasoned, legally sound, and binding resolution to conflicts, ensuring justice is not only done but seen to be done.
**Applicable Acts:** 
- Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), 2023 (Replaces CrPC)
- Code of Civil Procedure (CPC), 1908
- The Constitution of India, 1950
- Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023
**Relevant Constitutional Articles:**
- Article 141: Law declared by the Supreme Court to be binding on all courts.
- Article 142: Enforcement of decrees and orders of the Supreme Court.
**Relevant Sections:**
- BNSS Section 392 to Section 398 (Chapter on Judgment).
- CPC Section 2(9) defining Judgment.
- CPC Order XX dealing with Judgment and Decree.

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** All individuals, corporations, and state entities involved in litigation within the Indian territory.
- **Who is exempt:** Matters settled entirely outside of court through alternative dispute resolution (ADR) prior to any court filing may not require a formal court judgment, though arbitral awards carry similar weight.
- **Special conditions:** Certain special tribunals (like NGT, NCLT) pronounce "orders" or "awards" which function similarly to judgments but follow their specific statutory frameworks.

## Rights of Citizens
**English:** Citizens have the right to receive a copy of the judgment, the right to appeal to a higher court, the right to understand the judgment in a language they comprehend (often through translation), and the right to seek a stay of execution under specific conditions.
**Hindi:** नागरिकों को निर्णय की प्रति प्राप्त करने का अधिकार, उच्च न्यायालय में अपील करने का अधिकार, निर्णय को उस भाषा में समझने का अधिकार है जिसे वे समझते हैं (अक्सर अनुवाद के माध्यम से), और विशिष्ट शर्तों के तहत निष्पादन पर रोक लगाने का अधिकार है।
**Hinglish:** Citizens ko judgment ki copy paane ka right, higher court mein appeal karne ka right, apni language mein faisle ko samajhne ka right, aur execution par stay maangne ka right hai.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English:** Litigants must respect and abide by the judgment until it is overturned or modified by a higher court. Willful disobedience can lead to contempt of court proceedings.
**Hindi:** वादियों को उच्च न्यायालय द्वारा पलटे या संशोधित किए जाने तक निर्णय का सम्मान और पालन करना चाहिए। जानबूझकर अवज्ञा अदालत की अवमानना ​​की कार्यवाही का कारण बन सकती है।
**Hinglish:** Litigants ki duty hai ki wo court ke faisle ki izzat karein aur usko follow karein jab tak ki higher court use badal na de. Faisla na manne par contempt of court ho sakta hai.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:** 
1. Hearing Concludes: The judge finishes hearing all arguments.
2. Reserving Judgment: The court may reserve the judgment to study the files or pronounce it immediately.
3. Pronouncement: The judgment is pronounced in open court.
4. Signing: The presiding judge signs and dates the judgment.
5. Certified Copy: Parties apply for a certified copy of the judgment for record or appeal purposes.
**Hindi:**
1. सुनवाई समाप्त: न्यायाधीश सभी दलीलें सुनना समाप्त करता है।
2. निर्णय सुरक्षित रखना: अदालत फाइलों का अध्ययन करने के लिए निर्णय सुरक्षित रख सकती है या तुरंत सुना सकती है।
3. घोषणा: खुली अदालत में निर्णय सुनाया जाता है।
4. हस्ताक्षर: पीठासीन न्यायाधीश निर्णय पर हस्ताक्षर करता है और दिनांक डालता है।
5. प्रमाणित प्रति: पक्ष रिकॉर्ड या अपील उद्देश्यों के लिए निर्णय की प्रमाणित प्रति के लिए आवेदन करते हैं।
**Hinglish:**
1. Hearing Concludes: Judge sabhi arguments sunna khatam karte hain.
2. Reserving Judgment: Court case files study karne ke liye faisla reserve rakh sakti hai ya turant suna sakti hai.
3. Pronouncement: Open court mein faisla sunaya jata hai.
4. Signing: Presiding judge judgment par sign aur date dalte hain.
5. Certified Copy: Parties record ya appeal ke liye certified copy apply karti hain.

## Required Documents
- Court application form for Certified Copy.
- ID Proof of the applicant.
- Vakalatnama (if applied by an advocate).
- Requisite court fees (stamps).

## Government Authorities
- District and Sessions Courts
- High Courts of respective states
- Supreme Court of India
- Special Courts and Tribunals (e.g., CBI Courts, Family Courts)
- Legal Services Authorities (NALSA, SALSA)

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Contempt of Court:** Under the Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, non-compliance with a judgment can lead to simple imprisonment for up to 6 months, a fine up to ₹2000, or both.
- **Execution Proceedings:** Failure to satisfy a civil decree may result in attachment of property or civil imprisonment (up to 3 months).

## Exceptions
- Judgments passed ex-parte (where one party was absent) can be set aside upon a valid application showing sufficient cause for non-appearance.
- Inherent powers of the court can be invoked to rectify clerical or arithmetical mistakes in judgments under CPC Section 152.

## Important Timelines
- **Pronouncement:** Within 30 days of the conclusion of arguments in civil cases (extendable to 60 days under exceptional circumstances).
- **Appeal:** Typically 30 days for lower courts, 60-90 days for High Courts and the Supreme Court, subject to specific statutes and the Limitation Act, 1963.
- **Copy Delivery:** Usually within 7-14 days of applying for a certified copy.

## Common Legal Mistakes
- Missing the limitation period for filing an appeal.
- Failing to formally apply for a certified copy, relying only on internet copies which are often not accepted for official filings.
- Not seeking a stay on the lower court's judgment while an appeal is pending.
- Ignoring the directions given in a judgment, leading to contempt charges.

## Safety Tips
**English:** Always obtain a certified copy of the judgment. Do not fall for scams promising to 'alter' judgments outside the court system. Maintain all court receipts and records.
**Hindi:** हमेशा निर्णय की प्रमाणित प्रति प्राप्त करें। अदालत प्रणाली के बाहर निर्णयों को 'बदलने' का वादा करने वाले घोटालों में न पड़ें। अदालत की सभी रसीदें और रिकॉर्ड बनाए रखें।
**Hinglish:** Hamesha judgment ki certified copy lein. Court system ke bahar faisla 'badalne' ka wada karne wale scams se bachein. Apni court receipts aur records sambhal kar rakhein.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **What is a judgment?**
   **English:** A judgment is the final decision pronounced by a judge in a court of law after evaluating evidence and arguments.
   **Hindi:** निर्णय अदालत में साक्ष्य और दलीलों का मूल्यांकन करने के बाद न्यायाधीश द्वारा सुनाया गया अंतिम फैसला है।
   **Hinglish:** Judgment court mein sabhi suboot aur arguments dekhne ke baad judge ka final faisla hota hai.

2. **How is a judgment different from an order?**
   **English:** An order can be an interim directive during a case, while a judgment is the final determination of the rights and liabilities of the parties.
   **Hindi:** आदेश एक मामले के दौरान एक अंतरिम निर्देश हो सकता है, जबकि निर्णय पक्षों के अधिकारों और देनदारियों का अंतिम निर्धारण है।
   **Hinglish:** Order case ke dauran ek temporary nirdesh ho sakta hai, jabki judgment case ka final faisla hota hai.

3. **Can a judgment be changed after it is signed?**
   **English:** Once signed, a judge cannot alter or review their own judgment except to correct clerical or arithmetical errors.
   **Hindi:** एक बार हस्ताक्षर हो जाने के बाद, कोई न्यायाधीश लिपिकीय या अंकगणितीय त्रुटियों को सुधारने के अलावा अपने स्वयं के निर्णय को बदल या समीक्षा नहीं कर सकता है।
   **Hinglish:** Sign hone ke baad, judge apne faisle ko badal nahi sakte, bas typing ya calculation ki mistakes theek kar sakte hain.

4. **What is a reserved judgment?**
   **English:** When a judge finishes hearing a case but delays pronouncing the decision to study the matter further, it is called a reserved judgment.
   **Hindi:** जब कोई न्यायाधीश किसी मामले की सुनवाई समाप्त कर लेता है, लेकिन मामले का आगे अध्ययन करने के लिए निर्णय सुनाने में देरी करता है, तो इसे सुरक्षित निर्णय कहा जाता है।
   **Hinglish:** Jab judge sunwai puri karne ke baad faisla turant nahi sunate aur baad ke liye rakhte hain, toh use reserved judgment kehte hain.

5. **Is it mandatory for a judge to give reasons for a judgment?**
   **English:** Yes, a judgment must contain the points for determination, the decision, and the legal reasoning behind that decision.
   **Hindi:** हां, एक निर्णय में निर्धारण के बिंदु, निर्णय और उस निर्णय के पीछे का कानूनी तर्क शामिल होना चाहिए।
   **Hinglish:** Haan, faisle mein reasons aur legal logic hona zaroori hai.

6. **How do I get a copy of my judgment?**
   **English:** You or your advocate must file an application in the copying section of the court along with the requisite fees to get a certified copy.
   **Hindi:** प्रमाणित प्रति प्राप्त करने के लिए आपको या आपके वकील को आवश्यक शुल्क के साथ अदालत के प्रतिलिपि अनुभाग में एक आवेदन दाखिल करना होगा।
   **Hinglish:** Certified copy lene ke liye court ke copying branch mein ek application aur fees jama karni hoti hai.

7. **What is a certified copy?**
   **English:** It is an official copy of the judgment bearing the seal and signature of the court officer, proving its authenticity.
   **Hindi:** यह अदालत के अधिकारी की मुहर और हस्ताक्षर वाले निर्णय की एक आधिकारिक प्रति है, जो इसकी प्रामाणिकता साबित करती है।
   **Hinglish:** Yeh court ki seal aur sign wali official copy hoti hai jo asaliyat ka proof hai.

8. **Can I view a judgment online?**
   **English:** Yes, most district and high courts upload judgments on the eCourts portal and their official websites for public access.
   **Hindi:** हां, अधिकांश जिला और उच्च न्यायालय जनता की पहुंच के लिए ई-कोर्ट पोर्टल और अपनी आधिकारिक वेबसाइटों पर निर्णय अपलोड करते हैं।
   **Hinglish:** Haan, eCourts portal aur official websites par judgments online dekhe ja sakte hain.

9. **Are online copies valid for filing appeals?**
   **English:** Usually, a formally certified physical copy is required to file an appeal in a higher court, though rules are gradually relaxing.
   **Hindi:** आम तौर पर, उच्च न्यायालय में अपील दायर करने के लिए औपचारिक रूप से प्रमाणित भौतिक प्रति की आवश्यकता होती है।
   **Hinglish:** Appeal ke liye usually formally certified copy chahiye hoti hai, internet copy har jagah nahi chalti.

10. **What is an ex-parte judgment?**
    **English:** It is a judgment passed when only one party appears before the court and the other party fails to show up despite receiving notice.
    **Hindi:** यह तब पारित किया गया निर्णय है जब केवल एक पक्ष अदालत के सामने पेश होता है और दूसरा पक्ष नोटिस मिलने के बावजूद उपस्थित होने में विफल रहता है।
    **Hinglish:** Jab ek party court mein nahi aati aur court doosri party ki baat sun kar faisla suna de, toh use ex-parte judgment kehte hain.

11. **Can an ex-parte judgment be canceled?**
    **English:** Yes, the absent party can file an application to set it aside if they have a valid, justifiable reason for their absence.
    **Hindi:** हां, अनुपस्थित पक्ष यदि अपनी अनुपस्थिति का वैध कारण रखता है, तो इसे रद्द करने के लिए आवेदन दाखिल कर सकता है।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, agar na aane ka genuine reason ho toh us faisle ko cancel karwane ki application di ja sakti hai.

12. **How much time do I have to appeal a judgment?**
    **English:** The time limit varies but is generally 30 to 90 days depending on the nature of the case and the appellate court.
    **Hindi:** समय सीमा अलग-अलग होती है, लेकिन मामले की प्रकृति और अपीलीय अदालत के आधार पर आम तौर पर 30 से 90 दिन होती है।
    **Hinglish:** Appeal karne ka time case aur court par depend karta hai, mostly 30 se 90 din ka time milta hai.

13. **What happens if I don't follow a civil judgment?**
    **English:** The winning party can file an execution petition to enforce the judgment, which can lead to attachment of your property or bank accounts.
    **Hindi:** जीतने वाला पक्ष निर्णय को लागू करने के लिए एक निष्पादन याचिका दायर कर सकता है, जिससे आपकी संपत्ति कुर्क हो सकती है।
    **Hinglish:** Jeetne wali party execution petition dal sakti hai jisse aapki property ya bank account attach ho sakta hai.

14. **What is a decree?**
    **English:** A decree is the formal expression of an adjudication which conclusively determines the rights of the parties regarding civil matters in controversy.
    **Hindi:** एक डिक्री एक न्यायनिर्णयन की औपचारिक अभिव्यक्ति है जो विवाद में दीवानी मामलों के संबंध में पक्षों के अधिकारों को निर्णायक रूप से निर्धारित करती है।
    **Hinglish:** Decree ek formal order hota hai jo civil case mein parties ke rights final kar deta hai.

15. **Does the Supreme Court judgment apply to everyone?**
    **English:** Under Article 141 of the Constitution, the law declared by the Supreme Court is binding on all courts within the territory of India.
    **Hindi:** संविधान के अनुच्छेद 141 के तहत, सर्वोच्च न्यायालय द्वारा घोषित कानून भारत के क्षेत्र के भीतर सभी अदालतों के लिए बाध्यकारी है।
    **Hinglish:** Article 141 ke tahat, Supreme Court ka banaya law India ki saari courts par lagoo hota hai.

16. **Can a judgment be written in Hindi or a regional language?**
    **English:** Yes, subordinate courts can write judgments in the official language of the state, though High Courts and the Supreme Court predominantly use English.
    **Hindi:** हां, अधीनस्थ अदालतें राज्य की आधिकारिक भाषा में निर्णय लिख सकती हैं, हालांकि उच्च न्यायालय मुख्य रूप से अंग्रेजी का उपयोग करते हैं।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, lower courts state ki language (jaise Hindi) mein faisla likh sakti hain, par High Courts mainly English use karti hain.

17. **What is a translation of a judgment?**
    **English:** If the judgment is not in the language understood by the accused/parties, they have the right to request a translated copy.
    **Hindi:** यदि निर्णय आरोपी/पक्षों द्वारा समझी जाने वाली भाषा में नहीं है, तो उन्हें अनुवादित प्रति का अनुरोध करने का अधिकार है।
    **Hinglish:** Agar faisla aapki bhasha mein nahi hai, toh aap uski translated copy maang sakte hain.

18. **What is a stay of judgment?**
    **English:** A stay is a temporary halt ordered by an appellate court preventing the execution or enforcement of the lower court's judgment.
    **Hindi:** स्टे (रोक) एक अपीलीय अदालत द्वारा आदेशित एक अस्थायी रोक है जो निचली अदालत के निर्णय के निष्पादन को रोकती है।
    **Hinglish:** Stay ek order hai jisse higher court lower court ke faisle par temporary rok laga deti hai.

19. **Can the media publish a judgment immediately?**
    **English:** Yes, once pronounced in open court, judgments are public records and can be published unless specific gag orders or privacy laws apply.
    **Hindi:** हां, खुली अदालत में सुनाए जाने के बाद, निर्णय सार्वजनिक रिकॉर्ड होते हैं और प्रकाशित किए जा सकते हैं जब तक कि विशेष रोक आदेश लागू न हों।
    **Hinglish:** Haan, open court mein aane ke baad faisla public record hota hai aur media use chaap sakti hai, jab tak koi rok na ho.

20. **Is a judgment final?**
    **English:** It is final for the court that delivered it, but it can be appealed, reviewed, or revised by higher courts in accordance with the law.
    **Hindi:** यह उस अदालत के लिए अंतिम है जिसने इसे सुनाया है, लेकिन कानून के अनुसार उच्च अदालतों द्वारा इसकी अपील, समीक्षा या संशोधन किया जा सकता है।
    **Hinglish:** Uss court ke liye wo final hai, lekin higher court usme appeal, review ya changes kar sakti hai.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** A judgment is just a one-page document declaring win or loss.
   **Fact:** Judgments are detailed documents explaining the entire case history, evidence, legal reasoning, and final decision.
2. **Myth:** You can privately negotiate a change in the judgment with the judge after it is pronounced.
   **Fact:** Once a judgment is signed, the judge is *functus officio* (mandate expired) and cannot alter it except for clerical errors.
3. **Myth:** Online judgments from eCourts are sufficient for all legal purposes.
   **Fact:** Most courts require a physically stamped 'certified copy' to file an appeal.
4. **Myth:** If you do not agree with a judgment, you do not have to follow it.
   **Fact:** Judgments are legally binding. Non-compliance results in contempt of court or forced execution.
5. **Myth:** All judgments take years to be delivered.
   **Fact:** Timelines vary. Once arguments conclude, the law mandates pronouncement within 30-60 days.
6. **Myth:** Only the Supreme Court creates binding legal precedents.
   **Fact:** High Court judgments are also binding on all subordinate courts within their respective states.
7. **Myth:** An ex-parte judgment is completely invalid and unenforceable.
   **Fact:** An ex-parte judgment is fully valid and enforceable unless legally set aside by an application or appeal.
8. **Myth:** A judge can write anything they want in a judgment.
   **Fact:** Judgments must be strictly based on the pleadings, evidence on record, and applicable laws.
9. **Myth:** Judgments are secret documents meant only for the parties.
   **Fact:** Judgments are public documents (with exceptions in sensitive matters like sexual assault or family disputes).
10. **Myth:** Filing an appeal automatically stays the original judgment.
    **Fact:** An appeal does not operate as an automatic stay. You must file a specific application requesting a stay.

## Conversation Examples

**Conversation 1: Meaning of Judgment**
Citizen: Mera case khatam ho gaya, ab judge sahab ne 'judgment' reserve kiya hai. Iska kya matlab hai?
Assistant: Judgment reserve karne ka matlab hai ki court ne saari behas sun li hai. Ab judge sahab case ki files aur evidence ko dhyan se padh kar apna final faisla taiyar karenge, aur agli date par sunayenge. (The judge has heard all arguments and is studying the file to prepare the final decision).

**Conversation 2: Certified Copy**
Citizen: Mujhe mere case ka faisla mil gaya hai, par vakil keh rahe hain ki certified copy chahiye. Wo kya hoti hai?
Assistant: Certified copy court ki taraf se di gayi official copy hoti hai jis par court ki seal aur sign hote hain. Appeal karne ya record ke liye yehi copy valid maani jati hai. Aap copying branch se ise nikalwa sakte hain.

**Conversation 3: Ex-parte Judgment**
Citizen: Main court nahi ja saka aur judge ne ex-parte judgment de diya mere khilaf. Ab kya karun?
Assistant: Agar aapke paas court na aane ka ek pakka aur genuine karan (jaise medical emergency) tha, toh aapke lawyer ek application daal sakte hain is faisle ko set aside (cancel) karne ke liye.

**Conversation 4: Online Access**
Citizen: Kya main apna court ka judgment online padh sakta hu?
Assistant: Haan, aap eCourts services portal ya app par apne case number, party name ya FIR number dal kar judgment online padh ya download kar sakte hain.

**Conversation 5: Language Issue**
Citizen: Judgment English mein aaya hai par mujhe samajh nahi aati. Kya mujhe Hindi copy mil sakti hai?
Assistant: Haan, court ka farz hai ki agar aapko bhasha samajh nahi aati, toh aapko judgment ka anuvad (translation) samjhaya jaye. Aap apne lawyer ke through iski request kar sakte hain.

**Conversation 6: Execution**
Citizen: Main civil case jeet gaya aur judgment mere haq mein hai, par dusri party paise nahi de rahi.
Assistant: Aise mein aapko 'Execution Petition' file karni hogi. Court dusri party ko force karegi aur unki property attach kar sakti hai paisa recover karne ke liye.

**Conversation 7: Time for Appeal**
Citizen: Judgment aaye 15 din ho gaye hain, appeal karne ka kitna time hota hai?
Assistant: Aam taur par lower court ke faisle ke khilaf appeal karne ke liye 30 din ka time milta hai. Behtar hoga aap turant certified copy apply karein aur process shuru karein.

**Conversation 8: Changing the Order**
Citizen: Judge sahab se kahkar faisle mein kuch changes karwa sakte hain kya?
Assistant: Nahi, ek baar judgment sign ho jaye, toh judge use badal nahi sakte. Aapko sirf higher court mein appeal karni hogi isko badalne ke liye.

**Conversation 9: Contempt of Court**
Citizen: Agar main court ka judgment na manu toh kya hoga?
Assistant: Court ka order na manne par 'Contempt of Court' ka case lag sakta hai, jisme fine lag sakta hai ya jail bhi ho sakti hai.

**Conversation 10: Difference from Order**
Citizen: Order aur Judgment mein kya farak hai?
Assistant: Order case ke beech mein diya gaya koi nirdesh ho sakta hai (jaise bail dena ya notice bhejna). Par judgment case ke end mein final faisla hota hai jo decide karta hai ki kaun jita aur kaun hara.

**Conversation 11: Free Copy**
Citizen: Criminal case mein main aropi tha, kya mujhe judgment ki copy kharidni padegi?
Assistant: Nahi, criminal cases (BNSS ke tahat) mein judgment sunane ke baad aropi ko free of cost ek copy turant di jati hai.

**Conversation 12: Errors in Judgment**
Citizen: Faisle mein mera naam aur ek amount galat type ho gaya hai. Ab kya hoga?
Assistant: Typographical ya calculation mistakes (clerical errors) ko theek karne ke liye judge sahab se section 152 CPC ya BNSS ke relevant section ke under request ki ja sakti hai bina appeal ke.

**Conversation 13: Bail Pending Appeal**
Citizen: Mujhe saza mili hai, kya judgment sunte hi jail jana padega?
Assistant: Agar saza kam saal ki hai, toh court usi waqt kuch dino ki bail de sakti hai taaki aap higher court mein appeal file kar sakein aur stay le sakein.

**Conversation 14: Stay of Judgment**
Citizen: Appeal daal di hai, to kya purana judgment automatically ruk jayega?
Assistant: Nahi, automatically nahi rukta. Aapko higher court se specifically stay order maangna padega. Jab tak stay nahi milta, lower court ka faisla lagu rehta hai.

**Conversation 15: Publishing**
Citizen: Kya main apna judgement social media pe daal sakta hu?
Assistant: Aam taur par public court judgments open hote hain. Par agar divorce, sexual assault ya child protection jaisa sensitive case hai (jisme 'in-camera' hearing hui ho), toh bina court ki permission isko publish karna apradh hai.
