# Passport and Visa Guidelines under Indian Law

## Overview
**English:** An Indian passport is an official document issued by the Ministry of External Affairs, Government of India, to Indian citizens for international travel. A visa is an endorsement on a passport indicating that the holder is allowed to enter, leave, or stay for a specified period in a country. Both are governed by strict statutory guidelines in India.
**Hindi:** भारतीय पासपोर्ट विदेश मंत्रालय (MEA), भारत सरकार द्वारा जारी एक आधिकारिक दस्तावेज है, जो भारतीय नागरिकों को अंतरराष्ट्रीय यात्रा के लिए दिया जाता है। वीज़ा पासपोर्ट पर एक प्रविष्टि है जो दर्शाती है कि धारक को किसी देश में प्रवेश करने, छोड़ने या एक निर्दिष्ट अवधि तक रहने की अनुमति है।
**Hinglish:** Indian passport ek official document hai jo Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) Indian citizens ko international travel ke liye issue karti hai. Visa ek endorsement hota hai passport pe jo allow karta hai kisi country me enter, exit ya stay karne ke liye. Dono ke liye strict legal rules hain.

## Quick Summary
**English:** Passports are essential for establishing citizenship and traveling abroad. The Passports Act of 1967 regulates issuance, refusal, and impounding of passports. The Foreigners Act, 1946, regulates the entry, presence, and departure of foreigners in India (visas).

**Hinglish:** Citizenship establish karne aur abroad travel ke liye passports zaroori hain. Passports Act 1967 inki issuance aur cancellation ko regulate karta hai, jabki Foreigners Act 1946 India mein foreigners ke visa aur entry ko control karta hai.

## Definition
**English:**
- **Passport:** A travel document issued by a country's government to its citizens that verifies the identity and nationality of the holder for international travel.
- **Visa:** A conditional authorization granted by a territory to a foreigner, allowing them to enter, remain within, or to leave that territory.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Passports serve as the ultimate proof of citizenship abroad. Traveling without a valid passport or overstaying a visa constitutes a severe legal offense under national and international law, leading to deportation, hefty fines, or imprisonment.
**Hindi:** पासपोर्ट विदेशों में नागरिकता के अंतिम प्रमाण के रूप में कार्य करते हैं। बिना वैध पासपोर्ट के यात्रा करना या वीज़ा अवधि से अधिक रुकना राष्ट्रीय और अंतरराष्ट्रीय कानून के तहत एक गंभीर अपराध है, जिससे निर्वासन, भारी जुर्माना या कारावास हो सकता है।
**Hinglish:** Passports abroad mein citizenship ka ultimate proof hain. Valid passport ke bina travel karna ya visa overstay karna ek serious offense hai, jisse deportation, fine ya jail ho sakti hai.

## Legal Background
The legal framework governing passports and visas in India comprises multiple acts and rules:
- **The Passports Act, 1967:** This act regulates the issue of passports and travel documents to Indian citizens and other persons.
    - **Section 5:** Applications for passports, travel documents, etc., and orders thereon.
    - **Section 6:** Refusal of passports, travel documents, etc.
    - **Section 10:** Variation, impounding and revocation of passports and travel documents.
    - **Section 12:** Offenses and penalties.
- **The Foreigners Act, 1946:** Empowers the government to regulate the entry, presence, and departure of foreigners in India.
    - **Section 3:** Power to make orders to prohibit, regulate or restrict foreigners.
    - **Section 14:** Penalty for contravention of provisions of the Act (e.g., visa overstay).
- **The Citizenship Act, 1955:** Deals with the acquisition and termination of Indian citizenship (since passport issuance is deeply tied to citizenship).
- **Registration of Foreigners Act, 1939 & Rules, 1992:** Mandates registration of foreigners staying beyond a specified duration.
- **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 (applicable sections):** Offences related to forgery (e.g., Section 336 for forgery of documents, Section 340 for forged document usage) apply if counterfeit passports/visas are used.

## Applicability
- **Who is covered:** All Indian citizens seeking to travel abroad (Passports), and all foreign nationals seeking entry into, or currently residing in, India (Visas).
- **Who is exempt:** Nepal and Bhutan citizens do not generally need a visa to enter India (except under specific circumstances like entering from China), and often do not need a passport if possessing alternative valid identity proofs (like Voter ID).
- **Special Conditions:** OCI (Overseas Citizen of India) cardholders do not need a visa to enter India, but they must carry their foreign passport. Minors require consent from parents for passport issuance.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
### For Indian Passport:
**English:**
1. Register on the Passport Seva portal.
2. Fill the online application form and pay the required fee.
3. Schedule an appointment at a Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) or Post Office Passport Seva Kendra (POPSK).
4. Visit the PSK with original documents for verification, biometric data capture, and photograph.
5. Undergo Police Verification (Pre or Post issuance depending on the application type like Normal or Tatkaal).
6. Receive the passport via Speed Post.

1. पासपोर्ट सेवा पोर्टल पर पंजीकरण करें।

4. सत्यापन, बायोमेट्रिक डेटा कैप्चर और फोटोग्राफ के लिए मूल दस्तावेजों के साथ PSK जाएं।

### For Indian Visa (For Foreigners):
1. Visit the e-Visa portal or regular Indian Visa Online portal.
2. Fill out the application form accurately.
3. Upload necessary documents (photograph, passport data page).
4. Pay the visa fee online.
5. Receive the Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) via email (for e-Visa) or submit documents at the Indian Mission (for regular visa).

## Required Documents
For Passport:
1. Proof of Present Address (Aadhaar, Electricity Bill, Voter ID, Rent Agreement, etc.)
2. Proof of Date of Birth (Birth Certificate, Class 10 Marksheet, Aadhaar, PAN Card).
3. Documentary proof for any non-ECR (Emigration Check Not Required) category.
4. Annexures (if applicable, e.g., for minors, lost passports).

For Visa (General):
1. Valid passport with at least 6 months validity.
2. Recent passport-size photograph.
3. Proof of sufficient funds.
4. Return/onward ticket.
5. Specific documents based on visa type (e.g., employment letter for Employment Visa, admission letter for Student Visa).

## Government Authorities
1. **Ministry of External Affairs (MEA):** The apex body managing passports and consular services.
2. **Passport Seva Kendra (PSK) / Regional Passport Offices (RPO):** Handling the operational issuance of passports.
3. **Bureau of Immigration (BoI) / Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO):** Managing immigration, visas, and registration of foreigners in India.
4. **State Police:** Responsible for conducting police verification for passports.
5. **Indian Missions/Posts Abroad:** Handling visas for foreigners and consular services for Indians abroad.

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Applying with Fake Information/Documents:** Under Section 12 of the Passports Act, punishable with imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine up to ₹5,000, or both. BNS provisions for forgery (e.g. up to 7 years) may also apply.
- **Failure to Surrender Revoked Passport:** Imprisonment up to 2 years or fine or both.
- **Visa Overstay (Foreigners in India):** Under Section 14 of the Foreigners Act, punishable with imprisonment up to 5 years and fine. Deportation is standard.
- **Using Forged Passports/Visas:** Severe penalties under BNS, including imprisonment for 7-10 years and heavy fines.

## Exceptions
- **Diplomatic/Official Passports:** Issued to authorized government officials and diplomats, exempting them from certain visa requirements depending on bilateral agreements.
- **Emergency Certificates:** Issued for a one-way journey to India for citizens who lose their passport abroad and need to return urgently.
- **Visa-Free Travel:** Citizens of specific countries (like Nepal, Bhutan, Maldives) may have visa-free or visa-on-arrival entry conditions based on reciprocal arrangements.

## Important Timelines
- **Normal Passport Issuance:** Usually 30-45 days including police verification.
- **Tatkaal Passport Issuance:** 1-3 days (Police verification happens post-issuance).
- **Passport Validity:** 10 years for adults, 5 years for minors (or until they turn 18, whichever is earlier).
- **Visa Extension (FRRO):** Must be applied at least 60 days before the current visa expires.
- **FRRO Registration:** Foreigners arriving on visas valid for more than 180 days must register with FRRO within 14 days of arrival.

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. **Ignoring Police Verification:** Not being present at the address during verification can lead to adverse reports and delays.
2. **Incorrect Name Matching:** Discrepancies between Aadhaar, Birth Certificate, and Passport application names.
3. **Overstaying Visa:** Even an overstay of a single day is a violation of the Foreigners Act and can lead to bans or blacklisting.
4. **Working on a Tourist Visa:** Tourist visas strictly prohibit employment or business activities. Doing so results in deportation.
5. **Not Updating Address:** Failing to update the passport address after shifting residence.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Q: Can I apply for a passport if I am living in a rented house?**
   *A:* Yes, you can. You must provide the rent agreement along with another address proof like a utility bill or bank statement. Address proof for the last 1 year may be required.
2. **Q: What is the Tatkaal scheme?**
   *A:* Tatkaal is an expedited passport issuance scheme. Passports are dispatched within 1-3 days without prior police verification. Police verification happens after issuance.
3. **Q: How can I renew my passport?**
   *A:* You need to apply for 'Re-issue of Passport' through the Passport Seva portal before your current passport expires or within 3 years of expiry.
4. **Q: Is police verification necessary for a minor's passport?**
   *A:* Generally, if both parents hold valid passports with the spouse's name endorsed, police verification for a minor is not required.
5. **Q: What happens if my passport is lost abroad?**
   *A:* Immediately report the loss to the local police and obtain a report. Then contact the nearest Indian Embassy or Consulate to apply for a duplicate passport or an Emergency Certificate to travel back to India.
6. **Q: What is ECR and Non-ECR?**
   *A:* ECR (Emigration Check Required) is for applicants who haven't passed 10th standard and seek employment in specific countries. Non-ECR means no emigration clearance is required.
7. **Q: Can I hold dual citizenship in India?**
   *A:* No, the Indian Constitution does not allow dual citizenship. Acquiring foreign citizenship automatically terminates Indian citizenship.
8. **Q: What is an OCI card?**
   *A:* Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) is a form of permanent residency available to people of Indian origin and their spouses, which allows them to live and work in India indefinitely.
9. **Q: My name has a spelling mistake in my passport. How do I fix it?**
   *A:* You must apply for a "Re-issue" of the passport under the "Change in Existing Personal Particulars" category and provide documentary proof of the correct name.
10. **Q: What is the penalty for overstaying a visa in India?**
    *A:* Overstaying is a criminal offense under the Foreigners Act. It incurs heavy financial penalties, potential imprisonment, deportation, and blacklisting from future entry.
11. **Q: Do I need an agent to apply for a passport?**
    *A:* No. The online application process is straightforward, and the government strongly advises against using unauthorized agents.
12. **Q: How long is an Indian passport valid?**
    *A:* For adults, a regular passport is valid for 10 years. For minors under 18, it is valid for 5 years or until they attain 18 years of age, whichever is earlier.
13. **Q: What are the fees for a normal passport application?**
    *A:* For an adult normal 36-page passport, the fee is ₹1,500. For a 60-page passport, it is ₹2,000. Tatkaal carries an additional fee of ₹2,000.
14. **Q: I have a criminal case pending. Can I get a passport?**
    *A:* If criminal proceedings are pending in an Indian court, you must obtain a No Objection Certificate (NOC) or explicit permission from the court to get a passport.
15. **Q: What should I do if my visa application to India is rejected?**
    *A:* Visa issuance is a sovereign right. You cannot appeal the decision, but you may apply again with corrected or additional supporting documents after understanding the reason for rejection (if provided).
16. **Q: Can a foreigner convert a Tourist Visa to an Employment Visa while in India?**
    *A:* Generally, no. A Tourist Visa cannot be converted to any other type of visa. The foreigner must leave India and apply for an Employment Visa from their home country.
17. **Q: How long does Police Verification take?**
    *A:* Police verification typically takes 7-21 days depending on the state and district efficiency.
18. **Q: What is the mPassport Seva App?**
    *A:* It is an official mobile app by the Ministry of External Affairs that offers a wide variety of passport-related services, including application submission and status tracking.
19. **Q: Can I travel if my passport is valid for less than 6 months?**
    *A:* Most countries require a passport to have at least 6 months of validity remaining. It is highly recommended to renew your passport if it has less than 6 months validity.
20. **Q: How can foreigners register with FRRO?**
    *A:* Foreigners can register entirely online through the e-FRRO portal, uploading documents and receiving their Registration Certificate digitally without visiting the office.

## Common Myths vs Facts
1. **Myth:** You must own a house to get a passport.
   **Fact:** No, rent agreements, utility bills in parents' name, or employer letters are valid address proofs.
2. **Myth:** Agents can guarantee visa approval.
   **Fact:** Visa approval is strictly at the discretion of the respective embassy/consulate. No agent can guarantee it.
3. **Myth:** A pending traffic challan means my passport will be rejected.
   **Fact:** Minor offenses like traffic challans do not block passport issuance. Only pending criminal proceedings in court do.
4. **Myth:** You can travel on a damaged passport if the visa page is intact.
   **Fact:** A damaged passport is considered invalid and can lead to denied boarding or deportation.
5. **Myth:** E-Visa is a stamp on the passport before travel.
   **Fact:** E-Visa is an Electronic Travel Authorization. The actual stamp is placed at the immigration counter upon arrival.
6. **Myth:** Tatkaal passports don't require police verification.
   **Fact:** Police verification happens *after* the passport is issued in Tatkaal cases. If verification fails, the passport can be impounded.
7. **Myth:** You can hold both Indian and US passports.
   **Fact:** India does not allow dual citizenship. You must surrender your Indian passport upon acquiring foreign citizenship.
8. **Myth:** Tourist visas allow you to do short-term freelance work.
   **Fact:** No employment, freelance or otherwise, is permitted on a Tourist Visa.
9. **Myth:** Newborn babies can travel on their parents' passports.
   **Fact:** No, every individual, including a newborn baby, must have their own separate passport.
10. **Myth:** Visa rejection fees are refundable.
    **Fact:** Visa application fees cover the cost of processing and are strictly non-refundable, regardless of the outcome.

**Example 9: Visa Overstay**
*Foreigner:* My Indian visa expired 2 days ago. What is the penalty?
*Assistant:* Overstaying is a violation of the Foreigners Act. You must immediately apply for an exit permit through the e-FRRO portal. You will be subject to a financial penalty based on the duration of your overstay, which must be paid before departure.

**Example 10: Changing Address**
*Citizen:* Maine address change kar liya hai. Kya passport me change karna zaroori hai?
*Assistant:* Agar aap abroad travel karne wale hain, toh address update karna zaroori nahi hai kyunki passport ki validity same rehti hai. Lekin agar aap naya address update karna chahte hain, toh aapko 'Re-issue of Passport' ke liye apply karna hoga.

**Example 11: Surrender Certificate**
*Citizen:* I just got my Canadian citizenship. What do I do with my Indian passport?
*Assistant:* India does not allow dual citizenship. You must surrender your Indian passport to the nearest Indian High Commission/Consulate or Passport Office. You will receive a Surrender Certificate, which is required to apply for an OCI card.

**Example 12: Damaged Passport**
*Citizen:* Mere passport pe paani gir gaya aur photo thodi kharab ho gayi hai. Kya main ispe travel kar sakta hu?
*Assistant:* Nahi. Damaged passport pe travel karna risky hai, aapko immigration pe roka jaa sakta hai. Aapko 'Re-issue of Passport' under 'Damaged Passport' category apply karna chahiye naye passport ke liye.

**Example 13: OCI Card**
*Citizen:* Can an OCI cardholder buy agricultural land in India?
*Assistant:* No, OCI cardholders have parity with Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) in financial and economic fields, but they are not permitted to purchase agricultural land or plantation properties in India.

**Example 14: Name Change After Marriage**
*Citizen:* Shadi ke baad mujhe apna surname change karna hai passport me.
*Assistant:* Surname change karne ke liye aapko 'Re-issue of Passport' apply karna hoga. Aapko apni marriage certificate aur spouse ka passport (agar ho) as supporting documents dene honge.

**Example 15: FRRO Registration**
*Foreigner:* I am on a student visa in India valid for 2 years. Do I need to register?
*Assistant:* Yes, all foreigners arriving on visas valid for more than 180 days must register with the Foreigners Regional Registration Officer (FRRO) within 14 days of their arrival in India through the e-FRRO online portal.
