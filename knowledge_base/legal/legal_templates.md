# Legal Drafting and Standard Templates (कानूनी ड्राफ्टिंग और मानक टेम्पलेट)

## Overview
**English:** Legal drafting is the synthesis of law and fact in a language form. Standard legal templates provide a structural foundation for citizens to execute common agreements, affidavits, notices, and deeds without requiring extensive legal counsel for basic transactions. Proper drafting ensures that intentions are clearly documented, legally binding, and admissible in courts under the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA), 2023. This document serves as a master guide for understanding, utilizing, and registering legal templates in India.

**Hindi (Unicode):** कानूनी ड्राफ्टिंग कानून और तथ्यों को भाषा के रूप में प्रस्तुत करने की कला है। मानक कानूनी टेम्पलेट नागरिकों को बुनियादी लेनदेन के लिए व्यापक कानूनी परामर्श की आवश्यकता के बिना सामान्य समझौतों, हलफनामों, नोटिसों और विलेखों को निष्पादित करने के लिए एक संरचनात्मक आधार प्रदान करते हैं। उचित ड्राफ्टिंग यह सुनिश्चित करती है कि इरादों को स्पष्ट रूप से प्रलेखित किया गया है, वे कानूनी रूप से बाध्यकारी हैं, और भारतीय साक्ष्य अधिनियम (BSA), 2023 के तहत अदालतों में स्वीकार्य हैं। यह दस्तावेज़ भारत में कानूनी टेम्पलेट्स को समझने, उपयोग करने और पंजीकृत करने के लिए एक मास्टर गाइड के रूप में कार्य करता है।

## Definition
**English:** A Legal Template is a pre-formatted document outlining the standard clauses, terms, and conditions required for a specific legal transaction. It serves as a blueprint where parties can fill in their specific details to create a binding contract or declaration.

## Why It Matters
**English:** Using correct legal templates is crucial because verbal agreements are hard to prove in court. A well-drafted document with correct stamp duty and registration acts as primary evidence under the BSA 2023. It protects rights, defines obligations, and prevents expensive litigations.

**Hindi (Unicode):** सही कानूनी टेम्पलेट्स का उपयोग करना महत्वपूर्ण है क्योंकि अदालत में मौखिक समझौतों को साबित करना मुश्किल है। सही स्टाम्प ड्यूटी और पंजीकरण के साथ अच्छी तरह से तैयार किया गया दस्तावेज़ BSA 2023 के तहत प्राथमिक साक्ष्य के रूप में कार्य करता है। यह अधिकारों की रक्षा करता है, दायित्वों को परिभाषित करता है और महंगे मुकदमों को रोकता है।

## Legal Background
**History:** Legal drafting in India evolved from British common law principles. With digitization, e-stamping and standardized e-templates have been introduced by various state governments to ease the execution of documents.
**Purpose:** To standardize documentation, reduce ambiguity in agreements, prevent fraud, and ensure uniformity in the administration of justice.
**Applicable Acts:** 
- Indian Contract Act, 1872
- Registration Act, 1908
- Indian Stamp Act, 1899
- Transfer of Property Act, 1882
- Notaries Act, 1952
- Information Technology Act, 2000 (for e-contracts)
**Relevant Sections:**
- Section 10 of Indian Contract Act: What agreements are contracts.
- Section 17 of Registration Act, 1908: Documents of which registration is compulsory.
- Section 61, 62 of Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) 2023: Proof of contents of documents and primary evidence.
- Section 336 of Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023: Forgery of documents.
- Section 340 of BNS 2023: Forged documents used as genuine.

## Applicability
**Who is covered:** Any citizen of India aged 18 or above, of sound mind, and not disqualified by law from contracting.
**Who is exempt:** Minors, persons of unsound mind, and persons disqualified by law cannot legally execute binding contracts independently (though guardians can act on their behalf).
**Special conditions:** Certain documents (like sale of immovable property over Rs. 100) mandatorily require registration. Digital templates must be signed using Class 3 DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) or Aadhaar eSign to be valid under the IT Act.

## Rights of Citizens
**English:** Citizens have the right to freely negotiate the terms of their contracts, seek legal counsel before signing any document, and obtain certified copies of their registered documents from the Sub-Registrar's office.
**Hindi (Unicode):** नागरिकों को अपने अनुबंध की शर्तों पर स्वतंत्र रूप से बातचीत करने, किसी भी दस्तावेज़ पर हस्ताक्षर करने से पहले कानूनी सलाह लेने और उप-पंजीयक कार्यालय से अपने पंजीकृत दस्तावेजों की प्रमाणित प्रतियां प्राप्त करने का अधिकार है।
**Hinglish:** Citizens ko apne contract ke terms freely negotiate karne, sign karne se pehle legal advice lene, aur Sub-Registrar office se registered documents ki certified copy lene ka right hai.

## Duties & Responsibilities
**English:** Citizens must provide truthful information, pay the exact state-mandated stamp duty, ensure witnesses are present during signing, and not use forged documents.
**Hindi (Unicode):** नागरिकों को सच्ची जानकारी प्रदान करनी चाहिए, राज्य द्वारा अनिवार्य सटीक स्टाम्प ड्यूटी का भुगतान करना चाहिए, यह सुनिश्चित करना चाहिए कि हस्ताक्षर करते समय गवाह मौजूद हों, और जाली दस्तावेजों का उपयोग नहीं करना चाहिए।
**Hinglish:** Citizens ki duty hai ki wo sahi information dein, correct stamp duty pay karein, signing ke time witnesses present rakhein, aur fake documents ka use bilkul na karein.

## Step-by-Step Legal Procedure
**English:** 
1. Choose the Correct Template (e.g., Lease agreement, Will, Affidavit).
2. Fill in specific details (Names, Addresses, Aadhaar/PAN, Terms).
3. Purchase non-judicial e-stamp paper of the required value as per state laws.
4. Print the drafted template on the e-stamp paper or attach it legally.
5. Sign all pages. Both parties and at least two witnesses must sign the last page.
6. If required, get it notarized by a registered Notary Public.
7. For mandatory documents, visit the Sub-Registrar's office with parties, witnesses, and original IDs for registration.
**Hindi (Unicode):**
1. सही टेम्पलेट चुनें (जैसे, पट्टा समझौता, वसीयत, हलफनामा)।
2. विशिष्ट विवरण भरें (नाम, पते, आधार/पैन, शर्तें)।
3. राज्य के कानूनों के अनुसार आवश्यक मूल्य का गैर-न्यायिक ई-स्टाम्प पेपर खरीदें।
4. ई-स्टाम्प पेपर पर ड्राफ्ट किए गए टेम्पलेट को प्रिंट करें या इसे कानूनी रूप से संलग्न करें।
5. सभी पृष्ठों पर हस्ताक्षर करें। दोनों पक्षों और कम से कम दो गवाहों को अंतिम पृष्ठ पर हस्ताक्षर करने होंगे।
6. यदि आवश्यक हो, तो इसे एक पंजीकृत नोटरी पब्लिक द्वारा नोटरीकृत करवाएं।
7. अनिवार्य दस्तावेजों के लिए, पंजीकरण के लिए पार्टियों, गवाहों और मूल आईडी के साथ उप-पंजीयक कार्यालय जाएं।

## Required Documents
- Original ID Proofs (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, Passport) of all parties.
- Passport-size photographs.
- ID proofs of two witnesses.
- Title deeds (if dealing with property).
- E-stamp paper receipt.
- Registration fee payment receipt.

## Government Authorities
- Sub-Registrar / Registrar of Assurances (for property and document registration).
- Notary Public (appointed by State/Central Govt for attestation).
- Revenue Department (for stamp duty collection).
- Civil Courts (for enforcing contracts or resolving template-based disputes).

## Penalties & Punishments
- **Evasion of Stamp Duty:** Impounding of document and penalty up to 10 times the deficient stamp duty (Indian Stamp Act, 1899).
- **Forgery (Section 336 BNS 2023):** Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.
- **Forgery for purpose of cheating (Section 338 BNS 2023):** Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.
- **Making false declaration (Section 238 BNS 2023):** Imprisonment up to 3 years and fine.

## Exceptions
- Oral agreements are valid for small, non-property transactions (though hard to prove).
- Government documents and certain bank guarantees are exempt from standard stamp duty.
- Wills do not require stamp paper; they can be drafted on plain paper but registration is highly recommended.

## Important Timelines
- **Registration:** Documents must be presented for registration within 4 months from the date of execution.
- **Validity of Stamp Paper:** Generally, an unused stamp paper is valid for 6 months for a refund, but once executed, the document's validity depends on the contract terms.
- **Limitation for Breach of Contract:** A lawsuit for breach of contract must be filed within 3 years from the date the breach occurs (Limitation Act, 1963).

## Common Legal Mistakes
1. Drafting documents on under-valued stamp paper.
2. Forgetting to sign every page of a multi-page document.
3. Not getting mandatory documents (like rent agreements over 11 months) registered.
4. Using vague terms like "reasonable time" instead of specific dates.
5. Relatives acting as witnesses for a Will (beneficiaries should never be witnesses).

## Safety Tips
**English:** Always cross-verify the registration number of the Notary. Use Govt portals like SHCIL for purchasing e-stamps. Never sign blank stamp papers. Keep digital scans of all executed documents.
**Hindi (Unicode):** हमेशा नोटरी की पंजीकरण संख्या को क्रॉस-सत्यापित करें। ई-स्टाम्प खरीदने के लिए SHCIL जैसे सरकारी पोर्टल्स का उपयोग करें। कभी भी खाली स्टाम्प पेपर पर हस्ताक्षर न करें। सभी निष्पादित दस्तावेजों के डिजिटल स्कैन रखें।
**Hinglish:** Notary ka registration number hamesha check karein. E-stamps lene ke liye SHCIL jaise official govt portals ka use karein. Blank stamp paper pe kabhi sign na karein. Apne sabhi signed documents ka digital scan hamesha backup karke rakhein.

## Frequently Asked Questions

1. **Can I draft a legal document myself without a lawyer?**
   Yes, you can draft standard documents like affidavits, rent agreements, and wills yourself using templates. However, for complex property transactions or commercial contracts, consulting a lawyer is recommended to ensure all legal clauses are airtight under the Indian Contract Act.
   *(Haan, aap standard documents khud draft kar sakte hain. Lekin complex matters ke liye lawyer se consult karna better hota hai.)*

2. **Is a Will valid if written on plain paper?**
   Yes, a Will (Vasiyat) does not require stamp paper. It can be written on plain paper. However, it must be signed by the testator and attested by at least two independent witnesses.
   *(हाँ, वसीयत सादे कागज पर लिखी जा सकती है, लेकिन इस पर दो स्वतंत्र गवाहों के हस्ताक्षर होने चाहिए।)*

3. **What is the difference between Notarization and Registration?**
   Notarization is merely the attestation of signatures by a Notary Public. Registration involves recording the document with the Sub-Registrar, making it a public record. Certain documents strictly require registration.
   *(Notarization sirf signature verify karta hai, jabki Registration document ko government records mein legally darj karta hai.)*

4. **Why is a rent agreement usually made for 11 months?**
   Under Section 17 of the Registration Act, 1908, lease agreements for a period exceeding 11 months require mandatory registration. To avoid the hassle and cost of registration and stamp duty, parties often choose 11-month agreements.
   *(पंजीकरण अधिनियम के तहत 11 महीने से अधिक के पट्टे के लिए पंजीकरण अनिवार्य है, इसलिए लोग समय और पैसे बचाने के लिए 11 महीने का समझौता करते हैं।)*

5. **Is an e-signed contract legally valid in India?**
   Yes, under the Information Technology Act, 2000, contracts executed via secure electronic signatures (like Aadhaar eSign) are legally valid and enforceable, except for a few documents like Wills and Power of Attorney.
   *(Haan, IT Act ke tahat e-signed contracts bilkul valid hain, siwaye kuch specific documents jaise Will ya Power of Attorney ke.)*

6. **What happens if I under-pay stamp duty on an agreement?**
   If stamp duty is underpaid, the document cannot be admitted as evidence in court until the deficit is paid along with a penalty that can be up to 10 times the deficient amount.
   *(अगर स्टाम्प ड्यूटी कम दी गई है, तो जुर्माना (कमी का 10 गुना तक) चुकाए बिना अदालत इसे साक्ष्य के रूप में स्वीकार नहीं करेगी।)*

7. **Do both parties need to be present for Notarization?**
   Ideally, yes. The Notary must verify the identity of the persons signing the document. They may ask for original ID proofs before putting their seal and signature.
   *(Haan, identity verify karne ke liye ideally dono parties ka Notary ke saamne present hona zaroori hota hai.)*

8. **Can I use a legal template I downloaded from the internet?**
   Yes, internet templates serve as a good starting point. However, you must modify the clauses to suit your specific factual situation and ensure it complies with your state's specific laws.
   *(हाँ, आप इंटरनेट टेम्पलेट का उपयोग कर सकते हैं, लेकिन आपको इसे अपनी विशिष्ट स्थिति और राज्य के कानूनों के अनुसार संशोधित करना होगा।)*

9. **What is an Affidavit (Shapath Patra)?**
   An affidavit is a sworn statement of facts made voluntarily under oath before a person authorized by law (like an Oath Commissioner or Notary). Providing false information in it is a criminal offence.
   *(Affidavit ek sworn statement hota hai jo Notary ke saamne oath lekar diya jata hai. Isme jhoot bolna jurm hai.)*

10. **Is it mandatory to register a Power of Attorney (PoA)?**
    Registration is mandatory if the PoA involves the transfer, sale, or relinquishment of immovable property. For other general purposes, notarization is usually sufficient.
    *(यदि PoA अचल संपत्ति के हस्तांतरण से संबंधित है, तो पंजीकरण अनिवार्य है। अन्य सामान्य उद्देश्यों के लिए, नोटरीकरण पर्याप्त है।)*

11. **How long is an unregistered agreement valid?**
    An unregistered agreement is valid as a contract between the parties, but if it is legally required to be registered (e.g., sale of property), it will not grant any legal title or be accepted as evidence in court for property rights.
    *(Unregistered agreement parties ke beech valid ho sakta hai, par property transfer jaise cases mein bina registration ke legal rights nahi milte.)*

12. **Can a minor sign a legal contract?**
    No, according to the Indian Contract Act, a minor (under 18) is not competent to contract. Any agreement with a minor is void from the beginning (void ab initio).
    *(नहीं, नाबालिग कानूनी रूप से अनुबंध नहीं कर सकता। नाबालिग के साथ किया गया कोई भी समझौता शुरू से ही अमान्य (void) होता है।)*

13. **Who should keep the original legal document?**
    In most cases, the party receiving the rights (e.g., the buyer, the tenant, the employer) keeps the original. The other party should retain a certified or notarized photocopy.
    *(Zyadatar cases mein jise rights milte hain (jaise buyer ya tenant) wo original rakhta hai, aur doosri party certified copy rakhti hai.)*

14. **What is a Legal Notice?**
    A legal notice is a formal written communication between the parties intimating the sender’s intention to undertake legal proceedings against the receiver if their grievances are not resolved.
    *(कानूनी नोटिस एक औपचारिक लिखित संचार है जो प्राप्तकर्ता को चेतावनी देता है कि यदि शिकायत का समाधान नहीं हुआ तो कानूनी कार्रवाई की जाएगी।)*

15. **Does a legal notice need to be sent by a lawyer only?**
    No, an individual can draft and send a legal notice themselves using a standard template. However, having a lawyer draft it on their letterhead carries more weight and ensures proper legal language.
    *(Nahi, aap khud bhi legal notice bhej sakte hain. Par lawyer ke letterhead par bheja gaya notice zyada effective hota hai.)*

16. **How can I get an e-Stamp paper?**
    You can get e-Stamp papers from Stock Holding Corporation of India Limited (SHCIL) branches, authorized banks, or designated stamp vendors. Some states also allow online generation of e-stamps.
    *(आप SHCIL शाखाओं, अधिकृत बैंकों या नामित स्टाम्प विक्रेताओं से ई-स्टाम्प प्राप्त कर सकते हैं।)*

17. **What happens to a contract if one party dies?**
    Generally, contractual obligations pass on to the legal heirs unless the contract is of a personal nature (like painting a portrait) or specifically states that it terminates upon death.
    *(Agar contract personal nature ka nahi hai, toh maut ke baad obligations legal heirs (uttaradhikari) par transfer ho jati hain.)*

18. **Can witnesses to a Will also be beneficiaries?**
    No, a beneficiary (someone receiving property under the Will) should never be a witness. Doing so can invalidate the legacy given to that specific witness under the Indian Succession Act.
    *(नहीं, वसीयत का गवाह कभी भी लाभार्थी (जिसे संपत्ति मिल रही हो) नहीं होना चाहिए, अन्यथा वह वसीयत अमान्य हो सकती है।)*

19. **What is an MoU (Memorandum of Understanding)?**
    An MoU is a preliminary document expressing the intention of parties to enter into a contract in the future. It is generally not legally binding unless it contains specific enforceable clauses.
    *(MoU ek preliminary agreement hota hai jo parties ke intentions dikhata hai. Ye generally legally binding nahi hota jab tak isme specific clauses na hon.)*

20. **Can I correct a mistake in a registered document?**
    Yes, if there is a typographical or factual error in a registered document, you can execute and register a 'Rectification Deed' or 'Correction Deed' with the mutual consent of all parties.
    *(हाँ, यदि पंजीकृत दस्तावेज़ में कोई गलती है, तो सभी पक्षों की सहमति से 'सुधार विलेख' (Rectification Deed) पंजीकृत किया जा सकता है।)*

## Common Myths vs Facts

1. **Myth:** Notarized documents are as good as registered documents.
   **Fact:** Not true. Notarization only authenticates signatures. Registration makes the document a legal public record and is mandatory for certain property transfers.
2. **Myth:** A Will must be registered to be valid.
   **Fact:** A Will does not legally require registration. An unregistered Will signed by the testator and two witnesses is perfectly valid.
3. **Myth:** 100-rupee stamp paper is enough for all agreements.
   **Fact:** Stamp duty rates vary by state and the type/value of the transaction. Always check your state's Stamp Act schedule.
4. **Myth:** A contract signed in blue ink is invalid.
   **Fact:** Any legible ink is acceptable legally, though blue or black is standard. The signature's authenticity matters, not the color.
5. **Myth:** Legal notices must always be replied to.
   **Fact:** While it's highly recommended to reply to prevent the other party from gaining an advantage in court, there is no strict legal mandate to reply to every frivolous notice.
6. **Myth:** Only a lawyer can draft a valid contract.
   **Fact:** Anyone can draft a contract. If the elements of a valid contract (offer, acceptance, consideration, lawful object) are met, it is binding.
7. **Myth:** Electronic signatures are not accepted in Indian courts.
   **Fact:** Digital signatures (Class 3 DSC and Aadhaar eSign) are legally recognized under the IT Act, 2000, and accepted in courts.
8. **Myth:** An agreement on a blank piece of paper has no value.
   **Fact:** Oral and plain paper agreements are valid contracts if they fulfill Section 10 of the Contract Act, but they are difficult to prove without stamp duty and registration.
9. **Myth:** Witnesses are responsible if the contract is breached.
   **Fact:** Witnesses only testify that the parties signed the document in their presence. They have no liability for the actual contract terms.
10. **Myth:** If a stamp paper is older than 6 months, it cannot be used.
    **Fact:** An unexecuted stamp paper over 6 months old cannot be refunded, but there is no time limit for its use for executing a document (as per Supreme Court rulings).

## Conversation Examples

**Conversation 1: Drafting a Rent Agreement**
**Citizen:** Mujhe apne tenant ke sath rent agreement banana hai. Kya lawyer zaroori hai?
**Assistant:** Nahi, aap standard rent agreement template use kar sakte hain. Bas 11 mahine ka agreement banayein, usme rent, security deposit aur notice period clear likhein, aur state ke hisaab se sahi e-stamp paper par print karke notarize kara lein.

**Conversation 2: Importance of Will Registration**
**Citizen:** Maine saade kagaz par vasiyat likhi hai, kya yeh legal hai?
**Assistant:** Yes, a Will written on plain paper is completely legal in India. Ensure you have signed it and two independent witnesses have also signed it in your presence. Registration is optional but recommended to prevent future disputes.

**Conversation 3: E-Stamping**
**Citizen:** ई-स्टाम्प पेपर कहाँ से मिलता है? क्या मुझे कोर्ट जाना होगा?
**Assistant:** नहीं, आपको कोर्ट जाने की आवश्यकता नहीं है। आप ई-स्टाम्प पेपर अधिकृत बैंकों, SHCIL शाखाओं, या लाइसेंस प्राप्त स्टाम्प वेंडर्स से प्राप्त कर सकते हैं। कई राज्यों में इसे ऑनलाइन भी निकाला जा सकता है।

**Conversation 4: Legal Notice for Unpaid Salary**
**Citizen:** Meri company ne 2 mahine se salary nahi di hai. Kya main khud legal notice bhej sakta hu?
**Assistant:** Haan, aap standard legal notice template use karke company ke HR/Director ko registered post ya official email ke through notice bhej sakte hain. Notice mein unhe 15 din ka time dein payment clear karne ke liye.

**Conversation 5: Affidavit for Name Change**
**Citizen:** Mujhe apna naam change karna hai, affidavit ka format kya hoga?
**Assistant:** Name change affidavit mein aapka old name, new name, aur reason for change likha hona chahiye. Ise non-judicial stamp paper (usually Rs. 20 ya Rs. 50) par print karke Notary ya Oath Commissioner se attest karwana hoga.

**Conversation 6: Validity of Expired Stamp Paper**
**Citizen:** Mere paas 1 saal purana khali stamp paper rakha hai. Kya main uspe abhi agreement bana sakta hu?
**Assistant:** Yes, Supreme Court rulings state that there is no expiry date for the use of a purchased stamp paper for executing a document. However, if you want a refund for an unused stamp paper, that must be claimed within 6 months.

**Conversation 7: Power of Attorney**
**Citizen:** क्या मैं अपनी संपत्ति बेचने के लिए अपने भाई को पावर ऑफ अटॉर्नी (PoA) दे सकता हूँ?
**Assistant:** हाँ, आप दे सकते हैं। लेकिन संपत्ति बेचने के लिए आपको एक 'स्पेशल पावर ऑफ अटॉर्नी' (SPA) या 'जनरल पावर ऑफ अटॉर्नी' (GPA) बनानी होगी और उसे उप-पंजीयक (Sub-Registrar) के कार्यालय में अनिवार्य रूप से पंजीकृत (Register) करवाना होगा।

**Conversation 8: Difference between MoU and Agreement**
**Citizen:** Business deal ke liye pehle MoU sign karna sahi hai ya direct agreement?
**Assistant:** MoU ek preliminary step hai jo dikhata hai ki aap dono terms par agree kar rahe hain, par ye hamesha legally binding nahi hota. Agar aapko strict legal protection chahiye, toh direct Contract ya Agreement sign karna zyada safe hai.

**Conversation 9: Minor's Agreement**
**Citizen:** Mera beta 17 saal ka hai, wo ek software project ke liye contract sign kar raha hai. Valid hai?
**Assistant:** No, a contract signed by a minor (under 18) is 'void ab initio' (invalid from the start) under the Indian Contract Act. As a legal guardian, you must sign the contract on his behalf for it to be legally enforceable.

**Conversation 10: Promissory Note**
**Citizen:** Maine dost ko 50,000 rupaye udhar diye hain. Kaunsa document banwau?
**Assistant:** Aap ek 'Promissory Note' (Vachan Patra) banwa sakte hain. Yeh ek simple document hai jisme aapka dost likhit mein waada karta hai ki wo ek specific date par paise lauta dega. Is par revenue stamp lagana zaroori hai.

**Conversation 11: Rectifying a Mistake**
**Citizen:** Sale deed register ho gayi hai par usme plot number galat type ho gaya. Kya karun?
**Assistant:** Ghabrayein nahi. Aapko buyer aur seller dono ki consent se Sub-Registrar office mein ek 'Rectification Deed' (Sudhar Vilekh) register karwani hogi, jisme likha hoga ki correct plot number kya hai.

**Conversation 12: E-Signature Validity**
**Citizen:** Client doosre shahar mein hai, kya hum PDF par digital sign (Aadhaar eSign) kar sakte hain?
**Assistant:** Haan, IT Act 2000 ke anusar Aadhaar eSign ya Class 3 Digital Signature bilkul valid hain aur court mein admissible hain. Aap online document execution platform ka use kar sakte hain.

**Conversation 13: Gift Deed**
**Citizen:** मुझे अपनी बेटी को एक फ्लैट गिफ्ट करना है। क्या इसके लिए स्टाम्प ड्यूटी लगेगी?
**Assistant:** हाँ, अचल संपत्ति (Immovable property) के लिए गिफ्ट डीड बनाना और उसे पंजीकृत करवाना अनिवार्य है। हालांकि, कई राज्यों में रक्त संबंधियों (blood relatives) को संपत्ति गिफ्ट करने पर स्टाम्प ड्यूटी में भारी छूट मिलती है।

**Conversation 14: Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)**
**Citizen:** Naya startup shuru kar raha hu, employees se idea chori hone se kaise bachau?
**Assistant:** Aap apne sabhi employees aur partners ke sath ek Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) sign karein. Yeh unhe legally bind karega ki wo aapki confidential information aur ideas kisi aur ke sath share na karein.

**Conversation 15: Revoking a Power of Attorney**
**Citizen:** Maine jo PoA di thi, usko cancel kaise karu?
**Assistant:** PoA cancel karne ke liye aapko ek 'Deed of Revocation' draft karni hogi. Agar original PoA registered thi, toh revocation deed ko bhi usi Sub-Registrar ke paas register karwana hoga aur uski ek copy PoA holder ko bhejni hogi.
